# Democratic Health: Overview

## Purpose

This section tracks **positive indicators** of democratic functioning. While threat-tracking monitors warning signs, democratic health monitoring measures the vitality of democratic institutions, processes, and culture.

Healthy democracies exhibit:

- High public trust in electoral processes and institutions
- Robust press freedom and media pluralism
- Active civic participation beyond voting
- Transparent and accountable governance
- Peaceful transfers of power and respect for opposition
- Independent judiciary and rule of law

## Indicator Categories

### Institutional Trust

Measures of public confidence in democratic institutions:

- Trust in elections and vote counting
- Confidence in courts and judicial independence
- Faith in legislative processes
- Belief in governmental responsiveness

### Electoral Integrity

Quality of election administration:

- Voter access and registration ease
- Election security and accuracy
- Acceptance of results by candidates and parties
- Peaceful transitions of power

### Press Freedom

Media independence and pluralism:

- Journalist safety and legal protections
- Media ownership diversity
- Access to government information
- Platform for diverse viewpoints

### Civic Participation

Engagement beyond voting:

- Voter turnout trends
- Civic organization membership
- Public comment and testimony
- Local government participation

## Data Sources

Democratic health indicators draw from:

- **Surveys**: Pew, Gallup, ANES on institutional trust
- **Indices**: Freedom House, V-Dem, Economist Democracy Index
- **Election data**: Turnout, registration, wait times
- **Press reports**: Journalist prosecutions, FOIA compliance
- **Government data**: Public meeting attendance, comment periods

## Scoring Framework

Each indicator area uses a 1-10 scale:

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 9-10 | Exceptional - among best democracies globally |
| 7-8 | Healthy - strong functioning with minor concerns |
| 5-6 | Adequate - functioning but notable weaknesses |
| 3-4 | Concerning - significant problems threatening function |
| 1-2 | Crisis - democratic functioning severely compromised |
