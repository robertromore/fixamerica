# Appendix U: Model Regulations

---

## Overview

This appendix provides draft regulatory text for key ICS provisions, formatted in CFR style. These model regulations would be promulgated by the relevant agencies following statutory enactment.

---

## Part 1: DHS Regulations (8 CFR)

### Subpart A: CLP Status

```text
§ XXX.1 Definitions.

For purposes of this part:

(a) "Corridor Lawful Presence" or "CLP" means the immigration status
    established under [ICS Act], section XXX.

(b) "CLP holder" means an individual granted Corridor Lawful Presence.

(c) "Hub city" means a city designated by the Secretary for initial
    CLP placement under § XXX.20.

(d) "Corridor city" means a city within a designated corridor region
    where Tier B CLP holders may reside under § XXX.21.

(e) "Tier" means the progression level within CLP status:
    (1) "Tier A" means initial placement status with residence
        limited to the assigned hub city metropolitan area.
    (2) "Tier B" means intermediate status with residence permitted
        within the assigned corridor region.
    (3) "Tier C" means advanced status with residence permitted
        anywhere in the United States.

(f) "Legacy track" means the registration pathway for individuals
    with established presence in the United States as described
    in subpart D of this part.

§ XXX.2 Eligibility for CLP.

(a) IN GENERAL.—An individual is eligible for CLP if the individual:
    (1) Is physically present in the United States;
    (2) Has registered under § XXX.10;
    (3) Has completed biometric collection under § XXX.11;
    (4) Is not inadmissible under section 212(a) of the INA for:
        (i) Criminal grounds under section 212(a)(2), except as
            waived under § XXX.50;
        (ii) Security grounds under section 212(a)(3);
        (iii) Fraud under section 212(a)(6)(C)(i); or
        (iv) Prior removal under section 212(a)(9), except as
             waived under § XXX.51.
    (5) Has not been convicted of an aggravated felony as defined
        in section 101(a)(43) of the INA.

(b) DERIVATIVE ELIGIBILITY.—The spouse and unmarried children under
    age 21 of a principal CLP holder are eligible for derivative
    CLP status under § XXX.30.

§ XXX.3 Application for CLP.

(a) FORM.—Application for CLP shall be made on Form I-XXX.

(b) FILING.—Applications may be filed:
    (1) At a designated port of entry;
    (2) At a USCIS office designated for CLP processing;
    (3) At a hub city intake center; or
    (4) Electronically through the USCIS online system.

(c) FEE.—The application fee is $XXX, which may be waived for
    applicants with income below 150% of the federal poverty level.

(d) BIOMETRICS.—Applicants shall appear for biometric collection
    within 30 days of application filing.

§ XXX.4 Adjudication.

(a) TIMELINE.—USCIS shall adjudicate CLP applications within
    90 days of complete filing.

(b) DECISION.—
    (1) APPROVAL.—Upon approval, USCIS shall issue a CLP card
        indicating tier, geographic assignment, and expiration date.
    (2) DENIAL.—Upon denial, USCIS shall provide written notice
        of the reasons for denial and the right to appeal under
        § XXX.60.

(c) PROVISIONAL STATUS.—An applicant who has filed a complete
    application and completed biometrics shall be granted
    provisional CLP status pending adjudication, authorizing
    employment and residence.

§ XXX.5 Duration and renewal.

(a) INITIAL DURATION.—CLP status is valid for 2 years from issuance.

(b) RENEWAL.—CLP status shall be renewed for successive 2-year
    periods upon demonstration of continued eligibility and
    satisfaction of tier requirements.

(c) AUTOMATIC RENEWAL.—CLP holders in good standing who have
    applied for tier advancement or permanent residence shall have
    their status automatically extended pending adjudication.
```

### Subpart B: Tier Advancement

```text
§ XXX.20 Tier A requirements.

(a) RESIDENCE.—Tier A CLP holders shall reside within the
    metropolitan area of their assigned hub city.

(b) EMPLOYMENT.—Tier A CLP holders are authorized for employment
    with any registered employer nationwide, subject to residence
    requirements.

(c) SERVICES.—Tier A CLP holders shall:
    (1) Participate in English language instruction of not less
        than 10 hours per week, unless demonstrating proficiency
        at CEFR B1 level or higher;
    (2) Complete intake orientation within 30 days of placement;
    (3) Register with a case manager within 14 days of placement.

(d) ADVANCEMENT.—A Tier A CLP holder may apply for advancement
    to Tier B upon:
    (1) Completion of 6 months in Tier A status;
    (2) Demonstration of English proficiency at CEFR A2 level;
    (3) Verification of employment or active job search;
    (4) Completion of required civic orientation modules; and
    (5) No unresolved compliance violations.

§ XXX.21 Tier B requirements.

(a) RESIDENCE.—Tier B CLP holders may reside within any city
    in their assigned corridor region.

(b) EMPLOYMENT.—Tier B CLP holders are authorized for employment
    with any registered employer nationwide.

(c) ADVANCEMENT.—A Tier B CLP holder may apply for advancement
    to Tier C upon:
    (1) Completion of 12 months in Tier B status;
    (2) Demonstration of English proficiency at CEFR B1 level;
    (3) Verification of stable employment;
    (4) Completion of required civic orientation modules; and
    (5) No unresolved compliance violations.

§ XXX.22 Tier C requirements.

(a) RESIDENCE.—Tier C CLP holders may reside anywhere in the
    United States.

(b) INTERNATIONAL TRAVEL.—Tier C CLP holders may apply for
    advance parole for international travel under § XXX.40.

(c) PERMANENT RESIDENCE.—Tier C CLP holders are eligible to
    apply for adjustment of status to permanent residence under
    [ICS Act] section XXX.
```

### Subpart C: Legacy Track

```text
§ XXX.30 Legacy track eligibility.

(a) IN GENERAL.—An individual is eligible for the legacy track if:
    (1) The individual has been continuously physically present
        in the United States for not less than 5 years prior to
        the date of application;
    (2) The individual has not been convicted of a felony or
        3 or more misdemeanors;
    (3) The individual has filed federal income tax returns for
        3 of the 5 preceding years, or can demonstrate good cause
        for failure to file;
    (4) The individual is not inadmissible under § XXX.2(a)(4).

(b) REGISTRATION PERIOD.—Applications for legacy track must be
    filed within 18 months of the effective date of these
    regulations.

(c) CONTINUOUS PRESENCE.—For purposes of paragraph (a)(1),
    brief, casual, and innocent absences totaling not more than
    180 days shall not break continuous presence.

§ XXX.31 Legacy track processing.

(a) APPLICATION.—Legacy track applications shall be filed on
    Form I-XXX(L) with supporting documentation establishing
    continuous presence, tax compliance, and identity.

(b) INTERVIEW.—Applicants may be required to appear for interview.

(c) TIER PLACEMENT.—
    (1) Applicants demonstrating 10 or more years of presence
        and B1 English proficiency shall be placed in Tier C.
    (2) Applicants demonstrating 5-10 years of presence shall
        be placed in Tier B.
    (3) Geographic restrictions shall be waived for legacy track
        applicants with established residence and employment.

§ XXX.32 Evidence of continuous presence.

(a) PRIMARY EVIDENCE.—The following shall be considered primary
    evidence of continuous presence:
    (1) Tax returns or IRS transcripts;
    (2) Employment records;
    (3) School records;
    (4) Medical records;
    (5) Financial institution records;
    (6) Utility bills;
    (7) Lease agreements or mortgage records.

(b) SECONDARY EVIDENCE.—Where primary evidence is unavailable,
    the following may be considered:
    (1) Affidavits from credible witnesses;
    (2) Religious institution records;
    (3) Letters from employers or community organizations.
```

## Part 2: DOL Regulations (29 CFR)

### Employer Registration

```text
§ XXX.100 Employer registration requirement.

(a) REGISTRATION.—No employer shall employ a CLP holder unless
    the employer is registered under this section.

(b) APPLICATION.—Employer registration applications shall be
    filed electronically on Form ETA-XXX.

(c) ATTESTATIONS.—The employer shall attest that:
    (1) The employer will comply with all applicable federal,
        state, and local labor laws;
    (2) CLP workers will be paid wages not less than the
        prevailing wage for the occupation and area;
    (3) CLP workers will be provided working conditions that
        will not adversely affect similarly employed US workers;
    (4) The employer will not discriminate against CLP workers;
    (5) The employer will maintain records as required under
        § XXX.110.

(d) DETERMINATION.—The Department shall approve or deny
    registration within 10 business days.

§ XXX.101 Prevailing wage determination.

(a) REQUIREMENT.—Registered employers shall pay CLP workers
    wages that are not less than the prevailing wage for the
    occupation and area of employment.

(b) DETERMINATION.—The prevailing wage shall be determined by:
    (1) A collective bargaining agreement, if applicable;
    (2) The wage level for the occupation published by the
        Bureau of Labor Statistics for the metropolitan area; or
    (3) An employer-specific prevailing wage survey approved
        by the Department.

(c) MINIMUM.—In no case shall the prevailing wage be less than
    the federal, state, or local minimum wage, whichever is higher.

§ XXX.102 Recordkeeping.

(a) RECORDS.—Registered employers shall maintain for 3 years:
    (1) Payroll records for all CLP workers;
    (2) Time records documenting hours worked;
    (3) Evidence of wage payments;
    (4) Safety training records;
    (5) I-9 forms.

(b) INSPECTION.—Records shall be made available to the Department
    upon request.

§ XXX.103 Enforcement.

(a) INVESTIGATIONS.—The Wage and Hour Division may investigate
    registered employers for compliance with this part.

(b) VIOLATIONS.—Violations may result in:
    (1) Civil money penalties not to exceed $10,000 per violation;
    (2) Back wages owed to workers;
    (3) Suspension or revocation of registration; and
    (4) Debarment from future registration.

(c) APPEAL.—Employers may appeal adverse determinations to the
    Office of Administrative Law Judges.
```

## Part 3: HUD Regulations (24 CFR)

### RID Payments

```text
§ XXX.200 Resident Integration Dividend program.

(a) PURPOSE.—The Resident Integration Dividend program provides
    compensation to residents of communities hosting ICS
    participants.

(b) ELIGIBLE AREAS.—RID payments are available in:
    (1) Designated hub city census tracts;
    (2) Designated corridor city census tracts;
    (3) Areas designated by the Secretary based on ICS
        participant concentration.

§ XXX.201 Household eligibility.

(a) ELIGIBLE HOUSEHOLDS.—A household is eligible for RID if:
    (1) At least one member is a US citizen or lawful permanent
        resident;
    (2) The household has resided in an eligible area for not
        less than 12 months;
    (3) The household files an application under § XXX.202.

(b) INELIGIBLE HOUSEHOLDS.—CLP holder households are not eligible
    for RID.

§ XXX.202 Application and payment.

(a) APPLICATION.—Eligible households shall file Form HUD-XXX
    annually to receive RID payments.

(b) BASE PAYMENT.—The base RID payment is $300 per household
    per year.

(c) ADJUSTMENTS.—
    (1) EXPOSURE ADJUSTMENT.—An additional $100-300 may be paid
        based on the concentration of CLP holders in the census
        tract.
    (2) PARTICIPATION BONUS.—An additional $50-200 may be paid
        for documented participation in integration activities.

(d) MAXIMUM.—Total RID payments shall not exceed $1,500 per
    household per year.

(e) PAYMENT METHOD.—Payments shall be made via direct deposit,
    check, or prepaid card, as selected by the household.
```

## Part 4: ED Regulations (34 CFR)

### EL Surge Funding

```text
§ XXX.300 English Learner surge grants.

(a) PURPOSE.—English Learner surge grants support school districts
    enrolling CLP participant children.

(b) ALLOCATION.—Grants shall be allocated based on:
    (1) Number of CLP participant children enrolled;
    (2) Baseline EL population;
    (3) Rate of CLP enrollment increase.

(c) USES.—Grant funds may be used for:
    (1) Hiring EL-certified teachers;
    (2) EL instructional materials;
    (3) Professional development;
    (4) Family engagement programs;
    (5) Interpretation and translation services.

(d) SUPPLEMENT NOT SUPPLANT.—Grant funds shall supplement, not
    supplant, existing EL funding.

§ XXX.301 School capacity limits.

(a) LIMIT.—ICS placement shall not result in CLP participant
    children exceeding 5% of a school's enrollment in a single
    school year, unless:
    (1) The school district consents; and
    (2) Additional per-pupil funding is provided.

(b) MONITORING.—The Department shall monitor enrollment and
    adjust placement algorithms to maintain limits.
```

## Part 5: HHS Regulations (45 CFR)

### Healthcare Access

```text
§ XXX.400 FQHC surge support.

(a) PURPOSE.—FQHC surge grants support federally qualified health
    centers serving CLP participants.

(b) ELIGIBLE COSTS.—Grant funds may be used for:
    (1) Staffing expansion;
    (2) Facility expansion;
    (3) Interpretation services;
    (4) Cultural competency training;
    (5) Mental health services.

§ XXX.401 Health screening requirements.

(a) SCREENING.—All CLP applicants shall complete health screening
    including:
    (1) Communicable disease screening;
    (2) Vaccination status review;
    (3) Mental health screening;
    (4) Substance abuse screening.

(b) VACCINATIONS.—CLP applicants lacking required vaccinations
    shall be vaccinated or begin a vaccination series prior to
    hub city placement.

(c) TREATMENT.—CLP applicants with identified conditions shall
    be referred for treatment.
```
