# Appendix W: Bilateral Agreement Templates

---

## Overview

This appendix provides model bilateral agreement templates for coordination with source countries. These templates can be adapted for specific country relationships and cover migration management, repatriation, labor mobility, and anti-trafficking cooperation.

---

## Template 1: Migration Management Agreement

### Preamble

```text
AGREEMENT BETWEEN
THE GOVERNMENT OF THE UNITED STATES OF AMERICA
AND
THE GOVERNMENT OF [COUNTRY]
CONCERNING MIGRATION MANAGEMENT COOPERATION

The Government of the United States of America and the Government of
[Country] (hereinafter referred to as "the Parties"),

RECOGNIZING the importance of orderly, safe, and regular migration;

ACKNOWLEDGING the sovereign right of States to determine their migration
policies;

AFFIRMING their commitment to the protection of human rights of all
migrants regardless of their migration status;

DESIRING to strengthen cooperation in migration management;

HAVE AGREED as follows:
```

### Article I: Objectives

```text
ARTICLE I
OBJECTIVES

The objectives of this Agreement are to:

(a) Establish mechanisms for orderly migration flows between the Parties;

(b) Facilitate in-country processing for migrants seeking to travel to
    the United States through the Immigration Corridor System (ICS);

(c) Strengthen cooperation in preventing irregular migration;

(d) Ensure the humane treatment of migrants in all circumstances;

(e) Combat human trafficking and migrant smuggling.
```

### Article II: In-Country Processing

```text
ARTICLE II
IN-COUNTRY PROCESSING

1. The United States shall establish and operate Migration Processing
   Centers in [Country] for the purpose of processing applications for
   the Immigration Corridor System.

2. [Country] shall:
   (a) Provide suitable facilities for Processing Centers;
   (b) Ensure the security of Processing Center personnel and applicants;
   (c) Facilitate the issuance of identity documents for applicants;
   (d) Provide criminal background information upon request.

3. Processing Centers shall:
   (a) Accept applications for Corridor Lawful Presence status;
   (b) Collect biometric information;
   (c) Conduct interviews;
   (d) Issue travel authorizations to approved applicants.

4. The United States shall bear the costs of Processing Center operations.
```

### Article III: Information Sharing

```text
ARTICLE III
INFORMATION SHARING

1. The Parties shall exchange information relevant to migration
   management, including:
   (a) Criminal record information for individual applicants, upon request;
   (b) Document verification for identity documents;
   (c) Aggregate migration flow data;
   (d) Information on trafficking and smuggling networks.

2. Information exchanged under this Agreement shall be:
   (a) Used only for the purposes specified in this Agreement;
   (b) Protected in accordance with applicable privacy laws;
   (c) Subject to security measures to prevent unauthorized access.

3. Neither Party shall share information received under this Agreement
   with third parties without the consent of the originating Party.
```

### Article IV: Public Information

```text
ARTICLE IV
PUBLIC INFORMATION

1. The Parties shall cooperate in providing accurate information to
   potential migrants about:
   (a) Legal pathways for migration to the United States;
   (b) Requirements for the Immigration Corridor System;
   (c) Risks of irregular migration;
   (d) Rights and obligations of migrants.

2. Information campaigns shall be conducted through:
   (a) Government websites and social media;
   (b) Community outreach programs;
   (c) Coordination with civil society organizations;
   (d) Media engagement.
```

### Article V: Implementation

```text
ARTICLE V
IMPLEMENTATION AND REVIEW

1. The Parties shall establish a Joint Committee to oversee the
   implementation of this Agreement.

2. The Joint Committee shall:
   (a) Meet at least annually;
   (b) Review the operation of this Agreement;
   (c) Resolve any disputes arising under this Agreement;
   (d) Recommend amendments as appropriate.

3. Each Party shall designate a National Coordinator responsible for
   day-to-day implementation.

4. This Agreement shall enter into force upon signature and shall
   remain in force for five (5) years, automatically renewing for
   successive five-year periods unless either Party provides written
   notice of termination six (6) months in advance.
```

## Template 2: Repatriation Agreement

### Preamble

```text
AGREEMENT BETWEEN
THE GOVERNMENT OF THE UNITED STATES OF AMERICA
AND
THE GOVERNMENT OF [COUNTRY]
CONCERNING THE REPATRIATION OF NATIONALS

The Government of the United States of America and the Government of
[Country] (hereinafter referred to as "the Parties"),

RECOGNIZING that States have the obligation to readmit their own nationals;

AFFIRMING their commitment to the humane treatment of all persons
subject to repatriation;

DESIRING to establish efficient and dignified procedures for repatriation;

HAVE AGREED as follows:
```

### Article I: Scope

```text
ARTICLE I
SCOPE

1. This Agreement applies to nationals of [Country] who:
   (a) Are present in the United States without lawful immigration status;
   (b) Have been ordered removed from the United States; and
   (c) Are not subject to protection under international law preventing
       their return.

2. This Agreement does not apply to:
   (a) Persons who have been granted asylum or withholding of removal;
   (b) Persons protected under the Convention Against Torture;
   (c) Unaccompanied minors, except as provided in Article VI.
```

### Article II: Travel Documents

```text
ARTICLE II
TRAVEL DOCUMENTS

1. [Country] shall issue travel documents for its nationals subject to
   repatriation within:
   (a) Fifteen (15) business days for routine requests;
   (b) Five (5) business days for expedited requests;
   (c) Forty-eight (48) hours for emergency requests.

2. Requests for travel documents shall include:
   (a) Available identity documents;
   (b) Biometric information;
   (c) Statement of claimed nationality.

3. [Country] shall accept the following as proof of nationality:
   (a) Valid or expired passport;
   (b) National identity card;
   (c) Birth certificate issued by [Country];
   (d) Consular verification;
   (e) Other evidence as agreed by the Parties.

4. Where identity cannot be verified, [Country] shall issue emergency
   travel documents upon confirmation of nationality through interview
   or other means.
```

### Article III: Repatriation Procedures

```text
ARTICLE III
REPATRIATION PROCEDURES

1. The United States shall provide [Country] with advance notice of
   repatriation at least seventy-two (72) hours prior to departure.

2. Notice shall include:
   (a) Full name and date of birth;
   (b) Travel document number;
   (c) Flight information;
   (d) Any medical conditions requiring attention;
   (e) Any security concerns.

3. Repatriation shall be conducted:
   (a) In a safe and dignified manner;
   (b) Without the use of restraints except where necessary for safety;
   (c) With appropriate medical support where needed.

4. Returnees shall be permitted to bring:
   (a) Personal belongings;
   (b) Funds in their possession;
   (c) Essential medications.
```

### Article IV: Reception

```text
ARTICLE IV
RECEPTION

1. [Country] shall receive its nationals at designated ports of entry.

2. Upon arrival, [Country] shall:
   (a) Process returnees through immigration without undue delay;
   (b) Provide information on available reintegration services;
   (c) Not subject returnees to punishment solely for having been removed.

3. The United States shall provide [Country] with:
   (a) Copies of removal orders;
   (b) Medical records where relevant;
   (c) Information on property and funds to be returned.
```

### Article V: Reintegration Support

```text
ARTICLE V
REINTEGRATION SUPPORT

1. The United States shall provide reintegration assistance to returnees,
   including:
   (a) A one-time reintegration grant of [amount];
   (b) Referrals to IOM reintegration programs;
   (c) Information on available services in [Country].

2. [Country] shall facilitate access to:
   (a) Identity documents;
   (b) Employment services;
   (c) Social services;
   (d) Health services.

3. The Parties shall cooperate with IOM and other organizations providing
   reintegration assistance.
```

### Article VI: Minors and Families

```text
ARTICLE VI
MINORS AND FAMILIES

1. Families shall not be separated solely for the purpose of repatriation.

2. Unaccompanied minors shall only be returned:
   (a) To a parent or legal guardian;
   (b) To an appropriate government agency capable of providing care;
   (c) With advance notice of at least thirty (30) days.

3. [Country] shall designate an agency responsible for receiving
   unaccompanied minors and ensuring their welfare.

4. The United States shall provide [Country] with:
   (a) Case files including family tracing efforts;
   (b) Medical and psychological assessments;
   (c) Educational records.
```

## Template 3: Labor Mobility Agreement

### Preamble

```text
AGREEMENT BETWEEN
THE GOVERNMENT OF THE UNITED STATES OF AMERICA
AND
THE GOVERNMENT OF [COUNTRY]
CONCERNING LABOR MOBILITY

The Government of the United States of America and the Government of
[Country] (hereinafter referred to as "the Parties"),

RECOGNIZING the mutual benefits of orderly labor migration;

AFFIRMING their commitment to protecting the rights of migrant workers;

DESIRING to establish fair recruitment practices;

HAVE AGREED as follows:
```

### Article I: Scope

```text
ARTICLE I
SCOPE

1. This Agreement covers workers from [Country] seeking employment in
   the United States through:
   (a) The Immigration Corridor System;
   (b) Temporary worker visa programs (H-2A, H-2B);
   (c) Other lawful employment-based pathways.

2. This Agreement does not supersede the immigration laws of either Party.
```

### Article II: Recruitment Standards

```text
ARTICLE II
RECRUITMENT STANDARDS

1. Recruitment of workers shall be conducted through:
   (a) Licensed recruitment agencies registered with both Parties;
   (b) Direct employer recruitment approved by [Country]; or
   (c) Government-to-government recruitment programs.

2. Recruitment fees shall not be charged to workers.

3. Workers shall receive written contracts in a language they understand,
   including:
   (a) Wages and benefits;
   (b) Working conditions;
   (c) Duration of employment;
   (d) Housing arrangements;
   (e) Termination procedures.

4. [Country] shall maintain a registry of licensed recruitment agencies
   and share this registry with the United States.
```

### Article III: Credential Recognition

```text
ARTICLE III
CREDENTIAL RECOGNITION

1. The Parties shall cooperate to facilitate recognition of:
   (a) Educational credentials;
   (b) Professional licenses;
   (c) Occupational certifications;
   (d) Work experience.

2. [Country] shall provide, upon request:
   (a) Verification of educational institutions;
   (b) Authentication of credentials;
   (c) Information on credential equivalencies.

3. The Parties shall work toward mutual recognition agreements for
   specific professions where feasible.
```

### Article IV: Worker Protections

```text
ARTICLE IV
WORKER PROTECTIONS

1. Workers covered by this Agreement shall be entitled to:
   (a) Wages not less than the prevailing wage for the occupation;
   (b) Safe working conditions;
   (c) Freedom to change employers (for ICS participants);
   (d) Access to legal remedies for workplace violations;
   (e) Consular assistance.

2. The United States shall:
   (a) Enforce labor laws applicable to migrant workers;
   (b) Provide information to workers on their rights;
   (c) Investigate complaints of worker exploitation.

3. [Country] shall:
   (a) Provide pre-departure orientation on worker rights;
   (b) Maintain consular services for worker assistance;
   (c) Cooperate in investigations of worker exploitation.
```

### Article V: Social Security Coordination

```text
ARTICLE V
SOCIAL SECURITY COORDINATION

1. The Parties shall negotiate a Social Security Totalization Agreement
   to address:
   (a) Elimination of dual coverage;
   (b) Coordination of benefits;
   (c) Portability of credits.

2. Pending a Totalization Agreement, the Parties shall:
   (a) Provide information to workers on applicable coverage;
   (b) Facilitate benefit claims processing;
   (c) Exchange data necessary for benefit administration.
```

## Template 4: Anti-Trafficking Agreement

### Preamble

```text
MEMORANDUM OF UNDERSTANDING BETWEEN
THE GOVERNMENT OF THE UNITED STATES OF AMERICA
AND
THE GOVERNMENT OF [COUNTRY]
ON COMBATING TRAFFICKING IN PERSONS

The Government of the United States of America and the Government of
[Country] (hereinafter referred to as "the Participants"),

CONDEMNING trafficking in persons as a grave violation of human rights;

RECOGNIZING the transnational nature of trafficking in persons;

COMMITTED to the Protocol to Prevent, Suppress and Punish Trafficking
in Persons, Especially Women and Children;

HAVE REACHED the following understanding:
```

### Section I: Cooperation Areas

```text
SECTION I
AREAS OF COOPERATION

The Participants intend to cooperate in the following areas:

(a) Prevention of trafficking in persons;
(b) Protection of trafficking victims;
(c) Prosecution of traffickers;
(d) Partnership with civil society organizations.
```

### Section II: Prevention

```text
SECTION II
PREVENTION

1. The Participants intend to cooperate on prevention through:
   (a) Public awareness campaigns;
   (b) Training for officials to identify trafficking;
   (c) Research on trafficking patterns and trends;
   (d) Addressing root causes of trafficking vulnerability.

2. In the context of the Immigration Corridor System, prevention
   measures include:
   (a) Screening for trafficking indicators at intake;
   (b) Information to potential migrants on trafficking risks;
   (c) Monitoring of recruitment agencies;
   (d) Enforcement against fraudulent recruiters.
```

### Section III: Victim Protection

```text
SECTION III
VICTIM PROTECTION

1. The Participants intend to provide trafficking victims with:
   (a) Shelter and safety;
   (b) Medical and psychological care;
   (c) Legal assistance;
   (d) Immigration relief where eligible (T-visa in the United States).

2. Victims shall not be penalized for unlawful acts committed as a
   result of being trafficked.

3. Repatriation of victims shall be:
   (a) Voluntary to the extent possible;
   (b) Conducted with due regard for safety;
   (c) Accompanied by reintegration support.

4. The Participants intend to cooperate in:
   (a) Safe referral of victims between countries;
   (b) Family tracing for child victims;
   (c) Long-term monitoring of reintegrated victims.
```

### Section IV: Prosecution

```text
SECTION IV
PROSECUTION

1. The Participants intend to cooperate in prosecution through:
   (a) Information sharing on trafficking networks;
   (b) Joint investigations where appropriate;
   (c) Mutual legal assistance;
   (d) Extradition where applicable.

2. Cooperation mechanisms include:
   (a) Designated points of contact in law enforcement agencies;
   (b) Regular information exchanges;
   (c) Training exchanges for investigators and prosecutors.

3. Asset forfeiture cooperation shall be pursued to deprive traffickers
   of proceeds.
```

## Template 5: Development Partnership Agreement

### Framework

```text
DEVELOPMENT PARTNERSHIP FRAMEWORK
BETWEEN
THE UNITED STATES AGENCY FOR INTERNATIONAL DEVELOPMENT
AND
[COUNTRY DEVELOPMENT AGENCY]
TO ADDRESS ROOT CAUSES OF MIGRATION

PURPOSE

This Framework establishes a partnership to invest in economic opportunity,
security, and governance in [Country] as part of a comprehensive approach
to migration management.

OBJECTIVES

(a) Create economic opportunities that reduce migration pressure;
(b) Strengthen rule of law and reduce corruption;
(c) Improve citizen security;
(d) Build resilient communities;
(e) Support returning migrants' reintegration.
```

### Investment Areas

```text
INVESTMENT AREAS

1. ECONOMIC OPPORTUNITY
   (a) Job creation programs targeting migration-prone areas;
   (b) Vocational training aligned with labor market needs;
   (c) Support for small and medium enterprises;
   (d) Agricultural development and climate adaptation;
   (e) Investment attraction and private sector engagement.

2. GOVERNANCE AND RULE OF LAW
   (a) Anti-corruption initiatives;
   (b) Judicial system strengthening;
   (c) Civil registry and identity document systems;
   (d) Transparent government service delivery.

3. SECURITY
   (a) Community policing programs;
   (b) Gang intervention and prevention;
   (c) Border security cooperation;
   (d) Counter-narcotics coordination.

4. REINTEGRATION
   (a) Reception services for returnees;
   (b) Job placement assistance;
   (c) Psychosocial support;
   (d) Community reconciliation.
```

### Accountability

```text
ACCOUNTABILITY FRAMEWORK

1. PERFORMANCE INDICATORS
   (a) Jobs created in target areas;
   (b) Youth enrolled in training programs;
   (c) Businesses supported;
   (d) Reduction in irregular migration from target areas.

2. MONITORING
   (a) Quarterly progress reviews;
   (b) Annual impact assessments;
   (c) Independent evaluations every three years.

3. CONDITIONS
   (a) Continued partnership contingent on demonstrated progress;
   (b) Funding adjustments based on performance;
   (c) Anti-corruption compliance required.
```

## Implementation Notes

### Negotiation Considerations

| Factor | Consideration |
|--------|---------------|
| **Country capacity** | Adjust timelines and requirements |
| **Political relationship** | Leverage existing cooperation |
| **Migration patterns** | Focus on highest-priority flows |
| **Human rights** | Ensure protection standards |
| **Reciprocity** | Balance obligations |

### Adaptation Guidance

| Element | Adaptation |
|---------|------------|
| **Timelines** | Adjust based on country capacity |
| **Document types** | Adapt to country's civil registry |
| **Payment amounts** | Adjust to local economy |
| **Institutional names** | Use correct country agencies |
| **Languages** | Include official languages |
