# Appendix K: Model Interstate Compact

---

## Overview

This appendix provides draft language for the Interstate Corridor Compact, a multi-state agreement for coordinating CLP placement, service delivery, and benefit portability.

---

## Model Interstate Compact

### ARTICLE I - PURPOSE AND FINDINGS

**Section 1.01. Purpose**

The purpose of this Compact is to establish a cooperative framework among the party states for:

(a) Coordinating the placement of Corridor Lawful Presence (CLP) participants in designated hub and corridor cities;

(b) Ensuring portability of benefits and services for CLP participants who move between party states;

(c) Sharing data necessary for effective program administration while protecting participant privacy;

(d) Establishing uniform standards for service delivery across state lines; and

(e) Creating mechanisms for dispute resolution and compact governance.

**Section 1.02. Findings**

The party states find that:

(a) Immigration integration is a national function requiring coordinated state action;

(b) Geographic distribution of CLP participants benefits party states by preventing overconcentration;

(c) Benefit portability promotes participant stability and reduces administrative burden;

(d) Uniform standards improve service quality and program integrity; and

(e) State cooperation advances the interests of all party states and their residents.

---

### ARTICLE II - DEFINITIONS

**Section 2.01. Definitions**

As used in this Compact:

(a) "Compact" means this Interstate Corridor Compact.

(b) "Commission" means the Interstate Corridor Commission established under Article V.

(c) "Corridor city" means a city designated under federal law for secondary placement of CLP participants.

(d) "CLP" means Corridor Lawful Presence status as established under federal law.

(e) "Hub city" means a city designated under federal law for initial placement of CLP participants.

(f) "Participant" means an individual holding CLP status.

(g) "Party state" means a state that has enacted this Compact into law.

(h) "Sending state" means the party state from which a participant is transferring.

(i) "Receiving state" means the party state to which a participant is transferring.

---

### ARTICLE III - ELIGIBILITY AND ENTRY

**Section 3.01. State Eligibility**

Any state may become a party to this Compact by enacting this Compact into law.

**Section 3.02. Effective Date**

This Compact shall become effective and binding upon legislative enactment by any three states. Thereafter, this Compact shall become effective and binding as to any other state upon enactment.

**Section 3.03. Federal Consent**

This Compact is subject to Congressional consent pursuant to Article I, Section 10, Clause 3 of the United States Constitution.

---

### ARTICLE IV - PLACEMENT COORDINATION

**Section 4.01. Placement Authority**

(a) The federal government shall retain primary authority for CLP placement decisions.

(b) Party states agree to accept CLP participants placed in hub or corridor cities within their borders, subject to capacity limits established under federal law.

**Section 4.02. State Notification**

(a) The federal government shall provide party states with reasonable advance notice of intended placements.

(b) Notice shall include: number of participants, demographic characteristics, placement timeline, and anticipated service needs.

**Section 4.03. Capacity Communication**

(a) Party states shall provide the Commission with regular updates on capacity in hub and corridor cities.

(b) Updates shall include: housing availability, school capacity, healthcare capacity, and labor market conditions.

**Section 4.04. Opt-Out**

(a) A party state may request reduced placements based on demonstrated capacity constraints.

(b) Requests shall be submitted to the Commission with supporting data.

(c) The Commission shall review requests and make recommendations to federal agencies.

---

### ARTICLE V - INTERSTATE CORRIDOR COMMISSION

**Section 5.01. Establishment**

There is hereby established an agency known as the Interstate Corridor Commission.

**Section 5.02. Membership**

(a) The Commission shall consist of one commissioner from each party state.

(b) Each commissioner shall be appointed by the governor of their state.

(c) Commissioners shall serve terms of four years and may be reappointed.

**Section 5.03. Powers and Duties**

The Commission shall:

(a) Oversee the administration of this Compact;

(b) Establish bylaws and rules for its operation;

(c) Collect and share data among party states;

(d) Develop uniform standards for service delivery;

(e) Resolve disputes among party states;

(f) Report annually to party states and Congress;

(g) Recommend amendments to this Compact; and

(h) Perform other functions necessary to effectuate this Compact.

**Section 5.04. Voting**

(a) Each party state shall have one vote.

(b) Actions of the Commission shall require a majority vote of the commissioners present and voting, except as otherwise provided.

(c) A quorum shall consist of a majority of party states.

**Section 5.05. Meetings**

(a) The Commission shall meet at least twice annually.

(b) Special meetings may be called by the chair or by petition of one-third of party states.

**Section 5.06. Finance**

(a) The Commission shall establish an annual budget.

(b) Party states shall share costs based on a formula considering population and number of CLP participants.

(c) The Commission may accept federal funding and grants.

---

### ARTICLE VI - DATA SHARING

**Section 6.01. Data Exchange**

(a) Party states shall share data necessary for effective compact administration.

(b) Data sharing shall comply with federal and state privacy laws.

**Section 6.02. Shared Data Elements**

Party states shall share:

(a) Participant status and tier information;

(b) Benefit receipt and service utilization;

(c) Benchmark attainment and progress;

(d) Compliance and enforcement actions; and

(e) Capacity and resource data.

**Section 6.03. Privacy Protections**

(a) Shared data shall be used only for compact purposes.

(b) Party states shall maintain confidentiality of participant information.

(c) Data breaches shall be reported to the Commission within 72 hours.

**Section 6.04. Data Standards**

The Commission shall establish uniform data standards for interoperability among party states.

---

### ARTICLE VII - BENEFIT PORTABILITY

**Section 7.01. General Principle**

A CLP participant who moves from one party state to another shall retain eligibility for state-administered benefits and services, subject to the provisions of this Article.

**Section 7.02. Portable Benefits**

The following benefits shall be portable among party states:

(a) State-funded healthcare services;

(b) State educational services and programs;

(c) State professional and occupational licenses (subject to Section 7.04);

(d) State driver's licenses (subject to Section 7.05); and

(e) Other benefits as determined by the Commission.

**Section 7.03. Benefit Transfer Process**

(a) The sending state shall provide the receiving state with benefit records within 30 days of transfer notification.

(b) The receiving state shall honor existing benefit determinations for 90 days.

(c) The receiving state may conduct its own eligibility determination after 90 days.

**Section 7.04. Professional Licenses**

(a) Party states shall establish expedited license recognition for CLP participants.

(b) A CLP participant licensed in one party state shall be eligible for temporary practice authorization in another party state while seeking full licensure.

(c) Temporary authorization shall be valid for 180 days.

**Section 7.05. Driver's Licenses**

(a) A driver's license issued to a CLP participant by one party state shall be valid in all party states.

(b) A CLP participant who establishes residence in a new party state shall apply for that state's license within 60 days.

(c) The receiving state shall accept the driving record from the sending state.

---

### ARTICLE VIII - SERVICE STANDARDS

**Section 8.01. Uniform Standards**

The Commission shall develop uniform minimum standards for:

(a) English language instruction programs;

(b) Civic orientation curricula;

(c) Healthcare access and quality;

(d) Housing quality and safety; and

(e) Employment services and support.

**Section 8.02. Compliance**

(a) Party states shall implement Commission-adopted standards.

(b) The Commission shall monitor compliance through periodic reviews.

(c) Non-compliance shall be addressed through the dispute resolution process.

**Section 8.03. Best Practices**

The Commission shall identify and disseminate best practices among party states.

---

### ARTICLE IX - DISPUTE RESOLUTION

**Section 9.01. Informal Resolution**

Party states shall attempt to resolve disputes through informal negotiation before invoking formal procedures.

**Section 9.02. Mediation**

(a) If informal resolution fails, either party may request mediation.

(b) The Commission shall maintain a panel of qualified mediators.

(c) Mediation costs shall be shared equally by disputing parties.

**Section 9.03. Binding Arbitration**

(a) If mediation fails, either party may request binding arbitration.

(b) The Commission shall establish arbitration procedures.

(c) Arbitration decisions shall be final and binding on the parties.

**Section 9.04. Federal Court**

(a) Disputes involving interpretation of this Compact may be brought in federal court.

(b) The Commission may intervene in such proceedings.

---

### ARTICLE X - AMENDMENT AND WITHDRAWAL

**Section 10.01. Amendment**

(a) Amendments may be proposed by the Commission or by any party state.

(b) Amendments shall require approval by two-thirds of party states.

(c) Amendments shall become effective upon enactment by two-thirds of party states.

**Section 10.02. Withdrawal**

(a) A party state may withdraw from this Compact by repealing the same.

(b) Withdrawal shall be effective one year after notice to the Commission.

(c) Withdrawal shall not affect obligations incurred prior to the effective date of withdrawal.

**Section 10.03. Transition Upon Withdrawal**

(a) A withdrawing state shall cooperate in orderly transition of participants and programs.

(b) Benefits and services for participants shall continue during the transition period.

(c) The withdrawing state shall remain liable for its share of Commission expenses through the effective date of withdrawal.

---

### ARTICLE XI - CONSTRUCTION AND SEVERABILITY

**Section 11.01. Liberal Construction**

This Compact shall be liberally construed to effectuate its purposes.

**Section 11.02. Severability**

If any provision of this Compact is held invalid, the invalidity shall not affect other provisions that can be given effect without the invalid provision.

**Section 11.03. Relationship to Federal Law**

This Compact shall be construed consistent with federal immigration law. In the event of conflict, federal law shall prevail.

**Section 11.04. Relationship to State Law**

(a) Nothing in this Compact shall be construed to supersede state laws except as specifically provided.

(b) Party states retain authority over matters not addressed in this Compact.

---

## Implementation Notes

### Required State Legislation

Each party state must enact:
1. The Compact language (verbatim)
2. Enabling provisions for state agencies
3. Appropriations for Commission contributions
4. Conforming amendments to existing statutes

### Congressional Consent

The Compact requires Congressional consent via joint resolution because it:
- Affects federal immigration authority
- Creates interstate governance structures
- May affect political balance among states

### Model State Bill

States should enact the Compact with accompanying provisions for:
- Designation of commissioner
- Agency responsibilities
- Funding mechanism
- Effective date coordination
