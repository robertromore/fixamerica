## Title I: Corridor Lawful Presence

### Section 101. Establishment of CLP Status

```text
SEC. 101. CORRIDOR LAWFUL PRESENCE STATUS.

(a) IN GENERAL.—There is established a new category of immigration
status, to be known as "Corridor Lawful Presence" (CLP), under which
an alien may remain lawfully in the United States and be authorized
for employment, subject to the conditions specified in this title.

(b) DURATION.—CLP status shall be valid for a period not to exceed
[X] years, subject to renewal and tier advancement as provided in
section 103.

(c) WORK AUTHORIZATION.—An alien in CLP status shall be authorized
for employment in the United States without regard to any numerical
limitation.

(d) GEOGRAPHIC RESTRICTIONS.—An alien in CLP status shall be subject
to geographic residency requirements as specified in Title II.
```

### Section 102. Eligibility

```text
SEC. 102. ELIGIBILITY FOR CLP STATUS.

(a) ELIGIBLE ALIENS.—An alien is eligible for CLP status if the alien—
    (1) is physically present in the United States;
    (2) has not been convicted of—
        (A) a felony; or
        (B) three or more misdemeanors;
    (3) is not inadmissible under section 212(a)(3) of the Immigration
        and Nationality Act (security and related grounds);
    (4) registers with the Secretary in accordance with procedures
        established under this Act; and
    (5) agrees to comply with the geographic and programmatic
        requirements of this Act.

(b) EXCLUSIONS.—An alien is not eligible for CLP status if the alien—
    (1) has a final order of removal that was issued following a
        full hearing on the merits; or
    (2) is subject to extradition to another country for a serious
        criminal offense.
```

### Section 103. Tier Structure

```text
SEC. 103. STATUS TIERS AND ADVANCEMENT.

(a) TIER STRUCTURE.—CLP status shall consist of the following tiers:
    (1) TIER A.—Initial placement status for not less than 6 months
        and not more than 12 months.
    (2) TIER B.—Intermediate status following successful completion
        of Tier A requirements, for a period of 12 to 24 months.
    (3) TIER C.—Advanced status with full geographic mobility,
        following successful completion of Tier B requirements.

(b) ADVANCEMENT CRITERIA.—The Secretary shall establish by regulation
criteria for advancement between tiers, which shall include—
    (1) continuous residence within designated corridor areas;
    (2) employment or enrollment in approved training programs;
    (3) demonstrated progress in English language proficiency;
    (4) completion of civic orientation requirements; and
    (5) absence of disqualifying criminal conduct.

(c) AUTOMATIC ADVANCEMENT.—An alien who meets all advancement
criteria shall be advanced to the next tier without discretionary
denial, except for documented failure to meet specified benchmarks.
```

## Title II: Integration Infrastructure

### Section 201. Hub and Corridor Designation

```text
SEC. 201. DESIGNATION OF HUB AND CORRIDOR CITIES.

(a) HUB CITIES.—The Secretary, in consultation with the Secretary
of Housing and Urban Development and affected state and local
governments, shall designate not fewer than 5 and not more than 20
cities as "hub cities" for initial placement of aliens in CLP status.

(b) CORRIDOR CITIES.—The Secretary shall designate not fewer than
20 and not more than 80 cities as "corridor cities" for secondary
placement following initial processing.

(c) DESIGNATION CRITERIA.—In designating hub and corridor cities,
the Secretary shall consider—
    (1) housing availability and affordability;
    (2) labor market conditions and employment opportunities;
    (3) educational capacity and English learner services;
    (4) healthcare infrastructure;
    (5) community willingness and local government consent; and
    (6) transportation connectivity.

(d) OPT-IN REQUIREMENT.—No city may be designated as a hub or
corridor city without the consent of the relevant state and local
governments.
```

### Section 202. Placement Algorithm

```text
SEC. 202. PLACEMENT ALGORITHM AND STANDARDS.

(a) ALGORITHM DEVELOPMENT.—The Secretary shall develop and maintain
a placement algorithm to match aliens in CLP status with appropriate
hub and corridor cities based on—
    (1) family connections and sponsor availability;
    (2) language and cultural factors;
    (3) employment skills and labor market needs;
    (4) local capacity constraints; and
    (5) participant preferences, to the extent practicable.

(b) ALGORITHMIC FAIRNESS.—The algorithm shall be designed to—
    (1) avoid disparate impact based on national origin, race,
        religion, or other protected characteristics;
    (2) be subject to regular audit by the Independent Integration
        Oversight Office; and
    (3) be published in sufficient detail to permit public review.

(c) CAPACITY LIMITS.—No hub or corridor city shall receive placements
exceeding [X] percent of its baseline population in any fiscal year,
unless the city affirmatively opts to accept additional placements.
```

## Title III: Community Compensation

### Section 301. Resident Integration Dividend

```text
SEC. 301. RESIDENT INTEGRATION DIVIDEND PROGRAM.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—There is established a program to be known as
the "Resident Integration Dividend" (RID) under which the Secretary
of the Treasury shall make payments to eligible residents of hub
and corridor cities.

(b) ELIGIBLE RESIDENTS.—A resident is eligible for RID payments if
the resident—
    (1) is a United States citizen or lawful permanent resident;
    (2) has resided in a designated hub or corridor zone for not
        less than 12 months; and
    (3) is not employed in a government position with direct
        authority over CLP placement or benefits.

(c) PAYMENT STRUCTURE.—
    (1) BASE PAYMENT.—Each eligible household shall receive a base
        payment of not less than $200 and not more than $400 per year.
    (2) EXPOSURE ADJUSTMENT.—An additional payment of not less than
        $100 and not more than $300 shall be made to households in
        zones with capacity stress exceeding thresholds established
        by regulation.
    (3) PARTICIPATION BONUS.—An additional payment of not less than
        $50 and not more than $200 shall be made for qualifying
        activities including tutoring, mentoring, and compliant
        renting.
    (4) MAXIMUM.—Total annual payments to any household shall not
        exceed $1,500.
```

### Section 302. Stabilization Grants

```text
SEC. 302. LOCAL STABILIZATION GRANTS.

(a) AUTHORIZATION.—The Secretary of Housing and Urban Development
is authorized to make grants to state and local governments in
designated hub and corridor areas for—
    (1) housing supply expansion;
    (2) school capacity improvements;
    (3) healthcare infrastructure;
    (4) transportation services; and
    (5) public safety resources.

(b) FORMULA.—Grants under this section shall be allocated based on—
    (1) the number of CLP status holders placed in the jurisdiction;
    (2) baseline service capacity; and
    (3) demonstrated need.

(c) HOLD HARMLESS.—If state or local costs attributable to CLP
implementation exceed 120 percent of baseline costs, the federal
government shall provide supplemental funding to cover the excess.
```

## Title IV: Education and Workforce

### Section 401. English Learner Surge Fund

```text
SEC. 401. ENGLISH LEARNER SURGE FUND.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—There is established in the Treasury a fund to
be known as the "English Learner Surge Fund" to provide additional
resources to school districts in hub and corridor cities.

(b) AUTHORIZED USES.—Funds may be used for—
    (1) hiring additional English learner teachers and aides;
    (2) professional development for existing teachers;
    (3) instructional materials and technology;
    (4) extended learning time programs; and
    (5) family engagement and parent education.

(c) ALLOCATION.—Funds shall be allocated based on the number of
newly enrolled English learners attributable to CLP placements.

(d) AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS.—There is authorized to be
appropriated not less than $1,000,000,000 and not more than
$3,500,000,000 for each fiscal year to carry out this section.
```

### Section 402. Workforce Development

```text
SEC. 402. WORKFORCE DEVELOPMENT AND CREDENTIAL RECOGNITION.

(a) APPRENTICESHIP PATHWAYS.—The Secretary of Labor shall establish
expedited pathways for aliens in CLP status to enroll in registered
apprenticeship programs.

(b) CREDENTIAL RECOGNITION.—The Secretary of Labor, in coordination
with state licensing authorities, shall establish a program for
evaluation and recognition of foreign credentials.

(c) EMPLOYER TAX CREDITS.—
    (1) APPRENTICESHIP CREDIT.—An employer who enrolls a CLP status
        holder in a registered apprenticeship program shall be
        entitled to a tax credit of $2,500 to $5,000 per participant
        per year.
    (2) LANGUAGE TRAINING CREDIT.—An employer who provides or
        reimburses English language training shall be entitled to a
        tax credit of up to $1,500 per employee per year.
```

## Title V: Oversight and Civil Liberties

### Section 501. Independent Integration Oversight Office

```text
SEC. 501. INDEPENDENT INTEGRATION OVERSIGHT OFFICE.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—There is established an independent agency to be
known as the "Independent Integration Oversight Office" (IIOO).

(b) DIRECTOR.—
    (1) APPOINTMENT.—The IIOO shall be headed by a Director
        appointed by the President, by and with the advice and
        consent of the Senate, for a term of 5 years.
    (2) REMOVAL.—The Director may be removed only for cause.

(c) FUNCTIONS.—The IIOO shall—
    (1) audit enforcement actions and placement decisions;
    (2) hear appeals from aliens denied tier advancement or
        facing status revocation;
    (3) publish annual reports on program performance;
    (4) conduct red-team assessments of system vulnerabilities; and
    (5) investigate complaints of civil liberties violations.

(d) INDEPENDENCE.—The IIOO shall not be subject to the direction
or control of the Department of Homeland Security or any other
executive department.
```

### Section 502. Statutory Prohibitions

```text
SEC. 502. CIVIL LIBERTIES PROTECTIONS.

(a) PROHIBITED ACTIONS.—No agency implementing this Act shall—
    (1) establish demographic quotas based on national origin,
        race, ethnicity, or religion;
    (2) condition CLP status on political beliefs, religious
        practice, or associational activity protected by the
        First Amendment;
    (3) revoke CLP status en masse without individualized
        determinations and due process; or
    (4) create a permanent underclass by eliminating pathways
        to permanent residence and citizenship.

(b) DUE PROCESS.—Any alien facing denial of tier advancement,
status revocation, or removal shall be entitled to—
    (1) written notice of the grounds for the action;
    (2) an opportunity to present evidence and argument;
    (3) a decision by an impartial adjudicator; and
    (4) judicial review in federal court.
```

## Title VI: Interstate Compact

### Section 601. Compact Authorization

```text
SEC. 601. CONSENT TO INTERSTATE COMPACT.

(a) CONGRESSIONAL CONSENT.—The consent of Congress is hereby given
to any two or more states to enter into an agreement or compact for
the coordination of CLP placement, service delivery, and benefit
portability.

(b) REQUIRED PROVISIONS.—Any compact entered into under this section
shall include provisions for—
    (1) reciprocal recognition of CLP status and tier advancement;
    (2) data sharing for placement and service coordination;
    (3) dispute resolution mechanisms; and
    (4) withdrawal procedures that protect enrolled participants.

(c) FEDERAL PARTICIPATION.—The Secretary may participate in compact
governance as a non-voting observer.
```

## Title VII: Legacy Track

### Section 701. Adjustment for Long-Term Residents

```text
SEC. 701. LEGACY TRACK ADJUSTMENT.

(a) ELIGIBILITY.—An alien who has been continuously present in the
United States for not less than [X] years as of the date of
enactment of this Act may apply for CLP status under this section.

(b) REQUIREMENTS.—An alien applying under this section shall—
    (1) register with the Secretary within [X] months of the date
        of enactment;
    (2) pass security and criminal background checks;
    (3) pay any applicable fees or taxes; and
    (4) demonstrate community ties, including employment history,
        family connections, or property ownership.

(c) WAIVER.—The geographic residency requirements of Title II may
be waived for aliens qualifying under this section who have
established stable residence elsewhere.

(d) LIMITATION.—Not more than [X] aliens may adjust status under
this section in any fiscal year.
```
