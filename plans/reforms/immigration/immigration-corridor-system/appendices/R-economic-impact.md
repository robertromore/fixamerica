# Appendix R: Economic Impact Analysis

---

## Overview

This appendix analyzes the economic effects of ICS on labor markets, regional economies, housing markets, and public finances. The analysis considers both direct effects and broader macroeconomic impacts.

---

## Labor Market Effects

### Labor Supply Expansion

| Metric | Current Baseline | With ICS |
|--------|------------------|----------|
| **Annual labor force growth** | 0.5% | 0.8-1.0% |
| **Working-age population growth** | 0.3% | 0.6-0.8% |
| **Labor force participation (immigrant)** | 65% | 70-75% |

### Sector-Specific Impacts

| Sector | Current Vacancy Rate | Projected Impact |
|--------|---------------------|------------------|
| **Construction** | 5.4% | Fill 30-40% of vacancies |
| **Healthcare support** | 6.2% | Fill 25-35% of vacancies |
| **Agriculture** | 8.1% | Fill 40-50% of vacancies |
| **Food service** | 7.3% | Fill 20-30% of vacancies |
| **Manufacturing** | 4.8% | Fill 15-25% of vacancies |
| **Transportation** | 6.5% | Fill 20-30% of vacancies |

### Geographic Distribution

| Region | Current Labor Shortage | ICS Impact |
|--------|----------------------|------------|
| **Declining metros** | Severe | High positive impact |
| **Rural areas** | Moderate-severe | Moderate positive impact |
| **Growing metros** | Low-moderate | Minimal direct impact |
| **Coastal metros** | Low (competition) | Reduced pressure |

### Wage Effects

| Effect | Estimate | Mechanism |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| **Native low-skill workers** | -0.5% to +0.5% | Mixed; depends on complementarity |
| **Native high-skill workers** | +0.3% to +1.0% | Complementary labor |
| **Immigrant workers** | +5% to +15% | Legalization premium |
| **Overall wages** | +0.2% to +0.5% | Productivity gains |

## Housing Market Analysis

### Housing Demand

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **New household formation** | 150,000-300,000/year |
| **Geographic distribution** | Concentrated in hub/corridor cities |
| **Housing type** | 60% rental; 40% eventual homeownership |
| **Price point** | Primarily affordable/workforce housing |

### Supply Response

| Mechanism | Capacity |
|-----------|----------|
| **Vacant housing absorption** | 100,000-200,000 units/year |
| **New construction** | 50,000-100,000 units/year |
| **Landlord incentives** | Bring 25,000-50,000 units to market |

### Price Effects

| Market Type | Expected Impact |
|-------------|-----------------|
| **Declining markets (high vacancy)** | Stabilization; modest increase |
| **Stable markets** | Moderate increase (2-5%) |
| **High-demand markets** | Not primary targets |

### Displacement Risk

| Risk Level | Mitigation |
|------------|------------|
| **Low-income renters** | RID compensation; rent stabilization |
| **Existing residents** | Capacity limits; fair share distribution |
| **Gentrification** | Affordable housing requirements |
