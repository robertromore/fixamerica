# Implementation Blueprint for the Immigration Corridor System (ICS)

Purpose: operationalize the ICS plan with clear workflows, staffing, and service standards.

## 1. Operating Principles

- Preserve civil liberties and due process at every phase.
- Use time-bound processes and transparent standards.
- Prefer incentives and service access over enforcement pressure.
- Default to uniformity; allow local variation only with documented justification.

## 2. Governance and Accountability

- Lead agency: DHS (intake, biometrics, status issuance).
- Joint operations council: DHS, HHS, DOL, HUD, ED, DOJ, and state liaisons.
- Oversight office: independent audits, appeals review, and red-team reports.
- State and local compacts: define roles, funding, and data-sharing rules.

## 3. Intake Workflow (Phase 0)

1) Registration

- Location: hubs, border processing points, or remote registration sites.
- Collect core identity data and initial eligibility attestations.

1) Provisional lawful presence

- Issue provisional status upon intake initiation.
- Provide temporary ID credential and case number.

1) Screening

- Identity verification, security screening, and health screening.
- Issue provisional work authorization within 7 days.

1) Decision

- Pass: grant CLP and full work authorization.
- Conditional pass: medical follow-up; provisional status continues.
- Fail: written notice, 30-day appeal, and referral to existing immigration processes.

Service SLAs

- Intake initiation to decision: target <= 90 days.
- Credential issuance: <= 72 hours after intake initiation.

## 4. Placement and Housing Workflow (Phase 0 to Phase 2)

1) Capacity scoring

- Use housing vacancy, school/clinic utilization, and transit load.
- Set cap bands by published criteria.

1) Placement matching

- Apply placement algorithm with HUD/DOJ fair-housing impact metrics.
- Provide placement rationale and appeal rights.

1) Interim housing

- If placement delayed: use interim housing within 30-day stay cap.

Service SLAs

- Placement decision: <= 14 days from intake completion.
- Appeal response: <= 30 days.

## 5. Integration and Case Management (Phase 1 to Phase 2)

- Assign a case manager within 14 days of housing placement.
- Build individualized integration plans at 6 and 12 months.
- Provide ESL, credential pipelines, and employment support.
- Offer trauma-informed services and opt-in mental health screening.

## 6. Tier C Certification Workflow

- Verify benchmarks: language, economic participation, law compliance, civics.
- Use standardized documentation checklists.
- Issue portable digital credential within 30 days of eligibility.
- Provide automatic review on denial.

## 7. Appeals and Legal Aid

- Independent appeals unit with published timelines.
- Provide legal aid access for vulnerable applicants.
- Publish aggregate outcomes for transparency.

Service SLAs

- Appeal acknowledgment: <= 5 business days.
- Appeal decision: <= 30 days.

## 8. Interagency Coordination

- Weekly operational cadence during pilot; monthly thereafter.
- Shared incident management system for escalations.
- Unified guidance bulletins for state and local agencies.

## 9. Staffing and Capacity Model

- Intake: 1 case manager per 80 to 120 arrivals per month.
- Housing placement: 1 coordinator per 250 to 400 placements per month.
- Education integration: 1 EL coordinator per 1,000 students.
- Oversight: 1 auditor per 200,000 residents annually.

## 10. Vendor and Procurement Management

- Competitive bidding with audit-ready requirements.
- Conflict-of-interest disclosures and anti-kickback clauses.
- Quarterly vendor performance reviews and compliance audits.

## 11. Data Systems and Security

- Role-based access controls and audit logs.
- Encryption in transit and at rest.
- Participant access and correction rights.
- Incident response within 72 hours of breach.

## 12. Surge Operations

- Triggers tied to capacity thresholds.
- Temporary reception centers limited to 30-day stays.
- Automatic closure within 120 days unless Congress reauthorizes.
- Inspector general review at day 21.

## 13. Communications and Public Legitimacy

- Publish dashboards monthly during pilot; quarterly thereafter.
- Provide clear public definitions for status tiers and benefits.
- Local spokespersons lead crisis response.

## 14. Timeline and Rollout

- Year 0 to 1: pilot hubs, data systems build, staffing ramp.
- Year 1 to 3: scale to additional hubs and corridor cities.
- Year 3 to 5: national coverage and compact formalization.

## 15. Key Performance Indicators

- Intake completion time and backlog.
- Placement stability and housing retention.
- Employment and training participation.
- Tier C progression rates.
- Community impact metrics and public safety indicators.
