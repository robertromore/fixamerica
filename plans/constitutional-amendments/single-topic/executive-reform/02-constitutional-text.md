# Executive Reform Constitutional Provisions

## Draft Amendment Framework

*Note: This document outlines constitutional provisions for executive reform. Detailed amendment text to be developed.*

---

### Section 1. Executive Order Limits

(a) Executive orders shall be limited to implementation of existing statutes and shall not create new policy obligations.

(b) Executive orders with the effect of law shall expire after one year unless ratified by Congress.

(c) Congress may by resolution disapprove any executive order, and such disapproval is not subject to veto.

---

### Section 2. Signing Statements

(a) The President shall execute laws as enacted by Congress.

(b) A signing statement that purports to authorize non-enforcement of any provision is void.

(c) If the President believes a law is unconstitutional, the remedy is veto or judicial challenge, not selective non-enforcement.

---

### Section 3. Pardon Reform

(a) The President may not pardon:

- any person for conduct arising from investigation of the President,
- any person who has provided testimony or evidence against the President,
- any family member for conduct during the President's term,
- the President's own conduct.

(b) Pardons issued in the final sixty days of a presidential term shall be reported to Congress with justification within thirty days.

---

### Section 4. Removal Protections

(a) Heads of independent regulatory agencies may be removed only for cause.

(b) Inspectors general may be removed only for cause, with thirty days notice to Congress.

(c) "Cause" means malfeasance, neglect of duty, or incapacity.

(d) Policy disagreement is not cause for removal.

---

### Section 5. Accountability Requirements

(a) The President shall respond to congressional subpoenas within the time specified.

(b) Executive privilege does not apply to evidence of criminal conduct.

(c) Presidential records are public property and shall be preserved and disclosed as provided by law.

---

## Implementation Considerations

- Executive order limits enforceable by courts
- Pardon limits self-executing
- Removal protections create litigation rights
- Accountability requirements enforceable through contempt
