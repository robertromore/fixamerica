# Election Reform Implementation

## Overview

This document outlines implementation steps for the Election Reform Constitutional Amendment.

---

## Immediate Effects Upon Ratification

### Electoral College Abolition

- All presidential electors are abolished
- State electoral vote allocations are void
- National popular vote applies to next presidential election

### Certification Timelines

- Fourteen-day state certification deadline applies
- Twenty-one-day national certification deadline applies
- Automatic certification provisions take effect

---

## Enabling Legislation

### National Election Standards Act

**Purpose**: Establish uniform federal election standards.

**Key Provisions**:

- National vote counting standards
- Uniform ballot design requirements
- Early voting minimums (at least 14 days)
- Absentee voting standards
- Same-day registration procedures
- Recount procedures and standards

### Election Dispute Resolution Act

**Purpose**: Implement expedited court procedures.

**Key Provisions**:

- Expedited filing procedures
- Seven-day decision requirements
- Automatic interim certification if deadline missed
- Fourteen-day filing deadline enforcement
- Supreme Court original jurisdiction for certain disputes

### Presidential Transition Act Amendments

**Purpose**: Implement Section 7 transition requirements.

**Key Provisions**:

- Define transition resources required
- Establish security briefing procedures
- Create enforcement mechanisms
- Define violation as impeachable offense

---

## Vote Counting Infrastructure

### National Vote Aggregation

**New Requirements**:

- Real-time reporting to national authority
- Standardized data formats
- Redundant reporting systems
- Transparent aggregation process

**Proposed System**:

1. Counties report to state election authorities
2. States report to Federal Election Commission
3. FEC aggregates and publishes national totals
4. Multiple independent verification sources

### Majority Determination

If no candidate achieves majority:

**Option A: Ranked-Choice Voting**

- Ballots include ranked preferences
- Instant runoff eliminates lowest candidates
- Process continues until majority achieved

**Option B: Runoff Election**

- Top two candidates advance
- Runoff held within 21 days
- Winner determined by majority

Congress must establish mechanism by statute before next presidential election.

## Certification Procedures

### State Certification (Day 1-14)

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| 1-7 | Vote counting and preliminary canvass |
| 8-10 | County certification |
| 11-14 | State certification |

### Automatic Certification (Day 14)

If state fails to certify:

1. State election authority's tabulation becomes official
2. No action required by any official
3. Results reported to national aggregation
4. Late certification cannot change reported results

### National Certification (Day 21)

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| 15-18 | State results aggregated nationally |
| 19-20 | Final verification |
| 21 | National certification |

---

## Dispute Resolution Implementation

### Filing Requirements

- Within 14 days of election
- Specific allegations required
- Evidence threshold to proceed
- Bond requirement to prevent frivolous filings

### Court Procedures

**Day 1-7: State Trial Court**

- Expedited briefing (2 days)
- Hearing (1 day)
- Decision (4 days)

**Day 8-14: State Supreme Court**

- Direct appeal
- Expedited briefing (2 days)
- Argument (1 day)
- Decision (4 days)

**Day 15-21: U.S. Supreme Court**

- Certiorari automatic for federal questions
- Expedited briefing (2 days)
- Argument (2 days)
- Decision (3 days)

### Missed Deadline Effects

If any court misses its deadline:

- Results as tabulated become official
- Court may still rule
- Ruling applies prospectively only
- Cannot reverse post-inauguration

---

## Caretaker Governance

### Triggering Conditions

Caretaker governance applies when:

- Certification disputed after Day 21
- Supreme Court case pending after Day 21
- No clear winner by inauguration

### Limited Powers

Caretaker may:

- Continue routine operations
- Respond to imminent threats
- Execute existing law

Caretaker may NOT:

- Make new policy
- Appoint lifetime judges
- Deploy military offensively
- Sign significant legislation

### Automatic Succession (Day 45)

If dispute continues 45 days after scheduled inauguration:

1. Speaker of House assumes acting authority
2. Subject to caretaker limitations
3. Continues until dispute resolved

---

## Transition Implementation

### Certified President-Elect Rights

Beginning upon certification:

- Office space and staff support
- Security detail
- Intelligence briefings
- Agency cooperation for planning

### Enforcement

Withholding transition resources:

- Violation reportable to Congress
- Grounds for impeachment
- Enforceable by court order

---

## State Implementation

### Required State Actions

- Establish 14-day certification procedures
- Implement early voting (minimum 14 days)
- Provide absentee voting
- Enable same-day registration
- Designate expedited election courts

### Preemption

Federal standards preempt state laws on:

- Certification timelines
- Dispute filing deadlines
- Court decision timelines

States retain authority over:

- Ballot design (within standards)
- Polling place administration
- Poll worker training

---

## Metrics for Success

### Compliance

- All states certify within 14 days
- All disputes resolved within 21 days
- Transition resources provided promptly

### Process Quality

- Voter access maintained/improved
- Dispute filing rate (lower is better)
- Court deadline compliance

### Legitimacy

- Public confidence in results
- International observer assessments
- Acceptance by losing candidates
