# Impeachment Reform Implementation

## Overview

This document outlines implementation steps for the Impeachment Reform Constitutional Amendment.

---

## Immediate Effects Upon Ratification

### Timeline Requirements

All impeachment proceedings initiated after ratification must comply with:

- Thirty-day trial commencement requirement
- Ninety-day judgment deadline
- Procedural rights for accused

### Pending Impeachments

If articles of impeachment are pending at ratification:

- Timeline begins from ratification date
- Accused gains procedural rights immediately
- Senate must adapt procedures to comply

---

## Enabling Legislation

### House Impeachment Procedures Act

**Purpose**: Establish investigation and voting procedures.

**Key Provisions**:

- Committee investigation procedures
- Subpoena authority and enforcement
- Format for articles of impeachment
- Full House voting requirements
- Transmission procedures to Senate

**Article Format**:

```text
ARTICLES OF IMPEACHMENT

Against: [Officer name and title]

ARTICLE [Number]
[Statement of conduct alleged]

Constitutional Basis:
[Specific ground from Section 2]

Supporting Evidence:
[Summary of evidence supporting this article]

Therefore, [Officer] is impeached for [ground].
```

---

### Senate Impeachment Trial Act

**Purpose**: Establish trial procedures consistent with constitutional requirements.

**Key Provisions**:

- Trial commencement procedures
- Presiding officer designation
- Evidence and witness rules
- Accused's rights implementation
- Voting procedures
- Extension request procedures
- Default rule implementation

**Trial Schedule Template**:

| Day | Activity |
|-----|----------|
| 1 | Trial commences, rules adopted |
| 2-7 | Opening statements |
| 8-60 | Evidence presentation |
| 61-80 | Closing arguments |
| 81-90 | Deliberation and vote |

---

### Impeachment Judicial Review Act

**Purpose**: Establish procedures for Supreme Court procedural review.

**Key Provisions**:

- Standing for procedural challenges
- Expedited briefing and argument
- Fourteen-day decision requirement
- Scope of review limitations
- Remedies for procedural violations

**Review Procedure**:

1. Challenge filed in Supreme Court (original jurisdiction)
2. Response due within 48 hours
3. Oral argument within 7 days
4. Decision within 14 days of argument

---

## Senate Rule Amendments

The Senate should amend its impeachment trial rules to address:

### Evidence Standards

- Relevance requirement (similar to Federal Rules)
- Hearsay permitted with appropriate weight
- Documentary evidence authentication
- Expert witness qualifications

### Witness Procedures

- Both parties may call witnesses
- Cross-examination guaranteed
- Senate may also call witnesses
- Deposition procedures for unavailable witnesses

### Voting Procedures

- Secret ballot option (if requested by majority)
- Separate vote on each article
- Roll call for final judgment
- Disqualification vote separate from removal

## Implementation Timeline

### Months 1-3 (Post-Ratification)

**Congressional Action**:

- House drafts Impeachment Procedures Act
- Senate drafts Trial Act
- Judiciary Committee reviews judicial review provisions

**Rule Amendments**:

- House amends impeachment investigation rules
- Senate amends trial rules
- Joint committee coordinates procedures

### Months 4-6

**Training and Preparation**:

- Member training on new procedures
- Staff preparation for expedited timelines
- Coordination with Supreme Court on review procedures

## Procedural Rights Implementation

### Notice Requirements

The accused shall receive:

- Copy of all articles at least 14 days before trial
- List of witnesses House intends to call
- Summary of evidence supporting each article
- Explanation of rights under Section 7

### Counsel Rights

- Accused may be represented by counsel of choice
- If unable to afford counsel, Senate shall provide
- Counsel may participate in all proceedings
- Counsel has access to all evidence

### Witness Rights

- Accused may call any relevant witness
- Subpoena power to compel attendance
- Cross-examination of all House witnesses
- Right to present documentary evidence

---

## Default Rule Implementation

If Senate fails to render judgment within timeline:

### Day 90 (or Day 120 if extended)

1. Chief Justice/presiding officer issues order to conclude trial
2. No further evidence or argument permitted
3. Forty-eight hour voting period begins

### Day 92 (or Day 122)

1. If no vote has occurred:
   - Articles deemed rejected
   - Trial concluded
   - Accused remains in office
   - House may re-impeach on same or different grounds

### Documentation

- Chief Justice certifies timeline expiration
- Senate journal records default rejection
- No double jeopardy applies (re-impeachment permitted)

---

## Judicial Review Implementation

### Scope of Review

Courts may ONLY review:

1. **Quorum**: Was a majority of Senators present for vote?
2. **Vote threshold**: Did two-thirds present vote to convict?
3. **Presiding officer**: Was proper officer presiding?
4. **Accused rights**: Did accused receive Section 7 rights?

Courts may NOT review:

- Sufficiency of evidence
- Credibility of witnesses
- Whether conduct meets impeachment standard
- Proportionality of judgment

### Remedies

If procedural violation found:

- If pre-judgment: trial may continue with violation cured
- If post-judgment: judgment vacated, new trial ordered
- No damages or other relief

---

## Metrics for Success

### Process Compliance

- All impeachments commence within 30 days
- All judgments rendered within 90 days (120 with extension)
- All accused receive procedural rights

### Fairness Indicators

- Both parties able to present evidence
- Witnesses called as requested
- Cross-examination conducted
- Accused represented by counsel

### Judicial Review

- Challenges resolved within 14 days
- Review limited to procedural matters
- No substantive second-guessing
