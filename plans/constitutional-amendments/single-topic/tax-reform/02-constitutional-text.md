# Tax Reform Constitutional Principles

## Draft Amendment Framework

*Note: This document outlines constitutional principles for taxation. Detailed amendment text to be developed.*

---

### Principle 1. Progressive Taxation

(a) The tax system shall be structured so that persons with greater ability to pay bear a greater share of the tax burden.

(b) This principle applies to the tax system as a whole, not necessarily to each individual tax.

---

### Principle 2. Broad Base, Low Rates

(a) Tax bases shall be broad, with minimal exclusions, deductions, and credits.

(b) Broader bases permit lower rates while raising equivalent revenue.

(c) Deductions and credits shall be periodically reviewed for continued justification.

---

### Principle 3. Transparency

(a) Citizens shall be able to understand their total tax burden.

(b) Hidden taxes and obscure tax expenditures are disfavored.

(c) Congress shall publish annual reports on the distribution of tax burden by income level.

---

### Principle 4. Anti-Evasion

(a) Tax laws shall be enforced against all taxpayers equally.

(b) Congress shall provide adequate enforcement resources.

(c) Criminal penalties for tax evasion shall be meaningful deterrents.

---

### Principle 5. Fiscal Responsibility

(a) Government shall operate under sustainable fiscal policies.

(b) Emergency deficit spending shall be followed by restoration of balance.

(c) Long-term obligations shall be funded.

---

## Implementation Considerations

- These principles guide interpretation of tax statutes
- Congress retains authority to set specific rates and structures
- Courts may review whether tax system as a whole complies
- Periodic congressional review ensures ongoing compliance
