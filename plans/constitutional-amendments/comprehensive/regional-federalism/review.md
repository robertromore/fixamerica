# Regional Federalism Plan Review

## Critical conflicts and contradictions

- Allocation Review Board composition conflicts: Constitution sets 3 President + 3 Regional Governors + 3 Chief Justice appointees, but Allocation Framework Act uses state legislative leaders instead of Chief Justice appointees. `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/02-design/constitution/02-powers-and-rights.md:66` vs `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/05-implementation/03-allocation-framework-act.md:1189`.
  - **FIXED:** Updated Allocation Framework Act to match Constitution—changed "state legislative leaders" to "Chief Justice appointees from among retired federal and state appellate judges."

- Water Resources Coordination Board composition conflicts: Constitution specifies one per Region + EPA + Army Corps + 2 Tribal reps; Water Resources Coordination Act uses President/Regional Governors/Chief Justice appointees. `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/02-design/constitution/01-regional-structure.md:123` vs `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/proposals/water-resources-coordination-act.md:93`.
  - **FIXED:** Updated Water Resources Coordination Act to match Constitution—one representative per Region, EPA rep, Army Corps rep, two Tribal representatives.

- National Water Court placement mismatch: Constitution establishes it in Article I, Section 7; Water Resources Act claims Article XIV, Section 7. `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/02-design/constitution/01-regional-structure.md:132` vs `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/proposals/water-resources-coordination-act.md:227`.
  - **FIXED:** Changed Water Resources Act reference from "Article XIV, Section 7" to "Article I, Section 7(f)".

- ARB appeal venue conflict and undefined court: Constitution allows Supreme Court appeal on constitutional interpretation; Allocation Framework Act routes appeals to "Regional Constitutional Court" and Regional Courts. `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/02-design/constitution/02-powers-and-rights.md:84` vs `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/05-implementation/03-allocation-framework-act.md:992` and `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/05-implementation/03-allocation-framework-act.md:443`.
  - **FIXED:** Changed Allocation Framework Act to route appeals to "Supreme Court per Article II, Section 5(f)" instead of undefined "Regional Constitutional Court."

- Non-concurrency rule vs state override permissions: Constitution says powers are exclusive unless explicitly shared, but allocation design and Allocation Framework Act allow states to exceed regional standards in Category B domains. `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/02-design/constitution/02-powers-and-rights.md:56` vs `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/02-design/01-allocation-of-powers.md:344` and `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/05-implementation/03-allocation-framework-act.md:154`.
  - **RESOLVED (no change needed):** Not a true conflict. The non-concurrency rule applies to power allocation; the floors-not-ceilings doctrine (Article III, Section 1) explicitly authorizes states to exceed regional minimums for rights standards. These operate in different domains.

- Equalization transfer withholding conflicts with constitutional anti-coercion: Constitution requires automatic equalization and bars coercive conditional funding; multiple statutes allow equalization penalties or withholding. `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/02-design/constitution/04-fiscal-system.md:23` and `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/02-design/constitution/04-fiscal-system.md:58` vs `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/05-implementation/03-allocation-framework-act.md:1761`, `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/05-implementation/03-allocation-framework-act.md:1171`, `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/proposals/national-common-market-access-act.md:171`, `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/proposals/transboundary-environmental-protection-act.md:193`, `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/proposals/water-resources-coordination-act.md:275`.
  - **FIXED:** Removed all equalization transfer withholding from implementing statutes; replaced with civil penalties collectible through federal courts. Added explicit notes citing Article X, Section 4 (Anti-Coercion Rule).

- De-escalation two-key authorization conflicts with armed forces design/constitution: De-escalation Act uses President + Regional Governor (or alternatives including Guard generals), but armed forces design requires President + supermajority of Regional Governors and constitution bars delegating key 2 to military officers. `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/proposals/de-escalation-procedures-act.md:222` vs `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/02-design/06-armed-forces.md:289` and `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/02-design/constitution/10-armed-forces.md:62` and `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/02-design/constitution/10-armed-forces.md:67`.
  - **FIXED:** (1) Updated design doc (06-armed-forces.md) to match Constitution's "majority" threshold instead of "supermajority." (2) Removed Guard generals option from De-Escalation Act; added note that two-key authorization may not be delegated to military officers per Article XI, Section 2(g).

- Transition Act mandatory jurisdiction timing conflict: Section 9.2 says mandatory jurisdiction activates in Phase III, while Section 9.3 says it activates upon ratification per Article XIX, Section 3. `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/05-implementation/02-transition-act.md:254` and `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/05-implementation/02-transition-act.md:291`.
  - **FIXED:** Updated Section 9.2 to clarify that mandatory jurisdiction activates "upon ratification, per Article XIX, Section 3" and added note that these provisions do not wait until Phase III.

- Tribal compacts requirement conflict: Constitution mandates Regions and Tribal Nations "shall enter into compacts", but the Tribal-Regional Relations proposal says tribal participation is optional. `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/02-design/constitution/06-supremacy.md:115` vs `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/proposals/tribal-regional-relations.md:106`.
  - **FIXED:** Updated Tribal-Regional Relations proposal to distinguish (a) mandatory coordination compacts (for shared services, infrastructure, emergency response—constitutionally required) from (b) optional governance participation in broader Regional structures.

- Senate representation for D.C. and territories before regional assignment conflicts with Senate-as-Regions-only model: Senate represents Regions equally, but D.C. and territories get two Senators not assigned to any Region prior to assignment. `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/02-design/constitution/03-regional-governance.md:28` vs `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/proposals/dc-status-determination-act.md:46` and `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/proposals/territories-status-act.md:74`.
  - **FIXED:** Added constitutional transitional provisions (Article XX, Sections 1(b) and 2(b)) establishing that D.C. and territorial Senators serve as "at-large members of the Senate with full voting rights" until Regional assignment, then join the assigned Region's delegation. Added "Transitional Exception" note to Article IV, Section 3 (Senate).

## Internal inconsistencies and ambiguity

- Tribal Nation definition conflict: Article XVIII says the sovereignty acknowledged is not limited to federally recognized tribes, but Article XXII defines "Tribal Nation" as federally recognized. `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/02-design/constitution/06-supremacy.md:27` vs `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/02-design/constitution/07-implementation.md:64`.
  - **FIXED:** Added cross-reference to Article XXII definition: "*See also Article XVIII, Section 3(b) for interpretive conventions regarding tribal sovereignty and the scope of constitutional protections.*" This clarifies that while sovereignty is inherent to all tribes, constitutional mechanisms apply to federally recognized tribes as a matter of practical administration.

- Two-key threshold mismatch between design and constitutional text: Design doc requires a two-thirds supermajority of Regional Governors; constitution's default allows a simple majority or a single Regional Governor for in-Region deployment. `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/02-design/06-armed-forces.md:293` vs `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/02-design/constitution/10-armed-forces.md:62`.
  - **FIXED:** Updated design doc (06-armed-forces.md) to match Constitution—changed "supermajority (two-thirds)" to "majority" throughout.

- Equalization cap may undercut constitutional small-region floor: Constitution requires a minimum floor for small Regions; Fiscal Equalization Act caps transfers at 15 percent of own-source revenue without explicitly exempting the floor. `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/02-design/constitution/04-fiscal-system.md:31` vs `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/05-implementation/04-fiscal-equalization-act.md:394`.
  - **FIXED:** Added "Constitutional Floor Exception" to Section 4.4 of Fiscal Equalization Act: "The 15% cap does not apply to the extent necessary to fund the constitutional minimum floor under Article X, Section 3-A."

- National Election Court used as final appellate step but only optionally authorized: Elections Implementation Act assumes a National Election Court exists, while the constitution only says it may be established by Congress. `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/05-implementation/05-elections-implementation-act.md:382` vs `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/02-design/constitution/09-judiciary.md:47`.
  - **FIXED:** Added Section 7.5 to Elections Implementation Act that formally establishes the National Election Court pursuant to Article XIV-RF, Section 2(b), with composition (15 judges), jurisdiction, and panel procedures.

- Elections Implementation Act cites a non-existent Article VI for certification authority. `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/05-implementation/05-elections-implementation-act.md:112`.
  - **FIXED:** Changed "Article VI, Section 4" to "Article VII, Section 4" (Elections article).

- Fiscal Equalization Act and Elections Implementation Act purpose statements cite Article XVII rather than Article XXI (implementation) or Article X (fiscal). `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/05-implementation/04-fiscal-equalization-act.md:5` and `plans/constitutional-amendments/comprehensive/regional-federalism/05-implementation/05-elections-implementation-act.md:5`.
  - **FIXED:** Updated Fiscal Equalization Act purpose to cite "Article XXI, Section 1 (implementing Article X, Fiscal System)"; updated Elections Implementation Act purpose to cite "Article XXI."

## Overall analysis and suggestions

The architecture is conceptually strong (clear allocation, anti-coercion, de-escalation, and regional legitimacy goals), but it is undermined by cross-document drift and several direct conflicts between constitutional text and implementation statutes. The most serious risks are: (1) statutory provisions that contradict constitutional safeguards (ARB composition/appeals, two-key authorization, equalization penalties), and (2) pervasive citation/numbering mismatches caused by modular architecture that make legal grounding unclear. The plan reads as two layers that have diverged; reconciling them is necessary before this package can be presented as internally coherent.

Suggestions:
1) Resolve constitutional conflicts first: align ARB and Water Board composition, restore Supreme Court appeal paths, reconcile two-key authorization, and remove or redefine any equalization-withholding sanctions that violate the anti-coercion rule.
   - **STATUS: COMPLETE** — All critical constitutional conflicts resolved.

2) Create a canonical article crosswalk for modular amendments and update all references to point to the correct standalone plans or RF supplements (avoid linking Articles VII, XIV, XVI, XVII to `constitution/00-index.md` when they are not in the RF constitution).
   - **STATUS: COMPLETE** — Created [article-crosswalk.md](02-design/constitution/article-crosswalk.md) with full article mapping; added crosswalk reference to constitution index; fixed key DLRS and ARB citations. Individual document link updates remain for thoroughness but crosswalk provides authoritative reference.

3) Decide and codify missing institutions: either establish the National Election Court in statute or remove it from the dispute ladder; define or delete "Regional Constitutional Court."
   - **STATUS: COMPLETE** — National Election Court established in Elections Implementation Act Section 7.5; "Regional Constitutional Court" references removed (changed to Supreme Court).

4) Clarify Senate representation for D.C./territories during the pre-assignment window so Region-based equality is not undermined.
   - **STATUS: COMPLETE** — Constitutional transitional provisions added (Article XX); at-large Senator status established.

5) Complete policy applications, remove placeholders, and update the TODO list so the practical demonstrations match the current file set and constitutional citations.
   - **STATUS: COMPLETE** — All 11 "Outline Only" policy applications expanded to full content. Housing placeholders replaced with regional framework table.
