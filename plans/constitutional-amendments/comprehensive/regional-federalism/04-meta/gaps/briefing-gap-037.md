# Briefing: Gap 37 — Judicial Appointment Capture

## 1. The Problem

**Gap 37** identifies a structural vulnerability: Regional courts are established by Article XIV-RF but the constitution delegates appointment mechanisms entirely to Regional constitutions. While federal-level judicial protections are extensive (nominating commissions, term limits, court-packing prohibition, ethics code), none of these extend mandatorily to Regional courts.

### The Attack Vector

A politically dominant faction controlling a Regional government over multiple cycles could:

| Phase | Action | Effect |
|-------|--------|--------|
| 1 | Establish pure gubernatorial appointment with legislature confirmation | No merit-selection filter |
| 2 | Appoint ideologically aligned judges to trial and appellate courts | Control majority of Regional jurisprudence |
| 3 | Correctly implement Federal Floors (avoiding federal cert under §3) | Evade federal oversight for all non-Floor matters |
| 4 | Interpret Regional law, Regional constitution, and non-Floor disputes favorably | Capture the vast majority of cases citizens encounter |

### Why Existing Protections Are Insufficient

| Protection | What It Covers | What It Misses |
|-----------|---------------|----------------|
| Art XIV-RF §3 (Federal Floor certification) | Federal Floor enforcement | All Regional law disputes (~80%+ of caseload) |
| ARB alternative forum | Power-allocation disputes | Substantive Regional law interpretation |
| Art XIV §7 (default rules) | Judicial obstruction/delay | Subtle interpretive capture |
| Art XIV §14 (Nominating Commissions) | Federal judicial seats | Regional courts entirely |
| Art XIV-RF §1(c) (good behavior) | Removal standard | Appointment mechanism — no merit selection |
| Art VII-I §5-A(i) (permissive opt-in) | Regions that voluntarily adopt | Regions that decline — commitment problem |

### Status Contradiction

The gap source file (08-electoral-judicial.md) says "Partially mitigated by ARB as alternative forum and by default rules if courts fail to act." The 00-index says "Partially Mitigated." Both are accurate — federal-level protections exist but Regional-level appointment capture is unaddressed.

## 2. Existing Coverage Analysis

### Federal Judicial Protections (Art XIV)

- **§1**: 9-Justice Supreme Court, structure locked by amendment only
- **§2**: 18-year non-renewable terms, staggered vacancies
- **§3**: Appointment with Senate confirmation, 10-year judicial experience requirement
- **§9**: Judicial ethics code, Judicial Conduct Council
- **§10**: Court-packing prohibition (2/3 supermajority, 4-year cooling-off)
- **§11**: Compensation protection, anti-reassignment, appropriations protection
- **§12**: Quorum protection with emergency restoration
- **§13**: Vacancy acceleration with default confirmation (90-day Senate deadline)
- **§14**: Judicial Nominating Commissions — **federal seats only**

### Regional Court Protections (Art XIV-RF)

- **§1**: Establishment mandate (trial, appellate, Regional Supreme Court)
- **§1(c)**: "Good behavior" and "removal only for cause" — **but no appointment constraints**
- **§3**: Federal Floor certification (prevents circumvention of Floors)
- **§4**: Provisional relief and remedy preservation
- **§5**: Broad standing to enforce constitution

### The Gap

The constitution requires **no minimum appointment quality standard** for Regional judges. A Region could:

- Allow pure gubernatorial appointment with no commission input
- Allow indefinite terms or terms controlled by the appointing authority
- Impose no conflict-of-interest protections
- Allow judges with no minimum qualifications or experience

## 4. Proposed Approach: Minimum Judicial Independence Standards for Regional Courts

A single new section — **Art XIV-RF §1-A** — establishing constitutional minimums that Regional constitutions must meet for their judiciary. This follows the proven pattern used elsewhere in the constitution: establish a floor while preserving Regional flexibility on implementation details.

### Design Principles

1. **Floor, not ceiling**: Regions may adopt stronger protections but may not fall below minimums
2. **Structural, not substantive**: Controls the appointment *process*, not judicial *outcomes*
3. **Parallel to federal model**: Uses Art XIV §14 as template, adapted for Regional context
4. **Preserves Regional autonomy**: Regions choose commission composition details, term lengths within range, specific ethics rules
5. **Enforceable**: Judicial Conduct Board review with federal backstop

## 5. Proposed Constitutional Text

### Art XIV-RF §1-A. Minimum Judicial Independence Standards for Regional Courts

*Resolves Gap 37 — Judicial Appointment Capture. This section establishes constitutional minimums for Regional judicial appointment, tenure, and accountability to prevent sustained political capture of Regional courts while preserving Regional autonomy over implementation details.*

(a) **Merit Selection Requirement.** Each Region shall establish a Judicial Nominating Commission for appointments to its Regional courts:

- (1) The Commission shall consist of not fewer than seven (7) members, of whom: (A) at least two (2) shall be attorneys selected by the Regional Bar Association or equivalent professional body; (B) at least two (2) shall be non-attorneys selected by the Regional legislature from among residents who have never held partisan office; (C) at least one (1) shall be a retired judge; and (D) the remaining members shall be selected by the Regional Governor, subject to Regional legislative confirmation;
- (2) No single appointing authority may select a majority of Commission members;
- (3) Commission members shall serve staggered terms of not less than four (4) nor more than six (6) years and may serve no more than two (2) consecutive terms;
- (4) No person shall serve as a Commission member who, within the preceding five (5) years, has: (A) held partisan elected office; (B) served as an officer of a political party at any level (including chair, vice-chair, treasurer, or secretary); (C) served as campaign manager or finance chair for any candidate for Regional or federal office; (D) been a registered lobbyist; or (E) who is a close relative (spouse, parent, child, or sibling) of the Regional Governor or presiding officer of either chamber of the Regional legislature;
- (5) Regions may adopt commission structures that exceed these minimums in size, diversity of appointing authorities, or restrictions on partisan affiliation, but may not reduce them below the standards of this subsection.

(b) **Nomination List Process.** For all Regional court appointments:

- (1) The Judicial Nominating Commission shall publish a public call for applications and, within sixty (60) days, submit a list of not fewer than three (3) qualified candidates to the appointing authority designated by the Regional constitution;
- (2) The appointing authority shall select from the Commission's list. The appointing authority may request one (1) additional list of candidates if it provides written reasons for declining the initial list, but may not reject both lists;
- (3) If the appointing authority fails to select from the Commission's list within sixty (60) days, the Commission's highest-ranked candidate shall be deemed appointed;
- (4) The Regional legislature may establish confirmation requirements for the appointing authority's selection, provided that confirmation proceedings shall conclude within sixty (60) days or the selection shall be deemed confirmed;
- (5) If the Regional legislature rejects three (3) consecutive nominees selected by the appointing authority from the Commission's lists, the power to appoint shall transfer to the Regional Chief Justice or senior judge of the Regional Supreme Court, who shall select from the Commission's most recent list within thirty (30) days. This transfer continues until the next regular vacancy.

(c) **Term Limits for Regional Judges.** Regional judges appointed after the effective date of this section shall serve terms:

- (1) Of not less than ten (10) years nor more than sixteen (16) years for Regional Supreme Court justices;
- (2) Of not less than eight (8) years nor more than fourteen (14) years for Regional appellate court judges;
- (3) Of not less than six (6) years nor more than twelve (12) years for Regional trial court judges;
- (4) Regions shall determine specific term lengths within these ranges by Regional constitution or statute;
- (5) Terms shall be staggered to prevent any single appointing authority from filling a majority of seats on any court during a single term of office;
- (6) A judge who has served the maximum term on one level of Regional court may be appointed to a different level of Regional court through the Nominating Commission process;
- (7) Terms under this subsection are non-renewable. No judge may be reappointed, retained, or confirmed for an additional term on the same court. This prohibition may not be circumvented by redesignation, renaming, or reconstitution of the court.

(d) **Removal and Discipline.** Regional judges may be removed only for cause consistent with the "good behavior" standard defined in Article XXII, Section 1(c):

- (1) Each Region shall establish a Judicial Conduct Board, structurally independent from both the Regional executive and legislature, with authority to investigate complaints, impose discipline short of removal (including private reprimand, public censure, and temporary suspension), and recommend removal to the Regional legislature;
- (2) Removal from office shall require a two-thirds vote of the Regional legislature upon recommendation of the Judicial Conduct Board, or impeachment pursuant to the Regional constitution, but in no case may a Regional judge be removed by simple majority vote or by executive action alone;
- (3) No judge may be removed, reassigned, or have jurisdiction reduced as a consequence of decisions rendered, unless such decisions constitute grounds for discipline under the code of judicial conduct;
- (4) The Judicial Conduct Board shall include at least one (1) member who is not a resident of the Region, selected by lottery from a pool of retired Regional judges from other Regions maintained by the Judicial Conference of the United States, to provide external perspective and prevent insularity.

(e) **Minimum Qualifications.** No person shall be eligible for appointment to a Regional court who has not:

- (1) Been admitted to the practice of law for at least five (5) years for trial court appointments, eight (8) years for appellate court appointments, or ten (10) years for Regional Supreme Court appointments; or
- (2) Served at least three (3) years as a judge on any court for trial court appointments, five (5) years for appellate appointments, or seven (7) years for Regional Supreme Court appointments;
- (3) Regions may impose additional qualifications beyond these minimums but may not waive them.

(f) **Compensation and Budget Protection.** Regional judicial compensation:

- (1) Shall not be diminished during a judge's term of service;
- (2) Shall be adjusted at least every four (4) years to maintain real value;
- (3) The Regional judiciary shall receive annual appropriations not less than the prior fiscal year's appropriation adjusted for inflation, as certified by the Regional Judicial Conference or equivalent body. The Regional legislature may reduce this floor only upon a two-thirds vote accompanied by specific findings that decreased caseload or operational requirements justify the reduction. If the Regional legislature fails to appropriate funds meeting this floor for two (2) consecutive fiscal years, the Regional judiciary's funding shall be treated as mandatory spending at the level of the most recent compliant appropriation adjusted for inflation.

(g) **Ethics and Conflicts of Interest.** Each Region shall adopt a code of judicial conduct for its Regional courts:

- (1) Requiring financial disclosure by all Regional judges;
- (2) Establishing recusal standards for financial interests, family relationships, and prior representation;
- (3) Restricting extrajudicial political activity by sitting judges;
- (4) The code shall be developed by the Regional Judicial Conference or equivalent body and approved by the Regional Supreme Court, not by the Regional legislature or executive.

(h) **Court-Packing Prevention.** No Regional statute enacted within four (4) years after a change in the party controlling the Regional governorship may alter the size of any Regional court. Any statute altering the number of judges on any Regional court shall require a two-thirds vote of the Regional legislature.

(i) **Transition.** Regions shall bring their judicial systems into compliance with this section within five (5) years of ratification:

- (1) Judges serving at the time of ratification may complete their current terms regardless of whether their appointment complied with this section;
- (2) All new appointments after the five-year transition period must comply with this section;
- (3) During the transition period, Regions operating under the Capacity Development Program (Article XXI, Section 7) may request an extension of up to two (2) additional years from the ARB, upon showing that immediate compliance would impose an undue burden on the Region's developing institutional capacity;
- (4) The Judicial Conference of the United States shall certify Regional compliance and publish annual compliance reports.

(j) **Federal Backstop.** If the Judicial Conference certifies that a Region's judicial system fails to meet the minimums established by this section:

- (1) The Region shall have one (1) year to remedy non-compliance after certification;
- (2) If non-compliance persists: (A) judgments of the non-compliant Region's courts shall not be entitled to Full Faith and Credit in other Regions' courts or in federal courts until the Judicial Conference certifies compliance; and (B) the Supreme Court of the United States shall have mandatory appellate jurisdiction over final judgments of the non-compliant Region's Supreme Court, exercisable upon petition of any party;
- (3) These consequences continue until the Judicial Conference certifies compliance;
- (4) The federal backstop is a structural remedy, not a sanction; it does not affect the Region's other governmental powers or equalization transfers. The backstop operates on the Region's institutional compliance, not on the correctness of individual judicial decisions.

(k) **Savings Clause.**

- (1) This section establishes minimums; Regions may adopt stronger independence protections, longer terms within the specified ranges, more restrictive ethics codes, or additional appointment constraints;
- (2) Nothing in this section affects the independence requirements for federal courts or for specialized courts established under Section 2;
- (3) The permissive provisions of Article XIV-RF, Section 1(c) are superseded to the extent they permit appointment mechanisms, terms, or removal procedures that fail to meet the minimums of this section.

## 6. Design Questions for Reviewers

1. **Term limit ranges vs. fixed terms**: Is the range approach (8-18 years for Regional Supreme Court) appropriately flexible, or should there be a narrower range or single minimum?

2. **Commission composition**: Is the 7-member floor with divided appointing authorities sufficient to prevent commission capture, or should the non-partisan majority requirement be strengthened?

3. **Federal backstop proportionality**: Is expanded federal appellate review the right enforcement mechanism, or is it too intrusive? Alternatives: equalization transfer conditions, ARB certification, Political Institutional Failure referral.

4. **Court-packing prevention scope**: Should the 4-year cooling-off and 2/3 requirement apply to Regional courts, or is this excessive federal intrusion into Regional legislative prerogatives?

5. **External member on Judicial Conduct Board**: Does appointment by the Chief Justice of the United States create a federal-Regional tension, or is external perspective essential to prevent insularity?

6. **Transition for existing judges**: Is 5 years (with 2-year extension for capacity-development Regions) adequate for Regions to establish nominating commissions and transition their appointment processes?

## 8. Review Findings Incorporated

Two independent reviews were conducted. All findings have been incorporated into the revised text in Section 5 above.

### Finding Summary

| ID | Severity | Finding | Resolution |
|----|----------|---------|------------|
| F1 | CRITICAL | Terms must be explicitly non-renewable; without this, "merit selection" becomes screening for Governor's reappointment patronage | Added §1-A(c)(7): non-renewable terms with anti-circumvention language |
| F2 | HIGH | Commission "civilian" eligibility too narrow — excludes elected officeholders but permits party officers, campaign managers, major donors | Expanded §1-A(a)(4) to exclude party officers at any level, campaign managers, and finance chairs |
| F3 | HIGH | Federal backstop (Circuit Court review of Regional law) reverses Erie doctrine and punishes litigants more than the Region | Redesigned §1-A(j)(2): replaced expanded Circuit review with Full Faith and Credit suspension + mandatory Supreme Court appellate jurisdiction |
| F4 | MEDIUM | Confirmation pocket veto via serial rejection — legislature can block all appointments by rejecting every nominee | Added §1-A(b)(5): after 3 consecutive rejections, appointment power transfers to Regional Chief Justice |
| F5 | MEDIUM | "Adequate" appropriation baseline ambiguous — enables budget squeeze disguised as fiscal austerity | Revised §1-A(f)(3): defined floor as prior-year-adjusted-for-inflation, reducible only by 2/3 vote with specific findings |
| R1 | MEDIUM | Term ranges too wide (8-18/6-15/4-12), permitting terms too short for independence | Narrowed to 10-16 / 8-14 / 6-12 |
| R2 | LOW | External Conduct Board member selected by Chief Justice creates federal dominance perception | Changed to lottery selection from national pool of retired Regional judges maintained by Judicial Conference |

### Design Questions Resolved

| Question | Resolution |
|----------|------------|
| 1. Term limit ranges | Narrowed. 10-16 / 8-14 / 6-12 balances independence with accountability |
| 2. Commission composition | Strengthened eligibility exclusions; kept flexible 7-member floor |
| 3. Federal backstop proportionality | Redesigned. Full Faith and Credit suspension + mandatory Supreme Court jurisdiction avoids Erie reversal while creating institutional pressure |
| 4. Court-packing prevention | Retained. 4-year cooling-off and 2/3 requirement are proportionate |
| 5. External Conduct Board member | Modified selection mechanism. Lottery from national pool avoids federal dominance |
| 6. Transition period | Adequate. 5 years with 2-year extension for capacity-development Regions confirmed as sufficient |
