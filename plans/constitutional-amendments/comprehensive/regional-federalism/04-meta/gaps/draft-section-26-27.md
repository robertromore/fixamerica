# Draft: Article II, Sections 26-27 -- Housing Coordination Scope Limitation and Local Autonomy Floor

*Resolves Gap 96 -- The "Local Hollowing Out" via Regional Preemption. Fills the obligation referenced in Section 25(l) and Section 3-B(c)(4).*

---

## Constitutional Text

### Section 26. Housing Coordination Scope Limitation

*Resolves Gap 96 (Part 1) -- Constrains the scope of Regional "housing coordination and supply targets" authority under Section 2 to prevent granular preemption of local land-use decisions.*

(a) **Scope of Housing Coordination Authority.** The Regional authority over "housing coordination and supply targets" under Section 2 is limited to:

- (1) setting aggregate supply targets for the Region as a whole and allocating targets among States based on capacity, need, and demographic projections;
- (2) coordinating inter-State infrastructure to support housing development;
- (3) providing technical assistance and model codes for voluntary adoption; and
- (4) monitoring and publishing data on Regional housing supply, affordability, and target compliance.

(b) **Local Implementation Primacy.** Implementation of housing targets -- including site selection, density determination, type mix, and design standards -- is the primary responsibility of local governments, subject to aggregate target compliance under subsection (a)(1), State enabling legislation under Section 3, and the domain reconciliation framework of Section 27(c). A local government meeting its allocated share of Regional housing targets may determine the location, density, and type mix of development, may prioritize areas differently than Regional preference, and is not required to adopt specific Regional zoning provisions.

(c) **Prohibited Regional Actions.** Regions may not:

- (1) mandate specific zoning designations for identified parcels or parcel types;
- (2) require adoption of Regional zoning codes as a condition of target compliance;
- (3) assume local land-use authority based on target shortfall alone without proceeding through the preemption standard of Section 27(d); or
- (4) set targets at such granular geographic levels that aggregate target compliance is achievable only through a single implementation path.

(d) **Target Setting Standards.** Regional housing targets shall be:

- (1) based on documented housing need assessments, including current shortfall, projected population growth, and affordability metrics;
- (2) allocated to States, which may further allocate to local governments, in proportion to capacity -- including available land, infrastructure, and proximity to employment centers -- and need;
- (3) achievable through multiple implementation paths, preserving local discretion over means; and
- (4) subject to review by the ARB upon petition by any affected State under the subsidiarity review procedures of Section 25(t), or by any affected local government under Section 27(g).

### Section 27. Local Autonomy Floor

*Resolves Gap 96 (Parts 2-4) -- The "Local Hollowing Out" via Regional Preemption. Establishes the Local Autonomy Floor referenced in Section 25(l), protecting local government authority from hollowing out through granular Regional preemption across all domains.*

#### Purpose and Scope

(a) **Purpose.** This Section establishes the Local Autonomy Floor referenced in Section 25(l), protecting local government authority from erosion through granular Regional preemption. This Section applies to all Regional exercise of authority that affects local government powers, regardless of the domain classification under Section 1(e). The standing, fiscal anti-coercion, and savings provisions of this Section apply universally; the non-preemptible powers of subsection (b) apply only in Cooperative Implementation and Concurrent Authority domains as specified therein.

#### Non-Preemptible Local Powers

(b) **Enumerated Non-Preemptible Powers.** In Cooperative Implementation and Concurrent Authority domains under Section 25(b)(2)-(3), local governments retain exclusive authority over:

- (1) site-specific land-use decisions, including conditional use permits and variances;
- (2) design review and aesthetic standards for individual projects;
- (3) local infrastructure capacity determinations;
- (4) neighborhood-level planning and character preservation;
- (5) historic preservation designations;
- (6) environmental review for impacts of purely local scope;
- (7) public participation requirements for land-use decisions; and
- (8) local building code enforcement, fire safety, and health inspections, to the extent these do not conflict with Regional performance standards.

For Full Preemption domains under Section 25(b)(1), the protections of this subsection do not apply; Regional authority operates directly per Section 25(g).

#### Domain Reconciliation

(c) **Reconciliation of Domain Classification.** Where the classification of "Land use, zoning, and local planning" as an Exclusive Regional Domain under Section 1(e)(2)(ii) intersects with the retention of "land use frameworks" and "local government law" to States under Section 3:

- (1) Regional authority under Section 1(e)(2)(ii) encompasses Region-wide policy frameworks, standards, and objectives for land use and planning;
- (2) State authority under Section 3 encompasses the adoption and maintenance of land use enabling legislation, procedural requirements, and local government structural law;
- (3) local government authority under this Section encompasses site-specific implementation, project-level decision-making, and exercise of the non-preemptible powers enumerated in subsection (b); and
- (4) where Regional policy frameworks and State enabling legislation overlap, the tiered classification and preemption standards of Section 25 govern the Regional-State relationship, and this Section governs the relationship between either level and local governments.

#### Preemption Standard

(d) **Standard for Override.** When a Region seeks to override a non-preemptible local power enumerated in subsection (b) within a Cooperative Implementation or Concurrent Authority domain:

- (1) the Region must demonstrate to the ARB that the local exercise of authority substantially frustrates an identified Regional objective;
- (2) the Region must demonstrate that no less intrusive means is available to achieve the Regional objective;
- (3) if the ARB certifies that both conditions are met, preemption shall be limited to the specific local power creating the frustration and shall not extend to unrelated local authority; and
- (4) preemption authorized under this subsection shall terminate when the specific frustration is remedied, and in no case later than five (5) years from the date of authorization, renewable only upon a fresh showing that both conditions of paragraphs (1)-(2) remain satisfied.

#### Bad Faith Rebuttal

(e) **Bad Faith Rebuttal of Non-Preemptible Powers.** The protections of subsection (b) are rebuttable upon a finding by the ARB, by clear and convincing evidence, that a local government is exercising non-preemptible powers in bad faith to obstruct legitimate Regional objectives. Upon such a finding:

- (1) the ARB may authorize the Region to override the specific local power being exercised in bad faith;
- (2) the override shall be narrowly tailored to the specific obstruction and time-limited to no more than three (3) years, renewable upon a fresh showing of continued bad faith;
- (3) the local government may petition the ARB for restoration of its authority at any time upon demonstrating that the bad faith has ceased; and
- (4) a bad faith finding does not affect the local government's exercise of other non-preemptible powers not implicated in the finding.

#### Fiscal Anti-Coercion

(f) **Regional-to-Local Fiscal Anti-Coercion.**

- (1) A Region may not condition infrastructure funding, grants, technical assistance, or other fiscal transfers on a local government's adoption of specific zoning provisions, approval of specific development projects, or modification of local land-use decisions beyond what is necessary for aggregate target compliance.
- (2) Regional fiscal conditions are presumptively coercive if: (A) the potential funding loss from any single condition exceeds five percent (5%) of the local government's total annual budget from Regional sources within the affected functional category; or (B) the aggregate financial impact of all Regional conditions within a single functional category exceeds five percent (5%) of the local government's budget from Regional sources for that category in a fiscal year. For purposes of this paragraph, conditions that are functionally related or imposed in connection with the same Regional objective shall be aggregated regardless of their formal separation.
- (3) Regional fiscal conditions related to housing targets may require good-faith efforts toward target achievement but may not dictate specific means and may not penalize localities meeting targets through methods different from Regional preference.
- (4) The ARB may find Regional fiscal coercion upon petition by any affected local government or State. Upon such a finding, the coercive condition is severed; the local government receives the funds without the invalid condition. Non-coercive conditions within the same funding instrument remain in force.
- (5) This subsection adapts the federal-to-Regional anti-coercion principles of Article X, Section 4 to the Regional-to-local relationship. Article X, Section 4 itself is not directly applicable to Regional-to-local spending.

#### Standing and Enforcement

(g) **Local Government Standing.**

- (1) Any local government may petition the ARB directly for a finding that a Regional action violates this Section, including petitions for injunctive relief pending review.
- (2) Any State may petition the ARB on behalf of local governments within its jurisdiction for the same relief.
- (3) Local and State petitions operate as parallel tracks; neither forecloses the other.
- (4) In all proceedings under this Section, the ARB shall apply a presumption in favor of local authority, consistent with the anti-centralization presumption of Section 25(u).

(h) **State Intermediary Role.**

- (1) States may allocate Regional targets among local governments within their jurisdiction, consistent with the allocation standards of Section 26(d).
- (2) States may mediate disputes between Regions and local governments. Where mediation produces a resolution acceptable to all parties, the ARB shall defer to the mediated outcome, provided that: (A) the outcome does not compromise the protections of this Section or Section 26; (B) all local governments materially affected by the outcome have been parties to or given notice and opportunity to participate in the mediation; and (C) the outcome does not establish precedent binding on non-party local governments.
- (3) Local governments are not required to exhaust State mediation before petitioning the ARB directly.
- (4) States may enact legislation protecting local authority within Regional frameworks, provided such legislation does not frustrate legitimate Regional objectives.

#### Interaction and Savings Clauses

(i) **Interaction with Section 3-B.** Section 3-B protects cooperating sub-Regional units from State interference. This Section protects all local governments from Regional preemption. Where both protections apply -- a cooperating local unit facing both State interference and Regional preemption -- the more protective provision governs.

(j) **Non-Coercion Savings Clause.** Enforcement of the Local Autonomy Floor by the ARB does not constitute federal coercion under Article X, Section 4. The protections of this Section are self-executing constitutional rights of local governments, not conditional spending requirements imposed on Regions.

(k) **ARB Interpretive Guidelines.** The ARB shall, within two (2) years of ratification, publish interpretive guidelines for the application of this Section and Section 26, including criteria for determining: "single implementation path" under Section 26(c)(4); "purely local scope" under subsection (b)(6); and "good-faith efforts" under subsection (f)(3). Guidelines shall be updated no less than every five (5) years and are subject to notice-and-comment procedures.

## Conforming Amendments

### 1. Article II, Section 25(l) -- Update Forward Reference

**Replace** the second paragraph of §25(l) (beginning "Nothing in this subsection..."):

> Nothing in this subsection authorizes Regional preemption of local authority except as provided in subsection (g) for Full Preemption policies. For Cooperative Implementation and Concurrent Authority domains, the Local Autonomy Floor established in Article II-RF, Section 3 is preserved and Regional directives shall not override non-preemptible local powers enumerated therein.

**With:**

> Nothing in this subsection authorizes Regional preemption of local authority except as provided in subsection (g) for Full Preemption policies. For Cooperative Implementation and Concurrent Authority domains, the Local Autonomy Floor established in Section 27 is preserved and Regional directives shall not override non-preemptible local powers enumerated therein.

### 2. Article II, Section 3-B(c)(1) -- Fix Cost Recovery Cross-Reference (D9)

> cost recovery shall follow the procedures of Section 4(n) of this Article, including the fifteen percent (15%) administrative surcharge.

**With:**

> cost recovery shall follow the procedures of Section 25(n) of this Article, including the fifteen percent (15%) administrative surcharge.

*Rationale: §4(n) is now "Material Barrier Standard" after the §25 reorganization (Gap 59). Cost recovery is at §25(n).*

### 3. Article II, Section 3-B(c)(4) -- Fix Local Government Obstruction Cross-Reference and Update LAF Reference (D9)

**Replace** §3-B(c)(4):

> (4) **Interaction with Local Government Obstruction.** This section addresses State interference with cooperating sub-units and is complementary to Section 4(l), which addresses local refusal to cooperate. Where a State dissolves a cooperating unit and the successor unit ceases cooperation, the State action shall be treated as prohibited interference under subsection (b), and the State remains responsible for project implementation under Section 4(l)(1)-(2). The protections established by this section constitute the Local Autonomy Floor referenced in Section 4(l).

**With:**

> (4) **Interaction with Local Government Obstruction.** This section addresses State interference with cooperating sub-units and is complementary to Section 25(l), which addresses local refusal to cooperate. Where a State dissolves a cooperating unit and the successor unit ceases cooperation, the State action shall be treated as prohibited interference under subsection (b), and the State remains responsible for project implementation under Section 25(l)(1)-(2). The protections established by this section are complementary to the Local Autonomy Floor established in Section 27. Section 3-B addresses State interference with cooperating sub-units; Section 27 addresses Regional preemption of local authority.

*Rationale: §4(l) is now "Infrastructure Interoperability Mandate" after the §25 reorganization (Gap 59). Local Government Obstruction is at §25(l). The LAF is now established by §27 rather than constituted solely by §3-B.*

### 4. Article II, Section 3-B(c)(2) -- Fix Phantom Article II-RF Reference

**Replace** in §3-B(c)(2) (final sentence):

> the ARB may recommend reclassification of the affected project domains from Cooperative Implementation to Regional Exclusive Domain under the procedures of Article II-RF, Section 2, removing State implementation discretion entirely.

**With:**

> the ARB may recommend reclassification of the affected project domains from Cooperative Implementation to Full Preemption under the procedures of Section 25(f), removing State implementation discretion entirely.

*Rationale: "Article II-RF, Section 2" does not exist. Reclassification to Full Preemption is governed by §25(f). Also corrects the conflation of §25 tiers ("Cooperative Implementation") with §1(e) domain classifications ("Regional Exclusive Domain").*

### 5. Article II, Section 7(g) -- Add Local Government Standing

**Add** to §7(g) after existing item (5):

> (6) Petitions by local governments alleging Regional violation of the Local Autonomy Floor under Section 27, or Regional exceedance of housing coordination scope under Section 26.

*Rationale: Ensures local government standing before the ARB is anchored in the jurisdictional grant, not only in §27(g). Covers both §26 and §27 challenges.*
