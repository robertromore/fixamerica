### Source Gap Summary

Regional boundaries are revised decennially based on census data. A Region threatened with dissolution, merger, or unfavorable boundary adjustment could use emergency powers to obstruct census workers — creating statistical undercount that preserves the status quo. The emergency power becomes a shield against demographic accountability.

Three primary attack vectors: (1) public health emergency pretexts preventing door-to-door enumeration, (2) security emergency pretexts delaying census workers through background check requirements, (3) administrative system "failures" preventing data transmission to the Census Bureau.

The original proposal placed this as Art XVIII-RF §1(g)-(j) — four subsections of the declaration authority section. This draft restructures as Art XVII-RF §6 (new section) for consistency with the existing pattern where each gap resolution adds a self-contained section (§4 for Gap 206, §5 for Gap 215).

### Section 6. Census Immunity and Enumeration Protection

*Resolves Gap 126 — "Census Emergency" and Boundary Sabotage. Establishes constitutional immunity for federal census operations from emergency power abuse, authorizes alternative enumeration when obstruction occurs, creates an enforcement framework with ARB investigation and burden-shifting, and protects the decennial boundary revision timeline from data manipulation.*

[v1] (a) **Definitions.** For purposes of this Section:

- [v1] (1) "Census period" means the constitutionally mandated decennial enumeration period, beginning on the date designated by the Director of the Census Bureau and continuing until the Director certifies completion, plus any extension authorized by Congress;
- [v1] (2) "Census operations" means all activities conducted by or on behalf of the Census Bureau for purposes of enumeration, including door-to-door canvassing, mail collection, electronic data gathering, administrative records analysis, statistical estimation, and quality verification;
- [v1] (3) "Census obstruction" means any act or omission by a governmental entity — federal, Regional, or State — that delays, prevents, undermines, or materially impedes census operations, including but not limited to:
    - [v1] (A) emergency declarations that restrict census worker movement, access, or operations;
    - [v1] (B) denial or unreasonable delay of access to public facilities, records, or infrastructure needed for enumeration;
    - [v1] (C) data system shutdowns, transmission failures, or interoperability refusals not attributable to natural causes or genuine technical failure;
    - [v1] (D) threats, intimidation, or harassment of census personnel by governmental agents or with governmental acquiescence;
    - [v1] (E) imposition of licensing, credentialing, background check, or security requirements on census workers beyond those imposed by the Census Bureau itself;
- [v2: census response rate definition with methodology delegation] (4) "Census response rate" means the percentage of housing units within a Region from which the Census Bureau has obtained a completed enumeration response — whether by self-response, field enumeration, or administrative records verification — as certified by the Director of the Census Bureau; the Director shall publish the methodology for calculating response rates no later than one (1) year before each decennial census.

[v1] (b) **Census Immunity from Emergency Powers.**

- [v1] (1) No emergency declaration — federal, Regional, or State — may be used to delay, obstruct, or modify federal census operations within the declaring jurisdiction;
- [v1] (2) census workers shall have constitutional immunity from emergency restrictions imposed by any level of government during the census period; no governmental entity may detain, exclude, redirect, or impose conditions upon census workers engaged in lawful enumeration activities;
- [v1] (3) the Census Bureau may conduct enumeration using any lawful method notwithstanding any emergency order, declaration, or restriction;
- [v1] (4) governmental data systems that serve census operations shall remain operational during the census period; Regional and State governments shall maintain data transmission capacity to the Census Bureau and shall designate backup transmission methods in advance of each census period; [v3: cyber incident alignment] a genuine cyber incident affecting census data systems — verified by a Census Bureau information security officer — shall not constitute census obstruction under subsection (a)(3)(C) provided the affected government activates designated backup transmission methods within twenty-four (24) hours; failure to activate backup methods within this period creates a rebuttable presumption of census obstruction;
- [v1] (5) the immunity established by this subsection applies regardless of whether the underlying emergency declaration is legitimate; a genuine emergency does not authorize census obstruction as an incidental consequence;
- [v2: narrow federal-only safety restriction for imminent threats to life] (6) notwithstanding the foregoing, a federal official designated by the Director of the Census Bureau may temporarily restrict census operations within a specific geographic area when there is an imminent threat to the life or physical safety of census workers that cannot be mitigated by security measures:
    - [v2] (A) the restriction shall be limited to the specific area of imminent threat and shall not exceed seventy-two (72) hours; a single renewal of seventy-two (72) hours may be authorized if the threat persists;
    - [v2] (B) the Census Bureau shall be notified immediately and shall adjust enumeration schedules to compensate for any lost time;
    - [v2] (C) only federal officials may invoke this provision; Regional, State, and local officials may request federal invocation but may not independently restrict census operations on safety grounds;
    - [v2] (D) the Director shall conduct a post-action review within thirty (30) days; if the restriction was not justified by a genuine imminent threat, the restricting official's action constitutes census obstruction under subsection (a)(3);
    - [v3: prolonged hazard extension] (E) if an imminent threat to life or physical safety persists beyond one hundred forty-four (144) hours (two full renewal cycles), the designated federal official may petition the NCC for an extended restriction; the NCC shall rule within forty-eight (48) hours; extensions under this clause may not exceed thirty (30) days each and are renewable only upon fresh NCC approval; the Census Bureau shall conduct alternative enumeration under subsection (c) in any area subject to an NCC-approved extended restriction.

[v1] (c) **Alternative Enumeration Authority.**

- [v1] (1) if a Region's census response rate falls below eighty percent (80%) of the national average response rate, the Census Bureau may employ administrative records, statistical sampling, and other scientifically validated methods to complete enumeration;
- [v1] (2) alternative enumeration conducted under this subsection is valid for all constitutional purposes, including boundary revision under Article I, Section 4 and apportionment;
- [v1] (3) a Region in which census obstruction has been found under subsection (e) may not challenge the methodology of alternative enumeration employed in response to that obstruction; [v3: challenge bar tied to final determination] this prohibition takes effect upon final determination — either expiration of the sixty-day NCC appeal period without appeal, or NCC affirmance of the obstruction finding under subsection (e)(6);
- [v1] (4) the Director of the Census Bureau shall select alternative enumeration methods that maximize accuracy; the Director may not deliberately employ inferior methods to disadvantage any Region.

[v1] (d) **Enumeration Timeline Integrity.**

- [v1] (1) the constitutional census timeline shall not be extended due to Regional or State non-cooperation; boundary revisions under Article I, Section 4 proceed on schedule using the best data available at the constitutionally required deadline;
- [v1] (2) Regions that achieve census response rates at or above ninety percent (90%) of the national average receive a presumption of data accuracy in any boundary dispute under Article I, Section 4;
- [v1] (3) Regions with census response rates below eighty percent (80%) of the national average bear the burden of proving the accuracy of any population claims that diverge from Census Bureau estimates.

[v1] (e) **Obstruction Finding and Enforcement.**

- [v1] (1) the Allocation Review Board shall investigate potential census obstruction upon:
    - [v1] (A) referral by the Director of the Census Bureau;
    - [v1] (B) petition by any affected Tribal Nation, State, or Region;
    - [v1] (C) its own initiative when a Region's census response rate falls below seventy percent (70%) of the national average [v3: checkpoint date for auto-investigation] as measured at a checkpoint date set by the Director of the Census Bureau, which shall be no earlier than sixty (60) days before the Director's scheduled certification of completion;
- [v2: cooperation-dependent presumption + 60-day extension for non-cooperation] (2) the ARB shall complete its investigation and issue a written determination within ninety (90) days of initiation; if the investigated government fully cooperated with the investigation and the ARB fails to issue a determination within this period, a rebuttable presumption arises that no obstruction occurred; if the investigated government failed to produce records, respond to inquiries, or otherwise cooperate with the investigation, the ARB may extend the investigation by up to sixty (60) additional days, and the government's non-cooperation creates a rebuttable presumption that obstruction occurred; [v3: auto-trigger on extended deadline failure] if the ARB fails to issue a determination within the extended period, subsection (e)(4)(A) and (B) apply automatically — boundary revision proceeds using Census Bureau data and the investigated government bears the burden of proving any data adjustment would be inappropriate;
- [v1] (3) the standard of proof for an obstruction finding is preponderance of the evidence; the ARB may draw adverse inferences from a Region's failure to produce records or cooperate with investigation;
- [v1] (4) upon a finding of census obstruction:
    - [v1] (A) boundary revision under Article I, Section 4 shall proceed using the best available data as determined by the Census Bureau;
    - [v1] (B) the obstructing government bears the burden of proving that any data adjustment would be inappropriate;
    - [v2: corrected State-level review body] (C) the obstruction finding triggers automatic review of the obstructing government's emergency declaration authority by the Regional Assembly for Regional-level obstruction, or by the State legislature for State-level obstruction;
    - [v2: knowledge requirement + referral standard] (D) officials who knowingly directed or authorized census obstruction may be referred by the ARB or Attorney General for federal criminal prosecution; the ARB shall refer cases involving willful obstruction to the Attorney General for consideration;
- [v1] (5) a pattern of obstruction — defined as two or more obstruction findings within twenty (20) years — results in mandatory suspension of the obstructing government's authority to restrict census operations for the next two census cycles; during suspension, the Census Bureau operates within the jurisdiction without requiring governmental facilitation;
- [v1] (6) obstruction findings may be appealed to the NCC within sixty (60) days; NCC review is limited to whether the ARB applied the correct legal standard and whether the finding is supported by substantial evidence; the NCC may not substitute its judgment for the ARB's factual determinations.

[v1] (f) **Census Bureau Accountability.**

- [v1] (1) the Census Bureau shall apply uniform enumeration standards and methods across all Regions; variation in methodology is permitted only when justified by genuine geographic, demographic, or logistical differences, not by political considerations;
- [v1] (2) the Government Accountability Office shall audit Census Bureau operations in each decennial census and report to Congress on methodological uniformity, accuracy, and any evidence of political bias;
- [v1] (3) census workers and Census Bureau employees shall have whistleblower protections; no federal, Regional, or State official may retaliate against a census worker who reports obstruction, political interference, or methodological irregularities;
- [v1] (4) any Region may petition the ARB if it believes Census Bureau methods are being applied non-uniformly; the ARB shall investigate and, if it finds non-uniform application, may order corrective measures.

[v1] (g) **Coordination with Other Provisions.**

- [v1] (1) this Section supplements but does not replace the anti-ratcheting provisions of Section 2(e)-(h); an emergency declaration that constitutes census obstruction under this Section is independently reviewable regardless of whether it also violates Section 2;
- [v1] (2) federal emergencies declared by the President are subject to census immunity under subsection (b)(1); Section 3(a) (federal emergencies supersede conflicting Regional emergencies) does not authorize the President to supersede census operations;
- [v1] (3) the Boundary Review Commission established under Article I, Section 4 may request ARB investigation of census obstruction as part of any boundary revision proceeding;
- [v1] (4) Tribal Nations conducting their own census or enumeration pursuant to federal law are not subject to this Section; this Section governs only governmental obstruction of federal census operations;
- [v3: census schedule advance publication] (5) the Director of the Census Bureau shall publish the decennial census operational schedule, including all key dates, at least two (2) years before census day; any deviation of more than sixty (60) days from the published schedule requires written justification to Congress, published in the Federal Register, and is subject to GAO review under subsection (f)(2).

### Section 6. Census Immunity and Enumeration Protection

*Resolves Gap 126 — "Census Emergency" and Boundary Sabotage. Establishes constitutional immunity for federal census operations from emergency power abuse, authorizes alternative enumeration when obstruction occurs, creates an enforcement framework with ARB investigation and burden-shifting, and protects the decennial boundary revision timeline from data manipulation.*

(a) **Definitions.** For purposes of this Section:

- (1) "Census period" means the constitutionally mandated decennial enumeration period, beginning on the date designated by the Director of the Census Bureau and continuing until the Director certifies completion, plus any extension authorized by Congress;
- (2) "Census operations" means all activities conducted by or on behalf of the Census Bureau for purposes of enumeration, including door-to-door canvassing, mail collection, electronic data gathering, administrative records analysis, statistical estimation, and quality verification;
- (3) "Census obstruction" means any act or omission by a governmental entity — federal, Regional, or State — that delays, prevents, undermines, or materially impedes census operations, including but not limited to:
    - (A) emergency declarations that restrict census worker movement, access, or operations;
    - (B) denial or unreasonable delay of access to public facilities, records, or infrastructure needed for enumeration;
    - (C) data system shutdowns, transmission failures, or interoperability refusals not attributable to natural causes or genuine technical failure;
    - (D) threats, intimidation, or harassment of census personnel by governmental agents or with governmental acquiescence;
    - (E) imposition of licensing, credentialing, background check, or security requirements on census workers beyond those imposed by the Census Bureau itself;
- (4) "Census response rate" means the percentage of housing units within a Region from which the Census Bureau has obtained a completed enumeration response — whether by self-response, field enumeration, or administrative records verification — as certified by the Director of the Census Bureau; the Director shall publish the methodology for calculating response rates no later than one (1) year before each decennial census.

(b) **Census Immunity from Emergency Powers.**

- (1) No emergency declaration — federal, Regional, or State — may be used to delay, obstruct, or modify federal census operations within the declaring jurisdiction;
- (2) census workers shall have constitutional immunity from emergency restrictions imposed by any level of government during the census period; no governmental entity may detain, exclude, redirect, or impose conditions upon census workers engaged in lawful enumeration activities;
- (3) the Census Bureau may conduct enumeration using any lawful method notwithstanding any emergency order, declaration, or restriction;
- (4) governmental data systems that serve census operations shall remain operational during the census period; Regional and State governments shall maintain data transmission capacity to the Census Bureau and shall designate backup transmission methods in advance of each census period; a genuine cyber incident affecting census data systems — verified by a Census Bureau information security officer — shall not constitute census obstruction under subsection (a)(3)(C) provided the affected government activates designated backup transmission methods within twenty-four (24) hours; failure to activate backup methods within this period creates a rebuttable presumption of census obstruction;
- (5) the immunity established by this subsection applies regardless of whether the underlying emergency declaration is legitimate; a genuine emergency does not authorize census obstruction as an incidental consequence;
- (6) notwithstanding the foregoing, a federal official designated by the Director of the Census Bureau may temporarily restrict census operations within a specific geographic area when there is an imminent threat to the life or physical safety of census workers that cannot be mitigated by security measures:
    - (A) the restriction shall be limited to the specific area of imminent threat and shall not exceed seventy-two (72) hours; a single renewal of seventy-two (72) hours may be authorized if the threat persists;
    - (B) the Census Bureau shall be notified immediately and shall adjust enumeration schedules to compensate for any lost time;
    - (C) only federal officials may invoke this provision; Regional, State, and local officials may request federal invocation but may not independently restrict census operations on safety grounds;
    - (D) the Director shall conduct a post-action review within thirty (30) days; if the restriction was not justified by a genuine imminent threat, the restricting official's action constitutes census obstruction under subsection (a)(3);
    - (E) if an imminent threat to life or physical safety persists beyond one hundred forty-four (144) hours (two full renewal cycles), the designated federal official may petition the NCC for an extended restriction; the NCC shall rule within forty-eight (48) hours; extensions under this clause may not exceed thirty (30) days each and are renewable only upon fresh NCC approval; the Census Bureau shall conduct alternative enumeration under subsection (c) in any area subject to an NCC-approved extended restriction.

(c) **Alternative Enumeration Authority.**

- (1) if a Region's census response rate falls below eighty percent (80%) of the national average response rate, the Census Bureau may employ administrative records, statistical sampling, and other scientifically validated methods to complete enumeration;
- (2) alternative enumeration conducted under this subsection is valid for all constitutional purposes, including boundary revision under Article I, Section 4 and apportionment;
- (3) a Region in which census obstruction has been found under subsection (e) may not challenge the methodology of alternative enumeration employed in response to that obstruction; this prohibition takes effect upon final determination — either expiration of the sixty-day NCC appeal period without appeal, or NCC affirmance of the obstruction finding under subsection (e)(6);
- (4) the Director of the Census Bureau shall select alternative enumeration methods that maximize accuracy; the Director may not deliberately employ inferior methods to disadvantage any Region.

(d) **Enumeration Timeline Integrity.**

- (1) the constitutional census timeline shall not be extended due to Regional or State non-cooperation; boundary revisions under Article I, Section 4 proceed on schedule using the best data available at the constitutionally required deadline;
- (2) Regions that achieve census response rates at or above ninety percent (90%) of the national average receive a presumption of data accuracy in any boundary dispute under Article I, Section 4;
- (3) Regions with census response rates below eighty percent (80%) of the national average bear the burden of proving the accuracy of any population claims that diverge from Census Bureau estimates.

(e) **Obstruction Finding and Enforcement.**

- (1) the Allocation Review Board shall investigate potential census obstruction upon:
    - (A) referral by the Director of the Census Bureau;
    - (B) petition by any affected Tribal Nation, State, or Region;
    - (C) its own initiative when a Region's census response rate falls below seventy percent (70%) of the national average as measured at a checkpoint date set by the Director of the Census Bureau, which shall be no earlier than sixty (60) days before the Director's scheduled certification of completion;
- (2) the ARB shall complete its investigation and issue a written determination within ninety (90) days of initiation; if the investigated government fully cooperated with the investigation and the ARB fails to issue a determination within this period, a rebuttable presumption arises that no obstruction occurred; if the investigated government failed to produce records, respond to inquiries, or otherwise cooperate with the investigation, the ARB may extend the investigation by up to sixty (60) additional days, and the government's non-cooperation creates a rebuttable presumption that obstruction occurred; if the ARB fails to issue a determination within the extended period, subsection (e)(4)(A) and (B) apply automatically — boundary revision proceeds using Census Bureau data and the investigated government bears the burden of proving any data adjustment would be inappropriate;
- (3) the standard of proof for an obstruction finding is preponderance of the evidence; the ARB may draw adverse inferences from a Region's failure to produce records or cooperate with investigation;
- (4) upon a finding of census obstruction:
    - (A) boundary revision under Article I, Section 4 shall proceed using the best available data as determined by the Census Bureau;
    - (B) the obstructing government bears the burden of proving that any data adjustment would be inappropriate;
    - (C) the obstruction finding triggers automatic review of the obstructing government's emergency declaration authority by the Regional Assembly for Regional-level obstruction, or by the State legislature for State-level obstruction;
    - (D) officials who knowingly directed or authorized census obstruction may be referred by the ARB or Attorney General for federal criminal prosecution; the ARB shall refer cases involving willful obstruction to the Attorney General for consideration;
- (5) a pattern of obstruction — defined as two or more obstruction findings within twenty (20) years — results in mandatory suspension of the obstructing government's authority to restrict census operations for the next two census cycles; during suspension, the Census Bureau operates within the jurisdiction without requiring governmental facilitation;
- (6) obstruction findings may be appealed to the NCC within sixty (60) days; NCC review is limited to whether the ARB applied the correct legal standard and whether the finding is supported by substantial evidence; the NCC may not substitute its judgment for the ARB's factual determinations.

(f) **Census Bureau Accountability.**

- (1) the Census Bureau shall apply uniform enumeration standards and methods across all Regions; variation in methodology is permitted only when justified by genuine geographic, demographic, or logistical differences, not by political considerations;
- (2) the Government Accountability Office shall audit Census Bureau operations in each decennial census and report to Congress on methodological uniformity, accuracy, and any evidence of political bias;
- (3) census workers and Census Bureau employees shall have whistleblower protections; no federal, Regional, or State official may retaliate against a census worker who reports obstruction, political interference, or methodological irregularities;
- (4) any Region may petition the ARB if it believes Census Bureau methods are being applied non-uniformly; the ARB shall investigate and, if it finds non-uniform application, may order corrective measures.

(g) **Coordination with Other Provisions.**

- (1) this Section supplements but does not replace the anti-ratcheting provisions of Section 2(e)-(h); an emergency declaration that constitutes census obstruction under this Section is independently reviewable regardless of whether it also violates Section 2;
- (2) federal emergencies declared by the President are subject to census immunity under subsection (b)(1); Section 3(a) (federal emergencies supersede conflicting Regional emergencies) does not authorize the President to supersede census operations;
- (3) the Boundary Review Commission established under Article I, Section 4 may request ARB investigation of census obstruction as part of any boundary revision proceeding;
- (4) Tribal Nations conducting their own census or enumeration pursuant to federal law are not subject to this Section; this Section governs only governmental obstruction of federal census operations;
- (5) the Director of the Census Bureau shall publish the decennial census operational schedule, including all key dates, at least two (2) years before census day; any deviation of more than sixty (60) days from the published schedule requires written justification to Congress, published in the Federal Register, and is subject to GAO review under subsection (f)(2).

## Design Decisions

| # | Decision | Rationale |
|---|----------|-----------|
| DD-1 | Placed as Art XVII-RF §6 instead of proposal's Art XVIII-RF §1(g)-(j) | Consistent with existing pattern: §4 (Gap 206), §5 (Gap 215) are self-contained sections. Adding subsections to §1 (declaration authority) would mix conceptually distinct topics. Art XVIII-RF numbering doesn't match actual article numbering. |
| DD-2 | Added formal definitions subsection §6(a) | Original proposal lacked definitions. "Census obstruction" definition is critical for enforcement — without it, Regions can argue their actions don't constitute "obstruction." |
| DD-3 | Expanded worker protection to all government levels (federal, Regional, State) | Original proposal's §(g)(ii) only mentioned "Regional emergency restrictions" but §(g)(i) covered all levels. Inconsistency resolved by making worker immunity cover all levels explicitly. |
| DD-4 | Added data transmission mandate §6(b)(4) | Addresses "Administrative Emergency" gaming vector (G3). Original proposal's "any lawful method" provision doesn't prevent data infrastructure sabotage. Backup method requirement creates affirmative obligation. |
| DD-5 | Added §6(b)(5) explicit statement that genuine emergencies don't authorize incidental census obstruction | Closes potential argument that census immunity only applies to pretextual emergencies. A real pandemic during census season could still be used as cover. |
| DD-6 | Changed 80% threshold from absolute to relative (percentage of national average) | Original proposal used absolute 80% threshold. But if national response rate is 70%, requiring 80% penalizes all Regions. Relative threshold accounts for national variations in census methodology and response. |
| DD-7 | Added Census Bureau accountability subsection §6(f) | Addresses weaponization vector (G6). Without this, census immunity could become a tool of federal power against Regions — the reverse of the original problem. |
| DD-8 | Added GAO audit requirement | Independent audit creates accountability for Census Bureau itself. Complements ARB's role (ARB investigates Regional obstruction; GAO audits Census Bureau conduct). |
| DD-9 | Added NCC appeal of ARB obstruction findings §6(e)(6) | Due process for Regions facing obstruction findings. Consistent with NCC review pattern in other sections (e.g., Art XXIII §12(b)(4) for ARB certification review). Review limited to legal standard and substantial evidence — NCC cannot re-litigate facts. |
| DD-10 | Set 90-day ARB investigation timeline with rebuttable presumption §6(e)(2) | Prevents investigation delay from becoming its own form of gaming. Presumption of no obstruction if ARB misses deadline prevents indefinite investigation threat. |
| DD-11 | Set preponderance standard (not clear and convincing) for obstruction findings | Census obstruction is serious but the remedy (burden-shifting, emergency review) is procedural, not punitive. Higher standard would make findings too difficult. Criminal prosecution of officials under §6(e)(4)(D) would use criminal standards in that separate proceeding. |
| DD-12 | Pattern of obstruction: 2+ findings in 20 years → mandatory suspension | Original's "may result" was too discretionary. Twenty-year window spans two census cycles; two findings demonstrate deliberate pattern. Suspension removes authority to restrict census — not suspension of all emergency powers. |
| DD-13 | Added Boundary Review Commission cross-reference §6(g)(3) | Creates explicit link between census protection and boundary revision process. BRC can trigger ARB investigation during boundary proceedings. |
| DD-14 | Added Tribal Nation exemption §6(g)(4) | Tribal Nations conducting their own enumeration under federal law should not be captured by anti-obstruction provisions designed for Regions and States. Consistent with Art XXIII tribal sovereignty protections. |
| DD-15 | [v2] Cooperation-dependent ARB deadline presumption + 60-day extension (resolves S1) | v1's no-obstruction presumption at 90-day deadline created perverse incentive: Region stonewalls investigation to run out the clock. Flipping the presumption based on cooperation addresses this while preserving the original rationale (protecting Regions from indefinite investigation threat). Cooperative Regions still get the no-obstruction presumption. Non-cooperative Regions face presumption of obstruction + 60 extra days for ARB to complete. |
| DD-16 | [v2] Federal-only safety restriction with post-action review §6(b)(6) (resolves S2 + Q3/G9) | Absolute census worker immunity could force unsafe choices during genuine imminent threats (active violence, building collapse, hazmat release). Safety carve-out is strictly federal-controlled: only Census Bureau-designated federal officials, 72-hour limit with single renewal, immediate Census Bureau notification, and post-action review with obstruction finding if unjustified. Regional/State officials can request but cannot invoke — preventing pretext abuse while allowing genuine safety protection. |
| DD-17 | [v2] Defined "census response rate" §6(a)(4) with methodology delegation (resolves S3 + Q1) | v1 used "census response rate" in §6(c)(1), §6(d)(2)-(3), §6(e)(1)(C) without definition. Ambiguity over denominator (housing units vs. persons), counting method (self-response vs. total enumerated), and timing created litigation risk. Definition establishes constitutional meaning (completed responses per housing unit) while delegating methodology to Census Bureau Director, who must publish one year before each census. Confirms relative threshold (percentage of national average) as the correct approach — the definition grounds it in measurable terms. |
| DD-18 | [v2] Corrected State-level review body to State legislature (resolves S4) | v1 assigned State-level obstruction review to the Regional legislature, which has no authority over State emergency powers. The Regional Assembly reviews Regional-level obstruction; the State legislature reviews State-level obstruction. Each body reviews its own government's emergency authority. Federal-level check is provided by the ARB finding and NCC appeal regardless. |
| DD-19 | [v2] Added knowledge requirement + referral standard for criminal prosecution (resolves S5) | v1's "are subject to removal proceedings and federal criminal prosecution" implied automatic prosecution, which is both impractical and raises due process concerns. "Knowingly directed or authorized" adds mens rea requirement. "May be referred" + "ARB shall refer cases involving willful obstruction" creates a mandatory referral for willful conduct while preserving prosecutorial discretion. Criminal proceedings use criminal evidentiary standards. |
| DD-20 | [v3] Auto-trigger of boundary consequences on extended deadline failure (resolves R2-S1) | v2's cooperation-dependent presumption solved R1's incentive inversion but left a gap: if the ARB fails to act even after the 60-day extension, the non-cooperative government faces a presumption of obstruction but no automatic consequence. Adding auto-trigger of §6(e)(4)(A)-(B) — boundary revision proceeds using Census Bureau data, investigated government bears burden of proof — ensures the system cannot stall indefinitely. The 150-day total (90 + 60) gives the ARB adequate time; failure beyond that triggers the same consequences as an obstruction finding. |
| DD-21 | [v3] Challenge bar tied to final determination (resolves R2-S2) | v2's §6(c)(3) barred challenge upon ARB obstruction finding, but the finding is appealable to the NCC under §6(e)(6). Barring challenges before appeal is resolved creates due process tension. Tying the bar to "final determination" — NCC appeal period expired without appeal, or NCC affirmance — ensures the Region has exhausted its appeal before losing the right to challenge alternative enumeration methodology. Prevents premature preclusion while maintaining the bar's purpose. |
| DD-22 | [v3] NCC-approved extension for prolonged hazards (resolves R2-S3) | v2's 72-hour limit with single renewal (144 hours total) was adequate for acute events (active shooter, building collapse) but insufficient for prolonged hazards (wildfire, flooding, industrial contamination). Adding §6(b)(6)(E) creates a NCC-approved extension path: petition after 144 hours, NCC rules within 48 hours, 30-day extensions renewable with fresh NCC approval. Census Bureau conducts alternative enumeration during any extended restriction. NCC oversight prevents pretext abuse while accommodating genuine prolonged threats. |
| DD-23 | [v3] Cyber incident alignment with backup activation deadline (resolves R2-S4) | v2's §6(b)(4) "remain operational" mandate could classify genuine cyber incidents (ransomware, infrastructure attack) as census obstruction, penalizing victim governments. Adding a verified cyber incident exception — Census Bureau security officer verification + 24-hour backup activation deadline — distinguishes genuine incidents from deliberate sabotage. The backup activation deadline prevents manufactured "incidents" from becoming indefinite excuses. Failure to activate backup within 24 hours creates rebuttable presumption of obstruction, maintaining deterrence. |
| DD-24 | [v3] Director-set checkpoint date for auto-investigation trigger (resolves R2-S5) | v2's auto-investigation at 70% of national average applied throughout the census period, but early-period low rates are normal (canvassing starts in some areas before others). Setting the trigger at a Director-determined checkpoint date — no earlier than 60 days before scheduled certification — prevents premature investigations while ensuring late-period shortfalls are caught. The Director has operational knowledge to set the optimal checkpoint; the 60-day floor ensures the check happens close enough to completion to be meaningful. |
| DD-25 | [v3] Census schedule advance publication (resolves Q4/G10) | G10 identified that federal government could time census operations to coincide with known Regional emergencies or disaster seasons. Requiring 2-year advance publication of the operational schedule constrains strategic timing. The 60-day deviation threshold with Congressional notification + Federal Register publication + GAO review creates accountability for schedule manipulation without preventing genuine operational adjustments. Pairs with §6(f)(2) GAO audit to create comprehensive anti-manipulation coverage on the federal side. |
