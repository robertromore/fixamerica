# Transportation Under Regional Federalism

## Status: Outline Only

This document outlines how transportation policy would function under Regional Federalism. Full analysis to be developed.

---

## The Challenge

Transportation governance is poorly matched to actual travel patterns:

- Metropolitan areas cross state lines
- Interstate highway system federally funded but state-managed
- No mechanism for regional transit coordination
- Rail development blocked by fragmented authority
- Aviation federally controlled but airports locally run
- Climate goals require transportation transformation

## Regional Federalism Approach

### Federal Authority (retained)

- Interstate highway system (core network)
- Aviation (FAA)
- Rail safety (FRA)
- Maritime (Coast Guard)
- Vehicle safety standards (NHTSA)
- National transportation planning

### Regional Authority (new)

- Regional transit systems
- Regional highway planning
- Regional rail development
- Regional freight coordination
- Vehicle emissions standards (above federal)
- Regional transportation pricing

### State/Local Authority

- State highways
- Local transit
- Local roads
- Parking policy
