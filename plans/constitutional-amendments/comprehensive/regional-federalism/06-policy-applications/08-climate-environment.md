# Climate and Environment Under Regional Federalism

## Status: Outline Only

This document outlines how climate and environmental policy would function under Regional Federalism. Full analysis to be developed.

---

## The Challenge

Environmental policy under current federalism is dysfunctional:

- Federal policy swings dramatically with administrations
- States can't effectively address interstate pollution
- Climate policy paralyzed at federal level
- Race to bottom in environmental standards
- Transboundary issues (water, air) poorly addressed
- No mechanism for regional coordination on shared resources

## Regional Federalism Approach

### Federal Authority (retained)

- National minimum standards (floors)
- International agreements and treaties
- Interstate pollution disputes
- Endangered species (national concern)
- Federal lands management
- National parks and monuments

### Regional Authority (primary)

- Standards above federal floors
- Regional emissions targets
- Cap-and-trade or carbon pricing within region
- Regional renewable mandates
- Regional water management
- Air quality standards above federal floor
- Regional land use coordination

### Constitutional Provisions (already in document)

- Water rights framework (Article I, Section 7)
- Environmental border protection (Article I, Section 10)
- Transboundary pollution prohibition

## Climate-Specific Issues

### Regional Variation Risks

- Carbon leakage to low-regulation regions
- "Pollution havens" within United States
- Race to bottom on enforcement

### Regional Variation Benefits

- Laboratories of democracy for climate policy
- Regions can move faster than federal government
- Tailored approaches to regional conditions

### Design Questions

- Should there be federal climate floor?
- How aggressive can it be given regional variation?
- What mechanism prevents regions from undermining each other?
