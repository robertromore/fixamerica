# Changelog: 2026-02-19

## 2026-02-19: Gap 138 -- The "Double-Taxation" Border Blockade Resolved (Article X, Section 22)

One fiscal gap resolved through new Section 22 in Article X in `02-design/constitution/04-fiscal-system.md`, with conforming amendments to `01-regional-structure.md` (Art I §10, §8(t)(3)), and three amendments within `04-fiscal-system.md` (§14(b), §5(c)(7), §4(i)(4)). Drafted via multi-LLM protocol: coordinator performed overlap analysis finding ~35-40% existing coverage (revised upward after discovering Art I §10(hh)-(ii) partial framework), generated briefing with 10 design questions and 7 gaming vectors. Two external LLMs reviewed independently across 2 rounds. Round 1 surfaced 3 HIGH findings (phantom institution, missed overlap, under-specified enforcement). Round 2 surfaced 3 HIGH findings (cross-reference break, scope threshold, local levy gap) and 5 MEDIUM findings. All findings verified against source files and resolved with targeted fixes. Human resolved all 10 design decisions. Convergence in 2 rounds.

### Problem

**Gap 138 (The "Double-Taxation" Border Blockade):** Art X §3(q) establishes both residence-based taxation (183-day rule) and situs-based taxation (income earned within a Region) without any credit mechanism. A worker living in Region A and working in Region B could face combined 80-100% marginal tax rates. The existing Art I §10(hh)-(ii) provided a partial framework for remote workers only, using dollar-for-dollar credits and rate caps, but: (1) it was limited to "substantial remote work" and did not cover commuters, multi-site workers, or pass-through entities; (2) it referenced a phantom "Inter-Regional Fiscal Court" that was never constitutionally established; (3) enforcement was unspecified.

### Placement Correction

Original proposal targeted Article X-RF, Section 7(a)-(c). Corrected to Article X, Section 22 for two reasons:

1. Article X-RF does not exist -- Article X (Fiscal Architecture) is an RF Core article, not a standalone + RF supplement split
2. Section 7 is already occupied by Financial Conservatorship

### Key Existing Coverage (~35-40% overlap)

The following provisions already existed and were consolidated or cross-referenced:

- **Art I §10(hh) Economic Nexus for Services**: Dollar-for-dollar credit and rate cap for remote workers (REPEALED and migrated to §22)
- **Art I §10(ii) Apportionment for Multi-Regional Activity**: Uniform apportionment rules assigned to phantom court (REPEALED and migrated to §22 with IFC assignment)
- **Art X §3(q) Residence and Domicile Standards**: Creates the dual taxation authority -- supplements, not replaced
- **Art X §5(d) IFC Binding Determinations**: IFC's existing binding authority extended to tax disputes
- **Art X §3(t)-(w) Predatory Subsidy Enforcement**: Equalization adjustment enforcement pattern reused

### Resolution

New Section 22 added to Article X with 14 subsections (a)-(n) across 6 thematic blocks:

#### Source-First Taxation and Mandatory Credit (§22(a)-(d))

- **§22(a) Primary Taxing Jurisdiction**: Source Region (physical presence) has primary taxing right
- **§22(b) Residence Region Top-Up**: Dollar-for-dollar credit; Residence Region collects only the rate differential
- **§22(c) Aggregate Rate Cap**: Combined rate cannot exceed higher of two Regions' rates
- **§22(d) Provisional Credit**: Constitutional mandate for credit at withholding; anti-manipulation clause against inflated withholding rates

#### Scope (§22(e)-(f))

- **§22(e) Functional Definition of Covered Tax**: Any compulsory levy on earned income regardless of label or level of government (Region, State, subdivision); explicit earned-income definition
- **§22(f) Covered Workers**: Commuters, remote workers, multi-site employees, pass-through entity owners

#### Apportionment (§22(g))

- **§22(g) Multi-Regional Income Apportionment**: 2-Region case handled by §22(a)-(c); IFC promulgates uniform rules for 3+ Region workers

#### Dispute Resolution and Enforcement (§22(h)-(k))

- **§22(h) IFC Technical Arbitration**: Exclusive for apportionment, tax classification, credit calculation, and jurisdiction determination
- **§22(i) ARB Enforcement and Review**: Legal challenges and enforcement with clarified 60-day compliance + 30-day cure sequential timeline
- **§22(j) Graduated Enforcement**: Stage 1 (30-day cure) → Stage 2 (150% equalization adjustment up to 3 years, §3-A floor preserved, immediate Stage 3 escalation for floor Regions) → Stage 3 (ARB direct administration, personal liability, worker reimbursement)
- **§22(k) Anti-Circumvention**: Label look-through, timing manipulation prohibition, mandatory non-discretionary credits

#### Bilateral Agreements (§22(l))

- **§22(l) Bilateral Tax Treaties**: Optional alternative allocation; IFC registration; rate cap; worker rights preserved

#### Savings Clauses (§22(m)-(n))

- **§22(m) Non-Coercion Savings**: Enforcement is not federal coercion; self-executing constitutional rights
- **§22(n) Interaction with Existing Provisions**: Supplements §3(q) and §11-A; preserves §10(ff)-(gg) residency determination and §3(r) corporate apportionment

### Multi-LLM Protocol Notes

- **Round 1 critical discovery**: Art I §10(hh)-(ii) already contained a partial anti-double-tax framework that the initial briefing missed. This is now the second gap (after Gap 57) where overlap analysis was materially revised upward after external review. Lesson: always grep for related subsection letters in Art I §10 and Art I §8 before concluding overlap analysis.
- **Phantom institution**: "Inter-Regional Fiscal Court" was referenced in §10(ii) and §14(b) but never established. This is an independent bug fix piggybacking on the gap resolution.
- **Cross-reference fragility**: Renumbering subsections (§10(jj)→§10(ii)) broke a cross-reference at §8(t)(3). Lesson: always run a full cross-reference search before any renumbering operation.
