# Model Service Level Agreement (SLA)

This document provides model language for a Service Level Agreement (SLA) between
an Interregional Shared Services Authority (the "Authority") and a Regional
Agency (the "Client Agency"). This SLA is consistent with the
[Interregional Shared Services Authority Act](interregional-shared-services-authority-act.md).

---

## Purpose

This SLA defines the specific services to be provided by the Authority to the
Client Agency, the performance standards for those services, cost and billing
procedures, and the responsibilities of both parties.

---

## Model Statutory Text

### SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENT

This Service Level Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into on this [Day] day
of [Month], [Year] (the "Effective Date"), by and between:

**[Interregional Shared Services Authority Name]** (the "Authority"), an
interregional shared services authority formed by compact among the Regions of
[Region 1], [Region 2], and [Region 3], with its principal office at
[Authority Address];

AND

**[Client Agency Name]** (the "Client Agency"), an agency of the Region of
[Client Agency's Region], with its principal office at [Client Agency Address].

---

## Article I: Definitions

**1.1. Authority.** The Interregional Shared Services Authority.
**1.2. Client Agency.** The Regional agency procuring services under this Agreement.
**1.3. Service(s).** The specific administrative function(s) provided by the Authority
     to the Client Agency as detailed in Appendix A.
**1.4. Service Level(s).** The measurable performance standard(s) for each Service,
     as detailed in Appendix B.
**1.5. Fee Schedule.** The agreed-upon charges for Services, as detailed in Appendix C.
**1.6. Compact.** The interregional compact establishing the Authority.

---

## Article II: Scope of Services

**2.1. Services Provided.** The Authority shall provide the Services to the
     Client Agency as described in **Appendix A: Scope of Services**.

**2.2. Service Levels.** The Authority shall perform the Services in accordance
     with the Service Levels specified in **Appendix B: Service Levels**.

**2.3. Exclusions.** Services not explicitly listed in Appendix A are excluded
     from this Agreement.

---

## Article III: Responsibilities of the Parties

**3.1. Authority Responsibilities.** The Authority shall:
    (a) Provide the Services in a professional and timely manner, meeting the
        specified Service Levels.
    (b) Maintain the confidentiality and security of Client Agency data as
        per Article V.
    (c) Provide regular reports on Service performance as per Article VI.
    (d) Adhere to all applicable laws and regulations, including the
        [Interregional Shared Services Authority Act].

**3.2. Client Agency Responsibilities.** The Client Agency shall:
    (a) Provide timely and accurate information, data, and access necessary
        for the Authority to perform the Services.
    (b) Designate a primary point of contact for communication with the Authority.
    (c) Pay all fees for Services in accordance with Article IV.
    (d) Adhere to all applicable laws and regulations.

---

## Article IV: Fees and Billing

**4.1. Fee Schedule.** The Client Agency shall pay the Authority for Services
     rendered in accordance with the **Appendix C: Fee Schedule**.

**4.2. Billing Cycle.** The Authority shall invoice the Client Agency [monthly/quarterly]
     for Services provided in the preceding period.

**4.3. Payment Terms.** Invoices are due and payable within [30] days of receipt.
     Late payments may incur interest at a rate of [1]% per month.

**4.4. Fee Adjustments.** Fees may be adjusted annually by the Authority's
     Governing Board, subject to ratification by participating Regions as per
     Section 6(b) of the [Interregional Shared Services Authority Act].

---

## Article V: Data Governance and Security

**5.1. Data Ownership.** The Client Agency retains full ownership of all its
     data accessed or processed by the Authority.

**5.2. Data Access and Use.** The Authority shall access and use Client Agency
     data solely for the purpose of delivering the Services specified in this
     Agreement.

**5.3. Security Standards.** The Authority shall implement and maintain
     security measures, including encryption, access controls, and audit trails,
     to protect Client Agency data from unauthorized access, disclosure,
     alteration, or destruction, consistent with federal and Regional standards.

**5.4. Breach Notification.** In the event of a data breach affecting Client
     Agency data, the Authority shall notify the Client Agency within [24] hours
     of discovery and cooperate fully in investigation and remediation.

**5.5. Data Return/Destruction.** Upon termination of this Agreement or withdrawal
     from the Authority, the Authority shall return or securely destroy all
     Client Agency data as mutually agreed upon.

---

## Article VI: Reporting and Review

**6.1. Performance Reports.** The Authority shall provide the Client Agency with
     [monthly/quarterly] reports detailing performance against the Service Levels
     in Appendix B.

**6.2. Annual Review.** The parties shall conduct an annual review of this
     Agreement to assess performance, discuss any issues, and consider
     modifications.

**6.3. Audit Rights.** The Client Agency may, upon reasonable notice, audit the
     Authority's records pertaining to the Services provided under this Agreement
     to verify compliance with its terms.

---

## Article VII: Term and Termination

**7.1. Term.** This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue
     for a period of [e.g., one year], automatically renewing for successive
     [e.g., one-year] terms unless terminated earlier as provided herein.

**7.2. Termination for Convenience.** Either party may terminate this Agreement
     for convenience by providing [90] days written notice to the other party.

**7.3. Termination for Cause.** Either party may terminate this Agreement for
     material breach if the breach is not cured within [30] days of written
     notice.

**7.4. Effect of Termination.** Upon termination:
    (a) The Authority shall cease providing Services and submit a final invoice.
    (b) The Client Agency shall pay all outstanding fees for Services rendered.
    (c) Data return/destruction procedures in Section 5.5 shall be initiated.

---

## Article VIII: Dispute Resolution

**8.1. Informal Resolution.** The parties shall first attempt to resolve any
     disputes arising under this Agreement through good-faith negotiation.

**8.2. Escalation.** If informal resolution fails, disputes shall be escalated
     to senior management of both parties.

**8.3. Authority Compact Dispute Process.** If internal escalation fails, disputes
     shall be resolved through the dispute resolution process established in the
     Authority's compact, consistent with Section 13 of the
     [Interregional Shared Services Authority Act].

---

## Article IX: General Provisions

**9.1. Entire Agreement.** This Agreement, including its Appendices, constitutes
     the entire agreement between the parties.

**9.2. Amendments.** Any amendment to this Agreement must be in writing and
     signed by authorized representatives of both parties.

**9.3. Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the
     Region of [Client Agency's Region] and the [Interregional Shared Services
     Authority Act].

**9.4. Severability.** If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid
     or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and
     effect.

**9.5. Force Majeure.** Neither party shall be liable for delays or failures in
     performance due to causes beyond its reasonable control.

---

## Appendix A: Scope of Services

*(Example: Human Resources and Payroll Services)*

**A.1. Payroll Processing:**
    - Bi-weekly payroll processing for [Number] employees.
    - Direct deposit and check issuance.
    - Tax withholding and reporting (federal, Regional, local).
    - Garnishments and deductions management.
**A.2. Benefits Administration:**
    - Enrollment and changes for health, dental, vision, and retirement plans.
    - COBRA administration.
    - Leave management (FMLA, sick leave, vacation).
**A.3. HR Information System (HRIS) Access:**
    - Provision of secure access to the Authority's HRIS for Client Agency staff.
    - Employee self-service portal for pay stubs, benefits, and personal information.
**A.4. Compliance Support:**
    - Maintenance of federal and Regional HR compliance records.
    - Annual EEO-1 and VETS-4212 reporting.

---

## Appendix B: Service Levels

*(Example: Human Resources and Payroll Services)*

**B.1. Payroll Accuracy:**
    - Target: 99.9% accuracy rate for gross pay calculations.
    - Critical: 99.5% accuracy rate.
**B.2. Payroll Timeliness:**
    - Target: Bi-weekly payroll processed and funds disbursed by [Day of Week], [Time].
    - Critical: Payroll processed within 24 hours of target.
**B.3. HRIS Availability:**
    - Target: 99.5% uptime (excluding scheduled maintenance).
    - Critical: 99.0% uptime.
**B.4. Help Desk Response Time:**
    - Target: Initial response to HR-related inquiries within 4 business hours.
    - Critical: Initial response within 8 business hours.
**B.5. Benefits Enrollment Processing:**
    - Target: New enrollments processed within 3 business days of complete submission.
    - Critical: New enrollments processed within 5 business days.

---

## Appendix C: Fee Schedule

*(Example: Human Resources and Payroll Services)*

**C.1. Base Fee:** $[Amount] per month for core HRIS and payroll processing.
**C.2. Per Employee Fee:** $[Amount] per employee per month.
**C.3. Benefits Administration Fee:** $[Amount] per enrolled employee per month.
**C.4. One-Time Setup Fee:** $[Amount] (waived if Client Agency transitions from another Authority service).
**C.5. Administrative Overhead:** Capped at 12% of annual operating costs, as per Section 6(c) of the [Interregional Shared Services Authority Act].
