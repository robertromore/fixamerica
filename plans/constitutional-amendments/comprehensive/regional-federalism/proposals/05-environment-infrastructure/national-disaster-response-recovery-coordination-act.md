# National Disaster Response and Recovery Coordination Act

This proposal establishes a comprehensive framework for federal-Regional coordination in disaster preparedness, response, and long-term recovery for all natural hazards, ensuring an efficient and equitable system.

---

## Purpose

Establish a National Disaster Response and Recovery Coordination Framework that:

- Ensures federal-Regional coordination in disaster preparedness, response, and long-term recovery.
- Creates a National Disaster Coordination Council to facilitate collaboration and information sharing.
- Mandates a National Mutual Aid Compact for resource sharing between Regions.
- Provides clear and predictable federal funding streams for disaster-related activities.
- Preserves Regional authority while leveraging federal resources and expertise.

---

## Part I: Definitions and Scope

### Section 1. Definitions

For purposes of this Act:

(a) "Disaster" means any natural catastrophe, including earthquake, hurricane, tornado, storm, flood, wildfire, volcanic eruption, landslide, mudslide, drought, or other natural event, which causes damage of sufficient severity to warrant federal assistance.

(b) "Emergency" means any occasion or instance for which federal assistance is needed to supplement Regional and local efforts to save lives and protect property and public health.

(c) "Major disaster" means any natural catastrophe that the President determines causes damage of sufficient severity and magnitude to warrant federal disaster assistance under this Act.

(d) "Region" has the meaning given in the Regional Federal Constitution.

(e) "Council" means the National Resilience and Infrastructure Council established by the National Infrastructure Investment and Coordination Act.

### Section 2. Scope

(a) This Act applies to all natural disasters and emergencies affecting one or more Regions of the United States.

(b) This Act supplements but does not replace the Robert T. Stafford Disaster Relief and Emergency Assistance Act (42 U.S.C. ยง 5121 et seq.), except where explicitly amended herein.

(c) Nothing in this Act shall diminish Regional authority over disaster preparedness and response within Regional boundaries.

## Part II: Disaster Preparedness

### Section 11. National Mutual Aid Compact

(a) There is established the "National Mutual Aid Compact," which all Regions shall participate in as a condition of receiving federal disaster assistance.

(b) The Compact shall:
    - (1) Define clear procedures for requesting and providing assistance between Regions.
    - (2) Establish liability and worker's compensation standards for personnel deployed under the Compact.
    - (3) Set fair and transparent reimbursement rates for personnel, equipment, and services provided.

### Section 12. Regional Preparedness Standards

(a) FEMA shall provide guidance and technical assistance to Regions for developing and maintaining robust disaster preparedness programs.

(b) Guidance shall include, but not be limited to:
    - (1) Hazard mitigation planning and implementation.
    - (2) Emergency communication protocols and interoperability standards.
    - (3) Evacuation and sheltering plans, including special needs populations.
    - (4) Resource inventory management and tracking.
    - (5) Volunteer management and coordination procedures.

---

## Part III: Disaster Response

### Section 21. Federal Disaster Declarations

(a) The President may declare a major disaster or emergency based on a request from a Regional Governor, or on the President's own initiative if a disaster is of such severity and magnitude that effective response is beyond the capabilities of the affected Region(s).

(b) All requests and declarations shall follow the procedures as set forth in the Stafford Act (42 U.S.C. ยง 5121 et seq.) and implementing regulations, as amended by this Act.

### Section 22. Federal Assistance

(a) Upon a disaster declaration, the President may provide a range of federal assistance, including:
    - (1) Individual assistance (e.g., housing, crisis counseling, unemployment assistance).
    - (2) Public assistance (e.g., infrastructure repair, debris removal, emergency protective measures).
    - (3) Hazard mitigation assistance (e.g., flood control, fire prevention, seismic retrofitting).

### Section 23. Federal Coordination

(a) The National Resilience and Infrastructure Council (the "Council"), established by the `National Infrastructure Investment and Coordination Act`, shall coordinate federal, Regional, and local response efforts, emphasizing a "Regional-led, Federal-supported" approach.

(b) The Council shall facilitate resource deployment across jurisdictions, manage logistics and supply chains for critical resources, and provide public information and warnings.

---

## Part IV: Long-Term Recovery

### Section 31. Recovery Plans

(a) FEMA shall work with the affected Region(s) to develop a comprehensive long-term recovery plan within 90 days of a major disaster declaration.

(b) The recovery plan shall:
    - (1) Identify rebuilding priorities for infrastructure and communities.
    - (2) Assess economic recovery strategies and opportunities.
    - (3) Address housing needs, including affordable and resilient housing solutions.
    - (4) Promote community resilience and mitigation against future hazards.

### Section 32. Housing Assistance

(a) FEMA shall coordinate federal housing assistance programs, including:
    - (1) Temporary housing assistance for displaced individuals and families.
    - (2) Rental assistance and housing repair grants.
    - (3) Support for permanent housing solutions and community relocation where necessary.

### Section 33. Economic Recovery

(a) The Economic Development Administration (EDA) and other relevant federal agencies shall provide grants and technical assistance for:
    - (1) Business recovery loans and small business support.
    - (2) Workforce training and re-employment programs.
    - (3) Infrastructure improvements to support economic revitalization.

---

## Part V: Funding

### Section 41. Disaster Relief Fund

(a) There is established the "Disaster Relief Fund" in the Treasury of the United States.

(b) The Fund shall be used for:
    - (1) All federal disaster assistance provided under this Act.
    - (2) Reimbursement of Regions under the National Mutual Aid Compact.
    - (3) Administration of this Act and related federal disaster programs.

### Section 42. Funding Levels

(a) Congress shall appropriate funds sufficient to maintain a balance of not less than $50 billion in the Disaster Relief Fund at the beginning of each fiscal year.

(b) If the fund balance falls below $20 billion, Congress shall provide supplemental appropriations to restore the balance to $50 billion within 30 days.

---

## Part VI: Review and Sunset

### Section 51. Periodic Review

(a) The National Resilience and Infrastructure Council shall review this Act and its implementation every five years.

(b) Reviews shall assess effectiveness and recommend improvements.

### Section 52. Sunset Clause

(a) This Act shall sunset after 20 years unless reauthorized by Congress.

## Hostile Reinterpretation Stress Test

### Attack Vector 1: Federal Overreach

A hostile executive could use the Act to seize control of disaster response, sidelining Regional authorities.

**Countermeasures:**
    -Guaranteed Regional representation on the Coordination Council.
    -Emphasis on a Regional-led, Federal-supported approach in the Act's language.

### Attack Vector 2: Inadequate Funding

A Congress hostile to disaster relief could underfund the Disaster Relief Fund, rendering the Act ineffective.

**Countermeasures:**
    -Mandatory appropriations language to ensure a minimum fund balance.
    -Automatic supplemental appropriations requirement if the fund is depleted.

### Attack Vector 3: Discriminatory Assistance

A politically-motivated administration could direct federal assistance to favored Regions, neglecting others.

**Countermeasures:**
    -Objective criteria for federal disaster declarations.
    -Requirement for equitable access to federal resources for all Regions.
