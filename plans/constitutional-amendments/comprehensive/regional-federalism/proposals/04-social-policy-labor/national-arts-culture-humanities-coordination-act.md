# National Arts, Culture, and Humanities Coordination Act

This proposal establishes a framework for defining federal and Regional roles in supporting arts, culture, and humanities, ensuring the preservation of national cultural assets and the promotion of diverse regional expressions.

---

## Purpose

Establish a framework for National Arts, Culture, and Humanities Coordination that:

- Clarifies the governance and funding for national cultural institutions.
- Defines the role of federal grant-making bodies in supporting arts and humanities across Regions.
- Authorizes and supports Regional arts and humanities councils.
- Establishes a framework for public broadcasting coordination.
- Creates a coordinated process for the protection of cultural property, involving federal, Regional, and Tribal authorities.

---

## Part I: National Cultural Institutions and Federal Funding

### Section 1. National Cultural Institutions

(a) Federal institutions such as the Smithsonian Institution, the Library of Congress, the National Archives and Records Administration, and other federally designated national museums and cultural centers shall remain under exclusive federal governance and funding.

(b) These institutions shall serve as national assets, providing resources and access to all Regions and citizens.

### Section 2. National Endowment for the Arts (NEA) and Humanities (NEH)

(a) The National Endowment for the Arts (NEA) and the National Endowment for the Humanities (NEH) shall continue to operate as independent federal agencies.

(b) **Funding Mechanisms.** The NEA and NEH shall:
    - (1) Provide direct grants to individuals and organizations for projects of national significance or artistic/scholarly merit.
    - (2) Provide block grants to authorized Regional Arts and Humanities Councils (established in Part II) for distribution within their respective Regions, based on population and demonstrated need.

(c) **Equitable Distribution.** The NEA and NEH shall establish principles for equitable distribution of funds across all Regions, considering demographic diversity and cultural richness.

---

## Part II: Regional Arts and Humanities Support

### Section 11. Regional Arts and Humanities Councils

(a) Each Region shall establish a "Regional Arts and Humanities Council" (RAHC) to support and promote arts, culture, and humanities within its boundaries.

(b) RAHCs shall be eligible to receive federal block grants from the NEA and NEH.

(c) RAHCs may partner with federal agencies on projects of mutual interest and serve as a conduit for federal-Regional cultural initiatives.

### Section 12. Inter-Regional Cultural Compacts

(a) Regions are encouraged to form "Inter-Regional Cultural Compacts" under the `Interregional Agency Compact Act` to coordinate cultural initiatives that span Regional boundaries.

(b) Such compacts may include:
    - (1) Joint touring programs for artistic performances or exhibitions.
    - (2) Collaborative cultural heritage preservation projects.
    - (3) Shared educational programs in arts and humanities.

---

## Part III: Public Broadcasting and Cultural Property

### Section 21. Public Broadcasting Coordination

(a) The Corporation for Public Broadcasting (CPB) shall continue to provide federal funding and support to public television and radio stations.

(b) The CPB shall coordinate with Regional public media entities to ensure a robust public media ecosystem that serves both national and local interests.

(c) The CPB shall establish guidelines for equitable distribution of funds to Regional public media, promoting diverse content and local programming.

### Section 22. Cultural Property Protection

(a) The federal government shall retain primary authority for the protection and repatriation of cultural property of national or international significance.

(b) **Coordinated Process.** Federal agencies (e.g., National Park Service, Department of State) shall establish a coordinated process with Regional and Tribal authorities for:
    - (1) Identifying and protecting cultural heritage sites and artifacts.
    - (2) Preventing illicit trafficking of cultural property.
    - (3) Facilitating the repatriation of cultural property to Tribal Nations or foreign countries.

(c) **Tribal Consultation.** Federal and Regional agencies shall conduct government-to-government consultation with Tribal Nations regarding cultural property on or originating from Tribal lands.

---

## Part IV: Oversight and Coordination

### Section 31. National Arts and Culture Advisory Council

(a) There is established a "National Arts and Culture Advisory Council" (the "Council").

(b) **Composition.** The Council shall be composed of:
    - (1) The Chairs of the NEA and NEH (co-Chairs).
    - (2) The Librarian of Congress and the Archivist of the United States.
    - (3) One representative from each Regional Arts and Humanities Council.
    - (4) Representatives from Tribal Nations, appointed by the President.
    - (5) Experts in arts, humanities, and cultural policy, appointed by Congress.

(c) **Functions.** The Council shall:
    - (1) Advise federal agencies on national cultural policy.
    - (2) Facilitate information sharing and best practices among federal, Regional, and Tribal entities.
    - (3) Mediate disputes related to cultural policy or property.
    - (4) Publish an annual "State of the Nation's Culture" report.

## Hostile Reinterpretation Stress Test

### Attack Vector 1: Politicization of Funding

**Attack:** A federal administration attempts to politicize NEA/NEH funding, directing grants based on ideological alignment rather than artistic or scholarly merit.

**Countermeasure:**

- **Independent Agencies (Section 2(a)):** NEA and NEH operate as independent federal agencies, insulated from direct political control.
- **Peer Review Process:** Grant decisions are typically made through a peer review process by experts in the field, not political appointees.
- **Equitable Distribution (Section 2(c)):** Mandates equitable distribution, preventing concentration of funds for political reasons.
- **Council Oversight (Section 31(c)):** The National Arts and Culture Advisory Council can highlight and challenge politicized funding decisions.

**Outcome:** While political pressure is always a risk, the independent nature of the endowments and their peer review processes provide strong checks against overt politicization.

---

### Attack Vector 2: Regional Cultural Protectionism

**Attack:** A Region uses its RAHC or other mechanisms to promote only its own cultural forms, discriminating against or excluding artistic expressions from other Regions.

**Countermeasure:**

- **Federal Block Grant Guidelines (Section 2(b)(2)):** Federal block grants to RAHCs can include guidelines promoting diversity and inter-regional exchange.
- **Inter-Regional Cultural Compacts (Section 12):** Encourages and funds collaborative initiatives that inherently cross boundaries.
- **National Arts and Culture Advisory Council (Section 31(c)):** The Council can mediate disputes and promote best practices for inclusive cultural programming.

**Outcome:** While Regions have autonomy, federal funding mechanisms and inter-regional cooperation incentives mitigate extreme cultural protectionism.

---

### Attack Vector 3: Neglect of Cultural Property

**Attack:** Federal or Regional authorities neglect their responsibilities for protecting cultural property, leading to its destruction, theft, or improper repatriation.

**Countermeasure:**

- **Coordinated Process (Section 22(b)):** Mandates a coordinated process involving federal, Regional, and Tribal authorities.
- **Tribal Consultation (Section 22(c)):** Ensures Tribal Nations have a direct voice in protecting their heritage.
- **Federal Enforcement:** Federal laws (e.g., Native American Graves Protection and Repatriation Act, Archaeological Resources Protection Act) provide enforcement mechanisms.
- **Council Oversight (Section 31(c)):** The National Arts and Culture Advisory Council can monitor and report on cultural property protection efforts.

**Outcome:** The Act establishes a multi-layered approach to cultural property protection, with clear responsibilities and oversight to prevent neglect.

## Residual Vulnerabilities

| Vulnerability | Severity | Mitigation |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Funding shortfalls for arts and humanities** | High | Requires sustained Congressional appropriations; advocacy from cultural organizations and public. |
| **Defining "national significance" vs. "regional expression"** | Medium | Council to develop clear guidelines; peer review processes for grant-making. |
| **Digital divide impacting access to cultural resources** | Medium | Integration with `Inter-Regional Communications and Broadband Act` to ensure equitable digital access. |
| **Political pressure on public broadcasting content** | Medium | CPB's independent status; clear editorial guidelines; public oversight. |
