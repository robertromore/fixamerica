# Independent Fact-Checking Integration Act

This proposal establishes a framework for the Independent Election Commission (IEC) to partner with independent, non-partisan fact-checking organizations to provide verified information directly to voters, enhancing the integrity of the information environment around elections and referendums.

---

## Purpose

Establish an Independent Fact-Checking Integration Framework that:

- Provides voters with direct access to verified, non-partisan factual information related to federal elections and referendums.
- Enhances the credibility and trustworthiness of official election information.
- Supports independent journalism and fact-checking efforts.
- Operates transparently and with strict non-partisanship to maintain public trust.

---

## Part I: Program Establishment and Certification

### Section 1. Establishment

(a) The Federal Agency for Civic Engagement and Media Literacy (the "Agency") shall administer a "Fact-Checking Integration Program" (the "Program").

(b) The Program shall facilitate partnerships between the Agency and certified independent, non-partisan fact-checking organizations.

### Section 2. Certification of Fact-Checking Organizations

(a) The Agency shall establish and publish objective criteria for certifying independent, non-partisan fact-checking organizations. Criteria shall include:
    - (1) Adherence to a publicly available code of principles (e.g., International Fact-Checking Network's Code of Principles).
    - (2) Demonstrated editorial independence from political parties, candidates, and advocacy groups.
    - (3) Transparency in funding sources and methodology.
    - (4) A track record of accurate and unbiased fact-checking.
    - (5) Expertise in verifying claims related to elections, public policy, and public health.

(b) The Agency shall maintain a public registry of certified fact-checking organizations.

---

## Part II: Integration Mechanisms

### Section 11. Official Information Channels

(a) The Agency shall integrate verified factual information from certified fact-checking organizations into its official information channels, including:
    - (1) The IEC's public website and online portals.
    - (2) Official voter information guides and educational materials.
    - (3) Public service announcements related to elections and referendums.

(b) This integration shall be clearly labeled as "Verified by Independent Fact-Checkers" and include attribution to the specific organization(s).

### Section 12. Rapid Response to Disinformation

(a) Certified fact-checking organizations shall provide the Agency with expedited access to their findings on demonstrably false information related to federal elections and referendums.

(b) The Agency shall, upon verification of such findings, promptly publish corrective information through its official channels.

### Section 13. Research and Analysis

(a) The Agency may commission certified fact-checking organizations to conduct research and analysis on trends in disinformation affecting federal elections and referendums.

(b) Research findings shall be used to inform IEC public education efforts and policy recommendations.

---

## Part III: Funding and Oversight

### Section 21. Funding

(a) There is authorized to be appropriated $100,000,000 for each fiscal year to fund partnerships with certified fact-checking organizations and to support the Program's activities.

(b) Funds shall be allocated through grants or contracts based on competitive proposals and demonstrated capacity.

### Section 22. Program Integrity

(a) The Agency shall ensure that all integrated information is strictly non-partisan, objective, and focused on factual accuracy.

(b) The Agency shall establish clear guidelines and oversight mechanisms to prevent partisan bias or ideological capture of the Program.

## Hostile Reinterpretation Stress Test

### Attack Vector 1: Partisan Fact-Checkers

**Attack:** Political actors attempt to influence the IEC's certification process to favor partisan fact-checking organizations, or to de-certify legitimate ones.

**Countermeasure:**

- **Objective Certification Criteria:** Section 2(a) mandates objective, publicly available criteria for certification, based on established principles of journalistic ethics and independence.
- **IEC Independence:** The IEC's independent composition (established by the Independent Election Commission Act) protects against partisan capture of the certification process.
-   **Agency Independence:** The Agency is established as an independent body with an insulated Director.
- **Transparency:** The public registry of certified organizations and transparent methodology (Section 2(a)(3)) allows for public scrutiny.
- **Judicial Review:** IEC certification decisions are subject to federal judicial review for abuse of discretion.

**Outcome:** The rigorous, objective certification process and independent oversight make it difficult to systematically introduce partisan fact-checkers or remove legitimate ones without detection and challenge.

---

### Attack Vector 2: Public Distrust and "Truth Arbiters" Claims

**Attack:** Political actors delegitimize the Program by claiming it acts as a "truth arbiter" or suppresses dissenting views, leading to public distrust.

**Countermeasure:**

- **Focus on "Demonstrably False Information":** The Program's scope is limited to factual claims that can be objectively disproven, not opinions or interpretations.
- **Transparency and Attribution:** All integrated information is clearly labeled and attributed to specific, certified organizations, allowing users to verify sources.
- **Educational Focus:** The Program emphasizes critical thinking and media literacy skills, empowering individuals to evaluate information themselves, rather than simply dictating "truth."
- **No Censorship Authority:** The Program does not have direct censorship authority over platforms; it merely provides verified information.

**Outcome:** By focusing on verifiable facts, maintaining transparency, and empowering critical thinking, the Program can build trust and counter claims of being an arbitrary "truth arbiter."

---

### Attack Vector 3: Funding Cuts and Administrative Sabotage

**Attack:** A hostile administration or Congress attempts to defund or administratively sabotage the Program to weaken its effectiveness.

**Countermeasure:**

- **Mandatory Appropriation:** The Act could specify that Program funding is a mandatory appropriation, insulated from discretionary cuts.
- **IEC Independence:** The IEC's independent status protects it from direct executive interference in program administration.
- **Public Reporting:** Annual reports to Congress and the public create transparency and accountability for program effectiveness.

**Outcome:** While political pressure is possible, direct defunding or sabotage is made more difficult by the Program's independent administration and mandatory funding.

---

## Residual Vulnerabilities

| Vulnerability | Severity | Mitigation |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Maintaining public trust in fact-checkers** | Medium | Continuous transparency, rigorous adherence to non-partisanship, and clear communication of methodology. |
| **Rapid pace of disinformation vs. fact-checking speed** | Medium | Prioritize rapid response for high-impact disinformation; leverage AI tools for initial screening (with human oversight). |
| **Limited reach to echo chambers** | High | Targeted outreach strategies; partnerships with diverse community organizations and local media. |
