# Scenario 05: Coordinated Information Warfare

**Parent Document:** [Concrete Scenarios Overview](00-overview.md)

**Timeline:** Month 20 of Phase 1

**Incident Type:** Dual-narrative attack on framework credibility

**Status:** Draft

---

## Background

Month 20 of Phase 1. MCF mechanisms are operational; CMS enrollment is growing; trust is building but fragile. A coordinated information campaign launches from multiple directions, designed to destroy framework credibility with both populations simultaneously.

**Attack Vectors:**

1. **Israeli social media:** "MCF is a Palestinian Trojan horse—legitimizes terrorists, endangers Israelis"
2. **Palestinian social media:** "MCF is normalization—collaborates with occupation, legitimizes apartheid"
3. **International amplification:** Both narratives picked up by aligned media outlets
4. **Deepfake/manipulated content:** Fabricated videos of MCF officials making inflammatory statements
5. **Data breach claims:** Allegations that MCF is sharing participant data with security services

**Key Test:** Information attack designed to destroy credibility with both sides simultaneously. Framework must maintain factual authority without becoming partisan combatant in narrative war.

---

## Day 1: Attack Detection

### Attack Signature

| Indicator | Observation |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Coordinated timing** | Both narratives emerge within hours across multiple platforms |
| **Synthetic amplification** | Bot-like activity boosting both narratives |
| **Cross-referencing** | Israeli narrative cites Palestinian sources as "proof"; Palestinian narrative cites Israeli sources |
| **Deepfake elements** | Video of "JSVC official" making statement never made |
| **Data weaponization** | Screenshots of "leaked" participant data (fabricated or selectively presented) |

### Initial Assessment

| Category | Status |
|----------|--------|
| **Origin attribution** | Foreign state actor suspected (patterns consistent with known operations) |
| **Domestic amplification** | Genuine engagement from both populations |
| **MCF systems** | No actual data breach detected |
| **Content authenticity** | "Leaked" video confirmed as deepfake |

---

## Day 1-2: Immediate Response

### What MCF Does Immediately

| Action | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| **Internal verification** | Confirm no actual data breach; verify deepfake |
| **Rapid statement** | "MCF is aware of coordinated disinformation. We are verifying claims and will publish findings." |
| **No defensive posture** | Do not engage with specific allegations immediately |
| **Evidence preservation** | Document attack patterns for attribution work |

### Communication Principles

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Speed matters** | Acknowledge attack exists within hours |
| **Don't amplify** | Don't repeat specific false claims in denial |
| **Credibility preservation** | Promise verification, then deliver |
| **No panic** | Steady tone; framework continues normal operations |

### What MCF Does NOT Do

- Engage in point-by-point rebuttal immediately (amplifies attack)
- Accuse specific actors without evidence
- Suspend operations in response to information attack
- Issue emotional or defensive statements

---

## Day 2-3: Verification and Counter-Messaging

### Deepfake Response

**Technical verification:**

| Method | Result |
|--------|--------|
| **Metadata analysis** | File creation date inconsistent with claimed event |
| **Audio forensics** | Voice patterns inconsistent with known samples |
| **Visual forensics** | Artifacts consistent with AI generation |
| **Event verification** | Official not present at claimed location on claimed date |

**Public statement:**

> "The video circulating purporting to show [official] making [statement] is a fabrication. Technical analysis confirms: [specific findings]. The official was not present at [location] on [date] per verified records. This video is part of a coordinated disinformation campaign."

### Data Breach Response

**Internal audit:**

| System | Status |
|--------|--------|
| **CMS Registry** | No unauthorized access detected |
| **Communication systems** | No breach detected |
| **Personnel devices** | No compromise detected |
| **Access logs** | All access authorized and logged |

**Public statement:**

> "Circulating screenshots purporting to show MCF participant data are [fabricated/selectively presented/taken out of context]. MCF systems have been audited; no breach detected. Data protection measures remain in place."

### Narrative Response Strategy

**Key message:**

> "MCF is subject to coordinated disinformation from actors who oppose peaceful coexistence. We respond with facts, not accusations. The framework continues to operate."

**Targeted messaging:**

| Audience | Message |
|----------|---------|
| **Israeli skeptics** | "MCF has documented [specific findings favorable to Israeli security]. Review our public reports." |
| **Palestinian skeptics** | "MCF has documented [specific findings favorable to Palestinian concerns]. Review our public reports." |
| **International** | "This attack pattern is consistent with foreign information operations. MCF maintains factual standards." |

## Day 3-7: Attribution and Pattern Documentation

### Attribution Investigation

| Actor | Evidence | Confidence |
|-------|----------|------------|
| **Foreign state (e.g., Iran, Russia)** | Bot network signatures; timing patterns; geolocation of synthetic accounts | Medium-High |
| **Domestic spoilers (Israeli)** | Amplification by known accounts; coordination with political actors | Medium |
| **Domestic spoilers (Palestinian)** | Amplification by known accounts; coordination with factions | Medium |

### What MCF Can Attribute

| Finding | Basis |
|---------|-------|
| **Coordinated campaign** | Technical patterns of synthetic amplification |
| **Deepfake creation** | Forensic analysis |
| **Timing coordination** | Statistical analysis of posting patterns |

### What MCF Cannot Attribute

| Question | Limitation |
|----------|------------|
| **Specific government responsibility** | Technical attribution insufficient for political accusation |
| **Individual perpetrators** | Not a law enforcement body |
| **Intent beyond disruption** | Cannot prove specific strategic goal |

### Guarantor Coordination

| Action | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| **Technical briefing** | Share attribution findings with guarantor intelligence services |
| **Request counter-messaging support** | Guarantors may issue statements supporting MCF credibility |
| **Platform coordination** | Request takedown of verified deepfakes through guarantor channels |

---

## Day 7-14: Sustained Response

### Credibility Maintenance

| Mechanism | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Continued normal operations** | Framework operates as if attack did not occur |
| **Regular factual publications** | Continue publishing verification reports |
| **Transparency about attack** | Periodic updates on disinformation findings |
| **No defensiveness** | Tone remains professional, not aggrieved |

### Counter-Narrative Building

| Strategy | Implementation |
|----------|----------------|
| **Fact-based corrections** | Each false claim addressed with specific evidence |
| **Pre-bunking** | Warning about future disinformation tactics |
| **Participant testimonials** | Voluntary statements from CMS holders (not coerced) |
| **Track record emphasis** | Point to documented verification findings |

### Platform Engagement

| Platform | Action |
|----------|--------|
| **Social media** | Verified deepfakes reported; synthetic amplification documented |
| **Traditional media** | Briefings provided to journalists |
| **International media** | Counter-narrative materials distributed |

## Branches: Outcome Variations

### Branch A: Attack Fails—Credibility Maintained

**Indicators:**

- Participant enrollment continues at normal rates
- Public polling shows limited impact on MCF trust
- Platform takedowns reduce synthetic amplification
- Media coverage shifts to "MCF under attack" narrative

**Framework Response:**

| Action | Scope |
|--------|-------|
| **Documentation** | Attack documented for future reference |
| **Lessons learned** | Response protocols updated |
| **Resilience demonstrated** | Framework survived coordinated attack |
| **Normal operations** | Continue without special measures |

**Outcome:**

Framework emerges stronger. Attack provided opportunity to demonstrate factual credibility. Participant trust may actually increase due to visible survival of attack.

---

### Branch B: Partial Success—One Side Loses Trust

**Scenario B1: Israeli trust damaged**

**Indicators:**

- Israeli participants withdraw at elevated rate
- Israeli political figures cite attack narratives as "proof"
- Israeli media coverage turns hostile
- Palestinian trust maintained

**Framework Response:**

| Action | Scope |
|--------|-------|
| **Asymmetric outreach** | Enhanced engagement with Israeli skeptics |
| **Evidence emphasis** | Highlight documented findings favorable to Israeli concerns |
| **Guarantor support** | Request U.S. government statements on MCF credibility |
| **Continued operation** | Do not suspend Israeli-side operations |

**Outcome:**

Framework continues with reduced Israeli participation. Documentation continues. Path to trust restoration remains open.

---

**Scenario B2: Palestinian trust damaged**

**Indicators:**

- Palestinian participants withdraw at elevated rate
- Palestinian political figures cite attack narratives as "proof"
- Palestinian media coverage turns hostile
- Israeli trust maintained

**Framework Response:**

| Action | Scope |
|--------|-------|
| **Asymmetric outreach** | Enhanced engagement with Palestinian skeptics |
| **Evidence emphasis** | Highlight documented findings favorable to Palestinian concerns |
| **Guarantor support** | Request regional guarantor statements on MCF credibility |
| **Continued operation** | Do not suspend Palestinian-side operations |

**Outcome:**

Framework continues with reduced Palestinian participation. Documentation continues. Path to trust restoration remains open.

---

### Branch C: Attack Succeeds—Dual Credibility Collapse

**Indicators:**

- Mass withdrawals from both populations
- Political leaders from both sides denounce MCF
- Media coverage uniformly hostile
- Guarantor support wavering

**Framework Response:**

| Phase | Action |
|-------|--------|
| **Immediate** | Acknowledge crisis without admitting false claims |
| **Assessment** | Evaluate whether operation remains viable |
| **Regression consideration** | JSVC evaluates Phase regression if participation below viability |
| **Core preservation** | Even if operations contract, maintain verification capacity |

### Phase Regression Assessment

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|------------|
| **Participation viability** | If CMS enrollment drops below critical threshold |
| **Party cooperation** | If Israel and both Palestinian authorities withdraw official cooperation |
| **Guarantor support** | If guarantors reduce engagement |
| **Framework function** | If verification and documentation cannot continue |

### Regression Options

| Option | Trigger |
|--------|---------|
| **Maintain Phase 0** | If core functions continue despite reduced participation |
| **Partial regression** | Suspend CMS expansion; maintain verification-only |
| **Operational pause** | Suspend all operations except documentation pending credibility restoration |

### Recovery Path

Even in worst case:

1. UVB documentation capacity preserved
2. Factual record maintained
3. Guarantor relationships preserved
4. Path to restoration defined

**Key principle:** Information warfare cannot destroy facts that are already documented. The record survives even if current operations pause.

---

## Success Criteria

After a coordinated information attack, the framework:

- [ ] Still exists
- [ ] Still maintains factual credibility with at least one major population
- [ ] Still publishes verified information
- [ ] Still has guarantor support
- [ ] Still has path to trust restoration

**What MCF Does NOT Do:**

- Prevent information attacks
- Win the narrative war decisively
- Silence attackers
- Guarantee public belief

**What MCF Prevents:**

- Unchallenged disinformation
- Undocumented attack patterns
- Total credibility collapse
- Loss of factual baseline

---

## Key Design Validation

This scenario validates:

1. **Factual authority:** MCF credibility rests on documented facts, not narrative combat
2. **Verification capacity:** Deepfakes and false claims can be technically debunked
3. **Asymmetric resilience:** Framework can survive loss of trust with one side
4. **Documentation persistence:** Facts exist even when not believed
5. **Guarantor role:** External support crucial for counter-messaging
6. **Steady operations:** Continue functioning; do not grant attack victory through panic
