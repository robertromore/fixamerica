## Purpose

This directory translates stress tests into concrete operational timelines. Where [Stress Testing](../14-stress-testing.md) asks "What breaks?", these scenarios ask "What happens hour by hour?"

**Design Philosophy:**

MCF does not prevent violence. It prevents violence from destroying the procedural layer. ## Common Elements

### What All Scenarios Test

1. **Decoupling:** Does security response proceed while framework operates in parallel?
2. **Bounded Responses:** Are restrictions automatic, proportionate, and time-limited?
3. **Verification Continuity:** Does factual documentation continue?
4. **Humanitarian Floor:** Are medical and water PCCs protected?
5. **Uncertainty Tolerance:** Can framework function without immediate attribution?

### Standard Timeline Phases

| Phase | Duration | Focus |
|-------|----------|-------|
| **Immediate** | 0-2 hours | Activation, deconfliction, evidence preservation |
| **Short-term** | 2-24 hours | PCC protection mode, preliminary reporting |
| **Medium-term** | 24-72 hours | Attribution work, interim findings |
| **Review** | Day 3-7 | Formal severity assessment, rollback evaluation |
| **Restoration** | Week 2+ | De-escalation, service restoration |

### Success Criteria

After any crisis, the framework:

- [ ] Still exists
- [ ] Still publishes facts
- [ ] Still protects at least one corridor
- [ ] Still has participants who believe it can outlast the crisis
