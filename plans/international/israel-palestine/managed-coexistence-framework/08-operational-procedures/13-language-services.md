# Language Services Framework

**Parent Document:** [00-overview.md](00-overview.md)

**Applies To:** All Phases

**Objective:** Ensure effective multilingual communication across all MCF institutions and operations.

**Status:** Final Draft

---

## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Rationale

MCF operates across communities with different primary languages:
- **Hebrew:** Primary language of Israeli participants
- **Arabic:** Primary language of Palestinian participants (multiple dialects)
- **English:** Common second language, international legal/diplomatic standard

Effective language services are essential for:
- Equal access to MCF institutions and benefits
- Accurate understanding of rights and obligations
- Fair dispute resolution and legal proceedings
- Inclusive participation regardless of language background

### 1.2 Scope

**Covered:**
- Official MCF documents and communications
- MCF institutional proceedings (hearings, tribunals, councils)
- Public-facing services (applications, information, complaints)
- Inter-institutional communication
- Training and educational materials

**Not Covered:**
- Private communications between individuals
- Media content (subject to [11-media-civil-society.md](11-media-civil-society.md))
- Commercial transactions (parties choose their own language)

## 2. Official Languages

### 2.1 Core Languages

MCF operates in three official languages with equal status:

| Language | ISO Code | Script | Primary Users |
|----------|----------|--------|---------------|
| Arabic (Modern Standard) | ar | Arabic | Palestinian participants |
| Hebrew | he | Hebrew | Israeli participants |
| English | en | Latin | International, legal, diplomatic |

### 2.2 Language Equality Principle

**Equal Status:**
- All three languages have equal official status
- No language takes precedence in legal interpretation
- Participants may use any official language in MCF proceedings
- All binding documents must exist in all three languages

**Conflict Resolution:**
- Where translations differ in meaning, all three versions are consulted
- Legal interpretation considers intent across all versions
- Arbitration clause may specify tie-breaking language for specific documents
- Default: English for international law matters, Arabic/Hebrew for domestic law references

### 2.3 Dialect Considerations

**Arabic Dialects:**
- Official documents use Modern Standard Arabic (MSA)
- Interpretation services available for Palestinian colloquial dialects
- Gaza and West Bank dialect differences accommodated
- Levantine Arabic competency required for frontline staff

**Hebrew Variations:**
- Standard Modern Hebrew for official documents
- Accommodation for religious/traditional terminology where relevant

## 3. Translation Services

### 3.1 Document Translation

**Translation Requirements:**

| Document Type | Languages Required | Certification |
|--------------|-------------------|---------------|
| Legal instruments (CRC, charters) | All three | Sworn translation + legal review |
| Administrative regulations | All three | Certified translation |
| Forms and applications | All three | Certified translation |
| Informational materials | All three | Professional translation |
| Internal communications | At least two | Staff translation acceptable |

**Translation Standards:**
- Certified translators must hold recognized credentials
- Legal translations require legal terminology expertise
- Technical documents require subject-matter expertise
- All translations must be reviewed before publication

**Turnaround Times:**

| Priority | Document Type | Target |
|----------|--------------|--------|
| Emergency | Safety/security communications | 2 hours |
| Urgent | Legal proceedings documents | 24 hours |
| Standard | Administrative documents | 5 business days |
| Routine | Informational materials | 15 business days |

### 3.2 Translation Quality Assurance

**Quality Control:**
- Second translator review for legal documents
- Subject-matter expert review for technical content
- Periodic audit of translation accuracy
- Feedback mechanism for translation errors

**Terminology Management:**
- MCF terminology database (trilingual)
- Consistent translation of key terms
- Regular terminology updates
- Glossaries published for reference

### 3.3 Machine Translation Policy

**Permitted Uses:**
- Internal draft translation for review
- Non-binding informational summaries
- Personal communication assistance

**Prohibited Uses:**
- Official documents without human review
- Legal proceedings without human interpreter
- Any binding communication

**AI Translation Tools:**
- Approved tools listed by Language Services Office
- Data security requirements for translation tools
- Human verification always required for official use

## 4. Interpretation Services

### 4.1 Interpretation Modes

**Simultaneous Interpretation:**
- Required for: Council sessions, large meetings, conferences
- Equipment: Interpretation booths, headsets, microphones
- Staffing: Minimum 2 interpreters per language pair (rotation)

**Consecutive Interpretation:**
- Used for: Tribunal hearings, mediations, interviews
- Staffing: 1 interpreter per session (2 for long sessions)
- Note-taking requirements for accuracy

**Whispered Interpretation:**
- Used for: Individual assistance in group settings
- Limited to: Brief explanations, clarifications

### 4.2 Interpretation Quality Standards

**Interpreter Qualifications:**
- Certified court interpreters for legal proceedings
- Conference interpretation credentials for formal events
- MCF-specific training for institutional terminology
- Security vetting for sensitive proceedings

**Professional Standards:**
- Accuracy: Complete and faithful interpretation
- Impartiality: No personal opinion or editing
- Confidentiality: Protected information obligations
- Professionalism: Appropriate conduct and appearance

**Quality Monitoring:**
- Random session observation
- Participant feedback collection
- Performance reviews
- Continuing education requirements

### 4.3 Remote Interpretation

**Video Remote Interpretation (VRI):**
- Available for: Non-sensitive proceedings, information sessions
- Requirements: Secure connection, quality audio/video
- Limitations: Not suitable for complex legal proceedings

**Telephone Interpretation:**
- Emergency backup when in-person unavailable
- Short interactions only
- Security protocols for sensitive content

## 5. Language Access in Proceedings

### 5.1 Tribunal and Hearing Rights

**Participant Rights:**
- Right to use any official language
- Right to interpretation at no cost
- Right to translated documents in advance
- Right to object to inadequate interpretation
- Right to review translated decisions

**Proceeding Requirements:**
- Interpreters present for all hearings with non-English participants
- Documents provided in participant's preferred language
- Decisions issued in all three languages
- Recording of interpreted proceedings

### 5.2 CMS Application Process

**Application Assistance:**
- Forms available in all three languages
- Assistance available for completion
- Interview interpretation provided
- Decisions communicated in applicant's language

**Language Testing:**
- Not required for CMS eligibility
- Optional language courses offered
- Multilingualism encouraged but not mandatory

### 5.3 Emergency Communications

**Emergency Language Priority:**
- All emergency alerts in all three languages simultaneously
- No delay for translation of safety-critical information
- Pre-translated emergency templates maintained
- Interpretation available 24/7 for emergency response

## 6. Training and Education

### 6.1 MCF Staff Language Requirements

**Position Categories:**

| Category | Language Requirement |
|----------|---------------------|
| Senior leadership | Fluent in at least two official languages |
| Public-facing staff | Fluent in at least two official languages |
| Technical specialists | Fluent in one + working knowledge of second |
| Support staff | Fluent in one + basic understanding of second |

**Language Training:**
- Free language courses for all MCF staff
- Immersion programs available
- Language proficiency bonuses
- Release time for language study

### 6.2 Public Education Materials

**Civic Education:**
- All CMS civic education curriculum in three languages
- Video materials with subtitles in all languages
- Audio materials in multiple dialects
- Simplified language versions for accessibility

**Rights Information:**
- Know-your-rights materials in all languages
- Community outreach in local dialects
- Visual/pictorial guides for low-literacy populations
- Hotline with multilingual operators

### 6.3 Terminology Training

**Specialized Training:**
- Legal terminology for court interpreters
- Medical terminology for healthcare settings
- Technical terminology for infrastructure staff
- Cultural competency integrated with language training

## 7. Signage and Public Information

### 7.1 Physical Signage

**Requirements:**
- All MCF facility signage trilingual
- Equal prominence for all languages
- Script direction accommodated (RTL for Arabic/Hebrew)
- Pictograms supplement text where appropriate

**Formatting Standards:**
- Consistent font sizes across languages
- Adequate spacing for all scripts
- Color-coding for language identification (optional)
- Braille supplementation at key locations

### 7.2 Digital Presence

**Website and Applications:**
- Full trilingual websites
- Language selection prominent and persistent
- Same content in all languages (no reduced versions)
- Right-to-left layout for Arabic/Hebrew

**Mobile Applications:**
- Interface in all three languages
- Push notifications in user's selected language
- Offline translation tools where possible

### 7.3 Forms and Documents

**Form Design:**
- Trilingual forms preferred where space permits
- Language-specific versions with identical content
- Clear identification of language version
- QR codes linking to other language versions

## 8. Language Services Office (LSO)

### 8.1 Structure

**Functions:**
- Translation coordination and quality control
- Interpreter recruitment and management
- Terminology development and maintenance
- Language policy development
- Complaint handling for language services

**Staffing:**
- Director (trilingual required)
- Translation coordinators (by language pair)
- Interpretation coordinators
- Terminology specialists
- Quality assurance staff

### 8.2 Budget

**Cost Categories:**
- Staff salaries
- Contract translation services
- Interpretation equipment
- Training programs
- Technology tools

**Funding Sources:**
- MCF institutional budget
- Guarantor supplementary funding
- User fees for non-essential services (e.g., certified translations for personal use)

### 8.3 External Partnerships

**Partnerships with:**
- Universities (translation/interpretation programs)
- Professional interpreter associations
- Language technology providers
- International organizations (AIIC, ATA)

## 9. Accessibility Integration

### 9.1 Language and Disability

**Sign Languages:**
- Israeli Sign Language (ISL) interpretation available
- Palestinian Sign Language interpretation available
- Video relay services for deaf participants

**Visual Impairments:**
- Audio descriptions in all languages
- Screen reader compatibility for all digital content
- Braille materials in all languages

**Cognitive Accessibility:**
- Plain language versions of key documents
- Visual aids supplementing text
- Simplified forms for complex processes

### 9.2 Low-Literacy Populations

**Accommodations:**
- Oral explanation of documents
- Video and audio alternatives to text
- Picture-based guides
- In-person assistance for form completion
