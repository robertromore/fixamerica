**Status:** Draft

## Purpose

Vulnerable Populations Protections defines MCF's approach to **population groups with heightened protection needs**â€”children, women, disabled persons, and elderly individuals who face additional barriers to participation or risks within the conflict environment.

MCF's general protections apply to all participants. This document specifies additional measures for populations where standard protections are insufficient due to power imbalances, physical vulnerability, or discriminatory social structures.

---

## Core Principles

### 1. Baseline Plus Enhancement

Vulnerable populations receive all standard CRC protections plus additional measures tailored to their circumstances.

**What This Means:**

- No reduction of general rights for vulnerable populations
- Additional protections layered on top
- Enhanced access to services
- Priority in protection decisions

### 2. Agency Preservation

Protections enhance rather than replace individual agency. Vulnerable does not mean incapable.

**What This Means:**

- Supported decision-making over substituted judgment
- Consent still required (with appropriate support)
- Participation encouraged where safe
- No paternalistic exclusion

### 3. Intersectionality Recognition

Individuals may belong to multiple vulnerable categories. Protections address compound vulnerability.

**What This Means:**

- Disabled women face different barriers than disabled men
- Elderly Palestinians face different risks than elderly Israelis
- Children with disabilities require combined protections
- Assessment considers full context

### 4. Non-Discrimination in Protection

Protection measures do not create new discrimination or stigma.

**What This Means:**

- Confidential accommodation processes
- No public identification of vulnerability status
- Protection without isolation
- Integration as goal

## Children and Education

### 1. Child Protection Framework

**Age Definition:**

- Child: Under 18 years
- Special provisions for children under 12
- Youth category (15-18) for transitional provisions

**Core Protections:**

| Protection | Implementation |
|------------|----------------|
| **Physical safety** | Priority evacuation; protected from recruitment; separated detention prohibited |
| **Family unity** | No family separation by MCF action; reunification priority |
| **Identity preservation** | Name, nationality, family relations maintained |
| **Development rights** | Education continuity; play and recreation access |

**CMS for Children:**

- Children enrolled through parent/guardian
- Independent enrollment at 16 with parental notification
- Full independence at 18
- Child's views considered per maturity

### 2. Educational Access

**Principles:**

- Education continuity across communities
- No political indoctrination through MCF
- Recognition of both Israeli and Palestinian curricula
- Access to educational PCCs

**Educational PCCs:**

| Type | Coverage |
|------|----------|
| **School access** | Routes to schools in different communities |
| **Higher education** | University access corridors |
| **Vocational training** | Training facility access |
| **Examination** | Secure exam administration sites |

**Credential Recognition:**

- School completion certificates recognized across systems
- University entrance qualification portability
- Vocational certification alignment
- No curriculum standardization required

### 3. Educational Cooperation

**Permitted:**

- Teacher professional development
- Educational material sharing (voluntary)
- Student exchange programs
- Joint extracurricular activities

**Not Addressed:**

- Curriculum content (political question)
- Textbook approval (political question)
- Historical narrative (political question)
- Religious instruction (community matter)

### 4. Child-Specific Protections

**In Incidents:**

- Child victims prioritized in medical response
- Child witnesses handled with special protocols
- Child perpetrators treated per juvenile justice standards
- No prosecution of children under 12

**In Displacement:**

- Unaccompanied children identified immediately
- Family tracing services activated
- Temporary care arrangements
- Education continuity maintained

**In MCF Proceedings:**

- Child-friendly procedures
- Appropriate representation
- Privacy protections enhanced
- Decisions prioritize child's interests

## Women's Protections

### 1. Gender-Based Violence Response

**Covered:**

- Sexual violence
- Domestic violence
- Honor-based violence
- Trafficking
- Forced marriage

**Response Mechanisms:**

| Element | Implementation |
|---------|----------------|
| **Reporting** | Confidential channels; female responders available |
| **Protection** | Emergency safe housing; relocation assistance |
| **Justice** | Specialized investigation; trauma-informed process |
| **Support** | Medical care; psychosocial support; legal aid |

**Cross-Community Dimension:**

- GBV perpetrated across community lines treated seriously
- No impunity based on perpetrator community
- Coordination between community protection systems
- MCF provides gap-filling where community systems fail

### 2. Family Law Interface

**Challenge:**

Israeli and Palestinian family law systems differ significantly on marriage, divorce, custody, and inheritance. MCF cannot override either system.

**MCF Approach:**

| Issue | MCF Role |
|-------|----------|
| **Marriage** | Recognition for MCF purposes of marriages valid in either system |
| **Divorce** | No MCF authority; support access to appropriate forum |
| **Custody** | Enforce existing orders; not adjudicate disputes |
| **Inheritance** | No MCF authority; facilitate access to appropriate forum |

**Protections Within Family Law Limits:**

- GBV protection regardless of family law status
- Economic rights (employment, property) independent of marital status
- CMS independent of spousal status
- Exit support without requiring divorce

### 3. Participation Barriers

**Identified Barriers:**

- Mobility restrictions (social, security)
- Caregiving responsibilities
- Economic dependence
- Social pressure against participation

**MCF Responses:**

| Barrier | Response |
|---------|----------|
| **Mobility** | Women-priority PCC schedules; female escorts available |
| **Caregiving** | Childcare support for MCF participation |
| **Economic** | Independent economic benefits access |
| **Social pressure** | Confidential participation options |

### 4. Representation

**In MCF Institutions:**

- Gender balance targets in MCF staffing
- Female representation on all panels affecting women
- Gender expertise in relevant positions
- Gender impact assessment for policies

**Not Required:**

- Gender quotas in party delegations (party decision)
- Parity in all circumstances
- Gender balance over competence

## Disabled Persons

### 1. Accessibility Requirements

**Physical Accessibility:**

| Context | Requirements |
|---------|--------------|
| **MCF facilities** | Full accessibility (mobility, visual, hearing) |
| **PCCs** | Accessible routes; assistance available |
| **Services** | Multiple access modalities (in-person, remote, written) |
| **Information** | Accessible formats (large print, audio, easy-read) |

**Communication Accessibility:**

- Sign language interpretation
- Captioning for audio/video
- Plain language versions
- Assistive technology support

### 2. Reasonable Accommodation

**Obligation:**

MCF institutions must provide reasonable accommodation enabling disabled persons' equal participation.

**Process:**

1. Individual identifies accommodation need
2. Interactive dialogue on effective accommodation
3. Accommodation provided unless undue burden
4. Appeal if accommodation denied

**Examples:**

| Need | Accommodation |
|------|---------------|
| **Mobility impairment** | Accessible meeting location; remote participation |
| **Visual impairment** | Documents in accessible format; sighted guide |
| **Hearing impairment** | Interpretation; written communication |
| **Cognitive disability** | Plain language; additional time; support person |

### 3. Service Coordination

**Challenge:**

Disability services differ significantly between Israeli and Palestinian systems. Disabled CMS holders may need to navigate both.

**MCF Role:**

| Function | Implementation |
|----------|----------------|
| **Information** | Guide to available services in both systems |
| **Coordination** | Facilitate cross-system service access |
| **Portability** | Disability status recognition across systems |
| **Gap-filling** | MCF services where community systems fail |

**Disability Status:**

- Recognition of disability determinations from either system
- MCF does not re-adjudicate disability status
- Functional approach to accommodation (not diagnostic)

### 4. Protection Concerns

**Heightened Risks:**

- Exploitation and abuse
- Neglect in crisis situations
- Barriers to reporting
- Institutional violence

**Protective Measures:**

| Risk | Response |
|------|----------|
| **Exploitation** | Enhanced screening; monitoring; reporting channels |
| **Crisis neglect** | Priority in evacuations; dedicated assistance |
| **Reporting barriers** | Accessible reporting; proxy reporting; mandatory reporting in institutions |
| **Institutional** | Inspection; complaints mechanism; external oversight |

## Elderly Populations

### 1. Age-Related Protections

**Age Definition:**

- Elderly: 65 years and over
- Special provisions for 80+ (heightened vulnerability)

**Core Protections:**

| Protection | Implementation |
|------------|----------------|
| **Physical safety** | Priority in evacuations; protected from violence |
| **Healthcare access** | Geriatric care; chronic condition management |
| **Economic security** | Pension portability; economic program access |
| **Social connection** | Family contact; community access |

### 2. Healthcare Coordination

**Challenges:**

- Different healthcare systems
- Chronic condition management
- Medication access
- Specialized care needs

**MCF Responses:**

| Need | Response |
|------|----------|
| **Chronic care** | Medical PCCs; medication corridor |
| **Specialist access** | Referral pathways across systems |
| **Emergency** | Priority medical evacuation |
| **Palliative** | End-of-life care access regardless of location |

### 3. Economic Security

**Pension Portability:**

- CMS does not affect national pension eligibility
- MCF facilitates pension access across territories
- No double-benefit from multiple systems
- Gap protection for those falling between systems

**Employment:**

- Age discrimination prohibited in MCF-facilitated employment
- Flexible participation options
- Recognition of experience and skills
- Retirement not compelled

### 4. Elder Abuse Response

**Covered:**

- Physical abuse
- Neglect
- Financial exploitation
- Psychological abuse
- Abandonment

**Response:**

| Element | Implementation |
|---------|----------------|
| **Detection** | Training for MCF personnel; community awareness |
| **Reporting** | Accessible channels; confidential |
| **Protection** | Emergency intervention; alternative care |
| **Justice** | Prosecution support; compensation |

## Cross-Cutting Provisions

### 1. Identification and Assessment

**Vulnerability Identification:**

- Self-identification
- Professional identification (healthcare, social services)
- Family/community notification (with consent)
- MCF personnel observation

**Assessment Process:**

- Confidential assessment
- Functional approach (what support needed, not label)
- Regular review
- Individual-centered

### 2. Data Protection

**Enhanced Privacy:**

- Vulnerability status confidential
- Heightened access controls
- No disclosure without consent (unless protective necessity)
- Anonymization in reporting

**Data Retention:**

- Vulnerability data deleted when no longer needed
- Individual access to own records
- Correction rights

### 3. Capacity Building

**For MCF Personnel:**

- Vulnerability awareness training
- Specialized skills for relevant roles
- Trauma-informed approaches
- Cultural competence

**For Communities:**

- Awareness raising
- Service navigation support
- Advocacy support
- Community capacity building

### 4. Monitoring and Evaluation

**Monitoring:**

- Vulnerable population participation rates
- Protection measure effectiveness
- Complaint and grievance patterns
- Outcome disparities

**Evaluation:**

- Regular assessment of provisions
- Vulnerable population input
- External review
- Continuous improvement
