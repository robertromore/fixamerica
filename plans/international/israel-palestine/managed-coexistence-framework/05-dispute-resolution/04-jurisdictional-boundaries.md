# Jurisdictional Boundaries and Excluded Matters

**Parent Document:** [00-introduction.md](../00-introduction.md#dispute-resolution-mechanics)

**Phase:** All Phases

**Status:** Final Draft

---

## Purpose

Jurisdictional Boundaries define **what MCF dispute resolution can and cannot address**. These limits prevent dispute mechanisms from becoming sovereignty battlegrounds while ensuring credible, bounded dispute resolution.

Strict jurisdictional limits are essential to MCF's conflict-management function. Without them, dispute resolution would inevitably expand into territory adjudication, sovereignty claims, and political warfareâ€”destroying the framework's utility.

---

## Foundational Principle: Non-Prejudice

### The Non-Prejudice Rule

**No dispute resolution outcome may be used to assert or deny sovereignty, territorial claims, or final-status rights.**

This rule is absolute and applies across all MCF dispute mechanisms.

### What Non-Prejudice Means

**MCF proceedings cannot establish:**
- Territorial sovereignty
- Boundary legitimacy
- State recognition
- Population sovereignty
- Resource ownership
- Final political status

**MCF proceedings cannot deny:**
- Existing sovereignty claims
- Prior rights
- Legal positions
- Political aspirations
- Historical claims

### How Non-Prejudice Is Implemented

**Procedural safeguards:**
- Explicit non-prejudice statements in all determinations
- Prohibition on sovereignty-adjacent reasoning
- Review for prejudice violations
- Determination invalidation if prejudice found

**Substantive limits:**
- Excluded subject matters (below)
- Jurisdictional screening
- Scope limitations in proceedings

**Interpretive rules:**
- Ambiguous matters resolved toward non-prejudice
- Narrow construction of jurisdiction
- Deference to political processes for political questions

## Excluded Subject Matters

### 1. Criminal Matters

**Excluded:**
- Criminal prosecution
- Criminal investigation
- Criminal penalties
- Criminal procedural matters

**Rationale:**
- MCF lacks enforcement capacity for criminal law
- Criminal jurisdiction is sovereign function
- Due process requirements exceed MCF capacity
- International criminal law provides external mechanisms

**Where Criminal Matters Go:**
- National criminal courts
- International Criminal Court (where applicable)
- International humanitarian law mechanisms
- National prosecution services

**MCF Role:**
- Documentation and preservation
- Referral to appropriate authorities
- Witness protection support (where possible)
- No adjudication

### 2. Security Operations

**Excluded:**
- Military operational decisions
- Security force deployments
- Intelligence operations
- Counter-terrorism actions
- Armed response decisions

**Rationale:**
- Security operations require operational autonomy
- Judicial review inappropriate for military decisions
- Separate security accountability mechanisms exist
- MCF civilian dispute resolution not designed for security matters

**Where Security Matters Go:**
- Security protocols (separate process)
- Military justice systems
- International humanitarian law
- Guarantor security oversight

**MCF Role:**
- Civilian consequence complaints (to review panels)
- Documentation of civilian impact
- Coordination with security mechanisms
- No operational adjudication

### 3. Military Conduct

**Excluded:**
- Laws of armed conflict compliance
- Military discipline
- Command decisions
- Armed conflict conduct

**Rationale:**
- International humanitarian law governs
- Military justice systems apply
- MCF civilian framework inappropriate
- Expertise and authority limitations

**Where Military Matters Go:**
- Military courts
- International humanitarian law mechanisms
- ICC (where applicable)
- National military justice

**MCF Role:**
- Civilian protection documentation
- Referral to appropriate mechanisms
- No adjudication of military conduct

### 4. Sovereignty Claims

**Excluded:**
- Territorial sovereignty disputes
- State recognition questions
- Self-determination claims
- National identity determinations
- Political status questions

**Rationale:**
- Sovereignty is political question
- MCF designed to operate without resolving sovereignty
- Resolution would collapse framework
- Parties retain sovereign claims unaffected

**Where Sovereignty Matters Go:**
- Political negotiations
- International diplomatic processes
- Parties' own political processes
- Future final-status resolution

**MCF Role:**
- Operates alongside unresolved sovereignty
- No prejudice to any position
- Preserves all claims intact

### 5. Territorial Boundaries

**Excluded:**
- Border demarcation
- Boundary disputes
- Territorial allocation
- Land ownership (as sovereignty question)

**Rationale:**
- Boundaries are sovereignty questions
- Adjudication would prejudice final status
- MCF operates across boundaries, not on them
- Political resolution required

**MCF Role:**
- Operational arrangements that don't prejudice boundaries
- Functional cooperation across boundaries
- No boundary adjudication

### 6. Jerusalem Status

**Excluded:**
- Jerusalem sovereignty
- Holy site control
- Jerusalem governance
- Jerusalem boundary questions

**Rationale:**
- Explicitly excluded from MCF institutional authority
- Most sensitive final-status issue
- Any MCF involvement would be framework-collapsing
- Requires dedicated political process

**MCF Role:**
- May operate programs in Jerusalem (separate provisions)
- No governance or adjudication authority
- Explicit exclusion in all dispute mechanisms

### 7. Refugee Final Status

**Excluded:**
- Right of return adjudication
- Refugee compensation determination
- Refugee political status
- Refugee resettlement rights

**Rationale:**
- Final-status issue requiring political resolution
- MCF not designed to resolve refugee question
- Adjudication would prejudice negotiations
- Parties retain positions on refugee rights

**MCF Role:**
- Refugee participation in CMS (voluntary)
- Service provision to refugees (humanitarian)
- No adjudication of final-status rights

### 8. Religious Authority

**Excluded:**
- Religious site control
- Religious law interpretation
- Religious community governance
- Inter-religious disputes (theological)

**Rationale:**
- Religious authority outside MCF mandate
- Highly sensitive to all communities
- Existing religious authority structures
- MCF secular framework inappropriate

**MCF Role:**
- Protection of religious freedom (CRC)
- Access facilitation (operational)
- No religious authority adjudication

## Jurisdictional Screening

### 1. Screening Process

**At Filing:**
- Complaint reviewed for jurisdictional basis
- Subject matter screened against exclusions
- Sovereignty implications assessed
- Referral if outside jurisdiction

**During Proceedings:**
- Ongoing jurisdictional monitoring
- Party challenges permitted
- Panel/arbitrator authority to dismiss
- Scope narrowing if needed

### 2. Screening Criteria

**Subject Matter Test:**
- Does complaint fall within permitted categories?
- Is subject matter explicitly excluded?
- Are sovereignty implications present?
- Can matter be resolved without prejudice?

**Party Test:**
- Are parties within MCF jurisdiction?
- Do parties have standing?
- Is there proper consent to jurisdiction?

**Remedy Test:**
- Can MCF provide effective remedy?
- Is remedy within MCF authority?
- Would remedy prejudice excluded matters?

### 3. Screening Outcomes

**Accepted:**
- Proceed to appropriate mechanism
- Scope defined
- Prejudice safeguards noted

**Referred:**
- Outside MCF jurisdiction
- Referred to appropriate forum
- Assistance with referral where possible

**Dismissed:**
- No jurisdiction
- Written explanation
- Preservation of external options

### 4. Challenge Procedures

**Party Challenge:**
- Either party may challenge jurisdiction
- Challenge filed in writing
- Response opportunity
- Determination by panel/arbitrator

**Sua Sponte Dismissal:**
- Adjudicator may raise jurisdiction
- Notice to parties
- Opportunity to address
- Dismissal if jurisdiction lacking

## Boundary Management

### 1. Scope Definition

**At Proceeding Initiation:**
- Issues defined
- Scope boundaries stated
- Excluded matters identified
- Prejudice safeguards specified

**During Proceedings:**
- Scope maintained
- Drift monitored
- Scope creep prevented
- Refocusing as needed

### 2. Mixed Matters

**When Dispute Has Both Included and Excluded Elements:**
- Separate elements identified
- Included elements proceed
- Excluded elements not addressed
- Non-prejudice maintained

**Severability:**
- Proceed on severable included matters
- Stay or refer excluded matters
- Coordination with external processes

### 3. Peripheral Sovereignty Implications

**When Included Matter Has Peripheral Sovereignty Implications:**
- Narrow framing
- Explicit non-prejudice statements
- Avoid reasoning that could establish sovereignty positions
- Remedy limited to non-sovereignty dimensions

### 4. Escalating Matters

**When Initially Included Matter Becomes Sovereignty-Adjacent:**
- Pause proceedings
- Reassess jurisdiction
- Narrow scope or dismiss
- Non-prejudice documentation

## Forum Selection

### 1. Appropriate Forum Determination

**Arbitration Courts When:**
- Individual-to-individual dispute
- Commercial or civil matter
- Parties consented to arbitration
- Remedy within MCF authority

**Review Panels When:**
- Individual-to-authority dispute
- CRC or procedural violation alleged
- Framework-internal remedy sought
- Authority within MCF system

**Institutional Dispute Process When:**
- Institution-to-institution conflict
- Mandate or procedure interpretation
- MCF operational matter
- Institutional parties

### 2. Forum Shopping Prevention

**Anti-Forum Shopping Measures:**
- Clear jurisdictional rules
- First-filed priority
- Consolidation authority
- Transfer procedures

**When Multiple Forums Possible:**
- Primary forum identified
- Coordination between forums
- Sequential or parallel determination
- Consistency maintained

### 3. Referral Procedures

**Internal Referral:**
- From one MCF mechanism to another
- With party consent where required
- Coordinated transfer
- Record continuity

**External Referral:**
- To national courts
- To international mechanisms
- To guarantor processes
- With documentation

## Non-Prejudice Implementation

### 1. Determination Language

**Required in All Determinations:**
- Explicit non-prejudice statement
- Scope limitation acknowledgment
- No sovereignty reasoning
- Preservation of claims statement

**Example Non-Prejudice Statement:**
> "This determination addresses only the procedural and operational matters within MCF jurisdiction. It does not address, and shall not be construed to address, any question of sovereignty, territorial boundaries, or final political status. All parties' positions on such matters remain entirely unaffected by this proceeding and determination."

### 2. Reasoning Constraints

**Prohibited Reasoning:**
- "This territory belongs to..."
- "Sovereignty over this area..."
- "The legitimate authority is..."
- "The border should be..."

**Permitted Reasoning:**
- "MCF procedures require..."
- "The CRC guarantees..."
- "Operational arrangements provide..."
- "Framework-internal remedies include..."

### 3. Precedent Limitations

**Determinations Cannot:**
- Establish sovereignty precedent
- Be cited for sovereignty claims
- Create territorial rights
- Prejudice political negotiations

**Determinations Can:**
- Guide future MCF proceedings
- Establish procedural precedent
- Clarify MCF operational matters
- Inform MCF policy development

### 4. Violation Response

**If Non-Prejudice Violated:**
- Determination subject to challenge
- Offending reasoning struck
- Revised determination issued
- Adjudicator accountability

## External Coordination

### 1. National Court Relations

**MCF Does Not:**
- Override national court jurisdiction
- Claim superiority to national courts
- Interfere with national proceedings
- Enforce against national court orders

**MCF Does:**
- Provide parallel forum for MCF matters
- Seek recognition where possible
- Coordinate to avoid conflict
- Respect national court authority

### 2. International Body Relations

**MCF Does Not:**
- Replace international courts
- Adjudicate international law
- Interpret UN resolutions
- Substitute for international accountability

**MCF Does:**
- Document and preserve evidence
- Refer matters to international bodies
- Cooperate with international processes
- Complement international mechanisms

### 3. Guarantor Coordination

**MCF Dispute Mechanisms:**
- Inform guarantors of significant matters
- Seek guarantor support for enforcement
- Coordinate with guarantor processes
- Respect guarantor accountability mechanisms

## Documentation and Records

### 1. Jurisdictional Records

**Maintained:**
- All jurisdictional determinations
- Screening decisions
- Referrals made
- Scope limitations applied

**Purpose:**
- Consistency
- Precedent guidance
- Transparency
- Accountability

### 2. Non-Prejudice Documentation

**Every Proceeding:**
- Non-prejudice statement recorded
- Scope limitations documented
- Excluded matters noted
- Preservation of claims acknowledged

### 3. Access and Publication

**Accessible:**
- Published determinations (redacted)
- Jurisdictional guidance
- Procedural rules

**Restricted:**
- Party submissions (privacy)
- Deliberations
- Confidential evidence
