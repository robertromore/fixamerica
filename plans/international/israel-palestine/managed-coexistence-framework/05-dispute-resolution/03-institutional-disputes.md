## Purpose

Institution-to-Institution Dispute Resolution handles **conflicts between MCF institutions**â€”disagreements between JSVC units, host states, guarantors, SAZ administrators, and other Framework bodies.

The tiered mediation and certification process prevents institutional disputes from paralyzing MCF operations while respecting the distinct mandates and authorities of different institutions.

---

## Institutional Dispute Principles

### 1. Operational Continuity Priority

Disputes must not halt MCF operations:
- Interim arrangements during resolution
- Essential functions continue
- Deadlines for resolution
- Default positions if unresolved

### 2. Mandate Respect

Each institution has legitimate authority:
- Disputes involve mandate interpretation
- No institution claims superior authority over others
- Resolution clarifies boundaries
- Autonomy within mandates preserved

### 3. No Sovereignty Adjudication

Institutional disputes cannot:
- Determine territorial sovereignty
- Establish political claims
- Prejudice final status
- Create legal precedent for sovereignty

### 4. Collective Interest

Resolution serves MCF as a whole:
- Not zero-sum between institutions
- Framework success is shared goal
- Compromise orientation
- Creative solutions encouraged

## Dispute Categories

### 1. Mandate Interpretation Disputes

**Nature:**
- Overlapping authority claims
- Gaps in assigned responsibility
- Ambiguous mandate language
- Changed circumstances requiring interpretation

**Examples:**
- JSVC vs. SAZ police authority boundary
- Guarantor role scope disagreement
- Verification vs. governance function overlap
- Cross-border vs. internal jurisdiction

### 2. Procedural Compliance Disputes

**Nature:**
- Alleged failure to follow MCF procedures
- Process shortcut claims
- Documentation requirements disagreement
- Timeline compliance conflicts

**Examples:**
- Verification procedure not followed
- Notification requirements missed
- Approval process bypassed
- Reporting obligations unmet

### 3. Resource Allocation Disputes

**Nature:**
- Funding distribution disagreements
- Personnel assignment conflicts
- Facility use disputes
- Equipment allocation conflicts

**Examples:**
- Budget allocation between units
- Staff secondment terms
- Shared facility scheduling
- Equipment priority conflicts

### 4. Cooperation Disputes

**Nature:**
- Alleged failure to cooperate
- Information sharing conflicts
- Coordination breakdowns
- Support obligation disputes

**Examples:**
- Intelligence sharing refusal
- Joint operation coordination failure
- Cross-referral delays
- Support request denial

### 5. Performance Disputes

**Nature:**
- Alleged failure to meet standards
- Performance evaluation disagreements
- Accountability conflicts
- Outcome responsibility disputes

**Examples:**
- JSVC unit performance assessment
- SAZ governance quality dispute
- Guarantor commitment fulfillment
- Verification quality disagreement

## Tiered Resolution Process

### Tier 1: Direct Negotiation

**Process:**
- Parties attempt direct resolution
- Senior institutional representatives engage
- Good-faith negotiation required
- Documentation of discussions

**Timeline:**
- 30 days from dispute identification
- Extension by mutual agreement

**Outcome:**
- Negotiated agreement
- Joint protocol
- Escalation if unresolved

**Documentation:**
- Written record of positions
- Agreed points documented
- Remaining disputes identified

### Tier 2: Structured Mediation

**Trigger:**
- Direct negotiation unsuccessful
- Either party requests mediation
- 30-day deadline passed

**Mediator Selection:**
- Institutional Continuity Body leads
- Neutral mediator from MCF Secretariat
- No connection to disputing parties
- Professional mediation qualifications

**Process:**
- Joint session(s) with mediator
- Caucus sessions as needed
- Issue clarification
- Option development
- Agreement facilitation

**Timeline:**
- 45 days from mediation initiation
- Extension by mediator recommendation

**Outcome:**
- Mediated agreement
- Partial agreement with remaining issues identified
- Escalation if unresolved

**Documentation:**
- Mediation summary
- Agreement terms (if reached)
- Unresolved issues (for escalation)

### Tier 3: Guarantor Review Panel

**Trigger:**
- Mediation unsuccessful
- 45-day mediation period passed
- Either party requests escalation

**Panel Composition:**
- Three guarantors
- Functionally distinct (verification, leverage, regional)
- No guarantor party to dispute
- No guarantor directly implicated

**Panel Selection:**
- Guarantor Forum Chair designates
- Recusal for conflicts
- Balance considerations

**Process:**
- Written submissions by parties
- Response opportunity
- Oral presentation (if needed)
- Panel deliberation
- Written determination

**Timeline:**
- 60 days from escalation
- Extension for complex matters

**Determination Authority:**
- Binding procedural clarifications
- Mandate adjustments
- Resource allocation decisions
- Cooperation requirements
- Performance standards

**Determination Limits:**
- Cannot determine sovereignty
- Cannot prejudice final status
- Cannot override institutional autonomy on core mandate
- Cannot impose financial obligations beyond MCF resources

### Tier 4: Confederal Council Reference

**Trigger:**
- Guarantor panel cannot resolve
- Matter affects fundamental MCF design
- Constitutional interpretation needed
- Either party requests (with justification)

**Process:**
- Reference to Confederal Council
- Advisory opinion sought
- Council considers and advises
- Implementation by relevant bodies

**When Used:**
- Framework design questions
- Fundamental mandate issues
- Cross-cutting policy matters
- Precedent-setting interpretations

**Outcome:**
- Council guidance
- Framework clarification
- Potential amendment initiation

## Specific Dispute Contexts

### 1. JSVC Internal Disputes

**Between JSVC Units:**
- Mediation by JSVC leadership
- Escalation to MCF Secretariat
- Guarantor panel if unresolved

**JSVC vs. UVB:**
- Joint management committee first
- MCF Secretariat mediation
- Guarantor panel escalation

### 2. SAZ-Related Disputes

**Between SAZ Administrations:**
- Mediation by MCF Secretariat
- Guarantor panel if unresolved

**SAZ vs. MCF Central:**
- MCF Executive Committee mediation
- Guarantor panel if unresolved

**SAZ vs. Host/Neighboring Authority:**
- Diplomatic channel engagement
- Guarantor involvement
- Escalation as needed

### 3. Guarantor-Related Disputes

**Between Guarantors:**
- Guarantor Forum internal process
- Chair mediation
- External mediation if needed

**Guarantor vs. MCF Institution:**
- MCF Secretariat mediation
- Different guarantors on panel (recusal)
- Confederal Council if needed

### 4. Host State Disputes

**MCF vs. Host State:**
- Diplomatic engagement
- Guarantor involvement
- International mediation if needed

**Note:** Host states are not subject to MCF internal processes except by agreement.

## Interim Arrangements

### 1. Status Quo Maintenance

**During Resolution:**
- Existing arrangements continue
- No unilateral changes
- Essential functions maintained
- Harm minimization

### 2. Emergency Measures

**When Needed:**
- Immediate harm threatened
- Essential function at risk
- Security concern

**Process:**
- Emergency application
- Rapid assessment
- Interim order
- Review at resolution

### 3. Temporary Authority

**If Authority Uncertain:**
- Designate temporary authority
- Limited to essential functions
- Pending dispute resolution
- Clear termination trigger

## Outcomes and Implementation

### 1. Agreement Types

**Negotiated Agreement:**
- Binding on parties
- Self-enforced
- Monitored by MCF Secretariat

**Mediated Agreement:**
- Binding on parties
- Registered with Secretariat
- Compliance monitored

**Panel Determination:**
- Binding on parties
- Implemented as directed
- Guarantor enforcement support

### 2. Implementation Monitoring

**Monitoring Process:**
- Designated monitor
- Progress reports
- Compliance verification
- Issue escalation if needed

**Non-Implementation Consequences:**
- Escalation to guarantors
- Resource implications
- Institutional accountability

### 3. Precedent Effect

**Determinations:**
- Guide future disputes
- Not strictly binding precedent
- Persuasive authority
- Consistency valued

**Publication:**
- Determinations published
- Privacy redactions
- Institutional library maintained

## Relationship to Other Mechanisms

### 1. Individual Disputes

- Institutional disputes separate from individual complaints
- Individual may trigger institutional review
- Institutional resolution may affect individual cases
- Coordination between mechanisms

### 2. Guarantor Accountability

- Guarantors subject to dispute process (as parties)
- Recusal when implicated
- Forum accountability mechanisms apply
- Separate from guarantor coordination

### 3. External Relations

- Host state disputes may involve external diplomacy
- Guarantor states have external relationships
- MCF mechanisms primary for internal matters
- External escalation as last resort
