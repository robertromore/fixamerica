## Purpose

Confederal Arbitration Courts provide **neutral, predictable dispute resolution** for conflicts between individuals participating in MCF programs.

Arbitration does not replace national courts. It offers a venue where cross-jurisdictional disputes would otherwise be unresolvableâ€”where different legal systems, different enforcement mechanisms, and different political loyalties make national courts inadequate for disputes crossing community boundaries.

---

## Arbitration Principles

### 1. Civil, Non-Criminal Jurisdiction

Arbitration courts handle civil matters only:
- Commercial disputes
- Contractual conflicts
- Labor disagreements
- Property disputes
- CMS-related conduct issues

Criminal matters remain outside MCF jurisdiction.

### 2. Voluntary, Benefit-Linked Participation

Arbitration is opt-in:
- Participation linked to CMS membership or program enrollment
- Opting in is a condition of certain Framework benefits
- Parties choose arbitration; it is not imposed

This voluntary character preserves sovereignty while providing dispute resolution access.

### 3. Procedural Neutrality

Arbitration procedures are:
- Standardized across all cases
- Anchored in due process principles
- Transparent in evidence handling
- Equal in access regardless of community

No procedural advantage for either party based on identity.

### 4. Limited Enforcement

Enforcement is limited to MCF benefit scope:
- Contract enforcement within Framework
- Credential suspension
- Access revocation
- Benefit withholding

Arbitration cannot enforce beyond MCF's own authority.

## Court Structure

### 1. Arbitration Panels

**Composition:**
- Three arbitrators per panel
- Community balance (one from each major community, one neutral/international)
- Professional qualifications required
- Conflict-of-interest screening

**Selection:**
- Arbitrators drawn from qualified pool
- Parties may strike limited number
- Random assignment from remaining pool
- Presiding arbitrator selected by panel or rotation

### 2. Arbitrator Qualifications

**Required:**
- Legal training (law degree or equivalent)
- 5+ years relevant professional experience
- Arbitration certification (recognized program)
- Language proficiency (Arabic, Hebrew, English)
- No disqualifying conflicts

**Preferred:**
- Commercial law expertise
- Cross-cultural dispute resolution experience
- International arbitration experience
- Technical expertise relevant to dispute types

### 3. Arbitrator Pool Management

**Pool Composition:**
- Minimum 30 qualified arbitrators
- Community balance maintained
- Geographic distribution
- Specialty coverage

**Pool Administration:**
- Administered by MCF Secretariat
- Annual qualification review
- Performance evaluation
- Removal for cause

### 4. Administrative Support

**Secretariat Functions:**
- Case intake and registration
- Panel assignment
- Scheduling and logistics
- Record keeping
- Decision publication
- Fee administration

**Staffing:**
- Registrar (legal professional)
- Case managers
- Administrative support
- Translation services

## Procedures

### 1. Filing and Intake

**Filing Requirements:**
- Written complaint specifying:
  - Parties involved
  - Nature of dispute
  - Relief sought
  - Basis for arbitration jurisdiction
  - Relevant documentation
- Filing fee (means-tested)
- Confirmation of arbitration agreement

**Intake Process:**
- Jurisdictional screening
- Respondent notification
- Response deadline (30 days)
- Panel assignment

### 2. Pre-Hearing Procedures

**Case Management Conference:**
- Issues identification
- Document exchange schedule
- Witness identification
- Hearing scheduling
- Procedural agreements

**Discovery:**
- Limited document exchange
- Written interrogatories (limited)
- No depositions (cost control)
- Privilege protections

**Motions:**
- Jurisdictional challenges
- Dismissal motions
- Interim measures requests

### 3. Hearing Procedures

**Hearing Format:**
- In-person preferred, remote permitted
- Interpretation services provided
- Evidentiary standards specified
- Witness examination
- Party presentations
- Closing arguments

**Evidentiary Standards:**
- Relevance requirement
- Authentication standards
- Hearsay limitations (flexible)
- Expert testimony procedures
- Document admissibility

**Record:**
- Full transcription
- Recording (audio/video)
- Exhibit preservation
- Confidentiality maintained

### 4. Decision and Award

**Decision Timeline:**
- Award within 60 days of hearing close
- Extensions by panel decision (limited)

**Award Content:**
- Findings of fact
- Legal conclusions
- Relief awarded
- Costs allocation
- Reasoning explanation

**Award Form:**
- Written, signed by panel majority
- Dissent permitted
- Published (redacted for privacy)

### 5. Appeal Rights

**Grounds for Appeal:**
- Procedural irregularity affecting outcome
- Arbitrator misconduct or bias
- Award exceeds jurisdiction
- Manifest error of law

**Appeal Process:**
- Filed within 30 days
- Appellate panel (different arbitrators)
- Record review, not de novo
- Affirm, reverse, or remand

**Finality:**
- Appellate decision final within MCF
- No further internal appeal

## Subject Matter Jurisdiction

### 1. Commercial Disputes

**Included:**
- Contract disputes between CMS participants
- Business-to-business conflicts
- Service agreements
- Licensing disputes
- Partnership disagreements

**Requirements:**
- Both parties CMS participants or program enrollees
- Arbitration agreement (in contract or program terms)
- Commercial character of dispute

### 2. Labor Disputes

**Included:**
- Employment contract disputes
- Wage and benefit claims
- Wrongful termination (employment aspect only)
- Workplace condition disputes
- Labor agreement interpretation

**Limitations:**
- Individual disputes only (not collective bargaining)
- Employment relationship, not union matters
- Civil remedies only (no criminal sanctions)

### 3. Property Disputes

**Included:**
- Movable property disputes
- Commercial lease disputes
- Intellectual property (within MCF programs)
- Damage claims

**Excluded:**
- Real property ownership (sovereignty implications)
- Land title disputes
- Territorial claims
- Religious property

### 4. CMS-Related Conduct

**Included:**
- Disputes arising from CMS participation
- Benefit access conflicts
- Credential recognition disputes
- Program eligibility disagreements

**Limitations:**
- Between participants (not authority disputes)
- CMS revocation itself goes to review panels
- Cannot override CMS administrative decisions

## Enforcement

### 1. Framework-Internal Enforcement

**Enforcement Tools:**
- Withhold Framework benefits pending compliance
- Credential suspension
- Program access revocation
- CMS standing notation
- Guarantor-supported pressure

**Enforcement Process:**
- Award becomes enforceable after appeal period
- Non-compliance documented
- Notice to non-compliant party
- Enforcement measures activated
- Compliance restoration upon satisfaction

### 2. Recognition Agreements

**Domestic Court Recognition:**
- Pursue recognition agreements with both jurisdictions
- Awards treated as enforceable judgments where recognized
- Enforcement through domestic court mechanisms

**Recognition Limitations:**
- Depends on domestic court cooperation
- May face political obstacles
- Alternative: Framework-internal enforcement

**International Recognition:**
- New York Convention applicability explored
- International arbitration standards followed
- Cross-border enforcement where possible

### 3. Voluntary Compliance Incentives

**Compliance Benefits:**
- Maintained CMS standing
- Continued program access
- Good-faith notation in records
- Future dispute resolution access

**Non-Compliance Consequences:**
- CMS standing notation
- Benefit withholding
- Program exclusion
- Reputational effect

## Fees and Costs

### 1. Filing Fees

**Fee Structure:**
- Base filing fee: $100-500 (scaled by claim value)
- Expedited processing: Additional fee
- Small claims track: Reduced fee

**Means Testing:**
- Fee waiver for demonstrated need
- Payment plans available
- No denial of access for inability to pay

### 2. Arbitrator Fees

**Fee Calculation:**
- Daily rate for hearing days
- Preparation time (capped)
- Decision drafting time

**Fee Sharing:**
- Default: Equal split
- Award may reallocate based on outcome
- Prevailing party recovery possible

### 3. Administrative Costs

**Covered by MCF:**
- Secretariat operations
- Facilities
- Basic interpretation
- Record keeping

**Party-Paid:**
- Expert witnesses
- Special interpretation
- Expedited processing
- Document production beyond basic

### 4. Cost Recovery

**In Award:**
- Arbitrators may allocate costs
- Factors: Outcome, party conduct, resources
- No automatic prevailing party rule

**Limits:**
- No costs creating access barrier
- Proportionality to claim value
- Hardship considerations

## Special Procedures

### 1. Small Claims Track

**Eligibility:**
- Claims under $5,000
- Simple factual disputes
- Limited parties

**Simplified Procedures:**
- Single arbitrator
- Document-only option
- Abbreviated timeline
- Reduced fees

### 2. Expedited Procedures

**Eligibility:**
- Urgent matters
- Perishable goods
- Time-sensitive contracts
- Party agreement

**Accelerated Timeline:**
- Hearing within 30 days
- Decision within 15 days of hearing
- Limited discovery
- Additional fees

### 3. Interim Measures

**Available Measures:**
- Asset preservation
- Status quo maintenance
- Harm prevention
- Evidence preservation

**Interim Measure Process:**
- Emergency application
- Panel or emergency arbitrator
- Expedited consideration
- Reviewable at hearing

## Confidentiality

### 1. Proceeding Confidentiality

**Confidential:**
- Hearing proceedings
- Submitted documents
- Witness testimony
- Deliberations

**Not Confidential:**
- Existence of proceeding (unless agreed)
- Final award (redacted for publication)
- Precedential holdings

### 2. Party Privacy

**Protected:**
- Personal information
- Business confidential information
- Health information
- Financial details

**Disclosure Permitted:**
- To enforcement authorities
- To guarantors when relevant
- For accountability purposes
- With party consent

### 3. Publication

**Published (Redacted):**
- Decision summaries
- Procedural rulings
- Precedential interpretations

**Purpose:**
- Transparency
- Precedent development
- Public accountability
- System credibility
