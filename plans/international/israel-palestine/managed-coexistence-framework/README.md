# Managed Coexistence Framework (MCF)

**A Security-First Architecture for Living Together During Ongoing Conflict**

---

## Overview

The Managed Coexistence Framework (MCF) is a comprehensive conflict management architecture designed for the Israeli-Palestinian context. Unlike traditional peace processes that require political agreement before building institutions, MCF inverts this logic: it builds functioning institutions first, creating conditions under which political agreement becomes possible.

MCF is designed to:

- Begin operating without requiring mutual consent from conflict parties
- Provide immediate, measurable benefits to participants
- Scale incrementally based on demonstrated success
- Preserve all political options while delivering practical improvements

---

## Key Concepts

| Term | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **JSVC** | Joint Security Verification Commission - permanent verification body |
| **UVB** | Unilateral Verification Body - initial mechanism before JSVC |
| **PCC** | Protected Civic Corridor - single-purpose shared infrastructure |
| **CMS** | Civic Membership Status - voluntary legal status for participants |
| **SAZ** | Special Administrative Zone - territorial pilot governance areas |
| **CRC** | Confederal Rights Compact - charter of rights for CMS holders |

---

## Phase Progression

MCF operates through four phases, each building on the previous:

| Phase | Timeline | Focus |
|-------|----------|-------|
| **Phase 0** | Years 0-5 | Security layer, verification institutions, initial infrastructure |
| **Phase 1** | Years 5-20 | Opt-in civic membership, rights protections, economic access |
| **Phase 2** | Years 20-50 | Special Administrative Zones with shared governance |
| **Phase 3** | Years 50-100+ | Voluntary convergence (confederation, federation, or dual-state) |

Phase transitions are criteria-based, not calendar-based. Phases can regress if conditions deteriorate.

## Core Design Principles

1. **Security First**: Verification and deconfliction precede political arrangements
2. **Voluntary Participation**: No one is forced to participate; benefits attract enrollment
3. **No Worse Off**: Participants cannot be made worse off than non-participants
4. **Incremental Scaling**: Success in one area enables expansion to others
5. **Non-Predetermination**: Framework preserves all political end-states
6. **External Guarantees**: International guarantors provide credibility and backstop
7. **Rights Protection**: CRC protections cannot be stripped, only expanded
