## Purpose

The Guarantor Coordination Protocol defines how **multiple guarantors work together** to support MCF without creating coordination failures or conflicts among guarantors.

Multiple guarantors provide redundancy, credibility, and diverse capacityâ€”but they also create coordination challenges. Without clear protocols, guarantors may duplicate efforts, work at cross-purposes, or create confusion about roles. This document establishes the coordination architecture that prevents these failures.

---

## Coordination Principles

### 1. Collective Responsibility

All guarantors share responsibility for MCF success. No guarantor can claim success alone, and no guarantor can disclaim responsibility for collective failures.

**Collective Responsibility Means:**
- Shared commitment to MCF outcomes
- Mutual support among guarantors
- Gap coverage when one falters
- Collective accountability for results

### 2. Specialized Roles

While responsibility is collective, execution is specialized. Different guarantors lead on different functions based on capacity and comparative advantage.

**Specialization Means:**
- Lead guarantor assignments by function
- Clear role delineation
- Expertise concentration
- Accountability clarity

### 3. Non-Interference Among Guarantors

Guarantors do not interfere in each other's assigned functions or use guarantor coordination to pursue bilateral disputes unrelated to MCF.

**Non-Interference Means:**
- Respect assigned roles
- No undermining of other guarantors
- Bilateral disputes stay bilateral
- MCF focus maintained

### 4. MCF Institutional Primacy

Guarantors support MCF institutions; they do not replace them. Coordination among guarantors serves MCF institutional needs, not guarantor preferences.

**MCF Primacy Means:**
- MCF institutions set priorities
- Guarantors respond to MCF needs
- No guarantor diktat to MCF
- MCF outcome focus

## Coordination Structure

### 1. Guarantor Forum

**Purpose:**
The Guarantor Forum is the primary coordination body for collective guarantor action.

**Composition:**
- Representative from each state guarantor (ambassador level)
- Representative from each IO guarantor
- MCF Secretary-General (observer, non-voting)
- Confederal Council liaison (observer, non-voting)

**Leadership:**
- Chair rotates annually among state guarantors
- Vice-Chair from different region than Chair
- Secretariat support from MCF

**Meeting Frequency:**
- Regular meetings: Monthly
- Urgent meetings: As needed (24-hour convening)
- Annual strategic review: Once yearly

**Authority:**

- Coordination of guarantor activities
- Resource allocation recommendations
- New guarantor approval (with Confederal Council concurrence; see [Guarantor Selection](01-guarantor-selection.md#3-approval-requirements))
- Guarantor accountability

### 2. Specialized Committees

#### 2.1 Security Committee

**Composition:**
- Guarantors with security functions
- MCF Security Coordinator
- SAZ police liaison (when relevant)

**Functions:**
- Security response coordination
- Training coordination
- Intelligence sharing protocols
- Rapid-response planning

**Meeting Frequency:**
- Monthly routine
- Immediate for incidents

#### 2.2 Financial Committee

**Composition:**
- Major donor representatives
- MCF Chief Financial Officer
- Audit committee liaison

**Functions:**
- Funding coordination
- Gap identification
- Burden-sharing negotiations
- Financial accountability

**Meeting Frequency:**
- Quarterly routine
- As needed for issues

#### 2.3 Verification Committee

**Composition:**
- Guarantors with verification roles
- JSVC representative
- UVB representative

**Functions:**
- Verification coordination
- Technical assistance planning
- Capacity building
- Methodology alignment

**Meeting Frequency:**
- Monthly routine

#### 2.4 Other Specialized Bodies

**As Needed:**
- Ad hoc working groups
- Task forces for specific issues
- Phase-specific coordination bodies

### 3. Secretariat

**Functions:**
- Administrative support for Guarantor Forum
- Meeting preparation and documentation
- Information management
- Communication coordination
- Calendar management

**Location:**
- Housed within MCF Secretariat
- Operationally independent for guarantor coordination
- Staff from guarantor secondments + MCF

**Staffing:**
- Director (senior diplomat, rotating)
- Professional staff (5-10)
- Administrative support

## Decision-Making

### 1. Consensus Procedures

**Default Decision Mode: Consensus**

**Consensus Defined:**
- No guarantor formally objects
- Silence = assent after reasonable opportunity to object
- Concerns recorded even without objection

**Consensus Process:**
- Proposal circulated
- Discussion period (7 days minimum for significant decisions)
- Revision based on input
- Final proposal for adoption
- Adoption if no objection

### 2. Voting Procedures

**When Voting Applies:**
- Consensus not achievable after good-faith effort
- Time-sensitive decisions
- Matters designated for voting

**Voting Thresholds (computed by weighted vote):**

| Decision Type | Threshold |
|--------------|-----------|
| Routine coordination | Simple majority |
| Resource allocation | 2/3 majority |
| New guarantor admission | 2/3 majority |
| Guarantor sanction/removal | 3/4 majority |
| Framework amendments | Consensus preferred, 3/4 fallback |

**Voting Weights:**

- One guarantor, one vote (state guarantors)
- IO guarantors: Proportional to contribution (capped)
- No single guarantor >20% of weighted vote

**Threshold Calculation:** Thresholds are calculated against total weighted vote, not headcount. For example, if total weighted votes equal 100, a 2/3 threshold requires 67+ weighted votes in favor.

### 3. Deadlock Resolution

**If Deadlock Persists:**
1. Escalate to ministerial/principal level
2. Mediation by neutral party
3. Refer to MCF Confederal Council for input
4. Default to status quo if no resolution

**Deadlock Prevention:**
- Early consultation on sensitive issues
- Shuttle diplomacy by Chair
- Small group pre-negotiation
- Trade-off packaging

### 4. Emergency Decisions

**Emergency Decision Authority:**
- Chair + 2 other guarantors can authorize emergency action
- Full Forum ratification within 72 hours
- Emergency defined: Imminent threat requiring immediate response

**Emergency Scope:**
- Security responses
- Humanitarian emergencies
- Financial emergencies
- Political crises

## Burden-Sharing

### 1. Responsibility Allocation

**General Principle:**
- Responsibilities allocated based on capacity and commitment
- Each guarantor has assigned lead and supporting roles
- All guarantors contribute to core functions

**Role Categories:**
- Lead roles: Primary responsibility for function
- Supporting roles: Backup and supplementary support
- Contributing roles: Proportionate contribution
- Observer roles: Information and consultation

### 2. Capacity-Based Contributions

**Financial Contributions:**
- Based on economic capacity and commitment tier
- Minimum floor for all guarantors
- Additional contributions voluntary

**Personnel Contributions:**
- Based on capacity and specialized expertise
- Rotation to share burden
- Quality standards maintained

**Material Contributions:**
- Equipment, technology, facilities
- Based on capacity and need
- Standardization where possible

### 3. Rotation Mechanisms

**What Rotates:**
- Forum Chair and Vice-Chair
- Committee chairs
- Lead roles (periodic review)
- Secretariat leadership

**Rotation Principles:**
- Regional rotation
- No single guarantor dominance
- Capacity considerations
- Continuity balance

### 4. Shortfall Management

**When Guarantor Falls Short:**
- Early warning to Forum
- Gap analysis
- Burden redistribution
- Recovery plan
- Accountability discussion

**Temporary Coverage:**
- Other guarantors cover critical functions
- Documented as exceptional
- Cost-sharing for coverage
- Restoration timeline

## Lead Guarantor Arrangements

### 1. Lead Roles by Function

**Illustrative Lead Assignments:**

| Function | Lead Guarantor | Rationale |
|----------|---------------|-----------|
| Security rapid-response | US, EU (coordinating member-state forces) | Military capacity |

**Lead Role Requirements:**

- Credibility in function
- Commitment to role
- Coordination capability

- Demonstrated capacity
- Track record
- Commitment level
- Stakeholder acceptance

**Selection Process:**
- Nomination (self or other)
- Capacity assessment
- Forum approval
- Formal assignment

### 3. Lead Accountability

**Lead Guarantor Responsibilities:**
- Primary accountability for function
- Coordination of other guarantors in function
- Reporting on function performance
- Gap identification and escalation

**Lead Guarantor Reports:**
- Quarterly to Forum
- Annual performance review
- Immediate on significant issues

### 4. Lead Rotation

**Rotation Considerations:**
- Continuity needs
- Capacity changes
- Performance issues
- Equity concerns

**Rotation Process:**
- 3-year initial assignments
- Review at renewal
- Transition planning
- Handoff protocols

## Information Sharing

### 1. Information Classification

**Classification Levels:**

| Level | Description | Sharing |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| Public | No restrictions | Open |
| Internal | Guarantor use only | Forum members |
| Restricted | Need-to-know | Designated individuals |
| Confidential | Highly sensitive | Principals only |

**Classification Authority:**
- Originating guarantor classifies
- Receiving guarantor honors classification
- Downgrade by originator or Forum

### 2. Sharing Protocols

**Routine Sharing:**
- Monthly situation reports
- Quarterly function reports
- Annual assessments
- Meeting documentation

**Event-Driven Sharing:**
- Incident reports (immediate)
- Threat assessments (as developed)
- Political developments (as relevant)

**Request-Based Sharing:**
- Information requests through Secretariat
- Response within agreed timelines
- Refusal with explanation

### 3. Confidentiality Requirements

**Guarantor Confidentiality Obligations:**
- Protect classified information
- No unauthorized disclosure
- Secure handling and storage
- Personnel vetting

**Breach Consequences:**
- Access restriction
- Formal reprimand
- Role limitation
- Removal consideration

### 4. Public Communication

**Coordinated Messaging:**
- Common positions developed
- Coordinated announcement timing
- No unilateral statements undermining Forum positions

**Individual Communication:**
- National positions permitted
- No contradiction of Forum positions
- Coordination on sensitive matters
- MCF interests considered

## Dispute Resolution

### 1. Guarantor-to-Guarantor Disputes

**Types of Disputes:**
- Role and responsibility disputes
- Performance disagreements
- Resource allocation disputes
- Position conflicts

**Dispute Principles:**
- Good-faith resolution
- MCF interests paramount
- Bilateral disputes stay bilateral
- Forum focus on MCF-related disputes

### 2. Mediation Mechanisms

**First Level: Forum Chair Mediation**
- Chair facilitates discussion
- Proposes compromise
- Escalates if unresolved

**Second Level: Ministerial Engagement**
- Senior level involvement
- Direct principal discussion
- Political resolution

**Third Level: External Mediation**
- Neutral third party
- UN Secretary-General or designee
- Binding if agreed

### 3. Escalation Procedures

**Escalation Triggers:**
- Working-level impasse >30 days
- Dispute affecting MCF operations
- Public disagreement
- Request by either party

**Escalation Path:**
1. Forum working level
2. Forum Chair
3. Ministerial level
4. External mediation
5. Agreed resolution or managed disagreement

### 4. Managed Disagreement

**If Dispute Not Resolved:**
- Document disagreement
- Agree on modus vivendi
- Isolate dispute from MCF operations
- Review periodically

## Collective Accountability

### 1. Performance Assessment

**Annual Assessment:**
- Self-assessment by Forum
- MCF institutional input
- External review (periodic)
- Public summary

**Assessment Criteria:**
- MCF outcome achievement
- Coordination effectiveness
- Resource deployment
- Relationship with MCF institutions

### 2. Mutual Accountability

**Guarantors Accountable To Each Other:**
- Commitment fulfillment
- Role performance
- Coordination quality
- Fair burden-sharing

**Mutual Accountability Mechanisms:**
- Peer review
- Transparent reporting
- Forum discussion
- Performance feedback

### 3. External Review

**Periodic External Review:**
- Every 3 years
- Independent evaluation
- MCF stakeholder input
- Public report

**Review Scope:**
- Guarantor performance
- Coordination effectiveness
- MCF relationship
- Recommendations

### 4. Correction Mechanisms

**Performance Improvement:**
- Forum identifies issues
- Corrective action agreed
- Implementation monitored
- Progress reviewed

**Escalation for Persistent Issues:**
- Formal performance improvement plan
- Enhanced oversight
- Role limitation
- Removal consideration
