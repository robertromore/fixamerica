# Guarantor Withdrawal Procedures

**Parent Document:** [00-introduction.md](../00-introduction.md#external-guarantor-architecture)

**Phase:** All Phases

**Status:** Draft

---

## Purpose

Guarantor Withdrawal Procedures define how **guarantors can exit their role** in an orderly manner that preserves MCF stability and enables replacement.

Guarantor withdrawal is inevitable over decades-long timeframes. Political changes, capacity constraints, or strategic shifts may lead guarantors to exit. The question is not whether withdrawal will happen, but whether it will happen in ways that preserve MCF or ways that destabilize it. These procedures ensure orderly transitions.

---

## Withdrawal Principles

### 1. Orderly Transition Priority

Withdrawal must be orderly. Abrupt departure without transition creates gaps that harm MCF. Even in difficult circumstances, structured handoff is required.

**Orderly Means:**
- Advance notice
- Transition planning
- Responsibility handoff
- Gap coverage

### 2. Successor Arrangement Requirement

Withdrawing guarantors should facilitate successor arrangements. Withdrawal without ensuring continuity of critical functions is irresponsible.

**Successor Arrangement Means:**
- Identify potential successors
- Facilitate successor engagement
- Transfer knowledge and relationships
- Support successor onboarding

### 3. Minimum Threshold Maintenance

Withdrawal cannot reduce guarantor capacity below minimum thresholds. If withdrawal would breach minimums, additional measures are required (see "Minimum Threshold Procedures" section).

**Threshold Maintenance Means:**

- No withdrawal that breaches minimum guarantor count (withdrawal paused until replacement secured)
- No withdrawal that eliminates critical capacity
- Emergency measures if thresholds threatened
- MCF adaptation if minimums cannot be maintained

### 4. Surviving Obligations

Some obligations survive withdrawal. Guarantors cannot exit all responsibilities immediately even upon formal withdrawal.

**Surviving Obligations:**
- Financial commitments through current cycle
- Confidentiality obligations
- Transition cooperation
- Liability for pre-withdrawal actions

## Withdrawal Types

### 1. Planned Withdrawal

**Definition:**
Standard, non-emergency exit from guarantor role following full notice and transition procedures.

**Characteristics:**
- Full notice period observed
- Complete transition planning
- Successor arrangement sought
- Orderly handoff

**When Appropriate:**
- Strategic decision to exit
- Capacity constraints developing gradually
- Political change with transition period
- Natural end of commitment cycle

#### 1.1 Notice Requirements

**Notice Period: 24 months**

**Notice Content:**
- Formal notification to Guarantor Forum
- Reason for withdrawal (general)
- Proposed timeline
- Offered transition support

**Notice Recipients:**
- Guarantor Forum (formal)
- MCF Secretary-General
- Confederal Council
- Other guarantors (bilateral)

#### 1.2 Transition Timeline

| Phase | Duration | Activities |
|-------|----------|------------|
| Notice | Month 1 | Formal notification, initial consultations |
| Planning | Months 2-6 | Transition plan development, successor identification |
| Preparation | Months 7-18 | Successor onboarding, responsibility transfer |
| Handoff | Months 19-24 | Final transfers, closeout |
| Post-Withdrawal | Months 24-30 | Surviving obligations, support as needed |

#### 1.3 Handoff Procedures

**Responsibility Handoff:**
- Function-by-function transfer
- Successor confirmation
- Gap identification
- Documentation transfer

**Personnel Transition:**
- Staff notification
- Redeployment or transition support
- Institutional knowledge transfer
- Relationship handoffs

### 2. Expedited Withdrawal

**Definition:**
Accelerated exit due to circumstances requiring faster timeline than standard planned withdrawal.

**Characteristics:**
- Reduced notice period
- Compressed transition
- Prioritized handoffs
- Gap coverage arrangements

**When Appropriate:**
- Sudden capacity constraint
- Domestic political crisis
- Security concerns
- Relationship breakdown

#### 2.1 Qualifying Circumstances

**Expedited Withdrawal Permitted When:**
- Domestic political crisis affecting capacity
- Security situation affecting personnel
- Major financial crisis
- Other compelling circumstances (Forum determination)

**Expedited Withdrawal Not Permitted For:**
- Policy disagreement
- Burden-sharing disputes
- Bilateral disputes
- Convenience

#### 2.2 Reduced Notice

**Expedited Notice Period: 6 months**

**Requirements:**
- Documented qualifying circumstance
- Forum Chair notification
- Accelerated transition planning
- Gap coverage commitment

#### 2.3 Accelerated Replacement

**Replacement Acceleration:**
- Emergency successor recruitment
- Temporary coverage by other guarantors
- Priority Forum consideration
- Expedited approval processes

### 3. Emergency Withdrawal

**Definition:**
Immediate or near-immediate exit due to force majeure or extreme circumstances.

**Characteristics:**
- Minimal or no notice
- Immediate responsibility gaps
- Emergency coverage required
- Post-hoc transition arrangements

**When Appropriate:**
- Force majeure (war, catastrophe)
- Imminent personnel danger
- Complete capacity collapse
- Other extreme circumstances

#### 3.1 Qualifying Circumstances

**Emergency Withdrawal Only For:**
- Armed conflict involving guarantor
- Natural or man-made catastrophe
- Imminent threat to personnel
- Complete institutional collapse

**Forum Determination:**
- Emergency status confirmed by Forum (or emergency committee)
- Post-hoc ratification if immediate
- Documentation of circumstances

#### 3.2 Immediate Effect

**Upon Emergency Withdrawal:**
- Functions cease immediately
- Responsibility gap created
- Emergency coverage activated
- Post-hoc transition initiated

#### 3.3 Emergency Coverage

**Emergency Coverage Mechanisms:**
- Other guarantors assume critical functions
- MCF emergency procedures activated
- Temporary arrangements pending replacement
- Priority successor recruitment
- For SAZs, the External Security Continuity Protocol activates automatically (see [SAZ Security](../03-phase-2-saz/04-saz-security.md#11a-guarantor-withdrawal-contingency))

## Notice Requirements

### 1. Notice Periods by Withdrawal Type

| Type | Notice Period | Extension |
|------|--------------|-----------|
| Planned | 24 months | May extend |
| Expedited | 6 months | May extend |
| Emergency | Immediate | N/A |

### 2. Notice Content

**Required Elements:**
- Formal statement of intention to withdraw
- Withdrawal type invoked
- Proposed effective date
- Reason (general, not detailed required)
- Transition cooperation commitment
- Contact for transition coordination

**Optional Elements:**
- Successor suggestions
- Extended transition offer
- Continued engagement options
- Lessons learned

### 3. Recipient Parties

**Primary Recipients:**
- Guarantor Forum Chair
- All Guarantor Forum members
- MCF Secretary-General

**Secondary Notification:**
- Confederal Council
- MCF institutional heads
- Key personnel

## Transition Procedures

### 1. Responsibility Handoff

**Handoff Process:**

| Step | Activity | Responsible |
|------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | Function inventory | Withdrawing guarantor |
| 2 | Successor assignment | Forum |
| 3 | Transfer planning | Both |
| 4 | Knowledge transfer | Withdrawing guarantor |
| 5 | Capability confirmation | Successor |
| 6 | Formal handoff | Both + Forum |

**Function-Specific Handoffs:**
- Security functions: Capabilities, procedures, commitments
- Financial functions: Funding, processes, obligations
- Verification functions: Methods, relationships, access
- Other functions: As applicable

### 2. Documentation Transfer

**What Transfers:**
- Operational documentation
- Relationship records
- Institutional knowledge
- Lessons learned
- Active file handoff

**Transfer Format:**
- Standardized documentation package
- Digital transfer where appropriate
- Secure handling for sensitive material
- Inventory and confirmation

### 3. Personnel Transition

**Personnel Considerations:**
- Staff notification timeline
- Transition support
- Redeployment options
- Knowledge preservation

**Key Personnel:**
- Senior staff individual handoffs
- Relationship introductions
- Extended transition for critical positions
- Retention incentives if appropriate

### 4. Asset Transfer

**Transferable Assets:**
- Equipment provided for MCF use
- Facilities (if any)
- Technology and systems
- Documentation and records

**Non-Transferable:**
- National assets used for MCF
- Classified systems
- Personnel
- Sovereign capabilities

**Transfer Process:**
- Asset inventory
- Transfer agreement
- Valuation (if compensation involved)
- Physical transfer
- Documentation

## Replacement Process

### 1. Replacement Identification

**Replacement Sources:**
- Existing guarantor capacity increase
- New guarantor recruitment
- IO role expansion
- Function modification

**Replacement Criteria:**
- Capacity to fill gap
- Credibility in function
- Commitment level
- Stakeholder acceptance

### 2. Replacement Approval

**Approval Process:**

- Nomination (self or other)
- Capacity verification
- Guarantor Forum approval (2/3 majority; expedited timeline for replacements)
- Confederal Council concurrence (supermajority; same threshold as new guarantor admissions per [Guarantor Selection](01-guarantor-selection.md#3-approval-requirements))

**Expedited Approval:**

- Faster timeline for replacements (vetting compressed from 180 to 90 days)
- Pre-approved reserve pool (candidates pre-vetted for rapid activation)
- Temporary approval pending full vetting (for urgent gaps; full approval within 6 months)

### 3. Capacity Verification

**Verification Elements:**
- Stated capacity confirmed
- Resources committed
- Timeline agreed
- Performance standards accepted

**Verification Method:**
- Document review
- Capacity assessment
- Reference check
- Trial period (if appropriate)

### 4. Gap Coverage

**If No Replacement Available:**
- Other guarantors increase capacity
- MCF adapts operations
- Reduced scope if necessary
- Continued recruitment efforts

**Gap Coverage Duration:**

- Maximum 12 months without replacement
- Review at 6 months
- Escalation if gap persists
- MCF adaptation consideration

**Relationship to Minimum Thresholds:** This 12-month gap coverage applies to function-specific gaps where minimum guarantor thresholds (Section 1) are still met. If a withdrawal would breach minimum thresholds, the breach-prevention procedures (Section 2) take precedenceâ€”withdrawal is paused and emergency recruitment activated before the 12-month gap coverage clock begins.

## Surviving Obligations

### 1. Financial Obligations

**Commitments Through Current Cycle:**
- Committed funds disbursed
- Multi-year commitments honored
- Phase-out if extended
- No mid-cycle termination without penalty

**Post-Withdrawal Contributions:**
- Transition costs shared
- Gap coverage contributions (temporary)
- Successor support costs

### 2. Information Obligations

**Knowledge Transfer:**
- Complete documentation transfer
- Institutional knowledge capture
- Relationship handoff
- Lessons learned documentation

**Ongoing Information:**
- Response to successor queries (time-limited)
- Clarification as needed
- No new information obligations

### 3. Confidentiality Obligations

**Perpetual Confidentiality:**
- Classified information remains protected
- Confidential deliberations protected
- Personnel information protected
- Sensitive operational details protected

**Confidentiality Release:**
- Only by Forum authorization
- Time-limited for some categories
- Public information excepted

### 4. Liability

**Pre-Withdrawal Liability:**
- Actions during guarantor period
- Commitments made
- Representations made

**Liability Limits:**
- Sovereign immunity considerations
- Contractual limitations
- Time limitations

## Minimum Threshold Procedures

### 1. Threshold Definition

**Minimum Guarantor Requirements (aligned with [Guarantor Selection](01-guarantor-selection.md#1-geographic-balance)):**

- Minimum 5 guarantors
- At least 1 state major power (US, China, or Russia)
- At least 1 organization with major power capacity (EU or comparable)
- At least 1 regional state
- At least 1 neutral state
- Security capacity from at least 2
- Financial capacity from at least 3

**Function-Specific Minimums:**
- Each critical function covered by at least 2
- No single-point-of-failure
- Redundancy for essential capabilities

### 2. Threshold Breach Response

**If Withdrawal Would Breach Threshold:**
- Notice acknowledged but withdrawal paused
- Emergency recruitment activated
- Other guarantors increase capacity
- MCF notified of threshold risk

**Breach Prevention:**
- 6-month extension request to withdrawing guarantor
- Accelerated replacement process
- Burden redistribution
- Function consolidation

### 3. Emergency Guarantor Recruitment

**Emergency Recruitment:**
- Pre-identified candidate pool
- Expedited approval process
- Reduced vetting (with conditions)
- Rapid onboarding

**Recruitment Targets:**
- States with prior expressions of interest
- IOs with capacity
- Existing supporting states for upgrade
- Regional states

### 4. MCF Adaptation

**If Minimum Cannot Be Restored:**
- Phase regression consideration
- Scope reduction
- Function consolidation
- Enhanced internal capacity
- Permanent adaptation if necessary

**Adaptation Process:**
- Forum recommendation
- Confederal Council consideration
- Implementation plan
- Stakeholder communication
