# Civic Membership Status (CMS) Framework

**Parent Document:** [00-introduction.md](../00-introduction.md#phase-1-years-520-opt-in-civic-membership-without-territorial-change)

**Phase:** Phase 1 onwards

**Status:** Final Draft

---

## Purpose

Civic Membership Status (CMS) is a **voluntary legal status layered on top of existing citizenship**. It provides access to MCF benefits without altering national identity, citizenship, or requiring territorial change.

CMS is the mechanism through which MCF builds a **constituency for coexistence**â€”individuals who have demonstrated commitment to peaceful interaction and who benefit materially from the framework's success. Unlike citizenship, CMS does not imply political allegiance or identity transformation. It is a practical status that enables participation in specific programs and protections.

---

## Core Principle: Layered Status, Not Alternative Identity

CMS operates **alongside** existing citizenship and legal status, not as a replacement:

- A Palestinian with CMS remains a Palestinian
- An Israeli with CMS remains an Israeli
- CMS adds rights and access within MCF domains
- CMS does not alter rights or obligations under national law
- CMS can be acquired without abandoning any existing status

---

## 1. CMS Definition

### 1.1 Legal Nature

CMS is a **sui generis status** that exists only within the MCF framework:

| Characteristic | CMS | Citizenship | Residency |
|----------------|-----|-------------|-----------|
| Confers identity | No | Yes | No |
| Confers nationality | No | Yes | No |
| Grants domestic voting rights | No | Yes | Sometimes |
| Grants territorial residency rights | No | Usually | Yes |
| Layerable on other statuses | Yes | Sometimes | Sometimes |
| Voluntary acquisition | Yes | Sometimes | Sometimes |
| Voluntary renunciation | Yes | Sometimes | Yes |

### 1.2 What CMS Is

- A registration with MCF institutions confirming eligibility and commitment
- A basis for accessing MCF-specific benefits and protections
- A legal status recognized by guarantors and participating institutions
- A credential portable across MCF programs regardless of location

### 1.3 What CMS Is Not

- Citizenship of any state
- Residency permit for any territory
- Recognition of any political claim
- Endorsement of any final status position
- Membership in any political organization

### 1.4 International Legal Analogues

CMS draws conceptually from:

| Analogue | Similarity | Difference |
|----------|------------|------------|
| EU citizenship | Layered on national citizenship, grants cross-border rights | EU citizenship requires member state citizenship; CMS does not |
| Schengen participation | Functional integration without political merger | Schengen is state-level; CMS is individual-level |
| Professional licensure | Portable credential enabling specific activities | CMS is broader than professional scope |
| Refugee status | International status with specific protections | Refugee status is involuntary; CMS is voluntary |

## 2. Eligibility and Application

### 2.1 Basic Eligibility

**Who May Apply**

Any natural person who:

- Is at least 18 years of age
- Resides within the MCF operational area OR is a member of the recognized diaspora population
- Has no disqualifying criminal history (defined below)
- Is willing to complete eligibility requirements

**Geographic Scope**

Initial eligibility covers:

- Residents of Israel (including East Jerusalem)
- Residents of the West Bank
- Residents of Gaza (when MCF operations permit)
- Eligible diaspora populations (defined in Section 2.8)

**Exclusions**

The following categories are ineligible:

- Individuals with convictions for terrorism, war crimes, or crimes against humanity
- Individuals with convictions for serious violent offenses (murder, attempted murder, aggravated assault causing serious bodily harm, kidnapping, sexual violence); full list in Annex D
- Individuals currently subject to security detention by either party
- Individuals under active security investigation (eligibility suspended pending resolution; see Section 2.3 for time limits)
- Individuals who have previously had CMS revoked for cause (unless rehabilitated per Section 6)

### 2.2 Eligibility Requirements

CMS applicants must complete three requirements:

1. **Security Vetting** (Section 2.3)
2. **Civic Education Curriculum** (Section 2.4)
3. **Nonviolence Pledge** (Section 2.5)

All three must be completed; order is flexible.

### 2.3 Security Vetting

**Purpose**

Security vetting ensures that CMS holders do not pose a security threat within MCF domains. It is not a political loyalty test.

**Scope**

Vetting covers:

- Criminal history (both parties' records, international databases)
- Security service records (both parties, with appropriate access agreements)
- Open-source review (social media, public statements)
- Reference verification (where applicable)

**Vetting Does Not Include**

- Political opinions or affiliations (unless linked to violence)
- Religious practice or belief
- Family members' status (except direct security relevance)
- Historical residence or travel (unless security-relevant)

**Conducting Authority**

- Vetting is conducted by a **CMS Vetting Unit** under MCF institutional authority
- Vetting Unit staff are international personnel (not party nationals)
- Vetting Unit may request information from parties but makes independent determinations
- Guarantors provide oversight of vetting standards and processes

**Vetting Process**

1. Applicant submits application with identity documentation
2. Vetting Unit conducts records check (target: 30 days)
3. If concerns arise, applicant is notified and may respond
4. Vetting Unit issues determination: Cleared, Denied, or Pending (with explanation)
5. Denied or Pending applicants may appeal (Section 2.7)

**Vetting Standards**

| Finding | Result |
|---------|--------|
| No disqualifying information | Cleared |
| Disqualifying conviction | Denied |
| Active security concern | Denied or Pending |
| Unverifiable identity | Pending (awaiting documentation); Denied after 90 days if not resolved |
| Minor concerns, mitigated | Cleared with notation |

**Pending Status Time Limits**

"Pending" determinations are subject to strict time limits:

| Pending Reason | Maximum Duration | Resolution |
|----------------|------------------|------------|
| Active security investigation | 180 days | Must resolve to Cleared or Denied |
| Awaiting additional documentation | 90 days | Denied if documentation not provided |
| Inter-agency consultation | 120 days | Must resolve to Cleared or Denied |

- Applicant receives written notification at 30, 60, and 90 days with status update
- After maximum duration, Vetting Unit must issue final determination (Cleared or Denied)
- Applicant may appeal continued Pending status after 90 days using standard appeal process (Section 2.7)
- Guarantor ombudsman may intervene if Pending status exceeds 180 days without resolution

### 2.4 Civic Education Curriculum

**Purpose**

Civic education ensures CMS holders understand:

- MCF framework, institutions, and their rights and obligations
- Practical procedures for accessing benefits and reporting problems
- The context of coexistence without requiring agreement on narratives

**Curriculum Content**

The curriculum covers (approximately 20 hours total):

| Module | Content | Hours |
|--------|---------|-------|
| MCF Overview | Framework purpose, phases, institutions | 3 |
| CMS Rights and Obligations | What CMS provides and requires | 3 |
| Practical Procedures | How to access benefits, report issues, seek help | 4 |
| Conflict Context | Factual presentation of conflict history (multiple narratives acknowledged) | 4 |
| Coexistence Skills | Communication, de-escalation, cross-community interaction | 4 |
| Assessment | Knowledge verification | 2 |

**Curriculum Principles**

- **Narrative neutrality**: Curriculum acknowledges contested narratives without endorsing any
- **Practical focus**: Emphasis on what CMS holders need to know to participate
- **Accessibility**: Available in Arabic, Hebrew, and English; accommodations for disabilities
- **No indoctrination**: Curriculum does not require accepting any political position

**Delivery Methods**

- In-person courses at MCF facilities
- Online self-paced modules with proctored assessment
- Community-based delivery through certified organizations
- Hybrid formats combining online and in-person elements

**Assessment**

- Written or oral assessment (applicant choice)
- Pass threshold: 70% on knowledge components
- Unlimited retakes permitted (after 7-day waiting period)
- Assessment focuses on practical knowledge, not political views

### 2.5 Nonviolence Pledge

**Purpose**

The nonviolence pledge establishes a behavioral commitment as a condition of CMS participation. It is a commitment to act, not a statement of belief.

**Pledge Text**

> I pledge that while holding Civic Membership Status:
>
> 1. I will not use violence against any person within MCF domains except in immediate self-defense or defense of others from imminent physical harm.
>
> 2. I will not plan, assist, finance, or encourage violence against any person within MCF domains.
>
> 3. I will not use my CMS status or MCF access to facilitate violence or harm to others.
>
> 4. I will report credible threats of violence that I become aware of through MCF channels. (See Threat Reporting Protections below.)
>
> 5. I understand that violation of this pledge may result in CMS suspension or revocation.
>
> This pledge applies to my conduct within MCF domains and programs. It does not alter my rights or obligations under national law outside MCF domains.

**Threat Reporting Protections**

CMS holders who report threats under pledge item 4 receive:

- **Confidentiality**: Reporter identity protected from disclosure; reports anonymized before any external sharing
- **Safe harbor**: Good-faith reports cannot be grounds for CMS action, even if threat proves unfounded
- **Retaliation protection**: Retaliation against reporters is a high-severity Anti-Intimidation violation
- **No obligation to self-incriminate**: Reporting requirement does not compel disclosure of information that would expose reporter to criminal liability

**Imminent Threat Exception**: When MCF receives a threat report indicating imminent risk to life (attack expected within 72 hours), MCF may share actionable threat information with relevant authorities without reporter identity and without holder consent. This exception is narrowly construed: only the specific threat information necessary to prevent harm may be shared.

**Scope of Pledge**

The pledge applies to:

- Conduct within MCF programs and facilities
- Conduct affecting other CMS holders
- Conduct within SAZs (Phase 2+)
- Use of MCF benefits or credentials

The pledge does not apply to:

- Conduct entirely outside MCF domains
- Lawful self-defense under applicable law
- Political speech or advocacy (unless inciting imminent violence)
- Actions required by national law (though such actions may trigger CMS review)

**Pledge Administration**

- Pledge is signed as part of CMS application
- Pledge is renewed with each CMS renewal
- Pledge text is provided in applicant's language of choice
- Applicant may ask questions before signing

### 2.6 Application Process

**Application Submission**

1. Applicant obtains application form (online, at MCF facilities, through partner organizations)
2. Applicant provides:
   - Identity documentation (ID card, passport, or UNRWA registration)
   - Contact information
   - Basic biographical information
   - Consent for vetting
3. Application fee: None (MCF does not charge for CMS application)

**Processing Timeline**

| Stage | Target Duration |
|-------|-----------------|
| Application receipt and initial review | 7 days |
| Security vetting | 30 days |
| Civic education completion | Applicant-paced (typically 4-8 weeks) |
| Final determination | 7 days after all requirements met |
| **Total (typical)** | **2-3 months** |

**Determination**

Upon completion of all requirements:

- CMS is granted effective immediately
- CMS holder receives CMS credential (physical and digital)
- CMS holder is registered in CMS database
- CMS holder receives welcome materials with practical guidance

### 2.7 Appeal Process

Applicants denied CMS or in extended Pending status may appeal:

**Denial Appeals:**

1. Written appeal submitted within 30 days of denial
2. Appeal reviewed by CMS Appeals Board (independent of Vetting Unit)
3. Appellant may submit additional documentation or explanation
4. Board decision within 30 days
5. Board may: affirm denial, grant CMS, or order re-vetting
6. Board decision is final within MCF (guarantor oversight available for systemic issues)

**Pending Status Appeals:**

1. Written appeal submitted after 90 days in Pending status (per Section 2.3)
2. Appeal reviewed by CMS Appeals Board
3. Board decision within 14 days
4. Board may: order determination by specific deadline (max 30 days), grant CMS, or affirm continued Pending with justification
5. If Vetting Unit fails to meet Board-ordered deadline, status automatically converts to Cleared unless disqualifying information is documented

### 2.8 Family and Dependent Status

**Minor Children**

- Children under 18 cannot hold CMS independently
- Children of CMS holders may access certain benefits through parent's status
- At age 18, children must apply independently if they wish to hold CMS

**Spouses and Partners**

- CMS is individual; spouse/partner status does not transfer
- Each adult must apply and qualify independently
- Spousal applications may be processed together for efficiency

**Dependents**

- CMS holders may designate dependents for certain benefit access
- Dependent designation requires documentation of relationship
- Dependents do not hold CMS but may access specific programs

### 2.9 Diaspora Eligibility

**Eligible Diaspora Populations**

The following diaspora populations may apply for CMS:

- Palestinian refugees registered with UNRWA (any generation)
- Palestinians displaced in 1948 or 1967 and their descendants
- Israeli citizens residing abroad
- Persons with documented family origin in the MCF operational area

**Diaspora Application Process**

- Application may be submitted at MCF-affiliated facilities abroad
- Vetting includes verification of diaspora eligibility
- Civic education may be completed online
- CMS credential is issued but certain benefits require physical presence

**Diaspora Benefit Limitations**

Diaspora CMS holders may access:

- Recognition of status
- Participation in diaspora-specific programs
- External SAZ participation (where available)
- Credential portability for professional purposes

Diaspora CMS holders may not access (until physically present):

- Employment programs within operational area
- Physical facility access
- Most Phase 1 material benefits

## 3. CMS Administration

### 3.1 Registration Authority

**CMS Registry**

- Central database of all CMS holders maintained by MCF
- Registry includes: identity, status, issue date, renewal date, any restrictions
- Registry is confidential; access limited to authorized MCF personnel
- Registry data is protected under MCF data protection standards

**Registration Authority Structure**

- Director of CMS Registry (appointed by MCF leadership)
- Regional registration offices (located in accessible facilities)
- Mobile registration units (for underserved areas)
- Online portal for status verification and updates

### 3.2 CMS Credential

**Physical Credential**

- Card format with photo, name, CMS number, issue/expiry dates
- Security features: hologram, embedded chip, tamper-evident design
- Issued in applicant's language choice (Arabic, Hebrew, or English)
- Replacement available for lost/damaged credentials (verification required)

**Digital Credential**

- Smartphone application with secure authentication
- QR code for status verification
- Real-time status updates
- Offline verification capability for areas with limited connectivity

**Credential Use**

- CMS credential is required for accessing CMS-specific benefits
- Credential must be presented upon request by MCF personnel
- Credential is not a travel document or ID card for non-MCF purposes
- Misuse of credential is grounds for suspension

### 3.3 Status Verification

**Verification Methods**

- Online portal (real-time database check)
- QR code scan (from digital credential)
- Chip read (from physical credential)
- Manual verification (phone/radio to registry)

**Who May Verify**

- MCF institutional personnel
- Employers participating in MCF programs
- Service providers under MCF agreements
- Guarantor personnel for oversight purposes

**Verification Privacy**

- Verification confirms only: valid/invalid status and any relevant restrictions
- Verification does not disclose: application history, vetting details, personal information
- Verification is logged for audit purposes

## 4. CMS Benefits

### 4.1 Benefits Overview

CMS holders gain access to benefits that grow over MCF phases:

| Phase | Benefits |
|-------|----------|
| Phase 1 | Employment access, dispute resolution (Commercial Courts primary; arbitration by agreement), due process, credential portability |
| Phase 2 | SAZ residency eligibility, civic dividends, enhanced services |
| Phase 3 | Expanded political participation in convergence processes |

### 4.2 Employment Access

**Protected Employment Programs**

CMS holders may participate in MCF-facilitated employment:

- Cross-community employment opportunities
- Wage and labor protection under MCF standards
- Dispute resolution through [Confederal Commercial Courts](../07-economic-architecture/03-commercial-courts.md) (primary) or arbitration (by explicit agreement)
- Protection from discrimination based on community origin

**Work Authorization**

MCF employment programs operate within a negotiated work authorization framework:

- Guarantors facilitate work permit arrangements with both parties as part of MCF establishment
- MCF-registered employers receive streamlined permit processing for CMS employees
- CMS credential serves as eligibility documentation for MCF employment permit applications
- Permits are issued under national authority but processed through MCF employment program
- See [Labor Mobility](../07-economic-architecture/02-labor-mobility.md) for detailed permit procedures

*Note: CMS alone does not authorize employment; CMS holders must obtain applicable work permits through the MCF employment program. MCF facilitates but does not override national work authorization requirements.*

**Employment Protections**

CMS holders in MCF-facilitated employment receive:

- Minimum wage guarantees (set by MCF, pegged to regional standards)
- Working hour limits and rest requirements
- Health and safety standards
- Protection from arbitrary termination
- Access to grievance procedures

**Employer Participation**

Employers access CMS workforce by:

- Registering with MCF employment program
- Agreeing to MCF labor standards
- Submitting to MCF audit and dispute resolution
- Providing workplace data for monitoring

### 4.3 Dispute Resolution Access

CMS holders have access to MCF dispute resolution forums:

**Commercial Courts (Primary Forum):**

- Employment disputes arising from MCF programs
- Commercial disputes with other CMS holders or registered employers
- Contract enforcement under MCF commercial framework

See: [Confederal Commercial Courts](../07-economic-architecture/03-commercial-courts.md)

**Arbitration (By Explicit Agreement):**

- Disputes where parties' contract specifies arbitration
- CMS-related conduct and credential disputes
- Property disputes between participants
- Disputes where parties mutually agree to arbitrate after dispute arises

See: [Arbitration Courts](../05-dispute-resolution/01-arbitration-courts.md)

**Forum Priority:** For commercial and labor disputes, Commercial Courts are the primary forum. Arbitration is available only by explicit party agreement (contract clause or post-dispute mutual consent). CMS enrollment provides eligibility for both forums but does not automatically submit commercial/labor disputes to arbitration.

### 4.4 Due Process Guarantees

CMS holders receive due process protections within MCF domains:

- Right to notice of any MCF proceeding affecting their status
- Right to be heard before adverse MCF decisions
- Right to access evidence used against them (with security redactions where necessary)
- Right to appeal adverse decisions
- Right to representation in MCF proceedings

### 4.5 Credential Portability

CMS holders benefit from credential recognition:

- Educational credentials recognized across MCF participating institutions
- Professional credentials portable within MCF employment programs
- Training certifications recognized across communities
- Work experience documented and verifiable

See: [Credential Portability](../07-economic-architecture/04-credential-portability.md)

### 4.6 Phase 2+ Benefits

Upon Phase 2 activation, CMS holders gain eligibility for:

**SAZ Residency**

- Priority consideration for SAZ residence
- Housing access programs within SAZs
- Full participation in SAZ civic life

**Civic Dividends**

- Share in economic returns from MCF programs
- Community investment allocations
- Quality-of-life infrastructure access

## 5. CMS Limitations

### 5.1 Explicit Exclusions

CMS does **not** provide:

- Voting rights in either party's national elections
- Automatic residency rights in any territory
- Freedom from national law obligations
- Protection from national security measures
- Recognition of any political or territorial claim

### 5.2 National Law Supremacy

CMS holders remain fully subject to national law:

- CMS does not override national law within national jurisdictions
- Conduct outside MCF domains is governed by applicable national law
- National courts retain jurisdiction over national law matters
- CMS is not a defense to national law violations

### 5.3 Political Participation Limits

CMS does not confer political participation rights:

- No voting in national elections of the other community
- No eligibility for national office in the other community
- MCF institutional participation based on separate criteria
- SAZ governance participation (Phase 2+) based on SAZ residency

### 5.4 Conflict Between MCF and National Law

When national law and MCF obligations conflict:

- CMS holder should notify MCF of conflict
- MCF will assess whether national law compliance can be accommodated
- CMS holder is not penalized for national law compliance that conflicts with MCF
- MCF may provide guidance, legal support, or status protection as appropriate
- Persistent conflict may require CMS modification or suspension without prejudice

## 6. Renewal, Suspension, and Revocation

### 6.1 CMS Term and Renewal

**Initial Term**

- CMS is granted for a 3-year term
- Term begins on date of issuance

**Renewal Process**

- Renewal application submitted 90-60 days before expiration
- Renewal requires: confirmation of continued eligibility, pledge renewal, updated information
- Expedited vetting for renewal (review of intervening period only)
- Renewal granted for additional 3-year terms

**Automatic Extension**

- If renewal application is pending at expiration, CMS extends automatically until determination
- Extension is noted on credential

### 6.2 Suspension

**Grounds for Suspension**

CMS may be suspended (temporarily deactivated) for:

- Pending investigation of pledge violation
- Pending investigation of fraud
- Failure to respond to MCF inquiries
- Temporary security concern
- Administrative issues (e.g., unverifiable status)

**Suspension Process**

1. CMS holder notified of suspension and grounds
2. Holder may respond within 14 days
3. Investigation completed within 60 days
4. Resolution: reinstatement, continued suspension, or revocation

**Suspension Effects**

- CMS benefits are inaccessible during suspension
- CMS credential is deactivated
- Suspension is not disclosed to third parties except as necessary
- Employment and other arrangements may be affected

### 6.3 Revocation

**Grounds for Revocation**

CMS may be revoked (permanently terminated) for:

- Confirmed violation of nonviolence pledge
- Fraud in application or use of CMS
- Conviction for disqualifying offense during CMS term
- Serious or repeated MCF rule violations
- Conduct fundamentally incompatible with CMS purposes

**Revocation Process**

1. Investigation establishes grounds
2. CMS holder notified of proposed revocation and grounds
3. Holder may respond in writing and/or request hearing
4. Hearing before CMS Review Panel (if requested)
5. Review Panel determination: revoke, suspend, or reinstate with conditions
6. Appeal available to CMS Appeals Board

**Revocation Effects**

- CMS permanently terminated
- Credential invalidated and must be returned
- Benefits cease immediately
- Re-application prohibited for minimum 5 years (longer for serious violations)
- Revocation may be disclosed to MCF partners as necessary

### 6.4 Voluntary Withdrawal

CMS holders may withdraw at any time:

- Written notice to CMS Registry
- Credential returned or invalidated
- Benefits cease upon withdrawal
- No prejudice from withdrawal (per No Worse Off Guarantee)
- Re-application permitted without waiting period (subject to normal process)

## 7. Data Protection

### 7.1 Data Collection

MCF collects the following CMS-related data:

| Data Type | Purpose | Retention |
|-----------|---------|-----------|
| Identity information | Registration, verification | Duration of CMS + 7 years |
| Contact information | Communication | Duration of CMS |
| Vetting records | Eligibility determination | Duration of CMS + 10 years |
| Status history | Administration, audit | Duration of CMS + 10 years |
| Benefit usage | Program administration | Duration of CMS + 5 years |
| Complaints/proceedings | Accountability | Duration of CMS + 10 years |

### 7.2 Data Access

CMS data may be accessed by:

- CMS holder (their own data)
- Authorized MCF personnel (for administration)
- Guarantor oversight personnel (for audit)
- As required by MCF proceedings

CMS data may **not** be shared with:

- National governments of conflict parties (except as required for specific program operation with holder consent, or under the Imminent Threat Exception in Section 2.5)
- Employers (beyond status verification)
- Third parties without holder consent
- Media or public

**Imminent Threat Exception**: Actionable threat information may be shared with authorities to prevent imminent violence per Section 2.5 (Threat Reporting Protections). Reporter identity is never disclosed under this exception.

### 7.3 Data Upon Withdrawal or Revocation

When CMS ends:

- Status is marked inactive in registry
- Data is retained per retention schedules
- Active sharing ceases
- Holder may request data deletion after retention period

### 7.4 Data Security

- CMS data stored in encrypted systems
- Access logged and audited
- Regular security assessments
- Incident response procedures for breaches

See: [No Worse Off Guarantee](05-no-worse-off-guarantee.md) for data handling upon withdrawal

## 8. Relationship to Other Statuses

### 8.1 CMS and Citizenship

CMS does not affect citizenship:

- CMS holders retain full citizenship rights
- CMS does not confer citizenship
- Citizenship changes do not automatically affect CMS (eligibility confirmed)
- Dual citizens may hold CMS

### 8.2 CMS and Residency

CMS does not confer residency:

- CMS does not authorize residence in any territory
- Residency changes may affect CMS benefit access (geographic eligibility)
- SAZ residency (Phase 2+) is separate from CMS (though CMS is prerequisite)

### 8.3 CMS and Refugee Status

CMS does not affect refugee status:

- UNRWA-registered refugees retain registration
- CMS does not constitute durable solution
- CMS does not affect right of return claims
- Refugee status holders may apply for and hold CMS

### 8.4 CMS and Other MCF Statuses

CMS is prerequisite for:

- SAZ residency eligibility (Phase 2+)
- Certain MCF institutional positions
- Enhanced participation in convergence processes (Phase 3)

- CMS Registry established

- Civic education curriculum finalized

- Limited applications accepted
- Systems tested at scale
- Processes refined based on experience
- First CMS credentials issued

### 9.3 Full Launch

- Open applications
- Full benefit programs operational
- Monitoring and adjustment ongoing

### 9.4 Scaling

- Additional registration sites based on demand
- Process improvements based on experience
- Benefit expansion as Phase 1 matures
