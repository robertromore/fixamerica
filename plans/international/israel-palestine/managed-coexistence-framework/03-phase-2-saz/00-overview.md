## Purpose of This Section

This directory contains detailed implementation documents for the Special Administrative Zone (SAZ) system. Phase 2 represents the first **territorial component** of MCFâ€”bounded geographic areas where mixed governance can be piloted under controlled conditions.

## SAZ Legal Status (from Introduction)

- Territory leased or mandated for a fixed term (e.g., 30-50 years)
- Renewable only by mutual consent
- Symbolic sovereignty claims preserved

## SAZ Governance Structure (from Introduction)

- Locally elected council
- Independent judiciary
- Integrated civil police force under strict external audit

## Constitutional Floor (from Introduction)

- Confederal Rights Compact supersedes local law
- Guarantees: Equal protection, due process, anti-discrimination enforcement

## SAZ Security Model (from Introduction)

- Demilitarized zone
- No armed factions
- External rapid-response guarantees
- Zero tolerance for paramilitary activity

---

## Success Criteria (from Introduction)

- Sustained safety advantage over surrounding areas
- High residency demand
- Institutional legitimacy across communities

---

## Transition to Phase 3

SAZ pilots remain operational throughout Phase 3 and beyond. The transition to Phase 3 (Voluntary Convergence) requires sustained SAZ success and expressed demand for broader political integration options.

See: [Phase Transition Criteria](../00-introduction.md#phase-transition-criteria)
