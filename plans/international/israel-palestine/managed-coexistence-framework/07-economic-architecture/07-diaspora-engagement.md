# Diaspora Engagement Framework

**Parent Document:** [00-overview.md](00-overview.md)

**Applies To:** All Phases (expanding scope over time)

**Objective:** Enable non-refugee diaspora populations to participate in, invest in, and support MCF without prejudicing refugee-specific claims or implying political normalization.

**Status:** Draft

---

## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Rationale

Both Israeli and Palestinian diaspora communities represent significant human, financial, and political resources that could support MCF success. However, diaspora engagement raises distinct sensitivities:

- **For Palestinians:** Diaspora engagement must not undermine refugee right-of-return claims or substitute for addressing displacement
- **For Israelis:** Diaspora engagement must not imply acceptance of occupation or normalize unjust conditions
- **For both:** Diaspora involvement must not give external populations undue influence over local governance

This framework enables constructive diaspora participation while respecting these constraints.

### 1.2 Distinction from Refugee Framework

| Dimension | Refugee Framework | Diaspora Framework |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Target population** | Registered refugees and descendants with displacement claims | Non-refugee diaspora with no active displacement claims |
| **Rights addressed** | Return, compensation, resettlement | Participation, investment, advocacy |
| **Political sensitivity** | Final-status question (deferred) | Not a final-status question |
| **Legal basis** | UNRWA registration, international law | Voluntary affiliation |
| **MCF role** | Structured postponement (per [04-refugees-diaspora.md](../06-sensitive-issues/04-refugees-diaspora.md)) | Active engagement framework |

### 1.3 Eligible Diaspora Populations

**Israeli Diaspora:**
- Israeli citizens residing abroad
- Former Israeli citizens
- Jewish diaspora with demonstrated connection to Israel (Birthright participation, organizational membership, family ties)

**Palestinian Diaspora (non-refugee):**
- Palestinians in diaspora not registered with UNRWA
- Descendants of 1948/1967 displaced persons who do not hold refugee status
- Palestinian citizens of other countries with demonstrated connection

**Dual/Mixed Heritage:**
- Individuals with both Israeli and Palestinian family connections
- Joint diaspora families

## 2. Participation Pathways

### 2.1 Civic Membership System (CMS) Interface

**Phase 1+ Eligibility:**

Diaspora members may apply for CMS Diaspora Status (CMS-D), a formally defined subtype of CMS under the [CMS Framework](../02-phase-1-civic-membership/01-cms-framework.md#29-diaspora-eligibility). CMS-D holders are CMS holders with diaspora-specific scope limitations. CMS-D provides:

| Benefit | CMS-D Holders | Full CMS Holders |
|---------|---------------|------------------|
| Institutional access | Limited | Full |
| Employment in MCF programs | Eligible for remote/diaspora-specific | Full access |
| Dispute resolution access | For diaspora-specific matters | Full access |
| Voting rights in MCF governance | Observer status only | Weighted participation |
| Investment access | Full | Full |

**CMS-D Requirements:**
- Demonstrated diaspora status (documentation of heritage, prior residence, or organizational affiliation)
- Background verification (no security disqualifications)
- Commitment to MCF principles (non-violence, participation in good faith)
- Annual renewal with activity requirement

**CMS-D Limitations:**
- Does not confer residency rights
- Does not affect citizenship status
- Does not prejudice any political claims
- No local governance voting rights

### 2.2 Professional Engagement

**Remote Employment:**
- MCF institutions may employ diaspora professionals remotely
- Priority for skills in short supply locally (technical expertise, specialized legal/medical)
- Salary parity with local rates (no undercutting)
- Subject to MCF personnel standards

**Consulting and Advisory:**
- Diaspora experts may serve on MCF advisory bodies
- Academic and research partnerships
- Technical assistance programs

**Volunteer Programs:**
- Structured volunteer opportunities with MCF institutions
- Skills-based volunteering (legal aid, medical missions, education)
- Cultural exchange programs

### 2.3 Cultural and Educational Programs

**Heritage Programs:**
- Facilitated visits to ancestral sites (with security coordination)
- Genealogical research access
- Cultural preservation initiatives

**Educational Exchange:**
- University partnerships with diaspora institutions
- Student exchange programs (Phase 2+)
- Academic research collaboration

**Language and Culture:**
- Arabic/Hebrew language programs for diaspora
- Cultural competency training
- Interfaith/intercultural dialogue initiatives

## 3. Investment Framework

### 3.1 Diaspora Investment Vehicles

**MCF Development Bonds:**

| Bond Type | Term | Return | Purpose |
|-----------|------|--------|---------|
| Infrastructure Bond | 10 years | 3-4% (inflation-indexed) | PCC development, utilities |
| Social Bond | 7 years | 2-3% | Healthcare, education facilities |
| Enterprise Bond | 5 years | 4-6% | Small business support |

**Features:**
- Denominated in stable currency (USD, EUR, or basket)
- Guaranteed by MCF institutions with guarantor backing
- Tax treatment determined by investor's home jurisdiction
- Minimum investment: $1,000

**Diaspora Investment Fund (DIF):**

Pooled investment vehicle for diaspora capital:

- Professional management by MCF-approved fund managers
- Diversified across MCF economic activities
- Quarterly reporting and annual audit
- Investor representation on advisory board
- Returns distributed annually

**Direct Investment:**

Diaspora members may invest directly in:
- MCF-registered businesses (subject to due diligence)
- Real estate in SAZs (Phase 2+, subject to restrictions)
- Joint ventures with local partners

### 3.2 Investment Protections

**Due Diligence Requirements:**
- Source of funds verification
- Beneficial ownership disclosure
- Anti-money laundering compliance
- Conflict of interest screening

**Investor Rights:**
- Access to MCF commercial courts for disputes
- Repatriation of capital and returns (subject to exchange controls if any)
- Transparent reporting on investment performance
- Exit mechanisms with reasonable liquidity

**Restrictions:**
- No investment in security-sensitive sectors
- No land acquisition that could affect demographic balance (Phase 2+ territorial restrictions)
- No investment that circumvents existing legal requirements
- No anonymous or nominee investment

### 3.3 Remittance Facilitation

**Remittance Corridors:**
- Reduced-fee remittance channels for diaspora transfers
- Partnership with established remittance providers
- Mobile money integration
- Compliance with anti-money laundering requirements

**Family Support Transfers:**
- Streamlined processes for recurring family support
- Tax documentation for donor-country deductions where applicable
- Emergency transfer fast-track

## 4. Advocacy and Representation

### 4.1 Diaspora Advisory Council (DAC)

**Composition:**
- 12 members: 6 Israeli diaspora, 6 Palestinian diaspora
- Selected by diaspora organizations through transparent process
- 3-year terms, staggered renewal
- Geographic diversity required (North America, Europe, Latin America, other)

**Functions:**
- Advise MCF institutions on diaspora perspectives
- Recommend diaspora engagement policies
- Facilitate diaspora organization coordination
- Report on diaspora sentiment and concerns

**Limitations:**
- Advisory only; no governance authority
- Cannot represent on final-status questions
- No veto over MCF decisions

### 4.2 Diaspora Organization Interface

**Registration:**
- Diaspora organizations may register with MCF for coordination
- Registration requires: organizational documents, leadership disclosure, funding transparency
- Registered organizations receive: information access, consultation opportunities, program partnerships

**Coordination Mechanisms:**
- Annual diaspora organization conference
- Quarterly information briefings
- Issue-specific working groups
- Joint program development

**Disqualification:**
- Organizations promoting violence are ineligible
- Organizations opposing MCF principles may not register
- Registration may be revoked for policy violations

### 4.3 Political Activity Guidelines

**Permitted:**
- Advocacy for MCF support in home countries
- Fundraising for MCF programs
- Educational outreach about MCF
- Engagement with home-country policymakers on MCF-related matters

**Restricted:**
- Using MCF platforms to advocate on final-status questions
- Claiming to represent local populations
- Lobbying against parties to MCF
- Electoral intervention in Israeli or Palestinian politics

**Prohibited:**
- Material support for violence
- Incitement against MCF participants
- Disinformation campaigns
- Coercive pressure on local communities

## 5. Philanthropy and Civil Society

### 5.1 Diaspora Philanthropy

**MCF Foundation:**

Charitable vehicle for diaspora philanthropy:

| Program Area | Examples | Tax Status |
|--------------|----------|------------|
| Humanitarian | Medical aid, emergency relief | 501(c)(3) equivalent |
| Development | Education, infrastructure | 501(c)(3) equivalent |
| Cultural | Heritage preservation, arts | 501(c)(3) equivalent |
| Civic | Civil society support, research | May vary by activity |

**Features:**
- Transparent grant-making with public reporting
- Balanced allocation between communities
- Local partner organizations for implementation
- Donor-advised fund options

**Direct Philanthropy:**
- Diaspora donors may give directly to registered MCF civil society partners
- Due diligence information provided by MCF
- Tax documentation support

### 5.2 Civil Society Partnerships

**Diaspora CSO Partnerships:**
- Diaspora organizations may partner with local CSOs for joint programs
- MCF facilitates partnership matching
- Capacity building support for local partners
- Monitoring and evaluation requirements

**Volunteer Placement:**
- Diaspora volunteers may serve with local CSOs
- Vetting and training required
- Insurance and safety coordination
- Minimum commitment periods

## 6. Phase-Specific Implementation

### 6.1 Phase 0: Limited Engagement

- CMS-D registration begins (verification infrastructure development)
- Diaspora Advisory Council formation
- MCF Development Bond pilot issuance
- Remittance corridor agreements negotiated
- Diaspora organization registration opens

### 6.2 Phase 1: Expanded Participation

- Full CMS-D program operational
- Diaspora Investment Fund launched
- Professional engagement programs active
- Cultural and educational exchanges begin
- Philanthropy framework fully operational

### 6.3 Phase 2: SAZ Integration

- Direct investment in SAZ territories enabled
- Real estate investment under territorial restrictions
- Enhanced employment access in SAZs
- Expanded volunteer and exchange programs
- Diaspora representation in SAZ advisory bodies

### 6.4 Phase 3: Convergence Options

- Diaspora role in convergence deliberations (advisory)
- Potential citizenship/residency pathways for sustained engagement
- Deep integration options based on demonstrated commitment
- Framework evolution based on convergence outcome

## 7. Safeguards and Limitations

### 7.1 Non-Prejudice Principle

**Core Rule:** Diaspora engagement does not:
- Prejudice refugee right-of-return claims
- Substitute for addressing displacement
- Imply acceptance of territorial status quo
- Affect any party's political claims

**Implementation:**
- Explicit non-prejudice language in all diaspora agreements
- Separate tracking of diaspora vs. refugee matters
- No conflation in reporting or statistics
- Clear communication to all participants

### 7.2 Local Primacy

**Core Rule:** Local populations retain primacy in MCF governance:

| Domain | Local Role | Diaspora Role |
|--------|-----------|---------------|
| Governance decisions | Voting authority | Advisory only |
| Economic priorities | Primary beneficiaries | Investors, supporters |
| Cultural programs | Design and control | Participation, support |
| Political representation | Full rights | None through MCF |

**Rationale:** MCF serves those living with the conflict daily; diaspora support is welcome but cannot substitute for local agency.

### 7.3 Demographic Balance

**Concern:** Diaspora investment in land or real estate could affect demographic balance in contested areas.

**Safeguards:**
- No diaspora real estate investment in Jerusalem (excluded from MCF)
- SAZ territorial restrictions apply equally to diaspora investors
- No agricultural land acquisition by diaspora without local partner
- Residency rights not conferred by investment

### 7.4 Political Manipulation Prevention

**Risk:** Diaspora engagement could be weaponized for political purposes.

**Countermeasures:**
- Transparency requirements for all diaspora organizations
- Political activity guidelines with enforcement
- Balanced diaspora advisory structure
- Regular review of diaspora program impact

## 8. Operational Procedures

### 8.1 CMS-D Application Process

1. **Application:** Online portal or consular submission
2. **Documentation:** Heritage/connection documentation, identity verification
3. **Background Check:** Security screening through MCF verification systems
4. **Interview:** Video interview if documentation incomplete
5. **Approval:** CMS-D credential issued (digital + physical card)
6. **Renewal:** Annual renewal with activity confirmation

**Processing Time:** 60-90 days standard; 30 days expedited for established organizations' members

### 8.2 Investment Due Diligence

1. **Investor Registration:** Identity, source of funds, investment objectives
2. **Verification:** Document authentication, reference checks
3. **Risk Assessment:** Conflict of interest, security concerns
4. **Approval:** Investment account activation
5. **Monitoring:** Ongoing transaction monitoring, annual review

### 8.3 Dispute Resolution

Diaspora-related disputes are handled through:

| Dispute Type | Forum | Applicable Law |
|--------------|-------|----------------|
| Investment disputes | MCF Commercial Courts | MCF commercial rules + investor home jurisdiction |
| Employment disputes | MCF labor tribunal | MCF personnel standards |
| CMS-D status disputes | CMS appeals process | MCF membership rules |
| Organization registration | MCF administrative tribunal | MCF registration rules |

## 9. Reporting and Accountability

### 9.1 Transparency Requirements

**Public Reporting:**
- Annual diaspora engagement report (participation statistics, investment volumes, program outcomes)
- DAC meeting minutes and recommendations
- Investment fund performance reports
- Philanthropy grant allocations

**Confidential Reporting:**
- Individual participation data (protected)
- Security screening results (protected)
- Detailed investment holdings (investor access only)

### 9.2 Evaluation Metrics

| Dimension | Metrics |
|-----------|---------|
| Participation | CMS-D holders, active participants, geographic distribution |
| Investment | Capital deployed, returns, sector allocation |
| Programs | Volunteer hours, exchange participants, partnerships formed |
| Satisfaction | Participant surveys, stakeholder feedback |
| Impact | Economic contribution, skill transfer, cross-community engagement |

### 9.3 Program Review

- **Annual review:** DAC assessment of program effectiveness
- **Triennial evaluation:** Independent external evaluation
- **Guarantor oversight:** Integration with broader MCF monitoring

---

## 10. Coordination with Other Frameworks

### 10.1 Refugee Framework Interface

- Clear separation of refugee and diaspora tracks
- No conflation in eligibility or benefits
- Refugee claims unaffected by diaspora participation
- Information sharing where appropriate (family connections)

### 10.2 Regional Economic Interface

- Diaspora investment compatible with regional economic initiatives
- Coordination with Abraham Accords economic components
- Gulf investment coordination (where diaspora populations reside)

### 10.3 Guarantor Coordination

- Guarantor countries may facilitate diaspora engagement
- Consular support for CMS-D applications
- Investment guarantee coordination
- Emergency assistance for diaspora participants
