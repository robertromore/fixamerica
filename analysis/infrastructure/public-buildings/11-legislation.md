# Public Buildings: Legislation and Legal Framework

## Overview

Modernizing America's public buildings requires federal investment programs, state funding reform, accessibility compliance, and energy efficiency mandates. This document provides draft legislation for key reforms and analysis of existing legal frameworks.

## Federal Legislation

### Public Building Investment Act

**Purpose**: Increase investment in federal buildings and address deferred maintenance.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Public Building
Investment Act".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds that—
    (1) the federal government owns or leases
    over 376,000 buildings;
    (2) deferred maintenance exceeds $50 billion
    across the federal portfolio;
    (3) many buildings are energy inefficient
    and not fully accessible;
    (4) sustained investment is needed to
    protect federal assets;
    (5) comprehensive reform is necessary.

SEC. 3. FEDERAL BUILDING FUND REFORM.

(a) FULL SPENDING.—All rent collections in the
Federal Buildings Fund shall be available for
obligation for authorized purposes.

(b) MULTI-YEAR AUTHORITY.—The Administrator may
enter into multi-year contracts for—
    (1) major repairs and alterations;
    (2) new construction;
    (3) building systems replacement.

(c) LIFECYCLE BUDGETING.—Building budgets shall
include full lifecycle costs including—
    (1) initial construction;
    (2) ongoing maintenance;
    (3) major systems replacement;
    (4) disposal.

SEC. 4. DEFERRED MAINTENANCE.

(a) AUTHORIZATION.—$5,000,000,000 annually is
authorized to address deferred maintenance.

(b) PRIORITY.—Priority shall be given to—
    (1) health and safety deficiencies;
    (2) buildings with highest utilization;
    (3) cost-effective repairs;
    (4) accessibility improvements.

(c) REDUCTION TARGET.—Deferred maintenance shall
be reduced by 50% within 10 years.

SEC. 5. ENERGY EFFICIENCY.

(a) PERFORMANCE STANDARDS.—Federal buildings
shall reduce energy use intensity by—
    (1) 20% within 5 years;
    (2) 40% within 10 years;
    (3) carbon neutral by 2050.

(b) INVESTMENT.—$2,000,000,000 annually is
authorized for energy efficiency improvements.

(c) RENEWABLE ENERGY.—Where feasible, federal
buildings shall incorporate on-site renewable
energy generation.

SEC. 6. ACCESSIBILITY.

(a) FULL COMPLIANCE.—All federal buildings shall
achieve full ADA compliance within 10 years.

(b) INVESTMENT.—$500,000,000 annually is authorized
for accessibility improvements.

(c) REPORTING.—GSA shall report annually on
accessibility compliance progress.

SEC. 7. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act takes effect upon enactment.
```

### School Facilities Investment Act

**Purpose**: Establish federal investment in K-12 school facilities.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "School Facilities
Investment Act".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds that—
    (1) American public schools serve 50 million
    students;
    (2) school facility needs exceed $85 billion;
    (3) low-income districts have the worst
    facilities;
    (4) facility quality affects student health
    and learning;
    (5) federal investment is needed to address
    inequities.

SEC. 3. SCHOOL CONSTRUCTION GRANTS.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—A school construction grant
program is established.

(b) AUTHORIZATION.—$15,000,000,000 annually is
authorized for school construction grants.

(c) ALLOCATION.—Funds shall be allocated to
states based on—
    (1) school-age population (40%);
    (2) high-poverty school enrollment (40%);
    (3) facility condition need (20%).

(d) STATE ADMINISTRATION.—States shall administer
grants to local educational agencies.

SEC. 4. ELIGIBILITY.

(a) ELIGIBLE ACTIVITIES.—Grants may be used for—
    (1) new school construction;
    (2) renovation and rehabilitation;
    (3) environmental hazard remediation;
    (4) HVAC and ventilation systems;
    (5) accessibility improvements;
    (6) technology infrastructure.

(b) PRIORITY.—Priority shall be given to—
    (1) schools with highest poverty rates;
    (2) environmental hazards affecting health;
    (3) buildings with greatest deficiencies.

SEC. 5. REQUIREMENTS.

(a) MAINTENANCE OF EFFORT.—States and districts
shall maintain prior year facility investment.

(b) EQUITY.—States shall ensure equitable
distribution to high-poverty districts.

(c) STANDARDS.—Construction shall meet minimum
facility adequacy standards.

(d) ACCESSIBILITY.—All construction shall comply
with accessibility requirements.

(e) SUSTAINABILITY.—New construction shall meet
energy efficiency standards.

SEC. 6. BUREAU OF INDIAN EDUCATION SCHOOLS.

(a) AUTHORIZATION.—$1,000,000,000 annually is
authorized for BIE school construction and
renovation.

(b) PRIORITY.—Priority shall be given to schools
in the poorest condition.

SEC. 7. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act takes effect upon enactment.
```

### VA Facility Modernization Act

**Purpose**: Accelerate VA healthcare facility investment.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "VA Facility
Modernization Act".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds that—
    (1) the VA operates over 6,000 facilities;
    (2) deferred maintenance exceeds $70 billion;
    (3) many facilities do not meet modern
    healthcare standards;
    (4) seismic deficiencies pose safety risks;
    (5) accelerated investment is needed.

SEC. 3. CONSTRUCTION AUTHORIZATION.

(a) MAJOR CONSTRUCTION.—$5,000,000,000 annually
is authorized for major construction.

(b) MINOR CONSTRUCTION.—$2,000,000,000 annually
is authorized for minor construction.

(c) LEASING.—$1,500,000,000 annually is authorized
for community-based outpatient clinics.

SEC. 4. PRIORITY PROJECTS.

(a) SEISMIC.—Priority shall be given to seismic
retrofit of high-risk facilities.

(b) CAPACITY.—Projects expanding capacity in
underserved areas.

(c) MODERNIZATION.—Facilities requiring major
renovation to meet healthcare standards.

SEC. 5. PROCESS REFORM.

(a) STREAMLINED APPROVAL.—Construction approval
processes shall be streamlined.

(b) DESIGN-BUILD.—Design-build authority shall
be expanded.

(c) ACCOUNTABILITY.—Cost and schedule controls
shall be strengthened.

SEC. 6. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act takes effect upon enactment.
```

## State Model Legislation

### Model State School Construction Act

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "[State] School
Construction Act".

SECTION 2. STATE SCHOOL CONSTRUCTION FUND.

(a) A state school construction fund is established.

(b) The fund shall receive—
    (1) state appropriations;
    (2) federal grants;
    (3) bond proceeds;
    (4) other revenues.

SECTION 3. GRANT PROGRAM.

(a) Grants shall be available to local educational
agencies for school construction and renovation.

(b) Grants shall prioritize—
    (1) high-poverty districts;
    (2) facilities with health and safety deficiencies;
    (3) districts with limited bonding capacity.

(c) State share shall be [50-80]% of project costs.

SECTION 4. FACILITY STANDARDS.

(a) The state board shall establish minimum
facility adequacy standards.

(b) All construction shall meet accessibility
requirements.

(c) New construction shall meet energy efficiency
standards.

SECTION 5. PLANNING.

(a) Districts shall maintain facilities master
plans.

(b) Plans shall be updated every [5] years.

SECTION 6. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act takes effect on [date].
```

## Loopholes, Shortcomings, and Rectification

### Potential Loopholes

| Loophole | Description | Severity |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| **Maintenance diversion** | Operating funds used for programs | High |
| **Equity gaming** | Funds not reaching poorest districts | High |
| **Standard avoidance** | Minimal compliance | Medium |
| **Reporting gaps** | Incomplete condition data | Medium |

### Shortcomings

| Issue | Impact | Root Cause |
|-------|--------|------------|
| **Funding levels** | Below actual needs | Political will |
| **State variation** | Unequal implementation | Federalism |
| **Private buildings** | Federal leases not addressed | Scope limitation |
| **Technology** | Rapid obsolescence | Standards lag |

### Rectification Procedures

1. **Dedicated funding sources** with spending requirements
2. **Strong equity provisions** with enforcement
3. **Clear standards** with compliance monitoring
4. **Comprehensive reporting** requirements
5. **Accountability mechanisms** for outcomes
6. **Regular updates** to standards and requirements
7. **Technical assistance** for capacity building
8. **Independent assessment** of conditions
9. **Public transparency** on building conditions
10. **Consequences** for non-compliance

### General Implementation Concerns

| Concern | Mitigation |
|---------|------------|
| **Political cycles** | Multi-year commitments |
| **State capacity** | Technical assistance |
| **Local gaming** | Strong oversight |
| **Cost growth** | Project controls |
| **Maintenance** | Ongoing funding requirements |
