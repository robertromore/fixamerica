# Infrastructure Finance: Legislation and Legal Framework

## Overview

Sustainable infrastructure finance requires federal trust fund reform, user fee modernization, state revenue authority, and new financing mechanisms. This document provides draft legislation for key reforms and analysis of existing legal frameworks.

## Federal Legislation

### Highway Trust Fund Solvency Act

**Purpose**: Restore solvency to the Highway Trust Fund.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Highway Trust Fund
Solvency Act".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds that—
    (1) the Highway Trust Fund faces chronic
    revenue shortfalls;
    (2) the federal gas tax has not been increased
    since 1993;
    (3) inflation has eroded 47% of purchasing
    power;
    (4) electric vehicles pay no fuel tax;
    (5) sustainable revenue is essential for
    infrastructure investment.

SEC. 3. FUEL TAX ADJUSTMENT.

(a) INITIAL INCREASE.—Effective [date], the tax
imposed by section 4081 of the Internal Revenue
Code shall be increased by—
    (1) 10 cents per gallon for gasoline;
    (2) 10 cents per gallon for diesel fuel.

(b) INDEXING.—Beginning [year], fuel tax rates
shall be adjusted annually to reflect changes
in—
    (1) the Consumer Price Index; or
    (2) the National Highway Construction Cost
    Index, whichever is greater.

(c) ALTERNATIVE FUELS.—Alternative fuels shall
be taxed at energy-equivalent rates.

SEC. 4. ELECTRIC VEHICLE FEE.

(a) ANNUAL FEE.—An annual fee shall be imposed
on electric vehicles registered in any State,
equal to the average fuel tax paid by comparable
gasoline vehicles.

(b) COLLECTION.—Fees shall be collected through
State registration systems with federal
reimbursement for collection costs.

(c) DEPOSIT.—Fees shall be deposited in the
Highway Trust Fund.

SEC. 5. VMT FEE TRANSITION.

(a) PILOT PROGRAMS.—The Secretary shall establish
expanded VMT fee pilot programs in partnership
with volunteer States.

(b) AUTHORIZATION.—$50,000,000 annually for
pilot program administration.

(c) TRANSITION STUDY.—Within 3 years, the
Secretary shall submit a plan for transitioning
from fuel taxes to VMT fees.

SEC. 6. SPENDING ALIGNMENT.

(a) OBLIGATION LIMITATION.—The obligation
limitation shall be set at a level that does
not exceed projected trust fund revenues.

(b) RESERVE.—A reserve of not less than
$5,000,000,000 shall be maintained.

SEC. 7. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act takes effect upon enactment.
```

### National Infrastructure Bank Act

**Purpose**: Establish a national infrastructure bank to leverage federal investment.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "National
Infrastructure Bank Act".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds that—
    (1) infrastructure investment needs exceed
    current funding capacity;
    (2) federal credit programs have demonstrated
    high leverage ratios;
    (3) an infrastructure bank can multiply
    federal investment;
    (4) merit-based project selection improves
    outcomes.

SEC. 3. ESTABLISHMENT.

(a) CREATION.—The National Infrastructure Bank
is hereby established as a government corporation.

(b) PURPOSES.—The Bank shall—
    (1) provide loans, loan guarantees, and other
    credit assistance for infrastructure projects;
    (2) leverage federal funds with private
    capital;
    (3) support projects of national or regional
    significance;
    (4) promote innovation in project delivery.

SEC. 4. CAPITALIZATION.

(a) INITIAL CAPITAL.—$50,000,000,000 is
appropriated as initial capital.

(b) BORROWING AUTHORITY.—The Bank may borrow
up to $250,000,000,000 through debt issuance.

(c) LEVERAGE.—The Bank shall achieve leverage
ratios of at least 10:1 on federal capital.

SEC. 5. GOVERNANCE.

(a) BOARD.—The Bank shall be governed by a
Board of Directors consisting of—
    (1) the Secretary of Transportation;
    (2) the Secretary of the Treasury;
    (3) 5 members appointed by the President
    with expertise in infrastructure, finance,
    and regional development.

(b) INDEPENDENCE.—The Board shall operate
independently of political influence in
project selection.

(c) CEO.—A Chief Executive Officer shall
manage daily operations.

SEC. 6. ELIGIBLE PROJECTS.

(a) SECTORS.—Eligible sectors include—
    (1) surface transportation;
    (2) water infrastructure;
    (3) energy infrastructure;
    (4) broadband;
    (5) public buildings;
    (6) resilience projects.

(b) CRITERIA.—Projects shall be evaluated on—
    (1) economic benefits;
    (2) environmental benefits;
    (3) regional significance;
    (4) creditworthiness;
    (5) innovation.

SEC. 7. TERMS.

(a) INTEREST RATE.—Loans shall bear interest
at Treasury rates plus an administrative fee.

(b) TERM.—Loan terms may extend to 35 years.

(c) SUBORDINATION.—Bank loans may be subordinate
to senior debt.

SEC. 8. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act takes effect upon enactment.
```

### VMT Fee Authorization Act

**Purpose**: Authorize transition to vehicle miles traveled fees.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "VMT Fee
Authorization Act".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds that—
    (1) fuel tax revenue is declining due to
    vehicle efficiency and electrification;
    (2) VMT fees provide sustainable, equitable
    revenue;
    (3) pilot programs have demonstrated viability;
    (4) privacy-protective technologies exist.

SEC. 3. STATE VMT AUTHORIZATION.

(a) AUTHORIZATION.—States are authorized to
impose VMT fees in lieu of or in addition to
fuel taxes for vehicles registered in such State.

(b) INTERSTATE AGREEMENT.—States may enter
agreements for VMT fee collection on vehicles
traveling across state lines.

(c) FEDERAL COORDINATION.—The Secretary shall
facilitate interstate coordination.

SEC. 4. FEDERAL VMT PROGRAM.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—Within 5 years, the Secretary
shall establish a federal VMT fee program.

(b) RATE.—The initial rate shall be equivalent
to current fuel tax revenue per mile.

(c) TRANSITION.—Fuel tax shall be phased out
as VMT fees are phased in.

SEC. 5. PRIVACY PROTECTION.

(a) PROHIBITION.—Location data shall not be
stored or transmitted.

(b) OPTIONS.—Multiple reporting options shall
be available including—
    (1) odometer reporting;
    (2) time-based permits;
    (3) mileage-based insurance;
    (4) on-board technology (opt-in only).

(c) DATA SECURITY.—Strict data security
standards shall apply.

SEC. 6. EQUITY PROVISIONS.

(a) LOW-INCOME.—Low-income households shall
receive rebates or discounts.

(b) RURAL.—Rural area rates may be adjusted.

(c) ACCESSIBILITY.—Persons with disabilities
shall receive accommodations.

SEC. 7. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act takes effect upon enactment.
```

## State Model Legislation

### Model State Infrastructure Finance Act

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "[State] Infrastructure
Finance Act".

SECTION 2. GAS TAX INDEXING.

(a) Beginning [date], the motor fuel tax shall
be adjusted annually to reflect changes in
the Consumer Price Index.

(b) Adjustments shall be automatic without
further legislative action.

SECTION 3. ELECTRIC VEHICLE FEE.

(a) An annual fee is imposed on electric
vehicles equal to [amount].

(b) Fees shall be deposited in the [State]
Highway Fund.

(c) Fees shall be adjusted with gas tax
indexing.

SECTION 4. STATE INFRASTRUCTURE BANK.

(a) A state infrastructure bank is established.

(b) The bank may provide loans for eligible
infrastructure projects.

(c) Initial capitalization of $[amount].

SECTION 5. VALUE CAPTURE AUTHORITY.

(a) Local governments are authorized to
establish tax increment financing districts
for infrastructure.

(b) Special assessment authority is granted
for infrastructure benefiting specific
properties.

SECTION 6. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act takes effect on [date].
```

|-------|-------------|
| **Taxing power** | Fuel taxes, fees |
| **Commerce Clause** | Interstate transportation |
| **Spending Power** | Grant programs |
| **Necessary and Proper** | VMT administration |

### State Authority

|-------|-------------|

|-------|---------------|

## Loopholes, Shortcomings, and Rectification

### Potential Loopholes

| Loophole | Description | Severity |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| **Fuel tax avoidance** | Cross-border purchases | Medium |
| **VMT evasion** | Odometer tampering | Medium |
| **EV fee collection** | Enforcement gaps | Medium |
| **Bank project selection** | Political influence | Medium |

### Shortcomings

| Issue | Impact | Root Cause |
|-------|--------|------------|
| **Revenue adequacy** | May not close gap | Political limits |
| **Privacy** | Continued concerns | Technical challenges |
| **Equity** | Regressive impacts | User fee nature |
| **State variation** | Inconsistent implementation | Federalism |

### Rectification Procedures

1. **Strong enforcement** for tax/fee collection
2. **Privacy-by-design** in VMT systems
3. **Equity provisions** in all programs
4. **Federal standards** for state programs
5. **Independent bank governance** with accountability
6. **Regular rate adjustment** mechanisms
7. **Auditing and transparency** requirements
8. **Public reporting** on outcomes
9. **Periodic review** of effectiveness
10. **Adaptive management** based on results

### General Implementation Concerns

| Concern | Mitigation |
|---------|------------|
| **Political will** | Bipartisan coalition |
| **Technology** | Proven systems |
| **Privacy** | Protective design |
| **Equity** | Assistance programs |
| **Transition** | Phased approach |
