# Infrastructure Maintenance and Repair: Overview

## Executive Summary

America faces a crisis of deferred maintenance across all infrastructure categories. Decades of underinvestment have created a backlog exceeding $2 trillion, with roads, bridges, water systems, schools, and public buildings deteriorating faster than they can be repaired. This maintenance deficit increases lifecycle costs, reduces service quality, threatens safety, and creates economic drag. Addressing the backlog requires sustained investment, better asset management practices, and reformed governance that prioritizes preservation over new construction.

## Scope

This analysis examines maintenance and repair challenges across:

- **Transportation**: Roads, bridges, transit, rail, airports
- **Water**: Drinking water, wastewater, stormwater systems
- **Buildings**: Schools, public buildings, housing
- **Energy**: Grid infrastructure, generation facilities
- **Cross-cutting**: Asset management, workforce, technology

## Why This Matters

Infrastructure maintenance is foundational to economic competitiveness, public health, and quality of life. Every dollar invested in timely maintenance saves $4-25 in future costs. The current approach of deferring maintenance until emergency repairs are needed is fiscally irresponsible, economically damaging, and inequitable.
