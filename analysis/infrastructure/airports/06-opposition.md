# Aviation Infrastructure: Opposition

## Overview

Opposition to aviation infrastructure reform comes primarily from airlines protecting cost structures, communities opposing expansion, and political interests blocking institutional change. Understanding opposition arguments and tactics is essential for advancing needed investment and modernization.

## Airline Opposition

### Against PFC Increases

| Argument | Response |
|----------|----------|
| **Raises ticket prices** | Minor impact, airports need revenue |
| **Already pay enough** | Inflation has eroded PFC value |
| **Inefficient spending** | Airports accountable to passengers |
| **Hidden tax** | User fee for facilities used |

### Against Airport Investment

| Argument | Response |
|----------|----------|
| **Gold-plating** | Market demands quality |
| **Airport profits** | Revenue reinvested |
| **Competitive distortion** | Level playing field argument |

### Tactics

| Tactic | Example |
|--------|---------|
| **Lobbying** | Campaign contributions |
| **Coalition building** | Consumer groups |
| **Messaging** | Price increase framing |
| **Political pressure** | Congressional allies |
