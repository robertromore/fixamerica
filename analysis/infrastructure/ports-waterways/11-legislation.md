# Ports and Waterways: Legislation and Legal Framework

## Overview

Modernizing maritime infrastructure requires WRDA authorization reform, trust fund improvements, port investment programs, and environmental protection. This document provides draft legislation for key reforms and analysis of existing legal frameworks.

## Federal Legislation

### Maritime Infrastructure Investment Act

**Purpose**: Increase investment in ports and waterways.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Maritime Infrastructure
Investment Act".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds that—
    (1) America's ports and waterways are essential
    to national and international commerce;
    (2) over 99% of overseas trade moves by water;
    (3) inland waterway locks are aging beyond
    design life;
    (4) investment is needed for economic
    competitiveness;
    (5) comprehensive reform is necessary.

SEC. 3. HARBOR MAINTENANCE TRUST FUND.

(a) FULL SPENDING.—All Harbor Maintenance Tax
collections shall be spent for authorized purposes.

(b) FORMULA REFORM.—The Secretary shall revise
allocation formulas to better match harbor
maintenance needs.

(c) SMALL PORTS.—Not less than 12% shall be
available for emerging harbor projects at
small ports.

(d) RESILIENCE.—Climate resilience projects
shall be eligible uses.

SEC. 4. INLAND WATERWAYS TRUST FUND.

(a) FEDERAL SHARE.—The federal cost share for
inland waterway construction shall be increased
to 75%.

(b) FUEL TAX.—The inland waterway fuel tax shall
be increased to [amount] per gallon.

(c) AUTHORIZATION.—There is authorized $1 billion
annually for inland waterway construction.

SEC. 5. PORT INVESTMENT.

(a) PIDP EXPANSION.—The Port Infrastructure
Development Program is authorized at $1 billion
annually.

(b) INTERMODAL.—Projects improving rail and
highway connections to ports are eligible.

(c) RESILIENCE.—Climate resilience projects
are eligible.

(d) ENVIRONMENTAL.—Emission reduction projects
are eligible.

SEC. 6. PROCESS REFORM.

(a) EXPEDITED REVIEW.—Navigation projects shall
receive expedited environmental review.

(b) ADVANCE CONSTRUCTION.—Non-federal sponsors
may advance funds for construction.

(c) MULTI-PROJECT AUTHORIZATION.—Related projects
may be authorized together.

SEC. 7. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act takes effect upon enactment.
```

### Lock Modernization Act

**Purpose**: Accelerate inland waterway lock replacement.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Lock Modernization Act".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds that—
    (1) over 60% of inland waterway locks exceed
    their design life;
    (2) lock failures cause economic disruption;
    (3) agricultural exports depend on reliable
    navigation;
    (4) accelerated replacement is needed.

SEC. 3. PRIORITY LOCKS.

(a) IDENTIFICATION.—The Secretary shall identify
the 20 most critical locks for replacement or
major rehabilitation.

(b) CONSTRUCTION SCHEDULE.—Priority locks shall
be completed within 10 years.

(c) FUNDING.—$10 billion is authorized for
priority lock construction.

SEC. 4. FEDERAL SHARE.

(a) PRIORITY LOCKS.—The federal share for priority
locks shall be 85%.

(b) OTHER LOCKS.—The federal share for other
inland waterway construction shall be 75%.

SEC. 5. MAINTENANCE.

(a) REQUIREMENT.—Lock maintenance shall be
adequately funded to prevent deterioration.

(b) AUTHORIZATION.—$500 million annually for
lock maintenance.

SEC. 6. TECHNOLOGY.

The Secretary shall incorporate modern technology
in lock construction and operations.

SEC. 7. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act takes effect upon enactment.
```

### Clean Ports Act

**Purpose**: Reduce port emissions and protect communities.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Clean Ports Act".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds that—
    (1) ports are significant sources of air
    pollution;
    (2) port-adjacent communities bear
    disproportionate health burdens;
    (3) zero-emission technology is available;
    (4) federal support can accelerate transition.

SEC. 3. ZERO-EMISSION PORTS PROGRAM.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—A zero-emission ports program
is established.

(b) GRANTS.—$1 billion annually for—
    (1) shore power infrastructure;
    (2) zero-emission cargo handling equipment;
    (3) charging infrastructure;
    (4) zero-emission drayage trucks.

(c) ELIGIBILITY.—Ports and terminal operators
are eligible.

SEC. 4. EMISSION REQUIREMENTS.

(a) SHORE POWER.—Ports receiving federal funds
shall require shore power use by 2030.

(b) EQUIPMENT.—New cargo handling equipment
purchased with federal funds shall be zero-emission.

(c) TRUCKS.—Ports shall develop zero-emission
drayage truck plans.

SEC. 5. COMMUNITY PROTECTION.

(a) AIR MONITORING.—Ports receiving federal funds
shall conduct real-time air quality monitoring.

(b) COMMUNITY BENEFITS.—Environmental mitigation
shall benefit port-adjacent communities.

(c) HEALTH STUDIES.—Funding for port community
health studies.

SEC. 6. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act takes effect upon enactment.
```

Investment Act".

SECTION 2. STATE PORT FUND.

(a) A state port investment fund is established.

(b) The fund shall support—
    (1) matching for federal grants;
    (2) terminal investment;
    (3) intermodal connections;
    (4) environmental improvements.

SECTION 3. FUNDING.

[Revenue source for fund]

SECTION 4. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act takes effect on [date].

## Loopholes, Shortcomings, and Rectification

### Potential Loopholes

| Loophole | Description | Severity |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| **HMT diversion** | Spending for non-harbor purposes | Medium |
| **Cost share gaming** | Project structuring | Medium |
| **Environmental waivers** | Excessive exemptions | Medium |
| **Benefit-cost manipulation** | Project justification | Medium |

### Shortcomings

| Issue | Impact | Root Cause |
|-------|--------|------------|
| **Lock funding** | System deterioration | Revenue limits |
| **Port capacity** | Congestion | Investment gaps |
| **Intermodal** | Bottlenecks | Coordination |
| **Resilience** | Vulnerability | Underfunding |
| **Environmental** | Pollution | Inadequate standards |

### Rectification Procedures

1. **HMT full spending** with enforcement
2. **IWTF reform** with higher revenue, federal share
3. **Process streamlining** with timeline requirements
4. **Port program expansion** with adequate funding
5. **Intermodal requirements** for federal projects
6. **Climate resilience** standards and funding
7. **Emission requirements** for federal assistance
8. **Community protection** requirements
9. **Supply chain resilience** planning
10. **Accountability** reporting and oversight

### General Implementation Concerns

| Concern | Mitigation |
|---------|------------|
| **Industry opposition** | Coalition building |
| **Appropriations** | Trust fund reforms |
| **Process delays** | Streamlined procedures |
| **Environmental** | Clear standards |
