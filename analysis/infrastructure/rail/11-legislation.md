# Rail Infrastructure: Legislation and Legal Framework

## Overview

Modernizing American rail requires federal authorization reforms, stable funding mechanisms, governance restructuring, and safety improvements. This document provides draft legislation for key reforms and analysis of existing legal frameworks.

## Federal Legislation

### Passenger Rail Investment Act

**Purpose**: Establish predictable, long-term funding for passenger rail.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Passenger Rail
Investment Act".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds that—
    (1) passenger rail is essential for mobility,
    economic development, and environmental
    sustainability;
    (2) current funding mechanisms are inadequate
    and unpredictable;
    (3) sustained investment is necessary to
    develop a modern rail network;
    (4) other developed nations invest significantly
    more in passenger rail;
    (5) comprehensive reform is needed.

SEC. 3. RAIL INVESTMENT FUND.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—There is established in the
Treasury a Passenger Rail Investment Fund.

(b) DEPOSITS.—There shall be deposited into the
Fund—
    (1) amounts appropriated under subsection (d);
    (2) amounts transferred from other sources;
    (3) such other amounts as Congress may
    appropriate.

(c) EXPENDITURES.—Amounts in the Fund shall be
available for—
    (1) Amtrak capital and operating grants;
    (2) Federal-State Partnership grants;
    (3) corridor development grants;
    (4) high-speed rail development;
    (5) station improvements;
    (6) grade crossing elimination.

(d) AUTHORIZATION.—There is authorized to be
appropriated to the Fund—
    (1) $15,000,000,000 for fiscal year 1;
    (2) $16,000,000,000 for fiscal year 2;
    (3) $17,000,000,000 for fiscal year 3;
    (4) $18,000,000,000 for fiscal year 4;
    (5) $20,000,000,000 for fiscal year 5.

SEC. 4. AMTRAK FUNDING STABILITY.

(a) MULTI-YEAR GRANTS.—Amtrak shall receive
5-year grant agreements for—
    (1) Northeast Corridor operations and capital;
    (2) National Network operations and capital;
    (3) equipment acquisition.

(b) ADVANCE APPROPRIATIONS.—Amounts authorized
may be appropriated in advance.

SEC. 5. FEDERAL-STATE PARTNERSHIP.

(a) AUTHORIZATION.—The Federal-State Partnership
for Intercity Passenger Rail is authorized at
$5,000,000,000 annually.

(b) FEDERAL SHARE.—The federal share for
Partnership projects shall be up to 80%.

(c) ELIGIBILITY.—Eligible projects include—
    (1) capital improvements on existing corridors;
    (2) new corridor development;
    (3) high-speed rail;
    (4) station construction and improvement.

SEC. 6. HIGH-SPEED RAIL PROGRAM.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—A High-Speed Rail Development
Program is established.

(b) GRANTS.—The Secretary shall provide grants
for high-speed rail corridors capable of
sustained speeds of 186 mph or greater.

(c) AUTHORIZATION.—$3,000,000,000 annually is
authorized for high-speed rail grants.

(d) FEDERAL SHARE.—The federal share shall be
up to 80%.

SEC. 7. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act takes effect upon enactment.
```

### Amtrak Reform Act

**Purpose**: Strengthen Amtrak's governance and operations.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Amtrak Reform Act".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds that—
    (1) Amtrak's current governance structure
    impedes effective management;
    (2) operational flexibility is needed;
    (3) labor modernization can improve service;
    (4) performance accountability is essential.

SEC. 3. BOARD REFORM.

(a) COMPOSITION.—The Amtrak Board shall consist
of—
    (1) the Secretary of Transportation or designee;
    (2) the President of Amtrak;
    (3) seven public members appointed by the
    President with expertise in—
        (A) transportation;
        (B) finance;
        (C) labor relations;
        (D) passenger service.

(b) TERMS.—Members shall serve 5-year staggered
terms.

(c) QUALIFICATIONS.—No member shall be a
registered lobbyist or have financial conflicts.

SEC. 4. OPERATING FLEXIBILITY.

(a) ROUTE MODIFICATIONS.—Amtrak may adjust
routes and frequencies based on—
    (1) ridership demand;
    (2) cost effectiveness;
    (3) connectivity;
    (4) state partnership.

(b) CONGRESSIONAL NOTIFICATION.—Amtrak shall
notify Congress 90 days before discontinuing
service on any route.

SEC. 5. ON-TIME PERFORMANCE.

(a) STANDARDS.—The Surface Transportation Board
shall establish and enforce on-time performance
standards for passenger trains on freight
railroad tracks.

(b) PENALTIES.—Host railroads failing to meet
standards shall be subject to—
    (1) investigation by the Board;
    (2) remedial orders;
    (3) civil penalties.

(c) CAPACITY.—The Secretary may require capacity
improvements on corridors with persistent delays.

SEC. 6. WORKFORCE DEVELOPMENT.

(a) TRAINING.—Amtrak shall establish comprehensive
training programs for all employee categories.

(b) APPRENTICESHIPS.—Amtrak shall partner with
unions to develop apprenticeship programs.

SEC. 7. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act takes effect upon enactment.
```

### Grade Crossing Safety Act

**Purpose**: Accelerate grade crossing elimination and improve safety.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Grade Crossing
Safety Act".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds that—
    (1) approximately 2,100 highway-rail crossing
    incidents occur annually;
    (2) over 200 fatalities occur at crossings
    each year;
    (3) passive crossings lack adequate warning;
    (4) grade separation and closure programs
    can significantly reduce incidents;
    (5) increased investment is needed.

SEC. 3. GRADE CROSSING ELIMINATION PROGRAM.

(a) AUTHORIZATION.—$1,500,000,000 annually is
authorized for grade crossing elimination.

(b) ELIGIBLE PROJECTS.—
    (1) Grade separation construction;
    (2) Crossing closure;
    (3) Advanced warning systems;
    (4) Crossing surface improvements;
    (5) Sight line improvements.

(c) FEDERAL SHARE.—The federal share shall be
up to 90%.

(d) PRIORITY.—Priority shall be given to—
    (1) high-accident crossings;
    (2) crossings on passenger corridors;
    (3) crossings near schools and hospitals;
    (4) quiet zone implementation.

SEC. 4. CROSSING CLOSURE INCENTIVE.

(a) GRANTS.—Grants shall be available to
communities for crossing consolidation.

(b) CRITERIA.—Closure projects shall demonstrate—
    (1) safety benefit;
    (2) reasonable alternative access;
    (3) community support.

SEC. 5. TECHNOLOGY REQUIREMENTS.

(a) NEW CROSSINGS.—New public at-grade crossings
shall include—
    (1) active warning devices;
    (2) supplemental safety measures as appropriate.

(b) UPGRADE PROGRAM.—Passive crossings on
passenger corridors shall be upgraded within
10 years.

SEC. 6. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act takes effect upon enactment.
```

### Rail Equipment Modernization Act

**Purpose**: Facilitate procurement of modern rail equipment.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Rail Equipment
Modernization Act".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds that—
    (1) U.S. rail equipment is aging;
    (2) Buy America requirements limit options;
    (3) international equipment designs offer
    benefits;
    (4) regulatory modernization is needed.

SEC. 3. ALTERNATIVE SAFETY STANDARDS.

(a) AUTHORIZATION.—The Federal Railroad
Administration shall authorize use of equipment
meeting alternative safety standards if the
Administrator determines the equipment provides
an equivalent level of safety.

(b) CRITERIA.—Alternative standards may be
approved based on—
    (1) crash energy management;
    (2) operating environment;
    (3) track separation from freight;
    (4) international experience.

(c) TIMELINE.—Decisions on alternative standards
applications shall be made within 18 months.

SEC. 4. BUY AMERICA MODERNIZATION.

(a) CONTENT CALCULATION.—For rail rolling stock,
domestic content shall be calculated based on
total contract value including—
    (1) final assembly;
    (2) major components;
    (3) testing and commissioning.

(b) FINAL ASSEMBLY.—Final assembly shall occur
in the United States.

(c) COMPONENT SOURCING.—The Secretary may
issue waivers for components not available
domestically at reasonable cost.

SEC. 5. PROCUREMENT STREAMLINING.

(a) JOINT PROCUREMENT.—Multiple agencies may
conduct joint procurements for common equipment.

(b) OPTIONS.—Contracts may include options
for additional equipment.

(c) TECHNOLOGY SHARING.—Designs developed with
federal funds shall be available to other
public agencies.

SEC. 6. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act takes effect upon enactment.
```

## State Model Legislation

### Model State Rail Corridor Development Act

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "[State] Rail
Corridor Development Act".

SECTION 2. STATE RAIL PROGRAM.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—A state rail program is
established within the Department of
Transportation.

(b) PURPOSES.—The program shall—
    (1) develop intercity passenger rail corridors;
    (2) partner with Amtrak and federal programs;
    (3) invest in stations and equipment;
    (4) coordinate with neighboring states.

SECTION 3. STATE RAIL FUND.

(a) CREATION.—A state rail investment fund is
created.

(b) SOURCES.—The fund may receive—
    (1) state appropriations;
    (2) federal grants;
    (3) local contributions;
    (4) other revenues.

(c) USES.—Fund revenues shall be used for—
    (1) capital improvements;
    (2) operating support;
    (3) equipment acquisition;
    (4) federal grant matching.

SECTION 4. CORRIDOR COMMISSION.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—Multi-state corridor
commissions may be established by interstate
compact.

(b) AUTHORITY.—Commissions may—
    (1) plan corridor development;
    (2) coordinate service;
    (3) apply for federal grants;
    (4) manage corridor operations.

SECTION 5. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act takes effect on [date].
```

## Constitutional Considerations

### Federal Authority

| Basis | Application |
|-------|-------------|
| **Commerce Clause** | Interstate passenger and freight |
| **Spending Power** | Grants to states, Amtrak |
| **Post Roads Clause** | Transportation infrastructure |
| **Necessary and Proper** | Implementation authority |

### State Authority

| Basis | Application |
|-------|-------------|
| **Police power** | Safety, land use |
| **Compact Clause** | Interstate agreements |
| **Property** | Station ownership |
| **Taxation** | State funding |

### Preemption Issues

| Area | Federal-State Balance |
|------|----------------------|
| **Safety** | Federal preemption (limited state role) |
| **Economic** | Federal regulatory authority |
| **Environmental** | Shared jurisdiction |
| **Land use** | Primarily state/local |

## Loopholes, Shortcomings, and Rectification

### Potential Loopholes

| Loophole | Description | Severity |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| **Funding flexibility** | Diversion to other purposes | Medium |
| **On-time enforcement** | Weak penalty structure | High |
| **Equipment waivers** | Safety standard manipulation | Medium |
| **State matching** | Inability to participate | Medium |
| **Grant competition** | Political allocation | Medium |

### Shortcomings

| Issue | Impact | Root Cause |
|-------|--------|------------|
| **Funding levels** | Below international standards | Political will |
| **Freight priority** | Continued delays | Track ownership |
| **HSR gaps** | No comprehensive network | Project-by-project |
| **Equipment** | Continued aging | Procurement complexity |
| **Labor rules** | Operating constraints | Railway Labor Act |

### Rectification Procedures

1. **Dedicated funding source** with constitutional protection
2. **Strengthen STB enforcement** of on-time requirements
3. **FRA regulatory modernization** for equipment approval
4. **Federal share increases** for lower-capacity states
5. **Formula-based allocation** for base funding
6. **National HSR authority** for coordinated development
7. **Equipment standardization** across systems
8. **Labor modernization** through negotiation
9. **State capacity building** for program administration
10. **Accountability metrics** with public reporting

### General Implementation Concerns

| Concern | Mitigation |
|---------|------------|
| **Political cycles** | Multi-year commitments |
| **State capacity** | Technical assistance |
| **Freight opposition** | Capacity investment |
| **Cost growth** | Project controls |
| **Environmental review** | Programmatic approaches |
