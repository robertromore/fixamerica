# Roads and Bridges: History

## Overview

American road infrastructure evolved from colonial post roads through the Interstate Highway System that transformed the nation. Federal involvement grew from minimal to dominant over the 20th century, driven by military needs, economic development, and automobile proliferation. Understanding this history reveals how current funding structures, design standards, and institutional arrangements emergedâ€”and why reform is difficult.

## Pre-Automobile Era (Before 1900)

### Early Roads

| Period | Development |
|--------|-------------|
| **Colonial era** | Local roads, post roads |
| **Early republic** | Turnpikes, toll roads |
| **1811** | National Road authorized |
| **Mid-1800s** | Railroad dominance |
| **1880s-1890s** | Good Roads Movement begins |

### Good Roads Movement

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Bicyclists** | Initial advocates |
| **Rural interests** | Farm-to-market needs |
| **Post Office** | Rural free delivery |
| **Early autoists** | Growing constituency |

## Federal Aid Era (1916-1956)

### Federal Aid Road Act (1916)

| Element | Significance |
|---------|--------------|
| **First federal highway program** | Established federal role |
| **Matching funds** | 50-50 federal-state |
| **State highway departments** | Required for participation |
| **Rural focus** | Post roads emphasis |

### Interwar Development

| Development | Year | Impact |
|-------------|------|--------|
| **Federal Aid Highway Act** | 1921 | Created numbered U.S. routes |
| **Route 66** | 1926 | Iconic highway designation |
| **State gas taxes** | 1920s | New funding source |
| **Expanded network** | 1920s-30s | System growth |

### Depression-Era Investment

| Program | Impact |
|---------|--------|
| **Public Works Administration** | Road construction jobs |
| **Civilian Conservation Corps** | Park roads, trails |
| **Works Progress Administration** | Local road improvement |
| **Economic stimulus** | Counter-cyclical spending |

## Interstate Highway Era (1956-1980)

### Federal Aid Highway Act of 1956

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Interstate System** | 41,000+ miles planned |
| **Federal share** | 90% federal, 10% state |
| **Highway Trust Fund** | Dedicated gas tax revenue |
| **Completion target** | 1972 (extended multiple times) |
| **Total cost** | $130 billion (original estimate) |

### Highway Trust Fund

| Element | Significance |
|---------|--------------|
| **User fee principle** | Drivers pay for roads |
| **Dedicated revenue** | Separate from general fund |
| **Gas tax** | Primary revenue source |
| **Predictable funding** | Long-term planning enabled |

### Urban Impact

| Impact | Consequence |
|--------|-------------|
| **Urban renewal** | Highway-justified demolition |
| **Community destruction** | Minority neighborhoods targeted |
| **Suburbanization** | Highway-enabled sprawl |
| **Transit decline** | Competition for funds |
| **Environmental damage** | Air pollution, land use |

### Freeway Revolts

| City | Year | Outcome |
|------|------|---------|
| **San Francisco** | 1959 | Embarcadero Freeway stopped |
| **Boston** | 1960s | Southwest Expressway cancelled |
| **New York** | 1962 | Lower Manhattan Expressway stopped |
| **Washington, D.C.** | 1960s-70s | Multiple freeways cancelled |

## Surface Transportation Era (1980-2005)

### ISTEA (1991)

| Element | Significance |
|---------|--------------|
| **Intermodal Surface Transportation Efficiency Act** | First major reform |
| **Metropolitan Planning Organizations** | Local planning authority |
| **Flexible funding** | Transit, bike, pedestrian eligible |
| **Enhancement program** | Non-motorized, historic preservation |
| **Environmental review** | Strengthened requirements |

### TEA-21 (1998)

| Element | Impact |
|---------|--------|
| **Transportation Equity Act for the 21st Century** | Continued ISTEA reforms |
| **Revenue Aligned Budget Authority** | Guaranteed Trust Fund spending |
| **Increased funding** | $218 billion over 6 years |
| **Earmarks** | Proliferated |

### SAFETEA-LU (2005)

| Element | Impact |
|---------|--------|
| **Safe, Accountable, Flexible, Efficient Transportation Equity Act** | Last major pre-IIJA bill |
| **Record earmarks** | 6,371 projects designated |
| **Bridge collapse** | I-35W (2007) highlighted needs |
| **Trust Fund problems** | Shortfalls began |

## Recent Era (2005-Present)

### Great Recession Impact

| Effect | Response |
|--------|----------|
| **Reduced driving** | Gas tax revenue fell |
| **ARRA stimulus** | $27 billion highway investment |
| **Trust Fund crisis** | General fund bailouts |
| **Long-term bills** | Short extensions instead |

### MAP-21 and FAST Act

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **MAP-21 (2012)** | Performance-based planning |
| **FAST Act (2015)** | 5-year authorization |
| **Freight program** | New freight formula |
| **Trust Fund** | Continued bailouts required |

### Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (2021)

| Element | Significance |
|---------|--------------|
| **Largest investment** | Decades of underinvestment addressed |
| **Bridge funding** | $27 billion dedicated |
| **Resilience** | New PROTECT program |
| **Reconnecting Communities** | Address past highway damage |
| **Trust Fund** | Still needs long-term fix |

## Key Historical Patterns

| Pattern | Contemporary Relevance |
|---------|----------------------|
| **Construction preference** | Maintenance still underfunded |
| **Political earmarks** | Reform ongoing |
| **Urban destruction** | Legacy remains |
| **Federal dominance** | State dependency |
| **Gas tax reliance** | Declining viability |
| **Modal bias** | Cars prioritized |
