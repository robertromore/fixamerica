# Roads and Bridges: Root Causes

## Overview

Deteriorating road and bridge conditions result from structural funding problems, political incentives favoring new construction over maintenance, fragmented responsibility across government levels, and inadequate attention to safety and resilience. Addressing these root causes requires reforming financing mechanisms, changing institutional incentives, and rethinking infrastructure priorities.

## Funding Structure Problems

### Gas Tax Erosion

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Inflation** | 1993 tax worth ~40% today |
| **Fuel efficiency** | More miles, less tax |
| **Electric vehicles** | Pay no fuel tax |
| **Political resistance** | No federal increase since 1993 |
| **Real revenue decline** | Purchasing power falling |

| **General fund bailouts** | $275 billion since 2008 |
| **User fee erosion** | No longer true user-pays |
| **Political dysfunction** | Can't agree on fix |

### State Funding Variation

| Factor | Impact |

|--------|-----------|

| **Fiscal mismatch** | Needs vs. resources misaligned |
