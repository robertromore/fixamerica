# Education: Stakeholders

## Overview

Education involves a vast array of stakeholders with competing interests: students and families, teachers and administrators, unions and reformers, taxpayers and politicians, employers and colleges. Understanding who benefits from the status quo, who is harmed, and who has power to change things is essential for navigating the complex politics of education reform.

---

## Who Benefits from the Status Quo

### Wealthy Districts

| Benefit | Mechanism |
|---------|-----------|
| **Higher funding** | Property tax wealth yields more revenue |
| **Better teachers** | Higher pay attracts talent |
| **More resources** | Smaller classes, better facilities |
| **Political power** | Organized, high-voting parents |
| **College placement** | Pipeline to elite institutions |

### Education Industry

| Stakeholder | Benefit |
|-------------|---------|
| **Testing companies** | $2+ billion annual market |
| **Textbook publishers** | $8 billion market, adoption cycles |
| **Ed-tech vendors** | $40+ billion market |
| **Curriculum providers** | Standards-aligned materials |
| **Professional development** | Consultants, trainers |

### For-Profit Education

| Sector | Stake |
|--------|-------|
| **For-profit colleges** | $30+ billion (declining) |
| **Charter management orgs** | Per-pupil revenue |
| **Tutoring companies** | $8+ billion market |
| **Test prep industry** | $12+ billion market |
| **Student loan servicers** | $1.75 trillion in loans |

### Higher Education Establishment

| Stakeholder | Interest |
|-------------|----------|
| **Elite universities** | Selectivity, prestige |
| **Credentialing monopoly** | Degree requirements |
| **Administrative class** | Growing employment |
| **Athletic programs** | Billion-dollar enterprise |
| **Research universities** | Federal funding, status |

### Suburban/Exurban Communities

| Benefit | Mechanism |
|---------|-----------|
| **School quality as amenity** | Attracts homebuyers |
| **District boundaries** | Exclude urban students |
| **Property values** | School quality capitalized |
| **Local control** | Protect from regional sharing |

## Who Is Harmed

### Students in Underfunded Schools

| Group | Harm |
|-------|------|
| **Low-income students** | Fewer resources, less experienced teachers |
| **Students of color** | Segregated, underfunded schools |
| **Rural students** | Limited course offerings, fewer opportunities |
| **English learners** | Inadequate support services |
| **Special education students** | Underfunded mandates |

### Teachers

| Harm | Manifestation |
|------|---------------|
| **Low pay** | 20% below comparable professions |
| **Poor conditions** | Large classes, inadequate supplies |
| **Disrespect** | Cultural devaluation |
| **Burnout** | 60%+ report high stress |
| **Attrition** | 50% leave within 5 years |

### Families

| Group | Harm |
|-------|------|
| **Working parents** | Childcare costs, school schedules |
| **Low-income families** | Can't afford enrichment, tutoring |
| **Parents of special needs children** | Fight for services |
| **Immigrant families** | Language barriers, cultural mismatch |
| **First-generation college families** | Navigation challenges |

### Student Borrowers

| Harm | Scale |
|------|-------|
| **Debt burden** | $1.75 trillion total |
| **Default** | 10%+ rate |
| **Life delays** | Homebuying, family formation |
| **Career constraints** | Income-driven decisions |
| **Mental health** | Debt-related stress |

### Employers and Economy

| Harm | Manifestation |
|------|---------------|
| **Skills gaps** | Workers unprepared |
| **Training costs** | Employers must remediate |
| **Innovation limits** | STEM shortages |
| **Civic capacity** | Democratic participation weakened |

## Who Has Power to Change Things

### Federal Government

| Actor | Power | Constraint |
|-------|-------|------------|
| **Congress** | Legislation, appropriations | Polarization |
| **President** | Executive action, appointments | Limited K-12 authority |
| **Department of Education** | Regulation, enforcement | Budget, political will |
| **Supreme Court** | Constitutional interpretation | Conservative majority |

### State Governments

| Actor | Power | Scope |
|-------|-------|-------|
| **Governors** | Policy leadership, budget | Primary K-12 authority |
| **State legislatures** | Funding formulas, curriculum | Vast |
| **State boards of education** | Standards, accountability | Varies |
| **State superintendents** | Implementation | Varies |
| **Courts** | Funding adequacy rulings | Significant in some states |

### Local Entities

| Actor | Power | Scope |
|-------|-------|-------|
| **School boards** | District policy, budgets | 13,000+ districts |
| **Superintendents** | Day-to-day operations | Significant |
| **Principals** | Building-level decisions | Limited but important |
| **Teachers** | Classroom implementation | Direct impact |

### Private Sector

| Actor | Power |
|-------|-------|
| **Philanthropies** | Gates, Walton, Broad influence policy |
| **Employers** | Workforce demands shape education |
| **Universities** | Admissions, teacher prep, research |
| **Charter networks** | Model alternatives |

## Key Relationships

### Unions vs. Reformers

| Dynamic | Manifestation |
|---------|---------------|
| **Traditional conflict** | Accountability, choice, tenure |
| **Areas of agreement** | Funding, working conditions |
| **Political alignment** | Both largely Democratic |
| **Charter divide** | Unions oppose, reformers support |
| **Recent shifts** | Some convergence on social justice |

### Federal vs. State vs. Local

| Dynamic | Manifestation |
|---------|---------------|
| **Funding without mandates** | Federal $ with strings |
| **Standards battles** | Common Core backlash |
| **Civil rights enforcement** | Federal role vs. local control |
| **Implementation gaps** | State/local capacity varies |

### Parents vs. System

| Dynamic | Manifestation |
|---------|---------------|
| **Information asymmetry** | Parents don't know what quality looks like |
| **Voice and exit** | Choice vs. voice for improvement |
| **Trust erosion** | COVID, culture wars increased distrust |
| **Activism** | School board takeovers |

### K-12 vs. Higher Education

| Dynamic | Manifestation |
|---------|---------------|
| **Disconnected systems** | Different governance |
| **Remediation burden** | Colleges complain about K-12 |
| **Admissions pressure** | Shapes high school |
| **Teacher prep** | Universities train teachers |

## Power Analysis

### Most Powerful Stakeholders

| Rank | Stakeholder | Power Source |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| 1 | State legislatures | Control funding, policy |
| 2 | Governors | Budget, appointments |
| 3 | Teachers unions | Political influence, membership |
| 4 | Wealthy parents | Organize, vote, donate |
| 5 | Philanthropy | Foundation funding shapes policy |
| 6 | Testing/textbook industry | Contracts, lobbying |

### Least Powerful Stakeholders

| Rank | Stakeholder | Barrier |
|------|-------------|---------|
| 1 | Students | Can't vote |
| 2 | Low-income parents | Time, resources, access |
| 3 | Early childhood workers | Low wages, no organization |
| 4 | Student loan borrowers | Diffuse, stigmatized |
| 5 | Future generations | Not yet born |

## Stakeholder Alignment on Key Issues

| Issue | Support | Oppose |
|-------|---------|--------|
| **More K-12 funding** | Unions, equity advocates | Fiscal conservatives |
| **School choice/vouchers** | Reformers, conservatives | Unions, public school advocates |
| **Teacher pay raises** | Unions, most parents | Some fiscal conservatives |
| **Integration efforts** | Civil rights groups | Many suburban parents |
| **Student debt relief** | Progressives, borrowers | Fiscal conservatives |
| **Universal pre-K** | Broad coalition | Some fiscal conservatives |
| **Curriculum restrictions** | Conservative parents | Educators, civil liberties groups |
