# Early Childhood Education: Actions

## Individual and Family Actions

### As a Parent or Caregiver

**Choosing Quality Care**:

- Research provider quality ratings (state QRIS databases)
- Visit programs and observe teacher-child interactions
- Ask about staff qualifications, turnover, and compensation
- Check licensing history and inspection reports
- Trust your observations of how children and adults interact

**Questions to Ask Providers**:

- What is your staff-to-child ratio?
- What training and credentials do your teachers have?
- What is your staff turnover rate?
- How do you communicate with families?
- What curriculum do you use?
- How do you handle behavior guidance?

**Advocacy for Your Child**:

- Communicate regularly with providers about your child's needs
- Participate in parent activities and governance
- Provide feedback on program quality
- Advocate for inclusive practices if your child has special needs
- Support educators through appreciation and respect

**Supporting Quality at Home**:

- Engage in serve-and-return interactions with your child
- Read, talk, and sing to your child daily
- Provide developmentally appropriate learning materials
- Maintain consistent routines
- Limit screen time for children under 2

### As an Employer

**Assessing Your Workforce Needs**:

- Survey employees about childcare challenges
- Calculate costs of turnover, absenteeism, and reduced productivity
- Assess competitor benefits offerings
- Evaluate return on investment for childcare benefits

**Direct Childcare Support Options**:

| Option | Cost Range | Impact |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| On-site childcare | $1M+ setup, ongoing subsidy | Highest, but limited reach |
| Near-site partnership | $50-200K/year | Moderate impact, location-dependent |
| Backup care program | $500-1,000/employee/year | Reduces absenteeism |
| Childcare subsidies | $2,000-10,000/employee/year | Direct affordability help |
| Dependent Care FSA | Administrative only | Tax savings for employees |

**Indirect Support**:

- Flexible scheduling and remote work options
- Paid parental leave beyond FMLA requirements
- Phase-back programs for returning parents
- Emergency leave for care breakdowns

**Community Engagement**:

- Join local childcare business coalitions
- Support childcare advocacy organizations
- Advocate for public investment
- Partner with providers for workforce training

### As an Early Childhood Professional

**Advocating for Your Profession**:

- Join professional associations (NAEYC, state affiliates)
- Participate in advocacy days and campaigns
- Share your story with policymakers
- Connect with other professionals for collective voice

**Professional Development**:

- Pursue credentials and degrees through scholarship programs
- Seek coaching and mentoring opportunities
- Stay current with research and best practices
- Document your professional growth

**Organizing for Change**:

- Explore unionization opportunities
- Connect with organizations like Child Care Providers United
- Participate in wage and working condition campaigns
- Build solidarity with other care workers

**Quality Improvement**:

- Seek feedback and act on it
- Participate in quality rating and improvement
- Implement evidence-based practices
- Mentor newer professionals

## Community Actions

### Local Organizing

**Building Coalitions**:

- Connect parents, providers, employers, and advocates
- Partner with community organizations (libraries, healthcare, housing)
- Engage faith communities (many operate childcare)
- Involve local businesses affected by childcare shortages

**Local Government Engagement**:

- Attend city council and county board meetings
- Advocate for childcare in economic development plans
- Push for zoning changes to allow family childcare
- Request local childcare needs assessments

**Community Awareness**:

- Organize community conversations about childcare
- Share data on local childcare costs and availability
- Highlight childcare deserts in your area
- Connect childcare to workforce and economic issues

### School District Engagement

**Pre-K Expansion**:

- Advocate for school district-operated pre-K
- Support mixed delivery partnerships with community providers
- Push for full-day programs meeting working family needs
- Ensure quality standards in district programs

**Transition Support**:

- Advocate for coordinated kindergarten transition practices
- Push for preschool-to-elementary collaboration
- Support family engagement across transitions
- Ensure developmental continuity

**Facility Sharing**:

- Identify school facilities suitable for childcare
- Advocate for co-location of childcare in schools
- Support joint use agreements
- Address zoning and building code barriers

### Faith-Based Action

**Operating Quality Programs**:

- Pursue licensing and quality ratings
- Invest in staff training and compensation
- Partner with public programs (Head Start, subsidies)
- Provide scholarships for low-income families

**Advocacy**:

- Engage congregation in childcare advocacy
- Connect with denominational advocacy offices
- Provide meeting space for organizing
- Mobilize voters around childcare

## State-Level Advocacy

### Policy Priorities

**Funding Increases**:

- State pre-K establishment or expansion
- Subsidy funding to eliminate waiting lists
- Reimbursement rates at 75th percentile of market
- Workforce compensation initiatives

**Quality Improvement**:

- Stronger licensing standards
- QRIS enhancement and participation requirements
- Professional development funding
- Coaching and technical assistance

**Workforce Investment**:

- Wage supplements or floors for publicly-funded programs
- Scholarship programs for credentials and degrees
- Apprenticeship development
- Benefits access (health insurance, retirement)

**Access Expansion**:

- Eligibility expansion for subsidies
- Reduced cliff effects and smoother copay transitions
- Infant-toddler focus
- Rural and underserved area strategies

### Effective Advocacy Strategies

**Know Your State System**:

- Understand who administers childcare (licensing, subsidies, pre-K)
- Map the budget process and key decision points
- Identify legislative committees and key members
- Track agency rulemaking processes

**Build Relationships**:

- Meet with legislators in-district and at the capitol
- Develop relationships with legislative staff
- Engage with agency officials
- Build relationships across party lines

**Tell Your Story**:

- Personal stories are powerful advocacy tools
- Connect your experience to policy solutions
- Quantify impacts (costs, lost work time)
- Include child outcomes when possible

**Coalition Work**:

- Join statewide early childhood coalitions
- Connect with business coalitions
- Partner with K-12 education advocates
- Link with workforce development initiatives

### State Campaign Ideas

**Budget Campaigns**:

- "Fund Our Future" rallies and lobby days
- Budget testimony and written comments
- Media coverage of budget impacts
- Employer voices for investment

**Legislative Campaigns**:

- Bill sponsorship recruitment
- Committee hearing testimony
- Grassroots contact campaigns
- Coalition sign-on letters

**Ballot Initiatives**:

- Where available, pursue ballot measures for dedicated funding
- Colorado and New Mexico models for constitutional amendments
- Local funding measures (taxes, levies)

## Federal Advocacy

### Congressional Engagement

**Understanding Congress**:

- Childcare falls under multiple committees:
    - House Education and Labor
    - House Ways and Means
    - Senate HELP
    - Senate Finance
    - Appropriations committees
- Track relevant legislation and amendments
- Understand authorization vs. appropriation

**Constituent Contact**:

- Call congressional offices (local and DC)
- Write letters and emails (personalized, not form)
- Request meetings during recess periods
- Attend town halls and ask questions

**Advocacy Days**:

- Participate in national advocacy events (NAEYC, NHSA, etc.)
- State delegation visits to DC
- Coordinated state-level advocacy days
- Virtual advocacy opportunities

### Federal Policy Priorities

**Immediate**:

- CCDBG funding increases
- Head Start funding increases
- Child and Dependent Care Tax Credit expansion
- Childcare stabilization during crises

**Medium-Term**:

- Universal pre-K legislation
- Comprehensive childcare reform
- Workforce compensation investments
- Paid family leave

**Long-Term**:

- Entitlement to quality early care and education
- Parity investment with K-12
- Comprehensive birth-to-five system
- Integration with health and family support

### Building Political Power

**Vote and Mobilize Voters**:

- Register and vote in all elections
- Mobilize other parents and providers to vote
- Support candidates who prioritize early childhood
- Hold elected officials accountable

**Political Engagement**:

- Volunteer for campaigns supporting early childhood
- Contribute to candidates and PACs
- Run for office (school board, city council, state legislature)
- Testify and provide expertise to policymakers

## Getting Started

### First Steps for New Advocates

1. **Educate Yourself**:
   - Read this analysis and resources
   - Learn about your state's system
   - Understand current legislative debates

2. **Connect with Others**:
   - Join a local or state advocacy group
   - Attend a community meeting or advocacy event
   - Follow advocacy organizations on social media

3. **Tell Your Story**:
   - Document your childcare experiences
   - Practice sharing your story concisely
   - Connect your experience to policy solutions

4. **Take One Action**:
   - Call your state legislator about a pending bill
   - Attend a school board meeting and speak about pre-K
   - Write a letter to the editor about childcare

5. **Build the Habit**:
   - Set a monthly calendar reminder for advocacy
   - Track legislation and respond to action alerts
   - Bring others into the movement

### Sustaining Engagement

**Pace Yourself**:

- Advocacy is a marathon, not a sprint
- Celebrate small victories
- Take breaks when needed
- Share the work with others

**Build Skills Over Time**:

- Start with comfortable actions
- Gradually expand your advocacy repertoire
- Seek training opportunities
- Mentor new advocates

**Stay Connected**:

- Regular engagement with coalition
- Attend events and conferences
- Maintain relationships with policymakers
- Celebrate shared successes
