# Early Childhood Education: Resources

## Foundational Research

### Brain Development and Early Experience

Shonkoff, J.P., & Phillips, D.A. (Eds.). (2000). *From Neurons to Neighborhoods: The Science of Early Childhood Development*. National Academies Press. <https://doi.org/10.17226/9824>

- Landmark synthesis of developmental science
- Established scientific consensus on early brain development
- Influenced policy discussions for two decades
- Foundation for subsequent research

Center on the Developing Child at Harvard University. (2016). *From Best Practices to Breakthrough Impacts: A Science-Based Approach to Building a More Promising Future for Young Children and Families*. Harvard University.

- Updates and extends *Neurons to Neighborhoods* findings
- Emphasizes "serve and return" interactions
- Introduces toxic stress framework
- Policy implications of developmental science

National Scientific Council on the Developing Child. (2007). *The Science of Early Childhood Development: Closing the Gap Between What We Know and What We Do*. Harvard University.

- Accessible summary of brain science
- Connects research to policy
- Visual models of development
- Foundation for advocacy messaging

### Long-Term Outcome Studies

Schweinhart, L.J., Montie, J., Xiang, Z., Barnett, W.S., Belfield, C.R., & Nores, M. (2005). *Lifetime Effects: The High/Scope Perry Preschool Study Through Age 40*. High/Scope Press.

- Randomized controlled trial follow-up
- Documents outcomes through age 40
- Calculates return on investment ($7-16 per dollar)
- Most cited early childhood study

Campbell, F.A., Ramey, C.T., Pungello, E., Sparling, J., & Miller-Johnson, S. (2002). Early childhood education: Young adult outcomes from the Abecedarian Project. *Applied Developmental Science*, 6(1), 42-57.

- Full-day, year-round program from infancy
- Stronger effects than Perry (began earlier)
- Follow-up through age 30
- Health and mental health benefits

Heckman, J.J., Moon, S.H., Pinto, R., Savelyev, P.A., & Yavitz, A. (2010). The rate of return to the HighScope Perry Preschool Program. *Journal of Public Economics*, 94(1-2), 114-128.

- Nobel laureate's economic analysis
- Sophisticated return on investment calculation
- 7-10% annual return
- Influenced economic policy arguments

Chetty, R., Friedman, J.N., Hilger, N., Saez, E., Schanzenbach, D.W., & Yagan, D. (2011). How does your kindergarten classroom affect your earnings? Evidence from Project STAR. *Quarterly Journal of Economics*, 126(4), 1593-1660.

- Uses Tennessee class size experiment
- Long-term earnings effects
- Classroom quality matters
- Peer effects documented

### Head Start Evaluation

Puma, M., Bell, S., Cook, R., Heid, C., Broene, P., Jenkins, F., ... & Downer, J. (2012). *Third Grade Follow-up to the Head Start Impact Study Final Report*. Administration for Children & Families.

- National random assignment evaluation
- Showed initial gains but "fade-out" by third grade
- Controversial interpretation
- Quality variation not addressed

Deming, D. (2009). Early childhood intervention and life-cycle skill development: Evidence from Head Start. *American Economic Journal: Applied Economics*, 1(3), 111-134.

- Long-term benefits beyond test scores
- Higher high school graduation
- Lower crime, teen pregnancy
- Effects often missed in short-term studies

### State Pre-K Research

Barnett, W.S., Jung, K., Youn, M., & Frede, E.C. (2013). *Abbott Preschool Program Longitudinal Effects Study: Fifth Grade Follow-up*. National Institute for Early Education Research.

- New Jersey Abbott districts
- Effects through fifth grade
- High-quality program effects persist
- Comparison to typical pre-K

Gormley, W.T., Phillips, D., & Gayer, T. (2008). Preschool programs can boost school readiness. *Science*, 320(5884), 1723-1724.

- Oklahoma universal pre-K evaluation
- Large effects on school readiness
- Universal program evidence
- Regression discontinuity design

Cascio, E.U. (2021). Early childhood education in the United States: What, when, where, who, how, and why. *NBER Working Paper 28722*.

- Comprehensive review of evidence
- Synthesizes findings across studies
- Identifies what works and doesn't
- Policy implications
