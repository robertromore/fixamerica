# School Choice: Roadmap

## Overview

Reforming school choice policy requires strategic sequencing that builds political support, demonstrates success, and addresses stakeholder concerns progressively. This roadmap outlines implementation pathways at federal, state, and local levels.

## Phased Implementation Strategy

### Phase 1: Foundation Building (Years 1-2)

**Objective**: Establish information infrastructure and begin stakeholder alignment.

| Action | Timeline | Lead Actor |
|--------|----------|------------|
| School information portal development | Month 1-12 | State education agencies |
| Stakeholder convening | Month 1-6 | Governors, legislators |
| Research agenda establishment | Month 1-6 | Universities, foundations |
| Best practices documentation | Month 3-12 | NACSA, research orgs |
| Coalition building | Month 1-18 | Advocacy organizations |
| Model legislation drafting | Month 6-18 | Policy organizations |

**Key Deliverables**

| Deliverable | Success Metric |
|-------------|----------------|
| Information portal | 80% of schools with complete data |
| Stakeholder map | All major groups engaged |
| Research partnerships | 5+ rigorous studies launched |
| Model bills | Templates for 3+ reform areas |
| Coalition | Cross-ideological support secured |

### Phase 2: Transparency and Access (Years 2-4)

**Objective**: Implement information and access reforms with broad support.

| Action | Timeline | Lead Actor |
|--------|----------|------------|
| Common enrollment systems | Month 13-36 | Districts, states |
| Transportation coordination | Month 13-36 | Districts |
| Navigator program pilots | Month 13-24 | Community organizations |
| Reporting requirements | Month 18-30 | State legislatures |
| Application simplification | Month 18-30 | States, districts |

**Key Deliverables**

| Deliverable | Success Metric |
|-------------|----------------|
| Unified enrollment | 50% of choice-intensive districts |
| Transportation access | Low-income access increased 30% |
| Navigator programs | 10 major city pilots |
| Reporting compliance | 95% of schools reporting |
| Application system | Single application in 20 states |

### Phase 3: Accountability Enhancement (Years 3-5)

**Objective**: Implement accountability standards across all publicly funded schools.

| Action | Timeline | Lead Actor |
|--------|----------|------------|
| Authorizer standards legislation | Month 25-48 | State legislatures |
| Voucher testing requirements | Month 30-48 | State legislatures |
| Financial transparency rules | Month 30-42 | States |
| Civil rights compliance mechanisms | Month 36-48 | Federal government |
| Performance-based charter renewal | Month 36-60 | Authorizers |

**Key Deliverables**

| Deliverable | Success Metric |
|-------------|----------------|
| Authorizer standards | 30 states with quality standards |
| Voucher accountability | Testing in 75% of programs |
| Financial transparency | Public reporting in all programs |
| Civil rights enforcement | Complaint mechanisms operational |
| Low-performer closures | Bottom 10% of charters closed |

### Phase 4: Integration and Equity (Years 4-6)

**Objective**: Address segregation and equity concerns systematically.

| Action | Timeline | Lead Actor |
|--------|----------|------------|
| Controlled choice pilots | Month 37-60 | Districts |
| Weighted lottery requirements | Month 42-60 | States |
| Facilities equity programs | Month 48-72 | States |
| Special education strengthening | Month 42-60 | Federal, states |
| Integration incentive grants | Month 48-72 | Federal government |

**Key Deliverables**

| Deliverable | Success Metric |
|-------------|----------------|
| Controlled choice | 20 districts implementing |
| Diversity lotteries | 50% of charters using |
| Facilities equity | Charter-TPS gap reduced 50% |
| Special education | IDEA coverage extended |
| Integration incentives | 25 states participating |

### Phase 5: System Optimization (Years 5-8)

**Objective**: Achieve comprehensive reform and continuous improvement.

| Action | Timeline | Lead Actor |
|--------|----------|------------|
| Funding formula reform | Month 49-72 | States |
| Sector collaboration institutionalization | Month 49-72 | Districts |
| Community school expansion | Month 49-84 | Districts, states |

**Key Deliverables**

| Deliverable | Success Metric |
|-------------|----------------|

| Sector collaboration | Cross-sector planning in 50% of districts |
| Community schools | 3,000+ community schools |
| Virtual quality | Graduation rates above 70% |
| Outcome data | Post-secondary tracking in 40 states |

## State-Level Implementation

### Early Adopter States (High Feasibility)

**Characteristics**

| Characteristic | Indicators |
|----------------|------------|
| Bipartisan reform appetite | History of education innovation |
| Moderate choice presence | Neither fully public nor fully choice |
| Strong SEA capacity | Resources for implementation |
| Political balance | Neither extreme red nor blue |

**Target States**

| State | Opportunity | Priority Reforms |
|-------|-------------|------------------|
| Colorado | Balanced system, reform history | Transparency, authorization |
| Massachusetts | Strong charters, accountability culture | Integration, information |
| Tennessee | Rapid growth, willing to reform | Accountability, transparency |
| Georgia | Emerging choice, new leadership | Foundation building |
| Pennsylvania | Urgent fiscal issues | Funding reform, accountability |
| North Carolina | Growing choice, mixed politics | Transparency, access |

### Challenging States (Lower Feasibility)

**High-Choice States**

| State | Challenge | Long-term Strategy |
|-------|-----------|-------------------|
| Arizona | Universal ESA, weak accountability | Document problems, build opposition |
| Florida | Dominant choice, political support | Accountability incrementalism |
| Texas | Growing rapidly, limited oversight | Quality authorization focus |
| Indiana | Established vouchers | Research, gradual accountability |

**Low-Choice States**

| State | Challenge | Strategy |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| Kentucky | No charters, rural concerns | Community schools alternative |
| Montana | Limited options, small population | Virtual quality focus |
| Vermont | Strong TPS culture | Maintain quality, address rural |

## Risk Mitigation

### Political Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Partisan polarization | Maintain bipartisan framing |
| Election changes | Build institutional reforms |
| Interest group opposition | Stakeholder engagement |
| Media backlash | Proactive communication |

### Implementation Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Capacity constraints | Phased implementation |
| Technology failures | Tested pilots before scaling |
| Resistance from operators | Incentives for compliance |
| Funding shortfalls | Diverse funding sources |

### Unintended Consequences

| Risk | Monitoring |
|------|------------|
| Gaming of metrics | Multiple measures, auditing |
| Cream-skimming | Demographic tracking |
| Quality school closures | Due process, appeals |
| Innovation stifling | Flexibility provisions |
