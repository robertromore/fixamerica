# Special Education: Resources

## Federal Statutes

### IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act)

**Primary Statute**

- 20 U.S.C. Chapter 33 (Sections 1400-1482)
- Part A: General Provisions (20 U.S.C. §§ 1400-1409)
- Part B: Assistance for Education of All Children with Disabilities (20 U.S.C. §§ 1411-1419)
- Part C: Infants and Toddlers with Disabilities (20 U.S.C. §§ 1431-1444)
- Part D: National Activities to Improve Education of Children with Disabilities (20 U.S.C. §§ 1450-1482)

**Key Provisions**

- 20 U.S.C. § 1400(d): Purpose - FAPE for all children with disabilities
- 20 U.S.C. § 1412(a)(5): LRE requirement
- 20 U.S.C. § 1414(d): IEP requirements
- 20 U.S.C. § 1415: Procedural safeguards
- 20 U.S.C. § 1412(a)(24): Disproportionality requirements

**Implementing Regulations**

- 34 C.F.R. Part 300 (Part B regulations)
- 34 C.F.R. Part 303 (Part C regulations)

### Related Federal Laws

**Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act**

- 29 U.S.C. § 794
- Prohibits disability discrimination by federal fund recipients
- Broader eligibility than IDEA
- 34 C.F.R. Part 104 (implementing regulations)

**Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)**

- 42 U.S.C. Chapter 126
- Title II covers public schools (42 U.S.C. §§ 12131-12165)
- Accessibility and nondiscrimination requirements
- 28 C.F.R. Part 35 (implementing regulations)

**Elementary and Secondary Education Act (ESEA/ESSA)**

- 20 U.S.C. Chapter 70
- Assessment requirements for students with disabilities
- Title I services for students with disabilities
- Accountability for subgroup performance

## Key Supreme Court Cases

### Foundational Cases

**Board of Education v. Rowley**, 458 U.S. 176 (1982)

- First Supreme Court interpretation of FAPE
- Held: FAPE requires "personalized instruction with sufficient support services to permit the child to benefit educationally"
- Established procedural and substantive components of FAPE

**Irving Independent School District v. Tatro**, 468 U.S. 883 (1984)

- Defined "related services" under IDEA
- Held: Clean intermittent catheterization is related service, not excluded medical service
- Established "bright line" medical services test

**Honig v. Doe**, 484 U.S. 305 (1988)

- Addressed discipline and "stay-put" provision
- Held: Cannot unilaterally exclude student for disability-related behavior
- Schools may suspend up to 10 days; must seek court order for longer

**Florence County School District Four v. Carter**, 510 U.S. 7 (1993)

- Addressed private school reimbursement
- Held: Parents may be reimbursed even if private school not state-approved
- Expanded remedy options for FAPE denial

**Winkelman v. Parma City School District**, 550 U.S. 516 (2007)

- Addressed parent standing in IDEA proceedings
- Held: Parents have independent rights under IDEA, not just derivative rights
- Can represent themselves in due process and federal court

**Endrew F. v. Douglas County School District**, 580 U.S. 386 (2017)

- Clarified FAPE standard post-Rowley
- Held: IEP must be "reasonably calculated to enable a child to make progress appropriate in light of the child's circumstances"
- Rejected "merely more than de minimis" standard

**Fry v. Napoleon Community Schools**, 580 U.S. 154 (2017)

- Addressed exhaustion of IDEA procedures
- Held: Must exhaust IDEA procedures only when relief sought is also available under IDEA
- Clarified relationship between IDEA and ADA/504

### Circuit Court Cases of Note

**Daniel R.R. v. State Board of Education**, 874 F.2d 1036 (5th Cir. 1989)

- Established two-part LRE test widely adopted
- Can student be educated satisfactorily in general education with supplementary aids?
- If not, is student mainstreamed to maximum extent appropriate?

**Oberti v. Board of Education**, 995 F.2d 1204 (3d Cir. 1993)

- Applied Daniel R.R. framework
- Strong presumption in favor of inclusion
- Schools must make reasonable efforts before removing to segregated setting

**Sacramento City Unified School District v. Rachel H.**, 14 F.3d 1398 (9th Cir. 1994)

- Four-factor test for LRE
- Educational benefits, non-academic benefits, effect on other students, cost
- Influential in western circuits
