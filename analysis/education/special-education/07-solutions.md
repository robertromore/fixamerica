# Special Education: Solutions

## Funding Solutions

### Full Federal Funding

**Proposal**: Appropriate federal funds to cover 40% of excess cost as originally promised in 1975.

**Implementation**

- Phase in over 5-7 years to reach full funding
- Adjust calculation to reflect true current costs
- Index to inflation for sustained funding
- Tie to outcome requirements to ensure quality

**Cost Estimate**

- Current federal funding: ~$15 billion
- Full 40% funding: ~$45-50 billion
- Annual increase needed: ~$30-35 billion
- Phase-in: ~$5-7 billion per year for 5-7 years

**Benefits**

- Relieves unfunded mandate on states and districts
- Reduces pressure on local property taxes
- Enables adequate services without resource rationing
- Addresses geographic inequities in funding

**Potential Concerns**

- Significant federal budget increase
- Does not guarantee quality improvement
- States may reduce their own contributions (supplanting)
- Distribution formula may not target highest need

**Design Elements to Address Concerns**

- Maintenance of effort requirements to prevent supplanting
- Outcome accountability tied to funding
- Weighted distribution toward high-need districts
- Technical assistance to support effective use

### Weighted Student Funding Reform

**Proposal**: Reform state funding formulas to provide adequate, equitably distributed funding based on student needs.

**Key Elements**

- Base funding set at level determined by adequacy study
- Weights for disability categories reflecting actual excess cost
- Additional weights for concentrated poverty
- Geographic cost adjustment
- Cap on wealthy district spending to reduce inequality

**Example Weights**

| Category | Suggested Weight |
|----------|-----------------|
| Speech/Language Impairment | 0.5x base |
| Specific Learning Disability | 0.7x base |
| Other Health Impairment | 0.8x base |
| Autism | 1.5x base |
| Emotional Disturbance | 1.5x base |
| Intellectual Disability | 1.8x base |
| Multiple Disabilities | 2.5x base |
| Deaf/Hearing Impaired | 2.0x base |
| Visually Impaired | 2.0x base |

**Benefits**

- Funding follows students with appropriate amounts
- Reduces incentive for cost-based placement decisions
- More transparent than reimbursement systems
- Can be adjusted as costs change

### Medicaid Maximization

**Proposal**: Improve claiming of Medicaid funds for eligible special education services.

**Current Status**

- Medicaid can reimburse school-based health services for eligible students
- Many districts under-claim due to complexity
- Billions potentially available but not accessed

**Improvements Needed**

- Simplify claiming processes
- Train districts on allowable claims
- Provide state technical assistance
- Clarify CMS guidance on school-based services
- Increase federal matching rate for school Medicaid

**Benefits**

- Additional revenue without new state appropriations
- Leverages existing federal program
- Does not increase local property tax burden
- Can fund health-related special education services

## Inclusion Solutions

### Universal Design for Learning Implementation

**Proposal**: Require and support Universal Design for Learning (UDL) as framework for all instruction.

**UDL Principles**

- Multiple means of engagement (why of learning)
- Multiple means of representation (what of learning)
- Multiple means of action and expression (how of learning)

**Implementation**

- Include UDL in teacher preparation standards
- Provide professional development for current teachers
- Fund curriculum materials designed with UDL
- Require UDL consideration in IEP development
- Support technology that enables UDL

**Benefits**

- Proactive design reduces need for individual accommodations
- Benefits all learners, not just those with disabilities
- Reduces distinction between "special" and "general" education
- Evidence-based approach with research support

### Co-Teaching and Collaborative Models

**Proposal**: Fund and support co-teaching and other collaborative models for inclusive education.

**Co-Teaching Models**

- One teach, one assist
- Parallel teaching
- Station teaching
- Alternative teaching
- Team teaching

**Implementation Requirements**

- Common planning time (minimum 1 hour/week)
- Matched co-teachers with compatible styles
- Professional development on co-teaching
- Smaller class sizes in co-taught classrooms
- Administrative support and observation

**Benefits**

- Two teachers addressing diverse needs
- Students with disabilities educated with peers
- General and special educators share expertise
- Reduces need for separate settings

### Multi-Tiered Systems of Support (MTSS)

**Proposal**: Implement MTSS/RTI frameworks that provide early intervention before special education is needed.

**Three-Tier Framework**

| Tier | Population | Intervention |
|------|------------|--------------|
| Tier 1 | All students (80-85%) | High-quality core instruction |
| Tier 2 | Some students (10-15%) | Targeted small group intervention |
| Tier 3 | Few students (5-10%) | Intensive individualized intervention |

**Key Elements**

- Universal screening to identify students at risk
- Progress monitoring to evaluate intervention effectiveness
- Data-based decision making for movement between tiers
- Evidence-based interventions at each tier
- Fidelity monitoring of implementation

**Benefits**

- Early intervention prevents some disabilities
- Reduces inappropriate referrals
- General education takes more responsibility
- Data-driven decision making improves instruction

### Inclusive Placement Requirements

**Proposal**: Strengthen LRE requirements and provide resources for inclusion.

**Regulatory Changes**

- Require districts to demonstrate why general education with supports is not appropriate
- Mandate inclusion support services before more restrictive placement
- Set state-level inclusion targets with consequences for failure
- Require annual LRE review and justification for continued restrictive placement

**Resource Requirements**

- Inclusion specialists and coaches in districts
- Professional development on inclusive practices
- Supplementary aids and services funding
- Paraprofessional support in inclusive classrooms

**Benefits**

- Shifts presumption toward inclusion
- Requires schools to build capacity before segregating
- Creates accountability for LRE implementation
- Supports teachers in inclusive practice

## Disproportionality Solutions

### Comprehensive Evaluation Reform

**Proposal**: Reform evaluation practices to reduce bias and ensure accurate identification.

**Evaluation Standards**

- Multiple measures required (no single test determines eligibility)
- Culturally responsive assessment practices
- Evaluators with cultural competence training
- Assessment in native language or mode of communication
- Consideration of educational history and opportunity

**Workforce Diversity**

- Recruit diverse school psychologists and evaluators
- Cultural competence requirements for licensure
- Community-based evaluation options
- Parent input valued in evaluation

**Eligibility Decision Making**

- Structured decision-making protocols
- Diverse eligibility teams
- Training on implicit bias
- Documentation of alternative explanations considered

**Benefits**

- More accurate identification regardless of race
- Reduced subjective bias in evaluation
- Greater family trust in process
- Students receive appropriate services

### Significant Disproportionality Requirements

**Proposal**: Strengthen identification, monitoring, and remediation of significant disproportionality.

**Current Requirements**

- States must identify districts with significant disproportionality
- Districts must set aside 15% of IDEA funds for early intervention
- Must conduct root cause analysis and develop plan

**Strengthening Needed**

- Standardized methodology for determining significant disproportionality
- Lower thresholds for triggering identification
- Mandatory specific corrective actions
- State monitoring of district plans
- Consequences for failure to improve

**Root Cause Analysis Requirements**

- Examine referral, evaluation, and placement practices
- Analyze discipline data alongside identification
- Review general education quality and interventions
- Assess evaluator and teacher diversity
- Identify systemic contributors to disparities

### Discipline Disproportionality

**Proposal**: Address discipline disparities that contribute to and result from identification disparities.

**Discipline Reform**

- Positive behavioral interventions and supports (PBIS) required
- Restorative practices as alternative to exclusion
- Limits on suspensions and expulsions for all students
- Manifest determination process review
- Data collection and reporting on discipline by disability and race

**Restraint and Seclusion**

- Federal prohibition on prone restraint and mechanical restraint
- Seclusion only when imminent danger and no alternative
- Documentation and reporting requirements
- Parent notification within 24 hours
- State data collection and analysis

**Benefits**

- Reduces school-to-prison pipeline
- Keeps students in school learning
- Addresses intersection of race and disability in discipline
- Protects student safety and dignity

## Workforce Solutions

### Competitive Compensation

**Proposal**: Increase special education teacher compensation to address shortages.

**Federal Incentives**

- Loan forgiveness: Full forgiveness after 5 years in special education
- Salary supplements: Federal funding for $10,000+ annual supplements
- Housing assistance: Support for teachers in high-cost areas
- Signing bonuses: Recruitment incentives for high-need areas

**State Requirements**

- Minimum salary floors for special education teachers
- Premium pay for shortage areas
- Loan forgiveness programs
- Compensation competitive with other master's-level professions

**Benefits**

- Attracts candidates to special education
- Retains effective teachers
- Signals value of special education profession
- Addresses shortage crisis

### Improved Preparation

**Proposal**: Reform teacher preparation to produce effective, well-prepared special educators.

**Preparation Standards**

- Extended clinical experience (full year residency)
- Effective mentorship during preparation
- Evidence-based practices in curriculum
- Collaboration skills with general education
- Assessment and IEP writing integrated with instruction

**Residency Programs**

- Yearlong clinical experience in high-need school
- Stipend during residency
- Intensive mentorship
- Service commitment to high-need schools
- Federal funding for expansion

**General Education Preparation**

- Require disability and inclusion coursework for all teachers
- Co-teaching preparation in programs
- Clinical experience in inclusive settings
- UDL training for all teachers

### Paraprofessional Improvement

**Proposal**: Improve compensation, training, and roles for paraprofessionals.

**Compensation**

- Living wage ($20/hour minimum suggested)
- Benefits including health insurance
- Career ladder to teacher certification
- Recognition and professional development

**Training Requirements**

- Initial training on disability, behavior, instruction
- Ongoing professional development
- Training specific to students they support
- Supervision requirements

**Role Clarification**

- Teachers responsible for instruction; paras support
- Avoid one-on-one assignment as default
- Promote independence rather than dependence
- Clear job descriptions and expectations

## Transition Solutions

### Earlier and Stronger Transition Planning

**Proposal**: Begin transition planning earlier and make it more meaningful.

**Age Requirements**

- Transition planning beginning at 14 (restore from 16)
- Self-determination instruction in middle school
- Career exploration starting in elementary
- Post-secondary planning intensified in high school

**Planning Requirements**

- Measurable post-secondary goals in employment, education, independent living
- Transition assessments to inform goals
- Annual updating based on progress
- Student participation required

**Services Requirements**

- Community work experiences
- Instruction in self-advocacy
- Linkages to adult services
- Financial literacy and planning
- Social skills and relationships

### Interagency Collaboration

**Proposal**: Improve coordination between schools and adult service agencies.

**Required Partnerships**

- Vocational rehabilitation engaged before exit
- Developmental disability services involved in planning
- Mental health services coordinated
- Higher education disability services connected
- Community organizations included

**Data Sharing**

- Agreements to share information for planning
- Post-school outcome tracking
- Coordinated case management
- Smooth handoff at exit

**Benefits**

- Continuity of services at transition
- No "cliff" at age 22
- Adult agencies prepared for incoming individuals
- Better post-secondary outcomes

### Employment Outcomes Focus

**Proposal**: Make competitive integrated employment the primary goal of transition.

**Employment First**

- Competitive integrated employment as first option
- Sheltered workshops and day programs only when employment attempted and failed
- Phase out sub-minimum wage certificates
- Support customized employment and self-employment

**Work-Based Learning**

- Paid work experiences during school
- Internships with community employers
- Job coaching and supports
- Employer partnerships

**Benefits**

- Employment is route to independence
- Higher expectations lead to better outcomes
- Reduces long-term dependency on services
- Values contribution of people with disabilities

## Enforcement Solutions

### Strengthened Federal Monitoring

**Proposal**: Increase Department of Education capacity and will for enforcement.

**Monitoring Improvements**

- More frequent and intensive state monitoring
- Direct monitoring of districts with persistent problems
- Outcome measures in addition to compliance
- Unannounced monitoring visits

**Consequences**

- Progressive sanctions for persistent noncompliance
- Actual funding withholding for egregious violations
- Required corrective action with timelines
- Technical assistance with accountability

**Staffing**

- Increase OSEP staff capacity
- Regional presence for monitoring
- Specialized investigators
- Technical assistance providers

### State Enforcement

**Proposal**: Require states to effectively monitor and enforce within their jurisdictions.

**Monitoring Requirements**

- All districts monitored on meaningful cycle
- High-risk districts monitored more frequently
- Outcome data used in monitoring
- Student and parent input incorporated

**Enforcement Tools**

- Progressive interventions for noncompliance
- Required corrective action
- State authority to withhold local funds
- Technical assistance to build capacity

### Parent Enforcement Tools

**Proposal**: Make it easier for parents to enforce their children's rights.

**Due Process Improvements**

- Free or low-cost representation
- Streamlined hearing procedures
- Burden of proof on school districts
- Expanded remedies including compensatory education
- Attorney fee awards for prevailing parents

**Complaint Investigation**

- State complaints with real consequences
- Systemic complaint option
- Enforceable corrective action
- Follow-up monitoring

**Alternative Dispute Resolution**

- Mediation at no cost
- IEP facilitation option
- Ombudsman programs
- Resolution session improvements

## Systemic Reform

### Merge Special and General Education

**Proposal**: Move toward unified system rather than parallel special education system.

**Long-Term Vision**

- All teachers prepared to serve diverse learners
- Flexible supports available without categorical labels
- Resources based on need, not category
- Inclusion as default for all students
- Special expertise available to all

**Intermediate Steps**

- Combined preparation programs
- Shared professional development
- Collaborative teaching models
- Blended funding
- Unified accountability

**Benefits**

- Eliminates "us vs. them" mentality
- Reduces stigma of special education
- Ensures all teachers have skills for inclusion
- Creates more efficient use of resources

### Prevention Focus

**Proposal**: Invest in prevention and early intervention to reduce need for special education.

**Early Childhood**

- Universal pre-K with inclusion focus
- Early intervention (Part C) expansion
- Developmental screening for all children
- Family support services

**General Education Quality**

- Effective core instruction for all
- Multi-tiered support before referral
- Literacy and math intervention
- Behavioral supports in general education

**Benefits**

- Prevents some disabilities through early intervention
- Identifies needs before they become severe
- Reduces special education enrollment over time
- More effective use of resources
