# Education: History

## Overview

American education evolved from colonial religious instruction through the common school movement, compulsory attendance laws, desegregation battles, federal involvement, standards-based reform, and today's school choice debates. Each era has reshaped who receives education, who controls it, and what it's forâ€”reflecting broader struggles over democracy, equality, and the role of government.

## Key Turning Points

### *Brown v. Board of Education* (1954)

| Aspect | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Ruling** | Segregated schools unconstitutional |
| **Implementation** | "All deliberate speed" allowed delay |
| **Resistance** | Massive resistance, private academies |
| **Integration peak** | 1980s, then resegregation |
| **Legacy** | Symbolic victory, incomplete implementation |

### Elementary and Secondary Education Act (1965)

| Aspect | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Purpose** | Federal aid for disadvantaged students |
| **Mechanism** | Title I formula funding |
| **Scope** | Largest federal K-12 program |
| **Evolution** | Became NCLB (2001), then ESSA (2015) |
| **Legacy** | Established federal role, equity focus |

### "A Nation at Risk" (1983)

| Aspect | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Claims** | "Rising tide of mediocrity" |
| **Response** | Standards movement, accountability |
| **Critiques** | Overstated crisis, ignored context |
| **Policy impact** | Testing, standards, choice |
| **Legacy** | Shaped 40 years of reform debate |

### No Child Left Behind (2001)

| Aspect | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Requirements** | Annual testing, AYP, sanctions |
| **Goals** | 100% proficiency by 2014 |
| **Results** | Widespread "failure," teaching to test |
| **Backlash** | Opt-out movement, bipartisan opposition |
| **Legacy** | Replaced by ESSA, testing remains |

### COVID-19 School Closures (2020)

| Aspect | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Closures** | Most students remote at some point |
| **Duration** | Varied widely, some 18+ months |
| **Learning loss** | Decades of progress erased |
| **Inequity** | Low-income, minority students hit hardest |
| **Legacy** | Accelerated choice, chronic absenteeism crisis |

## Evolution by Domain

### Who Gets Educated

| Era | Access |
|-----|--------|
| Colonial | White propertied males |
| 1800s | Common schools for white children |
| 1860s-1960s | Separate and unequal for Black students |
| 1950s-1970s | Desegregation (partial) |
| 1975+ | Special education rights |
| Present | Universal K-12, unequal quality |

### Who Controls Education

| Era | Control |
|-----|---------|
| Colonial-1800s | Church, local communities |
| 1830s-1900s | State standardization, local operation |
| 1965+ | Federal role expands (funding, civil rights) |
| 1980s-2000s | Federal accountability mandates |
| 2010s+ | State standards, local flexibility |

### How Education Is Funded

| Era | Funding Model |
|-----|---------------|
| Colonial | Private, church |
| 1800s | Local property taxes |
| 1900s | State aid increases |
| 1965+ | Federal Title I, categorical programs |
| Present | Local 45%, state 45%, federal 10% |

### What Is Taught

| Era | Curriculum |
|-----|------------|

| 1800s | Reading, writing, arithmetic, citizenship |
| 1900s | Vocational tracks, comprehensive high school |
| 1980s-2000s | Standards, testing, accountability |

## History of School Segregation

### Legal Framework

| Period | Status |
|--------|--------|
| Pre-1954 | "Separate but equal" legal |
| 1954-1964 | Brown, but massive resistance |
| 1964-1980s | Active desegregation, court orders |
| 1990s-2000s | Courts release districts |
| 2007+ | Voluntary integration limited (*Parents Involved*) |

### Desegregation Progress

| Year | % Black students in majority-white schools |
|------|---------------------------------------------|
| 1954 | 0% |
| 1964 | 2% |
| 1972 | 36% (South peak) |
| 1988 | 44% (national peak) |
| 2016 | 23% |

### Resegregation Causes

| Factor | Mechanism |
|--------|-----------|
| Court release | Ended mandatory busing |
| White flight | Suburban migration |
| School choice | Sorting by race/class |
| Housing segregation | Neighborhood schools reflect housing |
| Political opposition | Anti-busing backlash |

## What History Teaches

### Patterns That Recur

| Pattern | Examples |
|---------|----------|
| **Reform cycles** | Progressive era, 1960s, 1980s standards, NCLB |
| **Implementation gaps** | Brown, Title I, NCLB all underimplemented |
| **Resistance to equity** | Segregation academies, Prop 13, funding lawsuits |
| **Federal-state tension** | Local control vs. civil rights |
| **Blame teachers** | Each reform blames educators |

### What Has Worked

| Approach | Evidence |
|----------|----------|
| **Desegregation** | Improved outcomes when implemented |
| **Funding increases** | Court-ordered adequacy cases show gains |
| **Early childhood** | Head Start, pre-K show lasting effects |
| **High-dosage tutoring** | Proven learning gains |
| **Small class size** | Tennessee STAR showed benefits |

### What Hasn't Worked

| Approach | Evidence |
|----------|----------|
| **Test-based accountability alone** | Narrowed curriculum, gaming |
| **Vouchers at scale** | Mixed-to-negative results |
| **Teacher evaluation by test scores** | Poor reliability, demoralized workforce |
| **For-profit colleges** | High debt, low completion |
| **Technology as silver bullet** | No consistent gains |

### Recurring Debates

| Debate | Framing |
|--------|---------|
| Equity vs. excellence | Raising floor vs. ceiling |
| Local control vs. standards | Who decides what's taught |
| Public vs. private | Common institution vs. choice |
| Integration vs. choice | Diversity vs. parent preference |
| Traditional vs. progressive | Basics vs. whole child |
