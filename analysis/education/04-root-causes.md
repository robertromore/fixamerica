# Education: Root Causes

## Overview

America's educational challenges stem from interconnected structural, economic, political, and cultural factors that perpetuate inequality, underfunding, and dysfunction. The reliance on local property taxes, fragmented governance, racial and economic segregation, undervaluation of teaching, and ideological battles over the purpose of education all contribute to a system that fails to deliver equal opportunity.

---

## Structural Causes

### Funding System

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Property tax reliance** | Wealth determines school resources |
| **Local control tradition** | 13,000+ districts with different capacities |
| **State formula complexity** | Often doesn't fully equalize |
| **Federal share too small** | 10% can't fix state/local inequity |
| **Categorical funding** | Fragmented, compliance-heavy |

### Governance Fragmentation

| Problem | Manifestation |
|---------|---------------|
| **13,000+ school districts** | Massive variation in size, capacity |
| **Local school boards** | Amateur governance, captured by interest groups |
| **State-local tension** | Unclear accountability |
| **Federal role limited** | Constitutional constraints |
| **Regional mismatch** | District boundaries don't match labor markets |

### Segregation Structures

| Factor | Mechanism |
|--------|-----------|
| **Neighborhood schools** | Reflect housing segregation |
| **District boundaries** | Separate cities from suburbs |
| **School choice sorting** | Parents choose by race/class |
| **Tracking within schools** | Resegregation inside buildings |
| **Gifted program access** | Disproportionately white/affluent |

### Teacher Pipeline

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Low prestige** | Less competitive applicants |
| **Weak preparation** | Variable ed school quality |
| **Credentialing barriers** | Restrict supply |
| **Geographic maldistribution** | Shortages in rural, urban |
| **Attrition rates** | 50% leave within 5 years |

## Economic Causes

### Funding Inequity

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Wealth-based funding** | $5,000-10,000 per-pupil gaps |
| **State disinvestment** | Reduced higher ed funding |
| **Recession cycles** | Education first cut, last restored |
| **Tax limitation movements** | Prop 13 model constrains revenue |
| **Competition for dollars** | Healthcare, pensions crowd out education |

### Teacher Compensation

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Pay gap** | 20% below comparable professions |
| **Flat salary schedules** | Don't reward effectiveness |
| **Benefits costs** | Crowd out salary increases |
| **Geographic variation** | Low-cost states pay less |
| **No market signals** | Shortages don't raise pay in field |

### Higher Education Costs

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **State disinvestment** | Shifted costs to students |
| **Amenities arms race** | Non-educational spending |
| **Administrative bloat** | Staff growth outpaces faculty |
| **Financial aid discounting** | List prices disconnected from costs |
| **Credential inflation** | Degrees required for more jobs |

### Childcare Market Failure

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Labor-intensive** | Can't increase "productivity" |
| **Cost-quality tradeoff** | Low wages = high turnover |
| **Private market model** | Unaffordable at quality wages |
| **Subsidy gaps** | Most families ineligible for help |

## Interconnections

### Reinforcing Cycles

```text
Funding Inequality Cycle:
Property tax funding → Wealthy areas have more → Better schools →
Higher property values → More tax revenue → Gap widens
```

```text
Segregation Cycle:
Housing segregation → Neighborhood schools segregated →
White/wealthy parents avoid diverse schools → School choice increases segregation →
Housing patterns reinforced → Segregation persists
```

```text
Teacher Quality Cycle:
Low pay → Less competitive applicants → Lower quality →
Justifies low pay → Working conditions worsen →
More attrition → Harder to recruit → Quality declines
```

### Cause Interactions

| Interaction | Example |
|-------------|---------|
| Structural + Economic | Local funding ensures wealthy districts have more |
| Political + Cultural | Culture wars distract from equity issues |
| Economic + Cultural | Meritocracy myth justifies inequality |
| Structural + Political | Fragmentation makes coordination impossible |
| Cultural + Structural | Racism shaped district boundaries |

### Why Problems Persist

| Reason | Mechanism |
|--------|-----------|
| **Beneficiaries have power** | Wealthy parents protect advantages |
| **Children can't vote** | No direct political voice |
| **Costs are hidden** | Inequality not visible to advantaged |
| **Results take time** | Political cycles shorter than education gains |
| **Complexity** | Easy to blame wrong causes |
| **Federalism** | No single actor can fix system |

## Causal Hierarchy

### Primary Causes (Most Fundamental)

| Cause | Why Primary |
|-------|-------------|
| **Property tax funding** | Creates wealth-based inequality |
| **Housing segregation** | Determines who attends which school |
| **Local control ideology** | Prevents equalization |
| **Teacher undervaluation** | Cultural devaluation of profession |

### Secondary Causes (Derive from Primary)

| Cause | Derives From |
|-------|--------------|
| Funding gaps | Property tax + local control |
| School segregation | Housing segregation + neighborhood schools |
| Teacher shortage | Low pay + low prestige |
| Achievement gaps | All of the above |

### Tertiary Causes (Symptoms)

| Issue | Actually Caused By |
|-------|-------------------|
| Low test scores | Poverty, segregation, underfunding |
| Teacher burnout | Working conditions, disrespect |
| School "failure" | Concentrated poverty |
| College completion gaps | K-12 preparation, cost |

## Why Markets Won't Solve It

| Market Failure | Explanation |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Information asymmetry** | Parents can't evaluate quality |
| **Cream-skimming** | Schools select easy-to-serve students |
| **Externalities** | Education benefits society broadly |
| **Public good** | Democratic citizenship not marketable |
| **Natural monopoly** | Not enough demand for competition everywhere |
| **Non-excludable benefits** | Can't capture all returns privately |
| **Equity concerns** | Markets don't ensure equal access |
