# Higher Education: Implementation Roadmap

## Strategic Approach

Higher education reform requires coordinated action across federal, state, and institutional levels. The sequence matters: some reforms enable others, while premature action on certain fronts can undermine broader goals.

### Phased Implementation

## Phase 1: Foundation (Years 1-2)

### Federal Actions

**Immediate Executive Actions**

| Action | Authority | Timeline |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| Strengthen gainful employment enforcement | Regulatory | Month 1-6 |
| Enhance College Scorecard data | Administrative | Month 3-12 |
| Streamline PSLF processing | Administrative | Month 1-6 |
| Improve income-driven repayment | Regulatory | Month 1-12 |
| Protect borrower rights | Regulatory | Month 3-12 |

**Year 1 Legislative Priorities**

1. **Pell Grant increase and indexing**
    - Increase maximum to $10,000 (Phase 1)
    - Index to inflation permanently
    - Restore eligibility for incarcerated students
    - Extend to DACA recipients
    - Cost: $15 billion/year

2. **Simplify FAFSA**
    - Reduce questions from 108 to 20
    - Use prior-prior year income (implemented)
    - Automatic Pell eligibility determination
    - Direct IRS data sharing

3. **Short-term Pell eligibility**
    - Programs as short as 150 hours eligible
    - Quality requirements (gainful employment)
    - Stackability toward longer credentials

**Year 2 Legislative Priorities**

1. **Free community college framework**
    - Federal-state partnership structure
    - Maintenance of effort requirements
    - Quality standards
    - Planning grants for states
    - Cost: $9 billion/year federal share

2. **Basic accountability measures**
    - Minimum completion rate standards
    - Debt-to-earnings disclosure requirements
    - Cohort default rate enforcement
    - Program-level data collection

### State-Level Priorities (Years 1-2)

**Early Adopter States**

Focus on states with existing momentum:

- Tennessee, Oregon: Expand promise programs
- California, New York: Strengthen existing systems
- New Mexico: Evaluate Opportunity Scholarship

**Model Legislation**

1. **Articulation and transfer**
    - Common course numbering systems
    - Guaranteed admission for AA completers
    - Credit-by-exam policies

2. **Transparency requirements**
    - Net price disclosure
    - Program outcome reporting
    - Standardized financial aid letters

3. **For-profit oversight**
    - State authorization requirements
    - Bond requirements
    - Consumer protection enforcement

### Institutional Actions (Years 1-2)

**Quick Wins**

| Action | Impact | Difficulty |
|--------|--------|------------|
| Emergency aid funds | Reduces dropout from financial crisis | Low |
| Proactive advising pilots | Early intervention | Medium |
| Textbook cost reduction | OER adoption | Low |
| Transfer pathway maps | Student planning | Medium |

**Planning and Assessment**

- Baseline outcome data by program
- Administrative cost inventory
- Faculty composition analysis
- Student support gap analysis

## Phase 2: Expansion (Years 3-5)

### Federal Actions

**Major Legislation**

1. **Higher Education Act Reauthorization**
    - Comprehensive reform vehicle
    - Every authorization expires without action
    - Opportunity for comprehensive changes

2. **Double the Pell Grant**
    - Maximum to $14,790
    - Cover full public tuition for lowest-income
    - Cost: $30 billion additional/year

3. **Federal-State Partnership Act**
    - Matching funds for state investment
    - Maintenance of effort requirements
    - Free community college national program
    - Funding for completion initiatives

4. **Accountability Framework**
    - Risk-sharing pilot programs
    - Outcome floor requirements
    - Accreditation reform
    - For-profit sector restrictions

**Regulatory Actions**

- Expand gainful employment to more programs
- Strengthen borrower defense rules
- Update accreditor recognition criteria
- Enhanced data collection requirements

### State-Level Expansion (Years 3-5)

**Scale Free Community College**

Target: 25+ states participating

| Year | Target States | Federal Support |
|------|---------------|-----------------|
| 3 | 15 states | Planning grants, 75% match |
| 4 | 25 states | Full partnership funding |
| 5 | 35 states | Enhanced completion incentives |

**Comprehensive State Reform**

- Outcome-based funding formulas
- Transfer guarantee systems
- State-level risk-sharing
- For-profit sector phase-out in some states

### Institutional Transformation (Years 3-5)

**Structural Changes**

| Change | Timeline | Support Needed |
|--------|----------|----------------|
| Guided pathways implementation | Years 3-4 | Technical assistance |
| Adjunct conversion (25% target) | Years 3-5 | State/federal incentives |
| Administrative efficiency (10% reduction) | Years 3-5 | Benchmarking, shared services |
| Assessment-based improvement | Years 3-5 | Accreditor requirements |

**Program-Level Actions**

- Eliminate low-performing programs
- Expand high-demand, high-return fields
- Create stackable credential pathways
- Integrate work-based learning

## Phase 3: System Transformation (Years 5-10)

### Federal Actions

**Debt-Free College**

By Year 7: No student borrowing for public college attendance

| Component | Year Implemented | Cost |
|-----------|------------------|------|
| Free community college (national) | Year 5 | $9B/year |
| Doubled Pell Grant | Year 5 | $30B/year |
| State matching for 4-year | Year 6-7 | $15B/year |
| Living expense support | Year 8-10 | $10B/year |

**Universal Accountability**

- All institutions subject to outcome standards
- Risk-sharing fully implemented
- Accreditation reformed or replaced
- Program-level accountability universal

**Student Debt Resolution**

- Remaining legacy debt addressed
- Bankruptcy rights restored
- Income-driven repayment simplified
- Long-term borrowers discharged

### State Systems (Years 5-10)

**Complete State Transformation**

- Universal free community college
- Debt-free four-year public college
- Unified transfer systems
- Outcome-based funding
- Eliminated for-profit sector predation

**Regional Coordination**

- Multi-state compacts for reciprocity
- Shared credentialing systems
- Regional labor market alignment
- Joint research and development

### Institutional Evolution (Years 5-10)

**New Models**

| Model | Features | Examples |
|-------|----------|----------|
| Competency-based | Flexible pacing, demonstrated mastery | Western Governors |
| Employer-integrated | Embedded work experience | Co-op schools |
| Stackable credentials | Modular, cumulative | Community colleges |
| Hybrid delivery | Online + in-person optimized | Many possibilities |

**Faculty and Teaching**

- Majority permanent faculty
- Teaching-research balance restored
- Pedagogical excellence rewarded
- Graduate education reformed

## Implementation Risks and Mitigations

### Political Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Change in administration | Build durability into programs |
| Congressional opposition | Bipartisan coalition building |
| State non-participation | Strong federal incentives |
| Legal challenges | Careful legal design |

### Implementation Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| State supplantation | Maintenance of effort requirements |
| Gaming metrics | Multiple metrics, regular audits |
| Institutional resistance | Phase-in periods, support resources |
| Unintended consequences | Pilot programs, evaluation |

### Sequencing Dependencies

```text
Phase 1: Foundation
├── Pell expansion (enables affordability)
├── FAFSA simplification (enables access)
├── Data systems (enables accountability)
└── State planning (enables partnerships)
    ↓
Phase 2: Expansion
├── Free community college (requires state partners)
├── Risk-sharing pilots (requires data)
├── HEA reauthorization (requires coalition)
└── Transfer systems (requires coordination)
    ↓
Phase 3: Transformation
├── Debt-free college (requires full funding)
├── Universal accountability (requires buy-in)
├── System integration (requires infrastructure)
└── New models (requires flexibility)
```
