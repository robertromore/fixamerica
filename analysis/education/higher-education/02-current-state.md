# Higher Education: Current State

## The Problem Today

### The Cost Crisis

American higher education costs have spiraled out of control, with prices rising far faster than inflation, wages, or the value delivered to students.

**Tuition Growth**

| Year | Public 4-Year (In-State) | Private 4-Year | % of Median Income |
|------|--------------------------|----------------|-------------------|
| 1980 | $2,320 (inflation-adjusted) | $10,930 | 6% |
| 1990 | $3,800 | $16,900 | 9% |
| 2000 | $4,730 | $22,960 | 12% |
| 2010 | $8,050 | $31,790 | 21% |
| 2023 | $11,260 | $41,540 | 29% |

Note: Inflation-adjusted to 2023 dollars.

### The Debt Burden

**Outstanding Student Loan Debt**

- Total federal student loans: $1.6 trillion
- Total private student loans: $130 billion
- Total outstanding: $1.75 trillion
- Number of borrowers: 43.5 million
- Average balance: $38,900
- Median balance: $20,000

**Distribution of Debt**

| Debt Level | Share of Borrowers | Share of Total Debt |
|------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Under $10,000 | 29% | 5% |
| $10,000-$25,000 | 24% | 12% |
| $25,000-$50,000 | 21% | 21% |
| $50,000-$100,000 | 15% | 26% |
| Over $100,000 | 11% | 36% |

**Repayment Status**

| Status | Borrowers | Amount |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| In repayment (current) | 53% | $850B |
| In grace/deferment | 12% | $190B |
| In forbearance | 14% | $220B |
| In default | 7% | $110B |
| In income-driven repayment | 14% | $230B |

**Who Owes the Most**

- Graduate students: 14% of borrowers, 48% of debt
- Parents (Parent PLUS loans): $108 billion outstanding
- For-profit attendees: 9% of students, 33% of defaults
- Non-completers: 35% of borrowers, majority of defaults
