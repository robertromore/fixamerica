# Higher Education: Citizen Actions

## Individual Actions

### For Current and Prospective Students

**Before Enrolling**

1. **Research actual costs and outcomes**
    - Use College Scorecard for program-level data
    - Calculate net price, not sticker price
    - Compare debt-to-expected-earnings ratios
    - Check graduation rates for students like you

2. **Maximize free money first**
    - Complete FAFSA every year
    - Search for private scholarships
    - Apply for institutional aid
    - Consider merit aid opportunities

3. **Make strategic choices**
    - Community college first (save 50%+ on first two years)
    - In-state public universities (major cost savings)
    - Compare similar programs at different price points
    - Avoid for-profit colleges

4. **Understand what you're signing**
    - Federal vs. private loans (federal has more protections)
    - Interest rates and repayment terms
    - Income-driven repayment options
    - Parent PLUS risks

**While Enrolled**

1. **Stay on track to graduate**
    - Meet with advisor regularly
    - Follow degree plan
    - Take full course load if possible
    - Use tutoring and support services

2. **Minimize borrowing**
    - Work part-time (under 20 hours)
    - Apply for work-study
    - Rent textbooks or use OER
    - Live affordably

3. **Build toward career**
    - Internships and co-ops
    - Professional networking
    - Career services engagement
    - Skills documentation

### For Student Loan Borrowers

**Understand Your Options**

1. **Know your loans**
    - Log in to studentaid.gov
    - Identify federal vs. private
    - Note servicers and balances
    - Track interest accrual

2. **Choose the right repayment plan**
    - Standard (10 years, highest payment)
    - Graduated (increasing payments)
    - Extended (up to 25 years)
    - Income-driven (IDR) options:
        - SAVE (new, most generous)
        - PAYE
        - IBR
        - ICR

3. **Pursue forgiveness if eligible**
    - Public Service Loan Forgiveness (PSLF): 10 years of public service
    - Income-driven repayment forgiveness: 20-25 years
    - Teacher Loan Forgiveness: 5 years in low-income schools
    - Borrower Defense: If school defrauded you

4. **Protect yourself**
    - Never pay for FAFSA help (it's free)
    - Avoid debt relief scams
    - Document everything
    - Know your rights

**If Struggling**

1. **Before default**
    - Contact servicer immediately
    - Request deferment or forbearance
    - Switch to income-driven repayment
    - Apply for disability discharge if applicable

2. **If already in default**
    - Rehabilitation (9 payments to restore)
    - Consolidation (one payment to exit)
    - Know collection limits
    - Seek legal aid if sued

### For Parents

**Planning Ahead**

1. **Start early**
    - 529 plans (tax advantages)
    - Understand expected family contribution
    - Have honest conversations about costs

2. **Think carefully about Parent PLUS**
    - You are the borrower, not your child
    - Debt follows you regardless of child's income
    - Consider alternatives first
    - Federal Parent PLUS has some protections; private parent loans have fewer

3. **Support completion**
    - Emotional support matters
    - Help navigate bureaucracy
    - Encourage full-time enrollment if possible
    - Recognize when transfer is smart move

### For Faculty and Staff

**Advocate Within Institutions**

1. **Support student success**
    - Proactive outreach to struggling students
    - Refer to campus resources
    - Flexible but rigorous pedagogy
    - Transparent about course demands

2. **Push for institutional reform**
    - Faculty governance engagement
    - Question administrative spending
    - Advocate for adjunct colleagues
    - Support student advocacy

3. **Organize**
    - Faculty unions (where legal)
    - Professional associations
    - Contingent faculty coalitions
    - Graduate student unions

## Collective Actions

### Contact Elected Officials

**Federal Level**

Contact your Senators and Representative about:

- Pell Grant increases
- Free community college
- Borrower protections
- For-profit accountability
- Higher Education Act reauthorization

**Sample Message (Federal)**

> I'm writing to urge you to support meaningful higher education reform. Student debt has reached $1.75 trillion, tuition has increased 1,200% since 1980, and millions of students fail to complete their degrees. I support [specific policy: doubling the Pell Grant/free community college/risk-sharing]. Please co-sponsor [specific legislation] and work toward affordable, quality higher education for all Americans.

**State Level**

Contact your state legislators and governor about:

- State appropriations for public universities
- Free community college programs
- Transfer and articulation policies
- For-profit regulation
- Workforce development funding

**Sample Message (State)**

> As a constituent, I urge you to restore state investment in our public colleges and universities. State funding per student has declined significantly, forcing tuition increases that burden students and families. I support [specific state policy: free community college/increased appropriations/improved transfer]. Higher education is essential to our state's economy and our residents' futures.

### Participate in Public Comment

**Regulatory Processes**

The Department of Education regularly issues regulations open for public comment:

1. Watch Federal Register for proposed rules
2. Submit comments during 30-60 day windows
3. Share personal experiences
4. Support effective policies

**Key Topics for Comment**

- Gainful employment rules
- Borrower defense regulations
- Accreditation standards
- Income-driven repayment terms

## Employer and Community Actions

### Employers

1. **Reduce credential requirements**
    - Hire for skills, not degrees
    - Recognize alternative credentials
    - Support apprenticeships

2. **Support employee education**
    - Tuition assistance programs
    - Flexible scheduling for students
    - Partner with community colleges
    - Pay for credentials gained

3. **Engage with institutions**
    - Advisory boards
    - Curriculum input
    - Internship programs
    - Hiring pipelines

### Community Organizations

1. **Financial literacy programs**
    - FAFSA completion assistance
    - College affordability education
    - Debt counseling

2. **Support services**
    - Emergency aid funds
    - Childcare for student parents
    - Transportation assistance
    - Food security programs

3. **Advocacy**
    - Support state investment
    - Hold institutions accountable
    - Connect residents to resources
