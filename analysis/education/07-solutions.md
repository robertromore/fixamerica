# Education: Solutions

## Overview

Improving American education requires addressing funding inequity, strengthening teaching, expanding access from early childhood through higher education, and ensuring all students have opportunity regardless of zip code. Solutions range from incremental reforms within current structures to fundamental restructuring of how education is funded and governed.

---

## Guiding Principles

### What Reform Should Achieve

| Principle | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| **Equity** | Resources match student needs |
| **Quality** | Effective instruction for all |
| **Access** | No barriers based on income, race, geography |
| **Accountability** | Outcomes matter, support precedes sanctions |
| **Democracy** | Civic preparation, public institutions |
| **Efficiency** | Resources used effectively |

### Evaluating Proposals

| Criterion | Questions |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Evidence** | Does research support this? |
| **Equity impact** | Who benefits, who is harmed? |
| **Scalability** | Can this work broadly? |
| **Sustainability** | Will gains persist? |
| **Political feasibility** | Is it achievable? |
| **Implementation** | Can it be done well? |

## Trade-offs

### Equity vs. Local Control

| Tension | Resolution |
|---------|------------|
| Equalization reduces local revenue control | State funding with local flexibility on spending |
| Standards reduce local curriculum control | Standards on what, local choice on how |

### Accountability vs. Autonomy

| Tension | Resolution |
|---------|------------|
| Accountability narrows curriculum | Multiple measures, support before sanctions |
| Teacher autonomy vs. consistency | Professional standards, not scripts |

### Integration vs. Choice

| Tension | Resolution |
|---------|------------|
| Choice can increase segregation | Controlled choice with diversity goals |
| Neighborhood schools vs. integration | Magnet options, boundary changes |

### Investment vs. Reform

| Tension | Resolution |
|---------|------------|
| More money vs. better use | Both: adequate and effective |
| New programs vs. core funding | Core funding first, targeted programs |

## Recommended Approach

### Near-Term

1. Increase teacher pay significantly
2. Fund universal pre-K
3. Implement high-dosage tutoring
4. Adopt science of reading
5. Simplify student loan repayment

### Medium-Term

1. State funding formula reform
2. IDEA full funding
3. Free community college
4. Integration incentives
5. Teacher residency expansion

### Long-Term

1. Wealth-neutral funding system
2. Regional/metro education governance
3. Debt-free public higher education
4. Universal 0-5 system
5. Teacher profession transformation
