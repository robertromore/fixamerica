# Education Equity: Resources

## Overview

This document provides citations, references, and resources for further research on education equity. Sources include legal documents, academic research, government reports, and advocacy materials.

## Key Legal Cases

### Foundational Cases

**Plessy v. Ferguson**, 163 U.S. 537 (1896)

- Established "separate but equal" doctrine
- Permitted state-mandated segregation
- Overturned by *Brown*

**Brown v. Board of Education**, 347 U.S. 483 (1954)

- Declared school segregation unconstitutional
- "Separate educational facilities are inherently unequal"
- Foundation of desegregation law

**Brown v. Board of Education II**, 349 U.S. 294 (1955)

- Implementation decision
- Required desegregation "with all deliberate speed"
- Created framework for court oversight

### Desegregation Era Cases

**Green v. County School Board**, 391 U.S. 430 (1968)

- Required affirmative integration
- "Freedom of choice" insufficient
- Established "Green factors"

**Swann v. Charlotte-Mecklenburg**, 402 U.S. 1 (1971)

- Approved busing as remedy
- Upheld racial quotas for schools
- Expanded federal court authority

**Keyes v. School District No. 1**, 413 U.S. 189 (1973)

- Applied desegregation to Northern cities
- Intentional segregation must be proven
- Segregative acts in part of district can justify system-wide remedy

### Limiting Cases

**Milliken v. Bradley**, 418 U.S. 717 (1974)

- Limited interdistrict remedies
- District lines protected unless suburban intent proven
- Major constraint on metropolitan integration

**Milliken v. Bradley II**, 433 U.S. 267 (1977)

- Permitted compensatory programs as remedy
- "Second best" remedy when integration limited

**Board of Education v. Dowell**, 498 U.S. 237 (1991)

- Established standards for ending court oversight
- "Unitary status" doctrine
- Enabled resegregation

**Freeman v. Pitts**, 503 U.S. 467 (1992)

- Allowed incremental release from court orders
- Courts can withdraw supervision by category
- Accelerated end of oversight

**Missouri v. Jenkins**, 515 U.S. 70 (1995)

- Limited remedial authority
- Cannot order "attract white students" remedy
- Constrained funding remedies

### Contemporary Cases

**Parents Involved in Community Schools v. Seattle School District No. 1**, 551 U.S. 701 (2007)

- Limited voluntary race-conscious assignment
- Plurality: Only compelling interests are remedying past discrimination and diversity in higher education
- Kennedy concurrence: Race-conscious goals permissible with race-neutral means

**Fisher v. University of Texas**, 579 U.S. ___ (2016)

- Upheld race-conscious admission in higher education
- Limited application to K-12 per *Parents Involved*

### State Constitutional Cases

***Sheff v. O'Neill***, 238 Conn. 1 (1996)

- Found Hartford segregation violated Connecticut constitution
- Required state action to integrate
- Led to regional magnet school program

***Abbott v. Burke***, Multiple decisions (NJ, 1985-present)

- Landmark school funding equity case
- Required "parity" funding for poor districts
- Led to comprehensive remediation

***Edgewood Independent School District v. Kirby***, 777 S.W.2d 391 (Tex. 1989)

- Found Texas school finance unconstitutional
- Led to "Robin Hood" equalization
- Ongoing litigation

## Federal Statutes

### Civil Rights Act of 1964

**Title VI** (42 U.S.C. § 2000d)

- Prohibits discrimination by recipients of federal funds
- Basis for school desegregation enforcement
- OCR enforcement authority

### Elementary and Secondary Education Act (as amended by ESSA)

**Title I** (20 U.S.C. § 6301 et seq.)

- Federal funding for high-poverty schools
- Comparability requirements
- Supplement-not-supplant rule

**Title IV, Part A** (20 U.S.C. § 7101 et seq.)

- Student Support and Academic Enrichment Grants
- Safe schools provisions

### Individuals with Disabilities Education Act

**IDEA** (20 U.S.C. § 1400 et seq.)

- Free appropriate public education
- Least restrictive environment
- Disproportionality provisions

### Other Relevant Statutes

**Equal Educational Opportunities Act** (20 U.S.C. § 1701 et seq.)

- Prohibits segregation by government action
- English language learner requirements

**Magnet Schools Assistance Program** (20 U.S.C. § 7231 et seq.)

- Federal support for voluntary integration magnets
- Desegregation assistance grants

## Academic Research

### Integration Research

Reardon, Sean F., and Ann Owens. "60 Years After Brown: Trends and Consequences of School Segregation." *Annual Review of Sociology* 40 (2014): 199-218.

- Comprehensive review of segregation trends

Johnson, Rucker C. "Long-Run Impacts of School Desegregation & School Quality on Adult Attainments." *NBER Working Paper* 16664 (2011).

- Found lasting benefits of desegregation

Wells, Amy Stuart, et al. *Both Sides Now: The Story of School Desegregation's Graduates*. Berkeley: University of California Press, 2009.

- Qualitative study of desegregation's impact

Mickelson, Roslyn Arlin, and Martha Bottia. "Integrated Education and Mathematics Outcomes: A Synthesis of Social Science Research." *North Carolina Law Review* 88 (2010): 993-1090.

- Meta-analysis of integration effects

### Funding Research

Jackson, C. Kirabo, Rucker C. Johnson, and Claudia Persico. "The Effects of School Spending on Educational and Economic Outcomes: Evidence from School Finance Reforms." *Quarterly Journal of Economics* 131, no. 1 (2016): 157-218.

- Found significant effects of spending increases

Baker, Bruce D. *Educational Inequality and School Finance: Why Money Matters for America's Students*. Cambridge: Harvard Education Press, 2018.

- Comprehensive treatment of school finance equity

Lafortune, Julien, Jesse Rothstein, and Diane Whitmore Schanzenbach. "School Finance Reform and the Distribution of Student Achievement." *American Economic Journal: Applied Economics* 10, no. 2 (2018): 1-26.

- Found court-ordered reforms improved outcomes

### Discipline Research

Skiba, Russell J., et al. "The Color of Discipline: Sources of Racial and Gender Disproportionality in School Punishment." *Urban Review* 34, no. 4 (2002): 317-342.

- Foundational study of discipline disparities

Losen, Daniel J., and Tia Elena Martinez. *Out of School and Off Track: The Overuse of Suspensions in American Middle and High Schools*. Los Angeles: UCLA Civil Rights Project, 2013.

- Comprehensive analysis of suspension patterns

Gregory, Anne, Russell J. Skiba, and Pedro A. Noguera. "The Achievement Gap and the Discipline Gap: Two Sides of the Same Coin?" *Educational Researcher* 39, no. 1 (2010): 59-68.

- Connected discipline and achievement disparities

### Tracking Research

Oakes, Jeannie. *Keeping Track: How Schools Structure Inequality*. New Haven: Yale University Press, 2005.

- Classic study of tracking's effects

Burris, Carol Corbett, and Kevin G. Welner. "Closing the Achievement Gap by Detracking." *Phi Delta Kappan* 86, no. 8 (2005): 594-598.

- Evidence on detracking effects

Grissom, Jason A., and Christopher Redding. "Discretion and Disproportionality: Explaining the Underrepresentation of High-Achieving Students of Color in Gifted Programs." *AERA Open* 2, no. 1 (2016): 1-25.

- Analysis of gifted program disparities
