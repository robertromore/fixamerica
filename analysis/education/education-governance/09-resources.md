# Education Governance: Resources

## Overview

This document provides resources for understanding education governance, researching local conditions, and supporting reform efforts. Resources include academic research, advocacy organizations, data sources, and practical tools for citizens and advocates.

## Academic Research

### School Board Governance

**Key Studies on Board Elections**

| Study | Authors | Key Finding |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| "Democracy Derailed" | Zoltan Hajnal, Paul Lewis, Hugh Louch | Off-cycle elections dramatically reduce turnout |
| "Who Votes for School Board?" | Brian Anzia | Composition differs significantly from general electorate |
| "The Calculus of Representation" | Kenneth Meier | At-large systems reduce minority representation |
| "Timing and Turnout" | Sarah Anzia | Interest groups benefit from low-turnout elections |

**Research on Board Effectiveness**

| Study | Authors | Key Finding |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| "Governing Urban Schooling" | Michael Usdan, et al. | Board dysfunction common in urban districts |
| "School Boards: A Review of Literature" | Land | Board training improves governance quality |
| "Board Member Training Effects" | Rice, et al. | Training associated with better outcomes |

**Citation**: Anzia, Sarah F. *Timing and Turnout: How Off-Cycle Elections Favor Organized Groups*. University of Chicago Press, 2014.

**Citation**: Hajnal, Zoltan, Paul G. Lewis, and Hugh Louch. "Municipal Elections in California: Turnout, Timing, and Competition." Public Policy Institute of California, 2002.

### Mayoral Control

**Key Studies**

| Study | Focus | Finding |
|-------|-------|---------|
| "Mayors in the Middle" | Wong, et al. | Mixed results but improved stability |
| "Impact of Mayoral Takeover" | Henig, et al. | Context-dependent outcomes |
| "NYC Mayoral Control" | Multiple | Generally positive academic results |
| "Boston Public Schools" | Portz, et al. | Stability benefits under mayoral control |

**Citation**: Wong, Kenneth K., et al. *The Education Mayor: Improving America's Schools*. Georgetown University Press, 2007.

**Citation**: Henig, Jeffrey R., and Wilbur C. Rich, eds. *Mayors in the Middle: Politics, Race, and Mayoral Control of Urban Schools*. Princeton University Press, 2004.

### State Intervention

**Key Studies**

| Study | Focus | Finding |
|-------|-------|---------|
| "State Takeovers of School Districts" | Wong, Shen | Generally disappointing results |
| "New Orleans Recovery District" | Multiple | Controversial outcomes |
| "Detroit Interventions" | Sugrue, et al. | Multiple failures |
| "Newark State Takeover" | Morel | Mixed results over 23 years |

**Citation**: Wong, Kenneth K., and Francis X. Shen. "When Mayors Lead Urban Schools: Toward Developing a Framework to Assess the Effects of Mayoral Takeover of Urban Districts." *Peabody Journal of Education* 78, no. 1 (2003): 40-64.

### District Consolidation

**Key Studies**

| Study | Focus | Finding |
|-------|-------|---------|
| "Economics of School District Consolidation" | Duncombe, Yinger | Mixed efficiency effects |
| "School District Consolidation" | Howley, et al. | Community impacts significant |
| "Size Matters" | Andrews, et al. | Optimal district size varies |

**Citation**: Duncombe, William, and John Yinger. "Does School District Consolidation Cut Costs?" *Education Finance and Policy* 2, no. 4 (2007): 341-375.

## Data Sources

### National Data

**National Center for Education Statistics (NCES)**

- URL: <https://nces.ed.gov>
- Data: District finance, enrollment, staffing
- Tools: ElSi (Elementary/Secondary Information System)
- Publications: Digest of Education Statistics

**Census Bureau School District Data**

- URL: <https://www.census.gov/programs-surveys/sdrp.html>
- Data: District boundaries, demographics, finance
- Tools: TIGER/Line shapefiles for mapping

**Common Core of Data (CCD)**

- URL: <https://nces.ed.gov/ccd/>
- Data: All public schools and districts
- Annual updates

### State Data Sources

**State Education Agency Websites**

Each state maintains data on:

- District performance (test scores, graduation rates)
- Financial data (budgets, spending)
- Personnel (staffing levels, salaries)
- Demographics (enrollment by group)

**State Board Election Data**

Usually available through:

- Secretary of State offices
- County election offices
- Board of Elections

### Election Data

**Ballotpedia**

- URL: <https://ballotpedia.org>
- Data: School board candidates, election results
- Coverage: Comprehensive for larger districts

**ICSPR Election Data**

- URL: <https://www.icpsr.umich.edu>
- Data: Historical election data
- Analysis: Academic research datasets

### Performance Data

**Stanford Education Data Archive (SEDA)**

- URL: <https://edopportunity.org>
- Data: Standardized achievement data by district
- Analysis: Comparable across states

**Urban Institute Education Data Portal**

- URL: <https://educationdata.urban.org>
- Data: Finance, demographics, outcomes
- Tools: API access for researchers
