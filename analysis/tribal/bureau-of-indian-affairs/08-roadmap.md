# Bureau of Indian Affairs: Roadmap

## Overview

This roadmap outlines a phased strategy for reforming the Bureau of Indian Affairs over a 10-year period. The sequencing prioritizes urgent service delivery and funding needs first, builds toward comprehensive institutional restructuring, and culminates in a transformed federal-tribal relationship where tribes exercise primary control over programs and the bureau serves a facilitation and trust administration role.

## Phase 1: Foundation (Years 1-3)

### Priority: Stabilize Funding, Expand Self-Governance, Address Urgent Needs

The first phase addresses the most immediate needs: funding stability, law enforcement staffing, trust land processing backlogs, and self-governance expansion to willing tribes.

### Action Items

| Action | Lead Actor | Supporting Actors | Timeline | Priority |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|----------|----------|
| Extend advance appropriations to all BIA programs | Congress (Appropriations) | NCAI, tribal governments | Year 1 | Critical |
| Offer self-governance compacting to all 574 tribes | BIA Office of Self-Governance | Self-Governance Consortium, NCAI | Year 1-3 | Critical |
| Begin law enforcement hiring surge (500 officers in Phase 1) | BIA OJS, Congress | DOJ, tribal police departments | Year 1-3 | Critical |
| Mandate 180-day land-into-trust processing for uncontested applications | Congress, BIA | Interior, tribal applicants | Year 1-2 | High |
| Triple OFA staffing for federal recognition | Congress, Interior | OFA, petitioning groups | Year 1-2 | High |
| Expand HEARTH Act to all trust land transaction types | Congress | BIA, tribal realty offices | Year 1-2 | High |
| Launch BIE facilities emergency construction program | Congress | BIE, tribal schools | Year 1 | High |
| Fund intertribal technical assistance for compacting | Congress | Self-Governance Consortium, intertribal organizations | Year 1-2 | Medium |
| Begin TAAMS technology modernization | BIA, Interior CIO | IT contractors, tribal trust staff | Year 1-3 | Medium |
| Publish completeness standards for land-into-trust applications | BIA Realty | Tribal realty staff | Year 1 | Medium |

### Phase 1 Success Criteria

- Advance appropriations enacted for all BIA programs
- Self-governance compacting offers extended to all 574 tribes; 50+ new compacts initiated
- 500 additional law enforcement officers hired
- Average land-into-trust processing time reduced to under 2 years
- OFA staffed at 36; processing timelines established
- BIE emergency facilities funding secured; 10+ school construction/renovation projects underway

---

## Phase 2: Transformation (Years 3-7)

### Priority: Institutional Restructuring, Mandatory Funding, Comprehensive Modernization

The second phase builds on Phase 1's foundations to restructure the BIA organizationally, convert funding to mandatory appropriations, and complete trust administration modernization.

### Action Items

| Action | Lead Actor | Supporting Actors | Timeline | Priority |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|----------|----------|
| Convert BIA core programs to mandatory appropriations | Congress (Budget, Appropriations) | NCAI, tribal governments, CBO | Year 3-5 | Critical |
| Separate trust administration into standalone Office of Trust Administration | Congress, Interior | BIA, tribal trust beneficiaries | Year 3-5 | Critical |
| Extend self-governance compacting to all federal agencies (DOT, EPA, HUD, Education, etc.) | Congress | Self-Governance Consortium, federal agencies | Year 3-4 | Critical |
| Complete law enforcement hiring surge (total 1,850 officers) | BIA OJS, Congress | DOJ, tribal police | Year 3-5 | High |
| Complete TAAMS modernization and record digitization | BIA, Interior CIO | IT contractors | Year 3-5 | High |
| Transfer all BIA-operated schools to tribal control | BIE, tribal education departments | Congress, NIEA | Year 3-7 | High |
| Implement needs-based funding formula with tribal consultation | BIA, Congress | NCAI, tribal budget experts | Year 3-4 | High |
| Resolve current federal recognition backlog | OFA | Petitioning groups, tribal advisors | Year 3-5 | High |
| Establish independent BIA accountability office with tribal oversight | Congress, Interior | NCAI, GAO | Year 4-5 | Medium |
| Modernize or replace all 24 BIA detention facilities | BIA OJS, Congress | DOJ | Year 3-7 | Medium |
| Extend Land Buy-Back program with additional $1 billion | Congress | BIA Trust Services | Year 3-5 | Medium |

### Phase 2 Success Criteria

- BIA core programs reclassified as mandatory spending
- Trust administration separated from BIA program functions
- Self-governance compacting available through 8+ federal agencies
- 450+ tribes in self-governance compacts
- Average land-into-trust processing time under 6 months
- BIE reading proficiency increased to 28%+; math to 25%+
- Federal recognition backlog cleared; new petitions processed within 5 years
- Law enforcement staffing at 1,850+; response times reduced to 15-25 minutes

---

## Phase 3: Consolidation (Years 7-10)

### Priority: Full Implementation, Evaluation, and Long-Term Institutional Design

The third phase completes the transformation, evaluates outcomes, and establishes the long-term institutional framework for the federal-tribal trust relationship.

### Action Items

| Action | Lead Actor | Supporting Actors | Timeline | Priority |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|----------|----------|
| Evaluate restructured BIA/OTA performance; adjust based on data | Interior, GAO | Tribal governments, Congress | Year 7-8 | Critical |
| Achieve 500+ tribes in self-governance compacts across agencies | Self-Governance Consortium | BIA, federal agencies | Year 7-10 | Critical |
| Complete fractionation reduction program | OTA, Congress | Tribal trust beneficiaries | Year 7-10 | High |
| Establish permanent tribal advisory commission for trust administration | Congress, Interior | NCAI, tribal governments | Year 7-8 | High |
| Achieve BIA law enforcement parity with comparable rural areas | BIA OJS | DOJ, tribal police | Year 7-10 | High |
| Complete BIE school transfer to tribal control (100%) | BIE, tribal education departments | Congress | Year 7-10 | High |
| Publish comprehensive historical accounting of BIA trust administration | OTA | Tribal historians, GAO | Year 7-9 | Medium |
| Develop long-term institutional design based on evaluation results | Congress, Interior, tribal commission | NCAI, academic advisors | Year 8-10 | Medium |
| Achieve per-capita BIA service parity with comparable federal programs | Congress | BIA/OTA, CBO | Year 7-10 | Medium |

### Phase 3 Success Criteria

- 500+ tribes in self-governance compacts; remaining tribes served at adequate levels
- Trust administration processing meets all timeline mandates
- BIE schools 95%+ under tribal control; student outcomes improved by 50%+ from baseline
- Law enforcement staffing at 2,000+; violent crime rate reduced to 1.5x national average
- Fractionated ownership interests reduced to 3 million or fewer
- Tribal satisfaction with federal trust administration rated moderate-to-high
- Long-term institutional design completed and endorsed by majority of tribal governments

## Risk Mitigation

### Political Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Administration change reverses reforms | High | High | Prioritize legislation over executive orders; mandatory funding protects against political whims |
| Congressional opposition to mandatory funding | High | High | Start with advance appropriations; build toward mandatory; use reconciliation if necessary |
| State backlash to expanded tribal self-governance | Medium | Medium | Demonstrate mutual benefits; maintain payments-in-lieu-of-taxes for local governments |
| BIA employee resistance to restructuring | Medium | Medium | Include workforce transition provisions; retrain and reassign rather than terminate |

### Implementation Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Tribal capacity varies widely | Certain | Medium | Phased opt-in; intertribal compacting; technical assistance funding |
| Technology modernization delays | High | Medium | Agile development approach; phased deployment; contractor accountability provisions |
| Law enforcement recruitment difficulty | High | Medium | Competitive pay; housing assistance; recruitment bonuses for remote posts |
| Federal recognition reform opposition from existing tribes | Medium | Medium | Transparent process; demonstrate that new recognitions do not reduce existing tribe funding |

### Legal Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Challenges to processing timeline mandates | Medium | Medium | Design mandates with compliance enforcement mechanisms (mandamus, APA review) |
| Constitutional challenge to mandatory funding | Low | Medium | Strong basis in Commerce Clause and treaty obligations |
| Litigation over BIA restructuring | Medium | Low | Extensive tribal consultation in design; legislative authority |
