# Bureau of Indian Affairs: Resources

## Overview

This document compiles key academic research, government reports, legal resources, organizations, and data sources relevant to Bureau of Indian Affairs reform analysis.

## Academic Research

### Foundational Texts

- Prucha, Francis Paul. *The Great Father: The United States Government and the American Indians*. 2 vols. University of Nebraska Press, 1984. -- Definitive history of the federal government's administration of Indian affairs from colonial era through the 1980s.

- Wilkinson, Charles. *Blood Struggle: The Rise of Modern Indian Nations*. W.W. Norton, 2005. -- History of the self-determination movement and the transformation of federal Indian policy.

- Deloria, Vine Jr., and Clifford M. Lytle. *The Nations Within: The Past and Future of American Indian Sovereignty*. University of Texas Press, 1984. -- Analysis of the IRA era and its implications for tribal self-governance.

- Getches, David H., Charles F. Wilkinson, Robert A. Williams Jr., Matthew L.M. Fletcher, and Kristen A. Carpenter. *Cases and Materials on Federal Indian Law*. 7th ed. West Academic, 2017. -- Standard law school casebook on federal Indian law.

- Stuart, Paul. *The Indian Office: Growth and Development of an American Institution, 1865-1900*. UMI Research Press, 1979. -- Institutional history of the Bureau of Indian Affairs during its formative period.

### Self-Determination and Self-Governance Research

- Cornell, Stephen, and Joseph P. Kalt. "Sovereignty and Nation-Building: The Development Challenge in Indian Country Today." *American Indian Culture and Research Journal* 22, no. 3 (1998): 187-214.

- Cornell, Stephen, and Joseph P. Kalt. *What Can Tribes Do? Strategies and Institutions in American Indian Economic Development*. UCLA American Indian Studies Center, 1992.

- Jorgensen, Miriam, ed. *Rebuilding Native Nations: Strategies for Governance and Development*. University of Arizona Press, 2007.

- Kalt, Joseph P., and Joseph William Singer. "Myths and Realities of Tribal Sovereignty: The Law and Economics of Indian Self-Rule." Harvard Project on American Indian Economic Development, 2004.

### Trust Administration Research

- Wood, Mary Christina. "Indian Land and the Promise of Native Sovereignty: The Trust Doctrine Revisited." *Utah Law Review* 1994, no. 4 (1994): 1471-1569.

- Newton, Nell Jessup. "Federal Power over Indians: Its Sources, Scope, and Limitations." *University of Pennsylvania Law Review* 132 (1984): 195-288.

- Royster, Judith V. "The Legacy of Allotment." *Arizona State Law Journal* 27 (1995): 1-78.

- Washburn, Kevin K. "The Legacy of Bryan v. Itasca County: How an Erroneous $147 Tax Assessment Helped Bring Tribes $200 Billion in Indian Gaming Revenue." *Minnesota Law Review* 92 (2008): 919-970.

### BIA-Specific Analysis

- National Research Council. *The Context of Natural Resource Policy: Insights from the Bureau of Indian Affairs*. National Academies Press, 2012.

- Taylor, Theodore W. *The Bureau of Indian Affairs*. Westview Press, 1984.

- Castile, George Pierre. *To Show Heart: Native American Self-Determination and Federal Indian Policy, 1960-1975*. University of Arizona Press, 1998.

## Key Legal Decisions

### Trust Responsibility Cases

| Case | Year | Significance |
|------|------|-------------|
| *Cobell v. Salazar*, No. 96-1285 (D.D.C.) | 1996-2009 | Class action over BIA trust fund mismanagement; $3.4 billion settlement |
| *Salazar v. Ramah Navajo Chapter*, 567 U.S. 182 | 2012 | BIA must pay full contract support costs under 638 contracts |
| *Cherokee Nation v. Leavitt*, 543 U.S. 631 | 2005 | Government liable for contract support cost shortfalls |

### Self-Determination Cases

| Case | Year | Significance |
|------|------|-------------|
| *Morton v. Mancari*, 417 U.S. 535 | 1974 | Indian Preference in BIA hiring is political, not racial classification |
| *Moe v. Confederated Salish and Kootenai Tribes*, 425 U.S. 463 | 1976 | State taxation limits in Indian Country |
| *Menominee Tribe v. United States*, 391 U.S. 404 | 1968 | Termination did not abrogate treaty rights |

### Land and Trust Cases

| Case | Year | Significance |
|------|------|-------------|
| *Carcieri v. Salazar*, 555 U.S. 379 | 2009 | Land-into-trust limited to tribes "under federal jurisdiction" in 1934 |
| *McGirt v. Oklahoma*, 591 U.S. ___ | 2020 | Muscogee (Creek) reservation never disestablished; implications for tribal land status |
| *County of Yakima v. Confederated Tribes*, 502 U.S. 251 | 1992 | State taxation of fee-patented land within reservations |

### BIA Authority Cases

| Case | Year | Significance |
|------|------|-------------|
| *Haaland v. Brackeen*, 599 U.S. ___ | 2023 | Upheld ICWA; affirmed Commerce Clause authority over Indian affairs |
| *Arizona v. Navajo Nation*, 599 U.S. ___ | 2023 | No affirmative duty to assess tribal water needs under treaty |
| *Lincoln v. Vigil*, 508 U.S. 182 | 1993 | BIA has discretion to allocate lump-sum appropriations |
