# Federal Recognition: Actions

## Overview

This document outlines practical steps that citizens, organizations, and officials can take to support reform of the federal recognition process. Actions are organized by the actor's position and capacity, from individual citizens to elected officials.

## Individual Citizens

### Learn and Educate

- **Understand the difference** between recognized and unrecognized tribes and why it matters for healthcare, education, sovereignty, and cultural survival
- **Study the history** of tribes in your state, including any that lack federal recognition
- **Challenge misconceptions**: Federal recognition is not about gaming; it is about sovereignty, treaty fulfillment, and justice
- **Follow the issue**: Track pending petitions and legislation through BIA's Office of Federal Acknowledgment website

### Contact Elected Officials

- **Write to your congressional representatives** urging support for:
    - Carcieri fix legislation
    - Increased funding for the Office of Federal Acknowledgment
    - Petitioner assistance grant programs
    - Congressional recognition of legitimate tribal petitioners
- **Attend town halls** and raise tribal recognition as an issue
- **Register for committee hearing notifications** from the Senate Committee on Indian Affairs and House Natural Resources Committee

### Support Affected Communities

- **Donate to** organizations supporting unrecognized tribes:
    - Native American Rights Fund (NARF): <https://www.narf.org>
    - Association on American Indian Affairs: <https://www.indian-affairs.org>
- **Volunteer with** local state-recognized tribal organizations
- **Attend public hearings** during comment periods for pending petitions to support legitimate claims

### Vote and Advocate

- **Vote for candidates** who support tribal sovereignty and recognition reform
- **Sign petitions** supporting specific recognition efforts
- **Share information** about recognition issues on social media and in community discussions

## Community Organizations

### Build Coalitions

- **Partner with** tribal organizations, civil rights groups, and religious organizations to build recognition reform coalitions
- **Engage local government** in dialogue about the benefits of tribal recognition, including economic development and cultural preservation
- **Host educational events** featuring speakers from unrecognized tribal communities

### Provide Direct Support

- **Pro bono professional services**: Genealogists, historians, anthropologists, and lawyers can contribute expertise to petitioning tribes
- **Archival research**: Community historical societies can assist in locating documentary evidence for petitions
- **Fundraising**: Organize events to support specific petitioning tribes' research and legal costs

### Advocate for Policy Change

- **Submit organizational comments** during Part 83 regulatory proceedings
- **Endorse recognition reform legislation** and communicate endorsements to Congress
- **Publish op-eds and articles** educating the public about recognition issues

## State-Recognized Tribes

### Strengthen State Recognition

- **Document governance** structures, community activities, and cultural practices to build the evidentiary record
- **Maintain membership rolls** and genealogical records
- **Engage in state legislative processes** to strengthen state recognition frameworks

### Pursue Federal Recognition

- **File a letter of intent** with the BIA if not already on record
- **Begin compiling evidence** for a Part 83 petition, starting with the most accessible documentation
- **Seek legal counsel** from NARF or other experienced Indian law firms
- **Explore congressional recognition** through your state's delegation

### Build Alliances

- **Join inter-tribal organizations** that advocate for recognition reform
- **Connect with similarly situated tribes** to share resources and strategies
- **Engage with academic researchers** who can contribute to petition evidence

## Federally Recognized Tribes

### Support Recognition Reform

- **Advocate through NCAI** for process reforms including adequate OFA funding and revised evidentiary standards
- **Support the Carcieri fix** to ensure all recognized tribes have equal land-into-trust authority
- **Mentor petitioning tribes** by sharing expertise in governance documentation and community organization

### Address Resource Concerns

- **Advocate for increased federal funding** for Indian programs so that recognition expansion does not dilute existing resources
- **Support mandatory appropriations** for treaty obligations and trust responsibility programs
- **Engage constructively** with resource-sharing concerns rather than opposing recognition outright

## Legal Professionals

### Provide Legal Support

- **Pro bono representation** for petitioning tribes in Part 83 proceedings
- **Amicus briefs** in recognition-related litigation
- **Legal research** on recognition reform options and constitutional issues
- **Litigation** challenging arbitrary denials or processing delays

### Policy Advocacy

- **Draft model legislation** for recognition reform
- **Provide expert testimony** at congressional hearings
- **Publish legal scholarship** analyzing recognition process deficiencies

## Academics and Researchers

### Research Contributions

- **Conduct historical research** supporting tribal petitions, particularly in regions where records were destroyed
- **Develop oral history methodologies** that can complement documentary evidence
- **Study comparative recognition systems** in other nations
- **Analyze outcomes** of past recognition decisions to identify systemic biases

### Expert Services

- **Serve as expert witnesses** in Part 83 proceedings (pro bono where possible)
- **Provide peer review** of petition evidence and BIA analyses
- **Train tribal members** in archival research and documentation techniques

## Elected Officials

### Federal Level

- **Introduce or cosponsor** the Carcieri fix and recognition reform legislation
- **Request increased OFA appropriations** in Interior Department budget
- **Hold oversight hearings** on recognition process delays and outcomes
- **Support congressional recognition** of tribes with documented prior federal relationships
- **Establish a Congressional Native American Caucus** study group on recognition reform

### State Level

- **Strengthen state recognition laws** with clear criteria and meaningful benefits
- **Appropriate state funds** for state-recognized tribal services
- **Lobby congressional delegations** for federal recognition reform
- **Pass state resolutions** supporting specific tribal petitions for federal recognition

### Local Level

- **Engage constructively** with tribal communities seeking recognition
- **Negotiate community benefit agreements** that address local concerns about land-into-trust
- **Support tribal cultural events** and education programs
- **Include tribal perspectives** in local planning and development decisions
