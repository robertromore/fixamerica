# Tribal Courts: Resources

## Government Sources

### Federal Agencies

- **Bureau of Indian Affairs, Office of Justice Services**: <https://www.bia.gov/bia/ojs> -- Tribal court funding, detention facility data, law enforcement statistics
- **Department of Justice, Office of Tribal Justice**: <https://www.justice.gov/otj> -- Federal coordination with tribal justice systems, tribal consultation
- **Department of Justice, Office on Violence Against Women**: <https://www.justice.gov/ovw> -- VAWA SDVCJ implementation resources, tribal grants
- **Department of Justice, Coordinated Tribal Assistance Solicitation (CTAS)**: <https://www.justice.gov/tribal> -- Competitive grant funding for tribal court improvement
- **Government Accountability Office**: GAO reports on tribal justice including funding adequacy and program effectiveness
- **Congressional Research Service**: "Tribal Criminal Jurisdiction and Federal Indian Law" (updated periodically)
- **Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration (SAMHSA)**: Tribal Healing to Wellness Court grant program data

### Regulations

- **25 C.F.R. Part 11**: Courts of Indian Offenses and Law and Order Code (CFR Courts)
- **25 U.S.C. 1301-1304**: Indian Civil Rights Act, as amended by TLOA and VAWA
- **18 U.S.C. 1153**: Major Crimes Act
- **18 U.S.C. 2265**: Full Faith and Credit for protective orders (VAWA)
- **25 U.S.C. 3601-3631**: Indian Tribal Justice Act (1993)
- **25 U.S.C. 3651-3682**: Indian Tribal Justice Technical and Legal Assistance Act (2000)

### Congressional Materials

- **Senate Committee on Indian Affairs**: Hearing records on tribal court funding, TLOA implementation, and VAWA SDVCJ
- **House Natural Resources Committee**: Subcommittee for Indian and Insular Affairs hearing records
- **Indian Law and Order Commission**: *A Roadmap for Making Native America Safer* (2013) -- comprehensive assessment of tribal justice needs

## Legal Sources

### Landmark Cases

| Case | Citation | Significance |
|------|----------|-------------|
| *Ex parte Crow Dog* | 109 U.S. 556 (1883) | Recognized tribal jurisdiction over intra-Indian crime; prompted Major Crimes Act |
| *Talton v. Mayes* | 163 U.S. 376 (1896) | Bill of Rights does not directly apply to tribal governments |
| *Oliphant v. Suquamish Indian Tribe* | 435 U.S. 191 (1978) | Stripped tribal courts of criminal jurisdiction over non-Indians |
| *Santa Clara Pueblo v. Martinez* | 436 U.S. 49 (1978) | ICRA enforcement limited to habeas corpus |
| *National Farmers Union v. Crow Tribe* | 471 U.S. 845 (1985) | Established tribal court exhaustion doctrine |
| *Iowa Mutual Insurance v. LaPlante* | 480 U.S. 9 (1987) | Extended exhaustion doctrine to diversity jurisdiction cases |
| *Duro v. Reina* | 495 U.S. 676 (1990) | Limited tribal jurisdiction over non-member Indians; overridden by congressional "Duro fix" |
| *United States v. Lara* | 541 U.S. 193 (2004) | Congress can recognize tribal jurisdiction over non-member Indians |
| *Plains Commerce Bank v. Long Family Land* | 554 U.S. 316 (2008) | Limited tribal civil jurisdiction over non-member fee land transactions |
| *United States v. Bryant* | 579 U.S. 140 (2016) | Tribal court convictions valid as predicate offenses for federal DV charges |
| *Oklahoma v. Castro-Huerta* | 597 U.S. ___ (2022) | Asserted state concurrent jurisdiction in Indian Country |
| *Haaland v. Brackeen* | 599 U.S. 255 (2023) | Upheld congressional authority under Indian Commerce Clause; ICWA affirmed |

### Key Statutes

- Major Crimes Act of 1885: 18 U.S.C. 1153
- Indian Civil Rights Act of 1968: 25 U.S.C. 1301-1304
- Indian Self-Determination and Education Assistance Act of 1975: 25 U.S.C. 5301-5423
- Indian Tribal Justice Act of 1993: 25 U.S.C. 3601-3631
- Indian Tribal Justice Technical and Legal Assistance Act of 2000: 25 U.S.C. 3651-3682
- Tribal Law and Order Act of 2010: P.L. 111-211, Title II
- Violence Against Women Act of 2013: P.L. 113-4 (SDVCJ provisions)
- Violence Against Women Act Reauthorization of 2022: P.L. 117-103 (expanded SDVCJ)
