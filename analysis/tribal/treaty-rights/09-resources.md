## Legal Resources

### Key Supreme Court Decisions

| Case | Year | Treaty Right Addressed | Holding |
|------|------|----------------------|---------|
| *Johnson v. McIntosh* | 1823 | Land title | Discovery doctrine; European-derived title superior |
| *Cherokee Nation v. Georgia* | 1831 | Sovereignty status | Tribes are "domestic dependent nations" |
| *Worcester v. Georgia* | 1832 | Sovereignty | State law has no force in Indian Country |
| *Lone Wolf v. Hitchcock* | 1903 | Plenary power | Congress may abrogate treaties unilaterally |
| *Winters v. United States* | 1908 | Water rights | Treaty reservations include implied water rights |
| *Seminole Nation v. United States* | 1942 | Trust responsibility | Federal fiduciary duty to tribes |
| *Menominee Tribe v. United States* | 1968 | Hunting/fishing rights | Termination did not abrogate treaty rights |
| *United States v. Washington* (Boldt) | 1974 | Fishing rights | Treaty tribes entitled to 50% of harvestable fish |
| *Oliphant v. Suquamish* | 1978 | Criminal jurisdiction | Tribes lack jurisdiction over non-Indians |
| *United States v. Sioux Nation* | 1980 | Land taking | Black Hills seizure violated Fifth Amendment |
| *Minnesota v. Mille Lacs Band* | 1999 | Hunting/fishing/gathering | Treaty rights survive statehood |
| *Herrera v. Wyoming* | 2019 | Hunting rights | Treaty rights not abrogated by statehood |
| *McGirt v. Oklahoma* | 2020 | Reservation boundaries | Creek reservation never disestablished |
| *Oklahoma v. Castro-Huerta* | 2022 | State jurisdiction | States may prosecute non-Indians on tribal land |
