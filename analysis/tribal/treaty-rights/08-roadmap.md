# Treaty Rights: Roadmap

## Phase 1: Foundation Building (Years 1-2)

### Coalition Development

**Build Broad-Based Support**

- Establish a national Treaty Rights Coalition bringing together tribal governments, legal advocacy organizations, environmental groups, veterans' organizations, and faith communities
- Develop bipartisan messaging emphasizing treaty compliance as a matter of national honor and legal obligation
- Engage law school clinics and bar associations in treaty rights advocacy
- Partner with tribal colleges and universities for community education

**Research and Documentation**

- Commission a comprehensive audit of all 374 ratified treaties, identifying unfulfilled obligations
- Develop economic analyses quantifying the cost of noncompliance vs. the cost of compliance
- Document health, education, and public safety outcomes attributable to treaty noncompliance
- Build a public database of treaty provisions and compliance status

### Legislative Groundwork

**Immediate Legislative Priorities**

| Priority | Vehicle | Status |
|----------|---------|--------|
| Advance appropriations for all treaty programs | Appropriations bill | IHS precedent set (FY 2024); extend to BIA, BIE |
| Treaty Impact Statement requirement | Standalone bill | Draft and introduce |
| Treaty rights education | Education reauthorization | Attach to ESEA reauthorization |
| Tribal consultation codification | Executive order + legislation | Biden EO 14112 (2023); codify statutorily |

**Congressional Strategy**

- Identify champion legislators in Senate Indian Affairs Committee and House Natural Resources Committee
- Prepare testimony and briefing materials for congressional hearings
- Build relationships with appropriations committee members
- Develop cost estimates from the Congressional Budget Office

### Public Education

**Awareness Campaign**

- Launch "Honor the Treaties" public education campaign
- Develop educational materials for K-12 teachers, accessible online
- Partner with media organizations for documentary and investigative coverage
- Engage social media influencers and content creators in Indigenous communities

## Phase 2: Legislative Action (Years 2-4)

### Priority Legislation

**Advance Appropriations Expansion**

- Extend the IHS advance appropriations model to BIA, BIE, tribal housing, and tribal law enforcement
- Target: Enacted within 2 years of campaign launch
- Strategy: Attach to must-pass appropriations legislation

**Treaty Rights Enforcement Commission Act**

- Introduce legislation establishing the independent Treaty Rights Enforcement Commission
- Build co-sponsor support through sustained lobbying and grassroots pressure
- Target: Committee passage within 3 years; floor vote within 4 years

**VAWA Jurisdiction Expansion**

- Expand tribal criminal jurisdiction restored under VAWA to cover additional offenses
- Build on the decade-long track record of VAWA tribal jurisdiction
- Target: Attach to VAWA reauthorization

### State-Level Action

**Treaty Education Mandates**

| State | Strategy | Timeline |
|-------|----------|----------|
| States with existing "Indian Education for All" laws | Strengthen implementation and funding | Year 2 |
| States with large tribal populations | Introduce new legislation | Years 2-3 |
| Remaining states | Build coalition for national mandate | Years 3-4 |

**State-Tribal Cooperation Compacts**

- Develop model state-tribal cooperation agreements addressing jurisdiction, taxation, and resource management
- Target: 10 new compacts within 4 years
- Focus states: Washington, Minnesota, Wisconsin, Montana, Oklahoma

### Judicial Strategy

**Support Active Treaty Rights Litigation**

- Fund and coordinate amicus briefs in pending treaty rights cases
- Develop legal arguments for expanding treaty protections
- Monitor Supreme Court docket for cases with treaty implications
- Build record for future litigation through administrative proceedings

## Phase 3: Structural Reform (Years 4-7)

### Major Legislative Campaigns

**Federal Indian Treaty Protection Act**

- Push for supermajority requirement for treaty abrogation
- Prohibit treaty abrogation through appropriations riders
- Require Treaty Impact Statements for legislation affecting Indian Country
- Target: Passage within 5-7 years

**Treaty Obligation Trust Fund**

- Establish mandatory funding mechanism for treaty-obligated programs
- Set initial funding at levels determined by needs-based formula
- Index to inflation and population growth
- Target: Passage within 5-7 years, potentially through budget reconciliation

**Water Rights Acceleration Act**

- Appropriate $500 million over 10 years for tribal water rights adjudication
- Fund tribal technical capacity for water rights litigation
- Prioritize settlements over adversarial litigation
- Target: Passage within 4-6 years

### Institutional Development

**Treaty Rights Enforcement Commission Implementation**

- Confirm commissioners and hire staff (Year 1 after enactment)
- Conduct first comprehensive treaty compliance audit (Year 2)
- Issue first annual report and compliance orders (Year 2)
- Establish complaint investigation procedures (Year 1)

**Tribal Court Capacity Building**

- Fund expansion of tribal court systems to handle restored criminal jurisdiction
- Train tribal judges, prosecutors, and defense attorneys
- Develop appellate review structures for expanded jurisdiction cases
- Target: Functional expanded jurisdiction within 3 years of legislative authorization

## Phase 4: Long-Term Transformation (Years 7-15)

### Constitutional Protections

**Treaty Rights Constitutional Amendment**

If legislative protections prove insufficient, pursue a constitutional amendment:

- Prohibit unilateral treaty abrogation
- Constitutionalize the trust responsibility
- Guarantee tribal access to federal courts for treaty enforcement
- Target: Proposal and ratification within 10-15 years

### Full Treaty Compliance

**Benchmarks for Success**

### International Engagement

**Long-Term International Strategy**

- Pursue voluntary international review of treaty compliance
- Support development of international Indigenous rights enforcement mechanisms
- Report on UNDRIP implementation as part of Universal Periodic Review
- Build international coalitions with Indigenous rights movements globally

## Risks and Contingencies

### Risk: Fiscal Opposition

**Threat**: Opposition frames mandatory treaty funding as budget-busting

**Mitigation**: Emphasize that $50 billion is 0.8% of federal budget; compare to military spending; highlight economic returns from healthy, educated tribal communities

**Contingency**: If full mandatory funding fails, pursue phased increases with automatic escalators

### Risk: Supreme Court Reversal

**Threat**: Court overrules treaty rights precedents or expands *Castro-Huerta*

**Mitigation**: Legislatively codify treaty rights independent of judicial interpretation; pursue statutory rather than constitutional arguments

**Contingency**: If Court narrows treaty rights, pursue legislative restoration through Congress

### Risk: State Opposition

**Threat**: States actively resist treaty compliance

**Mitigation**: Federal preemption provisions; incentive structures (federal funding conditions); state-tribal cooperation compacts

**Contingency**: Federal enforcement action through DOJ

### Risk: Political Backlash

**Threat**: Treaty rights framed as "special privileges" in political campaigns

**Mitigation**: Bipartisan coalition; emphasize honor and legal obligation; public education campaign

**Contingency**: If backlash materializes, focus on incremental gains and long-term public education

## Success Metrics

### Leading Indicators

- Number of co-sponsors on treaty rights legislation
- State treaty education mandates enacted
- Public awareness polling on treaty obligations
- Tribal-state cooperation compacts signed
- Media coverage volume and sentiment

### Lagging Indicators

- Federal appropriations for treaty-obligated programs
- Treaty compliance audit scores
- Health outcomes in Indian Country (life expectancy, infant mortality)
- Educational outcomes (graduation rates, facility conditions)
- Water rights adjudicated
- Criminal jurisdiction exercised
