# Tribal Cultural Property: Resources

## Key Federal Laws and Regulations

### Statutes

- **Native American Graves Protection and Repatriation Act (NAGPRA)**, 25 U.S.C. 3001-3013 (1990). Requires federal agencies and federally funded institutions to inventory, consult, and repatriate Native American human remains, funerary objects, sacred objects, and objects of cultural patrimony to lineal descendants and culturally affiliated tribes.
- **National Museum of the American Indian Act (NMAI Act)**, 20 U.S.C. 80q (1989). Established the National Museum of the American Indian and requires the Smithsonian Institution to inventory and repatriate Native American remains and objects under a separate framework from NAGPRA.
- **Archaeological Resources Protection Act (ARPA)**, 16 U.S.C. 470aa-470mm (1979). Criminalizes unauthorized excavation, removal, or damage to archaeological resources on federal and tribal land; requires permits for excavation and tribal consent on tribal land.
- **National Historic Preservation Act (NHPA)**, 54 U.S.C. 300101 et seq. (1966). Establishes Section 106 review process for federal undertakings affecting historic properties; authorizes Tribal Historic Preservation Officers; provides National Register of Historic Places.
- **American Indian Religious Freedom Act (AIRFA)**, 42 U.S.C. 1996 (1978). Policy statement supporting Native American religious freedom, including access to sacred sites and ceremonial objects.
- **Indian Arts and Crafts Act**, 25 U.S.C. 305 et seq. (1990). Prohibits misrepresentation in the marketing of Indian arts and crafts; enforced by the Indian Arts and Crafts Board.
- **Antiquities Act**, 54 U.S.C. 320301-320303 (1906). First federal archaeological resource protection law; authorizes the President to designate national monuments on federal land.
- **Convention on Cultural Property Implementation Act**, 19 U.S.C. 2601-2613 (1983). Implements the 1970 UNESCO Convention on the Means of Prohibiting and Preventing the Illicit Import, Export, and Transfer of Ownership of Cultural Property.

## Key Court Cases

### Supreme Court

- *Bonnichsen v. United States*, 367 F.3d 864 (9th Cir. 2004). Held that Kennewick Man remains were not "Native American" under NAGPRA because cultural affiliation could not be established; later overridden by DNA analysis and congressional action.
- *United States v. Corrow*, 119 F.3d 796 (10th Cir. 1997). Upheld NAGPRA's criminal trafficking provisions against constitutional challenge.
- *Morton v. Mancari*, 417 U.S. 535 (1974). Established that tribal-specific legislation is a political classification, not a racial one.
- *Haaland v. Brackeen*, 599 U.S. ___ (2023). Upheld congressional authority under the Indian Commerce Clause; affirmed constitutionality of tribal-specific legislation.

### Circuit and District Courts

- *Thorpe v. Borough of Jim Thorpe*, 770 F.3d 255 (3d Cir. 2014). Applied NAGPRA to the remains of Jim Thorpe; found that the borough was a "museum" under NAGPRA.
- *Yankton Sioux Tribe v. U.S. Army Corps of Engineers*, 209 F. Supp. 2d 1008 (D.S.D. 2002). Addressed tribal authority over remains found during federal construction on tribal land.
- *Na Iwi O Na Kupuna O Mokapu v. Dalton*, 894 F. Supp. 1397 (D. Haw. 1995). Addressed repatriation of Native Hawaiian remains under NAGPRA.
- *Pueblo of San Ildefonso v. Ridlon*, 103 F.3d 936 (10th Cir. 1996). Addressed cultural patrimony claims under NAGPRA.
