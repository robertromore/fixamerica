# Tribal Cultural Property: Roadmap

## Overview

This roadmap sequences cultural property protection and repatriation reforms across three phases, balancing immediate administrative actions achievable without new legislation against longer-term legislative and international strategies. The phasing reflects political feasibility, urgency of need, and the 2029 deadline established by the updated NAGPRA regulations.

## Phase 1: Foundation Building (Years 1-2)

### Administrative Actions (No Legislation Required)

#### 1.1 Surge NAGPRA Compliance Monitoring

- **Action**: Redirect existing National NAGPRA program resources and request supplemental appropriations to establish active compliance monitoring
- **Content**: Hire 15 additional compliance specialists; conduct compliance audits of the 50 institutions with the largest remaining holdings; establish public compliance dashboard
- **Timeline**: Months 1-12
- **Lead**: Department of the Interior, National Park Service
- **Dependencies**: Supplemental appropriations request; existing regulatory authority under 43 C.F.R. Part 10
- **Risk**: Budget limitations may constrain staffing; institutions may resist audits

#### 1.2 Enforce 2024 NAGPRA Regulations

- **Action**: Aggressively implement the 2024 updated regulations, including the 5-year compliance deadline (January 2029)
- **Content**: Issue implementation guidance; send formal compliance notices to all institutions with outstanding obligations; initiate civil penalty proceedings against the most egregious non-compliers
- **Timeline**: Months 1-24
- **Lead**: National NAGPRA program
- **Dependencies**: Existing regulatory authority; staff capacity
- **Risk**: Institutional litigation challenging new regulations

#### 1.3 Executive Order on Cultural Property Protection

- **Action**: Issue executive order strengthening federal cultural property protections
- **Content**: Direct all federal agencies to prioritize NAGPRA compliance; recognize tribal cultural heritage codes; establish inter-agency cultural property coordination; mandate tribal consent for research on federally held collections
- **Timeline**: Months 1-3
- **Lead**: White House Domestic Policy Council
- **Dependencies**: Presidential willingness; no congressional action needed
- **Risk**: Reversible by future administrations

#### 1.4 Tribal Repatriation Capacity Surge

- **Action**: Increase NAGPRA grant funding within existing appropriations and request supplemental funds
- **Target**: Double NAGPRA grant funding to $5.4 million within Year 1; request $25 million for Year 2
- **Timeline**: Months 1-18
- **Lead**: National NAGPRA program, BIA
- **Dependencies**: Appropriations; existing grant mechanisms

#### 1.5 Online Trafficking Crackdown

- **Action**: Federal investigations targeting online trafficking of Native cultural items; engage platforms on policy enforcement
- **Content**: DOJ/FBI cultural property investigations; meetings with eBay, Etsy, and major online auction platforms to develop compliance protocols; CBP enhanced screening at ports of entry
- **Timeline**: Months 3-24
- **Lead**: DOJ, FBI, CBP, HSI
- **Dependencies**: Existing statutory authority under NAGPRA and ARPA; agency prioritization

### Coalition Building

#### 1.6 Congressional Education Campaign

- **Action**: Organize repatriation-focused briefings and field visits for key committee members and staff
- **Priority committees**: Senate Indian Affairs, House Natural Resources, House Judiciary, Senate Judiciary
- **Content**: Site visits to tribal cultural centers, museum repatriation programs, and looted archaeological sites
- **Timeline**: Months 3-18
- **Lead**: NCAI, AAIA, NARF, tribal nations

#### 1.7 Museum Alliance Development

- **Action**: Build a coalition of progressive museums committed to accelerated repatriation and ethical practice
- **Target**: 50+ institutions publicly committed to complete repatriation by 2029
- **Timeline**: Months 1-24
- **Lead**: AAM, tribal advocacy organizations, progressive museum leaders

#### 1.8 International Diplomatic Initiative

- **Action**: Begin diplomatic engagement with major art market countries on Native American cultural property
- **Priority countries**: France (Drouot auction sales), United Kingdom, Germany, Switzerland
- **Content**: Formal diplomatic protests of auction sales; exploratory discussions on bilateral agreements
- **Timeline**: Months 6-24
- **Lead**: State Department, NCAI, tribal delegations

## Phase 2: Legislative Advance (Years 2-4)

### Priority Legislation

#### 2.1 NAGPRA Enforcement and Funding Act

- **Why first**: Builds on existing law with 34-year track record; 2024 regulations demonstrate momentum; bipartisan NAGPRA support exists
- **Content**: Increase funding to $50 million; create enforcement division; establish private right of action; increase penalties; mandate compliance audits
- **Strategy**: Introduce in Senate Indian Affairs Committee with bipartisan sponsors; leverage 2029 deadline urgency
- **Timeline**: Introduction Year 2; committee action Year 2-3; floor action Year 3
- **Lead**: Senate Committee on Indian Affairs
- **Dependencies**: Bipartisan co-sponsors; appropriations scoring
- **Risk**: Budget objections; institutional lobbying

#### 2.2 Cultural Property Trafficking Prevention Act

- **Content**: Extend trafficking prohibitions to private sales; mandatory provenance disclosure; dedicated FBI cultural property task force; platform compliance requirements
- **Strategy**: Frame as law enforcement strengthening; build on existing ARPA framework
- **Timeline**: Introduction Year 2; committee action Year 3; floor action Year 3-4
- **Lead**: Senate Judiciary Committee, House Judiciary Committee
- **Dependencies**: DOJ support; platform industry engagement
- **Risk**: Art market industry opposition; privacy concerns

#### 2.3 NAGPRA Scope Expansion

- **Content**: Extend NAGPRA to all institutions regardless of federal funding; voluntary private collector repatriation program with tax incentives
- **Strategy**: Introduce alongside enforcement legislation; build record through hearings
- **Timeline**: Introduction Year 3; hearings Year 3-4; passage Year 4-5
- **Lead**: Senate Indian Affairs Committee
- **Dependencies**: Enforcement Act passage; institutional compliance community response
- **Risk**: Private property arguments; constitutional challenges

### Supporting Legislation

#### 2.4 Tribal Cultural Heritage Authority Act

- **Content**: Federal recognition of tribal cultural heritage codes; tribal court jurisdiction over cultural property disputes; full faith and credit for tribal cultural property judgments
- **Strategy**: Package with broader tribal sovereignty legislation
- **Timeline**: Year 3-4
- **Lead**: Senate Indian Affairs Committee
- **Dependencies**: Tribal sovereignty political environment

#### 2.5 International Cultural Property Recovery Act

- **Content**: Bilateral agreement authority for Native American items; federal repatriation fund for international purchases; import/export restrictions
- **Strategy**: Package with State Department authorization; build on UNESCO Convention framework
- **Timeline**: Year 3-5
- **Lead**: Senate Foreign Relations Committee, House Foreign Affairs Committee
- **Dependencies**: Diplomatic groundwork in Phase 1; international partner willingness

## Phase 3: Systemic Change (Years 4-8)

### Full Repatriation Achievement

#### 3.1 2029 NAGPRA Deadline Enforcement

- **Action**: Enforce the 5-year compliance deadline established by the 2024 regulations
- **Content**: Initiate civil penalty proceedings against all non-compliant institutions; refer willful violators for criminal prosecution; publish non-compliance list
- **Timeline**: Year 5 (January 2029)
- **Lead**: National NAGPRA program, DOJ
- **Dependencies**: NAGPRA Enforcement and Funding Act passage; adequate staffing

#### 3.2 Complete Repatriation Campaign

- **Action**: Intensive effort to repatriate all remaining institutionally held Native American ancestors
- **Target**: Repatriation of remaining 108,000+ individuals by Year 8
- **Content**: Dedicated repatriation coordinators for each major holding institution; funded tribal participation; streamlined affiliation processes
- **Timeline**: Years 5-8
- **Lead**: National NAGPRA program, tribal nations, institutions
- **Dependencies**: Funding; institutional cooperation; tribal capacity

### International Framework

#### 3.3 Bilateral Cultural Property Agreements

- **Action**: Conclude bilateral agreements with major art market countries
- **Target**: Agreements with France, UK, Germany, Switzerland, Belgium by Year 6
- **Content**: Import/export restrictions on Native American cultural items; mutual legal assistance; information sharing
- **Timeline**: Years 4-6
- **Lead**: State Department
- **Dependencies**: Diplomatic progress; partner government willingness

#### 3.4 International Repatriation Program

- **Action**: Systematic effort to recover Native American cultural items held in foreign institutions and private collections
- **Content**: Diplomatic requests; supported tribal delegations; purchases where necessary; legal actions where possible
- **Timeline**: Years 5-8
- **Lead**: State Department, tribal nations, DOJ
- **Dependencies**: Bilateral agreements; international cooperation; repatriation fund

### Institutional Transformation

#### 3.5 Museum Practice Standards

- **Action**: Establish binding national standards for ethical museum practice regarding Native American collections
- **Content**: Collaborative curation requirements; tribal consent for exhibition; provenance research obligations; staff training mandates
- **Timeline**: Years 4-6
- **Lead**: American Alliance of Museums, NPS, tribal organizations
- **Dependencies**: Industry cooperation; NAGPRA amendments

#### 3.6 Digital Cultural Property Framework

- **Action**: Comprehensive legal and technological framework for digital cultural property protection
- **Content**: Platform compliance standards; 3D scanning consent requirements; tribal data sovereignty protocols; digital repatriation standards
- **Timeline**: Years 5-8
- **Lead**: Interior Department, FTC, tribal nations
- **Dependencies**: Technology development; legislative authority

## Risk Assessment

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|------------|
| Administration change reverses EO | High | Moderate | Pursue legislative codification in Phase 2 |
| Institutional lobbying blocks legislation | Moderate | High | Public pressure; progressive museum coalition; state-level action |
| 2024 regulations legally challenged | Moderate | High | DOJ defense; build administrative record |
| Insufficient appropriations | High | High | Build broad coalition; demonstrate cost-effectiveness |
| International partners refuse agreements | Moderate | Moderate | Multi-lateral approaches; public diplomacy; economic leverage |
| Art market moves further underground | Moderate | Moderate | Enhanced enforcement technology; informant networks; platform cooperation |
| Tribal capacity limitations | High | Moderate | Surge funding; inter-tribal consortia; technical assistance |
| Supreme Court limits NAGPRA authority | Low | Very High | Legislative fixes; careful case selection; broad coalition amicus support |
