# Alaska Natives: Implementation Roadmap

## Overview

This roadmap sequences reforms across three phases: immediate actions (Years 1-3), structural reforms (Years 4-10), and long-term transformation (Years 11-20). The sequencing reflects political feasibility, implementation dependencies, and urgency of need.

## Phase 1: Foundation Building (Years 1-3)

### Priority 1.1: Emergency Village Protection

**Timeline**: Months 1-12

- Appropriate $1 billion in emergency funding for the 10 most imminently threatened villages (Newtok/Mertarvik completion, Shishmaref, Kivalina, Shaktoolik, and others)
- Establish interagency task force for village relocation, co-chaired by Denali Commission and tribal representatives
- Issue executive order directing federal agencies to expedite permitting for village protection and relocation projects
- Fund emergency erosion protection measures for villages that have not yet decided to relocate

**Key milestones:**

- [ ] Emergency appropriation enacted
- [ ] Task force established and operational
- [ ] Mertarvik relocation completion plan funded
- [ ] Erosion protection construction initiated at 5+ villages

### Priority 1.2: Behavioral Health Surge

**Timeline**: Months 1-18

- Expand Community Health Aide training to include behavioral health certification (Behavioral Health Aide program expansion)
- Deploy telehealth behavioral health services to all village health clinics
- Fund 10 regional cultural healing camps operated by tribal health organizations
- Establish Alaska Native youth suicide prevention coordinator at IHS
- Increase residential substance abuse treatment beds by 200

**Key milestones:**

- [ ] 100 new Behavioral Health Aides trained and deployed
- [ ] Telehealth platforms operational in 150+ village clinics
- [ ] Cultural healing camps operational in each region
- [ ] Youth suicide rate reduction strategy published and funded

### Priority 1.3: Infrastructure Quick Wins

**Timeline**: Months 6-36

- Accelerate Village Safe Water projects for communities with no piped water (32 communities)
- Fund 50 community renewable energy projects through Alaska Energy Authority
- Extend broadband to 50 unserved communities through USDA ReConnect and NTIA Tribal Broadband Connectivity programs
- Begin design and construction of 2,000 housing units through NAHASDA supplemental funding

**Key milestones:**

- [ ] Honey bucket sanitation eliminated in 10+ communities
- [ ] 50 renewable energy projects in construction/completed
- [ ] 50 communities connected to broadband
- [ ] 500 housing units under construction

### Priority 1.4: ANCSA Governance Quick Reforms

**Timeline**: Months 6-24

- Introduce ANCSA amendments for corporate transparency, after-born enrollment standardization, and tribal-corporate coordination requirements
- Establish a federal advisory committee (with tribal and corporate representation) to recommend comprehensive ANCSA reform
- Issue BIA guidance clarifying tribal government authority in villages where tribal councils and village corporations overlap

**Key milestones:**

- [ ] ANCSA transparency amendments introduced in Congress
- [ ] Federal advisory committee chartered and convened
- [ ] BIA guidance issued

### Priority 1.5: Land-into-Trust Acceleration

**Timeline**: Months 1-36

- Hire additional BIA staff to process pending Alaska land-into-trust applications
- Issue departmental guidance streamlining the land-into-trust process for Alaska
- Process at least 20 land-into-trust applications within 3 years
- Conduct outreach to Alaska tribes on the land-into-trust process and benefits

**Key milestones:**

- [ ] Processing staff doubled
- [ ] 10 applications approved in Year 1
- [ ] 20+ applications approved by end of Year 3

## Phase 2: Structural Reform (Years 4-10)

### Priority 2.1: Indian Country Legislation

**Timeline**: Years 4-6

- Introduce and enact legislation defining Alaska Native village areas as Indian Country
- Establish 5-year phase-in period with federal technical assistance
- Fund tribal court development: $100 million over 5 years for court infrastructure, training, and operations
- Negotiate cross-jurisdictional agreements between tribal, state, and federal law enforcement
- Implement VAWA special tribal criminal jurisdiction in Alaska

**Key milestones:**

- [ ] Legislation enacted
- [ ] Phase-in implementation plan published
- [ ] 50 tribal courts operational or enhanced
- [ ] Cross-jurisdictional MOUs signed with state and federal agencies

### Priority 2.2: Comprehensive Subsistence Reform

**Timeline**: Years 4-8

- Enact federal subsistence legislation extending federal management to all Alaska lands and waters
- Establish regional co-management boards with majority Alaska Native representation
- Create climate-adaptive management protocols that integrate traditional ecological knowledge
- Legalize and protect customary trade in subsistence foods
- Fund tribal fish and game enforcement programs

**Key milestones:**

- [ ] Federal subsistence legislation enacted
- [ ] 6 regional co-management boards established
- [ ] Traditional ecological knowledge formally integrated into management decisions
- [ ] 50 tribal fish and game officers trained and deployed

### Priority 2.3: Alaska Village Infrastructure Initiative (Full Scale)

**Timeline**: Years 4-10

- Enact mandatory-funded Alaska Native Village Infrastructure Act
- Eliminate remaining sanitation deficiencies (all 32 communities with no piped water)
- Complete energy transition for 100+ communities
- Achieve universal broadband access
- Construct 5,000+ housing units
- Upgrade 100 rural airports and establish new surface transportation links

**Key milestones:**

- [ ] Infrastructure Act enacted with mandatory funding
- [ ] Honey bucket sanitation eliminated statewide
- [ ] 100 communities transitioned to renewable energy
- [ ] Universal broadband achieved
- [ ] Housing deficit reduced by 50%

### Priority 2.4: ANCSA Comprehensive Reform

**Timeline**: Years 5-8

- Enact comprehensive ANCSA amendments based on federal advisory committee recommendations
- Establish opt-in mechanism for village corporation land transfer to tribal trust
- Reform Section 7(i) revenue-sharing formulas
- Recognize cultural and community purposes in corporate charters
- Establish shareholder protections for after-born Natives

**Key milestones:**

- [ ] Comprehensive ANCSA amendments enacted
- [ ] 20+ village corporations opt to transfer land to tribal trust
- [ ] Section 7(i) disputes reduced by 75%

## Phase 3: Long-Term Transformation (Years 11-20)

### Priority 3.1: Complete Village Relocation Program

**Timeline**: Years 11-20

- Complete relocation of all imminently threatened villages (estimated 10-15 villages)
- Fund climate adaptation infrastructure for 50+ at-risk communities
- Establish permanent climate monitoring and early warning systems
- Transition relocation program from emergency response to planned adaptation

**Key milestones:**

- [ ] All imminently threatened villages relocated or protected
- [ ] Permanent climate adaptation program established
- [ ] No Alaska Native community at risk of imminent destruction

### Priority 3.2: Full Tribal Sovereignty

**Timeline**: Years 11-20

- All Alaska tribes exercising territorial jurisdiction through Indian Country framework
- Tribal courts operating as co-equal with state and federal courts
- Tribal regulatory authority over environmental protection, land use, and public safety fully operational
- Government-to-government consultation producing meaningful results

### Priority 3.3: Economic Self-Sufficiency

**Timeline**: Years 11-20

- Alaska Native community poverty rates within 5 percentage points of national average
- Unemployment rates within 5 percentage points of state average
- Diversified local economies in all regions
- ANCSA corporations and tribal enterprises operating in coordinated partnership

### Priority 3.4: Cultural Revitalization

**Timeline**: Years 11-20

- All 20 Alaska Native languages with active revitalization programs; at least 5 with growing numbers of child speakers
- Cultural education integrated into all schools serving Alaska Native students
- Traditional knowledge documented and transmitted through community-controlled programs
- Subsistence practices sustained through adequate resource management

## Implementation Dependencies

```
Land-into-Trust (Phase 1) --> Indian Country (Phase 2) --> Full Sovereignty (Phase 3)
                                      |
                                      v
Emergency Protection (Phase 1) --> Infrastructure Initiative (Phase 2) --> Economic Self-Sufficiency (Phase 3)
                                      |
                                      v
Behavioral Health Surge (Phase 1) --> Subsistence Reform (Phase 2) --> Cultural Revitalization (Phase 3)
                                      |
                                      v
ANCSA Quick Reforms (Phase 1) --> ANCSA Comprehensive Reform (Phase 2) --> Tribal-Corporate Partnership (Phase 3)
```

**Context**: $26 billion over 20 years ($1.3 billion/year average) represents approximately 0.02% of the federal budget. For comparison, the federal government spends approximately $25 billion annually on all tribal programs.

## Risk Assessment

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|-----------|
| Congressional inaction on sovereignty legislation | High | Critical | Build through executive actions and administrative reforms first |
| State opposition blocks implementation | High | High | Federal preemption where possible; build state-tribal relationships |
| Insufficient funding appropriated | High | High | Seek mandatory funding; use trust responsibility legal arguments |
| Climate change accelerates beyond projections | Moderate | Critical | Build adaptive capacity; prioritize most at-risk communities |
| ANCSA corporate opposition to reform | Moderate | Moderate | Engage corporations as partners; protect legitimate shareholder interests |
| Workforce shortages in rural Alaska | High | Moderate | Invest in Alaska Native workforce development; improve rural compensation |
