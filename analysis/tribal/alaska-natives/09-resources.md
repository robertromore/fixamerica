# Alaska Natives: Resources

## Essential Reading

### Books

- Case, David S., and David A. Voluck. *Alaska Natives and American Laws*. 3rd ed. Fairbanks: University of Alaska Press, 2012. -- The definitive legal reference on Alaska Native law.
- Mitchell, Donald Craig. *Sold American: The Story of Alaska Natives and Their Land, 1867-1959*. Hanover, NH: University Press of New England, 1997. -- Pre-ANCSA history of Alaska Native land dispossession.
- Mitchell, Donald Craig. *Take My Land, Take My Life: The Story of Congress's Historic Settlement of Alaska Native Land Claims, 1960-1971*. Fairbanks: University of Alaska Press, 2001. -- Detailed legislative history of ANCSA.
- Berger, Thomas R. *Village Journey: The Report of the Alaska Native Review Commission*. New York: Hill and Wang, 1985. -- Landmark review of ANCSA's impact on Alaska Native communities.
- Hensley, William L. Iggiagruk. *Fifty Miles from Tomorrow: A Memoir of Alaska and the Real People*. New York: Sarah Crichton Books, 2009. -- Memoir of a key leader in the land claims movement.
- Napoleon, Harold. *Yuuyaraq: The Way of the Human Being*. Fairbanks: Alaska Native Knowledge Network, 1991. -- Essential analysis of the "Great Death" and intergenerational trauma in Yup'ik communities.
- Haycox, Stephen. *Alaska: An American Colony*. Seattle: University of Washington Press, 2002. -- Alaska history with significant coverage of Native issues.
- Kawagley, Angayuqaq Oscar. *A Yupiaq Worldview: A Pathway to Ecology and Spirit*. Long Grove, IL: Waveland Press, 2006. -- Yup'ik perspective on education, ecology, and cultural identity.
- Langdon, Steve J. *The Native People of Alaska*. 5th ed. Anchorage: Greatland Graphics, 2014. -- Overview of Alaska Native cultures and contemporary issues.

### Academic Articles

- Thornton, Thomas F. "Alaska Native Subsistence: A Matter of Cultural Survival." *Cultural Survival Quarterly* 22, no. 3 (1998): 42-45.
- Wexler, Lisa. "Inupiat Youth Suicide and Culture Loss: Changing Community Conversations for Prevention." *Social Science & Medicine* 63, no. 11 (2006): 2938-2948.
- Rasmus, Stacy M., et al. "Healing the Community Through the Qungasvik Preventive Intervention." *American Journal of Community Psychology* 64, nos. 1-2 (2019): 200-213.
- Goldsmith, Scott. "The Remote Rural Economy of Alaska." *Institute of Social and Economic Research*, University of Alaska Anchorage, 2007.
- Kofinas, Gary P., et al. "Resilience of Athabascan Subsistence Systems to Interior Alaska's Changing Climate." *Canadian Journal of Forest Research* 40, no. 7 (2010): 1347-1359.

### Government Reports

- Government Accountability Office. "Regional Alaska Native Corporations: Prior Prior Prior Prior Prior Prior." GAO-12-552, 2012.

## Legal References

### Key Federal Statutes

| Statute | Citation | Relevance |
|---------|----------|-----------|
| Alaska Native Claims Settlement Act (ANCSA) | 43 U.S.C. 1601-1629h | Foundation of Alaska Native land and corporate framework |
| Alaska National Interest Lands Conservation Act (ANILCA) | 16 U.S.C. 3101-3233 | Subsistence rights (Title VIII); conservation lands |
| Indian Self-Determination and Education Assistance Act | 25 U.S.C. 5301-5423 | Tribal self-governance and contracting |
| Indian Child Welfare Act (ICWA) | 25 U.S.C. 1901-1963 | Tribal jurisdiction over child welfare |
| Violence Against Women Act (2013, 2022) | 34 U.S.C. 12291 et seq. | Tribal criminal jurisdiction (limited in Alaska) |
| Indian Reorganization Act (Alaska amendment) | 25 U.S.C. 5129 | Tribal organization authority |
| Marine Mammal Protection Act | 16 U.S.C. 1361-1423h | Alaska Native subsistence exemption |
| Alaska Statehood Act | P.L. 85-508 (1958) | Land selection; Native rights disclaimer |

### Key Court Decisions

| Case | Citation | Holding |
|------|----------|---------|
| *Alaska v. Native Village of Venetie Tribal Government* | 522 U.S. 520 (1998) | ANCSA lands are not "Indian Country" |
| *John v. Baker* | 982 P.2d 738 (Alaska 1999) | Alaska tribes have inherent sovereignty over domestic relations |
| *Katie John v. United States* | 247 F.3d 1032 (9th Cir. 2001) | Federal subsistence extends to navigable waters |
| *Sturgeon v. Frost* | 587 U.S. ___ (2019) | State regulations can apply on navigable waters within federal land |
| *Haaland v. Brackeen* | 599 U.S. 255 (2023) | ICWA upheld; affirms Indian Commerce Clause authority |
| *Oliphant v. Suquamish Indian Tribe* | 435 U.S. 191 (1978) | Tribes lack criminal jurisdiction over non-Indians |
| *McGirt v. Oklahoma* | 591 U.S. ___ (2020) | Congressional intent controls reservation disestablishment |
