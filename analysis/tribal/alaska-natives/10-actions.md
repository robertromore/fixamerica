# Alaska Natives: Actions

## Overview

This document outlines specific actions that citizens, advocates, policymakers, and organizations can take to support Alaska Native communities. Actions are organized by actor type and effort level, with priority given to activities that address the most urgent needs.

## For All Citizens

### Learn and Understand

- **Read about ANCSA and its consequences**: Understanding the corporate model and how it differs from the reservation system is essential to informed advocacy. Start with Thomas Berger's *Village Journey* or David Case and David Voluck's *Alaska Natives and American Laws*.
- **Understand subsistence**: Learn why subsistence hunting and fishing are not recreational activities but essential components of Alaska Native nutrition, economy, and culture. The Alaska Department of Fish and Game's Division of Subsistence publishes accessible community profiles.
- **Follow Alaska Native media**: Alaska Public Media, the Anchorage Daily News, and Indian Country Today provide regular coverage of Alaska Native issues.
- **Learn about the climate crisis in rural Alaska**: The physical destruction of villages by erosion and flooding is one of the most visible climate change impacts in the United States.

### Advocate

- **Contact your members of Congress**: Urge support for Alaska Native infrastructure funding, village relocation programs, subsistence protection, and tribal sovereignty legislation
- **Support organizations**: Donate to or volunteer with organizations working on Alaska Native issues (see Resources file for a directory)
- **Share information**: Use social media and personal networks to raise awareness about Alaska Native issues, which receive minimal national media coverage
- **Attend public comment periods**: Federal and state agencies accept public comments on subsistence regulations, environmental permits, and tribal consultations

### Consume Responsibly

- **Support Alaska Native artists and businesses**: Purchase art, crafts, and products from Alaska Native-owned businesses and cooperatives
- **Be informed about Alaska seafood**: Understand the relationship between commercial fishing and subsistence access when purchasing Alaska seafood
- **Support sustainable tourism**: If visiting Alaska, patronize Alaska Native-owned tourism operations and cultural centers

## For Alaska Residents

### Civic Engagement

- **Support constitutional amendment for subsistence**: Advocate for amending the Alaska Constitution to allow a rural subsistence priority, resolving the federal-state management conflict
- **Attend Board of Game and Board of Fisheries meetings**: These boards set hunting and fishing regulations that directly affect subsistence access; public testimony influences outcomes
- **Vote for candidates who support tribal sovereignty**: Evaluate candidates' positions on tribal sovereignty, subsistence, and rural infrastructure
- **Support land-into-trust applications**: When Alaska tribes apply for land-into-trust status, participate in public comment periods in support

### Community Action

- **Volunteer with rural service organizations**: Organizations serving rural Alaska communities need volunteers for construction projects, teaching, and community development
- **Support Alaska Native language programs**: Donate to or volunteer with language revitalization programs, language nests, and immersion schools
- **Participate in cross-cultural events**: Attend Alaska Federation of Natives conventions, Native Heritage Month events, and cultural celebrations

## For Alaska Native Community Members

### Governance and Advocacy

- **Participate in tribal government**: Attend tribal council meetings, vote in tribal elections, serve on tribal boards and committees
- **Engage with ANCSA corporations**: Attend shareholder meetings, review corporate financial reports, run for corporate board positions
- **Document traditional knowledge**: Work with elders to record oral histories, traditional ecological knowledge, and language materials
- **Participate in subsistence management**: Serve on federal Regional Advisory Councils, state advisory committees, and co-management bodies

### Community Resilience

- **Support youth programs**: Mentor young people, support cultural camps, and create opportunities for youth to connect with traditional practices
- **Participate in suicide prevention**: Complete gatekeeper training (QPR or ASIST); support community-level resilience programs
- **Support behavioral health services**: Reduce stigma around seeking help; support the Community Health Aide / Behavioral Health Aide programs
- **Engage in climate planning**: Participate in community climate adaptation planning; document environmental changes

## For Policymakers

### Federal Policymakers

- **Enact Indian Country legislation for Alaska**: Define Alaska Native village areas as Indian Country to enable full tribal jurisdiction
- **Reform federal subsistence management**: Extend federal subsistence authority to all Alaska lands and waters with meaningful Alaska Native participation in management
- **Fund village infrastructure**: Appropriate mandatory funding for water, sanitation, energy, broadband, and housing in Alaska Native communities
- **Establish village relocation program**: Create a dedicated, funded program for climate-threatened village relocation with community self-determination
- **Reform ANCSA**: Amend ANCSA to improve tribal-corporate coordination, after-born enrollment, and corporate governance transparency
- **Fully fund IHS in Alaska**: Increase IHS funding to meet the demonstrated need, not just the historical budget baseline
- **Expand VAWA tribal jurisdiction to Alaska**: Ensure that the Violence Against Women Act's tribal jurisdiction provisions are fully implementable in Alaska

### State Policymakers

- **Amend the Alaska Constitution for subsistence**: Adopt a constitutional amendment allowing a rural subsistence priority, bringing the state into compliance with ANILCA
- **Recognize tribal sovereignty**: Issue formal recognition of tribal governmental authority and establish clear protocols for government-to-government relations
- **Fund rural infrastructure**: Increase state investment in rural transportation, energy, and broadband
- **Improve Board of Game/Board of Fisheries representation**: Ensure Alaska Native communities have meaningful representation on regulatory boards
- **Address the rural public safety crisis**: Fund Village Public Safety Officers, improve law enforcement response times, and support tribal law enforcement

### Local Officials

- **Support cross-jurisdictional cooperation**: Establish agreements between tribal, borough, and municipal governments for shared services and law enforcement
- **Include Alaska Native perspectives in planning**: Ensure tribal governments are consulted in land use, infrastructure, and economic development planning
- **Support Alaska Native hiring**: Prioritize Alaska Native hiring in local government positions, particularly in communities with significant Native populations

## For Researchers and Academics

- **Center Alaska Native perspectives**: Ensure research on Alaska Native communities is community-driven, culturally appropriate, and benefits the communities studied
- **Follow tribal IRB processes**: Obtain tribal research permits and follow community review processes for research in Alaska Native communities
- **Document climate impacts**: Contribute to the scientific understanding of climate change effects on Alaska Native communities, infrastructure, and subsistence resources
- **Study what works**: Evaluate the effectiveness of interventions in behavioral health, education, economic development, and governance to identify scalable best practices
- **Support Alaska Native scholars**: Mentor, fund, and collaborate with Alaska Native researchers and students

## For Legal Professionals

- **Provide pro bono legal services**: Alaska Native tribal governments and individuals have significant unmet legal needs in areas including tribal law, environmental law, ICWA, and criminal defense
- **Support tribal court development**: Assist with drafting tribal codes, training tribal court personnel, and developing tribal court systems
- **Litigate for subsistence rights**: Support legal challenges to subsistence restrictions and defend Alaska Native harvesting rights
- **Advocate for VAWA implementation in Alaska**: Work to ensure tribal criminal jurisdiction is implementable in Alaska communities

## For Philanthropic Organizations

- **Fund Alaska Native-led organizations**: Direct grants to organizations led by and serving Alaska Natives, prioritizing capacity building and general operating support
- **Invest in language revitalization**: Language loss is urgent; fund immersion programs, technology-based learning tools, and master-apprentice programs
- **Support climate adaptation**: Fund community-driven climate adaptation planning and implementation
- **Address the behavioral health crisis**: Fund culturally grounded behavioral health programs, traditional healing initiatives, and suicide prevention
