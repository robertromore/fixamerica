# Tribal Housing: Implementation Roadmap

## Overview

This roadmap outlines a phased approach to addressing the tribal housing crisis over 15 years. The strategy sequences legislative, administrative, and programmatic actions to build momentum, address the most urgent needs first, and create the institutional capacity for sustained reform. Each phase builds on the preceding one, with early funding increases enabling capacity building that supports more ambitious later actions.

---

## Phase 1: Foundation and Immediate Relief (Years 1-3)

### Objectives

- Enact NAHASDA reauthorization with increased funding
- Remove the most critical trust land barriers to housing construction
- Launch infrastructure coordination for housing development
- Establish capacity-building programs for TDHEs
- Begin comprehensive housing needs data collection

### Action Table

| Action | Type | Lead | Timeline | Cost Estimate | Priority |
|--------|------|------|----------|---------------|----------|
| Enact NAHASDA reauthorization at $1.5 billion/year | Legislative | Congress (Senate Indian Affairs Committee) | Year 1 | $1.5B/year | Critical |
| Establish statutory 90-day BIA processing timeline for residential leases and Title Status Reports | Legislative | Congress | Year 1 | $30M/year (staffing) | Critical |
| Hire 200 BIA realty specialists for housing-related approvals | Administrative | DOI/BIA | Years 1-2 | $30M/year | High |
| Expand HEARTH Act to include residential site leasing | Legislative | Congress | Year 1 | Minimal (authorization) | High |
| Launch TDHE capacity-building program through NAIHC | Administrative | HUD/NAIHC | Year 1 | $50M/year | High |
| Begin comprehensive tribal housing needs assessment | Administrative | HUD/Census | Year 1-2 | $15M | High |
| Capitalize Native CDFIs for mortgage lending | Legislative/Administrative | Treasury/CDFI Fund | Year 1 | $200M (one-time) | High |
| Pilot tribal housing infrastructure coordination in 10 tribes | Administrative | HUD/IHS/BIA | Years 2-3 | $100M/year | Medium |
| Establish Native American homelessness data collection | Administrative | HUD | Year 1 | $10M | Medium |
| Launch climate-resilient housing design research | Administrative | HUD/DOE | Year 2 | $25M/year | Medium |

### Phase 1 Success Metrics

| Metric | Baseline (2025) | Year 3 Target |
|--------|-----------------|---------------|
| NAHASDA annual appropriation | $772 million | $1.5 billion |
| BIA residential lease processing time | 6-12 months | 90 days |

## Phase 2: Scaling and Infrastructure (Years 4-8)

### Objectives

- Increase NAHASDA funding to $2 billion annually
- Establish dedicated Tribal Housing Infrastructure Fund
- Expand tribal mortgage processing authority
- Build regional TDHE cooperatives
- Implement climate-resilient construction at scale

## Phase 3: Full Implementation and Systemic Reform (Years 9-15)

### Objectives

- Achieve full estimated NAHASDA funding ($2.9 billion/year)
- Eliminate the most severe housing conditions (lack of plumbing, electricity)
- Reduce overcrowding to near national levels
- Establish tribal housing systems that are self-sustaining and tribally controlled
- Achieve measurable improvements in housing-related health outcomes

### Action Table

| Action | Type | Lead | Timeline | Cost Estimate | Priority |
|--------|------|------|----------|---------------|----------|
| Increase NAHASDA appropriation to $2.9 billion/year | Legislative | Congress | Year 9 | $2.9B/year | Critical |
| Eliminate lack of complete plumbing in tribal housing | Programmatic | IHS/HUD/Tribes | Years 9-12 | Included in infrastructure fund | High |
| Extend electricity to all tribal homes | Programmatic | DOE/Tribes | Years 9-12 | $500M total | High |
| Expand tribal mortgage processing to all interested tribes | Administrative | DOI/HUD | Years 9-12 | $25M/year | High |
| Transition TDHE oversight from HUD to tribal self-certification | Administrative | HUD | Years 10-15 | Cost-neutral | Medium |
| Establish permanent tribal housing data system | Administrative | HUD/Census | Years 10-12 | $15M (one-time) | Medium |
| Evaluate and adjust housing infrastructure fund based on progress | Legislative | Congress | Year 12 | Variable | Medium |
| Comprehensive program evaluation and 20-year strategy development | Administrative | HUD/GAO/Tribes | Year 14-15 | $10M | Medium |

## Risk Mitigation

### Political Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation Strategy |
|------|-----------|--------|---------------------|
| Congressional resistance to NAHASDA funding increase | Medium | Very High | Build bipartisan coalition; frame as trust obligation; phase increases |
| Administration change reducing tribal housing priority | Medium | High | Codify reforms in statute; seek mandatory appropriations |
| Competing budget priorities reduce tribal housing share | High | High | Document cost of inaction; build advocacy coalition with national housing groups |
| State opposition to expanded trust land housing development | Medium | Medium | Revenue-sharing provisions; document economic benefits to surrounding communities |

### Implementation Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation Strategy |
|------|-----------|--------|---------------------|
| TDHE capacity insufficient for rapid funding increase | Medium | High | Phased increases; capacity building precedes or accompanies funding growth |
| BIA unable to meet 90-day processing targets | High | Medium | Hire additional staff; expand tribal processing authority as alternative |
| Infrastructure fund delayed by inter-agency coordination | Medium | High | Establish lead agency (HUD) with interagency MOUs; single application process |
| Construction costs escalate beyond projections | Medium | Medium | Invest in modular/prefabricated approaches; develop local workforce |
| Climate change damages new housing before design adaptation | Medium | Medium | Front-load climate-resilient design research; incorporate traditional knowledge |

### Legal Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation Strategy |
|------|-----------|--------|---------------------|
| Trust land mortgage reforms face legal challenges | Low | Medium | Build on established HEARTH Act authority; seek DOJ legal review |
| Equal protection challenges to tribal housing programs | Low | High | Ground all programs in political classification framework (*Morton v. Mancari*; *Brackeen v. Haaland*) |
| NAHASDA formula changes face tribal litigation | Medium | Medium | Conduct thorough tribal consultation; use negotiated rulemaking |

## Funding Strategy

### Total Estimated Costs by Phase

| Phase | Total Cost | Annual Average | Primary Funding Source |
|-------|-----------|----------------|----------------------|
| Phase 1 (Years 1-3) | $6.5 billion | $2.2 billion | Discretionary appropriations + one-time capitalization |
| Phase 2 (Years 4-8) | $15.5 billion | $3.1 billion | Discretionary + mandatory appropriations |
| Phase 3 (Years 9-15) | $27.5 billion | $3.9 billion | Mandatory appropriations |
| **Total** | **$49.5 billion** | **$3.3 billion** | **Mixed** |

### Funding Justification

- Total 15-year cost of $49.5 billion represents approximately $3.3 billion annually, or less than 0.5% of annual federal spending
- The housing crisis costs billions annually in preventable health expenditures, emergency services, and lost economic productivity
- COVID-19 demonstrated the catastrophic public health consequences of overcrowded, substandard tribal housing
- Federal trust responsibility and treaty obligations create a legal and moral imperative for adequate investment
- Australia's NPARIH invested AUD $5.5 billion over 10 years for a much smaller Indigenous population and achieved measurable reductions in overcrowding

## Accountability Framework

### Reporting Requirements

- Annual HUD report to Congress on NAHASDA implementation, housing production, and program outcomes
- Tribal housing needs assessment updated every 5 years (replacing outdated 2017 study)
- GAO audit of program effectiveness every 3 years
- Tribal advisory committee (through NAIHC) with authority to recommend program modifications

### Course Correction Triggers

- If housing production does not increase by at least 50% by Year 3, accelerate capacity-building investment
- If BIA processing times do not meet 90-day target by Year 2, expand tribal processing authority immediately
- If infrastructure coordination pilot does not reduce per-unit costs, restructure the inter-agency process
- If overcrowding rate does not decline by Year 5, redirect funds from new construction to rehabilitation and expansion of existing units
- If homelessness data shows no improvement by Year 5, increase dedicated homelessness funding by 50%
