# Tribal Sovereignty: Resources

## Overview

This document compiles key academic research, government reports, legal resources, organizations, and data sources relevant to tribal sovereignty analysis and reform.

## Academic Research

### Foundational Texts

- Wilkins, David E., and K. Tsianina Lomawaima. *Uneven Ground: American Indian Sovereignty and Federal Law*. University of Oklahoma Press, 2001. -- Comprehensive analysis of the legal and political foundations of tribal sovereignty.

- Wilkinson, Charles. *Blood Struggle: The Rise of Modern Indian Nations*. W.W. Norton, 2005. -- History of the self-determination movement from termination through the modern era.

- Deloria, Vine Jr., and Clifford M. Lytle. *The Nations Within: The Past and Future of American Indian Sovereignty*. University of Texas Press, 1984. -- Foundational work on the concept of inherent tribal sovereignty.

- Getches, David H., Charles F. Wilkinson, Robert A. Williams Jr., Matthew L.M. Fletcher, and Kristen A. Carpenter. *Cases and Materials on Federal Indian Law*. 7th ed. West Academic, 2017. -- Standard law school casebook covering all major aspects of federal Indian law.

- Fletcher, Matthew L.M. *Federal Indian Law*. West Academic, 2016. -- Accessible overview of federal Indian law for non-specialists.

### Self-Determination Research

- Cornell, Stephen, and Joseph P. Kalt. "Sovereignty and Nation-Building: The Development Challenge in Indian Country Today." *American Indian Culture and Research Journal* 22, no. 3 (1998): 187-214.

- Cornell, Stephen, and Joseph P. Kalt. *What Can Tribes Do? Strategies and Institutions in American Indian Economic Development*. UCLA American Indian Studies Center, 1992.

- Jorgensen, Miriam, ed. *Rebuilding Native Nations: Strategies for Governance and Development*. University of Arizona Press, 2007.

### Jurisdictional Analysis

- Washburn, Kevin K. "Federal Criminal Law and Tribal Self-Determination." *North Carolina Law Review* 84 (2006): 779-855.

- Riley, Angela R. "Crime and Governance in Indian Country." *UCLA Law Review* 63 (2016): 1564-1637.

- Skibine, Alexander T. "The Supreme Court's Last 30 Years of Federal Indian Law: Looking for Equilibrium or a Paradigm Shift." *Columbia Journal of Race and Law* 8 (2018): 189-237.

### Trust Responsibility

- Wood, Mary Christina. "Indian Land and the Promise of Native Sovereignty: The Trust Doctrine Revisited." *Utah Law Review* 1994, no. 4 (1994): 1471-1569.

- Newton, Nell Jessup. "Federal Power over Indians: Its Sources, Scope, and Limitations." *University of Pennsylvania Law Review* 132 (1984): 195-288.

## Government Reports

### Congressional

- U.S. Commission on Civil Rights. *Broken Promises: Continuing Federal Funding Shortfall for Native Americans*. 2018. -- Landmark report documenting systematic underfunding of federal obligations to tribes.

- Congressional Research Service. "Indian Tribes: Federal Obligations, Funding, and Services." R44451, updated 2024.

- Congressional Research Service. "Tribal Criminal Jurisdiction After the Violence Against Women Reauthorization Act of 2022." R47239, 2023.

- Congressional Research Service. "The Federal Trust Responsibility to Indians." R46893, 2023.

- Congressional Research Service. "Indian Water Rights Settlements." R44148, updated 2024.

### Executive Branch

- Government Accountability Office. "Indian Country Criminal Justice: Departments Should Enhance Data Collection and Take Additional Actions." GAO-23-105442, 2023.

- Government Accountability Office. "Indian Affairs: Better Management and Accountability Needed to Improve Indian Education." GAO-17-421, 2017.

- Department of Justice. *Indian Country Investigations and Prosecutions Annual Report*. Published annually. -- Data on federal prosecution and declination in Indian Country.

- Department of the Interior, Office of Inspector General. Various audits of BIA programs and trust management.

### Tribal

- National Congress of American Indians. *Indian Country Budget Request*. Published annually. -- Comprehensive tribal budget priorities document.

- National Congress of American Indians. *Tribal Nations and the United States: An Introduction*. 2020. -- Overview document for policymakers.

## Key Legal Decisions

### Foundational Cases

| Case | Year | Significance |
|------|------|-------------|
| *Johnson v. M'Intosh*, 21 U.S. 543 | 1823 | Established Doctrine of Discovery; limited tribal property rights |
| *Cherokee Nation v. Georgia*, 30 U.S. 1 | 1831 | Tribes are "domestic dependent nations" |
| *Worcester v. Georgia*, 31 U.S. 515 | 1832 | State laws have no force in Indian Country; exclusive federal-tribal relationship |
| *Lone Wolf v. Hitchcock*, 187 U.S. 553 | 1903 | Congressional plenary power over Indian affairs |
| *Morton v. Mancari*, 417 U.S. 535 | 1974 | Indian preference is political, not racial classification |

### Sovereignty-Eroding Cases

| Case | Year | Significance |
|------|------|-------------|
| *Oliphant v. Suquamish Indian Tribe*, 435 U.S. 191 | 1978 | Tribes lack criminal jurisdiction over non-Indians |
| *Montana v. United States*, 450 U.S. 544 | 1981 | Limits on tribal civil authority over non-members on fee land |
| *Strate v. A-1 Contractors*, 520 U.S. 438 | 1997 | Extended *Montana* to state highways within reservations |
| *Carcieri v. Salazar*, 555 U.S. 379 | 2009 | Land-into-trust limited to tribes "under federal jurisdiction" in 1934 |
| *Oklahoma v. Castro-Huerta*, 597 U.S. ___ | 2022 | States have concurrent jurisdiction over non-Indians in Indian Country |
| *Arizona v. Navajo Nation*, 599 U.S. ___ | 2023 | No affirmative duty to assess tribal water needs under treaty |

### Sovereignty-Affirming Cases

| Case | Year | Significance |
|------|------|-------------|
| *Williams v. Lee*, 358 U.S. 217 | 1959 | States cannot infringe on tribal self-governance right |
| *Santa Clara Pueblo v. Martinez*, 436 U.S. 49 | 1978 | Tribal sovereign immunity; tribal law governs membership |
| *Michigan v. Bay Mills Indian Community*, 572 U.S. 782 | 2014 | Reaffirmed tribal sovereign immunity |
| *McGirt v. Oklahoma*, 591 U.S. ___ | 2020 | Muscogee (Creek) reservation never disestablished |
| *Haaland v. Brackeen*, 599 U.S. ___ | 2023 | Upheld ICWA; affirmed Commerce Clause authority |
