# Tribal Education: Current State

## Overview

The current state of tribal education reflects a system that has made meaningful progress toward tribal control and cultural responsiveness since the self-determination era began in the 1970s, but that continues to be undermined by chronic underfunding, aging infrastructure, bureaucratic constraints, and the lingering effects of historical trauma. AI/AN students remain the lowest-performing demographic group on most educational metrics, and the institutions designed to serve them -- BIE schools and tribal colleges -- receive significantly less funding than their mainstream counterparts.

### TCU Challenges

- **Facilities**: Many TCU campuses consist of converted buildings not designed for higher education
- **Broadband**: 40% of TCUs lack adequate broadband connectivity for online learning (AIHEC, 2023)
- **Faculty**: Difficulty recruiting and retaining faculty due to remote locations and low salaries
- **Program offerings**: Limited by funding; most offer only associate degrees
- **Research capacity**: Minimal research infrastructure compared to mainstream institutions

## Boarding School Legacy

### Federal Indian Boarding School Initiative (2022-Present)

| Finding | Detail | Source |
|---------|--------|--------|
| Schools identified | 408+ federal Indian boarding schools operated between 1819-1969 | DOI Report Vol. 1, 2022 |
| Child burial sites identified | 65+ marked and unmarked burial sites | DOI Report Vol. 1, 2022 |
| Children who died | 973+ identified (estimated thousands more) | DOI Report Vol. 1, 2022 |
| States with boarding schools | 37 states (plus territories) | DOI Report Vol. 1, 2022 |
| Religious institutions involved | Multiple denominations operated schools under federal contracts | DOI Report Vol. 1, 2022 |

### Ongoing Impact

- Intergenerational trauma linked to boarding school attendance affects current AI/AN student performance and family educational engagement (Brave Heart, 2003)
- Language loss directly traceable to boarding school policies that punished Indigenous language use
- Community distrust of educational institutions rooted in boarding school experiences
- Federal investigation (Volume 2 report expected 2025-2026) continuing to document scope of abuse and death

### Key Workforce Issues

- Remote location of many BIE schools limits applicant pools
- Housing shortages on reservations make it difficult for teachers to find adequate housing
- Lower salaries than surrounding public school districts
- Limited professional development opportunities
- Cultural competency training inconsistent across BIE system

## Trends

### Positive Developments

- **Boarding School Initiative**: Federal investigation bringing accountability and attention to historical trauma (DOI, 2022)
- **Language revitalization funding increases**: Esther Martinez program reauthorization (2019) and increased appropriations
- **Tribal control expansion**: Increasing number of tribally controlled grant schools (BIE, 2024)
- **TCU enrollment growth**: Steady growth in TCU enrollment and program offerings (AIHEC, 2024)
- **Broadband expansion**: Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (2021) funding expanding connectivity to BIE schools

### Concerning Trends

- **BIE facility deterioration**: Deferred maintenance backlog growing despite construction appropriations
- **Teacher shortage worsening**: Post-pandemic teacher shortages disproportionately affecting BIE schools
- **Language loss accelerating**: Elder speakers dying without adequate transfer to younger generations
- **Achievement gap persistence**: NAEP scores show no significant improvement for AI/AN students over the past decade
- **Pandemic learning loss**: AI/AN students experienced disproportionate learning loss during COVID-19 (NCES, 2023)

## Key Data Gaps

- Comprehensive data on AI/AN student outcomes in public schools (disaggregated from overall data)
- Systematic assessment of culturally responsive education program effectiveness
- Longitudinal tracking of TCU graduates' career outcomes
- Complete accounting of boarding school deaths and burial sites
- Assessment of language immersion program outcomes on both language proficiency and academic achievement
