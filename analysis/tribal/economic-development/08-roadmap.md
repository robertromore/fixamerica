# Tribal Economic Development: Roadmap

## Phase 1: Quick Wins and Foundation (Years 1-2)

### Administrative Actions (No New Legislation Required)

**BIA Process Reform**

- Issue Interior Department directive establishing 30-day targets for routine trust land transaction approvals
- Expand HEARTH Act outreach to encourage all 574 tribes to opt in (currently over 80)
- Streamline energy lease approval process from 49 steps to 15 maximum
- Deploy electronic filing for trust land transactions
- Establish performance metrics and public reporting for BIA transaction processing times

**Executive Orders**

- Direct all federal agencies to prioritize tribal infrastructure projects in existing program allocations
- Strengthen tribal consultation requirements for economic development policy
- Direct SBA to assess and report on Native lending disparities

### Legislative Quick Wins

| Priority | Vehicle | Likelihood |
|----------|---------|------------|
| Native CDFI Fund increase to $100 million | Appropriations bill | High |
| HEARTH Act expansion to all leasing categories | Standalone bill or appropriations rider | High |
| Tribal broadband program acceleration | IIJA implementation guidance | Moderate |
| Indian Employment Credit extension | Tax bill | Moderate |
| Land Buy-Back Program reauthorization | Natural resources bill | Moderate |

### Coalition Building

- Establish Tribal Economic Development Coalition bringing together NCAI, NCAIED, NIGA, Native CDFI Network, and aligned organizations
- Engage Chamber of Commerce and National Association of Manufacturers as allies for trust land reform and regulatory streamlining
- Partner with rural development organizations (National Rural Electric Cooperative Association, rural broadband advocates)
- Build bipartisan congressional support through Senate Indian Affairs Committee and House Natural Resources Subcommittee

## Phase 2: Major Legislative Action (Years 2-4)

### Priority Legislation

**Tribal Trust Land Modernization Act**

- Universal HEARTH Act authority for all leasing categories
- 30-day mandatory BIA approval deadlines with automatic approval
- Expanded Land Buy-Back Program with $5 billion reauthorization
- Tribal land use planning authority with NEPA self-certification
- Modern title recording system funding ($200 million)

**Indian Country Capital Access Act**

- Native CDFI Fund increase to $200 million annually
- Section 184 guarantee expansion to $2 billion
- $5 billion tribal enterprise loan guarantee fund
- SBA Native lending set-aside
- Tribal bank and credit union charter authority

**Tribal Infrastructure Acceleration Act**

- $4 billion annual tribal infrastructure fund for 10 years
- Broadband completion mandate (100/20 Mbps to all tribal lands by 2030)
- BIA road deferred maintenance elimination
- IHS sanitation backlog elimination
- NAHASDA funding increase to $2 billion annually

### State-Level Action

**Model Tribal-State Tax Coordination Agreements**

- Develop model compact framework for tribal-state tax coordination
- Target: 10 new tax coordination agreements within 4 years
- Focus states: Washington, Minnesota, Wisconsin, Montana, Oklahoma, Arizona, New Mexico

**State Economic Development Partnerships**

- Establish state-tribal economic development zones with shared tax incentives
- Joint workforce development programs connecting tribal colleges with state university systems
- Shared infrastructure planning for roads, broadband, and utilities crossing reservation boundaries

## Phase 3: Structural Reform (Years 4-7)

### Major Policy Changes

**Tribal Regulatory Authority Act**

- Authorize all tribes to assume EPA-delegated regulatory authority (Treatment as State)
- Establish tribal-federal one-stop permitting centers
- Expand NEPA categorical exclusions for routine reservation development
- Streamline TERA eligibility for tribal energy development

**Tribal Revenue Sovereignty Act**

- Codify tribal taxation authority over all economic activity within tribal jurisdiction
- Federal preemption of conflicting state tax claims on tribal lands
- Model tax coordination framework mandating good-faith negotiation
- Expanded tribal bond authority

**Tribal Workforce and Innovation Act**

- Triple tribal college funding to $300 million annually
- $500 million Tribal Innovation Fund for diversification
- National tribal apprenticeship program
- Remote work skills training paired with broadband deployment

### Institutional Development

**Tribal Economic Development Corporation**

- Establish a quasi-governmental Tribal Economic Development Corporation (modeled on OPIC/DFC) to provide:
    - Project finance for tribal infrastructure and enterprise development
    - Technical assistance for tribal economic planning
    - Risk insurance for investors in tribal projects
    - Market analysis and business plan development support
- Initial capitalization: $2 billion
- Self-sustaining through loan repayments and insurance premiums within 10 years

### Long-Term Economic Diversification Goals

- No tribe dependent on a single industry for more than 50% of tribal government revenue
- Tribal renewable energy projects providing 10% of national renewable capacity
- Native-owned small business formation rate equal to national average
- Tribal broadband ISPs operating on all reservations
- Tribal college graduates employed at rates equal to national college graduate average

## Risks and Contingencies

### Risk: Federal Budget Constraints

**Threat**: Fiscal austerity prevents sustained tribal investment

**Mitigation**: Frame tribal infrastructure as economic investment with positive ROI; pair with deficit-neutral tax reform; emphasize bipartisan infrastructure precedent (IIJA)

**Contingency**: Prioritize trust land reform and regulatory streamlining (low cost, high impact) if major appropriations are unavailable

### Risk: State Opposition

**Threat**: States block tax coordination and regulatory reform

**Mitigation**: Federal preemption provisions; incentive structures; model compacts; engage governors directly

**Contingency**: Pursue federal-tribal reforms independent of state cooperation where possible

### Risk: BIA Institutional Resistance

**Threat**: BIA bureaucracy delays or undermines administrative reforms

**Mitigation**: Congressional oversight; performance metrics; Inspector General audits; political leadership committed to reform

**Contingency**: Shift functions from BIA to tribal administration through expanded 638 contracting

### Risk: Gaming Revenue Decline

**Threat**: Market saturation or regulatory changes reduce tribal gaming revenue

**Mitigation**: Diversification strategy reduces dependence; protect gaming sovereignty while building alternative revenue streams

**Contingency**: Accelerate diversification support if gaming revenue declines
