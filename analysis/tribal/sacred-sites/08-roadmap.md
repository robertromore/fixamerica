# Sacred Sites: Roadmap

## Overview

This roadmap sequences sacred site protection reforms across three phases, balancing immediate administrative actions that can be taken without legislation against longer-term legislative and judicial strategies. The phasing reflects both political feasibility and urgency of need.

## Phase 1: Foundation Building (Years 1-2)

### Administrative Actions (No Legislation Required)

#### 1.1 Executive Order on Sacred Sites

- **Action**: Issue comprehensive executive order replacing and strengthening EO 13007
- **Content**: Uniform consultation standards, agency sacred sites coordinators, pre-decisional consultation, annual reporting
- **Timeline**: Months 1-3
- **Lead**: White House Domestic Policy Council
- **Dependencies**: Presidential willingness; no congressional action needed
- **Risk**: Reversible by future administrations

#### 1.2 Strengthen Section 106 Consultation Guidance

- **Action**: ACHP issues updated guidance on sacred site consultation under NHPA
- **Timeline**: Months 3-9
- **Content**: Earlier consultation timing, senior official involvement, confidentiality protocols, outcome documentation
- **Lead**: Advisory Council on Historic Preservation
- **Dependencies**: ACHP rulemaking authority

#### 1.3 Emergency Mineral Withdrawals

- **Action**: Secretary of the Interior issues 20-year mineral withdrawals for the most threatened sacred landscapes
- **Priority sites**: Greater Chaco expansion, Badger-Two Medicine permanent protection, other at-risk areas
- **Timeline**: Months 1-12
- **Lead**: Department of the Interior
- **Dependencies**: Environmental review for each withdrawal

#### 1.4 THPO Capacity Surge

- **Action**: Redirect existing grant funds and request supplemental appropriations for THPO capacity
- **Target**: Increase THPO funding by 50% ($10 million) within existing authorities
- **Timeline**: Months 1-12
- **Lead**: National Park Service, BIA
- **Dependencies**: Appropriations; existing grant mechanisms

#### 1.5 Co-Management Pilot Expansion

- **Action**: Establish 5 new co-management agreements modeled on Bears Ears
- **Target sites**: Badger-Two Medicine (MT), Chaco region (NM), San Gabriel Mountains (CA), and 2 tribally nominated sites
- **Timeline**: Months 6-24
- **Lead**: BLM, USFS, NPS in partnership with tribal nations
- **Dependencies**: Tribal willingness; agency coordination

### Coalition Building

#### 1.6 Religious Freedom Alliance

- **Action**: Build formal coalition with religious freedom organizations (Becket Fund, First Liberty Institute, National Association of Evangelicals) around shared principle that government should not destroy places of worship
- **Timeline**: Months 1-12
- **Lead**: NCAI, NARF, tribal advocacy organizations

#### 1.7 Congressional Education Campaign

- **Action**: Organize sacred site field visits for key committee members and staff
- **Priority committees**: House Natural Resources, Senate Indian Affairs, Senate Energy and Natural Resources, House Judiciary
- **Timeline**: Months 3-18
- **Lead**: NCAI, NATHPO, tribal nations

#### 1.8 Public Awareness Initiative

- **Action**: Launch coordinated media and education campaign on sacred site issues
- **Platform**: Documentary projects, social media, editorial placements, educational curricula
- **Timeline**: Months 1-24
- **Lead**: Tribal nations, allied organizations

## Phase 2: Legislative Advance (Years 2-4)

### Priority Legislation

#### 2.1 RFRA Sacred Sites Amendment

- **Why first**: Builds on existing bipartisan RFRA framework; narrower scope increases feasibility
- **Content**: Clarify that government destruction of documented sacred sites constitutes substantial burden on religious exercise
- **Strategy**: Introduce in Senate Indian Affairs Committee with bipartisan sponsors; frame as religious freedom legislation
- **Timeline**: Introduction in Year 2; committee action Year 2-3; floor action Year 3-4
- **Lead**: Senate Committee on Indian Affairs
- **Dependencies**: Bipartisan co-sponsors; religious freedom coalition support
- **Risk**: Industry opposition; competing legislative priorities

#### 2.2 THPO Funding Authorization

- **Content**: Authorize $100 million annually for THPOs; $25 million for sacred site survey grants
- **Strategy**: Attach to Interior appropriations or standalone authorization
- **Timeline**: Year 2-3
- **Lead**: House/Senate Appropriations subcommittees on Interior
- **Dependencies**: Budget scoring; appropriations process

#### 2.3 NAGPRA Amendments

- **Content**: Enforceable compliance deadlines, increased penalties, expanded coverage, increased grant funding
- **Strategy**: Bipartisan introduction; build on 2024 final rule momentum
- **Timeline**: Year 2-3
- **Lead**: Senate Indian Affairs Committee
- **Dependencies**: Institutional compliance community response

#### 2.4 Save Oak Flat Act

- **Content**: Repeal Section 3003 of FY 2015 NDAA; designate Oak Flat as protected area
- **Strategy**: Standalone bill with high-visibility advocacy campaign
- **Timeline**: Introduction Year 2; sustained advocacy through passage
- **Lead**: Arizona delegation supporters, House Natural Resources Committee
- **Dependencies**: Overcoming mining industry opposition; bipartisan support

### Longer-Term Legislation

#### 2.5 Sacred Sites Protection Act (Comprehensive)

- **Content**: Enforceable sacred site protections, confidential registry, FPIC requirement, right of action
- **Strategy**: Introduce comprehensive bill to establish legislative record; may require multiple congressional sessions
- **Timeline**: Introduction Year 3; committee hearings Year 3-4; passage Year 4-6
- **Lead**: Senate Indian Affairs Committee, House Natural Resources Committee
- **Dependencies**: RFRA amendment as proof of concept; coalition strength; political alignment

#### 2.6 1872 Mining Law Reform

- **Content**: Cultural resource survey requirements, royalty system, sacred site withdrawal authority
- **Strategy**: Package with broader mining law modernization efforts
- **Timeline**: Year 4-6
- **Lead**: House Natural Resources Committee, Senate Energy and Natural Resources Committee
- **Dependencies**: Mining industry negotiation; Western state delegation support

## Phase 3: Systemic Change (Years 4-8)

### Judicial Strategy

#### 3.1 Treaty-Based Litigation

- **Action**: File treaty-based claims for access to and protection of sacred sites in ceded territories
- **Target circuits**: Courts with strong treaty-rights jurisprudence (9th, 10th Circuits)
- **Timeline**: File Years 4-5; appellate review Years 6-8
- **Lead**: NARF, tribal legal departments
- **Dependencies**: Suitable cases; funding for extended litigation

#### 3.2 RFRA Test Cases

- **Action**: Following legislative amendment, bring RFRA claims under the clarified substantial burden standard
- **Target**: Cases with strong factual records and sympathetic circumstances
- **Timeline**: Years 5-8
- **Lead**: NARF, Earthjustice, tribal legal departments
- **Dependencies**: RFRA amendment passage

### Institutional Development

#### 3.3 National Sacred Sites Commission

- **Action**: Establish a permanent, tribal-majority commission to oversee sacred site identification, protection, and monitoring
- **Structure**: 9-member commission with 5 tribal appointees, 2 presidential appointees, 2 congressional appointees
- **Authority**: Advisory authority on all federal actions affecting registered sacred sites; annual reporting to Congress
- **Timeline**: Authorization Year 4-5; operational Year 6
- **Lead**: Senate Indian Affairs Committee
- **Dependencies**: Comprehensive legislation passage

#### 3.4 International Engagement

- **Action**: Engage with international Indigenous rights frameworks to strengthen domestic protections
- **Content**: Support U.S. implementation of UNDRIP; participate in UN Permanent Forum on Indigenous Issues; exchange models with New Zealand, Australia, Canada, and Nordic countries
- **Timeline**: Ongoing from Year 4
- **Lead**: State Department, NCAI, tribal nations

### Knowledge Preservation

#### 3.5 Sacred Site Knowledge Initiative

- **Action**: Major federally funded, tribally controlled program for sacred site knowledge documentation and intergenerational transfer
- **Funding**: $50 million over 5 years
- **Content**: Elder-youth programs, language revitalization, confidential documentation, inter-tribal coordination
- **Timeline**: Authorization Year 4; operational Years 5-8
- **Lead**: BIA, NPS, tribal nations

## Risk Assessment

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|------------|
| Administration change reverses EO | High | High | Pursue legislative codification in Phase 2 |
| Industry lobbying blocks legislation | High | High | Religious freedom coalition; public pressure; incremental approach |
| Supreme Court further narrows protections | Moderate | Very High | Legislative fixes; avoid unfavorable test cases |
| Insufficient appropriations | High | Moderate | Build broad coalition; demonstrate cost-effectiveness |
| Inter-tribal disagreements on priorities | Moderate | Moderate | NCAI coordination; respect for individual tribal sovereignty |
| Public backlash framed as anti-development | Moderate | Moderate | Economic framing showing benefits of protection; jobs data |
| Confidentiality breach of sacred site data | Low | Very High | Strong data security protocols; tribal data sovereignty |
