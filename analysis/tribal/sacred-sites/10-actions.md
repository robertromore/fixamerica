# Sacred Sites: Actions

## Overview

This document outlines concrete actions that individuals, organizations, and communities can take to support sacred site protection. Actions are organized by effort level and stakeholder type.

## Individual Actions

### Immediate (Low Effort)

1. **Learn about sacred sites in your area**: Research which tribal nations are indigenous to your region and what sacred sites may be present. The Native Land Digital map (<https://native-land.ca>) is a starting point.

2. **Contact your elected representatives**: Call or write your U.S. Senators and Representative to express support for sacred site protections. Key asks:
    - Support the RFRA Sacred Sites Amendment
    - Support the Save Oak Flat Act
    - Increase funding for Tribal Historic Preservation Offices
    - Oppose legislative riders that bypass environmental and cultural review for extractive projects

3. **Sign public comment letters**: When federal agencies open public comment periods on projects affecting sacred sites, submit comments supporting tribal concerns. Federal Register notices and regulations.gov list open comment periods.

4. **Follow and amplify Indigenous voices**: Follow tribal nations, Indigenous journalists, and sacred site advocacy organizations on social media. Amplify their messages rather than speaking on their behalf.

5. **Donate to advocacy organizations**: Financial support for organizations working on sacred site protection:
    - Native American Rights Fund (NARF)
    - Association on American Indian Affairs
    - Apache Stronghold
    - First Nations Development Institute
    - Cultural Survival

### Medium Effort

6. **Attend public meetings**: When BLM, Forest Service, or other agencies hold public meetings on land management plans that affect sacred sites, attend and speak in support of tribal positions.

7. **Educate your community**: Share information about sacred site issues with your faith community, civic organization, or professional association. Many religious communities have adopted resolutions supporting Indigenous religious freedom.

8. **Practice responsible recreation**: When visiting public lands near sacred sites:
    - Respect all access restrictions and closures
    - Do not disturb archaeological sites, artifacts, or offerings
    - Follow posted guidelines for culturally sensitive areas
    - Stay on designated trails
    - Report vandalism or unauthorized digging to land managers

9. **Support tribal businesses and cultural tourism**: When available, participate in tribally operated cultural tourism programs that generate revenue for sacred site protection while respecting cultural protocols.

10. **Write letters to the editor and op-eds**: Respond to news coverage of sacred site issues with letters explaining why protection matters. Connect sacred site protection to broadly shared values like religious freedom, property rights, and honoring commitments.

### Sustained Engagement

11. **Join or support a sacred sites coalition**: Volunteer with organizations working on specific sacred site campaigns (Bears Ears, Oak Flat, Chaco region).

12. **Engage your professional expertise**: Lawyers, archaeologists, environmental scientists, journalists, educators, and other professionals can contribute specialized skills to sacred site advocacy.

13. **Run for local office or serve on advisory boards**: Local planning commissions, county commissions, and advisory committees influence land use decisions that affect sacred sites.

## Organizational Actions

### Faith Communities

- Adopt resolutions supporting Indigenous religious freedom and sacred site protection
- Educate congregations about the shared principle that places of worship deserve protection
- Partner with local tribal nations on interfaith sacred site advocacy
- Participate in amicus briefs supporting sacred site claims in court

### Environmental Organizations

- Integrate sacred site protection into land conservation campaigns
- Support tribal-led monument and conservation area proposals
- Include tribal consultation in organizational environmental advocacy positions
- Advocate for co-management of public lands with tribal nations

### Academic Institutions

- Complete NAGPRA compliance (inventory, consultation, repatriation)
- Establish ethical review protocols for research involving sacred sites
- Fund Indigenous-led research on sacred site protection
- Develop curricula that include Indigenous sacred geography and history
- Partner with THPOs on sacred site documentation projects (following tribal protocols)

### Legal Organizations

- Provide pro bono legal support for sacred site cases
- File amicus briefs in sacred site litigation
- Develop legal scholarship on sacred site protection
- Train tribal attorneys in sacred site law and litigation strategy

### Media Organizations

- Provide sustained, accurate coverage of sacred site issues (not only crisis-driven reporting)
- Center Indigenous voices and perspectives in reporting
- Investigate the gap between consultation requirements and actual outcomes
- Document the cultural and human impacts of sacred site destruction

## State and Local Government Actions

### State Legislatures

- Enact state-level sacred site protection laws supplementing federal protections
- Create state registers of culturally significant sites
- Require state agencies to consult with tribes before actions affecting sacred sites
- Appropriate state funds for tribal cultural resource programs

### Local Governments

- Include tribal cultural resource considerations in local land use planning
- Establish local historic preservation ordinances that recognize sacred sites
- Partner with tribal nations on cultural tourism development
- Oppose proposed developments that would destroy sacred sites within their jurisdictions

## Federal Employee Actions

### Land Management Agency Staff

- Advocate within your agency for stronger consultation practices
- Ensure sacred site information is handled with appropriate confidentiality
- Engage THPOs early in project planning, not after decisions are substantially made
- Document consultation outcomes and tribal concerns thoroughly
- Recommend alternatives that avoid sacred site impacts

### Congressional Staff

- Brief your Member on sacred site issues and pending legislation
- Arrange meetings between tribal leaders and your Member
- Monitor committee activity on sacred site bills
- Ensure tribal testimony is included in relevant hearings

## Tribal Nation Actions

### Government Actions

- Designate Tribal Historic Preservation Officers and support their work
- Document sacred sites using tribally controlled protocols
- Participate in Section 106 consultations and demand meaningful engagement
- Pursue co-management agreements with federal land management agencies
- Build inter-tribal coalitions for shared sacred landscapes
- Assert treaty-based claims to sacred site access and protection

### Community Actions

- Support intergenerational knowledge transfer programs
- Maintain language programs that preserve sacred site terminology and narratives
- Monitor sacred sites for unauthorized disturbance
- Engage tribal youth in sacred site stewardship
- Document oral histories related to sacred sites (with appropriate cultural protocols)
