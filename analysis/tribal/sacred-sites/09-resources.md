# Sacred Sites: Resources

## Key Federal Laws and Regulations

### Statutes

- **American Indian Religious Freedom Act (AIRFA)**, 42 U.S.C. 1996 (1978). Policy statement supporting Native American religious freedom and access to sacred sites.
- **Archaeological Resources Protection Act (ARPA)**, 16 U.S.C. 470aa-470mm (1979). Criminalizes unauthorized excavation or damage to archaeological resources on federal and tribal land.
- **National Environmental Policy Act (NEPA)**, 42 U.S.C. 4321 et seq. (1969). Requires environmental impact assessment for major federal actions, including cultural resource impacts.
- **National Historic Preservation Act (NHPA)**, 54 U.S.C. 300101 et seq. (1966). Establishes Section 106 review process and National Register of Historic Places; Section 304 provides confidentiality for sensitive site information.
- **Native American Graves Protection and Repatriation Act (NAGPRA)**, 25 U.S.C. 3001-3013 (1990). Protects burial sites and requires repatriation of remains and cultural items.
- **Religious Freedom Restoration Act (RFRA)**, 42 U.S.C. 2000bb et seq. (1993). Requires compelling government interest and least restrictive means for substantial burdens on religious exercise.

### Executive Orders

- **Executive Order 13007**, "Indian Sacred Sites" (May 24, 1996). Directs agencies to accommodate access and avoid adversely affecting sacred sites.
- **Executive Order 13175**, "Consultation and Coordination with Indian Tribal Governments" (November 6, 2000). Requires meaningful consultation before actions with tribal implications.
- **Executive Order 14112**, "Reforming Federal Funding and Support for Tribal Nations to Better Embrace Our Trust Responsibilities and Promote the Next Era of Tribal Self-Determination" (December 6, 2023). Strengthens consultation and directs best practices.

### Regulations

- 36 C.F.R. Part 800 (Section 106 regulations issued by ACHP)
- 43 C.F.R. Part 10 (NAGPRA regulations)
- 36 C.F.R. Part 60 (National Register of Historic Places criteria)

## Key Court Cases

### Supreme Court

- *Johnson v. M'Intosh*, 21 U.S. 543 (1823). Established the Doctrine of Discovery in American law.
- *Worcester v. Georgia*, 31 U.S. 515 (1832). Recognized tribal sovereignty and federal-tribal relationship.
- *Lone Wolf v. Hitchcock*, 187 U.S. 553 (1903). Established congressional plenary power over Indian affairs.
- *Lyng v. Northwest Indian Cemetery Protective Association*, 485 U.S. 439 (1988). Held that government may destroy sacred sites without violating the Free Exercise Clause.
- *Employment Division v. Smith*, 490 U.S. 872 (1990). Narrowed Free Exercise protections; motivated RFRA's enactment.
- *City of Boerne v. Flores*, 521 U.S. 507 (1997). Limited RFRA's application to federal, not state, actions.
- *United States v. Sioux Nation*, 448 U.S. 371 (1980). Found illegal taking of the Black Hills; awarded compensation (refused by Sioux).
- *McGirt v. Oklahoma*, 591 U.S. ___ (2020). Affirmed Muscogee (Creek) reservation was never disestablished.
- *Haaland v. Brackeen*, 599 U.S. ___ (2023). Upheld the Indian Child Welfare Act and Congress's Indian Commerce Clause authority.
- *Arizona v. Navajo Nation*, 599 U.S. ___ (2023). Held no affirmative duty to assess Navajo water needs under treaty.
- *Oklahoma v. Castro-Huerta*, 597 U.S. ___ (2022). State concurrent jurisdiction over non-Indians in Indian Country.

### Circuit Courts

- *Navajo Nation v. U.S. Forest Service*, 535 F.3d 1058 (9th Cir. 2008) (en banc). Rejected RFRA challenge to recycled wastewater snow on San Francisco Peaks.
- *Apache Stronghold v. United States*, 38 F.4th 742 (9th Cir. 2022) (en banc). Rejected RFRA challenge to Resolution Copper land exchange destroying Oak Flat.
- *Standing Rock Sioux Tribe v. U.S. Army Corps of Engineers*, 440 F. Supp. 3d 1 (D.D.C. 2020). Found NEPA deficiencies in Dakota Access Pipeline permitting.
- *Badoni v. Higginson*, 638 F.2d 172 (10th Cir. 1980). Rejected First Amendment challenge to operation of Glen Canyon Dam affecting Navajo sacred sites.
- *Crow v. Gullet*, 706 F.2d 856 (8th Cir. 1983). Rejected challenge to Bear Butte development affecting Lakota sacred site.

## International Frameworks

- United Nations Declaration on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples (UNDRIP), adopted 2007. Articles 11, 12, 25, 26, and 32 address sacred sites and Free, Prior, and Informed Consent.
- International Labour Organization Convention 169 (1989). Indigenous and Tribal Peoples Convention.
- *Te Awa Tupua (Whanganui River Claims Settlement) Act 2017* (New Zealand). Legal personhood for a sacred river.
- *Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Heritage Protection Act 1984* (Australia). Federal protection for significant Aboriginal areas.
