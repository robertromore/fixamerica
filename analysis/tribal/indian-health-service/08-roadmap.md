# Indian Health Service: Roadmap

## Overview

This roadmap outlines a phased strategy for transforming Indian health care over a 15-year period. The sequencing prioritizes life-saving behavioral health interventions and workforce stabilization first, builds toward mandatory funding and facility modernization, and culminates in a health system that meets treaty obligations and achieves health equity.

## Phase 1: Crisis Response and Stabilization (Years 1-3)

### Priority: Save Lives and Stabilize Workforce

The first phase addresses the most immediate needs -- the behavioral health crisis taking lives daily and the workforce shortage that undermines every other reform.

### Action Items

| Action | Lead Actor | Supporting Actors | Timeline | Priority |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|----------|----------|
| Fund 988 Tribal Crisis Lifeline and culturally competent crisis response | Congress, SAMHSA | IHS, tribal behavioral health programs | Year 1 | Critical |
| Expand MAT access for opioid and alcohol use disorders in all IHS areas | IHS, tribal programs | SAMHSA, DEA (waiver elimination) | Year 1-2 | Critical |
| Raise IHS health care provider salaries to Medicare-equivalent levels | Congress, IHS | OPM, tribal compact programs | Year 1 | Critical |
| Triple IHS Loan Repayment Program capacity | Congress, IHS | HRSA | Year 1 | High |
| Fund emergency provider housing in highest-need areas | Congress, HUD | IHS, tribal housing authorities | Year 1-2 | High |
| Expand Community Health Aide Program to all IHS areas | IHS, Congress | Tribal health programs, training institutions | Year 1-3 | High |
| Increase Purchased/Referred Care funding by 50% | Congress | IHS | Year 1 | High |
| Begin mandatory funding legislative process | Congress (Senate Indian Affairs) | NCAI, NIHB, tribal leaders | Year 1 (ongoing) | Critical |
| Address most critical CMS accreditation deficiencies | IHS | CMS, tribal health boards | Year 1-2 | High |
| Launch comprehensive behavioral health grant program | Congress, SAMHSA | Tribal behavioral health programs | Year 2 | High |

### Phase 1 Success Criteria

- Crisis intervention available in all 12 IHS areas
- Provider vacancy rate reduced from 25% to 18%
- MAT available in all IHS/tribal hospitals
- Loan Repayment Program serving 3,600+ participants (tripled from ~1,200)
- PRC fund depletion deferred by at least 2 months in each service area
- No IHS facility facing imminent CMS decertification

## Phase 2: Structural Reform (Years 3-7)

### Priority: Mandatory Funding, Facilities, and Self-Governance Expansion

The second phase enacts the fundamental structural changes needed for long-term transformation: mandatory funding, accelerated facility construction, and expanded tribal health management.

### Action Items

| Action | Lead Actor | Supporting Actors | Timeline | Priority |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|----------|----------|
| Enact mandatory IHS funding with needs-based formula | Congress | CBO, tribal consultation, NIHB | Year 3-4 | Critical |
| Begin accelerated facility construction (5 most critical hospitals) | IHS, Congress | Tribal health programs, Army Corps of Engineers | Year 3-5 | Critical |
| Address $14 billion deferred maintenance backlog (phased) | IHS | Congress, tribal facilities | Year 3-7 | High |
| Expand self-governance health compacting to 200+ tribes | IHS, tribal governments | SGCETC, NIHB | Year 3-5 | High |
| Establish multi-tribal health consortia for smaller tribes | Tribal governments | IHS, NIHB | Year 3-5 | High |
| Double Urban Indian Organization programs (41 to 80+) | Congress, IHS | NCUIH, tribal governments | Year 3-5 | High |
| Expand telehealth infrastructure with tribal broadband investment | FCC, USDA, IHS | Tribal utilities, telehealth vendors | Year 3-5 | High |
| Implement tribal health professional pipeline (scholarships, training programs) | IHS, tribal colleges | HRSA, medical schools | Year 3-7 | High |
| Establish tribal health data sovereignty framework | Tribal Epidemiology Centers, IHS | CDC, CMS | Year 4-5 | Medium |
| Develop IHS-VA coordination agreements in all service areas | IHS, VA | Tribal veterans programs | Year 3-5 | Medium |
| Expand Special Diabetes Program for Indians (SDPI) permanently | Congress | IHS, NIHB | Year 3 | High |

### Phase 2 Success Criteria

- IHS reclassified as mandatory spending with 5-year phase-in
- Construction underway on 5 replacement hospitals
- 200+ tribes in self-governance health compacts
- Urban Indian programs doubled
- Provider vacancy rate reduced to 12%
- Deferred maintenance reduced by $5 billion
- Telehealth available in all IHS service areas

## Phase 3: Health Equity (Years 7-15)

### Priority: Closing the Disparity Gap

The third phase sustains the momentum of Phases 1 and 2 to achieve measurable reductions in health disparities, with the goal of bringing AI/AN health outcomes within reach of national averages.

### Action Items

| Action | Lead Actor | Supporting Actors | Timeline | Priority |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|----------|----------|
| Complete all priority list facility replacements | IHS | Congress, tribal programs | Year 7-12 | Critical |
| Achieve full mandatory funding at adequate levels | Congress | NIHB, NCAI | Year 7 (ongoing) | Critical |
| Expand long-term care and elder services in Indian Country | IHS, tribal programs | CMS, ACL | Year 7-10 | High |
| Achieve workforce parity (vacancy rate <8%) | IHS, tribal programs | HRSA, medical schools | Year 7-10 | High |
| Implement comprehensive social determinants strategy | Multi-agency | Tribal governments, HUD, USDA, EPA | Year 7-15 | High |
| Establish Tribal Centers of Excellence for Indigenous health research | NIH, IHS | Tribal colleges, research universities | Year 8-12 | Medium |
| Achieve AI/AN life expectancy within 5 years of national average | All actors | -- | Year 15 | Critical |
| Expand traditional healing integration into all tribal health programs | Tribal programs | IHS, SAMHSA | Year 7-10 | Medium |
| Complete transition to tribal-managed care for all willing tribes | Tribal governments | IHS | Year 10-15 | High |

### Phase 3 Success Criteria

- AI/AN life expectancy within 5 years of national average (target: 73+ years)
- Per-capita IHS/tribal spending at 80%+ of Medicare levels
- All IHS facilities meeting current accreditation standards
- Provider vacancy rate below 8%
- Youth suicide rate reduced to within 1.5x national average
- Urban Indian programs accessible to 500,000+ AI/AN people
- Infant mortality within 1.2x national average

## Risk Mitigation

### Political Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Administration change reverses priorities | High | High | Mandatory funding protects against appropriations reversal; compacts create tribal ownership |
| Mandatory funding fails in Congress | Medium | Very High | Pursue interim advance appropriations for all programs; build case over multiple Congresses |
| State opposition to Medicaid coordination | Medium | Medium | Demonstrate 100% FMAP benefit; engage governors directly |
| Budget sequestration affects IHS | Medium | High | Include sequestration exemption in mandatory funding legislation |

### Implementation Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Workforce cannot be recruited even with better pay | Medium | High | Housing investment, telehealth, rotation models, CHAP expansion |
| Facility construction delays | High | Medium | Pre-approved designs, streamlined environmental review, multiple contractors |
| Tribal capacity for compacting varies | Certain | Medium | Consortium models, technical assistance, phased transition |
| Quality problems persist during transition | Medium | High | Concurrent quality improvement and accreditation support |

### Health Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| New pandemic disproportionately affects AI/AN communities | Medium | Very High | Pandemic preparedness plans for all tribal communities; emergency supply stockpiles |
| Behavioral health crisis worsens before programs take effect | High | High | Emergency crisis intervention in Phase 1; immediate MAT expansion |
| Climate change affects tribal health infrastructure | Certain | Medium-High | Resilient facility design; climate adaptation plans for all service areas |
