# Urban Indians: Roadmap

## Phase 1: Foundation Building (Years 1-3)

### Immediate Legislative Priorities

**Urban Indian Health Parity Act**

- Advocate for passage of legislation establishing UIHP funding parity
- Target: Increase UIHP funding authorization from approximately $100 million to $500 million over 5 years
- Build congressional support through the Native American Caucus and Senate Committee on Indian Affairs
- Develop cost-effectiveness analyses demonstrating long-term savings from preventive care
- Coordinate with NCUIH on legislative strategy

**Census Reform for 2030**

- Advocate for dedicated AI/AN outreach funding in the 2030 Census appropriation
- Partner with Census Bureau to develop urban AI/AN Complete Count Committee strategies
- Support research on reducing racial misclassification of AI/AN respondents
- Develop tribal enrollment data-sharing agreements for Census verification

### Organizational Capacity Building

**Strengthen Urban Indian Organizations**

- Establish core operating funding mechanisms for urban Indian centers (not just project grants)
- Develop leadership training programs for urban Indian organization directors
- Create shared administrative services (accounting, HR, IT) to reduce overhead for small organizations
- Build a national database of urban Indian organizations and services

**Build Advocacy Infrastructure**

- Expand NCUIH and NUIFC staff and capacity
- Develop urban Indian voter registration and civic engagement programs
- Create media strategies to increase visibility of urban Indian issues
- Establish relationships with allied organizations (NAACP, National Urban League, Asian Americans Advancing Justice) for coalition building

### Data and Research

- Commission a comprehensive national survey of urban AI/AN populations
- Fund academic research on urban Indian health, education, and socioeconomic outcomes
- Develop standardized data collection protocols for urban Indian organizations
- Create a publicly accessible urban Indian data dashboard

### Quick Wins

- Executive order directing federal agencies to report on their urban AI/AN service delivery
- IHS administrative expansion of telehealth to serve urban AI/AN populations
- HUD guidance encouraging public housing authorities to identify and serve AI/AN residents
- Department of Education guidance on Title VI eligibility for urban school districts with small AI/AN populations

## Phase 2: System Reform (Years 3-7)

### Major Legislative Action

**Person-Based Trust Responsibility Codification**

- Draft and introduce legislation codifying the principle that the trust responsibility follows individuals
- Seek bipartisan sponsors in both chambers
- Conduct tribal consultation through a formal process
- Navigate intra-Indian politics by demonstrating additive funding approach
- Build support through demonstration projects from Phase 1

**NAHASDA Amendment for Urban Housing**

- Amend NAHASDA to authorize tribally designated housing entities to serve off-reservation members
- Create Urban Indian Housing Block Grant program
- Expand Section 184 loan guarantee program with dedicated outreach
- Target: 10,000 new housing units serving urban AI/AN families

**Education Reform Package**

- Expand Title VI Indian Education eligibility and funding
- Authorize Johnson-O'Malley funding for urban school districts
- Create urban Indian education liaison program
- Fund Native language instruction in 50 largest urban school districts

### Service Delivery Expansion

**UIHP Expansion**

- Establish 20-30 new UIHPs in metropolitan areas currently lacking programs
- Upgrade existing UIHPs from referral-only to comprehensive ambulatory care
- Develop specialized UIHP services: women's health, elder care, pediatrics, behavioral health
- Implement electronic health records interoperable with IHS and tribal health systems

**Cultural Infrastructure**

- Fund construction or renovation of urban Indian cultural centers in 25 major metropolitan areas
- Establish urban language immersion programs for the 20 most critically endangered languages
- Create urban elder-in-residence programs connecting elders with urban youth
- Develop national culturally relevant K-12 curricula available to all urban school districts

### Institutional Development

**Office of Urban Indian Affairs**

- Establish within the Department of the Interior
- Staff with urban Indian policy expertise
- Assign interagency coordination responsibilities
- Create annual reporting requirements on urban AI/AN outcomes

**Urban Indian Advisory Board**

- Create a federally chartered advisory body with representation from urban Indian organizations, tribal governments, and federal agencies
- Charge with recommending policy changes and monitoring implementation
- Ensure representation from diverse geographic regions and tribal backgrounds

## Phase 3: Systemic Transformation (Years 7-15)

### Structural Reform

**Mandatory Funding Transition**

- Transition urban Indian program funding from discretionary to mandatory appropriations
- Establish formula-based funding tied to Census-enumerated urban AI/AN population
- Index funding to inflation and population growth
- Target: Full funding parity between urban and reservation-based per-capita spending by Year 15

**Comprehensive Jurisdictional Reform**

- Enact legislation clarifying federal, state, local, and tribal responsibilities for urban AI/AN populations
- Create intergovernmental compacts for urban Indian service delivery
- Establish urban Indian ombudsman positions in major metropolitan areas
- Develop urban AI/AN community governance structures (advisory, not competing with tribal sovereignty)

### Long-Term Capacity Goals

- All metropolitan areas with 5,000+ AI/AN population have fully operational UIHPs
- Urban Indian cultural centers in every state
- Native language instruction available in 100+ urban school districts
- Urban AI/AN poverty rate reduced to within 5 percentage points of the national average
- Urban AI/AN Census undercount reduced below 1%
- Urban AI/AN homeownership rate increased to 55% (from 47%)
- Urban AI/AN high school completion rate reaches 90% (from 72%)

### Measurement and Accountability

**Key Performance Indicators**

| Indicator | Baseline | Phase 1 Target | Phase 2 Target | Phase 3 Target |
|-----------|----------|----------------|----------------|----------------|
| UIHP funding (millions) | $100 | $250 | $500 | $1,000+ |
| Number of UIHPs | 41 | 50 | 70 | 90+ |
| Urban AI/AN served by UIHPs | 600,000 | 1,000,000 | 2,500,000 | 5,000,000+ |
| Census undercount rate | 5.64% | 4.0% | 2.0% | <1% |
| Urban AI/AN poverty rate | 22.1% | 20% | 18% | 17% |
| High school completion | 72% | 78% | 85% | 90% |

```
Census Reform ──> Accurate Data ──> Formula Funding ──> Adequate Resources
     │                                      │
     └──> Political Visibility ──> Congressional Support ──> Legislative Action
                                            │

                                            │
Tribal Consultation ──> Consensus Framework ──> Person-Based Trust Codification
```

## Risk Assessment

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|------------|
| Congressional inaction | High | High | Build bipartisan support; use administrative action |
| Tribal opposition to urban funding | Moderate | High | Ensure additive funding; tribal consultation |
| Census reform delays | Moderate | High | Advocate for supplemental surveys; alternative data |
| UIHP workforce shortage | High | Moderate | Loan repayment programs; competitive salaries |
| Political deprioritization | High | Very High | Voter engagement; coalition building; media strategy |
