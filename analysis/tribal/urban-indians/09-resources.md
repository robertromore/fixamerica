# Urban Indians: Resources

## Key Federal Agencies

- **Indian Health Service (IHS)** - [ihs.gov](https://www.ihs.gov): Primary federal health agency for AI/AN populations; administers UIHPs
- **Bureau of Indian Affairs (BIA)** - [bia.gov](https://www.bia.gov): Federal agency responsible for trust responsibility administration
- **Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD)** - [hud.gov](https://www.hud.gov): Administers NAHASDA and Section 184 programs
- **Administration for Native Americans (ANA)** - [acf.hhs.gov/ana](https://www.acf.hhs.gov/ana): Grants for Native community development
- **U.S. Census Bureau** - [census.gov](https://www.census.gov): Population enumeration and data

## National Organizations

- **National Council of Urban Indian Health (NCUIH)** - [ncuih.org](https://www.ncuih.org): Advocacy for Urban Indian Health Programs
- **National Urban Indian Family Coalition (NUIFC)** - [nuifc.org](https://www.nuifc.org): Network of urban Indian organizations
- **National Congress of American Indians (NCAI)** - [ncai.org](https://www.ncai.org): Largest national AI/AN advocacy organization
- **National Indian Health Board (NIHB)** - [nihb.org](https://www.nihb.org): Advocates for AI/AN healthcare
- **National Indian Education Association (NIEA)** - [niea.org](https://www.niea.org): Advocates for AI/AN education

## Key Legislation

### Current Law

- **Indian Health Care Improvement Act** (25 U.S.C. ch. 18): Authorizes IHS and UIHP services; permanently reauthorized by ACA in 2010
- **Indian Self-Determination and Education Assistance Act** (25 U.S.C. § 5301 et seq.): Authorizes tribal contracting and compacting with federal agencies
- **Native American Housing Assistance and Self-Determination Act (NAHASDA)** (25 U.S.C. § 4101 et seq.): Housing block grants for tribal housing entities
- **Indian Child Welfare Act (ICWA)** (25 U.S.C. § 1901 et seq.): Protections for AI/AN children in child welfare proceedings, including urban settings
- **Native American Languages Act** (25 U.S.C. § 2901 et seq.): Federal policy to preserve and protect Native languages
- **Section 184 Indian Home Loan Guarantee Program** (12 U.S.C. § 1715z-13a): Mortgage guarantee for AI/AN borrowers

### Pending Legislation (as of 2025)

- **Urban Indian Health Parity Act**: Would establish UIHP funding parity
- **Advancing Health Equity for Native Americans Act**: Would increase UIHP funding authorizations
- **Native Act** (Native American Tourism and Improving Visitor Experience Act): Economic development provisions with urban components

## Key Court Cases

- *Morton v. Mancari*, 417 U.S. 535 (1974): Federal Indian preference in employment is political, not racial classification
- *Brackeen v. Haaland*, 599 U.S. 255 (2023): Upheld ICWA, affirming Congress's plenary power and the political classification of Indian status
- *Students for Fair Admissions v. Harvard*, 600 U.S. 181 (2023): Restricted race-based affirmative action but did not address tribal political classifications
- *Oklahoma v. Castro-Huerta*, 597 U.S. 629 (2022): State criminal jurisdiction over non-Indians on tribal land; implications for jurisdiction off-reservation

## Data Sources

- **U.S. Census Bureau American Community Survey**: Annual demographic and socioeconomic data
- **IHS National Data Warehouse**: Health service utilization data (limited public access)
- **Urban Indian Health Institute (UIHI)**: Seattle-based research center affiliated with Seattle Indian Health Board
- **CDC Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System**: Health behavior data (AI/AN oversampling in some states)
- **BLS Current Population Survey**: Employment data (limited AI/AN sample size)
