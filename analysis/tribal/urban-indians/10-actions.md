# Urban Indians: Actions

## Overview

This document provides actionable steps for citizens, advocates, organizations, and policymakers who want to improve conditions for urban American Indians and Alaska Natives. Actions are organized by the level of commitment and resources required.

## Individual Actions

### Learn

- **Understand the history**: Read about the Indian Relocation Act and its consequences for urban Indian communities
- **Know your local urban Indian community**: Research which urban Indian organizations operate in your city or metropolitan area
- **Learn about the trust responsibility**: Understand that the federal obligation to AI/AN people extends beyond reservation boundaries
- **Follow urban Indian issues**: Read coverage from Indian Country Today, NCUIH reports, and local Native media
- **Challenge stereotypes**: Recognize that the majority of Native Americans live in urban areas, not on reservations

### Support Locally

- **Donate to urban Indian organizations**: Urban Indian centers, UIHPs, and cultural organizations in your area rely on private contributions
- **Volunteer**: Many urban Indian organizations need volunteers for events, mentoring, tutoring, and administrative support
- **Attend public events**: Powwows, art exhibitions, cultural festivals, and community gatherings organized by urban Indian groups
- **Shop at Native-owned businesses**: Support urban Native entrepreneurs and artists
- **Mentor**: If you have professional skills, offer mentoring to urban Native youth and young professionals

### Advocate

- **Contact your elected officials**: Urge support for the Urban Indian Health Parity Act and increased UIHP funding
- **Participate in Census outreach**: Help ensure accurate enumeration of AI/AN populations in your area
- **Support school board initiatives**: Advocate for culturally relevant curricula and AI/AN education liaisons in your school district
- **Write letters to the editor**: Raise visibility of urban Indian issues in local media
- **Attend city council and county board meetings**: Advocate for municipal recognition and inclusion of urban Indian populations in city planning

## Organizational Actions

### For Urban Indian Organizations

- **Build coalitions**: Partner with other underserved community organizations for shared advocacy
- **Strengthen data collection**: Systematically document the populations you serve and the unmet need in your service area
- **Develop board capacity**: Recruit board members with fundraising, legal, and policy expertise
- **Pursue diversified funding**: Reduce dependence on any single funding source by cultivating federal, state, local, and private support
- **Engage in policy advocacy**: Testify before Congress, state legislatures, and local governing bodies
- **Participate in Census planning**: Join or organize Complete Count Committees for the 2030 Census

### For Tribal Governments

- **Serve off-reservation members**: Explore mechanisms to extend tribal services to enrolled members living in urban areas
- **Support urban Indian organizations**: Partner with and provide resources to urban organizations serving your tribal members
- **Share data**: Work with Census Bureau and federal agencies on data-sharing that improves urban AI/AN enumeration without compromising tribal data sovereignty
- **Participate in urban policy advocacy**: Support federal legislation that increases resources for urban Indians without diminishing tribal funding

### For Healthcare Organizations

- **Partner with UIHPs**: Establish referral networks, shared care agreements, and collaborative programs
- **Train culturally competent providers**: Include AI/AN cultural competency in medical education and continuing education
- **Support Medicaid enrollment**: Help urban AI/AN individuals navigate Medicaid eligibility and enrollment
- **Address health disparities**: Include urban AI/AN populations in health equity initiatives and community health needs assessments

### For Educational Institutions

- **Recruit AI/AN students**: Develop targeted recruitment and retention programs for urban AI/AN students
- **Support Native studies programs**: Establish or expand academic programs in American Indian and Indigenous studies
- **Partner with urban Indian education programs**: Collaborate on tutoring, mentoring, and college readiness
- **Conduct research**: Prioritize research on urban Indian issues with community-based participatory methods
- **Hire Native faculty and staff**: Increase AI/AN representation in academic institutions

## Policy Actions

### Federal Level

- **Congress**: Pass the Urban Indian Health Parity Act, increase UIHP appropriations, codify person-based trust responsibility
- **IHS**: Expand telehealth services to urban AI/AN populations; increase UIHP administrative support
- **BIA**: Establish an Office of Urban Indian Affairs; develop urban Indian service standards
- **HUD**: Amend NAHASDA to allow off-reservation service delivery by tribally designated housing entities; expand Section 184 outreach
- **Department of Education**: Lower Title VI eligibility thresholds for urban districts; fund urban AI/AN education liaisons
- **Census Bureau**: Dedicate resources to reducing AI/AN undercount in 2030 Census; improve racial classification methodology
- **Executive Branch**: Issue executive order directing interagency coordination on urban Indian services

### State Level

- **Medicaid**: Ensure AI/AN individuals receive 100% Federal Medical Assistance Percentage (FMAP) reimbursement regardless of whether services are received at IHS, tribal, or urban facilities
- **Education**: Require Native American history in K-12 curricula; support Native language instruction
- **Housing**: Include AI/AN populations in state housing plans; fund urban Indian housing programs
- **Health**: Include UIHPs in state health planning and Medicaid managed care networks
- **Data**: Include AI/AN demographic breakdowns in state data reports

### Local Level

- **City government**: Recognize and include urban Indian communities in city planning, public health, and social service delivery
- **School districts**: Hire AI/AN education coordinators; adopt culturally responsive curricula; partner with urban Indian education organizations
- **Police departments**: Develop cultural competency training regarding AI/AN populations; build relationships with urban Indian communities
- **Libraries and parks**: Host AI/AN cultural programming; partner with urban Indian organizations
- **Public transit**: Ensure UIHP and urban Indian center locations are accessible by public transportation
