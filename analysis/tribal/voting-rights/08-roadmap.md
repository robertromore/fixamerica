# Native American Voting Rights: Implementation Roadmap

## Strategic Framework

Securing equal voting access for Native Americans requires a sequenced strategy that combines immediate administrative action, targeted legislation, and long-term structural reform. The roadmap accounts for political realities: the narrow margins for voting rights legislation in Congress, the role of the courts in shaping enforcement standards, and the need to build coalition support across ideological lines. Because many Native voting barriers can be addressed through executive action and state-level reform, the strategy does not depend entirely on federal legislation.

## Phase 1: Administrative and Executive Action (Years 1-2)

### Objective

Implement reforms that do not require legislation, using existing federal authority and tribal-state cooperation to address the most acute barriers.

### Actions

| Action | Mechanism | Lead Actor | Timeline |
|--------|-----------|------------|----------|
| Issue executive order on tribal voter access | Executive authority | President | Year 1 |
| Direct EAC to develop model non-traditional address standards | Existing EAC authority | Election Assistance Commission | Year 1 |
| Increase DOJ Voting Section staffing for Native cases | Budget request | Attorney General | Year 1 |
| Establish interagency tribal voter access task force | Executive coordination | White House Domestic Policy Council | Year 1 |
| Negotiate tribal-state satellite voting agreements | Government-to-government consultation | Tribal leaders, state election officials | Years 1-2 |
| Fund reservation addressing through existing USDA/BIA programs | Grant reallocation | BIA, USDA Rural Development | Years 1-2 |
| Issue DOJ guidance on tribal ID acceptance under HAVA | Regulatory guidance | DOJ Voting Section | Year 1 |
| Publish Section 203 compliance toolkit for AI/AN languages | Administrative guidance | DOJ, EAC | Year 2 |

### Success Metrics

- At least 10 new tribal-state satellite voting agreements signed
- DOJ Voting Section staffed with at least 2 additional attorneys dedicated to Native voting cases
- EAC non-traditional address standards published and adopted by at least 5 states
- Interagency task force operational with participation from DOJ, EAC, BIA, IHS, and USDA

### Political Strategy

- Frame as election administration improvement, not partisan voting rights expansion
- Emphasize cost-effectiveness of satellite voting compared to Section 2 litigation
- Engage tribal leaders as government-to-government partners, not interest group advocates
- Highlight bipartisan support for tribal ID acceptance in states like Montana

## Phase 2: Targeted Federal Legislation (Years 2-4)

### Objective

Pass legislation addressing the most specific and defensible Native voting barriers: ID acceptance, addressing, polling place access, and ballot collection.

### Priority Legislation

#### Native American Voting Rights Act (NAVRA)

- **Vehicle**: Standalone bill or inclusion in a broader voting rights package
- **Strategy**: Focus on provisions with the broadest coalition support -- tribal ID acceptance, satellite voting, language access -- while including ballot collection as a reservation-specific accommodation
- **Coalition**: NCAI, NARF, Four Directions, tribal governments, civil rights organizations, bipartisan co-sponsors from states with significant AI/AN populations
- **Opposition management**: Distinguish reservation ballot collection (addressing unique geographic barriers) from general ballot harvesting to reduce Republican opposition; include chain-of-custody requirements to address fraud concerns

#### John R. Lewis Voting Rights Advancement Act

- **Strategy**: Include Native-specific provisions in the broader preclearance restoration
- **Key provisions for Native voters**: Preclearance for polling place closures in counties with reservation land; preclearance for voter ID changes in states with tribal populations; Native-specific triggers in the coverage formula
- **Coalition building**: Connect Native voting rights to broader VRA restoration coalition

### Stakeholder Engagement

| Stakeholder | Engagement Strategy | Concessions |
|-------------|--------------------|----|
| Republican moderates | Focus on tribal ID acceptance and satellite voting as election integrity measures | Include chain-of-custody requirements for ballot collection |
| State election officials | Provide federal funding for compliance costs | Allow phased implementation; provide technical assistance |
| Tribal governments | Government-to-government consultation on all provisions | Ensure tribal administrative capacity through funding |
| Civil rights organizations | Integrate Native provisions into broader VRA restoration | Support broader voting rights agenda |
| County election officials | Federal cost reimbursement for satellite sites | Address legitimate resource concerns through funding |

### Success Metrics

- NAVRA enacted with core provisions (tribal ID, addressing, satellite voting)
- Lewis Act passed with enhanced Native-specific provisions
- Federal funding appropriated for satellite voting and language access
- At least 50 new satellite voting sites established on reservations nationwide

## Phase 3: Structural Reform (Years 4-7)

### Objective

Address deeper structural barriers: redistricting, Section 2 enforcement, reservation addressing infrastructure, and sustained civic engagement funding.

### Legislative Override of *Brnovich*

**Implementation sequence**:

1. **Year 4**: Introduce legislation restoring pre-*Brnovich* Section 2 standard
2. **Year 5**: Build coalition for passage as part of VRA modernization
3. **Year 5-6**: Enact Section 2 restoration with Native-specific provisions

**Key provisions**:

- Eliminate the "total opportunities" framework
- Establish that significant disparate impact on Native voters suffices for a Section 2 violation
- Provide attorneys' fees for successful Native voting rights plaintiffs
- Create DOJ Native Voting Rights Enforcement Unit

### National Reservation Addressing Initiative

| Phase | Year | Actions |
|-------|------|---------|
| Pilot | Year 4 | Fund addressing projects on 20 reservations in partnership with tribal governments |
| Expansion | Year 5-6 | Scale to all reservations with unaddressed homes; integrate with USPS and 911 systems |
| Completion | Year 7 | All reservation homes with standard addresses; voter registration systems updated |

### Redistricting Reforms

| Reform | Year | Prerequisites |
|--------|------|---------------|
| Federal tribal communities-of-interest requirement | Year 5 | NAVRA enacted; redistricting cycle data available |
| Independent redistricting commission mandate | Year 6-7 | Constitutional viability assessed; state models evaluated |
| Tribal representation on redistricting bodies | Year 5 | State-level implementation where feasible |

### Success Metrics

- *Brnovich* standard legislatively overridden
- Reservation addressing projects completed on at least 100 reservations
- Tribal communities of interest protected in next redistricting cycle
- At least 5 states with independent redistricting commissions including tribal representation

## Phase 4: Systemic Integration and Sustainability (Years 7-10)

### Objective

Ensure reforms are sustained, adequately funded, and producing measurable results in registration, turnout, and representation.

### Actions

| Action | Timeline | Mechanism |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| Comprehensive assessment of Native voter registration and turnout | Year 8 | GAO study with tribal participation |
| Evaluate NAVRA implementation and identify gaps | Year 8 | DOJ and EAC joint report |
| Assess redistricting outcomes for Native communities | Year 9 | Census Bureau and redistricting commission data |
| Establish permanent tribal voter data collection program | Year 9 | Census Bureau, in partnership with NCAI |
| Reauthorize and expand NAVRA funding | Year 10 | Congressional reauthorization |

### Long-Term Goals

- Native voter registration rates within 3 percentage points of the general population
- Native voter turnout within 5 percentage points of the state average in every state with significant AI/AN populations
- Native representation in state legislatures roughly proportional to state AI/AN population
- All reservations with adequate polling place access, addressing, and language assistance
- Sustained federal funding for tribal civic engagement and election administration
- Effective federal enforcement mechanisms preventing discriminatory election changes

## Risk Assessment

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| NAVRA fails in Congress | Medium-High | Very High | Pursue individual provisions through state legislation and executive action |
| Lewis Act fails in Congress | High | High | Focus on state-level preclearance and enhanced Section 2 litigation |
| Courts strike down ballot collection provisions | Medium | Medium | Design chain-of-custody requirements that survive judicial scrutiny |
| States resist tribal ID acceptance | Medium | High | Federal funding incentives; DOJ enforcement actions |
| Appropriations fall short of authorization | High | Medium | Prioritize highest-impact programs; seek mandatory appropriations |
| Reservation addressing projects delayed | Medium | Medium | Phase implementation by population density; prioritize high-impact reservations |
| Tribal civic engagement funding cut | Medium | Medium | Build endowment model for sustainable funding; diversify funding sources |

## State-Level Action Map

State-level reform can proceed independently of federal action:

| State Action | Status | Impact |
|-------------|--------|--------|
| Accept tribal IDs for voting | Enacted in MT, MN, OK, others; pending elsewhere | High -- directly addresses ID barriers |
| Same-day voter registration | Enacted in MT, MN, CO, others | High -- disproportionately benefits Native voters |
| Satellite early voting on reservations | Partial in AZ, NM; expanding | Very High -- reduces distance barriers |
| State-level ballot collection for reservations | Variable | High -- addresses distance barriers |
| Tribal communities of interest in redistricting | Partial in several states | High -- prevents vote dilution |
| Non-traditional address acceptance | Emerging in ND after litigation | High -- addresses registration barriers |

## Coalition Map

### Core Coalition

- National Congress of American Indians
- Native American Rights Fund
- Four Directions
- National Native Vote organizations
- Individual tribal governments in key states
- Civil rights organizations (NAACP LDF, ACLU, Brennan Center)

### Potential Allies

- State election officials seeking federal funding for compliance
- Good-government organizations supporting election administration reform
- Rural advocacy organizations facing similar distance and access barriers
- Veterans organizations (Native veteran voter access)
- Academic institutions conducting voting rights research

### Persuadable Opposition

- Moderate Republican members from states with tribal populations (tribal ID acceptance)
- County election officials (with federal cost reimbursement)
- Election security advocates (with chain-of-custody provisions for ballot collection)

### Likely Persistent Opposition

- State legislatures committed to restrictive voter ID laws
- Election security organizations opposing any ballot collection
- Anti-VRA legal movement opposing federal election mandates
- Officials benefiting from low Native voter turnout
