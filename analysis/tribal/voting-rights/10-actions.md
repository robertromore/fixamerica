# Native American Voting Rights: Actions

## Overview

This document outlines practical steps that citizens, organizations, and officials can take to protect and expand Native American voting access. Actions are organized by the actor's position and capacity, from individual citizens to elected officials. Because Native voting barriers operate at federal, state, and local levels, effective action requires engagement at every level of government.

## Individual Citizens

### Learn and Educate

- **Understand the barriers**: Native voters face a unique combination of distance, addressing, ID, language, and redistricting obstacles that are unlike those facing any other population
- **Study the history**: From the Indian Citizenship Act of 1924 through *Shelby County* (2013) and *Brnovich* (2021), the legal landscape has shifted dramatically -- understand why current protections are inadequate
- **Challenge misconceptions**: Native voting barriers are not about individual failure to obtain IDs or register -- they result from federal policies that isolated communities and state systems designed without regard for reservation realities
- **Follow current litigation**: Track cases like *Brakebill v. Jaeger* (North Dakota voter ID) and redistricting challenges in South Dakota and Alaska through NARF's voting rights database

### Contact Elected Officials

- **Write to your congressional representatives** urging support for:
    - The Native American Voting Rights Act (NAVRA)
    - The John R. Lewis Voting Rights Advancement Act
    - Federal funding for satellite voting locations on reservations
    - Tribal ID acceptance in all states
- **Contact your state legislators** urging:
    - State acceptance of tribal IDs for voting
    - Same-day voter registration
    - Satellite early voting locations on reservations
    - Non-traditional address acceptance in voter registration systems
- **Attend town halls** and raise Native voting access as an issue, even in states without significant AI/AN populations -- federal legislation requires national support
- **Register for committee hearing notifications** from the Senate Committee on Indian Affairs and Senate Judiciary Committee

### Support Affected Communities

- **Donate to** organizations working on Native voting access:
    - Native American Rights Fund (NARF): <https://www.narf.org>
    - Four Directions: <https://www.fourdirectionsvote.com>
    - National Congress of American Indians: <https://www.ncai.org>
    - First Nations Development Institute: <https://www.firstnations.org>
- **Volunteer with** voter registration drives on or near reservations during election season
- **Provide transportation** to polling places on election day through organized ride-to-vote programs
- **Serve as an election observer** in jurisdictions with histories of Native voting discrimination

### Vote and Advocate

- **Vote for candidates** at all levels who support voting rights expansion and Native-specific voting access reforms
- **Sign petitions** supporting NAVRA and VRA restoration
- **Share information** about Native voting barriers in community discussions, social media, and local media
- **Amplify Native voices** on voting rights by sharing reporting and advocacy from Native-led organizations

## Native American Voters and Communities

### Exercise the Right to Vote

- **Register to vote**: Contact your tribal enrollment office, county election office, or a voter registration organization for assistance
- **Know your rights**: You have the right to language assistance at the polls in Section 203-covered jurisdictions, the right to use tribal ID where accepted, and the right to assistance from a person of your choice (except your employer or union representative)
- **Report barriers**: Document any barriers you encounter -- long distances, rejected IDs, language access failures, intimidation -- and report them to NARF, Four Directions, or the DOJ Voting Section (1-800-253-3931)
- **Vote early** where available to avoid election day complications

### Build Community Engagement

- **Organize voter registration drives** in your community, at tribal events, and at tribal colleges
- **Create ride-to-vote programs** using community vehicles, tribal transportation, and volunteer drivers
- **Establish community ballot collection** where legally permitted, with proper chain-of-custody documentation
- **Host candidate forums** where candidates must address issues important to the tribal community
- **Engage youth** through tribal college civic engagement programs and intergenerational dialogue about voting

### Work with Tribal Government

- **Advocate for tribal-state voting agreements** that establish satellite polling locations, registration sites, and early voting on tribal lands
- **Request tribal ID improvements** that meet state voter ID requirements (residential address, expiration date) where needed
- **Support tribal voter education programs** funded through tribal budgets or federal grants
- **Encourage tribal leadership** to make voting access a government-to-government priority with state officials

## Community Organizations

### Build Coalitions

- **Partner with** tribal organizations, civil rights groups, and election administration organizations to build voting access coalitions
- **Connect with rural advocacy organizations** that share concerns about distance barriers, limited polling places, and mail service inadequacy
- **Engage faith communities** in voting access as a moral issue
- **Coordinate with academic institutions** conducting voting rights research

### Provide Direct Support

- **Pro bono legal services**: Lawyers can assist with Section 2 challenges, voter ID litigation, and redistricting cases
- **Transportation coordination**: Organizations with vehicles or volunteer networks can organize election day transportation
- **Translation and interpretation**: Bilingual community members can serve as poll interpreters
- **Technology assistance**: Help tribal communities establish online voter registration portals and election information websites

### Advocate for Policy Change

- **Submit organizational comments** during state rulemaking on voter ID, registration procedures, and polling place decisions
- **Endorse NAVRA and the Lewis Act** and communicate endorsements to Congress
- **Publish op-eds and reports** documenting Native voting barriers in specific jurisdictions
- **File amicus briefs** in voting rights litigation affecting Native communities
- **Monitor election administration** in counties with reservation land and report disparities

## Legal Professionals

### Provide Legal Support

- **Pro bono representation** for tribal communities in Section 2 voting rights cases
- **Amicus briefs** in Native voting rights litigation, including redistricting and voter ID challenges
- **Legal research** on state-by-state tribal ID acceptance requirements and non-traditional address standards
- **Litigation** challenging polling place closures, redistricting plans, and voter ID laws that disproportionately burden Native voters

### Policy Advocacy

- **Draft model state legislation** for tribal ID acceptance and non-traditional address accommodation
- **Provide expert testimony** at congressional hearings on NAVRA and VRA restoration
- **Publish legal scholarship** analyzing the impact of *Shelby County* and *Brnovich* on Native voting rights
- **Train tribal attorneys** on voting rights enforcement and election law

## Academics and Researchers

### Research Contributions

- **Conduct turnout studies** using ecological inference and survey methods to produce accurate AI/AN registration and turnout data, which most states do not track by race
- **Analyze redistricting impacts** on Native voting strength using racially polarized voting analysis
- **Study the effects** of voter ID laws, ballot collection bans, and polling place changes on Native voter participation
- **Document language access compliance** in Section 203-covered jurisdictions
- **Develop non-traditional addressing methodologies** that work with voter registration databases

### Expert Services

- **Serve as expert witnesses** in Section 2 litigation (racially polarized voting analysis, historical testimony)
- **Provide peer review** of redistricting plans for their impact on Native voting strength
- **Train tribal communities** in election monitoring and data collection
- **Partner with tribal colleges** on civic engagement research and programming

## Elected Officials

### Federal Level

- **Introduce or cosponsor** NAVRA and the John R. Lewis Voting Rights Advancement Act
- **Request increased appropriations** for DOJ Voting Section enforcement and EAC tribal election assistance
- **Hold oversight hearings** on Native voting barriers, Section 203 compliance, and post-*Shelby County* discrimination
- **Request GAO studies** of Native voter registration, turnout, and election administration disparities
- **Support federal funding** for reservation addressing projects and satellite voting infrastructure

### State Level

- **Pass tribal ID acceptance laws** that accept all federally recognized tribal government-issued identification
- **Enact same-day voter registration** which disproportionately benefits Native voters
- **Require satellite early voting locations** on reservations above population thresholds
- **Accept non-traditional addresses** in voter registration systems
- **Mandate tribal consultation** before any election administration change affecting reservation communities
- **Require redistricting processes** to treat reservations and tribal communities as communities of interest

### Local Level

- **Establish satellite polling places** on reservations within your county, with hours and staffing comparable to other polling locations
- **Recruit and train poll workers** from reservation communities
- **Coordinate with tribal governments** on voter registration drives and election logistics
- **Ensure Section 203 compliance** by providing language assistance at all covered polling places
- **Allocate election resources** proportionally to serve reservation communities
