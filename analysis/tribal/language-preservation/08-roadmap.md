# Language Preservation: Roadmap

## Overview

This roadmap outlines a phased strategy for reversing Indigenous language decline in the United States. The timeline reflects the extraordinary urgency of the crisis: with the majority of fluent speakers over 60 years of age, the window for many languages is measured in years, not decades. The roadmap prioritizes immediate emergency actions to capture elder knowledge and stabilize the most endangered languages, followed by sustained institutional development and systemic policy reform.

## Guiding Principles

- **Community sovereignty**: Tribal nations determine their own language priorities, methods, and pace
- **Urgency proportional to endangerment**: Languages with the fewest speakers and oldest remaining speakers receive priority
- **Federal responsibility**: The federal government bears primary financial responsibility given its role in causing language loss
- **Both documentation and revitalization**: Preserving records and producing new speakers are complementary, not competing, goals
- **Sustainability**: Programs must be designed for long-term operation, not dependent on short-term grant cycles

## Phase 1: Emergency Stabilization (Years 1-3)

### Objective

Prevent further language extinction and secure the knowledge of aging fluent speakers.

### Legislative Actions

| Action | Timeline | Lead |
|--------|----------|------|
| Pass Durbin Feeling Native American Languages Act | Year 1 | Congress (Senate Indian Affairs Committee) |
| Reauthorize and expand Esther Martinez Act with increased funding ($50 million) | Year 1 | Congress |
| Direct BIE to integrate language instruction in all schools where communities request it | Year 1 | Department of the Interior |
| Issue executive order on Indigenous language preservation as trust responsibility | Year 1 | President |

### Program Actions

| Action | Timeline | Lead | Estimated Cost |
|--------|----------|------|---------------|
| Launch Emergency Language Keeper Program (stipends for elder speakers) | Months 1-6 | ANA/BIA | $10 million/year |
| Fund 30 emergency master-apprentice programs for critically endangered languages | Months 3-12 | ANA | $7.5 million/year |
| Commission national survey of Indigenous language vitality | Months 1-12 | ANA/NSF | $3 million |
| Expand ANA language grant funding to $50 million/year | Year 1 budget | OMB/Congress | $34.3 million increase |
| Fund 20 new language nest (pre-K immersion) programs | Years 1-3 | ANA | $10 million/year |
| Establish tribal language data sovereignty protocols | Year 1 | ANA/tribal consultation | $1 million |

### Administrative Actions

| Action | Timeline | Lead |
|--------|----------|------|
| Streamline ANA grant application process | Year 1 | HHS/ANA |
| Extend grant duration from 3 years to 5 years | Year 1 | ANA |
| Establish dedicated ANA language program office | Year 1 | HHS |
| Begin federal-state consultation on teacher certification | Year 1 | Department of Education |

### Milestones

- [ ] National language vitality survey completed
- [ ] Emergency Language Keeper Program operating in at least 50 communities
- [ ] 30 critically endangered languages receiving emergency support
- [ ] ANA language funding doubled to at least $30 million
- [ ] 20 new language nest programs launched

## Phase 2: Infrastructure Building (Years 3-7)

### Objective

Build the institutional infrastructure necessary for sustained language revitalization across all Indigenous language communities.

### Legislative Actions

| Action | Timeline | Lead |
|--------|----------|------|
| Pass National Indigenous Language Preservation Act (comprehensive legislation) | Year 3-4 | Congress |
| Create formula-based language funding at $100 million/year | Year 4 | Congress |
| Establish National Indigenous Language Preservation Trust ($5 billion endowment) | Year 4-5 | Congress |
| Amend ESSA to explicitly protect Indigenous language immersion | Year 3-4 | Congress |

### Program Actions

| Action | Timeline | Lead | Estimated Cost |
|--------|----------|------|---------------|
| Fund 50 new immersion programs (schools and language nests) | Years 3-7 | ANA/Trust | $40 million/year |
| Train 300 new Indigenous language teachers | Years 3-7 | BIE/universities/tribal colleges | $25 million/year |
| Develop comprehensive K-12 curricula for 30 languages | Years 3-7 | Tribal nations/BIE | $15 million/year |
| Launch urban Indigenous language centers in 20 cities | Years 3-5 | ANA/urban Indian organizations | $10 million/year |
| Fund community-controlled language technology development | Years 3-7 | ANA/NSF | $15 million/year |
| Establish language teacher training programs at 15 tribal colleges | Years 3-5 | AIHEC/BIE | $10 million/year |

### State-Level Actions

| Action | Timeline | Target States |
|--------|----------|--------------|
| Enact Indigenous language teacher certification pathways | Years 3-5 | All states with significant Indigenous populations (start with AZ, NM, OK, MT, AK, WA, SD, MN) |
| Create state matching fund programs for language preservation | Years 3-5 | Model: New Mexico (2024), Montana |
| Modify testing requirements to accommodate immersion schools | Years 3-5 | All states with immersion programs |

### Milestones

- [ ] National Indigenous Language Preservation Act enacted
- [ ] Formula-based funding reaching all eligible tribes
- [ ] Language Preservation Trust capitalized and distributing funds
- [ ] 80+ immersion programs operating nationally (up from ~30)
- [ ] 300+ new certified Indigenous language teachers
- [ ] Curricula available for 30+ languages
- [ ] 20 urban language centers operational
- [ ] 15 tribal colleges offering language teacher training

## Phase 3: Systemic Transformation (Years 7-15)

### Objective

Achieve sustainable, community-driven language revitalization that stabilizes all viable Indigenous languages and creates conditions for long-term recovery.

### Program Actions

| Action | Timeline | Lead | Estimated Cost |
|--------|----------|------|---------------|
| Expand to 150+ immersion programs nationally | Years 7-15 | Trust/tribal nations | $60 million/year |
| Achieve 1,000+ certified Indigenous language teachers | Years 7-15 | States/tribal colleges | Ongoing |
| Full K-12 curricula available for 60+ languages | Years 7-15 | Tribal nations/BIE | Ongoing |
| Indigenous language media ecosystem (radio, TV, digital, social media) | Years 7-15 | Tribal nations/FCC | $20 million/year |
| AI-assisted language learning tools for 50+ languages | Years 7-15 | Tribal tech initiatives | $10 million/year |
| Intergenerational language transmission restored in 50+ communities | Years 7-15 | Community-led | Ongoing |

### Institutional Actions

| Action | Timeline | Lead |
|--------|----------|------|
| Establish Office of Indigenous Languages within the Department of the Interior | Year 7 | Congress/Interior |
| Create national Indigenous language teacher certification standards | Year 8 | Department of Education/tribal consultation |
| Integrate Indigenous language instruction into all BIE schools | Year 10 | BIE |
| Expand tribal college language programs to all 37 institutions | Year 10 | AIHEC/BIE |

### Long-Term Goals

| Goal | Target Year | Metric |
|------|-------------|--------|
| No additional language extinctions | Year 5 onward | Zero languages lost after program implementation |
| Double number of speakers for 50+ languages | Year 15 | Speaker census data |
| Intergenerational transmission restored for 30+ languages | Year 15 | Children acquiring language at home |
| 150+ immersion programs operating | Year 15 | Program count |
| 1,000+ certified teachers | Year 15 | Certification records |
| Indigenous language use in tribal governance | Year 10 | Tribal government proceedings |

### Milestones

- [ ] Office of Indigenous Languages established
- [ ] 150+ immersion programs operating
- [ ] 1,000+ certified language teachers
- [ ] Intergenerational transmission restored in 30+ communities
- [ ] No languages extinct after program implementation
- [ ] Indigenous language media broadly available
- [ ] AI tools available for 50+ languages

## Risk Factors

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Inadequate congressional appropriations | High | Critical | Build bipartisan coalition; emphasize trust responsibility and boarding school history |
| Elder speaker loss before programs launch | High | Critical | Prioritize emergency documentation and Language Keeper stipends |
| Administration change reverses policy | Moderate | High | Enshrine commitments in statute, not executive orders; establish Trust as independent entity |
| Community capacity limitations | Moderate | Moderate | Provide technical assistance; fund tribal college training programs |
| Technology misuse or data sovereignty violations | Low-Moderate | Moderate | Establish clear data sovereignty protocols before technology deployment |
| State resistance to certification reform | Moderate | Moderate | Tie federal education funding to certification pathway creation |
