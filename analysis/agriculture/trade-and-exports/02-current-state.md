# Trade & Exports: Current State

## Overview

U.S. agricultural exports remain strong but face intensifying global competition, persistent trade barriers, and growing concentration risks. Annual exports range from $170 billion to $200 billion, making agriculture one of the few sectors maintaining a trade surplus. However, export growth depends on reliable port capacity, market access agreements, consistent SPS compliance, and finance tools that remain inaccessible to many small and midsize exporters. Rising competition from Brazil, Argentina, and Black Sea producers, combined with geopolitical volatility, creates ongoing uncertainty.

### Market Access Barriers

| Barrier Type | Examples | Affected Products | Impact |
|--------------|----------|-------------------|--------|
| **Tariffs** | China retaliatory tariffs; EU tariffs | Soybeans, pork, whiskey | Significant price impact |
| **SPS measures** | EU hormone ban; Asian pest restrictions | Beef, poultry, fruit | Market closure or delays |
| **Technical barriers** | Labeling, testing, certification | Processed foods, dairy | Compliance costs |
| **Geographic indications** | EU GI protections | Cheese, wine, cured meats | Limits product naming |
| **Quotas** | EU TRQs; Japan rice | Dairy, rice, sugar | Limited access volumes |
| **State trading** | Canadian dairy; various cereals | Dairy, wheat | Distorted competition |

## SPS and Technical Standards

### Compliance Framework

| Agency | Role | Export Impact |
|--------|------|---------------|
| **USDA APHIS** | Plant health certification | Pest-free certifications |
| **USDA FSIS** | Meat and poultry inspection | Export establishment certification |
| **FDA** | Food safety; processed products | Facility registration; FSMA |
| **EPA** | Pesticide residue limits | MRL harmonization |
| **AMS** | Grading and standards | Quality certifications |

### Key SPS Issues

| Issue | Markets Affected | Products | Status |
|-------|------------------|----------|--------|
| **Hormone/beta-agonist restrictions** | EU, China, Russia | Beef | Long-standing barrier |
| **Biotech crop restrictions** | EU, Mexico, others | Corn, soybeans | Ongoing disputes |
| **Pathogen testing** | China, EU | Poultry, pork | Variable requirements |
| **Pest certifications** | Asia, Latin America | Fruit, vegetables | Time-consuming |
| **Maximum residue levels** | EU, Asia | Various crops | Harmonization gaps |
| **Traceability requirements** | EU, Japan | Meat, processed | Growing compliance burden |

### Compliance Costs

| Activity | Cost Range | Burden Distribution |
|----------|------------|---------------------|
| **Export certification** | $50-$500 per shipment | All exporters |
| **Facility certification** | $1,000-$50,000 annually | Meat/poultry plants |
| **Third-party audits** | $5,000-$50,000 annually | Medium-large exporters |
| **Laboratory testing** | $100-$1,000 per test | All; more for specialty |
| **Traceability systems** | $10,000-$500,000 setup | Highly variable |

## Assessment

### Strengths

| Strength | Basis |
|----------|-------|
| **Production capacity** | Abundant land, technology, productivity |
| **Quality reputation** | Strong grading, safety systems |
| **Trade infrastructure** | Extensive (though aging) |
| **Export finance** | GSM-102 and promotion programs |
| **Market relationships** | Long-standing trade partnerships |

### Vulnerabilities

| Vulnerability | Evidence | Trend |
|---------------|----------|-------|
| **Market concentration** | 60%+ to top 5 markets; China soy exposure | Improving slowly |
| **Competition pressure** | Brazil, Argentina expanding | Intensifying |
| **Infrastructure strain** | Port congestion; barge/rail issues | Mixed investment |
| **Small exporter access** | Programs favor large firms | Not improving |
| **Trade policy volatility** | Tariff disputes; agreement uncertainty | High |

### Critical Gaps

| Gap | Impact | Urgency |
|-----|--------|---------|
| **Market diversification** | Over-reliance on few markets | High |
| **Small exporter support** | Missed growth; equity | High |
| **SPS modernization** | Compliance costs; delays | Medium |
| **Infrastructure investment** | Competitiveness erosion | Medium |
| **Climate integration** | Trade policy lagging climate reality | Growing |
