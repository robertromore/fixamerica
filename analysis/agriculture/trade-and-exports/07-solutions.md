# Trade & Exports: Solutions

## Overview

Strengthening U.S. agricultural trade requires expanding market access, diversifying export destinations, modernizing compliance systems, investing in trade infrastructure, and ensuring small and midsize exporters can access programs and markets. These solutions aim to maintain U.S. competitiveness while reducing vulnerability to trade shocks and building a more equitable export ecosystem.

---

## Reform Framework

### Guiding Principles

- **Expand market access** while maintaining domestic food security and producer protections
- **Diversify export markets** to reduce geopolitical risk and concentration vulnerability
- **Strengthen SPS compliance** through modernization without overburdening exporters
- **Invest in infrastructure** to reduce bottlenecks and improve competitiveness
- **Ensure equitable access** to export programs for small and midsize producers
- **Enforce trade agreements** vigorously to maintain fair competition

### Success Criteria

| Criterion | Indicator |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Market access** | Value of exports to markets with reduced barriers |
| **Diversification** | Reduction in concentration to top 5 markets |
| **Small exporter participation** | Share of small/midsize firms accessing programs |
| **Infrastructure capacity** | Port throughput; reduced delays |
| **Compliance efficiency** | Time and cost for export certification |
| **Trade balance** | Agricultural trade surplus maintained or improved |

## Proposed Solutions

### Solution 1: Export Market Diversification Initiative

**Description**: A comprehensive strategy to expand trade promotion and technical assistance to develop new markets, with priority for emerging economies and high-growth regions.

**Components**:

| Component | Purpose | Mechanism |
|-----------|---------|-----------|
| **Emerging Market Development Fund** | Accelerate new market entry | $100M/year dedicated to Africa, Southeast Asia, South Asia |
| **Diversification Incentives** | Reward market expansion | Bonus MAP allocations for exporters entering new markets |
| **Market Intelligence Platform** | Identify opportunities | Real-time trade data and market analysis |
| **Trade Mission Expansion** | Build relationships | Increase trade missions to emerging economies |
| **Attaché Network Strengthening** | On-ground presence | Expand FAS attaché positions in growth markets |

**Evidence base**:

- USDA market development programs show higher export resilience when markets are diversified
- China tariff shock demonstrated costs of concentration (~$12B in losses)
- Africa and Southeast Asia have fastest-growing food import demand

**Implementation requirements**:

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **Funding** | $150M annually for diversification programs |
| **Authority** | Farm bill authorization; FAS administration |
| **Timeline** | 3-5 years for measurable diversification |
| **Coordination** | FAS, USTR, State Department alignment |

**Challenges and mitigations**:

| Challenge | Mitigation |
|-----------|------------|
| New markets have higher risk | Risk insurance and credit guarantees |
| Limited buyer relationships | Trade missions and attaché support |
| Infrastructure gaps in emerging markets | Partner with development agencies |

**Estimated impact**: Reduce concentration to top 5 markets from 60-65% to 50% within 5 years.

### Solution 2: SPS Compliance and Traceability Modernization

**Description**: Provide shared compliance resources, digital traceability tools, and streamlined certification pathways to reduce the cost and complexity of meeting export requirements.

**Components**:

| Component | Purpose | Mechanism |
|-----------|---------|-----------|
| **Digital Certification Platform** | Streamline documentation | Electronic certification integrated with trading partners |
| **Shared Compliance Hubs** | Reduce individual costs | Regional centers for testing, documentation, training |
| **Mutual Recognition Agreements** | Reduce duplicative requirements | Negotiate equivalence with major trading partners |
| **Traceability Technology Grants** | Support digital adoption | Cost-share for blockchain and tracking systems |
| **SPS Rapid Response Team** | Resolve barriers quickly | Dedicated staff for emerging SPS issues |

**Evidence base**:

- Harmonized SPS systems reduce transaction costs by 10-15%
- Digital certification cuts processing time by 30-50%
- Traceability investments pay back through premium market access

**Implementation requirements**:

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **Funding** | $75M annually for compliance modernization |
| **Authority** | APHIS, FSIS, FDA coordination |
| **Timeline** | 2-3 years for digital platform; ongoing for agreements |
| **Coordination** | Multi-agency; trading partner engagement |

**Challenges and mitigations**:

| Challenge | Mitigation |
|-----------|------------|
| Partner country resistance | Trade agreement leverage |
| Technology adoption barriers | Technical assistance; cost-share |
| Agency coordination | Interagency working group with clear authority |

**Estimated impact**: Reduce average certification time by 30%; cut small exporter compliance costs by 25%.

### Solution 3: Export Finance and Risk Insurance Expansion

**Description**: Expand GSM-102 credit guarantees and develop tailored risk insurance products for small and midsize exporters and new market entry.

**Components**:

| Component | Purpose | Mechanism |
|-----------|---------|-----------|
| **Small Exporter Credit Access** | Lower barriers | Simplified GSM-102 application; smaller transaction thresholds |
| **New Market Risk Insurance** | Encourage diversification | Subsidized coverage for first-time market entry |
| **Climate/Geopolitical Insurance** | Buffer external shocks | Risk products for trade disruptions |
| **Export Finance Training** | Build capacity | Technical assistance on trade finance tools |
| **Cooperative Credit Aggregation** | Pool small exporters | Group guarantees through cooperatives |

**Evidence base**:

- Export credit guarantees stabilize trade flows during market volatility
- Small exporters cite financing as top barrier to export growth
- EXIM Bank model shows viability of targeted small business support

**Implementation requirements**:

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **Funding** | $50M additional annual credit subsidy; risk insurance appropriation |
| **Authority** | Farm bill authorization; CCC funding |
| **Timeline** | 1-2 years for program expansion |
| **Coordination** | FAS, Commodity Credit Corporation |

**Challenges and mitigations**:

| Challenge | Mitigation |
|-----------|------------|
| Adverse selection risk | Careful underwriting; risk controls |
| Administrative burden | Streamlined application; digital platform |
| Budget scoring | Subsidy cost offsets through program efficiency |

**Estimated impact**: Increase small/midsize exporter participation in GSM-102 by 50% within 3 years.

### Solution 4: Trade Infrastructure Investment

**Description**: Invest in agricultural export corridors, port facilities, intermodal hubs, and cold chain infrastructure to reduce delays and improve competitiveness.

**Components**:

| Component | Purpose | Mechanism |
|-----------|---------|-----------|
| **Port Modernization Fund** | Increase capacity | $500M for agricultural export terminal upgrades |
| **Inland Waterway Investment** | Improve barge efficiency | Lock and dam rehabilitation; dredging |
| **Agricultural Rail Improvement** | Enhance grain car service | Short-line grants; car availability mechanisms |
| **Cold Chain Expansion** | Support specialty exports | Grants for cold storage at ports and inland hubs |
| **Intermodal Connectivity** | Reduce transfer delays | Investments in terminal-to-ship connections |

**Evidence base**:

- Port congestion costs U.S. exporters $1-2B annually in delays and premium freight
- Mississippi River lock delays average hours per transit; billions in annual costs
- Cold chain gaps cause 5-10% specialty crop loss

**Implementation requirements**:

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **Funding** | $750M over 5 years for infrastructure |
| **Authority** | Transportation bill; USDA coordination |
| **Timeline** | 3-5 years for major projects |
| **Coordination** | DOT, USDA, Army Corps of Engineers, port authorities |

**Challenges and mitigations**:

| Challenge | Mitigation |
|-----------|------------|
| Long project timelines | Prioritize highest-impact projects |
| Federal-state coordination | Grant incentives for state matching |
| Private sector role | Public-private partnerships |

**Estimated impact**: Reduce agricultural export delays by 25%; save $500M+ annually in logistics costs.

### Solution 5: Small Exporter Access Program

**Description**: Create dedicated programs to help small and midsize agricultural exporters access markets, comply with requirements, and secure financing.

**Components**:

| Component | Purpose | Mechanism |
|-----------|---------|-----------|
| **Small Exporter Technical Assistance** | Build capacity | Dedicated export advisors at SBDCs and extension |
| **Export Consortium Support** | Achieve scale | Grants for cooperative export groups |
| **MAP Set-Aside** | Ensure program access | 20% of MAP funding for small exporter initiatives |
| **Digital Export Platform** | Simplify market entry | One-stop portal for compliance, buyers, logistics |
| **Trade Show Participation Grants** | Build relationships | Cost-share for international trade show attendance |

**Evidence base**:

- Small exporters cite complexity and cost as primary barriers
- Cooperative and consortium models demonstrate successful aggregation
- SBDC export assistance shows positive ROI for small businesses

**Implementation requirements**:

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **Funding** | $60M annually for small exporter support |
| **Authority** | Farm bill authorization; SBA coordination |
| **Timeline** | 1-2 years to establish programs |
| **Coordination** | FAS, SBA, state trade offices, extension |

**Challenges and mitigations**:

| Challenge | Mitigation |
|-----------|------------|
| Reaching dispersed small exporters | Partner with existing networks (extension, SBDCs) |
| Limited individual capacity | Aggregation and consortium models |
| Program awareness | Targeted outreach; digital platform |

**Estimated impact**: Double number of small/midsize firms exporting within 5 years.

### Solution 6: Trade Agreement Enforcement and Expansion

**Description**: Strengthen enforcement of existing trade agreements, pursue new market access through targeted negotiations, and improve dispute resolution effectiveness.

**Components**:

| Component | Purpose | Mechanism |
|-----------|---------|-----------|
| **Agricultural Trade Enforcement Unit** | Vigorous enforcement | Dedicated USTR staff for agricultural disputes |
| **SPS Barrier Rapid Response** | Quick action on new barriers | Interagency team to escalate SPS issues |
| **Sector-Specific Agreements** | Targeted access | Negotiate product-specific access deals |
| **WTO Reform Advocacy** | Improve dispute system | Push for faster, more enforceable WTO process |
| **Bilateral Breakthrough Initiative** | Open priority markets | Focus on India, UK, other target markets |

**Evidence base**:

- USMCA enforcement mechanisms have resolved disputes
- SPS barriers cost U.S. exporters billions annually
- Sector-specific agreements have opened markets (e.g., Japan beef)

**Implementation requirements**:

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **Funding** | $25M annually for enforcement capacity |
| **Authority** | USTR; Trade Promotion Authority renewal |
| **Timeline** | Ongoing enforcement; 2-5 years for new agreements |
| **Coordination** | USTR, USDA, State Department |

**Challenges and mitigations**:

| Challenge | Mitigation |
|-----------|------------|
| Limited USTR resources | Dedicated agricultural trade staff |
| WTO dysfunction | Pursue bilateral alternatives |
| Partner resistance | Reciprocal market access offers |

**Estimated impact**: Resolve 5+ major SPS barriers annually; open 2-3 new significant markets within 5 years.

### Solution 7: Climate-Resilient Trade Strategy

**Description**: Integrate climate adaptation and sustainability into trade policy to address emerging barriers and position U.S. exports for evolving market requirements.

**Components**:

| Component | Purpose | Mechanism |
|-----------|---------|-----------|
| **Sustainability Certification Support** | Meet emerging requirements | Technical assistance for climate-smart practices |
| **EU Deforestation Compliance** | Maintain EU access | Traceability systems for affected commodities |
| **Carbon Footprint Verification** | Competitive positioning | Develop credible measurement and verification |
| **Climate-Trade Negotiations** | Shape global rules | Advocate for science-based standards |
| **Climate Disruption Contingency** | Prepare for shocks | Planning for trade route and production disruptions |

**Evidence base**:

- EU deforestation regulation creates new compliance requirements
- Sustainability credentials increasingly required for premium markets
- Climate disruptions are already affecting trade routes and production

**Implementation requirements**:

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **Funding** | $40M annually for climate-trade integration |
| **Authority** | USDA climate programs; USTR negotiations |
| **Timeline** | Immediate for EU compliance; ongoing for broader strategy |
| **Coordination** | USDA, USTR, EPA, State Department |

**Challenges and mitigations**:

| Challenge | Mitigation |
|-----------|------------|
| Evolving requirements | Active participation in standard-setting |
| Measurement complexity | Invest in verification technology |
| Cost burden on exporters | Technical assistance; cost-share |

**Estimated impact**: Maintain full access to markets with sustainability requirements; position U.S. as leader in verified sustainable exports.

**Description**: Improve trade data collection, analysis, and dissemination to help exporters identify opportunities and policymakers make informed decisions.

**Components**:

| Component | Purpose | Mechanism |
|-----------|---------|-----------|
| **Real-Time Trade Dashboard** | Current market information | Integrated data from multiple sources |
| **Predictive Analytics** | Anticipate opportunities | AI/ML analysis of trade patterns |
| **Small Exporter Data Access** | Level information playing field | Free access to market intelligence for small firms |
| **Trade Disruption Early Warning** | Prepare for shocks | Monitor for emerging barriers and disruptions |
| **Country Market Profiles** | Support market entry | Comprehensive guides for export destinations |

**Evidence base**:

- Large trading companies have significant information advantages
- Data-driven decision-making improves export success rates
- Early warning enables proactive response to barriers

**Implementation requirements**:

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **Funding** | $20M annually for data and analytics |
| **Authority** | FAS, ERS, AMS coordination |
| **Timeline** | 1-2 years for platform development |
| **Coordination** | USDA agencies; Census Bureau; CBP |

**Challenges and mitigations**:

| Challenge | Mitigation |
|-----------|------------|
| Data integration complexity | Phased platform development |
| Private sector data access | Aggregated, anonymized public data |
| Maintenance and updates | Sustainable funding model |

**Estimated impact**: Improve market intelligence access for 10,000+ small exporters; reduce information asymmetry.
