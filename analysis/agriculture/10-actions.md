### Political Actions

**Federal Advocacy:**

| Issue | Target | Message |
|-------|--------|---------|
| **Farm bill reform** | Congress | "Support farm bill reforms that target payments to small and midsize family farms, not large agribusinesses" |
| **Competition enforcement** | USDA, DOJ | "Enforce Packers and Stockyards Act; block further meat industry consolidation" |
| **Conservation funding** | Congress | "Increase funding for EQIP and CSP to reduce waitlists and support farmer-led conservation" |
| **Beginning farmer support** | Congress | "Expand FSA loan programs and land access initiatives for beginning farmers" |
| **Farmworker protections** | Congress | "Include farmworkers in NLRA organizing rights and mandate overtime protections" |
| **Local food support** | Congress | "Expand farm-to-school funding and SNAP farmers market incentives" |

**Sample Advocacy Messages:**

*For farm bill equity:*

> "I'm calling to urge support for farm bill reforms that target commodity program benefits to small and midsize family farms. The current system sends 77% of payments to the largest 10% of operations while small farms struggle. Please support payment caps that can't be circumvented through entity splitting, and means testing that phases out payments for millionaire farmers."

*For competition enforcement:*

> "I'm calling about meat industry concentration. Four companies control 85% of beef processing, giving ranchers few options to sell their cattle at fair prices. Please support USDA enforcement of Packers and Stockyards rules and DOJ action to restore competitive markets. Independent ranchers need fair markets to survive."

*For farmworker protections:*

> "I'm calling about farmworker rights. Agricultural workers were excluded from the National Labor Relations Act in 1935 and still lack basic organizing rights that other workers have. Farmworkers also face heat illness, pesticide exposure, and wage theft without adequate protection. Please support extending NLRA coverage to farmworkers and mandatory OSHA heat illness prevention standards."

*For conservation funding:*

> "I'm calling about conservation program funding. Thousands of farmers want to implement conservation practices but are turned away due to funding limitsâ€”EQIP and CSP have multi-year waitlists. Please support increased conservation program funding in the next farm bill to help farmers protect soil, water, and climate."

### How to Contact Representatives

**Federal:**

- Capitol switchboard: (202) 224-3121
- Find your representative: house.gov/representatives
- Find your senators: senate.gov/senators
- USDA public comment: regulations.gov
- Comment on rules: Federal Register notices

**State:**

- Find state legislators through your state legislature website
- State agriculture department: varies by state
- County farm bureau or extension offices

**Local:**

- County commissioners/supervisors
- Planning and zoning boards
- School boards (for farm-to-school)
- Parks departments (for community gardens)

**Effective Advocacy Tips:**

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Use personal stories about food, farms, local agriculture | Make only abstract policy arguments |
| Cite specific data about your area | Be vague about the problem |
| Request specific legislative action | Only express frustration |
| Thank supportive officials | Only contact when critical |
| Coordinate with farm organizations | Work in isolation |
| Follow up after initial contact | Make one-time contacts only |

## Taking Action: Getting Started

**Today:**

1. Look up your local farmers market or CSA
2. Find your legislators' contact information
3. Sign up for one agriculture newsletter

**This Week:**

1. Visit a farmers market or local farm
2. Call one legislator about farm bill priorities
3. Share information about local agriculture

**This Month:**

1. Join a food or agriculture organization
2. Attend a community meeting about food policy
3. Write a letter to the editor about agriculture issues

**This Year:**

1. Build ongoing relationship with local farmers
2. Participate in farm bill advocacy campaign
3. Support farmland preservation in your area
4. Volunteer with food access organization
5. Vote for candidates supporting sustainable agriculture
