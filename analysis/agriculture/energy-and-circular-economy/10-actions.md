## For Agricultural Producers

### Getting Started with Energy Projects

**Step 1: Assess Your Energy Use**

| Action | How |
|--------|-----|
| Review utility bills | Track 12+ months of electricity, fuel costs |
| Identify major loads | Irrigation, cooling, heating, drying |
| Calculate cost trends | Are costs rising? Seasonal patterns? |
| Request energy audit | Many utilities offer free assessments |

**Step 2: Explore Options**

| Technology | Best For | Typical ROI |
|------------|----------|-------------|
| Solar PV | High electricity users | 5-8 years |
| Energy efficiency | All operations | 2-5 years |
| Solar pumping | Irrigated farms | 3-7 years |
| Digesters | Large livestock with waste management needs | 7-15 years |

**Step 3: Evaluate Financing**

| Option | Pros | Cons |
|--------|------|------|
| REAP grant | Up to 50% cost covered | Competitive, application burden |
| Tax credits (ITC) | 30-50% credit | Requires tax liability |
| Direct pay (IRA) | No tax liability needed | Administrative complexity |
| Utility programs | Simple process | Often smaller amounts |
| Equipment loans | Familiar to lenders | May not include energy |

### Applying for REAP

**Application Timeline**

| Phase | Timing | Action |
|-------|--------|--------|
| Planning | 6-12 months before | Energy audit, project design |
| Application | During open window | Submit complete application |
| Review | 3-6 months | Agency evaluation |
| Award | If selected | Execute grant agreement |
| Implementation | Per agreement | Install, document |

**Tips for Success**

- Apply early in funding cycle
- Get technical assistance before applying
- Ensure complete documentation
- Work with experienced contractors
- Consider loan guarantee if grant unsuccessful

## For Rural Communities

### Building Shared Energy Infrastructure

**Community Solar**

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| Assess interest | Survey community members |
| Identify sites | Suitable land, grid connection |
| Evaluate models | Subscription vs. ownership |
| Find developer | Experienced community solar company |
| Negotiate terms | Fair allocation, pricing |

**Cooperative Models**

| Model | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Multi-farm solar | Shared array, allocated production |
| Regional digester | Aggregate feedstock from multiple farms |
| Equipment cooperative | Shared efficiency upgrades |
| Energy cooperative | Member-owned utility alternative |

### Engaging Your Electric Cooperative

**Member Actions**

| Action | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Attend annual meetings | Governance participation |
| Run for board | Decision-making authority |
| Request distributed generation policies | Enable member projects |
| Ask for agricultural rates | Fair pricing for farms |
| Propose community programs | Collective action |

**Questions to Ask**

- What is the interconnection process for solar?
- Does the co-op offer net metering?
- What are the demand charges for farms?
- Are there energy efficiency programs?
- Will the co-op consider community solar?

### Economic Development Connections

**Local Benefits of Farm Energy**

| Benefit | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Installation jobs | Local contractors |
| Reduced energy costs | Money stays local |
| Processing facilities | Circular economy |
| Energy resilience | Community reliability |
