## For Agricultural Producers

### Getting Started with Programs

**Step 1: Contact Your Local NRCS Office**

- Find your office: [USDA Service Center Locator](https://www.farmers.gov/service-locator)
- Request a meeting with a conservationist
- Ask about free conservation planning assistance
- No commitment required for initial consultation

**Step 2: Understand Your Options**

| Program | Best For | Commitment |
|---------|----------|------------|
| EQIP | Working land improvements | 1-10 year contracts |
| CSP | Rewarding existing stewardship | 5-year contracts |
| CRP | Retiring sensitive land | 10-15 year contracts |
| ACEP | Permanent easements | Perpetual |

**Step 3: Prepare Your Application**

- Gather farm records (acres, crops, livestock numbers)
- Document existing conservation practices
- Identify resource concerns (erosion, water quality, habitat)
- Review ranking criteria for your state

### Maximizing Your Chances

**Application Tips**

- Apply early in signup periods
- Address multiple resource concerns
- Consider bundling practices
- Ask about continuous signup options
- Request technical assistance for planning

**Building Relationships**

- Attend local conservation district meetings
- Participate in NRCS listening sessions
- Connect with other producers in programs
- Provide feedback on what's working

### If You're Underserved

**Resources for Beginning Farmers**

- Higher payment rates available (up to 90% cost-share)
- Beginning Farmer and Rancher Coordinator at state NRCS office
- FSA microloans for conservation implementation
- National Young Farmers Coalition resources

**Resources for Socially Disadvantaged Producers**

- USDA Equity Commission recommendations
- 2501 Program funding for outreach organizations
- Higher payment rates and priority ranking
- Organizations: Federation of Southern Cooperatives, Intertribal Agriculture Council

## For Landowners (Non-Operators)

### Conservation on Rented Land

**What You Can Do**

- Include conservation requirements in lease agreements
- Explore cost-sharing practices with tenants
- Consider long-term leases to enable multi-year practices
- Investigate easement options for sensitive areas

**Resources**

- American Farmland Trust lease resources
- Land grant extension publications on conservation leases
- USDA guides on landowner-operator partnerships

### Easement Options

**Agricultural Conservation Easement Program (ACEP)**

- Protects farmland from development permanently
- Wetland Reserve Easements restore wetlands
- Work with local land trusts or NRCS directly

## For Rural Communities

### Building Local Capacity

**Strengthen Your Conservation District**

- Attend district board meetings
- Consider running for district supervisor
- Advocate for district funding in county budget
- Support district outreach and education programs

**How Districts Can Improve Delivery**

| Action | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Host application workshops | Reduce producer barriers |
| Provide technical assistance | Supplement limited NRCS staff |
| Coordinate with land trusts | Expand easement capacity |
| Connect producers with TSPs | Increase planning support |

### Watershed-Scale Action

**Forming Watershed Groups**

- Identify shared resource concerns
- Apply for RCPP (Regional Conservation Partnership Program)
- Leverage local match to attract federal funds
- Coordinate practices across property lines

**Successful Models**

- Iowa Water Quality Initiative partnerships
- Chesapeake Bay watershed groups
- Minnesota Board of Water and Soil Resources partnerships

### Economic Development Connections

**Conservation as Economic Development**

- Conservation practice installation creates jobs
- Improved water quality benefits tourism, recreation
- Soil health increases long-term farm productivity
- Ecosystem services have economic value

## For Advocacy Organizations

### Policy Advocacy

**Farm Bill Engagement**

| Timing | Action |
|--------|--------|
| 2+ years before | Build relationships with Ag Committee staff |
| 1 year before | Submit coalition priorities, host Hill visits |
| During markup | Targeted amendments, constituent calls |
| Implementation | Comment on rulemaking, monitor rollout |

**Key Advocacy Targets**

- Conservation title funding levels
- Program simplification and access
- Technical assistance funding
- Equity and access provisions
- Outcome measurement requirements

**Building Coalitions**

- Connect sustainable agriculture and environmental groups
- Engage hunting and fishing organizations
- Build relationships with commodity groups on shared interests
- Include equity and environmental justice voices

### Program Implementation Advocacy

**Engaging USDA**

- Participate in NRCS listening sessions
- Comment on practice standard revisions
- Serve on state technical committees
- Provide feedback on ranking criteria

**State and Local Engagement**

- Advocate for conservation district funding
- Support state match for federal programs
- Push for delivery improvements at state level
- Highlight successful local models

### Research and Documentation

**What's Needed**

- Document delivery barriers experienced by producers
- Track program participation by demographics
- Evaluate outcomes of conservation investments
- Compare approaches across states

## For Technical Service Providers

### Expanding Capacity

**Becoming a TSP**

- Review NRCS certification requirements
- Complete required training
- Register with NRCS TechReg system
- Market services to producers in your area

**Best Practices**

- Stay current on practice standards
- Build relationships with local NRCS staff
- Focus on underserved areas and populations
- Provide quality planning that gets approved

## For Researchers and Educators

### Applied Research Needs

**Priority Questions**

- What practices work best in which conditions?
- How can application processes be simplified?
- What drives adoption among different producer types?
- How can outcomes be better measured?

**Partnership Opportunities**

- Collaborate with NRCS on demonstration projects
- Work with conservation districts on local studies
- Connect with USDA Climate Hubs
- Engage farmers in participatory research

### Extension and Outreach

**Effective Approaches**

- Field days and demonstration farms
- Peer-to-peer learning networks
- Technical guides in accessible language
- Targeted outreach to underserved communities
