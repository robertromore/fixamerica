# Technology & Innovation Policy

## Overview

Technology and innovation policy in agriculture shapes how research funding, data governance, and commercialization pathways translate into productivity gains, climate resilience, and equitable access across the farming sector. The United States maintains the world's most extensive agricultural research system—combining USDA agencies, land-grant universities, and private-sector innovation—yet adoption of new technologies remains deeply uneven. Large operations with capital, technical staff, and connectivity readily adopt precision agriculture, AI-driven decision tools, and advanced automation, while small and midsize farms face financing barriers, data privacy concerns, and limited technical assistance.

This subtopic examines how public R&D investment, extension services, data governance frameworks, and technology deployment policies can accelerate adoption while ensuring farmers retain control of their data and operations. Effective policy must bridge the gap between research innovation and on-farm practice, address the digital divide in rural America, and establish responsible governance for emerging technologies like AI and autonomous systems.

| **Technical support** | Limited extension capacity constrains adoption |

## Why This Matters

### Productivity and Competitiveness

Agricultural technology drives productivity growth essential for food security and global competitiveness. The Green Revolution demonstrated how public R&D investment transformed food production. Today, precision agriculture, digital decision tools, and automation represent similar transformative potential—but only if adoption spreads beyond the largest operations.

### Climate Resilience

Climate change demands new tools for adaptation and mitigation. Precision agriculture reduces input waste and environmental impact. Climate-smart practices often require technology for monitoring, verification, and optimization. Without technology access, farmers cannot fully participate in climate programs or markets.

### Equity and Rural Communities

Technology policy shapes rural economic opportunity. Uneven adoption deepens inequality between large and small operations, potentially accelerating consolidation. The digital divide in rural America compounds these disparities. Technology policy must address access, affordability, and workforce capacity to ensure broad benefits.

### Farmer Autonomy

As agriculture becomes more data-intensive, questions of data ownership, control, and autonomy become central. Farmers must retain control over their production data and avoid dependence on platforms that limit choice or extract value. Technology governance frameworks must balance innovation with farmer rights.

## Assessment

### Strengths of Current System

| Strength | Evidence |
|----------|----------|
| **World-leading research system** | USDA and land-grant network produce significant innovation |
| **Extensive extension reach** | National network with local presence |
| **Private sector investment** | Substantial private R&D complements public investment |
| **Growing precision adoption** | Significant progress on large-scale operations |
| **Federal broadband investment** | Recent infrastructure investments target rural gaps |

### Critical Gaps

| Gap | Impact |
|-----|--------|
| **Adoption inequality** | Technology benefits concentrated in largest operations |
| **Extension capacity decline** | Reduced staffing limits on-farm support |
| **Data governance vacuum** | No clear standards for ownership, privacy, portability |
| **Broadband gaps** | Connectivity limits technology adoption in many rural areas |
| **AI accountability** | No frameworks for transparency or bias in agricultural AI |
| **Workforce gaps** | Limited training for new technology operation and maintenance |

### Urgency

| Factor | Urgency Level |
|--------|---------------|
| **Climate adaptation** | High—technology essential for resilience |
| **Consolidation pressure** | Medium—technology gaps accelerate concentration |
| **Data governance** | Medium—window for establishing farmer-friendly standards |
| **AI governance** | Medium—frameworks needed before widespread deployment |
| **Workforce transition** | Medium—training needed before automation scales |
