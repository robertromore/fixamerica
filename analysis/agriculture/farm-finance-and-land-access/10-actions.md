## For Aspiring and Beginning Farmers

### Accessing Credit

**Step 1: Assess Your Starting Point**

| Factor | Questions to Answer |
|--------|---------------------|
| Credit history | Do you have established credit? Any negative marks? |
| Collateral | What assets can you offer as security? |
| Experience | Years of farming experience? Training/education? |
| Business plan | Do you have a written plan with financial projections? |
| Land situation | Do you have land, lease access, or need to purchase? |

**Step 2: Explore FSA Loan Programs**

- Find your local FSA office: [farmers.gov/service-locator](https://www.farmers.gov/service-locator)
- Request a meeting with a loan officer
- Ask specifically about:
    - Beginning Farmer designation (favorable terms)
    - Direct Operating Loans (up to $400,000)
    - Direct Farm Ownership Loans (up to $600,000)
    - Microloans (simplified process, up to $50,000)
    - Down Payment Program (for land purchase)

**Step 3: Build Your Application**

| Required Element | Tips |
|------------------|------|
| Business plan | Use AgPlan (agplan.umn.edu) or extension templates |
| Financial statements | Work with extension or farm business management |
| Farm experience documentation | Include apprenticeships, family experience |
| References | Line up farmers, employers, lenders who know you |
| Collateral inventory | List all assets, even if limited |

**If You're Denied**

- Request specific reasons in writing
- Ask about appeal procedures
- Seek help from Farmers' Legal Action Group (FLAG)
- Consider alternative lenders (CDFIs, Farm Credit)

### Finding Land

**Land Link Programs by Region**

| Region | Program | Website |
|--------|---------|---------|
| National | Land For Good | landforgood.org |
| Northeast | New England Farmland Finder | newenglandfarmlandfinder.org |
| California | California FarmLink | californiafarmlink.org |
| Midwest | Beginning Farmer Center (Iowa) | extension.iastate.edu |
| Pacific Northwest | Cascade Harvest Coalition | cascadeharvest.org |
| Southeast | Georgia Organics Land Link | georgiaorganics.org |

**Alternative Access Strategies**

| Strategy | Pros | Cons |
|----------|------|------|
| Long-term lease | Lower capital, flexibility | No equity building |
| Lease-to-own | Path to ownership | Complex agreements |
| Land trust ownership | Affordable, permanent | Restrictions on resale |
| Farm incubator | Low-cost entry, support | Temporary, limited scale |
| Partnership with retiring farmer | Mentorship, gradual transition | Relationship dependent |

**Building Relationships with Landowners**

- Attend farm tours and field days where landowners participate
- Network through conservation districts and farm bureaus
- Approach landowners directly with professional proposal
- Consider "farmer wanted" ads in agricultural publications
- Connect with land trusts who work with landowners

### Building Credit and Financial Capacity

**For Those with Limited Credit**

- Start with FSA Microloans (less stringent requirements)
- Build banking relationship with local institution
- Consider individual development accounts (IDAs) through nonprofits
- Participate in matched savings programs
- Document informal experience and training

**Financial Education Resources**

| Resource | Focus | Access |
|----------|-------|--------|
| Annie's Project | Women in agriculture | State extension offices |
| Farm Beginnings | Whole farm planning | Practical Farmers of Iowa, partners |
| SCORE | Small business mentorship | score.org |
| Extension farm management | Financial planning | Land grant universities |

## For Retiring Farmers and Landowners

### Planning Farm Succession

**Step 1: Start Early**

- Begin planning 5-10 years before transition
- Involve family members in conversations
- Consult attorney on estate planning
- Assess farm's financial situation

**Step 2: Explore Transfer Options**

| Option | Best For | Considerations |
|--------|----------|----------------|
| Sale to family member | Family continuity | Fair market value vs. family discount |
| Gradual transition | Mentoring new operator | Legal structure, income needs |
| Sale to employee/partner | Continuity of operation | Financing challenges |
| Land trust sale | Conservation goals | Price below market |
| Land link matching | Legacy, helping new farmer | Finding right match |

**Step 3: Use Available Resources**

- Land For Good farm transfer toolkits: landforgood.org
- Extension farm succession planning courses
- State-specific transition programs
- Tax planning with agricultural accountant

### Supporting New Farmers

**What Landowners Can Do**

- Offer long-term leases (5+ years) enabling conservation investment
- Consider lease-to-own arrangements
- Include new farmers in lease decisions
- Share equipment and infrastructure
- Provide mentorship and introductions

**Lease Agreement Best Practices**

- Written lease with clear terms
- Provisions for conservation practices
- Fair compensation for improvements
- Renewal and termination procedures
- Dispute resolution mechanisms

## For Communities

### Building Local Land Access Infrastructure

**Community Land Trusts**

- Research community land trust models (Equity Trust: equitytrust.org)
- Identify local partners (churches, foundations, local government)
- Conduct community needs assessment
- Develop governance structure with farmer input
- Seek seed funding from foundations, municipal sources

**Regional Food System Investment**

| Investment Type | Community Benefit |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| Processing facilities | Market access, added value |
| Aggregation hubs | Scale for small farms |
| Cold storage | Extend marketing season |
| Commercial kitchens | Value-added products |

### Engaging Local Government

**Municipal Actions**

- Purchase of development rights programs
- Tax incentives for agricultural use
- Farmland protection zoning
- Right-to-farm ordinances
- Agricultural economic development priorities

**County Actions**

- Farm-friendly land use planning
- Agricultural districts with enhanced protection
- Transfer of development rights programs
- Farm viability programs
- Support for conservation districts

### Cooperative and Shared Ownership Models

**Types of Cooperative Structures**

| Structure | Purpose | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| Farmer cooperative | Shared marketing, purchasing | Land O'Lakes, Organic Valley |
| Consumer cooperative | Community-owned farms | Many CSA models |
| Worker cooperative | Employee ownership | Various small farms |
| Land cooperative | Shared land ownership | Northeast models |
| Equipment cooperative | Shared machinery | Regional equipment rings |

**Starting a Land Cooperative**

1. Identify interested farmers and investors
2. Research legal structures (LLC, cooperative, trust)
3. Develop governance and membership agreements
4. Secure initial capital and land
5. Establish farm selection and support criteria

## For Advocacy Organizations

### Federal Policy Advocacy

**Farm Bill Priorities**

| Priority | Ask | Current Status |
|----------|-----|----------------|
| FSA loan funding | Increase direct loan authorization | Often oversubscribed |
| Beginning farmer set-aside | Maintain/expand loan set-asides | ~50% for beginning farmers |
| Microloan expansion | Raise limits, simplify | Popular but limited |
| Land access pilot programs | Authorize demonstration projects | Limited current authority |
| Down payment assistance | Expand down payment program | Helps with land purchase |

**Advocacy Timeline**

| Timing | Action |
|--------|--------|
| 2+ years before Farm Bill | Build relationships with Ag Committee staff |
| 1 year before | Submit coalition priorities, host Hill visits |
| During markup | Targeted amendments, constituent calls |
| Implementation | Comment on rulemaking, monitor rollout |

**Key Congressional Targets**

- Senate Agriculture Committee members
- House Agriculture Committee members
- Appropriations subcommittees (Ag, Financial Services)
- Members from agricultural districts

### State Policy Advocacy

**State-Level Opportunities**

| Policy Area | Potential Action |
|-------------|------------------|
| Beginning farmer tax credits | Advocate for new or expanded credits |
| Linked deposit programs | State funds guarantee farm loans |
| Agricultural finance authorities | State lending programs |
| Land protection funding | State purchase of development rights |
| Heirs' property legislation | Uniform Partition of Heirs Property Act |

**Building State Coalitions**

- Connect farm groups with conservation organizations
- Engage banking associations on credit access
- Include rural development organizations
- Partner with equity and justice groups
- Build relationships with state agriculture departments

### Equity and Discrimination Advocacy

**Addressing Historic Discrimination**

- Support implementation of Pigford and Keepseagle settlements
- Monitor FSA loan approval rates by race
- Document ongoing discrimination
- Advocate for USDA Equity Commission recommendations
- Support 2501 Program funding for outreach organizations

**Supporting Underserved Producers**

- Fund technical assistance in underserved communities
- Advocate for culturally appropriate outreach
- Support heirs' property resolution programs
- Connect producers with FSA designated advocates
- Document success stories and barriers

## For Researchers and Educators

### Research Priorities

**Understudied Questions**

- How do different ownership structures affect farm outcomes?
- What credit interventions most effectively help beginning farmers?
- How can heirs' property issues be systematically addressed?
- What policy changes would most reduce land concentration?

**Data Needs**

- Longitudinal tracking of beginning farmer outcomes
- Land ownership by demographic characteristics
- Credit denial reasons and patterns
- Alternative ownership model outcomes

### Extension and Education

**Effective Approaches**

- Peer learning networks (farmer-to-farmer)
- Bilingual and culturally appropriate programming
- Online and in-person hybrid delivery
- Financial coaching and mentorship
- Farm transfer planning workshops
