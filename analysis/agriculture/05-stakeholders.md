# Agriculture Policy: Stakeholders

## Overview

American agricultural policy affects a diverse array of stakeholders with varying interests and power. Understanding who is affected, who makes decisions, and who influences policy is essential for understanding why reforms succeed or fail and how coalitions can be built.

---

## Who Is Affected

### Primary Stakeholders

| Group | Size | Primary Impacts |
|-------|------|-----------------|
| **Farmers and ranchers** | 2.0 million farms | Income stability, program access, market conditions |
| **Beginning farmers** | ~400,000 | Land access, credit, training |
| **Socially disadvantaged farmers** | ~110,000 | Discrimination, program access, debt relief |
| **Farmworkers** | 2.4 million | Wages, safety, immigration status |
| **Rural communities** | 46 million residents | Economic base, services, infrastructure |
| **Consumers** | 330 million | Food prices, safety, nutrition |

### Farm Operators by Category

| Category | Number | Share of Production | Key Concerns |
|----------|--------|---------------------|--------------|
| **Small family farms** (<$350K) | 1.9 million | 21% | Survival, off-farm income, succession |
| **Midsize family farms** | 108,000 | 17% | Competitiveness, credit, consolidation pressure |
| **Large family farms** | 83,000 | 46% | Market access, labor, regulation |
| **Nonfamily farms** | 57,000 | 16% | Labor, tax policy, investment |

### Farmworkers

| Category | Estimated Size | Status | Primary Issues |
|----------|----------------|--------|----------------|
| **Year-round hired** | 1.1 million | Mixed citizenship | Job security, benefits |
| **Seasonal hired** | 700,000+ | Often immigrant | Housing, wages, safety |
| **H-2A guest workers** | 370,000+ | Temporary visa | Employer dependence, mobility |
| **Undocumented** | 800,000-1 million | No legal status | Deportation risk, exploitation |
| **Family labor** | 3+ million | Farm families | No wage protections |

### Secondary Stakeholders

| Group | Interest | Scale |
|-------|----------|-------|
| **Processors and packers** | Reliable supply, quality, price | Thousands of facilities |
| **Distributors and retailers** | Consistent supply, cost management | National chains, regional grocers |
| **Food banks** | Affordable, nutritious food | 200+ food banks, 60,000 pantries |
| **Input suppliers** | Market access, sales | Thousands of dealers |
| **Lenders** | Loan performance, risk | Banks, Farm Credit System |
| **Landlords** | Rental income, land value | Own 39% of farmland |
| **Environmental groups** | Conservation, pollution reduction | National and local organizations |
| **Tribal nations** | Sovereignty, land, water rights | 574 federally recognized tribes |

### Geographic Distribution

| Region | Primary Stakeholders | Key Issues |
|--------|---------------------|------------|
| **Midwest** | Corn/soy farmers, processors, rural towns | Consolidation, ethanol, runoff |
| **Great Plains** | Wheat/cattle producers, irrigation districts | Water scarcity, disaster risk |
| **West** | Fruit/vegetable growers, dairies, labor | Water rights, labor, fire |
| **Southeast** | Poultry growers, specialty crops | Contract terms, labor, storms |
| **Northeast** | Dairy, specialty, urban-adjacent | Land pressure, markets |

## Who Has Power

| **House Agriculture Committee** | Farm bill jurisdiction | Program design, funding |
| **Senate Appropriations Subcommittee** | Annual funding | Discretionary spending |
| **House Appropriations Subcommittee** | Annual funding | Discretionary spending |

**USDA Agencies:**

| Agency | Responsibility | Key Powers |
|--------|----------------|------------|

## Stakeholder Interests

### Interest Matrix

| Stakeholder | Primary Interest | Secondary Interests | Position on Reform |
|-------------|------------------|---------------------|-------------------|
| **Large commodity farms** | Price support, insurance | Trade, labor | Status quo |
| **Small/midsize farms** | Survival, fair markets | Conservation payments | Mixed |
| **Beginning farmers** | Land, credit, training | Mentorship | Strong support |
| **Farmworkers** | Wages, safety, status | Housing, benefits | Strong support |
| **Meatpackers** | Supply, price, labor | Regulation resistance | Oppose reform |
| **Input suppliers** | Sales, patents | Trade | Mixed |
| **Environmental groups** | Conservation, climate | Antitrust | Support reform |
| **Consumer groups** | Price, safety, nutrition | Labeling | Support reform |
| **Rural communities** | Jobs, services | Infrastructure | Mixed |

### Detailed Interest Analysis

**Large-Scale Commodity Producers:**

| Interest | Current Policy | Reform Position |
|----------|----------------|-----------------|
| Price support | ARC/PLC benefits | Oppose caps, means testing |
| Crop insurance | Major subsidies | Oppose cuts |
| Trade promotion | Export programs | Strong support |
| Labor | H-2A access | Support program |
| Conservation | Compliance acceptable | Oppose mandates |

**Small and Midsize Family Farms:**

| Interest | Current Policy | Reform Position |
|----------|----------------|-----------------|
| Program access | Often excluded | Support reform |
| Local markets | Limited support | Support expansion |
| Conservation | Can earn payments | Support increase |
| Antitrust | Not enforced | Support enforcement |
| Credit | Difficult access | Support reform |

**Farmworkers:**

| Interest | Current Policy | Reform Position |
|----------|----------------|-----------------|
| Wages | Minimum wage only | Support increase |
| Overtime | Not required | Support coverage |
| Organizing | NLRA excluded | Support inclusion |
| Immigration | H-2A or undocumented | Support pathway |
| Safety | Limited protections | Support standards |

## Power Dynamics

### Power Imbalances

| More Powerful | Less Powerful | Mechanism |
|---------------|---------------|-----------|
| Large farms | Small farms | Program design, scale economics |
| Processors | Producers | Market concentration |
| Employers | Workers | Legal exclusions, immigration status |
| Landowners | Renters | Contract terms, market power |
| Commodity groups | Specialty producers | Committee influence |
| Industry | Regulators | Lobbying, capture |

### Coalition Possibilities

**Natural Allies:**

| Coalition | Common Interest |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Small farms + consumers | Fair prices, local food |
| Workers + civil rights | Labor rights, immigration |
| Beginning farmers + environmental | Conservation, land access |
| Rural communities + infrastructure | Broadband, water |
| Family farms + antitrust advocates | Market competition |

**Potential Bridges:**

| Groups | Common Ground |
|--------|---------------|
| Commodity farms + conservation | Stewardship incentive payments |
| Labor + some growers | Stable workforce, immigration reform |
| Rural + urban | Nutrition-farm bill alliance |

### Leverage Points

| Actor | Leverage Mechanism |
|-------|-------------------|
| **Congress** | Appropriations, authorization |
| **USDA** | Regulatory authority, program administration |
| **Courts** | Antitrust, labor law, discrimination |
| **States** | Agricultural labor, water, land use |
| **Media** | Public attention, scandal exposure |
| **Consumers** | Market pressure, boycotts |
| **Elections** | Rural state Senate seats |
