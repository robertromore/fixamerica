# Water & Climate Resilience: Citizen Actions

## Overview

Building water and climate resilience in agriculture requires action at multiple levelsâ€”from individual farmers adopting efficient practices to communities coordinating watershed management to advocates pushing for water governance reform. This guide provides concrete steps for each audience, recognizing that sustainable water use requires both on-farm efficiency and systemic change.

## For Farmers

### On-Farm Water Efficiency

**Assessment First**

| Step | Approach |
|------|----------|
| Audit water use | Measure current consumption |
| Identify losses | Leaks, inefficient application |
| Evaluate options | Technology, practice changes |
| Plan investments | Prioritize highest return |

**Efficiency Technologies**

| Technology | Benefit | Consideration |
|------------|---------|---------------|
| Drip/micro irrigation | 90%+ efficiency | High cost, management |
| Center pivot upgrades | LEPA, precision | Capital requirement |
| Soil moisture sensors | Data-driven scheduling | Learning curve |
| Weather-based scheduling | Match crop needs | Data access |

**Management Practices**

| Practice | Benefit |
|----------|---------|
| Irrigation scheduling | Match water to need |
| Deficit irrigation | Strategic stress |
| Cover crops | Improve infiltration |
| Residue management | Reduce evaporation |
