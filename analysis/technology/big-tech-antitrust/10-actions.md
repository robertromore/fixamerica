# Big Tech Antitrust: Citizen Actions

## Overview

Restoring competition in digital markets requires sustained public pressure. This guide provides actionable steps for citizens at all engagement levels to support antitrust reform.

## Immediate Actions

### Learn and Stay Informed

| Action | Time | Impact |
|--------|------|--------|
| Read House Antitrust Report summary | 30 min | Foundation |
| Follow antitrust news sources | Ongoing | Stay current |
| Understand key cases | 1 hour | Context |
| Learn company market positions | 30 min | Specifics |

**Starting resources:**
- House Antitrust Subcommittee Report (2020)
- Open Markets Institute explainers
- American Economic Liberties Project materials

### Contact Elected Officials

| Action | When | Focus |
|--------|------|-------|
| Call Senators | Ongoing | Support antitrust bills |
| Email Representatives | Before votes | Specific legislation |
| Attend town halls | When available | Raise monopoly concerns |
| Submit written comments | Rulemaking periods | Technical input |

**Sample call script:**
> "I'm calling to urge [Senator/Representative] to support legislation to address Big Tech monopolies. Companies like Google, Amazon, and Apple have too much power over our economy and our lives. I support the American Innovation and Choice Online Act and other bills that would restore competition. This matters to me because [personal example]. Thank you."

### Support Enforcement

| Action | How | When |
|--------|-----|------|
| Public comments | Support DOJ/FTC cases | Case milestones |
| Share case news | Social media | Major developments |
| Attend hearings | When public | Available sessions |
| Write letters to editor | Local papers | Key moments |

## Civic Engagement

### Comment on Rulemakings

**Current opportunities:**

| Agency | Topic | How |
|--------|-------|-----|
| FTC | Merger guidelines | regulations.gov |
| FTC | Unfair practices | regulations.gov |
| DOJ | Competition policy | justice.gov |

**Effective comment elements:**
1. Personal experience with platform power
2. Specific harms observed
3. Support for stronger enforcement
4. Concrete suggestions

## Consumer Actions

### Make Informed Choices

| Action | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| Try alternatives | Support competition |
| Evaluate lock-in | Reduce switching costs |
| Use portability rights | Maintain options |
| Read terms carefully | Understand restrictions |

### Alternatives to Big Tech

| Dominant Platform | Alternatives |
|-------------------|--------------|
| Google Search | DuckDuckGo, Brave Search |
| Gmail | ProtonMail, Fastmail |
| Google Chrome | Firefox, Brave |
| Amazon shopping | Direct from retailers |
| AWS (if applicable) | Other clouds |
| Facebook/Instagram | Smaller networks |
| WhatsApp | Signal |

### Report Anticompetitive Behavior

**What to report:**
- Self-preferencing in search/marketplace
- Unfair contract terms
- Retaliation for using competitors
- Misleading practices

**Where to report:**
- FTC: reportfraud.ftc.gov
- State AG consumer protection
- DOJ Antitrust complaint form

## Professional Actions

### If You Work for a Tech Company

| Action | Consideration |
|--------|---------------|
| Raise concerns internally | Document practices |
| Support reform publicly | Where appropriate |
| Whistleblower protections | Know your rights |
| Career choices | Consider impact |

### If You're Affected by Platform Power

**Small business owners:**
- Join coalitions (Coalition for App Fairness, etc.)
- Document unfair practices
- Testify to legislators
- Connect with advocates

**Developers:**
- Report unfair practices
- Support alternative platforms
- Document self-preferencing
- Join professional advocacy

**Content creators:**
- Document platform changes
- Support creator unions
- Advocate for fair terms
- Diversify platforms

### If You Work in Competition Policy

| Economists | Research, expert support |
| Academics | Publish, testify |
| Journalists | Investigate, report |

## Action by Priority

### Highest Impact

1. Contact Senators on pending legislation
2. Support enforcement in key cases
3. Report anticompetitive behavior
4. Join advocacy organizations
5. Vote for pro-competition candidates

### Immediate/Easy

1. Follow antitrust news
2. Share information on social media
3. Use alternatives where practical
4. Sign petitions
5. Make small donations

### Ongoing Commitment

1. Join organization, pay dues
2. Regular advocacy activities
3. Attend events, meetings
4. Build expertise over time
5. Mentor others
