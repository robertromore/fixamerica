# Big Tech Antitrust: Stakeholders

## Overview

The debate over Big Tech antitrust involves diverse stakeholders with varying interests, resources, and perspectives. Understanding this landscape is essential for building coalitions and anticipating opposition.

## Major Technology Companies

### The Big Five

| Company | Primary Interest | Antitrust Exposure |
|---------|------------------|-------------------|
| **Google/Alphabet** | Preserve search/ad dominance | Highest - active DOJ cases |
| **Amazon** | Preserve e-commerce/cloud position | High - FTC case pending |
| **Apple** | Preserve iOS ecosystem control | High - DOJ case filed |
| **Meta** | Preserve social network position | Medium - FTC case dismissed |
| **Microsoft** | Maintain position, AI growth | Medium - less current exposure |

### Common Industry Positions

**Publicly stated:**
- Support "smart" regulation
- Emphasize consumer benefits
- Point to competition from each other
- Highlight innovation contributions

**Actual positions:**
- Oppose structural remedies
- Favor weak, industry-friendly rules
- Prefer federal preemption of state laws
- Use lobbying to weaken enforcement

### Differences Among Big Tech

| Issue | Google | Amazon | Apple | Meta | Microsoft |
|-------|--------|--------|-------|------|-----------|
| Interoperability | Oppose | Oppose | Strongly oppose | Mixed | Support |
| Open standards | Lip service | Oppose | Oppose | Mixed | Support |
| Privacy regulation | Complicate targeting | Minor concern | Competitive advantage | Threat | Minor concern |
| App store reform | Moderate threat | Minor | Existential | Moderate | Would benefit |

## Government Actors

### Federal Enforcers

| Agency | Leadership | Position | Resources |
|--------|------------|----------|-----------|
| **DOJ Antitrust** | Jonathan Kanter (AAG) | Aggressive enforcement | ~$200M budget |
| **FTC** | Lina Khan (Chair) | Aggressive enforcement | ~$400M budget |

**Current approach:**
- Active litigation against major platforms
- Expanded theories of harm
- Focus on structural remedies
- Coordination between agencies

### Congressional Actors

| Role | Key Figures | Position |
|------|-------------|----------|
| **Antitrust advocates** | Sen. Klobuchar, Rep. Cicilline (former) | Strong legislation |
| **Moderate Democrats** | Various | Cautious support |
| **Reform Republicans** | Sen. Lee, Rep. Buck | Bipartisan interest |
| **Industry-aligned** | Various | Oppose major reform |

**Legislative dynamics:**
- Bipartisan interest in principle
- Difficulty reaching consensus
- Industry lobbying effective
- Other priorities compete

## Power Analysis

### Resource Comparison

| Actor | Financial Resources | Legal Expertise | Political Access |
|-------|---------------------|-----------------|------------------|
| Big Tech | Unlimited | World-class | Extensive |
| Federal agencies | Limited | Strong | Moderate |
| Advocates | Limited | Growing | Growing |
| Small business | Very limited | Limited | Limited |

### Influence Mechanisms

| Mechanism | Big Tech | Advocates |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Lobbying | $100M+ annually | <$10M |
| Campaign contributions | Extensive | Minimal |
| Revolving door | Extensive | Limited |
| Think tank funding | Extensive | Limited |
| Media access | Strong | Growing |
| Litigation | Unlimited | Limited |

## Coalition Possibilities

### Pro-Enforcement Coalition
