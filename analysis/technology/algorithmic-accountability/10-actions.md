# Algorithmic Accountability: Actions for Citizens

## Overview

Citizens can advance algorithmic accountability through awareness, advocacy, exercise of rights, consumer choices, and support for reform organizations.

## Personal Awareness

### Know Your Rights

| Right | Where It Applies |
|-------|------------------|
| Adverse action notice | Credit, employment |
| Dispute rights | Credit reports |
| Access to data | State privacy laws |
| Opt-out | Some contexts |
| Human review | Some jurisdictions |

### Identify Algorithm Use

| Context | Questions to Ask |
|---------|------------------|
| Job applications | Is AI used in screening? |
| Credit applications | What factors affect decision? |
| Insurance quotes | What data determines price? |
| Housing applications | How is screening done? |
| Benefits | How is eligibility determined? |

### Document Issues

| What to Document | Why |
|------------------|-----|
| Decision notices | Evidence of algorithm use |
| Unexplained denials | Pattern recognition |
| Inconsistent treatment | Discrimination evidence |
| Missing information | Challenge inaccuracies |
| Timeline | Statute of limitations |

## Quick-Start Actions

### This Week

1. Check your credit reports for algorithmic errors
2. Learn your state's AI/privacy laws
3. Sign up for advocacy organization updates
4. Share this information with friends and family

### This Month

1. Contact your representative about algorithmic accountability
2. File a complaint about any algorithm issues you've experienced
3. Join a relevant advocacy organization
4. Review your digital privacy settings

### This Year

1. Participate in local advocacy efforts
2. Support algorithmic accountability legislation
3. Volunteer with civil rights or digital rights organizations
4. Share your story if you've been affected
