# Digital Divide: Legislation and Legal Framework

## Overview

Closing the digital divide requires sustained federal investment, permanent affordability programs, competition promotion, and digital equity initiatives. This document provides draft legislation for key reforms.

## Federal Legislation

### Universal Broadband Act

**Purpose**: Establish universal broadband access as national policy with sustained funding.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Universal Broadband Act of 2025".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds that—
    (1) broadband internet access is essential infrastructure for
    economic opportunity, education, healthcare, and civic participation;
    (2) tens of millions of Americans lack access to reliable,
    affordable broadband;
    (3) the digital divide disproportionately affects rural communities,
    low-income households, communities of color, and older Americans;
    (4) market forces alone have proven insufficient to achieve
    universal connectivity; and
    (5) sustained federal investment is necessary.

SEC. 3. UNIVERSAL BROADBAND POLICY.

(a) POLICY.—It is the policy of the United States that every
American household and business shall have access to reliable,
affordable broadband internet service.

(b) DEFINITION.—For purposes of this Act, "broadband" means
internet service providing—
    (1) download speeds of at least 100 megabits per second;
    (2) upload speeds of at least 100 megabits per second; and
    (3) latency suitable for real-time applications.

(c) STANDARD UPDATES.—The Federal Communications Commission shall
update the broadband standard at least every 3 years to reflect
technological and usage evolution.

SEC. 4. UNIVERSAL BROADBAND FUND.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—There is established in the Treasury a fund
to be known as the "Universal Broadband Fund".

(b) AUTHORIZATION.—There is authorized to be appropriated to the
Fund $10,000,000,000 annually for 10 fiscal years.

(c) USE OF FUNDS.—Amounts in the Fund shall be used for—
    (1) infrastructure deployment to unserved and underserved areas;
    (2) middle mile infrastructure;
    (3) last mile connections;
    (4) digital equity programs; and
    (5) ongoing maintenance and upgrades.

SEC. 5. DEPLOYMENT PRIORITIES.

(a) PRIORITY AREAS.—Funding shall prioritize—
    (1) areas without any broadband access;
    (2) areas with only one provider;
    (3) Tribal lands;
    (4) persistent poverty areas; and
    (5) areas with documented low adoption rates.

(b) TECHNOLOGY.—Funding shall prioritize technologies that—
    (1) are scalable to higher speeds;
    (2) provide symmetrical upload and download; and
    (3) offer low latency.

SEC. 6. PROVIDER ACCOUNTABILITY.

(a) BUILDOUT REQUIREMENTS.—Recipients of funding shall complete
buildout within specified timeframes.

(b) PERFORMANCE STANDARDS.—Recipients shall meet speed, latency,
and reliability standards.

(c) CLAWBACK.—Funds shall be recovered from recipients who fail
to meet requirements.

(d) REPORTING.—Recipients shall provide regular progress reports.

SEC. 7. OPEN ACCESS.

(a) REQUIREMENT.—Infrastructure funded under this Act shall be
available on a wholesale basis to other providers on reasonable
and nondiscriminatory terms.

(b) EXCEPTION.—The NTIA may waive this requirement where it would
significantly delay deployment.

SEC. 8. COORDINATION.

(a) NTIA LEAD.—The National Telecommunications and Information
Administration shall administer this Act.

(b) COORDINATION.—NTIA shall coordinate with—
    (1) the Federal Communications Commission;
    (2) the Department of Agriculture;
    (3) the Department of Housing and Urban Development; and
    (4) State broadband offices.

SEC. 9. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act takes effect upon enactment.
```

### Affordable Connectivity Restoration Act

**Purpose**: Restore and make permanent the Affordable Connectivity Program.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Affordable Connectivity Restoration
Act of 2025".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds that—
    (1) the Affordable Connectivity Program enrolled over 23 million
    households before its funding lapsed;
    (2) affordability remains a primary barrier to broadband adoption;
    (3) low-income households cannot benefit from infrastructure
    without affordability support; and
    (4) permanent funding is essential for sustained connectivity.

SEC. 3. PERMANENT AFFORDABLE CONNECTIVITY PROGRAM.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—There is established a permanent Affordable
Connectivity Program within the Universal Service Fund.

(b) BENEFIT.—Eligible households shall receive a benefit of—
    (1) $30 per month for broadband service; and
    (2) $75 per month for households on Tribal lands.

(c) DEVICE BENEFIT.—Eligible households may receive a one-time
discount of up to $100 toward the purchase of a connected device.

SEC. 4. ELIGIBILITY.

(a) ELIGIBLE HOUSEHOLDS.—A household is eligible if any member—
    (1) has household income at or below 200 percent of the Federal
    Poverty Guidelines;
    (2) participates in SNAP, Medicaid, SSI, Federal Public Housing
    Assistance, Veterans Pension, WIC, or the National School Lunch
    Program free lunch program;
    (3) participates in Lifeline;
    (4) participates in a Tribal assistance program;
    (5) receives a Federal Pell Grant; or
    (6) meets other criteria established by the Commission.

SEC. 5. ENROLLMENT.

(a) AUTOMATIC ENROLLMENT.—To the extent practicable, eligible
households shall be automatically enrolled based on participation
in qualifying programs.

(b) STREAMLINED APPLICATION.—The Commission shall establish a
simple, accessible application process for households not
automatically enrolled.

(c) OUTREACH.—The Commission shall conduct outreach to ensure
eligible households are aware of the program.

SEC. 6. PROVIDER PARTICIPATION.

(a) REQUIREMENTS.—Participating providers shall—
    (1) offer at least one service plan meeting minimum speed
    requirements fully covered by the benefit; and
    (2) not impose data caps on such plans.

(b) NONDISCRIMINATION.—Participating providers shall not
discriminate against program participants in service quality.

SEC. 7. FUNDING.

(a) AUTHORIZATION.—There is authorized to be appropriated
$7,000,000,000 annually for the Affordable Connectivity Program.

(b) UNIVERSAL SERVICE FUND.—The Commission may use Universal
Service Fund mechanisms for program administration.

SEC. 8. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act takes effect immediately upon enactment, with benefits
available within 60 days.
```

### Municipal Broadband Empowerment Act

**Purpose**: Remove barriers to municipal broadband and support community networks.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Municipal Broadband Empowerment Act
of 2025".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds that—
    (1) many states have enacted laws restricting or prohibiting
    municipal broadband networks;
    (2) such restrictions prevent communities from addressing their
    own connectivity needs;
    (3) municipal networks have proven successful in many communities;
    (4) local governments are well-positioned to serve community needs;
    (5) removing barriers enables local solutions.

SEC. 3. PREEMPTION OF STATE RESTRICTIONS.

(a) PREEMPTION.—No State or political subdivision may—
    (1) prohibit a local government from providing broadband service;
    (2) impose requirements on local government broadband that are
    more restrictive than requirements on private providers;
    (3) prohibit public-private partnerships for broadband; or
    (4) impose procedural barriers designed to prevent or delay
    local government broadband.

(b) SCOPE.—This section applies to any local government entity,
including municipalities, counties, public utility districts,
electric cooperatives, and other public entities.

SEC. 4. MUNICIPAL BROADBAND GRANT PROGRAM.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—The NTIA shall establish a grant program to
support the development of municipal and community broadband
networks.

(b) AUTHORIZATION.—There is authorized to be appropriated
$2,000,000,000 for this program.

(c) ELIGIBLE USES.—Grants may be used for—
    (1) feasibility studies;
    (2) network planning and design;
    (3) infrastructure construction;
    (4) equipment acquisition; and
    (5) technical assistance.

(d) PRIORITY.—Priority shall be given to—
    (1) unserved and underserved areas;
    (2) areas with limited competition;
    (3) communities with demonstrated need; and
    (4) projects serving low-income populations.

SEC. 5. TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE.

(a) PROGRAM.—The NTIA shall establish a technical assistance
program for communities considering or developing broadband
networks.

(b) ELEMENTS.—Technical assistance shall include—
    (1) feasibility assessment;
    (2) business planning;
    (3) engineering and design;
    (4) financing options;
    (5) operational guidance; and
    (6) best practices.

SEC. 6. POLE ATTACHMENT RIGHTS.

(a) LOCAL GOVERNMENT ACCESS.—Local government broadband providers
shall have the same pole attachment rights as private providers.

(b) RATES.—Pole attachment rates for local government providers
shall be just and reasonable.

SEC. 7. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act takes effect upon enactment.
```

## State Model Legislation

### Model State Broadband Investment Act

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "[State] Broadband Investment Act".

SECTION 2. STATE BROADBAND OFFICE.

(a) There is established the [State] Office of Broadband
Development within [department].

(b) The Office shall—
    (1) develop and implement a statewide broadband plan;
    (2) administer state and federal broadband funds;
    (3) coordinate with local governments and providers;
    (4) maintain broadband mapping and data; and
    (5) report annually to the legislature.

SECTION 3. STATE BROADBAND FUND.

(a) There is established the [State] Broadband Fund.

(b) The Fund shall be used for—
    (1) infrastructure grants to unserved areas;
    (2) match for federal programs;
    (3) digital equity initiatives; and
    (4) technical assistance.

SECTION 4. MUNICIPAL BROADBAND.

(a) Local governments may provide broadband service.

(b) No restriction shall prevent a local government from—
    (1) building broadband infrastructure;
    (2) providing broadband service; or
    (3) partnering with private entities.

SECTION 5. APPROPRIATION.

There is appropriated $[X] for the purposes of this Act.

SECTION 6. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act takes effect [date].
```

## Constitutional Considerations

### Commerce Clause

| Issue | Analysis |
|-------|----------|
| **Federal authority** | Clear commerce power |
| **State preemption** | Federal can preempt restrictions |
| **Municipal protection** | Federal can enable local action |

### Spending Power

| Issue | Analysis |
|-------|----------|
| **Conditional grants** | Well-established |
| **State requirements** | Permissible conditions |
| **Accountability** | Proper oversight |

### Tenth Amendment

| Issue | Analysis |
|-------|----------|
| **State sovereignty** | Not commandeering states |
| **Local governments** | Creatures of state, federal protection complex |

## Loopholes, Shortcomings, and Rectification

### Potential Loopholes

| Loophole | Description | Severity |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| **Definition gaming** | Claim service meets standard | High |
| **Subsidy capture** | Take money, underdeliver | High |
| **Speed throttling** | Degrade service | Medium |
| **Data cap circumvention** | Different plan structures | Medium |

### Shortcomings

| Issue | Impact | Root Cause |
|-------|--------|------------|
| **Workforce shortage** | Deployment delays | Training gap |
| **Supply chain** | Equipment delays | Global market |
| **Permitting delays** | Slow buildout | Process |
| **Political instability** | Program uncertainty | Elections |

### Rectification Procedures

1. **Speed testing** requirements with verification
2. **Clawback provisions** with enforcement teeth
3. **Anti-throttling** requirements in program rules
4. **Data cap prohibitions** for subsidized service
5. **Workforce development** tied to funding
6. **Streamlined permitting** requirements
7. **Long-term authorization** for stability
8. **Community accountability** mechanisms
9. **Regular assessment** and adjustment
10. **Technology neutrality** with performance focus
