# Digital Divide: Stakeholders

## Overview

Digital divide policy involves diverse stakeholders with varying interests, power, and vulnerability to connectivity gaps.

## Affected Populations

### Rural Communities

| Impact | Manifestation |
|--------|---------------|
| No broadband access | Economic disadvantage |
| Limited providers | No choice |
| High prices | Affordability burden |
| Economic development | Businesses can't locate |
| Healthcare access | Telehealth limited |

**Advocacy capacity**: Moderate (rural advocates, agriculture groups)

### Low-Income Households

| Impact | Manifestation |
|--------|---------------|
| Unaffordable service | Can't subscribe |
| Device limitations | Outdated equipment |
| Smartphone-only | Limited functionality |
| Educational impact | Students disadvantaged |
| Employment barriers | Job search difficulties |

**Advocacy capacity**: Low to moderate (consumer advocates)

### Communities of Color

| Impact | Manifestation |
|--------|---------------|
| Digital redlining | Infrastructure neglected |
| Affordability | Income disparities compound |
| Historical exclusion | Distrust, relevance concerns |
| Predatory practices | Exploitative pricing |

**Advocacy capacity**: Moderate (civil rights organizations)

### Older Americans

| Impact | Manifestation |
|--------|---------------|
| Lower adoption rates | 25%+ without home internet |
| Digital literacy gaps | Skills barriers |
| Accessibility needs | Disability intersection |
| Fixed income | Affordability challenges |
| Healthcare implications | Telehealth barriers |

**Advocacy capacity**: Moderate (AARP, senior advocates)

### Tribal Communities

| Impact | Manifestation |
|--------|---------------|
| Worst connectivity rates | 33% without access |
| Sovereignty complexities | Regulatory challenges |
| Remote locations | Infrastructure expensive |
| Funding gaps | Historically underfunded |

**Advocacy capacity**: Variable (tribal governments, NCAI)

### Students and Families

| Impact | Manifestation |
|--------|---------------|
| Homework gap | Can't complete assignments |
| Educational inequality | Achievement gaps |
| Parent engagement | Can't access school systems |
| Higher ed access | Application barriers |

**Advocacy capacity**: Moderate (education advocates)

## Industry Stakeholders

### Major ISPs

| Company | Interest |
|---------|----------|
| AT&T | Fiber investment, wireless focus |
| Comcast | Cable dominance, data caps |
| Charter/Spectrum | Cable, rural expansion |
| Verizon | Fiber, 5G focus |

**Interest**: Market protection, subsidy capture, regulation limits

### Regional and Small Providers

| Type | Interest |
|------|----------|
| Rural telcos | Subsidy access, buildout |
| Regional cable | Market maintenance |
| Wireless ISPs | Spectrum, pole access |
| Cooperatives | Community service |

**Interest**: Funding access, fair competition

### New Entrants

| Type | Interest |
|------|----------|
| Fiber overbuilders | Market access |
| Fixed wireless | Spectrum, poles |
| LEO satellite (Starlink) | Regulatory treatment |
| Municipal networks | State preemption removal |

**Interest**: Market access, fair rules

### Industry Associations

| USTelecom | Telephone industry |
| CTIA | Wireless |
| NTCA | Rural telcos |

## Stakeholder Mapping

### Power vs. Interest

| Quadrant | Stakeholders |
|----------|--------------|
| High power, oppose universal | Large ISPs |
| High power, support universal | FCC, NTIA, state offices |
| Low power, support universal | Unconnected populations |
| Moderate power, mixed | Smaller providers |

| Coalition | Members |
|-----------|---------|

| Competition | New entrants, municipal, cooperatives |
