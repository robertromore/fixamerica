# Artificial Intelligence: Citizen Actions

## Overview

Individual citizens can influence AI governance through multiple channels. This guide provides actionable steps for people at all engagement levels, from basic awareness to deep involvement.

## Immediate Actions

### Learn and Stay Informed

| Action | Time | Impact |
|--------|------|--------|
| Subscribe to AI policy newsletter | 5 min | Stay current |
| Follow key AI policy accounts | 10 min | Real-time updates |
| Read one AI governance explainer | 30 min | Build foundation |
| Explore AI tools yourself | 1 hour | Direct experience |

**Recommended starting points:**
- Stanford HAI AI Index Report (annual overview)
- MIT Technology Review AI coverage
- Import AI newsletter (weekly)
- AI Now Institute reports

### Contact Elected Officials

| Action | When | Template Topics |
|--------|------|-----------------|
| Call your Senators | Ongoing | Support AI legislation |
| Email your Representative | Before key votes | Specific bill support |
| Attend town halls | When available | Raise AI issues |
| Submit written comments | Rulemaking periods | Technical input |

**Sample call script:**
> "I'm calling to urge [Senator/Representative] to support comprehensive AI regulation. I'm concerned about AI discrimination in hiring, lack of transparency, and safety risks. I support legislation that requires AI companies to test their systems, provides transparency to affected individuals, and creates real accountability. This issue matters because [personal connection]. Thank you."

## Civic Engagement

### Comment on Regulations

**Federal opportunities:**

| Agency | Topic | Where to Comment |
|--------|-------|------------------|
| FTC | AI fairness rules | regulations.gov |
| EEOC | Hiring AI guidance | regulations.gov |
| NIST | AI standards | nist.gov |
| FDA | Medical AI | regulations.gov |

**How to comment effectively:**
1. Read the proposed rule summary
2. Identify issues that affect you
3. Provide specific, concrete input
4. Share personal experience if relevant
5. Submit before deadline

**Sample comment structure:**
```
Re: [Docket number]

I am [brief self-identification]. I am commenting on [specific provision].

[Personal experience or expertise relevant to issue]

[Specific recommendation with reasoning]

[Impact if recommendation not adopted]

Thank you for considering my input.
```

## Consumer Actions

### Exercise Your Rights

| Right | Where Available | How |
|-------|-----------------|-----|
| Data access | Many states (CCPA, etc.) | Submit request |
| Opt-out of profiling | Where provided | Use opt-out tools |
| Request explanations | Limited contexts | Ask for basis of decisions |
| File complaints | FTC, state AG | Document and report |

### Make Informed Choices

| Action | Context | Impact |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Read AI disclosures | Products, services | Informed consent |
| Choose privacy-respecting options | When available | Market signal |
| Report AI harms | When experienced | Documentation |
| Share experiences | Community | Collective knowledge |

### Document AI Encounters

**When AI affects you:**
1. Note what system was used
2. Save any outputs or decisions
3. Request explanation if available
4. Report to relevant agency if harmful
5. Consider sharing story (if comfortable)
