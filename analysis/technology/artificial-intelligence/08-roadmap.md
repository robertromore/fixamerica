# Artificial Intelligence: Reform Roadmap

## Overview

This roadmap outlines a realistic pathway for AI governance reform, sequencing actions based on feasibility, urgency, and interdependencies. The approach recognizes political constraints while maintaining ambitious goals.

## Strategic Context

### Current Political Window

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|------------|
| Bipartisan concern | High - unusual agreement on need for action |
| Industry positioning | Mixed - some support "smart" regulation |
| Public attention | High - ChatGPT raised awareness |
| International pressure | Growing - EU AI Act creates urgency |
| Major incidents | None yet catastrophic - but anticipated |

### Key Uncertainties

- Timing of next election cycles
- Major AI incidents
- Technology trajectory
- International developments
- Economic conditions

## Decision Points

### Congressional Strategy

**If bipartisan bill stalls:**
- Pursue executive action maximally
- State-level prioritization
- Election cycle strategy
- Prepare for window after incident

**If partial bill passes:**
- Implement what's possible
- Use as foundation
- Continue advocacy for expansion
- State supplementation

### Technology Trajectory

**If capabilities advance faster than expected:**
- Accelerate frontier AI provisions
- Emergency authority consideration
- International coordination urgency
- Public communication

**If AI winter materializes:**
- Focus on existing harms
- Algorithmic accountability priority
- Maintain institutional capacity
- Prepare for next wave

### Political Scenarios

**Favorable scenario (unified government + political will):**
- Comprehensive legislation possible
- New agency creation
- Significant investment
- International leadership

**Moderate scenario (divided government, bipartisan interest):**
- Targeted legislation feasible
- Agency action primary vehicle
- State action important
- Incremental progress

**Unfavorable scenario (political gridlock):**
- Executive action only
- State-level focus
- Civil society pressure
- Wait for window

## Contingency Plans

### If Major Incident Occurs

1. Rapid policy response prepared
2. Coalition messaging aligned
3. Legislative language ready
4. Not waste crisis on weak reform

### If International Framework Emerges

1. Participate constructively
2. Ensure US interests represented
3. Align domestic framework
4. Lead where possible

### If Technology Stalls

1. Focus on existing harms
2. Maintain institutional capacity
3. Build foundation for future
4. Address current discrimination, safety
