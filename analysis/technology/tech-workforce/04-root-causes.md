# Technology Workforce: Root Causes

## Overview

Persistent technology workforce challenges stem from structural educational gaps, immigration system failures, industry practices, cultural factors, and policy inadequacies.

## Educational Factors

### K-12 Pipeline

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| CS teacher shortage | Can't teach what don't have |
| Unequal school funding | Access disparities |
| Tracking | Early sorting limits access |
| Stereotype threat | Discourages underrepresented |

### Higher Education

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Capacity constraints | Can't admit all qualified |
| Faculty shortage | Can't expand programs |
| Cost barriers | Debt limits access |
| Credential requirements | Exclude capable workers |

### Skills Mismatch

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Curriculum lag | Doesn't match industry |
| Theory vs. practice | Gap between education and work |
| Rapid change | Skills quickly outdated |
| Specialization | Narrow preparation |

## Immigration System Failures

### H-1B Limitations

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Low cap | Can't meet demand |
| Lottery system | Random, not merit-based |
| Employer tied | Limits mobility |
| Processing delays | Uncertainty |

### Green Card Backlog

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Per-country caps | Discriminatory delays |
| Employment categories | Long queues |
| Policy gridlock | No reform in decades |
| Uncertainty | Discourages best talent |

### System Design

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Employer-based | Worker dependent |
| Complex process | Expensive, slow |
| No startup path | Entrepreneurs excluded |
| Family separation | Humanitarian issues |
