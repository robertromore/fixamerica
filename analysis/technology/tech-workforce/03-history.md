# Technology Workforce: History

## Overview

The technology workforce has evolved from a small group of specialists to a major sector of the economy, with policies and practices developing in response to industry growth, skills needs, and social challenges.

## Timeline

| Period | Development |
|--------|-------------|
| 1940s | First programmers (often women) |
| 1950s | Computing professionalization |
| 1960s | CS departments established |
| 1960s | Industry concentration begins |

## Immigration Policy Evolution

### H-1B Development

| Year | Development |
|------|-------------|
| 1990 | H-1B visa created |
| 1998 | Cap raised to 115,000 |
| 2000 | Cap raised to 195,000 |
| 2004 | Cap reduced to 65,000 |
| 2004 | 20,000 master's exemption added |
| Since 2004 | Various reform attempts failed |

### Green Card Backlog

| Period | Development |
|--------|-------------|

| 2020s | Multi-decade waits |

## Lessons from History

### What Worked

| Success | Evidence |
|---------|----------|
| H-1B creation | Addressed skills needs |
| CS education expansion | More graduates |
| Diversity awareness | Issue on agenda |
| Remote work adoption | Expanded access |

### What Failed

| Failure | Evidence |
|---------|----------|
| Per-country caps | Severe backlogs |
| Diversity efforts | Slow progress |
| K-12 CS access | Still unequal |
| Worker classification | Ongoing issues |
