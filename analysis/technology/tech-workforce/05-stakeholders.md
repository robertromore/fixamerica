# Technology Workforce: Stakeholders

## Overview

Technology workforce policy involves diverse stakeholders with varying interests, power, and stakes in workforce outcomes.

## Tech Workers

### Current Workers

| Group | Interest |
|-------|----------|
| Engineers/developers | Pay, conditions, mobility |
| Support staff | Classification, benefits |
| Contractors | Full-time conversion, benefits |
| Remote workers | Flexibility, infrastructure |

**Advocacy capacity**: Growing (organizing, social media)

### Underrepresented Workers

| Group | Interest |
|-------|----------|
| Women | Inclusion, advancement, safety |
| Black workers | Access, retention, advancement |
| Hispanic workers | Access, retention |
| LGBTQ+ workers | Inclusion, benefits |
| Workers with disabilities | Accessibility, accommodation |
| Older workers | Age discrimination |

**Advocacy capacity**: Moderate (ERGs, advocacy groups)

### Aspiring Workers

| Group | Interest |
|-------|----------|
| Students | Education, opportunity |
| Career changers | Pathways, training |
| Displaced workers | Reskilling, transition |
| International | Immigration, access |

**Advocacy capacity**: Low (diffuse, unorganized)
