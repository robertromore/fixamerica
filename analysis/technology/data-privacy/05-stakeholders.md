# Data Privacy: Stakeholders

## Overview

The data privacy debate involves diverse stakeholders with vastly different resources and interests. Understanding this landscape is essential for building coalitions and anticipating opposition to reform.

## Industry Stakeholders

### Big Tech Platforms

| Company | Data Interests | Position on Regulation |
|---------|----------------|------------------------|
| **Google** | Ad targeting, product improvement | Supports preemption; opposes strong rights |
| **Meta** | Ad targeting, core business model | Publicly supports "regulation"; opposes strong provisions |
| **Amazon** | E-commerce, AWS, advertising | Opposes comprehensive regulation |
| **Apple** | Privacy as brand; limited ad reliance | Supports regulation (competitive advantage) |
| **Microsoft** | Enterprise focus; some consumer | Moderate support for regulation |

### Data Brokers

| Category | Major Players | Interest |
|----------|---------------|----------|
| **Marketing data** | Acxiom, Oracle, Experian | Oppose any restrictions |
| **People search** | Spokeo, BeenVerified, Whitepages | Oppose opt-out requirements |
| **Risk/fraud** | LexisNexis, TransUnion | Carve-outs for fraud prevention |
| **Location** | Venntel, Babel Street, X-Mode | Oppose location restrictions |

**Industry associations:**
- Data & Marketing Association
- Interactive Advertising Bureau
- Network Advertising Initiative

### Advertising Industry

| Actor | Interest |
|-------|----------|
| Ad agencies | Targeting capabilities |
| Ad tech | Data access and use |
| Publishers | Revenue from data |
| Brands | Marketing effectiveness |

## Affected Communities

### General Public

| Segment | Concern Level | Awareness |
|---------|---------------|-----------|
| General population | High concern | Low understanding |
| Younger users | Moderate | Higher awareness |
| Parents | High (children) | Moderate |
| Older adults | High | Low |
| Marginalized communities | High | Variable |

### Specific Affected Groups

| Group | Specific Concerns |
|-------|-------------------|
| **Survivors of abuse** | Location tracking, stalking |
| **Immigrants** | Government access to data |
| **LGBTQ+ individuals** | Outing, discrimination |
| **Activists/journalists** | Surveillance, source protection |
| **Job seekers** | Employment decisions |
| **Patients** | Health data sharing |

### Small Businesses

| Concern | Position |
|---------|----------|
| Compliance costs | Want clarity, reasonable requirements |
| Data access | Need some data for operations |
| Competition | Disadvantaged vs. big platforms |
