# Surveillance Technology: History

## Overview

Government surveillance has evolved from analog methods to digital technologies, with legal frameworks consistently lagging behind technological capabilities.

## Timeline

### Pre-Digital Era (Pre-1970s)

| Period | Development |
|--------|-------------|
| Early 20th century | Wiretapping emerges |
| 1920s-30s | FBI surveillance programs |
| 1940s-50s | Wartime and Red Scare surveillance |
| 1960s | COINTELPRO, civil rights surveillance |

### Early Legal Framework (1970s)

| Year | Development |
|------|-------------|
| 1967 | *Katz v. United States* - reasonable expectation of privacy |
| 1968 | Title III wiretap law enacted |
| 1974 | Privacy Act passed |
| 1975-76 | Church Committee investigations |
| 1978 | FISA enacted |

### Digital Transition (1980s-1990s)

| Year | Development |
|------|-------------|
| 1986 | Electronic Communications Privacy Act (ECPA) |
| 1986 | Stored Communications Act |
| 1994 | CALEA - wiretap capabilities mandated |
| 1996 | Encryption export controls |
| 1999 | FBI Carnivore system revealed |

### Post-9/11 Expansion (2001-2013)

| Year | Development |
|------|-------------|
| 2001 | PATRIOT Act - expanded surveillance |
| 2002 | Total Information Awareness proposed |
| 2005 | NSA warrantless wiretapping revealed |
| 2007 | Protect America Act |
| 2008 | FISA Amendments Act - Section 702 |

### Snowden Era (2013-2016)

| Year | Development |
|------|-------------|
| 2013 | Snowden disclosures |
| 2013 | PRISM, XKeyscore revealed |
| 2015 | USA FREEDOM Act - limited reforms |
| 2015 | *Riley v. California* - phone searches |
| 2016 | Rule 41 amendments |

### Facial Recognition Era (2017-Present)

| Year | Development |
|------|-------------|
| 2017 | Clearview AI begins scraping |
| 2018 | *Carpenter v. United States* - location data |
| 2019 | San Francisco bans facial recognition |
| 2020 | Clearview AI exposed |
| 2020 | Portland comprehensive ban |
| 2021 | Wrongful arrests cases public |
| 2022 | State moratoriums expand |
| 2023-24 | AI integration accelerates |

## Key Legal Developments

### Supreme Court Cases

| Case | Year | Holding |
|------|------|---------|
| *Katz v. United States* | 1967 | Reasonable expectation of privacy |
| *United States v. Jones* | 2012 | GPS tracking requires warrant |
| *Riley v. California* | 2014 | Phone search requires warrant |
| *Carpenter v. United States* | 2018 | CSLI requires warrant |

| Law | Year | Significance |
|-----|------|--------------|

## Scandal and Reform Cycles

### Pattern

| Stage | Characteristics |
|-------|-----------------|
| Secret expansion | Technology deployed without oversight |
| Disclosure | Leak, lawsuit, or investigation |
| Public outrage | Media attention, advocacy |
| Limited reform | Partial restrictions |
| Renewed expansion | New technology, new loopholes |

### Examples

| Scandal | Year | Reform |
|---------|------|--------|
| Church Committee | 1975-76 | FISA |
| NSA warrantless | 2005 | FISA Amendments |
| Snowden | 2013 | USA FREEDOM |
| Clearview AI | 2020 | Local bans |

## Civil Rights Surveillance History

### Documented Programs

| Program | Target | Period |
|---------|--------|--------|
| COINTELPRO | Civil rights, antiwar | 1956-1971 |
| NSA watchlists | Antiwar activists | 1960s-70s |
| NYPD surveillance | Muslims | 2000s-2010s |
| Protest monitoring | BLM, others | 2010s-present |

### Patterns

| Pattern | Evidence |
|---------|----------|
| Target marginalized communities | Historical record |
| Justify with security claims | Consistent |
| Minimal accountability | Rare prosecutions |
| Eventual disclosure | Decades later |

## Lessons from History

### Consistent Themes

| Theme | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| Technology outpaces law | Repeated pattern |
| Secrecy enables abuse | Historical record |
| Reform requires disclosure | Every major reform |
| Expansion after reform | Documented cycles |

### Unlearned Lessons

| Lesson | Current Status |
|--------|----------------|
| Transparency needed | Still secret |
| Oversight essential | Still inadequate |
| Civil rights at risk | Still targeted |
| Technology not neutral | Bias documented |
