# Open Internet: Opposition

## Overview

Opposition to open internet regulation comes primarily from broadband providers, free-market advocates, and some conservative political actors. Understanding their arguments and tactics is essential for effective advocacy.

## Primary Opposition

### Internet Service Providers

#### Major Players

| Company | Opposition Activities |
|---------|----------------------|
| **Comcast** | Major lobbying, litigation |
| **AT&T** | Political donations, lobbying |
| **Verizon** | Brought successful lawsuit |
| **Charter** | Trade association support |

#### Industry Arguments

| Argument | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Investment harm** | Regulation reduces network investment |
| **Innovation** | Rules stifle new business models |
| **Heavy-handed** | Title II is utility regulation |
| **No problem** | Market works without rules |
| **State authority** | Federal rules preempt states |

#### Lobbying Activities

| Activity | Scale |
|----------|-------|
| **Federal lobbying** | $100M+ annually combined |
| **Campaign contributions** | Millions to both parties |
| **State lobbying** | Extensive local presence |
| **Grassroots astroturf** | Funded advocacy groups |
| **Advertising** | Millions on public campaigns |

### Trade Associations

## Arguments Against Net Neutrality

### Economic Arguments

| Argument | Counter-Evidence |
|----------|------------------|
| **Investment dropped under Title II** | Investment actually increased |
| **Price controls harm** | Net neutrality isn't price control |
| **Market self-corrects** | Markets remain concentrated |
| **Consumers can switch** | Most have 1-2 choices |

### Legal Arguments

| Argument | Counter |
|----------|---------|
| **FCC lacks authority** | Courts have upheld Title II |
| **Fifth Amendment takings** | No compensation required |
| **First Amendment** | ISPs not traditional speakers |
| **Administrative law** | Proper rulemaking followed |

### Technical Arguments

| Argument | Counter |
|----------|---------|
| **Traffic management needed** | Rules allow reasonable management |
| **Network investment** | Continued under Title II |
| **Specialized services** | Rules permit non-BIAS services |
| **Quality differentiation** | Can compete on speed, price |

## Astroturfing Examples

### Documented Cases

| Campaign | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Fake FCC comments** | Millions of fraudulent anti-net neutrality comments |
| **Industry-funded groups** | "Consumer" groups funded by ISPs |
| **Social media manipulation** | Coordinated messaging |
| **Identity theft** | Names used without permission |

### Red Flags

| Indicator | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Identical text** | Mass-submitted comments |
| **Implausible submitters** | Dead people, fake emails |
| **Industry funding** | Hidden financial support |
| **Misleading names** | "Freedom" groups opposing freedom |

## Weaknesses in Opposition

### Public Opinion

| Finding | Source |
|---------|--------|
| **83% support net neutrality** | University of Maryland poll |
| **Bipartisan support** | Majorities of both parties |
| **Issue understanding** | Public gets core concept |

### Evidence Problems

| Claim | Reality |
|-------|---------|
| **Investment dropped** | Actually increased under Title II |
| **Market competitive** | Most areas have 1-2 options |
| **No harm without rules** | Documented violations |

### Internal Contradictions

| Contradiction | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| **Won't block but oppose rules** | Why oppose if not planning violations? |
| **Support transparency** | But oppose disclosure requirements |
| **Free market** | While accepting government subsidies |

## Countering Opposition

### Fact-Checking

| Claim | Response |
|-------|----------|
| **Investment harm** | Data shows increase under Title II |
| **Heavy-handed** | Rules are targeted, not utility-style |
| **No problem** | Documented blocking, throttling |

### Building Support

| Strategy | Approach |
|----------|----------|
| **Public education** | Explain issues simply |
| **Business coalition** | Small business support |
| **Grassroots** | Mobilize authentic voices |
| **Research** | Produce counter-evidence |

### Political Strategy

| Approach | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Electoral pressure** | Make net neutrality campaign issue |
| **State action** | Work around federal opposition |
| **Industry splits** | Tech vs. ISP interests |
| **Bipartisan framing** | Small business, free speech |
