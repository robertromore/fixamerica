# Open Internet: Stakeholders

## Overview

The open internet debate involves diverse stakeholders with different interests, resources, and levels of influence. Understanding these stakeholders is essential for effective advocacy and policy design.

## Primary Stakeholders

### Internet Service Providers

#### Major Broadband ISPs

| Company | Position | Influence |
|---------|----------|-----------|
| **Comcast** | Opposes net neutrality regulation | Massive lobbying, media ownership |
| **Charter** | Opposes net neutrality | Major cable operator |
| **AT&T** | Opposes net neutrality | Large lobbying, political donations |
| **Verizon** | Opposes net neutrality | Brought lawsuit striking rules |
| **T-Mobile** | More neutral | Mobile focus |

**Interests:**

- Maximize revenue from network control
- Charge content providers for access
- Maintain investment flexibility
- Avoid common carrier regulation
- Preserve vertical integration benefits

#### Trade Associations

| Association | Members | Position |
|-------------|---------|----------|
| **NCTA** | Cable companies | Anti-net neutrality |
| **USTelecom** | Telecom companies | Anti-net neutrality |
| **CTIA** | Wireless carriers | Anti-net neutrality |
| **ACA Connects** | Smaller cable | Mixed |

### Internet Users

#### General Consumers

| Group | Primary Concerns |
|-------|------------------|
| **General public** | Speed, price, access |
| **Cord-cutters** | Streaming access |
| **Remote workers** | Reliability, speed |
| **Gamers** | Latency, throttling |
| **Rural users** | Availability |

**Interests:**

- Affordable, reliable service
- Access to all content

- Privacy protection

### Content and Edge Providers

#### Major Tech Companies

| Company | Position | Rationale |
|---------|----------|-----------|
| **Google** | Pro-net neutrality | Depends on open access |
| **Netflix** | Pro-net neutrality | Streaming depends on access |
| **Amazon** | Pro-net neutrality | E-commerce, AWS |
| **Meta** | Pro-net neutrality | Platform depends on access |
| **Microsoft** | Pro-net neutrality | Cloud, services |
| **Apple** | Generally supportive | Services growth |

**Interests:**

- Equal access to consumers
- No ISP gatekeeping
- Predictable costs
- Innovation freedom

#### Small Businesses and Startups

## Coalition Dynamics

### Pro-Net Neutrality Coalition

| Member | Motivation |
|--------|------------|
| **Consumer groups** | User protection |
| **Tech companies** | Business model |
| **Startups** | Competition |
| **Civil liberties** | Free speech |
| **Public interest** | Open access |

**Strengths:** Broad support, public opinion, tech resources
**Weaknesses:** Less lobbying money, fragmented

### Anti-Net Neutrality Coalition

| Member | Motivation |
|--------|------------|
| **ISPs** | Revenue, flexibility |
| **Some conservatives** | Anti-regulation |
| **Free market think tanks** | Ideology |

**Strengths:** Concentrated resources, political access
**Weaknesses:** Limited public support
