# Fair Housing: Resources

## Legal Framework

### Federal Statutes

**Fair Housing Act**

- Citation: 42 U.S.C. 3601-3619
- Enacted: 1968
- Major amendments: 1988 (Fair Housing Amendments Act)
- Prohibits discrimination based on race, color, religion, national origin, sex, familial status, disability

**Civil Rights Act of 1866**

- Citation: 42 U.S.C. 1981-1982
- Prohibits racial discrimination in property transactions
- No exemptions (broader than FHA in some respects)

**Equal Credit Opportunity Act**

- Citation: 15 U.S.C. 1691 et seq.
- Prohibits discrimination in lending
- Enforced by CFPB and other regulators

**Home Mortgage Disclosure Act**

- Citation: 12 U.S.C. 2801 et seq.
- Requires lending data disclosure
- Key tool for fair lending analysis

**Community Reinvestment Act**

- Citation: 12 U.S.C. 2901 et seq.
- Requires banks serve entire communities
- Addresses lending discrimination

### Key Regulations

**HUD Fair Housing Regulations**

- 24 C.F.R. Part 100 - Fair Housing Act implementation
- 24 C.F.R. Part 5 - AFFH requirements
- 24 C.F.R. Part 115 - Substantial equivalency

**Current AFFH Status**

- 2015 rule promulgated
- 2020 rule repealed by Trump administration
- Biden administration restoring; check current status

### Key Court Decisions

**Foundational Cases**

| Case | Citation | Holding |
|------|----------|---------|
| *Jones v. Alfred H. Mayer Co.* | 392 U.S. 409 (1968) | 1866 Civil Rights Act applies to private housing |
| *Trafficante v. Metropolitan Life* | 409 U.S. 205 (1972) | Broad standing under FHA |
| *Hills v. Gautreaux* | 425 U.S. 284 (1976) | Metropolitan-wide remedy appropriate |
| *Village of Arlington Heights v. MHDC* | 429 U.S. 252 (1977) | Intent required for constitutional claims |

**Modern Era Cases**

| Case | Citation | Holding |
|------|----------|---------|
| *Huntington v. NAACP* | 488 U.S. 15 (1988) | Disparate impact applies to zoning |
| *Texas Dept. Housing v. Inclusive Communities* | 576 U.S. 519 (2015) | Disparate impact valid under FHA |
| *Bank of America v. City of Miami* | 581 U.S. __ (2017) | Cities have standing for fair lending |

## Filing a Complaint

### With HUD

**How to File**

- Online: hud.gov/fairhousing/fileacomplaint
- Phone: 1-800-669-9777
- Mail: Local HUD office
- Deadline: 1 year from discrimination

**What Happens**

| Stage | Timeline |
|-------|----------|
| Intake | Immediate |
| Investigation | 100 days (target) |
| Determination | After investigation |
| Conciliation or charge | If discrimination found |

### With State/Local Agency

**Advantages of State/Local Filing**

| Advantage | Explanation |
|-----------|-------------|
| Local knowledge | Agency knows market |
| Additional protections | State law may cover more |
| Faster processing | Sometimes |
| Dual filing | Can file with both |

### Private Lawsuit

**When to Sue Privately**

| Consideration | Factor |
|---------------|--------|
| Administrative slow | Private suit may be faster |
| Major damages | Private suit for full recovery |
| Injunctive relief | Courts have broad authority |
| Class action | Pattern cases |

**Finding a Lawyer**

| Resource | Contact |

| Local fair housing organization | See directory above |
| Bar association | Lawyer referral services |
| Legal aid | If income-eligible |
