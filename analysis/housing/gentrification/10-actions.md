# Gentrification: What Citizens Can Do

## Overview

Combating displacement and promoting equitable development requires action at multiple levels: individual choices, community organizing, and political engagement. This document outlines concrete steps that concerned citizens can take based on their position, resources, and community context. Whether you are a long-time resident facing displacement, a newcomer concerned about your impact, or an advocate working on housing justice, there are meaningful actions available.

## For Long-Time Residents

### Know Your Rights

| Right | Action |
|-------|--------|
| Lease terms | Read and understand your lease completely |
| Eviction protections | Research just cause laws in your jurisdiction |
| Rent increase limits | Know if rent stabilization applies to your unit |
| Habitability | Document maintenance issues, report to housing agency |
| Retaliation protection | Know what constitutes illegal retaliation |

**Resources**

- Contact local tenant rights organization
- Attend know-your-rights training
- Keep copies of all landlord communications
- Document property conditions with photos/video

### Build Community Power

| Action | How |
|--------|-----|
| Talk to neighbors | Share concerns, identify common issues |
| Form tenant association | Organize building or neighborhood |
| Attend community meetings | Participate in planning, zoning decisions |
| Connect with organizations | Join local anti-displacement coalition |
| Register and vote | Participate in local elections |

### Respond to Pressure

| Situation | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| Buyout offer | Get legal advice before signing anything |
| Rent increase | Check if legal; challenge if excessive |
| Eviction notice | Contact lawyer immediately; don't move without advice |
| Harassment | Document everything; report to enforcement agency |
| Building sale | Inquire about TOPA rights if available |

### Build Housing Stability

| Action | Benefit |
|--------|---------|
| Pay rent on time | Avoid nonpayment eviction grounds |
| Document everything | Create paper trail for disputes |
| Build emergency savings | Buffer against income shocks |
| Explore ownership options | CLT, co-op, shared equity programs |
| Know assistance programs | Emergency rental assistance, legal aid |

## For Newcomers/Gentrifiers

### Be a Good Neighbor

| Action | Why It Matters |
|--------|----------------|
| Learn neighborhood history | Understand what you're entering |
| Introduce yourself | Build relationships with long-time residents |
| Support existing businesses | Patronize established local businesses |
| Attend community events | Participate in existing community life |
| Listen more than speak | Defer to long-time residents on neighborhood issues |

### Minimize Displacement Impact

| Action | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Don't overpay | Inflated prices accelerate gentrification |
| Avoid displacement-causing landlords | Research before renting |
| Support tenant neighbors | Advocate for their housing stability |
| Don't Airbnb your unit | Keeps housing in long-term market |
| Challenge newcomer-only spaces | Push businesses to serve all residents |

### Support Anti-Displacement

| Action | Mechanism |
|--------|-----------|
| Join anti-displacement groups | Bring resources to the movement |
| Donate to CLTs | Support community ownership |
| Vote for tenant protections | Use political voice for housing justice |
| Speak to other newcomers | Organize gentrifiers for anti-displacement |
| Accept development tradeoffs | Support inclusionary requirements |

## Taking Action Today

### Immediate Steps

1. **Learn**: Read about gentrification in your community
2. **Connect**: Find local tenant/housing justice organization
3. **Attend**: Go to a community meeting on housing
4. **Register**: Make sure you're registered to vote
5. **Talk**: Discuss housing with neighbors, friends, family

### Building Toward Longer-Term Impact

| Timeframe | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| This week | Identify one organization to contact |
| This month | Attend one meeting or event |
| This quarter | Commit to regular involvement |
| This year | Take on a specific role or responsibility |
| Ongoing | Build relationships, skills, and power |
