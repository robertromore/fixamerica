# Rural Housing: Citizen Actions

## Overview

Rural housing challenges require advocacy at federal, state, and local levels. While the systemic issues demand major policy changes, individual citizens, community organizations, and local governments can take meaningful actions to improve rural housing conditions. This document outlines concrete steps for different audiences.

## Individual Actions

### Understand the Issues

**Learn about rural housing**:

1. **Read the data**
   - Review Housing Assistance Council reports at [ruralhome.org](https://ruralhome.org/)
   - Examine your county's housing data through Census Bureau
   - Understand local housing conditions and needs

2. **Know the programs**
   - Learn about USDA Rural Housing programs
   - Understand Section 502, 515, and 521 programs
   - Know what tribal housing programs exist if relevant

3. **Connect issues**
   - Understand housing-healthcare connections
   - Recognize housing-economic development links
   - See broadband-housing relationships

### Advocate for Change

**Federal advocacy**:

1. **Contact Congress**
   - Call or write senators and representatives
   - Attend town halls and raise rural housing issues
   - Share personal stories about rural housing challenges
   - Request support for USDA Rural Housing funding increases

2. **Key asks for federal legislators**:
   - Increase Section 502 Direct Loan funding
   - Restore Section 515 new construction
   - Increase NAHASDA appropriations
   - Reform manufactured housing financing

**Sample talking points**:

> "Rural housing programs have been underfunded for decades. Section 502 direct loans serve only 7,000 families per year when millions are eligible. I urge you to support increased funding for USDA Rural Housing in the next appropriations bill."
>
> "Manufactured housing is the affordable housing of rural America, but residents face predatory financing and no protection from lot rent increases. Please support the [Manufactured Housing Community Protection Act]."

**State advocacy**:

1. **Contact state legislators**
   - Support manufactured housing tenant protections
   - Advocate for state housing trust funds with rural set-asides
   - Push for source of income protection laws

2. **Engage state agencies**
   - Comment on state housing finance agency policies
   - Advocate for rural LIHTC set-asides
   - Request rural representation on housing boards

**Local advocacy**:

1. **Attend local government meetings**
   - Town councils, county commissions
   - Planning and zoning meetings
   - Housing authority board meetings

2. **Advocate for**:
   - Manufactured housing community protections
   - Building code enforcement
   - Infrastructure investment in housing areas

### Support Your Community

**If you own rural property**:

1. **Maintain your property**
   - Prevent deterioration that affects neighborhood
   - Use available programs (Section 504 for repairs)
   - Consider energy efficiency improvements

2. **Support housing development**
   - Welcome affordable housing in your community
   - Counter NIMBY arguments with facts
   - Attend hearings to support housing projects

**If you rent in rural areas**:

1. **Know your rights**
   - Understand lease terms and tenant protections
   - Document housing conditions
   - Report code violations through proper channels

2. **Organize with neighbors**
   - Form tenant associations
   - Share information about rights and resources
   - Collectively advocate for improvements

### Financial Actions

**Support organizations**:

1. **Donate to rural housing organizations**
   - Housing Assistance Council
   - Local community development organizations
   - Habitat for Humanity rural affiliates

2. **Bank and invest responsibly**
   - Use community banks and credit unions active in rural lending
   - Consider CDFIs for savings
   - Support mission-driven lending

**Volunteer**:

1. **Build housing**
   - Habitat for Humanity builds
   - Self-help housing programs
   - Disaster recovery rebuilding

2. **Provide expertise**
   - Board service for housing organizations
   - Pro bono legal or professional services
   - Housing counseling volunteer work

## Community Organization Actions

### Build Local Capacity

**Establish or strengthen housing organizations**:

1. **Create housing committees**
   - Recruit diverse membership
   - Include affected residents
   - Connect to regional organizations

2. **Develop organizational capacity**
   - Seek technical assistance from HAC, LISC, RCAC
   - Train leaders in housing development
   - Build fundraising capacity

3. **Network regionally**
   - Connect with other rural housing organizations
   - Join state housing coalitions
   - Participate in national networks

### Assess and Plan

**Conduct housing needs assessment**:

1. **Gather data**
   - Census and American Community Survey data
   - Windshield surveys of housing conditions
   - Resident interviews and focus groups

2. **Identify priorities**
   - Worst housing conditions
   - Populations with greatest needs
   - Gaps in existing services

3. **Develop community housing plan**
   - Set goals and priorities
   - Identify resources and partners
   - Create implementation timeline

### Develop Housing

**Pursue housing development**:

1. **Apply for USDA programs**
   - Section 515 development (if funding restored)
   - Section 523 self-help housing
   - Section 514/516 farmworker housing

2. **Access LIHTC**
   - Partner with experienced developers
   - Apply to state housing finance agency
   - Emphasize rural housing need

3. **Develop affordable homeownership**
   - Self-help housing programs
   - Community land trusts
   - Shared equity models

### Organize Manufactured Housing Communities

**For MH community residents**:

1. **Form resident association**
   - Identify leaders
   - Build membership
   - Establish regular meetings

2. **Pursue community ownership**
   - Contact ROC USA for assessment
   - Organize for purchase opportunity
   - Develop cooperative governance

3. **Advocate for protections**
   - Document rent increases and conditions
   - Organize for state legislation
   - Share stories with media and policymakers

### Address Rural Homelessness

**Local homelessness response**:

1. **Count and identify**
   - Go beyond point-in-time counts
   - Connect with schools (McKinney-Vento)
   - Build by-name list where possible

2. **Develop services**
   - Prevention programs (emergency assistance)
   - Mobile outreach for dispersed populations
   - Housing navigation services

3. **Create housing pathways**
   - Rapid rehousing programs
   - Permanent supportive housing
   - Host home programs

## Local Government Actions

### Planning and Zoning

**Adopt housing-supportive policies**:

1. **Allow manufactured housing**
   - Permit in residential zones
   - Remove discriminatory restrictions
   - Allow manufactured housing communities

2. **Enable affordable development**
   - Reduce lot size minimums
   - Allow accessory dwelling units
   - Streamline approval processes

3. **Designate housing priority areas**
   - Identify sites for affordable housing
   - Provide infrastructure to priority sites
   - Pre-approve housing types

### Code Enforcement

**Improve housing quality through codes**:

1. **Establish and enforce codes**
   - Adopt reasonable building codes
   - Fund enforcement staff
   - Balance enforcement with assistance

2. **Target worst conditions**
   - Identify severely substandard properties
   - Prioritize health and safety hazards
   - Connect enforcement with repair resources

3. **Address rental housing**
   - Establish rental registration
   - Conduct regular inspections
   - Hold landlords accountable

### Infrastructure Investment

**Support housing through infrastructure**:

1. **Water and sewer**
   - Extend service to housing areas
   - Maintain existing systems
   - Plan for housing growth

2. **Broadband**
   - Support community broadband
   - Require broadband in new developments
   - Connect to federal programs

3. **Roads and transportation**
   - Maintain roads to housing areas
   - Consider transit where feasible
   - Connect housing to services

### Use Available Resources

**Access federal and state programs**:

1. **CDBG and HOME**
   - Apply for housing rehabilitation programs
   - Fund down payment assistance
   - Support housing development

2. **USDA programs**
   - Promote Section 502 and 504 programs
   - Host USDA information sessions
   - Support applications

3. **State resources**
   - Housing trust fund applications
   - State community development programs
   - Weatherization partnerships

### Protect Vulnerable Residents

**Adopt protective policies**:

1. **Manufactured housing protections**
   - Require long notice for community closure
   - Support resident purchase opportunities
   - Regulate lot rent increases where allowed

2. **Tenant protections**
   - Just cause eviction requirements
   - Right to counsel in eviction
   - Relocation assistance requirements

3. **Anti-displacement**
   - Monitor housing market changes
   - Protect long-term residents
   - Plan for affordable housing preservation

## Tribal Government Actions

### Exercise Sovereignty

**Use tribal authority for housing**:

1. **Develop tribal housing codes**
   - Appropriate for tribal context
   - Address quality and safety
   - Enable traditional housing forms

2. **Strengthen tribal housing authority**
   - Adequate staffing and resources
   - Professional development
   - Regional coordination

3. **Land use planning**
   - Housing-supportive land policies
   - Address fractionated ownership
   - Long-term land planning

### Maximize NAHASDA

**Optimize federal resources**:

1. **Full program utilization**
   - Apply for all eligible funds
   - Meet all program requirements
   - Document unmet needs

2. **Innovative uses**
   - Down payment assistance
   - Rehabilitation programs
   - Homelessness prevention

3. **Advocate for increases**
   - Testify before Congress
   - Document funding shortfalls
   - Join national advocacy efforts

### Address Unique Challenges

**Tribal-specific solutions**:

1. **Land title resolution**
   - Work with BIA on title clearing
   - Explore alternative tenure
   - Document barriers

2. **Infrastructure coordination**
   - Coordinate with IHS on water/sewer
   - Align BIA road improvements
   - Integrate utility planning

3. **Climate adaptation**
   - Assess climate risks to housing
   - Plan for resilient construction
   - Address flooding, permafrost, fire

## Coalition Building

### Form Regional Coalitions

**Regional rural housing coalitions**:

1. **Identify partners**
   - Housing organizations
   - Healthcare providers (housing-health connection)
   - Economic development entities
   - Faith communities

2. **Develop shared agenda**
   - Common policy priorities
   - Coordinated advocacy
   - Shared resources

3. **Execute joint campaigns**
   - State legislative advocacy
   - Federal appropriations push
   - Public awareness campaigns

### Connect to Healthcare Advocates

**Housing-health partnerships**:

1. **Partner with rural health systems**
   - Document housing-health connections
   - Joint advocacy for rural investment
   - Care coordination including housing

2. **Engage public health**
   - Housing as social determinant of health
   - Healthy housing initiatives
   - Data sharing on housing conditions

### Build Political Power

**Long-term power building**:

1. **Voter engagement**
   - Register rural housing residents
   - Educate on housing issues
   - Non-partisan civic engagement

2. **Candidate education**
   - Candidate questionnaires on rural housing
   - Candidate forums
   - Post-election accountability

3. **Media engagement**
   - Local media coverage of housing issues
   - Op-eds and letters to editor
   - Social media campaigns
