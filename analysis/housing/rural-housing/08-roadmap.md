# Rural Housing: Implementation Roadmap

## Overview

Transforming rural housing requires a sequenced approach that builds political support, develops organizational capacity, and phases in major program expansions. This roadmap outlines a 10-year implementation strategy with specific milestones, recognizing that rural housing reform must overcome decades of neglect and build from limited existing infrastructure.

## Phase 1: Foundation Building (Years 1-2)

### Political and Advocacy Capacity

**Objective**: Build the coalition and capacity needed for sustained reform

**Key actions**:

1. **National rural housing coalition**
   - Convene stakeholders: Housing Assistance Council, Rural LISC, tribal housing authorities
   - Establish unified policy agenda
   - Coordinate state-level advocacy
   - Timeline: Months 1-12

2. **State advocacy development**
   - Identify lead organizations in 15 priority states
   - Develop state-specific policy agendas
   - Train rural housing advocates
   - Timeline: Months 6-24

3. **Research and data infrastructure**
   - Commission updated rural housing needs assessments
   - Develop rural homelessness counting methodology
   - Create manufactured housing community database
   - Timeline: Months 1-18

4. **Narrative development**
   - Frame rural housing as economic development
   - Document health impacts of housing conditions
   - Highlight successful models
   - Timeline: Months 1-12

### Immediate Administrative Reforms

**Objective**: Improve existing programs without new legislation

**USDA actions**:

1. **Section 502 processing improvement**
   - Establish processing time targets (60 days)
   - Clear application backlogs
   - Technology upgrades for applications
   - Timeline: Months 1-18

2. **Manufactured housing integration**
   - Clarify manufactured housing eligibility
   - Train staff on manufactured housing
   - Partner with manufactured housing lenders
   - Timeline: Months 6-18

3. **Section 515 preservation focus**
   - Inventory at-risk properties
   - Prioritize rehabilitation funding
   - Streamline transfer to mission-driven owners
   - Timeline: Months 1-24

**HUD actions**:

1. **Manufactured Housing Consensus Committee reform**
   - Appoint reform-minded members
   - Prioritize energy efficiency and resilience standards
   - Accelerate rulemaking process
   - Timeline: Months 1-24

2. **Rural fair housing enforcement**
   - Establish rural enforcement priorities
   - Partner with state civil rights agencies
   - Focus on manufactured housing discrimination
   - Timeline: Months 12-24

### Early Legislative Wins

**Objective**: Build momentum with achievable victories

**Priority legislation**:

1. **NAHASDA reauthorization with funding increase**
   - Increase to $1 billion (from $654 million)
   - Bipartisan tribal support
   - Relatively low budget impact
   - Timeline: Year 1

2. **Rural housing study act**
   - Commission GAO study of rural housing needs
   - Build evidence base for larger reforms
   - Low cost, builds support
   - Timeline: Year 1

3. **Section 502 processing reform**
   - Statutory processing time limits
   - Staffing authorization
   - Technology funding
   - Timeline: Year 1-2

## Phase 2: Program Expansion (Years 3-5)

### Major Funding Increases

**Objective**: Scale programs to meet actual need

**Section 502 expansion**:

| Year | Loans | Volume | Increase |
|------|-------|--------|----------|
| Year 3 | 20,000 | $5B | +$3.6B |
| Year 4 | 35,000 | $8B | +$3B |
| Year 5 | 50,000 | $12B | +$4B |

**Implementation requirements**:

- Staff hiring and training
- Technology system upgrades
- Partnership expansion with housing counseling agencies
- Geographic targeting to underserved areas

**Section 515 revival**:

| Year | Preservation | New Construction |
|------|--------------|------------------|
| Year 3 | $300M | $100M |
| Year 4 | $400M | $200M |
| Year 5 | $500M | $300M |

**Implementation requirements**:

- Regulatory streamlining
- Development partnership capacity building
- LIHTC coordination
- Property identification system

**NAHASDA scale-up**:

| Year | Appropriation | Real Increase |
|------|--------------|---------------|
| Year 3 | $1.2B | +$200M |
| Year 4 | $1.5B | +$300M |
| Year 5 | $1.8B | +$300M |

### Manufactured Housing Reform

**Objective**: Transform manufactured housing financing and protections

**Federal legislation (Year 3-4)**:

1. **Manufactured Housing Financing Improvement Act**
   - GSE purchase requirements for manufactured housing loans
   - Chattel loan interest rate caps
   - Consumer disclosure requirements
   - Land-home financing incentives

2. **Community resident protection provisions**
   - Right of first refusal for community purchase
   - Notice requirements for community sale/closure
   - Relocation assistance requirements

**State-level campaign (Years 3-5)**:

- Target 15 states for tenant protection legislation
- Model legislation development and dissemination
- Coalition building with housing advocates
- Industry negotiation where possible

**HUD code update (Year 3-5)**:

- Energy efficiency standards (IECC equivalent)
- Enhanced wind resistance
- Installation standards update
- Timeline: Complete rulemaking by Year 5

### Rural Homelessness Investment

**Objective**: Build rural homeless services infrastructure

**Year 3-5 investments**:

1. **Rural CoC capacity**
   - $100M annual increase for rural CoCs
   - Technical assistance for rural homeless services
   - Mobile outreach funding
   - Prevention emphasis

2. **Counting improvements**
   - Implement alternative enumeration pilots
   - Integrate school homeless data
   - Fund rural research

3. **Housing First adaptation**
   - Rural Housing First toolkit development
   - Scattered-site housing emphasis
   - Transportation integration

### Infrastructure Integration

**Objective**: Connect housing investments with infrastructure

**Actions (Years 3-5)**:

1. **Broadband-housing coordination**
   - Require broadband readiness in USDA-funded housing
   - Co-locate housing and broadband investments
   - Digital equity requirements

2. **Water/sewer alignment**
   - Coordinate USDA housing with EPA water programs
   - IHS coordination for tribal housing
   - Community system investment

3. **Climate resilience integration**
   - Resilience requirements in rehabilitation programs
   - Manufactured housing retrofit funding
   - Flood-zone relocation programs

## Phase 3: Transformation (Years 6-8)

### Full Program Scaling

**Section 502 at scale**:

| Year | Loans | Volume |
|------|-------|--------|
| Year 6 | 65,000 | $15B |
| Year 7 | 80,000 | $18B |
| Year 8 | 100,000 | $22B |

**Implementation at this scale**:

- Regional processing centers fully operational
- Comprehensive lender partnerships
- Integrated technology platform
- Full geographic coverage

**Section 515 revival complete**:

| Year | Preservation | New Construction | Net Change |
|------|--------------|------------------|------------|
| Year 6 | $500M | $400M | +5,000 units |
| Year 7 | $500M | $500M | +7,000 units |
| Year 8 | $500M | $500M | +8,000 units |

**NAHASDA at adequate funding**:

| Year | Appropriation |
|------|--------------|
| Year 6 | $2.0B |
| Year 7 | $2.0B |
| Year 8 | $2.0B |

**Impact**: Begin reducing tribal housing backlog of 68,000 units

### Persistent Poverty Focus

**Objective**: Concentrated investment in highest-need areas

**Targeted initiatives (Years 6-8)**:

1. **Persistent poverty housing initiative**
   - Dedicated $500M annual fund
   - Multi-county regional approaches
   - Integrated housing/health/economic development
   - 5-year community commitments

2. **Colonia improvements**
   - $200M annual infrastructure investment
   - Housing rehabilitation focus
   - Land tenure regularization
   - Service connections

3. **Appalachian housing initiative**
   - $200M annual targeted investment
   - ARC coordination
   - Rehabilitation emphasis
   - Workforce development connection

### Farmworker Housing Transformation

**Objective**: Ensure all farmworkers have decent housing

**Year 6-8 investments**:

1. **Section 514/516 at scale**
   - $300M annual for new construction
   - Geographic coverage of agricultural areas
   - Year-round occupancy flexibility

2. **Employer housing standards**
   - Enhanced federal standards
   - Meaningful enforcement
   - Technical assistance for compliance

3. **Off-farm housing expansion**
   - Community-based farmworker housing
   - Mixed-income developments
   - Transportation integration

## Phase 4: Consolidation and Sustainability (Years 9-10)

### Institutionalizing Reforms

**Objective**: Make rural housing investment permanent

**Legislative actions**:

1. **Rural Housing Act consolidation**
   - Comprehensive reauthorization
   - Mandatory funding components
   - Updated definitions and targeting

2. **NAHASDA permanent authorization**
   - Remove annual appropriations uncertainty
   - Self-determination provisions expanded
   - Adequate funding floor established

3. **Manufactured housing permanent protections**
   - Federal tenant protection standards
   - Permanent GSE requirements
   - Consumer protection provisions

### Evaluation and Adjustment

**Year 9-10 activities**:

1. **Comprehensive program evaluation**
   - Impact assessment of all reforms
   - Cost-effectiveness analysis
   - Geographic coverage review
   - Outcome measurement

2. **Program adjustments**
   - Address identified gaps
   - Refine targeting mechanisms
   - Update standards and requirements
   - Respond to changing conditions

3. **Long-term sustainability planning**
   - Budget projection and planning
   - Workforce development for housing sector
   - Research agenda establishment

### Success Metrics by Year 10

**Quantitative targets**:

| Metric | Baseline | Year 10 Target |
|--------|----------|----------------|
| Section 502 loans/year | 7,000 | 100,000 |
| Section 515 units | 378,000 | 450,000 |
| NAHASDA funding | $654M | $2B |
| Tribal housing backlog | 68,000 | 34,000 |
| Rural substandard rate | 7.2% | 3.5% |
| Farmworker crowding rate | 26% | 10% |

**Qualitative outcomes**:

- Rural housing recognized as national priority
- Manufactured housing financing normalized
- Tribal housing on path to adequacy
- Rural homelessness accurately counted and served
- Climate-resilient rural housing standard

## Implementation Requirements

### Federal Capacity Building

**USDA Rural Housing Service**:

- Staff increase from 2,000 to 4,000
- Technology system modernization ($200M)
- Training and professional development
- Regional reorganization

**BIA/IHS**:

- Land title processing capacity doubled
- Housing coordination improved
- Infrastructure integration

### State and Local Capacity

**State housing finance agencies**:

- Rural housing expertise development
- LIHTC rural enhancement training
- Manufactured housing program capacity

**Local organizations**:

- Community development capacity in underserved areas
- Technical assistance for rural housing development
- Housing counseling network expansion

### Private Sector Alignment

**Lenders**:

- Community bank engagement
- Credit union partnerships
- GSE manufactured housing programs

**Developers**:

- Rural housing development training
- Nonprofit capacity building
- Small-scale development expertise
