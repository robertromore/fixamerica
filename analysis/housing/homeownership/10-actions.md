# Homeownership: Actions

## What Citizens Can Do

Individual citizens can take meaningful action to expand homeownership access and address the racial homeownership gap. This guide provides concrete steps organized by time commitment and skill level.

## For Aspiring Homebuyers

### Preparing for Homeownership

**Financial Preparation**

| Action | Timeline | Impact |
|--------|----------|--------|
| Check credit report for errors | Immediate | Fix errors for higher score |
| Pay down debt strategically | 6-12 months | Lower DTI ratio |
| Save consistently | Ongoing | Build down payment |
| Research assistance programs | Immediate | Find help you may qualify for |
| Complete homebuyer education | 1-2 months | Required for many programs |

**Finding Assistance Programs**

1. **Search your state HFA** - Every state has a housing finance agency with programs
2. **Check local programs** - Cities and counties often have additional assistance
3. **Explore employer programs** - Some employers offer housing help
4. **Look for profession-specific programs** - Teachers, healthcare workers, first responders
5. **Research nonprofit programs** - Habitat for Humanity, community land trusts

**Resources**

| Resource | Purpose | Where to Find |
|----------|---------|---------------|
| HUD-approved counseling | Free homebuyer education | hud.gov/housing/counseling |
| State HFA programs | Down payment assistance | ncsha.org |
| FHA loan information | Low down payment mortgages | hud.gov/fha |
| VA loan eligibility | Zero-down mortgages for veterans | va.gov/housing-assistance |
| USDA loans | Rural housing assistance | rd.usda.gov |

### Protecting Yourself in the Process

**Avoiding Predatory Practices**

| Red Flag | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Pressure to sign quickly | Take time, get second opinion |
| Excessive fees | Compare Good Faith Estimates |
| Adjustable rate without explanation | Understand all terms |
| Discouraged from shopping | Always compare multiple lenders |
| Steering to certain neighborhoods | Choose where you want to live |

**Know Your Rights**

| Right | What It Means |
|-------|---------------|
| Fair housing | Cannot be denied based on race, color, religion, sex, national origin, disability, familial status |
| Equal credit opportunity | Cannot be denied based on race, color, religion, national origin, sex, marital status, age |
| Reconsideration of value | Can challenge low appraisal |
| Truth in lending | Must receive clear disclosures |

**If You Experience Discrimination**

1. Document everything (dates, names, what was said)
2. File complaint with HUD (hud.gov/complaints)
3. Contact local fair housing organization
4. Consider consulting fair housing attorney
5. File complaint with CFPB if lending-related

## Quick Start Guide

**If You Have 1 Hour**

1. Check your credit report for errors
2. Search for down payment assistance in your state
3. Sign up for housing organization newsletter

**If You Have 1 Day**

1. Complete online homebuyer education course
2. Research local housing counseling agencies
3. Write to elected official about housing priority

**If You Have Ongoing Time**

1. Attend local planning/zoning meetings
2. Join housing advocacy organization
3. Mentor aspiring homebuyers
4. Serve on local housing advisory board
