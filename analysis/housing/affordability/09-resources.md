## Key Research

### Academic Research

**Supply and Prices**

| Citation | Finding |
|----------|---------|
| Glaeser, E. & Gyourko, J. (2018). "The Economic Implications of Housing Supply." *Journal of Economic Perspectives* 32(1). | Regulation adds 50%+ to housing costs in expensive markets |
| Hsieh, C. & Moretti, E. (2019). "Housing Constraints and Spatial Misallocation." *American Economic Journal: Macroeconomics* 11(2). | Housing constraints cost $1.6 trillion in lost GDP annually |
| Been, V., Ellen, I.G., & O'Regan, K. (2019). "Supply Skepticism." *Housing Policy Debate* 29(1). | New supply reduces rent increases in surrounding area |

**Affordability and Cost Burden**

| Citation | Finding |
|----------|---------|
| Joint Center for Housing Studies. (Annual). *State of the Nation's Housing*. Harvard University. | Comprehensive annual affordability assessment |
| National Low Income Housing Coalition. (Annual). *Out of Reach*. | Documents gap between wages and housing costs |
| Desmond, M. (2016). *Evicted: Poverty and Profit in the American City*. | Ethnographic study of eviction's impact |

**Policy Evaluation**

| Citation | Finding |
|----------|---------|
| Schuetz, J. (2020). *Fixer-Upper: How to Repair America's Broken Housing Systems*. Brookings. | Comprehensive policy analysis and recommendations |
| Lens, M. & Monkkonen, P. (2016). "Do Strict Land Use Regulations Make Metropolitan Areas More Segregated?" *Journal of the American Planning Association* 82(1). | Land use regulations increase segregation |
| Diamond, R., McQuade, T., & Qian, F. (2019). "The Effects of Rent Control Expansion on Tenants, Landlords, and Inequality." *American Economic Review* 109(9). | Rent control reduces displacement but may reduce supply |
