### Who Gets Evicted

**Demographics of Eviction**:

| Demographic | Eviction Rate Relative to Average |
|-------------|-----------------------------------|
| Black renters | 2.3x higher |
| Women-headed households | 1.8x higher |
| Households with children | 1.6x higher |
| Hispanic renters | 1.4x higher |
| Renters with disabilities | 1.3x higher |

**Black Women and Eviction**:

- Black women are evicted at highest rates of any demographic
- In many cities, Black women are 2-3x more likely to face eviction
- Intersectional discrimination compounds housing instability

### Eviction Court Dynamics

**Legal Representation**:

| Party | Representation Rate |
|-------|---------------------|
| Landlords with attorneys | 81% |
| Tenants with attorneys | 3% |
| Tenants with any assistance | 10% |

**Case Outcomes**:

| Outcome | Percentage |
|---------|------------|
| Default judgment (tenant no-show) | 40-50% |
| Landlord wins at hearing | 30-40% |
| Case dismissed/withdrawn | 15-20% |
| Tenant wins | 1-5% |

**Time to Eviction**:

| Jurisdiction Type | Filing to Removal |
|-------------------|-------------------|

| Tenant-protective (NY, CA) | 60-120+ days |

## Tenant Protections by State

### Rent Control

**State Rent Control Status**:

| Category | States |
|----------|--------|
| Rent control exists | CA, NY, OR, MD (some localities), NJ, DC |
| Rent control banned | 37 states |
| No specific law | Remainder |

**Types of Rent Regulation**:

| Type | Description | Where |
|------|-------------|-------|
| Strict rent control | Caps on increases, no vacancy decontrol | NYC (some units), SF (some) |
| Rent stabilization | Percentage caps, vacancy reset | OR, CA (Costa-Hawkins limits) |
| Anti-gouging | Emergency caps only | Several states |
| None | Market-rate only | Most of U.S. |

### Just Cause Eviction

**States with Just Cause Requirements**:

| State | Coverage |
|-------|----------|
| California | Statewide (AB 1482) |
| Oregon | Statewide |
| Washington | Statewide (2023) |
| New Jersey | Some localities |
| New York | NYC and upstate cities |
| Maryland | Baltimore only |
| Other states | Few local ordinances |

**States Without Just Cause**: 48 states allow no-cause eviction

- Maintains higher vacancies to support higher rents

## Rental Assistance Programs

### Section 8 Housing Choice Vouchers

**Program Scale**:

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Households served | 2.3 million |
| Average subsidy | $900/month |
| Annual federal cost | $28 billion |
| Waiting list | 2.5+ million households |
| Average wait time | 2-8 years |

**Voucher Challenges**:

| Challenge | Scope |
|-----------|-------|
| Landlord non-participation | 60-70% refuse vouchers |
| Source of income discrimination | Still legal in 30+ states |
| Payment standard below market | Many areas |
| Administrative barriers | Documentation, inspections |

### Emergency Rental Assistance

**COVID-Era ERAP**:

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Total funding | $46.5 billion |
| Households assisted | 10+ million |
| Average payment | $4,500-6,000 |
| States that spent quickly | ~15 |
| States that struggled | ~10 |

**ERAP Lessons**:

- Direct-to-landlord payments most efficient
- Self-attestation faster than documentation
- Collaboration with courts prevented evictions
- Infrastructure now exists for future crises
- Funding expired, need diminished only slightly

## Eviction Records and Screening

### Tenant Screening Industry

**Market Size**:

- $1.5+ billion industry
- Major players: TransUnion, CoreLogic, RentGrow
- Data on 30+ million eviction records
- Criminal, credit, and eviction combined

**Screening Report Contents**:

| Data Type | Retention Period |
|-----------|------------------|
| Eviction filings | 7 years |
| Eviction judgments | 7 years |
| Criminal records | Varies (7 years to lifetime) |
| Credit history | 7-10 years |
| Previous addresses | 7 years |

### Eviction Record Problems

**Issues with Current System**:

| Problem | Impact |
|---------|--------|
| Filings without judgment | Cases dismissed still appear |
| Sealed record non-compliance | Screening companies still report |
| Accuracy errors | Wrong person, outdated info |
| COVID-era filings | Filed but moratorium prevented judgment |
| No context provided | Reason for eviction unknown |

**Consequences of Eviction Records**:

- Automatic rejection from most housing
- Forces into substandard housing
- Higher deposits required
- Family doubled-up arrangements
- Homelessness

### Record Sealing

**States with Eviction Record Sealing**:

| Category | States |
|----------|--------|
| Automatic sealing (dismissed) | CA, NV, CO, MN |
| Petition-based sealing | NY, IL, WA, OR |
| Limited sealing | Several others |
| No sealing provision | Majority |
