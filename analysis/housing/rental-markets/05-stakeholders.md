# Rental Markets: Stakeholders

## Overview

The rental housing system involves multiple stakeholders with varying levels of power, resources, and interests. Understanding who benefits from the current system, who is harmed by it, and who has the power to change it is essential for effective reform strategy.

## Primary Stakeholders

### Renters

**Population Profile**:

| Characteristic | Percentage |
|----------------|------------|
| Total renter households | 44+ million |
| Low-income (<80% AMI) | 60% |
| Very low-income (<50% AMI) | 40% |
| Extremely low-income (<30% AMI) | 25% |
| People of color | 52% |
| Families with children | 33% |
| Seniors (65+) | 14% |
| People with disabilities | 15% |

**Renter Interests**:

| Interest | Priority |
|----------|----------|
| Affordable rent | High |
| Housing stability | High |
| Protection from eviction | High |
| Habitable conditions | High |
| Non-discrimination | High |
| Lease transparency | Medium |
| Security deposit return | Medium |
| Privacy | Medium |

**Barriers to Influence**:

- Less likely to vote than homeowners
- Geographic dispersion limits organizing
- Time constraints (work, caregiving)
- Fear of retaliation for organizing
- Transience undermines political power
- Lower campaign contribution capacity

### Landlords (Individual/Small)

**Profile**:

| Characteristic | Scope |
|----------------|-------|
| Own 1-4 units | 41% of rental stock |
| Often "accidental landlords" | Inherited, couldn't sell |
| May live in building | House-hacking |
| Limited business sophistication | Not professional managers |
| Dependent on rental income | Retirement, supplemental income |

**Interests**:

| Interest | Perspective |
|----------|-------------|
| Stable income | Need predictable rent |
| Property protection | Fear of damage |
| Tenant selection freedom | Screen for "good" tenants |
| Eviction capability | Remove non-paying tenants |
| Limited regulation | Compliance burden |
| Property value | Maintain investment |

**Distinguishing Factors**:

- Often have personal relationships with tenants
- May be more flexible on rent during hardship
- Less likely to use aggressive tactics
- May lack knowledge of landlord-tenant law
- More vulnerable to non-payment

### Landlords (Corporate/Institutional)

**Profile**:

| Type | Units Owned | Strategy |
|------|-------------|----------|
| REITs | Millions | Long-term hold, dividends |
| Private equity | Hundreds of thousands | Value extraction, exit |
| Institutional investors | Growing share | Portfolio diversification |
| Regional operators | Thousands-tens of thousands | Market dominance |

**Interests**:

| Interest | Approach |
|----------|----------|
| Returns to investors | Maximize rent, minimize costs |
| Portfolio growth | Acquire more properties |
| Operational efficiency | Standardize, automate |
| Rent optimization | Algorithmic pricing |
| Reduce vacancy loss | Quick turnover |
| Political influence | Shape regulation |

**Practices**:

- Aggressive eviction filing
- Fee maximization
- Deferred maintenance
- Algorithmic rent setting
- Lease non-renewal to avoid just cause
- Opposition to tenant protections

### Property Managers

**Role**:

| Function | Activities |
|----------|------------|
| Day-to-day operations | Rent collection, maintenance |
| Tenant relations | Complaints, lease enforcement |
| Legal compliance | Fair housing, habitability |
| Eviction execution | Filing, court appearance |
| Marketing | Filling vacancies |

**Interests**:

| Interest | Reason |
|----------|--------|
| Owner satisfaction | Keep management contract |
| Minimize problems | Evict "problem" tenants quickly |
| Limit liability | Follow procedures |
| Streamlined operations | Automation, efficiency |
| Professional standards | Industry credentialing |

## Industry Organizations

### Landlord Associations

**National Organizations**:

| Organization | Members | Focus |
|--------------|---------|-------|
| National Apartment Association (NAA) | 95,000+ properties | Multifamily advocacy |
| National Multifamily Housing Council (NMHC) | Large owners | Federal policy |
| National Association of Residential Property Managers (NARPM) | Property managers | Industry standards |
| Institute of Real Estate Management (IREM) | Professional managers | Credentialing |

**State/Local Associations**:

- Every state has apartment/landlord associations
- Lobby at state level against tenant protections
- Provide legal guidance to members
- Track and oppose tenant-friendly legislation
- Active in campaign contributions

**Positions**:

| Issue | Industry Position |
|-------|------------------|
| Rent control | Strongly oppose |
| Just cause eviction | Oppose |
| Eviction moratoriums | Oppose |
| Source of income laws | Mixed/oppose |
| Security deposit limits | Oppose |
| Right to counsel | Mixed |
| Habitability standards | Accept minimal |

### Real Estate Industry

**Realtor Associations**:

| Organization | Role |
|--------------|------|
| National Association of Realtors (NAR) | Largest trade group (1.5M members) |
| State Realtor associations | State-level lobbying |
| Local MLS organizations | Market access control |

**Positions on Rental**:

- Oppose rent control
- Support property rights
- Favor homeownership
- Mixed on tenant protections
- Active political donors

### Legal Aid and Services

**Legal Assistance Providers**:

| Type | Services |
|------|----------|
| Legal Aid societies | Free representation, low-income |
| Pro bono attorneys | Volunteer representation |
| Law school clinics | Student representation |
| Tenant hotlines | Advice, information |
| Self-help centers | Court navigation |

**Capacity**:

- Dramatically under-resourced
- 3% of tenants have representation
- 81% of landlords have attorneys
- Long waiting lists
- Triage severe cases only

**Multifamily Lenders**:

| Lender Type | Role |

### Investors

**Equity Investors**:

| Investor Type | Strategy |
|---------------|----------|
| Public REITs | Long-term, dividends |
| Private equity | Value-add, exit |
| Pension funds | Stable returns |
| Sovereign wealth | Portfolio diversification |
| Individual investors | Income, appreciation |

**Impact on Rental Markets**:

- Drive rent increases to meet return targets
- Consolidate ownership
- Professionalize operations
- Prioritize efficiency over residents

## Stakeholder Power Analysis

### Power Distribution

| Stakeholder | Power Level | Source |
|-------------|-------------|--------|
| Corporate landlords | High | Capital, lobbying, legal resources |
| Landlord associations | High | Organization, political donations |
| Real estate industry | High | Economic importance, lobbying |
| Small landlords | Medium | Numbers, property rights narrative |
| Government (state) | Medium | Legislative authority |
| Tenant advocates | Low-Medium | Organizing, moral claims |
| Renters (unorganized) | Low | Numbers without organization |
| Legal aid | Low | Limited capacity |

### Leverage Points

| Actor | Potential Leverage |
|-------|-------------------|
| Organized tenants | Disruption, electoral power |
| Progressive cities | Local innovation |
| Federal government | Funding conditions |
| Courts | Judicial interpretation |
| Media | Narrative, public attention |
| Researchers | Evidence, legitimacy |
