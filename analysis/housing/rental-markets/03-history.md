# Rental Markets: History

## Early American Housing (Pre-1900)

### Colonial and Early Republic

**Land and Housing Patterns**:

- Homeownership reserved for propertied classes
- Tenant farming common in agriculture
- Urban boarding houses for workers
- No formal landlord-tenant law
- Common law favored property owners

**Key Developments**:

| Period | Development |
|--------|-------------|
| 1600s-1700s | English common law imported |
| Late 1700s | Property rights enshrined in Constitution |
| Early 1800s | Urbanization creates rental housing demand |
| Mid-1800s | Tenement housing emerges in cities |

### Tenement Era

**Urban Density Growth**:

- Immigration drove demand for cheap urban housing
- Tenements packed dozens of families per building
- Little ventilation, sanitation, or light
- Fire and disease rampant
- Landlords maximized density for profit

**Tenement Reforms**:

| Reform | Year | Impact |
|--------|------|--------|
| NY Tenement House Act | 1867 | First housing standards |
| NY Tenement House Law | 1901 | Required light, air, toilets |
| Progressive Era codes | 1900s-1920s | Spread standards nationally |
| Zoning laws | 1916+ | Separated industrial/residential |

## The Homeownership Push (1900-1945)

### Shift Toward Ownership

**Federal Promotion of Homeownership**:

| Year | Development |
|------|-------------|
| 1913 | Mortgage interest deduction created |
| 1932 | Federal Home Loan Bank created |
| 1934 | FHA created, standardized 30-year mortgage |
| 1938 | Fannie Mae created |
| 1944 | GI Bill housing benefits |

**Ideology of Ownership**:

- "Owner democracy" as bulwark against socialism
- Renters seen as transient, less invested in community
- Homeownership as mark of citizenship and success
- Federal policy reinforced ownership preference

### Rental Market Neglect

**Policy Gaps**:

- Federal focus on ownership, not rental
- No national rental assistance until 1937
- Public housing limited in scope
- Private rental market unregulated
- Landlord-tenant law minimal

## Post-War Housing (1945-1970)

### Suburbanization and White Flight

**Housing Transformation**:

| Trend | Impact |
|-------|--------|
| Suburban development | Homeownership for white families |
| Urban disinvestment | Cities lose tax base, services decline |
| Redlining | Black families denied mortgages, forced to rent |
| Blockbusting | Landlords profit from racial transition |
| Urban renewal | Destroyed rental housing, displaced renters |

### Racial Discrimination in Rental

**Systematic Exclusion**:

- Explicit racial restrictions in leases
- "For Whites Only" rental advertisements
- Steering by landlords and agents
- Higher rents charged to Black tenants
- Worse conditions, less maintenance

### Fair Housing Origins

**Civil Rights Housing Advocacy**:

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1948 | *Shelley v. Kraemer* bans enforcement of racial covenants |
| 1962 | Kennedy executive order on discrimination |
| 1968 | Fair Housing Act passed |
| 1974 | Section 8 voucher program created |

## Rent Control Era (1940s-1970s)

### World War II Controls

**Emergency Rent Control**:

- Federal rent control during WWII
- Prevented profiteering during housing shortage
- Covered most rental housing nationally
- Ended post-war in most areas
- NYC maintained controls into peacetime

### State and Local Rent Control

**Expansion in the 1970s**:

| Location | Year | Trigger |
|----------|------|---------|
| New York (maintained) | 1943+ | WWII emergency |
| Washington, DC | 1974 | Inflation, tenant activism |
| Boston | 1970 | Tenant organizing |
| Los Angeles | 1978 | Proposition 13 aftermath |
| San Francisco | 1979 | Tenant organizing |
| New Jersey cities | 1970s | Various |

### Rent Control Rollback

**Industry Counteroffensive**:

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1980s | "Rent control causes housing shortage" narrative spreads |
| 1985 | Massachusetts rent control repealed |
| 1994 | Massachusetts ban on local rent control (Question 9) |
| 1995 | Costa-Hawkins Act (CA) limits rent control |
| 1997 | NYC Rent Regulation Reform Act weakens stabilization |
| 2000s | Pre-emption laws spread to 37 states |

## Tenant Rights Movement (1960s-1980s)

### Origins

**Movement Emergence**:

- Civil rights movement spillover
- Anti-war activism models applied to housing
- Welfare rights organizing included housing
- Tenant unions formed in major cities
- Rent strikes as tactic

**Key Organizations**:

| Organization | Founded | Focus |
|--------------|---------|-------|
| National Tenants Organization | 1969 | Federal policy |
| Met Council on Housing (NYC) | 1959 | NYC tenant organizing |
| Housing Now! | 1980s | Homelessness, renters |
| Tenant unions | Various | Local organizing |

### Legal Victories

**Landmark Cases**:

| Case | Year | Holding |
|------|------|---------|
| *Javins v. First National Realty* | 1970 | Implied warranty of habitability |
| *Robinson v. Diamond Housing* | 1972 | Retaliatory eviction illegal |
| *Green v. Superior Court* | 1974 | Habitability defense in CA |
| *Lindsey v. Normet* | 1972 | Limited due process in eviction |

**Statutory Protections**:

| Protection | Era | States |
|------------|-----|--------|
| Warranty of habitability | 1970s | Most states |
| Retaliatory eviction bans | 1970s-1980s | Many states |
| Security deposit limits | 1970s-1990s | Some states |
| Notice requirements | Various | Most states |

## Deregulation and Financialization (1980s-2000s)

### Reagan Era Retrenchment

**Federal Housing Cuts**:

| Year | Policy Change |
|------|---------------|
| 1981 | HUD budget cut 75% |
| 1983 | Public housing moratorium |
| 1986 | LIHTC created (privatized subsidy model) |
| 1990s | HOPE VI demolishes public housing |
| 1998 | Faircloth Amendment caps public housing |

### Rise of Investor Landlords

**Ownership Transformation**:

| Period | Development |
|--------|-------------|
| 1980s | REITs expand into rental housing |
| 1990s | Private equity discovers real estate |
| 2000s | Apartment complexes consolidated |
| 2008+ | Single-family home investors emerge |

### Pre-emption of Local Protections

**State-Level Rollback**:

| State | Pre-emption | Year |
|-------|-------------|------|
| California | Costa-Hawkins limits | 1995 |
| Massachusetts | Rent control banned | 1994 |
| Texas | Strong pre-emption | Various |
| Florida | Pre-emption of rent control | 1977 |
| Colorado | Pre-emption | 1981 |
| Many others | Ban local rent control | Various |

## Post-Financial Crisis (2008-2020)

### Foreclosure-to-Rental Pipeline

**Institutional Single-Family Landlords**:

| Development | Year | Impact |
|-------------|------|--------|
| Housing crash | 2008 | Millions lose homes to foreclosure |
| Investor purchases | 2010+ | Wall Street buys foreclosed homes |
| Single-family rental REITs | 2012+ | Blackstone, Invitation Homes, American Homes 4 Rent |
| Scale grows | 2015+ | Largest own 100,000+ homes |

### Rising Rents

**Post-Recovery Rent Surge**:

| Period | Trend |
|--------|-------|
| 2008-2010 | Rents flat during recession |
| 2011-2015 | Rents rise faster than wages |
| 2016-2019 | Continued rent escalation |
| 2020-2022 | Pandemic rent explosion (20%+) |

### Renewed Tenant Activism

**Movement Resurgence**:

| Development | Year |
|-------------|------|
| Occupy Wall Street housing focus | 2011 |
| Anti-eviction organizing (SF) | 2013+ |
| KC Tenants founded | 2019 |
| California Prop 10 (rent control) | 2018 |
| Oregon statewide rent control | 2019 |
| California AB 1482 | 2019 |

## COVID-19 Era (2020-2023)

### Eviction Moratoriums

**Federal and State Actions**:

| Action | Dates | Coverage |
|--------|-------|----------|
| CARES Act eviction moratorium | March-July 2020 | Federally-backed mortgages |
| CDC eviction moratorium | Sept 2020-Aug 2021 | National |
| State moratoriums | March 2020-2022 | Varied by state |

**Moratorium Impact**:

- Prevented estimated 1.5+ million evictions
- Kept families housed during pandemic
- Proved mass eviction not inevitable
- Demonstrated policy can prevent displacement

### Emergency Rental Assistance

**Historic Intervention**:

| Program | Amount | Households |
|---------|--------|------------|
| ERAP 1 | $25 billion | Millions |
| ERAP 2 | $21.5 billion | Millions |
| Total | $46.5 billion | 10+ million |

**Lessons Learned**:

- Direct-to-landlord payments work
- Self-attestation speeds assistance
- Court diversion programs effective
- Infrastructure now exists
- Prevention cheaper than eviction

### Post-Moratorium Landscape

**Return to Pre-Pandemic Patterns**:

| Trend | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Eviction filings | Returned to/exceeded pre-pandemic |
| Rent increases | Continued escalation |
| Rental assistance | Funding exhausted |
| Tenant protections | Minimal expansion |
| Corporate ownership | Continued growth |
