# Housing: Roadmap

## Overview

Transforming American housing requires a sequenced approach that builds political power, demonstrates success through early wins, and progressively tackles more structural barriers. This roadmap outlines phases from immediate actions to long-term transformation, recognizing that housing reform must occur at federal, state, and local levels simultaneously.

## Risks and Contingencies

### Political Risks

| Risk | Mitigation | Contingency |
|------|------------|-------------|
| **Election losses** | Build durable coalitions | State/local action |
| **Court challenges** | Careful legal drafting | Backup provisions |
| **NIMBY backlash** | Show benefits, build support | Incremental approach |
| **Industry opposition** | Coalition with builders | Divide opposition |

### Implementation Risks

| Risk | Mitigation | Contingency |
|------|------------|-------------|
| **Construction capacity** | Workforce investment | Modular/prefab |
| **Cost inflation** | Supply increase | Subsidy adjustment |
| **Administrative burden** | Streamlined programs | Simplified eligibility |
| **Local resistance** | State enforcement | Penalties for non-compliance |

### Economic Risks

| Risk | Mitigation | Contingency |
|------|------------|-------------|
| **Recession** | Countercyclical investment | Automatic stabilizers |
| **Interest rate spikes** | Fixed-rate support | GSE intervention |
| **Housing market crash** | Stabilization programs | Emergency assistance |
| **Investment pullback** | Public investment | Government production |

### Contingency Plans

| If This Fails | Then |
|---------------|------|
| Federal action blocked | State-led reform |
| State preemption fails | Local YIMBY campaigns |
| Market supply insufficient | Direct public production |
| Vouchers underfunded | Emergency rental assistance |
| Fair housing unenforced | Private litigation |
