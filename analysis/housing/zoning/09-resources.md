# Zoning Reform: Resources

## Key Research

### Economic Research

**Hsieh, Chang-Tai and Enrico Moretti. "Housing Constraints and Spatial Misallocation." American Economic Journal: Macroeconomics (2019).**

- Finding: Land use restrictions cost U.S. economy $1.6 trillion annually
- Mechanism: Workers cannot move to high-productivity areas
- Impact: Foundational research cited in policy debates

**Glaeser, Edward and Joseph Gyourko. "The Economic Implications of Housing Supply." Journal of Economic Perspectives (2018).**

- Finding: Housing supply constraints drive unaffordability
- Analysis: Detailed examination of supply-demand dynamics
- Recommendations: Reduce regulatory barriers

**Gyourko, Joseph, Albert Saiz, and Anita Summers. "A New Measure of the Local Regulatory Environment for Housing Markets." Urban Studies (2008).**

- Created: Wharton Residential Land Use Regulatory Index
- Finding: Quantified regulatory restrictiveness across jurisdictions
- Use: Enables comparative analysis

**Autor, David, Christopher Palmer, and Parag Pathak. "Housing Market Spillovers: Evidence from the End of Rent Control in Cambridge, Massachusetts." Journal of Political Economy (2014).**

- Finding: End of rent control increased nearby property values
- Implication: Supply constraints have spillover effects
- Relevance: Demonstrates interconnected housing markets

### Segregation and Fair Housing Research

**Rothstein, Richard. *The Color of Law: A Forgotten History of How Our Government Segregated America*. Liveright (2017).**

- Argument: Segregation was government policy, not private choice
- Evidence: Documents federal, state, and local segregation policies
- Impact: Major popular and policy influence

**Trounstine, Jessica. *Segregation by Design: Local Politics and Inequality in American Cities*. Cambridge University Press (2018).**

- Finding: Local governments actively created and maintain segregation
- Mechanism: Zoning, annexation, service provision
- Implication: Reform requires addressing local political economy

**Shertzer, Allison, Tate Twinam, and Randall Walsh. "Race, Ethnicity, and Discriminatory Zoning." American Economic Journal: Applied Economics (2016).**

- Finding: Early zoning in Chicago was racially motivated
- Method: Historical data analysis
- Impact: Documents zoning's racist origins

### YIMBY Research and Advocacy

**Schuetz, Jenny. *Fixer-Upper: How to Repair America's Broken Housing Systems*. Brookings Institution Press (2022).**

- Scope: Comprehensive housing policy analysis
- Approach: Research-based policy recommendations
- Focus: Zoning reform, subsidized housing, homeownership

**Phillips, Shane. *The Affordable City: Strategies for Putting Housing Within Reach*. Island Press (2020).**

- Focus: Practical zoning reform strategies
- Audience: Advocates and policymakers
- Content: Case studies, policy analysis

**Been, Vicki, Ingrid Gould Ellen, and Katherine O'Regan. "Supply Skepticism: Housing Supply and Affordability." Housing Policy Debate (2019).**

- Analysis: Reviews evidence on housing supply and affordability
- Finding: Supply increases moderate prices, with caveats
- Contribution: Nuanced take on supply arguments

### Missing Middle and ADU Research

**Parolek, Daniel. *Missing Middle Housing: Thinking Big and Building Small to Respond to Today's Housing Crisis*. Island Press (2020).**

- Definition: Coined and defined "missing middle" housing
- Content: Design guidance, policy recommendations
- Influence: Widely cited in reform efforts

**Chapple, Karen et al. "ADUs in California: A Revolution in Progress." Terner Center for Housing Innovation (2021).**

- Finding: California ADU reforms dramatically increased production
- Data: Comprehensive permit analysis
- Implications: Documents reform success
