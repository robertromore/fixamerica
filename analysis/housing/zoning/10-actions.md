# Zoning Reform: Actions

## Overview

Individual citizens can make meaningful contributions to zoning reform through advocacy, civic engagement, and direct action. This guide provides concrete actions organized by time commitment and impact.

## Medium-Commitment Actions (1-4 Hours/Month)

### Attend Public Meetings

**City Council Meetings**:

| Action | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Attend when housing on agenda | Counter NIMBY attendance |
| Speak during public comment | 2-3 minutes; prepared remarks |
| Bring supporters | Numbers matter |
| Thank supportive officials | Encourage pro-housing votes |

**Planning Commission Meetings**:

| Action | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Track housing applications | Know what's being proposed |
| Support good projects | Counter opposition |
| Oppose restrictive actions | Downzoning, denials |

**Tips for Effective Public Comment**:

1. Be concise (2-3 minutes)
2. State your position clearly upfront
3. Explain why you support/oppose
4. Connect to your personal experience
5. Be respectful but direct
6. Thank supportive decision-makers

### Join Local YIMBY Group

**Benefits**:

- Coordinated action
- Information sharing
- Training and support
- Community of like-minded advocates

**Activities**:

| Activity | Frequency |
|----------|-----------|
| Monthly meetings | 1-2 hours/month |
| Email list participation | Ongoing |
| Event attendance | As available |
| Project tracking | Varies |

### Write to Decision-Makers

**Effective Letter/Email Elements**:

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Introduction | Who you are; connection to community |
| Position | Clear statement of support/opposition |
| Reasoning | Why this matters to you |
| Evidence | Brief facts supporting position |
| Ask | Specific action requested |
| Closing | Thank you; follow-up intention |

**Key Targets**:

| Target | When |
|--------|------|
| City council members | Local projects and policies |
| Planning commissioners | Applications and plans |
| State legislators | State legislation |
| Governor | Signing/vetoing bills |

## High-Commitment Actions (4+ Hours/Month)

### Become a YIMBY Leader

**Roles**:

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|------------------|
| Chapter organizer | Recruit members; plan events |
| Policy lead | Track legislation; draft testimony |
| Communications lead | Social media; press relations |
| Event organizer | Plan rallies, tabling, socials |
| Volunteer coordinator | Recruit and deploy volunteers |

**Skills Developed**:

- Public speaking
- Coalition building
- Policy analysis
- Organizing
- Media relations

### Run for Local Office

**Relevant Positions**:

| Office | Relevance |
|--------|-----------|
| City council | Direct zoning authority |
| Planning commission | Appointed; reviews applications |
| School board | Impacts housing debates |
| County supervisor | Regional planning authority |
| State legislature | Preemption authority |

**Considerations**:

- Local elections often low-turnout; small margins
- YIMBY endorsement can help
- Housing can be central platform issue
- Long-term commitment

### Testify on Legislation

**State Legislature**:

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| Track bills | Follow YIMBY alerts; legislature website |
| Sign up to testify | Usually online registration |
| Prepare testimony | 2-3 minutes; written copy |
| Submit written testimony | Often accepted even if can't attend |
| Follow up | Thank supportive legislators |

**Effective Testimony Elements**:

1. State name, organization (if applicable), position
2. Personal connection to issue
3. Key supporting points (2-3 max)
4. Clear ask
5. Offer to provide additional information

## Actions by Location

### In Expensive Coastal Metros

**Priority Actions**:

- Support state preemption legislation
- Attend local project hearings
- Push for CEQA reform
- Support transit-oriented housing

### In Growing Sun Belt Cities

**Priority Actions**:

- Prevent new restrictions before they're adopted
- Support transit and infill over sprawl
- Advocate for affordability before crisis worsens
- Counter anti-apartment sentiment

### In Smaller Cities and Suburbs

**Priority Actions**:

- Support ADU legalization
- Challenge single-family-only zoning
- Advocate for regional fair share
- Support transit-oriented development

### In Rural Areas

**Priority Actions**:

- Support state legislation (affects all areas)
- Advocate for housing near jobs
- Support manufactured housing
- Challenge excessive lot size minimums

## Avoiding Burnout

**Sustainable Engagement**:

| Practice | Implementation |
|----------|----------------|
| Set realistic commitments | 2-4 hours/month for most people |
| Share responsibility | Work in teams |
| Celebrate wins | Acknowledge progress |
| Take breaks | Step back when needed |
| Focus on local | Can't fight every battle |

**Signs of Burnout**:

- Resentment toward the work
- Cynicism about impact
- Exhaustion and irritability
- Neglecting other responsibilities

**Response**:

- Reduce commitment temporarily
- Focus on highest-impact activities
- Connect with supportive community
- Remember why you started
