# Counterterrorism: Resources

This section provides a curated list of key government sources, data projects, academic research, legal authorities, and expert analysis on U.S. counterterrorism policy and the global terrorist threat.

---

## Primary Government Sources

- **National Counterterrorism Center (NCTC)**
    - The primary U.S. government organization for analyzing and integrating all intelligence pertaining to terrorism. Its annual "Report on Terrorism" provides statistical data on global terrorist attacks.
    - [https://www.dni.gov/index.php/nctc-home](https://www.dni.gov/index.php/nctc-home)

- **U.S. Department of State, Bureau of Counterterrorism**
    - Leads the State Department's CT efforts and publishes the annual "Country Reports on Terrorism," which provides a detailed overview of the threat landscape by country and organization.
    - [https://www.state.gov/bureaus-offices/bureau-of-counterterrorism/](https://www.state.gov/bureaus-offices/bureau-of-counterterrorism/)

- **Director of National Intelligence, Annual Threat Assessment**
    - The DNI's annual unclassified assessment of the most significant threats to U.S. national security, including terrorism. A key authoritative source for understanding how the intelligence community prioritizes and characterizes terrorist threats.
    - [https://www.dni.gov/index.php/newsroom/reports-publications](https://www.dni.gov/index.php/newsroom/reports-publications)

- **FBI Counterterrorism Division**
    - The lead federal agency for investigating and preventing domestic and international terrorism within the United States. The FBI Director's regular congressional testimony provides data on open investigations, disrupted plots, and threat trends.
    - [https://www.fbi.gov/investigate/terrorism](https://www.fbi.gov/investigate/terrorism)

- **Department of Homeland Security, Office of Intelligence and Analysis**
    - Publishes the "Homeland Threat Assessment," an annual unclassified report on threats to the U.S. homeland, including domestic terrorism and targeted violence.
    - [https://www.dhs.gov/topics/homeland-threat-assessment](https://www.dhs.gov/topics/homeland-threat-assessment)

## Key Legal Authorities

- **2001 Authorization for Use of Military Force (AUMF)**
    - Public Law 107-40. The full text of the law that forms the primary legal basis for most U.S. CT operations.
    - [https://www.congress.gov/107/plaws/publ40/PLAW-107publ40.pdf](https://www.congress.gov/107/plaws/publ40/PLAW-107publ40.pdf)

- **War Powers Resolution of 1973**
    - 50 U.S.C. 1541-1548. Requires congressional notification and limits on the duration of military deployments absent authorization.

- **USA PATRIOT Act (2001)**
    - Public Law 107-56. Expanded surveillance authorities, including roving wiretaps, business record access (Section 215), and information sharing between intelligence and law enforcement.

- **Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act (FISA), Section 702**
    - 50 U.S.C. 1881a. Authorizes warrantless surveillance of non-U.S. persons reasonably believed to be located outside the U.S. Reauthorized in 2024. Central to debates over incidental collection of Americans' communications.

- **Military Commissions Act of 2006 (as amended 2009)**
    - 10 U.S.C. Chapter 47A. Establishes the legal framework for military commissions at Guantanamo Bay.

- **DoD Directive 3000.09 (Autonomy in Weapon Systems)**
    - Updated 2023. Establishes DoD policy on human judgment in autonomous and semi-autonomous weapon systems.

## Data and Monitoring Projects

- **Airwars**
    - A non-profit organization that tracks and investigates claims of civilian harm from international airstrikes, including U.S. drone operations in Iraq, Syria, Somalia, Yemen, and other theaters. Maintains the most comprehensive independent database of civilian casualty allegations.
    - [https://airwars.org/](https://airwars.org/)

- **The Bureau of Investigative Journalism (TBIJ)**
    - A non-profit news organization that has conducted long-running, in-depth investigations into the U.S. covert drone war, including strike counts and casualty estimates for Pakistan, Yemen, Somalia, and Afghanistan.
    - [https://www.thebureauinvestigates.com/](https://www.thebureauinvestigates.com/)

- **Global Terrorism Database (GTD)**
    - An open-source database including information on over 200,000 terrorist events around the world from 1970 through the present. Maintained by the National Consortium for the Study of Terrorism and Responses to Terrorism (START) at the University of Maryland.
    - [https://www.start.umd.edu/gtd/](https://www.start.umd.edu/gtd/)

- **Costs of War Project, Brown University Watson Institute**
    - Comprehensive, ongoing research project documenting the human, economic, and political costs of the post-9/11 wars. Provides data on spending ($8+ trillion), casualties, displacement, and civil liberties impacts.
    - [https://watson.brown.edu/costsofwar/](https://watson.brown.edu/costsofwar/)

- **New America International Security Program**
    - Maintains databases on drone strikes, terrorism cases in the United States, and the use of lethal force in CT operations.
    - [https://www.newamerica.org/international-security/](https://www.newamerica.org/international-security/)

## Think Tanks and Expert Analysis

- **Center for Strategic and International Studies (CSIS), Transnational Threats Project**: Data-driven analysis of the global terrorist threat and U.S. CT policy. Led by Seth Jones, a leading analyst of the global jihadist threat.
- **RAND Corporation**: Extensive research on terrorism, counterterrorism strategy, and the effectiveness of different CT approaches. Key studies include *How Terrorist Groups End* (2008) and assessments of CVE program effectiveness.
- **Brookings Institution, Center for Middle East Policy**: Analysis of CT policy in the context of broader Middle East strategy.
- **Just Security**: An online forum based at the Reiss Center on Law and Security at NYU School of Law, featuring expert analysis on the legal and policy aspects of counterterrorism, targeted killing, surveillance, and war powers.
- **Lawfare**: A blog and podcast dedicated to "Hard National Security Choices," which frequently covers the legal debates surrounding the AUMF, surveillance, targeted killing, Guantanamo, and military commissions. Edited by Benjamin Wittes and affiliated with the Brookings Institution.
- **Quincy Institute for Responsible Statecraft**: Advocates for a restraint-oriented approach to CT, emphasizing the costs of military overreach and the need to prioritize diplomacy and prevention.
- **Stimson Center**: Focused on defense policy, including the use of drones, autonomous weapons, and the ethics of lethal force in CT.
- **Global Network on Extremism and Technology (GNET)**: Academic research network studying the intersection of technology and terrorism, including online radicalization, algorithmic amplification, and counter-narrative strategies.

## Books and Long-Form Analysis

- Bergen, Peter. *The Rise and Fall of Osama bin Laden*. New York: Simon & Schuster, 2021.
    - Definitive biography of bin Laden and the evolution of al-Qaeda, based on the Abbottabad documents.
- Wright, Lawrence. *The Looming Tower: Al-Qaeda and the Road to 9/11*. New York: Knopf, 2006.
    - Pulitzer Prize-winning account of the intelligence failures leading to 9/11.
- Scahill, Jeremy. *Dirty Wars: The World Is a Battlefield*. New York: Nation Books, 2013.
    - Investigative account of JSOC operations and the CIA's covert war, including the al-Awlaki killing.
- Mayer, Jane. *The Dark Side: The Inside Story of How the War on Terror Turned into a War on American Ideals*. New York: Anchor Books, 2009.
    - Investigation of the Bush administration's "enhanced interrogation" program and its legal foundations.
- Coll, Steve. *Directorate S: The C.I.A. and America's Secret Wars in Afghanistan and Pakistan*. New York: Penguin Press, 2018.
    - CIA operations in Afghanistan and Pakistan, based on extensive sourcing within the intelligence community.
- Kaag, John, and Sarah Kreps. *Drone Warfare*. Cambridge, UK: Polity Press, 2014.
    - Analysis of the legal, ethical, and strategic dimensions of the U.S. drone program.
- Bacevich, Andrew J. *After the Apocalypse: America's Role in a World Transformed*. New York: Metropolitan Books, 2021.
    - Critique of U.S. national security strategy, arguing for restraint in CT and other military commitments.
- The 9/11 Commission Report: Final Report of the National Commission on Terrorist Attacks Upon the United States. Washington, DC: Government Printing Office, 2004.
    - The foundational document on the intelligence failures that allowed 9/11 and the reforms needed to prevent future attacks.

## Key Investigative Journalism

- **The Intercept, "The Drone Papers" (2015)**: Leaked classified documents detailing the U.S. government's targeted killing program, including the process for placing individuals on kill lists and the high rate of unintended casualties.
- **New York Times, "The Civilian Casualty Files" (2021)**: Investigation revealing that U.S. airstrikes in Iraq, Syria, and Afghanistan killed far more civilians than the Pentagon acknowledged.
- **+972 Magazine, "Lavender" Investigation (2024)**: Investigation into the Israeli military's AI-assisted targeting system in Gaza, raising global questions about AI in the kill chain.
