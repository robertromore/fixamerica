# Counterterrorism: Resources

This section provides a curated list of key government sources, data projects, and expert analysis on U.S. counterterrorism policy and the global terrorist threat.

---

## Primary Government Sources

- **National Counterterrorism Center (NCTC)**
    - The primary U.S. government organization for analyzing and integrating all intelligence pertaining to terrorism. Its annual "Report on Terrorism" is a key resource.
    - [https://www.dni.gov/index.php/nctc-home](https://www.dni.gov/index.php/nctc-home)

- **U.S. Department of State, Bureau of Counterterrorism**
    - Leads the State Department's CT efforts and publishes the annual "Country Reports on Terrorism," which provides a detailed overview of the threat landscape by country.
    - [https://www.state.gov/bureaus-offices/bureau-of-counterterrorism/](https://www.state.gov/bureaus-offices/bureau-of-counterterrorism/)

- **2001 Authorization for Use of Military Force (AUMF)**
    - The full text of the law that forms the primary legal basis for most U.S. CT operations.
    - [https://www.congress.gov/107/plaws/publ40/PLAW-107publ40.pdf](https://www.congress.gov/107/plaws/publ40/PLAW-107publ40.pdf)

## Data and Monitoring Projects

- **Airwars**
    - A non-profit organization that tracks and investigates claims of civilian harm from international airstrikes, including U.S. drone operations.
    - [https://airwars.org/](https://airwars.org/)

- **The Bureau of Investigative Journalism (TBIJ)**
    - A non-profit news organization that has conducted long-running, in-depth investigations into the U.S. covert drone war.
    - [https://www.thebureauinvestigates.com/](https://www.thebureauinvestigates.com/)

- **Global Terrorism Database (GTD)**
    - An open-source database including information on terrorist events around the world from 1970 through the present. Maintained by the University of Maryland.
    - [https://www.start.umd.edu/gtd/](https://www.start.umd.edu/gtd/)

## Think Tanks and Expert Analysis

- **Center for Strategic and International Studies (CSIS), Transnational Threats Project**: Provides data-driven analysis of the global terrorist threat and U.S. CT policy.
- **Just Security**: An online forum based at the Reiss Center on Law and Security at NYU School of Law, featuring expert analysis on the legal and policy aspects of counterterrorism.
- **Lawfare**: A blog dedicated to "Hard National Security Choices," which frequently covers the legal debates surrounding the AUMF, surveillance, and targeted killing.
