# Force Structure: Resources

This section provides a curated list of key government sources, independent analyses, expert organizations, and academic works that inform the force structure debate.

---

## Primary Government Sources

- **Department of Defense**
    - *National Defense Strategy* (2022): The foundational document identifying China as the "pacing challenge" and establishing the strategic framework for force-sizing decisions.
    - *Annual Report on Military and Security Developments Involving the People's Republic of China*: DoD's annual assessment of Chinese military modernization, essential context for force-sizing.
    - *National Defense Industrial Strategy* (2024): DoD's assessment of industrial base capacity, including shipbuilding, munitions production, and surge capacity.
    - *Defense Manpower Data Center (DMDC)*: Official source for military personnel statistics, end strength data, and demographic information.
    - [https://www.defense.gov/](https://www.defense.gov/)

- **Congressional Budget Office (CBO)**
    - CBO produces the most rigorous independent analyses of defense force structure costs and options. Key reports include:
    - *The U.S. Military's Force Structure: A Primer* (updated periodically): A comprehensive overview of how the force is organized and what it costs. The most accessible entry point for understanding force structure.
    - *An Analysis of the Navy's Fiscal Year 2024 Shipbuilding Plan*: Detailed assessment of fleet size options and costs, including historical comparison of shipbuilding rates and industrial base capacity.
    - *Approaches for Managing the Costs of U.S. Nuclear Forces*: Interaction between nuclear modernization and conventional force structure budgets. The Columbia-class SSBN program's cost trajectory has implications for the entire Navy shipbuilding plan.
    - *The Cost of Replacing Today's Air Force Fleet*: Analysis of the cost implications of recapitalizing the Air Force's aging aircraft fleet, including fighters, bombers, and tankers.
    - [https://www.cbo.gov/topics/defense-and-national-security](https://www.cbo.gov/topics/defense-and-national-security)

- **Government Accountability Office (GAO)**
    - GAO audits DoD force structure, readiness, and recruiting programs. Key series include:
    - *High-Risk Series: DoD Weapon Systems Acquisition*: Annual identification of defense programs at highest risk of cost and schedule failure.
    - *Military Readiness* reports (annual): Detailed assessments of service-level readiness, including unit status data and maintenance backlog analysis.
    - *Military Personnel: DoD Needs to Improve Recruiting and Retention*: Comprehensive analysis of the recruiting crisis and recommended reforms.
    - *Navy Readiness: Actions Needed to Improve Maintenance at the Navy's Four Public Shipyards*: Documents the submarine and surface ship maintenance backlog.
    - *Defense Infrastructure: DOD Should Better Manage Risks Posed by Excess Facilities*: Analysis of excess base capacity and the case for BRAC authorization.
    - [https://www.gao.gov/](https://www.gao.gov/)

- **Congressional Research Service (CRS)**
    - CRS produces detailed, nonpartisan reports for Congress on every aspect of force structure:
    - *Defense Primer: The All-Volunteer Force*: History and current challenges of the AVF.
    - *Navy Force Structure and Shipbuilding Plans*: Updated annually with analysis of fleet size options and shipbuilding budget.
    - *Army Multi-Domain Transformation*: Analysis of the Army's organizational and doctrinal adaptation for great power competition.
    - *Marine Corps Force Design 2030*: Assessment of the Corps' transformation, including Congressional concerns.
    - *Military Personnel: Recruitment and Retention Challenges*: Data on recruiting shortfalls and policy options.
    - *Base Realignment and Closure (BRAC): Background and Issues for Congress*: History, savings estimates, and political dynamics of the BRAC process.
    - *Defense Primer: Readiness*: Overview of how readiness is measured and current readiness challenges.
    - [https://crsreports.congress.gov/](https://crsreports.congress.gov/)

## Independent Research and Analysis

- **RAND Corporation**
    - RAND is the DoD's largest federally funded research and development center and produces foundational force structure analysis:
    - *Force Planning for the Post-Cold War Era* (seminal framework study): Established the analytical methodology for evaluating force structure options against multiple scenarios.
    - Studies on recruiting, retention, reserve component readiness, and force mix optimization.
    - *Strengthening the Defense Innovation Ecosystem*: Analysis of how to integrate commercial technology firms and autonomous systems into the defense enterprise.
    - *Lessons from Russia's Operations in Ukraine* series: Analysis of attrition rates, munitions consumption, and force employment lessons relevant to U.S. force design.
    - [https://www.rand.org/topics/military-force-planning.html](https://www.rand.org/topics/military-force-planning.html)

- **Center for Strategic and International Studies (CSIS)**
    - CSIS produces extensive force structure analysis through multiple programs:
    - International Security Program: Force design, great power competition, and Indo-Pacific strategy.
    - Defense-Industrial Initiatives Group: Shipbuilding, acquisition, and industrial base analysis. Mark Cancian's annual *U.S. Military Forces in FY20XX* series is the most comprehensive publicly available assessment of force structure trends.
    - *Restoring American Power* report series: Recommendations for defense spending and force structure growth.
    - CSIS Missile Defense Project: Analysis of missile defense architecture and its force structure implications.
    - [https://www.csis.org/](https://www.csis.org/)

- **Center for a New American Security (CNAS)**
    - CNAS focuses on defense and national security with emphasis on technology, innovation, and force design:
    - Reports on autonomous systems, human-machine teaming, and defense innovation.
    - *As You Were: The Future of the All-Volunteer Force* (2023): Comprehensive analysis of the AVF's sustainability and reform options.
    - *Bold Endeavors: How to Invest in Defense Innovation*: Recommendations for accelerating technology adoption.
    - [https://www.cnas.org/](https://www.cnas.org/)

- **American Enterprise Institute (AEI)**
    - AEI's defense program produces analysis on force sizing, defense budgets, and military modernization, generally advocating for a larger and more capable force:
    - Elbridge Colby and others have published extensively on the China challenge and its force structure implications.
    - [https://www.aei.org/policy-areas/defense-and-national-security/](https://www.aei.org/policy-areas/defense-and-national-security/)

- **Hudson Institute**
    - Hudson's Center for Defense Concepts and Technology publishes analysis on naval force structure, shipbuilding, and fleet design:
    - Bryan Clark and others have proposed alternative fleet architectures emphasizing distributed operations and unmanned systems.
    - [https://www.hudson.org/](https://www.hudson.org/)

- **Stimson Center / Project on Government Oversight (POGO)**
    - Provide analysis from a reform-oriented perspective, focusing on waste, inefficiency, and the need for structural change in defense:
    - Stimson Center's defense program covers force sizing, BRAC, and budget reform.
    - POGO's defense work includes analysis of readiness spending, weapons program cost overruns, and base infrastructure waste.
    - [https://www.stimson.org/](https://www.stimson.org/)
    - [https://www.pogo.org/](https://www.pogo.org/)

- **Quincy Institute for Responsible Statecraft**
    - Provides analysis advocating for a more restrained approach to U.S. defense posture and force sizing:
    - Publishes critiques of threat inflation and arguments for a smaller, more focused force aligned with essential national interests.
    - [https://quincyinst.org/](https://quincyinst.org/)

## Key Books

- Cancian, Mark F. *U.S. Military Forces in FY2024: The Continued Decline of the Military*. CSIS, 2023. Comprehensive annual assessment of force structure trends across all services.
- Krepinevich, Andrew F. *The Army and Vietnam*. Johns Hopkins University Press, 1986. Classic study of how institutional culture shapes force structure decisions -- the Army built a force for conventional war in Europe and then tried to use it for counterinsurgency in Vietnam.
- Builder, Carl H. *The Masks of War: American Military Styles in Strategy and Analysis*. Johns Hopkins University Press, 1989. Foundational analysis of how service identities (the Army's dedication to the "profession of arms," the Navy's love of independence, the Air Force's worship of technology) drive force structure choices.
- O'Hanlon, Michael E. *The Art of War in an Age of Peace: U.S. Grand Strategy and Resolute Restraint*. Yale University Press, 2021. Argues for a disciplined approach to force sizing tied to realistic strategic objectives rather than maximalist threat assessments.
- Work, Robert O., and Greg Grant. *Beating the Americans at Their Own Game: An Offset Strategy with Chinese Characteristics*. CNAS, 2019. Analysis of Chinese force modernization and implications for U.S. force design, including the need for autonomous systems and distributed operations.
- Sapolsky, Harvey M., Eugene Gholz, and Caitlin Talmadge. *U.S. Defense Politics: The Origins of Security Policy*. 4th ed. Routledge, 2024. Leading textbook on how political forces shape defense decisions, including force structure and basing.
- Freedberg, Sydney J., Jr. *The Looming Hollow Force*. Breaking Defense analysis series, 2022-2024. Investigative reporting on readiness shortfalls and the gap between strategy and force structure.
- Scharre, Paul. *Army of None: Autonomous Weapons and the Future of War*. W.W. Norton, 2018. Essential analysis of how autonomous systems will reshape force structure and the ethical, legal, and strategic implications.
- Biddle, Stephen. *Military Power: Explaining Victory and Defeat in Modern Battle*. Princeton University Press, 2004. Academic analysis of the relationship between force employment, technology, and military effectiveness that challenges simplistic "quality vs. quantity" arguments.
- Colby, Elbridge. *The Strategy of Denial: American Defense in an Age of Great Power Conflict*. Yale University Press, 2021. Influential argument for sizing U.S. forces specifically for a Taiwan contingency as the primary force-sizing scenario.

## Commission Reports

- **National Defense Strategy Commission** (2018): *Providing for the Common Defense*. Bipartisan assessment warning that U.S. military superiority has eroded to a "dangerous degree" and recommending 3-5% real annual defense spending growth.
- **Commission on the National Defense Strategy** (2024): Updated assessment finding that the security environment is "the most dangerous since at least 1945" and that the current force is inadequately sized and shaped for the threat.
- **National Commission on the Future of the Army** (2016): Assessed Army force structure and the active-reserve balance, recommending against further active Army end-strength reductions.
- **Commission on Planning, Programming, Budgeting, and Execution Reform** (2024): Recommendations for modernizing the Pentagon's budget and resource allocation system, including portfolio-based budgeting and increased reprogramming flexibility.
- **National Commission on Military, National, and Public Service** (2020): *Inspired to Serve*. Recommendations for strengthening the All-Volunteer Force, including expanded national service options and military recruiting reform.

## Key Legal Authorities

- 10 U.S.C. Chapter 1003 -- Department of Defense Force Structure (end strength authorization)
- 10 U.S.C. 10102 -- Purpose of the Reserve Components
- 10 U.S.C. 12302 -- Ready Reserve Mobilization Authority
- Defense Base Closure and Realignment Act of 1990 (10 U.S.C. 2687) -- BRAC process
- Goldwater-Nichols Department of Defense Reorganization Act of 1986 (Pub. L. 99-433)
- National Defense Authorization Act (annual) -- Primary vehicle for force structure legislation
