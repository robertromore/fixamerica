# Force Structure: Resources

This section provides a curated list of key government sources, independent analyses, and expert organizations that inform the force structure debate.

---

## Primary Government Sources

- **Department of Defense**
    - *National Defense Strategy* (2022): The foundational document identifying China as the "pacing challenge" and establishing the strategic framework for force-sizing decisions.
    - *Annual Report on Military and Security Developments Involving the People's Republic of China*: DoD's annual assessment of Chinese military modernization, essential context for force-sizing.
    - [https://www.defense.gov/](https://www.defense.gov/)

- **Congressional Budget Office (CBO)**
    - CBO produces the most rigorous independent analyses of defense force structure costs and options. Key reports include:
    - *The U.S. Military's Force Structure: A Primer* (updated periodically): A comprehensive overview of how the force is organized and what it costs.
    - *An Analysis of the Navy's Fiscal Year 2024 Shipbuilding Plan*: Detailed assessment of fleet size options and costs.
    - *Approaches for Managing the Costs of U.S. Nuclear Forces*: Interaction between nuclear modernization and conventional force structure budgets.
    - [https://www.cbo.gov/topics/defense-and-national-security](https://www.cbo.gov/topics/defense-and-national-security)

- **Government Accountability Office (GAO)**
    - GAO audits DoD force structure, readiness, and recruiting programs. Key series include:
    - *High-Risk Series: DoD Weapon Systems Acquisition*
    - *Military Readiness* reports (annual)
    - *Military Personnel: DoD Needs to Improve Recruiting and Retention*
    - [https://www.gao.gov/](https://www.gao.gov/)

- **Congressional Research Service (CRS)**
    - CRS produces detailed, nonpartisan reports for Congress on every aspect of force structure:
    - *Defense Primer: The All-Volunteer Force*
    - *Navy Force Structure and Shipbuilding Plans*
    - *Army Multi-Domain Transformation*
    - *Marine Corps Force Design 2030*
    - [https://crsreports.congress.gov/](https://crsreports.congress.gov/)

## Independent Research and Analysis

- **RAND Corporation**
    - RAND is the DoD's largest federally funded research and development center and produces foundational force structure analysis:
    - *Force Planning for the Post-Cold War Era* (seminal framework study)
    - Studies on recruiting, retention, reserve component readiness, and force mix optimization.
    - [https://www.rand.org/topics/military-force-planning.html](https://www.rand.org/topics/military-force-planning.html)

- **Center for Strategic and International Studies (CSIS)**
    - CSIS produces extensive force structure analysis through multiple programs:
    - International Security Program: Force design and great power competition.
    - Defense-Industrial Initiatives Group: Shipbuilding, acquisition, and industrial base analysis.
    - *Restoring American Power* report series.
    - [https://www.csis.org/](https://www.csis.org/)

- **Center for a New American Security (CNAS)**
    - CNAS focuses on defense and national security with emphasis on technology, innovation, and force design:
    - Reports on autonomous systems, human-machine teaming, and defense innovation.
    - *As You Were: The Future of the All-Volunteer Force* (2023).
    - [https://www.cnas.org/](https://www.cnas.org/)

- **American Enterprise Institute (AEI)**
    - AEI's defense program produces analysis on force sizing, defense budgets, and military modernization, generally advocating for a larger and more capable force.
    - [https://www.aei.org/policy-areas/defense-and-national-security/](https://www.aei.org/policy-areas/defense-and-national-security/)

- **Hudson Institute**
    - Hudson's Center for Defense Concepts and Technology publishes analysis on naval force structure, shipbuilding, and fleet design.
    - [https://www.hudson.org/](https://www.hudson.org/)

- **Stimson Center / Project on Government Oversight (POGO)**
    - Provide analysis from a reform-oriented perspective, focusing on waste, inefficiency, and the need for structural change in defense.
    - [https://www.stimson.org/](https://www.stimson.org/)
    - [https://www.pogo.org/](https://www.pogo.org/)

## Key Books

- Cancian, Mark F. *U.S. Military Forces in FY2024: The Continued Decline of the Military*. CSIS, 2023. Comprehensive annual assessment of force structure trends.
- Krepinevich, Andrew F. *The Army and Vietnam*. Johns Hopkins University Press, 1986. Classic study of how institutional culture shapes force structure decisions.
- Builder, Carl H. *The Masks of War: American Military Styles in Strategy and Analysis*. Johns Hopkins University Press, 1989. Foundational analysis of how service identities drive force structure choices.
- O'Hanlon, Michael E. *The Art of War in an Age of Peace: U.S. Grand Strategy and Resolute Restraint*. Yale University Press, 2021. Argues for a disciplined approach to force sizing tied to realistic strategic objectives.
- Work, Robert O., and Greg Grant. *Beating the Americans at Their Own Game: An Offset Strategy with Chinese Characteristics*. CNAS, 2019. Analysis of Chinese force modernization and implications for U.S. force design.
- Sapolsky, Harvey M., Eugene Gholz, and Caitlin Talmadge. *U.S. Defense Politics: The Origins of Security Policy*. 4th ed. Routledge, 2024. Leading textbook on how political forces shape defense decisions.

## Commission Reports

- **National Defense Strategy Commission** (2018): *Providing for the Common Defense*. Bipartisan assessment warning that U.S. military superiority has eroded to a "dangerous degree."
- **National Commission on the Future of the Army** (2016): Assessed Army force structure and the active-reserve balance.
- **Commission on the National Defense Strategy** (2024): Updated assessment of defense strategy-resource alignment.
- **Commission on Planning, Programming, Budgeting, and Execution Reform** (2024): Recommendations for modernizing the defense resource allocation system.
