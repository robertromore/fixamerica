# Foreign Interventions: Resources

## Overview

This resource guide covers the essential academic research, books, reports, organizations, and datasets relevant to understanding U.S. foreign military interventions, their outcomes, and proposals for reform.

## Academic Research

### Key Studies on Intervention Outcomes

- Downes, Alexander B. and Jonathan Monten. "Forced to Be Free? Why Foreign-Imposed Regime Change Rarely Leads to Democratization." *International Security* 37, no. 4 (2013): 90-131. *Systematic analysis of 100+ cases showing foreign-imposed regime change rarely produces democracy.*
- Pape, Robert A. "When Duty Calls: A Pragmatic Standard of Humanitarian Intervention." *International Security* 37, no. 1 (2012): 41-80. *Proposes criteria for evaluating when humanitarian intervention is likely to succeed.*
- Enterline, Andrew J. and J. Michael Greig. "Beacons of Hope? The Impact of Imposed Democracy on Regional Peace, Democracy, and Prosperity." *Journal of Politics* 67, no. 4 (2005): 1075-1098. *Finds that imposed democracies are less stable and more conflict-prone than organically developed ones.*
- Sullivan, Patricia L. "War Aims and War Outcomes: Why Powerful States Lose Limited Wars." *Journal of Conflict Resolution* 51, no. 3 (2007): 496-524. *Explains why military superiority does not guarantee political success in limited interventions.*
- Mazarr, Michael J. "The Rise and Fall of the Failed-State Paradigm." *Foreign Affairs* 93, no. 1 (2014): 113-121. *Critiques the assumption that failed states require military intervention to prevent terrorism.*
- Byman, Daniel. "Understanding Proto-Insurgencies." *Journal of Strategic Studies* 31, no. 2 (2008): 165-200. *Analyzes how military interventions can create the insurgencies they are intended to prevent.*

### War Powers and Legal Authority

- Fisher, Louis. "The Law: When Wars Begin: Misleading Statements by Presidents." *Presidential Studies Quarterly* 40, no. 1 (2010): 171-184. *Documents presidential deception in initiating military operations.*
- Kriner, Douglas L. "Can Enhanced Oversight Repair the Broken Branch? Lessons from the National War Powers Commission." *Presidential Studies Quarterly* 40, no. 4 (2010): 702-724. *Evaluates proposals to reform congressional war powers oversight.*
- Waxman, Matthew C. "The Power to Wage War Successfully." *Columbia Law Review* 117, no. 3 (2017): 613-685. *Analyzes the constitutional allocation of war powers and modern challenges.*

### Civilian Casualties and Accountability

- Conetta, Carl. "Afghan Refugees and IDPs in Light of the US Air Campaign." *Project on Defense Alternatives Briefing Memo* 29 (2002). *Early systematic analysis of civilian harm from the Afghanistan intervention.*
- Crawford, Neta C. "Accountability for Killing: Moral Responsibility for Collateral Damage in America's Post-9/11 Wars." *Oxford University Press* (2013). *Comprehensive examination of civilian casualty patterns and moral accountability frameworks.*
- Grauer, Ryan. "Why Do Soldiers Give Up? A Self-Interest Model of Surrender on the Battlefield." *Security Studies* 23, no. 3 (2014): 622-655. *Relevant to understanding how intervention strategies affect combatant behavior and civilian exposure.*

## Key Books

### Critiques of Intervention Policy

- Bacevich, Andrew J. *America's War for the Greater Middle East: A Military History*. New York: Random House, 2016. *Comprehensive history of U.S. military engagement in the Middle East from 1980 to the present, arguing it represents a single, failed campaign.*
- Bacevich, Andrew J. *The Limits of Power: The End of American Exceptionalism*. New York: Metropolitan Books, 2008. *Argues that American exceptionalism fuels strategic overreach.*
- Posen, Barry R. *Restraint: A New Foundation for U.S. Grand Strategy*. Ithaca: Cornell University Press, 2014. *Makes the case for a strategy of restraint that reduces military intervention and forward deployment.*
- Walt, Stephen M. *The Hell of Good Intentions: America's Foreign Policy Elite and the Decline of U.S. Primacy*. New York: Farrar, Straus and Giroux, 2018. *Argues that the foreign policy establishment's commitment to liberal hegemony has produced repeated failures.*
- Mearsheimer, John J. and Stephen M. Walt. *The Israel Lobby and U.S. Foreign Policy*. New York: Farrar, Straus and Giroux, 2007. *Examines the influence of pro-Israel lobbying on U.S. Middle East intervention policy.*

### Historical Accounts

- Halberstam, David. *The Best and the Brightest*. New York: Random House, 1972. *Classic account of how brilliant, confident policymakers led the U.S. into the Vietnam quagmire.*
- Ricks, Thomas E. *Fiasco: The American Military Adventure in Iraq, 2003 to 2005*. New York: Penguin, 2006. *Detailed account of the failures of planning and execution in the Iraq invasion and occupation.*
- Whitlock, Craig. *The Afghanistan Papers: A Secret History of the War*. New York: Simon and Schuster, 2021. *Based on the Washington Post investigation, revealing systematic deception about the war's progress.*
- Chollet, Derek. *The Long Game: How Obama Defied Washington and Redefined America's Role in the World*. New York: PublicAffairs, 2016. *Insider account of the Obama administration's approach to intervention, including the Libya decision.*
- Woodward, Bob. *Plan of Attack*. New York: Simon and Schuster, 2004. *Detailed account of the decision-making process leading to the Iraq invasion.*
- Power, Samantha. *"A Problem from Hell": America and the Age of Genocide*. New York: Basic Books, 2002. *Examines the history of U.S. responses (and non-responses) to genocide, making the case for humanitarian intervention.*
- Kaplan, Fred. *The Insurgents: David Petraeus and the Plot to Change the American Way of War*. New York: Simon and Schuster, 2013. *History of the counterinsurgency movement and its influence on Iraq and Afghanistan strategy.*

### Nation-Building and State Failure

- Dobbins, James, et al. *America's Role in Nation-Building: From Germany to Iraq*. RAND Corporation, 2003. *Systematic comparison of U.S. nation-building efforts, with lessons for policy.*
- Fukuyama, Francis. *State-Building: Governance and World Order in the 21st Century*. Ithaca: Cornell University Press, 2004. *Examines the challenges of building state institutions from the outside.*

## Think Tank Reports

### Critical / Restraint-Oriented

- **Costs of War Project** (Brown University Watson Institute). *20 Years of War: A Costs of War Research Series*. Providence: Watson Institute, 2021-2024. *The most comprehensive accounting of the financial, human, and social costs of post-9/11 wars.* Available at: <https://watson.brown.edu/costsofwar/>
- **Quincy Institute for Responsible Statecraft**. Various research briefs on intervention reform, AUMF repeal, and diplomatic alternatives. Available at: <https://quincyinst.org/>
- **CATO Institute**. Thrall, A. Trevor and Erik Goepner. "Step Back: Lessons for U.S. Foreign Policy from the Failed War on Terror." Policy Analysis No. 814, 2017. *Argues the war on terror has been counterproductive.*
- **Stimson Center**. "Recommendations and Report of the Task Force on U.S. Drone Policy." 2015. *Bipartisan recommendations for reforming targeted killing operations.*

### Mainstream / Establishment

- **RAND Corporation**. Paul, Christopher, et al. *Paths to Victory: Lessons from Modern Insurgencies*. RAND, 2013. *Analysis of 71 insurgencies to identify factors associated with government victory.*
- **Brookings Institution**. O'Hanlon, Michael. "The Art of War in an Age of Peace: U.S. Grand Strategy and Resolute Restraint." *Brookings, 2021*. *Centrist argument for "resolute restraint" that maintains engagement while being more selective about intervention.*
- **Council on Foreign Relations**. "Preventive Priorities Survey" (annual). *Annual expert survey ranking potential international crises and U.S. response options.*
- **Center for a New American Security (CNAS)**. Various reports on great power competition and the balance between CT operations and strategic competition.

### Congressional and Government Reports

- **Congressional Research Service**. Weed, Matthew C. "Presidential References to the 2001 Authorization for Use of Military Force in Publicly Available Executive Actions and Reports to Congress." CRS Report, updated regularly. *Tracks every executive branch invocation of the 2001 AUMF.*
- **Special Inspector General for Afghanistan Reconstruction (SIGAR)**. "What We Need to Learn: Lessons from Twenty Years of Afghanistan Reconstruction." 2021. *Comprehensive assessment of reconstruction failures in Afghanistan.*
- **Iraq Study Group**. "The Iraq Study Group Report." 2006. *Bipartisan commission recommendations for Iraq policy.*
- **National War Powers Commission** (co-chaired by James Baker and Warren Christopher). "National War Powers Commission Report." 2008. *Proposed a new framework for executive-legislative consultation on military operations.*
