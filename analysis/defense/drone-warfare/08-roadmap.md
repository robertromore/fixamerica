# Drone Warfare: Implementation Roadmap

## Overview

Reforming U.S. drone warfare policy requires a phased approach that builds political momentum through early achievable wins while laying the groundwork for deeper structural reforms. The roadmap below sequences reforms based on political feasibility, institutional readiness, and the dependency relationships between different reform elements. Each phase builds on the accomplishments of the previous one, creating a ratchet effect that makes regression more difficult.

## Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-12)

**Objective**: Establish transparency and accountability mechanisms that build the evidentiary base for deeper reforms.

### Legislative Actions

| Action | Vehicle | Champion(s) | Feasibility |
|---|---|---|---|
| Enact statutory requirement for annual public drone strike reporting | NDAA amendment | Bipartisan (Kaine-Lee model) | High |
| Require congressional notification within 48 hours of all strikes | NDAA amendment | Armed Services Committee | Medium-High |
| Mandate GAO audit of civilian casualty assessment procedures | NDAA amendment | Appropriations Committee | High |
| Establish Civilian Victim Compensation Fund pilot program | NDAA amendment or standalone | Foreign Affairs Committee | Medium |

### Executive Branch Actions

| Action | Lead Agency | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| Full implementation of CHMR-AP across all combatant commands | DoD (USD Policy) | 6 months |
| Publish unclassified methodology for civilian casualty assessment | DoD (Inspector General) | 9 months |
| Begin declassification review of drone strike records older than 10 years | DNI | 12 months |
| Establish interagency working group on autonomous weapons governance | NSC | 3 months |

### Civil Society Actions

| Action | Lead Organizations | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| Build bipartisan congressional coalition for transparency legislation | ACLU, R Street Institute | Ongoing |
| Publish updated civilian casualty databases with standardized methodology | Airwars, Bureau of Investigative Journalism | Ongoing |
| Convene expert panel on meaningful human control standards | Partnership on AI, ICRC | 6 months |

## Phase 2: Legal Framework (Months 12-30)

**Objective**: Establish the statutory legal framework for drone strikes, autonomous weapons governance, and AUMF reform.

### Legislative Actions

| Action | Vehicle | Champion(s) | Feasibility |
|---|---|---|---|
| Introduce and advance the Drone Strike Transparency and Accountability Act | Standalone / NDAA | Bipartisan coalition | Medium |
| Introduce the Autonomous Weapons Governance Act | Standalone / NDAA | Armed Services Committee | Medium |
| Advance AUMF repeal and replacement legislation | Standalone (fast-track) | Kaine-Lee framework | Medium-Low |
| Enact War Powers Resolution modernization | Standalone | Foreign Relations Committee | Low-Medium |

### Executive Branch Actions

| Action | Lead Agency | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| Publish declassification review results for operations 2001-2015 | DNI | 18 months |
| Establish independent civilian harm review panel | DoD (Secretary) | 15 months |
| Issue DoD policy on meaningful human control for autonomous weapons | USD R&E / USD Policy | 18 months |
| Complete transfer of remaining CIA lethal strike authority to DoD | CIA / DoD (if legislated) | 24 months |

### International Actions

| Action | Lead | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| Propose CCW protocol on lethal autonomous weapons | U.S. delegation | 18 months |
| Initiate bilateral discussions with allies on drone use norms | State Department | 12-18 months |
| Strengthen MTCR provisions on armed UAS exports | State Department | 24 months |

## Phase 3: Structural Reform (Months 30-60)

**Objective**: Implement deep structural reforms that change institutional incentives and establish durable governance frameworks.

### Legislative Actions

| Action | Vehicle | Champion(s) | Feasibility |
|---|---|---|---|
| Enact comprehensive AUMF reform with sunset clause | Standalone (fast-track) | Bipartisan coalition | Medium (improved by Phase 1-2 groundwork) |
| Establish permanent independent civilian harm investigation body | Standalone | Judiciary / Armed Services | Medium |
| Codify autonomous weapons human control requirement into law | NDAA or standalone | Armed Services Committee | Medium |
| Establish drone warfare oversight commission (permanent, bipartisan) | Standalone | Intelligence Committees | Low-Medium |

### Executive Branch Actions

| Action | Lead Agency | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| Complete declassification review of all operations 2001-present | DNI | 48 months |
| Implement full independent civilian harm investigation system | DoD / Independent body | 36 months |
| Publish comprehensive historical accounting of civilian casualties | DoD / Independent body | 48 months |

### International Actions

| Action | Lead | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| Conclude multilateral agreement on lethal autonomous weapons | U.S. / UN | 48-60 months |
| Establish multilateral drone export control regime | State Department / multilateral | 48-60 months |
| Launch international civilian harm investigation standards | ICRC / State Department | 36 months |

## Phase 4: Consolidation (Months 60+)

**Objective**: Institutionalize reforms, conduct comprehensive review, and adapt governance frameworks to evolving technology.

### Key Actions

- Conduct five-year comprehensive review of all implemented reforms
- Update autonomous weapons governance framework based on technological developments
- Assess effectiveness of AUMF sunset mechanism after first renewal cycle
- Review civilian victim compensation fund operations and adequacy
- Evaluate international norm compliance and enforcement mechanisms
- Update drone export control regime based on proliferation developments

## Success Metrics

| Category | Metric | Baseline (2024) | Target (Year 3) | Target (Year 5) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Transparency** | Annual public strike reports published | 0 (no statutory requirement) | 3 reports published | 5 consecutive reports |
| **Transparency** | Percentage of historical operations declassified | <5% | 30% | 60% |
| **Civilian Harm** | Independent investigation rate for credible allegations | ~20% (estimated) | 80% | 100% |
| **Civilian Harm** | Compensation claims processed within 6 months | <10% | 50% | 80% |
| **Legal Framework** | Drone strikes conducted under post-2001 statutory authority | 0% | 0% (legislation pending) | 100% |
| **Autonomous Weapons** | Autonomous systems with statutory human control certification | 0 | Certification process established | All fielded systems certified |
| **Congressional Oversight** | Timely notification of strikes to committees | Inconsistent | 95% | 100% |
| **International Norms** | Nations endorsing meaningful human control standard | ~30 | 50 | 80+ |
| **Proliferation** | Armed drone exports with end-use monitoring | <50% | 75% | 90% |

## Risk Assessment

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Executive branch vetoes AUMF reform | High | High | Attach to must-pass NDAA; build veto-proof coalition |
| Terrorist attack triggers regression to pre-reform authorities | Medium | Very High | Build sunset and fast-track renewal into framework; avoid all-or-nothing design |
| Intelligence community circumvents transparency requirements | High | Medium | IG oversight; congressional enforcement through appropriations |
| Autonomous weapons technology outpaces governance | High | High | Build technology-neutral principles into legislation; require periodic updates |
| International negotiations stall | High | Medium | Pursue bilateral and plurilateral agreements as alternatives to universal treaty |
| Public indifference limits political pressure | High | Medium | Civil society campaigns tied to specific incidents and data releases |

## Coalition Building Strategy

### Core Coalition

The most viable reform coalition unites:

- **Left-leaning reformers** (motivated by civilian protection, anti-war values, civil liberties)
- **Constitutionalist/libertarian right** (motivated by Article I war powers, due process, limiting executive authority)
- **Centrist institutionalists** (motivated by legal durability, bipartisan process, sustainable policy)

### Messaging by Audience

| Audience | Frame | Key Message |
|---|---|---|
| Conservative/constitutionalist | War powers / Article I | "Congress must vote on war, not delegate it indefinitely" |
| Progressive/liberal | Civilian protection / human rights | "Accountability for civilian casualties is a moral imperative" |
| Libertarian | Limited government / due process | "No government should kill without oversight or accountability" |
| Centrist | Good governance / legal durability | "Codifying rules prevents whiplash between administrations" |
| Military community | Force legitimacy / operator welfare | "Clear rules protect operators and strengthen mission legitimacy" |
| General public | Democratic accountability | "Americans deserve to know how their government uses lethal force in their name" |
