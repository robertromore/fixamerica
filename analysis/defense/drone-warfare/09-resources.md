# Drone Warfare: Resources

## Overview

This resource guide covers the major academic, journalistic, legal, and organizational sources for understanding U.S. drone warfare policy, autonomous weapons development, civilian casualties, and the legal and ethical debates surrounding unmanned lethal force.

## Academic Research

### Books

- Scahill, Jeremy. *Dirty Wars: The World is a Battlefield.* Nation Books, 2013. Investigative account of JSOC and CIA targeted killing operations.
- Mayer, Jane. "The Predator War." *The New Yorker*, October 26, 2009. Foundational long-form journalism on the CIA drone program.
- Chamayou, Gregoire. *A Theory of the Drone.* Translated by Janet Lloyd. The New Press, 2015. Philosophical analysis of drone warfare's implications for democracy and ethics.
- Moyn, Samuel. *Humane: How the United States Abandoned Peace and Reinvented War.* Farrar, Straus and Giroux, 2021. Historical argument about how "humane war" technology enabled perpetual conflict.
- Kaag, John and Sarah Kreps. *Drone Warfare.* Polity Press, 2014. Analysis of the political, ethical, and legal dimensions of drone use.
- Gusterson, Hugh. *Drone: Remote Control Warfare.* MIT Press, 2016. Anthropological study of drone warfare's cultural and political implications.
- Bergen, Peter and Daniel Rothenberg, eds. *Drone Wars: Transforming Conflict, Law, and Policy.* Cambridge University Press, 2015. Multi-disciplinary collection of perspectives on drone warfare.
- Whittle, Richard. *Predator: The Secret Origins of the Drone Revolution.* Henry Holt, 2014. History of armed drone development at General Atomics and the CIA.
- Cockburn, Andrew. *Kill Chain: The Rise of the High-Tech Assassins.* Henry Holt, 2015. Critical analysis of the targeted killing program's effectiveness.
- Zenko, Micah. *Between Threats and War: U.S. Discrete Military Operations in the Post-Cold War World.* Stanford University Press, 2010. Analysis of low-visibility military operations.

### Journal Articles and Papers

- Chesney, Robert. "Military-Intelligence Convergence and the Law of the Title 10/Title 50 Debate." *Journal of National Security Law and Policy* 5 (2012): 539-629.
- Heyns, Christof. "Report of the Special Rapporteur on Extrajudicial, Summary or Arbitrary Executions." United Nations Human Rights Council, A/68/382, September 2013.
- Kreps, Sarah and Micah Zenko. "The Next Drone Wars: Preparing for Proliferation." *Foreign Affairs* 93, no. 2 (2014): 68-79.
- Mayer, Jane. "The Predator War." *The New Yorker*, October 26, 2009.
- McNeal, Gregory S. "Targeted Killing and Accountability." *Georgetown Law Journal* 102 (2014): 681-794.
- Rona, Gabor. "A Bull in a China Shop: The War on Terror and International Humanitarian Law in the Middle East." *Military Law Review* 191 (2007): 55-89.
- Scharre, Paul. "Autonomous Weapons and Operational Risk." Center for a New American Security, 2016.
- Stanford Law School International Human Rights and Conflict Resolution Clinic and NYU School of Law Global Justice Clinic. *Living Under Drones: Death, Injury, and Trauma to Civilians from US Drone Practices in Pakistan.* 2012.
- Stimson Center. *Recommendations and Report of the Task Force on U.S. Drone Policy.* 2d ed., 2015.
- Williams, Brian Glyn. "The CIA's Covert Predator Drone War in Pakistan, 2004-2010." *Studies in Conflict and Terrorism* 33, no. 10 (2010): 871-892.

## Reports and Databases

### Primary Monitoring Organizations

- **Bureau of Investigative Journalism (TBIJ)**
    - *Drone Warfare Database* - Comprehensive strike-by-strike data for Pakistan, Yemen, Somalia, and Afghanistan
    - URL: [https://www.thebureauinvestigates.com/projects/drone-war](https://www.thebureauinvestigates.com/projects/drone-war)
    - Updated continuously; most widely cited independent source

- **Airwars**
    - *Civilian Harm Monitoring* - Tracks civilian casualties from U.S. and coalition airstrikes across all theaters
    - URL: [https://airwars.org](https://airwars.org)
    - Cross-references multiple open sources; covers Iraq, Syria, Libya, Somalia, Yemen

- **New America Foundation**
    - *World of Drones* - Database of global drone strikes, proliferation, and policy
    - URL: [https://www.newamerica.org/international-security/reports/world-drones](https://www.newamerica.org/international-security/reports/world-drones)
    - Includes interactive maps and analysis; tracks proliferation

### Government Reports

- **Department of Defense Annual Report on Civilian Casualties** (when published; discontinued under Trump, restarted under Biden)
- **Government Accountability Office (GAO)**
    - GAO-22-105988, *Military Operations: DoD Should Take Additional Actions to Enhance Oversight of Civilian Casualties* (2022)
    - GAO-24-106337, *Civilian Harm: DOD Has Taken Steps but Could Improve Reporting and Monitoring* (2024)
- **Congressional Research Service (CRS)**
    - "The 2001 AUMF: Issues for the 118th Congress" (updated periodically)
    - "Drone Strikes: Background, U.S. Policy, and Issues" (multiple editions)
    - "Lethal Autonomous Weapon Systems: Issues for Congress" (updated periodically)
- **Department of Defense Inspector General**
    - Reports on individual strike investigations and systemic assessment of civilian harm procedures
- **DoD Civilian Harm Mitigation and Response Action Plan (CHMR-AP)** (2022)

### Think Tank and NGO Reports

- **RAND Corporation**
    - "The Drone War Comes Home: Unmanned Systems and the Future of Air Power" (2021)
    - Various classified and unclassified analyses of drone operations effectiveness
- **Center for a New American Security (CNAS)**
    - Scharre, Paul. *Army of None: Autonomous Weapons and the Future of War.* W.W. Norton, 2018.
    - Multiple reports on autonomous weapons policy and AI in warfare
- **Stimson Center**
    - *Task Force on U.S. Drone Policy* (2015; updated 2018)
    - Research on war powers reform and drone accountability
- **Columbia Law School Human Rights Institute**
    - *Counting Drone Strike Deaths* (2012)
    - Research on legal frameworks for targeted killing
- **Reprieve**
    - Legal casework on behalf of drone strike victims and their families
    - Documentation of civilian casualties in Yemen and Pakistan
- **Center for Civilians in Conflict (CIVIC)**
    - Reports on civilian harm mitigation and response
    - Advocacy for CHMR-AP implementation
- **Human Rights Watch**
    - Country-specific reports on drone strikes and civilian casualties
    - Research on autonomous weapons

## Legal Sources

### Statutory Authorities

- **2001 Authorization for Use of Military Force**, P.L. 107-40, 115 Stat. 224 (2001), 50 U.S.C. 1541 note
- **War Powers Resolution of 1973**, P.L. 93-148, 50 U.S.C. 1541-1548
- **National Security Act of 1947** (as amended), 50 U.S.C. 3001 et seq.
- **10 U.S.C. 127e** (Authority for support of special operations to combat terrorism)
- **10 U.S.C. 2249e** (Civilian harm reporting and response)
- **Executive Order 13732** (2016; revoked 2019) requiring annual civilian casualty reporting

### Key Case Law

- *Youngstown Sheet and Tube Co. v. Sawyer*, 343 U.S. 579 (1952) - Presidential power framework
- *Hamdi v. Rumsfeld*, 542 U.S. 507 (2004) - Due process for U.S. citizen enemy combatants
- *Al-Aulaqi v. Obama*, 727 F. Supp. 2d 1 (D.D.C. 2010) - Pre-strike challenge dismissed
- *Al-Aulaqi v. Panetta*, 35 F. Supp. 3d 56 (D.D.C. 2014) - Post-strike challenge dismissed
- *Boumediene v. Bush*, 553 U.S. 723 (2008) - Habeas rights extend beyond U.S. territory
- *Zivotofsky v. Clinton*, 566 U.S. 189 (2012) - Political question doctrine limitations

### International Law

- **Geneva Conventions** (1949) and Additional Protocols (1977) - Laws of armed conflict
- **UN Charter, Article 51** - Self-defense in international law
- **International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights (ICCPR)** - Right to life provisions
- **Convention on Certain Conventional Weapons (CCW)** - Framework for autonomous weapons discussions
