# Intelligence Oversight: Resources

## Key Government Reports and Investigations

### Church Committee Reports (1975-1976)

- **Senate Select Committee to Study Governmental Operations with Respect to Intelligence Activities.** *Final Report.* 6 books. Washington: U.S. Government Printing Office, 1976.
    - Book I: Foreign and Military Intelligence
    - Book II: Intelligence Activities and the Rights of Americans
    - Book III: Supplementary Detailed Staff Reports on Intelligence Activities and the Rights of Americans
    - Book IV: Supplementary Detailed Staff Reports on Foreign and Military Intelligence
    - Book V: The Investigation of the Assassination of President John F. Kennedy
    - Book VI: Supplementary Reports on Intelligence Activities
- **Significance**: The most comprehensive investigation of U.S. intelligence abuses ever conducted. Directly led to the creation of SSCI, HPSCI, and FISA.

### 9/11 Commission Report (2004)

- **National Commission on Terrorist Attacks Upon the United States.** *The 9/11 Commission Report: Final Report of the National Commission on Terrorist Attacks Upon the United States.* New York: Norton, 2004.
- **Significance**: Documented the intelligence failures leading to 9/11 and recommended the creation of the DNI and NCTC. Led to IRTPA of 2004.

### Senate Intelligence Committee Torture Report (2014)

- **Senate Select Committee on Intelligence.** *Committee Study of the Central Intelligence Agency's Detention and Interrogation Program.* Executive Summary. December 2014.
- **Significance**: Documented the CIA's "enhanced interrogation" program, concluding that the techniques constituted torture, were not effective, and that the CIA misled Congress and the executive branch about the program.

### PCLOB Reports

- **Privacy and Civil Liberties Oversight Board.** *Report on the Telephone Records Program Conducted Under Section 215 of the USA PATRIOT Act and on the Operations of the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Court.* January 2014.
- **Privacy and Civil Liberties Oversight Board.** *Report on the Surveillance Program Operated Pursuant to Section 702 of the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act.* July 2014.
- **Significance**: The first independent public assessments of the NSA's bulk metadata collection (Section 215) and targeted collection (Section 702) programs.

### Government Accountability Office (GAO)

- GAO-22-105585, "Intelligence Community: Actions Needed to Improve Management of Security Clearance Determination Timeliness" (2022).
- GAO-24-106872, "Classified Information: Agencies Need to Take Steps to Improve Over-Classification Reviews" (2024).

### Information Security Oversight Office (ISOO)

- ISOO, *Annual Report to the President.* Published annually. Contains data on classification and declassification activity across the government.

### DOJ Inspector General

- **Office of the Inspector General, Department of Justice.** *A Review of the FBI's Use of Section 215 Orders for Business Records.* March 2015.
- **Office of the Inspector General, Department of Justice.** *Audit of the Federal Bureau of Investigation's Compliance with Procedures for Querying Section 702 Information.* 2023.

## Key Statutes and Legal Authorities

| Statute | Citation | Significance |
|---|---|---|
| National Security Act of 1947 | 50 U.S.C. Chapter 44 | Foundational statute creating CIA and the IC |
| Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act (FISA) | 50 U.S.C. Chapter 36 | Established the FISA Court and framework for intelligence surveillance |
| Intelligence Reform and Terrorism Prevention Act (IRTPA) | Pub. L. 108-458 (2004) | Created the DNI, NCTC, and PCLOB |
| USA PATRIOT Act | Pub. L. 107-56 (2001) | Expanded surveillance authorities post-9/11 |
| USA FREEDOM Act | Pub. L. 114-23 (2015) | Ended bulk metadata collection under Section 215 |
| FISA Amendments Act of 2008 | Pub. L. 110-261 | Created Section 702 authority |
| Intelligence Community Whistleblower Protection Act | 5 U.S.C. App. 3, Section 8H | Established reporting channel for IC whistleblowers (no anti-retaliation protections) |
| Executive Order 12333 | 46 Fed. Reg. 59941 (1981) | Primary executive order governing U.S. intelligence activities |
| Executive Order 13526 | 75 Fed. Reg. 707 (2009) | Governs the classification and declassification system |
| Presidential Policy Directive 19 (PPD-19) | 2012 | Administrative protections for IC whistleblowers |
| Executive Order 14086 | 87 Fed. Reg. 62283 (2022) | Post-*Schrems II* safeguards on signals intelligence |

## Key Court Cases

| Case | Citation | Significance |
|---|---|---|
| *United States v. Reynolds* | 345 U.S. 1 (1953) | Established the state secrets privilege |
| *Keith Case (United States v. U.S. District Court)* | 407 U.S. 297 (1972) | Held that the Fourth Amendment requires judicial approval for domestic security surveillance |
| *Clapper v. Amnesty International* | 568 U.S. 398 (2013) | Denied standing to challenge Section 702 surveillance due to lack of proof of injury |
| *Jewel v. NSA* | (N.D. Cal., ongoing) | EFF challenge to NSA upstream surveillance; repeatedly stalled by state secrets claims |
| *Department of the Navy v. Egan* | 484 U.S. 518 (1988) | Affirmed executive authority over security clearance decisions |
| *Schrems II (CJEU)* | Case C-311/18 (2020) | EU Court invalidated Privacy Shield framework over U.S. surveillance concerns; led to EO 14086 |
| *Youngstown Sheet & Tube Co. v. Sawyer* | 343 U.S. 579 (1952) | Framework for evaluating executive power relative to congressional authority |
