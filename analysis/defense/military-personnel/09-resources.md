# Military Personnel: Resources

## Government Reports and Data

- **DoD Annual Demographics Report.** Department of Defense. Published annually. Provides comprehensive data on the demographic composition of the active-duty, reserve, and civilian workforce by service, rank, gender, race, and ethnicity. Available at: <https://www.militaryonesource.mil/data-research-and-statistics/military-community-demographics/>
- **DoD Annual Suicide Report.** Department of Defense. Published annually. Reports suicide data for active-duty, reserve, and National Guard personnel, including rates, demographics, and risk factors. Available through the Defense Suicide Prevention Office.
- **Military Compensation Background Papers.** Office of the Under Secretary of Defense for Personnel and Readiness. Updated periodically. Comprehensive reference on every element of military pay, allowances, and benefits. Available at: <https://militarypay.defense.gov/>
- **Congressional Budget Office, "Approaches to Changing Military Compensation."** CBO, 2020. Analyzes options for restructuring military pay, retirement, and healthcare benefits, with cost estimates and effects on recruiting and retention.
- **Congressional Budget Office, "The All-Volunteer Military: Issues and Performance."** CBO, 2007. Foundational analysis of the all-volunteer force's strengths and vulnerabilities.
- **Government Accountability Office, "Military Housing Privatization: DoD Should Take Steps to Improve Monitoring, Reporting, and Oversight" (GAO-23-105377).** GAO, 2023. Documents privatized housing conditions and oversight failures.
- **Government Accountability Office, "Military Recruiting: DoD and Services Need Better Data to Enhance Monitoring of Recruiter Irregularities" (GAO-15-163).** GAO, 2015.
- **DoD Inspector General, "Audit of the DoD's Management of the Military Housing Privatization Initiative" (DODIG-2023-088).** DoD IG, 2023.
- **DoD Office of People Analytics, Joint Advertising, Market Research & Studies (JAMRS).** Annual reports on youth propensity to serve, awareness of military opportunities, and attitudes toward military service.

## RAND Corporation Studies

- **Asch, Beth J., et al. "Can the Army Meet Its Recruiting Goals?" RAND Corporation, 2023.** Analysis of the Army's FY2023 recruiting shortfall and options for recovery.
- **Asch, Beth J., James Hosek, and Michael G. Mattock. "Toward Meaningful Military Compensation Reform." RAND Corporation, 2014.** Comprehensive review of military compensation with reform options.
- **Schaefer, Agnes Gereben, et al. "Assessing the Implications of Allowing Transgender Persons to Serve Openly." RAND Corporation, 2016.** Analysis of the costs and effects of open transgender service.
- **Meadows, Sarah O., et al. "The Deployment Life Study: Longitudinal Analysis of Military Families Across the Deployment Cycle." RAND Corporation, 2016.** Multi-year study of how deployments affect military families.
- **RAND Corporation. "Sexual Orientation and Transgender Identity in Military Service: Implementation of the Repeal of Don't Ask, Don't Tell." 2014.** Assessment of the effects of DADT repeal on force readiness and cohesion.
- **Wenger, Jennie W., et al. "Lateral Entry into Military Service: Opportunities and Challenges." RAND Corporation, 2019.** Analysis of lateral entry options for bringing civilian expertise into the military at mid-career ranks.

## Military Family Research

- **Blue Star Families. "Military Family Lifestyle Survey."** Published annually. The largest annual survey of military family life, covering employment, childcare, healthcare, housing, and overall well-being. Widely cited by Congress and DoD. Available at: <https://bluestarfam.org/research/>
- **Military Family Advisory Network. "Military Family Support Programming Survey."** Published annually. Documents food insecurity, financial stress, and access to support services among military families.
- **Military Child Education Coalition. "Research and Publications."** Research on educational outcomes for military-connected children, including the effects of school transitions and deployment on academic performance.
- **National Military Family Association. "NMFA Position Papers and Reports."** Policy advocacy publications on childcare, healthcare, spouse employment, and family readiness.

## Academic Literature

- Rostker, Bernard. *I Want You! The Evolution of the All-Volunteer Force.* RAND Corporation, 2006. The definitive history of the all-volunteer force from its origins through the early Iraq War.
- Bailey, Beth. *America's Army: Making the All-Volunteer Force.* Harvard University Press, 2009. Historical analysis of how the military adapted to the end of conscription.
- Segal, Mady Wechsler. "The Military and the Family as Greedy Institutions." *Armed Forces & Society* 13, no. 1 (1986): 9-38. Foundational article on the competing demands of military service and family life.
- Clever, Molly, and David R. Segal. "The Demographics of Military Children and Families." *The Future of Children* 23, no. 2 (2013): 13-39.
- Lien, Diana S., and Shane R. Sears. "Assessing the Impact of Military Spouse Employment Programs." *RAND Corporation*, 2021.

## Congressional Research Service Reports

- Kamarck, Kristy N. "Military Personnel Strengths and Force Readiness." Congressional Research Service, updated regularly. Tracks end-strength levels, readiness indicators, and personnel policy developments.
- Kamarck, Kristy N. "Diversity, Inclusion, and Equal Opportunity in the Armed Services: Background and Issues for Congress." CRS, 2023.
- Henning, Charles A. "Military Pay: Key Questions and Answers." CRS, 2022. Concise overview of the military pay system.
- Kapp, Lawrence. "Recruiting and Retention: An Overview of FY2023 Results." CRS, 2024.
- Schwemle, Barbara L. "Military Spouse Employment." CRS, 2023.

## Key Commissions and Reviews

- **The Gates Commission (1970).** "The Report of the President's Commission on an All-Volunteer Armed Force." The foundational document arguing for the end of conscription and the establishment of the all-volunteer force.
- **The Military Compensation and Retirement Modernization Commission (2015).** "Report of the Military Compensation and Retirement Modernization Commission." The commission that recommended the Blended Retirement System and other compensation reforms.
- **The National Commission on Military, National, and Public Service (2020).** "Inspired to Serve." Comprehensive examination of who serves and how to strengthen the pipeline of Americans willing to serve in the military and in civilian national service.
