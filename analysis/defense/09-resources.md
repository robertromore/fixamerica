# Defense Policy: Resources

## Academic Research

- Biddle, Stephen. "Military Power: Explaining Victory and Defeat in Modern Battle." *Princeton University Press* (2004).
- Brooks, Rosa. "How Everything Became War and the Military Became Everything." *Simon & Schuster* (2016).
- Posen, Barry R. "Restraint: A New Foundation for U.S. Grand Strategy." *Cornell University Press* (2014).
- Work, Robert, and Shawn Brimley. "20YY: Preparing for War in the Robotic Age." *Center for a New American Security* (2014).

## Reports and Data

- **Department of Defense**. "National Defense Strategy." 2022. [defense.gov](https://www.defense.gov/National-Defense-Strategy/)
- **Congressional Budget Office**. "Long-Term Implications of the 2024 Future Years Defense Program." 2024. [cbo.gov](https://www.cbo.gov/publication/59510)
- **Government Accountability Office**. "Weapon Systems Annual Assessment." 2024. [gao.gov](https://www.gao.gov/products/gao-24-106831)
- **Stockholm International Peace Research Institute (SIPRI)**. "SIPRI Military Expenditure Database." Updated annually. [sipri.org](https://www.sipri.org/databases/milex)
- **National Defense Industrial Association**. "Vital Signs 2024: The Health and Readiness of the Defense Industrial Base." 2024. [ndia.org](https://www.ndia.org/policy/vital-signs)

## Books

- *Kill Chain: Defending America in the Future of High-Tech Warfare* by Christian Brose (Hachette Books, 2020).
- *The Kill Chain* argues that the U.S. military is losing its technological edge and proposes a new strategy based on autonomous systems.
- *The Pentagon's Brain: An Uncensored History of DARPA* by Annie Jacobsen (Little, Brown and Company, 2015).
- *Destined for War: Can America and China Escape Thucydides's Trap?* by Graham Allison (Houghton Mifflin Harcourt, 2017).
- *Assignment Pentagon: How to Excel in a Bureaucracy* by Perry M. Smith and Daniel M. Gerstein (Potomac Books, 2015).
