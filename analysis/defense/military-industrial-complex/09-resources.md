# Military-Industrial Complex: Resources

## Books

- Hartung, William D. *Prophets of War: Lockheed Martin and the Making of the Military-Industrial Complex*. New York: Bold Type Books, 2011.
    - Comprehensive history of the largest defense contractor and its political influence.
- Hartung, William D. *The Profits of War: Corporate Beneficiaries of the Post-9/11 Pentagon Spending Surge*. Watson Institute, Brown University, 2021.
    - Analysis of defense contractor profits during the War on Terror era.
- Melman, Seymour. *Pentagon Capitalism: The Political Economy of War*. New York: McGraw-Hill, 1970.
    - Foundational academic analysis of the defense-industrial relationship as a permanent feature of the American economy.
- Melman, Seymour. *The Permanent War Economy: American Capitalism in Decline*. New York: Simon & Schuster, 1974.
    - Argues that permanent defense spending distorts the civilian economy and undermines industrial competitiveness.
- Ledbetter, James. *Unwarranted Influence: Dwight D. Eisenhower and the Military-Industrial Complex*. New Haven: Yale University Press, 2011.
    - Traces the origins of Eisenhower's warning and its subsequent relevance.
- Fallows, James. *National Defense*. New York: Random House, 1981.
    - Classic analysis of Pentagon procurement dysfunction and the reform movement of the 1980s.
- Wheeler, Winslow T., and Lawrence J. Korb. *Military Reform: A Reference Handbook*. Westport, CT: Praeger Security International, 2007.
    - Reference guide to defense reform efforts and proposals.
- Turse, Nick. *The Complex: How the Military Invades Our Everyday Lives*. New York: Metropolitan Books, 2008.
    - Investigates the expansion of the military-industrial complex into civilian life and commerce.
- Mazzucato, Mariana. *The Entrepreneurial State: Debunking Public vs. Private Sector Myths*. London: Anthem Press, 2013.
    - Challenges the narrative that the private sector is the sole source of defense innovation; argues for a more active government role.
- Thorpe, Rebecca U. *The American Warfare State: The Domestic Politics of Military Spending*. Chicago: University of Chicago Press, 2014.
    - Academic analysis of how geographic distribution of defense spending shapes congressional voting.

## Academic Research

- Alexander, Raquel, Stephen W. Mazza, and Susan Scholz. "Measuring Rates of Return for Lobbying Expenditures: An Empirical Case Study of Tax Breaks for Multinational Corporations." *Journal of Law and Economics* 52, no. 2 (2009): 401-426.
    - Foundational study demonstrating the high return on investment of corporate lobbying.
- Brunton, Bruce G. "Institutional Origins of the Military-Industrial Complex." *Journal of Economic Issues* 22, no. 2 (1988): 599-606.
    - Historical analysis of the institutional structures that created the MIC.
- Dunne, J. Paul, and Elisabeth Sk√∂ns. "The Changing Military Industrial Complex." *Economics of Peace and Security Journal* 6, no. 1 (2011): 25-31.
    - Analysis of post-Cold War defense industry consolidation and its global implications.
- Draper, Theodore. "Eisenhower's Warning." *New York Review of Books*, January 14, 1971.
    - Influential essay examining the context and implications of Eisenhower's farewell address.
- Adams, Gordon. *The Iron Triangle: The Politics of Defense Contracting*. New York: Council on Economic Priorities, 1981.
    - Foundational study of the triangular relationship among the Pentagon, Congress, and defense industry.
- Thorpe, Rebecca U. "The Role of Fiscal Illusion in the Domestic Politics of Defense Spending." *Political Science Quarterly* 129, no. 3 (2014): 385-409.
    - How geographic distribution of defense spending creates political support for programs regardless of strategic value.

## Government Reports and Analyses

### Government Accountability Office (GAO)

- GAO. *Defense Acquisitions Annual Assessment: Programs Are Not Consistently Achieving Expected Outcomes*. GAO-24-106556. Washington, DC: GAO, 2024.
    - Annual review of major defense acquisition program cost and schedule performance.
- GAO. *Defense Contracting: Actions Needed to Increase Competition*. GAO-24-106890. Washington, DC: GAO, 2024.
    - Analysis of declining competition rates and recommendations for increasing competitive procurement.
- GAO. *DOD Personnel: DoD Needs to Improve Oversight of Post-Government Employment of Former Officials*. GAO-21-416. Washington, DC: GAO, 2021.
    - Key study documenting 1,718 former senior DoD officials who moved to defense contractors (2014-2019).
- GAO. *F-35 Joint Strike Fighter: DOD Needs to Update Modernization Schedule and Improve Data on Software Development*. GAO-24-106417. Washington, DC: GAO, 2024.
    - Latest assessment of the F-35 program's cost, schedule, and performance challenges.

### Congressional Research Service (CRS)

- Schwartz, Moshe, and Heidi M. Peters. *Department of Defense Contractor and Troop Levels in Afghanistan and Iraq: 2007-2020*. CRS Report R44116. Washington, DC: CRS, 2021.
    - Data on the scale of defense contracting during the War on Terror.
- Schwartz, Moshe. *Defense Acquisitions: How DOD Acquires Weapon Systems and Recent Efforts to Reform the Process*. CRS Report RL34026. Washington, DC: CRS, 2014.
    - Overview of the defense acquisition process and reform history.
- Heidi M. Peters. *Defense Primer: The Defense Industrial Base*. CRS Report IF10548. Washington, DC: CRS, 2024.
    - Summary of the current state of the defense industrial base, including consolidation trends.

### Congressional Budget Office (CBO)

- CBO. *Long-Term Costs of the Administration's 2025 Future Years Defense Program*. Washington, DC: CBO, 2024.
    - Independent cost assessment of the Pentagon's long-term spending plans.
- CBO. *The U.S. Military's Force Structure: A Primer*. Washington, DC: CBO, 2021.
    - Analysis of force structure costs and tradeoffs.

### Department of Defense

- DoD. *National Defense Industrial Strategy*. Washington, DC: DoD, 2024.
    - The Pentagon's own assessment of industrial base challenges, including consolidation concerns.
- PPBE Reform Commission. *Final Report: Defense Resourcing for the Future*. Washington, DC: PPBE Reform Commission, 2024.
    - Recommendations for reforming the Pentagon's budget and resource allocation process.

## Investigative Journalism and Watchdog Reports

### Project on Government Oversight (POGO)

- POGO. *The Pentagon Revolving Door Database*. Updated continuously.
    - Comprehensive database tracking the movement of personnel between the Pentagon and defense industry.
    - URL: <https://www.pogo.org/database/pentagon-revolving-door>
- POGO. *Brass Parachutes: Defense Contractors' Capture of Pentagon Officials Through the Revolving Door*. POGO, 2018.
    - Report documenting how defense contractors recruit former Pentagon officials and the conflicts of interest that result.
- POGO. *Bad Business: Billions of Taxpayer Dollars Wasted on Hiring Contractors*. POGO, 2011.
    - Analysis of cost comparisons between government employees and contractors performing similar functions.

### Other Investigative Sources

- OpenSecrets (Center for Responsive Politics). Defense sector lobbying and campaign contribution data.
    - URL: <https://www.opensecrets.org/industries/indus?ind=D>
- Commission on Wartime Contracting in Iraq and Afghanistan. *Transforming Wartime Contracting: Controlling Costs, Reducing Risks*. Final Report. Washington, DC, 2011.
    - Estimated $31-60 billion in waste and fraud in wartime contracting.
- Stockholm International Peace Research Institute (SIPRI). *SIPRI Arms Industry Database*.
    - Global data on defense industry revenues and rankings.
    - URL: <https://www.sipri.org/databases/armsindustry>
- Federal Procurement Data System (FPDS). Contract award data.
    - URL: <https://www.fpds.gov>
