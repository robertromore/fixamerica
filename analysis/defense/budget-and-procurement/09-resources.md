# Budget and Procurement: Resources

This section provides a curated list of key government sources, reports, and expert analysis on the U.S. defense budget and acquisition system.

---

## Primary Government Sources

- **Department of Defense Comptroller**
    - The official source for all DoD budget documents, including the annual President's Budget request, R-1 (RDT&E) and P-1 (Procurement) exhibits, and historical budget data.
    - [https://comptroller.defense.gov/](https://comptroller.defense.gov/)

- **Government Accountability Office (GAO)**
    - The primary congressional watchdog. The GAO produces hundreds of reports on the performance of DoD programs, cost overruns, and the health of the acquisition system. Its annual "Weapon Systems Annual Assessment" is a key resource.
    - [https://www.gao.gov/](https://www.gao.gov/)

- **Congressional Budget Office (CBO)**
    - Provides non-partisan analysis of the defense budget, long-term cost projections, and the fiscal implications of various policy options.
    - [https://www.cbo.gov/](https://www.cbo.gov/)

- **Director, Operational Test & Evaluation (DOT&E)**
    - The DoD's independent testing office. Its annual report provides a blunt assessment of whether new weapon systems actually work in realistic combat conditions.
    - [https://www.dote.osd.mil/](https://www.dote.osd.mil/)

- **Defense Acquisition University (DAU)**
    - The DoD's internal university for training the acquisition workforce. Its website is a vast repository of information on acquisition policy and process.
    - [https://www.dau.edu/](https://www.dau.edu/)

## Think Tanks and Expert Analysis

- **Center for Strategic and International Studies (CSIS), Defense-Industrial Initiatives Group**
    - Provides excellent data-driven analysis of acquisition trends, the defense budget, and industrial base policy.
- **Project on Government Oversight (POGO)**
    - A non-partisan watchdog group that conducts in-depth investigations into waste, fraud, and abuse in defense contracting.
- **RAND Corporation**
    - A federally funded research and development center that produces a large volume of detailed research on defense acquisition and procurement policy.
