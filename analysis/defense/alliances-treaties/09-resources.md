# Alliances and Partnerships: Resources

This section provides a curated list of foundational treaties, official sources, and expert analysis on the U.S. alliance system.

---

## Foundational Treaties and Documents

- **The North Atlantic Treaty (1949)**
    - The founding document of NATO. Article 5, the collective defense clause, is the heart of the treaty.
    - [https://www.nato.int/cps/en/natohq/official_texts_17120.htm](https://www.nato.int/cps/en/natohq/official_texts_17120.htm)

- **U.S.-Japan Treaty of Mutual Cooperation and Security (1960)**
    - The cornerstone of the U.S.-Japan alliance.
    - [https://www.mofa.go.jp/region/n-america/us/q&a/ref/1.html](https://www.mofa.go.jp/region/n-america/us/q&a/ref/1.html)

- **AUKUS Joint Leaders' Statement (2021)**
    - The document announcing the creation of the AUKUS partnership.
    - [https://www.whitehouse.gov/briefing-room/statements-releases/2021/09/15/joint-leaders-statement-on-aukus/](https://www.whitehouse.gov/briefing-room/statements-releases/2021/09/15/joint-leaders-statement-on-aukus/)

## Primary Government Sources

- **NATO Public Diplomacy Division**
    - Publishes annual reports with data on member defense spending, as well as official communiques from summits.
    - [https://www.nato.int/](https://www.nato.int/)

- **U.S. Department of State, Bureau of Political-Military Affairs**
    - Manages U.S. security agreements and foreign military sales.
    - [https://www.state.gov/bureaus-offices/under-secretary-for-arms-control-and-international-security-affairs/bureau-of-political-military-affairs/](https://www.state.gov/bureaus-offices/under-secretary-for-arms-control-and-international-security-affairs/bureau-of-political-military-affairs/)

- **Congressional Research Service (CRS)**
    - Provides numerous, detailed, and non-partisan reports on NATO, burden-sharing, AUKUS, and U.S. strategy in Asia.

## Think Tanks and Expert Analysis

- **Center for Strategic and International Studies (CSIS)**: The Europe, Russia, and Eurasia Program and the Asia Program provide extensive analysis on U.S. alliances.
- **Council on Foreign Relations (CFR)**: Publishes backgrounders and analysis on NATO and other U.S. security commitments.
- **International Institute for Strategic Studies (IISS)**: Publishes "The Military Balance," which provides data on the armed forces of allied nations.
