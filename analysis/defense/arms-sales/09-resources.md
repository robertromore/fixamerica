# Arms Sales: Resources

This section provides a curated list of key government sources, data providers, and expert analysis on U.S. arms sales and security cooperation.

---

## Primary Government Sources

- **U.S. Department of State, Bureau of Political-Military Affairs (PM)**
    - The lead bureau for arms transfer policy. Its website provides official reports, fact sheets, and policy announcements.
    - [https://www.state.gov/bureaus-offices/under-secretary-for-arms-control-and-international-security-affairs/bureau-of-political-military-affairs/](https://www.state.gov/bureaus-offices/under-secretary-for-arms-control-and-international-security-affairs/bureau-of-political-military-affairs/)

- **Defense Security Cooperation Agency (DSCA)**
    - The DoD agency that manages the Foreign Military Sales (FMS) program. Its website includes a database of all major FMS sales notifications to Congress.
    - [https://www.dsca.mil/](https://www.dsca.mil/)

- **Arms Export Control Act (AECA)**
    - The full text of the primary U.S. law governing arms transfers.

- **International Traffic in Arms Regulations (ITAR)**
    - The detailed regulations that implement the AECA, including the U.S. Munitions List (USML).

## Key Data Sources

- **Stockholm International Peace Research Institute (SIPRI) Arms Transfers Database**
    - The most comprehensive, independent public database on global arms transfers. Essential for tracking trends and comparing suppliers and recipients.
    - [https://www.sipri.org/databases/armstransfers](https://www.sipri.org/databases/armstransfers)

- **Security Assistance Monitor**
    - A project by the Center for International Policy that tracks and analyzes all forms of U.S. security assistance, including arms sales, in a user-friendly database.
    - [https://securityassistance.org/](https://securityassistance.org/)

## Think Tanks and Advocacy Organizations

- **Cato Institute**
    - A libertarian think tank that is a prominent critic of U.S. arms sales policy, arguing it leads to foreign entanglements.
- **Amnesty International / Human Rights Watch**
    - Major human rights organizations that frequently report on the use of U.S. weapons in conflicts and advocate for greater restrictions on sales to abusive regimes.
- **Center for Strategic and International Studies (CSIS)**
    - Provides analysis on the strategic and industrial base implications of arms sales.
