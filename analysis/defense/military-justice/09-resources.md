# Military Justice: Resources

## Government Reports and Data

### Department of Defense

- **DoD SAPRO Annual Reports** - Annual reports on sexual assault prevention and response, including prevalence estimates, reporting data, and case disposition statistics. Available at [sapr.mil](https://www.sapr.mil/).
- **DoD Workplace and Gender Relations Survey (WGRS)** - Biennial survey estimating the prevalence of sexual assault and harassment across the active duty force. The 2021 survey found approximately 35,800 estimated sexual assaults.
- **DoD Annual Report on Military Justice** - Statistical compilation of courts-martial, non-judicial punishment, and administrative separations across all services.
- **Independent Review Commission on Sexual Assault in the Military (IRC) Report (2021)** - Secretary Austin's commission that recommended independent prosecution, prevention workforce professionalization, and command accountability reforms.

### Congressional Reports

- **GAO-19-344, "Military Justice: DoD and the Coast Guard Need to Improve Their Capabilities to Assess Racial and Gender Disparities" (2019)** - Found that Black service members were substantially more likely to be investigated, tried, and convicted at courts-martial. Recommended improved data collection and analysis.
- **GAO-22-105093, "Military Justice: DoD Should Take Additional Steps to Prevent Sexual Assault During Military Service" (2022)** - Assessed DoD's implementation of IRC recommendations and identified gaps.
- **Congressional Research Service, "Military Sexual Assault: A Framework for Congressional Oversight" (updated annually)** - Comprehensive overview of the legal framework, legislative history, and reform options.
- **Congressional Research Service, "The Uniform Code of Military Justice: An Overview" (2023)** - Summary of UCMJ structure, recent amendments, and appellate review procedures.

### Defense Advisory Committee Reports

- **DAC-IPAD Annual Reports (2018-present)** - The Defense Advisory Committee on Investigation, Prosecution, and Defense of Sexual Assault in the Armed Forces publishes detailed analyses of case outcomes, racial disparities, and systemic issues.
- **DAC-IPAD, "Report on Racial Disparities in Military Justice" (2020)** - Documented disparities in charging and conviction rates by race across services.
- **DAC-IPAD, "Report on the Office of Special Trial Counsel Implementation" (2024)** - Early assessment of OSTC operations and recommendations for improvement.

### Service-Specific Reports

- **Air Force Inspector General, "Report of Inquiry: Racial Disparities in the Department of the Air Force Military Justice System" (2020)** - Confirmed that Black Air Force members were 1.64 times more likely to face investigation.
- **Army People First Report (2021)** - Army-wide review of racial disparities in discipline, including NJP and courts-martial data.
- **Navy/Marine Corps Disparity Reviews (2021-2022)** - Service-specific analyses of racial disparities in military justice.

## Court Decisions

### Foundational Cases

- *Parker v. Levy*, 417 U.S. 733 (1974) - Established the "separate society" doctrine, holding that the military's unique mission justifies restrictions on individual rights that would be impermissible in civilian society.
- *Weiss v. United States*, 510 U.S. 163 (1994) - Upheld the constitutionality of the military judge appointment process against due process and jury trial challenges.
- *Solorio v. United States*, 483 U.S. 435 (1987) - Held that military jurisdiction depends on the accused's military status, not on the "service connection" of the offense.

### Command Influence Cases

- *United States v. Thomas*, 22 M.J. 388 (C.M.A. 1986) - Described unlawful command influence as the "mortal enemy of military justice."
- *United States v. Barry*, 78 M.J. 70 (CAAF 2018) - Addressed apparent unlawful command influence arising from senior leader statements about sexual assault.
- *United States v. Bergdahl*, 80 M.J. 230 (CAAF 2020) - Examined the impact of presidential statements on the fairness of military proceedings.

### Due Process and Rights Cases

- *United States v. Briggs*, 141 S. Ct. 467 (2020) - Supreme Court held that there is no statute of limitations for military rape prosecutions under the UCMJ.
- *Ortiz v. United States*, 585 U.S. 80 (2018) - Upheld the constitutionality of the military appellate court system and affirmed the Supreme Court's jurisdiction to review CAAF decisions.

## Academic Research

### Books

- Fidell, Eugene R. *Military Justice: A Very Short Introduction*. Oxford University Press, 2016. A concise overview of the military justice system by a leading military law scholar and former president of the National Institute of Military Justice.
- Lurie, Jonathan. *Military Justice in America: The U.S. Court of Appeals for the Armed Forces, 1775-1980*. University Press of Kansas, 2001. The definitive history of military appellate review.
- Morris, Lawrence J. *Military Justice: A Guide to the Issues*. Praeger, 2010. Comprehensive analysis of military justice policy debates.
- Hillman, Elizabeth Lutes. *Defending America: Military Culture and the Cold War Court-Martial*. Princeton University Press, 2005. Historical analysis of military justice culture and its relationship to broader American legal norms.

### Journal Articles and Papers

- Christensen, Don. "The Military Justice Improvement Act: Why It's Needed." *Georgetown Law Journal* 104 (2016): 1021-1048. Former Air Force Chief Prosecutor argues for independent prosecution.
- RAND Corporation. "Sexual Assault and Sexual Harassment in the U.S. Military." Multiple volumes, 2014-2022. Comprehensive research series on prevalence, risk factors, and policy responses.
- RAND Corporation. "Political Activities and the Military: Regulations, Attitudes, and Trends." 2021. Analysis of increasing politicization and its implications.
- Maurer, David, and Rachel Sondheimer. "Racial Disparities in Military Justice: An Analysis of Convening Authority Decisions." *Military Law Review* 228 (2020). Statistical analysis of racial disparities in court-martial referral decisions.
