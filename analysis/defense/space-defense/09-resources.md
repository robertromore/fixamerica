# Space Defense: Resources

This section provides a curated list of key government sources, research organizations, reports, and books on space defense, security, and sustainability.

---

## Primary Government Sources

- **U.S. Space Force (USSF)**
    - The official source for Space Force organization, doctrine, and capabilities.
    - [https://www.spaceforce.mil/](https://www.spaceforce.mil/)

- **U.S. Space Command (USSPACECOM)**
    - The unified combatant command for space operations. Publishes operational updates, commander's statements, and strategic guidance.
    - [https://www.spacecom.mil/](https://www.spacecom.mil/)

- **Space Development Agency (SDA)**
    - Responsible for the Proliferated Warfighter Space Architecture. Publishes acquisition announcements, program updates, and architecture documentation.
    - [https://www.sda.mil/](https://www.sda.mil/)

- **NASA Orbital Debris Program Office**
    - The leading U.S. authority on space debris modeling, mitigation, and the orbital environment. Publishes the *Orbital Debris Quarterly News*.
    - [https://orbitaldebris.jsc.nasa.gov/](https://orbitaldebris.jsc.nasa.gov/)

- **Department of Commerce, Office of Space Commerce**
    - Manages the transition of space traffic management and civilian SSA to a civilian agency. Developing the Open Architecture Data Repository.
    - [https://www.space.commerce.gov/](https://www.space.commerce.gov/)

- **Director of National Intelligence, Annual Threat Assessment**
    - Annual unclassified report to Congress that includes assessments of Chinese and Russian counterspace capabilities and intentions.

## Key Reports

- **CSIS Aerospace Security Project, *Space Threat Assessment***
    - Annual report from the Center for Strategic and International Studies providing the most comprehensive unclassified assessment of counterspace capabilities worldwide. Essential reading for understanding adversary ASAT programs.
    - [https://www.csis.org/programs/aerospace-security-project](https://www.csis.org/programs/aerospace-security-project)

- **Secure World Foundation, *Global Counterspace Capabilities: An Open Source Assessment***
    - Annual report providing detailed analysis of counterspace capabilities for all major space actors. Published jointly with CSIS.
    - [https://swfroundtabletracker.org/](https://swfroundtabletracker.org/)

- **Union of Concerned Scientists, Satellite Database**
    - The most comprehensive public database of all operational satellites, including owner, orbit, purpose, and launch date. Updated quarterly.
    - [https://www.ucsusa.org/resources/satellite-database](https://www.ucsusa.org/resources/satellite-database)

- **European Space Agency, Space Debris Environment Report**
    - Annual report on the space debris environment, including debris population models, conjunction statistics, and mitigation effectiveness.
    - [https://www.esa.int/Space_Safety/Space_Debris](https://www.esa.int/Space_Safety/Space_Debris)

- **DoD, *Defense Space Strategy Summary* (2020)**
    - The unclassified summary of the Department of Defense strategy for maintaining space superiority and deterring conflict in space.

- **National Space Council, *United States Space Priorities Framework* (2021)**
    - Outlines the Biden administration's priorities for national security space, civil space, and commercial space.

## Think Tanks and Expert Organizations

- **Center for Strategic and International Studies (CSIS), Aerospace Security Project**: Leading analysis on space security, counterspace capabilities, and defense space policy.
- **Secure World Foundation**: NGO focused on the long-term sustainability of space activities. Expertise in space governance, debris, and norms.
- **The Aerospace Corporation, Center for Space Policy and Strategy**: Federally funded research and development center supporting the Space Force. Publishes technical and policy analysis on space security topics.
- **RAND Corporation**: Produces research on space deterrence, architecture, and strategy for the Department of Defense.
- **Stimson Center, Space Security Program**: Focuses on space arms control, norms, and confidence-building measures.
- **Atlantic Council, Forward Defense**: Publishes analysis on space security, NATO space policy, and allied cooperation.

## Key Books

- Weeden, Brian, and Victoria Samson, eds. *Global Counterspace Capabilities: An Open Source Assessment*. Secure World Foundation, updated annually.
- Moltz, James Clay. *The Politics of Space Security: Strategic Restraint and the Pursuit of National Interests*. 3rd ed. Stanford University Press, 2019.
- Sheehan, Michael. *The International Politics of Space*. Routledge, 2007.
- Dolman, Everett C. *Astropolitik: Classical Geopolitics in the Space Age*. Routledge, 2001.
- Handberg, Roger, and Zhen Li. *Chinese Space Policy: A Study in Domestic and International Politics*. Routledge, 2007.
- Harrison, Todd, et al. *Space Threat Assessment 2023*. CSIS, 2023.
- Hitchens, Theresa. *Future Security in Space: Commercial, Military, and Arms Control Trade-Offs*. Center for Defense Information, 2004.

## Academic and Legal Sources

- **Outer Space Treaty (1967)**: Treaty on Principles Governing the Activities of States in the Exploration and Use of Outer Space, including the Moon and Other Celestial Bodies.
- **Liability Convention (1972)**: Convention on International Liability for Damage Caused by Space Objects.
- **Registration Convention (1975)**: Convention on Registration of Objects Launched into Outer Space.
- **UN COPUOS Space Debris Mitigation Guidelines (2007, updated 2019)**: Non-binding guidelines for debris mitigation adopted by the UN Committee on the Peaceful Uses of Outer Space.
- **Woomera Manual on the International Law of Military Space Operations**: An academic project (in progress) developing a comprehensive manual on how international law applies to military space operations, analogous to the Tallinn Manual for cyber warfare.
- Listner, Michael J. "International Space Law: A Regime in Transition." *Georgetown Journal of International Law* 42 (2011): 543-578.
- Weeden, Brian. "The Numbers Game: What's in Earth Orbit and How Do We Know?" *The Space Review*, 2020.
