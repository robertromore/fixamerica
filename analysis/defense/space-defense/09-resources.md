# Space Defense: Resources

This section provides a curated list of key government sources, research organizations, reports, books, academic sources, and online resources on space defense, security, and sustainability.

---

## Primary Government Sources

- **U.S. Space Force (USSF)**
    - The official source for Space Force organization, doctrine, and capabilities. Publishes the Chief of Space Operations' Planning Guidance, Space Capstone Publication *Spacepower*, and annual reports on Space Force activities.
    - [https://www.spaceforce.mil/](https://www.spaceforce.mil/)

- **U.S. Space Command (USSPACECOM)**
    - The unified combatant command for space operations. Publishes operational updates, commander's statements, strategic guidance, and public attributions of adversary space activities.
    - [https://www.spacecom.mil/](https://www.spacecom.mil/)

- **Space Development Agency (SDA)**
    - Responsible for the Proliferated Warfighter Space Architecture. Publishes acquisition announcements, program updates, architecture documentation, and tranche schedules. Key source for understanding the rapid acquisition model for proliferated LEO constellations.
    - [https://www.sda.mil/](https://www.sda.mil/)

- **NASA Orbital Debris Program Office**
    - The leading U.S. authority on space debris modeling, mitigation, and the orbital environment. Publishes the *Orbital Debris Quarterly News*, maintains the ORDEM debris engineering model, and supports the IADC. Essential source for Kessler syndrome risk assessments and debris mitigation policy analysis.
    - [https://orbitaldebris.jsc.nasa.gov/](https://orbitaldebris.jsc.nasa.gov/)

- **Department of Commerce, Office of Space Commerce**
    - Manages the transition of space traffic management and civilian SSA to a civilian agency. Developing the Open Architecture Data Repository. Key source for understanding commercial space regulation and space traffic coordination policy.
    - [https://www.space.commerce.gov/](https://www.space.commerce.gov/)

- **Director of National Intelligence, Annual Threat Assessment**
    - Annual unclassified report to Congress that includes assessments of Chinese and Russian counterspace capabilities and intentions. Published each spring by the Office of the Director of National Intelligence.

- **Government Accountability Office (GAO)**
    - Produces reports on Space Force acquisition programs, cost overruns, and organizational issues. GAO's annual assessment of major defense acquisition programs includes coverage of space systems. Search GAO.gov for "space force" or "space systems" reports.
    - [https://www.gao.gov/](https://www.gao.gov/)

- **Congressional Research Service (CRS)**
    - Publishes detailed reports on space security policy for Congress, including reports on the Space Force, ASAT weapons, space debris, commercial space regulation, and international space governance. CRS reports are available through the Congressional Research Service website or through congressional offices.

- **Federal Communications Commission (FCC), Space Bureau**
    - Regulates satellite licensing, spectrum allocation, and debris mitigation requirements for U.S.-licensed satellite operators. The FCC's 2022 adoption of the 5-year post-mission disposal rule was a landmark debris mitigation action.
    - [https://www.fcc.gov/space](https://www.fcc.gov/space)

## Key Annual Reports

- **CSIS Aerospace Security Project, *Space Threat Assessment***
    - Annual report from the Center for Strategic and International Studies providing the most comprehensive unclassified assessment of counterspace capabilities worldwide. Covers China, Russia, India, Iran, and other actors' ASAT programs, electronic warfare, cyber capabilities, and space doctrine. Essential reading for understanding adversary threats.
    - [https://www.csis.org/programs/aerospace-security-project](https://www.csis.org/programs/aerospace-security-project)

- **Secure World Foundation, *Global Counterspace Capabilities: An Open Source Assessment***
    - Annual report providing detailed analysis of counterspace capabilities for all major space actors. More technically detailed than the CSIS Space Threat Assessment, with extensive documentation of individual ASAT tests, proximity operations, and electronic warfare events. Published jointly with CSIS.
    - [https://swfound.org/counterspace/](https://swfound.org/counterspace/)

- **Union of Concerned Scientists, Satellite Database**
    - The most comprehensive public database of all operational satellites, including owner, orbit, purpose, and launch date. Updated quarterly. Enables independent analysis of the space environment, constellation sizes, and trends.
    - [https://www.ucsusa.org/resources/satellite-database](https://www.ucsusa.org/resources/satellite-database)

- **European Space Agency, Space Debris Environment Report**
    - Annual report on the space debris environment, including debris population models, conjunction statistics, fragmentation events, and mitigation effectiveness. The ESA Space Debris Office operates some of the most sophisticated debris modeling tools in the world.
    - [https://www.esa.int/Space_Safety/Space_Debris](https://www.esa.int/Space_Safety/Space_Debris)

- **DoD, *Defense Space Strategy Summary* (2020)**
    - The unclassified summary of the Department of Defense strategy for maintaining space superiority and deterring conflict in space. Outlines the four lines of effort: build a comprehensive military advantage in space, integrate space into national, joint, and combined operations, shape the strategic environment, and cooperate with allies and partners.

- **National Space Council, *United States Space Priorities Framework* (2021)**
    - Outlines the Biden administration's priorities for national security space, civil space, and commercial space. Emphasizes space sustainability, allied cooperation, and responsible behavior norms alongside deterrence and resilience.

- **U.S. Space Force, *Spacepower: Doctrine for Space Forces* (2020)**
    - The Space Force's foundational doctrinal publication, defining the nature of spacepower, the space domain, and the role of space forces in national defense. Establishes the conceptual framework for how the Space Force thinks about its mission.

## Think Tanks and Expert Organizations

- **Center for Strategic and International Studies (CSIS), Aerospace Security Project**: Leading analysis on space security, counterspace capabilities, and defense space policy. Publishes the annual *Space Threat Assessment* and frequent policy briefs. Key analysts include Todd Harrison and Kaitlyn Johnson.
- **Secure World Foundation (SWF)**: NGO focused on the long-term sustainability of space activities. Expertise in space governance, debris mitigation, norms development, and SSA sharing. Hosts the annual Summit for Space Sustainability. Key analysts include Brian Weeden and Victoria Samson.
- **The Aerospace Corporation, Center for Space Policy and Strategy**: Federally funded research and development center supporting the Space Force. Publishes technical and policy analysis on space security topics, acquisition reform, and architecture design. Unique position as the Space Force's FFRDC.
- **RAND Corporation**: Produces research on space deterrence, architecture trade studies, wargaming, and strategy for the Department of Defense. Key publications on space escalation dynamics and proliferated architecture analysis.
- **Stimson Center, Space Security Program**: Focuses on space arms control, norms of responsible behavior, and confidence-building measures. Key analysts include Michael Krepon (founder) and Samantha Masunaga.
- **Atlantic Council, Forward Defense**: Publishes analysis on space security, NATO space policy, and allied cooperation in the space domain.
- **Carnegie Endowment for International Peace**: Publishes analysis on space arms control, U.S.-China space competition, and the intersection of space and nuclear stability.
- **Chatham House (Royal Institute of International Affairs)**: Publishes UK and European perspectives on space security, governance, and sustainability. Useful for understanding allied views.
- **European Space Policy Institute (ESPI)**: Vienna-based think tank focused on European space policy, including security, sustainability, and governance.

## Key Books

- Weeden, Brian, and Victoria Samson, eds. *Global Counterspace Capabilities: An Open Source Assessment*. Secure World Foundation, updated annually. The most detailed open-source reference on ASAT capabilities worldwide.
- Moltz, James Clay. *The Politics of Space Security: Strategic Restraint and the Pursuit of National Interests*. 3rd ed. Stanford University Press, 2019. Comprehensive analysis of the tension between military uses of space and restraint norms.
- Sheehan, Michael. *The International Politics of Space*. Routledge, 2007. Foundational text on the geopolitics of space, covering the Cold War through the modern era.
- Dolman, Everett C. *Astropolitik: Classical Geopolitics in the Space Age*. Routledge, 2001. Applies classical geopolitical theory to space strategy. Influential in the "space control" school of thought.
- Harrison, Todd, et al. *Space Threat Assessment 2023*. CSIS, 2023. Comprehensive annual assessment of counterspace threats.
- Hitchens, Theresa. *Future Security in Space: Commercial, Military, and Arms Control Trade-Offs*. Center for Defense Information, 2004. Early analysis of the commercial-military integration challenge.
- Handberg, Roger, and Zhen Li. *Chinese Space Policy: A Study in Domestic and International Politics*. Routledge, 2007. Analysis of the domestic and international drivers of China's space program.
- Wright, David, Laura Grego, and Lisbeth Gronlund. *The Physics of Space Security: A Reference Manual*. American Academy of Arts and Sciences, 2005. Invaluable technical reference explaining the physics of orbits, ASAT weapons, missile defense, and debris in accessible terms. Available free online.
- Scharre, Paul. *Four Battlegrounds: Power in the Age of Artificial Intelligence*. W.W. Norton, 2023. Includes analysis of AI applications to space operations, autonomous systems, and decision-making in contested domains.
- Erwin, Sandra. *SpaceNews*. Journalism, not a book, but SpaceNews is the industry publication of record for space defense and commercial space news. Essential daily reading for anyone following space policy.

## Academic and Legal Sources

- **Outer Space Treaty (1967)**: Treaty on Principles Governing the Activities of States in the Exploration and Use of Outer Space, including the Moon and Other Celestial Bodies. The foundational legal framework for all space activities.
- **Liability Convention (1972)**: Convention on International Liability for Damage Caused by Space Objects. Establishes that a launching state is liable for damage caused by its space objects on the surface of the Earth or to aircraft in flight.
- **Registration Convention (1975)**: Convention on Registration of Objects Launched into Outer Space. Requires states to register objects launched into orbit with the UN.
- **UN COPUOS Space Debris Mitigation Guidelines (2007, updated 2019)**: Non-binding guidelines for debris mitigation adopted by the UN Committee on the Peaceful Uses of Outer Space. The international standard for responsible debris mitigation practices.
- **Woomera Manual on the International Law of Military Space Operations**: An academic project (in progress, expected completion 2026) developing a comprehensive manual on how international law applies to military space operations, analogous to the Tallinn Manual for cyber warfare. Led by the University of Adelaide, University of Exeter, and UNSW Canberra.
- Listner, Michael J. "International Space Law: A Regime in Transition." *Georgetown Journal of International Law* 42 (2011): 543-578.
- Weeden, Brian. "The Numbers Game: What's in Earth Orbit and How Do We Know?" *The Space Review*, 2020.
- Kessler, Donald J., and Burton G. Cour-Palais. "Collision Frequency of Artificial Satellites: The Creation of a Debris Belt." *Journal of Geophysical Research* 83 (1978): 2637-2646. The foundational paper describing the cascading debris collision risk now known as "Kessler syndrome."
- Krepon, Michael, and Julia Thompson, eds. *Anti-satellite Weapons, Deterrence and Sino-American Space Relations*. Stimson Center, 2013.
- Porras, Daniel. "Towards ASAT Test Guidelines." UNIDIR, 2018. Analysis of approaches to limiting destructive ASAT testing through international guidelines.

## Online Resources and Data Tools

- **Space-Track.org**: U.S. government portal for sharing space surveillance data with registered users, including satellite operators, researchers, and allied governments.
- **CelesTrak**: Maintained by T.S. Kelso, provides publicly accessible orbital element sets, conjunction data, and visualization tools for tracking satellites and debris.
- **Stuff in Space (stuffin.space)**: An interactive 3D visualization of objects in Earth orbit, useful for understanding the orbital debris environment.
- **ESA Discos Database**: The European Space Agency's Database and Information System Characterising Objects in Space, providing detailed information on space objects and events.
- **SpaceNews**: The industry publication of record for space defense and commercial space news. [https://spacenews.com/](https://spacenews.com/)
- **The Space Review**: Online journal publishing essays on space policy, strategy, and security. [https://www.thespacereview.com/](https://www.thespacereview.com/)
