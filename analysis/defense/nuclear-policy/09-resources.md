# Nuclear Policy: Resources

## Government and Official Sources

### U.S. Government

- **Department of Defense, 2022 Nuclear Posture Review**. Released as part of the 2022 National Defense Strategy. The authoritative statement of U.S. nuclear policy, strategy, and force structure.
- **Congressional Budget Office, "Projected Costs of U.S. Nuclear Forces, 2023 to 2032"** (2023). The most comprehensive unclassified cost estimate for nuclear modernization programs.
- **Congressional Research Service, "U.S. Strategic Nuclear Forces: Background, Developments, and Issues"** (updated regularly). Comprehensive overview of the U.S. nuclear arsenal and modernization programs.
- **Congressional Research Service, "Nuclear Arms Control: The Strategic Offensive Reductions Treaty"** and related reports on New START, INF Treaty, and CTBT.
- **Government Accountability Office, "Nuclear Weapons: NNSA Should Further Develop Cost, Schedule, and Risk Information for the W87-1 Warhead Program"** (2023).
- **Government Accountability Office, "Nuclear Triad: DOD and DOE Face Challenges Mitigating Risks to U.S. Deterrence Efforts"** (2024).
- **National Nuclear Security Administration, "Stockpile Stewardship and Management Plan"** (annual). NNSA's 25-year plan for the nuclear weapons complex.
- **U.S. Strategic Command, Commander's posture statements** to the Senate Armed Services Committee (annual).
- **Department of Defense, "Military and Security Developments Involving the People's Republic of China"** (annual). Includes the most authoritative unclassified U.S. assessment of China's nuclear forces.

### International Organizations

- **International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA)**. The UN agency responsible for nuclear safeguards and nonproliferation verification. [iaea.org](https://www.iaea.org)
- **Comprehensive Nuclear-Test-Ban Treaty Organization (CTBTO)**. Operates the International Monitoring System for detecting nuclear tests. [ctbto.org](https://www.ctbto.org)
- **United Nations Office for Disarmament Affairs (UNODA)**. Repository for nuclear disarmament treaties and agreements. [disarmament.unoda.org](https://disarmament.unoda.org)

## Key Books

- Allison, Graham. *Nuclear Terrorism: The Ultimate Preventable Catastrophe*. Times Books, 2004. Analysis of the threat of nuclear terrorism and policy responses.
- Ellsberg, Daniel. *The Doomsday Machine: Confessions of a Nuclear War Planner*. Bloomsbury, 2017. First-person account of nuclear war planning from a former RAND analyst, revealing the scale and irrationality of Cold War nuclear strategy.
- Kaplan, Fred. *The Bomb: Presidents, Generals, and the Secret History of Nuclear War*. Simon & Schuster, 2020. History of nuclear decision-making from Truman through Trump.
- Nolan, Janne E. *An Elusive Consensus: Nuclear Weapons and American Security After the Cold War*. Brookings Institution Press, 1999. Analysis of the failure to reform nuclear policy after the Cold War.
- Perry, William J. *My Journey at the Nuclear Brink*. Stanford University Press, 2015. Memoir by a former Secretary of Defense arguing for dramatic reductions in nuclear arsenals.
- Perry, William J. and Tom Z. Collina. *The Button: The New Nuclear Arms Race and Presidential Power from Truman to Trump*. BenBella Books, 2020. Argument for eliminating sole presidential launch authority and ICBMs.
- Rhodes, Richard. *The Making of the Atomic Bomb*. Simon & Schuster, 1986. Pulitzer Prize-winning history of the Manhattan Project.
- Sagan, Scott D. *The Limits of Safety: Organizations, Accidents, and Nuclear Weapons*. Princeton University Press, 1993. Analysis of near-misses and organizational failures in nuclear weapons management.
- Schlosser, Eric. *Command and Control: Nuclear Weapons, the Damascus Accident, and the Illusion of Safety*. Penguin Press, 2013. Investigation of nuclear weapons accidents and the fragility of safety systems.
- Tannenwald, Nina. *The Nuclear Taboo: The United States and the Non-Use of Nuclear Weapons Since 1945*. Cambridge University Press, 2007. Analysis of the norm against nuclear use and its role in restraining nuclear policy.
- Wellerstein, Alex. *Restricted Data: The History of Nuclear Secrecy in the United States*. University of Chicago Press, 2021. History of nuclear classification and its effects on democratic governance.

## Cost Estimates and Budget Analysis

- **Congressional Budget Office, "Projected Costs of U.S. Nuclear Forces, 2023 to 2032"** (October 2023). Estimates $756 billion over the decade for nuclear delivery systems, warheads, command and control, and infrastructure.
- **Congressional Budget Office, "Approaches for Managing the Costs of U.S. Nuclear Forces, 2023 to 2032"** (2023). Analysis of alternatives to full triad modernization, including cost savings from a dyad.
- **Kingston Reif and Shannon Bugos, "Understanding the Trillion Dollar Nuclear Triad"**, Arms Control Association (2021). Comprehensive breakdown of modernization program costs.
- **NNSA, "FY2025 Stockpile Stewardship and Management Plan"**. Twenty-five-year plan for weapons activities and infrastructure recapitalization.

## Academic and Policy Research

- Acton, James M. "Escalation Through Entanglement: How the Vulnerability of Command-and-Control Systems Raises the Risks of an Inadvertent Nuclear War." *International Security* 43, no. 1 (2018): 56-99. Analysis of how cyber and conventional threats to NC3 increase nuclear risk.
- Cohn, Carol. "Sex and Death in the Rational World of Defense Intellectuals." *Signs: Journal of Women in Culture and Society* 12, no. 4 (1987): 687-718. Seminal analysis of the language and culture of nuclear strategy.
- Gavin, Francis J. *Nuclear Weapons and American Grand Strategy*. Brookings Institution Press, 2020. Analysis of how nuclear weapons have shaped U.S. foreign policy.
- Glaser, Charles L., and Steve Fetter. "Should the United States Reject MAD? Damage Limitation and U.S. Nuclear Strategy toward China." *International Security* 41, no. 1 (2016): 49-98.
- Lieber, Keir A., and Daryl G. Press. "The New Era of Counterforce: Technological Change and the Future of Nuclear Deterrence." *International Security* 41, no. 4 (2017): 9-49. Analysis of how improved accuracy and intelligence are shifting the nuclear balance.
- Robock, Alan, et al. "Nuclear Winter Revisited with a Modern Climate Model and Current Nuclear Arsenals." *Journal of Geophysical Research* 112 (2007). Updated modeling of nuclear winter effects.
- Xia, Lili, et al. "Global Food Insecurity and Famine from Reduced Crop, Marine Fishery and Livestock Production Due to Climate Disruption from Nuclear War Soot Injection." *Nature Food* 3 (2022): 586-596. Modeling showing that even a regional nuclear war could cause over 2 billion deaths from famine.

## Legal References

- **Atomic Energy Act of 1946** (McMahon Act), Pub. L. 79-585. Established civilian control of nuclear energy and weapons.
- **Atomic Energy Act of 1954**, 42 U.S.C. 2011 et seq. Amended the 1946 act to allow private development of nuclear power and limited cooperation with allies.
- **Nuclear Non-Proliferation Treaty (NPT)**, opened for signature 1968, entered into force 1970. The cornerstone of the global nonproliferation regime.
- **Anti-Ballistic Missile Treaty (ABM Treaty)**, 1972 (U.S. withdrew 2002).
- **Intermediate-Range Nuclear Forces Treaty (INF Treaty)**, 1987 (U.S. withdrew 2019).
- **Strategic Arms Reduction Treaty (START I)**, 1991 (expired 2009).
- **Comprehensive Nuclear-Test-Ban Treaty (CTBT)**, opened for signature 1996 (not ratified by U.S.).
- **New Strategic Arms Reduction Treaty (New START)**, 2010 (expired 2026).
- **Treaty on the Prohibition of Nuclear Weapons (TPNW)**, 2021 (U.S. has not signed).
- **10 U.S.C. 2433a** (Nunn-McCurdy Act). Requires certification review for programs exceeding cost thresholds.
- **War Powers Resolution**, 50 U.S.C. 1541-1548.
- **Radiation Exposure Compensation Act (RECA)**, 42 U.S.C. 2210 note.
- **Energy Employees Occupational Illness Compensation Program Act (EEOICPA)**, 42 U.S.C. 7384 et seq.

## Multimedia and Data Tools

- **NUKEMAP** by Alex Wellerstein ([nuclearsecrecy.com/nukemap](https://nuclearsecrecy.com/nukemap)). Interactive tool for visualizing the effects of nuclear weapons on any location.
- **Nuclear Notebook**, Federation of American Scientists ([fas.org/issues/nuclear-weapons/status-world-nuclear-forces](https://fas.org/issues/nuclear-weapons/status-world-nuclear-forces/)). Regularly updated data on all nine nuclear-armed states' arsenals.
- **SIPRI Yearbook**, Stockholm International Peace Research Institute. Annual data on world nuclear forces, military spending, and arms control.
- ***The Day After* (1983 film)**. ABC television film depicting the effects of nuclear war on a Kansas community. Watched by over 100 million Americans and credited with influencing Reagan's arms control thinking.
- **"Countdown to Zero" (2010 documentary)**. Film examining the risks of nuclear weapons and the movement for abolition.
- **"Command and Control" (2016 PBS documentary)**. Based on Eric Schlosser's book, documenting the 1980 Damascus, Arkansas Titan II missile accident.
