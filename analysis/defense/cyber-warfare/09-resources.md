# Cyber Warfare: Resources

This section provides a curated list of key government sources, reports, and expert organizations that provide analysis on cyber warfare and cybersecurity policy.

---

## Primary Government Sources

- **U.S. Cyber Command (USCYBERCOM)**
    - The official source for information on U.S. military cyber operations, doctrine, and vision.
    - [https://www.cybercom.mil/](https://www.cybercom.mil/)

- **Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA)**
    - The lead civilian agency for domestic cybersecurity. Its website is a vast repository of alerts, best practices, and reports on threats to critical infrastructure.
    - [https://www.cisa.gov/](https://www.cisa.gov/)

- **Office of the National Cyber Director (ONCD)**
    - The White House office responsible for coordinating national cyber policy. Publishes the National Cybersecurity Strategy.
    - [https://www.whitehouse.gov/oncd/](https://www.whitehouse.gov/oncd/)

- **Director of National Intelligence (DNI), Annual Threat Assessment**
    - An annual unclassified report to Congress that provides a comprehensive overview of threats to U.S. national security, including detailed sections on adversary cyber capabilities.

## Key Reports and Legal Resources

- **DoD Cyber Strategy (2018 & 2023 Summaries)**
    - The foundational documents outlining the U.S. military's strategic approach to cyberspace, including the concepts of "persistent engagement" and "defend forward."

- **The Tallinn Manual**
    - A non-binding academic study on how international law applies to cyber conflicts and cyber warfare. It is the most influential resource on the legal aspects of cyber operations.

## Think Tanks and Expert Analysis

- **Center for Strategic and International Studies (CSIS), Strategic Technologies Program**: Provides leading analysis on cyber warfare, military technology, and international security.
- **Carnegie Endowment for International Peace, Cyber Policy Initiative**: A key source for analysis on cyber norms, deterrence, and international cyber policy.
- **Lawfare**: A blog dedicated to "Hard National Security Choices," which provides extensive, in-depth legal and policy analysis of cyber operations and related issues.
- **Council on Foreign Relations (CFR)**: Frequently publishes reports and expert analysis on U.S. cyber strategy and the challenges of deterring cyberattacks.
