# Cyber Warfare: Resources

This section provides a curated list of key government sources, strategic documents, reports, academic works, and expert organizations that provide analysis on cyber warfare, cybersecurity policy, and the intersection of cyber operations with national security.

---

## Primary Government Sources

- **U.S. Cyber Command (USCYBERCOM)**
    - The official source for information on U.S. military cyber operations, doctrine, and vision. Publishes the USCYBERCOM posture statement, fact sheets on the Cyber Mission Force, and information on hunt-forward operations.
    - [https://www.cybercom.mil/](https://www.cybercom.mil/)

- **Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA)**
    - The lead civilian agency for domestic cybersecurity. Its website is a vast repository of alerts, best practices, reports on threats to critical infrastructure, the Known Exploited Vulnerabilities (KEV) catalog, and guidance for critical infrastructure operators and state/local governments.
    - [https://www.cisa.gov/](https://www.cisa.gov/)

- **Office of the National Cyber Director (ONCD)**
    - The White House office responsible for coordinating national cyber policy. Publishes the National Cybersecurity Strategy, the National Cyber Workforce and Education Strategy, and implementation plans.
    - [https://www.whitehouse.gov/oncd/](https://www.whitehouse.gov/oncd/)

- **National Security Agency (NSA) Cybersecurity Directorate**
    - Publishes cybersecurity advisories, technical guidance for securing national security systems, and threat intelligence on state-sponsored cyber actors. The Cybersecurity Directorate was established in 2019 to consolidate NSA's defensive mission.
    - [https://www.nsa.gov/Cybersecurity/](https://www.nsa.gov/Cybersecurity/)

- **Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI) Cyber Division**
    - Publishes alerts, threat intelligence, and guidance on cyber threats. Operates the Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3), which publishes annual reports on cybercrime trends.
    - [https://www.fbi.gov/investigate/cyber](https://www.fbi.gov/investigate/cyber)
    - IC3 Annual Reports: [https://www.ic3.gov/](https://www.ic3.gov/)

- **Director of National Intelligence (DNI) Annual Threat Assessment**
    - An annual unclassified report to Congress that provides a comprehensive overview of threats to U.S. national security, including detailed sections on adversary cyber capabilities and intentions. Essential reading for understanding the threat landscape.
    - Published annually, available through [https://www.dni.gov/](https://www.dni.gov/)

## Key Strategic Documents

- **National Cybersecurity Strategy (March 2023)**
    - The most comprehensive articulation of U.S. cybersecurity policy, organized around five pillars. Proposes a fundamental shift in liability for software security from consumers to vendors.
    - [https://www.whitehouse.gov/wp-content/uploads/2023/03/National-Cybersecurity-Strategy-2023.pdf](https://www.whitehouse.gov/wp-content/uploads/2023/03/National-Cybersecurity-Strategy-2023.pdf)

- **DoD Cyber Strategy (2018 & 2023 Summaries)**
    - The foundational documents outlining the U.S. military's strategic approach to cyberspace, including the concepts of "persistent engagement" and "defend forward." The 2023 strategy emphasizes lessons from Ukraine and the China threat.

- **National Cyber Workforce and Education Strategy (2023)**
    - The White House strategy to address the cybersecurity workforce gap through expanded education, skills-based hiring, and diversity initiatives.

- **Cyberspace Solarium Commission Final Report (2020)**
    - The most comprehensive set of policy recommendations for U.S. cybersecurity, produced by a congressionally mandated, bipartisan commission. Proposed 82 recommendations across six pillars, approximately 75% of which have been implemented or are in progress. Essential background for understanding the current policy landscape.
    - [https://www.solarium.gov/](https://www.solarium.gov/)

## Reports and Legal Resources

- **The Tallinn Manual on the International Law Applicable to Cyber Operations (1.0, 2013; 2.0, 2017)**
    - A non-binding academic study produced by an international group of experts at the NATO CCDCOE. The most influential resource on how international law applies to cyber conflicts, including the law of armed conflict, sovereignty, and state responsibility.

- **CISA Cybersecurity Advisory Committee Reports**
    - The advisory committee, composed of private sector and academic experts, publishes recommendations to CISA on improving national cybersecurity. Reports cover topics including workforce, critical infrastructure resilience, and public-private partnerships.

- **Cyber Safety Review Board (CSRB) Reports**
    - The CSRB, established by Executive Order 14028 (2021), conducts independent reviews of major cyber incidents. Its 2023 review of the Log4Shell vulnerability and 2024 review of the Microsoft Exchange Online hack by the Chinese Storm-0558 group provide detailed analysis of systemic failures and recommendations.

## Think Tanks and Expert Analysis

- **Center for Strategic and International Studies (CSIS), Strategic Technologies Program**
    - Provides leading analysis on cyber warfare, military technology, and international security. Publishes the *Significant Cyber Incidents* timeline tracking major cyber events since 2006.
    - [https://www.csis.org/programs/strategic-technologies-program](https://www.csis.org/programs/strategic-technologies-program)

- **Carnegie Endowment for International Peace, Cyber Policy Initiative**
    - A key source for analysis on cyber norms, deterrence, and international cyber policy. Hosts the *Cyber Operations Tracker* documenting known state-sponsored cyber operations.
    - [https://carnegieendowment.org/programs/technology/cyber-policy](https://carnegieendowment.org/programs/technology/cyber-policy)

- **Lawfare Blog**
    - A blog dedicated to "Hard National Security Choices," which provides extensive, in-depth legal and policy analysis of cyber operations, FISA, surveillance authorities, and related issues. Published by the Brookings Institution.
    - [https://www.lawfaremedia.org/](https://www.lawfaremedia.org/)

- **Council on Foreign Relations (CFR)**
    - Publishes reports, expert analysis, and the *Cyber Operations Tracker* on U.S. cyber strategy and the challenges of deterring cyberattacks.
    - [https://www.cfr.org/cyber-operations/](https://www.cfr.org/cyber-operations/)

- **Atlantic Council, Digital Forensic Research Lab (DFRLab) and Cyber Statecraft Initiative**
    - Focuses on the intersection of cybersecurity, digital policy, and international affairs. Publishes analysis on information operations, election security, and cyber norms.
    - [https://www.atlanticcouncil.org/programs/digital-forensic-research-lab/](https://www.atlanticcouncil.org/programs/digital-forensic-research-lab/)

- **RAND Corporation**
    - Publishes research on cyber warfare, deterrence theory in cyberspace, and defense technology. Key reports include studies on cyber escalation dynamics and the integration of cyber operations with conventional military strategy.
    - [https://www.rand.org/topics/cyber-warfare.html](https://www.rand.org/topics/cyber-warfare.html)

- **Belfer Center for Science and International Affairs, Harvard Kennedy School**
    - Home of the National Cyber Power Index, which ranks countries' cyber capabilities across multiple dimensions. Publishes influential research on cyber strategy and deterrence.
    - [https://www.belfercenter.org/](https://www.belfercenter.org/)

## Books

- Sanger, David E. *The Perfect Weapon: War, Sabotage, and Fear in the Cyber Age*. New York: Crown, 2018.
    - Comprehensive account of the rise of cyber warfare, from Stuxnet to election interference, by the *New York Times* national security correspondent.

- Kaplan, Fred. *Dark Territory: The Secret History of Cyber War*. New York: Simon & Schuster, 2016.
    - History of U.S. cyber operations from the Reagan era through the Obama administration.

- Rid, Thomas. *Active Measures: The Secret History of Disinformation and Political Warfare*. New York: Farrar, Straus and Giroux, 2020.
    - History of information warfare and disinformation operations from the Cold War to the present.

- Buchanan, Ben. *The Hacker and the State: Cyber Attacks and the New Normal of Geopolitics*. Cambridge, MA: Harvard University Press, 2020.
    - Academic analysis of how states use cyber operations for espionage, attack, and destabilization.

- Perlroth, Nicole. *This Is How They Tell Me the World Ends: The Cyberweapons Arms Race*. New York: Bloomsbury, 2021.
    - Investigation of the zero-day exploit market, the Vulnerabilities Equities Process, and the proliferation of offensive cyber capabilities.

- Fischerkeller, Michael P., Emily O. Goldman, and Richard J. Harknett. *Cyber Persistence Theory: Redefining National Security in Cyberspace*. New York: Oxford University Press, 2022.
    - The foundational academic text on the theory of "persistent engagement" that underpins current U.S. cyber strategy.

- Greenberg, Andy. *Sandworm: A New Era of Cyberwar and the Hunt for the Kremlin's Most Dangerous Hackers*. New York: Doubleday, 2019.
    - Account of the Russian GRU hacking group responsible for the Ukraine power grid attacks, NotPetya, and the 2018 Winter Olympics attack.

- Clarke, Richard A., and Robert K. Knake. *The Fifth Domain: Defending Our Country, Our Companies, and Ourselves in the Age of Cyber Threats*. New York: Penguin Press, 2019.
    - Policy-oriented analysis of cyber threats and defenses by two former White House cybersecurity officials.

## Academic Research

- Valeriano, Brandon, and Ryan C. Maness. *Cyber War versus Cyber Realities: Cyber Conflict in the International System*. New York: Oxford University Press, 2015.
    - Empirical analysis of cyber operations between rival states, finding that restraint is more common than escalation.

- Gartzke, Erik. "The Myth of Cyberwar: Bringing War in Cyberspace Back Down to Earth." *International Security* 38, no. 2 (2013): 41-73.
    - Influential article arguing that cyber operations are more useful for espionage and disruption than for achieving decisive military effects.

- Lindsay, Jon R. "Stuxnet and the Limits of Cyber Warfare." *Security Studies* 22, no. 3 (2013): 365-404.
    - Analysis of Stuxnet's effectiveness and limitations as a strategic weapon.

- Healey, Jason, ed. *A Fierce Domain: Conflict in Cyberspace, 1986 to 2012*. Washington, DC: Cyber Conflict Studies Association, 2013.
    - The most comprehensive history of cyber conflict, with detailed case studies of major incidents.

## Key Legal Authorities

- **Title 10, U.S. Code** -- Armed Forces (authority for military cyber operations)
- **Title 50, U.S. Code** -- War and National Defense (authority for intelligence activities and covert action)
- **50 U.S.C. 1881a (Section 702, FISA)** -- Foreign surveillance authority (reauthorized 2024)
- **6 U.S.C. 681-681g (CIRCIA)** -- Cyber incident reporting for critical infrastructure
- **Cybersecurity Information Sharing Act of 2015** -- Liability protections for information sharing
- **Executive Order 14028 (2021)** -- Improving the Nation's Cybersecurity
- **National Security Presidential Memorandum 13 (2018)** -- Classified authorities for offensive cyber operations
- **Section 1642, FY2019 NDAA** -- Authority for clandestine military cyber activities
- **Presidential Policy Directive 21 (2013)** -- Critical infrastructure security and resilience (designates 16 sectors and SRMAs)
