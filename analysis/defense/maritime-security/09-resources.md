## Books

### Naval Strategy and History

- Mahan, Alfred Thayer. *The Influence of Sea Power Upon History, 1660-1783*. Little, Brown, 1890. The foundational text of modern naval strategy.
- Corbett, Julian S. *Some Principles of Maritime Strategy*. Longmans, Green, 1911. The British counterpoint to Mahan, emphasizing limited warfare and sea control.
- Hughes, Wayne P., and Robert P. Girrier. *Fleet Tactics and Naval Operations*. 3rd ed. Naval Institute Press, 2018. The standard reference on surface warfare tactics.
- Yoshihara, Toshi, and James R. Holmes. *Red Star Over the Pacific: China's Rise and the Challenge to U.S. Maritime Strategy*. 2nd ed. Naval Institute Press, 2018.
- Stavridis, James. *Sea Power: The History and Geopolitics of the World's Oceans*. Penguin Press, 2017.
- Till, Geoffrey. *Seapower: A Guide for the Twenty-First Century*. 4th ed. Routledge, 2018.
- Hattendorf, John B., ed. *U.S. Naval Strategy in the 1990s: Selected Documents*. Naval War College Press, 2006.
- Lehman, John F. *Oceans Ventured: Winning the Cold War at Sea*. W.W. Norton, 2018. The architect of the Reagan-era 600-ship Navy tells his story.

### China's Maritime Strategy

- Erickson, Andrew S., and Ryan D. Martinson, eds. *China's Maritime Gray Zone Operations*. Naval Institute Press, 2019.
- Yoshihara, Toshi. *Dragon Against the Sun: Chinese Views of Japanese Seapower*. CSBA, 2020.
- Fanell, James E. "China's Global Naval Strategy and Expanding Force Structure." Naval War College Review 72, no. 1 (2019).

### South China Sea

- Hayton, Bill. *The South China Sea: The Struggle for Power in Asia*. Yale University Press, 2014.
- Poling, Gregory B. *On Dangerous Ground: America's Century in the South China Sea*. Oxford University Press, 2022.
- Permanent Court of Arbitration. *The South China Sea Arbitration (The Republic of the Philippines v. The People's Republic of China)*. PCA Case No. 2013-19, Award of 12 July 2016.

### Taiwan

- Easton, Ian. *The Chinese Invasion Threat: Taiwan's Defense and American Strategy in Asia*. Eastbridge Books, 2019.
- Bush, Richard C. *Difficult Choices: Taiwan's Quest for Security and the Good Life*. Brookings, 2021.

## Periodicals and Journals

- *Proceedings* (U.S. Naval Institute): The premier journal of professional naval thought. Monthly.
- *Naval War College Review*: Quarterly journal of strategy, operations, and policy.
- *Journal of Strategic Studies*: Academic journal covering military strategy and international security.
- *International Security*: Leading academic journal on security studies.
- *War on the Rocks*: Online platform for defense analysis and commentary. <https://warontherocks.com/>
- *CIMSEC (Center for International Maritime Security)*: Online forum for maritime security analysis. <https://cimsec.org/>
- *The Maritime Executive*: Industry publication covering commercial shipping and maritime affairs.

## Legal References

- United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea (UNCLOS), 1982. The international legal framework for maritime zones, navigation rights, and dispute resolution. The U.S. has not ratified UNCLOS but considers most of its provisions to reflect customary international law.
- Taiwan Relations Act, Public Law 96-8, 1979. 22 U.S.C. 3301-3316.
- U.S.-Philippines Mutual Defense Treaty, 1951.
- AUKUS Joint Leaders Statement, September 15, 2021.
- National Defense Authorization Act for Fiscal Year 2018, Section 1025 (355-ship fleet requirement).
- War Powers Resolution, 50 U.S.C. 1541-1548.

## Data Sources

- Naval Vessel Register (NVR): Official inventory of U.S. Navy ships. <https://www.nvr.navy.mil/>
- CSIS ChinaPower Project: Data-driven analysis of China's military capabilities. <https://chinapower.csis.org/>
- SIPRI Military Expenditure Database: Global defense spending data. <https://www.sipri.org/>
- EIA World Oil Transit Chokepoints: Energy Information Administration data on global oil transit. <https://www.eia.gov/>
- UNCTAD Review of Maritime Transport: Annual data on global shipping and trade.
- Lloyd's List: Commercial shipping intelligence.
