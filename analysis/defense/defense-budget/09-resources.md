# Defense Budget: Resources

## Academic Research

- Harrison, Todd. "Analysis of the FY2025 Defense Budget." *Center for Strategic and International Studies* (2024). Comprehensive annual analysis of the defense budget request with historical context and trend analysis.
- Cancian, Mark F. "U.S. Military Forces in FY2025: The Continuing Effort to Rebuild the Military." *CSIS International Security Program* (2024). Examines force structure and budget priorities across the military services.
- Lofgren, Eric. "Reforming PPBE: Lessons from the Commission and the Path Forward." *George Mason University, Mercatus Center* (2024). Analysis of the PPBE Reform Commission's recommendations and implementation challenges.
- Greenwalt, William. "The PPBE Reform Commission Final Report: Analysis and Implications." *American Enterprise Institute* (2024). Conservative/reform perspective on budget process modernization.
- Schwartz, Moshe. "Defense Acquisitions: How DOD Acquires Weapon Systems and Recent Efforts to Reform the Process." *Congressional Research Service* R44010 (2024). Authoritative overview of the acquisition system and its relationship to the budget process.
- McGarry, Brendan W. "Defense Primer: The PPBE Process." *Congressional Research Service* IF10429 (2024). Concise overview of the PPBE system for congressional staff and the public.
- Smithberger, Mandy. "Brass Parachutes: The Problem of the Pentagon Revolving Door." *Project on Government Oversight* (2018). Empirical analysis of the movement of senior DoD officials to defense industry positions.
- Thorpe, Rebecca U. *The American Warfare State: The Domestic Politics of Military Spending*. Chicago: University of Chicago Press, 2014. Scholarly analysis of how the geographic distribution of defense spending shapes budget politics.
- Sapolsky, Harvey M., Eugene Gholz, and Caitlin Talmadge. *U.S. Defense Politics: The Origins of Security Policy*. 4th ed. New York: Routledge, 2024. Textbook treatment of the political economy of defense budgeting.
- Paltrow, Scot J., and Kelly Carr. "Unaccountable: The Pentagon's Bad Bookkeeping." *Reuters Investigates* (2013). Award-winning investigative series on DoD financial management failures.

## Reports and Data

### Congressional Budget Office (CBO)

- "Long-Term Implications of the 2025 Future Years Defense Program." CBO (2024). Projects the long-term costs of the DoD's planned force structure and modernization programs.
- "The Budget and Economic Outlook: 2025 to 2035." CBO (2025). Provides the fiscal context for defense spending, including projections of mandatory spending, deficits, and debt.
- "Approaches for Managing the Costs of U.S. Nuclear Forces, 2023 to 2032." CBO (2023). Analysis of the nuclear modernization "bow wave" and its impact on the defense budget.
- "Costs of Military Pay and Benefits in the Defense Budget." CBO (2020). Detailed analysis of military compensation trends and their impact on the budget.

### Government Accountability Office (GAO)

- GAO-24-106694, "Financial Audit: FY 2023 DoD Financial Statements." (2024). Annual assessment of DoD's audit status and material weaknesses.
- GAO-23-105536, "DoD Financial Management: Additional Actions Needed to Address Audit Findings and Improve Internal Controls." (2023). Recommendations for accelerating audit progress.
- GAO-24-106815, "Weapon Systems Annual Assessment: Programs Need to Improve Cost and Schedule Outcomes." (2024). Annual review of major defense acquisition programs, documenting cost growth and delays.
- GAO-13-531, "Federal Spending: Trends in Fourth Quarter Obligations." (2013). Documents the "use it or lose it" spending spike at the end of each fiscal year.
- GAO-18-368, "Continuing Resolutions: Uncertainty Limited Management Options and Increased Workload in Selected Agencies." (2018). Documents the operational impact of CRs on DoD and other agencies.

### DoD and Executive Branch

- Department of Defense Comptroller, "FY2025 Defense Budget Overview." (2024). The DoD's own presentation of its budget request.
- Department of Defense, "National Defense Strategy." (2022). The strategy document that is supposed to drive budget priorities.
- Commission on Planning, Programming, Budgeting, and Execution Reform. "Defense Resourcing for the Future: Final Report." (March 2024). The congressionally mandated commission's recommendations for PPBE reform.
- DoD Office of Inspector General, Report No. DODIG-2024-065, "Financial Management: Accounting Adjustments." (2024). Documents the scale of unsupported accounting adjustments.
- Office of Management and Budget, "Historical Tables: Budget of the United States Government." Updated annually. The primary source for historical federal spending data.

### Independent Research Organizations

- Stockholm International Peace Research Institute (SIPRI), "Military Expenditure Database." Updated annually. The authoritative source for international defense spending comparisons.
- Watson Institute, Brown University, "Costs of War Project." Ongoing. Comprehensive accounting of the total costs of post-9/11 military operations.
- Peter G. Peterson Foundation, "Understanding the National Defense Budget." Various publications. Fiscal context for defense spending within the broader federal budget.

## Books

- Adams, Gordon, and Cindy Williams. *Buying National Security: How America Plans and Pays for Its Global Role and Safety at Home*. New York: Routledge, 2010. Comprehensive analysis of the national security budget process.
- Cancian, Mark F. *Coping with Austerity: U.S. Military Spending in an Era of Fiscal Constraints*. Washington: CSIS, 2012. Analysis of budget reduction strategies and their strategic implications.
- Fox, J. Ronald, and David G. Allen. *The Defense Management Challenge: Weapons Acquisition*. Boston: Harvard Business School Press, 1988. Classic analysis of the acquisition system's structural problems.
- Hartung, William D. *Prophets of War: Lockheed Martin and the Making of the Military-Industrial Complex*. New York: Nation Books, 2011. Critical examination of the largest defense contractor and its political influence.
- Melman, Seymour. *Pentagon Capitalism: The Political Economy of War*. New York: McGraw-Hill, 1970. Early and influential critique of the defense-industrial political economy.
- Wheeler, Winslow T. *The Wastrels of Defense: How Congress Sabotages U.S. Security*. Annapolis: Naval Institute Press, 2004. Insider account of congressional defense budget dysfunction from a former Senate Budget Committee staffer.

## News and Media

### Regular Defense Budget Coverage

- **Defense News**: Comprehensive coverage of defense budget debates, appropriations process, and program funding. The annual "Top 100" defense companies list provides industry context.
- **Breaking Defense**: Daily coverage of defense policy, budget, and technology issues with analysis from former officials and industry experts.
- **Military Times**: Coverage focused on how budget decisions affect service members, including pay, benefits, and readiness impacts.
- **Federal News Network**: Detailed coverage of federal budget process, including defense financial management and audit issues.
- **The Hill / Defense**: Congressional perspective on defense budget politics, including committee markups and floor votes.
- **War on the Rocks**: Policy analysis and commentary on defense strategy and resourcing from academics and practitioners.

### Key Congressional Sources

- **HASC and SASC hearing transcripts**: Available on committee websites; the annual posture hearings (January-March) are the most informative source on budget priorities.
- **CRS Reports**: Available at crsreports.congress.gov; CRS produces authoritative analysis on virtually every defense budget issue.
- **Defense Appropriations Subcommittee markups**: The line-item decisions in the defense appropriations bills are the most consequential budget actions in the federal government.

### Data Sources

- **USASpending.gov**: Federal government spending data, including DoD contract awards and grant obligations.
- **SAM.gov**: Federal procurement data, including contract opportunities and awards.
- **SIPRI Military Expenditure Database**: International defense spending comparisons in current and constant dollars.
- **DoD Comptroller website**: Official budget documents, including the Budget Overview, Program Acquisition Costs by Weapon System, and the Future Years Defense Program (FYDP).
