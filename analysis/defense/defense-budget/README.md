# Defense Budget

Pentagon spending levels, budget toplines, appropriations process, financial auditing, budget priorities, and resource allocation across military services and accounts.

## Overview

This subdirectory focuses on **how much** the Pentagon spends and **where** those resources go - the overall defense budget levels, the appropriations process, budget priorities across services and mission areas, and financial accountability. It examines debates over defense spending adequacy, trade-offs between readiness and modernization, and the persistent failure to pass audits.

Related topics covered elsewhere:

- **Acquisition and procurement processes**: See [Defense Procurement and Acquisition](../budget-and-procurement/)
- **Defense contractor influence**: See [Military-Industrial Complex](../military-industrial-complex/)

## Scope

This analysis covers:

- **Budget Levels**: Total DoD budget, base budget vs. OCO (Overseas Contingency Operations), defense as % of GDP and federal budget
- **Budget Composition**: Personnel, operations & maintenance, procurement, RDT&E, military construction breakdown
- **Service Budgets**: Army, Navy, Air Force, Space Force, and combatant command allocations
- **Appropriations Process**: Congressional budget cycle, National Defense Authorization Act (NDAA), continuing resolutions
- **Budget Priorities**: Modernization vs. readiness, end strength decisions, force structure debates
- **Financial Management**: DoD audit failures, financial accountability, waste and inefficiency
- **Budget Debates**: Adequacy of defense spending, strategic vs. fiscal priorities, pacing threats
- **Sequestration and Budget Caps**: Budget Control Act impacts, predictability vs. top-line constraints
