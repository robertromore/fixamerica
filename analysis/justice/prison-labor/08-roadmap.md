# Prison Labor Reform: Roadmap

## Overview

This roadmap provides a phased implementation plan for comprehensive prison labor reform. It recognizes that transformative change requires sustained effort across multiple years, with strategic sequencing to build momentum and overcome resistance.

## Strategic Framework

### Theory of Change

```
Constitutional Reform + Economic Reform + Workplace Reform + Cultural Shift
                                    ↓
              End of Prison Slavery / Fair Labor Standards
                                    ↓
           Dignity, Reentry Success, Community Investment
```

### Key Principles

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| Center impacted people | Incarcerated and formerly incarcerated leadership |
| Multiple strategies | Legislation, litigation, organizing, education |
| Build power first | Capacity before major campaigns |
| Win early victories | Build momentum through achievable wins |
| Institutionalize gains | Ensure reforms are durable |

### Litigation Strategy

**Potential Legal Theories**:

| Theory | Basis | Potential |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| State constitution claims | New amendments | High in amended states |
| 8th Amendment (cruel/unusual) | Dangerous conditions | Moderate |
| ADA claims | Disability discrimination | Case-specific |
| FLSA claims | Creative interpretation | Long shot |
| International law | Treaty obligations | Symbolic value |

**Litigation Targets**:

| Target | Claim | Forum |
|--------|-------|-------|
| States with new amendments | Implementation failures | State courts |
| Dangerous workplaces | Constitutional conditions | Federal courts |
| Corporate defendants | Supply chain liability | State/federal |

## Phase 4: Transformative Change (Years 8-10)

### 13th Amendment Campaign

**Congressional Push**:

| Milestone | Target Date | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| House committee passage | Year 8 | Majority vote |
| House floor passage | Year 8-9 | 2/3 vote (290 members) |
| Senate committee passage | Year 9 | Majority vote |
| Senate floor passage | Year 9-10 | 2/3 vote (67 senators) |

**State Ratification Campaign**:

| Phase | States Needed | Timeline |
|-------|---------------|----------|
| Early adopters | 15 states | Months 1-6 post-passage |
| Building momentum | 25 states | Months 6-18 |
| Final push | 38 states | Months 18-36 |

### Implementation Legislation

**Post-Amendment Federal Legislation**:

| Bill | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Prison Labor Standards Act | Implement wage, hour, safety standards |
| Incarcerated Worker Protection Act | Define rights and remedies |
| Prison Industry Transition Act | Phase out exploitative programs |
| Reentry Employment Act | Post-release employment support |

### Institutional Transformation

| Change | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Prison industry conversion | From exploitation to vocational training |
| Wage implementation | All workers at minimum wage or above |
| Voluntary work standard | No punishment for work refusal |
| Safety enforcement | Full OSHA coverage |
