# Restorative Justice: Actions

## Introduction

Expanding restorative justice requires action at every levelâ€”from individuals incorporating restorative principles in daily life to national policy changes. This document outlines concrete actions that different actors can take to advance restorative justice in their spheres of influence. All actions are organized by actor type and include both immediate steps and longer-term commitments.

## Individual Actions

### Personal Practice

| Action | Description | Difficulty |
|--------|-------------|------------|
| Learn restorative questions | Practice asking "What happened? Who was affected? What needs to happen to make things right?" | Easy |
| Model accountability | When you cause harm, take responsibility and make amends | Easy |
| Choose dialogue over judgment | When harmed, consider what you need to heal rather than how to punish | Medium |
| Practice active listening | Give full attention when others share their experience | Easy |
| Participate in circles | Join community circles, talking circles at work or faith communities | Medium |
| Read foundational texts | Start with Zehr's "The Little Book of Restorative Justice" | Easy |

### In Your Household

| Action | Description | Impact |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| Use restorative language | Ask "What happened?" not "What did you do wrong?" | Shifts family dynamics |
| Hold family meetings | Regular circles to discuss issues, build connection | Prevents escalation |
| Model repair | When you harm family members, make genuine amends | Teaches accountability |
| Support harmed members | Focus on what they need, not just consequences for wrongdoer | Centers healing |
| Avoid punitive responses | Find alternatives to grounding, removal of privileges | Builds skills |

### Professional Development

| Action | Time Investment | Next Step |
|--------|-----------------|-----------|

## School-Based Actions

### For Parents

| Action | How | Impact |
|--------|-----|--------|
| Ask about RJ at your school | Meet with principal | Starts conversation |
| Advocate for RJ training | School board meetings | Builds support |
| Support policy change | Join parent organizations | Changes discipline code |
| Volunteer as facilitator | Get trained, offer services | Builds capacity |
| Model RJ at home | Apply principles in family | Reinforces learning |

### For Teachers

| Action | Classroom Focus | School-Wide Focus |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Use circle processes | Community building circles | Advocate for whole-school approach |
| Practice restorative questions | With individual students | Share with colleagues |
| Reduce referrals | Handle issues restoratively | Track and share data |
| Get trained | Pursue professional development | Lead training for others |
| Document outcomes | Keep records of RJ use | Build case for expansion |

### For Administrators

| Action | Phase | Expected Outcome |
|--------|-------|------------------|
| Assess current discipline | Month 1-2 | Baseline data on disparities |
| Explore RJ options | Month 2-4 | Understanding of models |
| Pilot in willing classrooms | Month 4-6 | Test feasibility |
| Train staff | Month 6-12 | Build capacity |
| Revise discipline code | Year 2 | Policy alignment |
| Implement school-wide | Year 2-3 | Comprehensive approach |

|--------|-------------|-----------|

| Action | Description | Benefit |
