# Victims' Rights and Services: Stakeholders

## Who Is Involved and What Are Their Stakes

### Primary Stakeholders: Crime Victims

#### Direct Victims

| Victim Category | Estimated Annual Numbers | Primary Needs |
|-----------------|-------------------------|---------------|
| Violent crime victims | 4.6 million | Safety, healing, justice, financial recovery |
| Property crime victims | 12.1 million | Financial recovery, security, acknowledgment |
| Domestic violence victims | 10 million incidents | Safety, housing, legal protection, counseling |
| Sexual assault victims | 430,000+ | Medical care, counseling, justice, privacy |
| Child abuse victims | 656,000 substantiated | Protection, services, long-term support |
| Homicide survivors | 600,000+ family members | Grief support, justice, financial stability |
| Human trafficking victims | 50,000+ | Safety, comprehensive services, immigration relief |
| Elder abuse victims | 2+ million | Protection, financial recovery, care |
| Fraud victims | 2.4 million reports | Financial recovery, acknowledgment |
| Hate crime victims | 13,000+ reported | Safety, community support, justice |

**Victim Stakes**:
- Physical and emotional safety
- Acknowledgment of harm
- Voice in the process
- Financial recovery
- Healing and recovery
- Prevention of future harm
- Closure (if possible)

#### Secondary Victims

| Category | Relationship to Crime | Specific Needs |
|----------|----------------------|----------------|
| Family of homicide victims | Lost loved one | Grief support, financial stability, justice |
| Children of DV victims | Witnessed abuse | Therapy, stability, protection |
| Parents of victimized children | Child harmed | Support, advocacy, healing |
| Siblings of victims | Family member harmed | Counseling, family support |
| Intimate partners of victims | Partner victimized | Relationship support, own trauma |
| Witnesses to violence | Observed crime | Counseling, safety planning |
| First responders | Occupational exposure | Mental health support |
| Communities affected | Collective harm | Community healing, safety |

### Criminal Justice Professionals

#### Prosecutors

| Role | Relationship to Victims | Stakes |
|------|------------------------|--------|
| **Elected DAs/State's Attorneys** | Policy, resources | Electoral accountability |
| **Line prosecutors** | Case-level interaction | Case outcomes, workload |
| **Victim-Witness Coordinators** | Direct victim support | Victim satisfaction, cooperation |
| **Chief prosecutors** | Office management | Resources, outcomes |

**Prosecutor Dilemmas**:
- Represent the state, not individual victims
- Need victim cooperation for cases
- Must balance victim preferences with legal judgment
- Resource constraints limit victim contact
- Political pressure on high-profile cases

#### Defense Attorneys

| Role | Relationship to Victims | Stakes |
|------|------------------------|--------|
| **Public Defenders** | Represent accused against victim's account | Client advocacy, fair process |
| **Private Defense Counsel** | Same | Client outcomes |
| **Appellate Defenders** | Challenge convictions | Constitutional compliance |

**Defense Stakes**:
- Protect defendant's constitutional rights
- Ensure victims' rights don't unfairly disadvantage defendants
- Access to evidence, ability to confront witnesses
- Fair process for their clients

#### Judiciary

| Role | Relationship to Victims | Stakes |
|------|------------------------|--------|
| **Trial Judges** | Implement victim rights in court | Fair process, efficiency |
| **Appellate Judges** | Interpret victim rights laws | Legal clarity |
| **Administrative Judges** | Court policy | System function |
| **Court Administrators** | Notification systems | Compliance, resources |

#### Law Enforcement

### Special Interest Stakeholders

#### Defendants and Their Advocates

| Stakeholder | Interest | Stakes |
|-------------|----------|--------|
| **Accused individuals** | Fair trial, liberty | Constitutional rights |
| **Incarcerated persons** | Fair sentencing, release | Proportional consequences |
| **Formerly incarcerated** | Reintegration | Restitution burdens |
| **Defense bar** | Client advocacy | Professional obligations |
| **Civil liberties organizations** | Constitutional balance | Rights protection |

**Defendant-Related Concerns**:
- Victim impact statements may inflame juries
- Victim presence may prejudice proceedings
- Victim rights may delay or complicate cases
- Some "victims" may be false accusers
- Restitution may be crushing for indigent defendants
