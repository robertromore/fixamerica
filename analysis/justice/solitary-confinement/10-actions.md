# Solitary Confinement Reform: Actions

## Overview

Ending the overuse of solitary confinement requires action at every levelâ€”from individuals to communities to national organizations. This document provides concrete, actionable steps for different audiences to contribute to reform.

## Actions for Individuals

### Immediate Actions (Today)

| Action | Time Required | Impact |
|--------|---------------|--------|
| Learn the basics | 30 minutes | Foundation for further action |
| Share information on social media | 10 minutes | Expands awareness |
| Sign up for advocacy alerts | 5 minutes | Stay informed |
| Sign a petition | 5 minutes | Demonstrates public support |

### Short-Term Actions (This Week)

| Action | How To | Why It Matters |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| **Contact your legislators** | Call, email, or write | Elected officials track constituent contacts |
| **Write a letter to the editor** | Local newspaper | Shapes public opinion |
| **Donate to advocacy organizations** | See resources list | Funds sustained campaigns |
| **Talk to friends and family** | Share what you've learned | Builds broader support |

#### Sample Script for Legislator Calls

> "Hello, my name is [NAME] and I'm a constituent from [CITY]. I'm calling to urge [LEGISLATOR] to support reforms to solitary confinement in our state. Prolonged isolation causes severe psychological harm and costs taxpayers more than regular housing. I'd like [LEGISLATOR] to support legislation limiting solitary to 15 days and banning it for vulnerable populations like youth and people with mental illness. Can I count on [LEGISLATOR]'s support?"

## Actions for Corrections Professionals

### Reform-Minded Administrators

| Action | Why It Matters |
|--------|----------------|
| Reduce use administratively | Don't wait for legislation |
| Implement alternatives | Prove they work |
| Train staff | De-escalation, mental health |
| Document outcomes | Data supports reform |
| Share successes | Conference, publications |
| Speak publicly | Professional credibility |

### Mental Health Staff

| Action | Ethical Basis |
|--------|---------------|
| Refuse to clear for extended solitary | Do no harm |
| Document harms | Medical record |
| Advocate internally | Professional obligation |
| Report to professional associations | Standards violations |
| Seek outside support | Whistleblower protections |

### Correctional Officers

| Action | Why |

| Support reform efforts | Safety improves with alternatives |
| Engage unions | Shift organizational position |
| Speak (carefully) | Where protected |

## Measuring Success

### Individual Level

- Did I take action this month?
- Am I more knowledgeable than before?
- Have I engaged others?
- Am I connected to a community of advocates?

### Collective Level

- Is solitary use declining?
- Has legislation advanced?
- Is media coverage increasing?
- Are conditions improving?
- Is oversight strengthening?
