# Criminal Justice Reform: Actions

## For Individuals

### Educate Yourself

**Read and Learn**:

- Read books by formerly incarcerated authors
- Follow criminal justice news (Marshall Project, Bolts)
- Understand local system (who is your prosecutor, sheriff?)
- Learn about your state's incarceration rate and policies

**Examine Assumptions**:

- Challenge "tough on crime" reflexes
- Consider root causes of crime
- Recognize humanity of all involved
- Understand how system perpetuates problems

### Engage Your Community

**Conversations**:

- Discuss issues with family and friends
- Share stories of system-impacted people
- Challenge harmful narratives
- Listen to affected community members

**Support System-Impacted People**:

| Action | How |
|--------|-----|
| Write to prisoners | Organizations connect pen pals |
| Support families | Assist with visitation, childcare |
| Hire returning citizens | If you're an employer |
| Welcome in community | Housing, social inclusion |

### Vote and Participate

**Elections**:

| Office | Why It Matters |
|--------|----------------|
| District Attorney | Charging decisions, diversion |
| Sheriff | Jail conditions, cooperation |
| Judges | Sentencing, bail |
| State legislators | Criminal laws, funding |
| Local officials | Police oversight, budgets |

**Beyond Voting**:

- Attend candidate forums
- Ask questions about criminal justice
- Support reform candidates
- Volunteer for campaigns

### Personal Actions

**Jury Service**:

- Serve when called
- Apply fair standards
- Understand jury nullification (in appropriate cases)
- Bring humanity to deliberations

**Witness**:

- Film police encounters safely
- Know your rights
- Support those stopped by police
- Provide witness statements when appropriate

## For Communities

### Build Local Coalitions

**Coalition Partners**:

| Sector | Why |
|--------|-----|
| Faith communities | Moral voice, congregations |
| Civil rights organizations | Advocacy infrastructure |
| Labor unions | Worker solidarity |
| Business community | Employment, economic |
| Service providers | Direct experience |
| Affected families | Authentic voice |

**Coalition Activities**:

- Regular meetings
- Shared advocacy agenda
- Joint public events
- Coordinated election engagement

### Community Oversight

**Police Oversight**:

- Advocate for civilian oversight board
- Participate if board exists
- Attend public meetings
- Monitor police union contracts

**Jail Oversight**:

- Visit local jail (if allowed)
- Attend sheriff meetings
- Monitor conditions
- Advocate for improvements

### Alternative Safety

**Violence Interruption**:

- Support Cure Violence programs
- Community mediation
- Youth intervention programs
- Conflict resolution training

**Community-Based Responses**:

| Need | Community Response |
|------|-------------------|
| Mental health crisis | Trained responders |
| Neighbor disputes | Mediation |
| Youth issues | Mentorship programs |
| Domestic violence | Support services |

### Reentry Support

**Welcome Returning Citizens**:

- Reentry programs
- Employment assistance
- Housing support
- Mentorship

**Address Barriers**:

- Advocate for ban the box locally
- Challenge housing discrimination
- Support record expungement
- Create second-chance employment

## For Organizations

### Employers

**Hiring Practices**:

| Action | Implementation |
|--------|----------------|
| Ban the box | Delay background check |
| Individualized assessment | Don't auto-reject |
| Partner with reentry programs | Recruitment pipeline |
| Provide training | Skill development |

**Workplace Support**:

- Flexibility for court/parole appointments
- Mentorship programs
- Non-discrimination policy
- Career advancement opportunities

### Faith Communities

**Ministry**:

- Prison ministry programs
- Family support during incarceration

- Restorative justice facilitation

**Advocacy**:

## Action Checklists

### For Someone New to This Issue

- [ ] Read one book (start with *Just Mercy* or *The New Jim Crow*)
- [ ] Follow The Marshall Project or Bolts
- [ ] Learn who your local prosecutor and sheriff are
- [ ] Attend one local meeting on criminal justice
- [ ] Have conversations with friends and family

### For Active Engagement

- [ ] Join a local reform organization
- [ ] Attend regular coalition meetings
- [ ] Testify at a legislative hearing or public meeting
- [ ] Write an op-ed or letter to editor
- [ ] Volunteer for a reform candidate
- [ ] Support a reentry program

- [ ] Document and share successes

## Finding Your Role

**Questions to Ask**:

1. What skills do I bring? (Legal, organizing, communications, etc.)
2. What access do I have? (Employer, institution, community)
3. What time can I commit? (Hours/week)
4. What is my proximity to the issue? (Directly impacted, ally)
5. What already exists locally? (Organizations, coalitions)

**Matching Skills to Needs**:

| If You're Good At | Consider |
|-------------------|----------|
| Writing | Op-eds, policy briefs |
| Speaking | Testimony, presentations |
| Organizing | Coalition building |
| Research | Policy analysis |
| Direct service | Reentry support |
| Legal | Pro bono work |
| Business | Employment programs |
