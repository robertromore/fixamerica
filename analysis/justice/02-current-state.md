# Criminal Justice Reform: Current State

## Incarceration

### Population

**Total Under Correctional Control**: Approximately 7 million Americans

| Category | Population |
|----------|------------|
| State prisons | ~1.25 million |
| Federal prisons | ~150,000 |
| Local jails | ~650,000 |
| Probation | ~3.5 million |
| Parole | ~800,000 |

**Incarceration Rate**: 664 per 100,000 (2020)

- Down from peak of 760 in 2008
- Still 5-10x higher than other Western democracies
- Significant state variation (Louisiana highest, Massachusetts lowest)

### Who Is Incarcerated

**By Offense Type (State Prisons)**:

| Offense Category | Percentage |
|------------------|------------|
| Violent | 55% |
| Property | 14% |
| Drug | 14% |
| Public order | 12% |
| Other | 5% |

**Demographics**:

| Group | Prison Rate (per 100,000) | Disparity |
|-------|--------------------------|-----------|
| Black men | 2,306 | 5.8x white men |
| Hispanic men | 831 | 2.1x white men |
| White men | 397 | Baseline |
| Black women | 83 | 1.8x white women |
| White women | 48 | Baseline |

### Jail Population

**Characteristics**:

- 74% not convicted (pretrial detention)
- Average stay: 26 days
- Many detained for inability to pay bail
- High turnover: 10+ million admissions annually

**Driving Factors**:

- Cash bail system
- Pretrial detention policies
- Failure to appear warrants
- Technical violations

## Policing

### Structure

**Number of Law Enforcement Agencies**: ~18,000

| Type | Number | Officers |
|------|--------|----------|
| Local police | ~12,000 | ~470,000 |
| Sheriff's offices | ~3,000 | ~175,000 |
| State police | 50 | ~60,000 |
| Federal agencies | Various | ~130,000 |

**Fragmentation**: Highly decentralized; no national standards.

### Use of Force

**Fatal Police Shootings**: ~1,000 annually (tracked since 2015)

- Racial disparities in rates
- Limited official data collection
- No national use of force database

**Less-Lethal Force**: Poorly tracked

- Tasers, batons, pepper spray, physical force
- Injury rates unknown nationally
- Inconsistent policies across departments

### Accountability

**Officers Charged**: Rare (estimates suggest 1-2% of fatal shootings)

**Officers Convicted**: Even rarer

**Civil Liability**: Limited by qualified immunity

- Officers personally liable only if "clearly established" law violated
- Courts have narrowed liability significantly

## Courts

### Public Defense

**Caseloads**: Dramatically exceed standards

| Jurisdiction Type | Actual Caseload | Recommended Max |
|-------------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Many jurisdictions | 500-1,000+ | 150 (felonies) |

**Funding**: Chronic underfunding

- Average spending: $200-300 per case
- Private attorneys: $10,000+ for similar cases
- 21 states have no state funding for public defense

### Bail System

**Pretrial Detention**:

- 470,000+ people in jail awaiting trial
- 74% of jail population is pretrial
- Median bail: $10,000 (most can't afford)

**Money Bail Problems**:

- Poor people detained; wealthy released
- Pressure to plead guilty to get out
- Job loss, family disruption, housing loss
- No improvement in court appearance rates

### Plea Bargaining

**Prevalence**: 94-97% of convictions from guilty pleas

**Concerns**:

- Coercive pressure (pretrial detention, mandatory minimums)
- Innocent people plead guilty
- Trials increasingly rare
- Limited judicial oversight

## Sentencing

### Mandatory Minimums

**Federal**:

- Drug offenses with 5, 10, 20-year minimums
- Firearms offenses
- Child exploitation
- Some reduced by First Step Act (2018)

**State**:

- Three strikes laws
- Drug minimums
- Weapons enhancements
- Habitual offender laws

### Sentence Length

**Average Time Served**:

| Offense | Average (State) |
|---------|-----------------|
| Murder | 15 years |
| Rape | 5 years |
| Robbery | 3 years |
| Drug trafficking | 1.5 years |

**Life Sentences**:

- ~200,000 serving life
- ~55,000 serving life without parole
- 1 in 7 prisoners serving life

### Disparities

**Racial**:

- Black defendants receive 20% longer sentences for same offense
- Persistent after controlling for criminal history
- Varies by jurisdiction and offense type

**Geographic**:

- Dramatic variation by county
- Same offense: probation in one county, prison in another

## Prison Conditions

### Overcrowding

- Many systems over capacity
- Double-bunking common
- Reduced programming
- Increased violence

### Violence

- Sexual assault: 4% of prisoners report victimization
- Physical violence common
- Staff-on-inmate abuse documented

### Solitary Confinement

- ~80,000 in isolation on any given day
- Average stays of months or years
- Documented psychological harm
- Used for discipline, protection, gang management

### Healthcare

- Constitutional minimum standard
- Chronic disease management poor
- Mental health treatment inadequate
- Hepatitis C, HIV significant issues

### Private Prisons

- ~8% of state prisoners, 16% of federal
- Cost-cutting incentives
- Documented quality concerns
- Lobbying for incarceration

## Reentry

### Recidivism Rates

| Timeframe | Rearrested | Reconvicted | Reincarcerated |
|-----------|------------|-------------|----------------|
| 1 year | 44% | 23% | 11% |
| 3 years | 68% | 47% | 25% |
| 5 years | 77% | 56% | 30% |

### Barriers

**Employment**:

- Background check discrimination
- Occupational licensing restrictions
- Skills gaps
- 27% unemployment rate post-release

**Housing**:

- Public housing bans
- Private landlord discrimination
- Homelessness rates 10x general population

**Voting**:

- ~5 million disenfranchised
- State laws vary dramatically
- Lifetime bans in some states

**Benefits**:

- SNAP/TANF restrictions
- Student loan barriers
- Medicaid gaps during reentry

## Recent Developments

### Federal

| Development | Year | Effect |
|-------------|------|--------|
| First Step Act | 2018 | Reduced some mandatory minimums, expanded early release |
| DOJ pattern-or-practice | Varies | Consent decrees with police departments |
| Sentencing Commission | Ongoing | Guideline amendments |

### State Reforms

**Trend**: Declining incarceration in most states

- Sentencing reform (mandatory minimum reduction)
- Bail reform
- Marijuana legalization
- Prosecutor elections

**Backlash**: Some jurisdictions reversing reforms amid crime concerns

### Movement Developments

- Black Lives Matter and police reform activism
- Progressive prosecutor movement
- Victim advocacy evolution
- Bipartisan criminal justice coalitions
