# Criminal Justice Reform: Stakeholders

## Those Harmed by the System

### Incarcerated People

**Population**: ~2 million currently incarcerated

**Harms**:

- Loss of liberty
- Exposure to violence
- Inadequate healthcare
- Family separation
- Loss of income and opportunity
- Psychological damage
- Stigma and discrimination post-release

**Voice**: Limited political power; cannot vote in most states while incarcerated.

### Formerly Incarcerated People

**Population**: ~20 million with felony convictions; ~70 million with any criminal record

**Ongoing Harms**:

- Employment discrimination
- Housing barriers
- Voting restrictions
- Professional licensing bans
- Social stigma
- Ongoing supervision (probation/parole)

**Organizing**: Growing movement of formerly incarcerated advocates.

### Families of Incarcerated People

**Population**: 2.7 million children with incarcerated parent; millions more with incarcerated family members

**Impacts**:

| Impact | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| Economic | Loss of income, visitation costs, phone costs |
| Emotional | Trauma, stigma, disrupted relationships |
| Housing | Loss of housing if leaseholder incarcerated |
| Children | Educational, behavioral, health effects |
| Collateral | Guilt by association, surveillance |

### Communities

**Concentrated Impact**:

- Certain neighborhoods bear majority of incarceration
- Removal of working-age adults
- Cycling in and out
- Disrupted social networks
- Over-policing and under-protection

## Those Who Benefit

### Private Prison Industry

**Key Players**:

| Company | Facilities | Revenue |
|---------|------------|---------|
| CoreCivic | 120+ | $1.9 billion |
| GEO Group | 130+ | $2.3 billion |

**Business Model**:

- Per diem payments per prisoner
- Occupancy guarantees in contracts
- Cost reduction (often through reduced services)
- Diversification into reentry, monitoring, immigration

**Political Activity**:

- Campaign contributions
- Lobbying against reform
- ALEC membership
- State-level influence

### Prison Services Companies

**Sectors**:

| Service | Key Players | Issues |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| Phone/video | Securus, GTL | Predatory rates ($1/minute+) |
| Commissary | Keefe, Trinity | Markup on essentials |
| Healthcare | Corizon, Wellpath | Inadequate care, cost-cutting |
| Money transfer | JPay | Fees on deposits |
| Food | Aramark, Trinity | Quality concerns |

### Law Enforcement Interests

**Police Unions**:

- Resist accountability measures
- Oppose use of force reforms
- Lobby against civilian oversight
- Protect officers from discipline
- Political influence through endorsements

**Sheriffs**:

- Elected officials with broad power
- Jail administration
- Immigration enforcement (287(g))
- Asset forfeiture beneficiaries

### Prosecutorial Interests

**Prosecutors' Associations**:

- National District Attorneys Association
- State prosecutors associations
- Lobby against sentencing reform
- Oppose discovery requirements
- Resist accountability measures

**Individual Prosecutors**:

- Career advancement through convictions
- Elected on "tough on crime" platforms
- Resist oversight

### Bail Industry

**Commercial Bail**:

- 10% fee on bail bonds
- $2+ billion industry
- Lobbies against bail reform
- ALEC involvement
- Only two countries allow commercial bail (U.S. and Philippines)

### Rural Communities

**Prison-Dependent Economies**:

- Prisons major employers
- Census counts prisoners in prison location
- Federal funding based on population
- Political representation benefits
- Resist prison closures

## Government Actors

### Federal

**DOJ**:

- Civil Rights Division (pattern-or-practice)
- Consent decrees with police departments
- Sentencing policy
- Grant funding

**Bureau of Prisons**:

- Federal prison administration
- First Step Act implementation

**Sentencing Commission**:

- Guideline development
- Retroactivity decisions
- Data collection

### State

**Governors**:

- Appointments (courts, corrections)
- Clemency power
- Policy agenda

**Legislators**:

- Sentencing laws
- Prison funding
- Reform legislation

**Corrections Departments**:

- Prison administration
- Programming
- Parole recommendations

### Local

**Prosecutors**:

- Charging decisions
- Plea negotiations
- Sentencing recommendations

**Judges**:

- Sentencing (within guidelines)
- Bail decisions
- Case management

**Sheriffs**:

- Jail administration
- Local enforcement
- Elected accountability

## Victims

### Victim Perspectives

**Diverse Views**:

- Some support harsh punishment
- Many prioritize healing over punishment
- Interest in accountability, not always incarceration
- Restorative justice interest

**Victim Organizations**:

| Orientation | Groups |
|-------------|--------|
| Traditional | National Organization for Victim Assistance |
| Reform-oriented | Crime Survivors for Safety and Justice |
| Restorative | Various local programs |

### Victim Needs

- Safety
- Information
- Voice in process
- Restitution
- Services
- Healing
