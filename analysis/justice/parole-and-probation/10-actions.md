# Parole and Probation Reform: Action Guide

## Overview

This document provides concrete actions for individuals, communities, organizations, and elected officials to advance parole and probation reform. Actions are organized by actor type and level of commitment.

## Individual Actions

### Quick Actions (Under 30 Minutes)

| Action | How To | Impact |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Learn the basics | Read overview documents, watch documentaries | Personal education |
| Sign petitions | Organization websites, change.org | Demonstrate support |
| Share information | Social media, conversations | Public education |
| Contact legislators | Phone, email, online form | Political pressure |
| Register to vote | State election website | Electoral power |
| Donate | Reform organizations | Financial support |

### Regular Engagement (Monthly)

| Action | How To | Impact |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Attend city/county meetings | Public comment on probation issues | Local accountability |
| Write letters to editor | Local newspapers, online outlets | Shape public opinion |
| Participate in advocacy days | Coordinated legislative visits | Amplify reform voice |
| Volunteer with organizations | Local reform groups | Build capacity |
| Attend community forums | Town halls, listening sessions | Community engagement |
| Support affected families | Practical help, connections | Direct assistance |

### Deeper Involvement (Ongoing)

| Action | How To | Impact |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Join reform coalition | Local or state organization | Collective power |
| Become trained advocate | Organizational training programs | Skilled advocacy |
| Testify at hearings | Legislative, administrative | Official record |
| Mentor supervised individuals | Formal programs | Individual support |
| Run for office | School board, local offices | Decision-making power |
| Pursue related career | Law, social work, policy | Systemic change |

### Personal Practices

| Practice | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Examine biases | Reflect on assumptions about supervised people |
| Use person-first language | "Person on probation" not "probationer" |
| Share opportunities | Employment, housing, connections |
| Challenge stigma | Speak up against prejudice |
| Support businesses | Patronize employers who hire fairly |

## Actions for Directly Impacted Individuals

### While on Supervision

| Action | Consideration |
|--------|---------------|
| Know your rights | Request conditions in writing, understand violations |
| Document everything | Keep copies of all communications |
| Connect with advocates | Legal aid, reform organizations |
| Share your story (if safe) | Anonymous options available |
| Support others | Peer support valuable |

### After Supervision

| Action | How To |
|--------|--------|
| Tell your story | Testimony, media, presentations |
| Join advocacy organizations | JustLeadershipUSA, local groups |
| Mentor others | Formal and informal support |
| Advocate for policy change | Legislative testimony, organizing |
| Run for office | Your experience is valuable |
| Pursue professional roles | Policy, law, social work |

### Safety Considerations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Retaliation while supervised | Anonymous participation, organizational support |
| Employment impact | Know your rights, choose disclosure carefully |
| Emotional toll | Self-care, support systems |
| Privacy concerns | Control your narrative |
