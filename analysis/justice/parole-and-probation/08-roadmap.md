# Parole and Probation Reform: Implementation Roadmap

## Overview

Comprehensive parole and probation reform requires sustained effort across multiple years, engaging diverse stakeholders, building political support, and implementing changes systematically. This roadmap provides a phased approach adaptable to different jurisdictions and political contexts.

## Risk Mitigation

### Political Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Change in administration | Bipartisan coalition, statutory (not regulatory) reform |
| High-profile crime | Pre-built response, data on outcomes |
| Opposition campaign | Proactive messaging, law enforcement allies |
| Budget cuts | Built-in savings, dedicated funding |

### Implementation Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Agency resistance | Early engagement, leadership changes, mandate |
| Staff resistance | Training, incentives, gradual change |
| Technology delays | Early planning, interim solutions |
| Service capacity | Phased implementation, partnerships |

### Sustainability Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Coalition fatigue | Distributed leadership, quick wins |
| Funding cuts | Demonstrated savings, advocacy capacity |
| Backsliding | Ongoing monitoring, accountability |
| Leadership turnover | Institutional knowledge transfer |
