## Legal Resources

### Key Federal Statutes

| Statute | Citation | Purpose |
|---------|----------|---------|
| Civil Rights Act (Section 1983) | 42 U.S.C. § 1983 | Civil liability for constitutional violations |
| Criminal Civil Rights | 18 U.S.C. § 242 | Criminal prosecution for deprivation of rights |
| Pattern or Practice Authority | 34 U.S.C. § 12601 | DOJ investigations of systemic misconduct |
| Conspiracy Against Rights | 18 U.S.C. § 241 | Criminal prosecution for conspiracy |
| Civil Rights Attorney's Fees | 42 U.S.C. § 1988 | Attorney fee recovery |

### Key Supreme Court Cases

| Case | Year | Holding | Impact |
|------|------|---------|--------|
| Monroe v. Pape | 1961 | Section 1983 available against state actors | Revived civil rights litigation |
| Bivens v. Six Unknown Agents | 1971 | Implied cause of action against federal officers | Federal officer liability |
| Monell v. Dept. of Social Services | 1978 | Municipalities can be sued, but not under respondeat superior | Limited city liability |
| Harlow v. Fitzgerald | 1982 | Objective qualified immunity standard | Modern QI doctrine created |
| Anderson v. Creighton | 1987 | "Clearly established" must be particularized | QI strengthened |
| Graham v. Connor | 1989 | "Objective reasonableness" for excessive force | Use of force standard |
| Pearson v. Callahan | 2009 | Courts can skip constitutional ruling | Precedent development harmed |
| City of Canton v. Harris | 1989 | Failure to train can be deliberate indifference | Training liability limited |
| Connick v. Thompson | 2011 | Single incident insufficient for Monell | City liability narrowed |
