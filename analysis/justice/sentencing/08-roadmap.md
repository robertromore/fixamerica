## Phase 2: Legislative Reform (Years 1-2)

### Federal Mandatory Minimum Reform

**Comprehensive Legislation**:

```text
SENTENCING REFORM ACT OF [YEAR]

TITLE I - MANDATORY MINIMUM ELIMINATION

SEC. 101. REPEAL OF MANDATORY MINIMUM DRUG SENTENCES.
(a) Repeal 21 U.S.C. 841(b) mandatory minimums.
(b) Repeal 21 U.S.C. 960(b) mandatory minimums.
(c) Retain guidelines as advisory.

SEC. 102. REFORM OF FIREARMS MANDATORY MINIMUMS.
(a) Eliminate 924(c) stacking requirement.
(b) Make 924(c) concurrent, not consecutive.
(c) Reduce ACCA minimum to 5 years.

TITLE II - RETROACTIVITY

SEC. 201. RETROACTIVE APPLICATION.
All provisions shall apply retroactively.

SEC. 202. RESENTENCING PROCESS.
Expedited resentencing within 1 year of enactment.
```

**Legislative Strategy**:

| Step | Timeline |
|------|----------|
| Committee hearings | Months 1-3 |
| Mark-up | Months 4-5 |
| Floor vote (Senate) | Months 6-8 |
| House passage | Months 9-10 |
| Conference | Month 11 |
| Signing | Month 12 |

## Phase 3: Structural Reform (Years 2-4)

### Federal Second Look Legislation

**Federal Second Look Act**:

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Eligibility | All federal prisoners after 10 years |
| Presumptive review | After 15 years |
| Presumptive release | After 20 years (with exceptions) |
| Process | Court review with counsel |
| Criteria | Rehabilitation, age, risk, release plan |
| Retroactivity | All current prisoners eligible |

**Implementation Timeline**:

| Milestone | Timeline |
|-----------|----------|
| Legislation enacted | Year 2 |
| Rules promulgated | Year 2, Q3 |
| First petitions filed | Year 2, Q4 |
| Initial resentencings | Year 3 |
| Full implementation | Year 4 |

### Federal Guidelines Reform

**Sentencing Commission Actions**:

| Reform | Timeline |
|--------|----------|
| Reduce drug guidelines 2 levels | Year 2 |
| Make retroactive | Year 2 |
| Reduce criminal history impact | Year 3 |
| Reform 924(c) guidelines | Year 3 |
| Comprehensive guideline review | Years 3-4 |

**Expected Guideline Changes**:

| Offense | Current Guideline | Reformed Guideline |
|---------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Drug trafficking (mid-level) | 78-97 months | 51-63 months |
| 924(c) first offense | +60-84 months | +24-36 months |
| Fraud | 24-30 months | 18-24 months |

### State Second Look Implementation

## Phase 4: Parole and Release Reform (Years 3-5)

### Federal Parole Restoration

**Components**:

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Eligibility | All federal prisoners after 10 years |
| Board | Independent U.S. Parole Commission (reconstituted) |
| Standards | Presumptive release; burden on government |
| Representation | Right to counsel at hearings |
| Appeal | To Article III court |

**Implementation**:

| Step | Timeline |
|------|----------|
| Legislation | Year 3 |
| Commission established | Year 3, Q3 |
| First hearings | Year 4 |
| Full caseload | Year 5 |

### State Parole Reform

**Priority Actions by State Category**:

| State Category | Reform Priority |
|----------------|-----------------|
| States without parole | Restore discretionary parole |
| States with limited parole | Expand eligibility |
| States with parole | Reform grant criteria |
| All states | Ensure due process, representation |

**Model Parole Reform Elements**:

1. Presumption of release at parole eligibility
2. Right to counsel at hearings
3. Clear, published criteria
4. Limit on victim veto
5. Professional (not political) board members
6. Expedited review for elderly prisoners

### Truth-in-Sentencing Reform

**Reduce Time-Served Requirements**:

| Current (many states) | Reformed |
|-----------------------|----------|
| 85% for violent offenses | 65% with earned time |
| No parole | Parole eligibility at 50% |
| Limited good time | Day-for-day good time |

**Federal Good Time Reform**:

| Current | Reformed |
|---------|----------|
| 54 days/year (15%) | Day-for-day (50%) |
| Limited First Step Act credits | Expanded earning categories |
| Many excluded offenses | Universal eligibility |

## Phase 5: Systemic Change (Years 5-10)

### Comprehensive Sentence Review

**National Sentence Review Commission**:

| Function | Details |
|----------|---------|
| Review all sentences 20+ years | Recommend reductions |
| Identify excessive sentences | Develop standards |
| Recommend clemency | To President/Governors |
| Report to Congress | Annual reports |

### Racial Disparity Elimination

**Mandatory Disparity Reporting**:

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Collect demographic data | All charging, plea, sentencing decisions |
| Publish disparity reports | Annual, by district/circuit |
| Explain disparities | Justify or correct |
| Consequences | Fund reduction for persistent disparity |

**Disparity Correction Mechanisms**:

1. Sentencing Commission review of disparity-producing guidelines
2. Inspector General audits of prosecutorial practices
3. Private right of action for discriminatory prosecution
4. Presumption of reduction for identified disparity cases

### Abolish LWOP

**National Campaign**:

| Phase | Timeline |
|-------|----------|
| Build coalition | Years 1-2 |
| State-by-state campaigns | Years 3-5 |
| Federal legislation | Years 6-8 |
| Constitutional litigation | Years 5-10 |

**Model Reform**:

| Current | Reformed |
|---------|----------|
| Life without parole | Life with parole after 25 years |
| Virtual life (50+ years) | Maximum 30 years to parole |
