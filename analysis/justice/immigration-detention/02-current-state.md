# Immigration Detention and Enforcement: Current State

## Overview of the Detention System

The U.S. immigration detention system is a vast, decentralized network of facilities operated by federal agencies, private corporations, state and local governments, and the U.S. Marshals Service. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) is the primary agency responsible for immigration detention, operating under the Department of Homeland Security (DHS).

## Detention Facilities

### Types of Facilities

**Service Processing Centers (SPCs)**
- Owned and operated directly by ICE
- 5 facilities nationwide
- Capacity: ~3,000 beds

**Contract Detention Facilities (CDFs)**
- Privately operated under contract with ICE
- 12+ major facilities
- Capacity: ~15,000 beds

**Intergovernmental Service Agreements (IGSAs)**
- State/local jails holding ICE detainees
- 100+ facilities
- Capacity: ~15,000 beds

**Family Residential Centers**
- South Texas Family Residential Center (Dilley, TX)
- Karnes County Residential Center (Karnes City, TX)
- Berks Family Residential Center (Leesport, PA)

## Current Policies

### Detention Mandates

**Mandatory Detention Categories (INA Section 236(c))**
- Aggravated felonies
- Certain drug offenses
- Firearms offenses
- Multiple crimes of moral turpitude
- Terrorism-related grounds

**Discretionary Detention**
- Flight risk determinations
- Danger to community
- Final orders of removal
- Pending removal proceedings

### Bond and Release

| Release Type | Requirements | Availability |
|--------------|--------------|--------------|
| Bond | Set by immigration judge | Limited by mandatory detention |
| Parole | DHS discretion | Inconsistently applied |
| Recognizance | Low risk determination | Rarely granted |
| Alternatives to Detention | Enrollment in program | Capacity limited |

### Enforcement Priorities

Current ICE enforcement priorities include:
1. National security threats
2. Recent border crossers
3. Individuals with criminal convictions
4. Immigration court absconders
5. Final order cases

## Asylum Seeker Detention

### Credible Fear Process

|-------|----------|------------------|
| Apprehension | Day 0 | Detained |
| Credible fear interview | 14-45 days | Detained |

| Immigration court | Months-years | Often detained |

### Expedited Removal

- Applied within 100 miles of border
- 14-day arrival window
- Limited review
- High stakes interviews while detained
