# Immigration Detention and Enforcement: Solutions

## Vision for Reform

A humane, effective immigration system would:
- Minimize use of detention
- Prioritize community-based alternatives
- Ensure due process for all
- Provide right to counsel
- End family separation
- Eliminate private profit from detention
- Maintain high appearance rates through support services
- Protect asylum seekers' rights

## Solution Categories

### I. Alternatives to Detention (ATD)

#### Community-Based Case Management

**Model Programs**

| Program | Appearance Rate | Cost/Day | Key Features |
|---------|-----------------|----------|--------------|
| Vera Institute pilot | 99.3% | $12-17 | Case management, legal orientation |
| Lutheran Immigration (CCIM) | 98%+ | $15-20 | Community support, services |
| NIJC community supervision | 99%+ | $10-15 | Legal help, check-ins |
| UK/Canada alternatives | 90-95% | Varies | Community sponsors |

**Essential Elements**
- Assigned case manager
- Connection to legal services
- Social services access (housing, healthcare)
- Regular check-ins (in-person or phone)
- Language-accessible communication
- Transportation assistance for hearings
- Mental health support

**Implementation Requirements**

| Component | Specification |
|-----------|--------------|
| Staff ratios | 1 case manager per 25-50 clients |
| Check-in frequency | Weekly to monthly based on risk |
| Services | Legal, housing, healthcare referrals |
| Technology | App or phone check-ins (optional) |
| Duration | Until case resolution |

#### Electronic Monitoring (Reformed)

**Current Problems with Ankle Monitors**
- Stigmatizing and prison-like
- Employment barriers
- Skin irritation and injury
- Mental health impacts
- Costly ($4-10/day)
- Operated by GEO subsidiary

**Reformed Approach**
- Smartphone apps instead of ankle monitors
- Less invasive verification methods
- Remove private profit motive
- Government-operated or nonprofit-run
- Privacy protections
- Reasonable check-in schedules

**SmartLINK-Type Programs (Reformed)**
- Facial recognition concerns addressed
- Privacy protections
- Reduced check-in burdens
- No ankle monitors unless highest risk

#### Community Sponsorship

**Model: Canadian Private Sponsorship**
- Community groups sponsor refugees/migrants
- Provide housing, support, orientation
- High success rates
- Reduces government costs
- Builds community integration

**U.S. Application**
- Faith communities as sponsors
- Immigrant community organizations
- Civic groups
- Family sponsorship formalized

#### Supervised Release

**For Low-Risk Individuals**
- Release on recognizance
- Periodic check-ins
- Address/phone verification
- Court date reminders
- No technology monitoring

### II. Legal System Reforms

#### Right to Appointed Counsel

**The Problem**
- No right to counsel in immigration proceedings
- 70%+ detained individuals unrepresented
- Vast disparity in outcomes

**The Solution: Universal Representation**

| Element | Specification |
|---------|--------------|
| Eligibility | All detained individuals |
| Funding source | Federal appropriations |
| Provider model | Public defender or contract attorneys |
| Training | Immigration law specialization |
| Caseload limits | 50-75 cases per attorney |

**Impact Projection**

| Outcome | Without Counsel | With Counsel |
|---------|----------------|--------------|
| Case success rate | 10-15% | 50%+ |
| Bond grant rate | 25% | 60%+ |
| Appeals filed | 15% | 75%+ |
| Voluntary departure | Higher | Lower (appropriate claims pursued) |

**Funding Estimate**
- 500,000 annual cases x average $5,000/case = $2.5 billion
- Compare: Current detention budget $3.4 billion

#### Bond Reform

**Current Problems**
- Mandatory detention categories too broad
- No bond hearings for many
- Unaffordable bond amounts
- No individualized assessment

**Reforms Needed**

| Reform | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| Eliminate mandatory detention | Individualized custody decisions |
| Bond hearing within 48 hours | Quick review of custody |
| Affordable bond | Based on ability to pay |
| Presumption of release | Detention as exception |
| Clear and convincing standard | Government burden to justify detention |

**Bond Amount Reform**

| Current Practice | Reformed Practice |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| $5,000-$25,000+ typical | Ability-to-pay assessment |
| Cash bond required | Unsecured bonds permitted |
| No payment plans | Installment payments allowed |
| Bail bond companies profit | Community bond funds |

#### Immigration Court Reform

**Independence**
- Remove EOIR from DOJ
- Create independent immigration court
- Article I court with life tenure
- Insulation from political pressure

**Capacity**
- Double number of immigration judges
- Support staff increases
- Technology improvements
- Reduce backlog

**Due Process**
- Adequate time for case preparation
- Continuances for good cause
- Full evidentiary hearings
- Right to examine evidence

### III. Policy Reforms

#### End Mandatory Detention

**Legislative Changes Needed**
- Repeal INA 236(c) mandatory detention
- Replace with individualized assessments
- Risk assessment tools (validated)
- Presumption of release

**Risk Assessment (Reformed)**

| Factor | Appropriate | Inappropriate |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| Past court appearance | Yes | No |
| Community ties | Yes | No |
| Past criminal conviction alone | No | N/A |
| Length of U.S. residence | Yes | No |
| Family in U.S. | Yes | No |
| Employment | Yes | No |
| Flight risk evidence | Yes | No |
| Race/national origin | No | N/A |

#### End Family Detention

**Policy Change**
- No detention of children
- No detention of families
- Community placement instead
- Case management for families

**Implementation**
- Close family detention centers (Dilley, Karnes, Berks)
- Repurpose funding for community programs
- Family case management model
- Expedited family court dockets

#### Protect Asylum Seekers

**Presumption Against Detention**
- Asylum seekers not detained
- Parole as default
- Credible fear = release
- Community support services

**Process Reforms**
- Credible fear interviews by trained officers
- Full explanation of rights
- Access to counsel for interviews
- No expedited removal for asylum seekers

#### Limit 287(g) and Local Cooperation

**Federal Policy Changes**
- Moratorium on new 287(g) agreements
- Phase out existing agreements
- End Secure Communities fingerprint sharing
- Prohibit ICE courthouse arrests

**State/Local Policies**
- Sanctuary policies
- Limit detainer compliance
- Prohibit local enforcement
- Protect community trust

### IV. End Private Detention

#### Phase Out Private Contracts

**Implementation Timeline**

| Phase | Timeline | Action |
|-------|----------|--------|
| Phase 1 | Year 1 | Freeze new contracts |
| Phase 2 | Years 1-3 | No contract renewals |
| Phase 3 | Years 3-7 | Transition existing facilities |
| Phase 4 | Year 7+ | Full public operation or closure |

**Alternative Models**
- Government-operated facilities (reduced)
- Nonprofit alternatives to detention
- Community-based programs
- No private profit from any immigration enforcement

#### Contract Reforms (Interim)

**If private contracts continue temporarily:**

| Current Practice | Reformed Practice |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| Per-bed payments | Performance-based payments |
| Guaranteed minimums | No bed guarantees |
| Profit maximization | Cost-plus contracts only |
| Limited oversight | Independent monitoring |
| Proprietary standards | Public, enforceable standards |

### V. Detention Conditions (While Detention Exists)

#### Medical Care Standards

| Standard | Specification |
|----------|--------------|
| Intake screening | Within 2 hours, comprehensive |
| Chronic disease care | Specialist access, medication continuity |
| Mental health | On-site mental health staff, crisis care |
| Emergency care | Hospital access within 30 minutes |
| Dental care | Regular access, not just emergency |
| Women's health | Full reproductive healthcare |

#### Living Conditions

| Standard | Specification |
|----------|--------------|
| Occupancy | Maximum 80% capacity |
| Personal space | Minimum 50 sq ft per person |
| Outdoor access | Daily, minimum 2 hours |
| Nutrition | Culturally appropriate, sufficient |
| Communication | Daily phone access, video calls |
| Visitation | Regular contact visits |

#### Legal Access

| Standard | Specification |
|----------|--------------|
| Attorney visits | Private, in-person, no time limits |
| Legal materials | Law library, computer access |
| Phone calls | Free legal calls |
| Know Your Rights | Presentation within 48 hours |
| Case documents | Access to own file |

#### Independent Oversight

| Mechanism | Function |
|-----------|----------|
| Independent monitor | Unannounced inspections |
| Ombudsperson | Complaint investigation |
| Public reporting | Quarterly condition reports |
| Stakeholder board | Community oversight |
| Federal oversight | DHS OIG, GAO regular reviews |

### VI. Decriminalization

#### Reform Criminal Immigration Provisions

**Current Criminal Provisions**

| Offense | Current Penalty | Reform |
|---------|----------------|--------|
| Illegal entry (8 USC 1325) | Misdemeanor, up to 6 months | Eliminate or reduce |
| Illegal reentry (8 USC 1326) | Felony, up to 20 years | Eliminate or reduce |
| Illegal presence | Civil violation | Maintain civil |

**End Operation Streamline**
- Stop mass criminal prosecutions
- Civil process for border crossers
- Redirect resources to civil system
- Reduce federal court burden

### VII. Community Investment

#### Alternative Investment

**Current Detention Budget**: ~$3.4 billion annually

**Alternative Allocation**

| Program | Investment | Impact |
|---------|------------|--------|
| Universal representation | $1.5 billion | All get counsel |
| Community alternatives | $1 billion | 500,000 supervised |
| Immigration court expansion | $500 million | Reduce backlog |
| Integration services | $300 million | Support successful cases |
| Oversight and monitoring | $100 million | Accountability |

#### Community-Based Reception

**For Arriving Asylum Seekers**
- Reception centers (not detention)
- Orientation to U.S. system
- Legal screening
- Health screening
- Connection to sponsors/community
- Travel assistance to final destination
