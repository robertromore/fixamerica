# Immigration Detention and Enforcement: Opposition

## Who Supports Expanded Detention?

Understanding the arguments and interests of those who support expanded immigration detention is essential for developing effective counter-strategies. This document maps the landscape of opposition to detention reform.

**Financial Interests**

| Company | ICE Revenue (2023) | Dependency |
|---------|-------------------|------------|
| GEO Group | $800+ million | 35%+ of total revenue |
| CoreCivic | $600+ million | 30%+ of total revenue |

**Arguments Made by Industry**
- "Providing safe, humane detention services"
- "Meeting government demand for capacity"
- "Creating jobs in underserved communities"
- "More cost-effective than government operation"
- "Professional standards and training"

**Actual Motivations**
- Shareholder returns
- Stock price maintenance
- Long-term contract security
- Market expansion
- Regulatory capture

**Political Influence Tactics**

| Tactic | Amount/Scope |
|--------|--------------|

| Campaign contributions | Key committee members |

### BI Incorporated (GEO Subsidiary)

**Alternative Detention Capture**
- Operates ISAP (Intensive Supervision Appearance Program)
- $2.2 billion contract (2020-2027)
- Ankle monitors, SmartLINK app
- Profits whether detained or monitored

**Argument**: "Alternatives to detention are humane"
**Reality**: Same company profits, surveillance instead of cages

## Political Organizations

### Federation for American Immigration Reform (FAIR)

**Profile**
- Founded 1979
- Headquarters: Washington, DC
- Budget: $10+ million annually
- Designated extremist group by SPLC

**Positions on Detention**
- Support mandatory detention expansion
- Oppose sanctuary policies
- Support 287(g) expansion
- Support expedited removal
- Oppose asylum protections

**Arguments**
- "Catch and release" fails
- Detention ensures removal
- Public safety requires detention
- Asylum system is abused

### Center for Immigration Studies (CIS)

**Profile**
- FAIR spinoff (1985)
- Think tank model
- Produces research supporting restriction
- Close media relationships

**Positions**
- Support expanded detention
- Oppose alternatives to detention
- Support mandatory detention laws
- Skeptical of asylum claims

**Arguments**
- Detention improves compliance
- Alternatives don't work
- National security concerns
- Rule of law requires enforcement

### NumbersUSA

**Profile**
- Grassroots mobilization focus
- Mass fax/email campaigns
- Grade congressional members

**Positions**
- Support detention expansion
- Oppose "amnesty" (any legalization)
- Support interior enforcement
- Oppose sanctuary policies

### Heritage Foundation

**Profile**
- Major conservative think tank
- Budget: $100+ million
- Significant policy influence

**Immigration Positions**
- Strong enforcement support
- Oppose legalization programs
- Support border security
- Support detention as tool

**Arguments**
- Rule of law
- National sovereignty
- Security concerns
- Fiscal responsibility (selective)

## Arguments Analysis

### Security Arguments

| Argument | Evidence Reality |
|----------|-----------------|
| "Immigrants commit more crime" | Studies show lower crime rates |
| "Detention prevents crime" | No evidence of crime reduction |
| "Terrorism risk" | Very few immigration-terrorism links |
| "Gangs enter through immigration" | Gangs are largely domestic |

### Compliance Arguments

| Argument | Evidence Reality |
|----------|-----------------|
| "Released migrants abscond" | 99%+ appearance rate with ATD |
| "Need detention for compliance" | Alternatives achieve equal/better compliance |
| "Final orders aren't enforced" | Most absconders have no ties, not released |

### Deterrence Arguments

| Argument | Evidence Reality |
|----------|-----------------|
| "Detention deters migration" | No evidence of deterrent effect |
| "Harsh conditions send message" | Push factors outweigh deterrence |
| "Word gets back to home countries" | Migration continues regardless |

### Rule of Law Arguments

| Argument | Evidence Reality |
|----------|-----------------|
| "Must enforce all laws" | Prosecutorial discretion is normal |
| "No one is above the law" | Discretion exercised throughout legal system |
| "Congress passed detention laws" | Laws can be changed; discretion exists |

### Fiscal Arguments

| Argument | Evidence Reality |
|----------|-----------------|
| "Detention is cost-effective" | $150-200/day vs. $2-10/day ATD |
| "Immigrants burden public services" | Studies show net fiscal contribution |
| "Private detention saves money" | Evidence of equal or higher costs |

## Counter-Strategy Considerations

### Weaknesses in Opposition

| Weakness | Opportunity |
|----------|-------------|
| Profit motive exposed | Public opinion on private prisons |
| Evidence contradicts claims | Data-driven advocacy |
| Moral high ground lacking | Faith-based opposition |
| Cost arguments fail | Fiscal conservative outreach |
| Bipartisan reform possible | Cross-partisan coalition building |

### Effective Counter-Arguments

**To Security Claims**
- Crime data shows immigrants safer
- Community trust enhances safety
- Detention doesn't prevent crime

**To Compliance Claims**
- ATD achieves 99%+ appearance
- Cost fraction of detention
- Humane and effective

**To Deterrence Claims**
- No evidence of deterrence
- Push factors drive migration
- Legal pathways reduce irregular migration

**To Rule of Law Claims**
- Discretion is legal and normal
- Current system is broken
- Reform is constitutional

**To Fiscal Claims**
- Detention costs 20-100x more than ATD
- Private prisons don't save money
- Immigrants contribute fiscally
