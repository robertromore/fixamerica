# Immigration Detention and Enforcement: Stakeholders

## Overview

Immigration detention involves a complex web of stakeholders with varying interests, power, and perspectives. Understanding these stakeholders is essential for effective advocacy and reform efforts.

## Detained Individuals and Families

### Detained Adults

**Demographics**
| Category | Percentage |
|----------|-----------|
| Men | 86% |
| Women | 14% |
| Ages 18-35 | 55% |
| Ages 36-55 | 35% |
| Ages 55+ | 10% |

**Primary Categories**
- Asylum seekers fleeing persecution
- Long-term residents with criminal convictions
- Visa overstays
- Border crossers
- Prior deportees reentering

**Stakes**
- Liberty and freedom
- Safety from persecution
- Family unity
- Access to counsel
- Humane treatment
- Due process rights

**Power**
- Very limited: cannot vote, limited resources
- Collective action (hunger strikes, protests) occasionally effective
- Dependent on outside advocates

### U.S. Citizen Family Members

**Who They Are**
- Citizen children of detained parents (estimated 5 million+ children with undocumented parents)
- Citizen spouses and partners
- U.S.-born siblings of detained individuals
- Extended family networks

**Stakes**
- Family unity and stability
- Economic security (detained breadwinners)
- Emotional and psychological wellbeing
- Children's development and education
- Avoiding own trauma/fear

**Power**
- Can vote and participate politically
- Can speak publicly without fear of deportation
- Organize through family-focused advocacy groups

## Government Stakeholders

### Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE)

**Enforcement and Removal Operations (ERO)**
- Primary responsibility for detention
- 6,000+ officers
- Manages contracts with facilities
- Makes custody determinations

**Homeland Security Investigations (HSI)**
- Criminal investigations
- Less involved in civil detention
- Focus on smuggling, trafficking

**Institutional Interests**
- Budget preservation and growth
- Discretion in enforcement
- Metrics showing productivity
- Union interests (National ICE Council)

### Customs and Border Protection (CBP)

**Role**
- Initial apprehension at border
- Short-term detention (72-hour limit, often exceeded)
- Transfer to ICE custody or release

**Stakes**
- Managing border processing
- Quick transfers out of CBP custody
- Avoiding liability for conditions

### Executive Office for Immigration Review (EOIR)

**Role**
- Immigration courts (under DOJ)
- Adjudicates detention cases
- Bond hearings (where permitted)
- Removal proceedings

**Stakes**
- Case management
- Reducing backlog
- Judicial independence (threatened)
- Resources and staffing

### Department of Homeland Security (DHS)

**Leadership Role**
- Oversees ICE, CBP, USCIS
- Sets enforcement priorities
- Budget allocation
- Policy direction

**Stakes**
- Political accountability
- Managing public perception
- Congressional relations
- Balancing enforcement with rights

### U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS)

**Role**
- Asylum officer interviews
- Credible fear determinations
- Benefits adjudication

**Stakes**
- Processing efficiency
- Asylum caseload management
- Quality of decisions

### Department of Justice (DOJ)

**Role**
- Houses EOIR (immigration courts)
- Prosecutes immigration crimes
- Defends government in litigation

**Stakes**
- Criminal immigration prosecution
- Managing federal court system
- Policy litigation defense

### State and Local Governments

**Participating Jurisdictions**
- 287(g) agreement localities
- Counties operating IGSAs (detention contracts)
- States with anti-sanctuary laws

**Stakes**
- Revenue from detention contracts
- Federal reimbursement
- Political positioning
- Public safety arguments

**Sanctuary Jurisdictions**
- States and cities limiting cooperation
- Protect immigrant residents
- Reduce local liability
- Maintain community trust

## Stakeholder Power Analysis

### Power Matrix

| Stakeholder | Power Level | Resources | Influence Type |
|-------------|-------------|-----------|----------------|
| Private prison companies | High | $$ billions, lobbying | Political, economic |
| ICE/CBP | High | Budget, discretion | Operational, political |
| Congress | High | Appropriations, legislation | Legal, budgetary |
| Advocacy organizations | Medium | Litigation, public pressure | Legal, public opinion |
| State/local governments | Medium | Policy authority | Operational, political |

| Detained individuals | Low | Limited | Moral, through allies |

|---|--------------|----------------|

| **Low Interest** | General public, most businesses | Congress (varies), media |
