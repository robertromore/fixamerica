# Immigration Detention and Enforcement: Actions

## Overview

This document provides concrete actions that individuals, communities, organizations, and institutions can take to reduce immigration detention, improve conditions, and support detained individuals and their families.

## Individual Actions

| Action | Time Required | Impact |
|--------|--------------|--------|

**Sample Call Script for Congress**

> "Hello, my name is [NAME] and I'm a constituent from [CITY/ZIP]. I'm calling to urge [REPRESENTATIVE/SENATOR NAME] to support ending immigration detention and investing in community-based alternatives. The current system costs taxpayers over $3 billion annually while causing tremendous harm to families and communities. I urge [him/her] to cosponsor the [SPECIFIC BILL] and oppose any funding increases for detention. Thank you."

### Establish Sanctuary Spaces

**Faith Communities**
- Declare physical sanctuary
- Host Know Your Rights trainings
- Provide meeting space for immigrants' rights groups
- Participate in accompaniment programs
- Advocate with elected officials

**Schools**
- Adopt sensitive locations policies
- Train staff on rights
- Connect families to resources
- Resist ICE access requests
- Provide trauma-informed support

**Hospitals and Health Centers**
- Adopt sensitive locations policies
- Train staff on patient rights
- Post rights information
- Resist ICE access without warrant
- Document any violations
