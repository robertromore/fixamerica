# Immigration Detention and Enforcement: Implementation Roadmap

## Overview

This roadmap outlines a phased approach to transforming the U.S. immigration detention system from one of mass incarceration to one based on community-based alternatives, due process, and human dignity. The timeline spans 10 years, with the most critical reforms front-loaded.

## Risk Management

### Political Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|------------|
| Administration change | Medium | Embed in statute, not just policy |
| Congressional opposition | High | Bipartisan coalition, public pressure |
| Court challenges | Medium | Strong legal foundation |
| Media backlash | Medium | Proactive communications |

### Implementation Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|------------|
| ATD capacity insufficient | Medium | Phased expansion, multiple providers |
| Legal services shortage | Medium | Law school clinics, training programs |
| ICE resistance | High | Leadership change, culture reform |
| Private contractor lobbying | High | Contract provisions, public campaigns |

### Contingency Plans

| Scenario | Response |
|----------|----------|
| Legislation blocked | Maximum executive action |
| Courts strike down reforms | Alternative legal strategies |
| Border "crisis" messaging | Data-driven response, stay the course |
| Budget cuts | Prioritize most impactful programs |
