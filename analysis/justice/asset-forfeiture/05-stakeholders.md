# Stakeholders in Civil Asset Forfeiture Reform

## Overview

Civil asset forfeiture reform involves a complex web of stakeholders with varying interests, resources, and influence. Understanding these stakeholders is essential for building effective reform coalitions.

## Law Enforcement Agencies

### Federal Agencies

| Agency | Role in Forfeiture | Annual Seizures |
|--------|-------------------|-----------------|
| DEA | Drug enforcement, highway interdiction | $800+ million |
| FBI | Organized crime, financial crimes | $600+ million |
| ATF | Firearms trafficking | $100+ million |
| IRS-CI | Tax crimes, money laundering | $500+ million |
| CBP | Border seizures | $400+ million |
| ICE-HSI | Immigration-related crimes | $300+ million |
| Secret Service | Financial crimes | $200+ million |
| Postal Inspection | Mail-related crimes | $100+ million |

### State and Local Agencies

| Category | Engagement Level | Forfeiture Dependency |
|----------|-----------------|----------------------|
| Large urban departments | High volume, moderate dependency | 1-5% of budget |
| Suburban departments | Moderate volume | Variable |
| Rural departments | Lower volume, but higher dependency | Can exceed 10% |
| Highway patrol | High volume through interdiction | Significant |
| Task forces | Often forfeiture-funded | Critical dependency |

### Agency Interests

| Interest | Mechanism | Strength |
|----------|-----------|----------|
| Budget supplementation | Direct retention of proceeds | Very strong |
| Equipment acquisition | Forfeiture-funded purchases | Strong |
| Operational flexibility | Funds outside normal budget | Strong |
| Officer training | Interdiction programs | Moderate |
| Professional standing | Seizure metrics | Moderate |

## Forfeiture Victims

### Demographic Profile

| Category | Percentage | Challenges |
|----------|------------|------------|
| Never charged with crime | 80%+ | Must prove innocence |
| Low/moderate income | 75%+ | Cannot afford attorney |
| Racial minorities | 50%+ | Disparate impact |
| Small seizures (<$5,000) | 60%+ | Uneconomical to contest |

### Victim Categories

| Type | Typical Situation | Resources |
|------|-------------------|-----------|
| Travelers | Cash seized at traffic stops | Limited |
| Small business owners | Operating cash seized | Moderate |
| Property owners | Home seized for relative's conduct | Variable |
| Vehicle owners | Car seized at stop | Limited |
| Bank account holders | Accounts frozen | Variable |

### Interests

| Interest | Barrier | Success Rate |
|----------|---------|--------------|
| Property return | Cost of challenge | ~10-15% (when contested) |
| Compensation | No mechanism | N/A |
| Justice | System stacked against them | Rare |
| Closure | Lengthy process | 1-2 years average |
