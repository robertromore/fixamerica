# Criminal Justice Reform: Roadmap

## Phase 1: Immediate Actions

### Executive Actions (Federal)

**DOJ Actions**:

| Action | Authority |
|--------|-----------|
| Resume pattern-or-practice investigations | 42 U.S.C. § 14141 |
| Consent decrees with problem departments | Existing authority |
| Guidance on use of force | Policy memoranda |
| Federal prosecution priorities | Prosecutorial discretion |
| End federal private prison contracts | Contract authority |

**BOP Actions**:

- Expand compassionate release
- Increase good time credits
- Improve conditions
- Expand programming

**Clemency**:

- Systematic commutation review
- Drug offense sentence reductions
- Elderly prisoner releases
- Pardon process reform

### State-Level Quick Wins

**Bail Reform**:

- Eliminate cash bail for misdemeanors
- Presumption of release
- Risk assessment implementation
- Pretrial services expansion

**Sentencing**:

- Safety valve expansion
- Prosecutorial diversion programs
- Drug court expansion
- Parole consideration reforms

**Policing**:

- Use of force policy updates
- Body camera deployment
- Complaint process reform
- Training curriculum changes

### Local Actions

**Prosecutor Policies**:

- Decline low-level offenses
- Diversion expansion
- Bail recommendations reform
- Charging guideline publication

**Police Department Changes**:

- De-escalation training
- Duty to intervene policies
- Early warning systems
- Community engagement

## Dependencies

### Sequencing Requirements

```text
End cash bail → Build pretrial services
       ↓
Reduce admissions → Reduce population
       ↓
Close facilities → Reinvest savings
       ↓
Community investment → Prevention
```

### Political Dependencies

| Reform | Requires |
|--------|----------|
| Federal policing standards | Filibuster reform or 60 votes |
| Qualified immunity elimination | Congressional action |
| Constitutional amendments | 2/3 Congress + 3/4 states |
| State sentencing reform | State legislative majorities |
| Prosecutor policies | Reform prosecutor elections |

### Resource Dependencies

| Reform | Requires |
|--------|----------|
| Public defense improvement | State funding commitment |
| Alternative response | Municipal investment |
| Reentry services | Community-based organizations |
| Prevention programs | Sustained multi-year funding |

### Knowledge Dependencies

| Reform | Requires |
|--------|----------|
| Risk assessment | Validated, bias-tested tools |
| Evidence-based programming | Evaluation and adaptation |
| Specialty courts | Training and technical assistance |
| Restorative justice | Practitioner development |

## Risks and Contingencies

### Political Risks

**Risk**: Crime increases blamed on reform

**Contingency**:

- Strong evaluation evidence
- Crime prevention investment
- Victim advocacy partnership
- Rapid response messaging

**Risk**: Reform prosecutor recalls

**Contingency**:

- Coalition building before elections
- Victim advocate support
- Transparency and communication
- Crime prevention focus

### Implementation Risks

**Risk**: Bail reform without services

**Contingency**:

- Phase in with service development
- Court reminder systems first
- Build pretrial services capacity
- Monitor failure-to-appear rates

**Risk**: Prison closures without reentry support

**Contingency**:

- Pre-release planning mandatory
- Medicaid enrollment before release
- Housing placement priority
- Employment programs

### Legal Risks

**Risk**: Court challenges to reforms

**Contingency**:

- Strong legal defense prepared
- Constitutional analysis upfront
- Pilot programs to build record
- Legislative backup options

**Risk**: Qualified immunity survives

**Contingency**:

- State-level action
- Federal funding conditions
- Department of Justice enforcement
- Section 1983 alternatives

### Resource Risks

**Risk**: Funding cuts to alternatives

**Contingency**:

- Justice reinvestment lock-box
- Multi-year appropriations
- Private philanthropy bridge
- Demonstrate cost savings

**Risk**: Workforce resistance

**Contingency**:

- Transition support for workers
- Retraining programs
- Community investment in affected areas
- Union engagement early
