# Implementation Roadmap for Fines and Fees Reform

## Overview

This roadmap provides a phased approach to implementing comprehensive fines and fees reform at local, state, and federal levels. It recognizes that reform requires both immediate relief for those currently harmed and structural changes to prevent future harm.

## Phase 1: Immediate Relief (0-6 Months)

### Emergency Actions

| Action | Timeline | Lead Entity | Resources Needed |
|--------|----------|-------------|------------------|
| Warrant clearing events | Month 1-2 | Courts, Public defenders | Volunteer attorneys, outreach |
| Moratorium on license suspension | Month 1 | DMV, Governor | Executive order |
| Suspend incarceration for non-payment | Month 1 | Courts, Sheriffs | Judicial directive |
| Halt new collections referrals | Month 2 | Court administrators | Administrative order |
| Freeze interest accrual | Month 2 | Courts | Administrative order |

### Administrative Reforms

| Action | Timeline | Lead Entity | Resources Needed |
|--------|----------|-------------|------------------|
| Issue ability-to-pay guidelines | Month 2-3 | Judicial council | Staff time, research |
| Create payment plan standards | Month 3-4 | Court administrators | Policy development |
| Inventory all fees/fines | Month 3-4 | Courts, Legislature | Data collection |
| Establish hardship waiver process | Month 4-5 | Courts | Forms, training |
| Begin data collection | Month 5-6 | Courts | Technology investment |

### Community Outreach

| Action | Timeline | Lead Entity | Resources Needed |
|--------|----------|-------------|------------------|
| Public notification of relief | Month 1 | Courts, Advocates | Communications budget |
| Community warrant clinics | Month 2-6 | Legal aid, Public defenders | Volunteer coordination |
| Multilingual information | Month 2-3 | Courts, Community groups | Translation services |
| Driver's license restoration events | Month 3-6 | DMV, Legal aid | Staffing |

## Phase 4: System Transformation (36-60 Months)

### Day Fines Implementation

| Step | Timeline | Implementation |
|------|----------|----------------|
| Pilot program design | Month 36-42 | Courts, Researchers |
| Pilot jurisdiction selection | Month 42-44 | Judicial council |
| Pilot implementation | Month 44-52 | Selected courts |
| Evaluation | Month 52-56 | Researchers |
| Statewide rollout | Month 56-60 | Legislature, Courts |

### Complete Fee System Overhaul

| Component | Timeline | Outcome |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| Comprehensive fee code reform | Month 36-48 | Simplified fee structure |
| Constitutional funding guarantee | Month 48-60 | Ballot initiative or amendment |
| Decoupled court funding | Month 48-60 | General fund reliance |
| Unified fine schedule | Month 48-60 | Standardized statewide |

### Debt Forgiveness Program

| Phase | Timeline | Target |
|-------|----------|--------|
| Uncollectable debt write-off | Month 36-42 | Debt older than 7 years |
| Indigent debt forgiveness | Month 42-48 | Below 200% poverty |
| Matching forgiveness | Month 48-54 | Reduce remaining for payments |
| Complete clearance | Month 54-60 | All remaining pre-reform debt |

## Risk Mitigation

### Political Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Legislative opposition | Bipartisan coalition building |
| Revenue concerns | Alternative funding proposals |
| "Soft on crime" attacks | Public safety messaging |
| Implementation resistance | Phased approach, stakeholder buy-in |

### Implementation Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Court system capacity | Technical assistance, training |
| Data system limitations | Technology investment |
| Stakeholder resistance | Early engagement, co-design |
| Funding gaps | Diversified funding strategy |

### Sustainability Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Political change | Lock in structural changes |
| Economic downturn | Constitutional protections |
| Mission drift | Community accountability |
| Coalition fragmentation | Regular convenings, shared goals |
