# Actions to Reform Fines and Fees

## Overview

This section provides concrete actions that individuals, communities, and organizations can take to advance fines and fees reform. Actions are organized by the type of actor and the level of engagement required.

## Individual Actions

### For Those Affected by Court Debt

| Action | Description | Resources Needed |
|--------|-------------|------------------|
| Know your rights | Learn about ability-to-pay hearings, payment options | Legal aid information |
| Request ability hearing | Ask court for hearing on ability to pay | Court forms |
| Document everything | Keep records of payments, communications | Organization system |
| Seek legal help | Contact legal aid for assistance | Legal aid referral |
| Join advocacy efforts | Connect with others affected | Advocacy organization |

## Government and Official Actions

### For Local Officials

| Action | Authority | Impact |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| **Mayors and councils** | | |
| Eliminate or cap municipal fees | Ordinance | Direct relief |
| End warrant arrests for fines | Policy | Reduce incarceration |
| Audit revenue dependence | Budget review | Transparency |
| Create payment assistance | Fund | Immediate help |
| **Police chiefs** | | |
| End ticket quotas | Department policy | Fair enforcement |
| Deprioritize non-payment arrests | Training, orders | Reduce incarceration |
| Community policing focus | Strategic shift | Trust building |

### For Judges

| Action | Mechanism | Effect |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Conduct ability-to-pay hearings | Bench practice | Constitutional compliance |
| Waive fees for indigent | Case-by-case | Individual relief |
| Order alternatives to fines | Sentencing | Options for poor |
| Advocate for reform | Judicial education | Peer influence |
| Report on fee impacts | Data collection | Transparency |

### For Prosecutors

| Action | Approach | Outcome |
|--------|----------|---------|
| Reduce fine recommendations | Charging, plea offers | Lower burdens |
| Support diversion | Pre-charge options | Avoid fees entirely |
| Agree to ability-to-pay | Case handling | Compliance |
| Advocate for reform | Public statements | Political cover |

### For Legislators

| Action | Level | Scope |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Sponsor reform bills | Individual | Bill introduction |
| Hold hearings | Committee | Public education |
| Commission studies | Body | Data development |
| Appropriate alternative funding | Budget | Enable reform |
| Override local practices | Statute | Statewide standards |

## Campaign Ideas

### Local Campaigns

| Campaign | Target | Ask | Tactics |
|----------|--------|-----|---------|
| End license suspension | City council | Ordinance | Petition, testimony, pressure |
| Municipal fee audit | Mayor | Study | FOIA, public records, media |
| Warrant amnesty | Court | Program | Stakeholder coalition |
