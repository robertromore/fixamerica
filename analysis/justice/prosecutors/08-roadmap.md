# Prosecutorial Reform: Roadmap

## Implementation Framework

### Priority Sequencing

| Priority | Reforms | Rationale |
|----------|---------|-----------|
| 1 | Transparency (data, open-file) | Foundation for all other reforms |
| 2 | Brady enforcement | Reduce wrongful convictions immediately |
| 3 | Conviction integrity units | Address past injustices |
| 4 | Charging guidelines | Reduce overcharging |
| 5 | External oversight | Sustainable accountability |
| 6 | Immunity reform | Long-term structural change |

### Implementation Levels

| Level | Key Actors | Primary Reforms |
|-------|------------|-----------------|
| Federal | Congress, DOJ | Funding conditions, guidelines |
| State | Legislatures, AGs | Mandatory reforms, oversight |
| Local | DAs, voters | Office policies, elections |
| Professional | Bar associations | Ethics enforcement |

## Risk Management

### Political Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Backlash elections | Defend, data, communications |
| State preemption | Legal challenges, federal floor |
| Crime wave narrative | Counter-messaging, research |
| Coalition fracture | Inclusive decision-making |

### Implementation Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Internal resistance | Training, personnel changes |
| Resource constraints | Phased implementation |
| Data gaming | Independent verification |
| Incomplete adoption | Enforcement mechanisms |
