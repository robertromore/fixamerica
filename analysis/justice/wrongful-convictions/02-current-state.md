| Race | Exonerations | U.S. Population |

### Disparities by Crime Type

**Murder**:

- Black exonerees: 50%
- Black murder prisoners: 41%
- Over-representation in wrongful convictions

**Sexual Assault**:

- Black exonerees: 59%
- Cross-racial misidentification significant
- Eyewitness issues more prevalent

**Drug Cases**:

- Black exonerees: 68%
- "Group exonerations" from police corruption
- Fabricated evidence common

## Compensation

### State Compensation Laws

| Status | States |
|--------|--------|
| Compensation statute | 37 + DC |
| No compensation law | 13 |
| Adequate compensation | ~10 |

### Compensation Amounts

| State | Per Year Compensation |
|-------|----------------------|
| Texas | $80,000/year + benefits |
| Florida | $50,000/year (capped) |
| Massachusetts | $500,000 lifetime cap |
| California | $140/day (~$51,000/year) |
| No statute states | Lawsuit only (usually fails) |

### Barriers to Compensation

- False confession may bar compensation
- Pleading guilty may bar compensation
- "Contributed to conviction" exceptions
- Time limits on filing
- Inadequate amounts

## Forensic Science

### Discredited Techniques

| Technique | Status | Exonerations Linked |
|-----------|--------|---------------------|
| Bite mark analysis | Discredited | 30+ |
| Microscopic hair analysis | Limited validity | 35+ |
| Fire investigation (old methods) | Largely discredited | 25+ |
| Blood spatter (some applications) | Overstated | 15+ |
| Bullet lead analysis | Abandoned by FBI | 5+ |

### Current Issues

- Crime lab scandals continue
- Accreditation not universal
- Analyst testimony exceeds science
- Defense access to experts limited
- No federal oversight

## Remaining Challenges

- Thousands of potential innocence cases
- Limited resources for investigation
- Procedural barriers to relief
- No compensation in many states
- Forensic science still unreliable
- Eyewitness reform incomplete
- Interrogation recording not universal
- Prosecutorial accountability lacking
