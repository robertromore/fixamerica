# Wrongful Convictions: Actions

## For Individuals

### Educate Yourself

**Learn About the Issue**:

- Read exoneree stories
- Watch documentaries
- Follow innocence organizations
- Understand causes
- Know the data

**Key Resources**:

| Resource | Format |
|----------|--------|
| National Registry of Exonerations | Database |

### Support the Wrongfully Convicted

**Direct Support**:

| Action | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Donate to innocence projects | Fund investigations |
| Volunteer with reentry programs | Help exonerees |
| Advocate for compensation | Policy change |
| Share exoneree stories | Raise awareness |

**Letter Writing**:

- Write to incarcerated innocence claimants
- Support exonerees in transition
- Provide human connection

### Jury Service

**Take It Seriously**:

| Duty | Importance |
|------|------------|
| Question eyewitness testimony | Memory is fallible |
| Scrutinize confessions | False confessions happen |
| Evaluate forensics critically | Science may be flawed |
| Hold prosecution to burden | Beyond reasonable doubt |
| Presume innocence | Constitutional right |

### Advocate for Reform

**Policy Advocacy**:

- Contact legislators about reform bills
- Support compensation legislation
- Advocate for recording requirements
- Push for eyewitness reform
- Demand forensic science reform
