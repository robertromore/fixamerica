# Death Penalty Reform and Abolition: Overview

The death penalty in the United States represents one of the most consequential and contested areas of criminal justice policy. Despite a decades-long decline in executions and death sentences, the U.S. remains the only Western democracy to retain capital punishment, standing alongside nations with troubling human rights records. This subtopic examines the current state of capital punishment, its deep-seated problems, and pathways toward meaningful reform or abolition.
