# Surveillance Technology in Criminal Justice: Action Guide

## Individual Actions

### Protect Yourself

| Action | Difficulty | Impact |
|--------|------------|--------|
| Use encrypted messaging (Signal) | Easy | High for personal privacy |
| Enable two-factor authentication | Easy | High for account security |
| Review app permissions | Easy | Medium |
| Use privacy-focused browsers | Easy | Medium |
| Limit social media exposure | Medium | Medium |
| Use VPN services | Medium | Medium |
| Opt out of facial recognition where possible | Medium | Low-Medium |
| Avoid Ring doorbells | Easy | Low |
| Request your data from police databases | Medium | Low |

### Educate Yourself

| Action | Time Investment | Resources |
|--------|-----------------|-----------|
| Read EFF's Surveillance Self-Defense | 2-3 hours | ssd.eff.org |
| Follow surveillance technology news | Ongoing | The Intercept, Motherboard, The Markup |
| Take a digital security training | 2-4 hours | EFF, Access Now workshops |
| Learn about local surveillance | 2-3 hours | Atlas of Surveillance, local news |
| Read key reports | 5-10 hours | See Resources document |
| Understand your legal rights | 2-3 hours | ACLU Know Your Rights |

## Quick Reference: Actions by Time Available

### 5 Minutes

- Sign a petition
- Share an article on social media
- Email an elected official
- Donate to an advocacy organization

### 1 Hour

- Write a letter to the editor
- Attend a virtual public hearing
- Complete a digital security checkup
- Read a key report

### Half Day

- Attend an in-person hearing and testify
- Meet with an elected official
- Participate in a community training
