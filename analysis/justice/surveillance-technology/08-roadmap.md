# Surveillance Technology in Criminal Justice: Implementation Roadmap

## Introduction

Reforming surveillance technology in criminal justice requires strategic, phased implementation that builds power, creates precedents, and addresses the most harmful technologies first. This roadmap provides a timeline for advocates, policymakers, and communities working toward comprehensive surveillance reform.

## Risk Assessment and Mitigation

### Political Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|------------|------------|
| Opposition mobilization | High | Build broad coalition early |
| Electoral changes | Medium | Engage multiple candidates |
| Preemption efforts | Medium | State-level advocacy |
| Federal hostility | Medium | Focus on state/local |

### Operational Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|------------|------------|
| Insufficient funding | Medium | Diversified fundraising |
| Staff turnover | Medium | Document processes, fair compensation |
| Coalition fragmentation | Medium | Regular communication, shared governance |
| Burnout | High | Sustainable pace, mutual support |

### Strategic Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|------------|------------|
| Technology changes | High | Flexible framework, principle-based |
| Co-optation | Medium | Clear principles, community leadership |
| Symbolic wins without substance | Medium | Strong implementation requirements |
| Backlash | Medium | Sustained organizing, rapid response |
