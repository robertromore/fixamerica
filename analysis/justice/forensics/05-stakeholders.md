# Forensic Science Reform: Stakeholders

## Who Is Involved and Their Stakes

Forensic science reform involves numerous stakeholders with varying interests, resources, and perspectives. Understanding these stakeholders is essential for building coalitions and anticipating obstacles to reform.

## Primary Stakeholders

### Criminal Defendants and the Wrongfully Convicted

| Characteristic | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| Interest | Accurate forensic evidence; protection from wrongful conviction |
| Stake | Liberty, reputation, life (in capital cases) |
| Resources | Generally limited; depends on representation |
| Influence | Low individually; moderate through advocacy organizations |
| Position | Strongly favor reform |

**Key Concerns:**
- Access to independent forensic testing
- Expert assistance for defense
- Disclosure of forensic limitations
- Remedies for past forensic errors

### Exonerees and Exoneree Organizations

| Characteristic | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| Interest | Accountability for wrongful convictions; preventing future injustices |
| Stake | Personal experience; moral authority |
| Resources | Limited financial; significant moral capital |
| Influence | High public impact; legislative testimony |
| Position | Strongly favor reform |

**Key Organizations:**
- Witness to Innocence
- Innocence Network
- Individual state innocence projects

### Prosecutors and District Attorneys

| Characteristic | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| Interest | Convicting guilty; avoiding wrongful convictions; case efficiency |
| Stake | Professional reputation; case outcomes; political career |
| Resources | Substantial; control evidence and laboratories |
| Influence | High; primary voice in criminal justice policy |
| Position | Mixed; reform threatens some practices but protects against reversals |

**Internal Divisions:**
- Progressive prosecutors increasingly support some reforms
- Traditional prosecutors often resist systemic changes
- Elected DAs face political pressures around "tough on crime"

### Defense Attorneys

| Characteristic | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| Interest | Effective representation; challenging unreliable evidence |
| Stake | Client outcomes; professional responsibility |
| Resources | Public defenders severely under-resourced; private counsel varies |
| Influence | Moderate; voice in bar associations and courts |
| Position | Generally favor reform |

**Key Organizations:**
- National Association for Public Defense
- National Legal Aid & Defender Association
- State public defender associations
- Criminal defense bar associations

### Forensic Scientists and Examiners

| Characteristic | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| Interest | Professional standing; methodological validity; employment security |
| Stake | Career; professional identity; credibility |
| Resources | Moderate through professional associations |
| Influence | Significant in standard-setting; expert testimony |
| Position | Divided; some advocate reform, others resist |

**Internal Divisions:**
- Academic forensic scientists often support rigorous validation
- Practicing examiners may resist changes that question their work
- Newer practitioners may be more open to reform

### Law Enforcement Agencies

| Characteristic | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| Interest | Effective investigation; case closure; officer safety |
| Stake | Organizational resources; case outcomes; public trust |
| Resources | Substantial; control crime laboratories |
| Influence | High; politically powerful |
| Position | Generally resist reforms that limit tools or create oversight |

**Key Organizations:**
- International Association of Chiefs of Police
- National Sheriffs' Association
- Fraternal Order of Police
- State and local police associations

### Judges

| Characteristic | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| Interest | Accurate fact-finding; efficient case processing; following precedent |
| Stake | Quality of justice; professional reputation; managing dockets |
| Resources | Judicial power; lifetime tenure (federal) |
| Influence | Control admissibility decisions |
| Position | Varies widely; often cautious about changing practice |

**Key Organizations:**
- American Bar Association (Judicial Division)
- National Center for State Courts
- State judicial conferences

### Crime Victims and Victim Advocates

| Characteristic | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| Interest | Accurate identification of perpetrators; closure; safety |
| Stake | Emotional investment; sense of justice |
| Resources | Moderate through advocacy organizations |
| Influence | Significant in legislative settings |
| Position | Complex; accuracy serves victims, but reform can feel threatening |

**Key Organizations:**
- National Organization for Victim Assistance
- Parents of Murdered Children
- State victim advocacy groups

## Secondary Stakeholders

### Academic Scientists and Researchers

| Characteristic | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| Interest | Scientific integrity; research opportunities; policy influence |
| Stake | Professional reputation; research funding |
| Resources | Research expertise; institutional affiliation |
| Influence | Moderate; provides scientific legitimacy to reform |
| Position | Generally favor reform based on scientific principles |

**Key Organizations:**
- National Academy of Sciences
- American Academy of Arts and Sciences
- American Association for the Advancement of Science
- University forensic science programs

### Professional Forensic Organizations

| Characteristic | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| Interest | Professional standards; member interests; credibility |
| Stake | Organizational survival; member certification value |
| Resources | Moderate; control certification and training |
| Influence | Significant in setting standards |
| Position | Varies; some embrace reform, others resist |

**Key Organizations:**

| Organization | Primary Focus | Reform Position |
|--------------|---------------|-----------------|
| American Academy of Forensic Sciences | Multidisciplinary | Moderate support |
| Association of Firearm and Tool Mark Examiners | Pattern evidence | Generally resistant |
| International Association for Identification | Fingerprints | Mixed |
| American Board of Forensic Odontology | Bite marks | Defensive |
| National Association of Medical Examiners | Death investigation | Generally supportive |
| OSAC (NIST) | Standards development | Supportive |

### Accreditation Bodies

| Characteristic | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| Interest | Quality assurance; organizational relevance |
| Stake | Credibility; revenue from accreditation |
| Resources | Moderate; standards authority |
| Influence | Significant in laboratory operations |
| Position | Generally supportive of reform within their authority |

**Key Organizations:**
- ANAB (ANSI National Accreditation Board)
- A2LA (American Association for Laboratory Accreditation)
- FQS (Forensic Quality Services)

| Characteristic | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| Interest | Criminal justice reform; evidence-based policy |
| Stake | Policy influence; organizational mission |
| Resources | Varies; research capacity |
| Influence | Moderate; shapes public discourse |
| Position | Generally supportive |

**Key Organizations:**

| Organization | Focus | Approach |
|--------------|-------|----------|
| Innocence Project | Wrongful convictions | Litigation and advocacy |
| The Justice Collaborative | Progressive reform | Policy development |
| RAND Corporation | Research | Evidence-based analysis |
| Brennan Center for Justice | Justice reform | Research and advocacy |
| Cato Institute | Limited government | Individual liberty focus |

### Media

| Characteristic | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| Interest | Compelling stories; public interest |
| Stake | Readership/viewership; credibility |
| Resources | Platform; investigative capacity |
| Influence | High for public awareness |
| Position | Varies; often attracted to wrongful conviction narratives |

### Legislators and Policymakers

| Characteristic | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| Interest | Effective policy; constituent satisfaction; re-election |
| Stake | Political capital; legacy |
| Resources | Legislative authority; appropriations |
| Influence | High; create legal framework |
| Position | Varies by ideology and constituency |

**Key Entities:**
- State legislative judiciary committees
- Congressional Judiciary Committees
- State governors
- State attorneys general

### Federal Agencies

| Characteristic | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| Interest | Public safety; agency mission; institutional credibility |
| Stake | Authority; budget; reputation |
| Resources | Substantial |
| Influence | High; sets national tone |
| Position | Mixed; administration-dependent |

**Key Agencies:**

| Agency | Role in Forensics | Reform Position |
|--------|-------------------|-----------------|
| DOJ/FBI | Major laboratory; training; standards | Historically resistant |
| NIST | Standards development (OSAC) | Generally supportive |
| NIJ | Research funding | Mission supports reform |
| NTSB | Investigation model | N/A (model for independence) |

## Stakeholder Positions on Key Reform Issues

| Stakeholder | Lab Independence | Error Rate Disclosure | Mandatory Accreditation | Defense Expert Access |
|-------------|------------------|----------------------|------------------------|----------------------|
| Defendants | Strongly Support | Strongly Support | Support | Strongly Support |
| Prosecutors | Mixed/Oppose | Mixed/Oppose | Support | Mixed/Oppose |
| Defense Attorneys | Strongly Support | Strongly Support | Support | Strongly Support |
| Law Enforcement | Oppose | Oppose | Mixed | Oppose |
| Judges | Mixed | Support | Support | Mixed |
| Forensic Scientists | Mixed | Mixed | Support | Mixed |
| Academics | Support | Strongly Support | Support | Support |
| Professional Orgs | Mixed | Mixed | Support | Mixed |

## Coalition Building Opportunities

### Potential Allies

| Issue | Potential Coalition |
|-------|---------------------|
| Laboratory accreditation | Prosecutors + Defense + Scientists + Accreditors |
| Research funding | Scientists + Academics + Reform organizations |
| Eyewitness reform (model) | All stakeholders (precedent for forensic reform) |
| DNA expansion | Prosecutors + Defense + Victims (common ground) |
| Testimony standards | Judges + Scientists + Academics |

### Bridging Stakeholder Interests

| Reform | Frame for Prosecution | Frame for Defense |
|--------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Error rate disclosure | Prevents reversals | Protects innocence |
| Lab independence | Increases credibility | Reduces bias |
| Blind testing | Strengthens findings | Ensures objectivity |
| Accreditation | Supports court admissibility | Quality assurance |
