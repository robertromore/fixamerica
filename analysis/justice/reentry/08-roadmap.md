# Reentry Reform: Roadmap

## Overview

This roadmap provides a phased implementation strategy for comprehensive reentry reform. The sequence prioritizes reforms that build momentum, create early wins, and establish foundations for more ambitious changes.

## Phase 1: Foundation Building (Years 1-2)

### Objective

Establish quick wins, build coalitions, and lay groundwork for larger reforms.

### Federal Actions

| Action | Mechanism | Timeline |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| Executive order on federal hiring | Presidential action | Month 1 |
| DOJ reentry grant priorities | Agency policy | Month 3 |
| HUD guidance on PHA policies | Agency guidance | Month 6 |
| Federal bonding program expansion | Administrative | Month 6 |
| Medicaid pre-release enrollment guidance | CMS policy | Month 12 |

### State Actions

| Action | Priority States | Timeline |
|--------|-----------------|----------|
| Ban the box (public employers) | States without | Months 1-12 |
| SNAP/TANF drug ban opt-out | 3 remaining full-ban states | Months 1-18 |
| Voting rights restoration (parole) | States with parole exclusion | Months 6-24 |
| Clean slate coalition building | All states | Ongoing |

### Local Actions

| Action | Priority Jurisdictions | Timeline |
|--------|----------------------|----------|
| Ban the box ordinances | Large cities without | Months 1-12 |
| PHA policy reform | High-population PHAs | Months 6-18 |
| Reentry council establishment | Major metros | Months 3-18 |
| Fair chance housing pilots | Willing jurisdictions | Months 12-24 |

### Coalition Building

| Activity | Target | Timeline |
|----------|--------|----------|
| Business coalition formation | Fortune 500, local chambers | Months 1-12 |
| Bipartisan legislative caucuses | Congress, state legislatures | Months 1-12 |
| Faith community engagement | Major denominations | Months 3-18 |
| Victim/survivor alliance building | Reform-supportive groups | Months 6-24 |

### Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| States with ban the box (public) | 40+ |
| States with SNAP opt-out | 30+ |
| PHAs with reformed policies | 50+ |
| Business coalition members | 100+ |

## Phase 2: State Momentum (Years 2-4)

### Objective

Achieve comprehensive reform in leading states; expand private-sector coverage; begin federal legislative push.

### State Legislative Priorities

| Priority | Target States | Timeline |
|----------|---------------|----------|
| Clean slate/automatic expungement | 20+ states | Years 2-4 |
| Ban the box (private employers) | 20+ states | Years 2-4 |
| Occupational licensing reform | 30+ states | Years 2-4 |
| Voting rights (automatic restoration) | 35+ states | Years 2-4 |
| Supervision reform (earned discharge) | 25+ states | Years 2-4 |

### Model State Package

Comprehensive state reform including:

```text
1. Ban the box (public + private employers)
2. Occupational licensing reform (individualized review)
3. Clean slate (automatic sealing after waiting period)
4. Voting rights (automatic restoration upon release)
5. SNAP/TANF full opt-out
6. Earned discharge from supervision
7. Technical violation reform (no incarceration)
8. Certificate of rehabilitation program
9. Medicaid continuity (suspend, not terminate)
10. Fair chance housing (public + private)
```

### Metrics

## Phase 3: Federal Framework (Years 4-7)

### Objective

Establish federal floor for reentry protections; achieve near-universal state reform on core issues.

### Federal Legislative Package

| Bill | Content | Priority |
|------|---------|----------|
| Comprehensive Reentry Act | Omnibus reform | High |
| Federal Clean Slate | Automatic federal record clearing | High |
| RESTORE Act (voting) | Federal voting rights restoration | High |
| Fair Chance Licensing | Occupational licensing standards | Medium |
| Supervision Reform Act | Reduce technical violations | Medium |

### Constitutional Considerations

| Issue | Approach |
|-------|----------|
| Voting rights (state elections) | Spending conditions; VRA expansion |
| Employment discrimination | Civil rights framework |
| Benefits restrictions | Direct federal law change |
| Federal-state coordination | Conditional funding |

### State Completion

| Reform | Target States | Timeline |
|--------|---------------|----------|
| Clean slate | 40+ states | Years 4-7 |
| Comprehensive licensing reform | 45+ states | Years 4-7 |
| Full voting rights restoration | 45+ states | Years 4-7 |
| Comprehensive supervision reform | 40+ states | Years 4-7 |

### Implementation Infrastructure

| Component | Purpose | Timeline |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| National reentry data system | Track outcomes | Year 4-5 |
| Technical assistance center | Support state/local | Year 4 |
| Research consortium | Evidence development | Year 4 |
| Workforce development integration | Connect to job systems | Year 5-7 |

### Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Comprehensive federal legislation | Passed |
| States meeting model standards | 40+ |
| Recidivism rate reduction | 10-15% |
| Employment rate (post-release) | 50%+ |
| Voting restoration (automatic) | 95%+ of states |

## Phase 4: Full Integration (Years 7-10)

### Objective

Normalize successful reentry as expected outcome; eliminate remaining barriers; institutionalize support systems.

### Remaining Reforms

| Area | Target | Timeline |
|------|--------|----------|
| Universal automatic voting rights | Constitutional amendment or legislation | Year 7-10 |
| Eliminate all categorical barriers | Federal and state | Year 7-10 |
| Healthcare integration | Full Medicaid continuity | Year 7-8 |
| Housing as right | Expanded fair chance housing | Year 8-10 |

### System Transformation

| Component | Description | Timeline |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| Probation/parole transformation | Support-focused supervision | Year 7-10 |
| Employer culture change | Fair chance as norm | Ongoing |
| Public opinion shift | Second chances accepted | Ongoing |
| Incarceration reduction | Address root causes | Long-term |

### Institutionalization

| Element | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| Automatic record clearing (universal) | No petition needed |
| Integrated workforce development | Criminal record not barrier |
| Healthcare continuity | No gap at release |
| Housing First | Immediate stable housing |
| Benefits access | Immediate eligibility |

### Metrics

| Metric | Long-term Target |
|--------|------------------|
| 5-year recidivism rate | Below 40% |
| Post-release employment | 70%+ |
| Homelessness at release | Below 5% |
| Voting rate (eligible) | Match general population |
| Technical violation incarceration | Eliminated |

## Critical Path Dependencies

```text
Year 1-2: Coalition Building + Quick Wins
    ↓
Year 2-4: State Momentum + Model Legislation
    ↓
Year 4-7: Federal Framework + Comprehensive State Reform
    ↓
Year 7-10: Full Integration + System Transformation
```

### Key Dependencies

| Milestone | Depends On |
|-----------|------------|
| State legislation wave | Coalition strength, political will |
| Federal legislation | State momentum, bipartisan support |
| Implementation success | Funding, administrative capacity |
| Culture change | Successful reentry examples, advocacy |
