# Actions: What Individuals, Communities, and Organizations Can Do

## Individual Actions

### Educate Yourself

| Action | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Learn about mental health | Understand symptoms, treatments, recovery |
| Take Mental Health First Aid | 8-hour public education course |
| Understand the local system | Know what happens when 911 is called |
| Learn about alternatives | CAHOOTS, 988, diversion programs |
| Follow news and research | Stay informed on developments |

### Support Those Affected

| Action | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Challenge stigma | Speak openly; correct misinformation |
| Support people in crisis | Listen; don't call police unless necessary |
| Help navigate systems | Accompany to appointments; advocate |
| Share 988 | Promote crisis line as alternative to 911 |
| Connect to peer support | Help find peer specialists, support groups |

### Civic Engagement

| Action | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Contact elected officials | City council; county commission; state legislators |
| Attend public meetings | Police oversight; city council; mental health board |
| Vote for reform candidates | Research positions on mental health crisis response |
| Submit public comments | Budget hearings; policy proposals |
| Speak at public hearings | Share personal experience or advocate for reform |

### Advocacy and Organizing

| Action | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Join advocacy organizations | NAMI; Mental Health America; reform groups |
| Share your story | Testimony is powerful; personal narratives matter |
| Build relationships | Connect with others affected; build coalition |
| Organize in your community | Neighborhood groups; faith communities; workplaces |
| Use social media | Raise awareness; share information; connect |

## Family Actions

### Prepare for Crisis

| Action | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Create crisis plan | What to do; who to call; preferences |
| Know 988 | Call 988 instead of 911 when possible |
| Identify alternative responders | Mobile crisis teams; peer support; trusted individuals |
| Designate healthcare agent | Legal authority for treatment decisions |
| Complete psychiatric advance directive | Document treatment preferences |

### During Crisis

| Action | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Call 988 first | If not immediately dangerous |
| Request CIT officer | If police must respond |
| Meet police outside | Explain situation before entry |
| Provide information | Diagnosis, medications, what works |
| Stay calm | Anxiety escalates situations |
| Document everything | Record encounters if possible |

### After Crisis

| Action | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Follow up on care | Ensure connection to services |
| File complaints if needed | Police misconduct; jail conditions |
| Seek support | Family support groups; therapy |
| Review and update crisis plan | Learn from experience |
| Advocate for better systems | Use experience to push for change |

### Advocacy as Families

| Action | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Join NAMI | Family support and advocacy |
| Share your story | Powerful testimony for change |
| Meet with officials | Bring lived experience to policy discussions |
| Support research | Participate in studies; donate to research |
| Mentor other families | Help those newly navigating system |

## Community Actions

### Local Advocacy

| Action | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Form community coalition | Bring together diverse stakeholders |
| Map local system | Understand current crisis response |
| Identify gaps | What's missing? What's not working? |
| Research alternatives | What works elsewhere? |
| Develop proposal | What should your community do? |
| Advocate to officials | City council; county commission; police chief |

### Engage with 911 and Police

| Action | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Request data | How many mental health calls? Outcomes? |
| Attend police commission meetings | Public input on policies |
| Advocate for CIT training | Push for comprehensive training |
| Advocate for alternative response | CAHOOTS-style programs |
| Participate in 911 oversight | If community board exists |
| Push for 911 reform | Call screening; alternative dispatch |

### Build Community-Based Alternatives

| Action | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Identify trusted responders | Peer specialists; community health workers |
| Create phone trees | Community members who can respond to crisis |
| Develop community response protocols | What neighbors can do |
| Partner with local organizations | Churches; community centers; clinics |
| Explore non-police response models | Community-led alternatives |

### Faith Communities

| Action | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Host Mental Health First Aid | Train congregation |
| Create mental health ministry | Support for members with mental illness |
| Partner with mental health organizations | Referral relationships |
| Offer space | Host support groups; crisis services |
| Advocate publicly | Moral voice for reform |
| Budget for mental health | Counseling access; emergency assistance |

### Schools

| Action | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Expand school counseling | More counselors; earlier intervention |
| Reduce police in schools | Limit SRO role in mental health |

## Organizational Actions

### Healthcare Organizations

| Action | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Develop crisis services | Mobile teams; stabilization; urgent care |
| Partner with 911 | Accept calls; provide consultation |
| Partner with law enforcement | Co-responder programs; training |
| Create drop-off protocols | Accept police/crisis team transports |
| Integrate behavioral health | Primary care integration |
| Address social determinants | Housing; employment; support services |

### Mental Health Providers

| Action | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Offer crisis services | After-hours availability; walk-in access |
| Train staff in crisis response | De-escalation; trauma-informed care |
| Partner with police | CIT training; consultation |
| Hire peer specialists | Lived experience staff |
| Advocate for system change | Professional voice for reform |
| Reduce barriers to care | Same-day access; reduced waitlists |

### Law Enforcement Agencies

| Action | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Implement comprehensive CIT | All officers; not just volunteers |
| Partner with mental health | Co-responder programs; drop-off agreements |
| Support alternative response | Collaborate with civilian teams |
| Change culture | Mental health calls are health calls, not crimes |
| Track and report data | Mental health call outcomes; use of force |
| Reform policies | De-escalation; duty to intervene; time and distance |

### Hospitals

| Action | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Reduce ER boarding | Create crisis alternatives |
| Accept police/crisis drop-offs | Streamline process |
| Partner on crisis services | Fund community alternatives |
| Train staff | Trauma-informed; de-escalation |
| Advocate for beds | Push for psychiatric capacity |

### Employers

| Action | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Provide mental health benefits | Comprehensive EAP; treatment coverage |
| Train managers | Mental health recognition; accommodation |
| Create supportive culture | Reduce stigma; support treatment |
| Hire people with mental health histories | Reduce discrimination |
| Support employees in crisis | Flexible leave; job protection |

### Foundations and Philanthropies

| Action | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Fund alternative response pilots | Seed money for new programs |
| Support advocacy organizations | Build capacity for reform |
| Fund research and evaluation | Evidence base for alternatives |
| Support workforce development | Training programs; scholarships |
| Convene stakeholders | Bring together diverse parties |

## Messaging and Framing

### Effective Messages

| Message | Audience |
|---------|----------|
| "Right response for the right call" | General public |
| "Mental health calls should get mental health response" | Policymakers |
| "Officers want to focus on crime, not mental health" | Police-friendly audiences |
| "Safer for everyone—person in crisis, family, responders" | Safety-focused audiences |
| "Cost-effective—treatment cheaper than jail" | Fiscal conservatives |
| "People with mental illness deserve help, not handcuffs" | Progressive audiences |

### Stories That Work

| Story Type | Impact |
|------------|--------|
| Personal recovery | Shows treatment works |
| Family experience | Humanizes impact of current system |
| Successful alternatives | Proves change is possible |
| Preventable tragedies | Creates urgency |
| Economic impact | Resonates with fiscal audiences |
