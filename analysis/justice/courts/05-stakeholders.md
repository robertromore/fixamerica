# Courts Reform: Stakeholders

## Those Harmed by Current System

### Indigent Defendants

**Population**: Vast majority of criminal defendants

**Harms**:

| Harm | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Inadequate representation | Public defenders with no time |
| Pretrial detention | Can't afford bail |
| Coerced pleas | Pressure to plead regardless of guilt |
| Worse outcomes | Longer sentences than those with resources |

**Voice**: Limited; can't organize while incarcerated; disenfranchised

### Pretrial Detainees

**Population**: ~470,000 at any time

**Specific Harms**:

| Harm | Frequency |
|------|-----------|
| Job loss | Common (often within days) |
| Housing loss | Frequent |
| Family separation | Universal |
| Violence exposure | Significant |
| Mental health decline | Common |
| Inability to fight case | Structural |

**Advocacy**: Growing movement; bail funds; policy advocates

### Families of Defendants

**Impacts**:

| Impact | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| Bail payment | Financial devastation |
| Lost income | If detained, family loses earner |
| Childcare disruption | Parent in jail |
| Stigma | Family stigmatized |
| Navigation burden | Must navigate complex system |

### Wrongfully Convicted

**Numbers**: Unknown; estimates of 2-8% of guilty pleas by innocent people

**Specific Harms**:

- Conviction of innocent person
- Often from inadequate defense
- Plea pressure on innocent
- Limited post-conviction relief

### Communities

**Concentrated Impact**:

- Court involvement concentrated in poor communities
- Removal of community members through detention
- Criminal records from coerced pleas
- Fines and fees extraction
- Distrust of legal system

## Public Defenders and Legal Aid

### Public Defender Offices

**Role**: Represent indigent defendants

**Challenges**:

| Challenge | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| Overwhelming caseloads | Can't provide adequate defense |
| Low pay | Recruitment and retention |
| Limited resources | No investigators, experts |
| Burnout | High turnover |
| Ethical dilemmas | Can't meet obligations |

**Advocacy**: Growing voice for reform; caseload caps; funding

### Private Assigned Counsel

**Role**: Private attorneys appointed to cases

**Problems**:

| Problem | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| Low pay rates | Inadequate time per case |
| No supervision | Quality varies wildly |
| No support | No investigators, paralegals |
| Perverse incentives | Quick pleas = more cases |

### Legal Aid Organizations

**Role**: Civil legal services for poor

**Connection to Criminal**:

- Housing cases (eviction from criminal record)
- Family cases (custody, protective orders)
- Benefits cases
- Reentry assistance

### Defense Bar Associations

| Organization | Focus |
|--------------|-------|
| National Association for Public Defense | Public defender advocacy |
| NACDL | Criminal defense lawyers |
| State defender organizations | State-level advocacy |

## Judges

### Trial Court Judges

**Role**: Preside over criminal cases; set bail; accept pleas; sentence

**Perspectives**:

| View | Subset |
|------|--------|
| Reform-minded | Support bail reform, defense funding |
| Case management focus | Efficiency over thoroughness |
| Tough-on-crime | Harsh bail, sentences |
| Deferential to prosecution | Prosecutor-friendly |

**Constraints**:

- Caseload pressure
- Election pressure (some jurisdictions)
- Appeal risk
- Culture and norms

### Appellate Judges

**Role**: Review trial court decisions; set precedent

**Relevant Decisions**:

- Ineffective assistance claims (rare reversals)
- Bail challenges
- Plea validity
- Sentencing appeals

### Judicial Organizations

| Organization | Orientation |
|--------------|-------------|
| American Bar Association | Generally reform-supportive |
| Conference of Chief Justices | Varies |
| State judicial conferences | Varies by state |

## Prosecution

### District Attorneys

**Role**: Adversary in court system; enormous power

**Interests**:

| Interest | Effect on Courts |
|----------|------------------|
| Conviction rates | Pressure for pleas |
| Resource advantage | Imbalance with defense |
| Political position | Tough-on-crime pressure |
| Discretion | Charging drives outcomes |

### Prosecutors' Associations

| Organization | Orientation |
|--------------|-------------|
| NDAA | Generally opposes bail reform |
| State associations | Often oppose defense funding equity |

**Relationship to Courts**: Prosecutors benefit from current imbalances; generally oppose reforms that level playing field.

## Bail Industry

### Commercial Bail Bond Companies

**Role**: Post bail for defendants who pay 10% fee

**Business Model**:

- Non-refundable 10% fee
- Collateral for full amount
- $2+ billion industry
- Profit from poverty

### Industry Organizations

**American Bail Coalition**:

- Lobbying arm of industry
- Opposes bail reform
- Funds opposition campaigns
- ALEC involvement

## Government Actors

### State Legislatures

**Role**: Fund public defense; set bail law; court structure

**Key Decisions**:

- Funding allocation
- Bail reform legislation
- Indigent defense structure
- Court fees and fines

### State Courts Administration

**Role**: Court management; rulemaking

**Relevant Actions**:

- Pretrial release rules
- Caseload standards
- Technology implementation
- Access initiatives

### Federal Courts

**Role**: Constitutional interpretation; pattern for states

**Relevant Actions**:

- Sixth Amendment interpretation
- Eighth Amendment (bail)
- Due process standards
- Federal defender funding
