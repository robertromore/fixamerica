# Courts Reform: Current State

## Public Defense

### Caseloads

**Typical Caseloads**:

| Jurisdiction Type | Actual | Recommended |
|-------------------|--------|-------------|
| High-volume urban | 500-1,000+ | 150 (felonies) |
| Moderate caseload | 300-500 | 400 (misdemeanors) |
| Better-resourced | 150-300 | Standards vary |

**Time Per Case**:

| Standard | Hours | Reality |
|----------|-------|---------|
| ABA standard (felony) | 40+ hours | Often 5-10 |
| Complex felony | 100+ hours | Often 20-30 |
| Misdemeanor | 10 hours | Often <1 hour |

### Funding

**Expenditure Comparison**:

| Category | Spending |
|----------|----------|
| Public defense per case (average) | $200-300 |
| Private attorney (similar case) | $10,000+ |
| Prosecution per case (average) | $500-800 |

**State Funding**:

| Funding Model | States |
|---------------|--------|
| Full state funding | ~10 states |
| Mixed state/local | ~20 states |
| Primarily local | ~20 states |
| No state funding | 21 states |

### Quality Indicators

**Strickland Standard** (*Strickland v. Washington*, 1984):

- "Reasonably effective assistance"
- Highly deferential to attorney
- Rarely found deficient
- Results in almost no remedy for poor representation

**Quality Problems**:

| Issue | Prevalence |
|-------|------------|
| Minimal investigation | Common |
| Brief client meetings | Pervasive |
| Late case review | Common |
| Failure to file motions | Significant |
| Pressure to plead | Widespread |

### Structure

**Delivery Systems**:

| System | Description | States |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| Public defender office | Staff attorneys | Most populous states |
| Assigned counsel | Private bar appointments | Many rural areas |
| Contract system | Private attorney contracts | Some states |
| Mixed system | Combination | Many states |

## Bail and Pretrial Detention

### Current Bail System

**Types of Release**:

| Type | Description | Frequency |
|------|-------------|-----------|
| Cash bail | Pay full amount | Common |
| Commercial bond | Pay 10% to bondsman | Very common |
| Release on recognizance | No payment | Varies widely |
| Supervised release | Conditions, no payment | Growing |
| Pretrial detention | No release | Common |

**Bail Amounts**:

| Offense Type | Median Bail |
|--------------|-------------|
| Felony | $10,000 |
| Violent felony | $35,000+ |
| Misdemeanor | $2,000-5,000 |

### Pretrial Detention Population

**Scale**: ~470,000 people in local jails pretrial

**Characteristics**:

| Characteristic | Percentage |
|----------------|------------|
| Not convicted | 74% of jail population |
| Unable to post bail | Majority |
| Average stay | 26 days |
| Mental health issues | 64% |
| Substance use disorders | 70%+ |

### Consequences of Pretrial Detention

**On Case Outcomes**:

| Outcome | Detained | Released |
|---------|----------|----------|
| Pleads guilty | Higher rate | Lower rate |
| Convicted | Higher rate | Lower rate |
| Sentenced to incarceration | Higher rate | Lower rate |
| Sentence length | Longer | Shorter |

**Collateral Consequences**:

- Job loss (within days)
- Housing loss
- Child custody issues
- Physical and sexual violence
- Mental health deterioration
- Inability to prepare defense

### Bail Reform Efforts

**States with Significant Reform**:

| State | Reform | Outcome |
|-------|--------|---------|
| New Jersey | Near-elimination of cash bail | 44% jail reduction |
| California | Prop 25 (failed); local reforms | Mixed |
| New York | Bail reform (partially rolled back) | Initial reduction, then controversy |
| Illinois | SAFE-T Act | Implemented 2023 |

**Risk Assessment Tools**:

- Used in most jurisdictions now
- Controversy over racial bias
- Validation concerns
- Not replacing bail, often adding to it

## Plea Bargaining

### Prevalence

**Conviction Method**:

| Level | Guilty Plea | Trial |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| Federal | 97-98% | 2-3% |
| State | 94-97% | 3-6% |

**Trial Decline**:

| Decade | Federal Trial Rate |
|--------|-------------------|
| 1980 | ~15% |
| 2000 | ~5% |
| 2020 | ~2% |

### Drivers of Pleas

**Pressure Points**:

| Pressure | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| Pretrial detention | Plead to get out |
| Trial penalty | Much longer sentence if lose at trial |
| Mandatory minimums | Risk of harsh sentence |
| Charge stacking | Multiple charges increase pressure |
| Weak defense | Can't adequately fight |

**Trial Penalty**:

| Federal Offense | Plea Sentence | Trial Sentence | Penalty |
|-----------------|---------------|----------------|---------|
| Drug trafficking | 5 years | 10+ years | 2x+ |
| Fraud | 2 years | 5 years | 2.5x |

### Concerns

**Innocence**:

- Estimated 2-8% of guilty pleas by innocent people
- Pressure overwhelms innocence
- Exonerations show plea failures

**Due Process**:

- Limited judicial oversight
- No discovery until after plea
- Waivers of rights without understanding
- Coercion not recognized

## Court Access

### Fees and Costs

**Types of Fees**:

| Fee Type | Range |
|----------|-------|
| Filing fees | $100-500+ |
| Jury fees | $50-200 |
| Transcript fees | $3-5 per page |
| Public defender fee | $50-500 |
| Electronic monitoring | $5-25/day |
| Drug testing | $10-30/test |

**Consequences of Non-Payment**:

- Bench warrants
- Driver's license suspension
- Additional fees
- Jail for non-payment
- Debt collection

### Language Access

**Limited English Proficiency**:

- 25+ million U.S. residents
- Interpreter availability varies
- Quality of interpretation varies
- Documents rarely translated

### Disability Access

**ADA Compliance**:

- Physical access improved but incomplete
- Cognitive accessibility often neglected
- Mental health accommodations inconsistent
- Sensory disabilities underserved

### Geographic Access

**Court Consolidation**:

- Rural areas lack nearby courts
- Transportation barriers
- Limited hours
- Remote hearings help but have limits
