# Courts Reform: Solutions

## Public Defense Reform

### Funding

**State Responsibility**:

- Full state funding (not local)
- Constitutionally mandated minimums
- Parity with prosecution
- Dedicated funding stream

**Funding Levels**:

| Standard | Target |
|----------|--------|
| Per-case spending | Match prosecution |
| Total defender budget | Equal to DA budget |
| Support services | Investigators, experts, paralegals |

### Caseload Limits

**Enforceable Standards**:

| Case Type | Maximum Annual Caseload |
|-----------|------------------------|
| Felonies | 150 |
| Misdemeanors | 400 |
| Juvenile | 200 |
| Appeals | 25 |

**Enforcement**:

- Courts refuse appointments beyond limits
- Workload studies required
- Consequences for exceeding limits

### Structure

**Independent Public Defender Offices**:

- Separate from courts and executive
- Chief defender appointed by independent commission
- Protected from political interference
- Professional management

**Institutional Defender Preferred**:

- Staff attorneys (not contract)
- Supervision and training
- Support services included
- Career development

### Quality Assurance

**Performance Standards**:

| Standard | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| Client contact | Prompt, regular communication |
| Investigation | Every case investigated |
| Motions practice | File appropriate motions |
| Trial readiness | Prepared to try every case |
| Client relationship | Respectful, responsive |

**Oversight**:

- Independent quality review
- Client feedback mechanisms
- Outcome tracking
- Continuing education requirements

## Bail Reform

### Eliminate Cash Bail

**Presumption of Release**:

- Release on personal recognizance default
- No money conditions
- Detention only for genuine risk
- Clear and convincing evidence for detention

**Risk-Based System**:

| Risk Level | Response |
|------------|----------|
| Low | Release, court reminder |
| Moderate | Supervised release, conditions |
| High | Enhanced supervision, possible detention hearing |

### Pretrial Services

**Components**:

| Service | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| Risk assessment | Identify risk level |
| Court reminders | Reduce failure to appear |
| Supervision | Monitoring where needed |
| Services connection | Treatment, support |

**Implementation**:

- Validated, bias-tested instruments
- Local validation required
- Human decision-making retained
- Regular review of detained defendants

### Speedy Trial

**For Detained Defendants**:

| Standard | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| Initial appearance | 24 hours |
| Detention hearing | 48 hours |
| Trial | 60-90 days |

**Consequences**:

- Release if deadlines not met
- No extensions without defendant consent
- Sanctions for delay

### Detention Standards

**Limited Grounds**:

- Serious violent offense AND
- Clear and convincing evidence of danger AND
- No conditions can mitigate risk

**Regular Review**:

- Every 14 days
- Defendant has counsel
- Burden on prosecution

## Discovery Reform

### Open File Discovery

**Prosecution Obligation**:

| Material | Timing |
|----------|--------|
| Police reports | Before arraignment |
| Witness statements | Before preliminary hearing |
| Expert reports | 30 days before trial |
| All exculpatory material | Ongoing duty |

**Brady Enforcement**:

- Sanctions for violations
- Personal consequences for prosecutors
- Conviction vacatur for violations
- Training and certification

### Early Disclosure

**Timeline**:

| Stage | Required Disclosure |
|-------|---------------------|
| Arrest | Basic evidence |
| Arraignment | Full police reports |
| Preliminary hearing | Witness statements |
| Pre-trial | Everything |

## Plea Bargaining Reform

### Judicial Oversight

**Court's Role**:

| Function | Standard |
|----------|----------|
| Voluntariness inquiry | Meaningful, not rote |
| Factual basis | Evidence supports plea |
| Understanding | Defendant knows consequences |
| Fairness | Plea not product of coercion |

### Trial Penalty Limits

**Reforms**:

- Limit sentence increase after trial
- Prohibit charge increases after rejection
- Document initial offers
- Transparency in plea process

### Pre-Plea Discovery

**Before Plea Offer**:

- Defendant has basic evidence
- Can make informed decision
- Not forced to choose blind

## Court Access

### Eliminate Financial Barriers

**Fees and Fines Reform**:

| Reform | Implementation |
|--------|----------------|
| Ability to pay | Required determination |
| No incarceration for non-payment | Prohibition |
| Payment plans | Affordable, reasonable |
| Fee waivers | For indigent defendants |

**Public Defender Fees**:

- Eliminate where possible
- Ability-to-pay determination
- Not charged until case over

### Language Access

**Interpretation**:

- Qualified interpreters for all proceedings
- Translated documents
- Remote interpretation available
- Quality standards

### Physical and Remote Access

**Accessibility**:

| Barrier | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Geographic | Remote hearings, satellite courts |
| Disability | ADA compliance, accommodations |
| Hours | Evening and weekend courts |
| Transportation | Virtual options, court at community locations |

## Judicial Selection Reform

### Merit Selection

**Components**:

| Element | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| Nominating commission | Screen candidates |
| Diverse composition | Avoid capture |
| Governor selection from list | Political accountability |
| Retention elections | Accountability without campaigns |

### Diversity Requirements

**Goals**:

| Category | Target |
|----------|--------|
| Racial diversity | Reflect population |
| Gender diversity | Parity |
| Professional diversity | Defense background, not just prosecution |
| Geographic diversity | Rural and urban |

### Accountability

**Performance Evaluation**:

- Regular evaluation
- Public reporting
- Consequence for poor performance
- Complaint process

### Campaign Finance Reform

**For Elected Judiciary**:

- Public financing
- Contribution limits
- Recusal requirements
- Disclosure

## What Works: Evidence

### Bail Reform

| Jurisdiction | Reform | Outcome |
|--------------|--------|---------|
| Washington D.C. | Non-money bail since 1990s | Works effectively |
| New Jersey | Near-elimination 2017 | 44% jail reduction |
| Kentucky | Pretrial services | High appearance rates |

### Public Defense Investment

**Studies Show**:

- Reduced wrongful convictions
- Reduced incarceration
- Reduced appeals
- Better outcomes for defendants

### Discovery Reform

| State | Reform | Outcome |
|-------|--------|---------|
| North Carolina | Open file discovery | Improved outcomes |
| Texas | Michael Morton Act | Better disclosure |
