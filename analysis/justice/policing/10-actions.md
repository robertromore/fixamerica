# Policing Reform: Actions

## For Individuals

### Educate Yourself

**Learn About Local Policing**:

- Who is your police chief? How are they appointed?
- What is your city's use of force policy?
- Does your city have civilian oversight? What powers does it have?
- What does your police union contract say?
- What does your city pay in misconduct settlements?

**Follow Policing News**:

- Local reporting on police
- National sources (Marshall Project, Mapping Police Violence)
- Your civilian oversight body reports
- City council police oversight discussions

### Know Your Rights

**During Police Encounters**:

| Right | Application |
|-------|-------------|
| Remain silent | You don't have to answer questions |
| Refuse consent to search | "I do not consent to a search" |
| Ask if free to leave | "Am I being detained?" |
| Document badge number | Request identification |
| Film police | Legal in public (keep safe distance) |

**After an Incident**:

- Write down everything immediately
- Take photos of injuries
- Get witness contact information
- File a complaint
- Contact an attorney if rights violated

### Witness Encounters

**Safe Witnessing**:

- Keep safe distance
- Record with phone
- Don't physically interfere
- State you are recording
- Offer to provide statement to person stopped

**Providing Information**:

- Give recording to person who was stopped
- Offer contact information
- Consider providing to oversight body
- Understand your safety

### File Complaints

**When to File**:

- Excessive force
- Discourtesy or rudeness
- Discriminatory treatment
- Policy violations
- Any misconduct

**How to File**:

- Civilian oversight body (if exists)
- Police department internal affairs
- City council member
- ACLU or local civil rights organization

### Vote and Participate

**Key Elections**:

| Office | Why It Matters |
|--------|----------------|
| Mayor | Appoints chief, sets priorities |
| City council | Budget, oversight authority |
| Sheriff | Jail, county policing |
| District attorney | Prosecution of officers |

**Beyond Voting**:

- Attend city council meetings
- Speak at public comment
- Contact elected officials
- Support reform candidates
