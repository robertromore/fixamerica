# Policing Reform: Solutions

## Use of Force Reform

### Standards

**From "Reasonable Officer" to "Necessary"**:

| Current Standard | Reformed Standard |
|------------------|-------------------|
| Reasonable officer would use force | Force necessary as last resort |
| Deference to officer perception | Objective necessity |
| Split-second justification | Consider actions leading to encounter |
| No duty to de-escalate | Affirmative duty to de-escalate |

**Key Elements**:

- Force only when necessary to prevent imminent harm
- Proportional to threat
- De-escalation required first
- Duty to intervene
- Comprehensive reporting

### Prohibited Techniques

| Technique | Reform |
|-----------|--------|
| Chokeholds | Ban completely |
| Neck restraints | Ban completely |
| No-knock warrants | Eliminate or strictly limit |
| Shooting at moving vehicles | Prohibit (unless vehicle is weapon) |
| Warning shots | Prohibit in most circumstances |

### De-Escalation

**Training Requirements**:

- Minimum hours in academy
- Annual refresher training
- Scenario-based practice
- Integration into all tactics training

**Policy Requirements**:

- De-escalation before force when feasible
- Time, distance, cover tactics
- Communication emphasis
- Slowing encounters down

### Force Continuum

**Graduated Response**:

1. Presence and communication
2. Verbal commands
3. Empty hand control
4. Intermediate weapons (OC spray, baton)
5. Less-lethal (TASER with restrictions)
6. Deadly force (only when necessary to prevent imminent death/serious injury)

### Reporting and Review

**Requirements**:

| Report Type | Trigger |
|-------------|---------|
| Force report | Any use of force |
| Supervisor review | All force incidents |
| Use of force review board | Serious force |
| Public data | Aggregate annual reporting |

## Accountability Mechanisms

### Qualified Immunity Reform

**Options**:

| Approach | Mechanism |
|----------|-----------|
| Federal legislation | Amend 42 U.S.C. ยง 1983 |
| State legislation | Eliminate for state law claims |
| Municipal policy | Indemnification limits |

**Model Language**:

- "Qualified immunity shall not be a defense"
- Eliminate "clearly established" requirement
- Entity liability for officer actions

### Civilian Oversight

**Effective Models**:

| Power | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Independent investigation | Own investigators, not police |
| Subpoena power | Compel testimony and documents |
| Binding discipline | Recommendations are binding |
| Policy review | Input on department policies |
| Sustained funding | Adequate resources |

**Structural Requirements**:

- Independent from police department
- Community-selected members
- Diverse representation
- Protected from retaliation

### Decertification

**National Database**:

- All decertifications reported
- All sustained misconduct reported
- Resignations under investigation tracked
- Mandatory checking before hiring

**Automatic Decertification Triggers**:

- Excessive force finding
- Sexual misconduct
- Perjury or falsification
- Failure to intervene
- Bias-based policing finding

### Body Cameras

**Effective Policies**:

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| When to record | All enforcement encounters |
| Who controls | Department, not individual officer |
| When released | Promptly for force incidents |
| Consequences | Discipline for non-recording |
| Storage | Adequate retention |

### Contract Reform

**Remove Barriers**:

| Provision to Eliminate | Replacement |
|------------------------|-------------|
| Waiting period before investigation | Immediate interview |
| Access to evidence before statement | Statement first |
| Anonymous complaint limits | Accept all complaints |
| Expungement of records | Permanent retention |
| Arbitration for discipline | Final discipline authority with chief |

## Training Reform

### Extended Academy

**Minimum Standards**:

| Component | Hours |
|-----------|-------|
| Total academy | 1,000+ hours (6+ months) |
| De-escalation | 80+ hours |
| Mental health | 40+ hours |
| Implicit bias | 24+ hours |
| Constitutional law | 40+ hours |
| Community relations | 40+ hours |
| Firearms | 40 hours (maintain proficiency) |

### Content Reform

**Shift Emphasis**:

| From | To |
|------|-----|
| Warrior mentality | Guardian mentality |
| Tactical focus | Communication focus |
| Fear-based | Trust-based |
| Force as tool | Force as last resort |
| "Survive the shift" | "Serve the community" |

### Ongoing Training

**Annual Requirements**:

| Topic | Hours |
|-------|-------|
| De-escalation | 16+ |
| Bias and cultural competency | 8+ |
| Mental health response | 8+ |
| Legal updates | 8+ |
| Wellness and resilience | 8+ |

### Field Training Reform

**Changes**:

- Screen field training officers for values
- Emphasize guardian approach
- Monitor for culture transmission
- Evaluate trainees on communication, not just control

## Structural Reform

### Scope Reduction

**Non-Police Responses**:

| Call Type | Alternative |
|-----------|-------------|
| Mental health crisis | Mental health responders |
| Homelessness | Social services |
| School discipline | Counselors, restorative practices |
| Traffic enforcement | Civilian enforcement (some cities) |
| Noise complaints | Non-police response |

**Examples**:

- CAHOOTS (Eugene, OR): Mental health response
- STAR (Denver): Crisis intervention
- NYC Mental Health Teams: Co-responder model

### Community Policing (Authentic)

**Elements**:

| Component | Implementation |
|-----------|----------------|
| Geographic assignment | Officers assigned to neighborhoods |
| Community engagement | Regular non-enforcement contact |
| Problem-oriented policing | Address root causes |
| Community input | Meaningful voice in priorities |
| Metrics reform | Measure community satisfaction |

### Department Culture

**Changing Metrics**:

| Old Metrics | New Metrics |
|-------------|-------------|
| Arrests | Community satisfaction |
| Stops | Complaints |
| Response time | Force incidents |
| Clearance rates | Repeat victimization |

### Hiring Reform

**Screening For**:

- Communication skills
- Emotional intelligence
- Cultural competency
- Bias indicators
- Problem-solving ability

**Diversity**:

- Recruit from policed communities
- Residency requirements or preferences
- Language skills
- Life experience

## What Works: Evidence

### De-Escalation Training

**Evidence**:

- Louisville: 28% reduction in use of force after training
- Las Vegas: Significant force reduction
- Seattle: Part of consent decree success

### Body Cameras

**Evidence**:

- Mixed results on force
- Strong evidence on complaints (reduce false complaints)
- Transparency value
- Effectiveness depends on policy

### Consent Decrees

**Evidence**:

| City | Results |
|------|---------|
| Los Angeles | Force reduction after consent decree |
| Seattle | Force reduction, bias reduction |
| New Orleans | Improvements in multiple areas |
| Newark | Significant improvements |

### Alternative Response

**Evidence**:

- CAHOOTS: Handles 17% of calls, minimal police backup needed
- Very few incidents result in force or injury
- Cost savings
