# Policing Reform: Stakeholders

## Those Harmed by Current System

### Communities of Color

**Disproportionate Impact**:

| Impact | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Fatal force | Black individuals 2.5-3x more likely to be killed |
| Stops | Stopped more frequently |
| Searches | Searched more frequently, lower hit rates |
| Arrests | Higher arrest rates for equivalent conduct |
| Force | More likely to have force used |

**Lived Experience**:

- "The Talk" with children about police encounters
- Fear during routine encounters
- Collective trauma from publicized killings
- Over-policing and under-protection simultaneously

**Organizing**: Movement for Black Lives, local organizations

### Victims of Police Violence

**Categories**:

| Type | Number |
|------|--------|
| Fatal | ~1,000/year |
| Seriously injured | Thousands/year |
| Minor force | Hundreds of thousands |
| Wrongful arrest | Millions affected |

**Aftermath**:

- Physical injury and disability
- Psychological trauma (PTSD)
- No compensation (qualified immunity)
- Limited recourse

**Advocacy**: Families of victims, victim advocacy organizations

### Families of Victims

**Ongoing Harm**:

- Grief and trauma
- No accountability or justice
- Re-traumatization through legal process
- Financial loss
- Forced advocacy to make death matter

**Organizations**:

- Mothers Against Police Brutality
- Mothers of the Movement
- Local victim family groups

### Over-Policed Communities

**Harm from Aggressive Enforcement**:

| Tactic | Community Impact |
|--------|------------------|
| Stop-and-frisk | Humiliation, fear, alienation |
| Arrests for minor offenses | Criminal records, job loss |
| Aggressive patrol | Community as occupied territory |
| Gang databases | Labeling, civil liberties |

**Dual Harm**: Both over-policed and under-protected

## Police Officers

### Rank-and-File Officers

**Challenges**:

| Issue | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Danger | Real risks (though often overstated) |
| Stress | Traumatic calls, high pressure |
| Inadequate training | Unprepared for many situations |
| Impossible scope | Expected to solve all problems |
| Vilification | Blamed for systemic problems |

**Spectrum of Views**:

- Some support reform
- Some resistant to any change
- Many conflicted
- Union positions don't represent all

### Reform-Minded Officers

**Existence**: Officers who support accountability, training improvement

**Challenges**:

- Blue wall of silence pressure
- Career consequences for speaking out
- Union opposition
- Limited paths to reform from within

### Chiefs and Administrators

**Position**:

- Often more reform-oriented than rank-and-file
- Constrained by unions, politics
- Accountable to mayors/councils
- Turnover limits sustained reform

## Police Unions and Associations

### Fraternal Order of Police (FOP)

**Size**: 360,000+ members

**Positions**:

| Issue | Position |
|-------|----------|
| Qualified immunity | Strongly defend |
| Use of force standards | Resist restrictions |
| Civilian oversight | Oppose meaningful oversight |
| Body cameras | Accept with officer control |
| Contract protections | Defend and expand |

### Police Benevolent Associations (PBAs)

**Similar Positions**:

- Defend officers in all circumstances
- Oppose accountability measures
- Political endorsements
- Lobbying against reform

### International Association of Chiefs of Police (IACP)

**More Moderate**:

- Represents leadership
- Some reform support
- Best practices promotion
- But defends profession broadly

### National Sheriffs' Association

**Focus**:

- Elected officials (sheriffs)
- Conservative orientation
- Opposition to jail reform
- Immigration enforcement support

## Government Actors

### Federal

**DOJ Civil Rights Division**:

- Pattern-or-practice authority
- Consent decrees
- Technical assistance
- Administration-dependent enforcement

**COPS Office**:

- Community policing grants
- Training support
- Technical assistance

### State

**Attorneys General**:

- Some states allow AG investigation of police killings
- Civil rights enforcement (varies)
- Pattern-or-practice (some states)

**POST Commissions**:

- Peace Officer Standards and Training
- Certification authority
- Training standards
- Decertification (underused)

### Local

**Mayors**:

- Appoint police chiefs
- Budget authority
- Political accountability
- Often constrained by unions

**City Councils**:

- Budget decisions
- Policy oversight
- Union contract approval
- Civilian oversight creation

**Civilian Oversight Bodies**:

- Where they exist, varied authority
- Often advisory only
- Struggle for resources and access

## Victims of Crime

### Diverse Perspectives

**Some Positions**:

- Desire for police protection
- Want responsive policing
- Fear crime
- May oppose aggressive tactics that harm communities

**Recognition**:

- Communities most victimized by crime often also over-policed
- Want effective policing, not abusive policing
- Safety and accountability not opposed

### Victim Advocacy Organizations

| Orientation | Groups |
|-------------|--------|
| Traditional | National Organization for Victim Assistance |
| Reform-oriented | Crime Survivors for Safety and Justice |
| Community-based | Various local organizations |

## Unusual Allies

### Conservative Reform Support

**Interests**:

| Group | Reason |
|-------|--------|
| Libertarians | Limited government, civil liberties |
| Fiscal conservatives | Lawsuit costs, efficiency |
| Religious conservatives | Human dignity, redemption |

### Business Community

**Interests**:

- Stability and predictability
- Lawsuit liability concerns
- Workforce development (second chances)
- Community relations

### Veterans

**Perspective**:

- Some veterans critical of militarization
- Rules of engagement comparison (more restrictive in war)
- Warrior transition challenges
