# Policing Reform: Roadmap

## Phase 1: Immediate Policy Changes

### Department-Level (Immediate)

**Use of Force Policy Updates**:

- Adopt "necessary" standard
- Ban chokeholds and neck restraints
- Require de-escalation
- Duty to intervene policy
- Comprehensive reporting requirements

**Training Additions**:

- De-escalation training for all officers
- Implicit bias training
- Crisis intervention training
- Policy training on new use of force standards

**Accountability Improvements**:

- Early warning system implementation
- Body camera policy reform
- Complaint process accessibility
- Supervisor review of all force incidents

### Local Government (Immediate)

**Oversight**:

- Strengthen or create civilian oversight
- Subpoena power for oversight body
- Independent investigation of serious incidents
- Public reporting requirements

**Budget**:

- Fund alternative response programs
- Fund training improvements
- Fund oversight capacity

**Transparency**:

- Publish use of force data
- Publish complaint data
- Publish settlement/judgment data

## Phase 2: Legislative Foundation

### State Legislation

**Priority Bills**:

| Bill | Content |
|------|---------|
| Qualified immunity elimination | End qualified immunity for state claims |
| Decertification reform | Mandatory decertification, national database participation |
| Use of force standards | Statewide standards with "necessary" threshold |
| Independent investigation | AG authority for police killings |
| Training requirements | Minimum standards, content requirements |

**Contract Reform**:

- Prohibit contract provisions that impede accountability
- Limit arbitration to narrow procedural issues
- Require transparency in discipline

### Federal Legislation

**George Floyd Justice in Policing Act** (or similar):

| Provision | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| Qualified immunity reform | Amend Section 1983 |
| National use of force standards | Funding conditioned on compliance |
| National decertification database | Prevent wandering officers |
| Pattern-or-practice authority | Strengthen DOJ enforcement |
| Data collection | Comprehensive use of force reporting |

**Funding Bills**:

- Community policing grants (reformed)
- De-escalation training funding
- Alternative response pilot funding
- Mental health co-responder programs

## Phase 3: Structural Transformation

### Training System Overhaul

**Academy Reform**:

| Change | Implementation |
|--------|----------------|
| Extended duration | Minimum 6 months |
| Curriculum overhaul | Guardian mentality, de-escalation focus |
| Hiring standards | Screen for communication, empathy |
| Instructors | Reform-minded, community input |

**Field Training Reform**:

- FTO selection for values
- Guardian mentality emphasis
- Community engagement integration
- Evaluation criteria reform

**Ongoing Professional Development**:

- Annual training requirements expanded
- De-escalation refresher mandatory
- Scenario-based training regular
- Wellness and resilience support

### Scope Transformation

**Alternative Response Implementation**:

| Call Type | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| Mental health crisis | Mental health responders |
| Welfare checks | Trained civilians |
| Homelessness | Social services |
| Minor traffic | Civilian enforcement |
| School issues | School-based alternatives |

**Scaling Alternatives**:

- Pilot programs
- Evaluation and adjustment
- Expansion based on evidence
- Full integration with dispatch

### Cultural Transformation

**Metrics Reform**:

| Old Metric | New Metric |
|------------|------------|
| Arrests | Community satisfaction |
| Stops | Force reduction |
| Activity | Problem resolution |
| Response time | Outcome quality |

**Leadership Development**:

- Promote reform-minded officers
- Accountability for supervisors
- Chief selection criteria
- Community input in leadership

### Union Contract Reform

**Negotiation Strategy**:

- Build political will before negotiations
- Community coalition support
- Legal analysis of current provisions
- Model contract language

**Provisions to Eliminate**:

- Investigation delays
- Evidence access before statements
- Discipline appeal procedures
- Record expungement

## Phase 4: Sustained Change

### Institutionalization

**Consent Decree Principles Without Consent Decree**:

- Independent monitoring (funded)
- Regular public reporting
- Community input mechanisms
- Benchmarks and accountability

**Sustainability Mechanisms**:

| Mechanism | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|
| Ongoing oversight | Prevent backsliding |
| Regular audits | Identify emerging problems |
| Community engagement | Maintain accountability |
| Data collection | Track progress |

### National Standards

**Federal Role**:

- Minimum national standards
- Certification requirements
- Funding tied to compliance
- Technical assistance

**State Role**:

- POST commission reform
- Statewide policies
- Training standards
- Decertification authority

## Risks and Contingencies

### Political Risks

**Crime Increases**:

- Contingency: Robust communication strategy
- Contingency: Prevention investments
- Contingency: Evidence on reform effectiveness

**Backlash to Incidents**:

- Contingency: Rapid response systems
- Contingency: Transparency to prevent misinformation
- Contingency: Coalition support ready

### Implementation Risks

**Union Resistance**:

- Contingency: Build political will first
- Contingency: Community coalition pressure
- Contingency: Legal strategy for contract provisions

**Insufficient Resources**:

- Contingency: Phase implementation
- Contingency: Reallocate existing funds
- Contingency: State/federal funding

**Cultural Resistance**:

- Contingency: Leadership replacement
- Contingency: New hiring priorities
- Contingency: Accountability for non-compliance

### Legal Risks

**Court Challenges**:

- Contingency: Strong legal analysis upfront
- Contingency: Prepare for litigation
- Contingency: Multiple jurisdictions (federal, state)

**Qualified Immunity Judicial Expansion**:

- Contingency: Statutory language clear and explicit
- Contingency: State law alternatives
- Contingency: Entity liability focus
