# Prison Reform: Stakeholders

## People Most Affected

### Incarcerated People

**Population Profile**:

| Characteristic | Data |
|----------------|------|
| Total population | ~1.4 million (state and federal prisons) |
| Average age | 37 years |
| Education pre-incarceration | 70% without high school diploma |
| Prior trauma | 70%+ experienced abuse or trauma |
| Mental health conditions | 45-65% |
| Substance use disorders | 65%+ |

**Primary Concerns**:

| Concern | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Safety | Violence from other prisoners and staff |
| Healthcare | Delayed, inadequate, expensive |
| Communication | Limited, expensive contact with family |
| Programming | Access to education, treatment |
| Sentence length | Release date, good time, parole |
| Conditions | Food, sanitation, housing |
| Solitary | Avoiding isolation |
| Reentry | Preparation for release |

**Barriers to Advocacy**:

| Barrier | Impact |
|---------|--------|
| Disenfranchisement | Cannot vote in most states |
| Limited communication | Censored mail, expensive phone |
| Retaliation risk | Speaking out invites punishment |
| Legal restrictions | PLRA limits litigation |
| Stigma | Public dismisses concerns |
| Dispersion | Scattered across thousands of facilities |

**Forms of Organizing**:

| Form | Examples |
|------|----------|
| Work stoppages | 2016, 2018 prison strikes |
| Hunger strikes | California, other states |
| Legal action | Class action lawsuits |
| Writing/art | Publications, media |
| Connections to outside advocates | Information sharing |

### Families of Incarcerated People

**Scale**:

| Relationship | Number Affected |
|--------------|-----------------|
| Children with incarcerated parent | 2.7 million |
| Adults with incarcerated family member | 33 million+ |
| Romantic partners | Millions |
| Parents of incarcerated adults | Millions |

**Burdens Faced**:

| Burden | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Financial | Lost income, phone costs, commissary, travel |
| Emotional | Trauma, stigma, fear, grief |
| Logistical | Visiting barriers, communication limits |
| Child care | Single parenting, child welfare involvement |
| Housing | Exclusion from public housing |
| Social | Stigma, isolation |

**Financial Impact**:

| Cost | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Phone calls | $0.50-$1.00/minute (historically) |
| Video visits | $0.50-$1.00/minute |
| Commissary deposits | $50-$300/month |
| Visiting travel | Hundreds per visit |
| Lost household income | $32,000 average annual loss |

**Advocacy Role**:

- Primary advocates for loved ones
- Source of information about conditions
- Political constituency for reform
- Organized through groups like Worth Rises, EJUSA

### Communities of Color

**Disproportionate Impact**:

| Community | Impact |
|-----------|--------|
| Black communities | 1 in 3 Black men incarcerated in lifetime |
| Latino communities | 1.3x white incarceration rate |
| Native American communities | Highest per capita rate |
| Low-income urban areas | Concentrated incarceration |

**Community Effects**:

| Effect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Family disruption | Parents removed from homes |
| Economic drain | Workers removed, remittances required |
| Political power | Census counts prisoners where incarcerated |
| Social capital | Community leaders removed |
| Generational trauma | Children experience parental incarceration |
| Stigma | Whole communities labeled |

### Survivors of Violence

**Complex Relationship to Reform**:

| Position | Perspective |
|----------|-------------|
| Traditional victim advocacy | Often aligned with prosecution, long sentences |
| Survivor-led reform | Many survivors support alternatives |
| Healing justice | Focus on what actually helps healing |
| Restorative approaches | Some survivors prefer |

**Key Organizations**:

| Organization | Orientation |
|--------------|-------------|
| Crime Survivors for Safety and Justice | Reform-oriented |
| National Organization for Victim Assistance | Traditional |
| Common Justice | Restorative alternatives |
| Alliance for Safety and Justice | Reform coalition |

## People with Power

### Corrections Officers and Staff

**Demographics**:

| Characteristic | Data |
|----------------|------|
| Total correctional officers | ~420,000 |
| Median salary | $47,000 |
| Turnover rate | 15-30% annually |
| Demographics | Majority white, majority rural |
| Union membership | Varies by state; high in CA, NY |

**Working Conditions**:

| Condition | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Violence exposure | Assaults, threats, trauma |
| Understaffing | Mandatory overtime common |
| Mental health | High rates of PTSD, depression |
| Physical health | Chronic conditions, injuries |
| Isolation | Working in remote areas |

**Relationship to Reform**:

| Factor | Complexity |
|--------|------------|
| Safety interests | Some reforms improve safety |
| Job security | Decarceration threatens jobs |
| Union positions | Often oppose reform |
| Individual variation | Some officers support reform |
| Working conditions | Could improve with reform |

### Corrections Officials and Administrators

**Decision-Making Authority**:

| Official | Authority |
|----------|-----------|
| Director of Corrections | Sets department policy |
| Wardens | Facility-level decisions |
| Bureau of Prisons Director | Federal system |
| Parole boards | Release decisions |
| Classification officers | Housing, programming |

**Incentives**:

| Incentive | Result |
|-----------|--------|
| Avoid incidents | Risk-averse management |
| Political pressure | Responsive to governors |
| Budget constraints | Cost-cutting pressures |
| Career advancement | Don't rock the boat |
| Liability avoidance | Documentation, CYA |

### Private Prison Companies

**Major Players**:

| Company | Revenue | Political Spending |
|---------|---------|-------------------|
| CoreCivic (CCA) | $1.8 billion | $2+ million/cycle |
| GEO Group | $2.2 billion | $2+ million/cycle |

**Business Interests**:

| Interest | Manifestation |
|----------|---------------|
| Occupancy | Guaranteed minimums in contracts |
| Per diem rates | Higher rates, more revenue |
| Immigration detention | Growing market |
| Diversification | Reentry services, monitoring |

### Political Leadership

**Governors**:

| Authority | Examples |
|-----------|----------|
| Clemency/pardons | Can release individuals |
| Budget | Prison funding decisions |
| Appointments | Corrections director, parole board |
| Policy direction | Reform or tough-on-crime |

**Legislators**:

| Authority | Examples |
|-----------|----------|
| Sentencing laws | Mandatory minimums, ranges |
| Prison conditions | Standards, oversight |
| Appropriations | Funding levels |
| Private prison authorization | Contract approval |

**Federal Officials**:

| Role | Authority |
|------|-----------|
| President | BOP policy, pardons, DOJ priorities |
| Attorney General | Federal prosecution, consent decrees |
| Congress | Federal sentencing, prison legislation |
| BOP Director | Federal prison administration |

### Judiciary

**Sentencing Power**:

| Judge Type | Authority |
|------------|-----------|
| Trial court judges | Impose sentences within guidelines |
| Federal judges | Bound by guidelines (advisory post-*Booker*) |
| Appellate courts | Review sentences, conditions |
| Parole boards | Release decisions |

**Conditions Litigation**:

| Authority | Examples |
|-----------|----------|
| Constitution enforcement | 8th Amendment cruel/unusual |

| Class action certification | Systemic challenges |
| Injunctive relief | Order changes |

## Stakeholder Power Analysis

### High Power, Low Interest

| Stakeholder | Engagement Strategy |
|-------------|---------------------|
| Most voters | Raise salience through stories |
| Business community | Economic arguments |
| Federal officials | Constituent pressure |

### High Power, High Interest

| Stakeholder | Relationship |
|-------------|--------------|
| Private prison companies | Opposition |
| Guard unions | Mixed/opposition |
| Governors | Varies |
| Corrections officials | Varies |

### Low Power, High Interest

| Stakeholder | Challenge |
|-------------|-----------|
| Incarcerated people | Amplify voices |
| Families | Organize and resource |
| Directly impacted advocates | Platform and support |

### Shifting Stakes

| Trend | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| COVID-19 exposure | Raised conditions visibility |
| George Floyd/racial reckoning | Brief momentum for reform |
| Crime increase (2020-22) | Reform backlash |
| Budget pressures | Fiscal conservatives engaged |
| Survivor-led reform | Changing victim narrative |
