# Juvenile Justice Reform: Overview

## Executive Summary

The juvenile justice system was created on the premise that children are different from adultsâ€”more amenable to rehabilitation, less culpable for their actions, and deserving of treatment rather than punishment. Yet America's juvenile system often abandons these principles, subjecting youth to adult-like punishment in facilities that cause harm rather than healing. Reform requires returning to the system's original rehabilitative purpose while addressing racial disparities and reducing reliance on incarceration.

This section addresses:

## Core Problems

### Incarceration of Youth

- Facilities that harm rather than rehabilitate
- Solitary confinement still used
- High recidivism rates
- Abuse and violence in facilities
- Separation from family and community

### Racial Disparities

- Black youth incarcerated at 5x white rate
- Latino youth incarcerated at 1.7x white rate
- Native American youth overrepresented
- Disparities at every decision point
- School discipline feeds disparities

### School-to-Prison Pipeline

- Police in schools (SROs)
- Zero tolerance policies
- Suspensions and expulsions
- Arrests for school behavior
- Pushout of struggling students

### Transfer to Adult System

- Thousands of youth tried as adults
- Adult prisons dangerous for youth
- No rehabilitative services
- Lifelong consequences
- Higher recidivism than juvenile system

### Collateral Consequences

- Juvenile records not truly sealed
- Education barriers
- Employment barriers
- Housing barriers
- Immigration consequences

## The Stakes

**For Youth**:

- Development interrupted by incarceration
- Trauma from confinement
- Lost educational opportunity
- Criminal identity formation
- Lifelong consequences

**For Families**:

- Family separation
- Financial burden
- Loss of family support
- Intergenerational impact

**For Communities**:

- Resources diverted from prevention
- Community members lost to system
- Cycle of poverty and incarceration
- Public safety undermined

**For Society**:

- Billions spent on ineffective incarceration
- Future workforce damaged
- Perpetuation of inequality
- Democratic participation reduced

## Reform Vision

### Immediate Goals

- Reduce youth incarceration by 50%
- Close large youth prisons
- End solitary confinement for youth
- Remove police from schools
- Raise age of jurisdiction to 21

### Medium-Term Goals

- End transfer of youth to adult system
- Automatic expungement of juvenile records
- Evidence-based community alternatives
- Address racial disparities at every point
- Support families, not punishment

### Long-Term Vision

- Youth incarceration rare and brief
- All youth receive developmentally appropriate response
- Racial equity achieved
- School-to-college/career pipeline
- Youth voices centered in reform

## Why Reform Is Possible Now

1. **Declining Numbers**: Youth incarceration already down 60% from peak
2. **Brain Science**: Research on adolescent development supports reform
3. **Evidence Base**: Community programs proven more effective
4. **Cost Pressure**: Incarceration expensive, alternatives cheaper
5. **Bipartisan Support**: Both parties support youth justice reform
6. **Youth Advocacy**: Young people leading reform movement
