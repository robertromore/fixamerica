# Juvenile Justice Reform: Roadmap

## Phase 1: Immediate Actions (Year 1)

### Detention Reform

**Quick Wins**:

- Implement detention risk assessment
- Release low-risk youth immediately
- Establish alternatives (electronic monitoring, day reporting)
- Cap detention population
- Reduce length of stay

**Measurable Goals**:

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Detention admissions | Reduce 25% |
| Average length of stay | Reduce 30% |
| Failure to appear | No increase |
| Re-arrest pretrial | No increase |

### School Discipline Reform

**Immediate Changes**:

- End suspensions for minor offenses
- Limit police involvement to safety
- Implement restorative alternatives
- Train staff on de-escalation
- Track discipline data by race

### End Harmful Practices

**Immediate Bans**:

- Solitary confinement over 4 hours
- Shackling in court
- Chemical agents (pepper spray)
- Punitive room confinement

## Phase 2: Systemic Changes (Years 2-3)

### Facility Closures

**Strategy**:

| Year | Action |
|------|--------|
| Year 2 | Identify facilities for closure |
| Year 2-3 | Phase down admissions |
| Year 3 | Close first facilities |
| Year 3-4 | Reinvest in alternatives |

**Reinvestment**:

- Community-based programs
- Small therapeutic facilities
- Family support services
- Prevention programs

### Transfer Reform

**Legislative Package**:

- Eliminate statutory exclusion
- Raise minimum age for transfer
- Burden of proof on prosecution
- Appeals process
- Reverse transfer option

### Record Reform

**Implementation**:

- Automatic sealing for diversions
- Expedited sealing process
- Employment application ban
- Housing application ban
- Education access protection

## Phase 3: Transformation (Years 4-5)

### Raise Age of Jurisdiction

**Implementation Steps**:

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| Legislation | Raise to 21 for all offenses |
| Capacity | Build programs for older youth |
| Training | Prepare staff for older population |
| Transition | Phase in over 2-3 years |

### Remove Police from Schools

**Transition Plan**:

| Year | Action |
|------|--------|
| Year 4 | Pilot schools without SROs |
| Year 4 | Hire counselors, social workers |
| Year 5 | Phase out SROs districtwide |
| Year 5 | Full alternative staffing |

### Community Investment

**Funding Shifts**:

- Redirect facility savings
- Youth employment programs
- Mental health services expansion
- After-school and summer programs
- Family support services

## Phase 4: System Redesign (Years 5+)

### Vision: Rare and Brief Incarceration

**Targets**:

| Metric | Current | Target |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Youth in residential placement | ~37,000 | <10,000 |
| Average length of stay | 6-12 months | 3-6 months |
| Racial disparity (Black:White) | 5:1 | <2:1 |
| Recidivism (3-year) | 50%+ | <25% |

### Community-Based System

**Components**:

- Diversion for most offenses
- Community supervision
- Family-based treatment
- Small therapeutic facilities (when needed)
- Rapid reintegration

### Youth Development Framework

**Shift from Justice to Development**:

| Justice Model | Development Model |
|---------------|-------------------|
| Punishment | Accountability and healing |
| Facility | Community |
| Control | Support |
| Isolation | Connection |
| Deficits | Strengths |

## Implementation Requirements

### Political Strategy

**Coalition Building**:

- Youth and family leadership
- Reform conservatives (fiscal)
- Civil rights organizations
- Faith communities
- Law enforcement reformers

**Messaging**:

| Audience | Frame |
|----------|-------|
| Conservatives | Fiscal responsibility, family values |
| Liberals | Racial justice, youth development |
| Parents | Keep youth close, opportunity |
| Business | Future workforce |

### Resource Requirements

**Investment Needs**:

| Category | Investment |
|----------|------------|
| Community programs | Major (redirect from facilities) |
| Staff training | Moderate |
| Transition support | Moderate (temporary) |
| Data systems | Moderate |

**Savings**:

- Facility closure saves $100,000+/youth/year
- Reduced recidivism saves future costs
- Prevention cheaper than intervention
- Reinvest savings in communities

### Capacity Building

**Workforce**:

- Train existing staff for community work
- Hire from affected communities
- Youth peer specialists
- Family advocates
- Credentialed counselors

**Program Infrastructure**:

- Contract with community organizations
- Build evidence-based program capacity
- Quality assurance systems
- Outcome tracking

## Risks and Contingencies

### Political Backlash

**Risk**: High-profile case used against reform

**Contingencies**:

- Build coalition before crisis
- Prepare messaging response
- Have data ready
- Testimonials from reform supporters

### Implementation Challenges

**Risk**: Alternatives not ready

**Contingencies**:

- Phase closures with alternative capacity
- Technical assistance from successful jurisdictions
- Quality standards for alternatives
- Adjustment based on data

### Economic Challenges

**Risk**: Budget cuts during transition

**Contingencies**:

- Protect reinvestment funding
- Demonstrate cost savings
- Build diverse funding
- Community investment coalition
