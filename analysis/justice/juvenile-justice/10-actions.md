# Juvenile Justice Reform: Actions

## For Individuals

### Educate Yourself

**Learn About Your Local System**:

- How many youth are incarcerated in your state?
- What are conditions like in local facilities?
- Does your school district have SROs?
- What are school discipline policies?
- Who are your juvenile court judges?

**Understand the Issues**:

- Read books on youth justice
- Follow youth justice news
- Attend a juvenile court session (public)
- Learn about adolescent development
- Understand racial disparities

### Support Youth

**Direct Support**:

| Action | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Mentor a young person | Relationship, opportunity |
| Tutor youth in facility | Education access |
| Volunteer with reentry programs | Successful return |
| Support families | Connection, resources |
| Hire system-involved youth | Economic opportunity |

**Youth Leadership**:

- Support youth-led organizations
- Amplify youth voices
- Include youth in reform efforts
- Respect lived experience
- Fund youth organizing

### Vote and Advocate

**Key Elections**:

| Office | Impact on Youth Justice |
|--------|------------------------|
| Juvenile court judges | Detention, disposition decisions |
| District attorney | Charging, transfer decisions |
| School board | Discipline, SRO policies |
| State legislators | Laws, funding |
| Governor | Agency leadership, policy |

**Advocacy Actions**:

- Contact legislators about reform bills
- Testify at hearings
- Write letters to editors
- Attend school board meetings
- Join advocacy coalitions

## For Advocates

### Policy Advocacy

**State Priorities**:

| Priority | Strategy |
|----------|----------|
| Raise age to 21 | Build coalition, fiscal argument |
| End transfer | Emphasize brain science |
| Close facilities | Document costs, harms |
| School discipline | Partner with education advocates |
| Record sealing | Clean slate coalition |

**Federal Priorities**:

- JJDPA reauthorization and funding
- School discipline guidance
- Civil rights enforcement
- Research funding
- Model legislation

### Litigation Strategy

**Types of Cases**:

| Issue | Legal Theory |
|-------|--------------|
| Conditions | Eighth Amendment, state constitution |
| Solitary | Eighth Amendment |
| Education | IDEA, state education law |
| Racial disparities | Title VI, equal protection |
| Transfer | Due process |

**Strategic Considerations**:

- Choose cases for impact
- Coordinate with advocates
- Plan for implementation
- Media strategy
- Build on victories

### Communications

**Framing**:

| Audience | Message |
|----------|---------|
| General public | Youth deserve second chances |
| Conservatives | Fiscal responsibility, family values |
| Progressives | Racial justice, youth rights |
| Parents | Keep youth close to home, opportunity |
| Business | Future workforce |

**Story-Telling**:

- Center youth and family voices
- Show successful rehabilitation
- Document harms of incarceration
- Highlight racial disparities
- Feature reform success
