## Books and Articles

### Essential Reading

**On Electoral Systems**

- *Breaking the Two-Party Doom Loop* by Lee Drutman - Why America needs multiparty democracy
- *The Politics Industry* by Katherine Gehl and Michael Porter - Political competition and reform
- *Electoral Systems and Party Systems* by Arend Lijphart - Comparative systems

**On Money in Politics**

- *Republic, Lost* by Lawrence Lessig - Institutional corruption
- *Dark Money* by Jane Mayer - Koch brothers and conservative donors
- *Corruption in America* by Zephyr Teachout - History of anti-corruption

**On Polarization**

- *Why We're Polarized* by Ezra Klein - Structural causes of polarization
- *Uncivil Agreement* by Lilliana Mason - Identity and partisanship
- *The Righteous Mind* by Jonathan Haidt - Moral psychology and politics

**On Democracy**

- *How Democracies Die* by Steven Levitsky and Daniel Ziblatt - Democratic erosion
- *On Tyranny* by Timothy Snyder - Lessons from 20th century
- *Democracy for Realists* by Christopher Achen and Larry Bartels - How voters actually behave

### Articles and Essays

- "America Is Not a Democracy" - The Atlantic (2018)
- "Testing Theories of American Politics" - Gilens and Page (2014) - Oligarchy research
- "The Constitution of the United States Threatens American Democracy" - Dahl
- FiveThirtyEight's gerrymandering and electoral coverage

### Documentaries

- *Slay the Dragon* (2019) - Gerrymandering
- *Dark Money* (2018) - Political spending
- *What Is Democracy?* (2018) - Democratic theory

## Getting Started

### Recommended First Steps

1. **Read** *The Politics Industry* or *Breaking the Two-Party Doom Loop* for comprehensive overview
2. **Follow** RepresentUs, FairVote, and Brennan Center for updates
3. **Check** OpenSecrets to see who funds your representatives
4. **Join** a local reform organization or start a chapter
5. **Subscribe** to reform-focused newsletters (FairVote, Issue One, etc.)
