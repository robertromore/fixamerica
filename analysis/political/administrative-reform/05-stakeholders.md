# Administrative Reform: Stakeholders

## Federal Employees

### Career Civil Servants

**Population**: ~2 million executive branch civilian employees

**Interests**:

- Job security and merit protections
- Fair compensation
- Professional development
- Mission accomplishment
- Protection from political interference

**Current Concerns**:

| Concern | Source |
|---------|--------|
| Schedule F threat | Could return in future administration |
| Political hostility | "Deep state" rhetoric |
| Resource constraints | Budget limits, hiring freezes |
| Morale issues | Leadership instability, mission confusion |

**Organizations**:

- American Federation of Government Employees (AFGE)
- National Treasury Employees Union (NTEU)
- National Federation of Federal Employees (NFFE)
- Professional associations by field

### Senior Executive Service

**Population**: ~7,000 senior leaders

**Interests**:

- Leadership development
- Performance recognition
- Career mobility
- Mission success

**Unique Position**:

- Higher accountability
- More vulnerable to political pressure
- Bridge between political and career

### Political Appointees

**Population**: ~4,000 positions

**Interests**:

- Implement administration policy
- Succeed in position
- Career advancement
- Policy impact

**Tensions**:

- Short tenures (average 2 years)
- Learning curve
- Distrust of career staff
- Conflicting loyalties

## Government Management Organizations

### Office of Personnel Management (OPM)

**Role**: Government-wide HR policy

**Interests**:

- Workforce quality
- Efficient processes
- Policy implementation
- Agency support

### Office of Management and Budget (OMB)

**Role**: Budget and management oversight

**Interests**:

- Efficiency
- Cost control
- Presidential agenda
- Cross-government management

### Government Accountability Office (GAO)

**Role**: Congressional oversight arm

**Interests**:

- Government effectiveness
- Accountability
- Evidence-based policy
- Waste reduction

### Merit Systems Protection Board (MSPB)

**Role**: Adjudicate personnel disputes

**Interests**:

- Merit principles
- Fair process
- Efficient adjudication
- Workforce protection

### Office of Special Counsel (OSC)

**Role**: Protect whistleblowers, investigate prohibited practices

**Interests**:

- Whistleblower protection
- Merit system integrity
- Hatch Act enforcement

## Congress

### Oversight Committees

**House Oversight and Accountability Committee**:

- Federal workforce issues
- Agency management
- Investigations

**Senate Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs Committee**:

- Government operations
- Federal workforce
- Nominations

### Appropriations Committees

Control agency budgets and staffing:

- Fund or defund positions
- Impose conditions
- Direct activities

### Authorization Committees

Create and structure agencies:

- Agency design
- Authority grants
- Structural changes

## Executive Branch Leadership

### White House

**Interests**:

- Policy implementation
- Political responsiveness
- Presidential agenda
- Efficient government

**Tensions**:

- Short-term political vs. long-term institutional
- Responsiveness vs. expertise
- Control vs. independence

### Agency Heads

**Interests**:

- Agency mission
- Workforce effectiveness
- Political success
- Legacy

**Challenges**:

- Short tenures
- Confirmation delays
- Career/political tensions
- Resource constraints

## The Public

### As Beneficiaries

The public benefits from:

- Effective government services
- Fair regulation
- Competent administration
- Protection from abuses

### As Stakeholders

Public interest in:

- Efficient use of tax dollars
- Responsive government
- Accountable bureaucracy
- Merit-based service

### Current Engagement

- Generally low attention to administrative issues
- Spikes during failures or scandals
- Mixed views of bureaucracy
- Values services, distrusts "government"

- Propose reforms

- Provide evidence
