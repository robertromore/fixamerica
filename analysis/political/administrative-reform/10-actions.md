# Administrative Reform: Actions

## For All Citizens

### Stay Informed

**Understand the Administrative State**:

- How agencies implement law
- What civil service protections exist
- How political appointees differ from career officials
- What regulatory capture means

**Key Resources**:

| Source | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| Government Executive | Federal workforce news |
| Partnership for Public Service | Workforce data, analysis |
| GAO reports | Government effectiveness |
| POGO | Accountability issues |

### Contact Officials

**Congress**:

- Support civil service protection legislation
- Advocate for workforce investment
- Demand oversight of political interference
- Support anti-capture reforms

**Key Messages**:

- "Protect the merit-based civil service"
- "Invest in the federal workforce"
- "Prevent Schedule F-style purges"
- "Strengthen agency independence"

### Vote and Advocate

**Elections**:

- Consider candidates' positions on government
- Ask about civil service protection
- Evaluate attitudes toward expertise
- Support good government candidates

**Advocacy**:

- Join good government organizations
- Participate in public comment processes
- Engage with local federal facilities
- Share information about federal workforce

## For Federal Employees

### Protect Yourself

**Know Your Rights**:

- Civil service protections
- Merit system principles
- Prohibited personnel practices
- Whistleblower rights

**Documentation**:

- Keep records of important communications
- Document inappropriate political pressure
- Preserve evidence of merit-based decisions
- Maintain professional records

### Engage Professionally

**Best Practices**:

| Practice | Benefit |
|----------|---------|
| Document decisions | Create administrative record |
| Follow procedures | Protect from arbitrary action |
| Maintain neutrality | Demonstrate professionalism |
| Seek guidance | Use ethics and legal resources |

### Collective Action

**Union Engagement**:

- Join employee organization if eligible
- Participate in collective activities
- Support workforce advocacy
- Build solidarity

**Professional Associations**:

- Engage with professional community
- Share best practices
- Develop expertise
- Build networks

## Key Questions for Officials

### For Executive Branch

1. "What is your position on Schedule F or similar proposals?"
2. "How will you protect career civil servants from political interference?"
3. "What will you do to address regulatory capture?"
4. "How will you invest in the federal workforce?"
5. "Will you protect scientific integrity?"

### For Congress

1. "Will you support legislation protecting the civil service?"
2. "What oversight will you conduct of political interference?"
3. "Will you invest in federal workforce capacity?"
4. "How will you address agency capture?"
