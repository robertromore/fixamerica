# Administrative Reform: History

## Early Republic

### Origins

The early federal government was small and informal:

- Departments of State, Treasury, War
- Few employees
- Personal relationships with President
- No formal civil service system

### Patronage Era

**Spoils System**: "To the victor belong the spoils"

- Jackson administration formalized rotation in office
- Loyalty over competence
- Massive turnover with each administration
- Corruption and inefficiency

## Civil Service Reform Era

### Pendleton Act (1883)

**Catalyst**: Assassination of President Garfield by disappointed office-seeker

**Key Provisions**:

| Provision | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| Civil Service Commission | Administered merit system |
| Competitive examinations | Replaced patronage |
| Protection from removal | Except for cause |
| Political activity restrictions | Later became Hatch Act |

**Initial Coverage**: Only 10% of positions; expanded over time

### Progressive Era Expansion

**Growth of Administrative State**:

- Interstate Commerce Commission (1887)
- Federal Reserve (1913)
- FTC (1914)
- Increasing regulation of economy

**Independent Agency Model**:

- Insulation from political pressure
- Expert decision-making
- Quasi-judicial functions
- Multi-member commissions

## New Deal Transformation

### Expansion

**New Agencies**:

- SEC (1934)
- NLRB (1935)
- Social Security Administration (1935)
- FCC (1934)
- Many others

**Size**: Federal workforce grew dramatically

### Administrative Procedure Act (1946)

**Purpose**: Standardize and constrain agency processes

**Key Features**:

- Notice-and-comment rulemaking
- Due process in adjudication
- Judicial review of agency action
- Transparency requirements

### Constitutional Challenges

**Humphrey's Executor v. FTC (1935)**:

- Upheld independent agency structure
- For-cause removal protection constitutional
- Distinguished from purely executive officers

## Post-War Development

### Senior Executive Service (1978)

**Civil Service Reform Act**:

- Created SES for senior managers
- More flexibility and accountability
- Performance-based pay
- Mobility across agencies

**Merit Systems Protection Board**:

- Adjudicates personnel disputes
- Protects merit principles
- Replaced Civil Service Commission

**Office of Personnel Management**:

- Personnel policy
- HR guidance
- Training and development

### Deregulation Movement

**Reagan Administration**:

- Executive Order 12291: Cost-benefit analysis
- OIRA review of regulations
- Deregulation agenda
- Skepticism of bureaucracy

**Lasting Effects**:

- Regulatory review institutionalized
- Cost-benefit framework
- Agency skepticism in some quarters

## Modern Era

### Reinventing Government (1990s)

**Clinton-Gore Initiative**:

- National Performance Review
- Streamlining and efficiency
- Customer service focus
- Workforce reduction

**Results**:

- Federal workforce reduced
- Some process improvements
- Changed management rhetoric

### Post-9/11 Changes

**Department of Homeland Security** (2002):

- Massive reorganization
- Combined 22 agencies
- Disruption and challenges
- New personnel systems (later abandoned)

**Security State Expansion**:

- Intelligence community growth
- Contractor workforce increase
- Classification expansion

### Obama Administration

**Initiatives**:

- Open Government
- Evidence-based policy
- Regulatory reform
- Some workforce improvements

**Challenges**:

- Continued hiring difficulties
- Budget constraints
- Partisan opposition

### Trump Administration

**Unprecedented Challenges**:

| Action | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| Schedule F | Threatened civil service protections |
| Agency leadership vacancies | Undermined agency capacity |
| Hostility to expertise | Politicized scientific findings |
| IG firings | Weakened oversight |
| Regulatory rollbacks | Changed regulatory approach |

**Schedule F Details**:

- Executive Order 13957 (October 2020)
- Reclassified policy-related positions
- Removed civil service protections
- Could have affected 50,000+ positions
- Rescinded by Biden January 2021

### Biden Administration

**Restorations**:

- Rescinded Schedule F
- Executive Order on merit principles
- Restored regulatory processes
- Emphasis on expertise

**Ongoing Issues**:

- Structural vulnerabilities remain
- Future administration could revive Schedule F
- Legislative protection needed

## Supreme Court Evolution

### Independent Agency Cases

**Humphrey's Executor v. FTC (1935)**:

- For-cause removal constitutional
- Independent agencies upheld

**Morrison v. Olson (1988)**:

- Upheld independent counsel
- For-cause removal for inferior officers

**Free Enterprise Fund v. PCAOB (2010)**:

- Dual for-cause removal unconstitutional
- Began limiting independence

**Seila Law v. CFPB (2020)**:

- Single-director with for-cause removal unconstitutional
- Narrowed Humphrey's Executor

**Collins v. Yellen (2021)**:

- FHFA structure unconstitutional
- Continued narrowing trend

### Chevron Deference

**Chevron v. NRDC (1984)**:

- Deference to agency statutory interpretation
- If statute ambiguous, defer to reasonable interpretation

**Recent Challenges**:

- Criticism from conservative justices
- Calls to limit or overturn
- Major questions doctrine

## Patterns and Themes

### Cycles of Reform

Reform typically follows:

1. Scandal or crisis
2. Public attention
3. Reform legislation
4. Gradual erosion
5. New scandal

### Tension Between

- Political responsiveness vs. expertise
- Efficiency vs. due process
- Independence vs. accountability
- Uniformity vs. flexibility

### Persistent Challenges

- Patronage pressure recurs
- Capture risk never eliminated
- Resource constraints chronic
- Technology lags private sector
