# Democratic Innovation: Roadmap

## Implementation Timeline

Democratic innovation requires a multi-decade strategy building from local experiments to federal institutionalization.

## Political Strategy

### Building Support

**Left-Leaning Audience**:

- Emphasize citizen empowerment
- Highlight inclusion of marginalized voices
- Connect to progressive policy outcomes
- Frame as alternative to corporate influence

**Right-Leaning Audience**:

- Emphasize local control
- Highlight citizen vs. bureaucrat decision-making
- Connect to republican (small-r) tradition
- Frame as alternative to elite capture

**Bipartisan Framing**:

- "Give regular people a seat at the table"
- "Government that listens"
- "Common-sense solutions from real Americans"
- "End the dysfunction"

## Risk Factors

### Implementation Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Poor quality processes undermine concept | Rigorous standards; certification |
| Politician capture of processes | Independent facilitation; transparency |
| Low participation | Outreach; compensation; multiple access points |
| Manipulation of outcomes | Random selection; balanced information |

### Political Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Partisan backlash | Maintain bipartisan framing and support |
| Funding cuts | Diversified funding; institutionalization |
| Repeal efforts | Build constituencies who benefit |
| Media attacks | Proactive communications; evidence |

### External Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Economic crisis diverts attention | Frame as part of solution |
| Democratic backsliding | Accelerate; frame as antidote |
| Technology failures | Paper backups; security investment |
| Pandemic/emergency disruption | Hybrid in-person/online options |

## Contingency Plans

### If Progress Is Slower Than Expected

- Focus on deepening in fewer jurisdictions rather than spreading thin
- Build stronger evidence base from existing programs
- Cultivate next generation of champions
- Maintain organizational infrastructure for future openings

### If Political Environment Becomes Hostile

- Emphasize nonpartisan nature
- Focus on local government where possible
- Build private sector applications
- Maintain international connections

### If Major Failure Occurs

- Transparent acknowledgment and analysis
- Distinguish implementation failure from concept failure
- Learn and improve
- Communicate lessons

## Dependencies on Other Reforms

### Electoral Reform

- Better voting systems create space for innovation
- RCV reduces partisan polarization
- Multi-member districts diversify representation

### Campaign Finance Reform

- Public financing reduces donor influence
- Disclosure increases transparency
- Reduces opposition from donor class

### Media Reform

- Quality journalism supports deliberation
- Platform accountability reduces manipulation
- Local news enables local participation
