# Primary Reform: Actions

## For Individuals

### Understand Your State's System

**Research Your State's Primary Rules**

| Task | How |
|------|-----|
| Learn your primary type | Check NCSL database or state election website |
| Know registration deadlines | Mark calendar for party affiliation changes |
| Understand your options | Can you participate in primaries? Which ones? |
| Check ballot initiative availability | Does your state allow citizen initiatives? |

**Assess Your Situation**

| Question | Implication |
|----------|-------------|
| Are you excluded from primaries? | Independent in closed primary state |
| Is your general election competitive? | Or is primary the real contest? |
| Are reform efforts active in your state? | Join existing campaign |
| Is there a ballot initiative process? | Path to citizen-led reform |

### Vote in Primaries

**Why Primary Voting Matters**

| Reason | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Low turnout | Your vote has more weight |
| Decisive election | In safe seats, primary determines winner |
| Candidate quality | Shape who appears in general election |
| Accountability signal | Elected officials watch primary participation |

**Action Steps**

1. Confirm your voter registration status
2. Note your state's primary date (mark calendar)
3. Research candidates before the primary
4. Request absentee ballot if needed
5. Vote in every primary (not just presidential)

### Advocate for Reform

**Contact Elected Officials**

| Official | Message Focus |
|----------|---------------|
| State legislators | Support primary reform legislation |
| Secretary of State | Election administration support |
| Governor | Sign reform legislation |
| Federal legislators | Support federal primary standards |

**Effective Advocacy**

| Method | Tips |
|--------|------|
| Phone calls | Brief, specific ask; identify yourself as constituent |
| Written letters | Personal stories more effective than form letters |
| Town halls | Ask about primary reform publicly |
| Office visits | Schedule appointment; bring specific request |

**Sample Talking Points**

- "I'm an independent voter and can't participate in primaries that my taxes fund."
- "Primary elections in our state produce extreme candidates who don't represent the majority."
- "Alaska and other states have shown that primary reform works. We should try it here."
- "Both parties benefit from a system that excludes independent voters. That's not right."
