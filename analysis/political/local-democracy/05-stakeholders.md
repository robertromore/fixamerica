# Local Democracy: Stakeholders

## Local Government Officials

### Mayors

**Interest**: Effective governance, reelection, city advancement.

**Key Organizations**:

- U.S. Conference of Mayors
- National League of Cities
- State municipal leagues

**Perspectives**:

| Issue | Typical Position |
|-------|------------------|
| State preemption | Oppose |
| Fiscal authority | Want more |
| Federal aid | Support |
| Participation reforms | Varies |

### City Council Members

**Interest**: Constituent service, policy influence, political advancement.

**Key Organizations**:

- National League of Cities
- State municipal leagues
- Local elected official networks

**Perspectives**:

- Generally oppose preemption
- Want council authority preserved
- Vary on participation mechanisms
- Concerned about workload

### City Managers and Administrators

**Interest**: Professional management, efficient operations, political navigation.

**Key Organizations**:

- International City/County Management Association (ICMA)
- State city manager associations

**Perspectives**:

- Professional autonomy valued
- Efficiency-oriented
- May be cautious about participation reforms
- Support professional development

### County Officials

**Interest**: County services, coordination with cities, state relations.

**Key Organizations**:

- National Association of Counties (NACo)
- State county associations

**Role**:

- Often overlooked
- Serve unincorporated areas
- Increasingly provide urban services
- Varied authority by state

## State Government

### State Legislators

**Interest**: Statewide policy, political advancement, constituent service.

**Perspectives on Local Government**:

| Position | Who Tends to Hold |
|----------|-------------------|
| Pro-preemption | Rural legislators, business allies |
| Pro-local authority | Urban legislators, some moderates |
| Mixed | Many, depending on issue |

**Key Dynamics**:

- Urban-rural divide
- Partisan considerations
- Business lobbying influence
- Local government lobbying

### Governors

**Interest**: Statewide issues, local government coordination.

**Role**:

- Appoint state officials overseeing locals
- Budget authority affecting locals
- Can veto preemption or local authority bills

### State Municipal Agencies

- Local government oversight
- Technical assistance
- Grant administration
- Regulatory role

**Key Organizations**:

**Focus Areas**:

## Business and Development

### Local Business Interests

**Organizations**:

- Local chambers of commerce
- Downtown business associations
- Industry associations

**Interests**:

| Issue | Typical Position |
|-------|------------------|
| Taxes | Lower |
| Regulation | Less |
| Development | More |
| Infrastructure | Support if business-serving |
| Minimum wage | Oppose local increases |

### Developers and Real Estate

**Interest**: Development approval, zoning flexibility, incentives.

**Influence Methods**:

- Campaign contributions
- Lobbying
- Community relations
- Legal/planning expertise

**Issues**:

- Zoning and land use
- Impact fees
- Affordable housing requirements
- Environmental review

### Large Employers

**Interest**: Business-friendly environment, workforce, infrastructure.

**Leverage**:

- Job creation/loss threat
- Tax base
- Community investment
- Lobbying resources

## Community Members

### Homeowners

**Interests**:

- Property values
- Neighborhood quality
- Taxes (often want lower)
- Services

**Participation Patterns**:

- Higher participation than renters
- Active in zoning, development issues
- Often NIMBY on housing
- Disproportionate influence

### Renters

**Interests**:

- Affordable housing
- Tenant protections
- Services
- Transportation

**Participation Patterns**:

- Lower participation
- Less organized traditionally
- Growing tenant organizing
- Underrepresented in decisions

### Youth

**Interests**:

- Schools
- Recreation
- Jobs
- Environment
- Policing

**Participation Patterns**:

- Very low in elections
- Some engagement through schools
- Youth councils in some cities
- Climate activism

### Seniors

**Interests**:

- Services for elderly
- Transportation
- Safety
- Tax relief

**Participation Patterns**:

- Higher voter turnout
- Active in civic organizations
- Meeting attendance higher
- Advocacy organizations

### Immigrants

**Interests**:

- Language access
- Immigration-related policies
- Services
- Representation

**Participation Patterns**:

- Varies by status and tenure
- Language barriers
- Fear (undocumented)
- Growing organizing

## Power Analysis

### Most Influential Stakeholders

| Stakeholder | Influence | Basis |
|-------------|-----------|-------|
| Mayors (in strong mayor cities) | High | Executive power |
| Developers | High | Resources, campaign funding |
| City managers | High | Administrative control |
| Business interests | High | Tax base, resources |
| Public employee unions | Moderate-High | Numbers, organization |
| State legislature | High | Can preempt |
| Homeowner groups | Moderate-High | Vote, attend meetings |
| General public | Low | Diffuse, unorganized |

### Who Benefits from Status Quo

- Incumbents
- Organized interests
- Homeowners (vs. renters)
- Wealthy communities
- Those with time to participate

### Who Loses from Status Quo

- Renters
- Working people
- Racial minorities
- Low-income residents
- Young people
- Recent immigrants
