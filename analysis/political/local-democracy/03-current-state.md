# Local Democracy: Current State

## Local Government Structure

### Municipal Government Forms

**Distribution**:

| Form | Percentage | Characteristics |
|------|------------|-----------------|
| Council-Manager | 55% | Professional manager, policy-making council |
| Mayor-Council | 34% | Elected mayor, varying powers |
| Commission | 1% | Commissioners lead departments |
| Town Meeting | 5% | Direct democracy (New England) |
| Representative Town Meeting | 3% | Elected representatives meet |

**Trends**:

- Council-manager continues to grow
- Strong mayor systems in large cities
- Hybrid forms emerging
- Professional management valued

### County Government

**Types**:

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Commission | Elected commissioners |
| Council-Administrator | Professional manager |
| Council-Elected Executive | Separate elected executive |
| Traditional | Limited functions |

**Functions Vary**:

- Some counties provide urban services
- Others primarily administrative
- Unincorporated areas served
- Growing importance in some states

### Special Districts

**Growth**:

| Year | Number of Special Districts |
|------|----------------------------|
| 1952 | 12,340 |
| 1982 | 28,078 |
| 2012 | 37,203 |
| 2022 | 38,542 |

**Types**:

- Fire protection
- Water and sewer
- Housing
- Transportation
- Parks and recreation

**Democratic Concerns**:

- Low visibility to citizens
- Often uncontested board elections
- Limited accountability
- Fragmented governance

## Local Elections

### Turnout Patterns

**General Patterns**:

| Election Type | Typical Turnout |
|---------------|-----------------|
| Presidential year (local on ballot) | 40-60% |
| Midterm year (local on ballot) | 25-40% |
| Off-cycle local only | 15-27% |
| Special elections | 5-15% |

**Factors Affecting Turnout**:

- On-cycle vs. off-cycle timing
- Competitive races
- Salient issues
- Weather and logistics
- Mail voting availability

### Election Timing

**Current Practice**:

| Timing | Cities |
|--------|--------|
| On-cycle (November even year) | Minority |
| Off-cycle (odd year/spring) | Majority |

**Consequences**:

- Off-cycle elections have lower turnout
- Participants differ demographically
- Interest groups gain influence
- Lower representativeness

### Electoral Systems

**Council Elections**:

| System | Prevalence | Characteristics |
|--------|------------|-----------------|
| At-large | ~60% | City-wide voting |
| District/Ward | ~30% | Geographic representation |
| Mixed | ~10% | Combination |

**Partisan vs. Nonpartisan**:

- ~80% of cities use nonpartisan elections
- No party label on ballot
- Varies by region and city size

**Ranked Choice Voting**:

Growing adoption:

- San Francisco (2004)
- Minneapolis (2009)
- New York City (2021)
- Maine (statewide, including local)
- Alaska (statewide, including local)
- 50+ jurisdictions total

## Citizen Participation

### Traditional Mechanisms

**Public Hearings and Meetings**:

| Characteristic | Typical Pattern |
|----------------|-----------------|
| Attendance | Low (often <20 people) |
| Speakers | Repeat participants |
| Demographics | Older, wealthier, homeowners |
| Timing | Daytime or evening inconvenient for many |

**Public Comment**:

- Limited time per speaker
- Often adversarial dynamic
- Dominated by organized interests
- Rarely changes decisions

### Newer Participation Forms

**Participatory Budgeting**:

| City | Status | Annual Amount |
|------|--------|---------------|
| New York City | Largest US program | $35+ million |
| Chicago | Multiple wards | Varies |
| Boston | Youth and city-wide | $1+ million |
| 50+ US cities | Various scales | Variable |

**Online Engagement**:

- Virtual town halls (increased post-COVID)
- Online comment systems
- Social media engagement
- 311 systems and apps

**Citizen Assemblies**:

- Climate assemblies
- Policy deliberations
- Growing use in some cities
- Limited but expanding

### Barriers to Participation

| Barrier | Population Affected |
|---------|---------------------|
| Meeting times (workday) | Working people |
| Language access | Non-English speakers |
| Childcare needs | Parents |
| Transportation | Non-drivers |
| Digital divide | Low-income, elderly |
| Lack of information | Most residents |

## Representation

### Demographic Gaps

**City Councils**:

| Group | Population % | Council % (approx.) |
|-------|--------------|---------------------|
| Women | 51% | 30-35% |
| Black Americans | 13% | 10-12% |
| Hispanic/Latino | 18% | 6-8% |
| Under 40 | 40% | 10-15% |
| Renters | 36% | Much lower |

**Mayors**:

- Similar patterns
- Some improvement in large cities
- Small cities often less diverse

### Factors Affecting Representation

- Electoral system (at-large vs. district)
- Election timing
- Candidate recruitment
- Campaign costs
- Incumbent advantages

## State-Local Relations

### Home Rule Status

**Types**:

| Type | Authority Level |
|------|-----------------|
| Constitutional home rule | Strongest |
| Legislative home rule | Legislature can modify |
| Dillon's Rule | Only expressly granted powers |

**State Distribution**:

- ~40 states have some home rule provision
- Scope and strength varies dramatically
- Many limitations even in home rule states

### Preemption Landscape

**Major Preemption Areas (2024)**:

| Policy Area | States Preempting |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Minimum wage | 25+ |
| Paid sick leave | 20+ |
| Rent control | 30+ |
| Plastic bag/foam bans | 15+ |
| Tobacco/vaping | 15+ |
| Gun regulations | 40+ |
| Telecommunications | 20+ |
| Sanctuary policies | 10+ |

**Punitive Preemption**:

- Florida: Personal liability for officials
- Texas: Funding penalties
- Arizona: Attorney general enforcement
- Others with similar provisions

## Fiscal Condition

### Revenue Sources

**Local Government Revenue Mix**:

| Source | % of Revenue |
|--------|--------------|
| Property tax | ~30% |
| Sales tax | ~15% |
| Intergovernmental | ~35% |
| Fees/charges | ~15% |
| Other | ~5% |

### Fiscal Challenges

| Challenge | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Property tax limitations | State caps and restrictions |
| Sales tax erosion | E-commerce shift |
| Unfunded mandates | Federal and state requirements |
| Pension obligations | Legacy costs |
| Infrastructure needs | Deferred maintenance |

### Fiscal Disparities

- Wealthy suburbs vs. central cities
- Tax base inequities
- Service quality differences
- Segregation reinforced
