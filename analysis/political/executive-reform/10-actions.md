# Executive Reform: Actions

## For All Citizens

### Educate Yourself

**Understand the Issues**

- Read the Constitution's Article II (it's short)
- Learn the difference between legitimate executive action and overreach
- Follow nonpartisan sources tracking executive power (Lawfare, Just Security)
- Understand the history of presidential power expansion

**Stay Informed**

- Track emergency declarations and their uses
- Follow war powers debates
- Monitor ethics and conflict issues
- Pay attention to inspector general reports

### Engage with Representatives

**Contact Congress**

- Call and write your representatives about executive power concerns
- Ask specifically about their position on reform legislation
- Request they support IG protection, emergency powers reform, war powers enforcement
- Demand oversight regardless of which party holds the presidency

**Key Messages**

- "Executive power is an institutional issue, not a partisan one"
- "Congress should defend its constitutional prerogatives"
- "I want my representative to support [specific reform bill]"
- "Oversight should apply to presidents of both parties"

### Support Reform Organizations

**Donate and Volunteer**

- Protect Democracy
- Brennan Center for Justice
- Citizens for Responsibility and Ethics (CREW)
- Project on Government Oversight (POGO)
- Government Accountability Project

**Amplify Their Work**

- Share reports and analysis on social media
- Write letters to the editor citing their research
- Attend events and webinars
- Connect them with local media

### Vote and Organize

**Consider Executive Power in Voting**

- Ask candidates their views on executive constraints
- Support candidates who commit to reform
- Vote in primaries where these issues can be raised
- Consider executive power positions regardless of party

**Build Local Support**

- Start or join local good government groups
- Organize community education events
- Partner with civic organizations
- Build coalitions across political lines

## For Professionals

### Lawyers and Legal Professionals

**Pro Bono Work**

- Support litigation challenging executive overreach
- File amicus briefs in key cases
- Provide legal support to whistleblowers
- Assist good government organizations

**Education and Advocacy**

- Write articles and op-eds on executive power issues
- Speak at bar associations and law schools
- Train other lawyers on relevant law
- Participate in ABA committees on government law

**Within Government**

- Take seriously oath to Constitution, not president
- Raise concerns through proper channels
- Document problematic directives
- Know whistleblower protections

### Journalists and Media

**Investigative Reporting**

- Document executive overreach regardless of party
- FOIA requests for executive branch information
- Track use of emergency and war powers
- Report on ethics violations

**Explainer Content**

- Help public understand constitutional issues
- Provide historical context for current actions
- Interview experts across ideological spectrum
- Avoid framing as purely partisan conflict

**Protect Sources**

- Maintain relationships with executive branch sources
- Protect whistleblower identities
- Fight for access to information
- Challenge overbroad classification

### Academics and Researchers

**Scholarship**

- Research executive power trends
- Analyze effectiveness of constraints
- Compare with other democracies
- Develop reform proposals

**Public Engagement**

- Write for general audiences
- Provide expert commentary to media
- Testify before Congress
- Train next generation of lawyers and officials

**Data and Transparency**

- Track executive actions systematically
- Create public databases
- Analyze patterns across administrations
- Make research accessible

### Current and Former Government Officials

**While in Government**

- Take constitutional oath seriously
- Document concerns through proper channels
- Know whistleblower protections
- Resign rather than participate in clear violations

**After Government Service**

- Speak publicly about abuses you witnessed
- Support reform efforts
- Provide expertise to reform organizations
- Testify before Congress when asked

**Across Administrations**

- Share institutional knowledge
- Build relationships with officials of other party
- Support norms of restraint
- Model ethical behavior

## Specific Campaign Targets

### Priority Legislation to Support

1. **Inspector General Independence and Empowerment Act**
   - Protect IGs from political removal
   - Status: Introduced in Congress periodically

2. **TRUST in Congress Act** (emergency powers)
   - Require congressional approval of emergencies
   - Status: Various versions introduced

3. **National Security Powers Act** (war powers)
   - Strengthen War Powers Resolution
   - Status: Various versions introduced

4. **Presidential Tax Transparency Act**
   - Require tax return disclosure
   - Status: Passed House, stalled in Senate historically

5. **Protecting Our Democracy Act**
   - Omnibus reform bill
   - Status: Passed House 2021, not enacted

### Key Votes to Track

- War authorization votes
- Emergency declaration votes
- Inspector general nominations
- Ethics legislation
- Subpoena enforcement

### Accountability Questions for Candidates

1. "Will you commit to releasing your tax returns?"
2. "Do you support congressional authorization for military action?"
3. "Will you respect inspector general independence?"
4. "Do you support limits on emergency powers?"
5. "Will you divest from business interests if elected?"

## Building Long-Term Power

### Coalition Building

**Find Common Ground**

- Libertarians: Executive restraint, civil liberties
- Conservatives: Constitutional limits, legislative authority
- Progressives: Accountability, ethics, war powers
- Independents: Good government, transparency

**Institutional Allies**

- Bar associations
- Journalism organizations
- Veterans groups (war powers)
- Business groups (regulatory predictability)
- Religious organizations (moral accountability)

### State and Local Action

**State Resolutions**

- Call for constitutional amendments
- Support for federal legislation
- State-level transparency requirements

**Local Government**

- Model transparency practices
- Civic education programs
- Community organizing base

### Long-Term Engagement

**Stay Engaged Between Crises**

- Executive power issues don't get attention until abuse occurs
- Build capacity before next crisis
- Maintain pressure regardless of which party holds power
- Think institutionally, not just about current president

**Prepare for Opportunities**

- Have reform proposals ready
- Build coalitions in advance
- Identify champions in both parties
- Create public understanding before crisis
