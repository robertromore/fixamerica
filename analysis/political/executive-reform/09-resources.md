## Essential Reading

### Books

**Historical and Theoretical**

- Schlesinger, Arthur M. *The Imperial Presidency*. Houghton Mifflin, 1973. (Classic account of executive power growth)
- Rudalevige, Andrew. *The New Imperial Presidency*. University of Michigan Press, 2005. (Updated analysis)
- Savage, Charlie. *Takeover: The Return of the Imperial Presidency*. Little, Brown, 2007.
- Fisher, Louis. *Presidential War Power*. University Press of Kansas, 3rd ed., 2013.
- Posner, Eric and Adrian Vermeule. *The Executive Unbound*. Oxford University Press, 2010. (Argues constraints impossible)

**Recent Developments**

- Goldsmith, Jack. *Power and Constraint*. W.W. Norton, 2012.
- Bauer, Bob and Jack Goldsmith. *After Trump: Reconstructing the Presidency*. Lawfare Press, 2020.
- Ackerman, Bruce. *The Decline and Fall of the American Republic*. Harvard University Press, 2010.
- Levitsky, Steven and Daniel Ziblatt. *How Democracies Die*. Crown, 2018.

**War Powers**

- Fisher, Louis. *Congressional Abdication on War and Spending*. Texas A&M Press, 2000.
- Waxman, Matthew. *Legislating War Powers*. Columbia Law Review, 2018.
- Glennon, Michael. *National Security and Double Government*. Oxford, 2015.

### Academic Articles

- Calabresi, Steven and Kevin Rhodes. "The Structural Constitution: Unitary Executive, Plural Judiciary." *Harvard Law Review* 105 (1992): 1153.
- Lessig, Lawrence and Cass Sunstein. "The President and the Administration." *Columbia Law Review* 94 (1994): 1.
- Kagan, Elena. "Presidential Administration." *Harvard Law Review* 114 (2001): 2245.

## Legal Resources

### Key Supreme Court Cases

**Executive Power Generally**

- *Youngstown Sheet & Tube Co. v. Sawyer*, 343 U.S. 579 (1952) (Jackson concurrence framework)
- *United States v. Nixon*, 418 U.S. 683 (1974) (Executive privilege limits)
- *Clinton v. Jones*, 520 U.S. 681 (1997) (No immunity for unofficial acts)
- *Trump v. United States*, 603 U.S. ___ (2024) (Presidential immunity)

**War Powers**

- *The Prize Cases*, 67 U.S. 635 (1863) (Emergency military action)
- *Hamdi v. Rumsfeld*, 542 U.S. 507 (2004) (Detention and due process)
- *Hamdan v. Rumsfeld*, 548 U.S. 557 (2006) (Military commissions)
- *Boumediene v. Bush*, 553 U.S. 723 (2008) (Habeas corpus)

**Independent Agencies**

- *Humphrey's Executor v. United States*, 295 U.S. 602 (1935) (For-cause removal)
- *Morrison v. Olson*, 487 U.S. 654 (1988) (Independent counsel)
- *Seila Law v. CFPB*, 591 U.S. ___ (2020) (Single-director limitation)

**Emergency Powers**

- *Korematsu v. United States*, 323 U.S. 214 (1944) (Now discredited)
- *Youngstown* (1952) (Limits on emergency domestic action)
