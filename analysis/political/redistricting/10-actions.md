# Redistricting: Actions

## For Individuals

### Learn First

**Understand the Problem**

- [ ] Read [Current State](02-current-state.md) to understand gerrymandering today
- [ ] Read [History](03-history.md) to learn how we got here
- [ ] Learn about your state's redistricting process
- [ ] Find your district at census.gov or ballotpedia.org
- [ ] Use Dave's Redistricting App to explore your state's maps

**Know Your State**

- [ ] Research who draws your state's maps (legislature vs. commission)
- [ ] Check if your state has a ballot initiative process
- [ ] Identify what reform organizations are active in your state
- [ ] Look up recent redistricting litigation in your state
- [ ] Find out when your state's next redistricting will occur

### Take Action

**Contact Representatives**

- [ ] Call your state legislators about redistricting reform
- [ ] Ask specifically: "Do you support an independent redistricting commission?"
- [ ] Follow up with written letters and emails
- [ ] Track their votes and public statements on redistricting
- [ ] Attend town halls and ask about fair maps

**Join the Movement**

- [ ] Sign up with Common Cause, League of Women Voters, or RepresentUs
- [ ] Join state-specific reform organizations (see [Resources](09-resources.md))
- [ ] Follow reform organizations on social media
- [ ] Subscribe to redistricting newsletters
- [ ] Attend local chapter meetings if available

**Spread the Word**

- [ ] Explain gerrymandering to friends and family
- [ ] Share educational content on social media
- [ ] Write letters to the editor about fair maps
- [ ] Start conversations about redistricting reform
- [ ] Use local examples to make it real

### Vote and Support

**Electoral Action**

- [ ] Research candidates' positions on redistricting reform
- [ ] Support candidates who commit to independent commissions
- [ ] Vote YES on redistricting reform ballot measures
- [ ] Support judicial candidates who will enforce fair maps
- [ ] Participate in primaries (where reform-minded candidates often emerge)

**Financial Support**

- [ ] Donate to redistricting reform organizations
- [ ] Contribute to ballot initiative campaigns in your state
- [ ] Support candidates who champion fair maps
- [ ] Fund legal organizations fighting gerrymandering

### Participate in Redistricting

**When Your State Redistricts**

- [ ] Attend public redistricting hearings
- [ ] Submit testimony about your community
- [ ] Propose your own maps using online tools
- [ ] Monitor the process and document irregularities
- [ ] Alert reform organizations to problems

**Community Mapping**

- [ ] Use Districtr to map your community of interest
- [ ] Participate in community input sessions
- [ ] Document why your community should be kept together
- [ ] Submit formal comments to redistricting body

## For Communities

### Local Reform Campaigns

**Assess the Landscape**

1. Research your state's redistricting process
2. Identify ballot initiative pathway (if available)
3. Find existing reform supporters and organizations
4. Assess political opportunities and obstacles
5. Determine resources available

**Build a Coalition**

1. Recruit bipartisan leadership (essential)
2. Engage League of Women Voters, civic organizations
3. Connect with business leaders frustrated by dysfunction
4. Partner with academic institutions
5. Build diverse, visible coalition

**Choose Your Strategy**

| Pathway | When to Use |
|---------|-------------|
| **Ballot initiative** | State allows initiatives; public support exists |
| **Legislative push** | Reform-friendly legislators; political opportunity |
| **Court challenge** | Strong state constitutional provisions |
| **Public pressure** | Build foundation for future action |

## For Advocates

### Running a Reform Campaign

**Phase 1: Preparation**

1. Research legal requirements and pathways
2. Study successful models (California, Michigan, Arizona)
3. Build core team and leadership structure
4. Develop budget and fundraising plan
5. Craft messaging strategy with polling/focus groups

**Phase 2: Coalition Building**

1. Recruit bipartisan, diverse leadership
2. Engage major civic organizations
3. Build business community support
4. Connect with academic and legal experts
5. Document coalition publicly

**Phase 3: Ballot Campaign** (if applicable)

1. Draft initiative language with legal review
2. Build signature-gathering operation
3. Gather required signatures plus buffer
4. Launch public campaign
5. GOTV for election

**Phase 4: Legislative Campaign** (if applicable)

1. Identify legislative champions
2. Draft model legislation
3. Build constituent pressure
4. Lobby individual legislators
5. Push for floor vote

**Phase 5: Implementation**

1. Work with officials on commission setup
2. Public education about new system
3. Monitor commissioner selection
4. Defend against attacks
5. Document success for other states

### Advocacy Best Practices

**Messaging Principles**

- Lead with anti-corruption, fairness
- Use specific local examples
- Make it bipartisan ("both sides do it, let's stop both")
- Focus on outcomes (competitive elections, accountability)
- Keep it simple ("Politicians shouldn't pick their voters")

**Coalition Management**

- Maintain bipartisan leadership
- Give partners ownership and visibility
- Regular communication and coordination
- Clear roles and responsibilities
- Celebrate wins together

**Working with Media**

- Build relationships with political reporters
- Provide clear data and visuals
- Offer compelling spokespeople
- Respond quickly to developments
- Submit regular op-eds

**Legal Strategy**

- Strong legal review of all materials
- Anticipate challenges during drafting
- Build record for potential litigation
- Coordinate with litigation organizations
- Prepare for all outcomes

**Ballot Initiative States**

| State | Status | Priority |
|-------|--------|----------|
| Nevada | Potential initiative | High |
| Oregon | Potential initiative | Medium |

**Legislative States**

| State | Strategy | Timeline |
|-------|----------|----------|
| States with reform-friendly governors | Legislative push | Near-term |
| States with divided government | Bipartisan coalition | Medium-term |
| States with one-party control | Build foundation | Long-term |

**Litigation States**

| State | Grounds | Status |
|-------|---------|--------|
| States with strong constitutional provisions | State court challenge | Assess |
| States with VRA violations | Federal litigation | Ongoing |

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Reform measures passed | Track ballot results |

- Efficiency gap of maps (target: <8%)

## State-Specific Action Guides

**California, Arizona, Michigan, Colorado, etc.**

2. Document and share successes

4. Support expansion/improvement

### States with Active Reform Efforts

**Pennsylvania, Wisconsin, Ohio, etc.**
**Actions**

2. Support litigation efforts
3. Build pressure on legislature
4. Prepare for 2030 redistricting

**Many states**
**Actions**

1. Research feasibility of reform
2. Connect with national organizations
3. Build local awareness
4. Identify potential coalition partners
5. Lay groundwork for future campaigns
