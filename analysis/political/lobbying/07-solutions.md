# Lobbying Reform: Solutions

## Guiding Principles

### Core Values

**Transparency**

- Public has right to know who's influencing government
- Disclosure should be comprehensive and accessible
- Real-time reporting preferred
- Searchable, machine-readable data

**Accountability**

- Meaningful enforcement required
- Penalties must deter violations
- Independent oversight essential
- Public accountability mechanisms

**Equality of Voice**

- Reduce resource imbalance
- Protect legitimate advocacy
- Distinguish corporate from citizen lobbying
- Level playing field

### Criteria for Evaluating Solutions

| Criterion | Question |
|-----------|----------|
| Effectiveness | Will it reduce undue influence? |
| Constitutionality | Does it survive First Amendment scrutiny? |
| Enforceability | Can it be implemented and enforced? |
| Unintended consequences | Does it harm legitimate advocacy? |
| Political feasibility | Can it pass and survive? |

## Disclosure Reform

### Expand Registration Requirements

**Close the Shadow Lobbying Loophole**

| Current Problem | Solution |
|-----------------|----------|
| 20% time threshold | Eliminate or reduce to 5% |
| "Strategic advice" exemption | Include all paid influence |
| Limited "covered officials" | Expand to all government officials |
| Grassroots exemption | Require disclosure of astroturf |

**New Registration Categories**

| Category | Coverage |
|----------|----------|
| Strategic consultants | All paid government influence advisors |
| Coalition managers | Those coordinating lobbying coalitions |
| Astroturf organizers | Paid grassroots campaign managers |
| Think tank advocates | Those engaging officials on funded research |

### Enhance Disclosure

**Real-Time Reporting**

| Information | Timeline |
|-------------|----------|
| Official contacts | Within 48 hours |
| Issues lobbied | Weekly |
| Spending | Monthly |
| Campaign contributions | Within 24 hours |

**Expanded Information Requirements**

| Category | Disclosure |
|----------|------------|
| Client identity | Beneficial ownership required |
| Specific positions | What outcome sought |
| Documents provided | Materials given to officials |
| Meetings | Who met, topics discussed |

### Improve Accessibility

**User-Friendly Systems**

| Feature | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| Searchable databases | Easy public access |
| API access | Research, journalism |
| Alert systems | Notify on specific issues |
| Visualization tools | Understand relationships |

## Revolving Door Reform

### Longer Cooling-Off Periods

**Extended Bans**

| Position | Current | Proposed |
|----------|---------|----------|
| Members of Congress | 1-2 years | 5-6 years |
| Senior staff | 1 year | 3-5 years |
| Agency heads | 1-2 years | 5 years |
| Regulatory officials | 1 year | 5 years |

### Broader Coverage

**Expand Definitions**

| Current Loophole | Reform |
|------------------|--------|
| Direct lobbying only | Include "strategic advice" |
| Congress and agencies | Include White House, judiciary |
| Specific committees | All matters before former employer |
| Federal only | Include state contractor work |

### Lifetime Bans

**Permanent Restrictions**

| Activity | Ban |
|----------|-----|
| Lobbying former colleagues directly | Permanent |
| Lobbying on matters personally worked on | Permanent |
| Foreign government lobbying | Permanent |

### Pre-Employment Restrictions

**Before Government Service**

| Reform | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| Disclosure of prior lobbying | Identify conflicts |
| Recusal requirements | Prevent bias |
| Waiting periods | Break relationships |

## Ethics Enforcement

### Independent Enforcement

**Create Independent Ethics Office**

| Feature | Design |
|---------|--------|
| Independent appointment | Not by Congress |
| Subpoena power | Real investigation authority |
| Public proceedings | Transparency |
| Meaningful penalties | Deterrence |

**Enforcement Authority**

| Power | Application |
|-------|-------------|
| Investigate violations | LDA, ethics rules |
| Issue fines | Automatic for violations |
| Refer for prosecution | Criminal violations |
| Public disclosure | All findings |

### Strengthen Penalties

**Criminal Enforcement**

| Violation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Failure to register | Criminal prosecution |
| False disclosure | Felony |
| Revolving door violation | Felony + fine |
| Bribery/corruption | Aggressive prosecution |

**Civil Penalties**

| Violation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Late filing | Automatic fine |
| Incomplete disclosure | Escalating fines |
| Pattern of violation | License revocation |

### Gift and Travel Bans

**Stricter Gift Rules**

| Category | Rule |
|----------|------|
| All gifts from lobbyists | Prohibited |
| Travel funded by interests | Prohibited |
| Entertainment | Prohibited |
| Charity donations (on behalf) | Prohibited |

## Campaign Finance Connection

### Bundling Restrictions

**Address Lobbyist Fundraising**

| Reform | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| Ban lobbyist bundling | Remove fundraising incentive |
| Disclosure of bundlers | Transparency of influence |
| Aggregate limits | Cap total from lobbying interests |

### Access-Based Contribution Limits

**Restrict Pay-to-Play**

| Reform | Application |
|--------|-------------|
| Lower limits for lobbyists | Reduce direct influence |
| No contributions while lobbying | Separate roles |
| Disclosure of all giving | Including to PACs, parties |

### Public Financing

**Reduce Money Dependence**

| System | Effect on Lobbying |
|--------|-------------------|
| Public campaign funding | Less need for lobbyist money |
| Small donor matching | Alternative funding source |
| Free media time | Reduce spending need |
