# Lobbying Reform: Roadmap

## Strategic Vision

### The Goal

**A Transparent, Accountable Lobbying System**

- All paid influence disclosed
- Revolving door closed
- Enforcement meaningful
- Public can track influence
- Policy serves public interest

### Success Metrics

| Metric | Current | Target |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Registered lobbyists | ~12,000 | 50,000+ (capturing shadow lobbying) |
| Disclosure compliance | Low | 95%+ |
| Enforcement actions | Rare | Regular |
| Public trust | 17% approval | 40%+ |
| Revolving door compliance | Weak | Strong |

## Risks and Contingencies

### Political Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|------------|------------|
| Opposition lobbying | High | Counter-coalition |
| Constitutional challenge | Medium | Careful drafting |
| Weak enforcement | High | Independent structure |
| Reform capture | Medium | Vigilance |
| Public attention fades | Medium | Sustained organizing |

### Contingency Plans

**If Federal Reform Blocked**

| Alternative | Approach |
|-------------|----------|
| State-level reform | 50-state strategy |
| Executive action | Administrative reform |
| Disclosure campaigns | Voluntary transparency |
| Enforcement focus | Existing law enforcement |

**If Constitutional Challenge Succeeds**

| Response | Approach |
|----------|----------|
| Narrow reforms | Within court limits |
| Disclosure focus | Most protected |
| Voluntary standards | Industry pressure |
| Public shaming | Reputational costs |

**If Enforcement Fails**

| Response | Approach |
|----------|----------|
| Independent oversight | Remove political control |
| Automatic penalties | Reduce discretion |
| Private enforcement | Citizen suits |
| Media accountability | Public exposure |
