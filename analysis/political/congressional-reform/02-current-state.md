**Substantive Legislation**

| Category | Trend |

| Committee mark-ups | Declining |

## Senate Dysfunction

### The Filibuster Reality

**Historical vs. Current Usage**

| Period | Cloture Motions Filed |
|--------|----------------------|
| 1917-1970 | ~50 total |
| 2007-2008 | 139 |
| 2019-2020 | 328 |
| 2021-2022 | 273 |

**Effect on Legislation**

- 60-vote threshold for most bills
- Simple majority insufficient
- Minority veto on legislation
- Gridlock on contentious issues

### Confirmation Battles

**Judicial Nominations**

| Category | Pre-2013 | Post-2013/2017 |
|----------|----------|----------------|
| Lower courts | 60 votes | Simple majority |
| Supreme Court | 60 votes | Simple majority |
| Average time to confirm | Weeks | Months to years |

**Executive Branch**

- Long vacancies in key positions
- Acting officials used extensively
- Confirmation battles normalized
- Partisan votes on nominees

### Individual Senator Power

**Holds**

| Practice | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| Anonymous holds | Secret blocking of nominations/bills |
| Blanket holds | One senator stops entire category |
| Leverage use | Extort concessions |
| Tradition protection | Rules enable abuse |

**Unanimous Consent**

- Single senator can object
- Routine business requires agreement
- Used to extract concessions
- Slowdown tactic

## House Dysfunction

### Majority Party Dominance

**Rules Committee Control**

| Function | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| Closed rules | No amendments allowed |
| Structured rules | Limited amendments |
| Time limits | Minimal debate |
| Content control | Leadership decides |

**Speaker's Power**

- Controls floor schedule
- Determines what gets votes
- Committee assignments
- Fundraising power over members

### Minority Powerlessness

**Limited Rights**

| Aspect | Reality |
|--------|---------|
| Amendments | Rarely allowed |
| Debate time | Minimal |
| Committee process | Bypassed |
| Alternatives | Blocked from floor |

### Increased Polarization

**Party Unity Voting**

| Period | Average Party Unity |
|--------|---------------------|
| 1970s | ~60% |
| 1990s | ~80% |
| 2010s | ~90%+ |

**Bipartisan Votes**

- Rare on substantive legislation
- Mostly procedural or ceremonial
- Compromise seen as weakness
- Primary threats for cooperation

## Committee System Decline

### Reduced Authority

**Traditional Role**

- Develop legislation
- Mark up bills
- Build bipartisan coalitions
- Develop expertise

**Current Reality**

| Function | Status |
|----------|--------|
| Bill drafting | Leadership-driven |
| Mark-ups | Often bypassed |
| Amendments | Controlled or blocked |
| Expertise | Undervalued |

### Oversight Decline

**Hearings**

| Metric | Trend |
|--------|-------|
| Oversight hearings | Declining |
| Days in session | Reduced |
| Subpoena compliance | Increasingly defied |
| Bipartisan investigations | Rare |

**Executive Accountability**

- Testimonial requests ignored
- Document production delayed
- Subpoenas litigated
- Criminal referrals unpursued

## Institutional Capacity

### Staff Reductions

**Congressional Staff Levels**

| Year | Personal Office Staff | Committee Staff |
|------|----------------------|-----------------|
| 1979 | ~11,500 | ~3,000 |
| 2015 | ~9,000 | ~2,000 |
| Trend | -22% | -33% |

**Effect**

- Less policy expertise
- More reliance on lobbyists
- Less constituent service capacity
- Institutional memory lost

### Support Agency Cuts

**Congressional Research Service**

| Metric | Trend |
|--------|-------|
| Staff | Declining |
| Reports produced | Declining |
| Response time | Increasing |

**Government Accountability Office**

| Metric | Trend |
|--------|-------|
| Staff | Reduced |
| Audits | Fewer |
| Savings identified | Reduced |

**Office of Technology Assessment**

- Eliminated 1995
- No replacement
- Technology expertise outsourced
- Industry fills vacuum

## Ethics and Transparency

### Financial Conflicts

**Stock Trading**

| Issue | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Member trading | Legal with disclosure |
| Timing around votes | Documented patterns |
| Enforcement | Minimal |
| Reform attempts | Stalled repeatedly |

**Outside Income**

- Limited but permitted
- Book deals, speeches
- Post-employment lucrative
- Conflicts of interest

### Campaign Finance Time

**Fundraising Burden**

| Chamber | Recommended Call Time |
|---------|----------------------|
| House | 4+ hours/day |
| Senate | 2-3 hours/day |

**Effect**

- Less legislating time
- Donor access prioritized
- Policy shaped by donors
- Public business neglected

### Revolving Door

**Post-Congressional Employment**

| Path | Prevalence |
|------|------------|
| Lobbying | ~50% of departed members |
| Corporate boards | Common |
| Consulting | Common |
| Advocacy groups | Some |

**Effect**

- Votes influenced by future employment
- Industry relationships cultivated
- Public interest secondary
- Trust undermined
