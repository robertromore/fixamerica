# Congressional Reform: Roadmap

## Strategic Vision

### The Goal

**A Functional Congress**

- Majority can legislate
- Minority can participate and be heard
- Oversight is effective
- Ethics are enforced
- Public trusts the institution

### Success Metrics

| Metric | Current | Target |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Congressional approval | ~17% | 40%+ |
| Major legislation passed | Rare | Regular |
| Regular order used | Exception | Standard |
| Government shutdowns | Frequent | None |
| Bipartisan bills | Rare | Common |
| Oversight hearings | Declining | Increasing |
| Staff levels | Reduced | Adequate |

## Phase 1: Immediate Actions (2024-2026)

### What Can Happen Now

**House Rules (Majority Decision)**

| Reform | Likelihood | Impact |
|--------|------------|--------|
| 72-hour rule | Medium | Transparency |
| Committee empowerment | Low-Medium | Deliberation |
| More open amendments | Low | Minority participation |
| Modernization recommendations | Existing | Ongoing improvements |

**Senate Rules (With Political Will)**

| Reform | Likelihood | Impact |
|--------|------------|--------|
| Talking filibuster | Medium | Makes obstruction costly |
| Carve-outs for specific issues | Medium | Targeted majority rule |
| Holds reform | Low-Medium | Reduced obstruction |

**Administrative Changes**

| Change | Requirement | Impact |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| Increased staff funding | Appropriations | Capacity |
| CRS/GAO/CBO increases | Appropriations | Expertise |
| Ethics training | Internal | Culture |

### Building Momentum

**Organizational Development**

- Strengthen reform organizations
- Build bipartisan reform caucus
- Develop detailed proposals
- Create messaging infrastructure

**Electoral Strategy**

- Support reform champions
- Primary worst obstructors
- Make reform an issue
- Build public pressure

**Milestone Checklist (Phase 1)**

- [ ] Modernization recommendations implemented
- [ ] Staff funding increased
- [ ] Support agency budgets restored
- [ ] Reform caucus established
- [ ] Public awareness campaign launched
- [ ] Specific reform proposals developed

## Dependencies

### What Must Happen First

```text

Phase 1 Prerequisites:

- Reform champions elected
- Public awareness built
- Organizations strengthened
- Proposals developed

Phase 2 Prerequisites:

- Electoral mandate for reform
- Sufficient Senate votes
- House majority commitment
- External pressure sustained

Phase 3 Prerequisites:

- Phase 2 reforms successful
- Public support strong
- Opposition weakened
- Institutional will

Phase 4 Prerequisites:

- Cultural shift begun
- New norms established
- Opposition marginalized
- Public trust rebuilding

```text

## Risks and Contingencies

### Political Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|------------|------------|
| Reform party loses power | Medium | Entrench changes |
| Leadership resistance | High | Electoral pressure |
| Public attention fades | Medium | Sustained organizing |
| Backlash | Medium | Gradual implementation |

### Contingency Plans

**If Senate Reform Blocked**

- Focus on House reform
- Build public pressure
- Wait for new Senate composition
- Use carve-outs where possible

**If Ethics Reform Blocked**

- State-level pressure
- Disclosure advocacy
- Public shaming
- Electoral consequences

**If Capacity Restoration Blocked**

- Outside fellowship programs
- Foundation support
- Private sector partnerships
- Volunteer expertise
