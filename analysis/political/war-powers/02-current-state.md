# War Powers Reform: Current State

## The Problem Today

### Constitutional Framework vs. Reality

**What the Constitution Says**

| Power | Branch | Constitutional Basis |
|-------|--------|---------------------|
| Declare war | Congress | Article I, Section 8 |
| Raise and support armies | Congress | Article I, Section 8 |
| Make rules for armed forces | Congress | Article I, Section 8 |
| Appropriate military funds | Congress | Article I, Section 8 |
| Commander-in-Chief | President | Article II, Section 2 |

**Current Practice**

| Action | Constitutional Requirement | Actual Practice |
|--------|---------------------------|-----------------|
| Major war | Congressional declaration | Presidential decision |
| Extended conflict | Congressional authorization | Executive continuation |
| Drone strikes | Unclear | Executive sole authority |
| Covert operations | Congressional notification | Limited "Gang of Eight" |
| Nuclear launch | None specified | Presidential sole authority |
| Cyber warfare | Unclear | Executive sole authority |

## Impact Assessment

### On Constitutional Structure

**Erosion of Separation of Powers**

| Era | Congressional Role | Executive Role |
|-----|-------------------|----------------|
| 1789-1945 | Declares wars | Conducts wars |
| 1945-1973 | Funds wars | Initiates and conducts |
| 1973-2001 | Occasionally objects | Initiates and conducts |
| 2001-present | Authorizes broadly | Total control |
