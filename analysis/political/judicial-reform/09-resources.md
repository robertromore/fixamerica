## Essential Reading

### Books

**Historical**

- Leuchtenburg, William E. *The Supreme Court Reborn: The Constitutional Revolution in the Age of Roosevelt*. Oxford, 1995.
- White, G. Edward. *The American Judicial Tradition*. Oxford, 3rd ed., 2007.
- Tushnet, Mark. *A Court Divided: The Rehnquist Court and the Future of Constitutional Law*. Norton, 2005.

**Contemporary Analysis**

- Epps, Daniel and Ganesh Sitaraman. "How to Save the Supreme Court." *Yale Law Journal* 129 (2019). (Term limits and balanced court proposals)
- Fishkin, Joseph and William Forbath. *The Anti-Oligarchy Constitution*. Harvard, 2022.
- Greenhouse, Linda. *Justice on the Brink*. Random House, 2021.
- Liptak, Adam. *To Have and to Hold: The Supreme Court and the Battle for Same-Sex Marriage*. (Times journalism on modern Court)

**Court Structure**

- Calabresi, Steven and James Lindgren. "Term Limits for the Supreme Court." *Harvard Journal of Law & Public Policy* 29 (2006).
- Carrington, Paul and Roger Cramton. *Reforming the Court*. Carolina Academic Press, 2006.
- Grove, Tara Leigh. "The Origins (and Fragility) of Judicial Independence." *Vanderbilt Law Review* 71 (2018).

**Ethics and Accountability**

- Bannon, Alicia. "Choosing State Judges: A Plan for Reform." Brennan Center, 2018.
- Wheeler, Russell. "Judicial Ethics, Judicial Immunity, and Judicial Legitimacy." Brookings, 2022.

### Academic Articles

**Term Limits**

- Cramton, Roger and Paul Carrington. "The Supreme Court Renewal Act: A Return to Basic Principles." (Arguing for 18-year terms)
- McGinnis, John and Michael Rappaport. "Supermajority Rules and the Judicial Confirmation Process." *William & Mary Law Review* 45 (2003).

**Court Size**

- Epstein, Lee et al. "The Norm of Prior Judicial Experience and Its Consequences for Career Diversity on the U.S. Supreme Court." *California Law Review* 91 (2003).
- Devins, Neal and Lawrence Baum. "Split Definitive: How Party Polarization Turned the Supreme Court into a Partisan Court." *Supreme Court Review* (2016).

**Ethics**

- Lubet, Steven. "Disqualifying the Supreme Court." Judicature, 2022.
- Green, Bruce and Rebecca Roiphe. "Regulating the Behavior of Judges." *Hofstra Law Review* 50 (2022).

## Legal Resources

### Key Cases

**Judicial Power**

- *Marbury v. Madison*, 5 U.S. 137 (1803) (Established judicial review)
- *Ex parte McCardle*, 74 U.S. 506 (1869) (Congress can limit Court jurisdiction)
- *United States v. Will*, 449 U.S. 200 (1980) (Judicial compensation)

**Ethics and Recusal**

- *Caperton v. A.T. Massey Coal*, 556 U.S. 868 (2009) (Due process requires recusal)
- *Liteky v. United States*, 510 U.S. 540 (1994) (Recusal standards)

**Term and Tenure**

- *Northern Pipeline v. Marathon Pipe Line*, 458 U.S. 50 (1982) (Article III tenure)
- *Nguyen v. United States*, 539 U.S. 69 (2003) (Proper composition of courts)

### Statutes

- Judiciary Act of 1789, 1 Stat. 73
- 28 U.S.C. ยง 1 (Supreme Court composition)
- 28 U.S.C. ยง 455 (Disqualification of justice, judge, or magistrate)
- Ethics in Government Act, 5 U.S.C. App.
- Code of Conduct for United States Judges

### Constitution

- Article III (Judicial power, tenure)
- Article I, Section 8 (Congressional power over courts)
- Article II, Section 2 (Appointment power)
