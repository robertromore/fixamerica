# Judicial Reform: Actions

## For All Citizens

### Educate Yourself

**Understand the Court**

- Read about how the Supreme Court works
- Learn the history of court reforms (it's been done before)
- Understand the confirmation process
- Follow nonpartisan court coverage (SCOTUSblog)

**Know the Issues**

- Ethics: Current scandals and lack of accountability
- Term limits: How they would work
- Court size: Historical changes
- Transparency: What's hidden and why

### Engage with Representatives

**Contact Congress**

- Call senators about judicial nominations
- Demand support for ethics legislation
- Ask about transparency reforms
- Support confirmation process reform

**Key Messages**:

- "Supreme Court justices should follow the same ethics rules as other federal judges"
- "I support term limits for Supreme Court justices"
- "The confirmation process needs reform to reduce partisan warfare"
- "The Court should allow cameras in the courtroom"

### Support Reform Organizations

**Donate and Volunteer**:

- Fix the Court (ethics and transparency focus)
- Take Back the Court (structural reform)
- Demand Justice (progressive judicial strategy)
- Brennan Center for Justice (research and advocacy)

**Amplify Their Work**:

- Share reports and analysis
- Write letters to the editor
- Attend events and webinars
- Connect with local media

### Vote and Advocate

**Consider Court Issues in Voting**:

- Ask candidates their positions on judicial reform
- Support candidates committed to ethics reform
- Consider Senate races (confirmation power)
- Participate in primaries

**Local Action**:

- Support state judicial reform efforts
- Learn about state court systems
- Advocate for merit selection
- Monitor local judicial elections

## For Professionals

### Lawyers

**Professional Responsibility**:

- Speak out on ethics issues
- Support bar association reform positions
- Pro bono work for court access
- Mentor diverse law students

**Advocacy**:

- Write articles on reform
- Testify before legislature
- Sign amicus briefs
- Join ACS or engage with Federalist Society constructively

**Within the System**:

- Model ethical behavior
- Report misconduct
- Diversify hiring and mentoring
- Support judicial diversity

### Judges

**Ethics Leadership**:

- Exceed minimum requirements
- Transparent about conflicts
- Support ethics reform
- Mentor for diversity

**Institutional Voice**:

- Speak on reform (within limits)
- Support Judicial Conference reform positions
- Engage with bar associations
- Model transparency

### Legal Academics

**Research**:

- Study reform proposals
- Document problems
- Evaluate state experiments
- Develop new proposals

**Public Engagement**:

- Write for general audiences
- Media commentary
- Congressional testimony
- Train next generation

### Journalists

**Coverage**:

- Investigate ethics issues
- Explain reform proposals
- Cover confirmation process critically
- Document court operations

**Accountability**:

- FOIA requests for court information
- Track financial disclosures
- Report on shadow docket
- Follow dark money in confirmations

## Specific Campaign Actions

### Ethics Reform Campaign

**Support Legislation**:

- Supreme Court Ethics, Recusal, and Transparency Act
- Contact senators to co-sponsor
- Testify at hearings
- Build bipartisan support

**Public Pressure**:

- Publicize ethics violations
- Demand accountability
- Social media campaigns
- Letters to the editor

### Term Limits Campaign

**Constitutional Amendment**:

- Support introduction of amendment
- Advocate in state legislatures
- Build public understanding
- Long-term commitment

**Statutory Alternative**:

- Support Supreme Court Term Limits Act
- Understand constitutional arguments
- Prepare for litigation

### Transparency Campaign

**Cameras in the Courtroom**:

- Support Cameras in the Courtroom Act
- Public education on benefits
- Counter Court's objections
- Build media coalition

**Disclosure Requirements**:

- Support enhanced financial disclosure
- Track and publicize current disclosures
- Demand compliance

## Building Long-Term Power

### Coalition Building

**Find Common Ground**:

- Ethics reform has bipartisan appeal
- Transparency benefits everyone
- Functioning courts serve all
- Legitimacy matters across spectrum

**Engage Diverse Constituencies**:

- Civil rights community (court impacts)
- Business community (predictability)
- Religious communities (fairness)
- Veterans (institutional integrity)

### State-Level Action

**Merit Selection**:

- Support in states without it
- Strengthen in states with it
- Use as model for federal

**Term Limits**:

- Enact in state courts
- Build experience and evidence
- Create momentum for federal

**Ethics Enforcement**:

- Strengthen state judicial conduct commissions
- Model effective enforcement
- Share lessons

### Long-Term Engagement

**Institutional Building**:

- Support reform organizations
- Train next generation
- Build research capacity
- Maintain coalition

**Responding to Opportunities**:

- Have legislation ready
- Build public support in advance
- Move quickly when windows open
- Learn from setbacks

## Key Questions for Candidates

1. "Do you support a binding ethics code for Supreme Court justices?"
2. "Should Supreme Court justices have term limits?"
3. "Do you support cameras in the Supreme Court?"
4. "How would you reform the confirmation process?"
5. "What is your position on Supreme Court expansion?"
