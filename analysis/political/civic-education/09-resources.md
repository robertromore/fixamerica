## Essential Reading

### Books

**Civic Education Research**

- Campbell, David E. *Why We Vote: How Schools and Communities Shape Our Civic Life*. Princeton University Press, 2006.
- Levine, Peter. *We Are the Ones We Have Been Waiting For: The Promise of Civic Renewal in America*. Oxford University Press, 2013.
- Galston, William. *Liberal Purposes: Goods, Virtues, and Diversity in the Liberal State*. Cambridge University Press, 1991.
- Macedo, Stephen et al. *Democracy at Risk: How Political Choices Undermine Citizen Participation and What We Can Do About It*. Brookings, 2005.

**Media Literacy**

- Wineburg, Sam et al. *Why Learn History (When It's Already on Your Phone)*. University of Chicago Press, 2018.
- boyd, danah. *It's Complicated: The Social Lives of Networked Teens*. Yale University Press, 2014.
- Breakstone, Joel et al. *Students' Civic Online Reasoning*. Stanford History Education Group, 2019.

**Classroom Practice**

- Hess, Diana and Paula McAvoy. *The Political Classroom: Evidence and Ethics in Democratic Education*. Routledge, 2014.
- Journell, Wayne. *Teaching Politics in Secondary Education*. SUNY Press, 2017.
- Kahne, Joseph and Ellen Middaugh. "Digital Media Shapes Youth Participation in Politics." *Phi Delta Kappan* 94, no. 3 (2012).

### Key Reports

**National Reports**

- *Guardian of Democracy: The Civic Mission of Schools*. Leonore Annenberg Institute, 2011.
- *Educating for American Democracy: Excellence in History and Civics for All Learners*. iCivics, 2021.
- *The Republic Is (Still) at Risk*. CivXNow Coalition, 2018.
- *Action Civics: A Declaration for Reinvigorating Our Democratic Traditions*. Generation Citizen, 2015.

**Research Reports**

- CIRCLE. Various reports on youth civic engagement.
- NAEP. *The Nation's Report Card: Civics*. Various years.
- Education Commission of the States. *50-State Comparison: Civic Education Policies*.

### Academic Articles

**Effective Practices**

- Kahne, Joseph et al. "What Works in Civic Education?" *Peabody Journal of Education* 91 (2016).
- Levinson, Meira. "The Civic Empowerment Gap: Defining the Problem and Locating Solutions." *Handbook of Research on Civic Engagement in Youth* (2010).
- Niemi, Richard and Jane Junn. *Civic Education: What Makes Students Learn*. Yale University Press, 1998.

**Media Literacy**

- Wineburg, Sam and Sarah McGrew. "Lateral Reading: Reading Less and Learning More When Evaluating Digital Information." Stanford History Education Group, 2017.
- McGrew, Sarah et al. "Can Students Evaluate Online Sources?" *Theory & Research in Social Education* 46, no. 2 (2018).

**Action Civics**

- Levinson, Meira. "Action Civics in the Classroom." *Social Education* 78, no. 2 (2014).
- Pope, Alice et al. "Closing the Civic Empowerment Gap." *Social Education* 75, no. 5 (2011).
