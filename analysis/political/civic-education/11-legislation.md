# Civic Education: Legislation

## Overview

This document provides model legislation to strengthen civic education at state and federal levels. Because education is primarily a state function, most detailed requirements should be enacted at the state level, with federal legislation providing support, resources, and incentives.

## Federal Legislation

### Civics Secures Democracy Act

**Purpose**: Provide federal support for civic education without mandating curriculum.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SEC. 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Civics Secures Democracy Act".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds that—
(1) Civic education is essential for the functioning of a
    democratic republic;
(2) Civic knowledge among American students and adults has
    declined significantly;
(3) The information environment has changed dramatically,
    requiring new skills;
(4) Civic education improves civic engagement and participation;
(5) Federal support can strengthen state and local civic
    education efforts.

SEC. 3. DEFINITIONS.

In this Act:
(a) "Civic education" means instruction in—
    (1) the Constitution and founding documents;
    (2) government structure and processes at all levels;
    (3) rights and responsibilities of citizenship;
    (4) history and traditions of American democracy;
    (5) civic participation and engagement;
    (6) media literacy and information evaluation.

(b) "Evidence-based" means supported by research meeting standards
    established by the What Works Clearinghouse or equivalent.

SEC. 4. AMERICAN HISTORY AND CIVICS EDUCATION GRANT PROGRAM.

(a) AUTHORIZATION.—There is authorized to be appropriated
    $1,000,000,000 annually for grants to support civic education.

(b) ALLOCATION.—Funds shall be allocated as follows:
    (1) 60 percent for formula grants to states based on
        student population;
    (2) 30 percent for competitive grants to states, districts,
        and nonprofit organizations;
    (3) 10 percent for research, evaluation, and national
        activities.

(c) USE OF FUNDS.—States and grantees may use funds for:
    (1) Professional development for teachers;
    (2) Curriculum development and implementation;
    (3) Instructional materials;
    (4) Action civics and service learning programs;
    (5) Media literacy instruction;
    (6) Community partnerships;
    (7) Assessment development;
    (8) Research and evaluation.

(d) STATE APPLICATIONS.—States applying for formula grants shall
    submit plans describing:
    (1) Current state of civic education;
    (2) Goals for improvement;
    (3) Strategies for achieving goals;
    (4) Plans for reaching underserved populations;
    (5) Evaluation methods.

SEC. 5. NATIONAL CIVIC EDUCATION COMMISSION.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—There is established a National Civic Education
    Commission within the Department of Education.

(b) COMPOSITION.—The Commission shall be composed of 15 members
    appointed by the Secretary of Education, including—
    (1) educators with civic education expertise;
    (2) researchers in civic education;
    (3) state education agency representatives;
    (4) civic education organization representatives;
    (5) representatives of diverse communities.

(c) DUTIES.—The Commission shall:
    (1) Identify effective practices in civic education;
    (2) Develop resources for states and districts;
    (3) Coordinate federal civic education activities;
    (4) Report to Congress on the state of civic education.

SEC. 6. NAEP CIVICS ENHANCEMENT.

(a) FREQUENCY.—The National Assessment of Educational Progress
    in civics shall be administered at grades 4, 8, and 12 every
    two years.

(b) STATE-LEVEL REPORTING.—NAEP civics shall provide state-level
    results for all participating states.

(c) FRAMEWORK UPDATE.—The NAEP Civics Framework shall be updated
    to reflect current challenges including media literacy.

SEC. 7. TEACHER PREPARATION.

(a) GRANTS.—The Secretary shall make grants to institutions of
    higher education to strengthen civic education in teacher
    preparation programs.

(b) USE OF FUNDS.—Grants may be used for:
    (1) Developing civic education courses for teacher candidates;
    (2) Training faculty in civic education pedagogy;
    (3) Integrating media literacy into preparation programs;
    (4) Creating field experiences in civic education.

SEC. 8. ACCOUNTABILITY AND TRANSPARENCY.

(a) ANNUAL REPORTS.—States receiving funds shall report annually
    on civic education indicators.

(b) EVALUATION.—The Secretary shall conduct a comprehensive
    evaluation of program effectiveness after 5 years.

(c) DISSEMINATION.—The Secretary shall disseminate effective
    practices and lessons learned.
```

### Media Literacy in Education Act

**Purpose**: Support media literacy instruction in schools.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SEC. 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Media Literacy in Education Act".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds that—
(1) The digital information environment poses unprecedented
    challenges for citizens;
(2) Misinformation and disinformation threaten democratic
    discourse;
(3) Young people are particularly vulnerable to online
    manipulation;
(4) Media literacy skills can be taught effectively;
(5) Schools are well-positioned to provide media literacy
    education.

SEC. 3. DEFINITIONS.

"Media literacy" means the ability to—
(a) Access information from diverse sources;
(b) Analyze and evaluate information and sources;
(c) Create and share information responsibly;
(d) Understand the role of media in society;
(e) Apply critical thinking to media messages.

SEC. 4. MEDIA LITERACY GRANTS.

(a) AUTHORIZATION.—There are authorized to be appropriated
    $200,000,000 annually for media literacy education.

(b) STATE GRANTS.—The Secretary shall make grants to states for:
    (1) Developing media literacy standards and curriculum;
    (2) Professional development for teachers;
    (3) Instructional materials;
    (4) Assessment development;
    (5) Research and evaluation.

(c) COMPETITIVE GRANTS.—The Secretary shall make competitive
    grants to districts and organizations for innovative
    approaches to media literacy instruction.

SEC. 5. NATIONAL MEDIA LITERACY RESOURCE CENTER.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—The Secretary shall establish a National
    Media Literacy Resource Center.

(b) FUNCTIONS.—The Center shall:
    (1) Develop and curate media literacy resources;
    (2) Provide technical assistance to states and districts;
    (3) Coordinate with researchers and practitioners;
    (4) Disseminate effective practices;
    (5) Update resources as the media environment changes.
```

## State Model Legislation

### Comprehensive Civic Education Act

**Purpose**: Establish comprehensive civic education requirements at the state level.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "[State] Comprehensive Civic
Education Act."

SECTION 2. FINDINGS AND PURPOSE.

(a) FINDINGS.—The Legislature finds that:
    (1) Civic education is essential for democratic
        self-governance;
    (2) Students in [State] receive inadequate civic education;
    (3) Civic knowledge and skills can be improved through
        quality instruction;
    (4) Strengthening civic education is a public priority.

(b) PURPOSE.—The purpose of this Act is to ensure all students
    in [State] receive comprehensive, high-quality civic
    education.

SECTION 3. CIVIC EDUCATION REQUIREMENTS.

(a) COURSE REQUIREMENT.—Beginning with the class of [year],
    all students shall complete at least:
    (1) One full-year course in United States history, including
        the Constitution and founding documents;
    (2) One semester course in United States government and
        civics;
    (3) One semester course in [State] history and government.

(b) CIVIC LEARNING STANDARDS.—The [State Board of Education]
    shall adopt comprehensive civic learning standards that
    include:
    (1) Knowledge of Constitution, government structure, and
        democratic processes;
    (2) Skills in information evaluation, deliberation, and
        civic participation;
    (3) Dispositions including civic responsibility and
        commitment to democratic values.

(c) MEDIA LITERACY.—The civic learning standards shall include
    media literacy standards addressing:
    (1) Evaluation of information sources;
    (2) Identification of bias and misinformation;
    (3) Responsible creation and sharing of information;
    (4) Understanding of media and technology influence.

SECTION 4. EXPERIENTIAL CIVIC LEARNING.

(a) ACTION CIVICS.—Each district shall provide opportunities
    for action civics, in which students:
    (1) Identify a community or policy issue;
    (2) Research the issue;
    (3) Develop and implement an action plan;
    (4) Reflect on their learning and experience.

(b) SERVICE LEARNING.—The [State Board of Education] shall
    develop guidelines for quality service learning that
    connects community service with academic content.

(c) CIVIC ENGAGEMENT OPPORTUNITIES.—Each school shall provide
    opportunities for authentic civic engagement, which may
    include:
    (1) Student government with meaningful responsibilities;
    (2) Community partnerships;
    (3) Simulations and competitions;
    (4) Interaction with public officials.

SECTION 5. ASSESSMENT.

(a) CIVIC KNOWLEDGE ASSESSMENT.—The [State Board of Education]
    shall develop or adopt an assessment of civic knowledge
    to be administered to all students in grade [8 and/or 11].

(b) ACCOUNTABILITY.—Civic education results shall be included
    in school and district report cards.

(c) CONTINUOUS IMPROVEMENT.—Assessment results shall be used
    to identify needs and improve instruction.

SECTION 6. TEACHER PREPARATION AND PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT.

(a) CERTIFICATION.—Teachers of civics and government courses
    shall hold endorsement in social studies with specific
    preparation in civic education.

(b) PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT.—The [Department of Education]
    shall provide ongoing professional development in:
    (1) Civic content knowledge;
    (2) Discussion and deliberation facilitation;
    (3) Media literacy instruction;
    (4) Action civics implementation.

(c) TEACHER PROTECTION.—Teachers shall be supported in teaching
    controversial issues in accordance with professional
    standards and district guidelines.

SECTION 7. SEAL OF CIVIC EXCELLENCE.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—There is established the [State] Seal of
    Civic Excellence.

(b) REQUIREMENTS.—A student may earn the Seal by:
    (1) Completing required civic education coursework with
        distinction;
    (2) Participating in service learning or action civics;
    (3) Demonstrating civic engagement; and
    (4) Passing an enhanced civic knowledge assessment.

(c) RECOGNITION.—The Seal shall be affixed to the diploma and
    transcript of qualifying students.

SECTION 8. FUNDING.

(a) APPROPRIATION.—There is appropriated from the General Fund
    $[amount] annually for civic education.

(b) USE OF FUNDS.—Funds shall be used for:
    (1) Professional development;
    (2) Curriculum and materials;
    (3) Assessment;
    (4) Action civics programs;
    (5) Administration.

SECTION 9. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act shall take effect on [date], with full implementation
required by [date].
```

### Media Literacy Standards Act

**Purpose**: Require media literacy instruction in schools.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "[State] Media Literacy Standards
Act."

SECTION 2. MEDIA LITERACY STANDARDS.

(a) ADOPTION.—The [State Board of Education] shall adopt media
    literacy standards for grades K-12.

(b) CONTENT.—Standards shall include:
    (1) Evaluating source credibility;
    (2) Distinguishing fact from opinion;
    (3) Identifying bias and perspective;
    (4) Understanding how information spreads online;
    (5) Recognizing misinformation and disinformation;
    (6) Creating and sharing information responsibly.

(c) INTEGRATION.—Media literacy shall be integrated across
    subject areas, with particular emphasis in social studies,
    English language arts, and library/media.

SECTION 3. IMPLEMENTATION.

(a) CURRICULUM.—The [Department of Education] shall provide
    model curriculum and resources for media literacy
    instruction.

(b) PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT.—The [Department] shall provide
    professional development for teachers in media literacy.

(c) TIMELINE.—Full implementation shall be required by [date].

SECTION 4. ASSESSMENT.

Media literacy skills shall be incorporated into state
assessments in relevant subjects.
```

## Regulatory Framework

### State Board of Education Rules

Model regulatory provisions for implementing civic education legislation:

1. **Standards Development**: Process for developing and updating civic learning standards
2. **Curriculum Guidance**: Guidelines for curriculum selection and development
3. **Assessment Specifications**: Requirements for civic education assessments
4. **Teacher Certification**: Endorsement requirements for civics teachers
5. **Action Civics Guidelines**: Standards for quality action civics programs
6. **Accountability**: Inclusion of civics in school and district accountability

## Legal Considerations

### Federal Role

- Education is primarily state function
- Federal role is support, resources, incentives
- Cannot mandate curriculum
- Can condition funding on participation

### First Amendment

- Students have speech rights
- Teachers have professional latitude
- Viewpoint neutrality in public schools
- Controversy can be taught

### Local Control

- State sets requirements
- Districts implement
- Some local adaptation appropriate
- Balance state standards with local flexibility

## Loopholes, Shortcomings, and Rectification

### Potential Loopholes

| Loophole | Description | Severity |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| Shallow implementation | Requirements met minimally | High |
| Unfunded mandates | Requirements without resources | High |
| Assessment gaming | Teaching to test only | Moderate |
| Teacher avoidance | Controversy still avoided | Moderate |
| Equity gaps persist | Resources don't reach all | High |

### Shortcomings

| Issue | Impact | Root Cause |
|-------|--------|------------|
| Federal limitation | Can only incentivize, not mandate | Constitutional structure |
| Political sensitivity | Reform can be derailed | Polarization |
| Measurement difficulty | Dispositions hard to assess | Nature of civic learning |
| Teacher preparation | Takes time to build capacity | Pipeline constraints |
| Competing priorities | Civics still competes with tested subjects | Accountability system |

### Rectification Procedures

1. **Implementation Quality**
    - Detailed implementation guidelines
    - Monitoring and reporting requirements
    - Technical assistance and support
    - Continuous improvement processes

2. **Adequate Funding**
    - Dedicated appropriations
    - Per-pupil funding formulas
    - Equity weights for high-need students
    - Sustained commitment

3. **Assessment Balance**
    - Multiple measures (knowledge, skills, dispositions)
    - Low stakes for students
    - School-level accountability
    - Improvement focus

4. **Teacher Support**
    - Robust professional development
    - Clear guidelines on controversy
    - Administrative support
    - Legal protection where needed

5. **Equity Assurance**
    - Equity focus in funding
    - Targeted support for high-need schools
    - Monitoring of access and outcomes
    - Intervention for persistent gaps

### General Implementation Concerns

1. **Political Sustainability**
    - Build bipartisan support
    - Focus on nonpartisan elements
    - Avoid culture war triggers
    - Demonstrate broad benefits

2. **Gradual Implementation**
    - Phase in requirements
    - Build capacity before mandating
    - Pilot and refine
    - Learn and adjust

3. **Quality Over Compliance**
    - Emphasize effective instruction
    - Support teacher development
    - Focus on student outcomes
    - Beyond checklist compliance
