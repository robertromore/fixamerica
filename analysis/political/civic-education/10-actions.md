# Civic Education: Actions

## For All Citizens

### Support Civic Education in Your Community

**Engage with Schools**:

- Attend school board meetings
- Ask about civic education curriculum
- Support civic education budget priorities
- Volunteer in classrooms

**Questions to Ask**:

- "How much time do students spend on civics?"
- "What media literacy instruction do students receive?"
- "Are there opportunities for action civics or service learning?"
- "How are teachers prepared for civic education?"

### Model Civic Engagement

**Be a Civic Role Model**:

- Vote in all elections (including local)
- Discuss current events with young people
- Participate in community activities
- Demonstrate respectful disagreement

**Family Civic Activities**:

- Visit local government offices
- Attend city council or school board meetings together
- Watch and discuss news together
- Participate in community service as a family

### Advocate for Reform

**Contact Officials**:

- State legislators: Support civic education requirements
- School board members: Prioritize civic education
- Governor/state education agency: Standards and assessment

**Key Messages**:

- "Students need more time for civic education"
- "Media literacy should be required in schools"
- "Teachers need professional development for civic education"
- "Civic education should be included in accountability"

### Support Organizations

**Organizations to Support**:

## For Educators

### Improve Your Practice

**Professional Development**:

- Complete iCivics professional development
- Take Stanford Civic Online Reasoning training
- Attend NCSS conference or state equivalent
- Join educator communities

**Classroom Improvements**:

- Increase discussion and deliberation
- Incorporate current events
- Use action civics approaches
- Integrate media literacy

**Discussion Strategies**:

| Strategy | Description | Resource |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| Structured Academic Controversy | Deliberate on policy questions | NCSS |
| Socratic Seminar | Question-based discussion | Various |
| Fishbowl | Inner/outer circle discussion | Common |
| Four Corners | Physical movement discussion | Common |
| Civil Discourse Framework | Guidelines for tough topics | NCSS |

### Use Quality Resources

**Free Curriculum**:

- iCivics games and lessons
- Stanford civic online reasoning
- News Literacy Project Checkology
- C-SPAN Classroom
- National Archives DocsTeach

**Teaching Current Events**:

- Newsela (current events, leveled)
- NewsLit educator resources
- AllSides (media bias)
- Local news partnerships

### Advocate Within Your School

**With Administrators**:

- Request professional development time
- Advocate for civics in schedule
- Propose action civics programs
- Request community partnerships

**With Colleagues**:

- Share resources and strategies
- Collaborate across subjects
- Build professional learning community
- Support each other on controversy

### Connect with Community

**Bring Community In**:

- Invite local officials to speak
- Arrange visits from journalists
- Partner with civic organizations
- Engage community members

**Take Students Out**:

- Field trips to government offices
- Court visits
- Legislative sessions
- Community events

## For Parents

### Support Civic Learning at Home

**Conversations**:

- Discuss current events
- Explain your civic participation
- Model respectful disagreement
- Ask about what they're learning

**Activities**:

- Visit historical and civic sites
- Attend community events
- Watch documentaries together
- Read news and discuss

### Engage with Schools

**Positive Engagement**:

- Support teachers doing civic education well
- Volunteer for civic-related activities
- Attend civic events (mock trials, etc.)
- Donate to civic education programs

**Advocacy**:

- Ask about civic education curriculum
- Support professional development
- Advocate at school board for civics
- Organize with other parents

### Navigate Concerns Constructively

**If Concerned About Content**:

- Talk to teacher first to understand
- Ask about learning objectives
- Review materials yourself
- Raise concerns through appropriate channels

**Balance**:

- Supplement at home if desired
- Recognize teacher professionalism
- Focus on skills, not just content
- Support free inquiry

## For State Policymakers

### Strengthen Requirements

**Legislative Priorities**:

1. Civics course requirement (if absent)
2. Media literacy standards
3. Teacher preparation requirements
4. Professional development funding
5. Assessment inclusion

**Model Legislation**:

- Comprehensive civic education act
- Media literacy requirement
- Civic education seal program
- Funding for civic education
