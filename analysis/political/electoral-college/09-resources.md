## Legal Resources

### Constitutional Provisions

- **Article II, Section 1**: Presidential selection
- **12th Amendment**: Separate ballots for president and vice president
- **23rd Amendment**: DC electoral votes
- **Article I, Section 10, Clause 3**: Compact Clause

### Key Court Cases

**Electoral College**

- *McPherson v. Blacker*, 146 U.S. 1 (1892): States have broad discretion over elector selection
- *Ray v. Blair*, 343 U.S. 214 (1952): States can require elector pledges
- *Chiafalo v. Washington*, 140 S. Ct. 2316 (2020): States can enforce elector pledges

**Elections Generally**

- *Bush v. Gore*, 531 U.S. 98 (2000): Equal protection in recounts
- *Arizona State Legislature v. Arizona Independent Redistricting Commission*, 576 U.S. 787 (2015): "Legislature" can include direct democracy

**Compacts**

- *Virginia v. Tennessee*, 148 U.S. 503 (1893): Compact Clause interpretation
- *U.S. Steel Corp. v. Multistate Tax Commission*, 434 U.S. 452 (1978): Compact consent requirements

### Statutes

- Electoral Count Act of 1887 (3 U.S.C. §§ 1-21, as amended)
- Electoral Count Reform Act of 2022 (P.L. 117-328)
- 3 U.S.C. § 7: Meeting of electors
- State NPV compact statutes (various)
