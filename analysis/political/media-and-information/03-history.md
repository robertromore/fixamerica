# Media and Information: History

## Origins

### The Founding Era

**Press and Democracy**

| Principle | Founders' View |
|-----------|----------------|
| First Amendment | Protected press freedom |
| Partisan press | Founders used partisan papers |
| Informed citizenry | Essential for republic |
| Government subsidies | Postal subsidies for newspapers |

**Early American Press**

- Explicitly partisan newspapers
- Limited reach
- Subsidized distribution
- Elite readership

### The Penny Press Era (1830s-1890s)

**Mass Media Emergence**

| Development | Effect |
|-------------|--------|
| Cheap printing | Mass circulation |
| Advertising model | Revenue shift |
| "Objective" reporting | Professional norms begin |
| Yellow journalism | Sensationalism for sales |

## Key Turning Points

### Broadcast Era (1920s-1980s)

**Radio and Television**

| Medium | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Radio (1920s) | National reach, FCC regulation |
| TV (1950s) | Visual news, three networks |
| Network news | Shared information environment |
| Fairness Doctrine | Balance requirements |

**Regulatory Framework**

| Regulation | Effect |
|------------|--------|
| FCC creation (1934) | Public interest standard |
| Fairness Doctrine (1949) | Balance requirement |
| Equal time | Political coverage rules |
| Localism | Community service requirements |

### The Trusted Era (1960s-1980s)

**Peak Media Trust**

| Feature | Reality |
|---------|---------|
| Three networks | Shared national conversation |
| Local papers | Community coverage |
| Professional norms | "Objective" journalism |
| High trust | 72% trusted media (1976) |

**Limits**

- Elite gatekeeping
- Limited perspectives
- Missed stories
- Establishment bias

### Cable and Deregulation (1980s-1990s)

**Key Changes**

| Year | Development |
|------|-------------|
| 1980 | CNN launches (24-hour news) |
| 1987 | Fairness Doctrine eliminated |
| 1996 | Telecommunications Act (deregulation) |
| 1996 | Fox News, MSNBC launch |

**Effects**

- 24-hour news cycle
- Opinion-driven content
- Partisan cable news
- Media consolidation

| Year | Development |

### 2000s: Platform Rise

| Year | Development |
|------|-------------|
| 2004 | Facebook launches |

**Local News Decline Begins**

| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| Craigslist | Classified revenue collapses |
| Google/Facebook | Ad revenue shifts |
| 2008 recession | Accelerates decline |
| Consolidation | Hedge funds buy papers |

### 2010s: Crisis Deepens

| Year | Development |
|------|-------------|
| 2010 | iPad, mobile news growth |
| 2011 | "Filter bubble" concept coined |
| 2014 | Facebook algorithm prioritizes engagement |
| 2016 | Election misinformation crisis |

**Key Developments**

- Social media becomes news source
- Algorithmic curation dominates
- Local news accelerates decline
- Misinformation spreads virally
- Foreign interference emerges

## Lessons from the Past

### What Has Worked

**Successful Approaches**

| Approach | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| Public interest standards | Some accountability |
| Professional journalism norms | Quality standards |
| Local ownership | Community connection |
| Nonprofit models | Mission focus |

### What Has Failed

**Failed Approaches**

| Approach | Problem |
|----------|---------|
| Market-only solutions | Quality suffers |
| Self-regulation | Insufficient |
| Deregulation | Consolidation, homogenization |
| Advertising dependence | Engagement over information |

### Historical Patterns

**Recurring Dynamics**

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| New technology disrupts | Print → radio → TV → internet |
| Business model challenges | Each transition creates crisis |
| Regulation follows | Eventually, rules adapt |
| Partisanship cycles | Periods of more/less partisan media |

### Lessons for Reform

**Historical Insights**

| Insight | Application |
|---------|-------------|
| Public investment can work | BBC, PBS models |
| Regulation can improve quality | Fairness Doctrine era |
| Technology isn't destiny | Choices shape outcomes |
| Business models matter | Advertising vs. subscription vs. public |
