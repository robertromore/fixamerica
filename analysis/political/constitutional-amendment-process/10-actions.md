# Constitutional Amendment Process: Actions

## For All Citizens

### Learn About the Amendment Process

**Understanding Article V**:

- Read Article V of the Constitution
- Learn about both proposal methods (Congress and convention)
- Understand ratification requirements
- Follow current amendment efforts

**Key Questions to Understand**:

- How many states needed to propose via convention? (34)
- How many states needed to ratify? (38)
- What amendments are currently proposed?
- What convention applications are pending?

### Engage with Amendment Campaigns

**Evaluate Amendment Proposals**:

- Research specific amendments (balanced budget, ERA, campaign finance)
- Consider process implications, not just substance
- Understand who supports and opposes
- Form informed opinion

**Support Process Reform**:

- Support efforts to clarify convention procedures
- Advocate for democratic participation
- Contact representatives about process issues
- Vote for candidates supporting reform

### Participate in State-Level Efforts

**State Constitutional Amendments**:

- Follow state ballot measures
- Vote on state constitutional amendments
- Engage in initiative campaigns where available
- Attend hearings on state constitution

**State Legislature Engagement**:

- Contact state legislators about convention applications
- Attend hearings on Article V resolutions
- Support state process reforms
- Monitor ratification votes

## For State Officials

### State Legislators

**Convention Applications**:

- Study Article V and convention procedures
- Research pending applications in your state
- Consider whether to support/oppose applications
- Participate in NCSL Article V discussions

**Key Considerations**:

| Question | Resources |
|----------|-----------|
| What amendments do I support? | Research organizations, constituents |
| Is convention path appropriate? | Expert analysis, coalition views |
| How would delegates be selected? | State law, NCSL models |
| What are risks and benefits? | Academic literature, advocacy groups |

**Ratification Votes**:

- Prepare for potential ratification votes
- Research proposed amendments
- Consider constituent views
- Participate in deliberation

### Governors

**Executive Role**:

- Influence state legislative action
- Public education on amendments
- Appointment to related commissions
- Advocacy with federal officials

**Coordination**:

- Engage NGA on amendment issues
- Coordinate with other governors
- Build bipartisan positions
- Public communication

### State Attorneys General

**Legal Analysis**:

- Advise on Article V procedures
- Analyze pending applications
- Prepare for litigation if needed
- Coordinate with other AGs

**Litigation**:

- Challenge unconstitutional procedures
- Defend state interests
- File amicus briefs
- Multistate coordination

## Key Questions for Candidates

### Federal Candidates

1. "Do you support clarifying the Article V convention process?"
2. "Would you support any constitutional amendments? Which ones?"
3. "Do you think Congress should propose [specific amendment]?"
4. "How do you view the balance between congressional and convention paths?"
5. "What reforms to the amendment process would you support?"

### State Candidates

1. "Do you support any pending Article V convention applications?"
2. "Would you vote to ratify [specific amendment] if it reached your state?"
3. "Should our state reform its own constitutional amendment process?"
4. "How would you ensure delegate accountability at a convention?"
5. "What state constitutional amendments do you support?"

## Issue-Specific Actions

### If You Support Convention Path

| Action | Level | How |
|--------|-------|-----|
| Support applications | State | Contact legislators, advocate |
| Demand clarification | Federal | Contact Congress |
| Prepare for convention | Both | Study procedures, build coalitions |
| Organize ratification | State | Build support in target states |

### If You Oppose Convention Path

| Action | Level | How |
|--------|-------|-----|
| Support congressional alternatives | Federal | Advocate for Congress to act |
| Monitor applications | State | Track and oppose where needed |
| Support safeguards | Federal | Advocate for strict rules |
| Public education | Both | Explain risks |

### If You Support Process Reform

| Action | Level | How |
|--------|-------|-----|
| Advocate clarification | Federal | Support legislation |
| State reform | State | Support state improvements |
| Build coalition | Both | Cross-partisan organizing |
| Long-term vision | Both | Support fundamental reform |
