## Essential Reading

### Books

**On the Amendment Process**:

- Kyvig, David E. *Explicit and Authentic Acts: Amending the U.S. Constitution, 1776-2015*. University Press of Kansas, 2016.
- Levinson, Sanford. *Our Undemocratic Constitution: Where the Constitution Goes Wrong*. Oxford University Press, 2006.
- Amar, Akhil Reed. *America's Constitution: A Biography*. Random House, 2005.
- Labunski, Richard. *The Second Constitutional Convention*. CreateSpace, 2000.

**On Article V Conventions**:

- Natelson, Robert. *The Law of Article V: State Initiation of Constitutional Amendments*. Federalist Society, 2013.
- Rappaport, Michael and John McGinnis. *Originalism and the Good Constitution*. Harvard University Press, 2013 (Chapter on Article V).
- Caplan, Russell. *Constitutional Brinksmanship: Amending the Constitution by National Convention*. Oxford University Press, 1988.

**On Constitutional Change**:

- Ackerman, Bruce. *We the People* (3 volumes). Harvard University Press.
- Strauss, David A. *The Living Constitution*. Oxford University Press, 2010.
- Balkin, Jack M. *Living Originalism*. Harvard University Press, 2011.

### Key Academic Articles

**Article V Process**:

- Paulsen, Michael Stokes. "A General Theory of Article V: The Constitutional Lessons of the Twenty-seventh Amendment." *Yale Law Journal* 103 (1993): 677.
- Dellinger, Walter. "The Legitimacy of Constitutional Change: Rethinking the Amendment Process." *Harvard Law Review* 97 (1983): 386.
- Tribe, Laurence. "Issues Raised by Requesting Congress to Call a Constitutional Convention to Propose a Balanced Budget Amendment." *Pacific Law Journal* 10 (1979): 627.

**Convention Procedures**:

- Natelson, Robert. "Proposing Constitutional Amendments by Convention: Rules Governing the Process." *Tennessee Law Review* 78 (2011): 693.
- Rappaport, Michael. "The Constitutionality of a Limited Convention." *Constitutional Commentary* 28 (2012): 53.
- Amar, Vikram. "The People Made Me Do It: Can the People of the States Instruct and Coerce Their State Legislatures in the Article V Constitutional Amendment Process?" *William and Mary Law Review* 41 (1999): 1037.

**ERA and Ratification**:

- Held, Allison, Sheryl Herndon, and Danielle Stager. "The Equal Rights Amendment: Why the ERA Remains Legally Viable and Properly Before the States." *William and Mary Journal of Women and the Law* 3 (1997): 113.
- Calabresi, Steven. "Text, Precedent, and the Constitution: Some Originalist and Normative Arguments for Overruling Planned Parenthood v. Casey." *Constitutional Commentary* 22 (2005): 311.

### Key Supreme Court Cases

**On Amendment Process**:

- *Coleman v. Miller*, 307 U.S. 433 (1939): Ratification questions are political
- *Dillon v. Gloss*, 256 U.S. 368 (1921): Congress can set ratification deadline
- *Idaho v. Freeman*, 529 F. Supp. 1107 (D. Idaho 1981) (vacated as moot): ERA rescission and deadline

**On Constitutional Interpretation**:

- Various cases on scope of amendments
- Incorporation doctrine cases
- Congressional power cases
