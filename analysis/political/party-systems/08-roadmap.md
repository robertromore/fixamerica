# Party Systems: Roadmap

## Strategic Vision

### The Goal

**A More Representative Democracy**

- Multiple viable parties
- Voter choice beyond binary
- Reduced polarization
- Competitive elections everywhere
- Governance that works

### Success Metrics

| Metric | Current | Target |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Viable parties | 2 | 4-6 |
| Third party representatives | <5 | 50+ |
| Competitive seats | ~10% | 50%+ |
| Voter satisfaction | Low | Higher |
| Congressional approval | ~17% | 40%+ |

## Risks and Contingencies

### Political Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|------------|------------|
| Major party opposition | High | Bipartisan framing |
| Voter confusion | Medium | Education campaigns |
| Unintended consequences | Medium | Careful design |
| Backlash | Medium | Gradual implementation |
| Court challenges | Medium | Constitutional design |

### Contingency Plans

**If State Reform Blocked**

| Alternative | Approach |
|-------------|----------|
| Local implementation | City by city |
| Ballot initiatives | Direct democracy |
| Judicial challenges | Equal protection arguments |
| Party pressure | Internal reform |

**If Federal Reform Impossible**

| Response | Approach |
|----------|----------|
| 50-state strategy | Every state reforms |
| Interstate compacts | Coordinated state action |
| Presidential election focus | NPVIC completion |
| Long-term pressure | Build for future |

**If Third Parties Remain Marginal**

| Response | Approach |
|----------|----------|
| Primary focus | Reform within major parties |
| Issue advocacy | Push policies, not parties |
| Fusion voting | Cross-party endorsements |
| Long-term building | Generational change |
