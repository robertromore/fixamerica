## Essential Reading

### Books

**FOIA and Government Secrecy**

- Pozen, David. "Freedom of Information Beyond the Freedom of Information Act." *University of Pennsylvania Law Review* 165 (2017).
- Roberts, Alasdair. *Blacked Out: Government Secrecy in the Information Age*. Cambridge University Press, 2006.
- Aftergood, Steven. Various publications on classification (FAS Secrecy News).
- Moynihan, Daniel Patrick. *Secrecy: The American Experience*. Yale University Press, 1998.

**Classification**

- Priest, Dana and William Arkin. *Top Secret America*. Little, Brown, 2011.
- Maret, Susan. *Government Secrecy* (Research in Social Problems and Public Policy, Vol. 19). Emerald, 2011.
- Edgar, Timothy. *Beyond Snowden: Privacy, Mass Surveillance, and the Struggle to Reform the NSA*. Brookings, 2017.

**Whistleblowing**

- Devine, Tom and Tarek Maassarani. *The Corporate Whistleblower's Survival Guide*. Berrett-Koehler, 2011.
- Ellsberg, Daniel. *Secrets: A Memoir of Vietnam and the Pentagon Papers*. Viking, 2002.
- Greenwald, Glenn. *No Place to Hide: Edward Snowden, the NSA, and the U.S. Surveillance State*. Metropolitan Books, 2014.

### Academic Articles

**FOIA Research**

- Kwoka, Margaret. "FOIA, Inc." *Duke Law Journal* 65 (2016): 1361-1437.
- Pozen, David. "The Mosaic Theory, National Security, and the Freedom of Information Act." *Yale Law Journal* 115 (2005).
- Kreimer, Seth. "The Freedom of Information Act and the Ecology of Transparency." *University of Pennsylvania Journal of Constitutional Law* 10 (2008).

**Classification Studies**

- Aftergood, Steven. "Reducing Government Secrecy: Finding What Works." *Yale Law & Policy Review* 27 (2009).
- Fuchs, Meredith. "Judging Secrets: The Role Courts Should Play in Preventing Unnecessary Secrecy." *Administrative Law Review* 58 (2006).

**Whistleblower Protection**

- Devine, Tom. "The Whistleblower Protection Act of 1989: Foundation for the Modern Law of Employment Dissent." *Administrative Law Review* 51 (1999).
- Moberly, Richard. "Sarbanes-Oxley's Whistleblower Provisions: Ten Years Later." *South Carolina Law Review* 64 (2012).

## Legal Resources

### Key Statutes

**FOIA and Access**

- Freedom of Information Act, 5 U.S.C. § 552
- Privacy Act, 5 U.S.C. § 552a
- Government in the Sunshine Act, 5 U.S.C. § 552b
- Federal Advisory Committee Act, 5 U.S.C. App.
- Presidential Records Act, 44 U.S.C. §§ 2201-2207

**Classification**

- Executive Order 13526 (Classification)
- Atomic Energy Act, 42 U.S.C. § 2011 et seq. (Restricted Data)
- Intelligence Community Classification Guidance

**Whistleblower Protection**

- Whistleblower Protection Act, 5 U.S.C. § 2302(b)(8)
- Intelligence Community Whistleblower Protection Act, 5 U.S.C. App. 3
- False Claims Act, 31 U.S.C. §§ 3729-3733 (qui tam)

### Key Court Cases

**FOIA**

- *EPA v. Mink*, 410 U.S. 73 (1973): Exemption 1 interpretation
- *Department of Air Force v. Rose*, 425 U.S. 352 (1976): FOIA purpose
- *Milner v. Department of Navy*, 562 U.S. 562 (2011): Exemption 2 narrowed
- *Food Marketing Institute v. Argus Leader Media*, 139 S. Ct. 2356 (2019): Exemption 4

**Classification**

- *United States v. Reynolds*, 345 U.S. 1 (1953): State secrets privilege
- *CIA v. Sims*, 471 U.S. 159 (1985): Intelligence sources
- *Snepp v. United States*, 444 U.S. 507 (1980): Prepublication review

**Whistleblowers**

- *Garcetti v. Ceballos*, 547 U.S. 410 (2006): Employee speech limits
- *Lane v. Franks*, 573 U.S. 228 (2014): Testimony protection
