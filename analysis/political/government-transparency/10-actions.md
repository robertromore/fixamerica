# Government Transparency: Actions

## For All Citizens

### Use Your Right to Know

**File FOIA Requests**

- Anyone can file a FOIA request (no citizenship requirement)
- Requests are free to file (fees may apply for processing)
- Be specific about what records you want
- Use online portals when available

**How to File**:

1. Identify the agency that has records
2. Find agency FOIA contact (FOIA.gov)
3. Write request describing records sought
4. Submit via email, portal, or mail
5. Track request and follow up

**Tips for Effective Requests**:

- Be specific but not too narrow
- Include date ranges
- Reference specific programs or offices
- Request fee waiver if eligible (news, educational, public interest)

**Resources**:

- MuckRock.com: File and track requests
- FOIA.gov: Central portal
- Reporters Committee: Request guides
- Agency reading rooms: Already-released records

## For Journalists

### Master FOIA

**Build FOIA Skills**:

- Take training courses (Reporters Committee, SPJ)
- Learn agency-specific procedures
- Develop relationships with FOIA officers
- Track requests systematically

**Strategic FOIA Use**:

- File early in investigation
- Cast wide net, then narrow
- Appeal denials systematically
- Consider litigation for important records

**Avoid Common Mistakes**:

- Requests too broad or too narrow
- Wrong agency
- Missing fee waiver justification
- Giving up after first denial

### Protect Sources

**For Whistleblower Sources**:

- Understand legal protections and gaps
- Use secure communication (Signal, SecureDrop)
- Explain risks honestly
- Connect sources with legal help
- Protect source identity absolutely

**Security Practices**:

- Encrypted communications
- Secure document handling
- Air-gapped computers for sensitive material
- Operational security training

## For Government Employees

### Know Your Rights

**Whistleblower Protections**:

- Understand what's protected
- Know the channels (IG, OSC, Congress)
- Document everything
- Consult with attorney before disclosing

**What's Protected**:

- Disclosure of waste, fraud, abuse
- Violation of law or regulation
- Gross mismanagement
- Danger to public health or safety

**What's Not Protected** (Generally):

- Classified information to press (complex area)
- Policy disagreements
- Personal grievances
- Information that would harm national security

### Promote Transparency From Within

**If You Process FOIA**:

- Default to disclosure
- Minimize redactions
- Meet deadlines
- Be helpful to requesters

**If You Classify Information**:

- Classify only what's necessary
- Use lowest appropriate level
- Set appropriate declassification dates
- Question over-classification

**If You Witness Wrongdoing**:

- Document carefully
- Use appropriate channels
- Seek legal advice
- Consider all options
