# Federalism Reform: Actions

## For All Citizens

### Understand Federal-State-Local Relations

**Learn the Basics**:

- Know which level of government is responsible for which services
- Understand how federal grants affect state programs
- Learn about unfunded mandates in your state
- Follow federal preemption issues

**Questions to Ask**:

- "Which level of government is responsible for this problem?"
- "How much of our state budget comes from federal grants?"
- "What unfunded mandates affect our state/locality?"
- "Has federal law preempted state action on this issue?"

### Engage at Multiple Levels

**Federal Level**:

- Contact members of Congress about federalism issues
- Support candidates who understand federal-state relations
- Comment on federal regulations affecting states
- Follow congressional action on mandates and preemption

**State Level**:

- Attend state legislative hearings on federal relations
- Support state officials who advocate for state authority
- Engage on interstate compact issues
- Monitor state implementation of federal programs

**Local Level**:

- Attend city council or county board meetings
- Ask about state preemption of local authority
- Support local officials advocating for local control
- Engage on federal grants to localities

### Advocate for Reform

**Key Messages**:

- "Fund the mandates or eliminate them"
- "States should be able to protect consumers beyond federal minimums"
- "Simplify the grant system to reduce waste"
- "Let states and localities experiment with solutions"

**Contact Points**:

| Level | Who to Contact | On What Issues |
|-------|----------------|----------------|
| Federal | Senators, Representative | UMRA reform, preemption |
| State | Governor, legislators | Federal mandate costs, interstate compacts |
| Local | Mayor, council | State preemption, grant access |

## For State Officials

### Governors

**Executive Actions**:

- Issue state federalism executive order
- Establish federal affairs office
- Coordinate with other governors through NGA
- Testify before Congress on mandate costs

**Administrative Reforms**:

- Streamline grant administration
- Track mandate costs
- Report on federal-state relations
- Negotiate flexibility with federal agencies

**Advocacy**:

- Use NGA platform for federalism issues
- Build bipartisan governor coalitions
- Engage media on federal overreach
- Support federal reform legislation

### State Legislators

**Legislative Actions**:

- Study federal mandate impacts on state budget
- Consider resolutions on federal relations
- Authorize interstate compact participation
- Fund grant compliance efficiently

**Oversight**:

- Hold hearings on federal program implementation
- Review unfunded mandate costs
- Examine grant conditions and flexibility
- Study preemption impacts

**Coordination**:

- Engage with NCSL on federalism agenda
- Build interstate relationships
- Share best practices across states
- Coordinate on compact negotiations

### State Attorneys General

**Legal Actions**:

- Challenge unconstitutional federal overreach
- Defend state laws from preemption
- Coordinate multistate litigation
- File amicus briefs on federalism

**Advisory Role**:

- Advise on state constitutional authority
- Review proposed interstate compacts
- Analyze federal regulation impacts
- Support legislative federalism efforts

## Key Questions for Candidates

### Federal Candidates

1. "Do you support strengthening the Unfunded Mandates Reform Act?"
2. "Should states be allowed to exceed federal consumer protection standards?"
3. "How would you simplify the federal grant system?"
4. "Do you support pre-authorizing interstate compacts?"
5. "How will you ensure federal-state consultation on new regulations?"

### State Candidates

1. "How will you advocate for state interests with the federal government?"
2. "What unfunded mandates should the federal government fund?"
3. "Which interstate compacts should our state join?"
4. "How would you protect local government authority?"
5. "How will you simplify state administration of federal programs?"

### Local Candidates

1. "How will you advocate for local authority against state preemption?"
2. "How can we improve access to federal grants?"
3. "What regional cooperation initiatives should we pursue?"
4. "How would you address unfunded mandates from state government?"
