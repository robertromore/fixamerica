## For Communities

### Local Reform Campaigns

**Assess the Landscape**

1. Research if your city can adopt RCV (check city charter)
2. Identify existing reform supporters
3. Find potential allies (civic groups, party reformers)
4. Assess city council receptiveness

**Build a Coalition**

1. Recruit bipartisan leadership (essential)
2. Engage local civic organizations
3. Connect with local party reformers from both sides
4. Include business and community leaders
5. Partner with local academic institutions

**Choose Your Path**

| Path | When to Use | Process |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **City Council adoption** | Council is receptive | Lobby council members, build public support |
| **City ballot initiative** | Council won't act | Gather signatures, campaign for passage |
| **Charter commission** | Charter revision coming | Advocate for RCV in new charter |

### Local Advocacy Actions

**City Council Engagement**

- [ ] Attend city council meetings and speak during public comment
- [ ] Request formal study of RCV for your city
- [ ] Submit written testimony supporting reform
- [ ] Meet with individual council members

**Community Education**

- [ ] Host informational events at libraries, community centers
- [ ] Partner with League of Women Voters for forums
- [ ] Organize debates or discussions on reform
- [ ] Create local educational materials

**Build Visible Support**

- [ ] Collect petitions supporting RCV (even if informal)
- [ ] Get op-eds from local leaders
- [ ] Secure endorsements from community groups
- [ ] Document local support for city council

### Model Local Campaign Timeline

| Phase | Activities |
|-------|------------|
| **Research** | Understand legal requirements, identify stakeholders |
| **Coalition Building** | Recruit leaders, organizations, validators |
| **Public Education** | Events, media, social media |
| **Council Lobbying** | Meetings, testimony, building votes |

## For Advocates

### Running a Ballot Initiative Campaign

**Phase 1: Preparation**

1. Research state ballot initiative requirements
2. Draft initiative language (get legal review)
3. Build core team and leadership structure
4. Establish budget and fundraising plan
5. Develop messaging and communications strategy

**Phase 2: Qualification**

1. Launch signature gathering operation
2. Train volunteers on petitioning
3. Build coalition and earn endorsements
4. Grow email list and social media following
5. Begin voter education

**Phase 3: Campaign**

1. Intensify voter contact (phones, doors, digital)
2. Earned media strategy (press, op-eds, events)
3. Paid media (ads if budget allows)
4. Build rapid response capability
5. GOTV operation for election day

**Phase 4: Victory and Beyond**

1. Celebrate and thank supporters
2. Support implementation
3. Voter education before first RCV election
4. Prepare defense against repeal attempts
5. Share lessons with other states

### Advocacy Best Practices

**Messaging Principles**

- Lead with voter benefits, not technical details
- Use simple language: "Rank candidates 1, 2, 3"
- Emphasize bipartisan support
- Use local validators and testimonials
- Pre-rebut opposition arguments

**Coalition Building**

- Recruit across partisan lines (essential)
- Include validators from unexpected quarters
- Build broad coalition, not just usual suspects
- Give partners ownership and credit
- Maintain bipartisan optics throughout

**Working with Media**

- Build relationships with local reporters
- Provide clear, quotable messaging
- Offer diverse spokespeople
- Respond quickly to opposition claims
- Submit op-eds from variety of voices

### Defending Against Repeal

**Prevention**

- Implement well (good voter education, smooth election)
- Build constituency who experiences and likes system
- Document success (voter satisfaction, results)
- Maintain active reform coalition post-victory

**If Repeal Campaign Emerges**

1. Activate coalition quickly
2. Mobilize voters who used and liked RCV
3. Use implementation success as evidence
4. Employ bipartisan defense
5. Raise rapid response funds
6. Counter misinformation immediately

## Measuring Impact

### Leading Indicators

**Awareness and Support**

| Metric | How to Measure |
|--------|----------------|
| Public awareness of RCV | Polling, surveys |
| Stated support for reform | Ballot measure polling |
| Organization sign-ups | Membership growth |
| Social media engagement | Followers, shares, engagement |

**Activity Metrics**

| Metric | How to Measure |
|--------|----------------|
| Volunteers recruited | Organization databases |
| Events held | Event tracking |
| Media coverage | Press clip tracking |
| Legislative sponsors | Bill tracking |

### Lagging Indicators

**Policy Outcomes**

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Cities adopting RCV | Track number, population covered |
| States adopting RCV | Track state legislation and ballot measures |
| Ballot measures passed | Win rate, margin |
| Repeal attempts defeated | Defense success rate |

**System Impact**

| Metric | How to Measure |
|--------|----------------|
| Voter satisfaction in RCV jurisdictions | Post-election surveys |
| Third-party/independent performance | Election results analysis |
| Campaign negativity | Content analysis |
| Spoiler elections prevented | Counterfactual analysis |

### Long-Term Success Metrics

**Ultimate Goals**

- Majority of Americans vote under alternative systems
- Third parties win significant representation
- Voters can express genuine preferences
- Policy better reflects public preferences
- Trust in elections increases

## State-Specific Action Guides

### States with Ballot Initiative Process

**High Priority**

- Nevada (confirm 2024 vote)
- Oregon (potential initiative)
- Arizona, Colorado (reform momentum)

**Actions**

1. Connect with state-specific reform organization
2. Volunteer for active campaigns
3. Prepare for signature gathering
4. GOTV for ballot measures

### States Without Initiative Process

**Strategy**: Legislative path or local adoption

**Actions**

1. Build legislative champions
2. Focus on city-level adoption
3. Create demonstration effects
4. Build public pressure for legislative action

### States with RCV

**Maine, Alaska**
**Actions**

1. Defend against repeal
2. Document and share success
3. Support expansion
4. Serve as resource for other states
