# Electoral Reform: Roadmap

## Phase 1: Immediate Actions

### Learn and Connect

**Education**

- Understand how RCV and alternative voting works
- Learn your state's ballot initiative process
- Research existing reform efforts in your state/city

**Connect**

- Join FairVote, RepresentUs, or local reform group
- Find local coalition building electoral reform support
- Connect with like-minded people across partisan lines

### Support Active Campaigns

**Current Opportunities**

- Nevada: RCV confirmation vote (2024)
- Oregon: Potential ballot initiative
- Multiple cities considering RCV adoption
- Support candidates who endorse reform

**How to Help**

- Volunteer for signature gathering
- Donate to ballot initiative campaigns
- Phone bank, canvass, or digital organizing
- Share information on social media

### Advocate Locally

**City-Level RCV**

- Research if your city can adopt RCV
- Attend city council meetings
- Build local coalition
- Push for pilot programs

## Phase 2: Short-Term Goals (1-2 Years)

### Ballot Initiative Campaigns

**Target States for RCV**

| State | Status | Strategy |
|-------|--------|----------|
| Nevada | Needs 2024 confirmation | GOTV, defend against opposition |
| Oregon | Initiative possible | Signature gathering, coalition building |
| Colorado | Reform momentum | Legislative or ballot initiative |
| Arizona | Competitive battleground | Build bipartisan support |
| Michigan | Recent redistricting success | Expand to electoral reform |

**Campaign Elements**

- Coalition building (bipartisan essential)
- Signature gathering infrastructure
- Messaging and voter education
- Opposition research and rapid response
- GOTV for election day

### City-Level Expansion

**Target Cities**

- State capitals (visibility, influence)
- College towns (receptive population)
- Cities with reform-minded councils
- Cities with recent close/spoiled elections

**Process**

1. Build local coalition
2. Research city charter/legal requirements
3. Approach city council or launch petition
4. Win adoption
5. Implement successfully

### Build Infrastructure

**Organizational Capacity**

- Train more volunteers and staff
- Develop campaign expertise
- Build donor base
- Create communication infrastructure

**Research and Evidence**

- Document results from existing implementations
- Commission academic studies
- Build database of voter satisfaction
- Counter opposition research

## Phase 3: Medium-Term Goals (3-5 Years)

### Critical Mass on RCV

**Target: 10+ States Using RCV**

- Continued ballot initiative campaigns
- Legislative adoption where possible
- Defend against repeal attempts
- Build demonstration effects

**Expected States**

- Current: Maine, Alaska
- Near-term: Nevada, Oregon, Colorado
- Mid-term: Arizona, Michigan, Wisconsin, others

### Combined Reforms

**Alaska Model Replication**

- Top-four nonpartisan primary
- RCV general election
- Open to all voters
- Comprehensive reform package

**Target States**

- States with large independent populations
- States with ballot initiative process
- States frustrated with partisan primaries

### Federal Legislation Groundwork

**Fair Representation Act**

- Multi-member districts for House
- RCV for elections
- Would transform Congress

**Building Support**

- Educate Congress members
- Build constituent pressure
- Develop implementation plans
- Prepare for window of opportunity

### National Popular Vote Progress

**Interstate Compact**

- Currently at 209 electoral votes
- Need 270 to take effect
- Target persuadable states

## Phase 4: Long-Term Transformation (5+ Years)

### Nationwide RCV Adoption

**Goal: Majority of Americans Vote Under RCV**

- 25+ states using RCV for state elections
- Most major cities using RCV
- Federal elections in RCV states

### Proportional Representation

**Multi-Member Districts**

- Fair Representation Act passes
- States experiment with multi-member districts
- Proportional outcomes for Congress

**What Changes**

- Multiple viable parties
- Coalition governance
- End of two-party dominance
- More representative outcomes

### Electoral College Reform

**National Popular Vote Takes Effect**

- Compact reaches 270 electoral votes
- Presidential elections decided by popular vote
- Every vote counts equally

### System Transformation

**New Political Landscape**

- 4-6 competitive parties
- Coalition government normal
- Voters express genuine preferences
- Policy reflects majority preferences

## Risks and Contingencies

### Risk: Repeal Campaigns

**Threat**: Opponents fund repeal efforts after implementation

**Mitigation**

- Implement well (voter education, smooth election)
- Build constituency who benefits
- Prepare defense campaigns
- Document success

**Contingency**: If repeal passes, learn lessons and try again later

### Risk: Legal Challenges

**Threat**: Courts strike down RCV

**Mitigation**

- Draft legislation to survive scrutiny
- Engage election law experts
- Document bipartisan support
- Prepare appeals

**Contingency**: RCV has survived every challenge so far; continue defending

### Risk: Opposition Outspending

**Threat**: Major parties flood campaigns with money

**Mitigation**

- Build grassroots strength
- Earned media strategy
- Pre-rebut opposition messaging
- Bipartisan coalition for credibility

**Contingency**: If outspent, focus on ground game and earned media

### Risk: Implementation Problems

**Threat**: Early implementations have issues, create ammunition for opponents

**Mitigation**

- Thorough preparation
- Voter education campaigns
- Test systems before election
- Rapid response to problems

**Contingency**: Acknowledge problems, fix them, document improvements

### Risk: Partisan Capture

**Threat**: Reform becomes identified with one party

**Mitigation**

- Maintain bipartisan leadership
- Use messengers from both sides
- Focus on process, not outcomes
- Avoid culture war associations

**Contingency**: Reframe messaging, recruit opposite-party validators

## Success Metrics

### Leading Indicators

- Public awareness of RCV (polling)
- Active reform organizations per state
- Volunteer engagement
- Media coverage (quantity and tone)
- Bills introduced in legislatures

### Lagging Indicators

- States with RCV
- Cities with RCV
- Successful elections under RCV
- Voter satisfaction in RCV jurisdictions
- Third-party representation

### Ultimate Success

- American elections produce majority-supported winners
- Voters can express genuine preferences
- Multiple parties are competitive
- Policy reflects public preferences
- Trust in elections restored
