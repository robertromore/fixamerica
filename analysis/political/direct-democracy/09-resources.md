## Legal Resources

### Key Court Cases

**Initiative Process**

- *Pacific States Telephone & Telegraph Co. v. Oregon*, 223 U.S. 118 (1912): Initiative doesn't violate republican form guarantee
- *James v. Valtierra*, 402 U.S. 137 (1971): Referendum requirements not inherently discriminatory
- *City of Eastlake v. Forest City Enterprises*, 426 U.S. 668 (1976): Referenda on zoning constitutional
- *Citizens Against Rent Control v. City of Berkeley*, 454 U.S. 290 (1981): Limits on initiative campaign contributions unconstitutional

**Minority Rights and Initiatives**

- *Romer v. Evans*, 556 U.S. 620 (1996): Colorado Amendment 2 (anti-gay) violated equal protection
- *Washington v. Seattle School District*, 458 U.S. 457 (1982): Initiative restructuring school busing violated equal protection
- *Hollingsworth v. Perry*, 570 U.S. 693 (2013): Prop 8 proponents lacked standing to appeal

**Electoral College**

- *McPherson v. Blacker*, 146 U.S. 1 (1892): States have broad power over elector selection
- *Chiafalo v. Washington*, 140 S. Ct. 2316 (2020): States can enforce elector pledges

### Statutes and Constitutional Provisions

**Federal**

- U.S. Constitution, Article IV, Section 4 (Republican Form Guarantee)
- U.S. Constitution, Article I, Section 10 (Compact Clause)
- U.S. Constitution, Article II, Section 1 (Electoral College)

**State Model Provisions**
