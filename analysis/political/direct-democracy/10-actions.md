# Direct Democracy: Actions

## For All Citizens

### Educate Yourself

**Understand Direct Democracy**

- Learn how your state's initiative/referendum process works
- Study the history of direct democracy reforms
- Follow ballot measures in your state and locality
- Understand both benefits and concerns about direct democracy

**Key Topics**:

- Ballot initiative vs. referendum vs. recall
- Citizen assemblies and deliberative democracy
- Participatory budgeting
- National Popular Vote compact

**Resources**:

- Ballotpedia.org for ballot measure tracking
- State Secretary of State websites
- Initiative and Referendum Institute
- Books in the Resources section

### Participate in Existing Processes

**Vote on Ballot Measures**

- Research all measures before voting (don't skip)
- Read official voter guides
- Look for Citizens' Statements (Oregon model)
- Consider arguments from multiple perspectives

**Attend Town Meetings and Public Hearings**

- Municipal budget hearings
- Planning and zoning meetings
- School board sessions
- City council public comment

**Participate in Participatory Budgeting**

If your city has PB:

- Attend idea collection meetings
- Volunteer as a budget delegate
- Vote on final projects
- Encourage others to participate

### Advocate for Reform

**Contact Elected Officials**

- State legislators: Support initiative process improvements
- City council: Request participatory budgeting pilot
- State officials: Support National Popular Vote

**Key Messages**:

- "I support expanding citizen participation in government"
- "Our city should pilot participatory budgeting"
- "I support the National Popular Vote Interstate Compact"
- "We need better voter information on ballot measures"

**Support Reform Organizations**

- Join Common Cause, League of Women Voters
- Support National Popular Vote campaign
- Donate to Participatory Budgeting Project
- Volunteer for democracy reform campaigns

### Build Skills for Participation

**Deliberation Skills**

- Practice active listening
- Learn to engage with opposing views
- Participate in community forums
- Join study circles or discussion groups

**Civic Knowledge**

- Understand how government works
- Learn about policy areas you care about
- Follow current events and policy debates
- Take civic education courses

## For Initiative and Referendum States

### Engage with Ballot Measures

**Before Signature Gathering**

- Learn about proposed initiatives
- Ask proponents and opponents for information
- Consider signing petitions you support
- Help gather signatures for measures you believe in

**During Campaign**

- Research funding sources for campaigns
- Look beyond advertising to substance
- Attend debates and forums
- Share accurate information with others

**At the Ballot**

- Don't skip ballot measures
- Use official voter guides
- Consider diverse perspectives
- Vote based on substance, not advertising

### Improve the Process

**Advocate for**:

- Citizen Initiative Review in your state
- Better ballot title processes
- Disclosure of campaign funding
- Free legal drafting assistance for proponents

**Oppose**:

- Efforts to restrict initiative access
- Increased signature requirements without justification
- Legislative repeal of voter-passed measures
- Manipulation of initiative process

### Monitor Implementation

**After Measures Pass**:

- Track implementation by government
- Hold officials accountable for faithful execution
- Advocate for adequate resources
- Report problems to media and advocates

## Campaign-Specific Actions

### National Popular Vote

**In Compact States (Already Joined)**

- Defend against repeal attempts
- Educate about how compact works
- Build public support
- Prepare for activation

**In Non-Compact States**

- Contact state legislators
- Support NPV legislation
- Organize constituent pressure
- Build bipartisan coalitions

**Key Messages**

- "Every vote should count equally in presidential elections"
- "The compact ensures the president reflects national majority"
- "States have constitutional authority to allocate electoral votes"
- "The current system ignores most voters"

### Participatory Budgeting Expansion

**If Your City Doesn't Have PB**

- Research successful programs
- Build coalition of supporters
- Approach city council members
- Propose pilot program

**If Your City Has PB**

- Participate actively
- Advocate for expansion
- Improve diversity of participation
- Document and share success

**Key Messages**

- "Let residents decide how to spend our tax dollars"
- "PB builds community and trust in government"
- "Successful programs in [nearby city] show it works"
- "Start small, learn, expand"

### Citizen Initiative Review

**In Oregon**

- Support CIR continuation and expansion
- Participate if selected
- Use Citizens' Statements when voting
- Share CIR model nationally

**In Other States**

- Advocate for CIR adoption
- Connect with Oregon practitioners
- Propose pilot programs
- Build legislative support

## Key Questions to Ask

### For Elected Officials

1. "Do you support the National Popular Vote Interstate Compact?"
2. "Would you support a participatory budgeting pilot in our city?"
3. "Do you support citizen initiative review for ballot measures?"
4. "How do you engage constituents in policy decisions?"
5. "Do you support reforms to improve our initiative process?"

### For Initiative Campaigns

1. "Who is funding this campaign?"
2. "What are the policy tradeoffs of this measure?"
3. "How will this be implemented if it passes?"
4. "What do independent analysts say about this measure?"
5. "Is there a Citizens' Statement on this measure?"
