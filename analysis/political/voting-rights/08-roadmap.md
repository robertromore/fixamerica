# Voting Rights: Roadmap

## Strategic Vision

### The Goal

**Universal, Easy, Secure Voting**

- Every eligible citizen can vote without barriers
- Voting is convenient, not burdensome
- Elections are secure and trustworthy
- No discriminatory impacts on any group
- Full participation in democracy

### Success Metrics

| Metric | Current | Target |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Voter registration rate | 70% of eligible | 95%+ |
| Voter turnout (presidential) | 60-66% | 80%+ |
| Racial turnout gap | 10-15 points | <3 points |
| Average wait time | Varies widely | <15 minutes everywhere |
| States with AVR | 23+ | 50 |
| States with no-excuse mail voting | ~35 | 50 |
| Felony disenfranchisement | 4.6 million | Near zero |

## Theory of Change

### How Change Happens

```text

Grassroots organizing builds pressure
         ↓
Public awareness of suppression grows
         ↓
State reforms demonstrate success
         ↓
Successful models spread
         ↓
Federal legislation becomes possible
         ↓
Courts uphold reforms
         ↓
Voting becomes universal right

```text

### Key Leverage Points

| Leverage Point | Why It Matters | How to Use |
|----------------|----------------|------------|
| State ballot initiatives | Bypass legislatures | Direct democracy in 26 states |
| Secretary of State races | Control administration | Target key states |
| Federal legislation | National standards | Build Senate majority |
| Court nominations | Favorable rulings | Support reform candidates |
| Public opinion | 60%+ support access | Mobilize latent support |

## Phase 3: Medium-Term Goals (2028-2030)

### Federal Legislation Window

**Prerequisites**

| Requirement | Strategy |
|-------------|----------|
| Senate majority | Win 2028 races |
| Filibuster reform | Build support for carve-out |
| House majority | Maintain or regain |
| Presidential support | Elect reform champion |

**Legislative Package**
*John Lewis Voting Rights Advancement Act*

- New coverage formula based on recent violations
- Restored preclearance for covered jurisdictions
- Enhanced DOJ enforcement
- Protection against known discriminatory practices

*Freedom to Vote Act*

- Automatic voter registration nationwide
- Same-day registration requirement
- Minimum 15 days early voting
- No-excuse mail voting
- Standards for polling place allocation
- Limits on voter roll purges

### Filibuster Strategy

**Options**

| Option | Pros | Cons |
|--------|------|------|
| Voting rights carve-out | Preserves filibuster generally | May not hold |
| Talking filibuster | Forces public debate | May not be sufficient |
| Full elimination | Enables all priorities | Risks future reversal |

### Implementation Planning

**Federal-State Coordination**

- Develop implementation guidance
- Provide funding for compliance
- Establish oversight mechanisms
- Create enforcement protocols

**Milestone Checklist (Phase 3)**

- [ ] Win necessary Senate seats
- [ ] Build filibuster reform coalition
- [ ] Pass John Lewis VRA
- [ ] Pass Freedom to Vote Act
- [ ] Defend legislation in courts
- [ ] Begin implementation

## Phase 4: Long-Term Transformation (2030+)

### Constitutional Amendment

**The Long-Term Goal**

> "The right of citizens of the United States to vote in any public election shall not be denied or abridged by the United States or by any State. Congress shall have power to enforce this article by appropriate legislation."

**Why Needed**

- Legislation can be repealed
- Court decisions can change
- State variation persists
- Constitutional right provides durable protection

**Ratification Path**

| Step | Requirement | Challenge |
|------|-------------|-----------|
| House passage | 2/3 majority | Need 290 votes |
| Senate passage | 2/3 majority | Need 67 votes |
| State ratification | 38 states | Need broad coalition |

**Strategy**

- Build over 10-20 years
- Demonstrate federal legislation success
- Shift public consensus
- Elect supportive state legislatures

### Institutional Transformation

**Election Administration Reform**

- Professional, nonpartisan administration
- Uniform national standards
- Adequate funding for all jurisdictions
- Modern, secure technology

**Civic Culture Building**

- Voting as civic norm
- High school civic education
- Community voting traditions
- Election Day celebrations

**Milestone Checklist (Phase 4)**

- [ ] Sustain 80%+ turnout in presidential elections
- [ ] Eliminate racial and demographic turnout gaps
- [ ] Achieve nonpartisan election administration in all states
- [ ] Begin constitutional amendment process
- [ ] Ratify voting rights amendment
