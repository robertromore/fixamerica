# Voting Rights: Actions

## For Individuals

### Exercise Your Rights

**Register and Vote**

| Action | How |
|--------|-----|
| Check your registration | vote.org/am-i-registered-to-vote |
| Register if not registered | vote.org or state website |
| Update registration after moving | Before deadlines |
| Vote in every election | Primary, general, local, special |
| Bring required ID | Know your state's requirements |

**Know Your Rights**

| Right | Details |
|-------|---------|
| Provisional ballot | If turned away, demand one |
| Time off to vote | Many states require it |
| Report problems | 866-OUR-VOTE |
| Language assistance | If your precinct qualifies |
| Accessible voting | If you have a disability |

### Educate Yourself

**Learn the Issues**

- Read [Current State](02-current-state.md) to understand restrictions
- Follow voting rights organizations
- Track your state's legislation
- Understand federal reform proposals

**Counter Misinformation**

- Learn to debunk voter fraud myths
- Share accurate information
- Correct false claims (respectfully)
- Point people to reliable sources

### Personal Advocacy

**Talk to Friends and Family**

| Approach | Tips |
|----------|------|
| Share your voting experience | Make it personal |
| Discuss why voting matters | Connect to shared values |
| Address misconceptions | Use evidence gently |
| Encourage participation | Offer to help |

**Social Media**

- Share voting information
- Amplify voting rights organizations
- Post about your voting experience
- Counter misinformation

### Financial Support

**Donate to Organizations**

| Type | Examples |
|------|----------|
| Legal organizations | ACLU, NAACP LDF, Lawyers' Committee |
| Advocacy groups | Brennan Center, Common Cause |
| Voter mobilization | Vote.org, NextGen America |
| Local organizations | State-specific groups |

**Give Strategically**

- Small recurring donations add up
- Fund lesser-known effective groups
- Support both legal and organizing work
- Give before elections when it matters most

## For Communities

### Voter Registration Drives

**How to Organize**

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1. Partner | Connect with established organizations |
| 2. Train | Learn registration rules in your state |
| 3. Target | High-traffic locations, underserved areas |
| 4. Execute | Tables, door-to-door, events |
| 5. Follow up | Ensure registrations are processed |

**Best Practices**

- Nonpartisan approach (register everyone)
- Know state registration deadlines
- Submit registrations promptly
- Provide voter information packets
- Collect contact info for follow-up

### Voter Education Programs

**Community Education**

| Format | Content |
|--------|---------|
| Workshops | Voting process, rights, ID requirements |
| Information sessions | What's on the ballot |
| Q&A events | Address community concerns |
| Multilingual materials | Reach all community members |

**Targeting Key Groups**

| Group | Focus |
|-------|-------|
| Young people | First-time voter education |
| Recently naturalized citizens | Registration, process |
| Formerly incarcerated | Rights restoration |
| Elderly | Absentee voting, accessibility |

### Get-Out-The-Vote (GOTV)

**Phone Banking**

| Element | Approach |
|---------|----------|
| Timing | Start 2+ weeks before election |
| Script | Brief, encouraging, informative |
| Information | Polling place, ID requirements |
| Follow-up | Day-of reminders |

**Canvassing**

| Element | Approach |
|---------|----------|
| Targeting | Registered voters, low-propensity |
| Conversations | Listen, connect, encourage |
| Materials | Leave polling info |
| Teams | Safety, efficiency |

**Transportation Programs**

- Rides to polls
- Carpool coordination
- Partnership with rideshare companies
- Accessible vehicle availability

### Election Protection

**Poll Monitoring**

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|------------------|
| Poll observers | Watch for problems legally |
| Voter assistance | Help confused voters |
| Problem reporters | Document and report issues |
| Legal backup | Connect to attorneys |

**Hotline Support**

- Staff 866-OUR-VOTE locally
- Provide language assistance
- Document problems systematically
- Escalate issues appropriately

## For Advocates

### Legislative Advocacy

**At the Federal Level**
*Support Key Legislation*

| Bill | Action |
|------|--------|
| John Lewis VRA | Contact Senators |
| Freedom to Vote Act | Demand floor vote |
| Filibuster reform | Build pressure |

*How to Advocate*

- Call congressional offices
- Write letters and emails
- Attend town halls
- Organize constituent meetings
- Join lobbying days

**At the State Level**
*Offensive Advocacy*

| Goal | Tactics |
|------|---------|
| Pass reform legislation | Lobby legislators |
| Qualify ballot initiatives | Signature gathering |
| Support reform candidates | Electoral engagement |

*Defensive Advocacy*

| Goal | Tactics |
|------|---------|
| Block restrictive bills | Testimony, lobbying |
| Governor vetoes | Pressure campaigns |
| Referendum challenges | Signature gathering |
