# Political Violence: Current State

## Threat Assessment

### Federal Assessments

**FBI Assessment (2024)**:

- Domestic terrorism remains highest domestic threat
- Racially or ethnically motivated violent extremists (RMVEs) primary concern
- Anti-government/anti-authority extremists significant threat
- Lone actors hardest to detect

**DHS Assessment**:

- Domestic violent extremism elevated threat
- Online radicalization accelerating
- Mass casualty attacks remain primary concern
- Threats to soft targets and public gatherings

### Threat Categories

| Category | Description | Assessment |
|----------|-------------|------------|
| White supremacist | Racially motivated terrorism | High threat |
| Anti-government | Militia, sovereign citizen | High threat |
| Anarchist/left-wing | Anti-capitalist, Antifa | Lower threat level |
| Issue-based | Abortion, environment | Moderate threat |
| QAnon-adjacent | Conspiracy-driven | Growing concern |

## Threats to Public Officials

### Federal Officials

**Congress**:

| Metric | 2016 | 2021 | Trend |
|--------|------|------|-------|
| Threats investigated | ~900 | ~9,600 | 10x increase |
| Arrests | ~130 | ~200+ | Increase |
| Security spending | Baseline | Much higher | Major increase |

**Recent Incidents**:

- Congressional baseball shooting (2017)
- January 6 Capitol attack (2021)
- Kavanaugh assassination attempt (2022)
- Paul Pelosi attack (2022)
- Numerous other threats and confrontations

**Judicial Branch**:

- Threats to federal judges increased
- Supreme Court security enhanced
- Marshals Service protection expanded

### State and Local Officials

**Election Officials**:

- Unprecedented threats post-2020
- Thousands of election workers threatened
- Many resigned citing safety
- Ongoing protection challenges

**State Legislators**:

- Increased threats and harassment
- Armed protests at capitols
- Online targeting campaigns
- Some physical confrontations

**School Board Members**:

- Dramatic increase in threats
- Resignations across country
- DOJ response controversial
- Ongoing security concerns

**Public Health Officials**:

- COVID-related threats peaked
- Many officials resigned
- Continued targeting
- Long-term impact on public health workforce

### Local Officials

**Mayors and Council Members**:

- Threats increased
- Less security resources than federal officials
- Deterrent effect on candidates
- Small towns affected as well as cities
