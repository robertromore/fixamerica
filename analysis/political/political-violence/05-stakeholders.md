# Political Violence: Stakeholders

## Government Actors

### Federal Law Enforcement

**FBI**:

- Primary domestic terrorism investigative authority
- Joint Terrorism Task Forces
- Threat assessment
- Approximately 2,700+ domestic terrorism investigations

**Key Interests**:

- Adequate resources and authority
- Clear legal framework
- Civil liberties compliance
- Public trust

**DHS**:

- Infrastructure protection
- Prevention programs (CP3)
- Intelligence sharing
- Grant programs

**ATF**:

- Firearms investigations
- Explosives expertise
- Arson investigations

**Secret Service**:

- Threat assessment research
- Protective intelligence
- Targeted violence prevention

### Department of Justice

**National Security Division**:

- Domestic terrorism policy
- Prosecution support
- Strategy development

**U.S. Attorneys**:

- Prosecutions
- District-level coordination
- Community engagement

**Key Interests**:

- Successful prosecutions
- Consistent application
- Civil liberties protection
- Public confidence

### Congress

**Relevant Committees**:

- House Homeland Security
- Senate Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs
- House and Senate Judiciary
- House and Senate Intelligence

**Roles**:

- Oversight of agencies
- Legislation
- Funding
- Hearings and investigations

**Key Interests**:

- Own security
- Constituent safety
- Political implications
- Effective response

### State and Local Law Enforcement

**State Police/Patrol**:

- Intelligence gathering
- Threat response
- Capitol security

**Local Police**:

- First responders
- Community policing
- Threat reporting

**Key Interests**:

- Resources
- Training
- Intelligence sharing
- Clear jurisdiction

## Targets of Political Violence

### Public Officials

**Elected Officials**:

- Members of Congress
- State legislators
- Local officials (mayors, council, school board)
- Judges

**Appointed Officials**:

- Election administrators
- Public health officials
- School administrators
- Various agency officials

**Key Concerns**:

- Personal and family safety
- Adequate protection
- Ability to do job
- Support from institutions

### Election Workers

**Categories**:

- State election directors
- Local election officials
- Volunteer poll workers
- Temporary election staff

**Key Concerns**:

- Unprecedented threats
- Limited protection
- Job security
- Emotional impact

## Power Analysis

### Most Influential Stakeholders

| Stakeholder | Influence | Basis |
|-------------|-----------|-------|
| Federal law enforcement | High | Enforcement authority |
| Congress | High | Legislation, funding |
| Political leaders | High | Rhetoric, norms |
| Tech platforms | High | Information environment |
| Courts | High | Legal interpretation |
| Media | Moderate-High | Public opinion |
| State/local officials | Moderate | Implementation |
| Civil society | Moderate | Advocacy, monitoring |
| Victims | Low-Moderate | Moral authority |

### Who Benefits from Status Quo

- Those using violent rhetoric without consequence
- Platforms benefiting from engagement
- Those politically advantaged by polarization
- Extremists facing limited consequences

### Who Loses from Status Quo

- Targets of political violence
- Public officials deterred from service
- Election workers
- Marginalized communities
- Democratic discourse broadly
