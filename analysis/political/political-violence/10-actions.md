# Political Violence: Actions

## For All Citizens

### Recognize and Report Threats

**Warning Signs**:

- Direct or implied threats of violence
- Detailed planning discussions
- Weapons acquisition
- Target identification
- Glorification of past attacks

**Reporting Options**:

| Situation | Report To |
|-----------|-----------|
| Imminent threat | 911 |
| Online threats | Platform + FBI tips |
| Election-related | Election officials + FBI |
| General concerns | FBI tips (1-800-CALL-FBI) |
| Workplace/school | Security + local police |

### Support Democratic Norms

**Personal Actions**:

- Reject violent rhetoric from any source
- Condemn political violence regardless of source
- Model civil disagreement
- Engage with different viewpoints respectfully

**Community Actions**:

- Support cross-partisan dialogue
- Participate in civic institutions
- Build relationships across lines
- Speak up against dehumanizing language

### Engage Constructively

**In Political Discussions**:

- Avoid dehumanizing language
- Distinguish disagreement from demonization
- Challenge violent rhetoric in your community
- Model productive engagement

**In Elections**:

- Support election workers
- Volunteer for election work
- Report intimidation
- Encourage peaceful acceptance of outcomes

- Accept election outcomes

**Policy Advocacy**:

## For Media

### Responsible Reporting

**Coverage Guidelines**:

- Don't amplify manifestos
- Don't glorify attackers
- Provide context
- Cover prevention, not just attacks

**Avoid**:

- Detailed attack methodology
- Excessive attacker coverage
- Sensationalism
- False equivalence

### Investigative Role

**Valuable Coverage**:

- Expose extremist networks
- Track threat patterns
- Hold platforms accountable
- Document official failures

## Key Questions for Candidates

### All Candidates

1. "Will you unequivocally condemn political violence from any source?"
2. "What will you do to protect election workers?"
3. "Do you support strengthening laws against threats to public officials?"
4. "Will you accept the outcome of fair elections?"
5. "How will you work to reduce political violence?"

### Federal Candidates

1. "Do you support the Domestic Terrorism Prevention Act?"
2. "What resources should go to preventing political violence?"
3. "How should social media platforms address extremist content?"

### State/Local Candidates

1. "How will you protect local officials from threats?"
2. "What security measures do you support for election workers?"
3. "How should schools address threat prevention?"

## If You Witness Political Violence

### Immediate Actions

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Ensure personal safety |
| 2 | Call 911 if emergency |
| 3 | Document if safe (photos, video) |
| 4 | Provide witness information |
| 5 | Seek support if traumatized |

### Follow-Up

- Provide statements to law enforcement
- Connect victims with resources
- Support community healing
- Advocate for prevention

## If You're Targeted

### Immediate Steps

| Priority | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Safety first | Remove from immediate danger |
| Report | Contact law enforcement |
| Document | Preserve all evidence |
| Support | Contact employer, counseling |
| Legal | Consider protective orders |

### Ongoing Safety

- Work with security professionals
- Protect personal information
- Vary routines
- Build support network
- Know resources available
