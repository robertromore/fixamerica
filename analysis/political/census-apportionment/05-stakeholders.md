# Census and Apportionment: Stakeholders

## Overview

Census and apportionment affect virtually every American, but some groups have more direct stakes and more organized interests. Understanding who benefits from current arrangements, who is harmed, and who has power to change them is essential for effective reform.

## Primary Stakeholders

### Federal Government

**Census Bureau**

| Interest | Position |
|----------|----------|
| Accurate count | Professional mission |
| Independence | Desire protection from political pressure |
| Adequate funding | Need resources to do job well |
| Modern methods | Want to use best statistical practices |

**Role in System**

- Conducts enumeration
- Develops methodology
- Produces data products
- Faces political pressure

**Current Concerns**

- 2020 political interference damaged morale and reputation
- 2030 planning needs stable funding and leadership
- Professional staff increasingly concerned about independence
- Technology modernization ongoing

**Commerce Department**

| Interest | Position |
|----------|----------|
| Political responsiveness | Serves administration priorities |
| Efficiency | Budget management |
| Control | Authority over Bureau |

**Congress**

| Chamber | Interest |
|---------|----------|
| House | Seat distribution among states |
| Senate | No direct apportionment stake, but oversight role |
| Both | Funding decisions, oversight authority |

**Key Committees**

- House Oversight Committee
- Senate Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs
- Appropriations subcommittees

### State Governments

**All States**

| Interest | Stakes |
|----------|--------|
| Representation | House seats, Electoral College votes |
| Funding | $1.5+ trillion allocated by census data |
| Redistricting | Need accurate data for district drawing |
| Planning | State program administration |

**Gaining States (2020)**

| State | Seats Gained | Interest |
|-------|--------------|----------|
| Texas | +2 | Maintain counting methods |
| Florida | +1 | Continue growth recognition |
| Colorado | +1 | Validate counting approach |
| Montana | +1 | Support current methodology |
| North Carolina | +1 | Preserve gains |
| Oregon | +1 | Defend results |

**Losing States (2020)**

| State | Seats Lost | Interest |
|-------|------------|----------|
| California | -1 | Challenge methodology, fight undercount |
| New York | -1 | Contested 89-person margin |
| Illinois | -1 | Question accuracy |
| Michigan | -1 | Advocate for adjustment |
| Ohio | -1 | Support status quo (Republican government) |
| Pennsylvania | -1 | Mixed response |
| West Virginia | -1 | Population decline undeniable |

**Small States**

| Concern | Position |
|---------|----------|
| House expansion | Generally oppose (dilutes relative power) |
| Apportionment method | Support Huntington-Hill (small-state bias) |
| Electoral College | Benefit from current structure |

**Large States**

| Concern | Position |
|---------|----------|
| House expansion | Generally support (more proportional representation) |
| Apportionment method | May prefer alternatives |
| Per-capita representation | Currently disadvantaged |

### Local Governments

**Cities**

| Interest | Stakes |
|----------|--------|
| Accurate count | Federal funding, political representation |
| Hard-to-count populations | Urban areas have more |
| Complete Count Committees | Investment in enumeration |

**Major City Concerns**

- Urban undercount costs representation and funding
- Immigrant populations face response barriers
- Homeless population counting challenging
- Rapid change between censuses

**Counties**

| Interest | Stakes |
|----------|--------|
| Planning data | Infrastructure, services |
| Federal funding | County programs |
| Redistricting | County lines in district drawing |

**Rural Areas**

| Interest | Concern |
|----------|---------|
| Prison populations | Count at prison benefits rural districts |
| Relative representation | House expansion would dilute |
| Data accuracy | Small-area data less reliable |

### Political Parties

**Republican Party**

| Position | Rationale |
|----------|-----------|
| Opposed citizenship question removal | Would benefit in redistricting |
| Generally oppose House expansion | Current Electoral College advantage |
| Support prison counting at facility | Benefits rural Republican districts |
| Question statistical adjustment | Would benefit urban Democratic areas |

**Democratic Party**

| Position | Rationale |
|----------|-----------|
| Favor Bureau independence | Protect against political manipulation |
| Support House expansion | More proportional representation |
| Support prison counting at home | Benefits urban areas |
| Favor statistical adjustment | Would address minority undercount |

| Large states | Electoral importance | Fair representation |

| Stakeholder | Power Source | Key Interest |
|-------------|--------------|--------------|

| Media | Public attention | Occasional coverage |

| Stakeholder | Limitation | Key Interest |
|-------------|------------|--------------|
| Undercounted communities | Marginalized | Accurate count |

### Stakeholder Positions on Key Issues

**Census Bureau Independence**

| Stakeholder | Position | Rationale |
|-------------|----------|-----------|
| Career staff | Support | Professional integrity |
| Reform advocates | Support | Prevent political interference |
| Administration (any) | Usually oppose | Want control |
| Congress | Split | Oversight vs. independence |

**House Expansion**

| Stakeholder | Position | Rationale |
|-------------|----------|-----------|
| Large states | Support | More seats |
| Small states | Oppose | Diluted relative power |
| Electoral College reformers | Support | Less distortion |
| Current members | Generally oppose | Diluted individual power |

**Prison-Based Gerrymandering**

| Stakeholder | Position | Rationale |
|-------------|----------|-----------|
| Urban communities | Oppose current practice | Lose representation |
| Rural prison communities | Support status quo | Gain representation |
| Criminal justice reformers | Support home counting | Principle |
| Prisoners | Support home counting | Representation |

**Citizenship Question**

| Stakeholder | Position | Rationale |
|-------------|----------|-----------|
| Immigrant communities | Oppose | Chilling effect |
| Civil rights groups | Oppose | Undercount concerns |
| Trump administration | Supported | Political advantage |
| Census Bureau staff | Opposed | Accuracy concerns |

**Differential Privacy**

| Stakeholder | Position | Rationale |
|-------------|----------|-----------|
| Privacy advocates | Support | Constitutional protection |
| States (redistricting) | Concerned | Accuracy for small areas |
| Researchers | Concerned | Data utility |
| Census Bureau | Implementing | Legal requirement |
