# Collective Bargaining: Stakeholder Analysis

## Workers

### Workers Covered by Collective Bargaining

**Profile**

- 16.2 million workers covered by contracts
- 11.8% of workforce
- Higher concentration in public sector, utilities, manufacturing
- Earn 10-20% more than comparable non-union workers

**Interests**

| Interest | Priority |
|----------|----------|
| Contract enforcement | High - protect existing gains |
| First contract success | High - for newly organized |
| Broader scope | Medium - expand what can be bargained |
| Strike protection | High - restore bargaining leverage |
| Pattern bargaining | Medium - extend gains across industry |

**Challenges Faced**

| Challenge | Impact |
|-----------|--------|
| Scope limitations | Cannot bargain over key decisions |
| Employer delay tactics | First contracts take 2+ years |
| Strike vulnerability | Permanent replacement threat |
| Erosion of gains | Concession bargaining |

### Workers Without Coverage

**Profile**

- 120+ million workers not covered
- Surveys show 48% would vote for union representation
- Concentrated in service industries, small workplaces
- Many unaware of collective bargaining rights

**Interests**

| Interest | Priority |
|----------|----------|
| Gaining coverage | High |
| Sectoral bargaining | High - coverage without organizing |
| First contract arbitration | High - guarantee results |
| Strike protection | High - need leverage |
| Reduced employer resistance | High |

**Barriers**

| Barrier | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| Enterprise model | Must organize each workplace |
| Employer opposition | Intense campaigns against organizing |
| First contract problem | Even winning doesn't guarantee contract |
| Retaliation fear | 34% of campaigns see illegal firings |

### Public Sector Workers

**Profile**

- 21.9 million total public sector workers
- 7.9 million covered by collective bargaining
- 36.1% coverage rate
- Teachers, police, firefighters, government employees

**Interests**

| Interest | Priority |
|----------|----------|
| Maintain existing rights | High - under attack in many states |
| Strike rights | High (most cannot strike) |
| Scope expansion | Medium - bargain over more issues |
| Janus protection | High - prevent free riding |
| State floor | High - federal minimum standards |

**Special Concerns**

| Concern | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| State variation | Rights depend on state law |
| Political employer | Bargaining inherently political |
| Essential services | Strike restrictions |
| Taxpayer framing | Opposition frames as "vs. taxpayers" |
| Janus aftermath | Free rider problem |

## Employers

### Large Corporations

**Profile**

- Fortune 500 and other major employers
- Generally hostile to collective bargaining
- Sophisticated legal and consulting resources
- Political influence through lobbying

**Interests**

| Interest | Priority |
|----------|----------|
| Avoid collective bargaining | High |
| Minimize labor costs | High |
| Maintain management prerogatives | High |
| Limit mandatory subjects | High |
| Preserve striker replacement | High |
| Weaken unions | High |

**Bargaining Approach**

| Approach | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Pre-election | Intense anti-union campaigns |
| First contract | Delay, surface bargain |
| Established unions | Push for concessions |
| Strikes | Threaten or use permanent replacement |

### Small and Medium Businesses

**Profile**

- 33 million small businesses
- Most never face organizing
- Some legitimately cannot afford union wages
- Often used as front for larger business interests

**Interests**

| Interest | Priority |
|----------|----------|
| Flexibility | High |
| Cost control | High |
| Avoid unionization | Medium-High |
| Level playing field | Medium - worry about union competitors |

**Variation**

| Type | Relationship to Bargaining |
|------|---------------------------|
| True small business | Rarely face organizing |
| Franchisees | May be dominated by franchisor |
| Subcontractors | May be pressured by lead contractors |
| Growing companies | May face first organizing |

### Employer Associations

**Role in Bargaining**

| Type | Function |
|------|----------|
| Multi-employer units | Bargain jointly with union |
| Industry associations | Coordinate employer positions |
| Employer councils | Share information, strategies |
| Construction groups | Negotiate project agreements |

**Key Associations**

| Association | Sector | Position |
|-------------|--------|----------|
| National Restaurant Association | Food service | Anti-bargaining |
| National Retail Federation | Retail | Anti-bargaining |
| Associated Builders and Contractors | Construction | Open shop advocacy |
| Associated General Contractors | Construction | Mixed (some union) |

## Government Actors

### National Labor Relations Board

**Profile**

- Five-member board (presidential appointees)
- General Counsel (presidential appointee)
- Regional offices conduct elections, investigate charges
- Political composition determines approach

**Role in Bargaining**

| Function | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Define bargaining subjects | Mandatory vs. permissive |
| Determine appropriate units | Who bargains together |
| Enforce duty to bargain | Investigate, prosecute violations |
| Issue bargaining orders | Remedy for extreme violations |
| Interpret bargaining law | Board decisions set precedent |

**Current Status (Biden Board)**

| Development | Effect |
|-------------|--------|
| Cemex doctrine | Bargaining orders for employer violations |
| Faster elections | Reduced delay opportunities |
| Stronger enforcement | More 10(j) injunctions |
| Pro-worker interpretations | Expanded worker protections |

### Federal Mediation and Conciliation Service

**Profile**

- Independent federal agency
- Provides mediation services
- Maintains panel of arbitrators
- Tracks bargaining activity

**Role**

| Function | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Mediation | Help parties reach agreement |
| Arbitrator referral | Provide arbitrators for disputes |
| Preventive mediation | Help parties before impasse |
| Data collection | Track contract expirations |

### State Labor Relations Agencies

**Function**

| State Type | Agency Role |
|------------|-------------|
| States with public sector law | Enforce state bargaining rights |
| States without | No agency (or limited) |
| Some states | Extend NLRA-like protections |

**Key State Agencies**

| State | Agency | Jurisdiction |
|-------|--------|--------------|
| California | PERB | Public sector |
| New York | PERB | Public sector |
| New Jersey | PERC | Public sector |
| Illinois | ILRB | Public sector |

**Profile**

- Also advise on bargaining strategy
