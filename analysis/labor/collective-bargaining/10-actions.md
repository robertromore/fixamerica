# Collective Bargaining: What Workers Can Do

## For Workers Covered by Collective Bargaining

### Know Your Contract

**Key Sections to Understand**

| Section | Why It Matters |
|---------|---------------|
| Recognition clause | Who is covered |
| Wages and raises | Your compensation |
| Benefits | Healthcare, pension, leave |
| Hours and overtime | Work schedules |
| Seniority | Layoffs, promotions |
| Grievance procedure | How to resolve problems |
| Management rights | Employer reserved powers |
| No-strike clause | Limitations during contract |

**Getting Your Contract**

| Method | How |
|--------|-----|
| Union representative | Ask your steward or rep |
| Union office | Request from local |
| Online portal | Many unions post contracts |
| HR department | Employer must provide copy |

### Use the Grievance Process

**When to File**

- Contract violation by employer
- Unjust discipline or termination
- Inconsistent rule enforcement
- Safety issues covered by contract
- Benefits denial
- Seniority violations

**How to File**

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Document the issue (dates, witnesses, details) |
| 2 | Contact your steward |
| 3 | Review contract language |
| 4 | Follow contract timelines |
| 5 | Participate in grievance meetings |
| 6 | Appeal if necessary |

**Weingarten Rights**

If you are called to a meeting that could lead to discipline:

| Right | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Request representation | "I request a union representative" |
| Wait for representative | Meeting should pause |
| Consult before questions | Speak with rep privately |
| Active participation | Rep can assist during meeting |

## For Workers Without Coverage

### Organize for Collective Bargaining

**Step 1: Assess Your Workplace**

| Question | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| What are the main issues? | Identify bargaining priorities |
| Who are natural leaders? | Build organizing committee |
| How do workers communicate? | Find outreach channels |
| What is management like? | Assess opposition intensity |

**Step 2: Build Support**

| Activity | How |
|----------|-----|
| One-on-one conversations | Talk to coworkers individually |
| Small group meetings | Discuss issues together |
| Map your workplace | Track supporters, undecided, opposed |
| Build supermajority | Aim for 65-80% before going public |

**Step 3: Contact a Union**

| Resource | How to Find |
|----------|-------------|
| AFL-CIO | aflcio.org/formaunion |
| Industry-specific unions | Search by industry |
| Emergency Workplace Organizing Committee | workerorganizing.org |

**Step 4: Win Recognition**

| Method | Process |
|--------|---------|
| NLRB election | File petition, campaign, vote |
| Card check | Majority sign cards, employer recognizes |
| Voluntary recognition | Employer agrees to recognize |

### Prepare for First Contract Bargaining

**Before Bargaining Begins**

| Preparation | Purpose |
|-------------|---------|
| Survey members | Identify priorities |
| Research employer | Financial information |
| Study comparable contracts | Benchmark terms |
| Build bargaining committee | Represent all workers |
| Develop proposals | Know what you want |

**During Bargaining**

| Action | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| Regular updates | Keep members informed |
| Solidarity actions | Show employer worker unity |
| Document employer positions | Track proposals |
| Prepare for delays | Anticipate employer tactics |

**If Employer Delays**

| Response | Action |
|----------|--------|
| File ULP charges | Document bad faith |
| Public pressure | Community and media |
| Political support | Elected officials |
| Economic action | Authorized job actions |

### Know Your Bargaining Rights

**Section 7 Rights Include**

| Right | Protection |
|-------|------------|
| Discuss wages | With coworkers |
| Discuss working conditions | Cannot be prohibited |
| Engage in concerted activity | Protected action together |
| Form or join union | Cannot be discriminated against |
| Bargain collectively | Employer must negotiate |

**Employer Cannot**

| Prohibited Action | Description |
|-------------------|-------------|
| Refuse to bargain | Must meet and negotiate |
| Surface bargain | Must intend to reach agreement |
| Unilateral changes | During negotiations |
| Direct dealing | Must bargain through union |
| Retaliate | For bargaining activity |

## If You Face Retaliation

### Document Everything

| What to Document | How |
|------------------|-----|
| Bargaining activity | Dates, participants, topics |
| Management responses | Exact words, witnesses |
| Changes in treatment | Before and after |
| Communications | Save emails, texts |

### File ULP Charges

**For Bargaining Violations**

| Violation | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| 8(a)(5) refusal to bargain | Surface bargaining, delays |
| 8(a)(3) discrimination | Retaliation for bargaining activity |
| 8(a)(1) interference | Threats, surveillance |

**How to File**

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| Contact NLRB | 1-844-762-6572 |
| Prepare information | Dates, facts, witnesses |
| File within 6 months | Strict deadline |
| Work with union | If represented |
