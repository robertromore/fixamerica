# Collective Bargaining: Implementation Roadmap

## Overview

Strengthening collective bargaining requires coordinated action across federal, state, and organizing fronts. This roadmap sequences reforms from immediately achievable to transformational, while recognizing that opportunities may arise unpredictably.

## Phase 1: Foundation Building (Years 1-2)

### NLRB Administrative Action

**Achievable Without Congress**

| Action | Timeline | Mechanism |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| Expedited elections | Immediate | Rulemaking |
| Bargaining order remedies | Ongoing | Case decisions |
| Stronger 10(j) injunctions | Ongoing | Enforcement policy |
| Surface bargaining enforcement | Ongoing | Case prosecution |

**Cemex Doctrine Implementation**

|---------|--------|

## Phase 2: Legislative Breakthrough (Years 2-4)

### Federal Reform Scenarios

**Scenario A: PRO Act Passage**

| Prerequisite | Path |
|--------------|------|
| Senate majority | 50+ Democrats |
| Filibuster reform | Carve-out or elimination |
| House passage | Already passed previously |
| Presidential signature | Assured |

**PRO Act Implementation**

| Phase | Timeline |
|-------|----------|
| Enactment | Year 2 |
| NLRB rulemaking | 6-12 months |
| Employer compliance | 90-day transition |
| First contract arbitration begins | Year 2-3 |
| Permanent replacement ban effective | Immediate |

**Scenario B: Partial Reform**

| Achievable Elements | Vehicle |
|---------------------|---------|
| NLRB funding increase | Appropriations |
| Public sector floor | Standalone bill |

| Worker classification reform | Regulatory action |

**Scenario C: Blocked Reform**

| Alternative | Action |
|-------------|--------|

| Executive maximalism | Push administrative limits |
| Organizing surge | Build power without law change |

## Dependencies and Sequencing

### Critical Path

```text
NLRB enforcement (immediate)
        |
        v
State-level wins (Years 1-3)
        |
        v
Electoral success (maintain/expand)
        |
        v
Federal reform (when possible)
        |
        v
Sectoral expansion (build on wins)
        |
        v
Works councils (longer term)
```

### Key Dependencies

| Action | Depends On |
|--------|------------|
| PRO Act | Senate composition, filibuster |
| Sectoral bargaining | State pilots first |
| First contract success | PRO Act or aggressive NLRB |
| Strike restoration | Federal legislation |
| Works councils | 8(a)(2) reform |

## Risk Assessment

### Major Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|------------|------------|
| Court challenges | High | Careful drafting, litigation strategy |
| Political reversal | Medium | Build state-level redundancy |
| Employer adaptation | High | Continuous strategy evolution |
| Filibuster persistence | High | State alternatives ready |
| NLRB composition change | Medium | Codify rules in statute |

### Scenario Planning

**Best Case**

- PRO Act passes Year 2
- Rapid first contract success
- State sectoral experiments succeed
- Federal sectoral framework by Year 8

**Base Case**

- Federal reform delayed
- Significant state progress
- Gradual coverage increase
- Sectoral pilots in 8+ states by Year 7

**Worst Case**

- Federal reform blocked
- Court invalidates NLRB rules
- State reforms limited
- Focus on organizing, litigation
