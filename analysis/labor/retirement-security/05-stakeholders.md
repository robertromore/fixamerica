# Retirement Security: Stakeholders

## Workers

### Workers with Coverage

**Characteristics**

| Factor | Profile |
|--------|---------|
| Employment type | Full-time, W-2 employees |
| Employer size | Typically 100+ employees |
| Industry | Finance, tech, government, healthcare |
| Income | Above-median |
| Union status | Often represented |

**Interests**

| Interest | Priority |
|----------|----------|
| Employer match | High |
| Low fees | Medium (often unaware) |
| Investment options | Medium |
| Flexibility | Medium |
| Portability | High for mobile workers |

**Concerns**

| Concern | Impact |
|---------|--------|
| Market volatility | Fear of losses near retirement |
| Adequacy | Uncertainty about having enough |
| Longevity | Outliving savings |
| Healthcare costs | Major unknown expense |

### Workers Without Coverage

**Profile: 57 Million Americans**

| Category | Number | Share |
|----------|--------|-------|
| Small business employees (<50) | 27 million | 47% |
| Part-time workers | 12 million | 21% |
| Self-employed | 9 million | 16% |
| Gig workers (primary) | 5 million | 9% |
| Other | 4 million | 7% |

**Barriers to Saving**

| Barrier | Prevalence |
|---------|------------|
| No plan offered | 100% (definition) |
| Income insufficient | 60%+ |
| Lack knowledge of IRAs | 50%+ |
| Competing financial needs | 70%+ |
| Student debt | 25%+ |

**Needs**

| Need | Current Solution |
|------|------------------|
| Access to plan | State auto-IRAs emerging |
| Lower barriers | Auto-enrollment |
| Appropriate products | Target-date funds |
| Financial education | Limited availability |
| Emergency savings | Competes with retirement |

### Near-Retirees (Ages 55-64)

**Current Situation**

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Median retirement savings | $134,000 |
| Median 401(k) balance | $71,000 |
| No retirement savings | 22% |
| Still working by necessity | 40%+ |

**Urgent Concerns**

| Concern | Urgency |
|---------|---------|
| Catch-up contributions | High |
| Healthcare before Medicare | Critical |
| Market timing risk | High |
| Social Security claiming | High |
| Long-term care planning | Growing |

### Low-Wage Workers

**Profile**

| Characteristic | Reality |
|----------------|---------|
| Plan access rate | 31% (bottom quartile) |
| Participation when offered | 38% |
| Average contribution rate | 4.5% |
| Median savings | <$10,000 |

**Specific Barriers**

| Barrier | Impact |
|---------|--------|
| Paycheck-to-paycheck | Cannot save |
| High-fee plans | Erode small balances |
| Early withdrawal pressure | Depletes savings |
| Job instability | Coverage gaps |
| No employer match | Reduced incentive |

### Women

**Disparities**

| Metric | Women | Men |
|--------|-------|-----|
| Median 401(k) balance | $42,000 | $63,000 |
| Social Security benefit | $1,556/mo | $1,986/mo |
| Poverty rate 65+ | 10.3% | 7.0% |
| Years in workforce (median) | 27 | 37 |
| Career earnings gap | 82 cents per dollar | $1.00 |

**Specific Challenges**

| Challenge | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| Wage gap | Lower savings capacity |
| Caregiving breaks | Lost contribution years |
| Longer life expectancy | Savings must last longer |
| Part-time work | Less plan access |
| Widowhood | Often drops to survivor benefit |

### Workers of Color

**Retirement Wealth Gap**

| Group | Median Retirement Savings (55-64) | Ratio to White |
|-------|-----------------------------------|----------------|
| White | $185,000 | 1.00 |
| Black | $27,000 | 0.15 |
| Hispanic | $31,000 | 0.17 |
| Asian | $156,000 | 0.84 |

**Contributing Factors**

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Wealth gap | Less family support |
| Employment discrimination | Lower wages, less access |
| Industry concentration | Service sectors with less coverage |
| Historical exclusion | Social Security originally excluded many |
| Less homeownership | Missing wealth-building vehicle |

## Employers

### Large Employers (500+ Employees)

**Current Practices**

| Feature | Adoption Rate |
|---------|---------------|
| Offer 401(k) | 90%+ |
| Auto-enrollment | 75% |
| Employer match | 85% |
| Auto-escalation | 55% |

**Motivations**

| Motivation | Importance |
|------------|------------|
| Attract talent | High |
| Tax benefits | High |
| Competitive necessity | High |
| Employee loyalty | Medium |
| Corporate responsibility | Variable |

**Concerns About Reform**

| Concern | Issue |
|---------|-------|
| Cost | Mandates increase expenses |
| Liability | Fiduciary duties |
| Administrative burden | Compliance requirements |
| Competitive position | If competitors don't comply |

### Small Employers (<50 Employees)

**Current Practices**

| Feature | Adoption Rate |
|---------|---------------|
| Offer any retirement plan | 28% |
| Offer 401(k) | 14% |
| Employer match | 18% |

**Barriers to Offering Plans**

| Barrier | % Citing |
|---------|----------|
| Cost | 47% |
| Employees prefer wages | 35% |
| Administrative burden | 32% |
| Uncertain business outlook | 24% |
| Don't know how | 18% |

**Needs**

| Need | Solution |
|------|----------|
| Lower costs | Pooled employer plans (MEPs) |
| Simplified administration | State-run programs |
| Reduced liability | Safe harbor provisions |
| Tax incentives | Startup credits (SECURE Act) |
