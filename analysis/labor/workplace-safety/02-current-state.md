# Workplace Safety: Current State

## The Problem Today

American workplaces kill and injure workers at rates far exceeding those of peer nations. Despite five decades of the Occupational Safety and Health Act, workers continue to die from preventable causes: falls from heights, trench collapses, forklift accidents, exposure to toxic substances, and increasingly, heat stroke. The enforcement apparatus meant to protect workers has been systematically weakened, leaving OSHA unable to inspect more than a tiny fraction of workplaces. Penalties for even fatal violations remain trivial compared to corporate profits, creating a rational business decision to ignore safety requirements.

## Heat Illness

### Current Impact

| Metric | Data |
|--------|------|
| Official heat-related deaths (2021) | 43 |
| Estimated actual deaths | 600-2,000 |
| Heat illness cases | 2,700+ reported |
| Industries most affected | Agriculture, construction, delivery |

### Underreporting

Heat deaths are massively undercounted because:

- Symptoms attributed to other causes (heart attack, stroke)
- Workers die off-site
- No mandatory reporting
- No federal heat standard to define violations

### Climate Change Projections

| Decade | Projected Outdoor Worker Heat Deaths |
|--------|-------------------------------------|
| 2020s | 600-800 annually |
| 2030s | 800-1,200 annually |
| 2040s | 1,200-2,000+ annually |

### Lessons Unlearned

- No permanent infectious disease standard
- PPE stockpile inadequate
- Employer reporting requirements weak
- Whistleblower retaliation widespread
- Essential workers lacked essential protections

## Whistleblower Retaliation

### Current Protections

| Metric | Data |
|--------|------|
| OSHA whistleblower complaints (2022) | ~3,000 |
| Complaints merit investigation | ~30% |
| Complaints with favorable outcome | ~2% |
| Time to resolve complaint | 2+ years average |
| Maximum damages | Reinstatement + back pay |

### Barriers to Protection

- Short filing deadline (30 days)
- OSHA investigates, not worker
- No private right of action under OSH Act
- Burden of proof on worker
- Weak remedies

### Performance Variation

| Performance | States |
|-------------|--------|
| Above federal standards | CA, WA, OR |
| Comparable to federal | Most state plans |
| Below federal standards | Several (monitored) |

California OSHA (Cal/OSHA) is generally considered the strongest state program with:

- Heat illness standard (since 2005)
- COVID-19 emergency standard
- Higher staffing per worker
- Stronger penalties
