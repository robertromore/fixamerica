# Gig Economy: Resources

## Worker Organizations

### National Organizations

**Gig Workers Rising**

- Website: [gigworkersrising.org](https://www.gigworkersrising.org)
- Focus: Uber, Lyft drivers; California-based but national scope
- Activities: Organizing, policy advocacy, worker support
- Contact: Available through website

**Rideshare Drivers United**

- Website: [drivers-united.org](https://drivers-united.org)
- Focus: Los Angeles rideshare drivers
- Activities: Driver organizing, strike coordination, policy advocacy
- Key victory: Major role in AB5 campaign

**Los Deliveristas Unidos**

- Website: [losdeliveristasunidos.org](https://losdeliveristasunidos.org)
- Focus: New York City delivery workers
- Activities: Organizing, safety advocacy, minimum wage campaign
- Key victory: NYC delivery worker minimum wage

**Gig Workers Collective**

- Website: [gigworkerscollective.org](https://www.gigworkerscollective.org)
- Focus: Cross-platform organizing, Instacart focus
- Activities: Organizing, tip campaigns, safety advocacy

**Mobile Workers Alliance**

- Focus: International coordination of gig worker organizing
- Activities: Cross-border solidarity, information sharing

### Labor Unions Active in Gig Economy

**Service Employees International Union (SEIU)**

- Website: [seiu.org](https://www.seiu.org)
- Gig economy focus: Care workers, some rideshare organizing
- Resources: Organizing support, legal assistance, political advocacy

**International Brotherhood of Teamsters**

- Website: [teamster.org](https://teamster.org)
- Gig economy focus: Delivery workers, drivers
- Resources: Traditional union organizing expertise

**Communications Workers of America (CWA)**

- Website: [cwa-union.org](https://cwa-union.org)
- Gig economy focus: Tech workers, platform workers
- Resources: Tech sector organizing experience

**AFL-CIO**

- Website: [aflcio.org](https://aflcio.org)
- Role: National policy advocacy, coalition coordination
- Resources: Political action, policy research

### Worker Centers and Community Organizations

**National Domestic Workers Alliance**

- Website: [domesticworkers.org](https://www.domesticworkers.org)
- Focus: Care workers, including platform-based care work
- Resources: Organizing, policy advocacy, worker support

**National Employment Law Project (NELP)**

- Website: [nelp.org](https://www.nelp.org)
- Focus: Worker rights policy and advocacy
- Resources: Legal analysis, policy briefs, advocacy support

**Jobs With Justice**

- Website: [jwj.org](https://www.jwj.org)
- Focus: Worker rights coalition building
- Resources: Campaign support, coalition coordination

**Coworker.org**

- Website: [coworker.org](https://www.coworker.org)

- Resources: Campaign tools, petition hosting, networking

## Legal Resources

### Legal Advocacy Organizations

**National Employment Law Project (NELP)**

- Website: [nelp.org](https://www.nelp.org)
- Services: Policy advocacy, legal analysis, amicus briefs
- Key work: Worker classification, wage and hour

**Legal Aid at Work**

- Website: [legalaidatwork.org](https://www.legalaidatwork.org)
- Services: Direct legal services, impact litigation
- Focus: California worker rights

**A Better Balance**

- Website: [abetterbalance.org](https://www.abetterbalance.org)
- Focus: Work-family issues, paid leave
- Resources: Legal advocacy, policy

**Workplace Justice Lab**

- Focus: Research on labor law enforcement
- Resources: Enforcement data, policy analysis

**Worker Rights Clinics** at various law schools provide free legal assistance:

- Harvard Law School Labor and Employment Clinic

- Rutgers Law Workplace Justice Project

### Key Legal Cases

**Classification Cases**

| Case | Significance |
|------|--------------|
| *Dynamex v. Superior Court* (2018) | California ABC test adoption |
| *Uber BV v. Aslam* (UK, 2021) | UK "worker" status for drivers |
| *O'Connor v. Uber* | Major class action (settled) |
| *Prop 22 litigation* | California constitutional challenges |
