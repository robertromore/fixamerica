# Gig Economy: Actions

## What Gig Workers Can Do

### Immediate Self-Protection

**Track Your Earnings**

| Action | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| Record all hours worked | Document true hourly wage including waiting time |
| Track expenses | Vehicle, gas, phone, insurance, maintenance |
| Calculate net earnings | Gross pay minus expenses = actual wage |
| Save records | Evidence for legal claims, organizing |

**Tools for Tracking**

| Tool | Function |
|------|----------|
| Gridwise app | Automatic earnings tracking |
| Spreadsheet | Manual tracking, expense categories |
| Mileage log | IRS-required for deductions |
| Screenshot pay | Document pay breakdown before it disappears |

**Protect Your Rating**

| Strategy | Application |
|----------|-------------|
| Understand rating factors | Know what impacts your score |
| Document unfair ratings | Screenshot and save |
| Avoid low-tip areas/orders | Protect your time value |
| Know when to decline | Unprofitable trips aren't worth it |

**Tax Preparation**

| Action | Why It Matters |
|--------|----------------|
| Set aside 25-30% of earnings | Cover self-employment tax |
| Track all deductible expenses | Reduce tax burden |
| Make quarterly estimated payments | Avoid penalties |
| Consider professional tax help | Complex self-employment rules |

### Know Your Rights

**Current Legal Protections**

Even as independent contractors, gig workers have some rights:

| Right | Application |
|-------|-------------|
| Contract terms | Platforms must honor stated terms |
| Anti-discrimination | Some civil rights protections apply |
| Safety reporting | Can report unsafe conditions |
| Wage claims | If misclassified, can claim wages owed |

**State-Specific Protections**

| State | Protection |
|-------|------------|
| California | AB5 (though Prop 22 carves out); can challenge classification |
| New York | Delivery minimum wage; unemployment during COVID |
| Washington | Rideshare minimum rates, paid sick leave |
| Minnesota | Rideshare minimum standards |
| Massachusetts | Post-settlement protections |

**File Complaints When Appropriate**

| Issue | Where to Complain |
|-------|-------------------|
| Wage theft | State labor agency, DOL Wage and Hour |
| Misclassification | State labor agency, IRS (Form SS-8) |
| Discrimination | EEOC, state civil rights agency |
| Unsafe conditions | OSHA, state occupational safety |
| Deceptive practices | FTC, state consumer protection |

## What Citizens Can Do

### Be an Informed Consumer

**Understand the Business Model**

| Reality | Implication |
|---------|-------------|
| Low prices come from worker exploitation | You're paying less because workers earn less |
| Tips often replace wages | Platform adjusts base pay based on expected tips |
| Workers bear all costs | Vehicle, gas, insurance on the worker |
| No benefits | Workers have no healthcare, retirement |

**Support Worker-Friendly Practices**

| Action | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Tip in cash | Ensures worker gets full tip |
| Tip generously | Tips are significant income |
| Rate workers fairly | Stars affect livelihood |
| Be patient | Workers face pressure and traffic |
| Report platform abuses | When you witness problems |

### Advocate for Change

**Contact Elected Officials**

| Level | Message |
|-------|---------|
| Federal legislators | Support PRO Act, gig worker protections |
| State legislators | Support ABC test, portable benefits |
| Local officials | Support local worker protections |

**How to Contact**

| Method | Effectiveness |
|--------|---------------|
| Phone calls | Most impactful, hard to ignore |
| Personal letters | More impactful than form letters |
| In-person meetings | Town halls, office visits |
| Emails | Lower impact but still counted |
| Social media | Public pressure |

**Sample Message Points**

For phone calls or letters:

- "I support classifying gig workers as employees"
- "Please support the PRO Act / ABC test legislation"
- "Gig companies should pay their fair share for worker benefits"
- "Workers deserve protection from arbitrary deactivation"
- "Algorithms that control work should be transparent"

### Document Conditions

**Research Priorities**

| Topic | Need |
|-------|------|
| True earnings | After expenses, including waiting time |
| Working conditions | Safety, surveillance, stress |
| Algorithmic discrimination | Racial, gender disparities |
| Health impacts | Mental health, injuries, lack of care |

**Research Ethics**

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| Worker voice | Include workers in research design |
| Accessibility | Share findings with workers and advocates |
| Independence | Don't take platform funding |
| Accuracy | Don't overstate or understate findings |

## Collective Action Tactics

### Strikes and Walkouts

**Planning a Work Stoppage**

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Build organization first (can't strike without organization) |
| 2 | Choose strategic timing (holidays, high-demand periods) |
| 3 | Communicate clearly (why, when, demands) |
| 4 | Engage media (visibility matters) |
| 5 | Coordinate across platforms (prevent scabbing) |
| 6 | Follow up (maintain pressure) |
