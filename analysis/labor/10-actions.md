# Labor: Actions

## Overview

Improving conditions for American workers requires action at every levelâ€”from individual workers talking with coworkers to national policy campaigns. This section provides concrete steps for different actors, from immediate personal actions to long-term systemic change efforts. Worker power is built through collective action, and every action contributes to building that power.

---

## For Workers

### At Your Workplace

| Action | How | Impact |
|--------|-----|--------|
| **Talk with coworkers** | Break room, lunch, after work | Build relationships, identify shared concerns |
| **Know your rights** | NLRB.gov, worker center resources | Protect yourself from retaliation |
| **Document violations** | Keep records, dates, witnesses | Evidence for complaints |
| **Report violations** | WHD, OSHA, EEOC hotlines | Trigger enforcement |
| **Contact a union** | Research unions in your industry | Get organizing support |

### Organizing Your Workplace

| Action | How | Impact |
|--------|-----|--------|
| **Map your workplace** | Identify coworkers, shifts, departments | Understand organizing potential |
| **Build an organizing committee** | Recruit leaders from all areas | Distributed leadership |
| **Identify issues** | Survey coworkers, listen | Focus on shared concerns |
| **Contact union organizers** | AFL-CIO, sector unions, worker centers | Professional support |
| **Run a campaign** | Cards, petition, election | Recognition |

### Protecting Your Rights

| Action | How | Impact |
|--------|-----|--------|
| **Join your union if available** | Sign membership card | Collective power |
| **Report retaliation** | NLRB unfair labor practice | Legal protection |
| **File complaints** | WHD for wages, OSHA for safety | Agency enforcement |
| **Document everything** | Emails, texts, notes | Evidence preservation |
| **Know your Weingarten rights** | Request union rep in discipline meetings | Procedural protection |

| Action | How | Impact |
|--------|-----|--------|

## Quick Reference

### If You Have 5 Minutes

- Learn about your workplace rights at NLRB.gov
- Sign a petition supporting worker campaigns
- Share a worker story on social media
- Text a coworker about an issue at work

### If You Have 1 Hour

- Attend a union meeting
- Volunteer at a worker center
- Write to your legislator about labor issues
- Join a picket line
- Document a workplace violation

### If You Have Ongoing Commitment

- Join or organize your workplace
- Become a union steward or officer
- Build a labor-community coalition
- Run for office as a labor candidate
- Start or expand a worker center
- Train other worker leaders
