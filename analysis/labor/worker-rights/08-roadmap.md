# Worker Rights: Roadmap

## Phase 1: Achievable Reforms (Current Congress)

### Non-Compete Reform

**Federal Level**:
- FTC finalizes non-compete ban rule
- Defend rule against legal challenges
- Congressional codification if rule struck down

**State Level**:
- Expand state bans to additional states
- Lower income thresholds in existing ban states
- Add disclosure and consideration requirements

**Milestones**:
- [ ] FTC rule implementation
- [ ] 20+ states with restrictions
- [ ] Federal legislation introduced

### Whistleblower Enhancement

**Actions**:
- Pass targeted whistleblower bills (sector-specific)
- Strengthen SEC whistleblower program
- Enhance OSHA whistleblower funding

**Milestones**:
- [ ] Increase OSHA whistleblower staff 50%
- [ ] Pass at least one sector-specific enhancement
- [ ] Reduce whistleblower case processing time

### Scheduling Predictability

**State/Local**:
- Pass fair workweek laws in 5+ additional cities
- Oregon-style statewide law in 2+ states
- Build evidence base from existing laws

**Federal**:
- Introduce federal fair workweek bill
- Build coalition support
- Committee hearings

## Phase 2: Building Momentum (2-4 Years)

### Arbitration Reform

**Legislative**:
- Build support for FAIR Act
- Pass for specific claim types (sexual harassment first)
- Expand to other employment claims

**Regulatory**:
- NLRB action on class action waivers in NLRA context
- EEOC guidance on arbitration and discrimination
- Consumer Financial Protection Bureau coordination

**Milestones**:
- [ ] Pass harassment arbitration ban
- [ ] Wage claim arbitration restrictions
- [ ] Build toward comprehensive reform

### Workplace Privacy

**State Innovation**:
- Biometric privacy laws in 10+ states
- Social media protection in 30+ states
- Electronic monitoring disclosure requirements

**Federal**:
- Introduce comprehensive employee privacy bill
- Build business support through clear standards
- Committee consideration

### EEOC Strengthening

**Funding**:
- Double EEOC budget
- Hire 1,000+ additional investigators
- Technology modernization

**Enforcement**:
- Reduce average charge processing time to 6 months
- Increase litigation of pattern and practice cases
- Enhanced guidance on emerging issues

## Phase 3: Structural Reform (5+ Years)

### Just-Cause Protection

**State Level**:
- Introduce just-cause bills in worker-friendly states
- Build support through union contracts covering all workers
- Develop model legislation

**Federal**:
- Build coalition for federal just-cause standard
- Congressional hearings on international comparisons
- Develop phase-in approach

**Milestones**:
- [ ] Just-cause law in 2+ states
- [ ] Federal bill introduced
- [ ] Major employer voluntary adoption

### Comprehensive Worker Rights Act

**Components**:
- Just-cause protection
- Non-compete ban codified
- Whistleblower unified statute
- Workplace privacy protections
- Arbitration reform
- Enhanced enforcement

**Building Blocks**:
- Pass individual components first
- Build on state-level successes
- Develop political coalition

## Implementation Considerations

### Business Community Engagement

**Approach**:
- Engage progressive employers
- Emphasize predictability benefits
- Address legitimate flexibility concerns
- Phase-in provisions

**Message**:
- Clear rules benefit responsible employers
- Level playing field with bad actors
- Reduced litigation through clear standards
- Worker retention benefits

### Coalition Building

**Core Coalition**:
- Labor unions
- Civil rights organizations
- Employment lawyers (plaintiff side)
- Worker centers
- Progressive business groups

**Outreach**:
- Small business concerns addressed
- Religious communities on worker dignity
- Conservative concerns on government overreach addressed

### Legal Strategy

**Defensive**:
- Defend FTC rule in courts
- Protect state laws from preemption challenges
- Build administrative record for regulations

**Offensive**:
- Strategic litigation to expand protections
- Test case selection
- Amicus coordination

## Metrics for Success

### Short-term (1-2 years)

- Non-compete restrictions in 25+ states
- FTC rule implemented (or legislation passed if rule fails)
- Whistleblower case win rate increase to 35%
- 10+ jurisdictions with fair workweek laws

### Medium-term (3-5 years)

- Mandatory arbitration restricted for employment
- EEOC backlog reduced 50%
- Workplace privacy laws in 20+ states
- First just-cause state law passed

### Long-term (5-10 years)

- Federal just-cause standard
- Comprehensive worker rights framework
- Arbitration available only by genuine choice
- Worker rights comparable to peer democracies
