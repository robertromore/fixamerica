# Worker Rights: Actions

## Individual Actions

### Know Your Rights

**Understand Your Status**:
- Review your employment agreement
- Know if you signed non-compete or arbitration clause
- Understand your state's at-will exceptions
- Know protected activity under NLRA Section 7

**Document Everything**:
- Keep copies of performance reviews
- Document positive feedback from supervisors
- Save emails showing good performance
- Note any changes in treatment

**Resources**:
- EEOC website for federal rights
- State labor department for state-specific rights
- Workplace Fairness organization for guidance

### Protect Yourself

**Before Problems**:
- Keep personal copies of important documents
- Maintain email records (forward to personal account if permitted)
- Document your accomplishments
- Build relationships with colleagues who can serve as witnesses

**If Facing Retaliation**:
- Document timeline of events
- Note who said what, when
- Preserve evidence
- Consult with employment attorney early

### Negotiate for Better Terms

**At Hiring**:
- Ask about non-compete before accepting
- Negotiate removal or limitation of non-compete
- Request arbitration opt-out if available
- Get promises in writing

**During Employment**:
- Request just-cause provision in employment agreement
- Ask about scheduling policies
- Inquire about monitoring policies

## Collective Actions

### Workplace Organizing

**Section 7 Rights**:
- Discuss working conditions with coworkers
- Raise concerns collectively (protected concerted activity)
- Support union organizing efforts
- Know your rights during organizing campaigns

**Building Solidarity**:
- Connect with worker centers
- Join professional associations that advocate for workers
- Support colleagues facing retaliation

### Community Organizing

**Local Advocacy**:
- Support fair workweek ordinances in your city
- Attend city council hearings on worker protections
- Connect with local worker advocacy groups
- Share your story with media

**State Advocacy**:
- Contact state legislators about worker rights bills
- Testify at legislative hearings
- Join coalitions for reform

## Political Action

### Legislative Advocacy

**Federal**:
- Contact representatives about FAIR Act
- Support FTC non-compete rule
- Advocate for whistleblower protection
- Push for EEOC funding increases

**State**:
- Support non-compete ban legislation
- Advocate for fair workweek laws
- Push for whistleblower protections
- Support just-cause proposals

### Electoral Engagement

**Candidate Evaluation**:
- Research candidates' positions on worker rights
- Ask candidates about specific policies
- Support pro-worker candidates
- Vote in primary elections

**Ongoing Engagement**:
- Join labor-aligned political organizations
- Participate in voter registration
- Educate others about worker rights issues

## Legal Action

### Filing Complaints

**EEOC Charges**:
- File within 180/300 days (varies by state)
- Can be filed online or in person
- Free to file
- Don't need lawyer initially

**State Agency Complaints**:
- Know state deadlines (often shorter)
- May have additional protections beyond federal
- Can sometimes file both

**OSHA Complaints**:
- Whistleblower retaliation (30 days for some)
- Safety complaint retaliation
- Various other whistleblower programs

### Finding Legal Help

**Employment Attorneys**:
- National Employment Law Association referral
- State bar lawyer referral
- Many work on contingency

**Legal Aid**:
- Free legal services for qualifying workers
- Employment law clinics
- Worker center legal support

### Class and Collective Actions

**Join Existing Actions**:
- Check if class action exists against your employer
- Collective action for wage claims
- Pattern and practice discrimination cases

**Consider Starting**:
- Consult with plaintiff employment lawyers
- Worker center support
- Class action may be more powerful than individual

## Employer Engagement

### Within Your Workplace

**Raise Concerns**:
- Use internal grievance procedures
- Document all communications
- Request policy explanations in writing
- Escalate through chain of command

**Build Support**:
- Connect with supportive managers
- Identify allies in HR
- Work with other affected employees

### For Managers and HR Professionals

**Advocate for Better Policies**:
- Propose just-cause termination policies
- Recommend limiting non-compete use
- Suggest predictable scheduling
- Advocate for reduced surveillance

**Model Good Practices**:
- Document legitimate performance concerns
- Follow progressive discipline
- Treat employees with dignity
- Respect privacy when possible
