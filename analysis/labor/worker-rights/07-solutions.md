# Worker Rights: Solutions

## Just-Cause Employment Protection

### Federal Just-Cause Standard

**Policy**: Require just cause for termination after probationary period

**Components**:
- 90-day probationary period
- After probation, termination only for:
  - Documented performance issues
  - Economic necessity (bona fide layoffs)
  - Serious misconduct
- Progressive discipline required except for gross misconduct
- Written notice with reason required

**Implementation**:
- Apply to employers with 15+ employees (matching Title VII)
- Phase in over 3 years
- EEOC or new agency enforcement
- Private right of action

**Benefits**:
- Eliminates arbitrary termination
- Encourages documentation and fair process
- Reduces discrimination disguised as at-will termination
- Increases worker voice and retention

### State-Level Approaches

**Montana Model Expansion**:
- Encourage other states to adopt Montana-style laws
- Cap damages to reduce litigation concerns
- Create exclusive remedy to limit uncertainty

## Non-Compete Reform

### Federal Non-Compete Ban

**FTC Rule Approach**:
- Ban most non-compete agreements
- Exception only for sale of business
- Invalidate existing non-competes
- Require notice to affected workers

**Legislative Alternative**:
- Workforce Mobility Act (proposed)
- Federal ban on non-competes
- Preempt state law

### State-Level Reforms

**Ban for Low-Wage Workers**:
- Prohibit non-competes below income threshold
- Currently in 12+ states
- Threshold varies ($75,000-$100,000)

**Disclosure Requirements**:
- Require disclosure before hiring
- Provide copy of agreement in advance
- Consideration requirement (not just continued employment)

## Whistleblower Protection Enhancement

### Comprehensive Federal Law

**Model**: Whistleblower Protection Enhancement Act

**Key Features**:
- Cover all private sector workers
- Unified filing deadline (180+ days)
- Burden shift to employer after prima facie case
- Adequate damages (back pay, reinstatement, compensatory)
- Anti-retaliation injunction available
- Attorney fees

### SEC Whistleblower Program Expansion

- Extend Dodd-Frank model to other agencies
- Financial incentives for reporting (10-30% of recovery)
- Independent review of retaliation claims

## Workplace Privacy Protection

### Federal Employee Privacy Act

**Components**:
- Notice requirement for monitoring
- Limits on monitoring personal devices
- Biometric data protections
- Social media password protection
- Off-duty conduct protections

**Exemptions**:
- Reasonable security monitoring
- Performance monitoring with notice
- Investigation of specific misconduct

### State-Level Privacy Laws

- Biometric privacy (Illinois BIPA model)
- Social media password protection
- Off-duty conduct protection
- Electronic monitoring disclosure

## Scheduling Predictability

### Federal Fair Workweek Standards

**Components**:
- 14 days advance notice of schedule
- Predictability pay for changes (time-and-a-half)
- Right to request schedule changes
- Right to rest between shifts (11 hours minimum)
- Access to hours for existing employees first

**Coverage**:
- Large retail and food service employers initially
- Expand to other sectors over time

### State and Local Laws

- Build on Oregon, Seattle, NYC models
- Sector-specific approaches
- City-level innovation

## Arbitration Reform

### FAIR Act (Forced Arbitration Injustice Repeal)

**Policy**: End mandatory pre-dispute arbitration

**Components**:
- Prohibit mandatory arbitration for employment claims
- Preserve voluntary post-dispute arbitration
- Prohibit class action waivers
- Apply to discrimination, wage, and other employment claims

**Alternative**: Reform Rather Than Ban

- Due process protocols required
- Employee choice of arbitrator
- Discovery rights
- Written reasoned decisions
- Appeal rights for significant awards
- Fee limitations

## Enforcement Enhancement

### EEOC Strengthening

- Increase funding (double current budget)
- Hire more investigators
- Reduce case backlog
- Enhance pattern and practice enforcement
- Require timely processing

### Private Attorney General

- Allow workers to sue on behalf of state
- Model: California PAGA (with reforms)
- Incentive for private enforcement
- Share recovery with state

### Damages Enhancement

- Remove Title VII caps
- Provide punitive damages for willful violations
- Ensure attorney fees are adequate
- Prejudgment interest standard

## Summary: Priority Reforms

| Reform | Impact | Feasibility |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Non-compete ban | High (30M workers) | High (bipartisan support) |
| Whistleblower enhancement | Medium | Medium (some bipartisan) |
| Scheduling predictability | Medium | Medium (state momentum) |
| Arbitration reform | High (60M workers) | Low (strong opposition) |
| Just-cause protection | Very high | Low (major shift) |
