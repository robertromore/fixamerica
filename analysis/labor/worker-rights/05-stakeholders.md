# Worker Rights: Stakeholders

## Workers and Their Representatives

### Workers Subject to At-Will Employment

**Profile**:
- 74% of private sector workers (100+ million)
- Disproportionately low-wage, minority, young workers
- Often unaware of limited rights

**Interests**:
- Job security
- Protection from arbitrary termination
- Ability to speak up without fear
- Predictable schedules
- Privacy at work

**Challenges**:
- Fear of retaliation
- Lack of resources to challenge termination
- Information asymmetry
- Individual vs. collective power

### Workers Bound by Non-Competes

**Profile**:
- 30 million workers currently bound
- Including low-wage workers in fast food, retail
- Often signed without negotiation

**Interests**:
- Career mobility
- Ability to use skills
- Access to better opportunities
- Fair competition for talent

### Whistleblowers

**Profile**:
- Workers who report legal violations
- Safety concerns, fraud, discrimination
- Often face severe retaliation

**Interests**:
- Protection from retaliation
- Meaningful investigation of concerns
- Compensation for career damage
- Confidentiality protections

### Labor Unions

**Organizations**: AFL-CIO, SEIU, UAW, UFCW, NEA, AFSCME

**Role**:
- Advocate for stronger worker protections
- Provide representation in grievances
- Collective bargaining includes just-cause provisions
- Political advocacy for reform

**Interests**:
- Expanding organizing rights
- Just-cause protections for all workers
- Limiting arbitration requirements
- Strengthening anti-retaliation laws

## Employers and Business Groups

### Large Employers

**Profile**: Fortune 500 companies, major employers

**Interests**:
- Workforce flexibility
- Ability to restructure quickly
- Protection from litigation
- Predictable legal environment

**Practices**:
- Mandatory arbitration clauses
- Non-compete agreements
- Extensive monitoring
- At-will employment emphasized

### Small Businesses

**Profile**: Under 500 employees

**Interests**:
- Similar flexibility concerns
- Concern about litigation costs
- May have personal relationships with employees
- Often exempt from some requirements

### Business Associations

**Organizations**: U.S. Chamber of Commerce, SHRM, NAM, NFIB

**Role**:
- Lobby against new regulations
- Defend arbitration system
- Support at-will doctrine
- Oppose non-compete bans

**Arguments**:
- Flexibility needed for economic efficiency
- Regulations increase costs
- Individual freedom of contract
- Market solutions preferable

## Legal Profession

### Plaintiff Employment Lawyers

**Organizations**: NELA (National Employment Law Association)

**Role**:
- Represent workers in discrimination, retaliation cases
- Contingency fee model
- Advocate for stronger protections

**Interests**:
- Access to courts (oppose arbitration)
- Adequate damages to fund representation
- Class actions for systemic violations
- Reasonable statutes of limitations

### Defense Employment Lawyers

**Role**:
- Represent employers in employment disputes
- Draft employment agreements
- Advise on compliance

**Interests**:
- Clear legal standards
- Arbitration as efficient forum
- Predictable litigation outcomes

### Arbitration Providers

**Organizations**: AAA, JAMS, NAM

**Role**:
- Administer employment arbitrations
- Maintain arbitrator rosters
- Set procedural rules

**Concerns**:
- Perceived fairness
- Repeat business from employers
- Due process protocols

## Government Actors

### Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC)

**Role**:
- Enforce federal anti-discrimination laws
- Investigate charges
- Issue guidance
- Litigate pattern and practice cases

**Challenges**:
- Resource constraints
- Large case backlog
- Limited ability to pursue individual cases
- Changing enforcement priorities

### Department of Labor (DOL)

**Role**:
- Enforce wage and hour laws
- Whistleblower programs (OSHA)
- Some retaliation protections

**Challenges**:
- Multiple programs across agencies
- Inconsistent procedures
- Limited resources

### National Labor Relations Board (NLRB)

**Role**:
- Protect Section 7 rights
- Address employer interference with organizing
- Expanding worker rights interpretation under some boards

### State Agencies

**Role**:
- Enforce state employment laws
- State-level discrimination claims
- Wage and hour enforcement

## Advocacy Organizations

### Worker Rights Groups

**Organizations**: National Employment Law Project, Workplace Fairness, Economic Policy Institute

**Activities**:
- Research on worker rights issues
- Advocacy for reform legislation
- Public education
- Support for litigation

### Civil Rights Organizations

**Organizations**: NAACP, ACLU, Lambda Legal

**Role**:
- Advocate for anti-discrimination enforcement
- Strategic litigation
- Intersectional worker rights issues

## Academic and Research Institutions

### Labor Economists

**Contributions**:
- Research on effects of non-competes
- Studies on discrimination
- Analysis of arbitration outcomes

### Legal Scholars

**Contributions**:
- Critique of at-will doctrine
- Comparative law analysis
- Reform proposals
