# Labor Unions: What Workers Can Do

## For Workers Considering Organizing

### Know Your Rights

**Section 7 of the NLRA Protects Your Right To**

| Right | Protection |
|-------|------------|
| Form or join a union | Cannot be fired for union activity |
| Bargain collectively | Employer must negotiate in good faith |
| Act together for mutual aid | Concerted activity protected |
| Discuss wages and working conditions | Cannot be prohibited |
| Distribute union literature | On your own time, in non-work areas |
| Wear union buttons or insignia | Generally protected |

**What Employers Cannot Do**

| Prohibited Action | Example |
|-------------------|---------|
| Threaten | "We'll close if you unionize" |
| Interrogate | "Are you involved with the union?" |
| Promise | "Vote no and we'll give raises" |
| Surveil | Spying on union meetings |
| Retaliate | Firing, discipline, demotion |

Remember: **TIPS** - Threats, Interrogation, Promises, Surveillance are illegal.

### Assess Your Workplace

**Questions to Consider**

| Question | Why It Matters |
|----------|---------------|
| What are the main issues? | Wages, safety, scheduling, respect |
| Who are the natural leaders? | Workers others listen to |
| How do workers communicate? | Find channels to discuss |
| What is management like? | Assess opposition intensity |
| Have there been previous attempts? | Learn from history |

**Mapping Your Workplace**

Create a map of your coworkers:

| Category | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Strong supporters | Will actively help organize |
| Supporters | Favorable but passive |
| Undecided | Need information, persuasion |
| Leaning against | Have concerns, may be movable |
| Opposed | Unlikely to support |

A successful campaign needs to identify and move people up this scale.

### Getting Started

**Step 1: Build a Committee**

- Identify 3-5 trusted coworkers
- Meet outside of work
- Discuss issues and goals
- Plan initial conversations

**Step 2: Have Conversations**

| Approach | Details |
|----------|---------|
| One-on-one | Most effective |
| Listen first | What are their concerns? |
| Share your experience | Connect issues |
| Avoid management | Keep conversations among workers |

**Step 3: Contact a Union**

| Option | How to Find |
|--------|-------------|
| Industry-specific union | Search for unions in your industry |
| AFL-CIO | aflcio.org/formaunion |
| Emergency Workplace Organizing Committee | workerorganizing.org |
| Worker center | If union not appropriate |

**Step 4: Build Majority Support**

| Target | Why |
|--------|-----|
| 30% to file petition | Legal minimum |
| 50%+ for election win | Majority required |
| 65-80% recommended | Buffer against employer campaign |

### Protect Yourself

**Documentation**

| What to Document | How |
|------------------|-----|
| Union activity | Dates, times, witnesses |
| Employer responses | Exact words, witnesses |
| Working conditions | Photos, records |
| Communications | Save emails, texts |

**Reducing Risk**

| Strategy | Reason |
|----------|--------|
| Organize in groups | Safety in numbers |
| Keep written records | Evidence if needed |
| Know your rights | Can assert them |
| Stay calm and professional | Harder to retaliate |
| Contact union/lawyer if threatened | Get help early |

### If Retaliated Against

**Immediate Steps**

1. Document everything
2. Contact your union representative
3. File ULP charge with NLRB (within 6 months)
4. Consider consulting employment lawyer
5. Do not resign (even if pressured)

**Filing a ULP Charge**

| Information Needed | Details |
|-------------------|---------|
| Your name and contact | Required |
| Employer name and address | Required |
| Description of violation | Be specific |
| Date of violation | Important for timeline |
| Witnesses | Names and contact info |

File online at: nlrb.gov or call 1-844-762-6572

### Participate in Your Union

**Ways to Get Involved**

| Activity | Impact |
|----------|--------|
| Attend meetings | Stay informed, have voice |
| Vote in elections | Choose leadership |
| Serve on committees | Shape priorities |
| Become a steward | Help coworkers |

### Know Your Contract

**Key Sections to Understand**

| Section | Why It Matters |
|---------|---------------|
| Grievance procedure | How to address violations |
| Seniority | Layoffs, promotions, scheduling |
| Wages and raises | What you're entitled to |
| Benefits | Healthcare, retirement, leave |
| Work rules | Hours, overtime, breaks |
| Just cause | Protections from discipline |

### Use the Grievance Process

**When to File**

- Contract violation by employer
- Discipline without proper procedure
- Inconsistent rule enforcement
- Safety violations
- Harassment or discrimination

**How to File**

1. Document the issue
2. Contact your steward
3. Follow contract timelines
4. Participate in meetings
5. Appeal if necessary

## Political Action

### Vote for Pro-Labor Candidates

**How to Identify**

| Source | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| AFL-CIO endorsements | aflcio.org |
| Your union endorsements | Check union communications |
| Voting records | Track legislative votes |
| Campaign statements | Look for specific commitments |

**Key Votes to Watch**

| Issue | Pro-Worker Position |
|-------|---------------------|
| PRO Act | Support |
| Minimum wage increase | Support |
| Right-to-work | Oppose |
| NLRB funding | Support |
| Worker classification | Support ABC test |

### Contact Elected Officials

**When to Contact**

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Legislation pending | Express support/opposition |
| Committee hearings | Request testimony consideration |
| Agency appointments | Support/oppose nominees |
| Local issues | City council, county |

**How to Be Effective**

| Tip | Reason |
|-----|--------|
| Be specific | Name the bill/issue |
| Share your story | Personal impact |
| Be concise | Staff time limited |
| Follow up | Persistence matters |
| Coordinate | Organized contact campaigns |

### Support Labor Legislation

**Federal Priorities**

| Bill | What It Does |
|------|--------------|
| PRO Act | Comprehensive labor reform |
| Raise the Wage Act | $15+ minimum wage |
| Public Service Freedom to Negotiate | Federal worker bargaining |
| Domestic Workers Bill of Rights | Protect domestic workers |

**State Priorities**

| Issue | What to Support |
|-------|-----------------|
| Minimum wage increases | State and local increases |
| Captive audience bans | Prohibit mandatory meetings |
| Card check (public sector) | Easier recognition |
| Prevailing wage | On public projects |

## For Specific Situations

### Gig Workers

**Current Limitations**

- Classified as independent contractors
- No NLRA coverage
- No employer required to bargain

**What You Can Do**

| Action | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Support classification reform | AB5-style laws |
| Join worker organizations | Driver's unions, worker centers |
| Collective action | Strikes, slowdowns (more risk) |
| Document conditions | Build evidence |
| Political action | Support pro-worker candidates |

### Immigrant Workers

**Know Your Rights**

| Right | Protection |
|-------|------------|
| NLRA applies | Regardless of immigration status |
| Cannot be reported | For union activity |
| Same remedies | Back pay, reinstatement |

**Finding Help**

| Resource | Services |
|----------|----------|
| Worker centers | Know your rights, support |
| Immigration legal aid | Legal questions |
| Unions | Some have immigrant member programs |
