# Workforce Development: Roadmap

## Phase 1: Foundation Building (Years 1-2)

### Apprenticeship Investment

**Federal Actions**:
- Pass National Apprenticeship Act with $2B initial appropriation
- Expand tax credits for apprenticeship employers
- Launch industry intermediary grant program
- Fund pre-apprenticeship expansion

**State Actions**:
- State apprenticeship agency capacity building
- State tax incentives aligned with federal
- Community college apprenticeship integration
- Youth apprenticeship pilots

**Milestones**:
- [ ] 100,000 new apprenticeship starts Year 1
- [ ] 10 sector intermediaries launched
- [ ] 25 states with aligned incentives

### Community College Reform

**Federal Actions**:
- Pass free community college legislation
- Short-term Pell grant expansion
- Workforce-focused completion grants
- Technical assistance for guided pathways

**State Actions**:
- State match for free tuition
- Performance funding implementation
- Non-credit to credit articulation
- Industry partnership requirements

**Milestones**:
- [ ] 20+ states with free community college
- [ ] Short-term Pell enacted
- [ ] Completion rates up 5 percentage points

### Sector Partnership Grants

**Federal Actions**:
- $500M sector partnership investment
- Multi-year grants (5+ years)
- Technical assistance center
- Evaluation framework

**Milestones**:
- [ ] 50 regional sector partnerships funded
- [ ] 100,000 workers served
- [ ] Evaluation shows positive outcomes

## Phase 2: System Building (Years 3-5)

### Apprenticeship Expansion

**Scale Up**:
- Increase appropriation to $5B
- Expand to healthcare, IT, advanced manufacturing
- Youth apprenticeship in all states
- Degree apprenticeships piloted

**Milestones**:
- [ ] 1 million active apprentices
- [ ] All sectors with apprenticeship options
- [ ] Youth apprenticeship in 40 states

### Community College Transformation

**Deepen Reforms**:
- Universal guided pathways implementation
- Completion rates up 15+ percentage points
- Strong employer partnerships in all programs
- Career services integrated

**Milestones**:
- [ ] 40%+ completion rate (up from 25%)
- [ ] 80%+ employment rate for graduates
- [ ] Earnings gains documented

### Displaced Worker System

**Universal Adjustment Assistance**:
- Expand TAA to cover all displacement
- Two-year training benefit
- Wage insurance for older workers
- Relocation assistance

**Early Warning System**:
- Rapid response within 48 hours
- Worker notification requirements
- Advance planning support

**Milestones**:
- [ ] Universal adjustment assistance enacted
- [ ] Average time to reemployment reduced 20%
- [ ] Earnings recovery improved

### Career Pathways Development

**Build Infrastructure**:
- Stackable credential frameworks in major sectors
- Articulation agreements across institutions
- Credit for prior learning standardized
- Career navigation systems operational

**Milestones**:
- [ ] Credential frameworks in 10 sectors
- [ ] 50%+ of community colleges with articulated pathways
- [ ] Career navigation systems in 30 states

## Phase 3: Mature System (Years 5-10)

### German-Level Apprenticeship

**Goal**: 3% of workforce in apprenticeship

**Requirements**:
- Employer infrastructure mature
- Quality standards robust
- Public support sustained
- Parity with college pathway

**Milestones**:
- [ ] 3 million active apprentices
- [ ] Apprenticeship respected as college alternative
- [ ] Strong earnings outcomes documented

### Integrated Workforce System

**Vision**: Seamless education-to-employment pathways

**Components**:
- Universal career guidance from middle school
- Clear pathways at all levels
- Portable credentials recognized
- Lifelong learning culture

**Milestones**:
- [ ] Youth unemployment at European levels
- [ ] Adult training participation doubled
- [ ] Skills shortages substantially reduced

### Employer Co-Investment

**Models Established**:
- Training levy or equivalent
- Sector financing mechanisms
- Tax incentives fully utilized
- Employers invested in system

**Milestones**:
- [ ] Employer training investment up 50%
- [ ] Small employer participation doubled
- [ ] Poaching problem addressed

## Implementation Considerations

### Stakeholder Engagement

**Employers**:
- Early engagement in design
- Demonstrate ROI
- Reduce administrative burden
- Flexibility within standards

**Education**:
- Partner, don't mandate
- Provide resources for change
- Respect institutional missions
- Build on strengths

**Workers**:
- Accessible programs
- Support services included
- Income support during training
- Quality outcomes

### Quality Assurance

**Apprenticeships**:
- Maintain registered system
- Occupation-specific standards
- Portable credentials
- Completion and wage outcomes

**Training Programs**:
- Outcome reporting required
- Consumer protection standards
- Accreditation streamlined
- Employer validation

### Equity Focus

**Access**:
- Pre-apprenticeship for underrepresented
- Support services for barriers
- Targeted outreach
- Inclusive recruitment

**Outcomes**:
- Disaggregated reporting
- Equity adjustments in funding
- Address discrimination
- Affirmative pathways

## Metrics for Success

### Short-Term (2 years)

- Apprenticeship starts up 50%
- Community college enrollment stable/growing
- Sector partnerships in 50 regions
- Short-term Pell enacted

### Medium-Term (5 years)

- 1 million apprentices
- Community college completion up 15 points
- Displaced worker outcomes improved
- Credential system rationalized

### Long-Term (10 years)

- 3 million apprentices (3% of workforce)
- World-class community college system
- Employer co-investment institutionalized
- Skills gap substantially reduced
