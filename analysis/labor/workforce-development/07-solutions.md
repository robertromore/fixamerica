# Workforce Development: Solutions

## Apprenticeship Expansion

### National Apprenticeship System

**Goal**: Increase from 600,000 to 3 million apprentices

**Components**:
- Federal funding: $5+ billion annually
- Tax credits for employers ($5,000-$10,000 per apprentice)
- Industry intermediaries to aggregate small employers
- Pre-apprenticeship pipelines for underrepresented groups
- Expansion to new sectors (IT, healthcare, advanced manufacturing)

**Implementation**:
- Strengthen registered apprenticeship system
- Quality standards maintained
- Portable, nationally recognized credentials
- Employer-union-government partnerships

### Sector-Based Intermediaries

**Model**: Industry associations organize training across employers

**Functions**:
- Aggregate small employer demand
- Develop common curricula
- Provide shared training infrastructure
- Manage apprentice rotation across employers
- Quality assurance

**Examples to Scale**:
- Building trades joint apprenticeships
- Health career collaboratives
- IT sector partnerships

## Community College Reform

### Free Community College

**Policy**: Eliminate tuition for workforce programs

**Components**:
- Federal-state cost sharing (75-25 or 90-10)
- Cover tuition and fees
- Additional support for low-income students
- Connected to completion reforms

### Completion Reforms

**Evidence-Based Practices**:
- Guided pathways (structured programs of study)
- Corequisite remediation (concurrent support)
- Intrusive advising and coaching
- Emergency financial assistance
- Simplified course sequences

**Performance Funding**:
- Base funding + completion bonuses
- Measure job placement and earnings
- Equity adjustments

### Short-Term Credential Investment

**Current Barrier**: Pell grants require 15+ weeks

**Reform**:
- Short-term Pell grants for 8+ week programs
- Quality standards for eligible programs
- Labor market outcome requirements
- Connection to longer pathways

## Sector Partnerships

### Scaling Proven Models

**Key Elements**:
- Employer leadership in design
- Industry-validated curricula
- Direct hiring commitments
- Comprehensive support services
- Data-driven continuous improvement

**Models to Scale**:
- Project QUEST (healthcare, IT)
- Year Up (corporate internships)
- Per Scholas (IT certification)
- CareerWise (youth apprenticeship)

### Federal Investment

- $1+ billion for sector partnership grants
- Multi-year funding (5+ years)
- Technical assistance and evaluation
- Regional labor market focus

## Employer Training Incentives

### Training Tax Credit

**Design**:
- 25% tax credit for qualified training expenditures
- Targeting workers without bachelor's degrees
- Minimum training hours required
- Portable credential outcome

**Benefit**:
- Reduces employer cost of training
- Addresses poaching problem
- Maintains employer flexibility
- Private sector leads

### Training Levy Alternative

**UK Model Adapted**:
- 0.5% payroll tax on employers over $1M payroll
- Employers receive training vouchers for their contribution
- Unspent funds support smaller employer training
- Government matches for apprenticeships

## Displaced Worker Support

### Enhanced Trade Adjustment Assistance

**Expanded Eligibility**:
- Cover technology displacement, not just trade
- Longer eligibility period (3 years)
- Better benefits during training

**Improved Services**:
- Two-year training allowance
- Wage insurance (50% of wage loss for 2 years)
- Relocation assistance
- Healthcare subsidy continuation

### Universal Adjustment Assistance

**Model**: Cover all displacement, regardless of cause

**Components**:
- Early warning and rapid response
- Income support during training
- Quality training options
- Job search and relocation support
- Wage insurance for older workers

## Career Pathways System

### Stackable Credentials

**Policy**: Build connected credential pathways

**Elements**:
- Clear articulation between short-term and longer credentials
- Industry recognition at each level
- Credit for prior learning
- Multiple on-ramps

### Career Guidance

**School-Level**:
- Universal career exploration in middle/high school
- Work-based learning opportunities
- Career counselor staffing standards
- Labor market information systems

**Adult-Level**:
- Lifelong career counseling access
- AI-powered career matching tools
- Comprehensive labor market information
- One-stop career navigation

## Digital Skills for All

### Universal Digital Literacy

**Goal**: Ensure all workers have baseline digital skills

**Components**:
- Free digital skills training at community colleges
- Library-based digital literacy programs
- Employer-provided digital upskilling
- Credential for digital workplace competency

### Tech Career Pathways

**Short-Term Credentials**:
- Coding bootcamps with quality standards
- IT certifications (CompTIA, AWS, Google)
- Cybersecurity pathways
- Data analytics certificates

**Quality Assurance**:
- Outcome reporting requirements
- Student protection standards
- Employer validation
