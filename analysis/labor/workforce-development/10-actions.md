# Workforce Development: Actions

## Individual Actions

### Job Seekers and Career Changers

**Explore Options**:
- Visit local American Job Center for free career services
- Use O*NET and CareerOneStop to explore occupations
- Research labor market demand in your region
- Consider apprenticeship opportunities

**Access Training**:
- Apply for WIOA-funded training through workforce system
- Explore community college workforce programs
- Research free and low-cost online credentials
- Investigate employer-sponsored tuition benefits

**Build Skills**:
- Identify in-demand credentials in your field
- Complete recognized industry certifications
- Develop digital skills (many free resources available)
- Build portfolio demonstrating competencies

**Network**:
- Connect with employers in target industries
- Join professional associations
- Attend job fairs and industry events
- Use LinkedIn strategically

### Current Workers

**Upskilling**:
- Ask employer about training and tuition benefits
- Pursue credentials during evenings/weekends
- Complete online courses from reputable providers
- Build toward stackable credentials

**Career Advancement**:
- Develop career plan with specific goals
- Seek mentors in desired career path
- Document accomplishments and skills
- Request stretch assignments and development opportunities

### Parents and Students

**Career Exploration**:
- Encourage career exploration in middle school
- Research apprenticeship and technical career pathways
- Visit community college workforce programs
- Consider career academies and CTE programs

**Decision Making**:
- Research labor market outcomes for different pathways
- Consider apprenticeship as alternative to college debt
- Evaluate credential value before enrolling
- Plan for lifetime of career development

## Employer Actions

### Training Investment

**Internal Training**:
- Assess current training ROI
- Invest in employee development
- Create advancement pathways for entry-level workers
- Partner with community colleges for customized training

**Apprenticeship Development**:
- Explore apprenticeship for skilled positions
- Partner with industry associations
- Use intermediaries if too small to go alone
- Access tax credits and grants

**Sector Engagement**:
- Participate in local sector partnerships
- Communicate skill needs to educators
- Provide work-based learning opportunities
- Hire from quality training programs

### Community Partnership

**Educational Partnerships**:
- Partner with community colleges on curriculum
- Offer internships and work-based learning
- Provide equipment and facility access
- Guest lecture and mentor students

**Workforce System Engagement**:
- Connect with local workforce board
- Use American Job Centers for recruitment
- Participate in job fairs
- Provide input on training needs

## Community and Organizational Actions

### Workforce Boards

**Strategic Planning**:
- Conduct comprehensive labor market analysis
- Develop sector strategies for key industries
- Build strong employer partnerships
- Invest in quality training providers

**System Improvement**:
- Implement career pathways frameworks
- Improve customer service at Job Centers
- Use data for continuous improvement
- Build effective business services team

### Community Colleges

**Workforce Focus**:
- Strengthen industry partnerships
- Implement guided pathways
- Improve completion rates
- Provide comprehensive student support

**Innovation**:
- Develop apprenticeship pathways
- Expand short-term credentials with value
- Build articulation with four-year institutions
- Partner with K-12 on career exploration

### Unions and Worker Organizations

**Joint Programs**:
- Expand joint apprenticeship programs
- Negotiate training benefits in contracts
- Partner with employers on skill development
- Advocate for quality training standards

**Member Services**:
- Provide career development support
- Offer upgrade training for members
- Support displaced member retraining
- Advocate for worker-centered policies

## Political Action

### Federal Advocacy

**Priority Issues**:
- National Apprenticeship Act funding
- Free community college legislation
- Short-term Pell grant expansion
- Trade adjustment assistance expansion

**Actions**:
- Contact representatives about workforce legislation
- Share stories about training impacts
- Support workforce-friendly candidates
- Participate in coalitions for reform

### State Advocacy

**Priority Issues**:
- State apprenticeship funding
- Community college investment
- Sector partnership support
- Training quality standards

**Actions**:
- Engage with state workforce boards
- Testify at legislative hearings
- Support state-level workforce initiatives
- Build local coalitions

### Local Engagement

**Actions**:
- Participate in workforce board meetings
- Advocate for local training investments
- Support community college funding
- Connect schools with career exploration

## Research and Learning

### Stay Informed

**Follow Issues**:
- National Skills Coalition updates
- Jobs for the Future research
- Workforce development policy news
- Labor market trends

**Data Literacy**:
- Understand labor market information
- Research credential value before enrolling
- Track outcomes of local programs
- Use evidence to inform advocacy

### Share Knowledge

**Personal Experience**:
- Share training and career stories
- Mentor others navigating workforce system
- Rate training programs and providers
- Contribute to program improvement

**Community Education**:
- Inform others about workforce resources
- Promote quality programs
- Challenge stigma against non-degree pathways
- Support career exploration for youth
