## Reporting and Filing Claims

### How to File a Wage Claim

**Federal (DOL)**

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Gather documentation (pay stubs, time records, employment info) |
| 2 | Call WHD hotline or visit local office |
| 3 | Complete intake interview |
| 4 | WHD investigates |
| 5 | Resolution (settlement, litigation, or closure) |

**State Labor Agency**

Process varies by state, but generally:
1. File complaint form (often online)
2. Agency review
3. Hearing or investigation
4. Decision
5. Appeal rights

**Private Lawsuit**

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Consult with attorney |
| 2 | Evaluate claim (amount, evidence, timeline) |
| 3 | File complaint in court |
| 4 | Discovery process |
| 5 | Settlement or trial |

### Documentation to Gather

**Essential Records**

| Document | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| Pay stubs | Prove what was paid |
| Time records | Prove hours worked |
| Employment records | Prove employment relationship |
| Schedules | Prove scheduled hours |
| Communications | Prove instructions, agreements |
| Bank records | Prove payments received |

**If You Don't Have Records**

| Alternative Evidence | Use |
|---------------------|-----|
| Personal notes | Contemporaneous records |
| Coworker testimony | Corroboration |
| Photos | Work location, conditions |
| Text messages | Work instructions |
| Calendars | Work schedule |

### Protecting Against Retaliation

**Know Your Rights**

| Protection | Law |
|------------|-----|
| Cannot be fired for filing complaint | FLSA anti-retaliation |
| Cannot be demoted or have hours cut | FLSA anti-retaliation |
| Cannot face immigration threats | FLSA applies regardless of status |

**If Retaliation Occurs**

| Action | How To |
|--------|--------|
| Document everything | Dates, witnesses, communications |
| Report to DOL | Retaliation complaint |
| Consult attorney | Evaluate legal options |
| Contact worker center | Support and assistance |
