# Labor: Roadmap

## Overview

Transforming American labor relations requires a phased approach that builds worker power, secures policy wins, and progressively addresses structural barriers. This roadmap sequences reforms from immediate administrative actions to fundamental restructuring, recognizing that labor reform requires simultaneous action at federal, state, and workplace levels.

| Goal | Mechanism |

## Risks and Contingencies

### Political Risks

| Risk | Mitigation | Contingency |
|------|------------|-------------|
| **Election losses** | Build bipartisan support | State/local action |
| **Court challenges** | Careful drafting | Alternative mechanisms |
| **Filibuster blocks** | Reconciliation, executive action | State innovation |
| **Business backlash** | Coalition with high-road employers | Public campaigns |

### Implementation Risks

| Risk | Mitigation | Contingency |
|------|------------|-------------|
| **Employer non-compliance** | Strong enforcement | Private rights of action |
| **Organizing failures** | Strategic targeting | Alt-labor models |
| **First contract stalls** | First contract arbitration | Public pressure |
| **Worker center limitations** | Capacity building | Union partnerships |

### Economic Risks

| Risk | Mitigation | Contingency |
|------|------------|-------------|
| **Recession** | Countercyclical policy | Emergency protections |
| **Automation acceleration** | Transition support | Universal benefits |
| **Capital flight threats** | Broad standards | International coordination |
| **Small business burden** | Graduated implementation | Technical assistance |

### Contingency Plans

| If This Fails | Then |
|---------------|------|
| Federal legislation blocked | State and local action |
| NLRB constrained | Sectoral bargaining, worker centers |
| Traditional organizing stalled | Alt-labor models |
| Enforcement underfunded | Private litigation |
| Public support declines | Reframe narrative |
