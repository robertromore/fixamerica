# Paid Leave: What Citizens Can Do

## Overview

Every American can take action to advance paid leave policy. Whether you are a worker needing leave, an employer considering policies, or a citizen advocating for change, this chapter provides concrete steps.

## For Workers

### Know Your Rights

**Federal Rights Under FMLA**

| Right | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| 12 weeks unpaid leave | For qualifying reasons |
| Job protection | Same or equivalent position |
| Health benefits continuation | During leave |
| No retaliation | For requesting or taking leave |

**FMLA Eligibility Checklist**

- [ ] Work for employer with 50+ employees within 75 miles
- [ ] Employed at least 12 months (need not be consecutive)
- [ ] Worked at least 1,250 hours in past 12 months
- [ ] Have qualifying reason (birth, adoption, serious health condition, family care)

**State Rights**

| If You Live In | Check |
|----------------|-------|
| CA, NJ, RI, NY, WA, MA, CT, OR, CO | State paid leave program |
| DC + 18 states | State paid sick leave law |
| Major cities | Local paid sick leave ordinance |

**How to Check Your Rights**

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Identify your state |
| 2 | Check state labor department website |
| 3 | Check if your city has additional protections |
| 4 | Review your employer's policy |
| 5 | Contact A Better Balance helpline if unsure |

### Taking Leave

**Before You Need Leave**

| Action | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| Review employer policy | Know your benefits |
| Understand state program | Know your rights |
| Document eligibility | Track tenure, hours |
| Build savings if possible | Supplement benefits |
| Discuss with supervisor | Plan for coverage |

**When You Need Leave**

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Give notice as soon as possible |
| 2 | Request in writing |
| 3 | Provide required documentation |
| 4 | Keep copies of everything |
| 5 | Apply for state benefits if available |

**If Your Rights Are Violated**

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| FMLA denial | File complaint with DOL (1-866-4-USWAGE) |
| Retaliation | Document everything, consult attorney |
| State program denial | Appeal through state agency |
| Employer refuses | Contact A Better Balance helpline |

## For Employers

### Providing Paid Leave

**Best Practices**

| Component | Recommendation |
|-----------|----------------|
| Parental leave | 12+ weeks paid, all parents |
| Medical leave | 12+ weeks paid |
| Sick leave | 10+ days paid |
| Family caregiving | 4+ weeks paid |
| Bereavement | 5+ days paid |

**Implementing a Policy**

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Assess current state of benefits |
| 2 | Research peer employer policies |
| 3 | Calculate costs and benefits |
| 4 | Draft policy with clear guidelines |
| 5 | Communicate to all employees |
| 6 | Train managers |
| 7 | Evaluate and improve |

**Small Business Solutions**

| Challenge | Solution |
|-----------|----------|
| Cost concerns | Phase in, start with sick leave |
| Coverage concerns | Cross-train, use temps |
| Administrative burden | Simple policy, clear documentation |
| Competitive disadvantage | Highlight as recruitment tool |

## Political Action

### Contacting Elected Officials

**Who to Contact**

| Level | Officials | Issues |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| Federal | Senators, Representative | FAMILY Act, Healthy Families Act |
| State | State legislators, Governor | State paid leave programs |
| Local | City council, Mayor | Local paid sick leave |

**How to Contact**

| Method | Effectiveness |
|--------|---------------|
| Phone call | High - direct contact |
| In-person meeting | Very high - but hard to get |
| Town hall | High - public visibility |
| Written letter | Moderate - documented |
| Email | Lower - but easy |
| Social media | Moderate - public pressure |

**What to Say**

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Identify yourself | Constituent, voter |
| State your ask | Support specific bill |
| Share your story | Personal impact |
| Reference data | Economic benefits |
| Request response | Commitment or explanation |

**Sample Script**

> "Hello, I'm [Name], a constituent from [City]. I'm calling to ask [Senator/Representative] to support the FAMILY Act, which would create a national paid family and medical leave program. As someone who [personal story], I know how important paid leave is for working families. I'd like to know the [Senator's/Representative's] position on this bill. Thank you."

### Voting for Paid Leave Champions

**Candidate Research**

| Source | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| Campaign websites | Policy positions |
| Voting records | Past votes on leave |
| Candidate questionnaires | Advocacy organization ratings |
| Town halls | Ask directly |
| Endorsements | Who supports them |

**Key Voting Record Questions**

- Has the candidate supported paid leave legislation?
- Have they opposed preemption of local leave laws?
- Do they support the FAMILY Act?
- Do they support the Healthy Families Act?
