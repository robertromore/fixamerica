# Paid Leave: Resources

## Overview

This chapter compiles key resources for understanding, advocating for, and implementing paid leave policies. Resources are organized by type and include national and state-level materials.

## Academic Literature

### Key Studies

**Economic Effects**

| Study | Author(s) | Finding |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| California PFL evaluation | Rossin-Slater et al. | No negative employment effects |
| New Jersey program study | Appelbaum & Milkman | Positive employer experiences |
| Rhode Island evaluation | Bartel et al. | Successful implementation |
| Leave and child health | Ruhm | Improved health outcomes |

**Health Effects**

| Study | Topic | Finding |
|-------|-------|---------|
| Infant mortality | Tanaka | Paid leave reduces mortality |
| Breastfeeding | Ogbuanu et al. | Extended leave increases rates |
| Maternal health | Aitken et al. | Leave improves recovery |
| Mental health | Aitken et al. | Reduces postpartum depression |

**Workforce Effects**

| Study | Topic | Finding |
|-------|-------|---------|
| Women's employment | Rossin-Slater | Paid leave increases participation |
| Return to work | Houser & Vartanian | High return rates with paid leave |
| Wage effects | Waldfogel | Leave reduces motherhood penalty |
