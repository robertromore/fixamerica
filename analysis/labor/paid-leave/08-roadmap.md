# Paid Leave: Implementation Roadmap

## Overview

Achieving comprehensive paid leave in the United States requires a multi-track strategy that advances federal legislation while continuing state-level progress and building the political coalition necessary for lasting change. This roadmap sequences reforms from immediately achievable to longer-term structural goals.

## Risk Mitigation

### Political Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Republican opposition | Build state evidence, bipartisan frame |
| Democratic division | Unified coalition, moderate options |
| Filibuster | Reform advocacy, reconciliation path |
| Court challenges | Careful drafting, multiple legal theories |
| Implementation failure | Learn from state programs |

### Implementation Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Administrative capacity | Invest in systems, staffing |
| Employer compliance | Clear rules, technical assistance |
| Worker awareness | Robust outreach campaign |
| Fraud | Verification systems, audits |
| Political reversal | Build durable coalition support |

### External Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Economic downturn | Frame as economic stabilizer |
| Public health emergency | Demonstrate need (COVID lesson) |
| Political realignment | Maintain bipartisan appeal |
| Competing priorities | Coalition discipline |
