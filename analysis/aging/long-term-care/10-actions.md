# Long-Term Care: Citizen Actions

## What You Can Do

### Personal Planning Actions

| Action | Description | Impact |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| **Learn about LTC options** | Understand HCBS, nursing homes, assisted living | Better decisions |
| **Have family conversations** | Discuss care preferences with loved ones | Reduce crisis decisions |
| **Assess housing** | Evaluate aging-in-place modifications needed | Enable home care |
| **Review finances** | Understand Medicaid rules, consider LTC insurance | Financial preparation |
| **Create advance directives** | Document healthcare wishes | Honor preferences |
| **Research local services** | Find Area Agency on Aging, local providers | Know resources |

### If You're a Caregiver

| Action | Benefit |
|--------|---------|
| **Connect with support groups** | Reduce isolation, share strategies |
| **Use respite care** | Prevent burnout |
| **Know your rights** | FMLA, state leave laws |
| **Track expenses** | Tax deductions, spend-down documentation |
| **Care for yourself** | Medical appointments, mental health |
| **Seek training** | Improve care skills |

### Community Actions

| Action | How to Participate |
|--------|-------------------|
| **Volunteer with ombudsman program** | Contact state ombudsman, complete training |
| **Support local senior centers** | Volunteer, donate, attend programs |
| **Help neighbors age in place** | Check in, assist with tasks |
| **Organize caregiving support** | Form support groups, share resources |
| **Advocate for accessibility** | Support universal design, accessible housing |
| **Mentor new caregivers** | Share experience, provide guidance |

### Workplace Actions

| Action | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Request caregiver-friendly policies** | Flexible schedules, EAP services |
| **Support colleagues who are caregivers** | Cover shifts, provide understanding |
| **Advocate for caregiver leave** | Company policy improvements |
| **Share resources** | Connect coworkers with support |
| **Reduce stigma** | Openly discuss caregiving challenges |

## Political Actions

### Contact Elected Officials

| Level | Ask For |
|-------|---------|
| **Federal - Congress** | HCBS entitlement, workforce investment, LTC financing |
| **Federal - Administration** | Staffing rule enforcement, quality oversight |
| **State - Governor** | Medicaid HCBS expansion, rate increases |
| **State - Legislature** | Public LTC insurance, wage standards |
| **Local - County** | Area Agency on Aging funding, services |

### Effective Communication

**Phone Call Script (Federal)**:

> "Hello, my name is [NAME] and I'm a constituent from [CITY/ZIP]. I'm calling to urge [REPRESENTATIVE/SENATOR] to support long-term care reform. [CHOOSE ONE]:
>
> - As a family caregiver for my [RELATIONSHIP], I've seen how the current system fails families.
> - The 800,000+ Americans on HCBS waitlists deserve care in their communities.
> - Care workers earning $15.77/hour cannot afford to stay in jobs we desperately need filled.
>
> Please support [SPECIFIC BILL OR ISSUE]. Thank you."

**Email Template**:

> Subject: Support Long-Term Care Reform
>
> Dear [TITLE LAST NAME],
>
> I am writing as a constituent concerned about America's long-term care crisis. [PERSONAL STORY: 1-2 sentences about your connection to the issue].
>
> I urge you to support:
>
> - Making home and community-based services a Medicaid entitlement
> - Investing in the care workforce through wage increases and training
> - Ensuring nursing home quality through staffing standards and transparency
>
> [SPECIFIC REQUEST: bill number, hearing, vote]
>
> Thank you for your attention to this critical issue.
>
> Sincerely,
> [NAME]
> [ADDRESS]
> [PHONE]

### Attending Town Halls and Meetings

**Questions to Ask**:

- "What are you doing to address the 800,000+ people waiting for home care services?"
- "Do you support making home and community-based services a Medicaid entitlement?"
- "Will you support meaningful nursing home staffing standards?"
- "What is your plan to improve care worker wages and reduce turnover?"
- "Do you support a public long-term care insurance option?"

### Share Your Story

Stories drive policy change. Consider sharing if you:

- Are a family caregiver
- Needed LTC yourself or for a loved one
- Work as a direct care worker
- Experienced nursing home quality problems
- Spent down assets to qualify for Medicaid
- Waited on an HCBS waitlist

**Where to Share**:

- Congressional testimony (contact your representative)
- State legislative hearings
- Media (local newspapers, TV)
- Advocacy organization campaigns
- Social media (#LongTermCare, #CareCantWait)

### Media Engagement

| Action | How |
|--------|-----|
| **Write letters to the editor** | Respond to coverage, raise issues |
| **Submit op-eds** | Share expertise, personal stories |
| **Contact reporters** | Offer to be a source |
| **Use social media** | Amplify coverage, share experiences |
| **Document problems** | Photo, video (with permission) |

**Sample Letter to Editor**:

> To the Editor:
>
> Your recent coverage of [TOPIC] highlights the urgent need for long-term care reform. As someone who [PERSONAL CONNECTION], I know firsthand how the current system fails families.
>
> America's 53 million family caregivers provide over $600 billion in unpaid care annually. Meanwhile, 820,000+ people wait for home care services, and care workers earning poverty wages leave for better-paying jobs at retail stores.
>
> We need [SPECIFIC REFORMS]. Our elected officials must act before the Boomer generation's care needs overwhelm our broken system.
>
> [NAME]
> [CITY]
