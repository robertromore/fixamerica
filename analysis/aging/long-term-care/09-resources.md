## Academic Research

### Financing and Policy

- Wiener, Joshua M. "Long-Term Care Financing, Service Delivery, and Quality Assurance: The International Experience." *Aging and Social Policy* 22, no. 2 (2012): 117-139.

- Mor, Vincent, et al. "The Revolving Door of Rehospitalization from Skilled Nursing Facilities." *Health Affairs* 29, no. 1 (2010): 57-64.

- Grabowski, David C. "The Cost-Effectiveness of Noninstitutional Long-Term Care Services: Review and Synthesis of the Most Recent Evidence." *Medical Care Research and Review* 63, no. 1 (2006): 3-28.

### Quality and Staffing

- Harrington, Charlene, et al. "Nursing Facilities, Staffing, Residents, and Facility Deficiencies, 2009 Through 2014." *Department of Social and Behavioral Sciences, UCSF* (2015).

- Castle, Nicholas G., and John Engberg. "Staff Turnover and Quality of Care in Nursing Homes." *Medical Care* 43, no. 6 (2005): 616-626.

- Werner, Rachel M., et al. "Impact of Public Reporting on Quality of Postacute Care." *Health Services Research* 44, no. 4 (2009): 1169-1187.

### Workforce

- Scales, Kezia. "Envisioning the Future of Home Care: Trends and Opportunities in Workforce Policy and Practice." *PHI* (2020).

- Stone, Robyn I. "The Direct Care Worker: The Third Rail of Home Care Policy." *Annual Review of Public Health* 25 (2004): 521-537.

- Howes, Candace. "Living Wages and Retention of Homecare Workers in San Francisco." *Industrial Relations* 44, no. 1 (2005): 139-163.

### Family Caregiving

- Reinhard, Susan C., et al. "Valuing the Invaluable: 2019 Update." *AARP Public Policy Institute* (2019).

- Wolff, Jennifer L., et al. "Family Caregivers of Older Adults, 1999-2015: Trends in Characteristics, Circumstances, and Role-Related Appraisal." *The Gerontologist* 58, no. 6 (2018): 1021-1032.

### International Comparisons

- Campbell, John Creighton, et al. "Long-Term Care Insurance Comes to Japan." *Health Affairs* 19, no. 3 (2000): 26-39.

- Comas-Herrera, Adelina, et al. "COVID-19: Implications for the Support of People with Social Care Needs in England." *Journal of Aging & Social Policy* 32, no. 4-5 (2020): 365-372.
