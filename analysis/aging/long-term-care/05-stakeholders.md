# Long-Term Care: Stakeholders

## Who Is Affected

### Primary Stakeholders

| Group | Impact | Size |
|-------|--------|------|
| **Seniors needing LTC** | Quality, access, financial security | 8 million 65+ |
| **People with disabilities** | Community integration, choice | 5 million under 65 |
| **Family caregivers** | Time, health, financial burden | 53 million Americans |
| **Direct care workers** | Wages, working conditions, benefits | 4.6 million workers |
| **Nursing home residents** | Quality of life, rights, safety | 1.2 million residents |
| **HCBS recipients** | Service availability, waitlists | 3.5 million people |

### Demographics of Care Recipients

| Characteristic | Share of LTC Recipients |
|----------------|------------------------|
| Women | 65% |
| Over age 85 | 40% |
| Cognitive impairment | 50% |
| Multiple chronic conditions | 75% |
| Medicaid eligible | 60% |
| Racial minorities | 25% (growing) |

### Family Caregiver Profile

| Characteristic | Percentage |
|----------------|------------|
| Female | 61% |
| Employed | 60% |
| Providing care 4+ years | 29% |
| Living with care recipient | 24% |
| Long-distance caregiving | 15% |
| Providing high-intensity care | 21% |

### Secondary Stakeholders

| Group | Connection to LTC | Interest |
|-------|------------------|----------|
| **Healthcare providers** | Discharge planning, post-acute care | Reduce readmissions |
| **State governments** | Medicaid financing, regulation | Control costs |
| **Employers** | Caregiver productivity, absenteeism | Workforce retention |
| **Insurance companies** | LTC insurance products | Market viability |
| **Real estate developers** | Senior housing, assisted living | Profit opportunities |
| **Technology companies** | Aging-in-place solutions | Emerging market |

## Who Has Power

### Federal Decision Makers

| Actor | Role | Power |
|-------|------|-------|
| **CMS Administrator** | Medicare/Medicaid policy | Sets rules, conditions of participation |
| **HHS Secretary** | Department oversight | Budget priorities, regulatory agenda |
| **Senate Finance Committee** | Medicare/Medicaid legislation | Authorizing, appropriations |
| **House Ways and Means** | Medicare legislation | Tax-based financing |
| **House Energy and Commerce** | Medicaid, public health | HCBS policy |

### State Decision Makers

| Actor | Role | Power |
|-------|------|-------|
| **Governors** | Medicaid expansion, budget | State program decisions |
| **State Medicaid directors** | HCBS waivers, rates | Implementation details |
| **State legislators** | Medicaid funding, regulation | Appropriations, policy |
| **Long-term care ombudsmen** | Resident advocacy | Investigation, visibility |
| **State survey agencies** | Nursing home inspection | Enforcement |

## Geographic Stakeholders

### Rural Communities

| Stake | Challenge |
|-------|-----------|
| Limited provider options | Access |
| Lower wages attract fewer workers | Workforce |
| Distance from specialty care | Quality |
| Older demographics | Demand |
| Hospital closures | Coordination |

### Urban Communities

| Stake | Challenge |
|-------|-----------|
| Higher costs | Affordability |
| Housing challenges | HCBS delivery |
| Transportation options | Access to services |
| Diverse populations | Cultural competency |
| More providers, more choices | Navigation |

### State Variation

| State Type | Characteristic | Stakes |
|------------|----------------|--------|
| High-HCBS states (OR, WA, MN) | More community options | Maintaining gains |
| Low-HCBS states (MS, WV, TX) | Institutional bias | Rebalancing |
| High-cost states (NY, CA, MA) | Affordability crisis | Cost containment |
| Low-Medicaid states (TX, FL) | Limited coverage | Access expansion |
