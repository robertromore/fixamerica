# Geriatric Healthcare Workforce: Current State

## Present Conditions

The geriatric healthcare workforce is in crisis. The United States has approximately 7,300 board-certified geriatricians to serve over 58 million adults aged 65 and olderâ€”a ratio of roughly 1 geriatrician per 10,000 older adults, far below the recommended 1:2,500. The situation is worsening as more geriatricians retire than enter the field. Fellowship positions go unfilled, medical schools provide minimal geriatrics training, and the compensation gap with other specialties discourages career choices in geriatric medicine.

## Impact of Shortages

### On Older Adults

| Impact | Evidence |
|--------|----------|
| Inappropriate prescribing | 29% receive Beers Criteria medications |
| Delayed diagnoses | Dementia, delirium often missed |
| Preventable hospitalizations | Higher rates where geriatricians scarce |
| Functional decline | Less focus on mobility, independence |
| Care misaligned with goals | Goals-of-care discussions rare |

### On Healthcare System

| Impact | Evidence |
|--------|----------|
| Hospital overuse | Geriatric conditions treated in ED, inpatient |
| Nursing home overuse | Earlier institutionalization |
| Emergency department crowding | Falls, medication errors |
| Higher costs | More intensive, less appropriate care |
| Quality measure failures | Readmissions, patient experience |

### On Existing Workforce

| Impact | Evidence |
|--------|----------|
| Geriatrician burnout | High panel sizes, complex patients |
| Primary care burden | Untrained physicians managing complexity |
| Nursing home staffing | Quality compromised |
| Caregiver exhaustion | Less professional support available |
