# Geriatric Healthcare Workforce: Stakeholders

## Who Is Affected

### Primary Stakeholders

| Group | Impact | Size |
|-------|--------|------|
| **Older adults (65+)** | Direct care quality and access | 58 million |
| **Family caregivers** | Support, guidance, respite availability | 53 million |
| **Geriatricians** | Career sustainability, workload, compensation | ~7,300 |
| **Geriatric NPs and other APPs** | Practice scope, training access | ~15,000 |
| **Medical students** | Career choice, debt burden | 95,000 enrolled |
| **Internal medicine/FM residents** | Fellowship decision | ~35,000 |

### Detailed Stakeholder Profiles

**Older Adults**:

| Subgroup | Specific Impact |
|----------|-----------------|
| Community-dwelling elders | Access to geriatric assessment, preventive care |
| Nursing home residents | Quality of physician oversight |
| Those with dementia | Specialized care availability |
| Rural older adults | Severe access limitations |
| Low-income elders | Medicaid access constraints |
| Minority elders | Cultural competency gaps |

**Family Caregivers**:

| Impact Area | Effect of Shortage |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Caregiver education | Less professional guidance available |
| Care coordination | Caregivers fill gaps in system navigation |
| Respite availability | Fewer professionals to provide relief |
| Decision support | Goals-of-care conversations limited |
| Emotional support | Professional support unavailable |

**Current Geriatric Workforce**:

| Role | Impact of Shortage |
|------|-------------------|
| Geriatricians | High patient volumes, burnout risk |
| Geriatric NPs | Expanded responsibilities, limited supervision |
| Geriatric social workers | Overwhelming caseloads |
| Long-term care nurses | Staff shortages, turnover |
| Direct care workers | Related workforce under stress |

### Secondary Stakeholders

| Group | How Affected |
|-------|--------------|
| **Primary care physicians** | Bear burden of complex geriatric patients without training |
| **Emergency departments** | Geriatric presentations without specialists |
| **Hospitals** | Readmissions, longer stays, complex discharges |
| **Nursing homes** | Medical director shortages, quality issues |
| **Home health agencies** | Limited physician oversight |
| **Medicare program** | Higher costs from suboptimal care |
| **Taxpayers** | Healthcare spending implications |
| **Medical schools** | Curriculum and training decisions |

## Stakeholder Interests

### Interest Matrix

| Stakeholder | Primary Interest | Position on Reform |
|-------------|------------------|-------------------|
| **Older adults** | Access to quality care | Support expansion |
| **Family caregivers** | Professional support | Support expansion |
| **Geriatricians** | Career sustainability | Support if compensation addressed |
| **Medical students** | Career viability | Support if debt/pay addressed |
| **Teaching hospitals** | Revenue, mission | Mixed (cost concerns) |
| **Primary care physicians** | Manageable workload | Support |
| **Procedural specialists** | Payment relativities | Oppose payment shifts |
| **Medicare program** | Cost and quality | Potentially supportive |
| **Taxpayers** | Efficient spending | Support if cost-effective |

### Detailed Stakeholder Positions

**American Geriatrics Society**:

| Position | Rationale |
|----------|-----------|
| GME expansion | Increase training capacity |
| Payment reform | Make geriatrics financially viable |
| Loan forgiveness | Reduce debt burden |
| Medical education | Increase geriatrics exposure |
| Research funding | Expand NIA budget |

**Teaching Hospitals**:

| Interest | Concern |
|----------|---------|
| GME funding | Want expansion but not redistribution |
| Fellowship support | Geriatrics low revenue vs. other fellowships |
| Workforce needs | Need geriatricians but prefer high-revenue hires |
| Quality improvement | Geriatrics improves outcomes |

**CMS/Medicare**:

| Interest | Position |
|----------|----------|
| Beneficiary access | Concerned about shortages |
| Cost control | Value-based payment may help |
| Quality improvement | Geriatricians improve quality metrics |
| GME spending | Budget constraints limit expansion |

**AMA and Specialty Societies**:

| Interest | Position |
|----------|----------|
| Physician income | Protect current payment structure |
| Scope of practice | Limit NP expansion |
| Training standards | Maintain specialty requirements |
| Workforce distribution | General support but resist payment shifts |

### Coalition Dynamics

**Pro-Expansion Coalition**:

| Member | Contribution |
|--------|--------------|
| AGS | Policy expertise, advocacy |
| AARP | Political influence, membership |
| Hartford Foundation | Funding, research |
| Academic geriatrics | Evidence, credibility |
| Consumer advocates | Public pressure |
| Some health systems | Quality improvement interest |

**Ambivalent/Resistant Stakeholders**:

| Actor | Concern |
|-------|---------|
| Teaching hospitals | Cost of unfunded expansion |
| Specialty societies | Payment redistribution |
| Budget hawks | Any spending increase |
| Some medical schools | Curriculum space |

### Power-Interest Grid

**High Power, High Interest**:

- Congress (GME funding)
- CMS (payment policy)
- ACGME (accreditation)
- Teaching hospitals (training slots)

**High Power, Lower Interest**:

- AMA (focused on broader physician issues)
- Budget committees (geriatrics one of many issues)
- HHS (multiple priorities)

**High Interest, Lower Power**:

- American Geriatrics Society
- Geriatricians
- Older adult advocates
- Family caregivers

**Lower Power, Lower Interest**:

- General public
- Most medical students
- Allied health professions

## Stakeholder Engagement Strategy

### Key Targets

| Target | Why | Approach |
|--------|-----|----------|
| **Congressional committees** | GME funding authority | Demonstrate ROI, constituent impact |
| **CMS leadership** | Payment policy | Quality improvement, cost data |
| **Medical school deans** | Curriculum decisions | Accreditation requirements |
| **Teaching hospital executives** | Training investment | Business case, mission alignment |
| **AARP leadership** | Political mobilization | Member impact stories |

### Building Support

| Stakeholder | Strategy |
|-------------|----------|
| Older adults | Public education, grassroots advocacy |
| Caregivers | Alliance building, storytelling |
| Primary care | Demonstrate support value |
| Health systems | Quality and cost data |
| Policymakers | Evidence-based advocacy |

### Overcoming Opposition

| Opponent | Approach |
|----------|----------|
| Budget hawks | Demonstrate cost savings |
| Procedure-focused specialties | Frame as expansion, not redistribution |
| Teaching hospitals | Tie funding to new slots |
| Resistant medical schools | Accreditation leverage |
