# End-of-Life Care: Current State

## Present Conditions

### Death in America

Most Americans die from chronic conditions after extended illness, yet healthcare systems remain oriented toward acute, curative care. The medicalization of death concentrates dying in institutions despite patient preferences for home death.

| Statistic | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Annual deaths | 3.4 million |
| Deaths age 65+ | 75% of all deaths |
| Leading causes | Heart disease, cancer, COVID-19, accidents, stroke |
| Average age at death | 77.4 years (2021) |
| Deaths from chronic illness | 70%+ |

### Where Americans Die

| Location | Percentage | Trend |
|----------|------------|-------|
| Hospital | 36% | Declining slowly |
| Home | 30% | Increasing |
| Nursing home | 21% | Declining |
| Hospice facility | 6% | Increasing |
| Other (emergency, other institution) | 7% | Stable |

**Preference vs. reality**: 80% of Americans express preference to die at home, but only 30% do so. The gap reflects system barriers, late hospice enrollment, and inadequate home support.

## Hospice Care

### Utilization

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Medicare hospice beneficiaries | 1.7 million annually |
| Percent of Medicare decedents | 57% |
| Primary diagnoses | Cancer (29%), dementia (18%), heart disease (16%) |
| Hospice locations | 90% home, 10% inpatient/residential |
| Number of hospice providers | 5,800+ |

### Timing Problems

| Issue | Data |
|-------|------|
| Median length of stay | 18 days |
| Average length of stay | 90 days (skewed by long stays) |
| Enrolled 7 days or less | 30% of patients |
| Enrolled 3 days or less | 16% of patients |
| Late referral consequence | Missed symptom management, bereavement support |

### Hospice Provider Characteristics

| Type | Share | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| For-profit | 71% | Increased from 30% in 2000 |
| Non-profit | 25% | Declining share |
| Government | 4% | Small, stable |

| Issue | Concern |
|-------|---------|
| For-profit growth | Associated with shorter stays, fewer RN visits |
| Private equity entry | Quality concerns, profit focus |
| Rural access | Fewer providers, longer distances |
| Live discharge rates | Rising; some providers "dump" high-cost patients |

## Advance Care Planning

### Document Completion Rates

| Population | Advance Directive Rate |
|------------|----------------------|
| All adults | 37% |
| Adults 65+ | 46% |
| Adults 75+ | 55% |
| Nursing home residents | 65-70% |
| Seriously ill | Variable; often still low |

### Document Types

| Document | Purpose | Completion |
|----------|---------|------------|
| Living will | Treatment preferences | ~25% of adults |
| Healthcare proxy | Decision-maker designation | ~35% of adults |
| POLST/MOLST | Actionable medical orders | ~10% of appropriate patients |
| Do Not Resuscitate | Specific CPR preference | Variable by setting |

### System Barriers

| Barrier | Impact |
|---------|--------|
| Documents inaccessible | Not in EHR, not transferred |
| Not updated | Preferences change with health |
| Too vague | "Heroic measures" undefined |
| Provider awareness | Often unaware of patient wishes |
| Legal variations | State-by-state differences |
