# End-of-Life Care: Citizen Actions

## What You Can Do

### Personal Planning Actions

| Action | Steps | Resources |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| **Complete advance directive** | 1. Reflect on values and preferences 2. Choose healthcare proxy 3. Complete state-specific form 4. Share with family, providers 5. Store accessibly | Five Wishes, state-specific forms |
| **Discuss with healthcare proxy** | 1. Choose trusted person 2. Explain your values 3. Discuss specific scenarios 4. Ensure they can advocate | Conversation Project guides |
| **Have family conversations** | 1. Initiate discussion 2. Share your wishes 3. Ask about their wishes 4. Document agreements | Death Over Dinner, Conversation Project |
| **Consider POLST** | 1. Discuss with physician 2. Review treatment options 3. Complete POLST form 4. Keep accessible | National POLST program |
| **Review periodically** | 1. Reassess after health changes 2. Update documents 3. Recommunicate wishes | Annual review recommended |

### Healthcare Navigation

| Situation | Action | Approach |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| **Serious diagnosis** | Request palliative care consult | Ask "Can I see a palliative care specialist?" |
| **Managing chronic illness** | Discuss prognosis honestly | "What should I expect going forward?" |
| **Considering treatment options** | Ask about quality of life | "How will this affect my daily life?" |
| **Declining health** | Explore hospice eligibility | "Would I qualify for hospice services?" |
| **Family disagreement** | Request family meeting | Ask for palliative care facilitation |
| **Pain/symptom management** | Advocate for comfort | "My pain is not controlled" |

### Questions to Ask Providers

**About prognosis:**

- "What is the likely course of my illness?"
- "What should I expect in the coming months?"
- "What are the signs that things are getting worse?"

**About treatment options:**

- "What are my options, including comfort-focused care?"
- "What are the benefits and burdens of each option?"
- "What would you recommend if I were your family member?"

**About end-of-life care:**

- "Do you have a palliative care team I could meet with?"
- "When should we talk about hospice?"
- "How can you help my family after I'm gone?"

### Family and Caregiver Actions

| Role | Actions |
|------|---------|
| **Spouse/Partner** | Learn CPR wishes; know healthcare proxy duties; ensure legal documents complete |
| **Adult child** | Initiate conversation with parents; understand their values; coordinate among siblings |
| **Caregiver** | Attend medical appointments; track symptoms and medications; seek respite before burnout |
| **Healthcare proxy** | Understand principal's values; ask clarifying questions; prepare for difficult decisions |
| **Long-distance family** | Stay informed via care updates; participate in family meetings; support local caregiver |

### Preparing for Caregiving

| Preparation | Actions |
|-------------|---------|
| **Legal readiness** | Ensure power of attorney; healthcare proxy; know financial accounts |
| **Care coordination** | Build provider contact list; understand medications; create emergency plan |
| **Home preparation** | Assess accessibility needs; plan for medical equipment; arrange 24/7 coverage |
| **Self-care plan** | Identify respite options; build support network; recognize burnout signs |
| **Hospice knowledge** | Understand Medicare benefit; know eligibility criteria; tour local hospices |

### Political Actions

**Federal Advocacy:**

| Action | Target | Message |
|--------|--------|---------|
| **Support Medicare palliative benefit** | Congress | "Create Medicare palliative care benefit for serious illness" |
| **Advocate for concurrent care** | Congress/CMS | "Allow curative and hospice care simultaneously" |
| **Fund hospice workforce** | Congress | "Increase HRSA funding for palliative care training" |
| **Improve hospice quality** | CMS | "Implement stronger hospice quality standards" |
| **Support ACP research** | NIH | "Fund advance care planning effectiveness research" |

| Issue | Action |
|-------|--------|

### Effective Advocacy Messages

**Personal story approach:**

> "I'm calling about end-of-life care policy. When my [family member] was dying, [describe experience]. I'm asking you to support [specific policy] so other families don't face the same challenges."

**Data-driven approach:**

> "I'm calling about the Medicare hospice benefit. Currently, 30% of hospice patients enroll with less than 7 days to liveâ€”too late to receive full benefits. Please support concurrent care legislation to allow earlier hospice enrollment."

**Values-based approach:**

> "I'm calling about advance care planning. Every American should have the right to make their own decisions about end-of-life care. Please support funding for advance care planning programs."

## Action Checklists

### For Adults at Any Age

- [ ] Complete healthcare proxy designation
- [ ] Draft advance directive
- [ ] Discuss wishes with healthcare proxy
- [ ] Inform primary care physician
- [ ] Share documents with family members
- [ ] Store documents accessibly (not safety deposit box)
- [ ] Review and update periodically

### When Diagnosed with Serious Illness

- [ ] Request palliative care consultation
- [ ] Ask about prognosis and trajectory
- [ ] Discuss goals of care with care team
- [ ] Update advance directive if needed
- [ ] Consider POLST completion
- [ ] Identify caregiver support needs
- [ ] Learn about hospice eligibility

### For Family Caregivers

- [ ] Confirm healthcare proxy designation
- [ ] Locate advance directive and POLST
- [ ] Build relationship with care team
- [ ] Create caregiver support network
- [ ] Arrange respite care options
- [ ] Learn hospice benefit details
- [ ] Plan for financial/legal needs

### After a Death

- [ ] Connect with grief support resources
- [ ] Obtain death certificates
- [ ] Notify relevant institutions
- [ ] Consider joining bereavement group
- [ ] Process experience over time
- [ ] Share story to help others (if desired)
- [ ] Consider advocacy involvement
