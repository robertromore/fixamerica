# Elder Abuse and Neglect: Stakeholders

## Who Is Affected

### Primary Stakeholders

| Group | Impact | Size |
|-------|--------|------|
| **Elder abuse victims** | Physical harm, financial loss, psychological trauma | ~5 million annually |
| **Family caregivers** | Stress, accusations, difficult situations | 53 million caregivers |
| **Nursing home residents** | Institutional abuse and neglect | 1.3 million residents |
| **Assisted living residents** | Less regulated; vulnerability | 850,000 residents |
| **Home care recipients** | Isolation, dependence | 8+ million receiving LTSS |

### Secondary Stakeholders

| Group | How Affected |
|-------|--------------|
| **Family members of victims** | Emotional distress, financial loss, guilt |
| **APS workers** | Caseload pressure, vicarious trauma |
| **Healthcare providers** | Detection responsibility, ethical dilemmas |
| **Law enforcement** | Investigation complexity, resource demands |
| **Financial institutions** | Fraud losses, compliance burden |
| **Communities** | Social fabric, trust erosion |

## Who Has Power

### Federal Government

| Entity | Power | Current Stance |
|--------|-------|----------------|
| **Congress** | Authorization, appropriations | Bipartisan support, limited funding |
| **HHS/ACL** | Policy, coordination | Implementing EJA |
| **CMS** | Nursing home regulation | Increased enforcement efforts |
| **DOJ** | Prosecution, coordination | Elder Justice Initiative |
| **CFPB** | Financial exploitation | Growing engagement |

| **Ombudsman programs** | Nursing home advocacy | Underfunded |

## Victim Voice

### Challenges to Representation

| Challenge | Impact |
|-----------|--------|
| **Cognitive impairment** | Cannot articulate needs |
| **Dependence on abuser** | Fear of retaliation |
| **Shame** | Reluctance to speak publicly |
| **Isolation** | No platform |
| **Mortality** | Cases outlast complainants |
