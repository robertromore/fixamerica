# Alzheimer's and Dementia Care: Solutions

## Reform Framework

### Principles

- **Person-centered**: Focus on dignity, autonomy, and quality of life
- **Research balanced**: Invest in both cure and care
- **Caregiver-inclusive**: Support for families as core component
- **Equity-focused**: Address disparities in access and outcomes
- **Prevention-oriented**: Risk reduction alongside treatment
- **Coordinated care**: Integrate medical, social, and community support

### Strategic Framework

| Dimension | Approach |
|-----------|----------|
| Research | Diversify beyond amyloid; increase funding |
| Detection | Early diagnosis with purpose; primary care capacity |
| Treatment | Access to approved therapies; managed coverage |
| Care delivery | Coordinated, dementia-capable systems |
| Caregiver support | Respite, training, financial assistance |
| Workforce | Training, wages, dementia specialty |
| Community | Dementia-friendly environments |

## Proposed Solutions

### Solution 1: Expand and Diversify Research Funding

**Description**: Increase NIH Alzheimer's funding to $6 billion annually while diversifying research approaches beyond amyloid.

**Key Components**:

| Component | Specification |
|-----------|---------------|
| Total funding target | $6 billion by 2028 |
| Non-amyloid research | 40% of funding |
| Care research | 20% of funding |
| Prevention research | 15% of funding |
| Implementation science | 10% of funding |
| Equity research | 10% of funding |

**Evidence**:

- Current $3.7B is 5x 2015 levels but still underinvests relative to burden
- 99% trial failure rate suggests need for diverse approaches
- Care research has demonstrated impact on quality of life
- Prevention trials show modifiable risk factors exist

**Challenges**:

- Budget constraints
- Research community paradigm resistance
- Long timelines to results
- Competing disease priorities

### Solution 2: Strengthen Early Detection and Diagnosis

**Description**: Build primary care capacity for dementia detection while ensuring diagnosis leads to care planning and support.

**Key Components**:

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Medicare cognitive screening | Mandatory at wellness visits |
| Primary care training | Dementia evaluation competency |
| Diagnostic disclosure | Standards for ethical disclosure |
| Care planning | Required post-diagnosis planning visit |
| Specialist access | Telehealth neurology for rural areas |

**Evidence**:

- 50% of dementia cases go undiagnosed
- Early diagnosis enables planning, safety measures
- Detection without support can cause harm
- Other countries have national screening programs

**Challenges**:

- Primary care time constraints
- Therapeutic nihilism
- Stigma barriers
- Specialist shortages

### Solution 3: Dementia Care Coordination Model

**Description**: Create a Medicare dementia care coordination benefit providing comprehensive care management.

**Key Components**:

| Element | Specification |
|---------|---------------|
| Care coordinator | RN or social worker per patient/family |
| Comprehensive assessment | Standardized needs assessment |
| Care plan | Individualized, regularly updated |
| Care transitions | Managed handoffs between settings |
| Caregiver support | Built into care plan |
| Quality measures | Dementia-specific outcome tracking |

**Evidence**:

- UCLA Alzheimer's and Dementia Care program reduced hospitalizations 40%
- GUIDE model showed cost savings and quality improvement
- Care coordination reduces caregiver burden
- Prevents unnecessary institutionalization

**Challenges**:

- Medicare payment model doesn't support
- Workforce shortages
- Implementation complexity
- Measurement difficulty

### Solution 4: Comprehensive Caregiver Support

**Description**: Establish federal caregiver support program including respite, training, financial assistance, and workplace protections.

**Key Components**:

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Paid family leave | 12 weeks at 60% pay for caregiving |
| Respite expansion | Guarantee 40+ hours/month |
| Training programs | Free, accessible caregiver education |
| Tax credit | $5,000 refundable caregiver credit |
| Workplace protections | Anti-discrimination, flexible scheduling |
| Support services | Counseling, support groups |

**Evidence**:

- Caregiver depression rates 30-40%
- Respite reduces institutionalization
- Training improves care and reduces stress
- Other countries provide comprehensive support

**Challenges**:

- Cost
- Business opposition to mandates
- Respite workforce shortage
- Program administration

### Solution 5: Dementia Workforce Investment

**Description**: Expand and upskill the dementia care workforce through training requirements, wage improvements, and career pathways.

**Key Components**:

| Component | Specification |
|-----------|---------------|
| Dementia training | Required for all care workers |
| Specialty certification | National dementia care credential |
| Geriatric workforce | Loan repayment, training grants |
| Direct care wages | Living wage floor |
| Career ladder | From aide to advanced practice |

**Evidence**:

- Current 75 hours CNA training has minimal dementia content
- Geriatrician shortage severe (26,000)
- High turnover (65%) due to wages
- Specialized training improves care quality

**Challenges**:

- Training capacity
- Funding
- Wage costs
- Workforce attraction

### Solution 6: Memory Care Quality Standards

**Description**: Establish and enforce quality standards for memory care in nursing homes, assisted living, and home care.

**Key Components**:

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| Staffing ratios | Minimum dementia-specific staffing |
| Training requirements | All staff dementia-trained |
| Environment | Dementia-friendly design standards |
| Care practices | Person-centered care requirements |
| Quality measures | Dementia-specific outcome reporting |
| Oversight | Regular inspection and enforcement |

**Evidence**:

- Wide quality variation in memory care
- Person-centered care improves outcomes
- Antipsychotic reduction achieved through quality focus
- Environmental design affects behavior

**Challenges**:

- Assisted living is state-regulated; no federal standards
- Enforcement resources
- Provider resistance
- Measurement complexity

### Solution 7: Dementia-Friendly Communities

**Description**: Fund and support communities to become dementia-friendly through education, accommodation, and inclusion.

**Key Components**:

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Community education | Public awareness campaigns |
| Business training | Retail, banking, public-facing staff |
| Public spaces | Design for cognitive accessibility |
| Transportation | Accessible, safe options |
| First responders | Police, EMS dementia training |
| Social inclusion | Programs for people with dementia |

**Evidence**:

- UK Dementia Friendly Communities program success
- Training reduces stigma
- Design changes improve safety
- Social engagement maintains function

**Challenges**:

- Coordination complexity
- Funding sustainability
- Measurement
- Urban-rural differences

### Solution 8: Advance Planning and End-of-Life Care

**Description**: Improve advance care planning and hospice access for people with dementia.

**Key Components**:

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Early planning | Planning discussions at diagnosis |
| POLST standardization | Consistent orders across states |
| Hospice eligibility | Clearer dementia prognosis criteria |
| Palliative care | Integration throughout disease course |
| Surrogate decision-making | Clear legal frameworks |

**Evidence**:

- Only 55% with dementia die with hospice
- Early planning reduces unwanted interventions
- Palliative care improves quality of life
- Advance directives reduce family burden

**Challenges**:

- Prognostication difficulty
- Cultural barriers
- Lack of early conversations
- Hospice 6-month rule

### Solution 9: Technology for Dementia Care

**Description**: Invest in and deploy technology to support people with dementia and their caregivers.

**Key Components**:

| Technology | Application |
|------------|-------------|
| Monitoring | Safety devices, fall detection |
| Telehealth | Specialist access, caregiver support |
| Cognitive aids | Memory, orientation assistance |
| Caregiver apps | Information, care coordination |
| Research tech | Brain health monitoring, trial recruitment |

**Evidence**:

- Technology can extend aging in place
- Telehealth improves specialist access
- Monitoring reduces caregiver anxiety
- Apps can provide training, support

**Challenges**:

- Digital divide
- Privacy concerns
- Usability for cognitively impaired
- Cost

### Solution 10: Brain Health and Prevention

**Description**: Invest in prevention through risk factor management and brain health promotion.

**Key Components**:

| Factor | Intervention |
|--------|--------------|
| Cardiovascular | Blood pressure, diabetes management |
| Hearing | Hearing aid access, treatment |
| Social | Social engagement programs |
| Physical | Exercise programs |
| Cognitive | Cognitive stimulation |
| Education | Lifelong learning support |

**Evidence**:

- 40% of dementia cases linked to modifiable risk factors
- Cardiovascular management reduces risk
- Hearing treatment associated with lower risk
- Social engagement protective

**Challenges**:

- Long timelines to see impact
- Individual behavior change
- Healthcare system integration
- Equity in access
