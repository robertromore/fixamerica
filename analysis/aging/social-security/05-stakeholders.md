# Social Security: Stakeholders

## Who Is Affected

### Primary Beneficiaries

| Group | Number | Stakes |
|-------|--------|--------|
| **Retired workers** | 51.3 million | Median benefit is primary income source |
| **Disabled workers** | 7.5 million | Only income for most recipients |
| **Survivors** | 5.9 million | Widows, widowers, children of deceased workers |
| **Spouses of beneficiaries** | 2.1 million | Dependent on worker's earnings record |
| **Children of beneficiaries** | 1.9 million | Children of retired, disabled, or deceased workers |

### Workers and Contributors

| Group | Number | Stakes |
|-------|--------|--------|
| **Current workers** | 175 million | Paying taxes, expecting future benefits |
| **Self-employed** | 25 million | Pay both employer and employee shares |
| **Low-wage workers** | 50+ million | Depend most heavily on Social Security |
| **Young workers** | 60+ million | Longest exposure to policy changes |
| **Public sector employees** | 6 million | Some not covered; WEP/GPO affects many |

### Demographic Groups with Special Stakes

| Group | Issue | Impact |
|-------|-------|--------|
| **Women** | Longer lives, lower lifetime earnings, career interruptions | 55% of beneficiaries; lower average benefits |
| **Black Americans** | Lower life expectancy, higher disability rates, lower wages | Earlier reliance on DI; lower retirement benefits |
| **Hispanic Americans** | Growing workforce share, lower average wages | Increasing program dependence |
| **Immigrants** | May not qualify for full benefits; totalization agreements | Complex benefit calculations |
| **LGBTQ+ individuals** | Marriage equality expanded spousal/survivor benefits | Recent access to family benefits |

### SSI Recipients

| Category | Number | Stakes |
|----------|--------|--------|
| **Aged (65+)** | 1.1 million | Means-tested supplement to low Social Security |
| **Blind or disabled adults** | 5.1 million | Primary income for severe disabilities |
| **Blind or disabled children** | 1.2 million | Family income support |

### Family Caregivers

| Role | Number | Stakes |
|------|--------|--------|
| **Unpaid family caregivers** | 53 million | Reduced lifetime earnings â†’ lower benefits |
| **Sandwich generation** | 23 million | Supporting parents and children simultaneously |
| **Working caregivers** | 24 million | Balancing employment and caregiving |

## Who Has Power

### Government Decision Makers

| Actor | Role | Influence |
|-------|------|-----------|
| **President** | Sets priorities, proposes budgets, signs legislation | Agenda-setting; veto power |
| **Treasury Secretary** | Manages trust fund investments | Administrative authority |
| **SSA Commissioner** | Administers program | Rule interpretation; service delivery |
| **House Ways and Means Committee** | Tax and benefit legislation | Primary jurisdiction |
| **Senate Finance Committee** | Tax and benefit legislation | Primary jurisdiction |
| **Congressional Budget Office** | Scores legislation | Shapes policy options |
| **Social Security Advisory Board** | Independent advice | Limited direct power |

### Trustees

The Board of Trustees issues annual reports and projections:

| Member | Position |
|--------|----------|
| Treasury Secretary (Managing Trustee) | Chair |
| Labor Secretary | Trustee |
| HHS Secretary | Trustee |
| SSA Commissioner | Trustee |
| Two Public Trustees | Independent voices (often vacant) |

### Advocacy Organizations

| Organization | Membership/Reach | Position |
|--------------|------------------|----------|
| **AARP** | 38 million members | Protect and expand benefits |
| **National Committee to Preserve Social Security and Medicare** | 1.4 million members | Oppose cuts; support expansion |
| **Social Security Works** | 800,000+ supporters | Expand benefits; lift cap |
| **Committee for a Responsible Federal Budget** | Policy influence | Fiscal sustainability; may support adjustments |
| **The Concord Coalition** | Fiscal advocates | Balance revenue and benefit changes |
| **Cato Institute** | Libertarian | Privatization; voluntary accounts |
| **Heritage Foundation** | Conservative | Benefit reductions; raise retirement age |

### Labor Organizations

| Organization | Focus | Position |
|--------------|-------|----------|
| **AFL-CIO** | 12+ million members | Protect/expand benefits; lift cap |
| **SEIU** | 2+ million members | Expand Social Security; caregiver credits |
| **AFSCME** | 1.4+ million members | Protect public employee coverage |
| **NEA** | 3+ million members | Repeal WEP/GPO |
| **National Domestic Workers Alliance** | Domestic workers | Coverage, caregiver credits |

### Financial Industry

| Actor | Interest |
|-------|----------|

| **Insurance companies** | Annuity products; compete with Social Security |
| **Banks** | Financial products for retirement savings |
| **Financial advisors** | Fee income from retirement planning |

### Beneficiary Priorities

| Group | Top Priorities |
|-------|---------------|
| **Current retirees** | Protect current benefits; adequate COLAs |
| **Near-retirees (55-64)** | No changes before retirement |
| **Workers 35-54** | Know what to expect; sustainable system |
| **Young workers (under 35)** | Fair deal; system exists when they retire |
| **Disabled workers** | Maintain DI; improve determination process |
| **Survivors** | Maintain survivor benefits |

## Coalition Dynamics

### Expansion Coalition

| Members | Common Ground |
|---------|---------------|
| Progressive Democrats | Expand benefits, especially for low earners |
| Labor unions | Lift cap, caregiver credits |
| Senior advocates | Improve benefits, protect structure |
| Disability advocates | Strengthen DI, improve SSI |
| Women's groups | Address career gap penalties |

### Reform Coalition (Fiscally Focused)

| Members | Common Ground |
|---------|---------------|
| Fiscal moderates | Long-term sustainability |
| Some business groups | Predictable future obligations |
| Some economists | Actuarial soundness |
| Good government groups | Responsible stewardship |

### Privatization Coalition

| Members | Common Ground |
|---------|---------------|
| Libertarian think tanks | Market-based approach |
| Some Republicans | Individual ownership |
| Financial industry | Manage privatized accounts |
| Free-market economists | Investment returns |

### Protect Status Quo Coalition

| Members | Common Ground |
|---------|---------------|
| Most politicians | Avoid controversy |
| Current beneficiaries | No changes |
| Near-retirees | Grandfathering |
| Risk-averse groups | Known system vs. unknown changes |

## Generational Stakes

### Current Beneficiaries (Born before 1960)

| Issue | Position |
|-------|----------|
| **Benefit levels** | Protect current benefits |
| **COLA formula** | Oppose any reduction |
| **Medicare interaction** | Keep premium deductions manageable |
| **Retirement age** | Already grandfathered |

### Gen X (Born 1965-1980)

| Issue | Position |
|-------|----------|
| **Trust fund depletion** | Will reach retirement around 2034 |
| **Retirement age** | Already increased to 67 |
| **Benefit formula** | Want to know what to expect |
| **Private savings** | Need to supplement Social Security |

### Millennials (Born 1981-1996)

| Issue | Position |
|-------|----------|
| **System viability** | Skeptical Social Security will exist |
| **Fairness** | Paying taxes, uncertain benefits |
| **Reform timing** | Earlier changes mean smaller changes |
| **Student debt** | Competing priority for payroll taxes |

### Gen Z (Born 1997-2012)

| Issue | Position |
|-------|----------|
| **Long-term sustainability** | Maximum exposure to changes |
| **Intergenerational fairness** | Will pay for current retirees |
| **Career trajectory** | Uncertain labor market |
| **Policy awareness** | Lower engagement with issue |
