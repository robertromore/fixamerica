# Technology and Aging: Stakeholders

## Who Is Affected

### Primary Stakeholders

| Group | Impact | Size |
|-------|--------|------|
| **Older adults (65+)** | Access to healthcare, social connection, daily services | 58 million |
| **Older adults without internet** | Excluded from digital services, telehealth, information | 15 million |
| **Low-income seniors** | Can't afford devices, broadband, or training | 10+ million |
| **Rural older adults** | Lack broadband infrastructure | 8+ million |
| **Seniors with disabilities** | Additional accessibility barriers | 25+ million |
| **Seniors with cognitive impairment** | Complex technology unusable | 6+ million |
| **Fraud victims (elderly)** | Financial and emotional harm | 180,000+ reported/year |

### Secondary Stakeholders

| Group | Impact | Size |
|-------|--------|------|
| **Family caregivers** | Technology enables remote monitoring, coordination | 53 million |
| **Healthcare providers** | Telehealth adoption, remote monitoring | Millions |
| **Long-term care facilities** | Technology for care delivery, family connection | 1.3 million residents |
| **Home health workers** | Technology-assisted care delivery | 3+ million |
| **Adult children of seniors** | Supporting parents' technology use | 50+ million |

### Demographic Breakdown

**Within the 65+ population:**

| Subgroup | Technology Status | Specific Needs |
|----------|-------------------|----------------|
| **65-74, higher income** | Highest adoption; most capable | Fraud protection; support |
| **65-74, lower income** | Moderate adoption; cost barriers | Subsidies; affordable devices |
| **75-84** | Lower adoption; declining skills | Simpler interfaces; support |
| **85+** | Lowest adoption; greatest barriers | Intensive support; accessibility |
| **With sensory impairment** | Additional barriers | Accessible design |
| **With cognitive impairment** | Most excluded | Cognitive support; caregiver proxy |
| **In nursing homes** | Dependent on facility | Facility-based access |
| **Rural** | Infrastructure barriers | Broadband investment |
| **Tribal** | Severe underservice | Targeted investment |

## Stakeholder Interests

### Interest Matrix

| Stakeholder | Primary Interest | Position on Reform |
|-------------|------------------|-------------------|
| **Older adults** | Access, affordability, usability | Strong support |
| **Family caregivers** | Remote monitoring; coordination | Strong support |
| **ISPs** | Revenue; limited regulation | Mixed; oppose mandates |
| **Tech companies** | Market share; growth | Mixed; accessibility costs |
| **Healthcare providers** | Efficiency; reimbursement | Support with payment |
| **PERS companies** | Medicare coverage | Strong support for coverage |
| **Hearing aid industry** | Professional model | Opposed OTC (now adapting) |
| **Taxpayers** | Cost efficiency | Varies by proposal |
| **Privacy advocates** | Data protection | Support with conditions |

### Detailed Stakeholder Analysis

**Internet Service Providers (ISPs):**

| Interest | Position |
|----------|----------|
| Maximize revenue | Oppose price regulation |
| Protect market position | Oppose municipal broadband |
| Avoid obligations | Oppose universal service mandate |
| Access subsidies | Support if receiving funds |
| Maintain regulatory environment | Oppose Title II classification |

**Technology Companies:**

| Interest | Position |
|----------|----------|
| Expand user base | Support adoption programs |
| Minimize development cost | Resist accessibility mandates |
| Data collection | Resist privacy regulation |
| Market reputation | Support some senior programs |
| Platform dominance | Resist interoperability |

**Healthcare Systems:**

| Interest | Position |
|----------|----------|
| Operational efficiency | Support telehealth |
| Reduce readmissions | Support remote monitoring |
| Control portal access | Resist interoperability |
| Limit liability | Cautious on technology |
| Maintain revenue | Support reimbursement parity |

**Senior Advocacy Organizations:**

| Interest | Position |
|----------|----------|
| Member welfare | Support access, affordability |
| Program expansion | Support funding increases |
| Consumer protection | Support fraud enforcement |
| Accessibility | Support design standards |
| Privacy | Support data protection |

## Power Dynamics

### Power Imbalances

| Powerful | Less Powerful | Imbalance |
|----------|---------------|-----------|
| ISPs | Rural consumers | No competitive alternatives |
| Tech platforms | Individual users | Take-it-or-leave-it terms |
| Healthcare systems | Patients | Portal design imposed |
| Device makers | Elderly consumers | Age-unfriendly defaults |
| Scammers | Fraud targets | Information asymmetry |
