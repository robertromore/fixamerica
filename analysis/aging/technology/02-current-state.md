# Technology and Aging: Current State

## Present Conditions

### The Digital Divide

Technology adoption among older adults has increased dramatically but still lags behind younger populations:

| Technology | Ages 65+ | Ages 18-29 | Gap |
|------------|----------|------------|-----|
| **Internet use** | 75% | 99% | 24 pts |
| **Smartphone ownership** | 61% | 96% | 35 pts |
| **Broadband at home** | 64% | 86% | 22 pts |
| **Tablet ownership** | 44% | 53% | 9 pts |
| **Social media use** | 45% | 84% | 39 pts |

**Within the 65+ population, divides persist by:**

| Factor | Higher Adoption | Lower Adoption |
|--------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Age** | 65-74 (80% online) | 75+ (58% online) |
| **Education** | College degree (90%+) | High school or less (55%) |
| **Income** | $75K+ (95%) | <$30K (52%) |
| **Disability** | Without disability (80%) | With disability (62%) |
| **Race/ethnicity** | White (78%) | Hispanic (66%), Black (71%) |
| **Geography** | Urban (78%) | Rural (65%) |

### Telehealth Adoption

COVID-19 dramatically accelerated telehealth adoption:

| Metric | Pre-COVID (2019) | Peak COVID (2020) | Current (2024-25) |
|--------|------------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Medicare telehealth visits** | 840,000/year | 52 million/year | ~25 million/year |
| **Beneficiaries using telehealth** | 1% | 43% | ~30% |
| **Video vs. audio-only** | 70%/30% | 35%/65% | 40%/60% |
| **Rural adoption** | Lower | Significant increase | Persists below urban |

**Current telehealth landscape:**

- Audio-only visits still predominate for 65+ population
- Many temporary COVID flexibilities made permanent
- Medicare reimburses most telehealth at parity with in-person
- Geographic and originating site restrictions largely lifted
- Mental health telehealth particularly successful
- Broadband access remains barrier in rural areas

### Assistive Technology

**Hearing aids:**

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Adults 65+ with hearing loss | 30 million |
| Hearing aid adoption rate | ~30% |
| OTC hearing aids available | Since 2022 |
| Average cost (traditional) | $4,700/pair |
| Average cost (OTC) | $500-1,500/pair |
| Medicare coverage | Limited (exams only) |

**Mobility aids:**

| Device | Medicare Coverage | Barriers |
|--------|-------------------|----------|
| Walkers | Covered with prescription | Copays; quality variation |
| Wheelchairs | Covered (manual and power) | Complex qualification; maintenance |
| Scooters | Covered with medical necessity | Documentation requirements |
| Home modifications | Not covered | High out-of-pocket cost |

**Vision aids:**

- Medicare covers limited vision services
- Eyeglasses generally not covered (except post-cataract)
- Low vision rehabilitation services available but underutilized
- Assistive technology for blind/low vision improving but expensive

### Smart Home and Monitoring

**Adoption rates:**

| Technology | Adults 65+ | Purpose |
|------------|------------|---------|
| Smart speakers (Alexa, Google) | ~27% | Voice control, reminders, connection |
| Medical alert systems | ~10% | Emergency response |
| Video doorbells | ~15% | Security, visitor identification |
| Smart thermostats | ~12% | Comfort, energy savings |
| Remote monitoring (clinical) | ~5% | Chronic disease management |

**PERS (Personal Emergency Response Systems):**

- 5-10 million older adults use PERS
- Traditional pendant/wrist devices dominate
- Smartwatch-based alternatives growing
- Medicare generally does not cover ($25-50/month)
- Some Medicaid waivers cover PERS

### Health Information Technology

**Patient portal adoption:**

| Measure | Adults 65+ | Adults 18-64 |
|---------|------------|--------------|
| Offered portal access | 80%+ | 80%+ |
| Ever accessed portal | ~50% | ~65% |
| Regular portal use | ~25% | ~40% |
| Can message provider | ~40% | ~55% |
| Can view test results | ~45% | ~60% |

**Barriers to portal use for older adults:**

- Complex login/authentication processes
- Small text and poor interface design
- Lack of training and support
- Privacy concerns
- Proxy access limitations for caregivers
- Multiple portals for different providers

## Recent Trends

### Positive Developments

**Telehealth permanence:**

- Many COVID emergency telehealth flexibilities made permanent (2024)
- Medicare audio-only coverage extended
- Geographic and originating site restrictions largely removed
- FQHCs and RHCs can provide telehealth

**OTC hearing aids:**

- FDA rule effective October 2022
- Significant price reductions ($500-1,500 vs. $4,700+)
- Retail availability (Walgreens, Best Buy, online)
- Growing awareness and adoption

**Technology design:**

- "Age-friendly" design receiving more attention
- Voice interfaces (Alexa, Siri) reducing barrier to entry
- Simplified tablet interfaces for seniors
- Caregiver proxy features improving

**Digital equity investments:**

- Affordable Connectivity Program (2021-2024)
- BEAD broadband infrastructure funding
- Digital equity grants to states
- Senior-focused digital literacy programs

### Concerning Developments

**Affordable Connectivity Program ended:**

- ACP provided $30/month broadband subsidy
- Program expired May 2024
- 23 million households affected
- Disproportionate impact on low-income seniors

**Increasing scam sophistication:**

- AI-generated voice cloning in grandparent scams
- Deepfakes in romance scams
- More convincing phishing
- Cryptocurrency fraud targeting retirement savings

**Technology complexity:**

- Multi-factor authentication barriers
- Frequent interface updates confuse users
- Account management increasingly digital-only
- Reduced in-person alternatives

**Healthcare technology fragmentation:**

- Multiple patient portals per person
- Lack of interoperability
- Complex login requirements
- Inconsistent proxy access policies

## Current Policy Landscape

### Federal Programs

| Program | Focus | Status |
|---------|-------|--------|
| **Medicare telehealth** | Healthcare delivery | Expanded; most flexibilities permanent |
| **FCC Lifeline** | Phone/internet subsidy | $9.25/month; underutilized |
| **ACP** | Broadband subsidy | Ended May 2024 |
| **BEAD** | Broadband infrastructure | $42.5B being deployed |
| **Digital Equity Act** | State digital equity plans | Initial grants awarded |
| **OAA technology** | Senior center technology | Limited funding |

### Gaps in Current Policy

| Gap | Impact |
|-----|--------|
| No ongoing broadband subsidy | Low-income seniors losing access |
| Limited Medicare AT coverage | High out-of-pocket costs |
| No digital literacy requirement | Seniors left behind |
| Fragmented fraud enforcement | Inadequate protection |
| No age-friendly design standards | Unusable technology |
| Limited telehealth reimbursement for some services | Care gaps |
