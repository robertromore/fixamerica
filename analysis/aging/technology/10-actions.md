# Technology and Aging: Citizen Actions

## What You Can Do

### Individual and Family Actions

| Action | Steps | Impact |
|--------|-------|--------|
| **Help seniors get online** | Assist with device setup, internet connection, basic training | Direct digital inclusion |
| **Teach technology skills** | Patient, repeated instruction on useful applications | Builds confidence and competence |
| **Research broadband options** | Find best local internet options; apply for subsidies | Improve access |
| **Set up fraud protection** | Enable call blocking, educate on scam recognition | Reduce financial harm |
| **Configure accessible settings** | Increase font size, enable voice features, simplify interface | Make technology usable |
| **Share your own experience** | Tell legislators and media about technology barriers | Humanizes the issue |

### Technology Support for Aging Family Members

**Getting Started:**

| Task | How To |
|------|--------|
| **Choose appropriate device** | Tablet often easier than computer; consider simplified phones |
| **Set up accounts** | Help create email, app store, Medicare accounts |
| **Enable accessibility** | Large text, voice control, hearing aid connectivity |
| **Install useful apps** | Video calling, health apps, medication reminders |
| **Document credentials** | Secure password storage; backup for family |
| **Plan ongoing support** | Regular check-ins; troubleshooting |

**Fraud Prevention Setup:**

| Protection | Steps |
|------------|-------|
| **Call blocking** | Enable carrier spam blocking; download blocking app |
| **Email protection** | Enable spam filtering; teach phishing recognition |
| **Financial alerts** | Set up transaction notifications; trusted contact |
| **Browser security** | Install ad blocker; enable safe browsing |
| **Social media privacy** | Review privacy settings; limit personal sharing |

### Political Actions

**Federal Advocacy:**

| Issue | Target | Message |
|-------|--------|---------|
| **ACP renewal** | Congress | "Renew and make permanent the Affordable Connectivity Program so low-income seniors don't lose broadband access" |
| **Telehealth permanence** | Congress/CMS | "Make permanent all COVID telehealth flexibilities, including audio-only visits for seniors without video capability" |
| **Medicare hearing aid coverage** | Congress | "Add hearing aid coverage to Medicare to improve access for the 30 million seniors with hearing loss" |
| **Elder fraud prevention** | Congress/FTC | "Increase funding for FTC elder fraud enforcement and require platforms to prevent scam advertising" |
| **Broadband investment** | Congress/FCC | "Complete rural broadband deployment and establish broadband as an essential service" |
| **Digital literacy funding** | Congress/ACL | "Increase OAA funding for technology training programs at senior centers and libraries" |

**Sample Advocacy Messages:**

*For ACP renewal:*

> "I'm calling to urge Congress to renew the Affordable Connectivity Program. The ACP provided $30/month broadband subsidies to 23 million households, including millions of low-income seniors. Without renewal, many older adults will lose their internet connection and access to telehealth, online services, and family communication. Please support permanent ACP funding."

*For telehealth permanence:*

> "I'm calling about Medicare telehealth policy. The COVID emergency showed that telehealth worksâ€”43% of Medicare beneficiaries used it. But many temporary flexibilities will expire without Congressional action. Please support legislation making telehealth expansions permanent, including audio-only visits that seniors without broadband can use."

*For fraud prevention:*

> "I'm calling about protecting seniors from fraud. Americans over 60 lost $3.4 billion to scams last year. The FTC and state attorneys general need more resources to investigate and prosecute elder fraud. Please support increased funding for elder fraud enforcement and require platforms to remove scam advertising."

**State Advocacy:**

| Issue | Target | Message |
|-------|--------|---------|
| **State broadband programs** | State legislature | Support state broadband funding and deployment |
| **Municipal broadband** | State legislature | Remove barriers to public broadband networks |

### How to Contact Representatives

**Federal:**

- Capitol switchboard: (202) 224-3121
- Find your representative: house.gov/representatives
- Find your senators: senate.gov/senators
- FCC comments: fcc.gov/consumers/guides/filing-informal-complaint

**State:**

- Find state legislators through your state legislature website
- State consumer protection: Attorney General's office
- State broadband office: varies by state

**Effective Advocacy Tips:**

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Use personal stories | Make abstract arguments only |
| Cite specific data | Be vague about the problem |
| Request specific action | Only express frustration |
| Thank supportive officials | Only contact when critical |
| Offer to provide information | Demand immediate action |
| Follow up after contact | Make one-time contacts only |

### Professional and Workplace Actions

**For Healthcare Providers:**

| Action | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Assess digital readiness** | Identify patients who can't access telehealth |
| **Refer to digital literacy** | Connect patients with training programs |
| **Offer multiple modalities** | Phone, video, and in-person options |
| **Simplify portal access** | Help with enrollment; proxy access for caregivers |
| **Advocate for interoperability** | Push for simpler, unified patient access |

**For Technology Professionals:**

| Action | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Design for all ages** | Build accessible products from the start |
| **Test with older users** | Include 65+ in usability testing |
| **Simplify interfaces** | Reduce complexity; increase customization |
| **Support existing technology** | Don't force constant upgrades |
| **Mentor senior programs** | Volunteer expertise to nonprofits |

**For Employers:**

| Action | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Support elder caregiving** | Flexibility for tech support of aging parents |
| **Offer tech benefits** | Include device/broadband assistance in benefits |
| **Partner with community** | Support local digital literacy programs |
| **Hire older workers** | Include age diversity in tech teams |

**For Financial Professionals:**

| Action | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Enable fraud protections** | Set up alerts; trusted contacts |
| **Provide digital alternatives** | Don't force online-only services |
| **Educate on scams** | Share fraud prevention information |
| **Report suspected exploitation** | Use elder abuse reporting systems |

## Action Checklists

- [ ] Assess aging family members' technology access and skills
- [ ] Help set up appropriate devices with accessibility features
- [ ] Configure fraud protection (call blocking, alerts)
- [ ] Teach essential applications (video calling, telehealth)
- [ ] Research broadband subsidy eligibility (if ACP renewed)
- [ ] Create secure password system with backup access
- [ ] Schedule regular technology check-ins
- [ ] Contact legislators about ACP and telehealth

### For Concerned Citizens

- [ ] Learn about digital divide and aging technology issues
- [ ] Contact federal legislators about ACP renewal
- [ ] Contact state legislators about broadband policy
- [ ] Volunteer with local digital literacy program
- [ ] Donate to advocacy or service organizations
- [ ] Attend town halls and raise technology access issues
- [ ] Support local library digital programs
- [ ] Vote for candidates supporting digital equity

### For Healthcare Professionals

- [ ] Assess patients' telehealth readiness
- [ ] Offer multiple visit modalities (phone, video, in-person)
- [ ] Refer to digital literacy programs
- [ ] Simplify patient portal access
- [ ] Advocate for interoperability within your system
- [ ] Support permanent telehealth coverage
- [ ] Train staff on technology-assisted care
- [ ] Include technology in care planning

### For Technology Professionals

- [ ] Include older users in product testing
- [ ] Design with accessibility from the start
- [ ] Support long-term compatibility (avoid forced obsolescence)
- [ ] Volunteer expertise with senior programs
- [ ] Advocate for age-friendly design standards
- [ ] Mentor on inclusive design practices
- [ ] Challenge ageist assumptions in your workplace

### For Community Leaders

- [ ] Assess community digital divide
- [ ] Partner with library on digital literacy
- [ ] Support senior center technology programs
- [ ] Advocate for municipal broadband if needed
- [ ] Connect aging services with tech volunteers
- [ ] Include digital equity in community planning
- [ ] Organize local tech help events

3. Enable call blocking on a senior's phone

1. Help one older adult set up or troubleshoot technology
2. Call one legislator about ACP renewal or telehealth
3. Research volunteer opportunities with local programs

**This Month:**

1. Complete volunteer training with a digital literacy program
2. Attend a community forum or town hall
3. Donate to an advocacy or service organization

**This Year:**

1. Build ongoing relationship with elected officials
2. Regularly volunteer as a tech mentor
3. Advocate for digital equity in your community
