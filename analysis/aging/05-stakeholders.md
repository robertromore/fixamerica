# Aging Policy: Stakeholders

## Who Is Affected

### Primary Stakeholders

| Group | Size | Key Impacts |
|-------|------|-------------|
| **Older adults 65+** | 58.9 million | Health coverage, retirement security, long-term care access, housing affordability |
| **Adults 85+** | 7 million | Highest care needs, cognitive decline, institutionalization risk |
| **Family caregivers** | 53 million | Lost wages, health impacts, emotional stress, career disruption |
| **Intensive caregivers (20+ hrs/week)** | 10 million | $304,000 lifetime earnings loss, burnout, social isolation |
| **Paid care workers** | 5 million | Low wages ($15.77/hr median), no benefits, high turnover |
| **Dual-eligible beneficiaries** | 12.5 million | Navigate two complex systems, highest needs |
| **Near-elderly (55-64)** | 42 million | Pre-retirement planning, caregiver responsibilities, bridge coverage |

### Affected Communities

| Community | Specific Vulnerabilities |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| **Low-income seniors** | Medicaid eligibility gaps, inadequate SSI, housing insecurity |
| **Seniors of color** | Higher poverty rates, care deserts, historical discrimination |
| **Rural seniors** | Provider shortages, hospital closures, transportation barriers |
| **LGBTQ+ seniors** | Discrimination fears, chosen family complications, isolation |
| **Immigrant seniors** | SSI restrictions, language barriers, documentation concerns |
| **Veterans** | Complex VA/Medicare coordination, service-connected conditions |
| **Women** | Longer lives with less wealth, majority of caregivers and care recipients |

### Secondary Stakeholders

| Group | Relationship to Aging Policy |
|-------|------------------------------|
| **Working-age adults** | Future beneficiaries, current payroll taxpayers, caregiver responsibilities |
| **Children and youth** | Grandparent relationships, intergenerational resource allocation |
| **Employers** | Retirement benefits, caregiver accommodations, workforce planning |
| **Local communities** | Senior housing, transportation, property tax base |
| **Taxpayers** | Fund Medicare, Social Security, Medicaid |

### Industry Stakeholders

| Industry | Size/Revenue | Primary Interests |
|----------|--------------|-------------------|
| **Health insurers** | Medicare Advantage: $450B+ annual revenue | Preserve MA model, resist price controls, maintain favorable regulations |
| **Hospitals** | Medicare: $250B+ annual payments | Protect reimbursement rates, resist readmission penalties |
| **Nursing home chains** | 15,000 facilities, $170B revenue | Resist staffing mandates, maintain Medicaid rates, limit liability |
| **Home health agencies** | 12,000+ agencies, $140B revenue | Payment rate stability, regulatory flexibility |
| **Pharmaceutical companies** | Medicare Part D: $200B+ spending | Oppose price negotiation, protect patent terms |
| **Medical device manufacturers** | $180B+ market | Coverage decisions, approval standards |
| **Long-term care insurers** | 7.4 million policies | Regulatory flexibility, rate increases |

## Stakeholder Interests and Positions

### Older Adults

| Interest | Position |
|----------|----------|
| **Retirement income** | Protect Social Security benefits; oppose cuts or means-testing |
| **Health care** | Preserve Medicare; expand benefits (dental, vision, hearing) |
| **Long-term care** | Want to stay home; adequate support to avoid institutionalization |
| **Housing** | Affordable, accessible housing; age in place |
| **Dignity** | Person-centered care; autonomy in decisions |

### Family Caregivers

| Interest | Position |
|----------|----------|
| **Financial support** | Tax credits, paid leave, Social Security credits |
| **Respite care** | Accessible, affordable breaks |
| **Training** | Skill development for complex care needs |
| **Recognition** | Acknowledgment in care planning and policy |
| **Workplace flexibility** | Leave protections, flexible schedules |

### Paid Care Workers

| Interest | Position |
|----------|----------|
| **Wages** | Living wages ($17-20+/hour); parity with other health workers |
| **Benefits** | Health insurance, paid leave, retirement |
| **Training** | Career ladders, certification, advancement |
| **Working conditions** | Manageable workloads, safety protections |
| **Organizing rights** | Union representation, collective bargaining |

### Health Care Industry

| Actor | Primary Interest | Secondary Interest |
|-------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Insurers** | Profitable Medicare Advantage contracts | Regulatory predictability |
| **Hospitals** | Adequate Medicare reimbursement | Managed post-acute networks |
| **Nursing homes** | Sufficient Medicaid rates; minimal regulation | Staffing flexibility |
| **Physicians** | Fair reimbursement; manageable documentation | Care coordination support |
| **Home health** | Sustainable payment models | Telehealth expansion |

### Government Payers

| Payer | Interest |
|-------|----------|
| **Federal government** | Control entitlement growth; improve outcomes |
| **State governments** | Manage Medicaid costs; federal funding maximization |
| **Local governments** | Minimize property tax burden; address senior housing |

## Coalition Dynamics

### Natural Alliances

| Coalition | Common Ground |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Seniors + workers** | Medicare expansion, Social Security protection |
| **Caregivers + workers** | Workforce investment, wages, working conditions |
| **Seniors + disability advocates** | HCBS, community-based care, civil rights |
| **Rural communities** | Provider access, telehealth, transportation |
| **Women's groups + caregivers** | Paid leave, Social Security credits, workplace protections |

### Tension Points

| Tension | Stakeholders |
|---------|--------------|
| **Benefit levels vs. fiscal sustainability** | Seniors vs. taxpayers; current vs. future beneficiaries |
| **Institutional vs. home care** | Nursing home industry vs. home care advocates |
| **Traditional Medicare vs. Medicare Advantage** | Beneficiary choice vs. cost concerns |
| **Provider autonomy vs. oversight** | Industry vs. regulators; quality vs. flexibility |
| **Family care vs. paid care** | Cultural expectations vs. economic reality |

### Cross-Cutting Issues

| Issue | Diverse Stakeholder Support |
|-------|----------------------------|
| **Caregiver support** | Seniors, families, workers, employers |
| **Telehealth expansion** | Rural advocates, providers, tech industry |
| **Dementia research** | Families, researchers, drug companies |
| **Fraud prevention** | Taxpayers, beneficiaries, honest providers |
| **Nursing home quality** | Residents, families, workers, public |
