# Senior Poverty and Economic Security: Stakeholders

## Who Is Affected

### Primary Stakeholders

| Group | Impact | Size | Key Concerns |
|-------|--------|------|--------------|
| **Seniors in poverty** | Direct experience of hardship | 5.8 million (official) | Basic needs; healthcare; housing |
| **Near-poor seniors** | Risk of falling into poverty | 13 million (below 200% FPL) | Economic insecurity; one crisis from poverty |
| **SSI recipients (65+)** | Dependent on inadequate program | 2.2 million | Benefit levels; asset limits; complexity |
| **Women living alone** | Highest poverty risk group | 8 million single women 65+ | Widowhood; lower Social Security |
| **Minority elderly** | Disproportionate poverty rates | 3 million Black/Hispanic 65+ | Compounded disadvantage; discrimination |
| **Rural elderly** | Geographic isolation + poverty | 7 million rural 65+ | Access; lower benefits; fewer services |
| **Oldest old (85+)** | Asset depletion; health costs | 6.7 million | Long-term care; outliving resources |

### Secondary Stakeholders

| Group | Relationship | Impact |
|-------|--------------|--------|
| **Adult children** | Caregivers and financial supporters | Support burdens; sandwich generation |
| **Grandchildren** | Raised by grandparents; inheritance | Lost opportunities; family stress |
| **Family caregivers** | Provide unpaid care | Lost earnings; career sacrifice |
| **Healthcare providers** | Serve low-income elderly | Uncompensated care; payment constraints |
| **Social service agencies** | Deliver programs | Inadequate funding; high demand |
| **State/local governments** | Fund supplements and services | Budget pressures; Medicaid costs |
| **Employers** | Retirement plan sponsors | Coverage decisions; benefit costs |
| **Financial industry** | Retirement product providers | Fee income; market access |

### Affected Communities

| Community | Characteristics |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **High-poverty urban areas** | Concentrated disadvantage; limited services |
| **Rural communities** | Fewer resources; transportation barriers |
| **Immigrant communities** | SSI restrictions; language barriers |
| **Native American reservations** | Extreme poverty rates; limited infrastructure |
| **Appalachian communities** | Generational poverty; declining services |

## Stakeholder Engagement Strategy

### For Seniors in Poverty

| Strategy | Approach |
|----------|----------|
| **Amplify voices** | Collect and share stories |
| **Reduce barriers** | Simplify program participation |
| **Organize** | Build senior advocacy groups |
| **Register to vote** | Increase political participation |

### For Advocates

| Strategy | Approach |
|----------|----------|
| **Build coalition** | Unite diverse senior advocacy organizations |
| **Develop research** | Document extent and causes of poverty |
| **Communicate effectively** | Frame reforms as honoring work and dignity |
| **Engage legislators** | Consistent presence; constituent connections |

### For Potential Allies

| Target | Strategy |
|--------|----------|
| **Healthcare providers** | Show connection between poverty and health costs |
| **Employers** | Demonstrate productivity benefits of secure retirement |
| **State governments** | Show Medicaid savings from poverty reduction |
| **General public** | Connect current workers to future security |

## Power Analysis

### Who Must Be Moved

| Target | Current Position | Path to Support |
|--------|------------------|-----------------|
| **Moderate Democrats** | Generally supportive | Solidify; mobilize |
| **Moderate Republicans** | Skeptical but reachable | Fiscal sustainability frame; constituent pressure |
| **Senate swing votes** | Varies | State-specific advocacy |
| **Administration** | Dependent on party | Election outcomes; pressure |

### Barriers to Change

| Barrier | Description | Strategy |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| **Deficit framing** | Reform seen as adding to debt | Show long-term savings; alternative revenue |
| **Generational conflict framing** | Reform seen as harming young | Show shared benefits across generations |
| **Complexity** | Public doesn't understand programs | Simplify messaging |
| **Apathy** | Not seen as urgent | Highlight human impact |
| **Industry opposition** | Well-funded lobbyists | Build countervailing coalition |
