# Caregiving Workforce: Stakeholders

## Who Is Affected

### Primary Stakeholders

| Group | Impact | Size |
|-------|--------|------|
| **Direct care workers** | Wages, benefits, working conditions | 4.6 million |
| **Care recipients** | Quality, continuity, access | 13 million+ |
| **Family caregivers** | Must fill gaps when workers unavailable | 53 million |
| **Nursing home residents** | Staffing directly affects safety | 1.2 million |
| **HCBS recipients** | Workforce availability determines access | 3.5 million |

### Worker Demographics

| Characteristic | Percentage | Implication |
|----------------|------------|-------------|
| Female | 87% | Gendered workforce issues |
| People of color | 62% | Racial equity central |
| Immigrants | 27% | Immigration policy relevant |
| Single parents | 25% | Need stable schedules, benefits |
| Age 45+ | 48% | Approaching retirement |
| Without college degree | 84% | Training access important |

### Secondary Stakeholders

| Group | Connection | Interest |
|-------|------------|----------|
| **Employers (providers)** | Hire, supervise, pay workers | Workforce stability, costs |
| **State Medicaid programs** | Set reimbursement rates | Cost containment, quality |
| **Healthcare systems** | Receive patients from care settings | Readmission rates |
| **Training programs** | Prepare workers | Curriculum, enrollment |
| **Taxpayers** | Fund Medicaid, worker public benefits | Cost efficiency |
| **Union members** | Represented workers | Collective bargaining |

## Who Has Power

### Federal Decision Makers

| Actor | Role | Power |
|-------|------|-------|
| **CMS Administrator** | Medicare/Medicaid rules | Staffing requirements, conditions |
| **Department of Labor** | Wage and hour enforcement | FLSA implementation |
| **Congress** | Legislation, appropriations | Funding levels, mandates |
| **HHS Secretary** | Policy priorities | Workforce initiatives |

### State Decision Makers

| Actor | Role | Power |
|-------|------|-------|
| **State Medicaid directors** | Set reimbursement rates | Wage ceilings |
| **Governors** | Budget priorities | Medicaid funding |
| **State legislatures** | Wage laws, training standards | State requirements |
| **Licensing boards** | Credentials, standards | Entry barriers |

### Industry Power

| Actor | Power Source | Influence |
|-------|--------------|-----------|
| **AHCA** | 14,000 nursing homes | Oppose wage mandates |
| **LeadingAge** | 5,000+ nonprofit providers | Quality advocacy |
| **Home care associations** | Agency representation | Regulatory influence |
| **Private equity** | $100B+ investment | Financial leverage |

### Worker Power

|-------|--------------|-----------|

| **AFSCME** | Public employee representation | State policy |

### Advocacy Power

|--------------|--------------|-----------|

## Generational Stakes

### Current Workforce (Gen X, Millennials)

| Stake | Concern |
|-------|---------|
| Immediate wages | Can't support families |
| Benefits access | Healthcare, retirement |
| Career prospects | Limited advancement |
| Working conditions | Physical, emotional toll |

### Future Workforce (Gen Z, future)

| Stake | Concern |
|-------|---------|
| Occupation attractiveness | Will they enter field? |
| Career viability | Is caregiving a good job? |
| Education investment | Worth training for? |
| Technology impact | How will jobs change? |

### Baby Boomers (Care Recipients)

| Stake | Concern |
|-------|---------|
| Workforce availability | Will anyone be there? |
| Care quality | Will they be well-cared for? |
| Affordability | Can they afford care? |
| Choice | Will they have options? |

## Geographic Stakeholders

### Rural Communities

| Stake | Challenge |
|-------|-----------|
| Workforce scarcity | Fewer workers, longer distances |
| Lower wages | Regional wage disparities |
| Training access | Programs not available |
| Provider viability | Facilities closing |

### Urban Communities

| Stake | Challenge |
|-------|-----------|
| Cost of living | Wages don't cover housing |
| Competition | Other jobs pay more |
| Transportation | Commute costs |
| Diversity | Cultural competency needs |

### State Variation

| State Type | Stakes |
|------------|--------|
| High-wage states (CA, WA) | Maintaining gains, cost pressure |
| Low-wage states (MS, LA) | Severe shortages, quality problems |
| Union-strong states (NY, IL) | Collective bargaining preservation |
| Right-to-work states (TX, FL) | Organizing barriers |
