# Aging Policy: Current State

## Present Conditions

The over-65 population exceeds 58 million, representing the largest cohort of older adults in American history. This demographic wave is reshaping every aspect of social policy, from health care financing to labor markets to housing design. Current systems struggle to adapt, leaving millions of seniors facing inadequate care, economic insecurity, and limited autonomy.

### COVID-19 Impact

Nursing homes accounted for over 200,000 COVID-19 deaths (23% of U.S. total) despite housing less than 1% of the population. This exposed staffing shortages, infection control failures, and regulatory gaps.

## Recent Developments

### Legislative Action

- **Inflation Reduction Act (2022)**: Capped insulin at $35/month for Medicare, allowed Medicare to negotiate some drug prices
- **Medicare hearing aid coverage (2022)**: Over-the-counter hearing aids allowed
- **BEAD broadband funding**: $42 billion for rural connectivity

### Regulatory Changes

- **Nursing home staffing rule (2024)**: CMS proposed minimum staffing requirements (3.0 hours per resident day)
- **Medicare Advantage oversight**: Increased scrutiny of prior authorization denials

### Market Trends

- Medicare Advantage enrollment surpassed traditional Medicare for the first time (2023)
- Private equity ownership of nursing homes increased to 11% of facilities
- Telehealth utilization stabilized at 4x pre-pandemic levels
