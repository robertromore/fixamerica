# Aging Veterans: Implementation Roadmap

## Overview

This roadmap outlines a phased implementation strategy for transforming care for aging veterans over a 10-year period. The phases are organized by feasibility, cost, and dependency relationships, with quick wins prioritized in Phase 1 to build momentum before pursuing capital-intensive infrastructure and structural reforms. The Vietnam-era aging surge is already underway, making urgency a defining feature of this roadmap--delays measured in years translate to hundreds of thousands of veterans receiving inadequate care during peak need.

## Phase 1: Immediate Actions (Years 1-2)

### Legislative Priorities

| Action | Vehicle | Status | Champion |
|--------|---------|--------|----------|
| PCAFC eligibility and application reform | Standalone bill or NDAA amendment | Elizabeth Dole Act introduced; pending committee | Senate/House VA Committees |
| Aid and Attendance anti-predator legislation | Standalone bill | GAO recommended; no current bill | Senate VA Committee |
| State Veterans Home per diem increase | VA appropriations | Proposed by NASVH; below requested level | House Appropriations MilCon-VA |
| Pension CPI-E indexing | Standalone bill | Conceptual | Senate Finance + VA Committees |

### Administrative Actions (No Legislation Required)

| Action | Responsible Office | Timeline | Cost |
|--------|-------------------|----------|------|
| Expand HBPC by 100 new teams | VHA Geriatrics and Extended Care | 6-12 months | $400M/yr |
| Simplify PCAFC application process | VA Caregiver Support Program | 3-6 months | Minimal |
| Establish independent PCAFC appeals process | VA Caregiver Support Program | 6-12 months | $25M/yr |
| Launch proactive Aid and Attendance outreach | VA Benefits Administration | 6-12 months | $50M/yr |
| Add social isolation screening to geriatric primary care | VHA Primary Care | 6-12 months | $25M/yr |
| Expand Veteran-Directed Care enrollment capacity | VHA Geriatrics and Extended Care | 12-18 months | $200M/yr |
| Deploy peer support specialists for aging veterans (pilot in 20 VISNs) | VHA Mental Health | 12-18 months | $50M/yr |
| Issue anti-predator guidance for A&A financial services | VA Office of General Counsel | 3-6 months | Minimal |

### Quick Wins

| Action | Impact | Timeline |
|--------|--------|----------|
| Universal cognitive screening protocol for veterans 65+ | Early detection for 7.8 million aging veterans | 6 months (clinical directive) |
| Free A&A application assistance through VSO partnerships | Eliminate predatory service market | 3 months (grant agreements) |
| Telehealth equipment for homebound aging veterans | Connects isolated veterans to care and social programs | 6 months (expand Digital Divide Consult) |
| Caregiver training modules online | Immediate support for 3.8 million caregivers | 6 months (adapt existing content) |
| Respite care awareness campaign | Increase utilization of existing respite programs | 30 days (communications) |

### Phase 1 Milestones

- [ ] HBPC expanded by 100 teams serving 10,000+ additional veterans
- [ ] PCAFC application processing time reduced by 30%
- [ ] Independent PCAFC appeals process operational
- [ ] Social isolation screening integrated into geriatric primary care
- [ ] Proactive A&A outreach identifies 50,000 potentially eligible veterans
- [ ] Peer support pilot operational in 20 VISNs
- [ ] Cognitive screening protocol issued as VA clinical directive

## Phase 3: Systemic Transformation (Years 6-10)

## Dependencies and Risks

### Critical Dependencies

| Dependency | Risk if Not Met | Mitigation |
|-----------|----------------|------------|
| Congressional appropriations growth | Phased rollout stalls; veterans wait for care | Prioritize administrative actions; build bipartisan support |
| Geriatric workforce pipeline | HBPC and CLC expansion impossible without staff | Enhanced recruitment, telehealth augmentation, scope of practice expansion |
| PCAFC legislative reform | Caregiver support remains at 1.4% coverage | Maximize administrative reforms within existing authority |
| VA-Medicare interoperability | Dual-eligible veterans receive fragmented care | Push for EHR interoperability; care coordinator workarounds |
| CLC construction timelines | Modernization delayed by VA construction bureaucracy | Prioritize renovations over new construction; use design-build contracting |

### Key Risks

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Response |
|------|------------|--------|----------|
| Budget austerity or sequestration | Medium | Very High | Protect geriatric care as mandatory spending; demonstrate cost-effectiveness |
| Geriatric workforce shortage worsens | High | High | Telehealth augmentation; community health worker expansion; international recruitment |
| Post-9/11 veteran needs compete for attention | Medium | Medium | Frame aging veteran care as fulfilling existing obligation, not new spending |
| Pandemic or public health emergency | Medium | High | Strengthen CLC infection control; maintain telehealth infrastructure |
| VA leadership turnover disrupts implementation | High | Medium | Embed metrics and programs in statute; build institutional champion network |
| Dementia prevalence exceeds projections | Medium | High | Accelerate TBI-dementia research; build flexible capacity |

## Measurement and Accountability

### Annual Reporting Requirements

The VA Secretary should be required to report annually to Congress on:

1. HBPC enrollment, capacity, and waitlists by VISN
2. PCAFC enrollment, denial rates, and caregiver outcomes
3. Cognitive screening rates and dementia care coordination coverage
4. CLC quality metrics, staffing ratios, and vacancy rates
5. Elderly veteran suicide rates and mental health access metrics
6. Aid and Attendance participation rates and outreach outcomes
7. Social isolation screening rates and intervention coverage
8. Geriatric workforce recruitment and retention data

### Independent Evaluation

GAO should conduct biennial evaluations of aging veteran care progress, with specific attention to:

- Whether HBPC and HCBS expansion is reducing nursing home admissions
- Whether PCAFC expansion is reducing caregiver burnout and veteran emergency utilization
- Whether cognitive screening is leading to earlier dementia intervention
- Whether social connection programs are reducing elderly veteran suicide
- Whether CLC modernization is improving quality outcomes
