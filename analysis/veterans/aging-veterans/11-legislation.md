# Aging Veterans: Legislation and Legal Framework

## Overview

The legislative landscape for aging veteran care involves the intersection of veterans affairs law, Medicare and Medicaid policy, long-term care financing, caregiver support, and geriatric workforce development. Current law--principally the VA MISSION Act (2018), the Caregivers and Veterans Omnibus Health Services Act (2010), the PACT Act (2022), and the Millennium Health Care Act (1999)--provides a foundation for VA geriatric services, but significant statutory gaps remain in home-based care capacity, caregiver eligibility, cognitive health infrastructure, institutional care quality, financial protections for elderly veterans, and VA-Medicare coordination. This document proposes federal legislation, state model legislation, and regulatory reforms to close these gaps.

## Federal Legislation

### Aging Veterans Comprehensive Care Act

**Purpose**: Comprehensive legislation addressing home and community-based services expansion, caregiver support reform, cognitive health infrastructure, institutional care modernization, and financial protections for aging veterans.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SEC. 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Aging Veterans Comprehensive Care Act".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds that--
(1) approximately 7,800,000 veterans are age 65 or older,
    representing 48 percent of all living veterans;
(2) by 2030, an estimated 4,800,000 veterans will be age 75
    or older, creating unprecedented demand for geriatric care,
    long-term services and supports, and cognitive health treatment;
(3) VA Home-Based Primary Care serves only 18 percent of
    estimated eligible homebound veterans;
(4) the Program of Comprehensive Assistance for Family
    Caregivers reaches only 1.4 percent of the estimated 3,800,000
    family caregivers of aging veterans;
(5) veterans age 75 and older have a suicide rate of 45.2 per
    100,000, among the highest of any demographic group;
(6) an estimated 500,000 to 800,000 veterans are eligible for
    but not receiving the Aid and Attendance enhanced pension; and
(7) the costs of caring for aging veterans are a long-term
    consequence of military service that must be planned and
    budgeted for, not treated as an unexpected crisis.

SEC. 3. HOME-BASED PRIMARY CARE EXPANSION.

(a) EXPANSION REQUIREMENT.--The Secretary of Veterans Affairs shall
    expand the Home-Based Primary Care program under section 1720C
    of title 38, United States Code, to serve not fewer than
    100,000 veterans by fiscal year 2031.
(b) STAFFING.--The Secretary shall hire not fewer than 300
    additional interdisciplinary Home-Based Primary Care teams
    across all Veterans Integrated Service Networks.
(c) RURAL HBPC.--The Secretary shall establish Home-Based Primary
    Care teams with extended service areas in not fewer than 50
    rural areas, using community health workers and telehealth
    to augment clinical teams.
(d) TELEHEALTH INTEGRATION.--Each Home-Based Primary Care team
    shall be equipped with remote patient monitoring and video
    telehealth capability.
(e) AUTHORIZATION.--There is authorized to be appropriated
    $1,400,000,000 annually for fiscal years 2027 through 2036
    to carry out this section.

SEC. 4. VETERAN-DIRECTED CARE EXPANSION.

(a) EXPANSION.--The Secretary shall expand the Veteran-Directed
    Care program to serve not fewer than 50,000 veterans by
    fiscal year 2031.
(b) BUDGET ADEQUACY.--Individual care budgets under the program
    shall reflect actual market costs for home health aides and
    personal care assistants in the veteran's geographic area,
    as determined by the Secretary using Bureau of Labor Statistics
    data updated annually.
(c) PARTNERSHIPS.--The Secretary shall enter into agreements with
    Area Agencies on Aging in every state for care coordination
    of veterans in the Veteran-Directed Care program.
(d) AUTHORIZATION.--There is authorized to be appropriated
    $1,500,000,000 annually for fiscal years 2027 through 2036
    to carry out this section.

SEC. 5. PROGRAM OF COMPREHENSIVE ASSISTANCE FOR FAMILY CAREGIVERS
         REFORM.

(a) ELIGIBILITY EXPANSION.--Section 1720G(a)(2) of title 38,
    United States Code, is amended--
    (1) by striking "serious injury (including traumatic brain
        injury, psychological trauma, or other mental disorder)"
        and inserting "injury, illness, or condition (including
        service-connected and non-service-connected conditions
        that require personal care services)"; and
    (2) by adding at the end the following: "The Secretary shall
        establish not fewer than 3 tiers of caregiver assistance
        based on the intensity of care required, as follows:
        (A) BASIC TIER.--For veterans requiring assistance with
            not fewer than 1 activity of daily living, a monthly
            stipend of not less than $1,200.
        (B) MODERATE TIER.--For veterans requiring assistance with
            not fewer than 3 activities of daily living, a monthly
            stipend of not less than $2,100.
        (C) INTENSIVE TIER.--For veterans requiring supervision,
            protection, or instruction on a daily basis, a monthly
            stipend of not less than $3,500.".
(b) APPLICATION SIMPLIFICATION.--Not later than 1 year after
    enactment, the Secretary shall--
    (1) reduce the PCAFC application to a streamlined clinical
        assessment that can be completed in a single visit;
    (2) establish an independent clinical review process for
        denied applications, with decisions rendered within
        60 days of appeal; and
    (3) permit telehealth-based clinical assessments for
        caregivers in rural areas.
(c) RESPITE GUARANTEE.--Each caregiver enrolled in the PCAFC
    shall be entitled to not fewer than 30 days of respite care
    per year, provided through VA facilities, community providers,
    or in-home respite services.
(d) AUTHORIZATION.--There is authorized to be appropriated
    $3,500,000,000 annually for fiscal years 2027 through 2036
    to carry out this section.

SEC. 6. GENERAL CAREGIVER SUPPORT SERVICES.

(a) UNIVERSAL CAREGIVER TIER.--The Secretary shall establish a
    General Caregiver Support tier available to all family
    caregivers of veterans enrolled in VA healthcare, regardless
    of PCAFC enrollment, providing--
    (1) free training on geriatric care, medication management,
        fall prevention, dementia care, and end-of-life support;
    (2) caregiver support groups at every VA Medical Center and
        through telehealth;
    (3) free mental health counseling for caregivers;
    (4) financial counseling and benefits navigation; and
    (5) coordination with community respite providers.
(b) AUTHORIZATION.--There is authorized to be appropriated
    $500,000,000 annually for fiscal years 2027 through 2036
    to carry out this section.

SEC. 7. COGNITIVE HEALTH AND DEMENTIA CARE.

(a) UNIVERSAL SCREENING.--The Secretary shall implement cognitive
    screening for all veterans age 65 and older at each annual
    primary care visit, using validated screening instruments.
(b) RISK-STRATIFIED MONITORING.--Veterans with a history of
    traumatic brain injury, post-traumatic stress disorder, or
    toxic exposure shall receive enhanced cognitive monitoring
    beginning at age 55.
(c) EARLY INTERVENTION.--The Secretary shall establish early
    intervention programs at every VA Medical Center for veterans
    with mild cognitive impairment, including cognitive
    rehabilitation, lifestyle modification, and pharmacological
    treatment.
(d) DEMENTIA CARE COORDINATORS.--Each VA Medical Center shall
    employ at least one dedicated dementia care coordinator
    responsible for coordinating services, caregiver training,
    and care transitions for veterans with dementia.
(e) TBI-AGING RESEARCH.--The Secretary shall establish a TBI-Aging
    Research Initiative with not less than $50,000,000 annually
    for 10 years to study the interaction between traumatic brain
    injury and age-related cognitive decline, develop prevention
    protocols, and create specialized treatment programs.
(f) AUTHORIZATION.--There is authorized to be appropriated
    $625,000,000 annually for fiscal years 2027 through 2036
    to carry out this section.

SEC. 8. COMMUNITY LIVING CENTER MODERNIZATION.

(a) FACILITY UPGRADES.--The Secretary shall modernize not fewer
    than 50 Community Living Centers within 7 years of enactment,
    upgrading to private rooms, dementia-friendly design standards,
    and therapeutic outdoor spaces.
(b) TARGETED EXPANSION.--The Secretary shall add not fewer than
    3,000 Community Living Center beds in states with the highest
    aging veteran populations, including Florida, Texas, Arizona,
    and California.
(c) STAFFING.--
    (1) PAY DIFFERENTIAL.--All direct care workers at Community
        Living Centers shall receive a pay differential of not
        less than 15 percent above the standard VA rate for
        equivalent positions.
    (2) STAFFING RATIOS.--Each Community Living Center shall
        maintain minimum staffing of 4.1 nursing hours per
        resident day.
(d) DEMENTIA UNITS.--The Secretary shall establish specialized
    dementia care units at not fewer than 40 Community Living
    Centers.
(e) AUTHORIZATION.--There is authorized to be appropriated
    $3,700,000,000 for capital improvements and $850,000,000
    annually for operating costs for fiscal years 2027 through
    2036 to carry out this section.

SEC. 9. STATE VETERANS HOME ENHANCEMENT.

(a) PER DIEM INCREASE.--Section 1741 of title 38, United States
    Code, is amended by increasing the per diem rate for basic
    care from $131.13 to $185.00 per day, adjusted annually for
    inflation.
(b) DEMENTIA PER DIEM.--The Secretary shall establish a
    dementia-specialized per diem rate of not less than $225.00
    per day for State Veterans Homes that maintain certified
    dementia care units meeting standards established by the
    Secretary.
(c) CONSTRUCTION GRANTS.--There is authorized to be appropriated
    $1,000,000,000 for State Veterans Home construction grants
    to add not fewer than 5,000 beds nationwide.
(d) QUALITY STANDARDS.--State Veterans Homes receiving per diem
    payments shall maintain quality standards at least equivalent
    to CMS 3-star rating; homes falling below this standard for
    2 consecutive years shall be subject to corrective action
    plans.
(e) INFECTION CONTROL.--Each State Veterans Home shall maintain
    infection control infrastructure and staffing meeting
    standards established by the Secretary in consultation with
    the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention.

SEC. 10. AGING VETERAN SOCIAL CONNECTION AND MENTAL HEALTH.

(a) SOCIAL CONNECTION PROGRAM.--The Secretary shall establish an
    Aging Veteran Social Connection Program providing--
    (1) peer support specialists conducting regular outreach to
        aging veterans identified as at risk for social isolation;
    (2) community engagement programs at VA facilities, VSO posts,
        and community centers;
    (3) virtual social connection programs for homebound veterans;
        and
    (4) social isolation screening integrated into all VA
        geriatric primary care visits.
(b) LATE-LIFE MENTAL HEALTH.--The Secretary shall establish a
    Late-Life Mental Health Initiative providing--
    (1) geriatric-adapted PTSD treatment programs at every VA
        Medical Center;
    (2) geriatric psychiatry capability at every VA Medical Center;
    (3) voluntary firearms safe storage programs designed for
        elderly veterans; and
    (4) bereavement and purpose programs for retired veterans.
(c) AUTHORIZATION.--There is authorized to be appropriated
    $750,000,000 annually for fiscal years 2027 through 2036
    to carry out this section.

SEC. 11. AID AND ATTENDANCE REFORM AND FINANCIAL PROTECTIONS.

(a) PROACTIVE OUTREACH.--The Secretary shall use healthcare
    enrollment data to proactively identify veterans potentially
    eligible for Aid and Attendance and conduct targeted outreach.
(b) APPLICATION SIMPLIFICATION.--The Secretary shall reduce Aid
    and Attendance application processing time to not more than
    60 days.
(c) ANTI-PREDATOR PROVISIONS.--
    (1) PROHIBITION.--It shall be unlawful for any person to
        charge a fee for assistance with the preparation or
        submission of an application for pension benefits under
        chapter 15 of title 38, United States Code, unless such
        person is accredited by the Secretary under section 5904
        of title 38.
    (2) PENALTY.--Any person who violates paragraph (1) shall be
        subject to a civil penalty of not more than $10,000 per
        violation.
    (3) ASSET RESTRUCTURING.--Any person who restructures a
        veteran's assets for the primary purpose of establishing
        eligibility for pension benefits, where such restructuring
        renders the veteran ineligible for Medicaid long-term care
        benefits, shall be subject to a civil penalty of not more
        than $25,000 per violation.
(d) AUTHORIZATION.--There is authorized to be appropriated
    $105,000,000 annually for fiscal years 2027 through 2036
    to carry out this section.

SEC. 12. VA-MEDICARE CARE COORDINATION.

(a) COORDINATION REQUIREMENT.--The Secretary of Veterans Affairs
    and the Administrator of the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid
    Services shall jointly establish protocols for coordinated care
    of veterans enrolled in both VA healthcare and Medicare,
    including--
    (1) shared electronic health record access or interoperable
        health information exchange;
    (2) joint medication reconciliation for dual-enrolled veterans;
    (3) coordinated care plans for veterans with complex geriatric
        conditions; and
    (4) designated care coordinators for high-risk dual-enrolled
        veterans.
(b) DUAL-ELIGIBLE PILOT.--The Secretary shall establish a pilot
    program in not fewer than 10 VA Medical Centers to test
    integrated VA-Medicare care delivery models for aging veterans.
(c) REPORT.--Not later than 2 years after enactment, the Secretary
    shall report to Congress on the pilot program results and
    recommendations for national implementation.

SEC. 13. GERIATRIC WORKFORCE INVESTMENT.

(a) GERIATRIC RESIDENCY POSITIONS.--The Secretary shall fund not
    fewer than 200 geriatric medicine fellowship and residency
    positions at VA Geriatric Research, Education, and Clinical
    Centers and affiliated academic medical centers.
(b) LOAN REPAYMENT.--The Secretary shall establish a Geriatric
    Workforce Loan Repayment Program providing up to $200,000 in
    student loan repayment for geriatricians, geriatric nurses,
    and geriatric social workers who complete a 4-year service
    obligation at a VA facility.
(c) DIRECT CARE WORKER INVESTMENT.--The Secretary shall increase
    wages for certified nursing assistants, home health aides,
    and personal care assistants employed by the VA to rates not
    less than the 75th percentile of local market wages.
(d) AUTHORIZATION.--There is authorized to be appropriated
    $500,000,000 annually for fiscal years 2027 through 2036
    to carry out this section.

SEC. 14. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS.

There is authorized to be appropriated to carry out this Act
$12,000,000,000 for each of fiscal years 2027 through 2036.
```

**Explanation**:

- **Section 3** addresses the most critical capacity gap by tripling HBPC, the VA's most cost-effective geriatric program
- **Section 4** expands veteran-directed care for veterans who prefer to manage their own services
- **Sections 5-6** fundamentally reform caregiver support, expanding PCAFC eligibility and creating universal support for all caregivers
- **Section 7** builds cognitive health infrastructure, critical for the TBI-dementia wave ahead
- **Sections 8-9** modernize institutional care without abandoning it, recognizing that some veterans need nursing-level care
- **Section 10** addresses social isolation and late-life mental health, directly targeting elderly veteran suicide
- **Section 11** protects elderly veterans from financial exploitation while expanding access to benefits
- **Section 12** begins the long-overdue integration of VA and Medicare care for dual-eligible veterans
- **Section 13** invests in the geriatric workforce pipeline

**Potential Challenges**:

| Challenge | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| Total cost ($12B/year) | Represents the true cost of fulfilling the obligation to aging veterans; CBO projects $18-22B needed by 2030 regardless |
| PCAFC eligibility expansion scope | Tiered system controls costs by matching support to need intensity |
| CLC construction timeline | VA construction is notoriously slow; authorize design-build contracting to accelerate |
| Geriatric workforce shortage | Legislation creates pipeline, but results take 5-10 years; telehealth and community health workers bridge the gap |
| State Veterans Home quality enforcement | Per diem tied to quality standards creates incentive alignment |

**Refinements**:

- **Fallback position**: If comprehensive bill is infeasible, pursue individual titles as standalone bills (PCAFC reform, cognitive health, A&A protections)
- **Pilot approach**: Authorize HBPC expansion, PCAFC reform, and cognitive screening as 5-year pilots in 10 VISNs before national deployment
- **Sunset provision**: Include 10-year authorization with mandatory GAO evaluation at years 3, 5, and 8

## State Model Legislation

### State Aging Veteran Support Act (Model)

**Purpose**: State-level legislation to supplement federal programs and address state-specific aging veteran needs.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "[State] Aging Veteran Support Act".

SECTION 2. STATE VETERANS HOME INVESTMENT.

(a) The [State Department of Veterans Affairs] shall develop a
    5-year plan for State Veterans Home capacity expansion, with
    priority given to regions with the highest aging veteran
    populations and longest waitlists.
(b) The state shall appropriate matching funds for federal
    construction grants received under 38 U.S.C. Section 8135.
(c) All State Veterans Homes shall maintain dementia care
    capability and infection control infrastructure meeting
    federal standards.

SECTION 3. CAREGIVER SUPPORT.

(a) The state shall establish a State Veteran Caregiver Support
    Program providing:
    (1) Respite care coordination and subsidies for family
        caregivers of aging veterans not enrolled in federal
        PCAFC;
    (2) A state tax credit of up to $3,000 annually for family
        members providing unpaid care to aging veterans; and
    (3) Caregiver training programs through the state community
        college system.
(b) The [State Department of Veterans Affairs] shall maintain a
    caregiver resource hotline and online resource directory.

SECTION 4. ELDER VETERAN FINANCIAL PROTECTIONS.

(a) It shall be a violation of the [State Consumer Protection Act]
    for any person to charge a fee for assistance with the
    preparation or submission of an application for VA pension or
    Aid and Attendance benefits, unless such person is accredited
    by the VA.
(b) The [State Attorney General] shall have enforcement authority
    over this section, with penalties of not more than $10,000 per
    violation.
(c) The [State Securities Regulator] shall monitor financial
    planning services marketed to aging veterans and take
    enforcement action against predatory asset restructuring.

SECTION 5. AGING-IN-PLACE SUPPORT.

(a) The [State Housing Finance Agency] shall establish a Veteran
    Home Modification Program providing grants of up to $15,000
    for accessibility modifications to the homes of veterans age
    65 and older.
(b) Eligible modifications shall include wheelchair ramps,
    bathroom safety features, stair lifts, widened doorways, and
    other accessibility improvements.

SECTION 6. SOCIAL ISOLATION PREVENTION.

(a) The [State Aging Agency] shall include aging veterans as a
    priority population in all Older Americans Act service plans.
(b) Area Agencies on Aging shall coordinate with County Veterans
    Service Officers to identify and serve isolated aging veterans.
(c) The state shall provide grants of up to $25,000 to community
    organizations that establish or expand social programs for
    aging veterans.
```

**Adaptations**: States with large aging veteran populations (Florida, California, Texas, Arizona, Pennsylvania) should prioritize Sections 2 and 5. States with weak consumer protection frameworks should prioritize Section 4. All states should implement Sections 3 and 6.

## Regulatory Framework

### PCAFC Eligibility and Assessment Standards

**Existing Authority**: 38 U.S.C. Section 1720G authorizes the Secretary to establish PCAFC eligibility criteria and assessment processes.

**Proposed Regulation**:

The Secretary should promulgate a rule amending 38 C.F.R. Part 71 to:

1. **Expand eligible conditions**: Include age-related functional decline in veterans with any service-connected condition, not only those with "serious injury" as currently interpreted
2. **Streamline assessment**: Replace the current multi-visit clinical assessment with a single-visit evaluation using standardized functional assessment tools (e.g., Katz ADL Index, Lawton IADL Scale)
3. **Establish tiered support**: Implement a three-tier stipend system matching support intensity to care needs
4. **Create appeals process**: Establish an independent clinical review panel for PCAFC denials, with 60-day decision requirement

### VA-Medicare Coordination Standards

**Existing Authority**: 38 U.S.C. Section 8111 authorizes the Secretary to coordinate with other federal healthcare programs.

**Proposed Administrative Action**: The Secretary and CMS Administrator should jointly issue guidance requiring:

1. Electronic health information exchange between VA and Medicare providers treating the same veteran
2. Joint medication reconciliation for dual-enrolled veterans with 5+ medications
3. Notification protocols when a dual-enrolled veteran is hospitalized in either system
4. Shared care planning for veterans with complex geriatric conditions

### CLC Quality and Staffing Standards

**Existing Authority**: 38 U.S.C. Section 1710A authorizes the Secretary to establish quality standards for VA long-term care.

**Proposed Regulation**: The Secretary should promulgate a rule establishing:

1. Minimum staffing ratios of 4.1 nursing hours per resident day at all CLCs
2. Mandatory dementia care training for all CLC staff
3. Annual quality benchmarking against CMS 5-star standards
4. Public reporting of CLC-level quality data

## Legal Considerations

### Constitutional Issues

- **Spending power**: All proposed programs are authorized expenditures under Congress's spending power and the established federal obligation to provide veteran benefits. The spending levels are proportionate to the documented need.
- **Commerce Clause**: Federal anti-predator provisions regulating financial services marketed to veterans are supportable under the Commerce Clause as applied to commercial activity.
- **Due process**: PCAFC appeals reform strengthens, rather than weakens, due process protections for veteran caregivers.

### Preemption Questions

- **State consumer protection**: Federal anti-predator provisions for A&A applications supplement but do not preempt state consumer protection laws. States retain concurrent enforcement authority.
- **Medicaid interaction**: Expanded VA home-based care interacts with Medicaid HCBS waiver programs. Legislation should clarify that VA-funded HCBS does not reduce Medicaid eligibility or benefits.
- **Medicare coordination**: VA-Medicare coordination protocols must comply with HIPAA and the Privacy Act while enabling information sharing necessary for patient safety.

### Enforcement Mechanisms

- **Annual Congressional reporting**: Creates transparency and accountability for geriatric care metrics
- **GAO evaluation**: Biennial independent evaluation ensures objective assessment of program outcomes
- **Quality-tied per diem**: State Veterans Home per diem payments linked to quality standards create financial incentives for care improvement
- **Civil penalties**: Anti-predator provisions create deterrent through financial penalties
- **VA OIG oversight**: Inspector General retains authority to audit all programs

## Loopholes, Shortcomings, and Rectification

### Aging Veterans Comprehensive Care Act

#### Potential Loopholes

| Loophole | Description | Severity |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| PCAFC tiered gaming | Veterans or caregivers may exaggerate care needs to qualify for higher tier stipends | Medium |
| HBPC geographic cream-skimming | VA could prioritize HBPC expansion in easy-to-serve urban areas while leaving rural areas underserved | High |
| CLC modernization without capacity | VA could modernize existing facilities without adding the 3,000 new beds required, claiming modernization satisfies the law | Medium |
| Per diem increase without quality | States could collect increased per diem without investing in quality improvements if enforcement is weak | High |
| Anti-predator evasion | Financial planners could restructure services to avoid "fee for application assistance" language while continuing exploitative practices | Medium |
| Workforce investment without retention | VA could fund training positions but fail to retain graduates if compensation and working conditions do not improve | Medium |

#### Shortcomings

| Issue | Impact | Root Cause |
|-------|--------|------------|
| No long-term care financing reform | Veterans still face catastrophic long-term care costs beyond VA coverage | Long-term care financing is a national crisis beyond VA scope |
| VA-Medicare integration incomplete | Dual-eligible veterans continue to receive fragmented care | CMS and VA are separate cabinet agencies with different missions |
| Geriatric workforce takes years to build | Short-term capacity gaps persist during pipeline development | Medical education timelines are structurally long |
| Caregiver support still reaches fraction of need | Even at 200,000 PCAFC enrollees, 95% of caregivers remain outside formal support | Full caregiver support would require national caregiving policy |
| Post-9/11 TBI-dementia wave not fully addressed | Current legislation focuses on Vietnam-era aging; post-9/11 aging begins in 2040s | Political attention focused on current crisis, not future one |

#### Rectification Procedures

1. **PCAFC tier verification**: Require annual functional reassessment for all PCAFC participants, conducted by assessors independent of the enrolled veteran's treatment team, with random audit of 10% of cases
2. **HBPC geographic equity**: Require that HBPC expansion achieve proportional coverage across urban, suburban, and rural areas, with annual reporting by geographic classification
3. **CLC capacity enforcement**: Amend Section 8 to require that CLC bed additions be certified as "net new capacity" separate from modernization, with GAO verification
4. **State Veterans Home quality enforcement**: Require states to submit annual quality improvement plans tied to per diem increases; reduce per diem by 10% for homes below 3-star for 2+ consecutive years
5. **Anti-predator broadening**: Amend Section 11(c) to prohibit any compensation arrangement in connection with VA pension applications, except for VA-accredited agents, attorneys, and claims agents; include disgorgement of fees
6. **Workforce retention incentives**: Add graduated retention bonuses (25% at year 5, 50% at year 8, 100% at year 10) beyond the 4-year loan repayment obligation period

### General Implementation Concerns

#### Systemic Issues

| Issue | Proposed Solution |
|-------|------------------|
| VA construction delays | Authorize design-build contracting for CLC modernization; set 3-year construction timelines with Congressional notification for delays |
| Interagency coordination weak | Codify VA-CMS coordination council with statutory reporting requirements |
| Budget competition within VA | Establish geriatric care as a separate budget line item with minimum funding floors |
| Data lag prevents real-time management | Invest in real-time geriatric care dashboards tracking capacity, waitlists, and outcomes |

#### Sunset and Review Provisions

- **Year 3**: GAO interim evaluation of HBPC expansion, PCAFC reform, and cognitive screening programs
- **Year 5**: Comprehensive GAO evaluation of all programs; Secretary reports to Congress with recommendations for modification
- **Year 8**: Congressional reauthorization hearing with updated cost-benefit analysis; evaluation of whether post-9/11 aging planning is adequate
- **Year 10**: Authorization expires unless reauthorized; all programs subject to evaluation
