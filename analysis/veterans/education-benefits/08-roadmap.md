# Education Benefits: Roadmap

## Phase 1: Immediate Actions (0-12 Months)

### Administrative Reforms (No Legislation Required)

**Veteran Protection**

- Issue VA directive requiring pre-enrollment counseling for veterans seeking to use GI Bill at schools on the risk watchlist
- Expand GI Bill Comparison Tool with mandatory school outcome data (graduation rates, employment, debt)
- Increase SAA inspection frequency for for-profit schools using existing authority
- Launch education counselor training program for VA regional offices

**Payment Processing**

- Implement advance housing allowance payment for first month of enrollment
- Deploy automated enrollment verification with participating schools
- Establish error resolution hotline for student veterans with payment issues
- Publish quarterly payment accuracy reports

**Benefit Information**

- Launch national awareness campaign on GI Bill comparison tools
- Partner with TAP to improve education counseling during transition
- Create veteran-facing mobile app with benefit tracking and school comparison
- Publish annual GI Bill outcomes report disaggregated by school type

### Advocacy and Coalition Building

**Build Support**

- Convene SVA, VES, and large VSOs for legislative priority alignment
- Brief Congressional VA committees on for-profit school outcome data
- Engage bipartisan Congressional champions for GI Bill modernization
- Develop talking points connecting reform to fiscal responsibility (not just veteran protection)

## Phase 2: Short-Term Goals (1-2 Years)

### Legislative Priorities

**GI Bill Modernization Act**

- Eliminate online student housing allowance penalty (housing parity)
- Expand non-degree training coverage (certifications, bootcamps, apprenticeships)
- Increase SAA funding to $75 million annually
- Extend STEM scholarship to additional fields
- Eliminate pre-2013 delimiting dates
- Establish advance payment requirement

**Veteran Education Protection Act**

- Outcome-based eligibility standards (graduation, employment, debt-to-earnings)
- Mandatory pre-enrollment counseling for high-risk schools
- School closure protection (entitlement restoration, credit recovery)
- Strengthened SAA enforcement authority
- Enhanced interagency data sharing (VA, DoE, DoL)

**Target: 119th-120th Congress (2025-2028)**

### IT Modernization

**Payment System Upgrade**

- Automate enrollment verification through school data partnerships
- Real-time payment tracking for veterans
- Automated recalculation for enrollment changes
- Integration with VA mobile app
- Estimated cost: $300-500 million over 2 years

### Oversight Infrastructure

**SAA Expansion**

- Hire 200+ additional SAA compliance specialists nationwide
- Standardize inspection protocols and training
- Deploy risk-based oversight model (focus resources on highest-risk schools)
- Create rapid response teams for school closures
- Establish interagency task force with DoE for for-profit school oversight

## Phase 3: Medium-Term Goals (2-4 Years)

### System Transformation

**Outcome-Based Accountability Operational**

- All GI Bill-eligible schools reporting standardized outcome data
- First probationary actions taken against schools failing minimum thresholds
- Schools losing eligibility for persistent poor outcomes
- Veterans redirected to higher-performing schools
- Outcome data published and accessible through GI Bill Comparison Tool

**Non-Degree Training Fully Integrated**

- Certification and bootcamp coverage operational
- Apprenticeship stipend structure reformed (flat rate, not declining)
- Microcredential coverage in place
- Employer-validated credential list maintained and updated
- Non-degree training enrollment reaching 20%+ of GI Bill users

**Credential Translation Advancing**

- National military-to-civilian credential database operational
- 500+ schools participating in binding credit acceptance agreements
- Bridge programs developed for highest-volume MOSs
- Licensing body partnerships reducing redundant training

### Payment System Performance

- Processing errors below $200 million annually (from ~$550 million)
- Average initial claims processing time below 15 days
- Zero veterans experiencing payment gaps exceeding 30 days at enrollment
- Overpayment collection for VA errors eliminated

## Phase 4: Long-Term Transformation (5+ Years)

### Full Benefit Modernization

**Education Savings Account Model**

- Veterans receive flexible education entitlement they can allocate across degrees, certifications, training, and credentials
- Entitlement is valued in dollars rather than months, adjusting for program type
- Veterans can use entitlement for multiple shorter programs or one longer program
- Unused entitlement does not expire for post-2013 separations

**Universal Quality Standards**

- All GI Bill-eligible schools meeting minimum outcome thresholds
- For-profit school share of GI Bill spending reflects outcome quality, not marketing
- Information parity: veterans choosing schools have full access to outcome data
- Credential value transparency across all program types

**Seamless Transition**

- Military-to-civilian credential translation automatic for all MOSs
- Education planning begins during active service (not just at transition)
- VA, DoD, and DoE systems fully integrated for benefit delivery
- Student veteran support services standard at all major campuses

## Risks and Contingencies

### Risk: For-Profit Industry Blocks Legislation

**Threat**: Industry lobbying prevents passage of outcome-based standards or oversight expansion

**Mitigation**

- Bipartisan framing (fiscal responsibility + veteran protection)
- Veteran testimony and storytelling campaigns
- Media partnerships for investigative reporting on worst-performing schools
- Coalition with consumer advocacy organizations

**Contingency**: Pursue maximum administrative reform without legislation; use Executive Orders for VA internal reforms

### Risk: IT Modernization Delays

**Threat**: VA payment system modernization is delayed or fails (common in federal IT projects)

**Mitigation**

- Modular, incremental development (not Big Bang replacement)
- Use proven commercial platforms where possible
- Independent technical oversight
- Pilot with volunteer schools before full rollout

**Contingency**: Maintain manual processing capability; prioritize automated enrollment verification as first module

### Risk: SAA Resistance or Capture

**Threat**: State Approving Agencies resist federal standards or are captured by schools they oversee

**Mitigation**

- Federal training and certification standards for SAA staff
- Conflict of interest rules for SAA personnel
- VA oversight of SAA performance with funding contingent on compliance
- Anonymous school compliance reporting channel

**Contingency**: Create federal backup inspection capability for states failing to meet standards

### Risk: Benefit Expansion Without Quality Improvement

**Threat**: Expanding benefits (housing parity, non-degree coverage) without quality standards funnels more money to poor-performing schools

**Mitigation**

- Sequence quality reforms before or alongside benefit expansion
- Tie expanded benefits to outcome thresholds
- Maintain and strengthen 90/10 rule enforcement
- Require outcome reporting for all newly eligible programs

**Contingency**: Sunset expansion provisions if outcome data does not improve within 3 years

### Risk: Political Reversal

**Threat**: New administration or Congress weakens oversight or reverses reforms

**Mitigation**

- Build reforms into statute (harder to undo than regulation)
- Create veteran constituency that benefits from and defends reforms
- Bipartisan legislation more durable than party-line action
- Publish transparent outcome data that makes reversal politically costly

## Success Metrics

### Leading Indicators

- GI Bill Comparison Tool usage rates (quarterly)
- Pre-enrollment counseling completion rates (quarterly)
- SAA inspection frequency and findings (semi-annual)
- Payment processing time and accuracy (monthly)
- School enrollment patterns by type (semester)

### Lagging Indicators

- GI Bill graduation rates by school type (annual)
- Post-graduation employment and earnings (annual)
- For-profit school share of GI Bill spending (annual)
- Student veteran satisfaction (annual)
- GI Bill ROI analysis (biennial)
