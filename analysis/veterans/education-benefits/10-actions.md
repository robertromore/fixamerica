# Education Benefits: Actions

## What You Can Do

Improving GI Bill education benefits requires sustained public engagement. Veterans deserve an education system that honors their service with genuine opportunity -- not one that allows predatory schools to profit from their sacrifice. The following actions are organized by audience and level of effort.

---

## For Veterans Considering Using the GI Bill

### Before You Enroll

**Research Schools Using the GI Bill Comparison Tool**

- Visit VA.gov/gi-bill-comparison-tool to compare schools by graduation rates, complaint histories, and cost coverage
- Check whether your school participates in the Yellow Ribbon Program if it is a private institution
- Compare outcomes at for-profit schools against community colleges and public universities offering similar programs
- Look up your school's cohort default rate on the Department of Education's College Scorecard

**Request Education Counseling**

- Contact a VA education counselor (available at VA regional offices and online) before enrolling
- Work with a Veterans Service Organization (VSO) representative to understand your benefit options
- Attend a Transition Assistance Program (TAP) education track if you are currently separating from service
- Ask about the differences between Chapter 33, Chapter 30, and other programs before making an irrevocable election

**Evaluate Your Options Carefully**

- Compare total cost of attendance (not just tuition) against your GI Bill benefit level
- Check whether your military training qualifies for academic credit through ACE credit recommendations
- Ask schools directly how many ACE-recommended credits they accept for your military occupational specialty
- Consider whether a public university or community college offers the same credential at lower cost with better outcomes than a for-profit alternative
- If a school's recruiter promises guaranteed job placement or salary, request written documentation and verify independently

**Understand Your Benefits**

- Know your eligibility tier (based on length of active duty service)
- Understand the housing allowance structure: full BAH rate for in-person full-time; prorated for part-time; reduced rate for exclusively online
- Know your delimiting date (when benefits expire) -- the Forever GI Bill eliminated this for post-2013 separations
- Understand transferability rules if you are considering transferring benefits to a spouse or dependent

### While You Are Enrolled

**Track Your Benefits and Payments**

- Monitor your remaining entitlement months through VA.gov
- Report enrollment changes promptly to avoid overpayments and debt collection
- Contact the GI Bill hotline (1-888-442-4551) if payments are late or incorrect
- Keep records of all enrollment certifications and payment receipts

**Report Problems**

- File complaints about your school through the VA GI Bill Feedback System at VA.gov
- Report aggressive or misleading recruitment to Veterans Education Success (vetsedsuccess.org)
- Contact your Congressional representative if VA payment issues are not resolved through normal channels
- If your school closes mid-term, contact VA immediately about entitlement restoration

**Maximize Your Benefit**

- Use tutorial assistance ($100/month, up to $1,200 total) if you need academic support
- Apply for the STEM Scholarship extension if pursuing a STEM degree and running out of entitlement
- Explore the VET-TEC program for technology training opportunities
- Connect with your campus veteran student services office or VetSuccess on Campus counselor

## For All Citizens

### Educate Yourself

**Understand the Stakes**

- The GI Bill is the primary mechanism by which military service translates into long-term economic opportunity
- For-profit colleges receive ~$3.3 billion annually in GI Bill funds while producing graduation rates below 25%
- Black veterans are disproportionately enrolled at for-profit schools (29% vs. 18% overall)
- The original GI Bill returned $7 for every $1 invested; protecting that return on investment benefits all taxpayers

**Know the Key Reform Needs**

- Close remaining loopholes that allow predatory schools to target veterans
- Equalize housing allowances for online and in-person students
- Expand non-degree training benefits for certifications, apprenticeships, and bootcamps
- Modernize payment processing to eliminate errors and delays
- Improve credential translation so veterans do not repeat training they already completed in the military

### Advocate for Reform

**Contact Your Members of Congress**

- Call or write your Representative and Senators about GI Bill modernization
- Specific asks are more effective than general support:
    - Support legislation to equalize online student housing allowances
    - Fund State Approving Agency oversight at adequate levels ($75 million/year)
    - Require outcome-based eligibility standards for schools receiving GI Bill funds
    - Expand non-degree training coverage under the GI Bill
    - Support credential translation initiatives between military and civilian systems
- Ask candidates their position on for-profit college oversight and veteran education protection

**Support Advocacy Organizations**

- Donate to or volunteer with organizations protecting veteran students:
    - Student Veterans of America (SVA): Campus chapters, research, and advocacy
    - Veterans Education Success (VES): Investigative work on predatory school practices
    - Iraq and Afghanistan Veterans of America (IAVA): Broad veteran policy advocacy
- Share investigative reporting on for-profit school targeting through social media and community networks

**Vote and Participate**

- Veterans' education issues enjoy bipartisan support; hold candidates from both parties accountable for specific commitments
- Support candidates who commit to adequate GI Bill funding and oversight
- Attend town halls and ask about veteran education policy

### Community Actions

**Help Veterans in Your Community**

- Connect veterans you know with GI Bill education counseling resources
- Share information about the GI Bill Comparison Tool and school research resources
- Help veteran family members understand transferability options
- If you work in higher education, advocate for veteran-friendly campus policies

**Engage Employers**

- Employers who hire veterans can support education benefit usage through flexible scheduling
- Encourage HR departments to understand GI Bill benefits and apprenticeship programs
- Provide veteran employees time off for school enrollment and VA education counseling appointments
- Offer tuition assistance programs that complement rather than replace GI Bill benefits

## For Veterans Service Organizations

### Strengthen Education Advocacy

- Track GI Bill outcomes at schools in your region and report findings publicly
- Provide education counseling as part of claims assistance services
- Train representatives on GI Bill program differences and school research
- Partner with Veterans Education Success on predatory school identification

### Policy Advocacy

- Coordinate with SVA and VES on legislative priorities for GI Bill modernization
- Testify before Congressional committees on veteran education experiences
- Publish reports on for-profit school outcomes for GI Bill students
- Advocate for SAA funding increases and stronger enforcement authority

### Member Engagement

- Educate members about their education benefit options and how to research schools
- Provide regular updates on GI Bill policy changes and legislative proposals
- Mobilize members for Congressional contact campaigns on key education votes
- Survey members on education benefit experiences to build evidence for reform

---

## For Educators and Institutions

### Support Student Veterans

- Establish or strengthen campus veteran student services offices
- Apply for VetSuccess on Campus (VSOC) counselor placement if enrollment exceeds 50 GI Bill students
- Create peer mentoring programs matching senior student veterans with new enrollees
- Provide academic advisors trained in military experience and credential translation
- Accept ACE credit recommendations for military training wherever academically appropriate

### Institutional Accountability

- Participate in the Yellow Ribbon Program to cover costs above the GI Bill cap
- Publish veteran-specific graduation and employment outcomes data
- Report honestly on the VA GI Bill Comparison Tool
- Ensure recruitment practices comply with the Principles of Excellence
- Avoid disproportionate spending on veteran recruitment relative to veteran student services

---

## For Policymakers

### Executive Actions (No Legislation Required)

- Expand VA GI Bill Comparison Tool with mandatory school outcome data
- Issue directive requiring pre-enrollment counseling for veterans seeking to enroll at high-risk schools
- Increase SAA inspection frequency for for-profit schools using existing authority
- Implement advance housing allowance payment for first month of enrollment
- Strengthen enforcement of the reformed 90/10 rule

### Legislative Actions

- Pass GI Bill modernization legislation equalizing online housing allowances
- Fund SAA oversight at $75 million annually (triple current levels)
- Establish outcome-based eligibility standards for GI Bill participation
- Expand non-degree training coverage (certifications, bootcamps, apprenticeships)
- Eliminate pre-2013 delimiting dates for all veterans
- Fund credential translation initiatives between DoD, VA, and civilian education systems

### Oversight Actions

- Require annual VA reporting on GI Bill outcomes disaggregated by school type
- Conduct regular hearings on for-profit school oversight and enforcement
- Demand implementation of outstanding GAO and OIG recommendations on education benefits
- Monitor 90/10 rule enforcement and for-profit school compliance
