# Education Benefits: Solutions

## Guiding Principles

### Criteria for Evaluating Solutions

| Criterion | Definition | Why It Matters |
|-----------|------------|----------------|
| **Outcome-focused** | Solutions should improve graduation, employment, and earnings | Benefits exist to produce opportunity, not just enrollment |
| **Veteran-centered** | Benefits should serve veterans' actual educational needs | Modern learners need flexible, diverse benefit structures |
| **Fraud-resistant** | System should protect against waste and predatory practices | $13+ billion in annual spending demands accountability |
| **Equitable** | Benefits should serve all veterans fairly regardless of background | Historical inequities (racial, geographic) must be addressed |
| **Cost-effective** | Maximize return on taxpayer investment | GI Bill ROI depends on quality outcomes |
| **Simple** | Benefits should be easy to understand and access | Complexity harms the veterans who need help most |

## Policy Proposals

### Tier 1: Protect Veterans from Predatory Schools

#### Outcome-Based Eligibility Standards

**What It Does**

Conditions GI Bill eligibility on schools meeting minimum outcome thresholds, rather than relying solely on accreditation and SAA approval.

**Specific Components**

- Minimum graduation rate threshold: schools must achieve at least 30% completion rate for GI Bill students (below the national average but above the worst performers)
- Minimum employment rate: 50%+ of graduates employed in a related field within 12 months
- Maximum student debt-to-earnings ratio: median debt-to-earnings ratio below 1.5:1
- Cohort default rate below 15% (3-year)
- Schools failing to meet thresholds enter probationary status with 2-year improvement window
- Schools failing probation lose GI Bill eligibility

**Evidence**

- Schools with the lowest graduation rates receive billions in GI Bill funds; outcome-based standards would redirect this spending (SVA NVEST data, 2024)
- Similar outcome-based standards have been proposed for all federal financial aid (Gainful Employment rule) with demonstrated effectiveness at identifying poor-performing programs
- Veterans who attend schools meeting these thresholds earn $15,000+ more annually than those at failing schools (Georgetown CEW, 2023)

**Implementation Requirements**

- School outcome data collection infrastructure (partially exists through NCES and VA)
- Phase-in period (2-3 years) to allow schools to improve
- Exemptions for schools with small GI Bill populations (avoid statistical noise)
- Appeals process for schools placed on probation

#### Strengthen State Approving Agencies

**What It Does**

Provides adequate funding, staffing, and authority for SAAs to function as effective gatekeepers.

**Specific Components**

- Triple SAA funding (from ~$25 million to ~$75 million annually)
- Require annual compliance inspections for all GI Bill-eligible for-profit schools
- Grant SAAs authority to issue binding compliance orders (not just recommendations)
- Standardize SAA training and certification nationwide
- Create interagency data sharing between VA, DoE, and SAAs
- Establish rapid response teams for school closures (protecting students)

#### Mandatory Pre-Enrollment Counseling

**What It Does**

Requires veterans to receive education counseling before enrolling at high-risk schools, while preserving school choice.

**Specific Components**

- Pre-enrollment counseling session (in-person, virtual, or phone) for veterans seeking to use GI Bill at schools on the VA's risk watchlist
- Counselors provide school outcome data, comparison with alternative schools, and financial impact analysis
- Veterans may still enroll at any eligible school after receiving counseling
- Counseling does not delay enrollment by more than 10 business days
- VA trains and certifies education counselors at all regional offices and major installations

### Tier 2: Modernize Benefit Structure

#### Housing Allowance Parity for Online Students

**What It Does**

Eliminates the housing allowance penalty for exclusively online students, providing the same BAH-based housing allowance regardless of delivery format.

**Specific Components**

- Online students receive housing allowance based on their home ZIP code (not school ZIP code)
- Full BAH rate for full-time enrollment; prorated for part-time
- Applies to all GI Bill programs with housing components
- Estimated additional cost: $500-800 million annually (based on online enrollment trends)

**Evidence**

- COVID-19 temporary parity demonstrated no increase in fraud or abuse
- Online students have the same housing costs as in-person students
- The penalty discourages disabled, rural, and caregiving veterans from flexible learning
- Many high-quality programs (including major university online degrees) are penalized

#### Expand Non-Degree Training Benefits

**What It Does**

Modernizes GI Bill to fully support vocational training, apprenticeships, certifications, and short-term credentials alongside traditional degree programs.

**Specific Components**

- Flat-rate housing allowance for apprentices (eliminate declining stipend)
- Coverage for industry-recognized certifications (CompTIA, AWS, PMP, etc.) with flexible entitlement usage
- Short-term program coverage (programs as short as 8 weeks) for high-value credentials
- Coding bootcamp and technology training eligibility expansion (build on VET-TEC)
- Microcredential coverage for stackable credentials that build toward degrees
- Employer-validated credential list maintained by VA in partnership with DoL

**Evidence**

- Apprenticeship completers earn $72,000 median annually (DoL, 2024)
- Short-term certifications in IT, healthcare, and skilled trades produce rapid employment at living wages
- Veterans in apprenticeship programs have 94% completion rates when adequately supported (DoL, 2024)
- The labor market increasingly values specific skills over degrees

#### Entitlement Flexibility

**What It Does**

Allows veterans to use their 36 months of entitlement more flexibly, accommodating career changes, graduate education, and non-linear educational paths.

**Specific Components**

- Allow entitlement months to be used for multiple programs or degrees (currently permitted but poorly communicated)
- Extend entitlement to 48 months for STEM and healthcare programs (building on existing STEM scholarship)
- Allow partial-month entitlement usage for short-term programs (currently minimum is one month)
- Create "education savings account" model allowing veterans to allocate entitlement across training, education, and credentialing
- Eliminate irrevocable election requirement (allow veterans to switch between Chapter 30 and 33 based on which is more beneficial)

### Tier 3: Improve Delivery and Outcomes

#### Payment Modernization

**What It Does**

Modernizes VA's payment processing to eliminate the delays, overpayments, and errors that create financial hardship for student veterans.

**Specific Components**

- Advance housing allowance payment at enrollment (first month paid before classes begin)
- Automated enrollment verification through school registrar data sharing
- Real-time payment tracking through VA mobile app
- Eliminate overpayment debt collection for VA processing errors (veteran is not at fault)
- Automatic recalculation when enrollment changes (no manual claim required)
- Estimated savings: $200-300 million annually from reduced error correction costs

#### Credential Translation Initiative

**What It Does**

Creates a systematic pathway for translating military training and experience into academic credit and civilian credentials.

**Specific Components**

- Expand ACE credit recommendations with binding agreements from participating schools
- Create national database of military-to-civilian credential equivalencies
- Fund "bridge programs" that fill specific gaps between military training and civilian credentials
- Require schools receiving GI Bill funds to accept ACE credit recommendations or provide written justification for rejection
- Partner with licensing bodies to create military experience pathways to civilian licenses
- DoD-VA-DoE interagency working group on credential translation

**Evidence**

- Veterans who receive credit for military training complete degrees 15-20% faster (ACE, 2024)
- Faster completion saves entitlement months and reduces program costs
- Many veterans retake courses they already mastered in military training, wasting time and benefits

#### Student Veteran Support Services

**What It Does**

Funds comprehensive support services for student veterans at educational institutions, going beyond VetSuccess on Campus (VSOC).

**Specific Components**

- Expand VSOC from ~100 campuses to all schools with 50+ GI Bill students
- Peer mentoring programs matching senior student veterans with new enrollees
- Dedicated veteran mental health counselors at high-enrollment campuses
- Academic advising with military experience awareness
- Family support services for student veterans with dependents
- Career services tailored to veteran skills and experience

## Implementation Priorities

### Sequencing

| Priority | Timeframe | Rationale |
|----------|-----------|-----------|
| Pre-enrollment counseling | 0-12 months | Administrative action; immediate veteran protection |
| Online housing parity | 0-12 months | Requires legislation but straightforward |
| SAA funding increase | 0-12 months | Appropriations action; immediate oversight improvement |
| Payment modernization | 1-2 years | IT investment with clear cost savings |
| Outcome-based eligibility | 1-3 years | Requires data infrastructure and phase-in period |
| Non-degree training expansion | 1-2 years | Requires legislation; growing demand |
| Credential translation | 2-4 years | Interagency coordination; institutional agreements |
| Student support services | 1-3 years | Campus-level implementation |

| Payment modernization | $300-500 million (IT) | $200-300 million annually in error reduction | VA OIG |
| Pre-enrollment counseling | $50-100 million | Redirect veterans from poor-performing schools | VES estimates |
| Non-degree training expansion | $500 million-$1 billion | Faster employment; higher earnings | DoL data |
