# Veteran Housing: Stakeholders

## Overview

Veteran housing involves a complex network of federal agencies, state and local governments, nonprofit organizations, private-sector partners, and advocacy groups. The stakeholder landscape is characterized by overlapping jurisdictions, fragmented funding streams, and varying levels of commitment to veteran-specific housing outcomes.

---

## Primary Stakeholders

### Veterans and Military Families

| Group | Population | Key Interests | Influence Level |
|-------|------------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Homeless veterans** | ~35,574 (PIT count) | Immediate housing, services, dignity | Low--dispersed, often disconnected from advocacy |
| **Veterans at risk of homelessness** | Estimated 1.5 million | Prevention services, financial assistance, employment | Low-Medium--often invisible until crisis |
| **Veteran homeowners** | ~12 million | VA loan access, foreclosure prevention, equity building | Medium--large but diffuse constituency |
| **Veteran renters** | ~4 million | Affordability, stability, anti-discrimination | Low-Medium--less politically organized |
| **Women veterans** | ~2.1 million | Gender-specific housing, family-friendly programs | Growing--increasing advocacy voice |
| **Disabled veterans** | ~5.4 million | Accessible housing, SAH grants, accommodations | High--strong advocacy through DAV and VSOs |
| **Aging veterans** | ~8.5 million (65+) | Aging in place, accessibility, affordability | Medium--AARP and VSO advocacy |
| **Military families in transition** | ~200,000 annually | Housing search support, BAH-to-civilian bridge | Medium--brief window of high need |

## Government Stakeholders

### Federal Agencies

| Agency | Role | Budget/Resources | Effectiveness |
|--------|------|------------------|---------------|
| **Department of Veterans Affairs** | SSVF, GPD, SAH, Home Loan, case management | ~$1 billion+ for homeless programs; self-funded home loan | Mixed--strong programs, implementation gaps |
| **Department of Housing and Urban Development** | HUD-VASH vouchers, public housing, CDBG | ~$500 million for HUD-VASH | Mixed--voucher underutilization; bureaucratic |
| **Department of Defense** | BAH, military housing, transition housing | Part of defense budget | Weak--responsibility ends at separation |
| **USDA Rural Development** | Rural housing loans and grants | Limited veteran-specific focus | Weak--minimal veteran targeting |
| **Federal Housing Finance Agency** | Fannie Mae, Freddie Mac, Federal Home Loan Banks | Regulatory authority | Indirect--sets market conditions for VA loans |
| **United States Interagency Council on Homelessness** | Federal coordination on homelessness | Coordination role; limited direct funding | Important--strategy and coordination |

### Congressional Committees

| Committee | Jurisdiction | Key Role |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| **Senate Veterans' Affairs Committee** | VA programs, SSVF, GPD, Home Loan | Authorization and oversight |
| **House Veterans' Affairs Committee** | VA programs | Authorization and oversight |
| **Senate Banking Committee** | HUD programs, housing finance | HUD-VASH authorization |
| **House Financial Services Committee** | HUD programs, housing finance | HUD-VASH authorization |
| **Senate/House Appropriations (MILCON-VA)** | VA funding | Direct funding authority |
| **Senate/House Appropriations (THUD)** | HUD funding | HUD-VASH voucher funding |

### State and Local Government

| Level | Role | Variation |
|-------|------|-----------|
| **State housing finance agencies** | State housing programs, tax credits | Varies--some target veterans, most do not |
| **Continuums of Care (CoCs)** | Local homelessness coordination | Varies dramatically by community capacity |
| **Local housing authorities** | HUD-VASH administration, public housing | Some prioritize veterans; many do not |
| **Mayors and city councils** | Local housing policy, zoning, land use | Key for supportive housing siting |
| **State veterans affairs departments** | State veteran housing programs | Ranges from robust to minimal |

## Decision Makers

### Key Decision Points

| Decision | Decision Maker | Factors Considered |
|----------|---------------|--------------------|
| **HUD-VASH voucher allocation** | HUD Secretary, in consultation with VA | Community need, utilization rates, PIT count data |
| **SSVF grant awards** | VA Secretary (delegated to program office) | Geographic coverage, performance, need |
| **VA Home Loan policy** | VA Secretary, Loan Guaranty Service | Market conditions, default rates, fee structure |
| **Funding fee levels** | Congress | Revenue needs, veteran access, program costs |
| **SAH grant amounts** | Congress (set by statute) | Construction costs, inflation, budget |
| **Zoning and land use** | Local governments | Community preferences, housing needs, political dynamics |
| **Fair Market Rent levels** | HUD Secretary | Market surveys, budget implications |
| **Built for Zero participation** | Local community leadership | Resources, commitment, political will |

## Coalition Dynamics

### Natural Alliances

| Coalition | Shared Interest | Strength |
|-----------|----------------|----------|
| **VSOs + housing advocates** | Expanded veteran housing programs | Strong--complementary expertise |
| **VA + HUD** | HUD-VASH implementation | Moderate--interagency friction but shared mission |
| **SSVF grantees + CoCs** | Coordinated entry and rapid re-housing | Growing--local coordination improving |
| **VA lenders + veteran homebuyers** | VA loan access and utilization | Strong--commercial alignment |
| **DAV + accessible housing advocates** | SAH expansion, accessibility standards | Strong--overlapping constituencies |

### Tension Points

| Stakeholders in Tension | Issue | Nature of Conflict |
|-------------------------|-------|-------------------|
| **Housing First advocates vs. treatment-first proponents** | Preconditions for housing | Philosophical and evidence-based disagreement |
| **VA vs. HUD** | HUD-VASH administration and voucher utilization | Bureaucratic turf; different agency cultures |
| **Communities vs. supportive housing developers** | NIMBY opposition to veteran housing projects | Local resistance to supportive housing siting |
| **VA Home Loan vs. conventional lenders** | Market competition; appraisal standards | Competitive dynamics; seller reluctance on VA offers |
| **Transitional housing vs. rapid re-housing** | GPD model vs. SSVF model | Funding competition; philosophical differences |
| **Rural vs. urban** | Resource allocation | Urban areas receive disproportionate program funding |
