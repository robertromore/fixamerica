# Veteran Housing: Implementation Roadmap

## Overview

This roadmap outlines a phased implementation strategy for transforming veteran housing policy over a 10-year period. The phases are organized by feasibility, cost, and dependency relationships, with quick wins prioritized in Phase 1 to build momentum and demonstrate results before pursuing more ambitious structural reforms. The roadmap assumes sustained bipartisan commitment to veteran housing--historically a reliable assumption, given that veteran homelessness reduction has enjoyed support across administrations.

## Phase 1: Immediate Actions (Years 1-2)

### Legislative Priorities

| Action | Vehicle | Status | Champion |
|--------|---------|--------|----------|
| Enact source-of-income discrimination protection for HUD-VASH | Standalone bill or housing omnibus | Proposed in prior Congress; not enacted | Senate Banking + VA Committees |
| Reduce VA Home Loan funding fee for first-time users | VA appropriations rider or standalone | Conceptual | House VA Committee |
| Authorize Transitional Housing Allowance | NDAA amendment or standalone | Conceptual | Senate/House Armed Services + VA Committees |
| Mandate Housing First for all federally funded veteran programs | VA authorization bill | Partially implemented by policy; needs statutory codification | Senate VA Committee |

### Administrative Actions (No Legislation Required)

| Action | Responsible Office | Timeline | Cost |
|--------|-------------------|----------|------|
| Launch landlord incentive program for HUD-VASH | HUD Office of Public and Indian Housing | 3-6 months | $200M/yr |
| Implement Small Area FMRs for HUD-VASH in high-cost markets | HUD Secretary | 6-12 months | $150M/yr |
| Hire 500 housing navigators for HUD-VASH lease-up | VA Homeless Programs Office | 6-12 months | $50M/yr |
| Integrate housing stability screening into VA primary care | VHA Office of Primary Care | 6-12 months | $20M/yr |
| Expand SSVF prevention allocation from 40% to 60% | VA SSVF Program Office | 3-6 months | Reallocation |
| Fund 100 additional unsheltered veteran outreach teams | VA Homeless Programs Office | 6-12 months | $75M/yr |
| Streamline VA appraisal process (7-day target) | VA Loan Guaranty Service | 6-12 months | $10M one-time |
| Launch national real estate industry education campaign on VA loans | VA Loan Guaranty Service | 3-6 months | $10M/yr |

### Quick Wins

| Action | Impact | Timeline |
|--------|--------|----------|
| Require by-name lists of homeless veterans in all CoCs | Enables real-time tracking and targeted outreach | 90 days (HUD directive) |
| Expand SSVF legal services for eviction prevention | Prevents homelessness at fraction of crisis cost | 6 months (grant modification) |
| Mandate pre-purchase counseling for VA loan applicants | Reduces foreclosure risk 30-40% | 6 months (VA policy change) |
| Create VA Housing Stability Screening tool | Early identification of at-risk veterans | 6 months (clinical integration) |
| Establish VA Foreclosure Prevention Hotline | Direct assistance to veterans facing housing loss | 3 months (contract award) |

### Phase 1 Milestones

- [ ] HUD-VASH voucher utilization rate increased from 76% to 85%
- [ ] SSVF prevention allocation shifted to 60%
- [ ] By-name lists operational in all CoCs receiving HUD funding
- [ ] VA appraisal turnaround reduced to 7 business days
- [ ] Housing stability screening integrated into VA primary care
- [ ] 100 new unsheltered outreach teams deployed

## Phase 2: Infrastructure and Capacity Building (Years 3-5)

### Housing Supply and Program Expansion

| Action | Description | Cost | Timeline |
|--------|-------------|------|----------|
| Permanent supportive housing development (10,000 units) | Fund construction/rehabilitation of veteran PSH through LIHTC, HOME, and VA partnerships | $1.5B one-time + $200M/yr operating | Years 3-5 |
| Women veteran housing (2,500 units) | Gender-specific PSH with family and childcare services | $400M one-time + $100M/yr | Years 3-5 |
| Built for Zero expansion (200 additional communities) | Fund Community Solutions to scale methodology nationally | $75M/yr | Years 3-5 |
| GPD-to-PSH conversion (5,000 beds) | Convert underperforming transitional housing to permanent supportive housing | $200M one-time | Years 3-4 |
| HUD-VASH case management expansion (1,000 staff) | Reduce caseloads to 25:1 ratio | $100M/yr | Years 3-4 |

### Transition and Homeownership

| Action | Description | Cost | Timeline |
|--------|-------------|------|----------|
| Housing transition planning integration into TAP | 12-month housing preparation program for separating service members | $100M/yr | Years 3-4 |
| Transitional Housing Allowance launch | 6-month housing cost bridge for qualifying separating service members | $800M/yr | Year 3 (ongoing) |
| VA Home Buyer Education Portal | Interactive financial literacy and market analysis tools | $15M one-time + $5M/yr | Year 3 |
| VA funding fee reduction (first-time users) | Reduce from 2.15% to 1.0% for first-time VA loan users | $600M/yr (revenue offset) | Year 3 (ongoing) |

### Equity Initiatives

| Action | Description | Cost | Timeline |
|--------|-------------|------|----------|
| VA Office of Housing Equity | Dedicated office to monitor and address racial disparities | $20M/yr | Year 3 |
| Targeted outreach to minority veteran communities | Fund community-based organizations for housing navigation | $50M/yr | Years 3-5 |
| Aging-in-place grant program | Moderate home modifications (up to $10,000) for aging veterans | $150M/yr | Years 3-5 |
| Triple SAH grant funding | Reduce wait times from 3-6 months to under 60 days | $240M/yr (incremental) | Year 3 |

### Phase 2 Milestones

- [ ] 10,000 new permanent supportive housing units in development or completed
- [ ] 2,500 women veteran housing units in development or completed
- [ ] 300 communities participating in Built for Zero (up from 100+)
- [ ] Transitional Housing Allowance operational and serving 80,000+ separatees
- [ ] VA Home Loan funding fee reduced for first-time users
- [ ] VA Office of Housing Equity established and reporting on disparities
- [ ] HUD-VASH voucher utilization rate at 90%+
- [ ] Veteran homelessness PIT count reduced to 25,000 or below

## Phase 3: Systemic Transformation (Years 6-10)

### Structural Reforms

| Action | Description | Timeline |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| VA-HUD Joint Housing Office | Permanent interagency coordination body for all veteran housing programs | Year 6 |
| Unified veteran housing application | Single application for HUD-VASH, SSVF, GPD, and SAH | Year 7 |
| Federal source-of-income protection | National ban on landlord discrimination against voucher holders | Year 6-7 |
| Veteran preference in LIHTC | Statutory veteran preference for LIHTC developments near VA facilities | Year 6 |

### Scale-Up

| Action | Description | Cost | Timeline |
|--------|-------------|------|----------|
| Remaining PSH development (10,000 additional units) | Complete 20,000-unit target | $1.5B one-time + $200M/yr | Years 6-8 |
| Remaining women veteran housing (2,500 additional units) | Complete 5,000-unit target | $400M one-time + $100M/yr | Years 6-8 |
| Built for Zero national coverage (400 communities) | All high-need communities participating | $100M/yr | Years 6-8 |
| Full funding fee reform | Reduce fees for all purchase categories; maintain for cash-out refinance | $1.0-1.2B/yr (revenue offset) | Year 8 |
| National aging-in-place program | Home modification grants available to all eligible aging veterans | $300M/yr | Years 6-10 |

## Dependencies and Risks

### Critical Dependencies

| Dependency | Risk if Not Met | Mitigation |
|-----------|----------------|------------|
| Housing supply | Vouchers and subsidies ineffective without units | Pursue supply-side reforms (LIHTC, zoning) in parallel |
| Congressional appropriations | Program expansion stalls | Prioritize administrative actions; build bipartisan support |
| DOD cooperation on transition | Housing transition planning cannot be implemented | Presidential directive requiring DOD-VA coordination |
| HUD-VA interagency coordination | Programs remain fragmented | Joint Housing Office with statutory authority |
| State and local partnership | Source-of-income protections, zoning reform | Federal incentives and model legislation |

### Key Risks

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Response |
|------|------------|--------|----------|
| Housing market downturn | Medium | Mixed (lower prices but foreclosure risk) | Expand foreclosure prevention; maintain funding |
| Budget sequestration or austerity | Medium | High | Protect veteran housing as mandatory spending |
| NIMBY resistance to supportive housing | High | Medium | State preemption laws; community benefit agreements |
| VA leadership turnover | High | Medium | Codify programs in statute, not executive policy |
| Rising construction costs | Medium | Medium | Rehabilitation and conversion strategies supplement new construction |
| Demographic shift (aging veteran population) | Certain | High | Accelerate aging-in-place and accessibility programs |

## Measurement and Accountability

### Annual Reporting Requirements

The VA Secretary and HUD Secretary should jointly report to Congress annually on:

1. Veteran homelessness PIT count and trends (overall and by subpopulation)
2. HUD-VASH voucher utilization rates (by housing authority and state)
3. SSVF outcomes (prevention vs. re-housing; return to homelessness rates)
4. VA Home Loan performance (origination, default, foreclosure by demographic)
5. Housing transition outcomes (first-year housing stability for separating service members)
6. Racial, gender, and geographic disparity metrics
7. SAH grant processing times and backlog
8. Built for Zero community progress

### Independent Evaluation

GAO should conduct biennial evaluations of veteran housing progress, with specific attention to:

- Whether functional zero is being achieved and sustained in participating communities
- Whether HUD-VASH reforms are increasing utilization and housing retention
- Whether the Transitional Housing Allowance is preventing early veteran homelessness
- Whether racial and gender disparities are narrowing
- Whether VA Home Loan reforms are improving homeownership access without increasing default risk
