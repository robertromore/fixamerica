# Transition Assistance: Roadmap

## Overview

This roadmap outlines a three-phase implementation plan for comprehensive military-to-civilian transition reform. Phase 1 focuses on immediate actions achievable through policy changes and executive action. Phase 2 builds the legislative and institutional foundation for systemic reform. Phase 3 establishes long-term community and cultural infrastructure for veteran integration.

## Phase 1: Quick Wins and Foundation (0-18 Months)

### Objective

Implement high-impact reforms that can be achieved through executive action, DOD policy changes, and targeted legislation, while building the evidence base and political support for systemic reform.

### Actions

| Action | Lead Agency | Timeline | Status |
|--------|-----------|----------|--------|
| Remove SkillBridge commander approval requirement (replace with notification) | DOD | 0-6 months | Pending policy change |
| Expand SkillBridge employer partnerships to 10,000+ | DOD/Hiring Our Heroes | 0-18 months | In progress |
| Create virtual SkillBridge options for remote/overseas installations | DOD | 6-12 months | Pilot stage |
| Establish Transition Outcome Metrics and begin tracking | DOD/VA/DOL | 6-12 months | Planning |
| Mandate protected transition time (minimum 30 days over 12 months) via DOD instruction | DOD | 3-6 months | Pending |
| Expand VA Solid Start from phone calls to comprehensive check-ins | VA | 6-12 months | Pilot stage |
| Launch credential translation online tool for all military MOS codes | DOD/DOL | 6-18 months | Development |
| Include spouses in TAP benefits briefings and career workshops | DOD/DOL | 3-6 months | Pending policy |
| Create TAP elective track for identity and purpose transition | DOD | 6-12 months | Curriculum development |
| Fund 5 AmericaServes-model community coordination pilots | VA/IVMF | 6-12 months | Pending funding |

## Phase 2: Systemic Reform (18-42 Months)

### Objective

| Enact 12/24 Transition Continuum legislation | Congress | 18-30 months | Bill drafting |

| Develop and pilot Identity Transition Program | DOD/VA/contractors | 18-30 months | Curriculum development |

### Objective

### Actions

|--------|-----------|----------|--------|
| National rollout of Veteran-Ready Communities program | VA | 42-72 months | Planning |
| Achieve 100,000+ annual SkillBridge participants | DOD | 42-60 months | Scaling |
| Full deployment of Veteran Transition Record across all services | DOD/VA | 42-54 months | Development |
| Identity Transition Program at all major installations | DOD/VA | 42-60 months | Piloting |
| Full Military Family Transition Support Program national rollout | DOD/VA | 42-72 months | Piloting |
| Implement performance-based contracting for TAP | DOD | 48-60 months | Evaluation data building |

## Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Commander resistance to protected transition time | High | High | Tie transition outcomes to commander performance evaluations; senior leader messaging |
| DOD budget competition | Medium | High | Frame transition investment as recruitment asset; cost-benefit analysis |
| Data system delays | High | Medium | Phased deployment; interim manual processes; experienced IT management |
| Identity transition program cultural rejection | Medium | Medium | Peer facilitation by veterans; reframe as "next mission" preparation; senior endorsement |
| Community certification program fails to attract communities | Medium | Medium | Federal grants; tie to economic development; employer engagement |
| Credential translation federal mandate challenged by states | Medium | Medium | Commerce clause authority; incentive-based approach as alternative |
| Post-separation engagement drops off | High | High | Peer connection; community-based delivery; digital tools; proactive outreach |

## Dependencies

| Dependency | Required For | Risk If Not Met |
|------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Congressional legislation (12/24 continuum, credential act) | Phases 2-3 | Reforms limited to executive action; insufficient scope |
| DOD cultural change on protected transition time | All phases | Commanders continue to delay TAP access |
| VA IT modernization | VTR system | Data silos persist; veterans continue re-navigating bureaucracy |
| Employer community engagement | SkillBridge, Veteran-Ready Communities | Job placement rates plateau; community integration stalls |
| Sustained bipartisan support | All phases | Funding and authorization gaps |
| Research and evidence base | Outcome tracking, program design | Cannot measure effectiveness or justify investment |
