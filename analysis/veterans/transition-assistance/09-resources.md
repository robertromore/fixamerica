# Transition Assistance: Resources

## Overview

This document compiles key resources for understanding, researching, and advocating on military-to-civilian transition issues. Resources are organized by type and include academic research, government data sources, advocacy organizations, employer programs, and practical tools for transitioning service members and veterans.

## Academic Research

### Key Studies and Reports

| Title | Author/Institution | Year | Significance |
|-------|-------------------|------|-------------|
| *Veterans Metrics Initiative (TVMI)* | Syracuse University IVMF | 2016-present | Comprehensive longitudinal study of veteran transition outcomes across 8 well-being dimensions |
| *The Deployment Life Study* | RAND Corporation | 2023 | Longitudinal study identifying predictors of successful military-civilian transition |
| *Veteran Employment Outcomes* | IVMF | 2024 | Annual analysis of veteran employment, underemployment, and career satisfaction |
| *TAP Assessment Report* | IVMF | 2024 | Most comprehensive independent evaluation of TAP effectiveness |
| *An Assessment of the Transition Assistance Program* | RAND Corporation | 2018 | Found limited impact of TAP on employment outcomes |
| *Military-to-Civilian Transition: Challenges and Opportunities* | Brookings Institution | 2023 | Policy analysis of transition system gaps and reform options |
| *Identity and Military Transition* | IVMF | 2023 | Research on identity loss as a predictor of transition difficulty |
| *Military Spouse Employment Report* | Blue Star Families | 2024 | Annual survey of military family transition experiences |
| *Veteran Homelessness: A Supplemental Report to the 2024 AHAR* | HUD | 2024 | Data on veteran homelessness and transition-period risk factors |
| *Military Credentialing and Licensing* | RAND Corporation | 2022 | Analysis of barriers to military credential recognition |
