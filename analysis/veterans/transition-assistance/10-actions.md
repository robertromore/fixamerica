# Transition Assistance: Actions

## Overview

Improving the military-to-civilian transition requires action from multiple stakeholders: transitioning service members themselves, their families, civilian communities, employers, educational institutions, and citizens who can advocate for systemic reform. This document provides specific, actionable steps organized by audience.

## For Transitioning Service Members

### Immediate Actions

1. **Begin transition planning at the 12-month mark**: Do not wait for your unit to schedule TAP. Request access to transition counseling and begin planning immediately when you are 12 months from separation
2. **Explore SkillBridge**: Research SkillBridge opportunities at skillbridge.osd.mil and begin conversations with your chain of command about participation
3. **Get a Transition Readiness Assessment**: Use DOD's assessment tools and work with a transition counselor to identify your specific needs
4. **Start credential translation now**: Use the O*NET Military Crosswalk and ACE Military Guide to identify how your military training translates to civilian credentials
5. **File VA disability claims before separation**: Use the Benefits Delivery at Discharge (BDD) program to file claims 180-90 days before separation
6. **Enroll in VA healthcare**: Register at va.gov/health before you separate
7. **Build civilian professional networks**: Join LinkedIn, attend industry events, connect with American Corporate Partners or Veterati for mentoring
8. **Attend all TAP sessions fully engaged**: TAP has limitations, but the information on benefits, financial planning, and job search is foundational
9. **Complete an elective track**: Choose the track (Higher Education, Career Technical Training, Entrepreneurship) that matches your goals
10. **Create a financial transition plan**: Build a budget based on your post-separation income, not your military income

### Career Preparation

- **Research your target career field**: Understand civilian salary ranges, credential requirements, and job markets in your planned location
- **Build a civilian resume**: Translate military experience into civilian language; use free resume services from Hire Heroes USA
- **Practice interviewing**: Civilian interviews differ from military culture; practice with a mentor or career coach
- **Consider geographic flexibility**: Some markets are more veteran-friendly than others; research before committing to a location
- **Explore apprenticeship and certification programs**: Many military skills map to civilian certifications (CompTIA, PMP, nursing, CDL)

### For Those Struggling After Separation

1. **Call VA Solid Start**: If you have not heard from VA, call 1-800-827-1000 for benefits assistance
2. **Contact a VSO**: VFW, DAV, and American Legion provide free transition assistance at local posts
3. **Use the Veterans Crisis Line**: If you are in crisis, call 988 (press 1) or text 838255
4. **Connect with peer support**: Team Red White & Blue, The Mission Continues, and Team Rubicon provide community and purpose
5. **Seek career coaching**: Hire Heroes USA (free), ACP mentoring (free), and Onward to Opportunity (free training and certification) are available
6. **Apply for VA Veteran Readiness and Employment**: If your disabilities affect your career, VR&E (Chapter 31) provides individualized vocational support

## For Military Families

### Pre-Separation Actions

1. **Participate in transition planning**: Attend TAP sessions open to family members; engage in financial planning and benefits orientation
2. **Plan spousal career transition**: Use MSEP employer connections and MyCAA for spouse education/training
3. **Research schools in your destination**: Use SchoolQuest from Military Child Education Coalition to find schools and plan transfers
4. **Address children's concerns**: Talk with children about the transition; connect with school counselors at the new school
5. **Build a family financial plan**: Account for income changes, health insurance transitions (TRICARE to VA/employer), and moving costs
6. **Seek family counseling if needed**: Military OneSource provides free confidential counseling for families in transition (available for 365 days after separation)

## For Employers

### Hiring Veterans

1. **Partner with SkillBridge**: Register as a SkillBridge employer at skillbridge.osd.mil; receive military talent at no salary cost during their final 180 days
2. **Join the Veteran Jobs Mission**: Commit to veteran hiring through the coalition of 300+ companies
3. **Train HR staff on military credential translation**: Use SHRM's veteran hiring resources to help recruiters understand military experience
4. **Create veteran employee resource groups**: Provide community and peer support within your organization
5. **Offer veteran-specific onboarding**: The first 90 days are critical; provide structured onboarding that addresses the cultural transition
6. **Retain veteran employees**: 44% of veterans leave their first post-military job within a year; invest in mentoring and career development to retain talent
7. **Partner with Hiring Our Heroes**: Participate in corporate fellowship programs that bridge the transition

### Understanding Military Experience

- Military rank does not equal civilian hierarchy; understand the leadership experience at each rank level
- MOS codes describe military occupations; use O*NET's military crosswalk to translate
- Military personnel often have more leadership, crisis management, and logistics experience than their age or formal education suggests
- Veterans may take longer to adapt to civilian workplace culture; patience and mentoring during the first 6 months pays dividends

## For Educational Institutions

### Supporting Veteran Students

1. **Accept ACE credit recommendations**: Military training evaluated by ACE should receive college credit automatically
2. **Create a veterans center on campus**: Dedicated space for veteran students to connect, study, and access services
3. **Join Student Veterans of America**: Support an SVA chapter on your campus
4. **Train faculty and staff on military culture**: Understand the unique strengths and challenges veteran students bring
5. **Offer flexible scheduling**: Many veteran students have work, family, and VA obligations that require non-traditional schedules
6. **Connect veteran students to peer mentors**: Pair incoming veteran students with currently enrolled veteran students
7. **Provide transparent outcome data**: Publish veteran graduation rates and employment outcomes so veterans can make informed enrollment decisions

## For All Citizens

### Contact Your Elected Officials

#### Template Email: Support Comprehensive Transition Reform

```text
Subject: Support Comprehensive Military-to-Civilian Transition Reform

Dear [Senator/Representative] [Last Name],

I am writing to urge you to support comprehensive reform of the
military-to-civilian transition system. Each year, approximately
200,000 service members leave the military, and too many of them
face unemployment, underemployment, homelessness, and mental
health crises because our transition assistance is inadequate.

The current Transition Assistance Program (TAP) is rated as
helpful by only 55% of participants. Only 48% of veterans feel
prepared for civilian life. The first year after separation carries
the highest suicide risk of any period in a veteran's life.

Specifically, I ask you to support legislation that would:

1. Extend transition support from 12 months before separation to
   24 months after (the 12/24 Transition Continuum)
2. Make SkillBridge participation a right for all separating
   service members, not subject to commander approval
3. Mandate federal recognition of military credentials for
   civilian occupational licensing
4. Require outcome-based evaluation of all transition programs
5. Fund community-level veteran integration programs

We owe our veterans more than a 5-day classroom course. We owe
them a genuine system for successful reintegration into the
civilian communities they served to protect.

Thank you for your attention to this critical issue.

Sincerely,
[Your Name]
[Your Address]
```

### Community Actions

1. **Welcome veterans into your community**: If veterans are moving to your area, connect them to community resources, introduce them to neighbors, invite them to civic organizations
2. **Support veteran-serving nonprofits**: Donate to or volunteer with Hire Heroes USA, Team RWB, The Mission Continues, or local veteran service organizations
3. **Encourage your employer to hire veterans**: Advocate for veteran hiring programs, SkillBridge partnerships, and veteran employee resource groups
4. **Understand the transition**: Read Sebastian Junger's *Tribe* or attend veteran-community dialogue events to understand the reintegration challenge
5. **Do not assume**: Not every veteran wants to be thanked for their service, and not every veteran is struggling. Ask about their experience and their goals, just as you would with any person

## Talking Points

### For Conversations About Transition Reform

- TAP is rated as helpful by only 55% of participants; 48% of veterans feel unprepared for civilian life
- The first year after separation is the highest-risk period for veteran suicide, unemployment, and homelessness
- SkillBridge has a 78% job placement rate but only 12% of eligible service members can access it
- 43% of veterans are underemployed within 2 years because their military skills are not recognized
- The UK, Australia, and Canada all provide more comprehensive transition support than the United States
- Investing in transition is cheaper than paying for unemployment, homelessness, and crisis intervention after the fact

### For Conversations with Transitioning Service Members

- Start planning now, even if separation is months away; earlier preparation leads to better outcomes
- Use every free resource available: SkillBridge, Hire Heroes, ACP, Onward to Opportunity--these are not charity; they are investments in your success
- The transition is hard, and it is normal to struggle; asking for help is a sign of strength, not weakness
- Your military skills are valuable, but you may need to learn how to communicate them in civilian terms
- Build your civilian network before you separate; relationships are the single most important transition asset
