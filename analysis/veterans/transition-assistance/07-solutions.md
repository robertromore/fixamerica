# Transition Assistance: Solutions

## Overview

Effective transition reform requires fundamental restructuring of how the military prepares service members for civilian life, not incremental improvements to the existing TAP curriculum. The solutions below address the core structural failures identified in the root causes analysis: timing, individualization, identity transition, the DOD-VA handoff gap, civilian preparedness, and outcome accountability. They draw on evidence from domestic programs (particularly SkillBridge), international models, and transition research.

## Solution 1: Extend the Transition Continuum to 12/24

### Description

Replace the current compressed TAP model with a 12/24 Transition Continuum: structured transition support beginning 12 months before separation and continuing 24 months after. Pre-separation includes individualized assessment, career planning, credential translation, financial planning, benefits enrollment, and SkillBridge participation. Post-separation includes peer mentoring, career coaching, housing support, health monitoring, and community integration.

### Evidence Base

- RAND study found that earlier transition planning is the strongest predictor of successful outcomes (RAND, *The Deployment Life Study*, 2023)
- VA Solid Start data shows that 3 check-in calls in the first year are insufficient; sustained engagement is needed (VA Solid Start Program Evaluation, 2024)
- SkillBridge's 78% job placement rate demonstrates the value of pre-separation experiential preparation (DOD SkillBridge Outcome Report, 2024)
- International models (UK, Australia, Canada) all provide 12+ months of transition support

### Implementation

- **Step 1**: Amend 10 U.S.C. Section 1142 to mandate 12-month pre-separation and 24-month post-separation transition support
- **Step 2**: Create a Transition Readiness Assessment administered at the 12-month mark, identifying individual needs and creating a personalized Transition Plan
- **Step 3**: Assign each separating member a Transition Coordinator (joint DOD-VA position) who manages their case from -12 months to +24 months
- **Step 4**: Establish milestones at -12, -6, -3, 0 (separation), +3, +6, +12, +18, and +24 months with specific activities and check-ins
- **Step 5**: Create protected transition time that commanders cannot override without written justification to the first general/flag officer in the chain

### Cost Estimate

- $2.5 billion annually for full implementation (200,000 annual separations x $12,500 average per service member for expanded services)

### Timeline

- Legislation and rulemaking: 12-18 months
- Pilot at 10 installations: 6-18 months
- Full implementation: 3-5 years

### International Comparison

- **United Kingdom**: Career Transition Partnership provides up to 2 years of pre-separation support and ongoing post-separation career counseling through a public-private partnership (UK Ministry of Defence, 2024)
- **Australia**: Defence Transition Program includes 12 months of pre-separation planning and ongoing post-separation support through Veterans' Affairs (Australian Department of Defence, 2024)
- **Canada**: Transition Group provides personalized transition services starting 2 years before release (Canadian Armed Forces, 2024)

## Solution 2: Universalize SkillBridge Access

### Description

Make SkillBridge participation available to all separating service members as a right, not a privilege subject to commander approval. Expand the program from 25,000 to 100,000+ annual participants by removing barriers, increasing employer partnerships, and creating virtual SkillBridge options for service members at remote locations.

### Evidence Base

- SkillBridge's 78% job placement rate is the highest of any federal transition program (DOD SkillBridge Outcome Report, 2024)
- Only 12% of eligible service members participate, primarily due to commander approval requirements and information gaps (DOD SkillBridge Office, 2024)
- Employer satisfaction with SkillBridge participants exceeds 90% (Hiring Our Heroes Survey, 2024)
- Service members who complete SkillBridge experience 40% lower unemployment rates in the first year compared to non-participants (IVMF, 2024)

### Implementation

- **Step 1**: Amend DOD policy to make SkillBridge participation a right for all service members in their final 180 days
- **Step 2**: Remove commander approval requirement; replace with notification requirement
- **Step 3**: Create virtual SkillBridge options for remote installations and overseas locations
- **Step 4**: Expand employer partnership team to recruit 10,000+ participating employers
- **Step 5**: Establish SkillBridge coordinators at every major installation
- **Step 6**: Create SkillBridge tracks aligned with high-demand civilian occupations (healthcare, IT, skilled trades, logistics, cybersecurity)

### Cost Estimate

- $400 million annually (incremental cost beyond current service member pay; primarily coordination and support staff)

### Timeline

- Policy change: 6-12 months
- Full implementation: 2-3 years

## Solution 3: Create a Military-to-Civilian Identity Transition Program

### Description

Develop and integrate into the transition continuum a structured program addressing the psychological, social, and identity dimensions of military-to-civilian transition. This program would include peer-facilitated group processing, individual coaching on identity and purpose, social network building, and community integration activities.

### Evidence Base

- 52% of veterans report loss of purpose after separation (Blue Star Families, 2024)
- 44% report difficulty adjusting to civilian life (Pew Research Center, 2024)
- Research on identity transition in military populations shows that identity loss is a stronger predictor of transition difficulties than employment or financial status (IVMF Identity and Transition Study, 2023)
- Programs like Team Red White & Blue and The Mission Continues demonstrate the value of purpose-based community engagement for veteran wellbeing

### Implementation

- **Step 1**: Develop an evidence-based Identity Transition Curriculum in partnership with military psychologists, veteran peer mentors, and identity transition researchers
- **Step 2**: Train veteran peer facilitators to deliver the program (creates employment for veterans while leveraging lived experience)
- **Step 3**: Integrate the Identity Transition Program into the pre-separation phase (months -6 to -3) and the post-separation phase (months +1 to +12)
- **Step 4**: Include family members in relevant components (the identity transition affects the entire household)
- **Step 5**: Connect to community-based veteran organizations (Team RWB, The Mission Continues, Team Rubicon) for ongoing purpose and belonging

### Cost Estimate

- $300 million annually (facilitator training, program delivery, community organization partnerships)

### Timeline

- Curriculum development: 12 months
- Facilitator training: 6-12 months
- Pilot: 12-18 months
- Full implementation: 3-4 years

## Solution 4: Build a Unified DOD-VA Transition Data System

### Description

Create a single, interoperable data system that seamlessly transfers service records, medical records, training and credential documentation, and transition plan information from DOD to VA and other agencies at the point of separation, eliminating the data gaps that force veterans to re-prove their service history and re-navigate bureaucracies.

### Evidence Base

- GAO has repeatedly identified DOD-VA data sharing as a critical transition gap (GAO Reports 2018, 2021, 2023)
- Veterans spend an average of 40+ hours navigating VA enrollment, disability claims filing, and benefits applications after separation (IVMF, 2024)
- The UK's Career Transition Partnership operates a unified data system that automatically transfers service records to Veterans UK (UK MOD, 2024)

### Implementation

- **Step 1**: Establish a Joint DOD-VA Transition Data Office with authority over both agencies' transition-related data systems
- **Step 2**: Create a standardized Veteran Transition Record (VTR) that automatically compiles service records, medical records, training certificates, exposure records, and transition plan at the -12 month mark
- **Step 3**: Transmit the VTR to VA at separation, enabling automatic healthcare enrollment, pre-populated disability claim forms, and education benefit activation
- **Step 4**: Allow veterans to authorize data sharing with state veterans affairs offices, employers (credential verification), and educational institutions
- **Step 5**: Integrate with DOL wage and employment data for outcome tracking (privacy-protected)

### Cost Estimate

- $500 million for system development; $100 million/year for maintenance

### Timeline

- System design: 12-18 months
- Development and testing: 18-30 months
- Full deployment: 3-5 years

## Solution 5: Implement Universal Military Credential Translation

### Description

Create a federal standard for translating military training, education, and experience into civilian-recognized credentials, certifications, and college credits, and mandate that all states accept these translations for occupational licensing purposes.

### Evidence Base

- 43% of veterans are underemployed within 2 years of separation, partly due to unrecognized military credentials (IVMF, 2024)
- Only 38% of veterans report using their military skills in civilian careers (Pew Research Center, 2024)
- The American Council on Education (ACE) has evaluated and recommended college credit for thousands of military courses, but institutional acceptance is voluntary
- 48 states have some credential recognition laws, but coverage varies widely and many occupations are excluded

### Implementation

- **Step 1**: Establish a Federal Military Credential Translation Commission to create standardized civilian equivalencies for all military training programs
- **Step 2**: Mandate that federally funded educational institutions accept ACE military credit recommendations
- **Step 3**: Pass federal legislation requiring all states to accept military training as meeting licensing requirements for occupations where military training is substantially equivalent
- **Step 4**: Create an online credential translation tool that generates a comprehensive civilian credential portfolio for each separating member
- **Step 5**: Partner with industry certification bodies (CompTIA, Project Management Institute, nursing boards) to create military-to-certification pathways

### Cost Estimate

- $200 million for commission and translation infrastructure; $50 million/year for ongoing maintenance

### Timeline

- Commission establishment: 6-12 months
- Initial credential translations: 12-24 months
- Federal legislation: 18-30 months
- Full implementation: 3-5 years

## Solution 6: Create Veteran-Ready Communities

### Description

Establish a national Veteran-Ready Communities program that prepares civilian communities--employers, educational institutions, healthcare providers, civic organizations--to receive and integrate transitioning veterans. Communities that meet readiness standards receive federal recognition and resources.

### Evidence Base

- AmericaServes (IVMF) coordinates community-level veteran service delivery in 18 communities with demonstrated improvements in service utilization and outcomes (IVMF AmericaServes Evaluation, 2024)
- Research shows that community-level integration is more predictive of successful transition than individual-level interventions (RAND, 2023)
- The UK's Armed Forces Covenant commits communities and employers to supporting veterans, with demonstrated positive effects (UK Armed Forces Covenant, 2024)

### Implementation

- **Step 1**: Develop Veteran-Ready Community standards covering employer engagement, healthcare provider training, educational institution support, civic organization involvement, and housing access
- **Step 2**: Create a certification process for communities that meet the standards
- **Step 3**: Provide federal grants to communities pursuing certification ($50,000-$500,000 based on veteran population)
- **Step 4**: Connect transitioning service members to certified Veteran-Ready Communities based on their planned destination
- **Step 5**: Leverage AmericaServes coordination model to connect community resources
- **Step 6**: Include employer military cultural competency training (understanding rank, MOS, military experience)

### Cost Estimate

- $500 million annually for community grants, certification, and coordination

### Timeline

- Standards development: 12 months
- Pilot communities: 12-24 months
- National rollout: 3-5 years

## Solution 7: Mandate Outcome-Based Program Evaluation

### Description

Require all federal transition programs to track and report participant outcomes at 6, 12, and 24 months post-separation, including employment, income, education, housing, health, and well-being measures. Tie program funding to demonstrated outcomes.

### Evidence Base

- TAP currently tracks participation but not outcomes; there is no systematic data on whether the program works (GAO, 2023)
- Federal job training programs under WIOA are required to track outcomes; transition programs should meet at least the same standard
- Outcome-based contracting in other government programs has demonstrated improved performance (OMB Evidence-Based Policymaking, 2023)

### Implementation

- **Step 1**: Establish a standard set of Transition Outcome Metrics (employment rate, income relative to pre-separation, education enrollment/completion, VA enrollment, housing stability, health status)
- **Step 2**: Require all federal transition programs to collect and report outcomes at 6, 12, and 24 months
- **Step 3**: Create a privacy-protected data matching system linking DOD, VA, DOL, and Education data
- **Step 4**: Publish annual Transition Outcomes Report to Congress and the public
- **Step 5**: Tie 20% of TAP contractor funding to outcome performance (with adjustments for population difficulty)
- **Step 6**: Fund independent program evaluation research

### Cost Estimate

- $150 million for data system and evaluation infrastructure; $50 million/year for ongoing tracking and reporting

### Timeline

- Metric development: 6 months
- Data system: 12-24 months
- First outcomes report: 30 months
- Performance-based contracting: 3-5 years

## Solution 8: Establish a Military Family Transition Support Program

### Description

Create a comprehensive family transition program that addresses spousal employment, children's education, family financial planning, and household-level adjustment. Recognize that the military-to-civilian transition is a family experience, not just an individual one.

### Evidence Base

- 68% of military spouses must find new employment at separation; spouse unemployment during transition is 21% (Blue Star Families, 2024)
- 54% of military families report financial stress during transition (Blue Star Families, 2024)
- 37% of military children report difficulty adjusting (Military Child Education Coalition, 2024)
- Research shows that family stability is the strongest predictor of overall transition success (RAND, 2023)

### Implementation

- **Step 1**: Include spouses in transition planning sessions and career counseling (beginning at -12 months)
- **Step 2**: Provide spousal career services including resume assistance, networking, and job placement
- **Step 3**: Create an education transition plan for each military child, including school selection, transcript transfer, and counseling referral
- **Step 4**: Extend military family support services (child care, family counseling) for 12 months after separation
- **Step 5**: Provide relocation assistance for families moving to non-military communities
- **Step 6**: Partner with Military Spouse Employment Partnership (MSEP) employers for spousal job placement

### Cost Estimate

- $800 million annually for comprehensive family transition services

### Timeline

- Program design: 12 months
- Pilot at 10 installations: 12-24 months
- Full implementation: 3-5 years

## Solution Sequencing

| Priority | Solution | Rationale |
|----------|----------|-----------|
| Immediate (0-12 months) | Universalize SkillBridge access | High impact; proven model; primarily policy change |
| Immediate (0-12 months) | Mandate outcome-based evaluation | Essential for measuring all other reforms |
| Short-term (1-2 years) | Universal credential translation | Addresses underemployment immediately |
| Short-term (1-2 years) | Extend transition continuum to 12/24 | Foundational reform; requires legislation |
| Medium-term (2-3 years) | Build unified data system | Technical infrastructure; enables other solutions |
| Medium-term (2-3 years) | Identity transition program | Requires curriculum development and facilitator training |
| Medium-term (2-3 years) | Family transition support | Requires program design and community partnerships |
| Long-term (3-5 years) | Veteran-Ready Communities | Requires community-level infrastructure and culture change |
