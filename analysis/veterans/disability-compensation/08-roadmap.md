# Veteran Disability Compensation: Roadmap

## Overview

This roadmap outlines a 10-year strategy to transform the VA disability compensation system from its current state (151-day average processing, 262,000+ claim backlog, outdated 1945-era rating schedule) to a modernized system that processes initial claims within 30 days, resolves appeals within 12 months, and uses a rating schedule that accurately reflects 21st-century conditions. The strategy is organized in three phases aligned with the solutions analysis.

## Phase 1: Foundation (Years 1-2)

### Objective

Address the immediate crisis: reduce the backlog, provide financial relief to waiting veterans, and launch modernization initiatives.

### Action Table

| Action | Lead Agency | Timeline | Cost (Annual) | Priority |
|--------|-------------|----------|---------------|----------|
| Launch emergency bridge payment program for claims pending >90 days | VBA | Months 1-6 | $800M | Critical |
| Deploy AI triage system at all regional offices | VBA IT | Months 1-12 | $100M | Critical |
| Establish presumptive conditions for PTSD (combat), hearing loss, tinnitus | VBA (legislative) | Months 1-6 | $3B (benefits) | Critical |
| Launch simplified 2-page claims application | VBA | Months 3-12 | $50M | High |
| Implement C&P exam minimum time and credentialing standards | VBA + contractors | Months 1-12 | $100M | High |
| Establish VASRD Modernization Office | VA Secretary | Months 1-6 | $30M | High |
| Begin VASRD musculoskeletal criteria revision | VASRD Office | Months 6-24 | $40M | High |
| Pilot AI-assisted processing at 5 regional offices | VBA IT | Months 6-18 | $80M | High |
| Establish Remand Resolution Teams at all regional offices | VBA | Months 3-12 | $60M | Medium |
| Launch claims quality improvement program (analyzing CAVC remand patterns) | VBA + CAVC | Months 1-12 | $20M | Medium |
| Hire 2,000 additional claims processors (surge) | VBA HR | Months 1-12 | $200M | Medium |

### Phase 1 Milestones

- Bridge payments operational for all claims pending >90 days within 6 months
- AI triage categorizing all incoming claims by complexity within 12 months
- New presumptive conditions effective within 6 months of legislation
- Simplified application available online and at all regional offices within 12 months
- VASRD Modernization Office established with published timeline within 6 months
- AI-assisted processing pilot producing results within 18 months

## Phase 2: Expansion (Years 3-5)

### Objective

Scale successful pilots, complete VASRD modernization, and transform the appeals system.

### Action Table

| Action | Lead Agency | Timeline | Cost (Annual) | Priority |
|--------|-------------|----------|---------------|----------|
| National rollout of AI-assisted processing for simple claims | VBA IT | Years 3-4 | $150M | Critical |
| Complete VASRD musculoskeletal and mental health updates | VASRD Office | Year 3 | $40M | Critical |
| Complete all remaining VASRD body system updates | VASRD Office | Years 3-5 | $60M | Critical |
| Establish presumptive conditions for MST and musculoskeletal (by MOS) | VBA (legislative) | Year 3 | $5B (benefits) | High |
| Increase BVA staffing from 115 to 200 Veterans Law Judges | BVA | Years 3-5 | $50M | High |
| Enact statutory appeals deadlines | Congress | Year 3 | Minimal | High |
| Create binding precedent authority for BVA decisions | Congress | Year 3 | Minimal | High |
| Establish specialty C&P exam panels at 50 VA medical centers | VBA + VHA | Years 3-5 | $150M | Medium |
| Expand AI assistance to moderate-complexity claims | VBA IT | Years 4-5 | $100M | Medium |
| Deploy national telehealth C&P exam capability | VBA + VHA | Years 3-5 | $80M | Medium |

### Phase 2 Milestones

- AI processing 40% of simple claims without human intervention within Year 4
- VASRD fully modernized across all 15 body systems by Year 5
- BVA fully staffed with 200 Veterans Law Judges by Year 5
- Statutory appeals deadlines in effect by Year 4
- Claims backlog below 100,000 by Year 5

## Phase 3: Transformation (Years 5-10)

### Objective

Achieve sustained performance targets and build a system that is trusted, efficient, and accurate.

### Action Table

| Action | Lead Agency | Timeline | Cost (Annual) | Priority |
|--------|-------------|----------|---------------|----------|
| Sustain 30-day average processing time | VBA | Ongoing | $1B | Critical |
| Maintain near-zero backlog | VBA | Ongoing | Included above | Critical |
| Continuous VASRD updates on 3-year review cycle | VASRD Office | Ongoing | $20M | High |
| Implement continuous quality improvement using AI analytics | VBA | Ongoing | $50M | High |
| Reduce claims processor workforce as AI matures (through attrition) | VBA HR | Years 5-10 | Savings | Medium |
| Achieve 85%+ veteran satisfaction with claims process | VBA | Year 10 | Included above | Ultimate goal |
| Evaluate shift from earning capacity to functional impairment model | VA Advisory Committee | Years 7-10 | $10M | Medium |

### Phase 3 Milestones

- 30-day average processing sustained for 3+ consecutive years
- Backlog consistently below 50,000
- Veteran satisfaction at 80%+ by Year 7, 85%+ by Year 10
- Appeals resolved within 12 months (all lanes)
- VASRD modernization completed and on 3-year review cycle

## Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation Strategy |
|------|------------|--------|-------------------|
| VASRD modernization produces political backlash | Medium | High | Grandfathering provisions; veteran advisory committee involvement; prospective-only application |
| AI system produces biased or inaccurate decisions | Medium | High | Rigorous testing across demographic groups; human review of all AI decisions initially; continuous monitoring |
| VA employee union blocks AI and process reforms | High | Medium | Early engagement; retraining programs; frame as job improvement (fewer routine tasks); attrition-based workforce adjustment |
| PACT Act volume continues to grow beyond projections | Medium | High | Surge AI processing capacity; expanded presumptive conditions reduce per-claim processing burden |
| Budget constraints limit reform investment | Medium | High | Frame as cost-saving (reduced administrative cost, overtime, appeals); bipartisan veteran support |
| C&P exam contractors resist quality standards | Medium | Medium | Contract modifications with consequences; develop in-house exam capacity as alternative |
| IT modernization fails (historical pattern) | Medium | High | Agile development; small-scale pilots before national rollout; independent oversight |
