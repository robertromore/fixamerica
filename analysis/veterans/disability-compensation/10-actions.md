# Veteran Disability Compensation: Actions

## What You Can Do

The veteran disability compensation system affects 6.2 million veterans and their families. Reform requires sustained public engagement from veterans, advocates, and citizens. The following actions are organized by audience and level of effort.

---

## For Veterans With Pending or Potential Claims

### Immediate Steps

**File a Fully Developed Claim (FDC)**

- Gather all medical evidence, service records (DD-214), buddy statements, and supporting documentation *before* filing
- Use the Fully Developed Claim program to bypass evidence development delays
- Claims filed as FDC are processed approximately 40% faster than standard claims (VA Benefits Administration, 2024)
- Work with an accredited VSO representative to ensure your claim is complete before submission

**Get Free Claims Assistance**

- Accredited VSO representatives file and manage claims at no cost to veterans
- Major VSOs (DAV, American Legion, VFW) have representatives at or near every VA regional office
- File VA Form 21-22 to appoint a representative
- VSO representatives have access to VA systems and can expedite evidence gathering
- Veterans Legal Clinic networks at law schools provide free legal assistance for complex claims and appeals

**Check Your PACT Act Eligibility**

- If you served in a location or era associated with toxic exposure (burn pits, Agent Orange, radiation), file for presumptive conditions
- The PACT Act expanded eligibility for 20+ conditions; 3.5 million+ veterans are newly eligible for VA healthcare
- Even if you were previously denied, you can file a supplemental claim under the new presumptions
- Visit VA.gov/PACT for eligibility screening
- File before any applicable deadlines to preserve retroactive benefits

**Track Your Claim Status**

- Use VA.gov to monitor your claim's progress through each processing stage
- Respond promptly to any VA requests for additional information or evidence
- Delays in responding to development requests significantly extend processing time
- Contact your VSO representative if your claim appears stalled for more than 30 days without explanation

### If Your Claim Is Delayed

**Contact Your Congressional Representative**

- Congressional inquiries to the VA on behalf of constituents can prompt action on stalled claims
- Your Representative and both Senators each have a casework office that handles VA issues
- Provide your claim number, filing date, and a brief description of the delay
- Congressional offices can request status updates and expedited processing from the VA

**Request Expedited Processing If Eligible**

- The VA grants "advance on docket" status for claims involving:
    - Financial hardship (documented inability to meet basic needs)
    - Terminal illness
    - Advanced age (75+)
    - Homelessness or imminent homelessness
    - ALS diagnosis
    - Former prisoners of war
- Submit a request with supporting documentation through your VSO representative

**Understand Your Appeal Options**

- If your claim is denied or underrated, you have three review options under the Appeals Modernization Act:
    - **Supplemental Claim**: Submit new evidence to the Regional Office (average 170 days)
    - **Higher-Level Review**: Senior reviewer re-examines the same evidence (average 130 days)
    - **Board of Veterans' Appeals**: Appeal to a Veterans Law Judge (average 550+ days)
- Each option has different advantages; consult with your VSO representative or attorney before choosing
- You have one year from the date of your decision to file in any lane

### Protect Yourself During the Process

**Document Everything**

- Keep copies of all claims submissions, VA correspondence, and medical records
- Record dates of C&P examinations, examiner names, and your recollection of what occurred
- Maintain a personal log of symptoms, functional limitations, and how your conditions affect daily life
- This documentation supports both your current claim and any future appeal

**Seek Medical Treatment**

- Consistent medical treatment records strengthen claims by establishing ongoing disability
- VA healthcare enrollment is separate from disability compensation; you do not need a rating to receive care for many conditions
- Private medical opinions supporting service connection carry significant weight in claims adjudication

## For All Citizens

### Educate Yourself

**Understand the Scale of the Problem**

- 850,000+ claims pending as of January 2025; 262,000+ in the backlog (>125 days)
- Average processing time of 151 days (FY 2024)
- 520,000+ appeals pending across all review lanes
- The VASRD has not been fundamentally updated since 1945
- $134 billion annual program that is growing at approximately 10% per year
- 75% reversal/remand rate at the Court of Appeals for Veterans Claims

**Know the Key Reform Proposals**

- VASRD modernization to reflect 21st-century conditions (PTSD, TBI, toxic exposures)
- AI-assisted claims processing with human oversight for simple claims
- Expanded presumptive conditions to reduce the adversarial burden of proof
- Emergency bridge payments for veterans waiting more than 90 days
- Appeals system reform with statutory deadlines and binding precedent
- C&P examination quality standards and specialty panels

### Advocate for Reform

**Contact Your Members of Congress**

- Call or write your Representative and Senators about disability compensation reform
- Specific asks are more effective than general complaints:
    - Support legislation requiring VASRD modernization completion by 2028
    - Fund AI-assisted claims processing with appropriate safeguards
    - Authorize emergency bridge payments for veterans in the backlog
    - Establish statutory processing deadlines with provisional benefits for missed timelines
    - Require expanded presumptive conditions for PTSD, hearing loss, and common musculoskeletal injuries
- Use the PACT Act backlog as a concrete, current example of why structural reform is needed

**Support Veterans Service Organizations**

- Donate to or volunteer with VSOs that provide free claims assistance
- Many VSOs rely on volunteers to help veterans navigate the claims process
- Key organizations: DAV, American Legion, VFW, AMVETS, IAVA, Wounded Warrior Project
- Law school veterans legal clinics also accept pro bono volunteers

**Vote and Participate in Elections**

- Veterans' issues are bipartisan; hold candidates from both parties accountable
- Ask candidates their specific plans for disability compensation reform
- Support candidates who commit to adequate VA funding and structural modernization
- Attend town halls and raise veteran disability compensation as an issue

### Community Actions

**Help Veterans in Your Community**

- Connect veterans you know with VSO claims assistance
- Many veterans, especially older or rural veterans, are not aware of benefits they have earned
- Share information about PACT Act eligibility through community organizations, churches, and social networks
- Volunteer at Stand Down events, veteran service fairs, and benefits outreach programs

**Engage Employers**

- Employers who hire veterans can support claims filing through HR programs
- Provide flexible scheduling for C&P examinations and medical appointments
- Connect veteran employees with VSO representatives
- Offer leave for claims-related activities during work hours

## For Veterans Service Organizations

### Strengthen Claims Assistance

- Invest in training for claims representatives on PACT Act conditions, VASRD updates, and new presumptive categories
- Develop digital tools for remote claims assistance to reach rural and isolated veterans
- Partner with law schools for pro bono legal support on appeals
- Track claims outcomes by representative and condition type to identify systemic issues and training needs
- Build expertise in emerging claim types: toxic exposures, military sexual trauma, TBI

### Advocacy Actions

- Coordinate across VSOs on legislative priorities for VASRD modernization, AI-assisted processing, and bridge payments
- Testify before Congressional Veterans' Affairs committees on disability compensation reform
- Publish annual reports on claims assistance outcomes and systemic barriers
- Engage media to maintain public attention on disability compensation beyond backlog crises
- Advocate for C&P examination quality standards and contractor accountability

### Member Engagement

- Educate members about the claims process, appeal options, and their rights under the Appeals Modernization Act
- Provide regular updates on claims processing trends, VASRD revision status, and legislative action
- Mobilize members for Congressional contact campaigns on key votes
- Survey members on claims experience to build evidence for reform advocacy
- Create peer support networks connecting veterans navigating the claims process

## For Policymakers

### Executive Actions (No Legislation Required)

- Direct VA to complete VASRD musculoskeletal and mental health updates by 2027 under existing rulemaking authority (38 U.S.C. 1155)
- Establish emergency bridge payment pilot program under existing VA management authority
- Require performance-based contracts for C&P examination vendors with quality metrics and minimum examination times
- Mandate quality review sample increases from 3% to 10% of completed claims
- Deploy AI triage system for claims complexity categorization under existing IT modernization authority
- Create Remand Resolution Teams at all regional offices under internal reorganization authority

### Legislative Actions

- Pass VASRD Modernization Act mandating completion of all body system updates by 2028
- Authorize AI-assisted claims processing with due process safeguards and bias audits
- Establish statutory processing timelines with automatic provisional benefits for missed deadlines
- Expand presumptive conditions for PTSD (combat), hearing loss, tinnitus, and common musculoskeletal conditions by MOS
- Fund emergency bridge payments for veterans waiting more than 90 days
- Increase BVA staffing authorization from 115 to 200 Veterans Law Judges
- Create binding precedent authority for BVA decisions

### Oversight Actions

- Require quarterly VA reporting on claims processing times, backlog metrics, accuracy data, and VASRD revision progress
- Conduct regular hearings on disability compensation system performance and PACT Act implementation
- Demand implementation of outstanding GAO and OIG recommendations on claims processing
- Commission independent assessment of VA claims processing IT modernization
- Monitor C&P examination contractor quality and hold vendors accountable for performance deficiencies
