# Veteran Disability Compensation: Legislation and Legal Framework

## Overview

Reforming the VA disability compensation system requires legislative action across multiple fronts: modernizing the outdated rating schedule, establishing accountability for claims processing timelines, authorizing AI-assisted processing with appropriate safeguards, expanding presumptive conditions, creating emergency bridge payments, and reforming the appeals system. This document provides draft legal text for key reforms identified in [Solutions](07-solutions.md) and [Roadmap](08-roadmap.md). No constitutional amendments are required; the federal government's authority to administer veteran disability compensation is well-established under Article I, Section 8 (military powers) and existing title 38 statutory authority.

## Federal Legislation

### VASRD Modernization Act

**Purpose**: Mandate and fund the completion of the VA Schedule for Rating Disabilities modernization across all 15 body systems, with a shift from average earning capacity to functional impairment criteria, and establish an ongoing review cycle.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SEC. 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Veterans Disability Rating
Modernization Act".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds the following:
(1) The VA Schedule for Rating Disabilities (VASRD), codified
    at 38 CFR Part 4, has not been fundamentally restructured
    since 1945, despite radical changes in the nature of
    military service, warfare, and medical understanding.
(2) Only 7 of 15 body systems have been updated since the
    VA began piecemeal revisions in 2017, and the two most
    heavily used systems--musculoskeletal and mental
    disorders--remain under outdated criteria.
(3) The VASRD's focus on "average impairment of earning
    capacity" (38 CFR 4.1) fails to capture the functional
    impairment and quality-of-life impact of modern
    conditions including post-traumatic stress disorder,
    traumatic brain injury, military sexual trauma, and
    toxic exposure-related diseases.
(4) Outdated rating criteria contribute to inconsistent
    ratings across regions, high appeal rates, and veteran
    dissatisfaction with the accuracy of disability
    evaluations.

SEC. 3. VASRD MODERNIZATION OFFICE.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.--Not later than 180 days after the date
    of enactment, the Secretary of Veterans Affairs shall
    establish within the Veterans Benefits Administration a
    VASRD Modernization Office with dedicated staff and
    authority to develop, publish, and implement updated
    rating criteria.

(b) DIRECTOR.--The Office shall be headed by a Director
    appointed by the Secretary, who shall report directly to
    the Under Secretary for Benefits.

(c) ADVISORY PANEL.--The Director shall convene a Medical
    Advisory Panel comprising--
    (1) physicians and psychologists with expertise in
        conditions commonly rated under the VASRD;
    (2) occupational medicine specialists;
    (3) representatives of veterans service organizations;
    (4) veterans who have experience with the disability
        rating process; and
    (5) disability policy researchers.

SEC. 4. MANDATORY COMPLETION SCHEDULE.

(a) MUSCULOSKELETAL AND MENTAL DISORDERS.--The Secretary
    shall publish final rules updating the musculoskeletal
    and mental disorders body systems not later than 2 years
    after the date of enactment.

(b) REMAINING BODY SYSTEMS.--The Secretary shall publish
    final rules updating all remaining body systems not
    later than 3 years after the date of enactment.

(c) FUNCTIONAL IMPAIRMENT CRITERIA.--Updated rating criteria
    shall--
    (1) evaluate disability based on functional impairment
        and quality-of-life impact, not solely on average
        impairment of earning capacity;
    (2) incorporate validated clinical instruments for
        measuring functional limitations;
    (3) include specific criteria for post-traumatic stress
        disorder, traumatic brain injury, military sexual
        trauma-related conditions, and toxic exposure-related
        diseases; and
    (4) reduce subjectivity in rating determinations through
        objective, measurable criteria where possible.

SEC. 5. GRANDFATHERING AND VETERAN CHOICE.

(a) EXISTING RATINGS PROTECTED.--No veteran's existing
    disability rating shall be reduced as a result of
    updated VASRD criteria unless the veteran requests
    re-evaluation.

(b) VETERAN ELECTION.--Veterans rated under prior criteria
    may elect re-evaluation under updated criteria. If the
    updated criteria would produce a higher rating, the
    veteran shall receive the higher rating effective on
    the date of the election. If the updated criteria would
    produce a lower rating, the veteran shall retain the
    prior rating.

(c) PROSPECTIVE APPLICATION.--Updated criteria shall apply
    to all claims filed on or after the effective date of
    each body system update.

SEC. 6. ONGOING REVIEW CYCLE.

(a) TRIENNIAL REVIEW.--Beginning 3 years after completion
    of all body system updates under section 4, the Secretary
    shall review and update each body system at least once
    every 5 years.

(b) EMERGING CONDITIONS.--The Secretary shall establish a
    process for adding rating criteria for newly recognized
    conditions within 12 months of a determination by the
    Secretary or the National Academies of Sciences,
    Engineering, and Medicine that a condition is linked to
    military service.

SEC. 7. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS.

There are authorized to be appropriated--
(1) $80,000,000 for fiscal year 2027;
(2) $100,000,000 for fiscal year 2028;
(3) $80,000,000 for fiscal year 2029;
(4) $40,000,000 for fiscal year 2030; and
(5) $20,000,000 for each fiscal year thereafter for ongoing
    review and maintenance.

SEC. 8. REPORTING.

The Secretary shall report annually to the Committees on
Veterans' Affairs of the House of Representatives and the
Senate on--
(1) progress toward completing body system updates;
(2) Medical Advisory Panel recommendations;
(3) impact of updated criteria on rating consistency;
(4) veteran satisfaction with rating accuracy; and
(5) appeal rates for claims decided under updated criteria.
```

**Explanation**:

- **Section 3**: Creates a dedicated office rather than relying on existing VBA staff, ensuring modernization receives sustained institutional focus
- **Section 4**: Sets firm deadlines; the musculoskeletal and mental health systems are prioritized because they are the most commonly used and most outdated
- **Section 4(c)**: The shift to functional impairment criteria is the most significant structural change, aligning with recommendations from the VA Advisory Committee and international best practices
- **Section 5**: Grandfathering protections eliminate the political risk of rating reductions for current beneficiaries while allowing veterans to benefit from improved criteria
- **Section 6**: Prevents future stagnation by requiring ongoing review

**Potential Challenges**:

| Challenge | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| Political resistance to any criteria change | Grandfathering and veteran choice provisions eliminate risk of rating reductions |
| Medical community disagreement on functional impairment measurement | Advisory Panel includes diverse medical expertise; validated instruments required |
| Training workforce on new criteria | Section 7 funding includes training costs; phased implementation allows gradual transition |
| Rulemaking timeline may slip | Mandatory deadlines with Congressional reporting create accountability |

**Refinements**:

- **Stronger version**: Require independent evaluation of updated criteria by the National Academies before implementation
- **Fallback version**: Advisory deadlines with sense-of-Congress language if mandatory deadlines face opposition
- **Extension provision**: Allow one 12-month extension upon certification that the delay is necessary for medical accuracy

### Veterans Disability Claims Bridge Payment Act

**Purpose**: Provide automatic interim financial support to veterans whose disability claims remain pending beyond 90 days, preventing financial hardship caused by processing delays.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SEC. 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Veterans Disability Claims
Bridge Payment Act".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds the following:
(1) The average processing time for VA disability compensation
    claims exceeded 150 days in fiscal year 2024, with
    262,000 or more claims pending beyond 125 days.
(2) Financial difficulties are present in approximately 40
    percent of veteran suicides, and claims processing delays
    exacerbate financial insecurity among disabled veterans.
(3) Veterans who have filed legitimate claims for service-
    connected disabilities should not face financial hardship
    due to VA processing delays.
(4) International models, including Veterans Affairs Canada,
    demonstrate that interim disability payments are
    administratively feasible and fiscally manageable.

SEC. 3. BRIDGE PAYMENT PROGRAM.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.--The Secretary of Veterans Affairs shall
    establish a Bridge Payment Program to provide interim
    disability compensation to veterans with pending initial
    claims.

(b) ELIGIBILITY.--A veteran shall be eligible for a bridge
    payment if--
    (1) the veteran has filed an initial claim for disability
        compensation under chapter 11 of title 38, United
        States Code;
    (2) the claim has been pending for more than 90 days; and
    (3) the veteran has not received a final rating decision.

(c) PAYMENT AMOUNT.--
    (1) PRELIMINARY ESTIMATE.--Upon filing, the Secretary
        shall generate a preliminary disability estimate
        based on the veteran's claimed conditions, military
        service records, and available medical evidence.
    (2) BRIDGE PAYMENT RATE.--The bridge payment shall equal
        75 percent of the estimated monthly compensation
        based on the preliminary estimate.
    (3) MINIMUM PAYMENT.--No bridge payment shall be less
        than the monthly compensation for a 30 percent
        disability rating.

(d) DURATION.--Bridge payments shall begin automatically on
    the 91st day after the claim filing date and continue
    until the earlier of--
    (1) the date of the final rating decision; or
    (2) 12 months after the bridge payment initiation date.

SEC. 4. RECONCILIATION.

(a) UNDERPAYMENT.--If the final rating decision results in
    monthly compensation exceeding the bridge payment amount,
    the Secretary shall pay the veteran a retroactive lump
    sum equal to the difference between the bridge payment
    and the correct compensation for each month of the
    bridge payment period.

(b) OVERPAYMENT.--If the final rating decision results in
    monthly compensation less than the bridge payment amount,
    the Secretary shall recover the overpayment through
    reduced future monthly payments not to exceed 10 percent
    of the monthly compensation amount. No lump-sum
    repayment shall be required.

(c) DENIED CLAIMS.--If the claim is denied in its entirety,
    the Secretary may recover bridge payments through a
    repayment plan of not less than 36 monthly installments.
    The Secretary may waive recovery if collection would
    cause undue financial hardship.

SEC. 5. ANTI-FRAUD PROVISIONS.

(a) PENALTIES.--Any person who knowingly submits false
    information for the purpose of obtaining bridge payments
    shall be subject to penalties under section 6102 of
    title 38, United States Code.

(b) AUDIT.--The Secretary shall conduct random audits of
    bridge payment recipients at a rate of not less than
    5 percent of all bridge payments per quarter.

SEC. 6. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS.

(a) IN GENERAL.--There are authorized to be appropriated
    such sums as may be necessary to carry out this Act.

(b) ADVANCE APPROPRIATION.--Amounts appropriated for bridge
    payments under this Act shall be available for obligation
    one fiscal year in advance.

SEC. 7. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act shall take effect 1 year after the date of enactment.
```

**Explanation**:

- **Section 3(c)**: The 75% rate balances providing meaningful financial support with minimizing overpayment risk
- **Section 3(c)(3)**: The minimum payment floor ensures even veterans with uncertain claims receive meaningful support
- **Section 4**: The reconciliation structure protects both veterans (no lump-sum repayment) and taxpayers (orderly recovery of overpayments)
- **Section 4(c)**: Hardship waiver prevents creating a new financial crisis through aggressive debt collection from denied claimants

**Potential Challenges**:

| Challenge | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| Fiscal cost uncertainty | CBO can estimate based on current claims volume and processing times; costs decline as processing improves |
| Overpayment recovery logistics | Recovery through reduced payments is administratively simple; VA already uses this method for other overpayments |
| Preliminary estimate accuracy | Conservative 75% rate provides buffer; minimum payment floor limits downside |
| Fraud risk | Anti-fraud provisions plus random audits; current VA fraud rate is below 1% |

**Refinements**:

- **Phased version**: Begin with claims pending >125 days, then expand to >90 days
- **Stronger version**: Increase to 100% of estimated compensation; eliminate overpayment recovery entirely
- **Budget-neutral version**: Fund through reallocation of overtime spending savings

### Expanded Presumptive Conditions Act

**Purpose**: Establish presumptive service connection for the most common, well-documented service-connected disabilities, reducing the adversarial burden of proof and accelerating claims processing.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SEC. 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Veterans Presumptive Service
Connection Expansion Act".

SEC. 2. PRESUMPTIVE SERVICE CONNECTION FOR PTSD.

(a) IN GENERAL.--Post-traumatic stress disorder shall be
    presumptively service-connected for any veteran who--
    (1) served in a combat zone or hostile fire area as
        documented by military service records; or
    (2) served in a military occupational specialty with
        documented high-stress or high-trauma exposure as
        determined by the Secretary.

(b) DOCUMENTATION.--A diagnosis of PTSD by a qualified
    mental health professional, combined with qualifying
    service under subsection (a), shall be sufficient to
    establish service connection. No additional nexus
    evidence shall be required.

SEC. 3. PRESUMPTIVE SERVICE CONNECTION FOR HEARING LOSS AND
TINNITUS.

(a) IN GENERAL.--Hearing loss and tinnitus shall be
    presumptively service-connected for any veteran who
    served in a military occupational specialty with
    documented noise exposure as determined by the Secretary.

(b) NOISE EXPOSURE DATABASE.--Not later than 180 days after
    the date of enactment, the Secretary, in consultation
    with the Secretary of Defense, shall publish a list of
    military occupational specialties and service conditions
    associated with hazardous noise exposure.

SEC. 4. PRESUMPTIVE SERVICE CONNECTION FOR MUSCULOSKELETAL
CONDITIONS.

(a) IN GENERAL.--The following conditions shall be
    presumptively service-connected for veterans who served
    in military occupational specialties involving repetitive
    physical stress as determined by the Secretary:
    (1) Degenerative disc disease of the lumbar and
        cervical spine.
    (2) Degenerative joint disease of the knees.
    (3) Degenerative joint disease of the shoulders.
    (4) Plantar fasciitis.

(b) QUALIFYING SERVICE.--The Secretary shall publish a list
    of qualifying military occupational specialties within
    180 days of enactment, including but not limited to
    infantry, airborne, armor, combat engineer, and
    military police.

SEC. 5. PRESUMPTIVE SERVICE CONNECTION FOR MILITARY SEXUAL
TRAUMA.

(a) IN GENERAL.--Mental health conditions, including but
    not limited to PTSD, depression, and anxiety disorders,
    shall be presumptively service-connected for any veteran
    who has a documented report of military sexual trauma
    in the veteran's service records or VA treatment records.

(b) ALTERNATIVE EVIDENCE.--If no formal report exists in
    service or VA records, the veteran may establish military
    sexual trauma through alternative evidence including
    but not limited to behavioral changes documented in
    service records, contemporaneous statements to medical
    professionals, or buddy statements from fellow service
    members.

SEC. 6. INTERAGENCY SCIENTIFIC REVIEW BOARD.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.--The Secretary shall establish an
    Interagency Scientific Review Board to evaluate the
    evidence for new presumptive conditions on a rolling
    basis.

(b) COMPOSITION.--The Board shall include representatives
    from--
    (1) the Department of Veterans Affairs;
    (2) the Department of Defense;
    (3) the National Institutes of Health;
    (4) the National Academies of Sciences, Engineering,
        and Medicine; and
    (5) veterans service organizations.

(c) TRIENNIAL REVIEW.--The Board shall review the
    presumptive conditions list at least every 3 years and
    recommend additions or modifications based on emerging
    medical evidence.

(d) EXPEDITED RULEMAKING.--Conditions recommended by the
    Board shall be added to the presumptive conditions list
    through expedited rulemaking within 12 months of the
    recommendation.

SEC. 7. EFFECTIVE DATE.

(a) PTSD AND HEARING LOSS.--Sections 2 and 3 shall take
    effect 180 days after the date of enactment.

(b) MUSCULOSKELETAL AND MST.--Sections 4 and 5 shall take
    effect 1 year after the date of enactment.

(c) SCIENTIFIC REVIEW BOARD.--Section 6 shall take effect
    upon enactment.
```

**Explanation**:

- **Section 2**: Builds on existing 38 U.S.C. 1154(b) combat presumption by simplifying the evidentiary standard for PTSD
- **Section 3**: Hearing loss and tinnitus are the two most commonly rated conditions; military noise exposure is well-documented and the nexus evidence requirement delays thousands of claims
- **Section 4**: Musculoskeletal conditions are the most commonly filed claims; the link between physically demanding MOSs and degenerative conditions is well-established
- **Section 5**: MST claims face unique evidentiary challenges because many incidents are unreported; the alternative evidence provision addresses this barrier
- **Section 6**: Creates a permanent scientific mechanism for updating presumptive conditions, preventing the decades-long delays experienced with Agent Orange

**Potential Challenges**:

| Challenge | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| Significant cost increase | Presumptive conditions reduce processing costs; total cost is the cost of wars that created these conditions |
| Risk of overinclusion | MOS-based criteria limit eligibility to veterans with documented exposure |
| Fraud concerns | Presumptive conditions still require medical diagnosis; fraud rate is below 1% |
| Congressional fiscal resistance | Phased effective dates spread costs; PACT Act precedent supports expansion |

### Veterans Appeals Accountability Act

**Purpose**: Reform the disability compensation appeals system by establishing statutory processing deadlines, creating binding precedent from BVA decisions, and expanding BVA capacity.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SEC. 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Veterans Appeals Accountability
Act".

SEC. 2. STATUTORY PROCESSING DEADLINES.

(a) SUPPLEMENTAL CLAIMS.--The Secretary shall complete
    adjudication of each supplemental claim not later than
    120 days after receipt.

(b) HIGHER-LEVEL REVIEWS.--The Secretary shall complete each
    higher-level review not later than 90 days after receipt.

(c) BOARD APPEALS.--The Board of Veterans' Appeals shall
    issue a decision on each appeal not later than--
    (1) 365 days for direct review appeals;
    (2) 365 days for evidence submission appeals; and
    (3) 545 days for hearing request appeals.

(d) REMANDS.--When the Board or the Court of Appeals for
    Veterans Claims remands a case to a regional office, the
    regional office shall complete re-adjudication not later
    than 90 days after receipt of the remand.

SEC. 3. PROVISIONAL BENEFITS FOR MISSED DEADLINES.

(a) AUTOMATIC TRIGGER.--If the Secretary or the Board fails
    to meet any deadline in section 2, the veteran shall
    receive provisional benefits at--
    (1) the last established rating, if any; or
    (2) a temporary rating of 30 percent if no prior rating
        exists, until the appeal is decided.

(b) NO RECOVERY.--If the final decision results in a lower
    rating than the provisional rating, the Secretary may
    not recover provisional overpayments.

SEC. 4. BINDING PRECEDENT.

(a) DESIGNATED PRECEDENTIAL DECISIONS.--The Chairman of the
    Board of Veterans' Appeals may designate any Board
    decision as a precedential decision binding on all
    regional offices and Board members on the legal
    question decided.

(b) PUBLICATION.--Precedential decisions shall be published
    and indexed in a searchable database accessible to
    veterans, representatives, and the public.

(c) CONSISTENCY.--Regional offices that depart from binding
    precedent in rating decisions shall document the
    factual basis for the departure. Departure on legal
    grounds shall not be permitted.

SEC. 5. BVA STAFFING.

(a) AUTHORIZATION.--The Board of Veterans' Appeals is
    authorized to employ not fewer than 200 Veterans Law
    Judges.

(b) HIRING TIMELINE.--The Chairman shall hire not fewer than
    25 additional Veterans Law Judges per year until the
    authorized level is reached.

(c) SUPPORT STAFF.--The Chairman is authorized to hire
    support staff proportional to the increase in Veterans
    Law Judges.

SEC. 6. QUALITY IMPROVEMENT.

(a) REMAND ANALYSIS.--The Under Secretary for Benefits shall
    analyze the reasons for remands from the Board and the
    Court of Appeals for Veterans Claims on a quarterly
    basis.

(b) CORRECTIVE ACTION.--When analysis identifies systemic
    error patterns, the Under Secretary shall issue
    corrective guidance to regional offices within 30 days
    and retrain affected staff within 90 days.

(c) ACCOUNTABILITY.--Regional offices with remand rates
    exceeding 150 percent of the national average for two
    consecutive quarters shall submit corrective action
    plans to the Under Secretary.

SEC. 7. EFFECTIVE DATE.

(a) DEADLINES.--Section 2 shall take effect 2 years after
    the date of enactment, to allow the Secretary to
    prepare implementation.

(b) BINDING PRECEDENT.--Section 4 shall take effect 1 year
    after the date of enactment.

(c) STAFFING AND QUALITY.--Sections 5 and 6 shall take
    effect upon enactment.
```

**Explanation**:

- **Section 2**: Deadlines are tighter than current average times but achievable with staffing increases and quality improvements
- **Section 3**: Provisional benefits create financial incentive for VA to meet deadlines; the no-recovery provision ensures veterans are not penalized for VA delays
- **Section 4**: Binding precedent eliminates the current problem where identical legal questions are decided differently by different regional offices, reducing both inconsistency and rework
- **Section 5**: Expanding BVA from 115 to 200 judges is proportional to the caseload increase since the AMA's implementation
- **Section 7**: Two-year implementation period for deadlines allows VA to build capacity

## State Model Legislation

### Veterans Disability Claims Assistance Act (State Model)

**Purpose**: Establish state-level programs to assist veterans in filing fully developed federal disability claims, reducing processing time and backlog contribution.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "[State] Veterans Disability
Claims Assistance Act".

SECTION 2. VETERANS DISABILITY CLAIMS ASSISTANCE PROGRAM.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.--The [State Department of Veterans
    Affairs/Veterans Commission] shall establish a Veterans
    Disability Claims Assistance Program.

(b) SERVICES.--The program shall provide--
    (1) claims filing assistance, including evidence
        gathering, documentation, and submission of fully
        developed claims to the United States Department
        of Veterans Affairs;
    (2) referral to accredited veterans service organization
        representatives and claims attorneys;
    (3) assistance obtaining state and local medical records,
        employment records, and other evidence relevant
        to disability claims;
    (4) preparation assistance for Compensation and Pension
        examinations; and
    (5) guidance on appeal options under the Appeals
        Modernization Act.

SECTION 3. OUTREACH.

(a) The [State Department of Veterans Affairs/Veterans
    Commission] shall conduct outreach to veterans in the
    State regarding disability benefits eligibility, with
    particular focus on--
    (1) rural veterans without proximity to VA regional
        offices;
    (2) veterans not enrolled in VA healthcare;
    (3) veterans of minority communities who have
        historically underutilized VA benefits;
    (4) veterans eligible for PACT Act presumptive
        conditions; and
    (5) elderly veterans who may have unclaimed service-
        connected conditions.

(b) Outreach shall be conducted through--
    (1) partnerships with county veterans service officers;
    (2) advertising through state media and social media
        channels;
    (3) coordination with state National Guard and Reserve
        units; and
    (4) participation in community events, health fairs,
        and veteran service fairs.

SECTION 4. STAFFING AND TRAINING.

(a) The program shall employ claims assistance specialists
    who are accredited by the United States Department of
    Veterans Affairs or who work under the supervision of
    accredited representatives.

(b) The [State Department of Veterans Affairs/Veterans
    Commission] shall provide annual training on federal
    VA disability compensation procedures, VASRD updates,
    and new presumptive conditions.

SECTION 5. REPORTING.

The [State Department of Veterans Affairs/Veterans Commission]
shall report annually to the [Governor/Legislature] on--
(1) the number of veterans assisted;
(2) claims outcomes for assisted veterans compared to
    state averages;
(3) outreach activities and populations reached; and
(4) recommendations for program improvement.

SECTION 6. APPROPRIATION.

There is appropriated from the general fund the sum of
$[amount] for the fiscal year ending [date] for the purposes
of this Act.
```

## Regulatory Framework

### VASRD Functional Impairment Rating Standards

**Existing Authority**: 38 U.S.C. 1155 grants the Secretary broad authority to "adopt and apply a schedule of ratings of reductions in earning capacity from specific injuries or combination of injuries." This authority extends to modifying the rating methodology.

**Draft Regulation**:

```text
PART 4--ADJUDICATION: FUNCTIONAL IMPAIRMENT STANDARDS

Section 4.1a Purpose.

This part establishes functional impairment standards to
supplement the existing rating criteria under part 4. These
standards ensure that disability evaluations reflect the
actual functional impact of service-connected conditions
on the veteran's daily life and occupational capacity.

Section 4.1b Functional Impairment Assessment.

(a) In evaluating the severity of a disability, the rating
    activity shall consider--
    (1) the veteran's ability to perform activities of
        daily living, including mobility, self-care,
        communication, and cognition;
    (2) the impact of the disability on occupational
        functioning across a range of employment types;
    (3) the impact of the disability on social functioning
        and quality of life; and
    (4) the veteran's own report of functional limitations,
        corroborated by medical evidence where available.

(b) Validated clinical instruments for measuring functional
    impairment shall be used where available, including but
    not limited to--
    (1) the World Health Organization Disability Assessment
        Schedule (WHODAS 2.0) for general functioning;
    (2) the PTSD Checklist (PCL-5) for mental health
        conditions;
    (3) standardized range-of-motion measurements for
        musculoskeletal conditions; and
    (4) audiometric testing for hearing conditions.

Section 4.1c Combined Rating Transparency.

(a) All rating decisions involving combined ratings under
    section 4.25 shall include a plain-language explanation
    of the combined value calculation.

(b) The rating decision shall state the combined rating
    percentage and the individual ratings that produced it.
```

### C&P Examination Quality Standards

**Existing Authority**: The Secretary's contracting authority and 38 U.S.C. 501 regulatory authority.

**Draft Regulation**:

```text
PART 3--ADJUDICATION: EXAMINATION STANDARDS

Section 3.3000 Minimum Examination Standards.

(a) MINIMUM EXAMINATION TIMES.--Compensation and Pension
    examinations shall meet the following minimum duration
    standards:
    (1) Mental health conditions (PTSD, depression,
        anxiety, TBI): 60 minutes
    (2) Musculoskeletal conditions: 45 minutes
    (3) Other single-condition evaluations: 30 minutes
    (4) Multi-condition evaluations: 30 minutes per
        additional condition beyond the first

(b) EXAMINER QUALIFICATIONS.--
    (1) Mental health examinations shall be conducted by
        a psychiatrist, psychologist, or licensed clinical
        social worker with documented training in military-
        related mental health conditions.
    (2) Musculoskeletal examinations shall be conducted by
        a physician, physician assistant, or nurse
        practitioner with documented orthopedic training.
    (3) Specialty examinations (TBI, toxic exposure) shall
        be conducted by clinicians with demonstrated
        expertise in the relevant condition.

(c) PERFORMANCE METRICS.--Contract examination providers
    shall maintain--
    (1) a sufficiency rate of not less than 95 percent
        (examinations returned as insufficient by rating
        staff);
    (2) a veteran satisfaction rate of not less than 75
        percent;
    (3) an appeal rate attributable to examination quality
        of not more than 5 percent.

Section 3.3001 Accountability.

(a) Contract examination providers failing to meet the
    standards in section 3.3000 for two consecutive quarters
    shall be required to submit a corrective action plan.

(b) Contract examination providers failing to meet standards
    for four consecutive quarters shall be subject to
    contract termination or non-renewal.

(c) Individual examiners receiving three or more sufficiency
    failures within a 12-month period shall be removed from
    the examination panel.
```

## Legal Considerations

### Constitutional Issues

**Due Process (Fifth Amendment)**:

- AI-assisted decision support must provide adequate notice and opportunity to be heard
- Veterans must be informed when AI tools are used in processing their claim and retain the right to request human-only review
- Bridge payments create due process *protections* for veterans, not burdens; they ensure financial security during processing delays
- Binding BVA precedent enhances due process by ensuring consistent legal standards

**Spending Clause**:

- All proposed legislation is within Congressional spending authority under Article I, Section 8
- Mandatory appropriations for bridge payments have precedent in other entitlement programs (Social Security, Medicare)
- Presumptive conditions expand eligibility under existing statutory authority; cost is a budget question, not a constitutional one

**War Powers and Military Powers**:

- Congress's authority to "raise and support Armies" (Art. I, Sec. 8, Cl. 12) and to "make Rules for the Government and Regulation of the land and naval Forces" (Art. I, Sec. 8, Cl. 14) provides broad authority over veteran benefits
- Veteran disability compensation has never been constitutionally challenged as beyond federal authority

### Preemption Questions

| State Action | Federal Impact |
|-------------|----------------|
| State claims assistance programs | Supplement federal system; no preemption issue |
| State-funded evidence gathering | Reduces federal development burden; no conflict |
| State veteran outreach | Increases claims volume but improves claim quality; net positive |
| State attorney fee regulation | May conflict with federal fee regulation under 38 U.S.C. 5904; preemption analysis required |

### Enforcement Mechanisms

| Provision | Enforcement |
|-----------|-------------|
| VASRD modernization deadlines | Congressional reporting requirements; appropriations leverage |
| Processing and appeals deadlines | Automatic provisional benefits trigger (self-enforcing) |
| Bridge payment program | Mandatory spending authority; advance appropriations |
| Presumptive conditions | Regulatory implementation under existing VA authority |
| C&P exam quality standards | Contract modification; performance-based contracting |
| Binding BVA precedent | Judicial review at CAVC for non-compliance |

### Sunset and Review Provisions

- VASRD Modernization Act includes ongoing 5-year review cycle (no sunset; ongoing obligation)
- Bridge Payment Act should be reviewed after 5 years to assess whether processing improvements have reduced the need
- Presumptive conditions are permanent once established but subject to Scientific Review Board updates
- Appeals deadlines should be reviewed after 3 years of implementation to assess feasibility

## Loopholes, Shortcomings, and Rectification

### VASRD Modernization Act

**Potential Loopholes**:

| Loophole | Description | Severity |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| Rulemaking delay | VA could delay rulemaking through prolonged notice-and-comment periods | High |
| Minimal updates | VA could publish technically compliant but substantively minimal updates that do not meaningfully modernize criteria | Medium |
| Advisory Panel capture | Advisory Panel could be dominated by VA insiders who resist functional impairment shift | Medium |
| Funding diversion | Appropriated funds could be redirected to general VBA operations | Low |

**Shortcomings**:

| Issue | Impact | Root Cause |
|-------|--------|------------|
| Does not address existing backlog | Modernization helps future claims but does not reduce current backlog | Scope limitation |
| Functional impairment measurement not standardized | No universally accepted functional impairment scale exists | Medical science limitation |
| Training gap | Thousands of claims processors must learn new criteria | Implementation challenge |

**Rectification Procedures**:

1. Add judicial enforcement provision allowing veterans to petition CAVC if VA misses rulemaking deadlines
2. Require that updated criteria demonstrate measurable improvement in rating consistency through pilot testing
3. Mandate that Advisory Panel include at least 50% external members (non-VA)
4. Ring-fence appropriations with anti-diversion provision

### Veterans Disability Claims Bridge Payment Act

**Potential Loopholes**:

| Loophole | Description | Severity |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| Preliminary estimate manipulation | VA could systematically underestimate preliminary ratings to minimize bridge payments | High |
| Processing slowdown incentive | Bridge payments remove pressure to process claims quickly for individual veterans | Medium |
| Claim volume gaming | Veterans could file claims primarily to receive bridge payments | Low |
| Reconciliation delays | Reconciliation of overpayments could take years | Medium |

**Shortcomings**:

| Issue | Impact | Root Cause |
|-------|--------|------------|
| Does not cover appeals | Veterans awaiting appeal decisions do not receive bridge payments | Scope limitation |
| Fiscal cost uncertainty | Total cost depends on processing speeds and claims volume | Budget unpredictability |
| Denied claim recovery | Recovery from denied claimants may be difficult | Administrative challenge |

**Rectification Procedures**:

1. Require preliminary estimates to be generated by validated algorithm rather than individual adjudicator judgment
2. Pair bridge payments with processing deadline incentives (Section 3 of Appeals Accountability Act)
3. Extend bridge payment eligibility to appeals pending more than 365 days
4. Cap annual bridge payment expenditures with automatic processing acceleration triggers if caps are approached

### Expanded Presumptive Conditions Act

**Potential Loopholes**:

| Loophole | Description | Severity |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| MOS list manipulation | VA could narrow the qualifying MOS lists to limit eligibility | High |
| Diagnostic gaming | Veterans could seek PTSD or other diagnoses primarily for presumptive benefit | Medium |
| Scientific Review Board inaction | Board could fail to recommend new conditions due to institutional inertia | Medium |
| Alternative evidence standard (MST) | Alternative evidence standard could be applied inconsistently | Medium |

**Shortcomings**:

| Issue | Impact | Root Cause |
|-------|--------|------------|
| Significant cost increase | $5-10 billion annually in new benefit payments | Fiscal reality |
| Does not cover all deserving conditions | Limited initial list may exclude legitimate conditions | Political compromise |
| MOS-based criteria may miss some veterans | Veterans in non-listed MOSs with genuine exposure not covered | Categorical limitation |

**Rectification Procedures**:

1. Require MOS lists to be developed in consultation with VSOs and published for public comment
2. Establish diagnostic integrity standards requiring clinicians to use validated instruments
3. Set statutory deadlines for Scientific Review Board recommendations
4. Allow individual nexus evidence for veterans in non-qualifying MOSs with documented exposure

### Veterans Appeals Accountability Act

**Potential Loopholes**:

| Loophole | Description | Severity |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| Quality sacrifice for speed | VA may rush decisions to avoid provisional benefits | High |
| Strategic denial | Denying appeals quickly to meet timeline, knowing veterans will refile | High |
| Receipt date gaming | Manipulating when appeals are officially "received" to extend timelines | Medium |
| Precedent avoidance | BVA Chairman could avoid designating precedential decisions on contested questions | Medium |

**Shortcomings**:

| Issue | Impact | Root Cause |
|-------|--------|------------|
| Speed-quality tension | Mandatory timelines may reduce decision quality | Incentive design |
| BVA hiring challenge | Finding and training 85 additional Veterans Law Judges takes years | Workforce constraint |
| Does not reform legacy appeals | 35,000+ legacy appeals not covered by new deadlines | Scope limitation |

**Rectification Procedures**:

1. Link processing deadlines to quality requirements (accuracy below 90% suspends timeline penalties for affected office)
2. Add anti-gaming provisions defining "receipt date" as the earliest of electronic submission, mail postmark, or in-person delivery
3. Require minimum number of precedential decisions per year (e.g., 20)
4. Include legacy appeals under the remand resolution timeline after a 1-year transition period

### General Implementation Concerns

**Systemic Issues**:

| Issue | Proposed Solution |
|-------|------------------|
| All legislation requires sustained Congressional appropriations commitment | Make key funding streams mandatory rather than discretionary |
| IT modernization has high historical failure rate at VA | Require agile development methodology and independent oversight |
| VA employee union resistance to automation and process changes | Engage AFGE early; include worker retraining provisions; frame as job improvement |
| Multiple bills must be coordinated for systemic impact | Pass as omnibus Veterans Disability Compensation Reform Act |

**Ongoing Monitoring Requirements**:

| Requirement | Frequency | Responsible Entity |
|-------------|-----------|---------------------|
| Claims processing metrics | Quarterly | VA Benefits Administration |
| VASRD revision progress | Semi-annually | VASRD Modernization Office |
| Bridge payment costs and utilization | Quarterly | VA CFO |
| Appeals deadline compliance | Quarterly | BVA |
| C&P exam quality metrics | Quarterly | VBA Quality Assurance |
| Presumptive conditions utilization | Annually | VA Benefits Administration |
| AI system bias audits | Annually | VA OIT with independent auditors |
