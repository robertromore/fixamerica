# Veteran Suicide Prevention: Roadmap

## Overview

This roadmap outlines a 10-year strategy to reduce the veteran suicide rate from its current level (33.9 per 100,000, 2021) to at or below the civilian age-adjusted rate (17.1 per 100,000). The strategy is organized in three phases: Foundation (Years 1-2), Expansion (Years 3-5), and Transformation (Years 5-10). Each phase builds on the previous one, with early actions focused on immediately actionable, high-impact interventions and later phases addressing structural and cultural changes that require longer timelines.

## Phase 1: Foundation (Years 1-2)

### Objective

Deploy the most immediately impactful interventions while building infrastructure for longer-term reform.

### Action Table

| Action | Lead Agency | Timeline | Cost (Annual) | Priority |
|--------|-------------|----------|---------------|----------|
| Expand gun lock distribution to all VA and Vet Center sites | VA OMHSP | Months 1-6 | $25M | Critical |
| Launch emergency bridge payment program for pending disability claims | VBA | Months 1-6 | $200M | Critical |
| Establish fast-track disability claims for veterans with suicidal ideation | VBA | Months 1-6 | $50M | Critical |
| Standardize safety planning at all VA mental health encounters | VA OMHSP | Months 1-12 | $15M | High |
| Pilot voluntary temporary firearm storage in 10 states | VA + NSSF + law enforcement | Months 3-12 | $30M | High |
| Increase Fox Grant Program funding to $300M | VA OCE | Months 1-12 | $226M (increase) | High |
| Launch real-time surveillance pilot with 20 states | VA + CDC | Months 6-18 | $40M | High |
| Begin 12-month transition reform pilot at 5 military installations | DOD + VA | Months 6-24 | $100M | Medium |
| Hire 500 additional VA peer support specialists | VA | Months 1-18 | $50M | Medium |
| Deploy mobile crisis teams in 200 highest-risk rural counties | VA + SAMHSA | Months 6-24 | $80M | Medium |

### Phase 1 Milestones

- Emergency bridge payments operational within 6 months
- Gun lock distribution capacity doubled within 6 months
- Temporary storage pilot agreements signed in 10 states within 12 months
- Fox Grant Program grants awarded to 200+ organizations within 12 months
- Transition reform pilot launched at 5 installations within 18 months
- Real-time surveillance data flowing from 20 states within 18 months

## Phase 2: Expansion (Years 3-5)

### Objective

Scale successful pilots to national coverage; implement legislative reforms; build community infrastructure.

### Action Table

| Action | Lead Agency | Timeline | Cost (Annual) | Priority |
|--------|-------------|----------|---------------|----------|
| National rollout of temporary firearm storage network | VA + NSSF + community | Years 3-4 | $80M | Critical |
| Expand Fox Grant Program to $750M; coordinators in all counties | VA OCE | Years 3-5 | $750M | Critical |
| Implement 12-month transition process at all installations | DOD + VA | Years 3-5 | $1.2B | Critical |
| Extend TRICARE to 12 months post-separation | DOD | Year 3 | $400-500M | High |
| Achieve 30-day average disability claims processing | VBA | Years 3-5 | $500M | High |
| Launch universal screening incentive for civilian providers | CMS + VA | Years 3-4 | $300M | High |
| Expand real-time surveillance to all 50 states | VA + CDC | Years 3-4 | $50M | High |
| Post-attempt follow-up at all hospitals receiving federal funds | HHS + VA | Years 3-5 | $200M | Medium |
| Complete rural broadband expansion for veteran telehealth | USDA + FCC + VA | Years 3-5 | $200M | Medium |
| Establish national peer support network | VA + VSOs | Years 3-5 | $100M | Medium |

### Phase 2 Milestones

- 5,000+ temporary storage locations operational nationally
- Veteran suicide prevention coordinator in every U.S. county
- Zero healthcare coverage gap during military-to-civilian transition
- Disability claims backlog eliminated
- Near-real-time data reporting from all 50 states
- 90% of rural veterans with telehealth access

## Phase 3: Transformation (Years 5-10)

### Objective

Achieve sustained reduction in veteran suicide rate through cultural change, institutional reform, and continuous improvement.

### Action Table

| Action | Lead Agency | Timeline | Cost (Annual) | Priority |
|--------|-------------|----------|---------------|----------|
| Sustain community prevention infrastructure | VA + states | Ongoing | $750M | Critical |
| Integrate veteran screening into all civilian healthcare | CMS + VA | Years 5-7 | $200M | High |
| Achieve cultural shift: help-seeking normalized in veteran community | VA + VSOs + DOD | Years 5-10 | $50M | High |
| Deploy predictive analytics across VA and civilian systems | VA + HHS | Years 5-8 | $100M | Medium |
| Evaluate and refine all programs based on real-time data | VA OMHSP | Ongoing | $75M | Medium |
| Establish permanent DOD post-separation accountability (5-year tracking) | DOD + VA | Years 5-7 | $50M | Medium |
| Achieve veteran suicide rate at or below civilian rate | All | Year 10 | N/A | Ultimate goal |

### Phase 3 Milestones

- Veteran suicide rate below 25 per 100,000 by Year 7
- Veteran suicide rate at or below civilian rate by Year 10
- Help-seeking stigma reduced: 70%+ of veterans report willingness to seek mental health care
- Universal screening identifies and connects 90%+ of at-risk veterans

## Legislative Requirements

### Federal Legislation Needed

| Bill | Purpose | Phase | Likelihood |
|------|---------|-------|------------|
| Veterans Transition Reform Act | Mandate 12-month transition process; extend TRICARE 12 months | Phase 1-2 | Moderate (bipartisan support likely) |
| Veteran Lethal Means Safety Act | Fund national voluntary storage network; NSSF partnership | Phase 1-2 | Low-Moderate (firearms lobby opposition) |
| Veteran Suicide Prevention Accountability Act | Real-time surveillance; outcome-based metrics; DOD accountability | Phase 1-2 | Moderate (data transparency popular) |
| Community Veteran Prevention Act | Increase Fox Grant to $750M; county coordinators | Phase 2 | Moderate (spending concerns) |
| Disability Claims Modernization Act | AI-assisted processing; bridge payments; presumptive conditions | Phase 1-2 | Moderate-High (bipartisan) |
| Universal Veteran Screening Act | CMS incentives for civilian provider screening | Phase 2-3 | Low-Moderate (privacy concerns) |

## Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation Strategy |
|------|------------|--------|-------------------|
| Firearms lobby blocks lethal means legislation | High | High | Pursue voluntary programs, executive action, and state-level legislation; engage NSSF as alternative to NRA |
| VA bureaucratic resistance to community-based model | High | Medium | Create incentive structures; protect VA workforce while expanding community complement |
| Insufficient workforce for community expansion | Medium | High | Invest in training pipelines; use veteran peers as force multiplier; leverage telehealth |
| Budget cuts reduce prevention funding | Medium | High | Build bipartisan coalition; frame as cost-saving (preventing downstream VA, criminal justice, and social costs) |
| Data system interoperability failures | Medium | Medium | Phased implementation; use existing CDC infrastructure; invest in state modernization |
| Cultural resistance to help-seeking persists | High | Medium | Veteran-led messaging; peer support expansion; leadership modeling; generational change |
| DOD refuses extended transition responsibility | Medium | High | Legislative mandate; budget earmarks; frame as readiness issue (healthy veterans support recruitment) |
