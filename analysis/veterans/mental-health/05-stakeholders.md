# Veteran Mental Health: Stakeholders

## Who Is Affected

### Primary Stakeholders

| Group | Impact | Size |
|-------|--------|------|
| **Veterans with mental health conditions** | Treatment access, quality, outcomes | ~4.8 million (estimated)[^1] |
| **Veterans receiving VA mental health treatment** | Wait times, provider continuity, therapy options | 1.7 million[^2] |
| **Veterans' families** | Secondary trauma, caregiver burden, family stability | ~10 million household members |
| **VA mental health clinicians** | Caseloads, burnout, compensation, working conditions | ~23,658[^3] |
| **Post-9/11 combat veterans** | High PTSD/TBI rates, complex co-occurring conditions | ~4.1 million[^4] |
| **Veterans with MST** | Specialized treatment needs, trust barriers | ~150,000 in VA care[^5] |
| **Rural veterans** | Geographic access barriers, provider shortages | ~1.2 million with mental health needs[^6] |

### Veteran Subpopulations

| Subpopulation | Distinct Mental Health Needs | Size |
|---------------|------------------------------|------|
| Women veterans | MST, perinatal mental health, gender-specific trauma | ~734,000 enrolled |
| Vietnam-era veterans | Late-onset PTSD, aging and depression, Agent Orange | ~5.9 million surviving |
| Post-9/11 veterans | Polytrauma, TBI + PTSD, multiple deployments | ~4.1 million |
| Reserve/Guard | Transition challenges, intermittent deployment | ~1.1 million |
| OTH discharge veterans | Restricted VA eligibility despite clinical need | ~500,000 post-9/11 |
| Homeless veterans | Co-occurring mental health and substance use | ~33,000 on any night |
| Justice-involved veterans | Untreated conditions leading to legal involvement | ~107,000 incarcerated |
| LGBTQ+ veterans | Identity-based stressors, discrimination history | ~1 million estimated |

### Secondary Stakeholders

| Group | Connection | Interest |
|-------|------------|----------|
| **Community mental health providers** | Receive VA community care referrals | Revenue, clinical competence |
| **DoD mental health system** | Pre-separation treatment, transition coordination | Continuity of care |
| **Veteran service organizations** | Advocacy, peer support, program delivery | Member welfare |
| **Medical schools and training programs** | Produce mental health workforce | Pipeline, curriculum |
| **Employers** | Veterans' mental health affects workplace functioning | Retention, accommodation |
| **Criminal justice system** | Interacts with untreated veteran mental health | Diversion, treatment courts |
| **Pharmaceutical industry** | Psychotropic medication development and sales | Market access |

## Underrepresented Groups

| Group | Why Underrepresented | Impact |
|-------|---------------------|--------|
| **OTH discharge veterans** | Excluded from many VA services | ~500,000 post-9/11 with limited mental health access |
| **Male MST survivors** | Severe stigma, underreporting | ~1.5% of male veterans screen positive; many more affected |
| **Incarcerated veterans** | No organized advocacy, lost VA eligibility during incarceration | ~107,000 in state/federal prison |
| **LGBTQ+ veterans** | Historical exclusion, ongoing stigma | Elevated mental health risk, underutilization |
| **Minority veterans** | Cultural barriers to VA mental health engagement | Disparities in treatment access and outcomes |
| **Veterans of limited English proficiency** | Language barriers to treatment | Concentrated among immigrant veterans |
