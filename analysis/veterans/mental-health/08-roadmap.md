# Veteran Mental Health: Implementation Roadmap

## Strategic Approach

### Theory of Change

Veteran mental health reform requires addressing both system capacity and cultural barriers simultaneously:

1. **Workforce first**: Mental health providers must be available before other reforms can take effect
2. **Stigma reduction**: Cultural change enables veterans to use the capacity being built
3. **Multiple entry points**: Different veterans need different pathways into treatment
4. **Integration**: Co-occurring conditions demand coordinated treatment, not separate silos
5. **Transition bridge**: The highest-risk period must be addressed through DoD-VA coordination

### Political Strategy

| Element | Approach |
|---------|----------|
| **Bipartisan appeal** | Veteran mental health has broad political support |
| **Suicide prevention framing** | Connect mental health access to suicide prevention |
| **Military family advocacy** | Families are powerful advocates with wide appeal |
| **Veteran storytelling** | Personal narratives overcome ideological resistance |
| **Cost-effectiveness argument** | Treatment is cheaper than downstream consequences |

### Key Leverage Points

| Leverage Point | Opportunity |
|----------------|-------------|
| Annual VA appropriations | Increase mental health-specific funding |
| COMPACT Act expansion | Permanent telehealth authorities |
| DoD transition policy | Mandate comprehensive mental health screening |
| VA rulemaking | Expand OTH discharge mental health eligibility |
| PACT Act funding | Mental health capacity for newly eligible veterans |

### Federal Actions

| Action | Lead Actor | Mechanism | Timeline |
|--------|------------|-----------|----------|
| Emergency psychiatry hiring authority | VA Secretary | Administrative action | Q1 2025 |
| Psychiatry pay parity implementation | Congress + VA | Legislation + rulemaking | 2025-2026 |
| Telemental health device distribution | VA + FCC | Administrative + grants | 2025-2026 |
| Anti-stigma campaign launch | VA + DoD | Funded program | 2025 |
| Mandatory transition mental health assessment | DoD | DoD directive | 2025-2026 |
| Peer support specialist expansion | VA | Hiring authority | 2025-2027 |

### State and Community Actions

| Action | Lead Actor | Model |

| State licensing compacts for telemental health | State legislatures | COMPACT Act state adoption |

## Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|------------|
| National psychiatry shortage limits hiring | High | High | Prescribing psychologists, telehealth, pipeline investment |
| Stigma reduction stalls | Medium | High | Veteran-led campaigns, military leadership engagement |
| DoD resists transition screening mandate | Medium | High | Congressional mandate, executive order |
| Budget constraints limit investment | Medium | High | Cost-effectiveness argument, bipartisan coalition |
| Community care providers resist competence requirements | Medium | Medium | Phase in requirements, provide free training |
| Complementary therapy evidence challenged | Low | Medium | Rigorous outcome tracking, conservative claims |
| OTH discharge expansion faces political opposition | Medium | Medium | Suicide risk data, veteran storytelling |

### Offsetting Savings

| Source | Estimated Savings (10-year) |
|--------|----------------------------|
| Reduced downstream crisis costs (ER, hospitalization) | $3-5B |
| Reduced homelessness services | $1-2B |
| Reduced incarceration costs | $500M-1B |
| Reduced disability compensation growth | $2-4B |
| Reduced community care referrals | $1-2B |
| **Total estimated offsets** | **$7.5-14B** |

### Net Investment

Net 10-year cost: approximately $7-13 billion above current spending trajectory, or $700M-1.3B per year in additional appropriations.
