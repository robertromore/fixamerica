# VA Reform: Legislation and Legal Framework

## Overview

Reforming the Department of Veterans Affairs requires legislation addressing governance instability, workforce competitiveness, technology modernization, community care management, and infrastructure investment. The legislative approach in this document focuses on structural reforms that create lasting institutional change rather than crisis-driven patches. Each bill targets a specific root cause identified in [Root Causes](04-root-causes.md) and implements solutions from [Solutions](07-solutions.md).

## Federal Legislation

### VA Governance Stability Act

**Purpose**: Establish an independent, bipartisan governance board to provide strategic oversight and continuity across political transitions, addressing the fundamental problem of leadership instability (average Secretary tenure of 2.5 years since 2009).

**Draft Text**:

```text
SEC. 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Veterans Affairs Governance
Stability Act".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds the following:
(1) The Department of Veterans Affairs has had 8 Secretaries
    since 2009, with an average tenure of approximately 2.5
    years, insufficient for sustained multi-year reform.
(2) Major reform initiatives, including electronic health
    record modernization, community care implementation,
    and workforce development, have been disrupted by
    leadership transitions.
(3) The lack of strategic continuity across administrations
    has prevented the VA from executing long-term
    institutional reform.
(4) Other federal entities with critical long-term missions,
    including the Federal Reserve, United States Postal
    Service, and Amtrak, operate with independent governance
    boards that provide strategic continuity.
(5) Veterans who rely on the VA for healthcare, disability
    compensation, and other essential services are harmed
    by the instability of VA leadership and strategic
    direction.

SEC. 3. ESTABLISHMENT OF VA GOVERNANCE BOARD.

(a) IN GENERAL.--There is established within the Department
    of Veterans Affairs a Veterans Affairs Governance Board
    (in this Act referred to as the "Board").

(b) COMPOSITION.--The Board shall consist of 11 members as
    follows:
    (1) 5 members appointed by the President, by and with
        the advice and consent of the Senate;
    (2) 2 members appointed by the Speaker of the House of
        Representatives, of whom 1 shall be from the majority
        party and 1 from the minority party;
    (3) 2 members appointed by the President pro tempore of
        the Senate, of whom 1 shall be from the majority
        party and 1 from the minority party; and
    (4) 2 members selected by a consensus of the 6 largest
        Veterans Service Organizations by membership, who
        shall be veterans.

(c) QUALIFICATIONS.--
    (1) Each member shall have demonstrated expertise in at
        least one of the following: healthcare administration,
        information technology, federal agency management,
        veterans' services, or organizational reform.
    (2) Not fewer than 6 members shall be veterans.
    (3) No member shall be a registered lobbyist or have
        been employed by a VA contractor within the preceding
        3 years.

(d) TERMS.--
    (1) Members shall serve staggered 6-year terms.
    (2) Initial appointments shall be divided into three
        classes of approximately equal size, serving initial
        terms of 2, 4, and 6 years respectively.
    (3) No member may serve more than 2 consecutive terms.

(e) COMPENSATION.--Members shall receive compensation at the
    rate of Level IV of the Executive Schedule for each day
    during which they are engaged in Board duties.

SEC. 4. POWERS AND DUTIES OF THE BOARD.

(a) STRATEGIC OVERSIGHT.--The Board shall--
    (1) approve a 5-year strategic plan for the Department,
        to be updated not less frequently than every 2 years;
    (2) review and approve major information technology
        investments exceeding $500,000,000;
    (3) review and approve facility construction, closure,
        or realignment decisions affecting facilities serving
        more than 10,000 enrolled veterans;
    (4) establish performance benchmarks for the Secretary
        and senior leadership;
    (5) publish an annual assessment of VA performance
        against the strategic plan; and
    (6) review community care access standards and recommend
        adjustments based on clinical outcomes and cost data.

(b) LIMITATIONS.--The Board shall not--
    (1) direct day-to-day operations of the Department;
    (2) make individual personnel decisions below the level
        of Under Secretary;
    (3) adjudicate individual veteran claims or appeals; or
    (4) override clinical decisions made by VA healthcare
        providers.

(c) RELATIONSHIP TO SECRETARY.--
    (1) The Secretary retains full operational authority for
        the Department.
    (2) The Secretary shall consult with the Board on
        matters within the Board's purview before taking
        final action.
    (3) If the Secretary declines to follow a Board
        recommendation, the Secretary shall provide written
        justification to the Board and to the Committees on
        Veterans' Affairs of the House and Senate within
        30 days.

SEC. 5. STRATEGIC PLAN REQUIREMENTS.

(a) CONTENTS.--The strategic plan required under section
    4(a)(1) shall include--
    (1) workforce targets, including vacancy rate goals,
        compensation benchmarks, and recruitment strategies;
    (2) technology modernization milestones with cost and
        performance metrics;
    (3) infrastructure priorities based on veteran
        population distribution and facility condition;
    (4) community care utilization targets based on
        evidence of clinical outcomes and cost-effectiveness;
    (5) veteran access and quality targets with measurable
        benchmarks; and
    (6) budget requirements to achieve plan objectives.

(b) PUBLIC AVAILABILITY.--The strategic plan and annual
    performance assessments shall be made publicly available
    on the Department's website.

SEC. 6. ANNUAL REPORT.

The Board shall submit to Congress and make publicly available
an annual report including--
(1) an assessment of VA performance against strategic plan
    benchmarks;
(2) recommendations for legislative or administrative
    action;
(3) an evaluation of major IT investments;
(4) community care spending trends and quality comparison
    with VA direct care; and
(5) workforce metrics including vacancy rates, turnover,
    and compensation competitiveness.

SEC. 7. EFFECTIVE DATE.

(a) This Act shall take effect on the date of enactment.
(b) Initial Board members shall be appointed not later than
    180 days after the date of enactment.
(c) The first strategic plan shall be adopted not later than
    1 year after the Board achieves a quorum.
```

**Explanation**:

- **Section 3**: The 11-member structure balances executive, Congressional, and veteran representation with bipartisan requirements
- **Section 3(c)**: Qualification requirements and anti-conflict provisions ensure board competence and independence
- **Section 3(d)**: Staggered 6-year terms ensure continuity regardless of election cycles
- **Section 4**: Powers are focused on strategic oversight, not day-to-day operations, reducing conflict with the Secretary
- **Section 4(c)**: The "comply or explain" mechanism preserves Secretarial authority while creating accountability
- **Section 5**: Strategic plan requirements ensure the board addresses the specific reform priorities identified in this analysis

**Potential Challenges**:

| Challenge | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| Executive branch resistance to independent board | Board is advisory with strategic oversight, not operational control; Secretary retains day-to-day authority |
| Senate confirmation delays for board members | Staggered terms and quorum provisions ensure board can function during vacancies |
| Board capture by interest groups | Anti-lobbying and anti-contractor provisions; veteran majority requirement |
| Duplicating Congressional oversight | Board provides institutional continuity that Congressional committees cannot; complements rather than replaces oversight |

**Refinements**:

- **Stronger version**: Give the Board binding authority over IT investments exceeding $1 billion and facility decisions
- **Fallback version**: Advisory-only board with no binding authority but public reporting requirements
- **Sunset provision**: 10-year authorization with reauthorization required

### VA Workforce Competitiveness Act

**Purpose**: Close the compensation gap between VA and private-sector clinical and technical positions, establish workforce pipeline programs, and reduce employee burnout through adequate staffing requirements.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SEC. 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Veterans Affairs Workforce
Competitiveness Act".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds the following:
(1) The Department of Veterans Affairs has an overall vacancy
    rate of 11.2 percent, representing approximately 52,000
    unfilled positions.
(2) VA physician salaries average 20 to 30 percent below
    private-sector equivalents in most specialties.
(3) The VA nursing vacancy rate of 10.2 percent exceeds the
    national nursing vacancy rate.
(4) Employee burnout rates at the VA increased from 35
    percent in 2019 to 44 percent in 2024.
(5) Workforce shortages and burnout directly contribute to
    increased wait times, reduced care quality, and
    reliance on higher-cost community care.

SEC. 3. MARKET-RATE COMPENSATION PROGRAM.

(a) BIENNIAL SURVEY.--The Secretary of Veterans Affairs
    shall conduct a biennial survey of compensation for
    all VA clinical and technical positions, comparing
    VA rates to the median compensation for equivalent
    positions in the private sector and other federal
    agencies, by specialty and geographic area.

(b) COMPENSATION TARGET.--
    (1) The Secretary shall set VA compensation for clinical
        and technical positions at not less than 90 percent
        of the market median identified in the survey under
        subsection (a).
    (2) For critical shortage specialties designated by the
        Secretary, compensation shall be set at not less
        than 95 percent of market median.

(c) GEOGRAPHIC DIFFERENTIALS.--The Secretary shall establish
    geographic pay differentials to address recruitment
    challenges in--
    (1) high-cost metropolitan areas; and
    (2) rural and underserved areas with persistent
        vacancies exceeding 15 percent for any clinical
        specialty.

(d) RETENTION INCENTIVES.--The Secretary shall establish
    retention bonuses for VA employees who have completed
    not fewer than 5 years of VA service, structured as
    follows:
    (1) 5 to 10 years of service: not less than 10 percent
        of base pay;
    (2) 10 to 20 years of service: not less than 15 percent
        of base pay; and
    (3) more than 20 years of service: not less than 20
        percent of base pay.

SEC. 4. VA SCHOLAR PROGRAM.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.--The Secretary shall establish the VA
    Scholar Program to provide full tuition coverage at
    accredited medical, nursing, and allied health programs
    in exchange for a VA service commitment.

(b) SERVICE OBLIGATION.--Each participant shall serve as a
    full-time VA employee for a period equal to the duration
    of the scholarship, but not less than 3 years.

(c) PRIORITY PLACEMENT.--Participants shall receive priority
    placement at VA facilities in rural or underserved
    areas with the highest vacancy rates.

(d) TARGET ENROLLMENT.--The program shall enroll not fewer
    than--
    (1) 500 participants in the first year;
    (2) 1,000 participants in the second year; and
    (3) 2,000 participants annually thereafter.

SEC. 5. VA CLINICAL RESIDENCY EXPANSION.

(a) The Secretary shall establish VA-specific clinical
    residency programs at not fewer than 30 academic
    medical centers affiliated with the VA.

(b) Residents completing VA residency programs shall receive
    guaranteed VA employment offers.

(c) Residency slots shall be targeted to specialties with
    the highest VA vacancy rates.

SEC. 6. STAFFING REQUIREMENTS.

(a) MINIMUM STAFFING RATIOS.--The Secretary shall establish
    minimum staffing ratios for clinical positions at VA
    medical centers, based on--
    (1) patient volume and acuity;
    (2) evidence-based staffing models; and
    (3) comparison with industry standards.

(b) MANDATORY OVERTIME LIMITS.--
    (1) No VA clinical employee shall be required to work
        more than 16 hours of overtime per pay period,
        except in declared emergencies.
    (2) Facilities exceeding the overtime limit for 3
        consecutive months shall be required to submit
        staffing corrective action plans to the Under
        Secretary for Health.

SEC. 7. EDUCATION DEBT REDUCTION PROGRAM EXPANSION.

(a) The annual authorization for the Education Debt
    Reduction Program under section 7683 of title 38,
    United States Code, is increased to $750,000,000.

(b) Maximum individual awards are increased to $250,000
    over 5 years.

SEC. 8. REPORTING.

The Secretary shall report annually to the Committees on
Veterans' Affairs on--
(1) vacancy rates by facility, specialty, and geographic
    area;
(2) compensation competitiveness relative to market survey;
(3) VA Scholar Program enrollment and placement;
(4) employee burnout and satisfaction metrics; and
(5) mandatory overtime utilization.

SEC. 9. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS.

There are authorized to be appropriated--
(1) $3,500,000,000 for fiscal year 2027;
(2) $4,000,000,000 for fiscal year 2028;
(3) $4,500,000,000 for fiscal year 2029;
(4) $5,000,000,000 for fiscal year 2030; and
(5) $5,000,000,000 for fiscal year 2031.
```

**Explanation**:

- **Section 3**: Market-rate benchmarking ensures VA compensation adjusts automatically with market conditions rather than requiring new legislation each cycle
- **Section 4**: The VA Scholar Program builds a dedicated pipeline, addressing the long-term structural workforce challenge
- **Section 6**: Minimum staffing ratios address the root cause of burnout (understaffing), not just its symptoms
- **Section 7**: Expanded debt reduction leverages the mission-driven appeal of VA service for clinical graduates with educational debt

**Potential Challenges**:

| Challenge | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| High cost ($3.5-5B annually) | Investment reduces community care spending ($30.8B and growing) by enabling VA direct care |
| Federal pay system disruption | VA already has Title 38 exceptions; this extends an established model |
| Union resistance to staffing ratios | Engage unions in ratio development; ratios protect workers, not threaten them |
| Private-sector recruitment competition | 90-95% of market median plus federal benefits and mission appeal is competitive |

**Refinements**:

- **Phased implementation**: Start with top 10 shortage specialties and expand annually
- **Performance linkage**: Tie retention bonuses to facility performance metrics
- **Rural incentive enhancement**: Additional 10% bonus for rural facility service

### VA Electronic Health Record Accountability Act

**Purpose**: Establish independent oversight and minimum safety and performance standards for the VA's electronic health record modernization, preventing further waste and patient harm.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SEC. 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Veterans Affairs Electronic
Health Record Accountability Act".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds the following:
(1) The VA awarded a contract for electronic health record
    modernization in 2018 with an initial value of
    $10,000,000,000, which has grown to a projected cost
    of $16,100,000,000 as of 2025.
(2) After 7 years, the new electronic health record has
    been deployed to only 5 of 171 VA medical centers.
(3) The VA Office of Inspector General documented 149 or
    more patient safety incidents at deployed sites linked
    to the new electronic health record system.
(4) VA clinicians report that the new system requires
    significantly more time per patient encounter than the
    legacy VistA system.
(5) The Government Accountability Office has designated the
    EHR modernization a high-risk program with significant
    management challenges.

SEC. 3. INDEPENDENT EHR ASSESSMENT COMMISSION.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.--There is established an independent
    Veterans EHR Assessment Commission (in this section
    referred to as the "Commission").

(b) COMPOSITION.--The Commission shall consist of 7
    members, as follows:
    (1) 2 members appointed by the Secretary of Veterans
        Affairs;
    (2) 2 members appointed by the Chairpersons and
        Ranking Members of the Committees on Veterans'
        Affairs, 1 from each chamber;
    (3) 1 member who is a VA clinician, selected by the
        VA's clinical advisory council;
    (4) 1 member who is a health information technology
        expert, selected by the National Academy of
        Medicine; and
    (5) 1 member who is a veteran and current VA patient.

(c) ASSESSMENT.--Not later than 9 months after the date
    of enactment, the Commission shall submit to Congress
    and the Secretary a report including--
    (1) a comprehensive comparison of clinical safety,
        usability, and cost between the Oracle Health
        system and the legacy VistA system at deployed
        sites;
    (2) an assessment of whether the Oracle Health system
        can meet VA clinical and operational requirements
        within a reasonable cost and timeline;
    (3) an evaluation of alternative approaches, including
        VistA modernization, hybrid models, and other
        commercial platforms;
    (4) recommended minimum safety and usability standards
        that must be met before further deployment; and
    (5) a recommended path forward with cost projections
        and milestones.

SEC. 4. DEPLOYMENT STANDARDS.

(a) MINIMUM STANDARDS.--No further deployment of the
    electronic health record system to additional VA
    medical centers shall occur unless the Secretary
    certifies to the Committees on Veterans' Affairs
    that the system meets the following standards at
    all currently deployed sites:
    (1) zero critical patient safety incidents attributable
        to the EHR system in the preceding 90 days;
    (2) clinician time per encounter at or below the
        average for equivalent encounters using VistA;
    (3) medication error rates at or below VistA baseline;
    (4) scheduling accuracy at or below VistA baseline
        error rates; and
    (5) interoperability with the Department of Defense
        MHS GENESIS system for the categories of data
        specified by the Secretary.

(b) CLINICIAN ADVISORY BOARD.--
    (1) The Secretary shall establish a Clinician Advisory
        Board for EHR modernization consisting of not
        fewer than 15 VA clinicians from diverse
        specialties and facility types.
    (2) The Clinician Advisory Board shall review and
        approve deployment readiness for each site.
    (3) No deployment shall proceed over the objection of
        a majority of the Clinician Advisory Board.

SEC. 5. CONTRACT ACCOUNTABILITY.

(a) PERFORMANCE METRICS.--The Secretary shall renegotiate
    the Oracle Health contract to include performance
    metrics tied to the standards in section 4(a), with
    financial penalties for failure to meet standards.

(b) SUNK COST PROHIBITION.--In determining whether to
    continue, modify, or terminate the Oracle Health
    contract, the Secretary shall not consider previously
    expended funds as justification for continued
    investment.

(c) TERMINATION AUTHORITY.--If the Commission under
    section 3 determines that the Oracle Health system
    cannot meet VA requirements within a reasonable
    cost and timeline, the Secretary shall have authority
    to terminate the contract for convenience without
    additional Congressional authorization.

SEC. 6. REPORTING.

The Secretary shall submit quarterly reports to the
Committees on Veterans' Affairs on--
(1) EHR deployment status and timeline;
(2) patient safety metrics at deployed sites;
(3) clinician satisfaction and usability data;
(4) cost expenditures against projections; and
(5) Commission and Clinician Advisory Board findings.

SEC. 7. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS.

There are authorized to be appropriated $15,000,000 for the
Commission established under section 3.
```

**Explanation**:

- **Section 3**: Independent assessment addresses the information asymmetry that has allowed the EHR program to continue despite poor performance
- **Section 4(a)**: Minimum deployment standards create concrete, measurable criteria that must be met, replacing subjective readiness determinations
- **Section 4(b)**: Clinician Advisory Board veto power ensures end-user buy-in, addressing a root cause of EHR failures
- **Section 5(b)**: The sunk cost prohibition is essential; $16.1 billion already spent should not compel continued investment in a failing program

**Potential Challenges**:

| Challenge | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| Oracle Health contract termination liability | Negotiate performance-based terms; termination for convenience is standard in federal contracts |
| Delay in modernization | Better to delay than deploy an unsafe system; VistA continues to function |
| Clinician Advisory Board could block all deployment | Majority vote requirement prevents individual obstruction; clinical expertise is essential |
| Commission may produce inconclusive findings | Clear terms of reference and 9-month deadline prevent indefinite study |

### VA Community Care Quality and Cost Management Act

**Purpose**: Establish quality monitoring, cost controls, and evidence-based access standards for VA community care to ensure it complements rather than undermines VA direct care.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SEC. 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Veterans Affairs Community Care
Quality and Cost Management Act".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds the following:
(1) Community care spending under the VA MISSION Act grew
    from $13,800,000,000 in fiscal year 2018 to
    $30,800,000,000 in fiscal year 2024, an increase of
    123 percent.
(2) Community care now represents 35 percent of VHA
    healthcare spending, up from 22 percent in fiscal
    year 2018, without a deliberate strategic decision
    by Congress or the VA to shift this balance.
(3) Community care providers are not currently required to
    report quality data comparable to the VA's Strategic
    Analytics for Improvement and Learning (SAIL) system.
(4) Research indicates that VA healthcare quality exceeds
    private-sector benchmarks for most comparable
    conditions, particularly veteran-specific conditions.
(5) Community care costs per encounter are generally
    15 to 30 percent higher than VA direct care for
    comparable services.

SEC. 3. EVIDENCE-BASED ACCESS STANDARDS.

(a) STUDY.--Not later than 18 months after the date of
    enactment, the Secretary shall commission an independent
    study comparing clinical outcomes, cost, and veteran
    satisfaction for VA direct care versus community care,
    disaggregated by--
    (1) condition type;
    (2) geographic area;
    (3) facility type; and
    (4) veteran demographic characteristics.

(b) REVISED ACCESS STANDARDS.--Based on the study under
    subsection (a), the Secretary shall establish revised
    community care access standards that--
    (1) designate conditions for which VA demonstrates
        superior clinical outcomes as "VA-preferred,"
        with community care available only when VA access
        standards for wait time or drive time are not met;
    (2) designate conditions for which VA and community
        care demonstrate equivalent outcomes as "veteran
        choice," allowing the veteran to choose either
        option; and
    (3) designate conditions for which the VA does not
        provide specialized care as "community-default,"
        with streamlined community care referral.

(c) VETERAN CHOICE.--Nothing in this section shall be
    construed to prevent a veteran from accessing community
    care when VA access standards for wait time or drive
    time are not met, regardless of condition designation.

(d) REVIEW.--Access standards shall be reviewed and updated
    not less frequently than every 3 years based on
    updated outcomes data.

SEC. 4. COMMUNITY CARE QUALITY REPORTING.

(a) REQUIREMENTS.--As a condition of participation in the
    VA community care network, providers shall report
    quality metrics comparable to the VA SAIL system,
    including--
    (1) clinical outcome measures;
    (2) patient safety indicators;
    (3) patient experience measures; and
    (4) care coordination metrics, including timeliness of
        records sharing with the VA.

(b) QUALITY BENCHMARKS.--
    (1) The Secretary shall establish minimum quality
        benchmarks for continued community care network
        participation.
    (2) Providers failing to meet benchmarks for 2
        consecutive reporting periods shall be placed on
        probation.
    (3) Providers failing to meet benchmarks for 4
        consecutive reporting periods shall be removed
        from the community care network.

(c) PUBLIC REPORTING.--Community care quality data shall be
    made publicly available alongside VA SAIL data, enabling
    veteran comparison of VA and community care quality.

SEC. 5. COST MANAGEMENT.

(a) COST COMPARISON.--The Secretary shall publish annual
    cost comparisons between VA direct care and community
    care by condition type and geographic area.

(b) COST-EFFECTIVENESS THRESHOLD.--When VA direct care is
    both available within access standards and costs less
    than community care for the same condition, VA direct
    care shall be the default recommendation, unless the
    veteran and referring clinician agree that community
    care is in the veteran's best medical interest.

(c) SPENDING TRAJECTORY REPORTING.--The Secretary shall
    report quarterly to Congress on community care spending
    as a percentage of total VHA healthcare spending, with
    projections for the current and subsequent 2 fiscal
    years.

SEC. 6. CARE COORDINATION REQUIREMENTS.

(a) RECORDS SHARING.--Community care providers shall
    transmit clinical records to the VA within 5 business
    days of each encounter.

(b) CARE COORDINATION.--The VA shall assign a care
    coordinator to each veteran receiving community care
    for ongoing treatment.

(c) FOLLOW-UP.--VA shall conduct follow-up contact with
    each veteran within 14 days of a community care
    encounter to ensure continuity of care.

SEC. 7. EFFECTIVE DATE.

(a) Quality reporting requirements under section 4 shall
    take effect 1 year after the date of enactment.
(b) Revised access standards under section 3 shall take
    effect upon completion of the study and rulemaking,
    but not later than 3 years after the date of enactment.
```

**Explanation**:

- **Section 3**: Evidence-based access standards replace the current one-size-fits-all approach with condition-specific defaults based on clinical outcomes
- **Section 3(c)**: Preserves veteran choice when VA access standards are not met; this is not a restriction on access but a default based on evidence
- **Section 4**: Quality reporting creates accountability for community care providers comparable to what VA facilities face
- **Section 5(b)**: Cost-effectiveness threshold prevents the community care "death spiral" where resources shift to higher-cost private care, degrading VA capacity

**Potential Challenges**:

| Challenge | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| Private healthcare industry opposition | Quality reporting and cost transparency benefit veterans; industry resistance reveals lack of confidence in their quality |
| "Restricting veteran choice" framing | Veterans retain full choice when VA access standards are not met; defaults are based on clinical evidence, not ideology |
| Data collection burden on community providers | Phased implementation; data infrastructure investment included in regulatory framework |
| MISSION Act statutory conflict | This Act amends and refines MISSION Act access standards; supersedes where conflicting |

### VA Advance Appropriations Expansion Act

**Purpose**: Extend advance appropriations to all VA operations, ending the disruption caused by continuing resolutions and enabling multi-year planning.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SEC. 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Veterans Affairs Advance
Appropriations Expansion Act".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds the following:
(1) The Veterans Health Care Budget Reform and Transparency
    Act of 2009 (Public Law 111-81) provided advance
    appropriations for VA medical care, significantly
    improving budget certainty for healthcare delivery.
(2) VA operations not covered by advance appropriations,
    including claims processing, information technology,
    and construction, have been disrupted by continuing
    resolutions in 5 of the last 8 fiscal years.
(3) Continuing resolutions prevent the VA from executing
    multi-year hiring plans, technology investments, and
    construction projects.
(4) The mismatch between advance-appropriated medical care
    and annually-appropriated operations creates planning
    dysfunction within the Department.

SEC. 3. ADVANCE APPROPRIATIONS FOR ALL VA OPERATIONS.

(a) Section 117 of title 38, United States Code, is amended
    to include the following additional accounts for advance
    appropriations:
    (1) Veterans Benefits Administration, General Operating
        Expenses;
    (2) Information Technology Systems;
    (3) Construction, Major Projects;
    (4) Construction, Minor Projects;
    (5) Office of Inspector General; and
    (6) General Administration.

(b) ADVANCE APPROPRIATIONS PERIOD.--Advance appropriations
    for accounts listed in subsection (a) shall be provided
    for the fiscal year following the fiscal year for which
    the regular appropriations Act is enacted, consistent
    with the advance appropriations period for medical care
    accounts.

SEC. 4. MULTI-YEAR PLANNING REQUIREMENT.

(a) The Secretary shall submit with each annual budget
    request a 3-year spending plan for all accounts
    receiving advance appropriations.

(b) The plan shall include projected staffing levels,
    technology milestones, and construction priorities.

SEC. 5. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act shall take effect beginning with the first fiscal
year commencing after the date of enactment.
```

**Explanation**:

- **Section 3**: Extends the proven advance appropriations model from medical care to all VA operations
- **Section 3(b)**: One-year advance appropriation provides budget certainty without excessively constraining Congressional discretion
- **Section 4**: Multi-year planning requirement ensures the VA uses advance appropriations for strategic investment, not just spending certainty

**Potential Challenges**:

| Challenge | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| Appropriations committee resistance | Advance appropriations for VA medical care has worked since 2009; this extends a proven model |
| Reduced Congressional leverage | Congress retains authority to set advance appropriation levels; certainty benefits both parties |
| Does not increase total spending | Correct; this is a timing reform, not a spending increase |

## State Model Legislation

### Veterans Healthcare Navigation Act (State Model)

**Purpose**: Establish state-level programs to help veterans navigate VA healthcare, community care options, and benefits coordination.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "[State] Veterans Healthcare
Navigation Act".

SECTION 2. VETERANS HEALTHCARE NAVIGATION PROGRAM.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.--The [State Department of Veterans
    Affairs] shall establish a Veterans Healthcare
    Navigation Program to assist veterans in this State
    in accessing and coordinating healthcare services
    from the Department of Veterans Affairs and
    community care providers.

(b) SERVICES.--The program shall provide--
    (1) assistance to veterans in understanding and
        exercising their healthcare options under the
        VA MISSION Act, including VA direct care and
        community care;
    (2) care coordination support for veterans receiving
        both VA and community care;
    (3) assistance with VA enrollment, appointment
        scheduling, and telehealth access;
    (4) outreach to eligible veterans not currently
        enrolled in VA healthcare, particularly--
        (A) rural veterans;
        (B) veterans not currently using VA services; and
        (C) veterans eligible for expanded benefits under
            the PACT Act.
```

## Regulatory Framework

### Revised Community Care Access Standards

**Existing Authority**: 38 U.S.C. Section 1703 (as amended by the MISSION Act) authorizes the Secretary to establish access standards for community care eligibility.

**Draft Regulation**:

```text
PART 17--COMMUNITY CARE: ACCESS STANDARDS

Section 17.4040 Access Standards for Community Care.

(a) WAIT TIME STANDARDS.--
    (1) Primary care: 20 calendar days from date of
        request.
    (2) Specialty care: 28 calendar days from date of
        request.
    (3) Mental health: 20 calendar days from date of
        request, or 14 calendar days for urgent mental
        health needs.

(b) DRIVE TIME STANDARDS.--
    (1) Primary care: 30 minutes average drive time.
    (2) Specialty care: 60 minutes average drive time.
    (3) Mental health: 30 minutes average drive time, or
        available via VA telehealth.

(c) CONDITION-BASED DEFAULTS.--
    (1) VA-PREFERRED CONDITIONS.--For conditions where VA
        demonstrates superior clinical outcomes, including
        but not limited to PTSD, traumatic brain injury,
        spinal cord injury, amputee care, and military
        sexual trauma, community care shall be authorized
        only when access standards in subsections (a) and
        (b) are not met.
    (2) VETERAN-CHOICE CONDITIONS.--For conditions where
        VA and community care demonstrate equivalent
        outcomes, the veteran and referring clinician may
        agree on the most appropriate care setting.
    (3) COMMUNITY-DEFAULT CONDITIONS.--For conditions for
        which the VA does not provide specialized care at
        the relevant facility, community care referral
        shall be streamlined.

(d) TELEHEALTH CONSIDERATION.--When a veteran can be served
    within access standards via VA telehealth, telehealth
    shall be offered before community care referral for
    applicable conditions.
```

### VA Workforce Compensation Standards

**Existing Authority**: 38 U.S.C. Sections 7401-7464 (Title 38 hiring and compensation authorities); RAISE Act (Public Law 118-81, 2024).

**Draft Regulation**:

```text
PART 17--WORKFORCE: COMPENSATION STANDARDS

Section 17.5000 Market-Rate Compensation Survey.

(a) The Under Secretary for Health shall conduct a biennial
    survey of market compensation for all VHA clinical and
    technical positions.

(b) The survey shall compare VA compensation to median
    compensation for equivalent positions in--
    (1) private-sector healthcare systems in the same
        geographic area;
    (2) other federal agencies; and
    (3) academic medical centers affiliated with the VA.

Section 17.5001 Compensation Adjustments.

(a) When the survey under section 17.5000 identifies a
    compensation gap exceeding 10 percent below market
    median for any position or specialty, the Under
    Secretary shall propose compensation adjustments
    within 90 days.

(b) Critical shortage specialties with vacancy rates
    exceeding 15 percent shall receive priority
    compensation adjustments.
```

## Legal Considerations

### Constitutional Issues

**Appointments Clause (Article II, Section 2)**:

- The Governance Board members appointed by the President require Senate confirmation under the Appointments Clause
- Congressional appointees serve as inferior officers and do not require Senate confirmation if Congress prescribes their appointment
- VSO-selected members require careful structuring to avoid Appointments Clause challenges; they should be structured as advisors or inferior officers

**Spending Clause**:

- All appropriations in these bills are within Congress's spending authority
- Advance appropriations are an established practice (VA medical care since 2009) and raise no constitutional issues

**Executive Authority**:

- The Governance Board is structured to advise rather than direct, avoiding separation-of-powers concerns
- The Secretary retains operational authority, with a "comply or explain" mechanism rather than binding board decisions

### Preemption Questions

| State Action | Federal Impact |
|-------------|----------------|
| State healthcare navigation programs | Supplement federal system; no preemption issue |
| State veteran healthcare quality reporting | May require coordination with VA data systems |
| State community care coordination | Complements federal MISSION Act framework |

### Enforcement Mechanisms

| Provision | Enforcement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Governance Board strategic plan | Annual report to Congress; public accountability |
| Workforce compensation targets | Biennial survey and reporting; Congressional oversight |
| EHR deployment standards | Secretary certification required; Clinician Advisory Board veto |
| Community care quality reporting | Network participation conditioned on compliance |
| Advance appropriations | Budget process; Congressional appropriations authority |

## Loopholes, Shortcomings, and Rectification

### VA Governance Stability Act

**Potential Loopholes**:

| Loophole | Description | Severity |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| Board appointment delays | President or Congress could delay appointments, leaving board non-functional | High |
| "Comply or explain" evasion | Secretary could provide pro forma justifications for ignoring board recommendations | Medium |
| Board capture | Board members could prioritize interest group preferences over veteran interests | Medium |
| Strategic plan as formality | Board could produce vague strategic plans that do not meaningfully constrain VA direction | Medium |

**Shortcomings**:

| Issue | Impact | Root Cause |
|-------|--------|------------|
| Does not address Secretary tenure directly | Leadership instability continues even with board oversight | Political appointment structure unchanged |
| Board has no operational authority | Cannot directly fix day-to-day VA problems | Design trade-off to preserve Secretarial authority |
| Does not guarantee board expertise | Qualification requirements may be interpreted loosely | Appointment discretion |

**Rectification Procedures**:

1. Add automatic quorum provisions: if fewer than 7 members are confirmed within 180 days, confirmed members constitute a quorum
2. Require Secretarial justifications for declining board recommendations to be published in the Federal Register
3. Add an ethics code for board members with financial disclosure and recusal requirements
4. Require strategic plan benchmarks to be specific, measurable, and time-bound

### VA Workforce Competitiveness Act

**Potential Loopholes**:

| Loophole | Description | Severity |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| Compensation survey manipulation | Market survey methodology could be designed to understate gaps | Medium |
| Pipeline program underfunding | Congress could authorize programs but not appropriate sufficient funds | High |
| Staffing ratio exemptions | VA could claim exemptions from minimum ratios due to budget constraints | Medium |
| Retention bonus absorption | Retention bonuses could be offset by slower base pay growth | Low |

**Shortcomings**:

| Issue | Impact | Root Cause |
|-------|--------|------------|
| High cost ($3.5-5B annually) | May face appropriations resistance | Structural underfunding of VA workforce |
| Does not address non-compensation factors | Some recruitment barriers are non-financial (location, bureaucracy) | Scope limitation |
| Pipeline takes years to produce results | 3-5 year lag before pipeline programs generate significant workforce additions | Time inherent in clinical education |

**Rectification Procedures**:

1. Require independent validation of market survey methodology by the Bureau of Labor Statistics
2. Make workforce pipeline funding mandatory rather than discretionary
3. Add non-financial recruitment incentives (expedited hiring, remote work, flexible schedules) as companion provisions
4. Establish interim contract staffing authority to bridge the gap while pipeline programs mature

### VA Electronic Health Record Accountability Act

**Potential Loopholes**:

| Loophole | Description | Severity |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| Deployment standards gaming | VA could define metrics narrowly to achieve technical compliance without meaningful safety improvement | High |
| Commission capture | Commission members with industry ties could favor continued Oracle Health investment | Medium |
| Clinician Advisory Board obstruction | Board could unreasonably block all deployment out of VistA preference | Medium |
| Sunk cost override | Future Congress could waive the sunk cost prohibition under industry pressure | Medium |

**Shortcomings**:

| Issue | Impact | Root Cause |
|-------|--------|------------|
| Does not fund alternatives | If Oracle Health is canceled, no funding for VistA modernization or replacement | Scope limitation |
| 9-month assessment may be insufficient | Complex technical assessment may require more time | Timeline constraint |
| Does not address DOD interoperability mandate | EHR reform must consider DOD MHS GENESIS compatibility | Interagency complexity |

**Rectification Procedures**:

1. Specify deployment metric definitions in the legislation rather than leaving to VA interpretation
2. Require Commission financial disclosure and recusal provisions for members with industry connections
3. Establish automatic authorization for VistA modernization funding if the Commission recommends Oracle Health cancellation
4. Require DOD-VA coordination on interoperability through a joint working group

### VA Community Care Quality and Cost Management Act

**Potential Loopholes**:

| Loophole | Description | Severity |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| Quality reporting gaming | Community providers could game metrics similar to VA wait-time gaming in 2014 | High |
| Access standard classification disputes | Disagreements over which conditions are "VA-preferred" vs. "veteran choice" could delay implementation | Medium |
| Cost comparison methodology | Different cost accounting methods could produce misleading comparisons | Medium |
| Network adequacy | If too many providers are removed for quality failures, network gaps could emerge | Low |

**Shortcomings**:

| Issue | Impact | Root Cause |
|-------|--------|------------|
| Does not cap community care spending | Spending could continue growing even with quality standards | Scope limitation |
| Study requirement delays implementation | 18-month study plus rulemaking could take 3+ years total | Analytical requirement |
| Does not address care coordination technology | Quality standards require data infrastructure that may not exist | Implementation gap |

**Rectification Procedures**:

1. Require independent auditing of community care quality data, modeled on the VA's STAR program
2. Establish interim access standards that can be applied while the comprehensive study is underway
3. Include appropriations for community care data infrastructure development
4. Add a community care spending growth trigger requiring Congressional review if spending exceeds 40% of VHA healthcare spending

### Cross-Cutting Issues

**General Implementation Concerns**:

- All five bills require sustained Congressional attention beyond initial passage; oversight provisions are essential
- Workforce and technology reforms have multi-year implementation timelines; impatient political environments may undermine follow-through
- The privatization-investment political dynamic could distort implementation of any bill regardless of legislative intent
- VA's organizational culture resistance to change may slow even well-designed reforms

**Ongoing Monitoring Requirements**:

| Requirement | Frequency | Responsible Entity |
|-------------|-----------|---------------------|
| Governance Board annual performance report | Annually | VA Governance Board |
| Workforce vacancy and compensation report | Annually | VA Human Resources |
| EHR deployment safety and usability report | Quarterly | VA OIT / Clinician Advisory Board |
| Community care quality and cost comparison | Annually | VA Office of Community Care |
| Advance appropriations utilization report | Annually | VA CFO |
| Comprehensive VA reform progress assessment | Biennially | GAO |
