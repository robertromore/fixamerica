# VA Reform: Roadmap

## Phase 1: Immediate Actions (0-12 Months)

### Quick Wins

**Community Care Quality Monitoring**

- Implement quality reporting requirements for community care providers
- Establish veteran satisfaction surveys for community care encounters
- Begin cost comparison analysis between VA direct care and community care by condition type
- Target: Baseline community care quality data within 6 months

**Employee Burnout Response**

- Reduce mandatory overtime at facilities with highest burnout rates
- Implement flexible scheduling and expanded telework where clinically appropriate
- Launch employee engagement pilot programs at 20 facilities
- Target: Reduce burnout rate from 44% to 38% within 12 months

**Digital Services Consolidation**

- Accelerate migration from legacy portals (eBenefits, MyHealtheVet) to VA.gov
- Launch unified mobile app for veterans
- Improve claims tracking and appointment scheduling interfaces
- Target: Single veteran-facing portal for 80% of services

### Foundation Building

**Governance Board Proposal**

- Draft legislation for VA Governance Board
- Build bipartisan Congressional support
- Engage VSOs and veteran advocacy groups in board design
- Target: Bill introduced in 119th Congress

**EHR Modernization Independent Review**

- Commission independent assessment of Oracle Health deployment results
- Compare clinician usability, patient safety, and cost metrics vs. VistA
- Evaluate hybrid modernization options
- Target: Independent review completed within 9 months

**Workforce Competitiveness Assessment**

- Conduct comprehensive market-rate survey for all VA clinical and technical positions
- Identify critical shortage specialties and geographic areas
- Develop phased compensation adjustment plan
- Target: Assessment and plan completed within 6 months

## Phase 2: Short-Term Goals (1-2 Years)

### Governance Reform

**VA Governance Board Establishment**

- Pass governance board legislation
- Appoint initial board members with staggered terms
- Board adopts first multi-year strategic plan
- Target: Board operational within 18 months of legislation

**Advance Appropriations Expansion**

- Extend advance appropriations to VBA operations, IT, and construction
- Enable multi-year hiring and procurement planning
- Target: Legislation enacted; first advance appropriation for FY 2028

### Workforce Investment

**Compensation Competitiveness Phase 1**

- Implement market-rate adjustments for top 10 shortage specialties
- Expand geographic pay differentials for high-cost and rural areas
- Increase Education Debt Reduction Program funding by 50%
- Target: Close compensation gap to within 10% of market median for critical specialties

**Workforce Pipeline Launch**

- Establish 10 new VA Scholar programs at medical and nursing schools
- Create VA-specific clinical residency tracks at 20 academic affiliates
- Launch community college partnerships for administrative positions
- Target: Pipeline producing 2,000 additional VA-committed graduates annually by Year 3

### Technology

**EHR Modernization Reset**

- Implement revised deployment strategy based on independent review
- Establish clinician advisory board with veto power over deployment decisions
- Define minimum safety and usability standards for each deployment site
- Renegotiate Oracle Health contract based on performance
- Target: Clear go/no-go criteria for next deployment wave

**Evidence-Based Access Standards**

- Complete comprehensive VA vs. community care outcomes study
- Publish evidence-based access standards by condition type
- Implement revised MISSION Act access standards through regulatory process
- Target: New standards operational within 24 months

## Phase 3: Medium-Term Goals (2-5 Years)

### Institutional Transformation

**Leadership Stability**

- If governance board is operational, push for Secretary term appointment legislation
- Board develops 10-year strategic vision transcending political transitions
- Career executive development program creates deep bench of VA leaders
- Target: First term-appointed Secretary (if legislation passes)

**Organizational Restructuring**

- Integrate VHA, VBA, and NCA data systems for seamless veteran experience
- Create cross-functional veteran service teams combining health, benefits, and support
- Streamline regional structures to match veteran population distribution
- Target: Veterans interact with "one VA" rather than three separate bureaucracies

### Workforce Maturation

**Compensation Competitiveness Full Implementation**

- All VA positions within 10% of market median
- Retention bonuses fully implemented for experienced staff
- Workforce vacancy rate reduced from 11.2% to below 6%
- Target: VA ranked as "employer of choice" for clinical and technical talent

**Burnout Sustained Reduction**

- Minimum staffing ratios implemented across clinical positions
- Employee burnout rate below 30%
- Turnover rate below 8%
- Target: VA employee satisfaction exceeds government-wide average

### Technology Modernization

**EHR Deployment (Revised)**

- EHR (Oracle Health or revised platform) deployed to 50+ sites with confirmed safety and usability
- Interoperability with DOD MHS GENESIS achieved for deployed sites
- Clinician satisfaction with EHR at or above VistA baseline
- Target: On track for system-wide deployment by Year 8-10

**Integrated Veteran Platform**

- Single veteran-facing platform for all VA services
- Fully integrated with community care provider systems
- Real-time care coordination between VA and community providers
- Target: 90% of veteran interactions through unified platform

## Phase 4: Long-Term Transformation (5-10 Years)

### Goal: A Modern, Sustainable VA

**Target Metrics**:

| Metric | Current (FY 2024) | Phase 4 Target |
|--------|-------------------|----------------|
| Primary care wait (new patient) | 28 days | <14 days |
| Specialty care wait (new patient) | 44 days | <21 days |
| Workforce vacancy rate | 11.2% | <5% |
| Employee burnout rate | 44% | <25% |
| Veteran trust score | 77.9% | >85% |
| Community care as % of VHA spending | 35% | 25-30% (evidence-based) |
| EHR deployment | 5 sites | All 171 medical centers |
| Infrastructure backlog | $52 billion | <$15 billion |

### Structural Outcomes

- Governance board providing strategic continuity across administrations
- Evidence-based balance between VA direct care and community care
- Modern, interoperable electronic health record system
- Competitive, sustainable workforce with low turnover
- Modernized infrastructure matching veteran population distribution
- Seamless veteran experience across all VA services

## Risks and Contingencies

### Risk: Political Reversal

**Threat**: New administration reverses reform direction

**Mitigation**: Governance board provides continuity; advance appropriations provide funding stability

**Contingency**: Board preserves strategic plan even if Secretary changes priorities

### Risk: EHR Modernization Failure

**Threat**: Oracle Health cannot be made safe and functional

**Mitigation**: Independent review provides objective assessment; hybrid option preserves VistA

**Contingency**: Cancel Oracle Health contract; invest in open-source VistA modernization

### Risk: Privatization Acceleration

**Threat**: Community care expansion defunds VA direct care

**Mitigation**: Evidence-based access standards; community care quality and cost monitoring

**Contingency**: Congressional action to cap community care spending as percentage of VHA budget

### Risk: Workforce Recruitment Failure

**Threat**: Unable to close compensation gaps and attract talent

**Mitigation**: Pipeline programs; loan repayment; mission-driven recruitment

**Contingency**: Expand telehealth and regional sharing; targeted contract staffing for acute shortages
