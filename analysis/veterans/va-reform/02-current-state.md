# VA Reform: Current State

|-----------|------|---------------------|-----------------------|

*Source: VA FY 2025 Budget Submission; VA Human Resources, FY 2024.*

## Community Care (MISSION Act)

### Overview

The VA MISSION Act (Public Law 115-182, 2018) consolidated and expanded veterans' access to private-sector healthcare through the VA Community Care program, replacing the problematic Veterans Choice Program.

### Eligibility Criteria

Veterans can access community care when:

1. The VA cannot provide the needed service
2. The VA does not operate a medical facility in the veteran's state
3. The veteran qualifies based on designated access standards (wait time >20 days for primary care, >28 days for specialty care; drive time >30 minutes for primary care, >60 minutes for specialty care)
4. The veteran and referring clinician agree community care is in the veteran's best interest

### Community Care Utilization

| Metric | FY 2021 | FY 2022 | FY 2023 | FY 2024 |
|--------|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| Community care appointments | 31.8M | 36.4M | 40.1M | 43.2M |
| Community care spending | $19.8B | $23.5B | $27.4B | $30.8B |
| Share of total VA healthcare spending | 28% | 31% | 33% | 35% |
| Unique veterans using community care | 3.1M | 3.5M | 3.8M | 4.0M |

*Source: VA Office of Community Care, FY 2021-2024; VA FY 2025 Budget Submission.*

Community care spending has grown approximately 56% in three years, raising concerns about long-term fiscal sustainability and the balance between VA direct care and private-sector care.

### Community Care Challenges

- **Care coordination**: Fragmented records between VA and community providers create continuity-of-care gaps
- **Cost growth**: Community care costs per encounter are generally higher than VA direct care
- **Quality variation**: Community providers may lack expertise in veteran-specific conditions (PTSD, TBI, military sexual trauma)
- **Scheduling**: Third-party administrators (TriWest, Optum) responsible for scheduling face their own delays
- **Payment delays**: Community providers report payment delays of 60-90+ days, reducing willingness to participate

## Electronic Health Record (EHR) Modernization

### Background

The VA's legacy electronic health record system, VistA, was developed in-house beginning in the 1980s and was once considered a model health IT system. In 2018, the VA awarded a $10 billion contract to Cerner Corporation (now Oracle Health) to replace VistA with a commercial EHR that would be interoperable with the Department of Defense's MHS GENESIS system.

### Current Status

| Metric | Status (January 2025) |
|--------|----------------------|
| Contract value | $16.1 billion (revised from $10 billion) |
| Sites deployed | 5 VA medical centers (of 171) |
| Deployment paused | Yes (since April 2023) |
| Patient safety incidents linked to EHR | 149+ documented (VA OIG, 2024) |
| Scheduled completion | Unknown; original 2028 target abandoned |
| GAO assessment | "High-risk program with significant management challenges" |

*Source: VA OIG EHR Modernization Reports, 2022-2024; GAO-24-106605, 2024.*

### Problems Identified

- **Patient safety**: The VA OIG documented 149+ patient safety incidents at deployed sites, including medication errors, missed referrals, and scheduling failures (VA OIG Report 23-01239-167, 2024)
- **Clinician burden**: VA clinicians reported that the Oracle Health system required significantly more time per patient encounter than VistA, reducing throughput
- **Cost overruns**: The projected cost has increased from $10 billion to $16.1 billion, with further increases expected
- **Interoperability gaps**: The stated goal of interoperability with DOD's MHS GENESIS has not been fully achieved at deployed sites
- **Deployment pause**: VA Secretary McDonough paused deployment in April 2023 pending resolution of safety and performance issues

## Workforce

### Staffing Overview

| Category | FY 2022 | FY 2023 | FY 2024 | Vacancy Rate (FY 2024) |
|----------|---------|---------|---------|----------------------|
| Physicians | 37,200 | 38,100 | 38,800 | 8.5% |
| Registered Nurses | 75,400 | 76,200 | 77,100 | 10.2% |
| Mental Health Professionals | 18,500 | 19,800 | 20,400 | 12.8% |
| All VHA clinical | 285,000 | 295,000 | 305,000 | 10.8% |
| All VA (total) | 395,000 | 405,000 | 412,000 | 11.2% |

*Source: VA Human Resources, FY 2022-2024; VA OIG Staffing Report, 2024.*

### Workforce Challenges

- **Vacancy rates**: The overall 11.2% vacancy rate means approximately 52,000 positions are unfilled across the VA
- **Compensation gaps**: VA physician salaries average 20-30% below private-sector equivalents in most specialties (VA Office of Academic Affiliations, 2024)
- **Geographic disparities**: Rural VA facilities face the most severe recruitment challenges, with some specialties unfilled for years
- **Burnout**: VA employee burnout rates increased from 35% in 2019 to 44% in 2024 (VA All Employee Survey, 2024)
- **Accountability**: The VA Accountability and Whistleblower Protection Act (2017) was intended to make it easier to fire underperforming employees, but implementation has been controversial and inconsistent

### Hiring Authorities

The VA has several special hiring authorities not available to most federal agencies:

| Authority | Purpose | Utilization |
|-----------|---------|-------------|
| Title 38 direct hire | Physicians, nurses, dentists without competitive process | Active; primary clinical hiring mechanism |
| Hybrid Title 38 | Certain clinical and technical positions | Active |
| RAISE Act (2024) | Increased pay caps for critical specialties | Recently enacted |
| Education Debt Reduction Program | Loan repayment for clinical staff | Active; $1.4B obligated through FY 2024 |

*Source: VA Human Resources; RAISE Act (Public Law 118-81, 2024).*

## Recent Developments (2024-2025)

### Policy Changes

- **EHR modernization reset**: The VA conducted a comprehensive reassessment of the Oracle Health deployment in 2024, resulting in a revised deployment strategy and additional contract modifications
- **PACT Act implementation**: Continued expansion of toxic exposure benefits processing, with 5.5+ million claims filed under the PACT Act through December 2024
- **MISSION Act community care expansion**: Community care utilization continued growing, with community care spending projected to exceed 35% of VHA healthcare spending
- **RAISE Act implementation**: New pay authorities for clinical specialties to address recruitment gaps

### Organizational Changes

- **New Secretary**: Doug Collins confirmed as VA Secretary in early 2025, bringing new priorities and potential policy shifts
- **DOGE influence**: The Department of Government Efficiency (DOGE) initiative has targeted VA for potential restructuring and cost reduction
- **Workforce concerns**: Union tensions over potential workforce reductions and organizational restructuring

### Ongoing Challenges

- EHR modernization remains paused at most sites; no clear timeline for resumption
- Community care spending growth threatens to crowd out investment in VA direct care
- Clinical vacancy rates remain above 10%, particularly in rural areas and mental health
- The VA's aging physical infrastructure requires an estimated $52 billion in construction and renovation (VA Office of Construction and Facilities Management, 2024)

## The Gap Between Problem and Action

| The Problem | Current Response | The Gap |
|-------------|-----------------|---------|
| 11.2% vacancy rate | Special hiring authorities, debt reduction program | Compensation still below market; hiring process too slow |
| EHR modernization failure | Deployment pause, contract renegotiation | No clear path forward; billions spent with limited return |
| Community care cost growth | MISSION Act access standards | Standards may be too permissive; driving unsustainable spending |
| Declining access metrics | Wait-time targets, telehealth expansion | Wait times increasing despite targets |
| Secretary turnover (8 in 16 years) | No structural solution | Political appointment system inherently unstable |
| $52B infrastructure backlog | Annual construction appropriations (~$2.7B) | At current pace, would take 19 years to address |
| Burnout (44% of employees) | Wellness programs | Does not address root causes: understaffing, mandatory overtime |
