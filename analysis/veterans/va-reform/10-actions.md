# VA Reform: Actions

## What You Can Do

The VA's structural challenges--leadership instability, workforce shortages, IT modernization failures, and the unresolved privatization debate--require sustained public engagement. Veterans, citizens, and organizations can all contribute to meaningful reform. The following actions are organized by audience and level of effort.

---

## For Veterans

### Engage With the VA System

**Use and Evaluate VA Healthcare**

- If eligible, enroll in VA healthcare and use VA services to maintain the system's patient base and relevance
- Provide honest feedback through VA patient satisfaction surveys (SHEP) and the Veterans Experience Office
- Report access problems, safety concerns, and quality issues through the VA patient advocate at your facility
- Use telehealth options when available to reduce wait times and expand access

**Participate in VA Governance**

- Attend town halls and listening sessions hosted by your local VA medical center
- Provide input during VA rulemaking comment periods, particularly on community care access standards and EHR modernization
- Join veteran advisory committees at your local VAMC or Veterans Integrated Service Network (VISN)
- Share your experience with VA care, both positive and negative, to inform reform debates

**File Complaints When the System Fails**

- Contact the VA Office of Inspector General hotline (1-800-488-8244) to report waste, fraud, or patient safety concerns
- File complaints with the VA Patient Advocate at your facility for care quality or access issues
- Contact your Congressional representative's casework office if VA is unresponsive to your concerns
- Use the White House Veterans Complaint Hotline for urgent issues

### If You Are Choosing Between VA and Community Care

**Make Informed Decisions**

- Research quality data for both VA and community care options using the VA's SAIL system and CMS Hospital Compare
- For veteran-specific conditions (PTSD, TBI, military sexual trauma, amputee care, spinal cord injury), VA specialized care often outperforms community alternatives
- Document your experience with community care coordination, including any gaps in record sharing between VA and private providers
- Report community care scheduling delays through TriWest or Optum and to the VA Office of Community Care

## For All Citizens

### Educate Yourself

**Understand the Key Issues**

- The VA is the second-largest federal agency with 412,000 employees and a $325 billion budget
- Wait times for specialty care average 44 days, well above the VA's 28-day target
- Community care spending has grown from 22% to 35% of VHA healthcare spending in six years, without a deliberate strategic decision to shift this balance
- The EHR modernization has cost $16.1 billion with only 5 of 171 medical centers deployed and 149+ patient safety incidents documented
- The VA's workforce has an 11.2% vacancy rate, representing approximately 52,000 unfilled positions
- Average VA Secretary tenure since 2009 is approximately 2.5 years, undermining long-term reform

**Know the Key Reform Proposals**

- VA governance board to provide strategic continuity across administrations
- Evidence-based community care access standards to prevent unplanned privatization
- Competitive compensation to close the 20-30% gap with private-sector clinical salaries
- EHR modernization reset based on independent assessment of patient safety and clinician usability
- Advance appropriations for all VA operations to end the disruption of continuing resolutions

### Advocate for Reform

**Contact Your Members of Congress**

- Call or write your Representative and Senators about VA reform priorities
- Specific asks are more effective than general complaints:
    - Support VA governance board legislation to provide leadership continuity
    - Fund VA workforce at competitive market rates to close the 52,000-position vacancy gap
    - Demand an independent assessment of EHR modernization before further deployment
    - Support expanding advance appropriations to all VA operations
    - Require evidence-based community care access standards tied to clinical outcomes data
- Ask for quarterly reporting on VA wait times, community care spending, EHR deployment, and workforce vacancies

**Support Veterans Service Organizations**

- Donate to or volunteer with VSOs that advocate for VA reform
- Many VSOs provide direct services (claims assistance, healthcare navigation) in addition to policy advocacy
- Key organizations: American Legion, VFW, DAV, AMVETS, IAVA
- Understand that different VSOs have different reform positions; choose organizations whose approach aligns with your values

**Vote and Participate in Elections**

- Veterans' issues are among the few remaining areas of genuine bipartisan potential; hold candidates from both parties accountable
- Ask candidates their specific positions on VA privatization, workforce investment, and EHR modernization
- Support candidates who commit to evidence-based VA reform rather than ideological approaches
- Engage in state and local elections where state departments of veterans affairs and veteran services are at stake

### Community Actions

**Help Veterans Navigate the System**

- Connect veterans you know with VA enrollment, healthcare, and benefits resources
- Many veterans, especially those from earlier service eras, are not aware of expanded eligibility under the PACT Act
- Share information about VA healthcare options through community organizations, faith communities, and employers
- Volunteer at Stand Down events, veteran service fairs, and benefits outreach programs

**Challenge Narratives Based on Incomplete Information**

- The VA debate is often distorted by both crisis-driven media coverage and ideological advocacy
- VA healthcare quality generally exceeds private-sector benchmarks on clinical metrics; share this data when the narrative focuses exclusively on failures
- Community care is not inherently superior to VA direct care; nor is VA direct care inherently superior for all conditions; advocate for evidence-based balance
- Recognize that VA employees are overwhelmingly dedicated professionals working in a flawed system, not the source of the system's problems

## For Veterans Service Organizations

### Advocacy Priorities

**Push for Governance Reform**

- Advocate for a VA governance board to provide strategic continuity beyond political transitions
- Support bipartisan approaches that combine investment and accountability
- Coordinate with other VSOs on shared reform priorities to present a unified voice to Congress
- Testify before Congressional committees on the impact of leadership instability on VA reform

**Monitor Community Care Growth**

- Track community care spending as a share of VHA healthcare spending and demand transparency
- Advocate for evidence-based access standards that preserve VA expertise in veteran-specific conditions
- Report on care coordination failures between VA and community providers
- Push for quality reporting requirements for community care providers comparable to VA's SAIL system

**Engage on EHR Modernization**

- Demand veteran and clinician input into EHR modernization decisions
- Advocate for patient safety benchmarks that must be met before further EHR deployment
- Support independent assessment of whether the Oracle Health platform can meet VA's needs
- Push for transparency in EHR cost projections and deployment timelines

### Member Engagement

- Educate members about VA reform proposals and their implications for veteran care
- Survey members on VA healthcare experience, community care experience, and technology use
- Mobilize members for Congressional contact campaigns on key VA reform votes
- Provide regular updates on VA performance metrics, budget decisions, and reform progress

## For Policymakers

### Executive Actions (No Legislation Required)

- Direct VA to conduct independent assessment of EHR modernization with clinician-centered criteria
- Establish evidence-based community care access standards through MISSION Act regulatory authority
- Implement competitive compensation adjustments using existing Title 38 and RAISE Act authorities
- Create cross-functional veteran service teams integrating VHA, VBA, and NCA at the facility level
- Expand telehealth capacity and remove regulatory barriers to VA telehealth across state lines

### Legislative Actions

- Pass VA governance board legislation to provide strategic continuity across administrations
- Expand advance appropriations to cover all VA operations, not just medical care
- Fund VA workforce at market-competitive rates with geographic differentials for high-cost and rural areas
- Establish VA infrastructure modernization funding through dedicated capital investment authority
- Require evidence-based community care access standards tied to clinical outcomes comparison

### Oversight Actions

- Require quarterly VA reporting on wait times, community care spending, EHR deployment, workforce vacancies, and veteran trust scores
- Conduct regular hearings on VA reform implementation, not just crisis response
- Demand implementation of outstanding GAO and OIG recommendations with specific timelines
- Commission periodic independent assessments of VA governance, workforce, and technology
- Evaluate community care quality and cost relative to VA direct care by condition type

## Stay Informed

### Key Websites

- VA.gov -- VA services, healthcare enrollment, and benefits information
- accesstocare.va.gov -- VA wait-time and access data by facility
- va.gov/oig -- VA Inspector General reports and hotline
- gao.gov -- GAO reports on VA programs and high-risk areas
- benefits.va.gov/reports -- VBA performance data

### Email Lists and Newsletters

- Veterans' Affairs Committee press releases (House and Senate)
- American Legion, VFW, DAV legislative update newsletters
- IAVA advocacy alerts
- Veterans Healthcare Policy Institute newsletter

### Media Coverage

- Follow reporting from Stars and Stripes, Military Times, and The War Horse
- ProPublica's ongoing VA coverage, particularly EHR modernization investigations
- Connecting Vets for daily VA news
- Local media coverage of VA facility issues in your community
