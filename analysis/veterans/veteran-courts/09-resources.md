## Research and Reports

### Essential Research

**VTC Outcomes and Effectiveness**

- RAND Corporation. *Veterans Treatment Courts: Outcomes and Practice*. Santa Monica, CA: RAND, 2020. The most rigorous national evaluation of VTC effectiveness, documenting 25-50% recidivism reduction
- National Drug Court Institute. *VTC Multi-Site Evaluation: Final Report*. Alexandria, VA: NDCI, 2023. Meta-analysis of 28 VTCs demonstrating consistent outcome improvements
- Hartley, R.D. and Baldwin, J.M. "Waging War on Recidivism Among Justice-Involved Veterans." *Criminal Justice Policy Review* 30, no. 1 (2019): 52-82. Three-year follow-up study at a single VTC
- Knudsen, K.J. and Wingenfeld, S. "A Specialized Treatment Court for Veterans with Trauma Exposure." *Criminal Justice and Behavior* 43, no. 7 (2016): 911-927. Early effectiveness study of VTC model
- Baldwin, J.M. "Investigating the Programmatic Attack: A National Survey of Veterans Treatment Courts." *Journal of Criminal Law and Criminology* 105, no. 3 (2015): 705-752. Comprehensive survey of VTC operations and eligibility criteria

**Justice-Involved Veterans**

- Bureau of Justice Statistics. *Veterans in Prison and Jail, 2022*. Washington, DC: BJS, 2024. Definitive federal data on incarcerated veteran characteristics
- Blonigen, D.M., et al. "Criminal Recidivism Among Justice-Involved Veterans Following Substance Use Disorder Treatment." *Drug and Alcohol Dependence* 222 (2021): 108657
- Tsai, J., et al. "Risk Factors for Homelessness Among US Veterans." *Epidemiologic Reviews* 37 (2015): 177-195. Examines justice involvement as a pathway to veteran homelessness

**VA-Court Coordination**

- VA Veterans Justice Programs. *Annual Report on Veterans Justice Outreach*. Washington, DC: VA, 2024. Operational data on VJO and HCRV programs
- Blue-Howells, J., et al. "The U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs Veterans Justice Programs and the Sequential Intercept Model." *Psychological Services* 10, no. 1 (2013): 48-53

**Equity and Access**

- National Drug Court Institute. *Equity and Inclusion in Veterans Treatment Courts*. Alexandria, VA: NDCI, 2023. Analysis of racial, gender, and geographic disparities in VTC access
- Justice for Vets. *Women Veterans in Treatment Courts: Best Practices Guide*. Alexandria, VA: NADCP, 2024. Gender-responsive programming recommendations
- Douds, A.S. and Ahlin, E.M. "The Veterans Treatment Court Movement: Striving to Serve Those Who Served." *Veteran and Military Family Health* 1, no. 1 (2020): 1-12

**Peer Mentoring**

- Justice for Vets. *Peer Mentor Handbook*. Alexandria, VA: NADCP, 2024. Standardized training curriculum and best practices for VTC peer mentors
- Resnick, S.G. and Rosenheck, R.A. "Integrating Peer-Provided Services: A Quasi-Experimental Study of Recovery Orientation, Confidence, and Empowerment." *Psychiatric Services* 59 (2008): 1307-1314

## Educational Resources

### For Veterans and Families

- **VA Veterans Justice Programs information** (va.gov/homeless/vjp.asp): Overview of VJO, HCRV, and other services for justice-involved veterans
- **Justice for Vets resources** (justiceforvets.org): Information about VTCs for veterans, families, and attorneys
- **Make the Connection** (maketheconnection.net): VA resource featuring veteran stories of recovery, including justice-involved veterans
- **National Military Family Association** (militaryfamily.org): Resources for families of veterans involved in the justice system

### For Judges and Court Practitioners

- **Justice for Vets Training Programs**: Multi-day training for VTC judges, coordinators, and team members
- **National Drug Court Institute Continuing Education**: Research-based training for specialty court professionals
- **National Judicial College**: Courses on specialty court operations and judicial leadership
- **PsychArmor Institute** (psycharmor.org): Free military cultural competency training for court staff and treatment providers

### For Attorneys

- **American Bar Association, Military and Veterans Law**: CLE courses on VTC practice and veteran legal issues
- **National Association of Criminal Defense Lawyers, Veteran Defense Program**: Training for defense attorneys on identifying and representing veteran clients
- **State-level CLE programs**: Continuing legal education on VTC practice available through state bar associations

### For Peer Mentors

- **Justice for Vets Peer Mentor Training**: Standardized national training curriculum for VTC peer mentors
- **VA Peer Support Training**: VA certification programs for peer support specialists
- **American Legion peer mentor programs**: Training through local American Legion posts

### For Researchers

- **National Drug Court Institute Research Division** (ndci.org): VTC evaluation methodologies and datasets
- **VA Health Services Research and Development** (hsrd.research.va.gov): Funding for veteran justice research
- **National Institute of Justice** (nij.ojp.gov): Grant programs for VTC evaluation research
- **RAND Corporation Veterans Policy Program** (rand.org): Published evaluations and working papers
