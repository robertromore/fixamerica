# Veteran Treatment Courts: Current State

## VTC Coverage and Distribution

### National Overview

As of 2024, approximately 600 Veterans Treatment Courts operate across 49 states and the District of Columbia. Wyoming remains the only state without a VTC, though veterans in Wyoming may access diversion programs in neighboring states or through general specialty courts (Justice for Vets, National Association of Drug Court Professionals, 2024).

| Metric | Count | Source |
|--------|------:|--------|
| Total VTCs | ~600 | Justice for Vets, 2024 |
| States with VTCs | 49 + D.C. | Justice for Vets, 2024 |
| Veterans served annually | 35,000-40,000 | National Drug Court Institute, 2024 |
| Average caseload per court | 30-60 participants at any time | National Drug Court Institute, 2023 |
| Average program duration | 12-24 months | National Drug Court Institute, 2023 |

### Geographic Distribution

VTC distribution is uneven, with concentration in states with large veteran populations and in urban/suburban jurisdictions:

|-------|---------------:|-------------------:|--------------------------:|--------|
| California | 56 | 1,574,000 | 3.6 | Justice for Vets, 2024; VA, 2024 |
| Texas | 42 | 1,586,000 | 2.6 | Justice for Vets, 2024; VA, 2024 |
| New York | 35 | 724,000 | 4.8 | Justice for Vets, 2024; VA, 2024 |
| Florida | 38 | 1,491,000 | 2.5 | Justice for Vets, 2024; VA, 2024 |
| Ohio | 30 | 720,000 | 4.2 | Justice for Vets, 2024; VA, 2024 |
| Pennsylvania | 28 | 753,000 | 3.7 | Justice for Vets, 2024; VA, 2024 |
| Illinois | 22 | 586,000 | 3.8 | Justice for Vets, 2024; VA, 2024 |

### Urban-Rural Divide

| Setting | % of VTCs | % of Veteran Population | Access Ratio | Source |
|---------|----------:|------------------------:|-------------:|--------|
| Urban/suburban | 82% | 67% | 1.22 | Justice for Vets, 2024; Census Bureau, 2023 |
| Rural | 18% | 33% | 0.55 | Justice for Vets, 2024; Census Bureau, 2023 |

Rural veterans are significantly underserved by VTCs. Veterans in rural jurisdictions without a VTC may face traditional criminal processing for offenses that would qualify for diversion in an urban court.

## Outcomes and Effectiveness

### Recidivism

| Study | Comparison | VTC Recidivism | Control Group | Reduction | Source |
|-------|-----------|---------------:|-------------:|---------:|--------|
| RAND Corporation (2020) | VTC vs. traditional court | 18% | 36% | 50% | RAND, "Veterans Treatment Courts," 2020 |
| National Drug Court Institute (2023) | Meta-analysis of 28 VTCs | 22% | 38% | 42% | NDCI, 2023 |
| Hartley & Baldwin (2019) | Single-site study, 3-year follow-up | 15% | 35% | 57% | Criminal Justice Policy Review, 2019 |
| Justice for Vets annual report (2024) | Program-wide averages | 20% | 35% (est.) | 43% | Justice for Vets, 2024 |

Recidivism is typically measured as rearrest within 2-3 years of program entry. VTCs consistently demonstrate significant recidivism reduction across studies, though methodological limitations (selection effects, varying definitions) warrant caution.

## Eligibility Criteria

### Variation Across VTCs

Eligibility criteria are set by individual courts and vary significantly:

| Criterion | % of VTCs Requiring | Trend | Source |
|-----------|--------------------:|-------|--------|
| Veteran status (any discharge) | 45% | Expanding | Justice for Vets survey, 2024 |
| Veteran status (honorable/general discharge only) | 55% | Narrowing | Justice for Vets survey, 2024 |
| Service-connected condition (PTSD, TBI, SUD) | 60% | Narrowing | Justice for Vets survey, 2024 |
| Nexus between condition and offense | 40% | Narrowing | Justice for Vets survey, 2024 |
| Misdemeanor only | 30% | Expanding to include some felonies | Justice for Vets survey, 2024 |
| Excludes violent felonies | 75% | Stable | Justice for Vets survey, 2024 |
| Excludes sex offenses | 90% | Stable | Justice for Vets survey, 2024 |
| Excludes domestic violence | 45% | Controversial; some courts expanding | Justice for Vets survey, 2024 |

### Discharge Status Barriers

Approximately 550,000 post-9/11 veterans received other-than-honorable (OTH) or bad conduct discharges (DoD data, 2024). Many of these veterans were separated for conduct related to PTSD, TBI, or substance abuse--the very conditions VTCs are designed to address.

| Discharge Type | VTC Access | VA Treatment Access | Source |
|----------------|-----------|-------------------|--------|
| Honorable | Full access | Full access | 38 U.S.C. 101(2) |
| General (under honorable conditions) | Full access at most VTCs | Full access | 38 U.S.C. 101(2) |
| Other Than Honorable (OTH) | Excluded at 55% of VTCs | Limited (mental health only under P.L. 115-271) | Justice for Vets, 2024; 38 U.S.C. 1720I |
| Bad Conduct Discharge | Excluded at most VTCs | Generally excluded | Justice for Vets, 2024 |
| Dishonorable Discharge | Excluded at all VTCs | Excluded | 38 U.S.C. 5303 |

## VA-Court Coordination

### Veterans Justice Outreach Program

The VA's Veterans Justice Outreach (VJO) program is the primary interface between VTCs and the VA healthcare system. VJO specialists serve as liaisons, connecting justice-involved veterans to VA treatment and benefits.

| Metric | FY 2022 | FY 2023 | FY 2024 | Source |
|--------|--------:|--------:|--------:|--------|
| VJO specialists nationwide | 310 | 325 | 340 | VA Veterans Justice Programs, FY 2022-2024 |
| Veterans served by VJO | 52,000 | 55,000 | 58,000 | VA Veterans Justice Programs, FY 2022-2024 |
| VJO specialists per VTC | 0.52 | 0.54 | 0.57 | Calculated from VA data |
| % of VTCs with dedicated VJO | 55% | 58% | 60% | VA Veterans Justice Programs, FY 2024 |

Nearly half of VTCs do not have a dedicated VJO specialist, relying instead on shared VJO coverage across multiple courts and jails.

## Funding

### Federal Funding Sources

| Source | FY 2024 Amount | Purpose | Source |
|--------|---------------:|---------|--------|
| BJA SJIS Grants (DOJ) | $25 million | VTC operations, training, mentoring | Bureau of Justice Assistance, FY 2024 |

| VA Supportive Services for Veteran Families (SSVF) | Portion of $500M total | Housing support for VTC participants | VA SSVF program data, FY 2024 |

### State Funding

State funding for VTCs varies dramatically:

| State | Dedicated VTC Funding | Structure | Source |
|-------|----------------------:|-----------|--------|
| Texas | $3.5 million/year | Legislative appropriation | Texas Legislature, FY 2024-2025 |
| California | $2.8 million/year | Judicial Council allocation | California Judicial Council, FY 2024 |
| Ohio | $1.5 million/year | Supreme Court grants | Ohio Supreme Court, FY 2024 |
| New York | $2.0 million/year | Unified Court System budget | NY Office of Court Administration, FY 2024 |
| Most states | $0 | No dedicated funding | Justice for Vets survey, 2024 |

Most VTCs operate without dedicated state funding, relying on federal grants, county budgets, and in-kind support from the VA and local VSOs.

### Sustainability Concerns

| Concern | Detail | Source |
|---------|--------|--------|
| Grant dependency | ~40% of VTCs depend primarily on federal grants | Justice for Vets survey, 2024 |
| Grant renewal uncertainty | BJA SJIS grants are competitive and time-limited | Bureau of Justice Assistance, FY 2024 |
| Judicial champion dependency | Many VTCs depend on individual judges; retirement or transfer can end a program | National Drug Court Institute, 2023 |
| Peer mentor volunteers | Most peer mentors are unpaid; burnout and turnover are persistent issues | Justice for Vets, 2024 |

## Recent Developments

### 2023-2025

- **FY 2024**: BJA SJIS grant program reauthorized at $25 million
- **2023**: VA expanded VJO program by 15 specialists (from 310 to 325)
- **2024**: Justice for Vets published updated best practices guide for VTC operations
- **2024**: Several states (Michigan, Oregon, Colorado) enacted legislation expanding VTC eligibility to include OTH discharge veterans
- **2025**: Pending federal legislation (Veterans Treatment Court Improvement Act, 119th Congress) would increase BJA funding and expand VJO staffing
- **2025**: VA piloting telehealth-based treatment for rural VTC participants in 5 states

### Emerging Issues

| Issue | Status | Source |
|-------|--------|--------|
| Women veterans in VTCs | Underrepresented (5% of VTC participants vs. 10% of justice-involved veterans); gender-responsive programming limited | Justice for Vets, 2024 |
| Racial disparities | Black veterans underrepresented in VTCs relative to incarceration rates | National Drug Court Institute, 2023 |
| Post-9/11 cohort aging | Increasing volume of post-9/11 veterans entering justice system | VA Veterans Justice Programs, 2024 |
| Marijuana legalization | Some VTCs adjusting drug testing and eligibility as states legalize marijuana | State VTC practice variations, 2024 |
| Telehealth for court compliance | COVID-era telehealth expanded VTC access; sustainability uncertain | Justice for Vets, 2024 |
