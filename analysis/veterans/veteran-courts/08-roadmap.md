# Veteran Treatment Courts: Roadmap

## Phase 1: Immediate Actions (0-12 Months)

### Administrative Actions (No Legislation Required)

**VA Internal Policy Changes**:

- Issue directive expanding Veterans Justice Outreach (VJO) specialist hours at existing VTCs to full-time coverage where caseloads justify it
- Establish priority scheduling tier for VTC-referred veterans, ensuring first mental health and substance use appointments within 14 days of court referral
- Authorize MISSION Act community care referrals for VTC-mandated treatment when VA wait times exceed 14 days
- Expand VA telehealth treatment to all VTC participants, including those in rural jurisdictions without nearby VA facilities
- Mandate standardized VJO data reporting on VTC participant outcomes across all VA medical centers

**DOJ and BJA Actions**:

- Issue Bureau of Justice Assistance guidance encouraging states to expand VTC eligibility to include veterans with Other-Than-Honorable discharges
- Modify SJIS grant application criteria to award bonus points for VTCs that serve OTH discharge veterans, include domestic violence protocols, and conduct racial equity audits
- Launch a BJA technical assistance initiative providing VTC startup support to 50 rural jurisdictions

**Justice for Vets and NADCP Actions**:

- Publish updated national VTC best practices guide incorporating domestic violence track protocols, gender-responsive programming, and racial equity standards
- Develop a standardized VTC outcome metrics framework for voluntary adoption by all VTCs
- Launch a national peer mentor training certification program with standardized curriculum

## Phase 2: Short-Term Goals (1-2 Years)

### Legislative Priorities

**Veterans Treatment Court Expansion Act** (proposed):

- Increase BJA SJIS grant funding from $25 million to $100 million annually
- Establish formula-based grant allocation replacing competitive-only grants
- Require a 25% state funding match for continued federal support
- Mandate VTC access in all 94 federal judicial districts within 3 years
- Fund 360 additional VJO specialists (from 340 to 700 nationwide)
- Authorize $15 million annually for a national VTC data system

**Veterans Treatment Court Eligibility Reform Act** (proposed):

- Require all federally funded VTCs to accept veterans regardless of discharge characterization, provided clinical criteria are met
- Prohibit blanket charge-type exclusions; require case-by-case risk assessment using validated instruments
- Mandate domestic violence track protocols at VTCs accepting DV cases
- Require annual racial and gender equity audits as a condition of federal funding

### Coalition Building

**Target Allies**:

- Veterans Service Organizations: American Legion, VFW, DAV for legislative lobbying and peer mentor recruitment
- National Association of Drug Court Professionals and Justice for Vets for standards development and training
- Bipartisan congressional champions on veterans' affairs and judiciary committees
- State chief justices and administrative offices of courts for state-level adoption
- Prosecutors' associations for education on VTC outcomes and referral practices

**Messaging Strategy**:

- Frame VTC expansion as fulfilling the nation's obligation to veterans whose service-connected conditions led to justice involvement
- Emphasize cost-effectiveness: VTC programs cost $5,000-12,000 per participant versus $35,000-45,000 for incarceration
- Highlight recidivism reduction data (25-50% compared to traditional processing)
- Use bipartisan framing: VTCs are both fiscally conservative (saves money) and compassionate (treats underlying conditions)
- Feature veteran peer mentors and VTC graduates as spokespeople

## Phase 3: Medium-Term Reforms (2-5 Years)

### Structural Changes

**Nationwide VTC Coverage**:

- Establish regional VTC model for rural areas: one VTC judge covering 3-5 counties with rotating in-person sessions and telehealth check-ins
- Achieve VTC coverage or equivalent diversion access in every county with 500 or more veterans
- Integrate VTC access information into law enforcement booking procedures nationwide through standardized veteran identification screening

**Standardization and Certification**:

- Launch national VTC certification program based on Justice for Vets Ten Key Components, with peer review and outcome benchmarks
- Require certification as a condition of federal funding eligibility
- Implement national VTC data system with real-time reporting of demographics, clinical data, justice outcomes, and equity metrics
- Establish minimum standards for peer mentor training, compensation, and supervision

**Eligibility Expansion**:

- Achieve universal acceptance of OTH discharge veterans at all VTCs nationwide
- Implement case-by-case felony eligibility using validated risk assessment tools at 50% or more of VTCs
- Establish DV-track protocols at all VTCs that accept domestic violence cases, incorporating lethality assessment, victim advocacy, and safety planning

**Equity Reforms**:

- Reduce racial disparities in VTC referral, acceptance, and completion rates by 50%
- Achieve gender parity in VTC participation relative to justice-involved veteran demographics (women veterans at 10% of VTC participants, matching their share of justice-involved veterans)
- Ensure gender-responsive programming (MST screening, childcare support, female peer mentors) at all VTCs

## Phase 4: Long-Term Systemic Change (5-10 Years)

### Justice System Integration

- Integrate veteran identification screening into all booking, pretrial, and sentencing procedures nationwide
- Establish VTC or equivalent veteran diversion in every jurisdiction, eliminating geographic access gaps
- Develop post-VTC support programming providing continued mentoring, housing, and employment assistance for 2 years after graduation
- Create a seamless data bridge between VTC outcome systems, VA healthcare records, and criminal justice databases (with privacy protections)

### Prevention and Upstream Intervention

- Expand Transition Assistance Program to include criminal justice risk screening for separating service members, with VA referral for those at elevated risk
- Establish law enforcement veteran identification training as standard in all police academies and in-service programs
- Create community-based veteran crisis intervention teams in the 100 communities with the highest rates of veteran justice involvement
- Integrate justice-risk screening into VA primary care and mental health intake to enable early intervention before arrest

### Vision for 2035

A fully realized veteran treatment court system would include:

- VTC or equivalent diversion access in every jurisdiction with a meaningful veteran population, eliminating geographic barriers
- Universal eligibility based on veteran status and clinical need, without discharge type or blanket charge exclusions
- A dedicated VJO specialist at every VTC, with telehealth backup for rural courts
- Racial, gender, and geographic equity in VTC access, participation, and outcomes
- National VTC data system tracking standardized metrics across all courts, enabling continuous quality improvement
- Recidivism rates below 12% for VTC graduates, compared to 35-40% for traditionally processed veterans
- Peer mentor programs that are professionally supported, adequately compensated, and sustainably staffed
- No veteran incarcerated for conduct primarily driven by untreated service-connected PTSD, TBI, or substance use disorder when treatment-based diversion could address the underlying cause

## Implementation Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Congressional budget cuts to BJA VTC grants | Medium | High | Build bipartisan coalition; demonstrate cost-effectiveness and recidivism data |
| VJO specialist recruitment and retention challenges | High | High | Competitive compensation; telehealth to extend coverage; phased hiring |
| Prosecutorial resistance to expanded eligibility | Medium | Medium | Education campaigns; outcome data sharing; invite prosecutors to observe VTC sessions |
| VA treatment capacity unable to meet VTC demand | High | High | MISSION Act community care referrals; telehealth expansion; priority scheduling |
| State legislatures unwilling to provide matching funds | Medium | Medium | Federal formula grants as incentive; cost-effectiveness data; pilot programs |
| Judicial champion retirements ending local VTCs | Medium | Medium | Institutionalize VTCs in court structure (not dependent on individual judges); certification standards |
| Political backlash against expanded DV and felony eligibility | Medium | Medium | Robust victim safety protocols; risk assessment data; phased implementation |
| Peer mentor burnout and turnover | High | Medium | Stipend/compensation programs; training; supervision; boundary support |
