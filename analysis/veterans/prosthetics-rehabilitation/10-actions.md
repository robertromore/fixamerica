# Prosthetics and Rehabilitation: Actions

## For Individuals

### Learn First

**Understand the Problem**

- [ ] Read [Current State](02-current-state.md) to understand the scope of VA prosthetic and rehabilitation services
- [ ] Read [Root Causes](04-root-causes.md) to understand why veterans face procurement delays, geographic barriers, and technology gaps
- [ ] Learn the difference between prosthetic K-levels and why advanced components matter for veteran function and quality of life
- [ ] Understand that prosthetics encompasses far more than limb replacement: hearing aids, wheelchairs, orthotics, home modifications, and sensory aids serve millions of veterans

**Know the System**

- [ ] Understand the VA prosthetics formulary and how it determines which devices veterans can access
- [ ] Learn about the VA's tiered rehabilitation system: Polytrauma Rehabilitation Centers, Regional Amputation Centers, SCI/D Centers
- [ ] Know that the average wait for a prosthetic limb at VA is 52 days, compared to 14-28 days in the commercial sector
- [ ] Understand the DOD-to-VA transition gap that affects wounded warriors moving from military to VA rehabilitation

**Know the Resources**

- [ ] Locate your nearest VA Medical Center and its prosthetic services (va.gov/find-locations)
- [ ] Learn about VA Polytrauma Rehabilitation Centers (5 locations: Tampa, Richmond, Minneapolis, Palo Alto, San Antonio)
- [ ] Find your nearest Regional Amputation Center (7 nationally)
- [ ] Learn about VA adaptive sports programs (va.gov/adaptivesports)

### Take Action

**If You Are a Veteran with Prosthetic Needs**

- [ ] If you are not enrolled in VA healthcare, apply at va.gov/health-care/apply or call 1-800-698-2411
- [ ] Ask your VA provider about prosthetic device options appropriate for your activity level (K-level classification)
- [ ] If you are prescribed a prosthetic device and experience delays beyond 30 days, contact the Patient Advocate at your VA Medical Center
- [ ] Request a referral to a Regional Amputation Center or Polytrauma Rehabilitation Center if your local VA lacks specialized expertise
- [ ] Ask about telerehabilitation options if you live far from a specialized VA center
- [ ] Inquire about adaptive sports and recreation programs at your VA facility
- [ ] If you need home or vehicle modifications, ask your VA provider about Specially Adapted Housing grants and Automobile Adaptive Equipment grants
- [ ] If your prosthetic device needs repair, contact your VA prosthetics department for emergency repair service
- [ ] If you are transitioning from DOD to VA, request a Federal Recovery Coordinator to manage your rehabilitation transition

**If You Are a Family Member or Caregiver**

- [ ] Learn about the VA Caregiver Support Program (1-855-260-3274) if you care for a veteran with prosthetic and rehabilitation needs
- [ ] Understand your veteran's prosthetic device maintenance needs (battery charging, socket care, skin inspection)
- [ ] Advocate for your veteran if they experience prosthetic procurement delays or access barriers
- [ ] Connect with Fisher House Foundation if your veteran needs inpatient rehabilitation far from home
- [ ] Explore respite care options through VA caregiver programs
- [ ] Learn about community adaptive sports programs that can benefit your veteran's physical and mental health

**Contact Representatives**

- [ ] Call your U.S. senators and representative to support VA prosthetics procurement reform
- [ ] Urge support for increased VA prosthetics funding, especially for advanced technology adoption
- [ ] Advocate for legislation requiring maximum delivery timelines for VA prosthetic devices
- [ ] Support workforce legislation that increases VA prosthetist salaries and scholarship programs
- [ ] Advocate for DOD-VA transition reform to eliminate care gaps for wounded warriors
- [ ] Attend town halls and ask about veteran prosthetic and rehabilitation policy

**Join the Movement**

- [ ] Join the Amputee Coalition to connect with advocacy and support networks
- [ ] Support Paralyzed Veterans of America, which advocates for wheelchair access and SCI/D services
- [ ] Volunteer with Challenged Athletes Foundation or Disabled Sports USA for adaptive sports programs
- [ ] Follow Wounded Warrior Project for updates on prosthetics policy and veteran support
- [ ] Share educational content about veteran prosthetic needs to build public awareness
- [ ] Participate in National Veterans Wheelchair Games, Creative Arts Festival, or other VA adaptive events as a volunteer or supporter

### Reduce Barriers

**In Your Community**

- [ ] Support accessibility improvements in your community that benefit veterans with physical disabilities
- [ ] Advocate for ADA compliance in public spaces, transportation, and workplaces
- [ ] Challenge assumptions about disability: veterans with prosthetics lead active, productive lives
- [ ] If you are an employer, learn about workplace accommodations for employees with prosthetic devices
- [ ] Support local adaptive sports programs and facilities

## For Communities

### Build Local Support

**Establish or Support Adaptive Sports Programs**

- [ ] Partner with VA to host community-based adaptive sports events
- [ ] Apply for Challenged Athletes Foundation grants to purchase adaptive sports equipment for community use
- [ ] Create inclusive recreation programs that welcome veterans with physical disabilities
- [ ] Train volunteer coaches in adaptive sports instruction
- [ ] Provide accessible facilities for adaptive sports (wheelchair basketball courts, accessible kayak launches, adaptive fitness equipment)

**Employer Actions**

- [ ] Learn about the Americans with Disabilities Act requirements for workplace accommodations
- [ ] Partner with VA Vocational Rehabilitation for veteran employee support
- [ ] Adopt flexible scheduling for employees who need prosthetic maintenance appointments
- [ ] Ensure workplace accessibility for employees with mobility devices
- [ ] Participate in the VA's Compensated Work Therapy program for veterans in rehabilitation

**Healthcare Community**

- [ ] If you are a healthcare provider, screen patients for military service history
- [ ] If you are a prosthetist or orthotist, consider joining the VA Community Care Network to serve veteran referrals
- [ ] Complete VA-approved military cultural competency training if you serve veteran patients
- [ ] If you are a prosthetics or rehabilitation student, explore VA scholarship and residency opportunities
- [ ] Support efforts to expand prosthetics/orthotics training program capacity nationally

### Local Government Actions

- [ ] Ensure local infrastructure (sidewalks, public buildings, transportation) meets ADA accessibility standards
- [ ] Include veteran disability access in community planning and zoning decisions
- [ ] Support local adaptive recreation facility development
- [ ] Train first responders to assist individuals with prosthetic devices and mobility aids during emergencies
- [ ] Partner with VA for community health worker programs serving veterans with disabilities

## For Organizations

### Veterans Service Organizations

- [ ] Advocate legislatively for VA prosthetics procurement reform and funding increases
- [ ] Integrate prosthetics and rehabilitation information into benefits counseling services
- [ ] Host prosthetic device technology demonstrations at post and chapter meetings
- [ ] Partner with VA for adaptive sports events at VSO facilities
- [ ] Support peer mentorship programs connecting experienced prosthetic users with newly injured veterans
- [ ] Raise awareness of prosthetic needs beyond combat amputees, including the larger dysvascular amputee population

### Research and Academic Institutions

- [ ] Pursue VA Rehabilitation Research and Development funding for prosthetic innovation
- [ ] Collaborate with VA centers on clinical trials for new prosthetic technologies
- [ ] Expand prosthetics and orthotics training programs to address the national workforce shortage
- [ ] Support technology transfer pathways that move research advances to clinical application more rapidly
- [ ] Engage veteran patients as co-designers in prosthetic research to ensure technology meets real-world needs

### Prosthetic Industry

- [ ] Work with VA to streamline formulary adoption processes for new devices
- [ ] Offer VA-specific pricing that reflects the government's volume purchasing power
- [ ] Invest in training programs for VA prosthetists on new device technologies
- [ ] Support 3D printing and digital fabrication research in partnership with VA
- [ ] Participate in VA community care quality improvement initiatives

## For Policymakers

### Federal Level

- [ ] Fund VA prosthetics at levels that match the cost of providing advanced technology to all clinically eligible veterans
- [ ] Pass legislation mandating maximum delivery timelines for prosthetic devices (30-day target)
- [ ] Reform the VA formulary process to include parallel VA-FDA review and provisional listing
- [ ] Establish a VA Prosthetic Care Trust Fund for lifetime care obligation accounting
- [ ] Increase VA prosthetist salaries to competitive market levels through special salary rate authority
- [ ] Fund VA prosthetics scholarship and loan repayment programs to build the workforce pipeline
- [ ] Mandate seamless DOD-VA rehabilitation transition with zero care gaps
- [ ] Expand VA adaptive sports funding from $18 million to $50 million annually
- [ ] Authorize VA Prosthetics Breakthrough Therapy designation for accelerated technology adoption

### State Level

- [ ] Ensure state health insurance programs cover prosthetic devices at parity with medical/surgical benefits
- [ ] Adopt prosthetic parity laws requiring private insurers to cover advanced prosthetic components when clinically indicated
- [ ] Fund state-level adaptive sports and recreation programs for veterans and all people with disabilities
- [ ] Support prosthetics/orthotics education programs at state universities
- [ ] Include prosthetic and rehabilitation access in state veteran service plans

### Local Level

- [ ] Ensure local ADA compliance in public infrastructure
- [ ] Fund accessible recreation facilities and adaptive sports programs
- [ ] Include disability access in emergency preparedness planning
- [ ] Support local veteran employment programs that accommodate prosthetic and rehabilitation needs
- [ ] Partner with VA for community-based rehabilitation outreach
