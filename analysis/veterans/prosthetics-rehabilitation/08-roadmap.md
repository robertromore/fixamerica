# Prosthetics and Rehabilitation: Roadmap

## Phase 1: Immediate Actions (0-12 Months)

### Administrative Actions (No Legislation Required)

**VA Internal Policy Changes**:

- Issue directive establishing "prosthetic fast track" procurement for formulary devices, eliminating redundant funding authorization steps
- Authorize direct prosthetist ordering for standard formulary components within established K-level criteria
- Pre-authorize funding for prosthetic device replacement at defined intervals (3-5 years for limbs, 5-7 years for wheelchairs) to eliminate re-authorization delays
- Establish interim formulary listing for newly FDA-approved devices while Technology Assessment Program review proceeds
- Mandate electronic procurement workflows at all VA Medical Centers, replacing paper-based processing
- Expand emergency prosthetic repair authority allowing same-day or next-day service at all VA facilities

**Workforce and Compensation**:

- Implement special salary rate for VA certified prosthetists/orthotists to close the 12% gap with private-sector pay ($92,000 to $105,000+)
- Announce VA Prosthetics Scholarship Program with first cohort of 50 students
- Activate recruitment bonuses of $25,000-$50,000 for critical rehabilitation positions (physiatrists, rehabilitation engineers, recreation therapists) at facilities with highest vacancy rates
- Launch VA Prosthetics Technician career pathway for veterans with relevant military experience

**DOD Coordination**:

- Station VA Prosthetic Liaison Officers at Walter Reed National Military Medical Center and San Antonio Military Medical Center
- Establish device portability protocol ensuring DOD-fitted prosthetic devices are maintained by VA without refitting
- Begin joint rehabilitation planning for separating service members 12 months before medical board completion

## Phase 2: Short-Term Goals (1-2 Years)

### Legislative Priorities

**Veterans Prosthetic Care Modernization Act** (proposed):

- Mandate maximum 30-day delivery timeline for prosthetic limbs from prescription to fitting
- Require parallel VA-FDA technology review, beginning VA evaluation during FDA review
- Establish VA Prosthetics Scholarship Program in statute with $30 million annual authorization
- Create VA Prosthetics Breakthrough Therapy designation for accelerated formulary review
- Authorize 3D printing expansion at all RACs and PRCs

**Veterans Rehabilitation Access Act** (proposed):

- Fund hub-and-spoke rehabilitation network connecting RACs and PRCs to 50 satellite clinics
- Authorize telerehabilitation as a standard modality for prosthetic follow-up care
- Reduce Federal Recovery Coordination Program caseloads to 25 veterans per coordinator
- Require seamless DOD-VA electronic prosthetic records

### Coalition Building

**Target Allies**:

- Veterans Service Organizations: Paralyzed Veterans of America, Wounded Warrior Project, Amputee Coalition for legislative lobbying
- Professional organizations: American Academy of Orthotists and Prosthetists, American Congress of Rehabilitation Medicine for clinical standards advocacy
- Bipartisan congressional champions: Members of Senate and House Veterans' Affairs Committees with personal connections to wounded warrior issues
- Research institutions: VA Center for Limb Loss and MoBility (CLiMB), Johns Hopkins Applied Physics Lab for innovation advocacy

**Messaging Strategy**:

- Frame prosthetic reform as fulfilling the nation's obligation to those wounded in service
- Emphasize that veterans wait months for devices available commercially in weeks--a concrete, understandable disparity
- Use veteran amputee advocates as spokespeople to humanize procurement and formulary reform
- Present cost-effectiveness data: advanced prosthetics reduce falls, hospitalizations, and long-term healthcare costs
- Avoid partisan framing; maintain bipartisan appeal built on wounded warrior support

## Phase 3: Medium-Term Reforms (2-5 Years)

### Structural Changes

**Procurement System Overhaul**:

- Implement VA-specific prosthetics procurement authority outside standard Federal Acquisition Regulations, modeled on DOD's Other Transaction Authority for rapid technology acquisition
- Create a national prosthetics contract vehicle allowing VA prosthetists to order any formulary device from any approved manufacturer through a single streamlined system
- Establish performance benchmarks: 95% of prosthetic limbs delivered within 30 days; 100% of emergency repairs within 48 hours
- Automate prosthetic replacement ordering based on device age and clinical criteria

**Hub-and-Spoke Network Build-Out**:

- Open 50 satellite rehabilitation clinics connected to RAC and PRC hubs
- Deploy mobile prosthetic teams (prosthetist, rehabilitation therapist, prosthetic technician) on rotating schedules to underserved VA facilities
- Establish telerehabilitation as a standard modality at all VA Medical Centers and CBOCs
- Create a national VA prosthetics scheduling system allowing veterans to book appointments at any hub or spoke facility

**3D Printing and Digital Fabrication**:

- Achieve 3D printing capability at all 7 RACs and 5 PRCs
- Begin deployment of 3D scanning and digital socket design at 25 additional VA Medical Centers
- Establish VA-wide standards for 3D-printed prosthetic components
- Launch virtual fitting program for remote veterans combining 3D scanning, digital design, and shipped printed sockets

**Community Care Quality Framework**:

- Implement mandatory credentialing standards for community prosthetic providers receiving VA referrals
- Establish outcomes tracking for all prosthetics delivered through community care
- Require community providers to demonstrate veteran-specific expertise or complete VA-approved military cultural competency training
- Create a VA Prosthetics Community Care Quality Report published annually

## Phase 4: Long-Term Systemic Change (5-10 Years)

### Lifetime Care Obligation Model

- Establish VA Prosthetic Care Trust Fund with actuarial projections for lifetime prosthetic costs
- Capitalize the Trust Fund with initial appropriation based on current veteran amputee population obligations
- Transition from annual appropriations to actuarial-based funding for prosthetic services
- Guarantee uninterrupted prosthetic care regardless of annual budget fluctuations
- Publish annual actuarial reports projecting future prosthetic obligations

### Research-to-Care Pipeline

- Achieve 3-5 year total timeline from research breakthrough to veteran patient availability (reduced from current 7-14 years)
- Establish VA Prosthetics Innovation Center operating at the intersection of research and clinical care
- Deploy brain-computer interface prosthetics in clinical trials at VA centers
- Advance regenerative medicine research (tissue engineering, nerve regeneration) toward clinical application
- Expand osseointegration from 4 VA centers to 15, with outcomes data supporting wider adoption

### Technology Integration

### Vision for 2035

A fully realized veteran prosthetics and rehabilitation system would include:

- Every veteran receives the most clinically appropriate prosthetic device within 21 days of prescription
- No veteran is more than 90 minutes from specialized prosthetic and rehabilitation care (in person or via telerehabilitation)
- VA leads the world in prosthetic research with a 3-5 year lab-to-patient pipeline
- Lifetime prosthetic care is guaranteed through an actuarially funded trust, removing annual budget uncertainty
- The DOD-to-VA rehabilitation transition is seamless, with zero care gaps
- Adaptive sports and recreation programs are available to every veteran with a physical disability
- 3D printing and digital fabrication have reduced prosthetic socket wait times to days
- Brain-computer interface and advanced myoelectric prosthetics provide near-natural limb function
- The VA prosthetist workforce is fully staffed and the profession attracts top talent

## Implementation Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Congressional budget cuts to VA prosthetics | Medium | High | Build bipartisan coalition; demonstrate cost-effectiveness of advanced devices |
| Prosthetist workforce shortage worsens nationally | High | High | Scholarship programs; expand training capacity; develop technician pathways |
| Technology costs outpace budget growth | High | Medium | Formulary negotiation; 3D printing cost reduction; volume purchasing |
| DOD-VA institutional resistance to transition reform | Medium | Medium | Joint executive committee mandates; Congressional oversight |
| Electronic health record transition disrupts prosthetic workflows | Medium | High | Prosthetics-specific EHR module testing before deployment |
| 3D printing regulatory barriers delay expansion | Medium | Medium | Engage FDA early; establish VA-specific quality standards |
| Trust Fund initial capitalization is politically infeasible | High | High | Phase implementation; start with pilot program for post-9/11 amputees |
| Community care quality standards face industry resistance | Medium | Medium | Phase requirements; provide technical assistance to community providers |
