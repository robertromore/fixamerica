# VA Claims Backlog: Current State

## Backlog Volume and Trends

### Overall Pending Inventory

The VA tracks two key metrics: total pending claims and "backlogged" claims (those pending beyond the 125-day target). Both have surged since the PACT Act's passage.

| Period | Total Pending | Backlogged (>125 days) | Backlog Rate | Source |
|--------|---------------|------------------------|--------------|--------|
| September 2013 (peak) | 836,000 | 611,000 | 73.1% | VA Monday Morning Workload Report, 2013 |
| September 2015 (low) | 363,000 | 75,000 | 20.7% | VA Monday Morning Workload Report, 2015 |
| September 2019 | 390,000 | 72,000 | 18.5% | VA Monday Morning Workload Report, 2019 |
| September 2020 | 428,000 | 118,000 | 27.6% | VA Monday Morning Workload Report, 2020 |
| August 2022 (PACT Act signed) | 445,000 | 145,000 | 32.6% | VA Monday Morning Workload Report, 2022 |
| September 2023 | 812,000 | 305,000 | 37.6% | VA Monday Morning Workload Report, 2023 |
| September 2024 | 880,000 | 275,000 | 31.3% | VA Monday Morning Workload Report, 2024 |
| January 2025 | 907,000 | 290,000 | 32.0% | VA Monday Morning Workload Report, January 2025 |

*Source: VA Benefits Administration Monday Morning Workload Reports, 2013-2025.*

### Trend Analysis

- The **PACT Act** (Public Law 117-168, signed August 10, 2022) triggered the largest single surge in claims history, approximately doubling the pending inventory within 18 months
- The total pending inventory has exceeded 900,000 for the first time, surpassing even the 2013 crisis peak in absolute terms
- The backlog rate (percentage of claims beyond 125 days) has improved slightly from its post-PACT Act peak of 37.6% but remains significantly elevated
- The VA completed a record 1.98 million claims in FY 2024 (VA Benefits Administration, FY 2024 Performance Report), but new filings outpaced completions
- Without the PACT Act, the VA was on track to reduce the backlog to near pre-pandemic levels by FY 2024

Supplemental claims--requests to reopen previously denied claims with new evidence--represent the fastest-growing category. Many supplemental claims are filed after initial denials due to insufficient evidence, creating a cycle of rework that strains the system.

## Processing Metrics

|------------|---------|---------|---------|---------|-----------|
| Initial claims | 134 days | 128 days | 155 days | 151 days | 125 days |
| Supplemental claims | 95 days | 88 days | 112 days | 118 days | 125 days |
| Higher-level reviews | 73 days | 82 days | 96 days | 89 days | 125 days |
| All rating claims (combined) | 118 days | 112 days | 138 days | 136 days | 125 days |

*Source: VA Benefits Administration Performance Reports, FY 2021-2024.*

### Accuracy Rates

The VA measures accuracy at two levels: claim-level (was the overall decision correct?) and issue-level (was each individual medical condition correctly rated?).

| Metric | FY 2021 | FY 2022 | FY 2023 | FY 2024 | VA Target |
|--------|---------|---------|---------|---------|-----------|
| Claim-level accuracy | 97.3% | 96.8% | 96.4% | 96.7% | 96% |
| Issue-level accuracy | 92.8% | 92.1% | 90.8% | 91.3% | 92% |
| Benefits entitlement accuracy | 94.5% | 93.8% | 93.1% | 93.4% | 95% |

*Source: VA Systematic Technical Accuracy Review (STAR) Program, FY 2021-2024.*

**Key findings**:

- Claim-level accuracy exceeds the VA's target, but this metric masks significant issue-level errors
- Issue-level accuracy has declined since FY 2021, coinciding with increased processing pressure
- The gap between claim-level and issue-level accuracy means many veterans receive ratings that are partially wrong--potentially underrating some conditions while correctly rating others
- The VA Inspector General estimated in 2023 that approximately 140,000 claims per year contain at least one issue-level error (VA OIG Report 22-02122-153, 2023)

## PACT Act Impact

### Scope of Expansion

The **Sergeant First Class Heath Robinson Honoring Our Promise to Address Comprehensive Toxics (PACT) Act** (Public Law 117-168) was the largest expansion of VA benefits in decades.

| PACT Act Provision | Impact | Status (January 2025) |
|-------------------|--------|----------------------|
| New presumptive conditions (23 cancers, respiratory diseases) | Millions of veterans newly eligible | Claims being processed |
| Burn pit exposure presumption (post-9/11) | 3.5 million post-9/11 veterans eligible for presumption | Active |
| Agent Orange expansion (Thailand, Guam, others) | Additional Vietnam-era veterans eligible | Active |
| Radiation exposure expansion | Expanded eligibility for nuclear veterans | Active |
| Toxic Exposure Fund | $280.5 billion over 10 years for healthcare and benefits | Funded through FY 2034 |
| Enhanced screening for toxic exposure | All enrolled veterans offered screening | Implementing |

*Source: PACT Act (Public Law 117-168); VA PACT Act Implementation Dashboard, January 2025.*

### Claims Surge Timeline

| Period | PACT Act Claims Filed | Cumulative Total |
|--------|----------------------|------------------|
| August-December 2022 | 380,000 | 380,000 |
| January-June 2023 | 820,000 | 1,200,000 |
| July-December 2023 | 950,000 | 2,150,000 |
| January-June 2024 | 1,100,000 | 3,250,000 |
| July-December 2024 | 1,050,000 | 4,300,000 |
| January 2025 | 200,000+ | 4,500,000+ |

*Source: VA PACT Act Dashboard, January 2025.*

### Processing Challenges Specific to PACT Act

- **Medical evidence**: Many toxic exposure claims require specialized medical opinions that take longer to obtain
- **Service connection documentation**: Proving presence at specific locations during specific periods requires accessing military records that are often incomplete
- **Multi-condition claims**: Veterans exposed to toxins often file for multiple conditions simultaneously, increasing per-claim complexity
- **Backlog prioritization**: The VA initially prioritized PACT Act claims for terminal conditions, creating a queue within the queue

## Appeals Backlog

### Board of Veterans' Appeals (BVA)

The appeals backlog runs parallel to the initial claims backlog and often takes even longer to resolve.

| Metric | FY 2021 | FY 2022 | FY 2023 | FY 2024 |
|--------|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| Appeals received | 96,000 | 103,000 | 112,000 | 118,000 |
| Appeals decided | 102,000 | 95,000 | 99,000 | 104,000 |
| Appeals pending | 185,000 | 193,000 | 206,000 | 215,000 |
| Average wait time (docket) | 2.8 years | 3.1 years | 3.4 years | 3.6 years |
| BVA judges | 102 | 110 | 115 | 118 |

*Source: Board of Veterans' Appeals Annual Reports, FY 2021-2024.*

### Appeals Modernization Act (AMA) Impact

The Appeals Modernization Act (Public Law 115-55, 2017) created three review lanes to reduce the legacy appeals backlog:

| Review Lane | Average Processing Time (FY 2024) | Volume (FY 2024) |
|------------|-----------------------------------|-------------------|
| Higher-Level Review | 89 days | 201,000 |
| Supplemental Claim | 118 days | 745,000 |
| Board Appeal (direct review) | 540 days | 42,000 |
| Board Appeal (evidence submission) | 680 days | 38,000 |
| Board Appeal (hearing) | 1,200 days | 38,000 |

*Source: VA Appeals Modernization Act Dashboard, FY 2024.*

The AMA successfully eliminated the legacy appeals backlog (pre-2019 appeals), but the new system is now building its own backlog as incoming appeals exceed capacity.

## Regional Variation

### Processing Time by Regional Office

VA regional offices show significant variation in processing speed and accuracy.

**Fastest Regional Offices (Average Days, FY 2024)**:

| Regional Office | Average Days | Accuracy Rate |
|-----------------|-------------|---------------|
| Salt Lake City, UT | 112 | 97.2% |
| Lincoln, NE | 118 | 96.9% |
| Sioux Falls, SD | 121 | 97.1% |
| Boise, ID | 124 | 96.5% |
| Fargo, ND | 125 | 96.8% |

**Slowest Regional Offices (Average Days, FY 2024)**:

| Regional Office | Average Days | Accuracy Rate |
|-----------------|-------------|---------------|
| New York, NY | 198 | 95.1% |
| Oakland, CA | 192 | 95.4% |
| Los Angeles, CA | 188 | 95.8% |
| Philadelphia, PA | 181 | 95.9% |
| Atlanta, GA | 178 | 96.0% |

*Source: VA Benefits Administration Regional Office Performance Reports, FY 2024.*

### Variation Drivers

- **Caseload volume**: Urban offices handle far more claims but do not always receive proportional staffing
- **Workforce experience**: Offices with higher turnover have less experienced adjudicators
- **Claim complexity**: Regional variation in veteran demographics affects claim complexity
- **Military records access**: Proximity to National Personnel Records Center and other archives affects evidence gathering speed
- **Management quality**: Leadership effectiveness varies significantly across offices

### Training and Readiness

- New VSRs require approximately **18-24 months** of training before reaching full productivity
- RVSR training is more intensive, requiring **2-3 years** before independent rating capability
- The rapid hiring surge in FY 2023-2024 means a significant portion of the workforce is still developing proficiency
- The VA's National Training Curriculum has been revised multiple times to accommodate PACT Act requirements

## Technology and Automation

### Current Systems

| System | Purpose | Status |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Veterans Benefits Management System (VBMS) | Primary claims processing platform | Active; ongoing enhancements |
| Decision Ready Claims (DRC) | Expedited processing for fully developed claims | Active; ~15% of claims |
| Automated Decision Support (ADS) | AI-assisted rating suggestions | Pilot phase |
| National Work Queue (NWQ) | Distributes claims across regional offices | Active |
| VA.gov claims tracker | Veteran-facing claim status portal | Active |

*Source: VA Office of Information and Technology, 2024.*

### Automation Progress

- The VA estimated in 2024 that automation tools saved approximately **2.3 million processing hours** (VA CIO testimony, House Veterans' Affairs Committee, March 2024)
- Automated processing for certain straightforward claims (hypertension increases, hearing loss) reduced per-claim processing time by 30-40%
- AI-assisted rating recommendations are in pilot testing but face concerns about algorithmic bias and due process

## Recent Developments (2024-2025)

### Policy Changes

- **PACT Act anniversary**: The VA marked two years of PACT Act implementation in August 2024, claiming 5.5 million PACT Act claims processed and $4.2 billion in retroactive benefits paid
- **Automated claims expansion**: The VA expanded automated processing to additional condition types in late 2024
- **Claims accuracy review**: The VA OIG released a critical report in September 2024 identifying systemic errors in burn pit presumptive claims processing
- **Workforce expansion**: The VA continued hiring toward a target of 30,000 total claims processing employees by end of FY 2025

### Legislative Activity

- Multiple bills introduced in 119th Congress to address claims processing, including proposals for mandatory processing timelines and independent accuracy audits
- Congressional hearings in November 2024 focused on post-PACT Act backlog trajectory and VA plans for sustained reduction
- Bipartisan concern about the long-term fiscal impact of PACT Act claims on the Toxic Exposure Fund
