# VA Claims Backlog: Actions

## What You Can Do

The VA claims backlog is a solvable problem, but it requires sustained public engagement. The following actions are organized by level of effort and impact.

---

## For Veterans With Pending Claims

### Immediate Steps

**File a Fully Developed Claim (FDC)**

- Gather all medical evidence, service records, and buddy statements *before* filing
- Use the FDC program to bypass evidence development delays
- Claims filed as FDC are processed approximately 40% faster than standard claims (VA Benefits Administration, 2023)
- Work with a VSO representative to ensure your claim is complete

**Track Your Claim Status**

- Use VA.gov to monitor your claim's progress
- Note any requests for additional information and respond promptly
- Delays in responding to VA development requests extend processing time significantly
- Contact your VSO representative if your claim appears stalled

**Request a VSO Representative**

- Accredited VSO representatives file and manage claims at no cost
- They have access to VA systems and can expedite evidence gathering
- Major VSOs (DAV, American Legion, VFW) have representatives at or near every regional office
- File VA Form 21-22 to appoint a representative

**Consider Filing Claims for PACT Act Conditions**

- If you served in a location or era associated with toxic exposure, file for presumptive conditions
- The PACT Act expanded eligibility for burn pit exposure, Agent Orange, radiation, and other toxic exposures
- Even if you were previously denied, you can file a supplemental claim under the new presumptions
- Visit VA.gov/PACT for eligibility screening

### If Your Claim Is Delayed

**Contact Your Congressional Representative**

- Congressional inquiries to the VA on behalf of constituents can prompt action on stalled claims
- Your Representative and Senators each have a casework office that handles VA issues
- Provide your claim number, filing date, and a brief description of the delay
- Congressional offices can request status updates and expedited processing

**Request Expedited Processing If Eligible**

- The VA grants "advance on docket" status for claims involving:
    - Financial hardship
    - Terminal illness
    - Advanced age (75+)
    - Homelessness
    - ALS diagnosis
- Submit a request with supporting documentation

## For All Citizens

### Educate Yourself

**Understand the Scale of the Problem**

- 907,000+ claims pending as of January 2025
- Average processing time of 151 days (FY 2024)
- 215,000 appeals pending at the Board of Veterans' Appeals
- Veterans waiting years for disability decisions they earned through service

**Know the Key Reform Proposals**

- Mandatory capacity funding linked to eligibility expansions
- AI-assisted claims processing with human oversight
- Presumptive approval for straightforward claims
- Sustainable workforce investment and reduced turnover

### Advocate for Reform

**Contact Your Members of Congress**

- Call or write your Representative and Senators about the claims backlog
- Specific asks are more effective than general complaints:
    - Support the Claims Processing Capacity Act (or equivalent) requiring linked appropriations
    - Fund VA technology modernization at requested levels
    - Support multi-year VBA workforce funding
    - Demand quarterly reporting on backlog metrics

**Support Veterans Service Organizations**

- Donate to or volunteer with VSOs that provide claims assistance
- Many VSOs rely on volunteers to help veterans navigate the claims process
- Key organizations: DAV, American Legion, VFW, AMVETS, IAVA

**Vote and Participate in Elections**

- Veterans' issues are bipartisan; hold candidates from both parties accountable
- Ask candidates their specific plans for reducing the claims backlog
- Support candidates who commit to adequate VA funding

### Community Actions

**Help Veterans in Your Community**

- Connect veterans you know with VSO claims assistance
- Many veterans, especially older or rural veterans, are not aware of benefits they have earned
- Share information about PACT Act eligibility through community organizations
- Volunteer at Stand Down events, veteran service fairs, and benefits outreach programs

**Engage Employers**

- Employers who hire veterans can support claims filing through HR programs
- Flexible scheduling for medical appointments and claims-related meetings
- Connect veteran employees with VSO representatives

## For Veterans Service Organizations

### Strengthen Claims Assistance

- Invest in training for claims representatives on PACT Act conditions
- Develop digital tools for remote claims assistance
- Partner with law schools for pro bono legal support on appeals
- Track claims outcomes to identify systemic issues

### Advocacy Actions

- Coordinate with other VSOs on legislative priorities for claims processing reform
- Testify before Congressional committees on backlog impacts
- Publish annual reports on claims assistance outcomes and systemic barriers
- Engage media to maintain public attention between backlog crises

### Member Engagement

- Educate members about the claims process and their rights
- Provide regular updates on claims processing trends and legislative action
- Mobilize members for Congressional contact campaigns on key votes
- Survey members on claims experience to build evidence for reform advocacy

---

## For Policymakers

### Executive Actions (No Legislation Required)

- Direct VA to expand presumptive approval categories
- Require interagency data sharing agreements with DOD and SSA
- Establish performance-based contracts for C&P examination vendors
- Mandate quality review sample increases from 3% to 10%

### Legislative Actions

- Pass Claims Processing Capacity Act requiring linked appropriations for eligibility expansions
- Authorize multi-year VBA workforce funding not subject to annual appropriations uncertainty
- Fund technology modernization for claims processing systems
- Establish mandatory processing timelines with accountability

### Oversight Actions

- Require quarterly VA reporting on backlog metrics, accuracy, and workforce data
- Conduct regular hearings on claims processing performance
- Demand implementation of outstanding GAO and OIG recommendations
- Commission independent assessments of VA technology modernization progress
