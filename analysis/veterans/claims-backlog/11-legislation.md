# VA Claims Backlog: Legislation and Legal Framework

## Overview

Addressing the VA claims backlog requires legislative action at the federal level to restructure how Congress funds claims processing capacity, modernize the claims system, and establish accountability for timely processing. This document provides draft legal text for key reforms identified in [Solutions](07-solutions.md).

## Federal Legislation

### Veterans Claims Processing Capacity Act

**Purpose**: Require all legislation expanding VA benefits eligibility to include mandatory appropriations for claims processing capacity, preventing future backlog surges caused by unfunded eligibility expansions.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SEC. 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Veterans Claims Processing
Capacity Act".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds the following:
(1) Every major expansion of VA benefits eligibility in the
    past two decades has produced a claims processing backlog
    surge because processing capacity was not expanded
    proportionally.
(2) The PACT Act (Public Law 117-168) expanded eligibility
    for millions of veterans but did not include specific
    appropriations for claims processing workforce or
    technology, resulting in a backlog exceeding 900,000
    pending claims.
(3) The mismatch between eligibility authorization and
    processing appropriation is a structural deficiency
    that will produce future backlog crises unless corrected.
(4) Veterans who have earned benefits through military
    service should not be forced to wait months or years
    for claims decisions due to Congressional failure to
    fund processing capacity.

SEC. 3. CAPACITY IMPACT ASSESSMENT.

(a) REQUIREMENT.—Any bill or joint resolution that expands
    eligibility for veterans' benefits under title 38, United
    States Code, shall not be reported by any committee unless
    accompanied by a Capacity Impact Assessment prepared by
    the Congressional Budget Office.

(b) CONTENTS.—A Capacity Impact Assessment shall include—
    (1) an estimate of the number of additional claims
        expected to result from the eligibility expansion
        over a 10-year period;
    (2) an estimate of the additional full-time equivalent
        employees required to process such claims within
        the 125-day target established by the Department
        of Veterans Affairs;
    (3) an estimate of the additional technology investments
        required to support processing of such claims;
    (4) the total estimated cost of processing capacity
        expansion; and
    (5) a recommended appropriation level for each of the
        first 5 fiscal years following enactment.

SEC. 4. MANDATORY PROCESSING APPROPRIATION.

(a) IN GENERAL.—Any Act that expands eligibility for
    veterans' benefits under title 38 shall include an
    appropriation for claims processing capacity equal to
    not less than the amount recommended in the Capacity
    Impact Assessment under section 3.

(b) AVAILABILITY.—Amounts appropriated under subsection (a)
    shall be available upon enactment of the eligibility
    expansion and shall remain available until expended.

(c) PROHIBITION ON RESCISSION.—Amounts appropriated under
    subsection (a) may not be rescinded or sequestered.

SEC. 5. VA IMPLEMENTATION PLAN.

(a) REQUIREMENT.—Not later than 90 days after the enactment
    of any Act expanding eligibility for veterans' benefits,
    the Secretary of Veterans Affairs shall submit to the
    Committees on Veterans' Affairs of the House and Senate
    a plan for—
    (1) hiring and training additional claims processing
        personnel;
    (2) deploying technology to support increased claims
        volume;
    (3) projecting claims volume by quarter for the first
        3 years; and
    (4) maintaining average processing times at or below
        125 days.

(b) QUARTERLY REPORTS.—The Secretary shall report quarterly
    to the Committees on Veterans' Affairs on implementation
    of the plan, including actual claims volume compared to
    projections, staffing levels, and processing times.

SEC. 6. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act shall apply to any bill or joint resolution introduced
on or after the date of enactment of this Act.
```

**Explanation**:

- **Section 3**: Creates a procedural requirement analogous to CBO scoring that cannot be waived without a supermajority vote
- **Section 4**: Makes processing funding mandatory rather than discretionary, ensuring it is available immediately
- **Section 4(c)**: Protects processing funds from sequestration or rescission
- **Section 5**: Requires the VA to plan for expansion rather than reacting to it

**Potential Challenges**:

| Challenge | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| Limits Congressional flexibility | Waiver provision could be added for urgent national security situations |
| CBO capacity to produce assessments | Similar to existing CBO scoring requirements; additional staff may be needed |
| May delay urgent benefits expansion | Assessment can be expedited for time-sensitive legislation |
| Mandatory appropriation unusual | Precedent exists in entitlement spending; this is implementation funding |

**Refinements**:

- **Stronger version**: Require processing capacity to be in place *before* eligibility expansion takes effect
- **Fallback version**: Advisory assessment only, with sense-of-Congress language on appropriations
- **Sunset provision**: Review requirement after 10 years

### Veterans Claims Accountability Act

**Purpose**: Establish statutory maximum processing times for VA disability claims with consequences for failure.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SEC. 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Veterans Claims Accountability Act".

SEC. 2. MAXIMUM PROCESSING TIMES.

(a) INITIAL CLAIMS.—The Secretary of Veterans Affairs shall
    complete adjudication of each initial claim for disability
    compensation under chapter 11 of title 38, United States
    Code, not later than 120 days after the date of receipt.

(b) SUPPLEMENTAL CLAIMS.—The Secretary shall complete
    adjudication of each supplemental claim not later than
    90 days after the date of receipt.

(c) HIGHER-LEVEL REVIEW.—The Secretary shall complete each
    higher-level review not later than 60 days after the
    date of receipt.

(d) BOARD APPEALS.—The Board of Veterans' Appeals shall issue
    a decision on each appeal not later than—
    (1) 365 days for direct review appeals;
    (2) 365 days for evidence submission appeals; and
    (3) 545 days for hearing request appeals.

SEC. 3. PROVISIONAL BENEFITS.

(a) INITIAL CLAIMS.—If the Secretary fails to complete
    adjudication of an initial claim within the period
    specified in section 2(a), the veteran shall receive
    provisional benefits at—
    (1) the disability rating claimed by the veteran; or
    (2) if no specific rating is claimed, a temporary
        rating of 30 percent,
    until the claim is finally adjudicated.

(b) SUPPLEMENTAL CLAIMS AND REVIEWS.—If the Secretary fails
    to complete adjudication within the applicable period,
    the veteran shall continue to receive benefits at the
    rate most favorable to the veteran pending completion.

(c) RECOVERY.—If a final adjudication results in a lower
    rating than the provisional rating, the Secretary may
    not seek to recover provisional overpayments.

SEC. 4. REPORTING AND ACCOUNTABILITY.

(a) QUARTERLY REPORTS.—The Secretary shall report quarterly
    to the Committees on Veterans' Affairs on—
    (1) the number of claims exceeding each processing
        timeline;
    (2) the cost of provisional benefits paid; and
    (3) actions being taken to bring processing within
        timelines.

(b) CORRECTIVE ACTION PLAN.—If more than 10 percent of
    claims in any quarter exceed the applicable timeline,
    the Secretary shall submit a corrective action plan
    within 30 days.

SEC. 5. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act shall take effect 2 years after the date of enactment,
to allow the Secretary to prepare implementation.
```

**Explanation**:

- **Section 2**: Establishes timelines tighter than the current 125-day target, creating structural incentive for capacity investment
- **Section 3**: Provisional benefits are the key accountability mechanism; they create financial pressure on the VA to meet timelines
- **Section 3(c)**: Veterans are not penalized for VA delays; no clawback of provisional payments
- **Section 5**: Two-year implementation period allows the VA to build capacity

**Potential Challenges**:

| Challenge | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| Provisional benefits could be gamed | Anti-fraud provisions could be added; current fraud rate is below 1% |
| Fiscal cost of provisional payments | Creates incentive to meet timelines, reducing long-term costs |
| VA may rush decisions to meet timelines | Quality requirements and independent accuracy monitoring essential companion measures |
| Capacity may not exist | Two-year lead time; companion capacity legislation required |

### Veterans Claims Technology Modernization Act

**Purpose**: Authorize and fund a multi-year technology modernization program for VA claims processing, including AI-assisted decision support.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SEC. 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Veterans Claims Technology
Modernization Act".

SEC. 2. MODERNIZATION PROGRAM.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—The Secretary of Veterans Affairs shall
    establish a Veterans Claims Processing Modernization
    Program to develop and deploy modern technology for
    disability claims adjudication.

(b) COMPONENTS.—The program shall include—
    (1) a cloud-based claims processing platform capable
        of scaling with demand;
    (2) automated evidence aggregation from the Department
        of Defense, Social Security Administration, and
        private medical providers;
    (3) artificial intelligence-assisted decision support
        tools for claims triage and rating recommendations;
    (4) real-time analytics for workload management and
        quality monitoring; and
    (5) a veteran-facing portal providing transparent
        claim status tracking.

SEC. 3. AI SAFEGUARDS.

(a) HUMAN OVERSIGHT.—No final rating decision shall be made
    solely by automated means. All AI-generated rating
    recommendations shall be reviewed by a human adjudicator
    before issuance.

(b) TRANSPARENCY.—The Secretary shall—
    (1) publish the criteria and methodology used by
        AI decision support tools;
    (2) provide veterans with notice when AI tools were
        used in processing their claim;
    (3) ensure veterans retain the right to request human-
        only review of their claim; and
    (4) conduct annual bias audits of AI tools, with
        results reported to Congress.

(c) PROHIBITION ON ADVERSE AUTOMATION.—AI decision support
    tools shall not be used to deny claims or reduce
    disability ratings without independent human review.

SEC. 4. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS.

(a) IN GENERAL.—There are authorized to be appropriated—
    (1) $200,000,000 for fiscal year 2027;
    (2) $250,000,000 for fiscal year 2028;
    (3) $200,000,000 for fiscal year 2029;
    (4) $150,000,000 for fiscal year 2030; and
    (5) $100,000,000 for fiscal year 2031.

(b) AVAILABILITY.—Amounts authorized under subsection (a)
    shall remain available until expended.

SEC. 5. REPORTING.

The Secretary shall submit annual reports to the Committees
on Veterans' Affairs on—
(1) modernization program progress and milestones;
(2) AI decision support deployment and performance;
(3) bias audit results;
(4) impact on processing times and accuracy; and
(5) veteran satisfaction with technology tools.
```

**Explanation**:

- **Section 2**: Comprehensive technology program rather than piecemeal IT projects
- **Section 3**: AI safeguards address due process and fairness concerns while enabling efficiency gains
- **Section 3(c)**: AI can assist with approvals but cannot independently deny claims
- **Section 4**: Multi-year funding provides stability for long-term technology investment

## State Model Legislation

### Veterans Claims Assistance Act (State Model)

**Purpose**: Establish state-level programs to assist veterans in filing fully developed federal disability claims, reducing processing time and rework.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "[State] Veterans Claims
Assistance Act".

SECTION 2. VETERANS CLAIMS ASSISTANCE PROGRAM.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—The [State Department of Veterans Affairs]
    shall establish a Veterans Claims Assistance Program to
    help veterans of this State file fully developed disability
    claims with the United States Department of Veterans
    Affairs.

(b) SERVICES.—The program shall provide—
    (1) claims filing assistance, including evidence
        gathering and documentation;
    (2) referral to accredited Veterans Service Organization
        representatives;
    (3) assistance obtaining state and local records
        relevant to claims; and
    (4) outreach to veterans unaware of potential
        eligibility, particularly under the PACT Act.

SECTION 3. OUTREACH.

(a) The [State Department of Veterans Affairs] shall conduct
    outreach to veterans in the State regarding disability
    benefits eligibility, with particular focus on—
    (1) rural veterans;
    (2) veterans not enrolled in VA healthcare;
    (3) veterans of minority communities; and
    (4) veterans eligible for PACT Act presumptive
        conditions.

SECTION 4. APPROPRIATION.

There is appropriated from the general fund the sum of
$[amount] for the fiscal year ending [date] for the
purposes of this Act.
```

## Regulatory Framework

### VA Claims Processing Quality Standards

**Existing Authority**: 38 U.S.C. Section 501 gives the Secretary broad authority to prescribe regulations for VA operations.

**Draft Regulation**:

```text
PART 3—ADJUDICATION: QUALITY STANDARDS

§ 3.2000 Purpose.

This part establishes quality standards for the adjudication
of claims for disability compensation to ensure accurate and
timely processing.

§ 3.2001 Accuracy Targets.

(a) The Veterans Benefits Administration shall maintain—
    (1) a claim-level accuracy rate of not less than 97
        percent; and
    (2) an issue-level accuracy rate of not less than 95
        percent.

(b) Regional offices failing to meet the targets in
    subsection (a) for two consecutive quarters shall
    submit corrective action plans to the Under Secretary
    for Benefits.

§ 3.2002 Quality Review.

(a) The Systematic Technical Accuracy Review (STAR) program
    shall review a random sample of not fewer than 10 percent
    of completed claims.

(b) Review results shall be reported monthly by regional
    office, condition type, and adjudicator experience level.

(c) Individual adjudicators whose accuracy falls below 90
    percent over any six-month period shall receive additional
    training and mentoring.

§ 3.2003 Error Correction.

(a) When a systemic rating interpretation error is identified
    through quality review, the Veterans Benefits Administration
    shall—
    (1) issue corrective guidance within 30 days;
    (2) identify affected claims using electronic search; and
    (3) initiate correction of affected claims within 90 days.
```

## Legal Considerations

### Constitutional Issues

**Due Process (Fifth Amendment)**:

- AI-assisted decision support must provide adequate notice and opportunity to be heard
- Veterans must be informed when AI tools are used and retain human review rights
- Provisional benefits for missed timelines create due process *protections*, not burdens

**Spending Clause**:

- Mandatory appropriations linked to eligibility expansions are within Congressional spending authority
- No constitutional barrier to requiring capacity impact assessments

### Preemption Questions

| State Action | Federal Impact |
|-------------|----------------|
| State claims assistance programs | Supplement federal system; no preemption issue |
| State-funded evidence gathering | Reduces federal development burden |
| State veteran outreach | Increases claims volume but improves claim quality |

### Enforcement Mechanisms

| Provision | Enforcement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Capacity Impact Assessment | Committee rules; procedural point of order |
| Processing timelines | Automatic provisional benefits trigger |
| Technology modernization | Annual reporting; appropriations oversight |
| Quality standards | VA internal regulation; OIG oversight |

## Loopholes, Shortcomings, and Rectification

### Veterans Claims Processing Capacity Act

**Potential Loopholes**:

| Loophole | Description | Severity |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| Waiver abuse | Congress could routinely waive the assessment requirement | High |
| Underestimated assessments | CBO could underestimate claims volume, providing inadequate funding | Medium |
| Definitional gaming | Legislation could be structured to avoid triggering "eligibility expansion" definition | Medium |
| Delayed implementation | VA could delay implementing the plan submitted under Section 5 | Medium |

**Shortcomings**:

| Issue | Impact | Root Cause |
|-------|--------|------------|
| Retroactive application impossible | Does not address existing backlog from prior expansions | Structural |
| Assessment accuracy uncertain | CBO estimates are projections with inherent uncertainty | Methodological |
| Does not address non-expansion backlogs | Backlogs caused by workforce issues or technology failures not covered | Scope limitation |

**Rectification Procedures**:

1. Add anti-waiver provision requiring supermajority vote to waive assessment requirement
2. Require VA to provide independent claims volume projections to supplement CBO estimates
3. Broaden the definition of "eligibility expansion" to include regulatory changes and court-ordered expansions
4. Add enforcement mechanisms for VA implementation plan compliance

### Veterans Claims Accountability Act

**Potential Loopholes**:

| Loophole | Description | Severity |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| Quality sacrifice for speed | VA may rush decisions to avoid provisional benefits | High |
| Strategic denial | Denying claims quickly to meet timeline, knowing veterans will appeal | High |
| Gaming receipt dates | Manipulating when claims are officially "received" to extend timelines | Medium |
| Provisional benefits fraud | Veterans filing inflated claims knowing they receive provisional benefits without clawback | Low |

**Shortcomings**:

| Issue | Impact | Root Cause |
|-------|--------|------------|
| May create perverse incentives | Speed pressure could reduce quality | Incentive design |
| Fiscal cost uncertainty | Provisional benefits cost depends on VA compliance rates | Budget uncertainty |
| Does not address appeals backlog adequately | BVA timelines may be unrealistic given current capacity | Scope limitation |

**Rectification Procedures**:

1. Link processing timelines to companion quality requirements (accuracy below target suspends timelines)
2. Add anti-gaming provisions defining "receipt date" precisely
3. Phase in timelines, starting with supplemental claims and expanding to initial claims
4. Increase BVA judicial appointments proportional to caseload

### Cross-Cutting Issues

**General Implementation Concerns**:

- All three bills require sustained Congressional commitment to appropriations, which is historically unreliable
- Technology modernization has a high failure rate in federal agencies; agile methodology and independent oversight are essential
- AI safeguards must be regularly updated as technology evolves; static regulations may become obsolete
- VSO opposition to automation features could delay implementation; early stakeholder engagement is critical

**Ongoing Monitoring Requirements**:

| Requirement | Frequency | Responsible Entity |
|-------------|-----------|---------------------|
| Claims backlog reporting | Quarterly | VA Benefits Administration |
| Accuracy audits | Monthly | Independent quality board |
| AI bias audits | Annually | VA OIT with independent auditors |
| Capacity assessment accuracy | After each expansion | GAO |
| Provisional benefits cost tracking | Quarterly | VA CFO |
