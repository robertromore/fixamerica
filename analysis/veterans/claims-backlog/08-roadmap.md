# VA Claims Backlog: Roadmap

## Phase 1: Immediate Actions (0-12 Months)

### Quick Wins

**Presumptive Approval Expansion**

- Identify 10-15 condition-service combinations with strongest presumptive evidence
- Implement automatic approval for straightforward claims meeting clear criteria
- Target: Remove 150,000-200,000 claims annually from standard processing pipeline
- Responsible: Under Secretary for Benefits, with regulatory change authority

**Medical Examination Streamlining**

- Expand acceptance of existing medical evidence in lieu of C&P examinations
- Deploy telehealth C&P examinations for mental health and internal conditions nationally
- Consolidate multiple examination requests into single appointments
- Target: Reduce average examination turnaround from 45 to 30 days

**National Work Queue Optimization**

- Refine workload distribution algorithms to better match claim complexity with office expertise
- Establish dedicated surge processing teams at offices with excess capacity
- Target: Reduce regional processing time variation from 80+ days to 50 days

### Foundation Building

**Rework Reduction Pilot**

- Launch "Right First Time" pilot at 5 regional offices
- Enhanced intake screening to identify evidence gaps before claims enter queue
- Pre-adjudication quality review of complex claims
- Target: Reduce supplemental claim rate by 20% at pilot offices

**AI Decision Support Expansion**

- Expand Automated Decision Support from pilot to 20 regional offices
- Add condition types beyond hypertension and hearing loss
- Establish transparency and audit protocols for AI-assisted decisions
- Target: AI-assisted processing for 10% of rating claims

**Workforce Stabilization**

- Implement retention bonuses for experienced RVSRs (5+ years)
- Reduce mandatory overtime through better workload distribution
- Launch accelerated training curriculum for new hires
- Target: Reduce turnover from 14% to 10%

## Phase 2: Short-Term Goals (1-2 Years)

### Process Transformation

**Right First Time National Rollout**

- Expand pilot to all 56 regional offices based on lessons learned
- Implement standardized evidence checklists for the 50 most common conditions
- Establish feedback loops connecting appeals outcomes to initial rating guidance
- Target: Reduce overall rework rate by 30%

**Specialized Processing Teams**

- Organize pilot teams by body system at 10 high-volume offices
- Musculoskeletal, mental health, respiratory, and cardiovascular teams
- Develop specialized training curricula and quality metrics
- Target: 15% improvement in accuracy for specialized conditions

**Evidence Integration**

- Complete digital integration with DOD personnel records system
- Implement automated records requests through health information exchanges
- Launch veteran-facing evidence submission portal with status tracking
- Target: Reduce evidence development time by 25%

### Legislative Action

**Claims Processing Capacity Act**

- Introduce legislation requiring CBO capacity impact assessments for all benefits expansions
- Mandate multi-year processing appropriations linked to eligibility authorizations
- Establish statutory maximum processing times (aspirational initially, mandatory in Phase 3)
- Target: Bipartisan sponsorship in both chambers

**Technology Investment**

- Secure multi-year appropriations for claims processing technology modernization
- Fund cloud infrastructure transition for VBMS
- Invest in interagency data sharing infrastructure
- Target: $500 million multi-year technology authorization

## Phase 3: Medium-Term Goals (2-5 Years)

### System Modernization

**Next-Generation Claims Platform**

- Design and begin deployment of modern cloud-based claims processing system
- Built-in AI decision support, automated workflow, and real-time analytics
- API-based integration with evidence sources
- Target: Platform operational at 20 regional offices by Year 4

**Full AI Integration**

- AI-assisted processing for 40% of rating claims (human-supervised)
- Automated triage routing all claims by complexity
- Predictive analytics identifying at-risk claims for quality intervention
- Target: 30% reduction in average processing time

**Independent Quality Agency**

- Establish independent claims quality review board
- Increase accuracy review sample to 10% of claims
- Implement mandatory corrective action for systemic error patterns
- Target: Issue-level accuracy above 95%

### Workforce Maturation

**Sustainable Workforce Model**

- Achieve permanent workforce floor sized for projected demand plus 20% surge buffer
- Establish claims processing reserve corps
- Competitive compensation aligned with market rates
- Target: Net workforce growth of 3,000+ over 3 years; turnover below 8%

**Specialization Nationwide**

- All regional offices organized by specialized processing teams
- Cross-training programs for multi-condition claims coordination
- Advanced certification and career progression for specialized adjudicators
- Target: All offices meeting or exceeding accuracy targets

### Rating Schedule Reform

- Complete VASRD modernization for top 10 body systems
- Simplify severity rating criteria with clearer, more objective standards
- Align diagnostic codes with current medical evidence and ICD-11
- Target: Reduce average adjudication time per issue by 20%

## Phase 4: Long-Term Transformation (5-10 Years)

### Goal: Sustainably Low Backlog

**Target Metrics**:

| Metric | Current (FY 2024) | Phase 4 Target |
|--------|-------------------|----------------|
| Total pending claims | 907,000+ | <300,000 |
| Backlogged claims (>125 days) | 290,000 | <50,000 |
| Average processing time | 151 days | <90 days |
| Issue-level accuracy | 91.3% | >96% |
| Appeals pending at BVA | 215,000 | <75,000 |
| Employee turnover | 13.8% | <8% |

**Structural Changes**:

- Mandatory processing timelines with provisional approval for delays
- Fully integrated evidence platform connecting all data sources
- AI-assisted processing for 60%+ of claims with human oversight
- Complete VASRD modernization
- Permanent surge capacity through reserve corps and scalable technology

### System Resilience

**Future-Proofing**:

- Automatic capacity scaling when new eligibility legislation is enacted
- Real-time claims volume forecasting linked to workforce planning
- Continuous quality improvement through AI-powered error detection
- Technology infrastructure capable of handling 2x current volume

## Risks and Contingencies

### Risk: New Eligibility Expansion Creates Another Surge

**Threat**: Congress passes major new benefits legislation without capacity funding

**Mitigation**: Claims Processing Capacity Act requires linked funding; surge reserve corps

**Contingency**: Emergency overtime authorization; temporary contractor augmentation

### Risk: Major IT Project Failure

**Threat**: Next-generation claims platform experiences cost overruns, delays, or failure

**Mitigation**: Agile development methodology; incremental deployment; fallback to enhanced VBMS

**Contingency**: Extend VBMS with modular improvements rather than full replacement

### Risk: AI Bias or Error

**Threat**: Automated decision support produces systematically biased outcomes

**Mitigation**: Bias auditing before deployment; continuous monitoring; veteran appeal rights preserved

**Contingency**: Suspend automated processing for affected condition types; human review of all AI-assisted decisions

### Risk: Workforce Recruitment Failure

**Threat**: Unable to attract and retain sufficient claims processors despite investments

**Mitigation**: Competitive compensation, telework flexibility, career development

**Contingency**: Expand use of contract adjudicators (with quality controls); reduce training timeline through simulation-based methods
