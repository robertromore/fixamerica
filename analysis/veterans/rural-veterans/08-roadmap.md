# Rural Veterans: Implementation Roadmap

## Overview

This roadmap outlines a phased implementation strategy for closing the rural veteran access gap over a 10-year period. The phases are organized by feasibility, cost, and dependency relationships, with quick wins prioritized in Phase 1 to build momentum and demonstrate results before pursuing more ambitious structural reforms.

## Phase 1: Immediate Actions (Years 1-2)

### Legislative Priorities

| Action | Vehicle | Status | Champion |
|--------|---------|--------|----------|
| Pass PRESCRIBE Act (cross-state telehealth licensing) | Standalone bill or NDAA amendment | Introduced; awaiting committee action | Senate/House VA Committees |
| Reform Beneficiary Travel mileage rate | VA appropriations rider | Proposed; no current bill | House Appropriations MilCon-VA |
| Authorize VA-FQHC partnership framework | Standalone bill | Conceptual | Senate HELP + VA Committees |
| Extend COMPACT Act emergency care to rural urgent care | COMPACT Act amendment | Conceptual | Senate VA Committee |

### Administrative Actions (No Legislation Required)

| Action | Responsible Office | Timeline | Cost |
|--------|-------------------|----------|------|
| Triple rural recruitment bonuses (up to $75K physicians) | VHA Workforce Management | 3-6 months | $80M/yr |
| Expand Digital Divide Consult to all eligible rural veterans | Office of Connected Care | 6-12 months | $100M/yr |
| Revise community care network adequacy standards for rural areas | Office of Community Care | 6-12 months | Minimal |
| Deploy 25 additional ATLAS community telehealth sites | Office of Rural Health | 12 months | $20M |
| Launch Community Health Worker pilot (10 states) | Office of Rural Health | 12-18 months | $30M/yr |
| Automate Beneficiary Travel claims via GPS verification | Office of Finance | 12-18 months | $15M one-time |
| Establish rural-specific VA performance metrics | VHA Performance Office | 6-12 months | Minimal |

### Quick Wins

| Action | Impact | Timeline |
|--------|--------|----------|
| Eliminate prior authorization for rural veteran telehealth | Removes barrier to 1.2M rural telehealth users | 30 days (administrative) |
| Satellite internet subsidy for frontier veterans | Enables telehealth for ~100K most-isolated veterans | 90 days (contract modification) |
| Rural veteran mobile app for scheduling and navigation | Improves access for tech-enabled rural veterans | 6 months (development) |
| Partner with DAV to expand volunteer driver network to 50 underserved counties | Addresses transportation gap for non-driving veterans | 6 months (grant agreement) |

### Phase 1 Milestones

- [ ] PRESCRIBE Act passed and signed into law
- [ ] Rural recruitment bonuses implemented at all VISN levels
- [ ] 100,000 additional devices distributed through Digital Divide Consult
- [ ] Community Health Worker pilot operational in 10 states
- [ ] Beneficiary Travel mileage rate increased to IRS standard
- [ ] Rural performance metrics integrated into VA dashboard

## Phase 2: Infrastructure and Capacity Building (Years 3-5)

### Facility and Workforce Investment

| Action | Description | Cost | Timeline |
|--------|-------------|------|----------|
| Enhanced CBOC upgrades (first 50 sites) | Add mental health, dental, radiology, PT to highest-volume rural CBOCs | $250M one-time + $150M/yr | Years 3-4 |
| Specialty Telehealth Hub network (first 10 hubs) | Staff specialty hubs for psychiatry, dermatology, neurology, cardiology | $25M one-time + $125M/yr | Years 3-4 |
| Mobile Health Unit fleet (first 50 units) | Procure, staff, and deploy in highest-need areas | $100M one-time + $75M/yr | Years 3-5 |
| Rural residency program (first 100 positions) | Fund residency training at rural VA facilities | $75M/yr | Year 3 (ongoing) |
| Rural provider loan repayment (500 participants) | Full loan repayment for 4-year rural VA commitment | $100M/yr | Year 3 (ongoing) |
| VA-FQHC partnerships (first 200 sites) | Establish formal care agreements with rural FQHCs | $80M/yr | Years 3-5 |

### Phase 2 Milestones

## Phase 3: Systemic Transformation (Years 6-10)

### Structural Reforms

| Action | Description | Timeline |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| VA budget model reform (VERA rural adjustment) | 20% rural cost supplement in VERA allocation formula | Year 6 |
| Federal Rural Veteran Coordination Office | Interagency coordination body co-chaired by VA and USDA | Year 6 |
| Rural veteran access guarantee | Statutory guarantee of 45-minute primary care / 90-minute specialty | Year 7 |
| Community care network redesign | Rural-specific network adequacy standards with enforcement | Year 7 |

### Scale-Up

| Action | Description | Cost | Timeline |

## Dependencies and Risks

### Critical Dependencies

| Dependency | Risk if Not Met | Mitigation |
|-----------|----------------|------------|
| BEAD broadband deployment | Telehealth strategy undermined | VA-specific satellite and ATLAS alternatives |
| Congressional appropriations | Phased rollout stalls | Prioritize administrative actions; build bipartisan support |
| Provider pipeline (medical schools) | Workforce solutions delayed 5+ years | Enhanced recruitment from existing workforce as bridge |
| Community care network reform | Rural veterans stuck in inadequate networks | Direct VA telehealth as alternative pathway |
| State telehealth licensing reform | PRESCRIBE Act fails | VA federal supremacy authority; negotiate interstate compacts |

### Key Risks

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Response |
|------|------------|--------|----------|
| Budget sequestration or austerity | Medium | High | Protect rural programs as mandatory spending |
| VA leadership turnover | High | Medium | Embed rural metrics in statute, not policy |
| Rural hospital closures accelerate | Medium | High | VA-FQHC partnerships provide backup |
| Technology disruption | Low | Medium | Maintain physical facility and mobile options |
| Cultural resistance to telehealth | Medium | Medium | Hybrid models, peer champions, gradual adoption |

## Measurement and Accountability

### Annual Reporting Requirements

The VA Secretary should be required to report annually to Congress on:

1. Rural veteran access metrics vs. targets (by VISN and state)
2. Rural provider vacancy rates and recruitment/retention outcomes
3. Telehealth utilization and satisfaction (rural vs. urban)
4. Community care network adequacy in rural areas
5. Rural veteran suicide rates and mental health access
6. Mobile Health Unit utilization and coverage area
7. ORH budget execution and program outcomes

### Independent Evaluation

GAO should conduct biennial evaluations of rural veteran access progress, with specific attention to:

- Whether MISSION Act community care is functioning in rural areas
- Whether telehealth is providing quality equivalent to in-person care
- Whether workforce initiatives are producing measurable vacancy reduction
- Whether rural-urban outcome disparities are narrowing
