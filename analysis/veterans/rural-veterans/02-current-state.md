# Rural Veterans: Current State

## Rural Veteran Demographics

### Population and Distribution

Approximately 4.7 million veterans live in rural or highly rural areas, representing 25% of the total U.S. veteran population of 18.6 million (VA Office of Rural Health, 2024). The VA classifies veteran geography using Rural-Urban Commuting Area (RUCA) codes:

| Classification | Definition | Veteran Population | Share |
|---------------|------------|-------------------|-------|
| Urban | Metro area core or high commuting to metro | 13.9 million | 75% |
| Rural | Non-metro area with some urban influence | 3.96 million | 21% |
| Highly rural | Non-metro area with minimal urban influence | 740,000 | 4% |

*Source: VA Office of Rural Health Annual Report, 2024; Census Bureau ACS, 2023.*

### Demographic Characteristics of Rural Veterans

| Characteristic | Rural Veterans | Urban Veterans | Source |
|---------------|---------------|---------------|--------|
| Median age | 64 | 58 | Census Bureau ACS, 2023 |
| Male | 92% | 88% | Census Bureau ACS, 2023 |
| White, non-Hispanic | 84% | 64% | Census Bureau ACS, 2023 |
| Service-connected disability | 29% | 25% | VA Benefits Administration, 2024 |
| Poverty rate | 12.8% | 9.4% | Census Bureau ACS, 2023 |
| Uninsured rate | 6.2% | 4.8% | Census Bureau ACS, 2023 |

Rural veterans are older, more likely to be white, more likely to have service-connected disabilities, and more likely to live in poverty than their urban counterparts. The aging rural veteran population faces compounding health challenges as chronic conditions increase while access to care diminishes.

## VA Enrollment and Utilization

### Enrollment Rates

| Category | Rural Veterans | Urban Veterans | Gap |
|----------|---------------|---------------|-----|
| VA-enrolled | 52% | 44% | +8 pts |
| Used VA healthcare in past year | 38% | 33% | +5 pts |
| Rely on VA as primary source of care | 28% | 22% | +6 pts |

*Source: VA Survey of Veteran Enrollees' Health and Use of Health Care, 2023.*

Rural veterans enroll in and use VA healthcare at higher rates than urban veterans, reflecting both greater need and fewer alternatives. This higher reliance makes VA access barriers particularly consequential for rural populations.

### Satisfaction Disparities

| Metric | Rural VA Users | Urban VA Users | Source |
|--------|---------------|---------------|--------|
| Overall satisfaction | 71% | 76% | SHEP Patient Satisfaction Survey, FY 2024 |
| Access satisfaction | 62% | 74% | SHEP Patient Satisfaction Survey, FY 2024 |
| Wait time satisfaction | 58% | 69% | SHEP Patient Satisfaction Survey, FY 2024 |
| Telehealth satisfaction | 78% | 75% | VA Office of Connected Care, 2024 |

Access and wait time satisfaction show the largest rural-urban gaps, while telehealth satisfaction is slightly higher among rural users, suggesting that virtual care resonates with rural populations when connectivity permits.

### Travel Time Standards

The MISSION Act (2018) established access standards that trigger community care eligibility:

| Service Type | Drive Time Standard | Rural Veterans Exceeding Standard | Urban Veterans Exceeding Standard |
|-------------|-------------------|----------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Primary care | 30 minutes | 41% | 8% |
| Mental health | 30 minutes | 48% | 11% |
| Specialty care | 60 minutes | 29% | 5% |

*Source: VA Office of Community Care, FY 2024 Access Report.*

### Beneficiary Travel Program

The VA Beneficiary Travel program reimburses eligible veterans for travel to VA facilities:

| Metric | FY 2022 | FY 2023 | FY 2024 |
|--------|---------|---------|---------|
| Total claims processed | 8.2M | 8.9M | 9.4M |
| Total reimbursement | $1.03B | $1.12B | $1.19B |
| Average reimbursement per claim | $126 | $126 | $127 |
| Mileage rate (per mile) | $0.415 | $0.415 | $0.415 |
| Claims denied | 12% | 11% | 11% |

*Source: VA Beneficiary Travel Annual Reports, FY 2022-2024.*

The mileage reimbursement rate ($0.415/mile) has not kept pace with actual travel costs. The IRS standard mileage rate for medical purposes was $0.22/mile in 2024, but actual rural travel costs--including vehicle wear, fuel prices, and lost work time--substantially exceed both rates.

## Community-Based Outpatient Clinics (CBOCs)

### Network Status

CBOCs are the VA's primary strategy for extending care into rural communities:

| Metric | 2015 | 2020 | 2024 |
|--------|------|------|------|
| Total CBOCs | 904 | 1,012 | 1,074 |
| Rural CBOCs | 421 | 472 | 502 |
| Highly rural CBOCs | 68 | 79 | 84 |
| Average rural CBOC enrollment | 2,800 | 3,100 | 3,200 |

*Source: VHA Site Tracking System, 2024; VA Office of Rural Health, 2024.*

### CBOC Service Limitations

Most rural CBOCs provide only primary care and limited mental health services. Services typically unavailable at rural CBOCs include:

- Specialty medical care (cardiology, neurology, oncology)
- Advanced diagnostic imaging (MRI, CT, PET)
- Surgical services
- Comprehensive rehabilitation
- Dental care (beyond emergency)
- Substance use disorder residential treatment

Veterans requiring these services must travel to VA Medical Centers or use community care referrals.

## Telehealth Utilization

### Growth and Adoption

| Metric | FY 2019 | FY 2021 | FY 2023 | FY 2024 |
|--------|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| Rural veterans using VA telehealth | 180,000 | 890,000 | 1.1M | 1.2M |
| Share of rural VA encounters via telehealth | 4% | 28% | 33% | 36% |
| VA Video Connect rural sessions | 42,000 | 520,000 | 680,000 | 740,000 |
| Telephone-only encounters (rural) | 61,000 | 410,000 | 380,000 | 350,000 |

*Source: VA Office of Connected Care Annual Reports, FY 2019-2024.*

The COVID-19 pandemic catalyzed a dramatic expansion of VA telehealth, with rural adoption growing nearly fivefold. However, growth has plateaued since FY 2022, suggesting that remaining non-adopters face structural barriers rather than awareness gaps.

### Broadband Barriers

| Metric | Rural Veteran Households | Urban Veteran Households | Source |
|--------|------------------------|------------------------|--------|
| Broadband access (25/3 Mbps+) | 78% | 97% | FCC Broadband Deployment Report, 2024 |
| Broadband access (100/20 Mbps+) | 54% | 93% | FCC Broadband Deployment Report, 2024 |
| Telehealth-capable connectivity | 71% | 95% | VA Office of Connected Care, 2024 |
| Smartphone-only internet access | 18% | 8% | Census Bureau ACS, 2023 |

The 22% of rural veterans lacking reliable broadband are effectively excluded from the VA's expanding telehealth model. VA-provided tablets and hotspots (distributed through the Digital Divide Consult program) have reached approximately 100,000 rural veterans as of FY 2024, but demand significantly exceeds supply.

### Provider Network Gaps

The MISSION Act's community care program relies on third-party administrator networks (currently Optum for most regions). In rural areas, network adequacy is a persistent challenge:

| Specialty | Rural Counties with Zero Network Providers | Source |
|-----------|------------------------------------------|--------|
| Psychiatry | 62% | VA Community Care Network Adequacy Report, 2024 |
| Cardiology | 38% | VA Community Care Network Adequacy Report, 2024 |
| Orthopedics | 31% | VA Community Care Network Adequacy Report, 2024 |
| Dermatology | 48% | VA Community Care Network Adequacy Report, 2024 |
| Oncology | 44% | VA Community Care Network Adequacy Report, 2024 |
| Neurology | 55% | VA Community Care Network Adequacy Report, 2024 |

In many rural areas, the MISSION Act's promise of community care alternatives is hollow because no private-sector providers exist to fill the gap.

## Recent Developments (2024-2025)

### Policy Changes

- **PACT Act rural outreach (2022-present)**: VA conducted targeted outreach to rural veterans for toxic exposure eligibility, enrolling approximately 280,000 previously unenrolled rural veterans through FY 2024
- **MISSION Act access standards revision (2024)**: VA proposed tightening drive time standards from 30 to 20 minutes for primary care, potentially expanding community care eligibility for rural veterans
- **VA ATLAS expansion**: VA installed telehealth access points (Accessing Telehealth through Local Area Stations) at Walmart, American Legion posts, and other community locations--148 sites as of FY 2024, up from 80 in FY 2022
- **Rural Emergency Hospital designation**: CMS created a new rural emergency hospital designation that some rural VA partners are utilizing to maintain emergency care access

### Ongoing Challenges

- Community Care Network contract transitions have disrupted care for some rural veterans
- VA broadband connectivity remains dependent on BEAD program implementation timelines
- Rural CBOC consolidation proposals have generated community opposition
- Workforce shortages continue to worsen in behavioral health despite recruitment incentives

## The Gap Between Problem and Action

| The Problem | Current Response | The Gap |
|-------------|-----------------|---------|
| 41% of rural veterans exceed drive time standards | MISSION Act community care | 7.4% of authorizations unfulfilled due to no provider |
| 38% psychiatrist vacancy rate at rural VA | Recruitment bonuses, loan repayment | Vacancies worsening year over year |
| 22% of rural veterans lack broadband | Digital Divide Consult (tablets/hotspots) | 100K devices vs. 1M+ eligible veterans |
| Rural suicide rate 67% higher than urban | Telehealth expansion, crisis line | Rate gap has not narrowed since tracking began |
| Rural CBOCs offer limited services | Community care referrals | Rural providers equally scarce |
| Travel costs exceed reimbursement | Beneficiary Travel Program at $0.415/mile | Rate frozen; actual costs far higher |
