# Survivors Benefits: Actions

## For Individuals

### Educate Yourself

**Understand the Current System**:

- Learn the difference between DIC (service-connected death) and Survivors Pension (wartime veteran, need-based)
- Understand the remarriage penalty and how it affects benefit eligibility at different ages
- Know the SBP-DIC offset history and its full elimination as of January 2023
- Research CHAMPVA coverage and its limitations compared to TRICARE
- Learn about education benefits available to survivors (Fry Scholarship vs. DEA)

**Follow Survivor Policy News**:

| Source | Focus | Access |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Military Times | Legislative developments, benefit changes | militarytimes.com |
| Stars and Stripes | Congressional action on survivor issues | stripes.com |
| TAPS Legislative Updates | Survivor-specific advocacy priorities | taps.org/advocacy |
| VA News Releases | Benefit rate changes, program updates | news.va.gov |
| Congressional Veterans' Affairs Committee | Hearings and legislation | veterans.senate.gov / veterans.house.gov |

### If You Are a Surviving Spouse or Family Member

**Apply for All Benefits You May Be Eligible For**:

| Benefit | How to Apply | Key Form |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| DIC | File VA Form 21-534EZ online at va.gov or through a VSO | VA Form 21-534EZ |
| Survivors Pension | File VA Form 21-534EZ (same form, different eligibility) | VA Form 21-534EZ |
| CHAMPVA | File VA Form 10-10d after DIC is established | VA Form 10-10d |
| Fry Scholarship | Apply through va.gov education portal | VA Form 22-5490 |
| DEA | Apply through va.gov education portal | VA Form 22-5490 |
| VA Home Loan | Apply through VA-approved lender | VA Form 26-1817 |
| Burial Benefits | Apply through National Cemetery Administration | VA Form 40-1330 |
| Social Security Survivor Benefits | Contact SSA at 1-800-772-1213 | SSA application |
| SBP | Contact DFAS at 1-800-321-1080 | DD Form 2656 |

**Get Free Help**:

- Contact a Veterans Service Organization (American Legion, VFW, DAV) for free claims assistance
- Call TAPS at 1-800-959-TAPS (8277) for grief support and benefit navigation
- Visit your County Veterans Service Officer (CVSO) for in-person assistance
- Contact your congressional representative's caseworker if your claim is delayed
- Never pay for help filing VA claims--accredited VSO representatives provide this service for free

**Know Your Rights**:

| Right | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Duty to Assist | VA is legally obligated to help you gather evidence for your claim (38 U.S.C. 5103A) |
| Benefit of the Doubt | If evidence is approximately balanced, VA must decide in your favor (38 U.S.C. 5107(b)) |
| Appeal rights | You can appeal any denied claim through Higher-Level Review, Supplemental Claim, or Board of Veterans' Appeals |
| Retroactive pay | If granted, DIC is typically paid from the date of application or the date of the veteran's death (whichever is later, within one year) |
| Legal representation | You have the right to hire an attorney for appeals, with fees limited to 20% of past-due benefits |

### If You Are an Active-Duty Service Member or Veteran

**Plan for Your Family**:

- Ensure your service-connected conditions are documented and rated by the VA
- Discuss survivor benefit eligibility with your spouse and family
- Register for SBP during retirement if applicable; understand the coverage and cost
- Designate beneficiaries for SGLI and update regularly
- Keep records of service, deployments, and health conditions organized and accessible
- Consider transferring GI Bill benefits to dependents if eligible

## For Community Members

### Support Survivor Families

**Direct Actions**:

- Volunteer with TAPS, Snowball Express, or other survivor support organizations
- Offer practical support (meals, childcare, transportation) to newly bereaved military families
- Maintain long-term connection--survivor isolation increases over time, not just in the first weeks
- Support Gold Star family recognition events in your community
- Hire military survivors--their career disruptions during service make employment re-entry challenging

**Community Education**:

- Organize events for Gold Star Mothers Day (last Sunday in September) and Gold Star Spouses Day (April 5)
- Invite TAPS or VSO representatives to speak at civic organizations
- Share information about survivor benefits through community networks
- Advocate for local businesses to offer discounts or services to Gold Star families

## For Advocates

### Legislative Advocacy

**Priority Issues**:

| Issue | Action | Target |
|-------|--------|--------|
| Remarriage penalty elimination | Contact your Senators and Representative; urge co-sponsorship of survivor benefit bills | 119th Congress (2025-2026) |
| DIC rate increase | Support legislation indexing DIC to 55% of E-7 base pay | Congressional veterans' committees |
| CHAMPVA modernization | Urge Congress to authorize CHAMPVA-TRICARE network integration | Senate/House Veterans' Affairs Committees |
| Education benefit equalization | Advocate for DEA-Fry Scholarship equalization | Congressional education/veterans committees |

**Effective Advocacy Tactics**:

1. **Personal stories**: Connect legislators with survivors in their district who are affected by current policy gaps
2. **Constituent meetings**: Request meetings with legislators or staff to discuss survivor issues; bring specific policy asks
3. **Written testimony**: Submit written testimony to Veterans' Affairs Committee hearings
4. **Coalition building**: Coordinate with TAPS, Gold Star Wives, and VSOs to amplify messaging
5. **Social media**: Use #WidowsTax, #GoldStarFamilies, and #SurvivorBenefits to raise awareness
6. **Op-eds**: Write opinion pieces for local newspapers highlighting survivor benefit gaps

### Legal Advocacy

- Support pro bono legal clinics that assist survivors with DIC claims and appeals
- Advocate for increased funding for County Veterans Service Officers
- Report unaccredited individuals charging fees for VA claims assistance (prohibited by 38 U.S.C. 5905)
- Support litigation challenging the constitutionality of the remarriage penalty

## For Employers

### Hire Military Survivors

**Why It Matters**: Military spouses and survivors often have significant career gaps due to frequent relocations during their spouse's service. After the service member's death, survivors need employment but face resume gaps and geographic limitations.

**Actions**:

| Action | Implementation |
|--------|---------------|
| Recognize military spouse/survivor career gaps | Train hiring managers to value military family experience |
| Offer flexible work arrangements | Remote work, flexible scheduling for survivors managing family responsibilities |
| Partner with TAPS Employment Program | Access a pipeline of qualified survivor candidates |
| Participate in military family hiring initiatives | Department of Labor VETS program, Hiring Our Heroes |
| Provide mentorship | Pair survivor hires with mentors for career development |

## For Healthcare Providers

### Accept CHAMPVA Patients

**Why It Matters**: CHAMPVA beneficiaries face provider access challenges because reimbursement rates are set at Medicare levels, and there is no provider network.

**Actions**:

| Action | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Register as a CHAMPVA provider | Contact VA Health Administration for provider enrollment |
| Accept CHAMPVA assignment | Accept CHAMPVA reimbursement as payment in full |
| Connect patients with CHAMPVA Meds by Mail | Help patients access mail-order prescriptions |
| Provide grief-informed care | Recognize that CHAMPVA patients are bereaved family members with specific emotional needs |

## For Researchers

### Fill the Data Gaps

**Priority Research Questions**:

| Question | Current State of Knowledge |
|----------|---------------------------|
| What is the poverty rate among DIC recipients? | Unknown; VA does not systematically track |
| How does the remarriage penalty affect survivor behavior? | Anecdotal evidence only; no rigorous study |
| What is the gap between eligible and enrolled survivor populations? | Unknown; no comprehensive outreach study |
| How do survivor benefits interact with state-level programs? | State variation documented but not systematically analyzed |
| What are long-term mental health outcomes for surviving children? | Limited longitudinal research |
| How does CHAMPVA access affect health outcomes? | Limited beyond GAO provider participation study |

**Research Opportunities**:

- Partner with VA's Office of Policy and Planning for access to administrative data
- Apply for funding through the RAND National Defense Research Institute
- Collaborate with TAPS Research Department for survey access and survivor outreach
- Submit research proposals to the VA Health Services Research and Development Service
