# Survivors Benefits: Solutions

## Overview

Reform of the survivors benefits system should address four core failures: inadequate benefit levels, punitive eligibility restrictions, administrative complexity, and data gaps that prevent evidence-based policymaking. The solutions below are organized from the most immediately achievable to the most structurally ambitious.

---

## DIC Rate Reform

### Increase DIC Base Rate to 55% of E-7 Pay

**Current Problem**: DIC's flat rate of $1,612.75/month replaces only 37% of an E-7's base pay and falls below self-sufficiency for most households.

**Proposal**: Index the base DIC rate to 55% of the base pay of an E-7 with 10 years of service, matching the replacement rate of the Survivor Benefit Plan.

| Metric | Current | Proposed | Change |
|--------|--------:|--------:|-------:|
| Monthly DIC | $1,612.75 | $2,410 | +$797 |
| Annual DIC | $19,353 | $28,920 | +$9,567 |
| As % of E-7 pay (10 yrs) | 37% | 55% | +18 pts |
| Estimated annual cost | -- | +$4.1B | -- |

**Rationale**: The 55% replacement rate is already established as the standard for military survivor benefits (SBP). Linking DIC to the E-7 pay grade provides a realistic benchmark for middle-enlisted families while creating an automatic adjustment mechanism through annual military pay raises.

**Implementation**: Phase in over 3 years to manage budget impact. Update annually with military pay adjustments.

### Enhanced DIC for Survivors with Children

**Current Problem**: The per-child add-on of $361.69/month does not reflect actual child-rearing costs, which the USDA estimates at $1,100-1,400/month per child (USDA, *Expenditures on Children by Families*, 2024 update).

**Proposal**: Increase the per-child add-on to $750/month for each child under 18, and extend the add-on to age 23 for children in post-secondary education.

| Current | Proposed | Source |
|--------:|--------:|--------|
| $361.69/child/month | $750/child/month | USDA child-rearing cost data, 2024 |

## Remarriage Penalty Elimination

### Full Elimination of Remarriage Restrictions on DIC

**Proposal**: Eliminate the remarriage age threshold entirely. DIC should continue regardless of marital status, as is the case for Social Security survivor benefits after age 60.

**Arguments for Full Elimination**:

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|------------|
| Precedent | Social Security survivor benefits continue after remarriage at 60; Federal Employee survivor benefits are not affected by remarriage |
| Equity | Benefit was earned by service member's death, not the survivor's marital status |
| Impact on behavior | Current policy discourages remarriage or encourages cohabitation without marriage |
| Cost | Estimated $280 million/year (CBO, 2024) |
| Political viability | Bipartisan cosponsorship in multiple Congresses |

**Implementation**: Effective immediately upon enactment; restore benefits to previously terminated survivors who apply within 2 years.

### Extend Remarriage Protection to Survivors Pension and CHAMPVA

**Current Problem**: Even if DIC remarriage penalties are eliminated, Survivors Pension and CHAMPVA eligibility are still terminated upon remarriage at any age.

**Proposal**: Apply the same remarriage rules to all VA survivor benefits.

## CHAMPVA Modernization

### Establish a CHAMPVA Provider Network

**Current Problem**: CHAMPVA has no provider network. Beneficiaries must find providers who will accept CHAMPVA reimbursement at Medicare rates. In the GAO's 2023 survey, 23% of CHAMPVA beneficiaries reported difficulty finding providers (GAO-24-106523).

**Proposal**: Contract with TRICARE regional contractors to include CHAMPVA beneficiaries in existing TRICARE Select networks, with reimbursement rates at TRICARE levels.

| Feature | Current CHAMPVA | Proposed |
|---------|----------------|----------|
| Provider network | None | TRICARE Select network |
| Reimbursement rate | Medicare rates | TRICARE rates (15-20% higher than Medicare) |
| Provider participation | Declining | Maintained through network contracts |
| Estimated cost increase | -- | $300-600 million/year |

### Add Prescription Drug Network

**Current Problem**: CHAMPVA Meds by Mail is the primary pharmacy benefit, but retail pharmacy coverage is limited. Survivors often pay out-of-pocket for prescriptions at retail pharmacies.

**Proposal**: Integrate CHAMPVA beneficiaries into the TRICARE retail pharmacy network through Express Scripts (or successor contractor).

## Education Benefit Reform

### Equalize DEA and Fry Scholarship Benefits

**Current Problem**: DEA (Chapter 35) provides $1,298/month with no tuition coverage, while the Fry Scholarship provides full tuition, housing allowance, and book stipend. This two-tier system disadvantages survivors of veterans who died from service-connected conditions but not in the line of duty.

**Proposal**: Convert all DEA eligibility to Fry Scholarship-equivalent benefits, providing full tuition, housing, and book stipend coverage.

| Feature | Current DEA | Current Fry | Proposed (unified) |
|---------|------------|-------------|-------------------|
| Tuition coverage | None (flat rate only) | Full in-state or $27,120/yr private | Full in-state or private cap |
| Housing allowance | None | E-5 BAH rate | E-5 BAH rate |
| Books and supplies | None | $1,000/year | $1,000/year |
| Months | 36-45 | 36 | 36 |

### Extend Eligibility Windows

**Current Problem**: Fry Scholarship eligibility for children ends at age 33; DEA at age 26. Surviving children who serve in the military, face health challenges, or delay education for other reasons may age out.

**Proposal**: Extend education benefit eligibility to age 36 for all survivor education programs, and eliminate time limits for surviving spouses.

## Administrative Simplification

### Unified Survivor Application

**Current Problem**: Survivors must file separate applications with VA, DoD, SSA, and state agencies. During acute grief, this administrative burden is overwhelming.

**Proposal**: Create a single intake application (digital and paper) that simultaneously initiates:

- DIC or Survivors Pension claim with VA
- SBP election/modification with DoD
- Social Security survivor benefit application
- CHAMPVA enrollment
- Education benefit eligibility determination
- State veteran benefit applications (where states opt in)

**Implementation**: Requires interagency data-sharing agreements and legislation authorizing cross-agency processing. Model on the TSP (Thrift Savings Plan) death benefit process, which already coordinates across agencies.

### Automatic DIC for Survivors of 100% Rated Veterans

**Current Problem**: When a veteran rated 100% service-connected disabled for 10+ continuous years dies, the surviving spouse must still file a DIC claim and wait for adjudication (average 110 days).

**Proposal**: Automatically initiate DIC payments to the surviving spouse of any veteran who was rated 100% disabled and continuously for 10+ years, using existing VA records. The surviving spouse would need only to confirm identity and provide banking information.

**Legal Authority**: This could be accomplished through VA rulemaking under existing authority in 38 U.S.C. 1318, without new legislation.

### Fast-Track DIC for Combat Deaths

**Proposal**: For deaths that occur in combat zones or are clearly documented as line-of-duty, process DIC claims within 14 days using expedited procedures modeled on the DoD Casualty Assistance framework.

## Data and Accountability

### Survivor Outcome Tracking

**Current Problem**: The VA does not systematically measure whether survivor benefits achieve their goal of preventing financial hardship.

**Proposal**: Mandate annual reporting by the VA on:

| Metric | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| Poverty rate among DIC recipients | Measure benefit adequacy |
| Employment rate among working-age DIC recipients | Assess workforce participation |
| CHAMPVA access (provider participation, wait times) | Measure healthcare access |
| Education benefit utilization rates | Identify access barriers |
| Geographic variation in all metrics | Target interventions |
| Remarriage rates and benefit terminations | Quantify remarriage penalty impact |

### Periodic Adequacy Review

**Proposal**: Require the VA, in consultation with the Bureau of Labor Statistics, to conduct a DIC adequacy review every 5 years. The review would:

1. Compare DIC rates to cost-of-living benchmarks for survivor household types
2. Assess whether COLA adjustments have maintained real purchasing power
3. Recommend rate adjustments to Congress if adequacy gaps are identified
4. Report on interactions between DIC and other federal benefits

## Structural Reforms

### Survivor Benefits Ombudsman

**Proposal**: Establish a Survivor Benefits Ombudsman within the VA, independent of VBA, with authority to:

- Investigate complaints from survivors about benefit access and processing
- Recommend systemic reforms to the Secretary
- Report annually to Congress
- Advocate for survivors within the VA's policymaking process

**Model**: Based on the Taxpayer Advocate Service within the IRS, which has demonstrated effectiveness in identifying systemic problems and advocating for individual taxpayers.

### Proactive Outreach to Eligible Non-Enrollees

**Current Problem**: An unknown number of survivors who are eligible for DIC or other benefits have never applied, due to lack of awareness, geographic isolation, or distrust of government.

**Proposal**: Using VA records of deceased veterans with service-connected disabilities, identify surviving spouses and children who have not applied for DIC. Conduct targeted outreach through:

- Direct mail to last known addresses
- Partnership with VSOs and CVSOs for community outreach
- Social media and digital outreach through veteran-serving platforms
- Collaboration with Social Security (which has death records) to cross-reference eligibility

## International Comparisons

Several allied nations provide more comprehensive survivor benefits:

| Country | Key Feature | Source |
|---------|------------|--------|
| United Kingdom | War Widows' Pension continues regardless of remarriage since 2015 | UK Ministry of Defence, 2015 policy change |
| Canada | Survivor benefits continue regardless of remarriage | Veterans Affairs Canada, *Survivor Benefits*, 2024 |
| Australia | War widows' pension continues regardless of remarriage; no age restriction | Australian Department of Veterans' Affairs, 2024 |
| Germany | Comprehensive survivor pension, no remarriage penalty | German Federal Ministry of Defence, 2024 |

The remarriage penalty in U.S. survivor benefits is an international outlier among allied nations.
