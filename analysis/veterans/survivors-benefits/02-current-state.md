# Survivors Benefits: Current State

## DIC Benefits and Enrollment

### Monthly Compensation Rates

Dependency and Indemnity Compensation (DIC) is the primary benefit for surviving spouses, children, and parents of veterans who died from service-connected causes or who were rated totally disabled for a continuous period of at least 10 years prior to death.

| Benefit Category | Monthly Rate (2025) | Annual Total | Source |
|-----------------|--------------------:|-------------:|--------|
| Basic DIC (surviving spouse) | $1,612.75 | $19,353 | 38 U.S.C. 1311; VA 2025 rates |
| Additional per dependent child | $361.69 | $4,340 | 38 U.S.C. 1311(b) |
| Aid and Attendance supplement | $387.15 | $4,646 | 38 U.S.C. 1311(c) |
| Housebound supplement | $181.54 | $2,179 | 38 U.S.C. 1311(d) |
| Transitional benefit (first 2 years with children) | $329.00 | $3,948 | 38 U.S.C. 1311(f) |
| Additional for 8+ years of service-connected disability | $316.83 | $3,802 | 38 U.S.C. 1311(a)(2) |

### DIC Adequacy Analysis

The basic DIC rate of $1,612.75 per month ($19,353 annually) falls below several commonly used benchmarks for income adequacy:

| Benchmark | Annual Amount (2024) | DIC as % of Benchmark | Source |
|-----------|---------------------:|---------------------:|--------|
| Federal poverty line (single person) | $15,060 | 129% | HHS Poverty Guidelines, 2024 |
| Federal poverty line (parent + 1 child) | $20,440 | 95% | HHS Poverty Guidelines, 2024 |
| Median household income (single person) | $40,480 | 48% | Census Bureau, 2023 ACS |
| E-5 base pay (10 years) | $47,616 | 41% | DoD Military Pay Tables, 2024 |
| Social Security survivor benefit (average) | $17,532 | 110% | SSA Annual Statistical Supplement, 2024 |

For a surviving spouse with one dependent child, total DIC ($1,612.75 + $361.69 = $1,974.44/month, or $23,693/year) falls slightly above the two-person federal poverty line but substantially below the median income such a household would have received while the veteran was alive.

## Survivors Pension

### Eligibility and Rates

The Survivors Pension (formerly Death Pension) is a needs-based benefit for surviving spouses and children of wartime veterans, regardless of whether the death was service-connected.

| Category | Annual Rate (2025) | Monthly Equivalent | Source |
|----------|-------------------:|-------------------:|--------|
| Surviving spouse alone | $10,346 | $862 | 38 U.S.C. 1541; VA 2025 rates |
| Surviving spouse with one dependent | $13,550 | $1,129 | 38 U.S.C. 1541(c) |
| Surviving spouse (housebound) | $12,649 | $1,054 | 38 U.S.C. 1541(d) |
| Surviving spouse (Aid and Attendance) | $16,509 | $1,376 | 38 U.S.C. 1541(d) |
| Surviving child alone | $2,523 | $210 | 38 U.S.C. 1542 |

### Pension Enrollment Trends

| Metric | FY 2020 | FY 2022 | FY 2024 | Source |
|--------|--------:|--------:|--------:|--------|
| Survivors Pension recipients | 195,000 | 185,000 | 175,000 | VA Annual Benefits Report, FY 2020-2024 |
| New pension awards | 18,000 | 16,000 | 14,500 | VA Annual Benefits Report, FY 2020-2024 |

| Annual pension spending (millions) | $2,100 | $1,950 | $1,800 | VA Budget Submission, FY 2022-2026 |

The declining pension enrollment reflects the aging and death of World War II and Korean War-era survivors, partially offset by new Vietnam-era beneficiaries.

### Net Worth Limitation

Since October 2018, the VA applies a net worth limit (equal to the Community Spouse Resource Allowance used by Medicaid, set at $150,538 in 2024) and a 3-year look-back period for asset transfers. These provisions were enacted to prevent pension poaching--predatory financial advisors who helped wealthy individuals artificially qualify for Aid and Attendance benefits (VA Rule, 83 Fed. Reg. 47246, 2018).

## SBP-DIC Offset

### Background

The Survivor Benefit Plan (SBP) is a DoD-administered annuity program under which military retirees pay premiums (typically 6.5% of retired pay) to provide their surviving spouse with 55% of their retired pay upon death. Historically, DIC payments dollar-for-dollar offset SBP annuities, effectively negating the insurance that retirees had purchased. This was widely known as the "widows tax."

### Legislative Resolution

The National Defense Authorization Act for FY 2020 (P.L. 116-92, Sec. 622) mandated a phased elimination of the SBP-DIC offset:

| Year | Offset Reduction | Effective |
|------|----------------:|-----------|
| FY 2021 | 1/3 eliminated | January 2021 |
| FY 2022 | 2/3 eliminated | January 2022 |
| FY 2023 | Fully eliminated | January 2023 |

### Current Status (2025)

| Metric | Detail | Source |
|--------|--------|--------|
| SBP-DIC offset | Fully eliminated as of January 2023 | P.L. 116-92, Sec. 622 |
| Affected survivors | ~65,000 | DoD Actuary, FY 2024 |
| Average monthly benefit increase | $950 | CBO estimate, 2023 |
| Retroactive refund of premiums | Special Survivors Indemnity Allowance (SSIA) replaced by full SBP | P.L. 116-92 |

While the offset has been eliminated going forward, survivors who were affected during the decades when the offset was in place did not receive retroactive compensation for the years of reduced benefits.

## CHAMPVA Healthcare

### Coverage and Enrollment

The Civilian Health and Medical Program of the Department of Veterans Affairs (CHAMPVA) provides healthcare coverage to survivors and dependents of veterans who are permanently and totally disabled from a service-connected condition, or who died from a service-connected condition.

| Metric | FY 2022 | FY 2023 | FY 2024 | Source |
|--------|--------:|--------:|--------:|--------|
| Total CHAMPVA enrollees | 395,000 | 410,000 | 420,000 | VA Health Administration data, FY 2022-2024 |
| Annual claims processed | 7.2M | 7.5M | 7.8M | VA Health Administration data, FY 2022-2024 |
| Claims processing time (avg days) | 28 | 32 | 35 | VA Health Administration data, FY 2022-2024 |
| Annual CHAMPVA spending (millions) | $1,900 | $2,050 | $2,200 | VA Budget Submission, FY 2024-2026 |

### Coverage Limitations

CHAMPVA coverage, while valuable, has significant gaps compared to TRICARE (which covers active-duty and retiree family members):

| Feature | CHAMPVA | TRICARE (Select) | Source |
|---------|---------|-------------------|--------|
| Annual deductible | $50 individual / $100 family | $150 individual / $300 family | VA CHAMPVA Handbook, 2024; TRICARE, 2024 |
| Cost share | 25% of allowable charges | 20-25% of negotiated rates | VA CHAMPVA Handbook, 2024; TRICARE, 2024 |
| Catastrophic cap | $3,000/year | $3,500/year | VA CHAMPVA Handbook, 2024; TRICARE, 2024 |
| Network | No network; any willing provider | Established provider network | VA CHAMPVA Handbook, 2024; TRICARE, 2024 |
| Provider acceptance | Many providers decline CHAMPVA | Broad network participation | GAO Report, "CHAMPVA Provider Participation," 2023 |
| Prescription drug coverage | CHAMPVA Meds by Mail; retail limited | Comprehensive pharmacy benefit | VA CHAMPVA Handbook, 2024 |
| Mental health parity | Coverage available but access limited | Parity with medical coverage | VA CHAMPVA Handbook, 2024 |

The lack of a provider network is a persistent problem. Because CHAMPVA reimburses at Medicare rates, many providers decline to accept CHAMPVA patients, particularly in areas with high cost-of-living (GAO Report, "CHAMPVA Provider Participation," 2023).

## Education Benefits

### Fry Scholarship

The Marine Gunnery Sergeant John David Fry Scholarship provides Post-9/11 GI Bill benefits to children and spouses of service members who died in the line of duty after September 10, 2001.

| Feature | Detail | Source |
|---------|--------|--------|
| Tuition coverage | Full in-state at public institutions; up to $27,120.05/year at private (2024-2025) | 38 U.S.C. 3311; VA Education Service, 2024 |
| Housing allowance | E-5 with dependents BAH rate | 38 U.S.C. 3313 |
| Books and supplies | $1,000/year | 38 U.S.C. 3313 |
| Months of entitlement | 36 months | 38 U.S.C. 3311 |
| Eligibility (children) | Between ages 18 and 33 | 38 U.S.C. 3311 |
| Eligibility (spouses) | 15 years from date of death | 38 U.S.C. 3311 |

### Dependents' Educational Assistance (DEA)

DEA (Chapter 35) provides education benefits to dependents of veterans who are permanently and totally disabled or who died from service-connected causes.

| Feature | Detail | Source |
|---------|--------|--------|
| Monthly benefit rate | $1,298 (full-time, 2024-2025) | 38 U.S.C. 3532; VA Education Service, 2024 |
| Months of entitlement | 36 months (up to 45 with extension) | 38 U.S.C. 3511 |
| Eligibility (children) | Ages 18-26 (extensions available) | 38 U.S.C. 3512 |
| Eligibility (spouses) | 10 years from VA determination | 38 U.S.C. 3512 |

DEA benefits are substantially less generous than the Fry Scholarship, creating a two-tier system based on the era and circumstances of the veteran's death.

## Remarriage Penalties

### Current Rules

| Situation | Effect on Benefits | Source |
|-----------|-------------------|--------|
| Remarriage before age 55 (DIC) | All DIC benefits terminated | 38 U.S.C. 103(d)(2)(B) |
| Remarriage after age 55 but before 57 | DIC restored if later marriage ends | 38 U.S.C. 103(d)(2)(B) |
| Remarriage at age 57 or older | DIC continues | P.L. 108-183, Sec. 101 (2003) |
| Remarriage (Survivors Pension) | Pension terminated regardless of age | 38 U.S.C. 1541(a) |
| Remarriage (CHAMPVA) | CHAMPVA eligibility lost | VA CHAMPVA eligibility rules |
| Remarriage (VA home loan) | Eligibility lost | 38 U.S.C. 3701(b)(2) |

### Impact

Approximately 400,000 surviving spouses are of an age where remarriage would result in loss of some or all VA benefits (Congressional Research Service, "Survivors Benefits for Veterans' Families," 2024). This creates a financial disincentive to remarry that:

- Disproportionately affects younger surviving spouses, who have decades of life ahead
- Penalizes survivors of service members killed in combat, who tend to be younger
- Has no analog in Social Security survivor benefits, which continue regardless of remarriage after age 60
- Forces survivors to choose between personal relationships and financial security

## Recent Developments

### 2023-2025

- **January 2023**: SBP-DIC offset fully eliminated, benefiting approximately 65,000 survivors (P.L. 116-92)
- **August 2023**: VA expanded PACT Act presumptive conditions, increasing DIC eligibility for toxic exposure-related deaths
- **January 2024**: 3.2% COLA applied to DIC and pension rates (matching Social Security COLA)
- **June 2024**: GAO report on CHAMPVA access finding 23% of surveyed beneficiaries had difficulty finding providers (GAO-24-106523)
- **January 2025**: 2.5% COLA applied to DIC and pension rates
- **2025**: Pending legislation to lower or eliminate the remarriage age threshold (S. 345, Veterans Survivors Benefits Improvement Act, 119th Congress)

### Ongoing Challenges

| Challenge | Status | Source |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| DIC adequacy | No legislation pending to increase base rate beyond COLA | Congressional Research Service, 2024 |
| Remarriage age threshold | Bills introduced but not advanced in multiple Congresses | Congressional Research Service, 2024 |
| CHAMPVA provider access | GAO recommended improvements; VA concurred but action pending | GAO-24-106523 |
| DIC claims processing | Average 110 days; PACT Act claims adding volume | VA Benefits Administration, FY 2024 |
| Surviving parent DIC | Only ~9,000 recipients; stringent income limits | VA Annual Benefits Report, FY 2024 |
