# Survivors Benefits: Legislation and Legal Framework

## Overview

This document proposes federal legislation to address the primary gaps in the veterans survivors benefits system: inadequate DIC rates, punitive remarriage penalties, CHAMPVA access barriers, education benefit disparities, and administrative complexity. No constitutional amendments are needed, as these reforms operate within Congress's established authority under the Spending Clause and its power to provide for the common defense and general welfare.

---

## Federal Legislation

### Veterans Survivors Equity Act

**Purpose**: Increase DIC rates to ensure adequacy, eliminate the remarriage penalty, and modernize the survivors benefits system.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SEC. 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Veterans Survivors Equity Act".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds the following:
(1) Dependency and Indemnity Compensation rates have not been
    systematically reviewed for adequacy since 1993.
(2) The basic DIC rate replaces approximately 37 percent of the
    base pay of an E-7 with 10 years of service, substantially
    below the 55 percent replacement rate established by the
    Survivor Benefit Plan.
(3) Approximately 400,000 surviving spouses face loss of all VA
    benefits upon remarriage before age 57, a restriction with
    no analog in Social Security survivor benefits.
(4) Allied nations including the United Kingdom, Canada, and
    Australia have eliminated remarriage penalties for military
    survivors.

SEC. 3. DIC RATE INCREASE.

(a) IN GENERAL.--Section 1311(a)(1) of title 38, United States Code,
is amended by striking the existing dollar amount and inserting "an
amount equal to 55 percent of the monthly basic pay payable to a
member of the Armed Forces in pay grade E-7 with 10 years of
service, as adjusted annually."

(b) PHASED IMPLEMENTATION.--The increase required by subsection (a)
shall be phased in as follows:
    (1) For the first fiscal year beginning after the date of
        enactment, the rate shall be the greater of the rate in
        effect on the day before enactment or 45 percent of the
        applicable basic pay.
    (2) For the second fiscal year, 50 percent.
    (3) For the third fiscal year and thereafter, 55 percent.

(c) CHILD ADD-ON.--Section 1311(b) of title 38, United States Code,
is amended by striking the existing dollar amount and inserting
"$750, adjusted annually by the percentage by which benefit amounts
payable under title II of the Social Security Act are increased."

SEC. 4. ELIMINATION OF REMARRIAGE PENALTY.

(a) DIC.--Section 103(d)(2)(B) of title 38, United States Code, is
amended by striking "age 57" and inserting "age 18".

(b) SURVIVORS PENSION.--Section 1541(a) of title 38, United States
Code, is amended by inserting after "surviving spouse" the following:
", regardless of whether the surviving spouse has remarried,".

(c) CHAMPVA.--The Secretary of Veterans Affairs shall not terminate
CHAMPVA eligibility under section 1781 of title 38, United States
Code, solely on the basis of the beneficiary's remarriage.

(d) HOME LOAN.--Section 3701(b)(2) of title 38, United States Code,
is amended by inserting ", regardless of remarriage status," after
"surviving spouse".

(e) RESTORATION.--Any surviving spouse whose DIC, Survivors Pension,
CHAMPVA eligibility, or home loan eligibility was previously
terminated solely due to remarriage may apply for restoration of
benefits within 2 years of the date of enactment.

SEC. 5. AUTOMATIC DIC FOR CERTAIN SURVIVORS.

(a) IN GENERAL.--The Secretary of Veterans Affairs shall, upon
notification of the death of a veteran who was in receipt of
disability compensation at a total rating for a continuous period
of not less than 10 years immediately preceding death, automatically
initiate Dependency and Indemnity Compensation payments to the
surviving spouse without requiring a separate claim, subject to
verification of identity and payment information.

(b) IMPLEMENTATION.--Not later than 180 days after the date of
enactment, the Secretary shall issue regulations to carry out
subsection (a).

SEC. 6. PERIODIC ADEQUACY REVIEW.

(a) IN GENERAL.--Not less frequently than every 5 years, the
Secretary of Veterans Affairs, in consultation with the Commissioner
of the Bureau of Labor Statistics, shall conduct a review of the
adequacy of DIC rates.

(b) BENCHMARKS.--The review shall assess DIC rates against:
    (1) The federal poverty guidelines for applicable household
        sizes.
    (2) The cost of living for survivor household types in
        representative geographic areas.
    (3) The replacement rate relative to military compensation.
    (4) Comparable survivor benefit programs in allied nations.

(c) REPORT.--Not later than 180 days after completion of each
review, the Secretary shall submit a report to the Committees on
Veterans' Affairs of the Senate and House of Representatives
containing the findings and any recommendations for rate adjustments.

SEC. 7. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act shall take effect on the first day of the first month
beginning not less than 180 days after the date of enactment.
```

**Explanation**:

- Section 3 indexes DIC to E-7 base pay at the 55% SBP replacement rate, creating an automatic adjustment mechanism that eliminates the need for periodic legislative increases
- Section 4 eliminates the remarriage penalty across all VA survivor benefits, including DIC, Survivors Pension, CHAMPVA, and home loans, and provides a 2-year restoration window for previously affected survivors
- Section 5 reduces administrative burden by automatically initiating DIC for survivors of veterans who were clearly eligible under existing law
- Section 6 creates an ongoing accountability mechanism for benefit adequacy

**Potential Challenges**:

| Challenge | Analysis |
|-----------|----------|
| Cost (estimated $5-6 billion/year when fully phased in) | Phased implementation reduces first-year impact; cost is less than 2% of total VA budget |
| Budget scoring | CBO will score as mandatory spending increase; may require offset |
| Remarriage restoration retroactivity | 2-year window limits fiscal impact while providing meaningful relief |

**Refinements**:

- If full DIC increase is not achievable, a 45% replacement rate would still represent a significant improvement
- If full remarriage elimination is politically infeasible, reducing the threshold from 57 to 50 would provide substantial relief
- The automatic DIC provision could be implemented through VA rulemaking if legislative action is delayed

### CHAMPVA Modernization Act

**Purpose**: Improve healthcare access for CHAMPVA beneficiaries by establishing a provider network and modernizing benefits.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SEC. 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "CHAMPVA Modernization Act".

SEC. 2. CHAMPVA PROVIDER NETWORK.

(a) IN GENERAL.--Not later than 2 years after the date of enactment,
the Secretary of Veterans Affairs shall establish a provider network
for the Civilian Health and Medical Program of the Department of
Veterans Affairs (CHAMPVA) by entering into agreements with managed
care support contractors serving the TRICARE program under chapter
55 of title 10, United States Code, to include CHAMPVA beneficiaries
in existing TRICARE Select network arrangements.

(b) REIMBURSEMENT.--Providers participating in the CHAMPVA network
under subsection (a) shall be reimbursed at rates not less than
TRICARE Select reimbursement rates.

(c) PHARMACY.--The Secretary shall integrate CHAMPVA beneficiaries
into the TRICARE retail pharmacy network for prescription drug
coverage, in addition to maintaining the CHAMPVA Meds by Mail
program.

(d) PILOT PROGRAM.--Prior to nationwide implementation, the Secretary
shall conduct a 12-month pilot program in not fewer than 3 TRICARE
regions to assess feasibility, cost, and beneficiary satisfaction.

(e) REPORT.--Not later than 90 days after completion of the pilot
program, the Secretary shall submit a report to the Committees on
Veterans' Affairs of the Senate and House of Representatives on the
results and a plan for nationwide implementation.

SEC. 3. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS.

There are authorized to be appropriated such sums as may be
necessary to carry out this Act.
```

**Explanation**: This Act addresses the primary barrier to CHAMPVA effectiveness--provider access--by leveraging existing TRICARE infrastructure rather than building a new network from scratch.

**Potential Challenges**:

| Challenge | Analysis |
|-----------|----------|
| Cost of higher reimbursement rates | Estimated $300-600M/year above current CHAMPVA spending |
| TRICARE contractor capacity | Pilot program assesses whether contractors can absorb CHAMPVA population |
| VA-DoD coordination | Requires interagency agreement on data sharing and billing |

### Survivors Education Equity Act

**Purpose**: Equalize education benefits for all survivors by converting DEA to Fry Scholarship-equivalent benefits.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SEC. 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Survivors Education Equity Act".

SEC. 2. EQUALIZATION OF EDUCATION BENEFITS.

(a) IN GENERAL.--Section 3532 of title 38, United States Code,
is amended to provide that educational assistance under chapter 35
shall be provided at the same rates and in the same manner as
educational assistance under section 3313 of such title (Post-9/11
GI Bill).

(b) TUITION AND FEES.--A covered individual receiving educational
assistance under chapter 35 shall be entitled to payment of the
established charges for tuition and fees to the institution of
higher learning, not to exceed the maximum amount payable under
section 3313(c)(1).

(c) HOUSING.--A covered individual enrolled in a program of
education on a more than half-time basis shall be paid a monthly
housing stipend equal to the basic allowance for housing for a
member of the Armed Forces in pay grade E-5 with dependents.

(d) BOOKS AND SUPPLIES.--A covered individual shall be paid a
stipend of $1,000 per academic year for books and supplies.

SEC. 3. EXTENDED ELIGIBILITY.

(a) CHILDREN.--Section 3512(a) of title 38, United States Code,
is amended by striking "26 years of age" and inserting "36 years
of age".

(b) SPOUSES.--Section 3512(b) of title 38, United States Code,
is amended by striking "10 years" and inserting "20 years".

SEC. 4. EFFECTIVE DATE.

The amendments made by this Act shall apply to terms of enrollment
beginning after the date that is 180 days after the date of
enactment.
```

**Explanation**: Eliminates the two-tier education benefit system that disadvantages survivors of veterans who died from service-connected conditions (DEA) compared to those who died in the line of duty (Fry Scholarship).

## State Model Legislation

### Model State Survivor Support Act

**Purpose**: Provide state-level supplemental benefits and protections for military survivors.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "[State] Military Survivors Support Act".

SECTION 2. PROPERTY TAX EXEMPTION.

(a) The surviving spouse of a veteran who died from a service-connected
cause, as determined by the United States Department of Veterans
Affairs, shall be exempt from property taxes on the surviving
spouse's primary residence.

(b) This exemption shall continue regardless of the surviving
spouse's remarriage.

SECTION 3. STATE TUITION WAIVER.

(a) The children and surviving spouse of a veteran who died from a
service-connected cause shall be entitled to a waiver of tuition
and mandatory fees at any public institution of higher education
in this state.

(b) This waiver shall be available for up to 120 credit hours of
undergraduate education or 60 credit hours of graduate education.

SECTION 4. SURVIVOR EMPLOYMENT PREFERENCE.

(a) The surviving spouse of a veteran who died from a service-connected
cause shall receive preference in hiring for state government
positions, equivalent to the preference provided to veterans.

(b) This preference shall remain in effect for 10 years from the
date of the veteran's death or until the surviving spouse obtains
permanent state employment, whichever occurs first.

SECTION 5. STATE BENEFITS COORDINATION.

The [State Department of Veterans Affairs] shall designate a
Survivor Benefits Coordinator to assist surviving military families
with navigating both federal and state benefits, including referrals
to federal agencies, VSOs, and community resources.
```

**Adaptations**: States may adjust property tax provisions based on their tax structure. States without an income tax may substitute other tax benefits. Tuition waiver provisions should be calibrated to state higher education costs.

## Regulatory Framework

### VA Rulemaking: Automatic DIC Initiation

**Existing Authority**: 38 U.S.C. 1318, 38 U.S.C. 501(a) (Secretary's rulemaking authority)

**Draft Regulation**:

Upon notification of the death of a veteran who was in receipt of service-connected disability compensation at a total rating for a continuous period of not less than 10 years immediately preceding death, the Secretary shall:

1. Identify the surviving spouse from VA records
2. Notify the surviving spouse of automatic DIC eligibility
3. Initiate DIC payments upon verification of the surviving spouse's identity and receipt of payment information
4. Complete this process within 30 days of notification of death

**Explanation**: This regulation eliminates the need for survivors of clearly eligible veterans to file a separate DIC claim, reducing processing time from an average of 110 days to approximately 30 days for this population.

## Legal Considerations

### Constitutional Issues

No significant constitutional obstacles exist for the proposed reforms:

| Issue | Analysis |
|-------|----------|
| Spending Clause authority | Congress has broad authority to appropriate funds for veteran benefits; no constitutional challenge |
| Equal protection | Eliminating the remarriage penalty reduces, rather than creates, classification-based distinctions |
| Due process | Automatic DIC initiation enhances due process by reducing procedural barriers |
| Retroactive restoration | The 2-year restoration window for previously affected survivors does not raise Takings or retroactivity concerns, as it provides benefits rather than imposing obligations |

### Preemption Questions

- Federal survivor benefits preempt state law where they conflict
- State supplemental benefits (property tax exemptions, tuition waivers) operate independently and are not preempted
- CHAMPVA network integration with TRICARE does not create preemption issues

### Enforcement Mechanisms

| Mechanism | Application |
|-----------|------------|
| VA adjudication | DIC claims and eligibility determinations |
| Board of Veterans' Appeals | Appeals of denied claims |
| U.S. Court of Appeals for Veterans Claims | Judicial review of BVA decisions |
| VA Inspector General | Oversight of implementation and compliance |
| GAO | Periodic audits of CHAMPVA network and DIC processing |

## Loopholes, Shortcomings, and Rectification

### Veterans Survivors Equity Act

#### Potential Loopholes

| Loophole | Description | Severity |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| Phased implementation delay | 3-year phase-in means full DIC increase not realized until Year 3 | Medium |
| Restoration window limitation | 2-year window for previously affected survivors may miss those who are unaware of the change | Medium |
| Automatic DIC limited to 10+ year total ratings | Veterans rated 100% for less than 10 years are excluded from automatic processing | Low |
| No retroactive DIC for remarriage years | Survivors who lost benefits due to remarriage do not receive back payments for the years of lost benefits | Medium |

#### Shortcomings

| Issue | Impact | Root Cause |
|-------|--------|------------|
| E-7 benchmark may not reflect all family situations | Higher-grade veteran families may still experience significant income loss | Flat-rate design trades simplicity for individual accuracy |
| No childcare benefit | DIC increase does not address the specific cost of childcare for working surviving parents | DIC is structured as income replacement, not expense-based |
| No inflation protection beyond COLA | If military pay raises lag inflation, DIC will also lag | DIC indexed to military pay, which has its own political dynamics |

#### Rectification Procedures

1. Extend the restoration window to 5 years and direct the VA to conduct proactive outreach to previously affected survivors
2. Study the feasibility of a supplemental childcare benefit for surviving parents with children under 6
3. Include a floor provision ensuring DIC never falls below 130% of the federal poverty line for the applicable household size
4. Expand automatic DIC to veterans rated 100% for 5+ years (rather than 10+) after the first implementation review

### CHAMPVA Modernization Act

#### Potential Loopholes

| Loophole | Description | Severity |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| TRICARE contractor refusal | Contractors may decline to include CHAMPVA beneficiaries if terms are unfavorable | High |
| Geographic gaps | TRICARE network coverage varies; some areas may have limited participation | Medium |
| Cost growth | Higher reimbursement rates may increase CHAMPVA spending faster than projected | Medium |

#### Shortcomings

| Issue | Impact | Root Cause |
|-------|--------|------------|
| Dependent on TRICARE contractor cooperation | VA has limited leverage over DoD contractors | Interagency dependency |
| No dental coverage | CHAMPVA does not cover dental care | Scope limitation of existing authority |

#### Rectification Procedures

1. Include mandatory participation provisions in TRICARE managed care contracts
2. Authorize standalone CHAMPVA provider contracting as a backup if TRICARE integration is infeasible
3. Add dental coverage through a CHAMPVA dental supplement program

### General Implementation Concerns

#### Systemic Issues

| Issue | Proposed Solution |
|-------|------------------|
| VA IT system modernization needed for automatic DIC | Include IT funding in appropriations; leverage existing death notification systems |
| Interagency data sharing barriers | Mandate data sharing through legislation; build on existing VA-DoD-SSA connections |
| County Veterans Service Officer capacity | Provide federal grants to states for CVSO staffing and training |
| Survivor awareness of new benefits | Mandate proactive notification within 90 days of enactment |

#### Sunset and Review Provisions

- The DIC adequacy review (every 5 years) provides a built-in assessment mechanism
- CHAMPVA pilot program includes a formal evaluation before nationwide rollout
- Congress should require a GAO report 3 years after enactment assessing implementation effectiveness
