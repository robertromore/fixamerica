# Minority Veterans: Citizen Actions

## Overview

Every American can contribute to addressing minority veteran disparities, whether as a veteran, a family member, a community member, or an engaged citizen. The actions in this guide range from immediate personal steps to sustained civic engagement. They are organized by the level of commitment and expertise required.

---

## Immediate Actions: What Anyone Can Do

### 1. Learn About Minority Veteran History and Disparities

- Read the VA OIG report on racial disparities in disability compensation (Report No. 21-01052-57, 2022), available at va.gov/oig
- Visit the National Museum of African American History and Culture's military galleries or explore online exhibits
- Learn the history of the Tuskegee Airmen, Navajo Code Talkers, 442nd Regimental Combat Team, and Borinqueneers
- Share minority veteran stories with your network to increase visibility

### 2. Support Minority Veteran Organizations

- Donate to organizations focused on minority veteran advocacy: National Association of Black Veterans (nabvets.org), American GI Forum (agif.org), Minority Veterans of America (minorityvets.org), Black Veterans Project (blackveteransproject.org)
- Volunteer with local veteran service organizations that serve diverse communities
- Offer professional skills (legal, medical, translation, financial planning) to organizations supporting minority veterans

### 3. Help Minority Veterans Access Benefits

- If you know a minority veteran not enrolled in VA healthcare, help them begin the enrollment process at va.gov/health-care/how-to-apply
- Connect AIAN veterans with tribal veteran representatives who can navigate VA benefits
- Share information about the discharge upgrade process with veterans who may be eligible (Legal services: nvlsp.org, swords-to-plowshares.org)
- Help LEP veterans find translation assistance when interacting with the VA

### 4. Support Businesses Owned by Minority Veterans

- Use the VA's VetBiz directory (vetbiz.va.gov) to find minority veteran-owned businesses
- Patronize and promote these businesses in your community
- If you are a business owner, consider supplier diversity programs that include minority veteran-owned businesses

## Community-Level Actions

### 5. Advocate for Cultural Competency at Local VA Facilities

- Attend town hall meetings at your local VA medical center and raise questions about language access and cultural competency
- Request data on patient satisfaction by race/ethnicity from your facility's Patient Advocate
- If interpretation services are inadequate, document the gap and report it to the VA Office of Health Equity (healthequity@va.gov)
- Connect local minority community organizations with VA outreach coordinators

### 6. Support Discharge Upgrade Efforts

- If you are a lawyer, volunteer with legal aid organizations that provide free discharge upgrade representation (Swords to Plowshares, National Veterans Legal Services Program, local law school veterans' clinics)
- If you are not a lawyer, help identify veterans in your community who may be eligible for discharge upgrades and connect them with legal resources
- Support the Discharge Upgrade Information Campaign by sharing materials in community centers, houses of worship, and social media

### 7. Build Local Coalitions

- Connect your local NAACP, Urban League, or tribal organization with veteran service organizations to build cross-issue coalitions
- Organize community events that bring minority veterans together with VA outreach staff
- Create minority veteran peer support groups in communities where they do not exist
- Partner with minority professional associations to create veteran employment pipelines

### 8. Address Veteran Homelessness Disparities

- Volunteer with HUD-VASH programs and supportive housing organizations serving homeless veterans
- Advocate with your local government for veteran housing programs that address racial disparities
- Support organizations like Final Salute Inc. (finalsaluteinc.org) that focus on minority women veteran homelessness
- Participate in your community's annual Point-in-Time count of homeless individuals (HUD, late January)

## Civic Engagement and Advocacy

### 9. Contact Your Elected Officials

**Federal Level:**

- Call or write your U.S. Senators and Representative to support increased funding for the VA Center for Minority Veterans
- Advocate for the Minority Veterans Equity Act (if introduced) or similar legislation addressing disability rating disparities
- Support legislation requiring mandatory disaggregated data reporting by the VA
- Ask members of the House and Senate Veterans' Affairs Committees to hold hearings on minority veteran disparities
- Request that your representatives co-sponsor legislation to resolve the tribal trust land VA home loan barrier

**State Level:**

- Advocate for state veteran agencies to collect and report data by race and ethnicity
- Support state-funded veteran service officer positions in underserved minority communities
- Push for state recognition of minority veteran contributions through memorials and commemorations
- Support state licensing reciprocity programs that help minority veterans with military training enter civilian professions

**Sample Communication:**

> Dear [Representative/Senator]:
>
> As your constituent, I urge you to support legislation addressing racial disparities in VA disability compensation. The VA Inspector General's 2022 report documented that Black veterans receive disability ratings 8-12% lower than white veterans for the same conditions. I ask that you support: (1) mandatory bias auditing of VA claims outcomes, (2) increased funding for the Center for Minority Veterans, and (3) expanded discharge upgrade outreach for veterans with potentially discriminatory less-than-honorable discharges. These reforms honor our obligation to all who serve, regardless of race.

### 10. Engage with the VA System

- Attend VA Advisory Committee on Minority Veterans public meetings (schedule at va.gov/advisory-committee-minority-veterans)
- Submit written comments during VA rulemaking processes related to equity or healthcare access
- Participate in VA town halls and listening sessions
- If you are a veteran, complete VA patient experience surveys and provide specific feedback about cultural competency

### 11. Support Political Candidates Who Prioritize Veteran Equity

- During election campaigns, ask candidates about their positions on minority veteran disparities
- Support candidates who commit to specific policy actions (funding, legislation, oversight)
- Attend candidate forums and raise minority veteran issues
- Share candidates' veteran equity positions with minority veteran organizations and communities

## Actions for Specific Audiences

### For Veterans

- Share your experience of racial disparities in military service or VA care with advocacy organizations and researchers
- Mentor minority service members transitioning to civilian life
- Participate in VA research studies examining health disparities (contact your VA medical center's research office)
- Join a VSO and advocate for equity priorities within the organization
- If eligible, apply for a discharge upgrade and share your experience to help others

### For Healthcare Professionals

- Pursue cultural competency training specifically focused on veteran populations
- If you work in the community care network, learn about minority veteran healthcare barriers
- Advocate within your professional organizations for attention to minority veteran health disparities
- Participate in VA academic partnership programs that address health equity

### For Educators and Researchers

- Include minority veteran history in American history and military history curricula
- Conduct research on minority veteran outcomes and disparities
- Publish findings in accessible formats that can inform policy
- Partner with the VA Office of Research and Development on health equity studies
- Mentor minority veteran students and support their academic success

### For Employers

- Develop veteran hiring programs that specifically recruit from minority veteran communities
- Partner with organizations like Minority Veterans of America for employment pipeline development
- Ensure that veteran hiring initiatives do not inadvertently exclude those with less-than-honorable discharges (where legally permissible)
- Provide veteran employee resource groups that address diverse veteran experiences

### For Legal Professionals

- Provide pro bono discharge upgrade representation through legal aid organizations
- Assist minority veterans with VA claims appeals
- Support policy advocacy for discharge upgrade reform
- Participate in veterans' legal clinics at law schools
