# Toxic Exposure: Actions

## Overview

Citizens, veterans, family members, and community organizations can take concrete actions to support toxic-exposed veterans, hold government institutions accountable for implementation, and advocate for continued reform. This document provides specific, actionable steps organized by audience and level of engagement.

## For Toxic-Exposed Veterans

### Immediate Actions

1. **Check your eligibility under the PACT Act**: Visit va.gov/pact or call 1-800-698-2411 to determine if you qualify for expanded healthcare or disability benefits
2. **Enroll in VA healthcare**: Toxic-exposed combat veterans are now eligible for VA healthcare regardless of disability rating under the PACT Act
3. **File a disability claim**: If you have a condition linked to toxic exposure, file a claim through va.gov, a VSO, or a VA regional office
4. **Register on exposure registries**: Enroll in the Airborne Hazards and Open Burn Pit Registry, Agent Orange Registry, or other applicable registries
5. **Request your exposure records**: Use the DOD Individual Longitudinal Exposure Record (ILER) system to verify your documented exposures
6. **Get a toxic exposure screening**: Request a screening at your next VA healthcare appointment

### Claims Filing Guidance

- **Use free VSO assistance**: Organizations like DAV, VFW, and American Legion provide free claims filing help
- **Document your service**: Gather DD-214, deployment orders, unit histories, and buddy statements
- **Get medical evidence**: Obtain current medical records documenting your conditions
- **File for all applicable conditions**: Review the presumptive conditions list for your exposure type
- **Do not wait**: File now even if you are not currently symptomatic; early filing establishes a claims date

### For Camp Lejeune Affected Individuals

- **File a Camp Lejeune Justice Act claim**: Submit through the administrative process at justice.gov/civil/camp-lejeune
- **Consult with a CLJA attorney**: Attorney fees are capped at 20% by statute; many accept cases on contingency
- **Document your time at Camp Lejeune**: Gather any records showing you lived or worked at the base between 1953 and 1987
- **Register for VA healthcare**: If you served at Camp Lejeune during the affected period, you may be eligible for VA healthcare for 8 presumptive conditions

## For Family Members

### Actions for Military Family Members Exposed on Installations

1. **Check Camp Lejeune family member eligibility**: Family members who lived at Camp Lejeune during 1953-1987 may be eligible for VA healthcare and CLJA claims
2. **Document health conditions**: Maintain records of any health conditions that may be linked to installation contamination
3. **Contact your congressional representatives**: Advocate for expansion of benefits to military family members at contaminated installations
4. **Join advocacy organizations**: Connect with Camp Lejeune Community Assistance Panel, Military Family Advisory Network, or similar groups

### For Survivors of Deceased Veterans

1. **File for Dependency and Indemnity Compensation (DIC)**: If a veteran died from an exposure-related condition, survivors may be eligible
2. **File accrued benefits claims**: If a veteran had a pending claim at the time of death, survivors may receive retroactive benefits
3. **Apply for PACT Act survivor benefits**: The PACT Act expanded eligibility for survivors of toxic-exposed veterans

### Community Actions

1. **Share PACT Act information**: Many eligible veterans do not know about their benefits; share va.gov/pact through social media, community boards, and veteran networks
2. **Support VSOs**: Donate to or volunteer with organizations that provide free claims assistance to toxic-exposed veterans
3. **Attend town halls**: Ask your representatives about PACT Act implementation at public events
4. **Support veteran-owned businesses**: Many toxic-exposed veterans face employment challenges; patronizing veteran-owned businesses supports their economic stability

## For Healthcare Providers

### Actions for Civilian Healthcare Providers

1. **Screen for military service and toxic exposure**: Ask patients about military service history and potential toxic exposures
2. **Learn about PACT Act conditions**: Familiarize yourself with the presumptive conditions list to identify potentially eligible patients
3. **Refer veterans to the VA**: Help non-enrolled veterans connect with VA healthcare for toxic exposure screening
4. **Provide nexus letters**: For veterans filing non-presumptive claims, provide medical opinions linking conditions to service when appropriate
5. **Complete VA Community Care referrals**: If contracted through the VA Community Care Network, ensure proper documentation of toxic exposure conditions

## For Researchers

### Research Priorities

1. **Participate in DOD/VA toxic exposure studies**: Contribute to the Millennium Cohort Study, Airborne Hazards research, and other longitudinal studies
2. **Study underresearched exposures**: PFAS, depleted uranium, JP-8 jet fuel, and K2 contamination need additional research
3. **Investigate intergenerational effects**: Research the health effects on children and grandchildren of toxic-exposed veterans
4. **Develop biomarkers**: Identify biological markers of exposure that can support claims processing
5. **Publish and disseminate findings**: Make research accessible to policymakers and the public

## Talking Points

### For Conversations with Skeptics

- The PACT Act costs $280 billion over 10 years--a fraction of the $2.3 trillion spent on the wars that created the exposures
- Fraud rates in VA disability compensation are less than 0.5%; presumptive conditions still require medical diagnosis and service documentation
- Veterans were ordered into hazardous environments; the government has a moral and legal obligation to care for the health consequences
- Agent Orange recognition took 20 years; burn pit recognition took 13 years; every year of delay means veterans suffering and dying without care
- This is bipartisan: the PACT Act passed the Senate 86-11 and the House 342-88

### For Conversations with Veterans Who Have Not Filed

- PACT Act benefits are available now; do not wait for symptoms to worsen
- Filing is free through VSOs; you do not need a lawyer for VA claims
- Presumptive conditions do not require you to prove your specific exposure caused your condition
- Your service records and deployment history are often sufficient to establish eligibility
- Benefits are retroactive to the date of filing; filing early protects your effective date
