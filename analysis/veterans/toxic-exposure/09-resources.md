# Toxic Exposure: Resources

## Overview

This document compiles key resources for understanding, researching, and advocating on veteran toxic exposure issues. Resources are organized by type and include academic research, government data sources, advocacy organizations, and practical tools for veterans.

## Academic Research

### Key Studies and Reports

| Title | Author/Institution | Year | Significance |
|-------|-------------------|------|-------------|
| *Veterans and Agent Orange* series (11 updates) | National Academies of Sciences, Engineering, and Medicine | 1994-2018 | Authoritative scientific reviews linking Agent Orange to presumptive conditions |
| *Gulf War and Health* series (10 volumes) | National Academies of Sciences, Engineering, and Medicine | 2000-2020 | Comprehensive review of Gulf War deployment health effects |
| *Gulf War Illness and the Health of Gulf War Veterans* | Research Advisory Committee on Gulf War Veterans' Illnesses | 2008 | Definitive establishment of Gulf War Illness as a physiological condition |
| Millennium Cohort Study publications | DOD Naval Health Research Center | 2004-present | Longitudinal health data on 200,000+ service members including deployment exposures |
| Camp Lejeune mortality study | Agency for Toxic Substances and Disease Registry | 2014, updated 2024 | Documented elevated cancer rates among Camp Lejeune residents |
| *Airborne Hazards Related to Deployment* | VA Airborne Hazards Center of Excellence | 2020 | Clinical guidance for burn pit-related respiratory conditions |
| *Assessment of the Department of Veterans Affairs Airborne Hazards and Open Burn Pit Registry* | National Academies | 2017 | Evaluated the burn pit registry methodology and data quality |
| *Respiratory Health Effects of Airborne Hazards Exposures in the Southwest Asia Theater* | DOD/VA | 2023 | Epidemiological analysis of burn pit respiratory effects |
