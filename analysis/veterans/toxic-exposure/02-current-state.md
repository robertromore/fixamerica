# Toxic Exposure: Current State

## Overview

The landscape of veteran toxic exposure policy has been transformed by the PACT Act (Public Law 117-168), signed into law on August 10, 2022. This legislation represents the most significant expansion of VA healthcare and benefits in decades, yet its implementation reveals both progress and persistent challenges. As of late 2025, the VA is processing an unprecedented volume of toxic exposure claims while simultaneously conducting outreach to millions of potentially eligible veterans who have not yet applied.

| Government estimated liability | $21 billion (CBO estimate) | Congressional Budget Office, 2022 |

### Key Legal Issues

- **Statute of limitations**: The CLJA waived prior limitations, allowing claims back to 1953
- **Causation standard**: Claimants must show contaminated water was "as likely as not" a contributing cause
- **Administrative vs. judicial track**: Most claims processed through administrative elective option before litigation
- **Government delays**: DOJ criticized for slow processing; Congress has pressed for faster resolution

## Current Challenges

### Claims Processing

- **Backlog growth**: PACT Act claims volume has overwhelmed VBA capacity despite new hires
- **Consistency concerns**: Rating variability across regional offices for toxic exposure claims
- **Evidence gathering**: Many veterans lack service records documenting specific exposures
- **Nexus letters**: Requirement for medical nexus opinions creates bottleneck and expense

### Scientific Gaps

- **Burn pit composition data**: Limited records of what was burned at specific locations
- **Long-latency conditions**: Cancers and neurological diseases may not appear for 20-40 years
- **Synergistic effects**: Health impacts of simultaneous multiple exposures poorly understood
- **PFAS exposure**: Emerging research with significant implications for currently serving and veteran populations

### Outreach Gaps

- **Unaware eligible veterans**: VA estimates millions of eligible veterans have not applied
- **Surviving family members**: Many eligible survivors unaware of Dependency and Indemnity Compensation
- **Rural and disconnected veterans**: Those outside VA system hardest to reach
- **Distrust**: Decades of denial have created deep skepticism about government intentions

## Trends

### Positive Developments

- PACT Act passage and implementation represents historic expansion of coverage
- Streamlined presumptive process reduces individual burden of proof
- Bipartisan consensus on toxic exposure remains strong
- VA toxic exposure screening becoming standard at enrollment

### Concerning Developments

- Claims processing times remain long and backlogs are growing
- Camp Lejeune Justice Act litigation proceeding slowly
- Emerging hazards (PFAS, K2 base contamination) creating new cohorts
- Long-latency diseases in aging Vietnam-era population increasing caseload
- Budget pressures could undermine sustained commitment
