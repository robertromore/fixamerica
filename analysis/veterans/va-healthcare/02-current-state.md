# VA Healthcare: Current State

## Present Conditions

The Veterans Health Administration is simultaneously the nation's most praised and most criticized healthcare system. It delivers specialized care for service-connected conditions that has no private-sector equivalent, while also struggling with access backlogs, workforce shortages, aging infrastructure, and a troubled electronic health record modernization. The PACT Act of 2022 brought millions of newly eligible veterans into the system, intensifying demand on already strained capacity.

## System Overview (FY2024)

### Facility Network[^1]

| Facility Type | Count | Trend |
|---------------|-------|-------|
| VA Medical Centers (VAMCs) | 172 | Stable |
| Community-based outpatient clinics (CBOCs) | 1,149 | Slight increase |
| Community living centers (nursing) | 134 | Stable |
| Domiciliary programs | 41 | Stable |
| Vet Centers (readjustment counseling) | 300 | Growing |
| Mobile clinics | 84 | Expanding |
| Total facilities | ~1,880 | Growing |

The VHA facility network has expanded primarily through outpatient clinics and mobile units, while the core hospital infrastructure remains largely unchanged in footprint.

### Patient Volume (FY2024)[^2]

| Metric | Value | Change from FY2022 |
|--------|-------|--------------------|
| Unique patients treated | 7.2 million | +8% |
| Outpatient visits | 115.2 million | +12% |
| Inpatient admissions | 589,000 | +3% |
| Emergency department visits | 2.8 million | +7% |
| Telehealth encounters | 17.4 million | +15% |
| Community care authorizations | 4.5 million | +22% |
| Prescriptions filled | 318 million | +6% |

Telehealth has grown substantially since the pandemic, but community care referrals have grown even faster, reflecting persistent VA capacity constraints.[^2]

### Budget (FY2025)[^3]

| Category | Amount | Share |
|----------|--------|-------|
| Medical services | $72.3 billion | 61% |
| Medical community care | $28.4 billion | 24% |
| Medical support and compliance | $10.2 billion | 9% |
| Medical facilities | $7.8 billion | 6% |
| **Total VHA appropriation** | **$118.7 billion** | 100% |

Community care now consumes nearly one-quarter of the VHA budget, up from approximately 15% before the MISSION Act implementation.[^3]

## Access and Wait Times

### Current Wait Times (Q4 FY2024)[^4]

| Specialty | New Patient (days) | Established Patient (days) |
|-----------|--------------------|---------------------------|
| Primary care | 26.3 | 4.2 |
| Mental health | 30.1 | 5.8 |
| Cardiology | 41.2 | 12.4 |
| Orthopedics | 38.7 | 11.1 |
| Dermatology | 52.6 | 18.3 |
| Gastroenterology | 44.1 | 14.7 |
| Audiology | 35.9 | 9.6 |
| Optometry/ophthalmology | 33.4 | 8.2 |

Wait times vary significantly by facility, with some VAMCs achieving under 14 days for new patients while others exceed 60 days in specialty care.[^4]

### Wait Time Trends (FY2020-2024)[^4]

| Year | Primary Care (new) | Specialty Care (new) | Mental Health (new) |
|------|--------------------|--------------------|---------------------|
| FY2020 | 22.1 days | 31.4 days | 25.3 days |
| FY2021 | 24.8 days | 34.2 days | 28.7 days |
| FY2022 | 25.1 days | 35.6 days | 29.4 days |
| FY2023 | 25.9 days | 36.1 days | 29.8 days |
| FY2024 | 26.3 days | 36.8 days | 30.1 days |

Wait times have trended upward despite increased community care referrals, suggesting demand growth is outpacing combined VA and community capacity.[^4]

### Geographic Variation[^5]

| Region | Average Wait (new, all specialties) | Notes |
|--------|-------------------------------------|-------|
| Northeast | 28.4 days | Dense facility network |
| Southeast | 34.2 days | High growth, aging population |
| Midwest | 30.7 days | Rural access challenges |
| West | 32.1 days | Vast distances, growing demand |
| Pacific | 35.8 days | High cost of living, recruitment difficulty |

### Rural vs. Urban Access[^6]

| Metric | Urban | Rural | Highly Rural |
|--------|-------|-------|--------------|
| Average drive time to VAMC | 28 min | 74 min | 142 min |
| Average drive time to CBOC | 18 min | 42 min | 86 min |
| Community care availability | High | Moderate | Low |
| Telehealth utilization rate | 22% | 31% | 38% |
| Satisfaction with access | 86% | 78% | 71% |

Rural veterans face longer travel times, fewer community care options, and lower satisfaction with access despite higher telehealth utilization.[^6]

### Eligibility Criteria[^7]

| Standard | Threshold |
|----------|-----------|
| Wait time | 20 days (primary/mental health) or 28 days (specialty) |
| Drive time | 30 minutes (primary/mental health) or 60 minutes (specialty) |
| Service unavailable at VA | Automatic referral |
| Best medical interest | Clinician determination |
| Grandfathered access | Veterans with prior community care eligibility |

### Community Care Challenges[^7]

| Challenge | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Care coordination | Records not shared between VA and community providers |
| Quality oversight | VA has limited visibility into private-sector care quality |
| Cost growth | Community care spending growing faster than direct care |
| Provider participation | Many private providers decline VA rates |
| Billing complexity | Administrative burden discourages provider enrollment |

### Asset and Infrastructure Review (AIR)[^12]

The AIR Commission, established by the MISSION Act to recommend facility realignments, was effectively canceled in 2022 when President Biden declined to forward its recommendations to Congress. The underlying facility modernization needs remain unaddressed, with no replacement planning mechanism established.

## Electronic Health Record Modernization

### Cerner/Oracle Health Transition (2024)[^13]

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Sites deployed | 6 (of planned 170+) |
| Original completion date | 2028 |
| Revised completion date | TBD (program paused) |
| Total estimated cost | $16+ billion (revised from $10 billion) |
| Reported patient safety events | 148+ at deployed sites |
| Program status | Strategic review underway |

### Key Issues[^13]

| Problem | Impact |
|---------|--------|
| System performance | Slow response times, frequent crashes |
| Medication ordering errors | Patient safety incidents |
| Referral management failures | Lost or delayed referrals |
| User satisfaction | Low among clinicians |
| Cost overruns | $6+ billion above original estimate |
| Interoperability gaps | VA-DoD record sharing incomplete |

The EHR modernization, originally intended to create seamless records between VA and DoD, has become one of the most troubled federal IT programs, with deployment paused pending strategic review.[^13]
