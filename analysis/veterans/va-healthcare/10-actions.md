# VA Healthcare: Citizen Actions

## What You Can Do

### If You Are a Veteran

| Action | How | Impact |
|--------|-----|--------|
| **Enroll in VA healthcare** | Apply online at va.gov or at any VAMC | Access benefits you earned |
| **Know your PACT Act eligibility** | Check toxic exposure screening | Newly eligible conditions |
| **Use telehealth** | Request VA Video Connect appointments | Reduce travel, increase access |
| **Provide feedback** | Complete SHEP surveys, contact patient advocates | Drive improvement |
| **File complaints formally** | Use VA patient advocate, OIG hotline | Accountability mechanism |
| **Share your experience** | Testify at hearings, contact media | Public awareness |
| **Join a VSO** | American Legion, VFW, DAV, IAVA | Collective advocacy |
| **Vote** | Research candidates on veteran issues | Electoral pressure |

### If You Are a Veteran's Family Member

| Action | How | Impact |
|--------|-----|--------|
| **Help navigate the system** | Assist with enrollment, appointments | Improve veteran's access |
| **Learn caregiver benefits** | VA Caregiver Support Program | Access support services |
| **Advocate for your veteran** | Attend appointments, communicate with providers | Better care coordination |
| **Contact legislators** | Share family impact stories | Personal advocacy |
| **Support caregiver legislation** | Contact Congress on caregiver support bills | Expand family benefits |

### If You Are a VA Employee

| Action | How | Impact |
|--------|-----|--------|
| **Report safety concerns** | Use Patient Safety Reporting System | Prevent harm |
| **Advocate for resources** | Work through union and professional channels | Better working conditions |
| **Participate in quality improvement** | Join facility quality committees | Drive change from within |
| **Use whistleblower protections** | OIG hotline, Office of Special Counsel | Protected accountability |
| **Mentor new staff** | Support onboarding and retention | Workforce stability |

### General Citizen Actions

| Action | How | Impact |
|--------|-----|--------|
| **Learn the facts** | Read VA quality data, not just scandal headlines | Informed advocacy |
| **Contact legislators** | Call, email, write about VA investment | Political pressure |
| **Vote** | Research candidates on VA positions | Electoral impact |
| **Counter misinformation** | Share VA quality data when privatization is discussed | Public understanding |
| **Volunteer** | VA Voluntary Service (VAVS) | Direct support |
| **Donate** | VSOs and veteran support organizations | Fund advocacy |

## Political Actions

### Contact Your Representatives

**Federal Issues**:

- VA healthcare funding increases
- Workforce pay parity legislation
- MISSION Act reauthorization reform
- EHR modernization accountability
- Infrastructure modernization funding
- Oppose privatization legislation

**State Issues**:

- State licensing compacts for VA clinicians
- State veterans homes funding
- Rural broadband for telehealth
- Veteran employment programs

### Template Email: Federal Representative

> Dear [Representative/Senator name],
>
> I am writing to urge your support for strengthening VA healthcare. The VA serves 9.1 million enrolled veterans through the nation's largest integrated healthcare system, and it consistently outperforms the private sector on most quality measures.
>
> However, the VA faces serious challenges that require Congressional action:
>
> - Over 46,000 unfilled clinical positions threaten access and quality
> - 60% of VA facilities are over 50 years old with a $35 billion maintenance backlog
> - Community care spending has reached $28 billion annually--50% more per encounter than VA direct care--without resolving access problems
> - The electronic health record modernization has cost $16+ billion with only 6 of 170+ sites deployed
>
> I am asking you to:
>
> 1. Support increased VA medical care appropriations to close the workforce gap
> 2. Pass VA workforce pay parity legislation to compete with the private sector
> 3. Fund infrastructure modernization to replace aging facilities
> 4. Reform community care to ensure it supplements rather than replaces VA direct care
> 5. Hold Oracle Health accountable for EHR deployment failures
>
> The promise to care for those who have borne the battle must be backed by adequate resources and smart policy. Thank you for your attention to this critical issue.
>
> Sincerely,
> [Your name]
> [Your address]

## Talking Points

### For Conversations About VA Healthcare

1. **VA quality is strong**: VA outperforms the private sector on most quality measures, including chronic disease management, preventive care, and patient safety.[^1]

2. **The real problem is capacity**: VA's challenges stem from workforce shortages (46,000+ vacancies) and aging infrastructure ($35B maintenance backlog), not from being a government system.[^2]

3. **Community care costs more**: Private-sector care costs ~50% more per VA-funded encounter ($6,200 vs. $4,100), and referrals have doubled without fixing access.[^3]

4. **Privatization threatens specialized care**: VA provides unique services for TBI, spinal cord injury, prosthetics, and military sexual trauma that the private sector cannot replicate.

5. **VA trains 70% of physicians**: The VA-medical school affiliation system is essential to the U.S. healthcare workforce pipeline.

6. **Veterans support VA**: Patient satisfaction is 89.4%, above the national average.

7. **PACT Act needs follow-through**: Congress expanded eligibility for 3.5 million veterans but has not fully funded the staffing and infrastructure to serve them.

## Common Misconceptions

| Misconception | Reality |
|---------------|---------|
| "VA healthcare is terrible" | VA outperforms private sector on most quality measures; satisfaction is above national average |
| "Veterans would be better off with vouchers" | Community care costs more, lacks coordination, and cannot provide military-specific services |
| "VA employees can't be fired" | VA fires thousands annually; 2017 Accountability Act expanded removal authority |
| "Community care solved the wait-time problem" | Wait times have continued to increase despite $28B in community care spending |
| "VA is too big to reform" | The Kizer transformation (1994-1999) proved VA can be reformed with sustained leadership |
| "Only veterans with combat injuries use VA" | 48% of enrolled veterans rely on VA as their primary healthcare |
| "The EHR failure means VA can't do IT" | VistA, built by VA, was a pioneering healthcare IT system for decades |
