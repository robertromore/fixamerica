# VA Healthcare: Stakeholders

## Who Is Affected

### Primary Stakeholders

| Group | Impact | Size |
|-------|--------|------|
| **Enrolled veterans** | Direct recipients of VA healthcare | 9.1 million[^1] |
| **Veterans using VA healthcare** | Active patients affected by access and quality | 7.2 million[^1] |
| **VA clinical staff** | Working conditions, burnout, compensation | ~164,000[^2] |
| **Veterans' families** | Caregiver burden, family health effects | ~20 million household members |
| **Post-9/11 veterans** | Complex polytrauma, mental health, toxic exposure | ~4.1 million[^3] |
| **Rural veterans** | Geographic access barriers | ~4.7 million[^4] |

### Veteran Demographics

| Characteristic | Percentage/Number | Implication |
|----------------|-------------------|-------------|
| Age 65+ | 47% (~4.2 million) | Growing geriatric care demand |
| Service-connected disability | 60% (~5.4 million) | Higher utilization, complex needs |
| Women | 11% (~734,000 enrolled) | Gender-specific care requirements |
| Minority veterans | 25% | Health equity concerns |
| Vietnam era | 33% | Agent Orange, aging-related needs |
| Post-9/11 era | 23% | TBI, PTSD, toxic exposure |
| Below median income | 48% | Reliant on VA as primary healthcare |

### Secondary Stakeholders

| Group | Connection | Interest |
|-------|------------|----------|
| **Community care providers** | Deliver VA-funded care in private sector | Revenue, referral volume, payment rates |
| **Medical schools** | 70% of physicians train at VA facilities | Training pipeline, research partnerships |
| **Defense Department** | Transitioning service members to VA care | Continuity, health record sharing |
| **Taxpayers** | Fund VA through appropriations | Cost efficiency, accountability |
| **Private insurance industry** | VA reduces demand on Medicare/Medicaid | Cost shifting implications |
| **Pharmaceutical industry** | VA is largest federal drug purchaser | Pricing, formulary decisions |
| **Medical device companies** | VA prosthetics and rehabilitation programs | Procurement contracts |

## Who Has Power

### Federal Decision Makers

| Actor | Role | Power |
|-------|------|-------|
| **VA Secretary** | Cabinet-level leadership | Policy direction, budget priorities |
| **Under Secretary for Health** | VHA operational leadership | Healthcare delivery decisions |
| **VISN Directors (22)** | Regional network management | Resource allocation, facility oversight |
| **CMS Administrator** | Medicare/Medicaid interaction | Dual-eligible coordination |
| **OMB Director** | Budget formulation | Funding levels before Congress |
| **President** | Nominations, executive orders | Leadership selection, policy directives |

### Congressional Decision Makers

| Actor | Role | Power |
|-------|------|-------|
| **Senate Veterans' Affairs Committee** | Authorization, oversight | Legislation, confirmations |
| **House Veterans' Affairs Committee** | Authorization, oversight | Legislation, investigations |
| **Senate Appropriations (MilCon-VA)** | Funding | Annual budget levels |
| **House Appropriations (MilCon-VA)** | Funding | Annual budget levels |
| **Senate/House Armed Services** | DoD-VA coordination | Transition, health records |

### Veteran Service Organizations

| Organization | Membership | Stance on Key Issues |
|-------------|------------|---------------------|
| **American Legion** | 1.6 million | Pro-VA, cautious on community care expansion |
| **Veterans of Foreign Wars (VFW)** | 1.1 million | Pro-VA, support targeted community care |
| **Disabled American Veterans (DAV)** | 1.0 million | Strong pro-VA, oppose privatization |
| **Iraq and Afghanistan Veterans of America (IAVA)** | 425,000 | Pragmatic, focus on post-9/11 needs |
| **Paralyzed Veterans of America (PVA)** | 55,000 | Specialized care advocacy, pro-VA |
| **Vietnam Veterans of America (VVA)** | 65,000 | Toxic exposure, aging veteran needs |
| **Concerned Veterans for America (CVA)** | N/A (advocacy) | Pro-privatization, community care expansion |
| **VoteVets** | 700,000+ | Progressive, oppose privatization |

### Industry Stakeholders

| Actor | Power Source | Influence |
|-------|-------------|-----------|
| **Oracle Health (Cerner)** | $16+ billion EHR contract | IT modernization direction |

| **Nurses Organization of VA (NOVA)** | VA nurse representation | Staffing, working conditions |

## Coalition Mapping

### Pro-VA Direct Care Coalition

Groups that prioritize strengthening VA's direct healthcare delivery:

| Actor | Primary Motivation |
|-------|-------------------|
| DAV | Service-connected care expertise |
| PVA | Specialized rehabilitation programs |
| AFGE | Federal employee jobs and conditions |
| VoteVets | Oppose privatization ideology |
| Academic medical centers | Training pipeline and research |
| VA clinicians | Professional mission and autonomy |

### Pro-Community Care / Reform Coalition

Groups that prioritize expanding private-sector access:

| Actor | Primary Motivation |
|-------|-------------------|
| CVA (Koch-funded) | Ideological preference for private markets |
| Some Republican members of Congress | Government efficiency, private-sector solutions |
| Private healthcare industry | Revenue from VA-funded patients |
| Some rural veteran advocates | Geographic access |
| American Hospital Association | Member hospital revenue |

### Swing Stakeholders

| Actor | Key Concern | Could Go Either Way On |
|-------|------------|------------------------|
| **American Legion** | Access for members | Level of community care expansion |
| **VFW** | Timely, quality care | Balance between VA and community care |
| **IAVA** | Practical solutions for post-9/11 vets | Mechanism less important than outcomes |
| **Rural health advocates** | Any improvement in access | Who delivers care matters less than access |
| **Senate centrists** | Budget impact, constituent feedback | Funding levels and care delivery model |

## Power Dynamics

### Key Asymmetries

| Dynamic | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Diffuse beneficiaries, concentrated opposition** | 9 million veterans vs. organized private healthcare industry |
| **Information asymmetry** | VA data complex; public relies on scandal narratives |
| **Geographic dispersion** | Veterans in every congressional district but concentrated influence in few |
| **Political valence** | "Support our troops" rhetoric used by all sides |
| **Revolving door** | VA officials move to private healthcare and lobbying |

### Underrepresented Groups

| Group | Why Underrepresented | Impact |
|-------|---------------------|--------|
| **Homeless veterans** | Cannot organize, low political power | ~33,000 on any given night |
| **Incarcerated veterans** | Lose VA eligibility during incarceration | ~107,000 in state/federal prison |
| **Women veterans** | Historically excluded from VSO leadership | Gender-specific care underfunded |
| **LGBTQ+ veterans** | Recent policy changes, ongoing stigma | Barriers to care seeking |
| **Undocumented veteran family members** | Fear of immigration enforcement | Avoid VA facilities |
