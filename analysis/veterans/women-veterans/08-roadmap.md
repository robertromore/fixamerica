# Women Veterans: Roadmap

## Phase 1: Immediate Actions (0-12 Months)

### Administrative Reforms (No Legislation Required)

**MST Treatment Access**

- Issue VA directive mandating 15-day access standard for MST-related mental health appointments
- Expand MST-specific telehealth options at all VA medical centers
- Hire additional MST-specialized therapists (target: 200 new positions)
- Launch peer support specialist program for MST at every VAMC

**Disability Claims Reform**

- Issue VBA guidance establishing presumptive service connection for MST-related PTSD
- Deploy mandatory training for all disability claims adjudicators on gender-specific conditions
- Begin audit of gender disparities in disability rating decisions
- Establish quality review team to monitor gender equity in claims outcomes

**Quick Wins**

- Expand VA childcare pilot from ~50 to 100 sites
- Update VA imagery, signage, and communications at all facilities
- Launch women veteran outreach campaign through social media and community partners
- Establish women veteran advisory councils at all VA medical centers that lack them

### Advocacy and Education

**Build Coalition**

- Convene women veteran advocacy organizations for policy alignment
- Brief Congressional VA committees on data gaps and reform priorities
- Engage large VSOs (DAV, American Legion, VFW) for joint advocacy
- Launch employer education initiative on hiring women veterans

**Data Collection**

- Begin baseline assessment of women's health services at all VA facilities
- Implement gender-disaggregated performance reporting across VHA and VBA
- Commission GAO study on women veteran benefits utilization patterns

## Phase 2: Short-Term Goals (1-2 Years)

### Legislative Priorities

**Women Veterans Healthcare Improvement Act**

- Mandate comprehensive women's health services at all VAMCs
- Authorize funding for facility privacy upgrades
- Require designated Women's Health Providers with specialty training
- Establish standards for community care coordination for maternity and gynecological services

**Veterans Childcare Act**

- Authorize universal childcare during all VA services (not just healthcare)
- Fund on-site childcare at all VA medical centers and regional offices
- Extend childcare coverage to residential treatment stays (up to 30 days)
- Include childcare during TAP workshops and employment training

**Target: 119th Congress (2025-2026)**

### Facility Upgrades

**Phase 2A: Critical Infrastructure**

- Privacy renovations at the 50 highest-volume VA medical centers
- Installation of women's health examination rooms and equipment
- Renovation of residential treatment facilities to accommodate women (and women with children)
- Estimated cost: $500 million-$1 billion

**Phase 2B: Staffing**

- Recruit 500+ women's health specialists (OB/GYN, midwives, nurse practitioners)
- Deploy Women's Health training program for existing primary care providers
- Establish women's health fellowships within VA academic affiliations
- Target: 100% of designated Women's Health Providers meet specialty training standards

### Homelessness Intervention

**New Programs**

- Launch 10 women-specific transitional housing facilities with childcare
- Establish women veteran rapid rehousing program in 20 metropolitan areas
- Create IPV screening and intervention protocol for all VA homeless programs
- Implement HUD-VASH voucher set-aside for women veterans (10% of new allocations)

## Phase 3: Medium-Term Goals (2-4 Years)

### System Transformation

**Healthcare Delivery**

- All VA medical centers providing comprehensive on-site women's health services
- Maternity care coordination standardized with quality metrics across all VISNs
- Women's health telehealth platform operational for all gender-specific services
- Rural women veterans accessing full-spectrum care through telehealth and mobile units

**MST Response System**

- 30 MST-specific residential treatment programs operational (up from 17)
- MST treatment wait times below 10 days nationally
- Integrated MST screening and treatment pathway at all VA entry points
- Independent evaluation of MST treatment outcomes published annually

**Economic Security**

- Women veteran employment program operational at all American Job Centers
- Credential translation initiative covering 90% of women-heavy MOSs
- Women veteran entrepreneurship grants supporting 5,000+ businesses
- Gender disparity in disability ratings reduced to <3 points

### Culture Change Milestones

- Annual women veteran satisfaction survey showing measurable improvement
- VSO posts reporting active women's programming at 75%+ of locations
- Media representation of veterans reflecting gender diversity
- VA workforce including women veterans at rates matching population proportion

### Research and Data

- Longitudinal Women Veterans Health Study enrolled and collecting data
- Annual public report on women veterans' outcomes established
- VA Women's Health Research Network publishing 50+ studies annually
- Gender-disaggregated data informing all VA policy decisions

## Phase 4: Long-Term Transformation (5+ Years)

### Full Integration

**Healthcare**

- Women's health fully integrated into VA primary care model (not a separate "program")
- Gender-specific care indistinguishable in quality from best civilian healthcare
- All VA providers competent in women's health (not just designated providers)
- Research-informed clinical practice for military-specific women's health conditions

**MST**

- Prevention programs demonstrably reducing MST incidence in active force
- MST treatment integrated into standard VA mental health care
- MST survivors' outcomes comparable to combat PTSD treatment outcomes
- Intergenerational trauma prevention programs for families

**Economic Parity**

- Women veteran employment and earnings approaching parity with male veterans
- Disability rating gender gap eliminated
- Women veteran homelessness reduced by 75% from 2024 baseline
- Women veteran poverty rate at or below male veteran rate

### Institutional Culture

**VA Culture**

- Women veterans feel equally welcome and served at all VA facilities
- "Veteran" identity culturally inclusive of all genders
- VA leadership reflecting gender diversity of veteran population
- Women veteran advisory councils embedded in VA governance structure

**Broader Military Culture**

- MST incidence substantially reduced through prevention and accountability
- Women's military contributions recognized in education, media, and public discourse
- Post-service support systems fully adapted to diverse veteran population

## Risks and Contingencies

### Risk: Insufficient Funding

**Threat**: Congress appropriates less than needed for facility upgrades and staffing

**Mitigation**

- Prioritize highest-impact investments (childcare, MST treatment)
- Leverage community partnerships to reduce capital costs
- Demonstrate return on investment through cost-avoidance data
- Build bipartisan coalition for sustained funding

**Contingency**: Phase implementation over longer timeline; focus on administrative reforms that do not require new appropriations

### Risk: Provider Recruitment Failure

**Threat**: Cannot hire enough OB/GYN and women's health specialists

**Mitigation**

- Expand VA loan repayment and scholarship programs for women's health specialties
- Use telehealth to extend specialist reach to rural facilities
- Partner with academic medical centers for rotational staffing
- Train existing VA providers through women's health mini-residencies

**Contingency**: Expand community care partnerships for gender-specific services

### Risk: Cultural Resistance

**Threat**: VA staff and veteran communities resist culture change

**Mitigation**

- Leadership accountability (performance evaluations tied to women's health metrics)
- Zero-tolerance policies for hostile behaviors
- Positive reinforcement for facilities demonstrating excellence
- Women veteran peer support to create welcoming communities

**Contingency**: Stronger mandates through legislation if voluntary culture change is insufficient

### Risk: Political Reversal

**Threat**: Changes in administration or Congress reverse reform priorities

**Mitigation**

- Embed reforms in legislation rather than executive action where possible
- Build bipartisan support that survives political transitions
- Create constituency of women veteran beneficiaries who will defend programs
- Demonstrate outcomes that make reversal politically costly

**Contingency**: Protect core programs through multi-year appropriations and statutory mandates
