# Women Veterans: Actions

## For Individuals

### Learn First

**Understand the Problem**

- [ ] Read [Current State](02-current-state.md) to understand the data on women veterans
- [ ] Read [Root Causes](04-root-causes.md) to understand why the system fails women veterans
- [ ] Learn about Military Sexual Trauma and its lasting effects
- [ ] Watch *The Invisible War* documentary on military sexual assault
- [ ] Review the VA Advisory Committee on Women Veterans annual report

**Know Your Community**

- [ ] Research whether your nearest VA medical center has a designated Women's Health Provider
- [ ] Find out if your state has women veteran-specific programs
- [ ] Identify local women veteran organizations
- [ ] Learn about homelessness resources for women veterans in your area

### Take Action

**Contact Representatives**

- [ ] Call your U.S. Senators and Representative about women veteran healthcare legislation
- [ ] Email state legislators about women veteran housing and employment programs
- [ ] Attend town halls and ask about women veterans' issues
- [ ] Write letters to the editor highlighting women veteran challenges

**Join the Movement**

- [ ] Sign up with Service Women's Action Network (SWAN) or Women Veterans Interactive
- [ ] Join your local VSO and advocate for women's inclusion
- [ ] Follow women veteran advocacy organizations on social media
- [ ] Attend women veteran events and conferences

**Spread the Word**

- [ ] Share women veteran stories and statistics on social media
- [ ] Correct the assumption that "veteran" means only male combat veterans
- [ ] Educate friends and family about MST and women's military service
- [ ] Share resources with women you know who may be veterans

### Vote and Support

**Direct Support**

- [ ] Donate to women veteran organizations (see [Resources](09-resources.md))
- [ ] Donate to women veteran homelessness programs (Final Salute Inc.)
- [ ] Volunteer at women veteran events or service programs
- [ ] Provide mentorship to transitioning servicewomen

**Electoral Action**

- [ ] Vote for candidates who support women veteran healthcare and MST reform
- [ ] Ask candidates about their positions on VA women's health
- [ ] Support women veteran candidates for public office
- [ ] Participate in veteran advocacy days at state capitols

## For Women Veterans

### Access Your Benefits

**Healthcare**

- [ ] Call the VA Women Veterans Call Center: 1-855-VA-WOMEN (1-855-829-6636)
- [ ] Enroll in VA healthcare if eligible (apply at va.gov or your local VA)
- [ ] Request a designated Women's Health Provider for primary care
- [ ] Ask about MST-related care (no copay, no enrollment requirement)
- [ ] Learn about maternity care coordination if pregnant or planning pregnancy

**Disability Benefits**

- [ ] File for service-connected disability (consider using a VSO representative for free assistance)
- [ ] If you experienced MST, file for MST-related PTSD (relaxed documentation standards apply)
- [ ] Review your current disability rating for potential increases
- [ ] Appeal denied claims (38% of initial denials are overturned on appeal)

**Housing**

- [ ] If at risk of homelessness, call the National Call Center for Homeless Veterans: 1-877-4AID-VET
- [ ] Apply for HUD-VASH housing voucher through your local VA
- [ ] Contact Final Salute Inc. for women-specific housing assistance
- [ ] Explore SSVF (Supportive Services for Veteran Families) for prevention assistance

**Employment**

- [ ] Register with your state's veteran employment program
- [ ] Use the VA Veteran Readiness and Employment (VR&E) program for career support
- [ ] Explore SBA resources for women veteran entrepreneurs
- [ ] Connect with women veteran mentorship networks

### Build Community

- [ ] Connect with other women veterans through local and online communities
- [ ] Attend women veteran conferences and events
- [ ] Join a women veteran peer support group
- [ ] Mentor other women transitioning from military to civilian life
- [ ] Consider joining a VSO post and advocating for women's inclusion

### Advocate for Change

- [ ] Share your story (if comfortable) to increase visibility of women veterans
- [ ] Participate in VA women's advisory councils or town halls
- [ ] Provide feedback on VA services through patient surveys
- [ ] Contact your elected officials about issues affecting you
- [ ] Join advocacy organizations working on policy reform

## For Organizations

### Veterans Service Organizations

**Improve Inclusion**

- [ ] Assess your post's culture for women veterans: Is it welcoming?
- [ ] Recruit women veteran members actively
- [ ] Ensure leadership positions include women veterans
- [ ] Create women veteran committees or affinity groups
- [ ] Update imagery, language, and marketing to reflect gender diversity

**Expand Services**

- [ ] Train staff and volunteers on women veteran-specific issues
- [ ] Offer women veteran peer support programs
- [ ] Partner with women veteran organizations for events and services
- [ ] Include MST awareness in organizational training
- [ ] Advocate for women veteran-specific policy reforms

### Healthcare Organizations

**For VA Facilities**

- [ ] Conduct privacy assessment for women's health services
- [ ] Ensure all Women's Health Providers have specialty training
- [ ] Implement trauma-informed care training for all staff
- [ ] Establish or strengthen women veteran advisory councils
- [ ] Monitor and report gender-disaggregated outcomes data

**For Community Providers**

- [ ] Train clinical staff on military culture and veteran-specific issues
- [ ] Learn about MST and its health consequences
- [ ] Coordinate effectively with VA for referred patients
- [ ] Screen women patients for veteran status (many do not self-identify)

### Employers

- [ ] Include women veterans in veteran hiring initiatives (not just male combat veterans)
- [ ] Train HR staff to recognize women's military experience and skills
- [ ] Partner with women veteran employment organizations
- [ ] Offer flexible work arrangements for veterans managing health conditions
- [ ] Create employee resource groups for veteran employees including women

### Researchers

- [ ] Pursue gender-disaggregated research in veteran health and services
- [ ] Include women veterans as study participants in proportionate numbers
- [ ] Research understudied areas: reproductive health effects of military service, intersectional disparities, childcare barriers
- [ ] Partner with VA Women's Health Research Network
- [ ] Publish findings in accessible formats for policymakers

## For Policymakers

### Federal Level

- [ ] Co-sponsor and pass the Women Veterans Healthcare Improvement Act
- [ ] Appropriate adequate funding for VA women's health facility upgrades
- [ ] Mandate universal childcare during all VA services
- [ ] Strengthen presumptive service connection for MST-related conditions
- [ ] Require gender-disaggregated reporting in all VA performance data
- [ ] Fund the Longitudinal Women Veterans Health Study
- [ ] Conduct oversight hearings on women veteran homelessness

### State Level

- [ ] Establish state-level women veteran coordinator positions
- [ ] Fund state veteran housing programs with women-specific capacity
- [ ] Support women veteran employment and entrepreneurship initiatives
- [ ] Ensure state veteran benefits recognize women's service
- [ ] Address childcare gaps for veterans in state programs

### Local Level

- [ ] Recognize women veteran contributions in community events and memorials
- [ ] Ensure local homeless services accommodate women veterans with children
- [ ] Support women veteran small businesses through local procurement preferences
- [ ] Include women veteran needs in community health assessments
