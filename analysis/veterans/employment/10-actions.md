# Veteran Employment: Actions

## Overview

This document outlines specific actions that citizens, veterans, employers, and communities can take to improve veteran employment outcomes. It includes template communications for contacting elected officials, key talking points, and organizations to support.

## Veteran-Specific Actions

### Career Transition

| Action | When | How |
|--------|------|-----|
| **Begin career planning 18 months before separation** | As early as possible | Work with career counselor; identify target occupations |
| **Apply for SkillBridge fellowship** | 180 days before separation | Visit skillbridge.osd.mil; work with chain of command |
| **Obtain civilian credentials before separation** | 12+ months before | Use DOD COOL program; apply for certifications while active |
| **Build civilian network** | 12+ months before | LinkedIn, ACP mentorship, industry events |
| **Complete TAP thoroughly** | 90 days before (minimum) | Attend all modules; use career tracks |
| **Register with state workforce agency** | Immediately after separation | American Job Centers provide priority service |
| **Apply for VR&E if eligible** | When disability rating established | Contact VA VR&E; do not wait |
| **Consider Boots to Business** | If entrepreneurship-interested | Available during TAP or post-separation |

### Rights and Protections

| Right | Law | How to Exercise |
|-------|-----|-----------------|
| **Reemployment after service** | USERRA (38 U.S.C. Chapter 43) | Notify employer; apply within time limits; file complaint with DOL if denied |
| **Veterans' preference in federal hiring** | 5 U.S.C. 2108 | Claim on USAJobs applications; provide DD-214 |
| **Anti-discrimination protection** | VEVRAA, ADA | File complaint with OFCCP (contractors) or EEOC (all employers) |
| **Disability accommodations** | ADA, Rehabilitation Act | Request reasonable accommodation in writing |
| **State licensing military credit** | State-specific laws | Contact state licensing board; cite military experience provisions |

## Template Email to Members of Congress

### Topic: Military Credential Reciprocity

```
Subject: Support Military Credential Reciprocity for Veterans

Dear [Senator/Representative] [Last Name],

I am writing to urge you to support legislation establishing national
military credential reciprocity standards.

Currently, veterans who have spent years operating complex equipment,
providing medical care, or performing skilled technical work in the
military must often retrain and retest for civilian licenses they
are already qualified to hold. A combat medic with 1,800+ hours of
training cannot work as an EMT without starting over. A military
truck driver with a flawless safety record must complete duplicative
commercial driver licensing.

This is not a public safety issue--studies by RAND and others show
military-trained professionals perform comparably or better than
civilian-trained counterparts. States that have adopted military
reciprocity report no increase in safety incidents.

This is a recognition issue. Our service members deserve to have
their training and experience honored when they transition to
civilian careers.

I ask that you:

1. Co-sponsor legislation requiring states to recognize military
   training for civilian occupational licensing
2. Support funding for competency-based bridge programs where
   genuine skill gaps exist
3. Support the development of a national digital credential
   portfolio for separating service members

Thank you for your attention to this important issue.

Sincerely,
[Your Name]
[Your Address]
[Your Phone Number]
```

## Template Phone Script

### Topic: Extended Transition Timeline

```
Hello, my name is [Your Name] and I'm a constituent from [City, State].

I'm calling to ask [Senator/Representative] [Last Name] to support
reforms to the military Transition Assistance Program.

Currently, service members have as little as 90 days to prepare for
civilian employment. Research shows that veterans who begin career
planning 12-18 months before separation have significantly better
employment outcomes.

I'm asking [Senator/Representative] [Last Name] to support:

- Extending the mandatory transition planning timeline to 18 months
- Expanding the SkillBridge program to more employer partners
- Funding post-separation career counseling for 24 months

Would you like me to provide any additional information?

Thank you for your time.
```

## Talking Points

### For General Audiences

- Veterans are not charity cases--they are highly trained professionals facing structural barriers that the civilian sector has failed to address
- Military credential barriers cost the economy billions in lost productivity and force veterans into positions below their skill level
- The Transition Assistance Program gives service members as little as 90 days to prepare for a career change that would take any civilian months to navigate
- Veteran underemployment--working in jobs below their skill level--affects up to one-third of recently separated veterans
- Every dollar invested in veteran employment programs returns an estimated $3-5 in economic output

### For Employer Audiences

- Veteran hires who receive proper onboarding perform at or above the level of civilian peers (SHRM, 2023)
- Companies with veteran-specific onboarding programs report 62% higher two-year retention (IVMF, 2023)
- Veterans bring leadership, discipline, security awareness, and mission focus that are difficult to develop through civilian training alone
- The Work Opportunity Tax Credit provides $2,400-$9,600 per eligible veteran hire
- SkillBridge partnerships let employers evaluate military talent before making a hiring commitment--a low-risk trial period

### For Veteran Audiences

- You have rights: USERRA protects your civilian employment during and after military service
- Start early: The most successful transitions begin 12-18 months before separation
- Use every resource: SkillBridge, COOL, VR&E, American Job Centers, and nonprofit programs exist to help
- Network matters more than resumes: 70% of veteran job placements come through networking, not job boards
- Your skills are valuable: Military training often exceeds civilian equivalents in both hours and rigor
