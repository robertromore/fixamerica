# Veteran Employment: Stakeholders

## Overview

Veteran employment involves a complex ecosystem of government agencies, employers, nonprofit organizations, and advocacy groups. Understanding the interests, resources, and influence of each stakeholder is essential for designing effective reform strategies.

---

## Primary Stakeholders

### Veterans and Military Families

| Group | Population | Key Interests | Influence Level |
|-------|------------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Transitioning service members** | ~200,000 annually | Meaningful employment, career match, income stability | Medium--rely on institutional support |
| **Post-9/11 veterans** | ~4.1 million | Skills recognition, career advancement, entrepreneurship | High--politically visible cohort |
| **Women veterans** | ~2.1 million | Pay equity, childcare, MST-informed workplaces | Growing--fastest growing veteran demographic |
| **Disabled veterans** | ~5.4 million (service-connected) | Accommodations, VR&E access, discrimination protection | High--strong legal protections and advocacy |
| **Guard/Reserve members** | ~800,000 | USERRA enforcement, employer relations, career continuity | Medium--employer relationship complexity |
| **Military spouses** | ~1 million | License portability, remote work, career continuity | Growing--policy attention increasing |
| **Veteran entrepreneurs** | ~1.8 million business owners | Capital access, contracting opportunities, mentorship | Medium--SBA advocacy |

## Government Stakeholders

### Federal Agencies

| Agency | Role | Budget/Resources | Effectiveness |
|--------|------|------------------|---------------|
| **Department of Defense** | TAP, SkillBridge, pre-separation preparation | Part of $840B+ defense budget | Mixed--TAP is compliance-driven; SkillBridge growing |
| **Department of Veterans Affairs** | VR&E (Chapter 31), education benefits | ~$2.1B for VR&E (FY 2025) | Mixed--long wait times; high caseloads |
| **Department of Labor (VETS)** | JVSG, HVRP, USERRA, employer outreach | ~$320M (FY 2025) | Underfunded--limited reach relative to need |
| **Small Business Administration** | Boots to Business, SDVOSB, VBOCs | ~$100M for veteran programs | Moderate--narrow focus on entrepreneurship |
| **Office of Personnel Management** | Federal veterans' preference implementation | Regulatory authority | Important--31% of federal workforce are veterans |
| **OFCCP** | VEVRAA enforcement for federal contractors | Part of DOL budget | Weak--self-reporting with limited auditing |

### Congressional Committees

| Committee | Jurisdiction | Key Role |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| **Senate Veterans' Affairs Committee** | VA programs, veteran benefits | Authorization and oversight |
| **House Veterans' Affairs Committee** | VA programs, veteran benefits | Authorization and oversight |
| **Senate Armed Services Committee** | DOD transition programs, SkillBridge | Military policy authority |
| **House Armed Services Committee** | DOD transition programs | Military policy authority |
| **Senate HELP Committee** | Workforce development, licensing | Labor policy jurisdiction |
| **House Education and Workforce Committee** | Workforce development, WOTC | Labor policy jurisdiction |

### State and Local Government

| Level | Role | Variation |
|-------|------|-----------|
| **State workforce agencies** | American Job Centers, veteran representatives | Varies widely by state funding and commitment |
| **State licensing boards** | Credential recognition, military reciprocity | 50 different regimes; uneven progress |
| **State veterans affairs departments** | Employment programs, coordination | Ranges from robust (TX, VA) to minimal |
| **Local workforce boards** | Regional employment services | Dependent on local priorities and funding |
| **Governors' offices** | Executive orders on military licensing | Growing engagement; uneven implementation |

## Decision Makers

### Key Decision Points

| Decision | Decision Maker | Factors Considered |
|----------|---------------|--------------------|
| **TAP curriculum and policy** | DOD (Under Secretary for Personnel and Readiness) | Cost, unit readiness impact, mandate compliance |
| **VR&E program design** | VA (Under Secretary for Benefits) | Budget, caseload capacity, congressional oversight |
| **JVSG funding levels** | Congress (appropriations committees) | Budget constraints, competing priorities |
| **Credentialing reciprocity** | State legislatures and licensing boards | Public safety, incumbent practitioner interests |
| **WOTC extension and design** | Congress (Ways and Means / Finance) | Revenue impact, employer lobbying |
| **SkillBridge expansion** | DOD (each service branch) | Unit readiness, retention concerns |
| **Federal hiring preferences** | OPM Director; agency hiring managers | Merit system balance, workforce needs |
| **VEVRAA enforcement** | OFCCP Director | Resources, enforcement philosophy |

## Coalition Dynamics

### Natural Alliances

| Coalition | Shared Interest | Strength |
|-----------|----------------|----------|
| **VSOs + congressional veterans** | Expanded benefits and programs | Very strong--bipartisan support |
| **Employers + DOL** | Effective workforce matching | Moderate--employer engagement varies |
| **VSOs + disability advocates** | Disabled veteran employment protections | Strong--overlapping constituencies |
| **Military families + licensing reformers** | Credential portability | Growing--bipartisan appeal |
| **Veteran entrepreneurs + SBA** | Small business support | Moderate--narrow focus |

### Tension Points

| Stakeholders in Tension | Issue | Nature of Conflict |
|-------------------------|-------|-------------------|
| **DOD readiness vs. transition quality** | SkillBridge removes members from units | Commanders resist losing personnel |
| **State licensing boards vs. credential reform** | Military reciprocity | Boards cite public safety; resist external pressure |
| **Employers vs. VEVRAA enforcement** | Affirmative action compliance | Employers prefer voluntary commitments over mandates |
| **Large employers vs. small businesses** | Veteran hiring capacity | Large firms dominate programs; small firms lack resources |
| **VSOs vs. each other** | Organizational turf and funding | Competition for members, donations, and political access |
| **VA VR&E vs. GI Bill** | Which benefit to use first | Competing programs with different counseling approaches |

## Stakeholder Influence Map

### High Influence, High Interest

| Stakeholder | Basis of Influence |
|-------------|-------------------|
| **Congressional veterans' affairs committees** | Authorization and funding authority |
| **Major VSOs (American Legion, VFW, DAV)** | Political access, grassroots mobilization |
| **DOD** | Controls pre-separation phase |
| **VA** | Largest veteran program administrator |
| **Large defense contractors** | Major veteran employers; lobbying resources |

### High Influence, Lower Interest

| Stakeholder | Why Lower Interest |
|-------------|-------------------|
| **Congressional appropriations committees** | Veterans compete with many priorities |
| **State legislatures** | Licensing reform is one of many issues |
| **OPM** | Veterans' preference is established; incremental changes |
| **Major tech companies** | Veteran hiring is CSR initiative, not core mission |

### Lower Influence, High Interest

| Stakeholder | Why Lower Influence |
|-------------|-------------------|
| **Individual transitioning veterans** | Dispersed; temporary status |
| **Small veteran employment nonprofits** | Limited resources and reach |
| **Military spouses** | Growing but still underrepresented in policy |
| **Rural veteran communities** | Geographic isolation limits political access |
| **Veteran entrepreneurs** | Dispersed; limited organized advocacy |
