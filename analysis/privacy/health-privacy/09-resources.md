# Health Privacy: Resources

## Key Federal Laws and Regulations

| Law/Regulation | Citation | Relevance |
|---------------|----------|-----------|
| Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) | Pub. L. 104-191 (1996) | Primary federal health privacy framework |
| HIPAA Privacy Rule | 45 CFR Parts 160, 164 | Implements HIPAA privacy protections |
| HIPAA Security Rule | 45 CFR Part 164, Subpart C | Electronic health information security standards |
| HITECH Act | Pub. L. 111-5, Title XIII (2009) | Enhanced HIPAA enforcement, breach notification, and penalties |
| Genetic Information Nondiscrimination Act (GINA) | Pub. L. 110-233 (2008) | Prohibits genetic discrimination in health insurance and employment |
| 42 CFR Part 2 | 42 CFR Part 2 | Heightened protections for substance use disorder treatment records |
| FTC Act, Section 5 | 15 U.S.C. 45 | Authority over unfair and deceptive health data practices |
| Health Breach Notification Rule | 16 CFR Part 318 | FTC notification requirements for health data breaches |
| COPPA | 15 U.S.C. 6501-6506 | Children's health data protections online |
| 21st Century Cures Act | Pub. L. 114-255 (2016) | Health data interoperability and information blocking |
| California Confidentiality of Medical Information Act (CMIA) | Cal. Civ. Code 56-56.37 | California state health privacy law |
| Washington My Health My Data Act | RCW 19.373 (2023) | First state law covering all consumer health data |

## Key Court Cases

| Case | Citation | Significance |
|------|----------|-------------|
| *Whalen v. Roe* | 429 U.S. 589 (1977) | Recognized constitutional right to informational privacy |
| *Doe v. Southeastern Pennsylvania Transportation Authority* | 72 F.3d 1133 (3d Cir. 1995) | Applied constitutional privacy to HIV medication records |
| *Doe v. City of New York* | 15 F.3d 264 (2d Cir. 1994) | Found constitutional privacy interest in HIV status |
| *Jaffee v. Redmond* | 518 U.S. 1 (1996) | Established psychotherapist-patient privilege under federal law |
| *Dobbs v. Jackson Women's Health Organization* | 597 U.S. 215 (2022) | Overturned constitutional right to abortion, creating reproductive health data risks |
| *Carpenter v. United States* | 585 U.S. 296 (2018) | Required warrant for cell-site location data; implications for health-related location data |
| *In re 23andMe Customer Data Security Breach Litigation* | N.D. Cal. (2024) | Multidistrict litigation over genetic data breach |

## Academic Research

### Privacy and Health Data

- Sweeney, Latanya. "Only You, Your Doctor, and Many Others May Know." *Technology Science* (2015). -- Demonstrated re-identification risks for de-identified health data.
- Grundy, Quinn, et al. "Data Sharing Practices of Medicines Related Apps and the Mobile Ecosystem." *BMJ* 364 (2019). -- Found widespread data sharing by health apps with third parties.
- Huckvale, Kit, et al. "Assessment of Compliance with Data Protection Regulation of Health Apps." *BMJ* 373 (2021). -- Assessed GDPR compliance of health apps.
- Marks, Mason. "Emergent Medical Data: Health Information Inferred by Artificial Intelligence." *UC Irvine Law Review* 11 (2021). -- Analyzed AI-inferred health data and regulatory gaps.
- Cohen, I. Glenn, and Michelle M. Mello. "Big Data, Big Tech, and Protecting Patient Privacy." *JAMA* 322.12 (2019): 1141-1142. -- Addressed tech company expansion into health care.

### Reproductive Health Data

- Harwell, Drew. "Period Tracker Apps Are Generating Fears in a Post-Roe World." *Washington Post* (2022). -- Documented reproductive data risks after *Dobbs*.
- Fowler, Leah R., et al. "Reproductive Health Data Surveillance in the Post-Dobbs Era." *New England Journal of Medicine* 387 (2022). -- Analyzed legal risks of reproductive health data.
- Zuboff, Shoshana. "The Age of Surveillance Capitalism." New York: PublicAffairs, 2019. -- Broader framework for understanding health data as part of surveillance capitalism.

### Genetic Privacy

- Ram, Natalie. "Genetic Privacy After *Carpenter*." *Virginia Law Review* 105 (2019). -- Analyzed Fourth Amendment implications for genetic data.
- Murphy, Erin. "Inside the Cell: The Dark Side of Forensic DNA." New York: Nation Books, 2015. -- Examined law enforcement use of genetic databases.

### Mental Health Data

- Huckvale, Kit, et al. "Unaddressed Privacy Risks in Accredited Health and Wellness Apps." *BMC Medicine* 13 (2015). -- Found privacy failings in mental health apps.
- Martinez-Martin, Nicole, et al. "Data Mining for Health: Staking Out the Ethical Territory of Digital Phenotyping." *npj Digital Medicine* 1 (2018). -- Addressed ethical issues in digital mental health.
