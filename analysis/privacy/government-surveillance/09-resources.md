# Government Surveillance: Resources

## Overview

This document compiles research, organizations, tools, legal references, and data sources relevant to understanding and advancing reform of government surveillance in the United States.

## Legal Resources

### Key Cases

| Case | Citation | Relevance |
|------|----------|-----------|
| *Katz v. United States* | 389 U.S. 347 (1967) | Established "reasonable expectation of privacy" test for Fourth Amendment |
| *United States v. Miller* | 425 U.S. 435 (1976) | Third-party doctrine: bank records not protected by Fourth Amendment |
| *Smith v. Maryland* | 442 U.S. 735 (1979) | Third-party doctrine: phone metadata not protected |
| *Carpenter v. United States* | 585 U.S. 296 (2018) | Cell-site location data requires warrant; limited third-party doctrine |
| *Clapper v. Amnesty International* | 568 U.S. 398 (2013) | Restricted standing to challenge surveillance (must show imminent injury) |
| *Jewel v. NSA* | No. C-08-4373 (N.D. Cal.) | EFF challenge to NSA upstream surveillance; ongoing |
| *ACLU v. Clapper* | 785 F.3d 787 (2d Cir. 2015) | Found NSA bulk telephony metadata program exceeded Section 215 authority |
| *United States v. Moalin* | 973 F.3d 977 (9th Cir. 2020) | Found NSA mass surveillance may have been illegal; evidence suppression |
| *Shelby County v. Holder* | 570 U.S. 529 (2013) | Weakened Voting Rights Act oversight (relevant to surveillance discrimination) |
| *Riley v. California* | 573 U.S. 373 (2014) | Cell phone search requires warrant; "privacy comes at a cost" |
| *Doe v. Ashcroft* | 334 F. Supp. 2d 471 (S.D.N.Y. 2004) | NSL gag order provisions unconstitutional (partially) |
| *In re Directives* | (FISC-R 2008) | Upheld constitutionality of Protect America Act surveillance |

### Key Statutes

| Statute | Citation | Relevance |
|---------|----------|-----------|
| Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act (FISA) | 50 U.S.C. ss 1801-1885d | Core statutory framework for intelligence surveillance |
| FISA Amendments Act / Section 702 | 50 U.S.C. ss 1881a | Warrantless collection targeting non-US persons abroad |
| USA PATRIOT Act | Pub. L. 107-56 (2001) | Expanded surveillance authorities post-9/11 |
| USA FREEDOM Act | Pub. L. 114-23 (2015) | Ended bulk telephony metadata collection; created FISC amicus |
| Reforming Intelligence and Securing America Act (RISAA) | Pub. L. 118-49 (2024) | Reauthorized Section 702 through April 2026 |
| Electronic Communications Privacy Act (ECPA) | 18 U.S.C. ss 2510-2522, 2701-2712, 3121-3127 | Electronic surveillance framework (1986) |
| Communications Assistance for Law Enforcement Act (CALEA) | 47 U.S.C. ss 1001-1010 | Requires telecom carriers to build interception capability |
| Executive Order 12333 | Presidential directive (1981, amended) | Governs foreign intelligence collection outside FISA |
| Intelligence Community Whistleblower Protection Act | 5 U.S.C. App. 8H | IC whistleblower protections (inadequate) |
| Espionage Act | 18 U.S.C. ss 793-798 | Used to prosecute surveillance whistleblowers |
