# Communications Privacy: Roadmap

## Overview

This roadmap sequences communications privacy reforms across three phases: immediate executive, judicial, and state actions (Phase 1), comprehensive federal legislation and institutional reform (Phase 2), and structural transformation of the communications privacy framework (Phase 3). Each phase builds on the previous, creating political momentum and institutional capacity for the next. The Section 702 reauthorization timeline (expiring 2026) creates a critical near-term window for reform.

---

## Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Probability | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|------------|
| Terrorist attack used to justify expanded surveillance before reforms enacted | Moderate | Pre-position reform bills; build durable bipartisan coalition; emphasize that reform strengthens security |
| Section 702 reauthorized again without reform | High | Concentrate advocacy resources on reauthorization window; support litigation challenges |
| EARN IT Act or equivalent passes, undermining encryption | Low-Moderate | Build tech industry and civil liberties coalition; educate Congress on technical realities |
| FISA Court continues rubber-stamping surveillance requests | Moderate | Mandatory amici requirement; adversarial reform; transparency provisions |
| Courts narrow *Carpenter* rather than expanding it | Moderate | Pursue statutory protections that do not depend on judicial interpretation |
| Intelligence community classification prevents public debate | High | Mandatory declassification timelines; strengthen congressional oversight; whistleblower protections |
| State-level reforms preempted by weak federal legislation | Low-Moderate | Design federal floor without ceiling preemption; build state coalition against preemption |
| Political leadership change reverses executive actions | Moderate | Embed reforms in statute; build bipartisan support; prioritize legislative over executive action |
