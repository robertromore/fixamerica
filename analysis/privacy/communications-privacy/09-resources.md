## Key Legal Cases

| Case | Citation | Year | Significance |
|------|----------|------|-------------|
| *Olmstead v. United States* | 277 U.S. 438 | 1928 | Wiretapping not a "search"; Brandeis dissent on technology and privacy |
| *Katz v. United States* | 389 U.S. 347 | 1967 | Fourth Amendment protects "people, not places"; reasonable expectation of privacy |
| *Smith v. Maryland* | 442 U.S. 735 | 1979 | Third-party doctrine: phone numbers dialed lack Fourth Amendment protection |
| *United States v. Warshak* | 631 F.3d 266 (6th Cir.) | 2010 | Email content protected by Fourth Amendment regardless of storage duration |
| *Clapper v. Amnesty International* | 568 U.S. 398 | 2013 | Standing barriers prevent challenges to surveillance programs |
| *Riley v. California* | 573 U.S. 373 | 2014 | Warrant required to search cell phone incident to arrest |
| *Carpenter v. United States* | 585 U.S. 296 | 2018 | Cell-site location information requires a warrant; narrows third-party doctrine |
