# Algorithmic Accountability: Actions

## Overview

This document outlines concrete steps that individuals, communities, organizations, and institutions can take to advance algorithmic accountability. Actions range from personal protective measures to systemic advocacy.

## Individual Actions

### Know Your Algorithmic Rights

| Action | How | Impact |
|--------|-----|--------|
| **Learn what algorithms affect you** | Research algorithmic decision-making in credit, employment, housing, insurance, and public benefits | Understanding is prerequisite to action |
| **Request explanations for adverse decisions** | When denied credit, insurance, housing, or employment, request the specific reasons and whether automated systems were involved | ECOA and FCRA require some explanations; exercising rights builds enforcement pressure |
| **Review your credit and consumer reports** | Request free annual credit reports from Equifax, Experian, TransUnion; review for inaccuracies | Correct errors that feed algorithmic systems |
| **Opt out where possible** | Under CCPA, Colorado, Connecticut, and other state laws, exercise opt-out rights for automated profiling | Reduces data available to algorithmic systems |
| **Document algorithmic harms** | Record instances where algorithmic decisions appear unfair or discriminatory | Evidence supports enforcement and advocacy |

### Protective Measures

| Action | How | Impact |
|--------|-----|--------|
| **Minimize data footprint** | Limit unnecessary data sharing; use privacy tools; review app permissions | Reduces inputs to algorithmic profiling |
| **Be strategic with online presence** | Understand that hiring algorithms may review social media, online activity | Awareness enables informed choices |
| **Use privacy-protective tools** | Ad blockers, tracker blockers, VPNs, privacy-focused browsers and search engines | Reduces tracking data that feeds algorithmic systems |
| **Read terms of service for algorithmic provisions** | Check whether services disclose automated decision-making and your rights | Informed consent and awareness |

### When You Experience Algorithmic Harm

| Step | Action | Resource |
|------|--------|----------|
| 1 | **Document everything** | Save screenshots, correspondence, denial letters, and timelines |
| 2 | **Request explanation** | Ask the decision-maker for the specific reasons and whether automated tools were used |
| 3 | **File regulatory complaints** | FTC (ftc.gov/complaint), CFPB (consumerfinance.gov/complaint), EEOC (eeoc.gov), state AG |
| 4 | **Contact civil rights organizations** | ACLU, NAACP Legal Defense Fund, National Fair Housing Alliance, local legal aid |
| 5 | **Report to researchers** | AI Incident Database (incidentdatabase.ai), Algorithmic Justice League |
| 6 | **Contact journalists** | Investigative reporters at ProPublica, *The Markup*, local media |
| 7 | **Consult an attorney** | Consumer rights, employment, or civil rights attorney for potential legal action |

## Community Actions

### Education and Awareness

| Action | How | Target Audience |
|--------|-----|-----------------|
| **Host community forums on algorithmic impacts** | Partner with libraries, community centers, faith organizations | General public |
| **Organize know-your-rights workshops** | Train community members on rights when facing algorithmic decisions | Affected communities |
| **Support digital literacy programs** | Fund and participate in programs teaching critical evaluation of technology | Youth, older adults, underserved communities |
| **Create community documentation projects** | Systematically document algorithmic harms in your community | Community members, researchers |

### Local Advocacy

| Action | How | Target |
|--------|-----|--------|
| **Advocate for local algorithmic transparency** | Demand disclosure of algorithms used by city/county government | City council, county commissioners |
| **Participate in public comment on government AI** | Comment on proposed government adoption of algorithmic systems | Local and state agencies |
| **Support municipal algorithmic accountability ordinances** | Advocate for local laws modeled on NYC Local Law 144 | City council |
| **Demand school district transparency** | Push for disclosure and oversight of algorithms used in student assessment, admissions, and proctoring | School boards |
| **Monitor local law enforcement technology** | Track adoption of predictive policing, facial recognition, and surveillance algorithms | Police oversight boards, city council |

### Coalition Building

| Action | Partners | Goal |
|--------|----------|------|
| **Join or form local tech accountability coalitions** | Civil rights groups, consumer organizations, labor unions, faith communities | Unified advocacy at local and state level |
| **Connect with affected communities** | Workers screened by hiring algorithms, tenants denied by screening tools, defendants scored by risk tools | Center affected voices in advocacy |
| **Engage with academic researchers** | Local university computer science and law programs | Partner on algorithmic auditing and impact studies |
| **Build cross-partisan alliances** | Privacy advocates, small government proponents, civil rights groups, faith communities | Broaden support base for accountability reforms |

## Organizational Actions

### For Employers

| Action | Benefit | Urgency |
|--------|---------|---------|
| **Audit your hiring algorithms** | Reduce discrimination risk; comply with emerging laws | High -- NYC Law 144 model spreading |
| **Provide transparency to applicants** | Build trust; comply with state requirements | Medium |
| **Require bias testing from vendors** | Reduce liability; improve hiring quality | High |
| **Maintain human review for consequential decisions** | Due process; error correction; legal protection | High |
| **Document algorithmic decision processes** | Regulatory compliance; internal accountability | Medium |

### For Technology Companies

| Action | Benefit | Urgency |
|--------|---------|---------|
| **Conduct impact assessments** | Identify risks before deployment; prepare for regulation | High |
| **Publish transparency reports** | Build trust; demonstrate responsible development | Medium |
| **Invest in fairness testing** | Improve product quality; reduce liability | High |
| **Support external auditing** | Demonstrate accountability; inform standards development | Medium |
| **Engage affected communities in design** | Better products; social license to operate | Medium |

### For Government Agencies

| Action | Benefit | Urgency |
|--------|---------|---------|
| **Inventory all algorithmic systems** | Foundation for oversight and accountability | Immediate |
| **Conduct public impact assessments** | Transparency; identify and mitigate harms | High |
| **Establish community oversight mechanisms** | Democratic legitimacy; error detection | High |
| **Require independent validation before deployment** | Accuracy; fairness; due process | High |
| **Publish algorithmic system details** | Public accountability; researcher access | Medium |

## Political and Advocacy Actions

### Federal Level

| Action | How | Target |
|--------|-----|--------|
| **Contact your members of Congress** | Call, write, and email in support of algorithmic accountability legislation | Senators and representatives |
| **Testify at congressional hearings** | Share personal experiences with algorithmic harms | Congressional committees |
| **Submit public comments on agency rulemaking** | Comment on FTC, EEOC, CFPB proposals related to algorithms | Federal agencies |
| **Support organizations advocating for accountability** | Donate to and volunteer with ACLU, EFF, Algorithmic Justice League, CDT | Advocacy organizations |

### State Level

| Action | How | Target |
|--------|-----|--------|
| **Support state algorithmic accountability bills** | Testify, write letters, organize constituent support | State legislators |
| **Advocate for state AG enforcement** | Urge state attorneys general to investigate algorithmic discrimination | State AG offices |
| **Run for or support candidates committed to accountability** | Electoral engagement | State and local offices |
| **Monitor state AI task forces** | Participate in or follow state AI advisory bodies | State government |

### Voter Actions

| Action | When | Impact |
|--------|------|--------|
| **Evaluate candidates on technology accountability positions** | Every election cycle | Electoral pressure for reform |
| **Support ballot initiatives for algorithmic transparency** | When available | Direct democracy on technology policy |
| **Attend town halls and ask about algorithmic accountability** | Throughout the year | Raises issue salience with elected officials |

## Professional Actions

### For Technologists

| Action | How | Impact |
|--------|-----|--------|
| **Advocate within your organization** | Push for impact assessments, fairness testing, and transparency in systems you build | Change from within |
| **Refuse to build harmful systems** | Exercise professional ethics when asked to build discriminatory or deceptive systems | Personal integrity; industry norm-setting |
| **Contribute to open-source accountability tools** | Help build and improve fairness testing, auditing, and explainability tools | Democratize accountability |
| **Publish research on algorithmic harms** | Document and share findings about biased or harmful systems | Inform public and policy debates |
| **Support whistleblower protections** | Advocate for legal protections for tech workers who report harmful algorithmic practices | Enable internal accountability |

### For Lawyers

| Action | How | Impact |
|--------|-----|--------|
| **Bring algorithmic discrimination cases** | Represent individuals and classes harmed by biased algorithms | Legal precedent; deterrence |
| **Provide pro bono representation** | Represent affected individuals who cannot afford legal counsel | Access to justice |
| **Develop legal theories for algorithmic accountability** | Publish scholarship applying existing law to algorithmic contexts | Inform judicial and legislative responses |
| **Draft model legislation** | Develop bill text for state and federal algorithmic accountability laws | Legislative infrastructure |

### For Researchers

| Action | How | Impact |
|--------|-----|--------|
| **Conduct external algorithmic audits** | Test commercial systems for bias, accuracy, and fairness | Evidence base for reform |
| **Publish findings publicly** | Make research accessible to policymakers and the public | Inform public debate |
| **Engage with policymakers** | Testify before legislatures, advise agencies, participate in standard-setting | Translate research into policy |
| **Train the next generation** | Teach courses on algorithmic fairness, accountability, and transparency | Build future capacity |
