# Biometric Privacy: Roadmap

## Overview

This roadmap sequences biometric privacy reforms across three phases: immediate local and state actions that build on existing momentum (Phase 1), federal legislation and standards development (Phase 2), and comprehensive biometric governance including international coordination (Phase 3). Each phase builds political momentum and legal infrastructure for the next.

---

## Phase 1: Immediate Actions (0-18 Months)

### Local and State Actions

| Action | Actor | Timeline | Difficulty |

## Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Probability | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|------------|
| Law enforcement opposition blocks federal legislation | Very High | Pursue moratorium and accuracy standards separately; build support through wrongful arrest awareness |
| Industry lobbying defeats BIPA-style federal law | High | Pursue state-level BIPA expansion simultaneously; use state momentum to pressure federal action |
| Federal law preempts BIPA | Moderate | Design legislation with explicit non-preemption; build Illinois political coalition to oppose preemption |
| Technology advances outpace standards | Moderate | Technology-neutral drafting; agency rulemaking authority for emerging biometric technologies |
| BIPA backlash leads to Illinois amendment | Moderate | Public awareness of BIPA's effectiveness; organize Illinois stakeholders |
| Government databases continue expanding without oversight | High | Use appropriations process to condition funding on privacy compliance |
| Consumer DNA companies fail, data at risk | Moderate | Enact Consumer DNA Privacy Act with insolvency provisions requiring data destruction |
| Emotion recognition deployed despite scientific concerns | Moderate | Enact ban early (Phase 1-2); leverage EU AI Act precedent |
