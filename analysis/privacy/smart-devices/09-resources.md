# Smart Devices and IoT: Resources

## Overview

This document compiles key resources for understanding and addressing smart device privacy, including academic research, government reports, advocacy organization materials, tools for consumers, and legal references.

## Academic Research

### Foundational Studies

| Title | Author(s) | Year | Key Findings |
|-------|-----------|------|-------------|
| "IoT Inspector: Crowdsourcing Labeled Network Traffic from Smart Home Devices" | Huang et al. (Princeton) | 2020 | Documented extensive undisclosed data transmissions from IoT devices to third parties |
| "Asking for a Friend: Evaluating Response Biases in Research on the Willing Acceptance of Smart Home Surveillance" | Zeng et al. (Carnegie Mellon) | 2021 | Found consumers significantly underestimate data collection scope |
| "Monetizing Car Data" | McKinsey Center for Future Mobility | 2023 | Projected $250-$400 billion in connected vehicle data revenue by 2030 |
| "Data Brokers and the Sale of Americans' Mental Health Data" | Sherman (Duke University) | 2023 | Documented sale of health data from wearables and apps without HIPAA protections |
| "The Biggest Data Breach" | Irish Council for Civil Liberties | 2022 | Quantified RTB data exposure including IoT device data |
| "Privacy Not Included: Cars" | Mozilla Foundation | 2023 | Reviewed 25 car brands; found 100% fail minimum privacy standards |

### Alternative Products

| Category | Privacy-Friendly Options | Trade-offs |
|----------|------------------------|------------|
| Smart home automation | Home Assistant (local processing) | Requires technical setup; less polished interface |
| Voice assistant | Mycroft (open source, local) | Limited ecosystem; less capable |
| Security cameras | Local NVR systems (Reolink, Amcrest local) | No cloud features; self-managed storage |
| Fitness tracking | Gadgetbridge + open-source firmware | Limited device compatibility; reduced features |
| Router/network | OpenWrt, pfSense | Requires technical expertise |

## Legal References

### Key Cases

| Case | Citation | Relevance |
|------|----------|-----------|
| *Carpenter v. United States* | 585 U.S. ___ (2018) | Warrant required for cell location data; implications for IoT |
| *Riley v. California* | 573 U.S. 373 (2014) | Warrant required to search cell phone; extends to smart devices |
| *Kyllo v. United States* | 533 U.S. 27 (2001) | Thermal imaging of home requires warrant; implications for IoT sensors |
| *FTC v. Vizio* | Settlement (2017) | Smart TV tracking without consent as unfair practice |
| *FTC v. Amazon.com (Ring)* | Settlement (2023) | Doorbell camera privacy and security failures |
| *FTC v. Amazon.com (Alexa)* | Settlement (2023) | Voice assistant children's privacy violations |

### Key Statutes

| Statute | Citation | Relevance |
|---------|----------|-----------|
| Federal Trade Commission Act, Section 5 | 15 U.S.C. 45 | Authority for IoT unfair/deceptive practice enforcement |
| Children's Online Privacy Protection Act | 15 U.S.C. 6501-6506 | Connected toy and children's device provisions |
| Electronic Communications Privacy Act | 18 U.S.C. 2510-2522 | Wiretap provisions applicable to always-on microphones |
| IoT Cybersecurity Improvement Act | Pub. L. 116-207 (2020) | Federal procurement IoT security standards |
| California Consumer Privacy Act | Cal. Civ. Code 1798.100 et seq. | State IoT data rights model |
| Illinois Biometric Information Privacy Act | 740 ILCS 14 | Biometric data from IoT devices |
