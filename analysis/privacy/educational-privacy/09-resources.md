# Educational Privacy: Resources

## Overview

This document provides key resources for understanding and advocating for educational privacy reform, including research organizations, advocacy groups, legal references, data sources, and educational materials.

## Legal References

### Federal Law

| Law | Citation | Relevance |
|-----|----------|-----------|
| Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act | 20 U.S.C. 1232g; 34 C.F.R. Part 99 | Primary federal student privacy law |
| Children's Online Privacy Protection Act | 15 U.S.C. 6501-6506 | Online privacy for children under 13 |
| Protection of Pupil Rights Amendment | 20 U.S.C. 1232h | Student surveys and evaluations |
| Children's Internet Protection Act | 47 U.S.C. 254(h)(5) | Internet filtering requirements |
| Individuals with Disabilities Education Act | 20 U.S.C. 1400 et seq. | Student records provisions for IEPs |
| Every Student Succeeds Act | Pub. L. 114-95 | Data collection and privacy provisions |

### Key Court Cases

| Case | Citation | Significance |
|------|----------|-------------|
| *Gonzaga University v. Doe* | 536 U.S. 273 (2002) | FERPA creates no private right of action |
| *Owasso Independent School District v. Falvo* | 534 U.S. 426 (2002) | Peer-graded papers are not "education records" |
| *United States v. Miami University* | 294 F.3d 797 (6th Cir. 2002) | Disciplinary records protected under FERPA |
| *Vernonia School District v. Acton* | 515 U.S. 646 (1995) | Reduced privacy expectations in schools |
| *New Jersey v. T.L.O.* | 469 U.S. 325 (1985) | School search and seizure standards |
