# Workplace Surveillance: Actions

## Overview

While structural reform requires legislation and regulatory action, individuals, workers, organizations, and communities can take concrete steps now to protect workplace privacy, build power for reform, and shift the political environment. These actions range from personal protective measures to collective organizing and civic engagement.

## Individual Actions

### Know Your Rights

| Action | How | Resources |
|--------|-----|-----------|
| **Learn your state's monitoring laws** | Check whether your state requires monitoring notice (CT, DE, NY, CA, CO, and others); know your rights under state biometric privacy laws (IL, TX, WA) | NCSL Employee Monitoring Laws tracker; state AG websites |
| **Read your employer's monitoring policy** | Request a copy of all monitoring policies from HR; ask specific questions about what is monitored, stored, and accessed | Your employee handbook; company privacy policy |
| **Understand your devices** | Know whether your employer has installed monitoring software on company devices; check running processes and installed applications | EFF's "Am I Being Monitored?" guide; CDT bossware resources |
| **Know your NLRA rights** | Understand that concerted activity (discussing working conditions with coworkers) is protected under Section 7 regardless of monitoring | NLRB.gov, "Interfering with Employee Rights" |

### Protect Yourself

| Action | How | Why |
|--------|-----|-----|
| **Separate personal and work devices** | Use personal devices for personal communications; never access personal email, banking, or health services on employer devices | Employer monitoring of company devices is legal in most states; personal devices offer more protection |
| **Use personal phone for sensitive communications** | Discuss working conditions, union activities, or personal matters on personal devices on personal data plans | Employer-provided WiFi and devices may be monitored |
| **Review app permissions** | Check what data employer-required apps access on personal devices (location, contacts, photos) | Employer MDM (mobile device management) software may access personal data |
| **Document monitoring practices** | Keep records of what monitoring your employer uses, when it was implemented, and what notice was provided | Documentation supports potential legal claims and collective action |
| **Use encrypted messaging for organizing** | Communicate with coworkers about working conditions using encrypted personal messaging apps (Signal, WhatsApp) on personal devices | End-to-end encryption protects conversations from employer interception |

### Respond to Excessive Monitoring

| Situation | Action | Consideration |
|-----------|--------|--------------|
| Employer installs new monitoring without notice | Ask HR in writing for details about what is monitored and why; reference state notice laws if applicable | Create paper trail; know your state's requirements |
| Employer monitors personal social media | Review your state's social media privacy law; many states prohibit employers from requiring social media passwords or access | 25+ states have social media password protection laws |
| Biometric data collection without consent | In Illinois, file BIPA complaint or contact attorney; in other states, document and raise with HR | Illinois BIPA provides private right of action with $1,000-$5,000 per violation |
| Monitoring feels retaliatory | If monitoring intensifies after protected activity (union organizing, safety complaints, whistleblowing), file NLRB ULP charge or retaliation complaint | Retaliation for protected activity is illegal under NLRA, OSHA, and other laws |
| Off-duty monitoring | Document employer tracking of location, communications, or activities during non-work hours; consult employment attorney | Some states restrict off-duty monitoring; NLRA protects concerted activity |

## Workplace Actions

### Talk to Your Coworkers

| Action | How | Impact |
|--------|-----|--------|
| **Share monitoring concerns** | Discuss what you know about employer monitoring with trusted coworkers; compare experiences | Builds awareness; identifies shared concerns (protected under NLRA Section 7) |
| **Survey monitoring impact** | Organize informal survey of monitoring effects on stress, productivity, and well-being | Creates evidence base for collective action |
| **Identify monitoring allies** | Find coworkers and managers who share concerns about excessive monitoring | Builds internal coalition |

### Collective Action

| Action | How | Impact |
|--------|-----|--------|
| **Petition employer** | Collective petition to management requesting monitoring transparency, notice, and limits | Direct engagement; protected concerted activity under NLRA |
| **Negotiate monitoring limits** | If unionized, include monitoring provisions in contract bargaining; model language available from AFL-CIO | Binding contractual protections |
| **File NLRB charges** | If monitoring chills organizing or is retaliatory, file ULP charges with NLRB | Formal legal process; can result in cease-and-desist orders |
| **Contact worker organizations** | Reach out to Coworker.org, NELP, Warehouse Worker Resource Center for support and strategy | Access to experienced organizers and legal resources |
| **Launch public campaign** | If employer refuses to address monitoring concerns, consider public campaign with worker organization support | Media attention; reputational pressure on employer |

### For Managers and HR Professionals

| Action | How | Impact |
|--------|-----|--------|
| **Conduct monitoring audit** | Review all monitoring currently deployed; evaluate necessity and proportionality | Identify unnecessary monitoring for elimination |
| **Adopt transparency policy** | Implement clear written notice of all monitoring even in states that do not require it | Best practice; builds trust; prepares for likely regulation |
| **Evaluate outcome-based alternatives** | Pilot managing by results rather than activity monitoring for knowledge workers | Research shows better outcomes than surveillance (MIT Sloan, 2023) |
| **Implement data minimization** | Reduce monitoring data collection to what is genuinely needed; shorten retention periods | Reduces risk and compliance burden |
| **Consult workers before implementing monitoring** | Include worker input in monitoring policy development | Increases buy-in; identifies less invasive alternatives |

## Community and Civic Actions

### Legislative Advocacy

| Action | How | Resources |
|--------|-----|-----------|
| **Contact state legislators** | Advocate for state employee monitoring laws; provide worker testimony and research | NCSL contact tool; state legislative websites |
| **Testify at hearings** | Share personal monitoring experiences at state legislative committee hearings | Contact worker organizations for hearing preparation support |
| **Support privacy ballot initiatives** | Where direct democracy allows, support ballot measures for worker privacy protections | State ballot initiative procedures |
| **Contact federal representatives** | Advocate for federal workplace privacy legislation; reference specific bills (Stop Spying Bosses Act, Algorithmic Accountability Act) | Congress.gov; contact forms for representatives and senators |

### Regulatory Engagement

| Action | How | Resources |
|--------|-----|-----------|
| **File FTC complaints** | Report undisclosed monitoring practices to FTC | ftc.gov/complaint |
| **File NLRB charges** | Report surveillance that chills organizing activity | nlrb.gov; regional NLRB offices |
| **Submit comments on regulations** | When NLRB, FTC, or EEOC propose rules on monitoring, submit public comments | Federal Register; regulations.gov |
| **File EEOC complaints** | If monitoring is used in discriminatory ways | eeoc.gov |
| **Contact state AG** | Report violations of state monitoring notice or biometric privacy laws | State AG consumer protection divisions |

### Public Education

| Action | How | Impact |
|--------|-----|--------|
| **Share research and reporting** | Distribute articles and reports on workplace surveillance via social media and community networks | Raises public awareness |
| **Organize community forums** | Host public discussions on workplace privacy with worker organizations and legal experts | Builds local knowledge and coalition |
| **Engage faith communities** | Frame worker dignity and surveillance through religious and moral lens | Expands coalition beyond traditional labor allies |
| **Support investigative journalism** | Provide tips and documentation to journalists covering workplace surveillance | Public accountability through media |

## Professional Actions

### For Attorneys

| Action | How | Impact |
|--------|-----|--------|
| **Take monitoring cases pro bono** | Represent workers in BIPA claims, NLRB charges, and state privacy law cases | Develops precedent; demonstrates viable legal claims |
| **Develop workplace privacy practice** | Build expertise in emerging workplace privacy law | Meets growing demand; influences legal development |
| **Publish legal analysis** | Write law review articles on ECPA reform, AI monitoring, and worker data rights | Shapes legal discourse and judicial thinking |

### For Technology Professionals

| Action | How | Impact |
|--------|-----|--------|
| **Refuse to build invasive tools** | Apply ethical judgment to employment at monitoring software companies | Individual moral stand; industry norm-setting |
| **Build privacy-preserving alternatives** | Develop productivity tools that provide management insights without individual surveillance | Creates market alternatives to invasive monitoring |
| **Audit existing systems** | If employed at company using monitoring, advocate internally for review of necessity and proportionality | Insider advocacy for change |
| **Support tech worker organizing** | Join or support tech worker organizations (Tech Workers Coalition, Alphabet Workers Union) that address surveillance | Collective power within industry |

### For Researchers

| Action | How | Impact |
|--------|-----|--------|
| **Study monitoring effects** | Conduct research on productivity, mental health, and equity effects of workplace monitoring | Builds evidence base for reform |
| **Audit AI monitoring tools for bias** | Test and publish findings on bias in sentiment analysis, productivity scoring, and predictive analytics | Creates accountability; informs regulation |
| **Document worker experiences** | Qualitative research on how monitoring affects workers' daily lives | Humanizes statistical data; provides testimony for advocacy |
