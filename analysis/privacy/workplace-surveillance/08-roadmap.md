# Workplace Surveillance: Roadmap

## Overview

This roadmap sequences workplace privacy reform from immediately achievable actions to long-term structural change. The strategy operates on multiple tracks simultaneously -- regulatory action that does not require Congress, state legislation in receptive jurisdictions, building public awareness and coalition power, and positioning for federal legislation when political conditions allow.

## Phase 1: Foundation Building (Years 1-2)

### Regulatory and Administrative Action

**Objective**: Use existing authority to establish workplace privacy norms without waiting for Congress.

| Action | Timeline | Lead Actor | Dependencies |
|--------|----------|------------|--------------|
| NLRB rulemaking on electronic surveillance and Section 7 rights | Months 1-12 | NLRB | Sympathetic NLRB General Counsel and board majority |
| FTC enforcement actions against undisclosed monitoring | Months 1-6 | FTC | Identification of clear unfairness/deception cases |
| EEOC guidance on AI monitoring and employment discrimination | Months 3-12 | EEOC | Coordination with existing AI hiring guidance |
| OSHA investigation of monitoring-related stress and health effects | Months 6-18 | OSHA | Research demonstrating health impacts |
| DOL guidance on monitoring data use in wage and hour compliance | Months 6-12 | DOL | Coordination with existing enforcement |

**Metrics**:

| Indicator | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| NLRB proposed rule published | Within 12 months |
| FTC enforcement actions filed | 3-5 cases within 18 months |
| EEOC AI monitoring guidance issued | Within 12 months |
| OSHA general duty clause complaints on monitoring stress | 10+ filed within 18 months |

### Coalition Building

**Objective**: Build the coalition necessary to sustain legislative and regulatory campaigns.

| Action | Timeline | Lead Actors |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| Convene workplace privacy coalition (unions, civil liberties, worker centers) | Months 1-3 | AFL-CIO, EFF, CDT, NELP |
| Commission and publish research on monitoring harms | Months 1-12 | Academic researchers, think tanks |
| Document worker stories and testimonials | Ongoing | Coworker.org, NELP, unions |
| Engage sympathetic employers as reform champions | Months 3-12 | Coalition leadership |
| Build media relationships for sustained coverage | Ongoing | Communications staff |

**Metrics**:

| Indicator | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| Coalition member organizations | 50+ within 12 months |
| Research publications on monitoring harms | 5+ peer-reviewed papers or major reports |
| Worker testimonial database | 200+ documented stories |
| Employer partners supporting reform | 10+ named companies |

### Union Contract Campaigns

**Objective**: Negotiate monitoring protections into collective bargaining agreements as proof of concept.

| Action | Timeline | Lead Actors |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| Develop model monitoring contract language | Months 1-6 | AFL-CIO, major unions |
| Prioritize monitoring in upcoming contract negotiations | Months 1-24 | Teamsters, CWA, SEIU, UFCW |
| Win monitoring protections in high-profile contracts | Months 6-24 | Individual unions |
| Publicize contract wins as models | Ongoing | Coalition communications |

## Phase 2: State-Level Wins (Years 2-4)

### Priority State Legislation

**Objective**: Pass comprehensive employee monitoring acts in receptive states to build momentum and demonstrate feasibility.

| State | Bill Type | Viability | Strategy |
|-------|-----------|-----------|----------|
| **California** | Comprehensive monitoring act (notice + consent + limits) | High | Build on CPRA employee provisions; leverage strong labor movement |
| **Illinois** | Expand BIPA to cover additional monitoring; pass comprehensive employee monitoring act | High | Build on BIPA success; strong labor and privacy coalition |
| **New York** | Expand 2022 monitoring notice law to include consent and limitations | High | Build on existing law; strengthen provisions |
| **Washington** | Employee monitoring act | Moderate-High | Strong tech sector workforce concerned about monitoring; labor support |
| **Colorado** | Extend Privacy Act employee provisions to include monitoring specifics | Moderate-High | Build on existing privacy act |
| **Massachusetts** | Employee monitoring transparency and protection act | Moderate | Strong civil liberties tradition; union support |
| **Minnesota** | Employee monitoring act | Moderate | Recent privacy law momentum |

**Timeline**:

| Milestone | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| Bills introduced in 5+ states | Year 2 |
| First comprehensive state monitoring act passed | Year 2-3 |
| 3+ states with comprehensive monitoring laws | Year 3-4 |
| 5+ states with monitoring notice or consent laws | Year 4 |

### State Attorney General Enforcement

**Objective**: Use existing state consumer protection and privacy laws to take enforcement actions against egregious monitoring practices.

| Action | Timeline | Lead Actors |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| Identify AG offices receptive to monitoring enforcement | Months 12-18 | Coalition legal teams |
| File complaints on behalf of workers in states with applicable laws | Months 18-36 | Workers, coalition legal teams |
| Support AG investigations of monitoring software vendors | Months 18-36 | CDT, EFF, ACLU |

## Phase 3: Federal Action (Years 3-6)

### Federal Legislation Track

**Objective**: Pass federal workplace privacy legislation, starting with achievable transparency measures and building toward comprehensive reform.

**Step 1: Employee Monitoring Transparency Act (Years 3-4)**

| Action | Timeline | Lead Actors |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| Draft bill with congressional allies | Months 24-30 | Coalition legislative team, congressional champions |
| Committee hearings featuring worker testimony | Months 30-36 | Senate HELP Committee, House Education and Workforce Committee |
| Mark up and report bill | Months 36-42 | Committee leadership |
| Floor vote and passage | Months 42-48 | Congressional leadership |

**Step 2: AI Workplace Accountability Act (Years 4-5)**

| Action | Timeline | Lead Actors |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| Draft bill building on AI regulation momentum | Months 36-42 | Coalition legislative team |
| Hearings on AI monitoring harms | Months 42-48 | Senate Commerce Committee |
| Markup and passage | Months 48-60 | Congressional leadership |

### Congressional Strategy

| Element | Approach |
|---------|----------|
| **Champion identification** | Senators Cory Booker, Brian Schatz, Ron Wyden; Representatives Bobby Scott, Mark Takano, Jan Schakowsky |
| **Committee strategy** | Multiple committee jurisdictions (HELP, Commerce, Judiciary); need to coordinate or pick lead committee |
| **Bipartisan appeal** | Frame notice requirements as "transparency" (appeals to libertarians); frame AI accountability as protecting workers from Big Tech (appeals to populists) |
| **Industry engagement** | Engage privacy-forward employers as supporters; neutralize opposition from smaller vendors |
| **Grassroots pressure** | Constituent advocacy in swing districts; worker testimony at town halls |

## Phase 4: Comprehensive Reform (Years 5-10)

### Comprehensive Workplace Privacy Act

**Objective**: Enact omnibus federal legislation establishing proportionality standards, consent requirements, data minimization, worker rights, and meaningful enforcement.

| Component | Timeline |
|-----------|----------|
| Build on momentum from transparency and AI bills | Years 5-6 |
| Draft comprehensive bill with stakeholder input | Years 6-7 |
| Legislative campaign for passage | Years 7-10 |
| Implementation and rulemaking | Years 8-12 |

### Institutional Development

| Fund state enforcement of workplace privacy laws | Years 5-10 |
| Create worker privacy complaint mechanism | Years 5-8 |

| Action | Timeline |
|--------|----------|

## Risk Assessment

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|------------|
| NLRB board composition changes with administration | High | High | Pursue statutory protections alongside regulatory action |
| Industry successfully blocks federal legislation | High | High | Maintain state-level strategy as alternative path |
| AI monitoring technology outpaces regulation | High | Moderate | Technology-neutral, principle-based legislation |
| Court challenges to workplace privacy laws | Moderate | High | Build litigation strategy into coalition; careful constitutional drafting |
| Economic downturn shifts priorities | Moderate | Moderate | Frame monitoring reform as cost-saving (reduced turnover, improved engagement) |
| Worker apathy or fatalism | Moderate | Moderate | Sustained public education; compelling worker narratives |
| Weak federal bill preempts stronger state laws | Moderate | Very High | Insist on non-preemption clause in all federal legislation |
