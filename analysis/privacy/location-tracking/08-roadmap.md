# Location Tracking: Roadmap

## Phase 1: Immediate Actions (0-12 Months)

### Build Awareness and Coalitions

**Education**

- Educate the public about the scale of location tracking: 200-600+ data points per phone per day, sold to brokers and government agencies
- Publicize the Gravy Analytics breach (January 2025) and GM/LexisNexis scandal (2024) as concrete examples of location data harm
- Distribute guides on managing location permissions, disabling background tracking, and using privacy-protective tools
- Engage national security experts on the risk of commercial location data exposing military and intelligence personnel

**Coalition Building**

- Unite privacy advocates (EFF, ACLU, EPIC), civil liberties organizations, and consumer groups around location privacy legislation
- Recruit bipartisan congressional champions: Sen. Wyden (D-OR) for Fourth Amendment Is Not For Sale Act; privacy-focused Republicans for warrant requirements
- Engage state AGs who have been active on location privacy enforcement (California, Illinois, New York)
- Connect reproductive rights organizations with privacy advocates on location data near clinics
- Build alliances with immigrant rights organizations affected by ICE/CBP location data purchases
- Coordinate with national security and defense communities on risks of commercial location data tracking of military personnel

### Support Active Efforts

**Federal**

- Advocate for reintroduction and passage of the Fourth Amendment Is Not For Sale Act
- Support FTC enforcement actions against location data brokers (building on Kochava and X-Mode/Outlogic precedents)
- Push for increased appropriations for FTC privacy enforcement
- Encourage FTC rulemaking on location data practices under Section 5 authority
- Support DOD review of commercial location data risks to military personnel

**State**

- Support adoption of Utah-style warrant requirements for law enforcement location data access in additional states
- Push for geofence warrant restrictions modeled on the 4th Circuit *Chatrie* decision
- Advocate for Illinois-style Location Privacy Protection Act adoption in additional states
- Support state AG enforcement actions against location data brokers

### Advocate

- Submit public comments on any FTC location privacy rulemakings
- Testify at state legislative hearings on location privacy bills
- Contact members of Congress about government location data purchases
- Organize constituent meetings with legislators focused on location data stories and documented harms

## Phase 2: Short-Term Goals (1-2 Years)

### Federal Legislative Priorities

**Priority 1: Close the Carpenter Gap**

- Pass federal legislation requiring a warrant for all government access to location data, including purchases of commercial data
- Include emergency exception with post-hoc judicial review within 48 hours
- Apply to all federal agencies (ICE, CBP, FBI, IRS, Secret Service, DIA, and others)
- Target: Introduction in first session, committee passage within 12 months

**Priority 2: Geofence Warrant Reform**

- Enact federal legislation prohibiting or strictly limiting geofence warrants and reverse location searches
- Codify the 4th Circuit's *Chatrie* holding that geofence warrants violate the Fourth Amendment's particularity requirement
- Require anonymization steps and minimization procedures for any permitted reverse location requests
- Target: Introduction within 6 months, passage within 18 months

**Priority 3: Location Data Broker Registration**

- Require all entities selling location data to register with the FTC
- Mandate disclosure of data sources, customer categories, and data retention practices
- Prohibit sale of sensitive location data (health facilities, religious institutions, political events, shelters)
- Target: FTC rulemaking within 12 months; legislative codification within 24 months

### State Legislative Priorities

| Priority | Target States | Model |
|----------|--------------|-------|
| Warrant requirement for location data access | New York, Massachusetts, New Jersey, Oregon, Michigan | Utah Location Data Protection Act (2023) |
| Geofence warrant restrictions | All states without restrictions | New York proposed ban; Utah restrictions; 4th Circuit *Chatrie* |
| Location privacy consent requirements | States without comprehensive privacy laws | Illinois Location Privacy Protection Act (2024) |
| Connected vehicle privacy | California, New York, Illinois | California Privacy Protection Agency proposed rulemaking |

### Enforcement Actions

- Push for FTC to increase location data enforcement actions to 5+ per year
- Support state AG coalition actions against location data brokers
- Encourage litigation challenging government purchases of commercial location data under the Fourth Amendment
- Support private lawsuits under state location privacy laws to build case law

## Phase 3: Medium-Term Goals (2-4 Years)

### Comprehensive Federal Location Privacy Law

**Core legislation:**

- Enact comprehensive federal location data protection law covering all entities that collect, share, or sell location data
- Include: broad location data definition, opt-in consent, data minimization, purpose limitation, retention limits, private right of action
- Establish warrant requirement for all government access (closing the Carpenter gap permanently)
- Prohibit sale of location data without affirmative consent
- Create individual rights: access, deletion, opt-out of sale, portability
- Establish federal floor without preempting stronger state protections

**Target timeline:**

| Milestone | Target Date |
|-----------|-------------|
| Committee hearings | Year 2, first session |
| Committee markup | Year 2, second session |
| Floor votes | Year 3 |
| Enactment | Year 3-4 |
| Compliance deadline (large entities) | 12 months after enactment |
| Compliance deadline (small entities) | 24 months after enactment |

### Connected Vehicle Privacy Legislation

- Enact dedicated connected vehicle privacy legislation
- Require opt-in consent for sharing or selling vehicle location and driving data
- Prohibit automatic data sharing with insurers without specific, separate consent
- Require right to disable non-safety tracking
- Establish ALPR data retention limits (30 days maximum without law enforcement purpose)

### RTB Location Data Reform

- Restrict location precision in real-time bidding to zip-code level without user consent
- Require advertising exchanges to enforce contractual prohibitions on location data aggregation
- Mandate annual audits of RTB location data practices
- Establish retention limits for location data received through advertising auctions

### Location Data Broker Regulation (Full Framework)

- Expand FTC registration to require annual compliance reports
- Mandate affirmative consent for all location data sales
- Require brokers to provide individual access and deletion mechanisms
- Prohibit government purchases from brokers without warrants
- Impose criminal penalties for re-identification of claimed de-identified location data

## Phase 4: Long-Term Goals (4-8 Years)

### Institutional Reform

**Dedicated Privacy Enforcement Authority**

- Establish a federal privacy enforcement division within FTC or a new independent data protection authority with dedicated location privacy expertise
- Fund with adequate resources (target: $150+ million annual budget for all privacy enforcement, with location privacy as a priority area)
- Grant rulemaking, enforcement, and audit authority
- Staff with technical experts who understand location tracking technologies

**International Harmonization**

- Negotiate data adequacy agreements with EU under GDPR framework for location data
- Align US location privacy standards with international best practices
- Participate in global location data governance initiatives
- Facilitate cross-border location data flows for legitimate research with privacy safeguards

### Technology-Forward Protections

- Establish privacy-by-design requirements for location technologies
- Require privacy impact assessments for new location data systems
- Create standards for privacy-preserving location analytics (differential privacy, aggregation with k-anonymity)
- Address emerging technologies: ultra-wideband tracking, indoor positioning systems, AI-powered location inference from non-location signals

### Cultural Shift

- Normalize location privacy as a fundamental right, not a consumer preference
- Integrate location data literacy into digital citizenship education
- Train law enforcement on constitutional standards for location data access
- Establish industry best practices for privacy-respecting location services

## Risk Factors

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|------------|
| Federal legislation stalls due to partisan division | High | Continue state-level action; use FTC rulemaking as interim measure |
| Industry lobbying weakens legislation | High | Build strong public awareness campaign; engage bipartisan champions; use breach scandals for political momentum |
| Federal preemption eliminates stronger state laws | Moderate | Insist on floor-not-ceiling approach in any federal legislation |
| Technology evolves faster than regulation | High | Build technology-neutral definitions into legislation; require periodic regulatory review |
| Supreme Court narrows *Carpenter* rather than expanding | Moderate | Pursue statutory warrant requirements rather than relying solely on constitutional interpretation |
| National security exemptions swallow the rule | Moderate | Require FISA Court oversight for national security location data access; limit exemption scope |
| Enforcement resources remain inadequate | High | Tie enforcement funding to penalty revenue; enable private right of action |
| Industry develops new tracking methods that evade regulation | High | Use technology-neutral definitions covering any data revealing physical location |
