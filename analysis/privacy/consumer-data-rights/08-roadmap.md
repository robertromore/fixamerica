# Consumer Data Rights: Roadmap

## Overview

This roadmap sequences consumer data rights reforms across three phases: immediate executive and state actions (Phase 1), federal legislation and institutional reform (Phase 2), and structural transformation of the data economy (Phase 3). Each phase builds on the previous, creating momentum and political conditions for the next.

## Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Probability | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|------------|
| Federal legislation blocked by industry lobbying | High | Pursue state-level reforms simultaneously; use executive authority |
| Preemption dispute kills federal bill | High | Design federal floor with explicit non-preemption; build CA coalition |
| Private right of action opposition blocks legislation | High | Include reasonable safeguards; separate standalone data broker bill |
| Industry compliance is superficial | Moderate | Strong enforcement; technical auditing capacity; civil penalty authority |
| New technology creates new gaps | High | Technology-neutral drafting; agency rulemaking authority for emerging issues |
| Political leadership change reverses progress | Moderate | Embed reforms in statute (harder to reverse than executive orders); build bipartisan support |
| Small business compliance burden | Moderate | Scaled thresholds; free compliance tools; SBA partnerships |
| International data flow disruptions | Moderate | Negotiate adequacy agreements; build interoperable frameworks |
