# Consumer Data Rights: Current State

## Federal Landscape

### No Comprehensive Federal Privacy Law

The United States remains the only major democracy without a comprehensive federal data privacy law. Federal protection is limited to sector-specific statutes that leave most commercial data collection unregulated.

**Existing Federal Statutes**:

| Statute | Year | Scope | Key Gaps |
|---------|------|-------|----------|
| Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) | 1970 | Credit reports, background checks | Does not cover data brokers outside credit reporting |
| Privacy Act | 1974 | Federal agency records | No private sector coverage |
| Electronic Communications Privacy Act (ECPA) | 1986 | Wiretapping, stored communications | Outdated; weak protections for digital data |
| Video Privacy Protection Act (VPPA) | 1988 | Video rental/streaming records | Narrow scope |
| Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) | 1998 | Children under 13 online | Does not cover teens; limited platform scope |
| Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA) | 1999 | Financial data | Opt-out only; narrow definition of financial institution |
| Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) | 1996 | Health data held by covered entities | Does not cover health apps, wearables, or most tech companies |
| CAN-SPAM Act | 2003 | Commercial email | Weak; preempts stronger state laws |
| Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA) | 1991 | Telemarketing, robocalls | Limited to phone calls and texts |
| FTC Act Section 5 | 1914 | Unfair or deceptive practices | No rulemaking authority for privacy; case-by-case enforcement |

### FTC Enforcement

The Federal Trade Commission is the *de facto* federal privacy regulator but lacks dedicated privacy authority.

**FTC Privacy Capabilities**:

| Capability | Status |
|------------|--------|
| Rulemaking authority | Limited; no general privacy rulemaking power |
| Section 5 enforcement | Available for "unfair or deceptive" practices |
| Civil penalties (first offense) | Not available under Section 5 (requires prior order) |
| Budget (FY 2025) | ~$430 million total (privacy is fraction) |
| Privacy/data security staff | ~100 (estimated) |
| Annual privacy enforcement actions | ~20-30 |

**Notable FTC Actions (2020-2025)**:

| Company | Year | Issue | Outcome |
|---------|------|-------|---------|
| Facebook/Meta | 2019 | Cambridge Analytica, privacy misrepresentations | $5 billion settlement |
| Fortnite/Epic Games | 2022 | COPPA violations, dark patterns | $520 million settlement |
| X-Mode Social (data broker) | 2024 | Selling sensitive location data | Ban on selling sensitive data |
| InMarket | 2024 | Location data without consent | Consent requirements, data deletion |
| Kochava | 2024 | Selling geolocation data near sensitive sites | Settlement with restrictions |

### Failed Federal Legislation

| Bill | Year | Status | Key Feature | Reason for Failure |
|------|------|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| American Data Privacy and Protection Act (ADPPA) | 2022 | Passed committee; no floor vote | Bipartisan, comprehensive | Preemption dispute (CA); private right of action dispute |
| Consumer Online Privacy Rights Act | 2019-2021 | Did not advance | FTC enforcement, data minimization | Partisan disagreement |
| Setting an American Framework to Ensure Data Access, Transparency, and Accountability (SAFE DATA) Act | 2020 | Did not advance | Republican alternative | Weaker than Democratic proposals |
| American Privacy Rights Act (APRA) | 2024 | Passed committee; no floor vote | Updated bipartisan effort | Preemption concerns; leadership disagreements |

### Dark Patterns

Deceptive design practices that undermine consumer choice are widespread.

**Prevalence**:

| Dark Pattern Type | Description | Prevalence |
|-------------------|-------------|------------|
| Pre-checked consent boxes | Consent assumed unless user unchecks | 60%+ of websites (Mathur et al., Princeton, 2019) |
| Difficult opt-out flows | Multiple screens, confirmation dialogs | Standard practice in major platforms |
| Forced action | Requiring data sharing for basic features | Common in mobile apps |
| Confusing language | Jargon-heavy, misleading descriptions | Near-universal in privacy policies |
| Hidden information | Key data practices buried deep in policies | Standard industry practice |
| Social proof manipulation | "Everyone else shares their data" messaging | Growing in apps |

## Trends

- **State law acceleration**: After California's 2018 CCPA, state privacy laws have proliferated rapidly; 19 states enacted laws by 2025 with more advancing
- **Federal stalemate continues**: Despite bipartisan interest (ADPPA 2022, APRA 2024), preemption and private right of action disputes block passage
- **Data broker scrutiny intensifying**: FTC enforcement actions, state registration requirements, and consumer awareness increasing
- **Dark pattern regulation emerging**: FTC and state AGs bringing cases against deceptive design; CPRA includes dark pattern prohibition
- **Universal opt-out mechanisms**: Colorado, Connecticut, and California requiring companies to honor Global Privacy Control (GPC) signals
- **AI and data rights intersection**: Generative AI training on personal data raises new consumer rights questions not addressed by existing frameworks
- **Enforcement gaps widening**: State privacy agencies remain underfunded relative to the scale of the data economy they regulate
