# Data Brokers: Resources

## Overview

This document compiles key research, organizations, data sources, and references for understanding and reforming the data broker industry.

## Academic Research

### Foundational Studies

- Solove, Daniel J. *The Digital Person: Technology and Privacy in the Information Age*. New York: NYU Press, 2004. Foundational analysis of how digital profiling threatens privacy.
- Nissenbaum, Helen. *Privacy in Context: Technology, Policy, and the Integrity of Social Life*. Stanford: Stanford University Press, 2010. Framework for understanding privacy as contextual integrity.
- Zuboff, Shoshana. *The Age of Surveillance Capitalism: The Fight for a Human Future at the New Frontier of Power*. New York: PublicAffairs, 2019. Comprehensive analysis of the surveillance business model.

### Data Broker-Specific Research

- Sherman, Justin. "Data Brokers and the Sale of Americans' Mental Health Data." Duke University Sanford School of Public Policy, 2023. Documented the sale of sensitive mental health data by data brokers with no restrictions.
- Keats Citron, Danielle, and Daniel J. Solove. "Privacy Harms." *Boston University Law Review* 102 (2022): 793-863. Comprehensive taxonomy of privacy harms enabling legal analysis.
- Crain, Matthew. *Profit over Privacy: How Surveillance Advertising Conquered the Internet*. Minneapolis: University of Minnesota Press, 2021. Political economy of the advertising-surveillance complex.

### Government Data Purchases

- Harlan Yu, et al. "American Dragnet: Data-Driven Deportation in the 21st Century." Georgetown Law Center on Privacy and Technology, 2022. Comprehensive FOIA-based investigation of federal agency data purchases.
- Office of the Director of National Intelligence. "Senior Advisory Group Report on Commercially Available Information." Declassified January 2023. Intelligence community's own acknowledgment that purchased data can be as revealing as communications content.
- Thompson, Alex. "The Government Buys Data on Americans. Can Congress Stop It?" *Politico*, March 2024. Overview of legislative efforts to restrict government data purchases.

### Behavioral Economics and Consent

- McDonald, Aleecia M., and Lorrie Faith Cranor. "The Cost of Reading Privacy Policies." *I/S: A Journal of Law and Policy for the Information Society* 4, no. 3 (2008): 543-568. Found reading all privacy policies encountered annually would take 76 workdays.
- Acquisti, Alessandro, Laura Brandimarte, and George Loewenstein. "Privacy and Human Behavior in the Age of Information." *Science* 347, no. 6221 (2015): 509-514. Overview of behavioral economics of privacy decisions.

## Legal References

### Key Court Cases

| Case | Year | Significance |
|------|------|-------------|
| *United States v. Miller*, 425 U.S. 435 | 1976 | Established third-party doctrine for bank records |
| *Smith v. Maryland*, 442 U.S. 735 | 1979 | Extended third-party doctrine to phone metadata |
| *Sorrell v. IMS Health*, 564 U.S. 552 | 2011 | Applied heightened First Amendment scrutiny to data sale restrictions |
| *Carpenter v. United States*, 585 U.S. 296 | 2018 | Required warrant for historical cell-site location data |
| *Spokeo v. Robins*, 578 U.S. 330 | 2016 | Standing requirements for data privacy claims |
| *TransUnion v. Ramirez*, 594 U.S. 413 | 2021 | Narrowed standing for statutory data violations |
| *Rosenbach v. Six Flags*, 2019 IL 123186 | 2019 | No actual injury required under Illinois BIPA |
