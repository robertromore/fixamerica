# Privacy: Roadmap

## Phase 1: Immediate Actions (Now)

### Executive Branch Actions

| Action | Authority | Impact |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Executive order on government data purchases | Presidential authority | Limit agency purchases |
| FTC rulemaking on surveillance advertising | Magnuson-Moss (lengthy, contested) | Industry-wide rules if successful |
| HHS HIPAA guidance clarification | Existing authority | Clarify when apps are covered entities/BAs (expansion requires legislation) |
| CFPB action on financial data | Existing authority | Open banking rules |

### Agency Enforcement

| Action | Target | Effect |
|--------|--------|--------|
| FTC enforcement actions | Major platforms | Deterrence |
| State AG coordinated enforcement | Data brokers | Multiply resources |
| CPRA enforcement | California violators | National impact |

### Congressional Oversight

| Action | Committee | Purpose |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Section 702 oversight hearings | Intelligence, Judiciary | Document abuses |
| Data broker hearings | Commerce | Build record |
| Tech platform hearings | Multiple | Public awareness |

## Phase 3: Medium-Term Goals (3-5 Years)

### Comprehensive Federal Privacy Law

**Essential Elements:**

| Element | Minimum Acceptable |
|---------|-------------------|
| Consumer rights | Access, delete, correct, port |
| Private right of action | With statutory damages |
| Preemption | Floor only (preserve state laws) |
| Enforcement | FTC + State AGs + Private |
| Scope | All personal data |

**Political Path:**

| Scenario | Likelihood | Strategy |
|----------|------------|----------|
| Democratic trifecta | Moderate | Push strong bill |
| Divided government | Common | Narrow bipartisan bills |
| State pressure | Ongoing | Industry seeks federal |
| Major scandal | Possible | Window of opportunity |

### Surveillance Reform

**Section 702 Next Reauthorization:**

| Reform Goal | Strategy |
|-------------|----------|
| Warrant requirement for US queries | Bipartisan coalition |
| Shorter sunset | More frequent review |
| Enhanced transparency | Declassification |
| FISA Court reform | Outside advocates |

### Constitutional Litigation

| Case Type | Goal |
|-----------|------|
| *Carpenter* extension | More data types protected |
| Data purchase cases | Government purchases as searches |
| State constitution cases | Stronger state protections |

### Dedicated Privacy Agency

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| Legislative authorization | In comprehensive bill |
| Funding | Dedicated, adequate |
| Independence | Structure for autonomy |
| Expertise | Technical capacity |

## Phase 4: Long-Term Transformation (5+ Years)

### Constitutional Amendment (If Needed)

**Draft Language:**

> "The right of the people to be secure in their persons, papers, communications, and personal information against unreasonable collection, retention, use, and disclosure shall not be violated."

**Path:**

| Step | Difficulty |
|------|------------|
| Congressional proposal | 2/3 both houses |
| State ratification | 3/4 of states |
| Alternative: Convention | Untested, risky |

### Structural Economic Change

| Goal | Mechanism |
|------|-----------|
| End surveillance capitalism | Advertising alternatives |
| Data minimization norm | Liability for collection |
| Privacy-by-design | Regulatory requirements |
| Decentralized technology | Technical alternatives |

### International Harmonization

| Goal | Mechanism |
|------|-----------|
| US-EU adequacy | Meet GDPR standards |
| Global privacy framework | International agreement |
| Cross-border enforcement | Mutual recognition |

## Risks and Contingencies

### Legislative Risks

| Risk | Probability | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|------------|
| Preemption poison pill | High | Refuse to accept |
| Weak enforcement | High | Insist on private right |
| Exemptions swallow rule | Moderate | Narrow exemptions |
| Industry capture | Moderate | Advocacy pressure |

### Judicial Risks

| Risk | Probability | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|------------|
| *Carpenter* limited | Moderate | Legislative backup |
| State laws challenged | Moderate | Strong drafting |
| Federal preemption rulings | Low | Anti-preemption language |

### Political Risks

| Risk | Probability | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|------------|
| Terrorism event | Moderate | Maintain reform coalition |
| Economic downturn | Moderate | Jobs narrative ready |
| Tech backlash reversal | Low | Sustained advocacy |

### Contingency Plans

| Scenario | Contingency |
|----------|-------------|

| Supreme Court hostile | State constitutions |
| Industry captures agency | Private enforcement emphasis |
| Public interest wanes | Crisis response preparation |
