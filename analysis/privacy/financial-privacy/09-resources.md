# Financial Privacy: Resources

## Overview

This document compiles key research, organizations, data sources, and references for understanding and reforming financial privacy in the United States. It covers the full scope of financial privacy -- banking, credit reporting, government financial surveillance, fintech, cryptocurrency, and financial data brokerage.

## Academic Research

### Foundational Studies

- Solove, Daniel J. *The Digital Person: Technology and Privacy in the Information Age*. New York: NYU Press, 2004. Foundational analysis of how digital profiling, including financial profiling, threatens privacy.
- Nissenbaum, Helen. *Privacy in Context: Technology, Policy, and the Integrity of Social Life*. Stanford: Stanford University Press, 2010. Contextual integrity framework applicable to financial data sharing.
- Zuboff, Shoshana. *The Age of Surveillance Capitalism: The Fight for a Human Future at the New Frontier of Power*. New York: PublicAffairs, 2019. Comprehensive analysis of data-driven business models, including financial data exploitation.

### Financial Privacy-Specific Research

- Regan, Priscilla M. *Legislating Privacy: Technology, Social Values, and Public Policy*. Chapel Hill: University of North Carolina Press, 1995. History of financial privacy legislation including BSA and RFPA.
- Swire, Peter P. "Financial Privacy and the Theory of High-Tech Government Surveillance." *Washington University Law Quarterly* 77, no. 2 (1999): 461-542. Analysis of government financial surveillance systems.
- Solove, Daniel J. "Digital Dossiers and the Dissipation of Fourth Amendment Privacy." *Southern California Law Review* 75 (2002): 1083-1167. Analysis of how financial data aggregation erodes Fourth Amendment protections.
- Citron, Danielle Keats, and Daniel J. Solove. "Privacy Harms." *Boston University Law Review* 102 (2022): 793-863. Comprehensive taxonomy of privacy harms applicable to financial privacy.

### Credit Reporting Research

- Wu, Chi Chi, and Birny Birnbaum. "Credit Scoring and Insurance: Costing Consumers Billions and Perpetuating the Racial Wealth Gap." National Consumer Law Center and Center for Economic Justice, 2007. Analysis of credit scoring in insurance.
- Bernerth, Jeremy B., et al. "An Empirical Investigation of Dispositional Antecedents and Performance-Related Outcomes of Credit Scores." *Journal of Applied Psychology* 97, no. 2 (2012): 469-478. Study finding weak relationship between credit scores and job performance.
- Brevoort, Kenneth P., Philipp Grimm, and Michelle Kambara. "Data Point: Credit Invisibles." Consumer Financial Protection Bureau, 2015. Study documenting 26 million credit-invisible Americans.

### Government Financial Surveillance Research

- Greenberg, Michael, et al. "Terrorist Financing." RAND Corporation, 2009. Analysis of BSA/AML effectiveness in counter-terrorism.
- Cassella, Stefan D. "The Bank Secrecy Act and the 'War on Terrorism.'" *Journal of Money Laundering Control* 7, no. 2 (2003): 133-148. Assessment of BSA effectiveness post-9/11.
- Findley, Michael G., Daniel L. Nielson, and Jason C. Sharman. *Global Shell Games: Experiments in Transnational Relations, Crime, and Terrorism*. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press, 2014. Research demonstrating limits of mass financial reporting.

### Behavioral Economics and Consent

- McDonald, Aleecia M., and Lorrie Faith Cranor. "The Cost of Reading Privacy Policies." *I/S: A Journal of Law and Policy for the Information Society* 4, no. 3 (2008): 543-568. Found reading all privacy policies encountered annually would take 76 workdays.
- Acquisti, Alessandro, Laura Brandimarte, and George Loewenstein. "Privacy and Human Behavior in the Age of Information." *Science* 347, no. 6221 (2015): 509-514. Overview of behavioral economics demonstrating opt-out model failures.
- Thaler, Richard H., and Cass R. Sunstein. *Nudge: Improving Decisions about Health, Wealth, and Happiness*. New Haven: Yale University Press, 2008. Behavioral economics framework explaining why default settings drive 90%+ of behavior.

## Legal References

### Key Court Cases

| Case | Year | Significance |
|------|------|-------------|
| *United States v. Miller*, 425 U.S. 435 | 1976 | Established third-party doctrine for financial records |
| *California Bankers Association v. Shultz*, 416 U.S. 21 | 1974 | Upheld BSA against Fourth Amendment challenge |
| *Carpenter v. United States*, 585 U.S. 296 | 2018 | Created exception to third-party doctrine for cell-site data; declined to address financial records |
| *Spokeo v. Robins*, 578 U.S. 330 | 2016 | Standing requirements for data privacy claims including credit reporting |
| *TransUnion v. Ramirez*, 594 U.S. 413 | 2021 | Narrowed standing for FCRA class actions |
| *CFPB v. Community Financial Services Association*, 601 U.S. 416 | 2024 | Upheld CFPB funding mechanism |
| *National Small Business United v. Yellen* | 2024 | Found Corporate Transparency Act unconstitutional (on appeal) |
