# Psychedelics Policy: Resources

This section provides a curated list of key research, reports, and sources on psychedelics policy, clinical evidence, and regulatory frameworks.

## Academic Research

- Goodwin, G.M., et al. "Single-Dose Psilocybin for a Treatment-Resistant Episode of Major Depression." *New England Journal of Medicine* 387 (2022): 1637-1648. <https://doi.org/10.1056/NEJMoa2206443>
- Davis, A.K., et al. "Effects of Psilocybin-Assisted Therapy on Major Depressive Disorder: A Randomized Clinical Trial." *JAMA Psychiatry* 78, no. 5 (2021): 481-489. <https://doi.org/10.1001/jamapsychiatry.2020.3285>
- Raison, C.L., et al. "Single-Dose Psilocybin Treatment for Major Depressive Disorder: A Randomized Clinical Trial." *JAMA* 330, no. 9 (2023): 843-853. <https://doi.org/10.1001/jama.2023.14530>
- Griffiths, R.R., et al. "Psilocybin Produces Substantial and Sustained Decreases in Depression and Anxiety in Patients with Life-Threatening Cancer." *Journal of Psychopharmacology* 30, no. 12 (2016): 1181-1197. <https://doi.org/10.1177/0269881116675513>
- Bogenschutz, M.P., et al. "Percentage of Heavy Drinking Days Following Psilocybin-Assisted Psychotherapy vs Placebo in the Treatment of Adult Patients With Alcohol Use Disorder." *JAMA Psychiatry* 79, no. 10 (2022): 953-962. <https://doi.org/10.1001/jamapsychiatry.2022.2096>
- Johnson, M.W., et al. "Pilot Study of the 5-HT2AR Agonist Psilocybin in the Treatment of Tobacco Addiction." *Journal of Psychopharmacology* 28, no. 11 (2014): 983-992. <https://doi.org/10.1177/0269881114548296>
- Griffiths, R.R., et al. "Psilocybin Can Occasion Mystical-Type Experiences Having Substantial and Sustained Personal Meaning and Spiritual Significance." *Psychopharmacology* 187, no. 3 (2006): 268-283. <https://doi.org/10.1007/s00213-006-0457-5>

### Peer-Reviewed Studies -- MDMA

- Mitchell, J.M., et al. "MDMA-Assisted Therapy for Severe PTSD: A Randomized, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled Phase 3 Study." *Nature Medicine* 27 (2021): 1025-1033. <https://doi.org/10.1038/s41591-021-01336-3>
- Mitchell, J.M., et al. "MDMA-Assisted Therapy for Moderate to Severe PTSD: A Randomized, Placebo-Controlled Phase 3 Trial." *Nature Medicine* 29 (2023): 2473-2480. <https://doi.org/10.1038/s41591-023-02565-4>

### Peer-Reviewed Studies -- LSD and Other Compounds

### Peer-Reviewed Studies -- Ketamine/Esketamine

- Wilkinson, S.T., et al. "The Effect of a Single Dose of Intravenous Ketamine on Suicidal Ideation: A Systematic Review and Individual Participant Data Meta-Analysis." *American Journal of Psychiatry* 175, no. 2 (2018): 150-158.

### Peer-Reviewed Studies -- Safety and Adverse Events

- Schlag, A.K., et al. "Adverse Effects of Psychedelics: From Anecdotes and Misinformation to Systematic Science." *Journal of Psychopharmacology* 36, no. 3 (2022): 258-272.
- Halpern, J.H., and Pope, H.G. "Hallucinogen Persisting Perception Disorder: What Do We Know After 50 Years?" *Drug and Alcohol Dependence* 69, no. 2 (2003): 109-119.
- Nutt, D.J., King, L.A., and Phillips, L.D. "Drug Harms in the UK: A Multicriteria Decision Analysis." *Lancet* 376 (2010): 1558-1565. - Foundational drug harm ranking placing psychedelics among lowest-harm substances

### Reviews and Meta-Analyses

- Nutt, D.J., et al. "Effects of Schedule I Drug Laws on Neuroscience Research and Treatment Innovation." *Nature Reviews Neuroscience* 14 (2013): 577-585. - Foundational analysis of how scheduling impedes research
- Carhart-Harris, R.L., and Goodwin, G.M. "The Therapeutic Potential of Psychedelic Drugs: Past, Present, and Future." *Neuropsychopharmacology* 42 (2017): 2105-2113.
- Reiff, C.M., et al. "Psychedelics and Psychedelic-Assisted Psychotherapy." *American Journal of Psychiatry* 177, no. 5 (2020): 391-410. - Comprehensive review in leading psychiatry journal
- Krebs, T.S., and Johansen, P.O. "Lysergic Acid Diethylamide (LSD) for Alcoholism: Meta-Analysis of Randomized Controlled Trials." *Journal of Psychopharmacology* 26, no. 7 (2012): 994-1002. - Meta-analysis of historical LSD alcoholism trials
- Johnson, M.W., et al. "Classic Psychedelics: An Integrative Review of Epidemiology, Therapeutics, Mystical Experience, and Brain Network Function." *Pharmacology & Therapeutics* 197 (2019): 83-102. - Comprehensive review of psychedelic pharmacology, epidemiology, and neuroscience
- Michaels, T.I., et al. "Inclusion of People of Color in Psychedelic-Assisted Psychotherapy: A Review of the Literature." *BMC Psychiatry* 18, no. 1 (2018): 245. - Analysis of racial diversity gaps in psychedelic clinical trials

### Neuroscience and Mechanism Studies

- Carhart-Harris, R.L., et al. "Neural Correlates of the Psychedelic State as Determined by fMRI Studies with Psilocybin." *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences* 109, no. 6 (2012): 2138-2143. - First modern neuroimaging of psilocybin brain effects
- Carhart-Harris, R.L. "The Entropic Brain: A Theory of Conscious States Informed by Neuroimaging Research with Psychedelic Drugs." *Frontiers in Human Neuroscience* 8 (2014): 20.
- Muthukumaraswamy, S.D., et al. "Broadband Cortical Desynchronization Underlies the Human Psychedelic State." *Journal of Neuroscience* 33, no. 38 (2013): 15171-15183.

## Books

- Pollan, Michael. *How to Change Your Mind: What the New Science of Psychedelics Teaches Us About Consciousness, Dying, Addiction, Depression, and Transcendence*. Penguin Press, 2018. - Bestselling book that brought psychedelic research to mainstream audiences
- Shroder, Tom. *Acid Test: LSD, Ecstasy, and the Power to Heal*. Blue Rider Press, 2014. - History of MDMA therapy and MAPS
- Doblin, Rick. *Psychedelic Medicine: The Healing Power of LSD, MDMA, Psilocybin, and Ayahuasca*. Park Street Press, 2022. - By the founder of MAPS
- Fadiman, James. *The Psychedelic Explorer's Guide*. Park Street Press, 2011. - Practical and historical guide
- Sessa, Ben. *The Psychedelic Renaissance: Reassessing the Role of Psychedelic Drugs in 21st Century Psychiatry and Society*. Muswell Hill Press, 2017.
- Grob, Charles S., and Grinspoon, Lester, eds. *Hallucinogens: A Reader*. TarcherPerigee, 2002. - Collection of foundational texts
- Lee, Martin A., and Shlain, Bruce. *Acid Dreams: The Complete Social History of LSD*. Grove Press, 1985. - Definitive history of LSD including MKUltra
- Strassman, Rick. *DMT: The Spirit Molecule*. Park Street Press, 2000. - Account of the first modern FDA-approved psychedelic study
- Shulgin, Alexander, and Shulgin, Ann. *PiHKAL: A Chemical Love Story*. Transform Press, 1991. - Foundational pharmacological reference on phenethylamines including MDMA and mescaline
- Shulgin, Alexander, and Shulgin, Ann. *TiHKAL: The Continuation*. Transform Press, 1997. - Companion volume on tryptamines including psilocybin, DMT, and LSD
- Stamets, Paul. *Mycelium Running: How Mushrooms Can Help Save the World*. Ten Speed Press, 2005. - Mycology reference including psilocybin mushroom cultivation
- Richards, William A. *Sacred Knowledge: Psychedelics and Religious Experiences*. Columbia University Press, 2015. - Academic treatment of psychedelic mystical experiences
- Davis, Wade. *One River: Explorations and Discoveries in the Amazon Rain Forest*. Simon & Schuster, 1996. - Ethnobotanical account of ayahuasca and other plant medicines
- Hofmann, Albert. *LSD: My Problem Child*. McGraw-Hill, 1980. - Memoir by the discoverer of LSD
- Grof, Stanislav. *Realms of the Human Unconscious: Observations from LSD Research*. Viking Press, 1975. - Foundational text on psychedelic therapy from the first wave of research

## Legal Resources

### Case Law

- *Gonzales v. O Centro Espirita Beneficente Uniao do Vegetal*, 546 U.S. 418 (2006). - Unanimous Supreme Court ruling protecting religious ayahuasca use under RFRA
- *Church of the Holy Light of the Queen v. Mukasey*, 615 F.Supp.2d 1210 (D. Or. 2009). - Federal court injunction protecting Santo Daime ayahuasca use
- *Employment Division v. Smith*, 494 U.S. 872 (1990). - Supreme Court ruling on religious use of peyote (later superseded by RFRA)
- *MDMA Scheduling Hearing*, DEA Administrative Law Judge Francis Young (1985). - ALJ recommended Schedule III; DEA Administrator overruled to Schedule I

### Statutory References

- 21 U.S.C. Section 812 - Controlled Substances Act schedules (Schedule I definition and substance listing)
- 21 U.S.C. Section 811 - Attorney General scheduling authority and procedures
- 42 U.S.C. Section 1996a - American Indian Religious Freedom Act Amendments (peyote exemption)
- 42 U.S.C. Section 2000bb - Religious Freedom Restoration Act (basis for ayahuasca decisions)
- Oregon Revised Statutes Chapter 475A - Oregon Psilocybin Services Act
- Colorado Revised Statutes Title 12, Article 170 - Natural Medicine Health Act
