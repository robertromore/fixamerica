# International Drug Policy: Resources

This section provides a curated list of key research, reports, data sources, and organizations working on international drug policy and reform.

---

## Academic Research

### Peer-Reviewed Studies

- Csete, J., et al. "Public Health and International Drug Policy." *The Lancet* 387, no. 10026 (2016): 1427-1480. <https://doi.org/10.1016/S0140-6736(16)00619-X> - Comprehensive review by the Johns Hopkins-Lancet Commission on Drug Policy and Health; foundational evidence base for health-oriented reform.
- Greenwald, G. "Drug Decriminalization in Portugal: Lessons for Creating Fair and Successful Drug Policies." *Cato Institute White Paper* (2009). <https://www.cato.org/white-paper/drug-decriminalization-portugal> - Early influential analysis of Portugal's decriminalization outcomes.
- Hughes, C.E., & Stevens, A. "What Can We Learn from the Portuguese Decriminalization of Illicit Drugs?" *British Journal of Criminology* 50, no. 6 (2010): 999-1022.
- Werb, D., et al. "Effect of Drug Law Enforcement on Drug Market Violence: A Systematic Review." *International Journal of Drug Policy* 22, no. 2 (2011): 87-94. - Meta-analysis finding that drug law enforcement increases violence.
- Mejia, D., & Restrepo, P. "The Economics of the War on Illegal Drug Production and Trafficking." *Journal of Economic Behavior & Organization* 126 (2016): 255-275. - Economic analysis of the balloon effect and supply-side failure.
- Kilmer, B., et al. "What America's Users Spend on Illegal Drugs, 2006-2016." *RAND Corporation* (2019). <https://www.rand.org/pubs/research_reports/RR3140.html>
- Caulkins, J.P., Reuter, P., & Coulson, C. "Basing Drug Scheduling Decisions on Scientific Ranking of Harmfulness." *Addiction* 106, no. 11 (2011): 1886-1890.

### Working Papers and White Papers

- LSE Expert Group on the Economics of Drug Policy. "Ending the Drug Wars." London School of Economics. 2014. <https://www.lse.ac.uk/ideas/projects/drug-policy> - Expert analysis of the economic failures of the War on Drugs.
- Rydell, C.P., & Everingham, S.S. "Controlling Cocaine: Supply Versus Demand Programs." RAND Drug Policy Research Center. 1994. <https://www.rand.org/pubs/monograph_reports/MR331.html> - Foundational RAND study showing treatment is 7x more cost-effective than enforcement.
- Walsh, J.M. "Are We There Yet? Measuring Progress in the U.S. War on Drugs in Latin America." WOLA Briefing Paper. 2004.
- Bewley-Taylor, D. "Towards Revision of the UN Drug Control Conventions: Harnessing Like-Mindedness." *International Journal of Drug Policy* 24, no. 1 (2013): 60-68.

## Government Reports and Data

### Federal Agencies

- Office of National Drug Control Policy (ONDCP). "National Drug Control Strategy." Annual. <https://www.whitehouse.gov/ondcp/> - Annual U.S. drug policy strategy including international programs and budget.
- ONDCP. "National Drug Control Budget." FY2025. <https://www.whitehouse.gov/ondcp/> - Detailed breakdowns of international and domestic drug spending.
- U.S. State Department Bureau of International Narcotics and Law Enforcement Affairs (INL). "International Narcotics Control Strategy Report (INCSR)." Annual. <https://www.state.gov/international-narcotics-control-strategy-reports/> - Comprehensive country-by-country assessment of drug production, trafficking, and control efforts.

### Congressional Reports

- Congressional Research Service. "International Drug Control Policy: Background and U.S. Responses." CRS Report R44599. <https://crsreports.congress.gov/>
- Congressional Research Service. "Colombia: Background and U.S. Relations." CRS Report R43813. <https://crsreports.congress.gov/>
- Congressional Research Service. "Mexico: Evolution of the Merida Initiative." CRS Report R41349. <https://crsreports.congress.gov/>
- Government Accountability Office. "Drug Control: Certain DOD and USAID-Funded Programs Need Better Performance Data." GAO-20-474 (2020). <https://www.gao.gov/>
- Special Inspector General for Afghanistan Reconstruction (SIGAR). "Counternarcotics: Lessons from the U.S. Experience in Afghanistan." SIGAR 18-52-LL (2018). <https://www.sigar.mil/> - Devastating assessment of $9 billion failure.
- Western Hemisphere Drug Policy Commission. "Report to Congress." 2020. - Bipartisan commission recommending shift to health-based approaches.

### Agency Data Portals

- UNODC. World Drug Report Data Portal. <https://dataunodc.un.org/> - Global drug production, seizure, and use statistics.
- EMCDDA/EUDA. European Drug Report. <https://www.emcdda.europa.eu/> - Comprehensive European drug monitoring data.
- CBP. Drug Seizure Statistics. <https://www.cbp.gov/newsroom/stats/drug-seizure-statistics> - U.S. border drug seizure data by fiscal year.

## Books

- Bewley-Taylor, D.R. *International Drug Control: Consensus Fractured*. Cambridge University Press, 2012. - Definitive history of the international drug control regime and its fracturing.
- Csete, J. *From the Mountaintops: What the World Can Learn from Drug Policy Change in Switzerland*. Open Society Foundations, 2010. - Analysis of Swiss harm reduction innovation.
- Musto, D.F. *The American Disease: Origins of Narcotic Control*. Oxford University Press, 1999. - Classic history of U.S. drug policy and its racial origins.
- Youngers, C.A., & Rosin, E. *Drugs and Democracy in Latin America: The Impact of U.S. Policy*. Lynne Rienner Publishers, 2005. - Comprehensive examination of U.S. drug policy impacts in Latin America.

- Isacson, A. *The War on Drugs in the Western Hemisphere*. Washington Office on Latin America, 2005. - Policy analysis of hemispheric drug control.
- Mares, D.R. *Drug Wars and Coffeehouses: The Political Economy of the International Drug Trade*. CQ Press, 2006.
- Rolles, S. *After the War on Drugs: Blueprint for Regulation*. Transform Drug Policy Foundation, 2009. - Detailed regulatory framework proposals.

## Legal Resources

### Case Law

- *Gonzales v. Raich*, 545 U.S. 1 (2005) - Upheld federal authority to prohibit marijuana under Commerce Clause despite state legalization; key to federal-state drug policy conflict.
- *ICJ Advisory Opinion on the Legality of Nuclear Weapons* (1996) - Established principles of treaty interpretation relevant to drug convention flexibility debates.
- *Bolivia v. INCB* (2012-2013) - Bolivia's withdrawal and re-accession to the 1961 Convention with coca reservation; precedent for treaty modification.

### Statutory References

- 21 U.S.C. Ch. 13 (Controlled Substances Act) - Domestic scheduling system that parallels international convention schedules.
- 22 U.S.C. Section 2291-2291k (International Narcotics Control, Foreign Assistance Act) - Authorizes U.S. international drug programs.
- Single Convention on Narcotic Drugs, 1961 (as amended by 1972 Protocol) - Foundational international drug control treaty.
- Convention on Psychotropic Substances, 1971 - Controls synthetic and psychotropic drugs.
- Convention against Illicit Traffic in Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances, 1988 - Enforcement-oriented treaty.
