## Legal Resources

### Case Law

- *Skinner v. Railway Labor Executives' Association*, 489 U.S. 602 (1989). - Upheld post-accident drug testing of railroad employees under the Fourth Amendment "special needs" exception.
- *National Treasury Employees Union v. Von Raab*, 489 U.S. 656 (1989). - Upheld suspicionless testing of U.S. Customs employees.
- *Vernonia School District 47J v. Acton*, 515 U.S. 646 (1995). - Upheld random drug testing of student athletes.
- *Chandler v. Miller*, 520 U.S. 305 (1997). - Struck down Georgia's requirement that candidates for state office pass a drug test; found the interest was "symbolic, not special."
- *Board of Education v. Earls*, 536 U.S. 822 (2002). - Extended student drug testing to all extracurricular activities.
- *Lebron v. Secretary, Florida DCF*, 710 F.3d 1202 (11th Cir. 2013). - Struck down suspicionless drug testing of TANF applicants as unconstitutional under the Fourth Amendment.
- *Coats v. Dish Network*, 350 P.3d 849 (Colo. 2015). - Held that Colorado's lawful off-duty activities statute did not protect medical marijuana use from employer termination.
- *Marchwinski v. Howard*, 309 F.3d 330 (6th Cir. 2002). - Struck down Michigan's suspicionless welfare drug testing program.

### Statutory References

- 21 U.S.C. Section 863 - Federal drug paraphernalia statute (relevant to drug checking legalization)
