## Government Reports and Data

### Federal Agencies

- Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. "Alcohol-Related Disease Impact (ARDI) Application." Updated annually. <https://www.cdc.gov/alcohol/ardi/alcohol-related-icd-codes.html> - Primary data source for alcohol-attributable deaths
- National Highway Traffic Safety Administration. "Fatality Analysis Reporting System (FARS)." Updated annually. <https://www.nhtsa.gov/research-data/fatality-analysis-reporting-system-fars> - Comprehensive DUI crash and fatality data
- Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration. "National Survey on Drug Use and Health (NSDUH)." Annual reports. <https://www.samhsa.gov/data/data-we-collect/nsduh-national-survey-drug-use-and-health> - Consumption patterns, AUD prevalence, treatment data
- National Institute on Alcohol Abuse and Alcoholism. "Alcohol Epidemiologic Data System (AEDS) Surveillance Reports." Biennial. <https://www.niaaa.nih.gov/publications/surveillance-reports> - Per capita consumption, alcohol-related mortality trends
- Federal Trade Commission. "Self-Regulation in the Alcohol Industry: Report of the Federal Trade Commission." 2022. <https://www.ftc.gov/reports/self-regulation-alcohol-industry-report-federal-trade-commission>
- Alcohol and Tobacco Tax and Trade Bureau (TTB). "Statistical Reports." Annual. <https://www.ttb.gov/statistics> - Production, revenue, and regulatory compliance data

### Congressional Reports

- Congressional Research Service. "Alcohol Excise Taxes: Current Law and Issues for Congress." Report R45558 (Updated 2023). <https://crsreports.congress.gov>
- Congressional Budget Office. "Revenue Effects of Extending the Craft Beverage Modernization and Tax Reform Act." Score (2020). <https://www.cbo.gov>
- Government Accountability Office. "Underage Drinking: Federal Strategy Effort Is Under Way, But Coordination and Accountability Need Strengthening." GAO-15-63 (2015). <https://www.gao.gov/products/gao-15-63>

### Agency Data Portals

- CDC WONDER. Multiple Cause of Death Database. <https://wonder.cdc.gov> - Searchable mortality data including alcohol-related causes
- NIAAA. Alcohol Policy Information System (APIS). <https://alcoholpolicy.niaaa.nih.gov> - Comprehensive database of state-by-state alcohol laws
- SAMHSA. Treatment Episode Data Set (TEDS). <https://www.samhsa.gov/data/data-we-collect/teds-treatment-episode-data-set> - Treatment admission and discharge data

## Legal Resources

### Case Law

- *South Dakota v. Dole*, 483 U.S. 203 (1987). - Upheld Congressional authority to condition highway funds on MLDA 21 under the Spending Clause
- *Michigan Department of State Police v. Sitz*, 496 U.S. 444 (1990). - Upheld sobriety checkpoints as reasonable under the Fourth Amendment
- *Central Hudson Gas & Electric Corp. v. Public Service Commission*, 447 U.S. 557 (1980). - Established four-part test for commercial speech regulation (critical for advertising restrictions)
- *Zauderer v. Office of Disciplinary Counsel*, 471 U.S. 626 (1985). - Established lower standard of review for compelled factual disclosure in commercial context (relevant to health labeling)
- *44 Liquormart, Inc. v. Rhode Island*, 517 U.S. 484 (1996). - Struck down state ban on alcohol price advertising; limits on content-based advertising restrictions
- *Granholm v. Heald*, 544 U.S. 460 (2005). - Held that states cannot discriminate between in-state and out-of-state wineries under the Commerce Clause, even under the Twenty-first Amendment
- *Tennessee Wine and Spirits Retailers Association v. Thomas*, 588 U.S. 504 (2019). - Held that the Twenty-first Amendment does not authorize states to impose durational residency requirements on liquor store licensees

### Statutory References

- 23 U.S.C. Section 158 - National Minimum Drinking Age Act (federal highway funding condition)
- 26 U.S.C. Sections 5001, 5041, 5051 - Federal alcohol excise tax rates
- 27 U.S.C. Sections 201-211 - Federal Alcohol Administration Act (labeling, advertising, trade practices)
- 27 U.S.C. Section 215 - Alcoholic Beverage Labeling Act of 1988 (warning labels)
- 42 U.S.C. Section 290bb-25 - SAMHSA substance abuse prevention and treatment programs
- U.S. Const. amend. XXI - Repeal of Prohibition; state regulatory authority
