## Books

- Mazzucato, M. *The Entrepreneurial State: Debunking Public vs. Private Sector Myths*. Anthem Press, 2013. - Argues that public investment drives innovation, including in pharmaceuticals
- Angell, M. *The Truth About the Drug Companies: How They Deceive Us and What to Do About It*. Random House, 2004. - Former NEJM editor's critique of pharmaceutical industry pricing and marketing
- Posner, E. & Weyl, E.G. *Radical Markets: Uprooting Capitalism and Democracy for a Just Society*. Princeton University Press, 2018. - Includes analysis of patent reform and pharmaceutical pricing
- Emanuel, E.J. *The Trillion Dollar Revolution: How the Affordable Care Act Transformed Politics, Law, and Health Care in America*. PublicAffairs, 2020. - Includes chapters on drug pricing provisions in the ACA
- Sood, N., & Shih, T. *The Future of Drug Pricing*. Cambridge University Press, 2024. - Comprehensive academic analysis of drug pricing mechanisms and reform options
- Feldman, R. *Drug Wars: How Big Pharma Raises Prices and Keeps Generics Off the Market*. Cambridge University Press, 2017. - Detailed analysis of pharmaceutical patent strategies, pay-for-delay, and market manipulation
- Amin, T. & Kesselheim, A.S. *Overpatented, Overpriced: How Excessive Pharmaceutical Patenting Is Extending Monopolies and Driving Up Drug Prices*. I-MAK, 2018. - Analysis of patent thickets for top-selling drugs
- Luo, J. *Expensive People: How a Culture of Health Care Overutilization Costs Us All*. Yale University Press, 2024. - Examination of drug pricing within the broader healthcare cost crisis
- Roy, V. & King, L. *The Pharmaceutical Industry and Global Health*. Palgrave Macmillan, 2023. - International comparative analysis of pharmaceutical pricing systems

## Legal Resources

### Case Law

- *FTC v. Actavis, Inc.*, 570 U.S. 136 (2013) - Supreme Court ruled that pay-for-delay settlements can violate antitrust law under rule of reason analysis
- *Daraprim Antitrust Litigation*, No. 20-cv-0706 (S.D.N.Y.) - FTC case against Vyera Pharmaceuticals for maintaining monopoly through anticompetitive distribution restrictions
- *Bristol-Myers Squibb Co. v. Becerra*, No. 23-cv-3335 (D.D.C. 2024) - Constitutional challenge to IRA drug price negotiation (rejected by district court)
- *Merck & Co. v. Becerra*, No. 23-cv-1615 (D.D.C. 2024) - IRA constitutional challenge on Fifth Amendment grounds (rejected by district court)
- *Novo Nordisk v. Becerra*, No. 23-cv-20814 (D.N.J. 2024) - IRA constitutional challenge (rejected by district court)
- *AstraZeneca v. Becerra*, No. 23-cv-931 (D. Del. 2024) - IRA constitutional challenge on First and Fifth Amendment grounds (rejected by district court)
- *PhRMA v. Becerra*, No. 23-cv-1323 (D.D.C. 2024) - Trade association challenge to IRA on behalf of industry (rejected by district court)
- *Sanofi v. HRSA*, No. 21-cv-634 (D.N.J.) - Manufacturer challenge to HRSA 340B contract pharmacy enforcement
- *AbbVie v. FTC* (various) - Challenges to FTC investigation of Humira patent settlement practices
- *Rutledge v. Pharmaceutical Care Management Assn.*, 592 U.S. ___ (2020) - Supreme Court upheld state PBM regulation against ERISA preemption challenge, enabling state-level PBM reform

### Statutory References

- 42 U.S.C. Section 1395w-111 (Medicare Part D non-interference clause, partially repealed by IRA)
- 42 U.S.C. Section 1395w-114c (IRA Medicare Drug Price Negotiation Program)
- 42 U.S.C. Section 256b (340B Drug Pricing Program)
- 21 U.S.C. Section 355 (Hatch-Waxman Act ANDA provisions)
- 42 U.S.C. Section 262(k) (Biologics Price Competition and Innovation Act)
- P.L. 117-169, Sections 11001-11003 (Inflation Reduction Act drug pricing provisions)
- 21 U.S.C. Section 384 (Drug importation provisions; Section 804 of the FFDCA)
- 35 U.S.C. Sections 154, 156, 271 (Patent term, patent term restoration, and patent infringement provisions)
- 28 U.S.C. Section 1498 (Federal government use of patented inventions; potential compulsory licensing authority)
- CREATES Act, P.L. 116-94, Division N, Title VI (2019) (Remedy for REMS abuse blocking generic access to drug samples)
- 26 U.S.C. Section 280E (Tax deduction prohibition for cannabis businesses -- referenced in context of federal drug scheduling reform)

## Glossary of Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| ANDA | Abbreviated New Drug Application -- the FDA approval pathway for generic drugs |
| BPCIA | Biologics Price Competition and Innovation Act -- created the U.S. biosimilar pathway (2010) |
| Copay accumulator | Insurance practice preventing manufacturer copay assistance from counting toward deductibles |
| DIR fees | Direct and indirect remuneration fees -- PBM fees charged to pharmacies after the point of sale |
| Evergreening | Filing secondary patents on minor drug modifications to extend effective monopoly |
| Gross-to-net | The gap between a drug's list price and the price actually paid after rebates and discounts |
| IRA | Inflation Reduction Act of 2022 -- authorized Medicare drug price negotiation |
| IRP | International reference pricing -- benchmarking drug prices against other countries |
| NME | New Molecular Entity -- a genuinely novel drug compound |
| Orange Book | FDA publication listing patents and exclusivities for approved drugs |
| Pay-for-delay | Brand-name manufacturer paying a generic company to delay market entry |
| PDAB | Prescription Drug Affordability Board -- state body with authority to review drug prices |
| PBM | Pharmacy benefit manager -- intermediary managing prescription drug benefits |
| REMS | Risk Evaluation and Mitigation Strategy -- FDA safety program sometimes misused to block generic access |
| Spread pricing | PBM practice of charging plans more than they reimburse pharmacies |
| 340B | Federal program requiring discounted drug prices for safety-net providers |
