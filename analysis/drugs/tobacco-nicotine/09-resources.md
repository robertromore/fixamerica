# Tobacco and Nicotine Policy: Resources

This section provides a curated list of key research, government reports, and data sources on tobacco and nicotine policy in the United States.

---

## Academic Research

### Peer-Reviewed Studies

- Donny, E.C., et al. "Randomized Trial of Reduced-Nicotine Standards for Cigarettes." *New England Journal of Medicine* 373, no. 14 (2015): 1340-1349. DOI: 10.1056/NEJMsa1502403
    - Landmark trial demonstrating that very low nicotine content cigarettes reduce dependence and consumption without significant compensatory smoking

- Benowitz, N.L. "Nicotine Addiction." *New England Journal of Medicine* 362, no. 24 (2010): 2295-2303. DOI: 10.1056/NEJMra0809890
    - Authoritative review of the neurobiology of nicotine addiction and its implications for treatment

- Levy, D.T., et al. "A Framework for Evaluating the Public Health Impact of E-Cigarettes and Other Vaporized Nicotine Products." *Addiction* 112, no. 1 (2017): 8-17. DOI: 10.1111/add.13394
    - Modeling framework for assessing the population-level effects of e-cigarette availability

- Chaiton, M., et al. "Estimating the Number of Quit Attempts It Takes to Quit Smoking Successfully in a Longitudinal Cohort of Smokers." *BMJ Open* 6, no. 6 (2016): e011045. DOI: 10.1136/bmjopen-2016-011045
    - Found that the median number of quit attempts before successful cessation exceeds 30

- Chaloupka, F.J., et al. "Tobacco Taxes as a Tobacco Control Strategy." *Tobacco Control* 21, no. 2 (2012): 172-180. DOI: 10.1136/tobaccocontrol-2011-050417
    - Comprehensive review of the evidence on price elasticity of demand for tobacco products

- Friedman, A.S., and Xu, S. "Associations of Flavored E-Cigarette Uptake With Subsequent Smoking Initiation and Cessation." *JAMA Network Open* 3, no. 6 (2020): e203826
    - Analyzed the relationship between flavored e-cigarette use and both smoking initiation (among youth) and cessation (among adults)

- Farrelly, M.C., et al. "The Real Cost Campaign Prevented 587,000 Youths From Starting to Smoke." *American Journal of Preventive Medicine* 53, no. 5 (2017): 652-660
    - Evaluation of the FDA's flagship youth anti-smoking campaign showing significant prevention impact

- Apelberg, B.J., et al. "Potential Public Health Effects of Reducing Nicotine Levels in Cigarettes in the United States." *New England Journal of Medicine* 378, no. 18 (2018): 1725-1733
    - FDA-funded modeling study estimating that a nicotine reduction standard could prevent 8.5 million smoking deaths

- Chaiton, M., et al. "Association of Ontario's Ban on Menthol Cigarettes With Smoking Behavior 1 Month After Implementation." *JAMA Internal Medicine* 178, no. 5 (2018): 710-711
    - Early evidence that Canada's menthol ban increased quit attempts among menthol smokers

- Lee, J.G., et al. "Inequalities in Tobacco Retailer Density by Race, Ethnicity, and Socioeconomic Status." *Tobacco Control* 29, no. 5 (2020): 540-546
    - Found that predominantly Black neighborhoods had 2-3 times more tobacco retailers per capita than predominantly white neighborhoods

- Villanti, A.C., et al. "Menthol Cigarettes and Smokeless Tobacco: A Review of the Evidence on Cessation, Recruitment, and Health Effects." *Nicotine & Tobacco Research* 19, no. 10 (2017): 1079-1089
    - Comprehensive review demonstrating that menthol facilitates initiation and hinders cessation

- Jackler, R.K., and Ramamurthi, D. "Nicotine Arms Race: JUUL and the High-Nicotine Product Market." *Tobacco Control* 28, no. 6 (2019): 623-628
    - Analysis of how JUUL's nicotine salt technology enabled unprecedented nicotine delivery in e-cigarettes

- Kingsley, M., et al. "Short-Term Impact of a Flavored Tobacco Product Restriction: Changes in Cigarette Sales in Massachusetts." *American Journal of Preventive Medicine* 65, no. 4 (2023): 624-632
    - Evaluated the impact of Massachusetts's comprehensive flavor ban on cigarette sales patterns

- Maciosek, M.V., et al. "Greater Health Care Savings from Tobacco Cessation Relative to Prevention." *American Journal of Preventive Medicine* 52, no. 3 (2017): S149-S157
    - Cost-effectiveness analysis showing $2-3 return on investment for every $1 spent on cessation

### Working Papers and White Papers

- Levy, D.T., et al. "Modeling the Impact of a Menthol Ban on Smoking Prevalence and Smoking-Related Deaths." Tobacco Regulatory Science Group. 2021.
    - Estimated that a menthol ban could prevent over 654,000 deaths over 40 years

- National Academies of Sciences, Engineering, and Medicine. *Public Health Consequences of E-Cigarettes*. Washington, DC: The National Academies Press, 2018. DOI: 10.17226/24952
    - The most comprehensive evidence review of e-cigarette health effects, finding substantial evidence of reduced toxicant exposure compared to combustible cigarettes

- Cantrell, J., et al. "The Impact of the Tobacco Retail Outlet Environment on Adult Cessation and Youth Smoking." Center for Tobacco Control Research and Education Working Paper. 2015.
    - Evidence that high tobacco retail density is associated with lower cessation rates and higher youth initiation

- Tobacco Products Scientific Advisory Committee (TPSAC). "Menthol Cigarettes and Public Health: Review of the Scientific Evidence and Recommendations." FDA, 2011.
    - Advisory committee report recommending removal of menthol cigarettes from the marketplace

## Government Reports and Data

### Federal Agencies

- U.S. Department of Health and Human Services. *The Health Consequences of Smoking -- 50 Years of Progress: A Report of the Surgeon General*. 2014. <https://www.surgeongeneral.gov/library/reports/50-years-of-progress/>
    - Comprehensive 50-year review of the health consequences of smoking and progress in tobacco control

- U.S. Department of Health and Human Services. *E-Cigarette Use Among Youth and Young Adults: A Report of the Surgeon General*. 2016. <https://e-cigarettes.surgeongeneral.gov/>
    - First Surgeon General's report on e-cigarettes, documenting the rise of youth vaping

- U.S. Department of Health and Human Services. *Smoking Cessation: A Report of the Surgeon General*. 2020. <https://www.hhs.gov/surgeongeneral/reports-and-publications/>
    - Updated evidence review on cessation interventions and the treatment gap

- FDA Center for Tobacco Products. "Premarket Tobacco Product Applications." Updated continuously. <https://www.fda.gov/tobacco-products/market-and-distribute-tobacco-product/premarket-tobacco-product-applications>
    - Current status of PMTA review, authorized products, and marketing denial orders

- FDA. "Tobacco Product Standard for Nicotine Level of Combusted Cigarettes." Advance Notice of Proposed Rulemaking. *Federal Register* 83, no. 52 (March 16, 2018): 11818-11843
    - ANPRM initiating the process toward a nicotine reduction standard

- FDA. "Tobacco Product Standard for Menthol in Cigarettes." Proposed Rule. *Federal Register* 87, no. 86 (May 4, 2022): 26454-26554
    - The proposed menthol cigarette ban, including regulatory impact analysis

- FDA. "Required Warnings for Cigarette Packages and Advertisements." Proposed Rule. *Federal Register* 85, no. 52 (March 18, 2020): 15638-15691
    - Proposed graphic health warnings covering 50% of cigarette packages

- CDC. "Current Cigarette Smoking Among Adults in the United States." Updated 2024. <https://www.cdc.gov/tobacco/data_statistics/fact_sheets/adult_data/cig_smoking/>
    - Annual adult smoking prevalence data from the National Health Interview Survey

- CDC. "Youth and Tobacco Use." Updated 2024. <https://www.cdc.gov/tobacco/data_statistics/fact_sheets/youth_data/tobacco_use/>
    - Annual youth tobacco and nicotine use data from the National Youth Tobacco Survey

### Congressional Reports

- Congressional Research Service. "FDA Regulation of Tobacco Products." Report R45867 (2023). <https://crsreports.congress.gov/product/pdf/R/R45867>
    - Overview of FDA tobacco regulatory authority and current issues

- Congressional Research Service. "Federal Excise Tax on Tobacco Products: Rates, Revenue, and Issues." Report RL33938 (2023). <https://crsreports.congress.gov/product/pdf/RL/RL33938>
    - Analysis of federal tobacco tax structure, revenue, and reform proposals

- Government Accountability Office. "Electronic Cigarettes: FDA Has Made Progress but Should Take Additional Steps to Oversee Marketing Authorization Reviews." GAO-23-105217 (2023).
    - GAO assessment of FDA PMTA review process, identifying backlog and enforcement gaps

- Government Accountability Office. "Tobacco Regulation: FDA Should Strengthen Its Enforcement of Marketing Authorization Requirements." GAO-24-106230 (2024).
    - Follow-up report on FDA enforcement against unauthorized tobacco products

### Agency Data Portals

- CDC. Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System (BRFSS). <https://www.cdc.gov/brfss/> - Annual state-level smoking prevalence data
- FDA/CDC. National Youth Tobacco Survey (NYTS). <https://www.cdc.gov/tobacco/data_statistics/surveys/nyts/> - Annual youth tobacco and nicotine use data
- SAMHSA. National Survey on Drug Use and Health (NSDUH). <https://www.samhsa.gov/data/data-we-collect/nsduh-national-survey-drug-use-and-health> - Substance use prevalence including tobacco
- FDA. PATH Study (Population Assessment of Tobacco and Health). <https://pathstudyinfo.nih.gov/> - Longitudinal tobacco use and health outcomes data
- FTC. Cigarette Report and Smokeless Tobacco Report. Annual. <https://www.ftc.gov/reports/federal-trade-commission-cigarette-report> - Industry marketing expenditure and sales data
- CDC. STATE System (State Tobacco Activities Tracking and Evaluation). <https://www.cdc.gov/statesystem/> - State-level tobacco control policies and outcomes

## Books

- Brandt, Allan M. *The Cigarette Century: The Rise, Fall, and Deadly Persistence of the Product That Defined America*. Basic Books, 2007. - Definitive history of the cigarette and tobacco industry's campaign of deception

- Kluger, Richard. *Ashes to Ashes: America's Hundred-Year Cigarette War, the Public Health, and the Unabashed Triumph of Philip Morris*. Alfred A. Knopf, 1996. - Pulitzer Prize-winning history of the tobacco industry

- Proctor, Robert N. *Golden Holocaust: Origins of the Cigarette Catastrophe and the Case for Abolition*. University of California Press, 2011. - Comprehensive analysis of the industry's role in the smoking epidemic, with a case for ending cigarette sales

- Kessler, David A. *A Question of Intent: A Great American Battle with a Deadly Industry*. PublicAffairs, 2001. - Former FDA Commissioner's account of the first attempt to regulate tobacco

- Glantz, Stanton A., et al. *The Cigarette Papers*. University of California Press, 1996. - Analysis of leaked Brown & Williamson internal documents

- Eriksen, Michael, et al. *The Tobacco Atlas*. American Cancer Society, 6th ed., 2018. - Global tobacco use data, industry activity, and policy responses in atlas format

- Derthick, Martha A. *Up in Smoke: From Legislation to Litigation in Tobacco Politics*. CQ Press, 3rd ed., 2012. - Political science analysis of the shift from legislative to litigation-driven tobacco policy

## Legal Resources

### Case Law

- *FDA v. Brown & Williamson Tobacco Corp.*, 529 U.S. 120 (2000). - Supreme Court held that Congress had not granted FDA authority to regulate tobacco products; led to the 2009 Tobacco Control Act
- *Sottera, Inc. v. FDA*, 627 F.3d 891 (D.C. Cir. 2010). - Held that e-cigarettes are tobacco products, not drug delivery devices, establishing FDA jurisdiction under the Tobacco Control Act
- *United States v. Philip Morris USA, Inc.*, 449 F. Supp. 2d 1 (D.D.C. 2006). - Found major tobacco companies guilty of civil RICO violations for decades-long conspiracy to deceive the public
- *R.J. Reynolds Tobacco Co. v. FDA*, 696 F.3d 1205 (D.C. Cir. 2012). - Struck down FDA's first set of graphic health warnings on First Amendment grounds
- *FDA v. Wages and White Lion Investments*, cert. denied (2024). - Supreme Court declined to review Fifth Circuit decisions questioning FDA PMTA review standards
- *United States v. Safehouse*, 985 F.3d 225 (3rd Cir. 2021). - While primarily about supervised injection sites, relevant to enforcement design for tobacco regulations

### Statutory References

- 21 U.S.C. Chapter 9, Subchapter IX (Family Smoking Prevention and Tobacco Control Act) - FDA tobacco regulatory authority
- 26 U.S.C. Section 5701 et seq. - Federal tobacco excise taxes
- 15 U.S.C. Section 1331 et seq. (Federal Cigarette Labeling and Advertising Act) - Warning label and advertising requirements
- Consolidated Appropriations Act, 2022, Section 601 et seq. - Extension of FDA jurisdiction to synthetic nicotine products

## Tobacco Industry Documents

- UCSF Truth Tobacco Industry Documents Archive. <https://www.industrydocuments.ucsf.edu/tobacco/> - More than 14 million internal tobacco industry documents made public through litigation. The most important primary source for understanding industry conduct. Key document collections include:
    - Philip Morris documents revealing nicotine manipulation research
    - Brown & Williamson "doubt is our product" memoranda
    - R.J. Reynolds "Project SCUM" marketing documents targeting minority communities
    - JUUL internal communications regarding youth marketing (released through litigation, 2022-2024)
