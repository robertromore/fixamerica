# Cannabis Legalization: Resources

## Overview

This document compiles key resources for understanding cannabis legalization policy, including academic research, government reports, advocacy organization materials, data sources, legal references, and media coverage. Resources are organized by category and annotated with descriptions of their scope and utility.

---

## Academic Research

### Comprehensive Reviews

- National Academies of Sciences, Engineering, and Medicine. *The Health Effects of Cannabis and Cannabinoids: The Current State of Evidence and Recommendations for Research*. Washington, DC: National Academies Press, 2017.
    - The most comprehensive review of cannabis health evidence to date. Reviews over 10,000 studies. Foundational reference for health policy discussions.

- Caulkins, Jonathan P., Beau Kilmer, and Mark A.R. Kleiman. *Marijuana Legalization: What Everyone Needs to Know*. 2nd ed. New York: Oxford University Press, 2016.
    - Accessible policy overview covering legalization design, public health, criminal justice, and economic considerations.

- Room, Robin, et al. *Cannabis Policy: Moving Beyond Stalemate*. Oxford: Oxford University Press, 2010.
    - International comparative analysis of cannabis policy options.

### Health and Public Health

- Di Forti, Marta, et al. "The Contribution of Cannabis Use to Variation in the Incidence of Psychotic Disorder Across Europe." *Lancet Psychiatry* 6, no. 5 (2019): 427-436.
    - Key study on the association between high-potency cannabis use and psychosis risk across multiple European cities.

- Volkow, Nora D., et al. "Adverse Health Effects of Marijuana Use." *New England Journal of Medicine* 370, no. 23 (2014): 2219-2227.
    - Review by the NIDA director of health risks associated with cannabis use, including cognitive effects, addiction potential, and mental health.

- ElSohly, Mahmoud A., et al. "Changes in Cannabis Potency Over the Last 2 Decades (1995-2014): Analysis of Current Data in the United States." *Biological Psychiatry* 79, no. 7 (2016): 613-619.
    - Documents the significant increase in THC potency in U.S. cannabis products over two decades.

- Bradford, Ashley C., et al. "Medical Marijuana Laws Reduce Prescription Medication Use in Medicare Part D." *Health Affairs* 37, no. 7 (2018): 1150-1159.
    - Found that medical cannabis legalization was associated with a 6.6% reduction in opioid prescriptions and reductions in other medication categories.

- Santaella-Tenorio, Julian, et al. "Association of Recreational Cannabis Laws in Colorado and Washington State with Changes in Traffic Fatalities, 2005-2017." *JAMA Internal Medicine* 180, no. 8 (2020): 1062-1068.
    - Examines the relationship between recreational cannabis legalization and traffic fatalities.

### Criminal Justice and Racial Disparities

- American Civil Liberties Union (ACLU). *A Tale of Two Countries: Racially Targeted Arrests in the Era of Marijuana Reform*. New York: ACLU, 2020.
    - Comprehensive analysis of racial disparities in cannabis arrests using FBI and Census data. Documents 3.73:1 Black-white arrest ratio nationally.

- Alexander, Michelle. *The New Jim Crow: Mass Incarceration in the Age of Colorblindness*. New York: The New Press, 2010.
    - Foundational text on the role of drug enforcement in mass incarceration and racial inequality.

- Prescott, J.J., and Sonja B. Starr. "Expungement of Criminal Convictions: An Empirical Study." *Harvard Law Review* 133, no. 8 (2020): 2460-2555.
    - Empirical study finding that criminal record expungement leads to significant increases in employment and wages.

- Rothwell, Jonathan. "Drug Offenders in American Prisons: The Critical Distinction Between Stock and Flow." *Brookings Institution*, 2015.
    - Analysis of incarceration data distinguishing between snapshot and flow measures for drug offenders.

### Economics and Market Analysis

- Hansen, Benjamin, Keaton Miller, and Caroline Weber. "The Taxation of Recreational Marijuana: Evidence from Washington State." *National Tax Journal* 73, no. 4 (2020): 879-908.
    - Examines the design and revenue effects of Washington State's cannabis tax structure.

- Smart, Rosanna, et al. "Variation in Cannabis Potency and Prices in a Newly Legal Market: Evidence from 30 Million Cannabis Sales in Washington State." *Addiction* 112, no. 12 (2017): 2167-2177.
    - Analysis of price and potency trends in the early Washington State legal market.

- Anderson, D. Mark, et al. "Association of Marijuana Laws with Teen Marijuana Use." *JAMA Pediatrics* 173, no. 9 (2019): 879-881.
    - Found no evidence that recreational marijuana legalization leads to increased teen use.

### History and Policy

- Bonnie, Richard J., and Charles H. Whitebread. *The Marijuana Conviction: A History of Marijuana Prohibition in the United States*. New York: Lindesmith Center, 1999 (originally 1974).
    - Definitive legal and political history of cannabis prohibition in America.

- Lee, Martin A. *Smoke Signals: A Social History of Marijuana -- Medical, Recreational and Scientific*. New York: Scribner, 2012.
    - Comprehensive social history from ancient use through modern legalization movement.

- Musto, David F. *The American Disease: Origins of Narcotic Control*. 3rd ed. New York: Oxford University Press, 1999.
    - Classic history of American drug policy, including the racial and political origins of cannabis prohibition.

- Baum, Dan. "Legalize It All: How to Win the War on Drugs." *Harper's Magazine*, April 2016.
    - Contains the John Ehrlichman quote admitting the political motivation behind Nixon-era drug policy.

## Government Reports and Data

### Federal Government

- Congressional Research Service. "Cannabis Rescheduling." R47934. Washington, DC: CRS, 2024.
    - Analysis of the rescheduling process, legal implications, and congressional options.

- Congressional Research Service. "Marijuana: An Overview of Federal Policy and Selected Legal Issues." R47657. Washington, DC: CRS, 2023.
    - Comprehensive overview of federal cannabis law and policy.

- U.S. Department of Health and Human Services. "Scheduling Recommendation for Marijuana." August 2023.
    - HHS recommendation to DEA to reschedule cannabis to Schedule III.

- Drug Enforcement Administration. "Proposed Rule: Schedules of Controlled Substances: Rescheduling of Marijuana." 89 Fed. Reg. 44920 (2024).
    - The DEA's proposed rule to reschedule cannabis from Schedule I to Schedule III.

- Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (FinCEN). "BSA Expectations Regarding Marijuana-Related Businesses." FIN-2014-G001. February 2014.
    - Treasury Department guidance enabling limited cannabis banking.

- National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA). "Drug and Alcohol Crash Risk: A Case-Control Study." DOT HS 812 355. Washington, DC: NHTSA, 2017.
    - Study of drug and alcohol involvement in vehicle crashes.

- Government Accountability Office (GAO). "Marijuana Enforcement: DOJ Has Not Articulated Specific Goals for Prioritizing Efforts." GAO-16-596. September 2016.
    - Review of federal cannabis enforcement priorities and resource allocation.

### FBI Crime Data

- Federal Bureau of Investigation. Crime Data Explorer. https://crime-data-explorer.fr.cloud.gov/
    - Annual data on cannabis arrests, including demographic breakdowns.

- FBI. Uniform Crime Reporting (UCR) Program. Annual reports.
    - Historical arrest data showing cannabis arrest trends from 1990s peak through present.

### State Government Reports

- Colorado Department of Revenue. Marijuana Enforcement Division. Annual reports.
    - Comprehensive data on Colorado's cannabis market, enforcement, and revenue.

- Colorado Department of Public Health and Environment. *Monitoring Health Concerns Related to Marijuana in Colorado*. Annual reports.
    - Public health data on cannabis use, youth trends, emergency department visits, and other health indicators.

- Washington State Liquor and Cannabis Board. Annual reports and data dashboards.
    - Market data, enforcement, and public health metrics for Washington's cannabis program.

- California Department of Cannabis Control. Annual reports.
    - Regulatory data, licensing statistics, and enforcement actions.

- Illinois Department of Financial and Professional Regulation. Annual Cannabis Report.
    - Data on Illinois's cannabis program including social equity metrics.

## Legal References

### Federal Statutes

- Controlled Substances Act, 21 U.S.C. 801 et seq. (1970).
- Internal Revenue Code Section 280E (prohibiting deductions for drug trafficking businesses).
- Bank Secrecy Act, 31 U.S.C. 5311 et seq.
- Rohrabacher-Farr (Rohrabacher-Blumenauer) Amendment (annual appropriations rider since 2014).
- 2018 Farm Bill, Agriculture Improvement Act of 2018, 7 U.S.C. 1639o et seq. (hemp legalization).

### Key Court Cases

- *Gonzales v. Raich*, 545 U.S. 1 (2005) -- Supreme Court held that Congress has authority under the Commerce Clause to criminalize cannabis even in states where it is legal, because local production and consumption affect interstate commerce.
- *Conant v. Walters*, 309 F.3d 629 (9th Cir. 2002) -- Physician recommendations for medical cannabis are protected speech under the First Amendment.
- *United States v. Oakland Cannabis Buyers' Cooperative*, 532 U.S. 483 (2001) -- No medical necessity defense to federal cannabis prohibition.
- *Standing Akimbo, LLC v. United States*, No. 20-1248 (U.S. Tax Court, 2022) -- Challenged 280E application to state-legal cannabis businesses.
- *South Dakota v. Noem* (S.D. Supreme Court, 2021) -- Struck down voter-approved legalization on procedural grounds.

### Congressional Legislation (Recent)

- SAFE Banking Act (H.R. 2891, 118th Congress)
- MORE Act (H.R. 3617, 117th Congress)
- Cannabis Administration and Opportunity Act (S. 4591, 117th Congress)
- STATES Act (S. 1028, 116th Congress)
- HOPE Act (S. 1985, 118th Congress)

### International Law

- Single Convention on Narcotic Drugs, 1961 (as amended by the 1972 Protocol).
- Convention on Psychotropic Substances, 1971.
- UN Convention Against Illicit Traffic in Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances, 1988.
