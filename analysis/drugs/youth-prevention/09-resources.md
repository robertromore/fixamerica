# Youth Drug Prevention: Resources

This section provides a curated list of key research, government reports, data sources, and organizations in the field of youth drug prevention.

---

## Academic Research

### Peer-Reviewed Studies

- Botvin, G. J., & Griffin, K. W. "Life Skills Training: A Competence Enhancement Approach to Tobacco, Alcohol, and Drug Abuse Prevention." *Handbook of Adolescent Health Risk Behavior* (2014): 135-155. - Foundational reference for the most widely validated school-based prevention curriculum.
- Hawkins, J. D., Oesterle, S., Brown, E. C., et al. "Sustained Decreases in Risk Exposure and Youth Problem Behaviors After Installation of the Communities That Care Prevention System in a Randomized Trial." *Archives of Pediatrics and Adolescent Medicine* 166, no. 2 (2012): 141-148. <https://doi.org/10.1001/archpediatrics.2011.244>
- Kellam, S. G., Brown, C. H., Poduska, J. M., et al. "Effects of a Universal Classroom Behavior Management Program in First and Second Grades on Young Adult Behavioral, Psychiatric, and Social Outcomes." *Drug and Alcohol Dependence* 95, Suppl. 1 (2008): S5-S28. <https://doi.org/10.1016/j.drugalcdep.2008.01.004> - Landmark study showing first-grade intervention reduced substance abuse disorders by ~50% at ages 19-21.
- Spoth, R., Redmond, C., Shin, C., et al. "PROSPER Community-University Partnership Delivery System Effects on Substance Misuse Through 6 1/2 Years Past Baseline From a Cluster Randomized Controlled Intervention Trial." *Preventive Medicine* 56, no. 3-4 (2013): 190-196.
- Ennett, S. T., Tobler, N. S., Ringwalt, C. L., & Flewelling, R. L. "How Effective Is Drug Abuse Resistance Education? A Meta-analysis of Project DARE Outcome Evaluations." *American Journal of Public Health* 84, no. 9 (1994): 1394-1401. - First rigorous meta-analysis demonstrating D.A.R.E.'s ineffectiveness.
- Lynam, D. R., Milich, R., Zimmerman, R., et al. "Project DARE: No Effects at 10-Year Follow-Up." *Journal of Consulting and Clinical Psychology* 67, no. 4 (1999): 590-593. - 10-year longitudinal study confirming D.A.R.E. had no lasting effect on drug use.
- Faggiano, F., Minozzi, S., Versino, E., & Buscemi, D. "Universal School-Based Prevention for Illicit Drug Use." *Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews* no. 12 (2014). - Comprehensive systematic review of school-based prevention effectiveness.
- Knight, J. R., Sherritt, L., Shrier, L. A., Harris, S. K., & Chang, G. "Validity of the CRAFFT Substance Abuse Screening Test Among Adolescent Clinic Patients." *Archives of Pediatrics and Adolescent Medicine* 156, no. 6 (2002): 607-614. - Validation study for the primary adolescent substance use screening tool.
- Casey, B. J., Heller, A. S., Gee, D. G., & Cohen, A. O. "Development of the Emotional Brain." *Neuroscience Letters* 693 (2019): 29-34. - Key reference on adolescent brain development and substance vulnerability.

### Working Papers and White Papers

- RAND Corporation. "The Cost and Effectiveness of School-Based Drug Prevention Programs." Working Paper WR-A2115. 2022. - Economic analysis of universal evidence-based prevention implementation.
- Washington State Institute for Public Policy. "Prevention Program Benefit-Cost Analysis." Updated December 2023. <https://www.wsipp.wa.gov/BenefitCost> - Comprehensive cost-benefit analyses of prevention programs with specific ROI estimates.
- Blueprints for Healthy Youth Development. "Program Selection Guide." University of Colorado Boulder. 2024. <https://www.blueprintsprograms.org>

## Government Reports and Data

### Federal Agencies

- SAMHSA. "National Survey on Drug Use and Health (NSDUH): Detailed Tables." Annual. <https://www.samhsa.gov/data/data-we-collect/nsduh-national-survey-drug-use-and-health> - Primary national household survey on substance use, including adolescent data.
- SAMHSA Center for Substance Abuse Prevention. "Prevention of Substance Abuse and Mental Illness." Program descriptions and funding information. <https://www.samhsa.gov/prevention>
- National Institute on Drug Abuse. "Monitoring the Future Survey Results." Annual. <https://monitoringthefuture.org/> - Gold standard school-based survey of adolescent substance use since 1975.
- Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. "Youth Risk Behavior Surveillance System (YRBSS)." Biennial. <https://www.cdc.gov/yrbs/> - School-based survey covering substance use and other health risk behaviors.
- CDC WONDER. "Multiple Cause of Death Data." <https://wonder.cdc.gov/> - Overdose death data by age, geography, and substance.
- Drug Enforcement Administration. "National Drug Threat Assessment." Annual. <https://www.dea.gov/documents/2024/drug-threat-assessment> - Assessment of drug supply threats including fentanyl.
- Office of National Drug Control Policy. "National Drug Control Budget." Annual. <https://www.whitehouse.gov/ondcp/> - Federal drug control spending by category.
- Government Accountability Office. "Drug Prevention: Federal Efforts to Identify Effective Programs Need Stronger Design." GAO-20-432 (2020). - Assessment of federal prevention program effectiveness standards.

### Congressional Reports

- Congressional Research Service. "Federal Drug Control Programs: Background and FY2025 Budget." Report R47890 (2024). <https://crsreports.congress.gov>
- Congressional Research Service. "Youth Substance Use Prevention: Federal Programs and Evidence." Report R46782 (2023). <https://crsreports.congress.gov>
- Government Accountability Office. "Youth Illicit Drug Use Prevention: DARE Long-Term Evaluations and Federal Efforts to Identify Effective Programs." GAO-03-172R (2003). - Key GAO assessment of D.A.R.E. effectiveness.

### Agency Data Portals

- SAMHSA. NSDUH Interactive Data Tables. <https://pdas.samhsa.gov/> - Customizable substance use data queries by age, state, and demographic characteristics.
- CDC. WONDER Online Database. <https://wonder.cdc.gov/> - Mortality data including drug overdose deaths by age group.
- Monitoring the Future. Data Tables and Figures. <https://monitoringthefuture.org/data/> - Annual survey data on adolescent substance use trends.

## Books

- Botvin, G. J., & Griffin, K. W. *Life Skills Training: Promoting Health and Personal Development*. Princeton Health Press, 2014. - Comprehensive guide to the most widely validated school-based prevention program.
- Hawkins, J. D., & Catalano, R. F. *Communities That Care: Action for Drug Abuse Prevention*. Jossey-Bass, 1992. - Foundational text on the community-level prevention model.
- Milam, A. *Chasing the Scream: The First and Last Days of the War on Drugs*. Bloomsbury, 2015. - Accessible history of drug policy including prevention approaches.
- Hart, C. *Drug Use for Grown-Ups: Chasing Liberty in the Land of Fear*. Penguin Press, 2021. - Challenging conventional drug prevention narratives.
- Macy, B. *Dopesick: Dealers, Doctors, and the Drug Company That Addicted America*. Little, Brown, 2018. - Essential context on the opioid crisis that drives current prevention urgency.
- Jensen, F. E., & Nutt, A. E. *The Teenage Brain: A Neuroscientist's Survival Guide to Raising Adolescents and Young Adults*. Harper, 2015. - Accessible overview of adolescent brain development and substance vulnerability.
- West, S. L., & O'Neal, K. K. *D.A.R.E. Outcome Effectiveness Revisited*. American Journal of Public Health Press, 2004. - Systematic review of D.A.R.E. evaluation literature.

## Legal Resources

### Case Law

- *Vernonia School District 47J v. Acton*, 515 U.S. 646 (1995) - Supreme Court upheld random drug testing of student athletes under the Fourth Amendment.
- *Board of Education v. Earls*, 536 U.S. 822 (2002) - Extended random drug testing to students participating in any extracurricular activity.
- *Safford Unified School District v. Redding*, 557 U.S. 364 (2009) - Limited scope of student searches; relevant to drug testing privacy boundaries.

### Statutory References

- 20 U.S.C. Section 7101-7122 (ESSA Title IV-A) - Current federal framework for school-based prevention funding.
- 21 U.S.C. Section 1521-1548 (Drug-Free Communities Act) - Authorization for community coalition grants.
- 42 U.S.C. Section 300x-21 (SAPT Block Grant) - Substance Abuse Prevention and Treatment Block Grant with 20% prevention set-aside.
- 21 U.S.C. Section 801 et seq. (Controlled Substances Act) - Federal drug scheduling framework that shapes prevention context.
- Family Smoking Prevention and Tobacco Control Act, Pub. L. 111-31 (2009) - FDA authority over tobacco/nicotine products including e-cigarettes.
