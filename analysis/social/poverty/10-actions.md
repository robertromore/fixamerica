# Poverty: Actions for Citizens

## Overview

Reducing poverty requires collective action, but that action starts with individuals. Whether you have an hour, a day, or a lifetime to dedicate, there are meaningful ways to contribute. This document provides concrete actions organized by time commitment, resources, and skills.

## Immediate Actions (Today)

### Learn and Share

| Action | Time | Impact |
|--------|------|--------|
| Read one article about poverty | 15 min | Build understanding |
| Share a statistic on social media | 5 min | Raise awareness |
| Watch a documentary | 1-2 hours | Deepen knowledge |
| Discuss with family/friends | 30 min | Spread awareness |

**Key Statistics to Share**

- 37.9 million Americans live in poverty
- 16.3% of children are poor
- 2021 CTC expansion cut child poverty nearly in half
- US poverty rate is double that of peer nations
- Millions of workers remain poor despite working full-time

| Action | Impact |

## Avoiding Common Pitfalls

### What Not to Do

| Pitfall | Why Problematic |
|---------|-----------------|
| Paternalism | Assumes you know best |
| Saviorism | Centers self, not affected |
| One-time action | Change requires sustained effort |
| Charity only | Doesn't address root causes |
| Pity | Disempowering and dehumanizing |

### Best Practices

| Practice | Why Important |
|----------|---------------|
| Listen to those affected | They know their needs |
| Follow their lead | Support, don't supplant |
| Stay for the long haul | Change takes time |
| Address systems | Individual aid necessary but insufficient |
| Maintain dignity | Everyone deserves respect |
