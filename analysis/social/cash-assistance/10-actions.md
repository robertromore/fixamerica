# Cash Assistance: Actions

## For Individuals

### Learn and Understand

**Educate Yourself**:

- Read the Center on Budget and Policy Priorities' TANF reports
- Understand your state's TANF-to-poverty ratio
- Learn about TANF history and the 1996 reform
- Follow researchers and advocates on social media

**Key Questions to Research**:

- What is your state's maximum TANF benefit?
- What percentage of poor families in your state receive TANF?
- How does your state spend its TANF funds?
- What work requirements and time limits apply?

### Speak Up

**Share Information**:

- Discuss TANF's failures with friends and family
- Share articles and research on social media
- Write letters to the editor when TANF is discussed
- Challenge stereotypes about welfare recipients

**Personal Stories**:

- If you have experience with TANF, consider sharing
- Connect others with storytelling opportunities
- Support those who share their experiences
- Challenge narratives that stigmatize the poor

### Support Recipients Directly

**Mutual Aid**:

- Contribute to local mutual aid funds
- Support emergency assistance programs
- Help families navigate bureaucracy
- Provide informal support (child care, transportation)

## For Advocates

### State-Level Advocacy

**Legislative Campaigns**:

- Identify champions in legislature
- Develop relationships with staff
- Testify at hearings
- Organize constituent contacts

**Key Legislative Priorities**:

1. Benefit increases
2. Sanction reform
3. Time limit extensions/elimination
4. Work requirement exemptions
5. Spending transparency

**Administrative Advocacy**:

- Engage state TANF agency
- Comment on regulations
- Participate in advisory bodies
- Monitor implementation

### Federal Advocacy

**Congressional Engagement**:

- Build relationships with offices
- Provide policy expertise
- Organize constituent visits
- Support TANF reauthorization reform

**Executive Branch**:

- Engage HHS during comment periods
- Advocate for guidance changes
- Support administrative improvements
- Monitor enforcement

### Campaign Strategies

**Narrative Change**:

- Challenge "welfare reform success" narrative
- Elevate child poverty impacts
- Share personal stories
- Reframe from individual to structural

**Coalition Building**:

- Connect TANF to broader anti-poverty agenda
- Build labor-welfare alliances
- Engage racial justice organizations
- Include immigrant rights groups

**Public Mobilization**:

- Organize days of action
- Build grassroots pressure
- Engage media
- Create electoral accountability

## Measuring Impact

### Indicators of Progress

**Policy Wins**:

- States increasing benefits
- States reforming sanctions
- States extending time limits
- Federal legislation introduced

**Narrative Change**:

- Media coverage shifting
- Public opinion on cash assistance
- Political discourse evolving
- Recipient voice elevated

**System Improvement**:

- TANF-to-poverty ratio increasing
- Benefits as percentage of poverty rising
- Sanctions declining
- Emergency assistance expanding

### Tracking Methods

**Data Monitoring**:

- Annual state-by-state analysis
- TANF-to-poverty ratio tracking
- Spending pattern analysis
- Caseload trends

**Qualitative Assessment**:

- Recipient experience documentation
- Coalition capacity assessment
- Political landscape analysis
- Media coverage monitoring

### Learning and Adaptation

**Regular Review**:

- What's working and what's not
- Adjust strategies based on results
- Share lessons with other advocates
- Build on successes
