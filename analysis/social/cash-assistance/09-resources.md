# Cash Assistance: Resources

## Research and Reports

### Essential Reading

**Center on Budget and Policy Priorities**:

- "TANF Reaching Few Poor Families" (updated annually)
- "TANF Benefits Remain Below Poverty Line" (state-by-state analysis)
- "TANF Spending: A Primer"
- "How States Use Federal and State Funds Under the TANF Block Grant"

**Urban Institute**:

- "The TANF Program in 2020: A Long Road to Help for Low-Income Families"
- "Welfare Rules Database" (comprehensive state policy data)
- "From Welfare to Work: Has TANF Been Successful?"
- "What's Happening to TANF Caseloads?"

**CLASP (Center for Law and Social Policy)**:

- "TANF at 25: A Legacy of Racist Policies"
- "No More Double Punishments: Lifting the Ban on SNAP and TANF for People with Prior Felony Drug Convictions"
- State policy analysis and recommendations

**Brookings Institution**:

- "Safety Net for Families with Children No Longer Provides Adequate Protection"
- "Work Requirements and Safety Net Programs"

### Academic Research

**Kathryn Edin and H. Luke Shaefer**:

- *$2.00 a Day: Living on Almost Nothing in America* (2015)
- Research on extreme poverty in America

**LaDonna Pavetti** (Urban Institute):

- Research on work requirements and sanctions
- TANF caseload dynamics

**Robert Moffitt** (Johns Hopkins):

- Economics of welfare programs
- Work incentive effects

**Race and TANF Studies**:

- Soss, Fording, and Schram, *Disciplining the Poor* (2011)
- Research on racial disparities in TANF administration
- State policy variation and race
