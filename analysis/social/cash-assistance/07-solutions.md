# Cash Assistance: Solutions

## Guiding Principles

### Adequacy

Benefits must be sufficient to meet basic needs:

- Minimum benefit tied to poverty level or fair market rent
- Automatic adjustment for inflation
- Recognition that cash enables family choices
- Supplement rather than replace other supports

### Accessibility

Assistance must reach those who need it:

- Presumptive eligibility based on income
- Simplified application processes
- Right to apply and receive decision
- No arbitrary barriers or deterrents

### Stability

Support must be reliable and consistent:

- No arbitrary time limits
- Gradual phase-out rather than cliff effects
- Counter-cyclical response to economic conditions
- Predictability for family planning

### Dignity

Programs must treat recipients with respect:

- Presumption of good faith
- Privacy protections
- Choice in how to use benefits
- No punitive surveillance or testing

### Racial Equity

Reforms must address historical and ongoing disparities:

- Federal standards to prevent state-level discrimination
- Data collection and accountability
- Community voice in program design
- Affirmative remediation of past harms

## Policy Proposals

### Convert TANF Block Grant to Entitlement

**Description**: Replace fixed block grant with open-ended matching grant that responds to need.

**Key Elements**:

- Federal matching rate of 75-80%
- Automatic increase during recessions
- Maintenance of effort requirements for states
- Individual entitlement restored

**Expected Impact**:

- Immediate increase in families served
- Counter-cyclical response to downturns
- Reduced state incentive to cut caseloads
- More adequate state investment

### Establish Minimum Federal Benefit Standards

**Description**: Require all states to provide minimum benefit levels.

**Key Elements**:

- Minimum benefit of 50% of poverty line
- Indexed to inflation annually
- Phase-in period for lowest-benefit states
- Federal funding to support increases

**Expected Impact**:

- Benefit increases in 35+ states
- Reduce deep poverty
- Limit race to the bottom
- Improve child outcomes

### Reform Work Requirements

**Description**: Make work requirements more realistic and supportive.

**Key Elements**:

- Count education and training toward requirements
- Recognize caregiving as valuable work
- Provide job quality standards for work placements
- Increase exemptions for barriers to employment

**Expected Impact**:

- Better long-term employment outcomes
- Recognition of unpaid care work
- Higher quality job placements
- Reduced sanctions for inability to comply

### Eliminate Arbitrary Time Limits

**Description**: Remove 60-month lifetime limit and state shorter limits.

**Key Elements**:

- Federal law amended to remove time limit
- States prohibited from imposing lifetime limits
- Continue work and engagement requirements
- Maintain incentives for self-sufficiency

**Expected Impact**:

- Families not cut off during continued need
- Reduced deep poverty
- Less arbitrary punishment
- Better child outcomes

### Reform Sanctions

**Description**: Limit sanctions to proportionate responses rather than full-family termination.

**Key Elements**:

- Partial sanctions only (reduce benefit, not terminate)
- Children's portion protected from sanction
- Due process before sanction imposed
- Automatic restoration procedures

**Expected Impact**:

- Reduced harm from non-compliance
- Children protected from parental actions
- More proportionate accountability
- Fewer families with zero income

### Expand Emergency Assistance

**Description**: Strengthen short-term crisis intervention.

**Key Elements**:

- Dedicated federal funding for emergency aid
- Broader eligibility (not just TANF recipients)
- Rapid response (within 48 hours)
- Flexible use (rent, utilities, car repairs)

**Expected Impact**:

- Prevent homelessness and utility shutoffs
- Reduce need for ongoing assistance
- Stabilize families before crisis deepens
- Cost-effective intervention

## Structural Reforms

### Accountability Mechanisms

**Require Outcome Measurement**:

- Track poverty reduction, not just caseload
- Measure employment quality (wages, benefits, stability)
- Report TANF-to-poverty ratios
- Annual public reporting by state

**Create Enforcement Tools**:

- Federal review of state performance
- Funding consequences for poor performance
- Technical assistance for improvement
- Recognition for positive outcomes

### Spending Requirements

**Ensure Cash Goes to Cash**:

- Minimum 50% of TANF for basic assistance
- Cap administrative costs at 10%
- Restrict "other" spending category
- Prohibit supplantation of state funds

### Racial Equity Requirements

**Address Disparities**:

- Require disaggregated data by race
- Disparate impact analysis required
- Affirmative action to reduce gaps
- Community input in program design

## Alternative Approaches

### Basic Income / Guaranteed Income

**Description**: Replace categorical programs with universal or near-universal cash.

**Arguments For**:

- Simplicity and dignity
- Eliminates categorical complexity
- Removes stigma
- Reaches everyone in need

**Arguments Against**:

- Political feasibility questions
- Cost concerns
- May not address specific needs
- Transition challenges

### Child Allowance

**Description**: Universal or near-universal payment per child.

**Arguments For**:

- Proven effective (2021 Child Tax Credit expansion)
- Reduced child poverty by 40%
- Universal reduces stigma
- Simple to administer

**Arguments Against**:

- Cost (though offset by benefits)
- Does not address childless adults
- Political challenges to permanence

### Negative Income Tax

**Description**: Tax-based system that supplements income below threshold.

**Arguments For**:

- Integration with tax system
- Automatic phase-out
- Work incentives built in
- Administrative simplicity

**Arguments Against**:

- Annual reconciliation challenges
- Delay in reaching families
- Does not reach non-filers

## What Has Worked Elsewhere

### United Kingdom: Universal Credit

**Features**:

- Unified benefit replacing multiple programs
- Automatic adjustment to income
- Single application process
- Digital-first administration

**Lessons**:

- Consolidation can improve access
- Implementation challenges substantial
- Work incentives can be maintained
- Transition period critical

### Canada: Child Benefit

**Features**:

- Universal benefit per child
- Income-tested with phase-out
- Monthly payments
- Tax system delivery

**Results**:

- Significant child poverty reduction
- High take-up rates
- Popular across political spectrum
- Automatic stabilizer function

### Australia: Family Tax Benefit

**Features**:

- Integrated with tax system
- Income-tested
- Both annual and fortnightly payments
- Supplements rather than replaces work income

**Lessons**:

- Integration with work income possible
- Regular payments more useful than annual
- Complexity has costs
- Continuous adjustment helps

## Trade-offs

### Adequacy vs. Political Feasibility

- Higher benefits are more costly
- Costs create political opposition
- Phased approaches may be necessary
- Coalition building takes time

### Universality vs. Targeting

- Universal programs have higher costs
- Targeted programs have higher stigma
- Universal programs more politically durable
- Targeting concentrates resources on need

### Federal Standards vs. State Flexibility

- Federal standards ensure equity
- State flexibility allows innovation
- Federal enforcement has limits
- State politics can be more regressive

### Work Requirements vs. Adequacy

- Work requirements popular politically
- Work requirements cause harm
- Alternative engagement approaches possible
- Tension between support and requirements

### Short-Term Reform vs. Structural Change

- Incremental reform is achievable
- Fundamental change may be needed
- Building toward larger goals
- Not making perfect enemy of good
