# Safety Net Design: Actions

## Individual Actions

### If You Are Navigating the System

**Know Your Rights**:
- You have the right to apply for any program
- You have the right to a timely decision
- You have the right to appeal denials
- You have the right to confidentiality

**Document Everything**:
- Keep copies of all applications
- Save confirmation numbers
- Document conversations (date, name, summary)
- Retain pay stubs and other verification

**Use Available Resources**:
- Benefits.gov for eligibility screening
- State benefit portals
- Legal aid organizations
- Community action agencies
- Navigator programs

**Understand Cliff Effects**:
- Calculate total benefit value before accepting raises
- Consider timing of income changes
- Ask about transition programs
- Plan for benefit changes

**Appeal When Denied**:
- Most denials can be appealed
- Request fair hearings
- Seek legal aid assistance
- Document reasons for denial

### If You Want to Help Others

**Volunteer**:

- Transportation assistance

**Mentor**:
- Help others navigate the system

**Donate**:

## Action Priority Matrix

### High Impact, Low Barrier

| Action | Time | Skill | Impact |
|--------|------|-------|--------|
| Contact legislators | 1 hour | None | Medium-High |
| Share personal story | 1 hour | None | Medium-High |
| Join advocacy organization | 1 hour | None | Medium |
| Volunteer for tax prep | 8 hours training | Basic | High |

### High Impact, Higher Barrier

| Action | Time | Skill | Impact |
|--------|------|-------|--------|
| Testify at hearing | 4+ hours | Public speaking | High |
| Run for local office | Major | Various | Very high |
| Lead advocacy campaign | Major | Organizing | Very high |
| Conduct research | Major | Research skills | High |

### Ongoing Engagement

| Action | Time/Month | Focus |
|--------|------------|-------|
| Stay informed | 2-4 hours | Reading, following news |
| Regular contact with legislators | 1-2 hours | Calls, emails |
| Volunteer work | 4-8 hours | Direct service or advocacy |
| Coalition participation | 2-4 hours | Meetings, actions |

### This Week

1. Learn about one program you don't know well
2. Find your state's benefit portal
3. Identify one advocacy organization to follow
4. Contact one legislator about one issue

### This Month

1. Volunteer for one direct service activity
2. Attend one advocacy event or meeting
3. Share what you've learned with three people
4. Make a recurring contribution to an organization

### This Year

1. Develop expertise in one policy area
2. Build relationships with key stakeholders
3. Take on a leadership role in advocacy
4. Track and celebrate progress
