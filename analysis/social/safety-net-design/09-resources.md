## Essential Reading

### Books

**Administrative Burden**

Herd, Pamela and Donald P. Moynihan. *Administrative Burden: Policymaking by Other Means*. Russell Sage Foundation, 2018.
- Foundational work on bureaucratic barriers
- Learning, compliance, and psychological costs
- Policy implications
- Case studies across programs

**Safety Net Design**

Danziger, Sheldon and Robert Haveman, eds. *Understanding Poverty*. Russell Sage Foundation, 2001.
- Comprehensive overview
- Multiple perspectives
- Historical context
- Policy analysis

**Welfare Reform History**

Weaver, R. Kent. *Ending Welfare as We Know It*. Brookings, 2000.
- 1996 reform legislative history
- Political analysis
- Policy design
- Implementation preview

**Deep Poverty**

Edin, Kathryn J. and H. Luke Shaefer. *$2.00 a Day: Living on Almost Nothing in America*. Houghton Mifflin Harcourt, 2015.
- Deep poverty reality
- Safety net failures
- Family experiences
- Policy implications

**Work and Poverty**

Autor, David. "Work of the Past, Work of the Future." *AEA Papers and Proceedings*, 2019.
- Labor market changes
- Implications for safety net
- Future of work considerations

### Academic Articles

**Benefit Cliffs and Marginal Tax Rates**

Maag, Elaine et al. "How Marginal Tax Rates Affect Families at Various Levels of Poverty." *Tax Policy Center*, 2012.
- Comprehensive EMTR analysis
- Program interactions
- Family scenarios
- Policy recommendations

Romich, Jennifer L. "Is Raising the Minimum Wage a Good Idea? Evidence and Implications for Social Work." *Social Work* 62(4), 2017.
- Work incentive analysis
- Cliff effects
- Policy design implications

**Administrative Burden**

Moynihan, Donald et al. "Administrative Burden: Learning, Psychological, and Compliance Costs in Citizen-State Interactions." *Journal of Public Administration Research and Theory* 25(1), 2015.
- Theoretical framework
- Empirical evidence
- Policy implications

Heinrich, Carolyn J. "The Bite of Administrative Burden: A Theoretical and Empirical Investigation." *Journal of Public Administration Research and Theory* 26(3), 2016.
- Administrative burden costs
- Program participation effects
- Design implications

**Program Integration**

Bloom, Howard S. and James A. Riccio. "Using Place-Based Random Assignment and Comparative Interrupted Time-Series Analysis to Evaluate the Jobs-Plus Employment Program for Public Housing Residents." *Annals of the American Academy of Political and Social Science* 599, 2005.
- Integration evaluation
- Employment outcomes
- Design lessons

**Work Requirements**

Hoynes, Hilary W. and Diane Whitmore Schanzenbach. "Work Incentives and the Food Stamp Program." *Journal of Public Economics* 96(1-2), 2012.
- SNAP work effects
- Participation analysis
- Policy implications

Gray, Colin et al. "Do Fiscal Rules Cause Fiscal Discipline Over the Electoral Cycle?" *Journal of Policy Analysis and Management*, 2023.
- Work requirement evidence
- Employment effects
- Administrative impacts

**Child Tax Credit**

Parolin, Zachary et al. "The Initial Effects of the Expanded Child Tax Credit on Material Hardship." *NBER Working Paper* 29285, 2021.
- 2021 CTC effects
- Hardship reduction
- Monthly payment benefits

Collyer, Sophie et al. "The Effects of the 2021 Monthly Child Tax Credit on Child and Family Well-Being." *Center on Poverty and Social Policy*, 2022.
- Comprehensive CTC analysis
- Poverty reduction
- Family impacts
