# Supplemental Security Income (SSI): Stakeholders

## Primary Stakeholders

### SSI Recipients

**Total**: 7.5 million individuals

#### Aged Recipients (1.1 million)

**Profile**:

- Age 65 and older
- Limited Social Security benefits (or none)
- Below asset and income thresholds
- Often immigrant elders who worked in informal economy

**Interests**:

- Adequate benefits for housing and food
- Preservation of Medicaid eligibility
- Simplified administration
- Protection from overpayment collection

**Challenges**:

| Challenge | Impact |
|-----------|--------|
| Housing costs | Benefits insufficient for most markets |
| Healthcare needs | Medicare gaps, prescription costs |
| Isolation | Limited transportation, mobility |
| Cognitive decline | Difficulty navigating system |

#### Disabled Adults (5.3 million)

**Profile**:

- Ages 18-64 (majority), plus 65+
- Physical, mental, or intellectual disabilities
- Cannot work at substantial gainful activity (SGA)
- Many have concurrent SSDI

**Primary Diagnoses**:

| Category | Percentage |
|----------|------------|
| Mental disorders | 35% |
| Intellectual disability | 13% |
| Musculoskeletal | 12% |
| Nervous system | 10% |
| Other | 30% |

**Interests**:

- Increased asset limits for financial security
- Work incentives that don't risk benefits
- Elimination of marriage penalty
- Reduced administrative burden

**Challenges**:

| Challenge | Impact |
|-----------|--------|
| Employment barriers | Discrimination, lack of accommodations |
| Housing | Accessible housing scarce |
| Healthcare | Dependent on Medicaid |
| Isolation | Limited social connections |

#### Children with Disabilities (1.1 million)

**Profile**:

- Under age 18
- Marked and severe functional limitations
- Parent/guardian receives benefits on child's behalf
- Must re-qualify as adult at age 18

**Common Conditions**:

| Category | Percentage |
|----------|------------|
| Mental disorders (ADHD, autism, etc.) | 55% |
| Intellectual disability | 15% |
| Nervous system disorders | 10% |
| Congenital anomalies | 8% |
| Other | 12% |

**Interests**:

- Family financial stability
- Access to services and therapies
- Smooth transition to adult SSI
- Educational support

**Challenges**:

| Challenge | Impact |
|-----------|--------|
| Parental income deeming | Reduces/eliminates benefits |
| Age-18 redetermination | Many lose benefits |
| Service access | SSI alone insufficient |
| Stigma | Families judged for receiving benefits |

### Families and Caregivers

**Profile**:

- Parents of children receiving SSI
- Spouses of adult recipients
- Adult children caring for aged parents
- Unpaid family caregivers

**Interests**:

- Adequate benefits to support recipient
- Elimination of deeming rules
- Recognition of caregiving costs
- Flexibility for employment

**Challenges**:

| Challenge | Impact |
|-----------|--------|
| Caregiver burden | Often cannot work full-time |
| Financial strain | Family income counted against recipient |
| Deeming rules | Modest income eliminates child's benefits |
| Marriage penalty | Legal marriage reduces benefits |

### Applicants in Process

**Profile**:

- 2.5 million applications annually
- 65% denied at initial level
- Many in appeals process
- Average wait: 7+ months initially, 2-3 years if appealing

**Interests**:

- Faster processing
- Fair evaluation
- Assistance with evidence gathering
- Income support during application

**Challenges**:

| Challenge | Impact |
|-----------|--------|
| Wait time | Years without income |
| Evidence burden | Must prove disability |
| Denial rates | Most must appeal |
| Health decline | Conditions worsen during wait |

## Advocacy Organizations

### National Disability Rights Organizations

**The Arc**:

- Focus: Intellectual and developmental disabilities
- Advocacy for SSI reform
- Individual assistance and legal support
- Policy research and education

**National Disability Rights Network**:

- Protection and Advocacy (P&A) agencies
- Legal representation for SSI appeals
- Systems advocacy
- Individual rights protection

**Autistic Self Advocacy Network (ASAN)**:

- Self-advocate led organization
- SSI and asset limit reform advocacy
- Nothing About Us Without Us philosophy
- Youth transition support

### Poverty and Economic Justice Organizations

**Center on Budget and Policy Priorities**:

- SSI policy analysis
- Asset limit reform advocacy
- Data and research
- Congressional testimony

**National Academy of Social Insurance**:

- Research on social insurance programs
- SSI policy studies
- Expert analysis and recommendations
- Convening experts

**Coalition on Human Needs**:

- Broad coalition for safety net
- SSI as component of anti-poverty policy
- Federal budget advocacy
- State advocacy support

### Legal Aid Organizations

**National Organization of Social Security Claimants' Representatives (NOSSCR)**:

- Attorney/representative training
- SSI appeals representation
- Policy advocacy
- Best practices development

**Legal Aid Societies**:

- Limited capacity relative to need
- Impact litigation
- Know-your-rights education

### Senior Advocacy Organizations

**National Committee to Preserve Social Security and Medicare**:

- Advocacy for aged SSI recipients

- Grassroots organizing

**Justice in Aging**:

- Legal advocacy for low-income seniors

- Litigation
- Training and technical assistance

## Government Stakeholders

### Social Security Administration (SSA)

**Role**:

- Administers SSI program
- Determines eligibility
- Pays benefits
- Conducts CDRs
- Processes appeals

**Interests**:

- Adequate administrative funding
- Manageable workload
- Clear policy guidance
- Technology modernization

**Challenges**:

| Challenge | Impact |
|-----------|--------|
| Underfunding | Cannot meet demand |
| Legacy technology | Inefficiency |
| Staffing shortages | Delays and errors |
| Complex rules | Administrative burden |

### State Disability Determination Services (DDS)

**Role**:

- Medical disability evaluation
- Evidence gathering
- Initial decisions and reconsiderations
- State agency under federal contract

**Interests**:

- Adequate federal funding
- Clear guidance from SSA
- Reasonable caseloads
- Staff retention

**Challenges**:

| Challenge | Impact |
|-----------|--------|
| Examiner turnover | Training costs, inconsistency |
| Medical evidence gaps | Difficult decisions |
| Pressure for production | Quality concerns |
| Federal-state tensions | Conflicting priorities |

### Office of Hearings Operations (OHO)

**Role**:

- Administrative Law Judge hearings
- Appeals processing
- Quality review
- Decision writing

**Interests**:

- ALJ recruitment and retention
- Reduced backlogs
- Decision quality
- Hearing efficiency

**Challenges**:

| Challenge | Impact |
|-----------|--------|
| Backlog | 500,000+ pending cases |
| ALJ shortage | Insufficient capacity |
| Case complexity | Mental health cases difficult |
| Consistency | Varying allowance rates |

### State Agencies

**State SSI Supplement Programs**:

- 44 states provide optional supplements
- Administration varies (state or SSA)
- Benefit levels vary widely

**State Medicaid Programs**:

- SSI recipients automatically eligible in most states
- "209(b) states" have separate rules
- Medicaid is often more valuable than SSI cash

## Healthcare Stakeholders

### Medicaid

**Relationship to SSI**:

- SSI recipients automatically Medicaid-eligible (most states)
- Medicaid often more valuable than cash benefit
- Creates work disincentive (fear of losing coverage)
- 209(b) states have separate eligibility

**Interest in SSI**:

- SSI determination = Medicaid eligibility
- Cost implications of caseload changes
- Coordination with SSI reporting

### Healthcare Providers

**Relationship to SSI**:

- Provide medical evidence for claims
- Treat SSI recipients
- Often Medicaid-dependent providers
- Completing forms part of patient care

**Interests**:

- Reasonable documentation requests
- Payment for form completion
- Patient access to benefits
- Streamlined processes

### Disability Examiners

**Role**:

- Evaluate medical evidence
- Apply disability criteria
- Make initial determinations
- State DDS employees

**Interests**:

- Clear guidance
- Reasonable caseloads
- Consistent policies
- Job security

## Opposition Stakeholders

### Fiscal Conservatives

**Concerns**:

- SSI cost to federal budget (~$60 billion/year)
- Potential for fraud and abuse
- Work disincentives
- Dependency concerns

**Preferred Policies**:

- Stricter eligibility
- More frequent CDRs
- Work requirements
- Benefit limitations

### Think Tanks

**Heritage Foundation**:

- Views SSI as "welfare"
- Advocates for stricter eligibility
- Skeptical of disability claims
- Promotes work requirements

**American Enterprise Institute**:

- Research on disability programs
- Concerns about growth
- Work incentive focus
- Market-based alternatives

### Some Employers

**Concerns**:

- SSI may reduce labor supply
- Disability accommodations costly
- Skepticism about disability claims

**Note**: Many employers supportive of disabled workers; opposition concentrated in certain sectors

## Stakeholder Alignment

### Coalition Potential

**Asset Limit Reform**:

- Strong support: Disability advocates, poverty groups, legal aid
- Potential support: Moderate legislators, some fiscal conservatives (savings simplification)
- Opposition: Budget hawks concerned about cost

**Benefit Increases**:

- Strong support: All advocacy groups
- Potential support: Progressive legislators
- Opposition: Fiscal conservatives, anti-welfare groups

**Process Improvement**:

- Strong support: SSA, applicants, advocates
- Potential support: Bipartisan (efficiency appeals)
- Opposition: Those preferring gatekeeping

**Marriage Penalty Elimination**:

- Strong support: Disability advocates, family groups
- Potential support: Marriage-promotion conservatives
- Opposition: Fiscal concerns about cost
