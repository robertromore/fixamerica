# Supplemental Security Income (SSI): Current State

## Program Overview

### Eligibility Categories

SSI serves three categories of recipients, each with distinct eligibility pathways:

| Category | Recipients | Share | Eligibility |
|----------|-----------|-------|-------------|
| Aged (65+) | 1.1 million | 15% | Age 65+, meet income/asset limits |
| Blind | 66,000 | 1% | Legal blindness, meet income/asset limits |
| Disabled (adults) | 5.3 million | 70% | Meet SSA disability definition, income/asset limits |
| Disabled (children) | 1.1 million | 14% | Marked and severe functional limitations |

### Federal Benefit Rates (2024)

| Recipient Type | Monthly Benefit | Annual Benefit |
|----------------|-----------------|----------------|
| Individual | $943 | $11,316 |
| Couple (both eligible) | $1,415 | $16,980 |
| Essential person | $472 | $5,664 |

**Note**: The couple rate is only 150% of the individual rate, not 200%, creating a marriage penalty.

### Asset Limits

| Category | Limit | Last Updated |
|----------|-------|--------------|
| Individual | $2,000 | 1989 |
| Couple | $3,000 | 1989 |

**Countable Resources Include**:

- Cash and bank accounts
- Stocks, bonds, investments
- Life insurance (cash value over $1,500)
- Real property other than primary residence
- Vehicles (generally one excluded)

**Excluded Resources**:

- Primary residence (any value)
- One vehicle (with limitations)
- Household goods and personal effects
- Burial funds up to $1,500
- ABLE accounts (up to $100,000)
- Property essential for self-support

### Income Rules

**Income Exclusions**:

| Exclusion Type | Amount |
|----------------|--------|
| General income exclusion | $20/month |
| Earned income exclusion | $65/month |
| Student earned income exclusion | $2,290/month (up to $9,230/year) |
| Impairment-related work expenses | Actual cost |
| Plan for Achieving Self-Support | As approved |

**Benefit Reduction**:

- After exclusions, unearned income reduces benefits dollar-for-dollar
- Earned income reduces benefits $1 for every $2 earned
- "Deeming" counts spouse/parent income and resources

## Disability Determination Process

### Initial Application

**Application Rate**: ~2.5 million applications/year

**Denial Rate**: ~65% of initial applications denied

**Process Flow**:

1. Application filed at SSA field office or online
2. Non-medical eligibility determined by SSA
3. Medical determination made by state Disability Determination Services (DDS)
4. Decision issued

**Average Processing Time**: 7+ months

### Sequential Evaluation Process (Adults)

| Step | Question | If Yes | If No |
|------|----------|--------|-------|
| 1 | Working at SGA level ($1,550/month)? | Denied | Proceed |
| 2 | Severe medically determinable impairment? | Proceed | Denied |
| 3 | Meets/equals Listings? | Allowed | Proceed |
| 4 | Can do past relevant work? | Denied | Proceed |
| 5 | Can do other work? | Denied | Allowed |

### Childhood Disability Standard

Children must demonstrate:

- A medically determinable physical or mental impairment
- That results in "marked and severe functional limitations"
- Expected to last 12+ months or result in death

**Functional Domains Evaluated**:

1. Acquiring and using information
2. Attending and completing tasks
3. Interacting and relating with others
4. Moving about and manipulating objects
5. Caring for self
6. Health and physical well-being

## Continuing Disability Reviews (CDRs)

### CDR Frequency

| Diary Category | Review Frequency |
|----------------|-----------------|
| Medical Improvement Expected | 6-18 months |
| Medical Improvement Possible | 3 years |
| Medical Improvement Not Expected | 5-7 years |

### CDR Outcomes

| Outcome | Percentage |
|---------|------------|
| Benefits continued | 90%+ |
| Medical cessation | <10% |
| Benefits terminated for other reasons | Variable |

### CDR Backlog

- SSA has conducted fewer CDRs than required due to funding constraints
- Estimated cost savings of $9 for every $1 spent on CDRs
- Backlog results in some recipients not reviewed on schedule

## Work Incentives

### Section 1619(a) - Continued SSI Eligibility While Working

Allows SSI recipients to continue receiving (reduced) benefits while working above the Substantial Gainful Activity (SGA) level.

### Section 1619(b) - Continued Medicaid While Working

Allows individuals to maintain Medicaid eligibility even when earnings eliminate SSI cash benefits, if:

- Eligible for SSI in prior month
- Still disabled
- Still meet other requirements
- Need Medicaid to continue working
- Earnings below state threshold ($40,000-$75,000 depending on state)

### Plan for Achieving Self-Support (PASS)

Allows recipients to set aside income and resources for a work goal:

- Approved by SSA
- Must have specific work goal
- Set-aside income/resources not counted against SSI
- Underutilized (fewer than 5,000 active PASS plans)

### Impairment-Related Work Expenses (IRWE)

Deducts disability-related work costs from earnings:

- Medical devices
- Attendant care
- Transportation modifications
- Medications

### Ticket to Work

Provides vocational rehabilitation and employment services:

- Voluntary program
- Protects against medical CDRs while using ticket
- Limited participation rates

## SSI vs. SSDI Comparison

| Feature | SSI | SSDI |
|---------|-----|------|
| Basis | Means-tested | Work history |
| Funding | General revenue | Social Security trust fund |
| Asset limit | $2,000/$3,000 | None |
| Income limit | Yes | Yes (while working) |
| Waiting period | None | 5 months |
| Health coverage | Medicaid | Medicare (after 24 months) |
| Cost-of-living adjustments | Yes | Yes |
| Average benefit (2024) | $698 | $1,537 |
| Recipients | 7.5 million | 7.5 million |

### Concurrent Recipients

- ~2.5 million people receive both SSI and SSDI
- Typically have limited work history, so SSDI is low
- SSI "tops up" SSDI to SSI level
- Receive both Medicaid and Medicare
