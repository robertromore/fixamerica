# Unemployment Insurance: Actions

## What You Can Do

### Immediate Actions

#### Educate Yourself

- Learn your state's UI eligibility rules and benefit levels
- Understand who is covered and who is excluded
- Know the history of racial exclusions in UI
- Familiarize yourself with reform proposals

#### Share Information

- Help others understand UI is earned, not welfare
- Counter "dependency" and "fraud" narratives
- Share stories of UI system failures
- Explain how low recipiency hurts the economy

#### Know Your Rights

- Understand eligibility if you lose your job
- Know how to appeal a denial
- Learn about available legal help
- Document any mistreatment in the system

### Electoral Actions

#### Vote for UI Reformers

- Research candidates' positions on UI
- Support candidates who favor expansion
- Oppose candidates who want to cut benefits
- Prioritize state legislative races (UI is largely state-administered)

#### Engage in Primaries

- Primary challengers can push incumbents on UI
- Support pro-worker candidates in both parties
- Volunteer for reform-minded campaigns

#### Support Ballot Initiatives

- Some states allow direct democracy on UI issues
- Signature gathering for reform measures
- Campaign for initiatives that expand coverage

### Advocacy Actions

#### Contact Elected Officials

**State Legislators**:

- Call and write about UI benefit adequacy
- Demand coverage for gig workers
- Oppose duration cuts or eligibility restrictions
- Request hearings on UI system failures

**Congressional Representatives**:

- Support federal minimum standards legislation
- Push for FUTA wage base update
- Demand administrative funding increases
- Call for pandemic lessons implementation

**Governors**:

- Urge administrative improvements
- Request UI agency investment
- Demand outreach to underserved populations

#### Testify at Hearings

- Share personal experiences with UI system
- Support reform legislation
- Counter employer opposition testimony
- Bring data and research to support reform

#### Write Letters and Op-Eds

- Local newspapers
- Online media
- Letters to editor
- Op-ed pieces on UI reform

### Organizing Actions

#### Join Worker Organizations

**Labor Unions**:

- Support union organizing (unions advocate for UI)
- Participate in union political action
- Engage in worker education

**Worker Centers**:

- Join local worker centers
- Support gig worker organizing
- Connect with unemployed worker organizations

**Advocacy Organizations**:

- National Employment Law Project
- Center on Budget and Policy Priorities
- State-level workforce coalitions
- Jobs With Justice

#### Build Community Power

- Organize in your neighborhood around economic security
- Connect UI reform to broader worker rights
- Build coalitions across issues
- Engage faith communities

#### Participate in Direct Action

- Rally at state capitols for UI reform
- Picket employers who misclassify workers
- Support strikes and walkouts for worker rights
- Nonviolent protest of benefit cuts

### Professional Actions

#### For Researchers

- Study UI recipiency and disparities
- Evaluate reform impacts
- Document pandemic lessons
- Publish accessible findings

#### For Journalists

- Investigate UI system failures
- Report on worker experiences
- Examine employer influence
- Hold officials accountable

#### For Lawyers

- Provide legal help to denied claimants
- Challenge improper denials
- Litigate classification issues
- Support policy advocacy

#### For Social Workers

- Help clients navigate UI system
- Document barriers to access
- Advocate for administrative improvements
- Connect clients to legal help

### Business Actions

#### For Ethical Employers

- Classify workers correctly
- Don't contest legitimate claims
- Pay adequate UI taxes
- Support fair UI standards

#### For Business Advocates

- Support level playing field (against misclassifiers)
- Advocate for stable workforce policies
- Recognize UI's economic stabilization role
- Join responsible business coalitions

### If You Are Unemployed

#### Claim Your Benefits

- Apply for UI promptly after job loss
- Gather necessary documentation
- Meet all requirements carefully
- Appeal if denied

#### Get Help If Needed

- Legal aid for appeals
- Worker centers for support
- Advocacy organizations for information
- Social services for additional help

#### Share Your Story

- Document your experience
- Contact advocacy organizations
- Write to legislators
- Speak to media (if comfortable)

#### Connect with Others

- Unemployed worker groups
- Online communities
- Support networks
- Advocacy coalitions

## State-Specific Actions

### States with Inadequate Benefits

**Priority Actions**: Push for benefit increases, longer duration, waiting week elimination

**Target States**: Mississippi, Louisiana, Florida, Arizona, North Carolina

**Strategy**:

- Legislative advocacy
- Candidate support
- Public education
- Coalition building

### States with Coverage Gaps

**Priority Actions**: ABC test adoption, gig worker coverage, part-time worker inclusion

**Target States**: Most states without strong classification laws

**Strategy**:

- Support classification legislation
- Expose misclassification
- Organize affected workers
- Build employer support

### States with Administrative Problems

**Priority Actions**: IT modernization, processing improvements, outreach expansion

**Target States**: States with old systems, long delays, low recipiency

**Strategy**:

- Demand administrative investment
- Document problems
- Support workers in system
- Pressure for improvement

## Federal Actions

### Congressional Advocacy

**Key Legislation to Support**:

- UI Modernization Act (or equivalent)
- Worker classification reform
- FUTA wage base update
- Automatic stabilizers legislation

**Committees to Contact**:

- House Ways and Means
- Senate Finance
- House Education and Labor
- Senate HELP Committee

### Executive Branch Engagement

**Department of Labor**:

- Comment on proposed regulations
- Support strong worker classification rules
- Advocate for administrative funding
- Push for enhanced state oversight

**White House**:

- Support presidential attention to UI reform
- Engage Domestic Policy Council
- Connect to economic messaging

## Measuring Success

### Track Progress

**State Level**:

- Benefit levels and duration
- Recipiency rates
- Coverage expansion
- Administrative improvements

**Federal Level**:

- Legislation introduced and passed
- Administrative action taken
- Funding levels
- Regulatory changes

### Hold Officials Accountable

- Monitor promises vs. actions
- Track voting records
- Document implementation
- Publicize results (good and bad)

## Long-Term Engagement

- Develop ongoing relationships with legislators
- Build permanent organizations
- Train new leaders
- Maintain coalition connections

### Prepare for Opportunities

- Next recession will spotlight UI failures
- Electoral changes may open windows
- Maintain reform proposals ready
- Keep coalition engaged

### Connect to Broader Movements

- Economic justice
- Racial equity
- Labor rights
- Democratic reform
