# Child Poverty: Overview

## Executive Summary

One in six American children lives in poverty--11.6 million children in the wealthiest nation on earth. The United States has among the highest child poverty rates of any developed country, with rates 2-3 times higher than peer nations. This is not inevitable: the 2021 Child Tax Credit expansion proved that bold policy can cut child poverty nearly in half, reducing the rate from 9.7% to 5.2% under the Supplemental Poverty Measure. When the expansion expired, child poverty immediately spiked to 12.4%, demonstrating that child poverty in America is fundamentally a policy choice.

Child poverty inflicts lasting damage. It stunts brain development during the critical 0-5 years, reduces educational achievement, creates lifelong health problems, and perpetuates intergenerational disadvantage. The economic costs are staggering--an estimated $1 trillion annually in lost productivity, increased healthcare costs, and criminal justice expenses. Beyond economics, child poverty represents a moral failure: children bear no responsibility for their circumstances yet suffer the severest consequences.

Deep child poverty--living below 50% of the poverty threshold--affects 5% of American children (approximately 3.6 million), condemning them to severe deprivation with the steepest barriers to escape. Child poverty rates vary dramatically by race (Black and Hispanic children experience rates 2-3 times higher than white children), family structure (single-parent families face 4 times the poverty rate of married couples), and geography.

## The Core Problem

### Child Poverty in America

**The Numbers (2022)**

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Children in poverty (Official) | 11.6 million |
| Child poverty rate (Official) | 16.3% |
| Child poverty rate (SPM) | 12.4% |
| Deep child poverty | 5.1% (3.6 million) |
| Near-poor children (100-200% FPL) | 21% |

**The 2021 Transformation**

| Metric | 2020 | 2021 (Expanded CTC) | 2022 (Expired) |
|--------|------|---------------------|----------------|
| Child poverty rate (SPM) | 9.7% | 5.2% | 12.4% |
| Children in poverty | 7.0 million | 3.8 million | 9.0 million |
| Children lifted out | - | 3.7 million | - |

**What the 2021 Expansion Did**

- Increased credit to $3,600/child under 6, $3,000 ages 6-17
- Made fully refundable (reached poorest children)
- Provided monthly advance payments
- Reached 61 million children

### International Comparison

**Child Poverty Rates (After Taxes and Transfers)**

| Country | Child Poverty Rate |
|---------|-------------------|
| Denmark | 3.7% |
| Finland | 3.5% |
| Germany | 8.0% |
| France | 8.4% |
| Canada | 8.1% |
| United Kingdom | 11.0% |
| **United States** | **15.7%** |

The U.S. child poverty rate is 2-4 times higher than most peer nations.

**Why the Difference**

| Factor | U.S. Approach | Peer Nation Approach |
|--------|---------------|---------------------|
| Child benefits | Limited CTC, partially refundable | Universal child allowances |
| Paid leave | None (federal) | 6-18 months paid |
| Childcare | Limited subsidies | Universal or heavily subsidized |
| Healthcare | Employment-tied | Universal |
| Minimum wage | $7.25 federal | Higher, indexed |

### Types of Child Poverty

**By Depth**

| Category | Definition | Children Affected |
|----------|------------|-------------------|
| Deep poverty | Below 50% of poverty line | 3.6 million (5.1%) |
| Poverty | Below 100% of poverty line | 11.6 million (16.3%) |
| Near poverty | 100-200% of poverty line | 15 million (21%) |
| Low-income | Below 200% of poverty line | 26.6 million (37%) |

**By Age**

| Age Group | Poverty Rate |
|-----------|--------------|
| Under 6 | 18.3% |
| 6-11 | 15.8% |
| 12-17 | 15.0% |

Young children (0-5) face the highest poverty rates during the most critical developmental period.

### Effects of Child Poverty

**Brain Development (0-5)**

- Toxic stress affects brain architecture
- Reduced gray matter in regions critical for learning
- Impaired executive function
- Effects persist into adulthood

**Educational Outcomes**

| Metric | Poor vs. Non-Poor Gap |
|--------|----------------------|
| Kindergarten readiness | 1 year behind |
| Math achievement | 25% score gap |
| Reading achievement | 27% score gap |
| High school graduation | 11 percentage points lower |
| College enrollment | 29 percentage points lower |

**Health**

| Health Outcome | Association with Child Poverty |
|----------------|-------------------------------|
| Infant mortality | 1.5x higher |
| Low birth weight | 1.5x higher |
| Chronic disease | 2x higher |
| Mental health problems | 2-3x higher |
| Adult health status | Significantly worse |

**Lifetime Earnings**

- Children who grow up in poverty earn 25-30% less as adults
- Estimated lifetime earnings loss: $500,000-$800,000 per child
- Intergenerational transmission: poverty passes to next generation

## Key Concepts

### Child Poverty vs. Adult Poverty

| Factor | Children | Adults |
|--------|----------|--------|
| Responsibility | None for circumstances | Some agency |
| Malleability | High; brain developing | Lower |
| Duration effects | Lifelong impact | More variable |
| Moral urgency | Highest | High |

### The Child Tax Credit

**Current Structure (Post-2021)**

| Element | Value |
|---------|-------|
| Maximum credit | $2,000/child |
| Refundable portion | Up to $1,700 |
| Phase-in | 15% of earned income above $2,500 |
| Phase-out | Begins at $200,000 (single), $400,000 (married) |

**2021 Expansion (Expired)**

| Element | Value |
|---------|-------|
| Amount | $3,600/child under 6, $3,000 ages 6-17 |
| Refundability | Fully refundable (no earnings requirement) |
| Delivery | Monthly advance payments |
| Result | Cut child poverty nearly in half |

### Child Allowance

**Concept**

A regular cash payment to families for each child, regardless of employment status--a universal acknowledgment that raising children is socially valuable and costly.

**Features**

- Monthly payments
- All or most children eligible
- Simple administration (automatic at birth)
- Reduces child poverty effectively

**International Examples**

| Country | Benefit | Monthly Amount |
|---------|---------|----------------|
| Canada | Canada Child Benefit | ~$500 (varies by income) |
| Germany | Kindergeld | ~$250 |
| UK | Child Benefit | ~$100-170 |
| Austria | Family Allowance | ~$200+ |

- Dreams deferred or destroyed

- Reduced economic productivity
- Democratic participation undermined

### For the Economy

- $1 trillion annual cost of child poverty
- Workforce quality reduced
- Consumer demand diminished
- Innovation suppressed

### Moral Dimension

- Wealthiest nation; unacceptable that children suffer
- Children bear no responsibility for circumstances
- Reflects societal values
