# Social Services: Actions

## What You Can Do

### Overview

Transforming America's social services system requires sustained engagement from citizens, professionals, and communities. This guide provides concrete actions for individuals at different levels of commitment and capacity.

## Individual Actions

### Stay Informed

**Learn About Social Services**

| Action | Time | Resources |
|--------|------|-----------|
| Read KIDS COUNT data for your state | 1 hour | datacenter.kidscount.org |
| Follow key organizations | Ongoing | See Resources page |
| Sign up for newsletters | 15 minutes | CBPP, CLASP, First Focus |
| Learn about local services | 2-3 hours | 211, local CAA |

### Civic Engagement

**Contact Elected Officials**

| Level | Focus | Message |
|-------|-------|---------|
| Federal | Funding for home visiting, Head Start | Evidence shows returns on investment |
| State | TANF spending, child welfare prevention | State has flexibility to improve |
| Local | Community services, school-based programs | Local impact |

**Sample Messages**

For federal representatives:
> "I urge you to support expanded funding for home visiting programs. Nurse-Family Partnership shows a $5.70 return for every $1 invested. We should scale what works."

For state legislators:
> "Our state should require that more TANF funds go to cash assistance and employment services rather than being diverted to unrelated purposes."
