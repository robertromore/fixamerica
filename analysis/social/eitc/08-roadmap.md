# Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC): Implementation Roadmap

## Strategic Overview

EITC reform benefits from bipartisan history and strong evidence base. The strategy builds on the 2021 childless expansion demonstration, state-level momentum, and the program's work-based framing. This roadmap sequences reforms from immediately achievable to transformative, building constituencies and political capital along the way.

## Guiding Principles

### Sequencing Logic

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Build on proven success** | 2021 expansion demonstrated impact |
| **Maintain bipartisan framing** | "Work bonus" not "welfare" |
| **Start with childless workers** | Clear gap, strong case |
| **State-level proof points** | Demonstrate at state level |
| **Simplify while expanding** | Address compliance concerns |

### Political Strategy

| Element | Approach |
|---------|----------|
| **Framing** | "Reward work," "help workers," "tax cut for workers" |
| **Champions** | Bipartisan sponsors where possible |
| **Coalition** | Labor, business (some), anti-poverty, faith |
| **Opposition response** | Address improper payments through simplification |
| **Evidence** | 2021 data, research, state examples |

## Phase 1: Immediate Priorities (2025-2026)

### Year 1 Priorities

**Federal Legislative Priorities**

| Priority | Rationale | Pathway |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| Permanent childless expansion | 2021 proved success | Reconciliation or standalone |
| Remove age restrictions | Arbitrary exclusion | Include with expansion |
| Simplify qualifying child rules | Reduce errors | Bipartisan appeal |
| Increase VITA funding | Improve participation | Appropriations |

**Key Legislation: EITC Enhancement Act**

| Component | Specification |
|-----------|---------------|
| Childless maximum | $1,800 (from $632) |
| Age range | 18-no maximum |
| Phase-in rate | 15.3% |
| Income limit (single) | $25,000+ |
| Qualifying child simplification | Include |

**Executive Actions**

| Action | Authority | Timeline |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| Enhanced outreach | IRS discretion | Immediate |
| Simplified forms | IRS discretion | Tax season 2026 |
| VITA expansion | Grants | FY 2025 |
| Data matching improvements | Treasury | Ongoing |

**State-Level Priorities**

| Priority | Target States |

## Risk Assessment and Mitigation

### Political Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Congressional gridlock | Focus on bipartisan framing; state action |
| Administration change | Design for durability; build broad support |
| Budget constraints | Revenue options; demonstrate ROI |
| Backlash | Maintain work focus; address improper payments |

### Implementation Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| IRS capacity | Phased implementation; technology investment |
| Low advance payment uptake | Voluntary; learn from experience |
| Simplification errors | Careful rule drafting; transition period |
| State resistance | Federal incentives; technical assistance |

### Sustainability Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Future cuts | Build constituency; universal elements |
| Inflation erosion | Automatic indexing |
| Improper payment scrutiny | Continuous simplification |
| Program complexity creep | Maintain simplification focus |
