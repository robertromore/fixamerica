# Rural Healthcare: Citizen Actions

## What You Can Do

### Individual Actions

- **Know your local healthcare**: Understand what services are available locally and what requires travel
- **Use local providers**: Support local healthcare facilities when possible
- **Enroll in coverage**: Use ACA marketplace, Medicaid (if eligible), or employer coverage
- **Engage in telehealth**: Take advantage of virtual care options when appropriate
- **Share your story**: Personal experiences with rural healthcare challenges matter for policy
- **Know emergency resources**: Understand nearest emergency services and when to use them
- **Preventive care**: Use available preventive services to reduce need for emergency/specialty care

### Community Actions

- **Support local hospital**: Attend hospital board meetings; support fundraising; advocate for community resources
- **Organize transportation**: Volunteer driver programs; coordinate rides for neighbors
- **Community health education**: Partner with providers for health fairs, screenings
- **Advocate for broadband**: Support local broadband initiatives; engage with utilities
- **Support workforce recruitment**: Welcome healthcare workers; community amenities
- **Local foundation support**: Contribute to healthcare foundations

### Political Actions

- **Contact elected officials**: Advocate for rural healthcare funding, Medicaid expansion, telehealth policy
- **Testify at hearings**: Share personal experiences at state and federal hearings
- **Support Medicaid expansion**: In non-expansion states, advocate for expansion ballot measures or legislation
- **Engage local government**: County commissioners, hospital boards are critical decision makers
- **Join advocacy organizations**: NRHA, Farm Bureau, and other organizations advocating for rural health
- **Vote**: Prioritize candidates' positions on rural healthcare

## Staying Informed

### Key Dates and Processes

- **ACA Open Enrollment**: November 1 - January 15 (marketplace coverage)
- **Hospital board meetings**: Monthly in most communities
- **State legislative sessions**: Monitor rural health legislation
- **Federal appropriations**: Annual budget process affects rural programs
- **Medicaid waiver applications**: State-level policy changes

### Information Sources

- **Rural Health Information Hub**: [ruralhealthinfo.org](https://www.ruralhealthinfo.org) - Comprehensive resource
- **State Office of Rural Health**: State-specific information
- **Local hospital communications**: Community updates
- **Daily Yonder**: [dailyyonder.com](https://dailyyonder.com) - Rural news
- **NRHA**: [ruralhealth.us](https://www.ruralhealth.us) - Policy updates

### Warning Signs to Monitor

- **Hospital financial distress**: Watch for layoffs, service cuts, bond rating downgrades
- **Provider departures**: Loss of physicians, nurses may signal problems
- **Pharmacy closures**: Pharmacies are often early indicators of community healthcare decline
- **EMS response times**: Increasing times may indicate capacity problems
- **Insurance market exits**: Insurers leaving rural markets limits coverage options

- Specific policy requests

### Building Local Coalitions

**Natural allies**:

- Hospital administration and board
- Local physicians and nurses
- EMS and first responders
- Farm Bureau and agricultural organizations
- Chambers of commerce
- County commissioners
- School districts
- Churches and civic organizations

### Engaging State Officials

- **Governor's office**: Medicaid expansion decisions
- **State legislators**: State rural health programs, workforce
- **State Office of Rural Health**: Coordination, advocacy
- **State hospital association**: Collective voice

## Special Circumstances

### If Your Hospital Is at Risk

1. **Understand the situation**: Request information from hospital leadership
2. **Engage community**: Town halls, community meetings
3. **Explore options**: Transformation to REH, merger, community ownership
4. **Contact officials**: State and federal support may be available
5. **Advocate for funding**: Emergency stabilization fund access

### If You're a Healthcare Worker

- **Loan repayment**: National Health Service Corps, state programs
- **Rural residency**: Consider rural-focused training programs
- **Scope of practice**: Advocate for appropriate scope expansion
- **Professional organizations**: Join rural-focused sections

### If You're a Student

- **Rural training opportunities**: AHEC, rural rotations
- **Rural scholarships**: Many programs prioritize rural commitment
- **Career planning**: Rural practice can be rewarding and sustainable
