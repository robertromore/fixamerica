# Rural Healthcare: Stakeholders

## Who Is Affected

### Primary Stakeholders

| Group | Impact | Size |
|-------|--------|------|
| Rural residents | Healthcare access, outcomes | 60 million |
| Rural elderly | Medicare-dependent; higher needs | 12 million |
| Rural workers | Employer coverage gaps; occupational health | 25 million |
| Agricultural workers | Seasonal, often uninsured | 2.4 million |
| Rural children | Pediatric access; developmental services | 10 million |
| Rural pregnant women | Maternal care access crisis | 500,000 annually |

### Secondary Stakeholders

**Healthcare Providers**

- Rural hospitals: Financial survival at stake
- Critical Access Hospitals: 1,350 facilities
- Rural Health Clinics: 4,500+ locations
- Community Health Centers: Safety net in rural areas
- Rural physicians: Practice viability, professional isolation
- Rural nurses: Workload, scope of practice issues
- EMS providers: Often volunteer, underfunded

**Employers**

- Agricultural businesses: Worker health and productivity
- Rural manufacturers: Healthcare costs and access
- Small businesses: Coverage affordability
- Government (schools, counties): Largest employers in many rural areas

**Community Stakeholders**

- Local governments: Economic anchor, tax base
- Schools: Nurse access, employee coverage
- Churches and civic organizations: Community health
- Economic development: Healthcare key to recruitment

**State and Federal Government**

- CMS: Rural payment policy
- HRSA: Health professional shortage programs
- USDA: Rural development programs
- State Medicaid agencies: Rural provider participation
- State Offices of Rural Health: Coordination, advocacy

## Who Has Power

### Decision Makers

**Federal**:

- Congress: Rural hospital programs, Medicare/Medicaid policy
- CMS: Payment rates, provider designations, telehealth rules
- HRSA: Workforce programs, community health centers
- USDA: Rural development, broadband
- FCC: Broadband policy

**State**:

- Governors: Medicaid expansion decisions
- State legislatures: State rural health programs
- State Medicaid agencies: Provider rates, managed care
- State health departments: Rural health planning

**Local**:

- County commissioners: Hospital boards, funding
- Hospital boards: Facility decisions
- Community foundations: Local support

### Provider Perspectives

| Concern | Position |
|---------|----------|
| Payment adequacy | Seek higher rural rates |
| Regulatory burden | Reduce requirements for small facilities |
| Workforce support | Loan repayment, training programs |
| Telehealth policy | Permanent expansion of COVID flexibilities |
| Scope of practice | Expand for NPs, PAs in rural areas |
