# Medicare: Citizen Actions

## What You Can Do

### Individual Actions

- **Understand your coverage**: Learn the differences between traditional Medicare and Medicare Advantage before making enrollment decisions
- **Compare plans annually**: Medicare Advantage and Part D plans change yearly; review during Open Enrollment (October 15 - December 7)
- **Appeal denials**: Medicare has strong appeal rights; use them when coverage is denied
- **Report fraud**: Contact 1-800-MEDICARE or OIG if you suspect billing fraud
- **Use preventive services**: Medicare covers many preventive services at no cost
- **Know your rights**: Medicare beneficiaries have protections against discrimination and improper billing
- **Share your story**: Personal experiences shape policy debates

### For Those Approaching Medicare

- **Understand enrollment periods**: Initial Enrollment (around 65th birthday), Annual Open Enrollment, Special Enrollment
- **Research options**: Traditional Medicare + Medigap vs. Medicare Advantage
- **Plan for costs**: Budget for premiums, cost-sharing, and services Medicare doesn't cover
- **Consider working with SHIP counselors**: Free, unbiased help available in every state

### Community Actions

- **Volunteer as SHIP counselor**: Help neighbors navigate Medicare
- **Support senior centers**: Many provide Medicare education
- **Organize enrollment events**: Partner with community organizations
- **Advocate for local resources**: Ensure community has adequate Medicare-accepting providers
- **Support safety net providers**: FQHCs and hospitals serving Medicare populations
- **Help with appeals**: Assist beneficiaries challenging coverage denials

### Political Actions

- **Contact elected officials**: Call, write, or visit representatives about Medicare policy
- **Testify at hearings**: Share personal experiences at congressional or state hearings
- **Support Medicare expansion**: Advocate for lower eligibility age, expanded benefits
- **Oppose cuts and privatization**: Monitor legislative threats to traditional Medicare
- **Vote on healthcare**: Prioritize candidates' Medicare positions
- **Join advocacy campaigns**: Participate in organized Medicare advocacy

## Staying Informed

### Key Dates

| Period | Dates | Action |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Medicare Open Enrollment | October 15 - December 7 | Change MA/Part D plans |
| Medicare Advantage Open Enrollment | January 1 - March 31 | Switch MA plans or return to traditional |
| Initial Enrollment | 7 months around 65th birthday | Enroll in Medicare |
| Special Enrollment | Various triggers | Enroll due to life changes |

### Warning Signs to Monitor

- **Trust Fund depletion**: 2031 projected depletion without action
- **Medicare Advantage oversight**: Prior authorization abuses, network adequacy
- **Drug price negotiation implementation**: IRA provisions rolling out 2024-2029
- **Benefit cuts**: Budget proposals threatening traditional Medicare
- **Premium support proposals**: Voucher plans that would undermine guaranteed benefits
- **Provider access**: Physicians limiting Medicare patients

## Effective Advocacy

### Communicating with Legislators

**What works**:

- Personal stories about Medicare's impact
- Specific policy requests (not just "support Medicare")
- Local data and constituent perspectives
- Constituent meetings (in-district or DC)
- Coordinated campaigns with allied organizations

**Key messages**:

1. "Strengthen traditional Medicare, don't privatize it"
2. "Close coverage gaps for dental, vision, and hearing"
3. "Hold Medicare Advantage plans accountable"
4. "Expand drug negotiation to reduce costs"
5. "Ensure solvency without cutting benefits"

### Building Coalitions

**Natural allies**:

- Senior centers and advocacy groups
- Disability rights organizations
- Labor unions (retiree benefits)
- Healthcare providers serving Medicare populations
- Faith communities with elderly members
- Progressive political organizations

### Media Engagement

- Write letters to the editor on Medicare issues
- Share personal stories with journalists covering healthcare
- Amplify Medicare policy coverage on social media
- Participate in town halls and public forums
- Document problems with MA plans for investigations

## Long-term Engagement

### Ongoing Commitments

- Stay informed about Medicare policy developments
- Vote in every election, prioritizing Medicare positions
- Support organizations advocating for Medicare
- Help others navigate the program
- Share experiences to humanize policy debates

### Building Capacity

- Develop expertise in Medicare policy
- Train others on advocacy skills
- Build relationships with elected officials
- Connect with national advocacy networks
- Mentor new advocates
