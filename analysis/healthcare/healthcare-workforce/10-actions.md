# Healthcare Workforce: Actions

## What Citizens Can Do

Healthcare workforce policy may seem technical, but citizens can take meaningful action to address shortages and maldistribution in their communities. This guide outlines concrete steps for individuals at various levels of engagement.

## Individual Actions

### If You're Considering a Healthcare Career

**Explore High-Need Fields**:
- Primary care (family medicine, internal medicine, pediatrics)
- Psychiatry and mental health
- Geriatrics
- Nursing (especially nurse practitioner)
- Physician assistant
- Community health worker

**Investigate Underserved Area Practice**:
- National Health Service Corps scholarships and loan repayment
- State loan repayment programs
- Indian Health Service opportunities
- Rural training tracks in residency
- FQHC employment options

**Understand Financial Options**:
- NHSC scholarship covers tuition for service commitment
- Public Service Loan Forgiveness (10 years public sector)
- Income-driven repayment plans
- State-specific programs (vary by location)

### If You're a Healthcare Professional

**Address Your Own Sustainability**:
- Recognize burnout symptoms early
- Seek mental health support (confidential options available)
- Set boundaries where possible
- Connect with peer support networks
- Know licensing board mental health question policies in your state

**Advocate Within Your Profession**:
- Join professional associations actively engaged in workforce policy
- Participate in surveys on burnout and working conditions
- Mentor students and trainees
- Serve as preceptor for training programs
- Support scope of practice expansion for appropriate roles

**Consider Underserved Practice**:
- Part-time service at FQHCs
- Telehealth for rural areas
- Free clinic volunteering
- Teaching at community-based programs
- J-1 visa waiver physician mentoring

### If You're a Healthcare Consumer

**Support Local Healthcare Infrastructure**:
- Use local providers rather than traveling when possible
- Attend community health center board meetings (they're public)
- Advocate for rural hospital preservation
- Support telehealth access expansion

**Contact Elected Officials**:
- Ask about GME expansion support
- Request loan repayment program funding
- Inquire about scope of practice positions
- Express concern about healthcare access in your area

## Community Organizing

### Local Advocacy

**Health Professional Shortage Area Designation**:
- Check if your area qualifies as HPSA
- If not designated but underserved, petition for review
- HRSA data warehouse has designation criteria
- Local health departments can assist

**Community Health Center Support**:
- Federally Qualified Health Centers serve underserved areas
- Volunteer for FQHC boards (consumer majority required)
- Advocate for FQHC expansion in your area
- Support FQHC funding in local and federal budgets

**Local Workforce Development**:
- Support healthcare career pathways in high schools
- Advocate for nursing program capacity at community colleges
- Encourage local Area Health Education Centers
- Connect youth to healthcare career exposure

### Coalition Building

**Rural Health Coalitions**:
- State Rural Health Associations in every state
- National Rural Health Association for federal issues
- Hospital associations often have rural sections
- Farm Bureau sometimes engages on rural health

**Patient Advocacy Groups**:
- Condition-specific groups often address access
- AARP engaged on workforce for aging population
- Disability rights organizations address accessibility
- Mental health advocacy groups address psychiatry shortage

**Professional Association Engagement**:
- State medical societies have workforce committees
- State nursing associations active on scope of practice
- Attend public meetings and comment periods

## Political Action

### Federal Level

**Contact Congress About**:
- Resident Physician Shortage Reduction Act (GME expansion)
- THCGME reauthorization and funding
- NHSC appropriations
- Conrad 30 visa waiver reauthorization
- Telehealth permanence legislation

**Sample Message Points**:
- "I support expanding Medicare-funded residency positions to address the physician shortage."
- "Please fund the National Health Service Corps to place more providers in underserved areas."
- "I urge reauthorization of the Conrad 30 program to bring physicians to shortage areas."
- "Please make telehealth flexibilities permanent to improve access."

**Key Congressional Contacts**:
- Senate HELP Committee (authorizing)
- Senate Finance Committee (Medicare)
- Senate Appropriations (funding)
- House Energy and Commerce Committee
- House Ways and Means Committee
- House Appropriations

### State Level

**Scope of Practice Legislation**:
- Full practice authority for nurse practitioners
- PA practice agreement modernization
- Pharmacist prescribing expansion
- Interstate licensure compact adoption

**State Loan Repayment**:
- Advocate for state loan repayment programs
- Support state matching for federal programs
- Fund state workforce development initiatives

**Nursing Education**:
- Appropriations for nursing faculty
- State university nursing program expansion
- Community college nursing capacity

**How to Engage**:
- Find your state legislators
- Attend town halls and public meetings
- Submit written testimony on relevant bills
- Join state-level advocacy organizations

### Local Level

**Hospital Boards**:
- Many hospital boards are elected or appointed publicly
- Advocate for workforce development investment
- Support GME program creation or expansion
- Address clinician working conditions

**School Boards and Community Colleges**:
- Healthcare career pathway programs
- Nursing and allied health program capacity
- Partnership with local healthcare employers

## Healthcare Professional Actions

### For Physicians

**Support GME Expansion**:
- Contact AAMC and specialty society representatives
- Support medical school expansion appropriately
- Serve as GME faculty in community settings
- Mentor students considering primary care

**Address Scope of Practice Thoughtfully**:
- Support team-based care models
- Engage evidence rather than reflexive opposition
- Focus on collaboration rather than supervision
- Recognize workforce realities

**Reduce Administrative Burden**:
- Participate in practice transformation
- Support prior authorization reform
- Advocate for EHR improvement
- Document burden for policymakers

### For Nurses

**Scope of Practice Advocacy**:
- Support state NP full practice authority campaigns
- Contact state legislators
- Provide testimony on nurse practitioner outcomes
- Counter misinformation about NP quality

**Address Nursing Shortage**:
- Consider faculty roles (even part-time)
- Precept nursing students
- Support workplace improvements
- Participate in union or professional association advocacy

**Well-Being Advocacy**:
- Advocate for safe staffing legislation
- Support workplace violence prevention
- Demand mental health resources
- Model sustainable practice

### For Students and Trainees

**Advocate for Training Expansion**:
- Support GME cap increase through AMSA, SNMA
- Contact Congress about residency funding
- Share unmatched graduate experiences
- Advocate for loan repayment expansion

**Consider Underserved Practice**:
- Explore NHSC opportunities early
- Investigate rural training tracks
- Connect with FQHC mentors
- Research state loan repayment programs

## Employer and Institutional Actions

### Healthcare Employers

**Workforce Development**:
- Grow-your-own training programs
- Tuition support for employees
- Partnership with local schools
- Pipeline from high school through employment

**Retention Investment**:
- Address burnout systematically
- Competitive compensation
- Flexible scheduling options
- Mental health support without stigma

**Advocacy Engagement**:
- Support GME expansion
- State and federal policy engagement
- Coalition participation

### Educational Institutions

**Expand Capacity**:
- Increase class sizes where sustainable
- Add faculty positions
- Develop simulation capacity
- Create accelerated pathways

**Mission-Driven Training**:
- Recruit diverse and underserved-origin students
- Community-based training emphasis
- Primary care and generalist focus
- Underserved area placement support

## Ongoing Engagement

### Stay Informed

**Key Sources**:
- AAMC workforce projections (biennial)
- Kaiser Family Foundation state data
- HRSA workforce reports
- Professional association newsletters

### Build Relationships

**With Elected Officials**:
- Regular communication on workforce issues
- Share personal stories and data
- Offer to serve as resource
- Attend town halls consistently

**With Professional Associations**:
- Join and participate actively
- Serve on workforce committees
- Connect local concerns to policy

**With Community Organizations**:
- Health-related nonprofits
- Community health centers
- Rural health coalitions
- Patient advocacy groups
