# Mental Health Integration: Implementation Roadmap

## Strategic Approach

Mental health system transformation requires immediate crisis response improvements while building long-term integration and workforce capacity. The roadmap sequences visible improvements to build momentum for systemic change.

## Phase 1: Crisis Response (Years 1-2)

### 988 and Crisis System Enhancement

**Actions**:

- Full funding for 988 call center capacity
- Grant program for mobile crisis teams in all states
- Crisis stabilization center seed funding
- 911-988 dispatch integration pilots
- Crisis workforce training

**Milestones**:

- 988 answer rate >95% nationwide
- Mobile crisis available in 50% of communities by Year 2
- 100 new crisis stabilization centers
- 10 state dispatch integration pilots

### Parity Enforcement Acceleration

**Actions**:

- Hire 100 federal parity enforcement staff
- Launch proactive audit program
- Require insurer comparative analysis reports
- Issue enforcement guidance
- First significant penalties for violations

**Milestones**:

- Audit program operational Year 1
- First insurer compliance reports Year 2
- Penalty actions initiated
- Public reporting of parity data

### Immediate Workforce Support

**Actions**:

- Emergency loan repayment funding increase
- Crisis workforce training programs
- Telehealth policy permanence
- Burnout prevention programs

**Milestones**:

- 5,000 additional loan repayment awards by Year 2
- 10,000 crisis workers trained
- Telehealth permanent in Medicare, Medicaid

## Phase 2: Integration Acceleration (Years 3-5)

### Primary Care Integration

**Actions**:

- Require all payers to cover Collaborative Care Model
- Practice transformation grants for primary care
- Regional psychiatric consultation networks
- Integration quality measures in payment
- Primary care mental health training

**Milestones**:

- CoCM coverage universal by Year 4
- 30% of primary care practices using integration models
- Psychiatric consultation available in all regions
- Depression screening rates above 80%

### Crisis Continuum Completion

**Actions**:

- Mobile crisis teams in all communities
- Crisis stabilization center in every region
- Crisis residential expansion
- Psychiatric bed capacity increase
- IMD exclusion modification

**Milestones**:

- Mobile crisis available nationwide by Year 5
- ED boarding reduced by 50%
- Crisis stabilization in all metropolitan areas
- Inpatient capacity adequate for demand

### Workforce Pipeline

**Actions**:

- Double psychiatry residency slots
- Expand psychology, social work, counseling training
- Psychiatric NP program growth
- Peer specialist expansion
- Diversity pipeline programs

**Milestones**:

- 2,000 new psychiatry residency slots
- 50% increase in behavioral health training capacity
- 20,000 new peer specialists
- Workforce diversity increased 25%

## Phase 3: System Transformation (Years 5-10)

### Financing Integration

**Actions**:

- Phase out behavioral health carve-outs
- Integrated behavioral health in all Medicaid managed care
- Medicare behavioral health improvements
- Commercial market integration requirements
- Value-based payment for behavioral health

**Milestones**:

- Carve-outs eliminated in Medicaid
- Integrated payment models in 50% of commercial market
- Behavioral health included in ACOs, bundles

### Workforce Adequacy

**Actions**:

- Sustained training expansion
- Full loan repayment program
- Competitive reimbursement rates
- Scope of practice modernization
- Geographic redistribution

**Milestones**:

- Workforce shortage reduced by 50%
- Reimbursement rates at parity with physical health
- Full practice authority for NPs in all states
- Mental health HPSAs reduced by 40%

### Comprehensive System

**Actions**:

- Full crisis continuum in all communities
- Integration standard of care
- Outcome measurement and accountability
- Health equity achievement
- Sustained funding mechanisms

**Milestones**:

- Treatment gap reduced to 25% (from 55%)
- Crisis response appropriate in 90% of cases
- ED psychiatric boarding eliminated
- Suicide rate declining

## Risk Mitigation

### Political Risks

**Concerns**:

- Funding sustainability
- Parity enforcement backlash
- State resistance

**Mitigation**:

- Build bipartisan coalition (strong support exists)
- Demonstrate cost-effectiveness
- Federal-state partnership model
- Incremental implementation

### Implementation Risks

**Concerns**:

- Workforce takes years to develop
- Crisis system build-out complex
- Integration requires culture change

**Mitigation**:

- Multiple workforce pathways
- Technical assistance for implementation
- Peer learning networks
- Phased approach

### Unintended Consequences

**Concerns**:

- Integration could dilute specialty care
- Crisis diversion without adequate services
- Workforce redistribution creates new gaps

**Mitigation**:

- Maintain specialty capacity
- Crisis continuum completeness
- Geographic equity requirements
