# Mental Health Integration: Solutions

## Reform Framework

### Principles

- **Integration**: Mental health as part of overall health, not separate system
- **Early intervention**: Treat early to prevent crisis and chronic illness
- **Accessible crisis response**: Appropriate care for mental health emergencies
- **Parity enforcement**: Legal requirements must be real, not theoretical
- **Workforce adequacy**: Cannot treat people without providers
- **Equity**: Address disparities in access and outcomes
- **Person-centered**: Treatment should respect individual preferences and goals

## Proposed Solutions

### Solution 1: Comprehensive Parity Enforcement

**Description**: Make mental health parity law real through aggressive enforcement and transparency.

**Components**:

1. **Data transparency**
   - Require insurers to report comparative data (mental health vs. medical)
   - Prior authorization denial rates
   - Network adequacy by specialty
   - Reimbursement rates by service type
   - Out-of-network utilization rates

2. **Enforcement strengthening**
   - Dedicated parity enforcement staff at DOL, HHS, state level
   - Proactive audits (not just complaint-based)
   - Meaningful penalties for violations
   - Private right of action

3. **Specific standards**
   - Network adequacy standards (time and distance)
   - Reimbursement rate floors (percentage of Medicare)
   - Prior authorization limits
   - Out-of-network coverage requirements

**Evidence**: States with stronger parity enforcement show better access. Current enforcement is inadequate.

**Challenges**: Insurance industry opposition; enforcement resource requirements; defining violations.

### Solution 2: Primary Care Integration

**Description**: Scale collaborative care and other integration models nationwide.

**Components**:

1. **Payment reform**
   - Require all payers to cover Collaborative Care Model (CoCM)
   - Value-based payment alternatives to fee-for-service
   - Integration incentives in Medicare, Medicaid
   - Bundled payment for integrated care

2. **Practice transformation**
   - Technical assistance for primary care practices
   - Workflow redesign support
   - Health IT integration
   - Care manager training

3. **Consulting psychiatry**
   - Regional psychiatric consultation services
   - Tele-psychiatry for rural and underserved
   - Consultation-liaison programs
   - Project ECHO for primary care

4. **Measurement**
   - Integration quality measures
   - Depression screening and follow-up tracking
   - Patient outcome monitoring
   - Payment tied to integration

**Evidence**: Collaborative Care Model has strongest evidence base for primary care integration. Improves outcomes, reduces costs for depression.

**Challenges**: Practice transformation is difficult; workforce constraints; payment change resistance.

### Solution 3: Crisis Continuum Development

**Description**: Build comprehensive crisis response system nationwide.

**Components**:

1. **988 enhancement**
   - Adequate funding for call centers
   - Staffing for demand
   - Integration with dispatch
   - Follow-up after calls

2. **Mobile crisis teams**
   - Every community with 24/7 mobile crisis
   - Mental health responders (not just police)
   - Co-responder models where appropriate
   - Dispatch integration with 911/988

3. **Crisis stabilization centers**
   - 23-hour observation/stabilization
   - Alternative to ED for psychiatric crisis
   - Step-down from inpatient
   - Every region served

4. **Crisis residential**
   - Short-term residential for stabilization
   - Peer support
   - Community-based alternative to hospital
   - Transition support

5. **Inpatient capacity**
   - Adequate psychiatric bed supply
   - IMD exclusion modification
   - Timely transfer from ED
   - Discharge planning integration

**Evidence**: Crisis continuum reduces ED utilization, improves outcomes, reduces law enforcement involvement.

**Challenges**: Building infrastructure takes time; workforce for crisis services; funding sustainability.

### Solution 4: Justice System Diversion and Crisis Response

**Description**: Create non-police mental health crisis response systems that prevent unnecessary criminalization and provide appropriate care.

**Components**:

1. **CAHOOTS Model (Unarmed Crisis Responders)**
   - Mobile teams of EMTs and mental health crisis workers
   - Respond to mental health calls instead of police
   - No weapons, no law enforcement authority
   - Focus on de-escalation, assessment, and connection to services
   - Eugene, Oregon model: handles 17% of 911 calls at fraction of police cost
   - Expanding to Denver (STAR), San Francisco, Oakland, and 20+ cities

2. **Crisis Stabilization Centers**
   - 23-hour observation and stabilization facilities
   - Walk-in and drop-off access (including by police)
   - Alternative to emergency department for psychiatric crisis
   - Lower cost, better outcomes than ED boarding
   - "No wrong door" policyâ€”accept all who present
   - Staffed for medical clearance on-site

3. **988 Crisis Line Infrastructure**
   - National suicide and mental health crisis line (launched 2022)
   - Local call centers with trained crisis counselors
   - Integration with mobile crisis dispatch
   - Text and chat options
   - Specialized lines (veterans, LGBTQ+, Spanish-language)
   - Follow-up protocols after crisis calls
   - Funding gap: $billions needed to staff adequately nationwide

4. **Non-Police Mental Health Response**
   - Dedicated dispatch codes for mental health calls
   - 911 call-taker training to identify divertible calls
   - Co-responder models (clinician + officer) as transition
   - Civilian-only response as goal for non-violent situations
   - Community paramedicine programs
   - Peer support specialist integration

5. **Pre-Arrest Diversion Programs**
   - Law Enforcement Assisted Diversion (LEAD)
   - Officer discretion to refer to services instead of arrest
   - No booking, no charges for low-level offenses
   - Case management and service connection
   - Evidence shows reduced recidivism, lower costs

**Evidence**: CAHOOTS model diverts 3-8% of 911 call volume with zero police backup needed in 99%+ of responses. Crisis stabilization centers reduce ED psychiatric boarding by 30-50%. Pre-arrest diversion programs show 60% reduction in recidivism.

**Challenges**: Building trust with law enforcement; 911 dispatch integration; sustainable funding; workforce availability; liability concerns.

**Cross-Domain Connection**: The criminalization of mental illness results from decades of deinstitutionalization without adequate community investment. ### Solution 5: Workforce Development

**Description**: Dramatically expand the behavioral health workforce.

**Components**:

1. **Training expansion**
   - Double psychiatry residency slots
   - Increase psychology, social work, counseling training
   - Psychiatric NP and PA programs
   - Community health worker training
   - Peer specialist certification

2. **Loan repayment**
   - NHSC expansion for behavioral health
   - State loan repayment programs
   - Higher amounts for shortage areas
   - Expanded eligible professions

3. **Scope of practice**
   - Full practice authority for psychiatric NPs
   - Psychologist prescribing in more states
   - Counselor insurance inclusion
   - Peer specialist Medicaid billing

4. **Retention**
   - Competitive reimbursement
   - Administrative burden reduction
   - Burnout prevention
   - Supervision and support

5. **Diversity**
   - Pipeline programs for underrepresented groups
   - Culturally competent training
   - Community-based training sites
   - Loan forgiveness targeting diversity

**Evidence**: Workforce shortage is primary access barrier. Loan repayment programs increase rural/underserved practice.

**Challenges**: Training pipeline takes years; scope of practice politics; reimbursement economics.

### Solution 6: Financing Reform

**Description**: Restructure mental health financing to support integration and access.

**Components**:

1. **End behavioral health carve-outs**
   - Integrate behavioral health into medical benefit
   - Single network for all services
   - Coordinated care management
   - Unified prior authorization

2. **Medicaid improvements**
   - IMD exclusion modification or repeal
   - Increased provider rates
   - Integration incentives
   - Managed care accountability

3. **Medicare improvements**
   - Expand telehealth permanently
   - Reduce barriers to psychology/social work billing
   - CoCM adoption incentives
   - Marriage and family therapist coverage

4. **Commercial market**
   - Network adequacy standards
   - Reimbursement floors
   - Prior authorization limits
   - Out-of-network protections

**Evidence**: Carve-outs create fragmentation. Payment adequacy determines provider participation.

**Challenges**: Insurer business model changes; transition complexity; cost concerns.

## Sequencing

1. **Immediate**: Parity enforcement; 988 funding; crisis team expansion
2. **Near-term**: CoCM payment requirement; workforce loan repayment; crisis centers
3. **Medium-term**: Integration across systems; carve-out elimination; workforce pipeline
4. **Long-term**: Full integration; adequate workforce; comprehensive crisis system
