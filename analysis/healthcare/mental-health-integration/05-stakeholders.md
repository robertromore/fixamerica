# Mental Health Integration: Stakeholders

## Who Is Affected

### Primary Stakeholders

| Group | Impact | Size |
|-------|--------|------|
| People with mental illness | Treatment access, quality, outcomes | 50 million |
| People with serious mental illness | Most acute need | 14 million |
| People with substance use disorders | Often co-occurring; similar access issues | 46 million |
| Families and caregivers | Support burden; system navigation | 50+ million |
| Children/adolescents with mental health needs | Early intervention critical | 9.5 million |
| Suicidal individuals | Crisis services access | 12 million (serious thoughts annually) |

### Secondary Stakeholders

**Healthcare Providers**

- Primary care providers: Most mental health first seen in primary care
- Psychiatrists: Severe shortage; overloaded
- Psychologists: Key treatment providers
- Clinical social workers: Largest behavioral health workforce
- Psychiatric nurses: Hospital and community care
- Counselors: Large workforce; insurance barriers

**Healthcare Systems**

- Hospitals: ED boarding crisis; inpatient psychiatric
- Community mental health centers: Safety net care
- Federally qualified health centers: Integration models
- Insurance companies: Coverage, network, reimbursement decisions

**Other Systems**

- Law enforcement: Default mental health responders
- Jails/prisons: De facto psychiatric facilities
- Schools: Youth mental health identification
- Employers: Workplace mental health

## Who Has Power

### Decision Makers

**Federal**:

- Congress: Parity enforcement, funding, Medicaid rules
- CMS: Medicare coverage, Medicaid oversight
- SAMHSA: Block grants, 988, treatment programs
- DOL: ERISA parity enforcement
- HHS: Parity regulations, coordination

**State**:

- State mental health authorities: System planning
- Medicaid agencies: Largest payer; program design
- Insurance commissioners: Commercial market regulation
- Governors: Priority setting, funding

**Local**:

- County mental health agencies: Local services
- Law enforcement: Crisis response
- School districts: Youth services

### Influencers

| Actor | Influence | Position |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| NAMI (National Alliance on Mental Illness) | Largest advocacy org | Pro-investment, parity |

| APA (American Psychological Association) | Psychologist voice | Scope of practice, integration |

## Stakeholder Interests

### Patient and Family Perspectives

| Concern | Priority |
|---------|----------|
| Access to treatment | Critical |
| Affordable care | Critical |
| Quality of care | High |
| Crisis response | High |
| Reduced stigma | Important |
| Family involvement | Important |
| Integration with physical health | Growing |

### Provider Perspectives

| Concern | Position |
|---------|----------|
| Reimbursement adequacy | Primary concern |
| Administrative burden | Major issue |
| Prior authorization | Barrier to care |
| Workforce support | Need training, loan repayment |
| Scope of practice | Varies by profession |

### System Perspectives

| Stakeholder | Interest |
|-------------|----------|
| Primary care | Support for integration; don't want to be overwhelmed |
| Hospitals | ED boarding is crisis; need discharge options |
| Insurance | Manage costs; comply with parity |
| Community mental health | Adequate funding; Medicaid rates |
| Law enforcement | Diversion options; crisis services |
