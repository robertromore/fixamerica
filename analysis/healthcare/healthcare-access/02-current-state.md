# Healthcare Access: Current State

## Overview

The United States healthcare system produces paradoxical outcomes: world-class care for some, inaccessibility for many. Access varies dramatically by state, insurance status, geography, race, and income. While the Affordable Care Act reduced the uninsured rate by nearly half, 27.5 million Americans remain without coverage, and tens of millions more are underinsured with coverage that fails to protect against financial catastrophe.

## Insurance Coverage Landscape

### Coverage by Source

| Coverage Type | Population | Percentage | Trend |
|---------------|------------|------------|-------|
| Employer-sponsored | 156 million | 49% | Stable but eroding |
| Medicare | 65 million | 20% | Growing (aging population) |
| Medicaid/CHIP | 85 million | 26% | Post-pandemic decline |

| Uninsured | 27.5 million | 8.4% | Near historic low |

### Who Remains Uninsured

| Population | Uninsured Rate | Primary Barrier |
|------------|----------------|-----------------|
| Adults in coverage gap | 100% | Non-expansion states |
| Undocumented immigrants | ~45% | Legal exclusion |
| Young adults (19-25) | 12.2% | Affordability, invincibility |
| Low-income workers | 15%+ | No employer coverage, income too high for Medicaid |
| Part-time/gig workers | 18%+ | Excluded from employer plans |
| Hispanic/Latino | 17.7% | Multiple barriers |
| American Indian/Alaska Native | 19.1% | IHS underfunding, rural access |

### Underinsurance Crisis

Beyond the uninsured, underinsurance affects an estimated 43 million Americans:

**High Cost-Sharing**

| Metric | 2014 | 2024 | Change |
|--------|------|------|--------|
| Average deductible (individual) | $1,217 | $1,735 | +43% |
| Average deductible (family) | $2,271 | $3,755 | +65% |
| Out-of-pocket maximum (individual) | $6,350 | $9,450 | +49% |
| Workers with deductible >$2,000 | 18% | 31% | +72% |

**Underinsurance Consequences**

| Effect | Prevalence |
|--------|------------|
| Delayed care due to cost | 29% of adults |
| Skipped medications due to cost | 21% of adults |
| Problems paying medical bills | 41% of adults |
| Medical debt | 100 million Americans |

## Access Barriers Beyond Insurance

### Financial Barriers

| Barrier | Prevalence | Impact |
|---------|------------|--------|
| High deductibles | 31% have >$2k | Delayed primary care |
| Copays for specialists | $50+ common | Avoid specialty care |
| Prescription costs | 1 in 4 ration medications | Non-adherence |
| Surprise billing | Reduced by No Surprises Act | Still occurs out-of-network |

### Non-Financial Barriers

| Barrier | Affected Population | Consequence |
|---------|---------------------|-------------|
| Transportation | 3.6 million miss care annually | Delayed treatment |
| Language | 25 million LEP individuals | Communication gaps |
| Disability access | 61 million with disabilities | Physical/communication barriers |
| Work schedule conflicts | Hourly workers | Cannot take time off |
| Childcare | Parents | Cannot attend appointments |

### Provider-Side Barriers

| Issue | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| Medicaid acceptance | Only 71% of physicians accept new Medicaid patients |
| Primary care shortages | 100 million in shortage areas |
| Wait times | Weeks/months for specialists |
| Prior authorization | Average 20 hours/week per practice |
| Narrow networks | Limited provider choice |
