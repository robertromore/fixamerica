# Healthcare Access: Stakeholders

## Overview

Healthcare access involves a complex web of stakeholders with divergent and sometimes conflicting interests. Understanding who is affected, who has power, and what motivates each actor is essential for building coalitions and designing politically viable reforms.

---

## Who Is Affected

### Primary Stakeholders

| Group | Size | Primary Impact | Key Concerns |
|-------|------|----------------|--------------|
| Uninsured individuals | 27.5 million | No coverage, delayed care | Medical debt, emergency-only access |
| Underinsured individuals | 43 million | High out-of-pocket costs | Rationing care, financial stress |
| Rural residents | 60 million | Provider shortages | Hospital closures, travel burden |
| Coverage gap adults | 2.2 million | No affordable options | Non-expansion states |
| Gig/part-time workers | 20+ million | No employer coverage | Expensive individual market |
| Undocumented immigrants | 11 million | Legal exclusion | Emergency-only care |
| People with chronic conditions | 133 million | Ongoing access needs | Pre-existing protections vital |
| Low-income elderly | 12+ million | Medicare gaps | Part D costs, supplemental needs |
| Disabled individuals | 61 million | Complex care needs | Accessibility, Medicaid dependence |

### Geographic Communities

| Community Type | Access Challenge | Size |
|----------------|------------------|------|
| Rural areas | Hospital closures, provider shortages | 60 million |
| Health professional shortage areas | Limited primary care | 100+ million |
| Frontier areas | Extreme distance to care | 6 million |
| Urban underserved | Safety net strain | Varies |
| Border communities | Mixed documentation status | Millions |

### Demographic Groups with Disparities

| Group | Disparity | Contributing Factors |
|-------|-----------|----------------------|
| Hispanic/Latino | 2.5x uninsured rate | Documentation, coverage gap |
| American Indian/Alaska Native | 2.7x uninsured rate | IHS underfunding, rural |
| Black Americans | 1.4x uninsured rate | Employment patterns, discrimination |
| Young adults (19-34) | Highest uninsured rates | Cost, perceived invincibility |
| Non-citizens | High uninsured | Legal barriers, fear |

## Healthcare Industry Stakeholders

### Insurance Industry

**Key Actors**

| Organization | Role | Membership |
|--------------|------|------------|
| AHIP (America's Health Insurance Plans) | Trade association | Major insurers |
| Blue Cross Blue Shield Association | Nonprofit coordination | 35 BCBS plans |
| Individual insurers (UHC, Cigna, Aetna, etc.) | Market participants | Billions in revenue |

**Interests and Positions**

| Interest | Position on Expansion |
|----------|----------------------|
| Enrollment growth | Supports expansion (more customers) |
| Regulatory burden | Opposes mandates, requirements |
| Public option/Medicare for All | Strongly opposes |
| ACA subsidies | Supports (stabilizes market) |
| Medicaid managed care | Supports (revenue opportunity) |

### Hospital Industry

**Key Actors**

| Organization | Role | Membership |
|--------------|------|------------|
| American Hospital Association (AHA) | Trade association | 5,000+ hospitals |
| Federation of American Hospitals | For-profit hospitals | Investor-owned |
| America's Essential Hospitals | Safety-net hospitals | 300+ hospitals |
| National Rural Health Association | Rural hospitals | 21,000 members |

**Interests and Positions**

| Interest | Position |
|----------|----------|
| Uncompensated care reduction | Strongly supports expansion |
| Medicaid expansion | Strongly supports |
| Medicaid reimbursement rates | Wants increases |
| DSH payments | Wants maintained |
| Rural hospital support | Priority |

### Physician Community

**Key Actors**

| Organization | Focus | Membership |
|--------------|-------|------------|
| American Medical Association (AMA) | Broad physician advocacy | 240,000+ |
| American Academy of Family Physicians (AAFP) | Primary care | 130,000+ |
| American College of Physicians (ACP) | Internal medicine | 160,000+ |
| Specialty societies | Specialty-specific | Varies |

**Interests and Positions**

| Interest | Position |
|----------|----------|
| Patient access | Generally supports expansion |
| Physician autonomy | Concerns about government control |
| Reimbursement rates | Wants adequate payment |
| Administrative burden | Opposes prior authorization |
| Scope of practice | Mixed on expanding non-physician roles |

### Pharmaceutical Industry

**Key Actors**

| Organization | Role |
|--------------|------|
| PhRMA | Trade association |
| BIO | Biotech industry |
| Individual manufacturers | Market participants |

**Interests and Positions**

| Interest | Position |
|----------|----------|

## Employer Stakeholders

### Large Employers

**Interests**

| Interest | Concern |
|----------|---------|
| Cost control | Rising premiums burden |
| Workforce health | Productivity, retention |
| Administrative simplicity | Complex compliance |
| Competitive position | Disadvantage vs. other countries |

**Positions**

| Issue | Position |
|-------|----------|
| Employer mandate | Divided (burden vs. level playing field) |
| Public option | Some support (exit strategy) |
| Single-payer | Divided (cost shift vs. off the hook) |

### Small Employers

**Interests and Positions**

| Interest | Position |
|----------|----------|
| Affordability | Struggle to provide coverage |
| Mandate exemption | Want maintained (under 50 employees) |
| Association health plans | Support (pooling) |
| SHOP marketplace | Limited use |
