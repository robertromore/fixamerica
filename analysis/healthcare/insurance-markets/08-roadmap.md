# Insurance Markets: Implementation Roadmap

## Strategic Approach

Insurance market reform requires building on ACA successes while addressing remaining gaps. The roadmap sequences politically viable reforms to reduce the uninsured, improve coverage quality, and build toward universal coverage.

## Phase 1: Foundation (Years 1-2)

### Permanent ACA Enhancement

**Actions**:

- Make ARP-enhanced subsidies permanent
- Remove 400% FPL subsidy cliff permanently
- Increase cost-sharing reductions for <200% FPL
- Fully fund navigator programs ($500 million annually)
- Restore risk corridor payments for marketplace stability

**Milestones**:

- Legislation enacted within Year 1
- Uninsured rate reduced by 2-3 million
- Marketplace premiums stabilized

### Auto-Enrollment Implementation

**Actions**:

- Link ACA enrollment to tax filing
- Default uninsured tax filers into lowest-cost plan with opt-out
- Coordinate with state Medicaid systems
- Continuous enrollment for income-eligible

**Milestones**:

- IRS-CMS data sharing agreement by Year 1
- Auto-enrollment operational by Year 2
- Projected enrollment increase: 3-5 million

### Prior Authorization Reform

**Actions**:

- Federal standards for prior auth timing (24-72 hours)
- Require electronic processing
- Establish gold card programs
- Public reporting of denial rates
- Automatic external review for upheld denials

**Milestones**:

- Regulations finalized Year 1
- Full implementation by Year 2
- Reduction in inappropriate denials

## Phase 2: Building Momentum (Years 3-5)

### Public Option Creation

**Actions**:

- Establish Medicare Part E (public option)
- Available on all ACA exchanges
- Provider participation tied to Medicare
- Payment rates at Medicare + 5%
- Employer purchase option

**Milestones**:

- Legislation enacted Year 2
- Enrollment begins Year 4
- Projected enrollment: 5-10 million by Year 5
- Premium benchmark established

### Antitrust Action

**Actions**:

- FTC/DOJ healthcare merger review strengthening
- Challenge recent anticompetitive mergers
- Prohibit most-favored-nation clauses
- Review vertical integration (insurer-PBM-provider)
- Hospital system conduct investigation

**Milestones**:

- Enhanced merger guidelines by Year 2
- Major merger challenges initiated
- Market concentration metrics established

### Network Adequacy Enforcement

**Actions**:

- Strengthen federal network standards
- Require appointment availability testing
- Secret shopper audit program
- Substantial penalties for violations
- Extend to ERISA plans where possible

**Milestones**:

- Regulations finalized Year 3
- Audit program operational Year 4
- Consumer access metrics improved

## Phase 3: Structural Reform (Years 5-10)

### Employer Market Transformation

**Actions**:

- Cap employer tax exclusion at 80th percentile plan value
- Make exclusion refundable for low/moderate income
- Allow employer contribution to public option
- Portable coverage pilot programs
- Small employer purchasing cooperatives

**Milestones**:

- Tax legislation enacted Year 5-6
- Cap implemented Year 7
- Revenue directed to coverage expansion

### All-Payer Rate Setting Pilots

**Actions**:

- Federal waivers for state all-payer systems
- Technical assistance for implementation
- Evaluation framework
- Expansion based on results

**Milestones**:

- 5 state pilots by Year 7
- Evaluation complete by Year 10
- National framework developed

### Universal Coverage Path

**Actions**:

- Reduce uninsured to <5% through reforms above
- Evaluate public option uptake and performance
- Assess Medicare for All political feasibility
- Develop transition plan for remaining uninsured

**Milestones**:

- Uninsured rate <5% by Year 8
- Universal coverage legislation by Year 10
- Implementation plan for final gaps

## Success Metrics

| Goal | Metric | Target |
|------|--------|--------|
| Coverage | Uninsured rate | <5% |
| Affordability | Avg. premium as % income | <8% |
| Adequacy | Underinsured rate | <15% |
| Access | Network adequacy compliance | >95% |
| Competition | Markets with 3+ insurers | >90% |
| Satisfaction | Consumer satisfaction | >80% |

## Risk Mitigation

### Political Resistance

**Risks**:

- ACA repeal attempts continue
- Public option faces insurance industry opposition
- Tax exclusion reform unpopular with some

**Mitigation**:

- Build coalitions around popular provisions
- Demonstrate savings to employers and consumers
- Phase changes to minimize disruption
- Protect beneficiary choice

### Market Disruption

**Risks**:

- Public option destabilizes private market
- Insurers exit markets
- Provider participation declines

**Mitigation**:

- Set public option payment rates appropriately
- Maintain level playing field
- Monitor market indicators
- Adjust implementation as needed

### Implementation Challenges

**Risks**:

- CMS capacity constraints
- IT system limitations
- State partnership failures
- Enrollment processing backlogs

**Mitigation**:

- Increase administrative funding
- Phase implementation
- Provide state technical assistance
- Contract for surge capacity
