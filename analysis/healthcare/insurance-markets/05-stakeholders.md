# Insurance Markets: Stakeholders

## Who Is Affected

### Primary Stakeholders

| Group | Impact | Size |
|-------|--------|------|
| ESI enrollees | Primary coverage; cost-sharing | 156 million |
| Marketplace enrollees | ACA coverage; subsidy-dependent | 20 million |
| Off-exchange individual market | Full-cost coverage | 3 million |
| Uninsured | No coverage; financial exposure | 27 million |
| Underinsured | Inadequate coverage | 43 million |
| Small business employees | Variable coverage quality | 30 million |

### Secondary Stakeholders

**Employers**

- Large employers: Self-insure and manage costs directly
- Small employers: Purchase group coverage; face affordability challenges
- All employers: Healthcare costs affect competitiveness and wages

**Healthcare Providers**

- Negotiate payment rates with insurers
- Face administrative burden from multiple payers
- Revenue depends on coverage status of patients
- Uninsured/underinsured create uncompensated care

**State Governments**

- Regulate insurance markets (within ERISA limits)
- Operate exchanges (state-based marketplace states)
- Medicaid expansion decisions affect market dynamics
- Bear costs of uncompensated care

**Federal Government**

- Provides tax subsidies ($300+ billion for ESI; $60+ billion for ACA)
- Regulates exchanges and essential health benefits
- Enforces antitrust (limited)
- Operates federal marketplace

## Who Has Power

### Decision Makers

**Federal**:

- Congress (ACA structure, subsidies, tax treatment)
- CMS (exchange regulation, essential health benefits)
- DOL (ERISA enforcement)
- Treasury/IRS (tax treatment, HSAs)
- FTC/DOJ (antitrust, mergers)

**State**:

- Insurance commissioners (rate review, market conduct)
- State legislatures (mandates, consumer protections)
- Governors (exchange decisions, Medicaid expansion)

**Private**:

- Employer HR departments (plan selection, contributions)
- Insurance company executives (pricing, network design, coverage decisions)
- Hospital system executives (pricing, contracting)

### Influencers

| Actor | Influence | Position |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| AHIP | Major lobbying force | Protect insurer interests |
| Blue Cross Blue Shield Association | Represents largest insurers | Market stability, insurer interests |
| Chamber of Commerce | Employer voice | Limit mandates; control costs |
| Small business associations (NFIB) | Small employer voice | Affordability; flexibility |
| AHA/hospital associations | Provider pricing | Resist rate regulation |
| AMA/physician societies | Provider voice | Administrative simplification |
| Families USA | Consumer advocacy | Expand coverage; consumer protection |
| Kaiser Family Foundation | Research/information | Neutral analysis |

## Stakeholder Interests

### Consumer Perspectives

| Concern | Priority |
|---------|----------|
| Affordability | #1 issue for most consumers |
| Provider access | Network adequacy; specialty access |
| Coverage adequacy | Benefits when sick |
| Simplicity | Ability to understand and navigate |
| Stability | Consistent coverage without disruption |
| Choice | Options among plans and providers |

### Employer Perspectives

| Concern | Position |
|---------|----------|
| Cost control | Primary focus |
| Competitive benefits | Attract/retain workers |
| Administrative burden | Minimize compliance costs |
| Flexibility | Resist mandates |
| Predictability | Budget planning |

### Insurer Perspectives

| Concern | Position |
|---------|----------|
| Risk pool health | Avoid adverse selection |
| Administrative flexibility | Prior auth, network design |
| Payment adequacy | Premiums cover costs + margin |
| Regulatory clarity | Predictable rules |
| Market access | Ability to enter/exit markets |

### Provider Perspectives

| Concern | Position |
|---------|----------|
| Payment rates | Seek higher reimbursement |
| Administrative burden | Reduce prior auth, billing complexity |
| Patient coverage | More insured patients = less bad debt |
| Autonomy | Clinical decision-making authority |

### Positions on Key Reforms

| Stakeholder | Public Option | Employer Mandate | Price Regulation | Single-Payer |
|-------------|---------------|------------------|------------------|--------------|
| Large employers | Cautious | Neutral (already provide) | Support (cost control) | Divided |
| Small employers | Support | Oppose | Support | Divided |
| Insurers | Oppose | Support | Oppose | Strongly oppose |
| Hospitals | Cautious | Support | Oppose | Divided |
| Consumer groups | Support | Support | Support | Support |
| Conservatives | Oppose | Oppose | Oppose | Strongly oppose |
