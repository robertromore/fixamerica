# Healthcare Costs: Stakeholders

## Who Is Affected

### Primary Stakeholders

| Group | Impact | Size |
|-------|--------|------|
| Patients/families | Premiums, cost-sharing, medical debt | 330 million |
| Employers | Premiums, administrative burden, competitiveness | 6 million businesses |
| Medicare beneficiaries | Premium costs, cost-sharing, benefit limits | 65 million |
| Medicaid beneficiaries | State budget constraints limit benefits | 85 million |
| Uninsured | Bear full costs when care is needed | 27 million |
| Workers | Wage stagnation as compensation goes to healthcare | 150+ million |

### Secondary Stakeholders

**Governments**

- Federal: Medicare, Medicaid, employee benefits consume budget
- State: Medicaid is largest/second-largest budget item
- Local: Employee health costs, public health burden

**Healthcare Workers**

- Physicians: High income but increasing administrative burden
- Nurses: Staffing shortages, burnout
- Support staff: Jobs tied to administrative complexity

**Taxpayers**

- Tax exclusion for employer coverage costs $300+ billion/year
- Medicare/Medicaid funded by payroll and general taxes
- Subsidies for ACA marketplace coverage

## Who Has Power

### Decision Makers

- **Congress**: Controls Medicare/Medicaid policy, tax treatment
- **CMS**: Sets Medicare payment rates, regulations
- **State legislatures**: Medicaid policy, insurance regulation
- **State insurance commissioners**: Rate review, market rules
- **Employers**: Coverage decisions, plan design

### Influencers

| Actor | Power Source | Annual Lobbying |
|-------|--------------|-----------------|
| Pharmaceutical industry | Pricing, patents | $350+ million |
| Hospital associations | Local economic power | $100+ million |
| Insurance industry | Coverage decisions | $150+ million |
| Physician groups | Professional authority | $50+ million |
| Medical device makers | Technology adoption | $50+ million |
