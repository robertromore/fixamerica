# Healthcare Costs: Resources

## Overview

This resource guide connects researchers, advocates, policymakers, and citizens with the data, organizations, and tools needed to understand healthcare costs and advocate for reform.

## Academic Research

### Foundational Studies

- Anderson, Gerard F., et al. "It's the Prices, Stupid: Why the United States Is So Different from Other Countries." *Health Affairs* 22 (2003): 89-105.
- Papanicolas, Irene, et al. "Health Care Spending in the United States and Other High-Income Countries." *JAMA* 319 (2018): 1024-1039.
- Reinhardt, Uwe. "Why Does U.S. Health Care Cost So Much?" *JAMA* 320 (2018): 2205-2206.

### Hospital Pricing

- Cooper, Zack, et al. "The Price Ain't Right? Hospital Prices and Health Spending on the Privately Insured." *Quarterly Journal of Economics* 134 (2019): 51-107.
- Gaynor, Martin, et al. "The Industrial Organization of Health-Care Markets." *Journal of Economic Literature* 53 (2015): 235-284.
- Scheffler, Richard, and Daniel Arnold. "Insurer Market Power Lowers Prices in Numerous Concentrated Provider Markets." *Health Affairs* 36 (2017): 1539-1546.

### Consolidation Effects

- Beaulieu, Nancy D., et al. "Changes in Quality of Care after Hospital Mergers and Acquisitions." *New England Journal of Medicine* 382 (2020): 51-59.
- Dafny, Leemore, et al. "The Price Effects of Cross-Market Mergers: Theory and Evidence from the Hospital Industry." *RAND Journal of Economics* 50 (2019): 286-325.

### Drug Pricing

- Kesselheim, Aaron S., et al. "The High Cost of Prescription Drugs in the United States: Origins and Prospects for Reform." *JAMA* 316 (2016): 858-871.
- Feldman, Robin. *Drugs, Money, and Secret Handshakes: The Unstoppable Growth of Prescription Drug Prices*. Cambridge University Press, 2019.

## Books

### Healthcare Costs and Reform

- Rosenthal, Elisabeth. *An American Sickness: How Healthcare Became Big Business and How You Can Take It Back*. Penguin, 2017.
- Reinhardt, Uwe. *Priced Out: The Economic and Ethical Costs of American Health Care*. Princeton University Press, 2019.
- Brill, Steven. *America's Bitter Pill: Money, Politics, Backroom Deals, and the Fight to Fix Our Broken Healthcare System*. Random House, 2015.
- Emanuel, Ezekiel. *Which Country Has the World's Best Health Care?* PublicAffairs, 2020.
- Cutler, David M. *The Quality Cure: How Focusing on Health Care Quality Can Save Your Life and Lower Spending Too*. University of California Press, 2014.

### Market Dynamics

- Goldhill, David. *Catastrophic Care: Why Everything We Think We Know About Health Care Is Wrong*. Vintage, 2013.
- Porter, Michael, and Elizabeth Teisberg. *Redefining Health Care: Creating Value-Based Competition on Results*. Harvard Business School Press, 2006.
