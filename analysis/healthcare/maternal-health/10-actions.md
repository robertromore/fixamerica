# Actions for Maternal Health Advocacy

## Individual Action

Every person can contribute to improving maternal health outcomes. This section provides concrete actions organized by the level of commitment and type of engagement.

### For Everyone

**Raise Awareness:**
- Share maternal health statistics on social media
- Discuss maternal mortality with friends and family
- Amplify stories of maternal health advocates and survivors
- Challenge myths about pregnancy and childbirth
- Use Black Maternal Health Week (April) for focused advocacy

**Support Affected Families:**
- Listen to and believe mothers' experiences
- Offer practical support to pregnant and postpartum women
- Check in during the postpartum period (not just immediately after birth)
- Connect struggling mothers with resources
- Reduce stigma around perinatal mental health

**Engage Politically:**
- Register and vote for candidates who prioritize maternal health
- Contact elected officials about maternal health legislation
- Attend town halls and ask about maternal health
- Sign petitions for maternal health reform
- Donate to maternal health advocacy organizations

### For Healthcare Consumers

**Advocate for Yourself:**
- Know the warning signs of pregnancy complications
- Trust your instincts if something feels wrong
- Ask questions and expect answers from providers
- Request a second opinion if concerned
- Document symptoms and concerns

**Choose Care Wisely:**
- Research hospital maternal health outcomes (Leapfrog ratings)
- Consider midwifery care for low-risk pregnancy
- Explore birth center options
- Consider hiring a doula
- Evaluate provider responsiveness to concerns

**Know Your Rights:**
- Right to informed consent for all procedures
- Right to refuse treatments
- Right to change providers
- Right to have support persons present
- Right to file complaints about care

### For Healthcare Professionals

**Improve Your Practice:**
- Complete implicit bias training
- Participate in simulation drills for obstetric emergencies
- Implement evidence-based safety bundles
- Listen to and believe patient concerns
- Provide trauma-informed care

**Champion System Change:**
- Advocate for quality improvement in your institution
- Report safety concerns through appropriate channels
- Mentor colleagues in best practices
- Participate in maternal mortality review
- Support scope of practice expansion for midwives and doulas

**Address Bias:**
- Examine your own implicit biases
- Call out biased behavior in colleagues
- Advocate for equity measures in your institution
- Ensure equal treatment regardless of race or insurance status
- Partner with community organizations

### For Pregnant and Postpartum Women

**Prepare for Pregnancy:**
- Manage chronic conditions before pregnancy
- Achieve healthy weight if possible
- Discuss medications with provider
- Plan for prenatal care access
- Address mental health needs

**During Pregnancy:**
- Attend all prenatal appointments
- Report concerning symptoms immediately
- Build a support network
- Plan for postpartum period
- Create a birth plan with preferences

**Postpartum:**
- Attend postpartum visits
- Monitor for warning signs (HEAR HER campaign)
- Seek help for mood changes
- Continue chronic disease management
- Arrange support for recovery

## State-Level Advocacy

### Legislative Priorities

**Priority Bills to Support:**

| Issue | Model Legislation | Talking Points |
|-------|------------------|----------------|
| 12-month postpartum Medicaid | ARP extension implementation | Prevents deaths, saves money, already federal option |
| Midwifery full practice authority | APRN Compact model | Evidence supports safety, increases access |
| Doula Medicaid coverage | MN/OR/FL models | Reduces interventions, improves outcomes |
| Maternal mortality review | CDC-supported model | Identifies preventable deaths |
| Implicit bias training | CA model | Addresses documented discrimination |
| Birth center licensure | AABC model | Safe, lower-cost alternative |

**How to Engage:**
1. Identify relevant bills in your state legislature
2. Find sponsors and cosponsors
3. Testify at committee hearings
4. Meet with legislators and staff
5. Organize constituent contact campaigns
6. Build coalitions with aligned groups

### Regulatory Advocacy

**State Agency Engagement:**

| Agency | Relevant Authority | Advocacy Focus |
|--------|-------------------|----------------|
| State Medicaid agency | Coverage, rates, programs | Doula coverage, rate increases |
| Health department | Maternal mortality review, facility licensing | MMRC strengthening, quality |
| Licensing boards | Scope of practice | Midwifery expansion |
| Insurance commissioner | Private insurance regulation | Maternity coverage standards |

**How to Engage:**
- Submit comments on proposed regulations
- Attend public meetings
- Request meetings with agency staff
- Provide data and research
- Participate in advisory committees

### Electoral Engagement

**Candidate Accountability:**
- Research candidates' positions on maternal health
- Ask questions at candidate forums
- Request maternal health platform commitments
- Publicize responses (or non-responses)
- Support pro-maternal health candidates

**Voter Education:**
- Register voters in affected communities
- Create maternal health voter guides
- Organize get-out-the-vote efforts
- Focus on low-turnout elections

## Healthcare System Advocacy

### Hospital Accountability

**Push for Transparency:**
- Request hospital maternal mortality and morbidity data
- Ask about safety bundle implementation
- Inquire about C-section rates
- Demand implicit bias training programs
- Review hospital quality ratings (Leapfrog, CMS)

**Advocate for Change:**
- Request meetings with hospital leadership
- Participate in patient/family advisory councils
- Submit complaints through formal channels
- Contact hospital boards
- Engage local media when appropriate

### Insurance Advocacy

**Private Insurance:**
- Review maternity coverage in your plan
- File complaints about coverage denials
- Participate in employer benefits advocacy
- Support state insurance regulation

**Medicaid:**
- Attend Medicaid advisory committee meetings
- Provide testimony on maternal health
- Advocate for rate increases
- Support doula and midwifery coverage

### Quality Improvement Participation

**Engage with Quality Initiatives:**
- Join patient/family advisory councils
- Participate in maternal mortality review (where possible)
- Provide feedback on care experience
- Support perinatal quality collaborative work

## Media and Communications

### Earned Media

**Generate Coverage:**
- Write letters to the editor
- Submit op-eds to local papers
- Pitch stories to health reporters
- Provide expert commentary
- Share personal stories

**Social Media:**
- Use hashtags (#BlackMaternalHealth, #MomsDeserveBetter, etc.)
- Share data and stories
- Amplify advocacy organizations
- Engage with journalists
- Correct misinformation

### Strategic Communications

**Key Messages:**
1. "80% of maternal deaths are preventable"
2. "Black mothers are 3 times more likely to die"
3. "The U.S. is the most dangerous developed country to give birth"
4. "California reduced maternal mortality 55% - we can too"
5. "Every mother deserves to survive childbirth"

**Story Elements:**
- Personal stories humanize statistics
- Data provides credibility
- Solutions show path forward
- Urgency motivates action
