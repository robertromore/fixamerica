# Roadmap for Maternal Health Reform

## Implementation Strategy

Transforming America's maternal health outcomes requires a phased, multi-level approach. This roadmap outlines a realistic pathway from the current crisis to a system where maternal deaths are rare, racial disparities are eliminated, and every woman has access to high-quality maternity care.

## Guiding Principles

1. **Urgency**: Women are dying preventable deaths every dayâ€”action cannot wait
2. **Equity**: Racial disparities must be central to every intervention
3. **Evidence**: Prioritize interventions with proven effectiveness
4. **Feasibility**: Build political will through winnable battles
5. **Sustainability**: Create lasting structural change, not temporary programs
6. **Accountability**: Measure outcomes and enforce standards

## Phase 1: Immediate Actions (Year 1)

### Federal Level

**Executive Branch Actions (No Congressional Approval Needed):**

| Action | Implementing Agency | Timeline |
|--------|---------------------|----------|
| Require 12-month postpartum Medicaid for all states | CMS | 6 months |
| Mandate MMRC participation for Medicaid hospitals | CMS | 6 months |
| Issue maternal health equity guidance | HHS/OMH | 3 months |
| Expand Rural Maternity and Obstetrics Management Strategies | HRSA | 6 months |
| Increase Title V maternal health focus | HRSA | 6 months |
| Launch maternal safety bundle implementation initiative | CDC/HRSA | 6 months |

**Regulatory Actions:**

| Regulation | Purpose | Timeline |
|------------|---------|----------|
| Hospital Conditions of Participation update | Require hemorrhage/hypertension protocols | 12 months |
| Maternal Level of Care designation guidance | Standardize facility capabilities | 12 months |
| Medicaid managed care maternal health requirements | Quality standards for MCOs | 12 months |
| Doula reimbursement guidance | Enable state Medicaid doula programs | 6 months |

### State Level (Priority States)

**Focus States:** States with highest maternal mortality, largest disparities, or policy opportunities

| State Category | Priority Actions |
|----------------|------------------|
| Non-expansion states | Medicaid expansion advocacy |
| 60-day postpartum states | Extend to 12 months |
| Restrictive midwifery states | Scope of practice legislation |
| High maternal mortality states | Quality improvement collaboratives |
| Large Black populations | Equity-focused interventions |

**Model State Actions:**
- Establish/strengthen Maternal Mortality Review Committees
- Join AIM initiative
- Launch perinatal quality collaboratives
- Medicaid doula coverage
- Birth center development incentives

**Workforce Actions:**

## Phase 4: Achieving Parity (Years 7-10)

### Outcome Targets

**Mortality Reduction:**

| Metric | Current | Year 5 Target | Year 10 Target |
|--------|---------|--------------|----------------|
| Overall maternal mortality | 32.9/100K | 20/100K | 10/100K |
| Black maternal mortality | 69.9/100K | 40/100K | 15/100K |
| Black-white ratio | 2.6:1 | 2.0:1 | 1.2:1 |
| Indigenous maternal mortality | 65.2/100K | 35/100K | 12/100K |
| Severe maternal morbidity | 144/10K | 100/10K | 70/10K |

**Quality Targets:**

| Metric | Current | Target |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Safety bundle implementation | 50-70% | 100% |
| Preventable maternal deaths | 60-80% | <20% |
| First trimester prenatal care | 77.5% | 95% |
| Low-risk primary C-section rate | 26% | 15% |

### Equity Achievement

**Disparity Elimination:**

- Eliminate 3x Black maternal mortality disparity (reduce to <1.5x)
- Close Indigenous mortality gap
- Eliminate rural-urban mortality differential
- Equal access regardless of insurance status
- Quality care in all communities

### System Sustainability

**Sustainable System Elements:**

| Element | Sustainability Mechanism |
|---------|-------------------------|
| Workforce pipeline | Training program expansion institutionalized |
| Quality improvement | Self-sustaining collaborative networks |
| Data systems | Real-time surveillance fully funded |

- [ ] Maternal mortality rate below 25/100,000
- [ ] Black-white mortality ratio below 2.5:1

- [ ] All birthing hospitals with level of care designation

- [ ] Maternal mortality rate below 20/100,000
- [ ] Black-white mortality ratio below 2.0:1
- [ ] Maternity care deserts reduced by 40%
- [ ] Preventable deaths below 40%

- [ ] Maternal mortality rate at or below 10/100,000
- [ ] Racial disparities largely eliminated
- [ ] Maternity care accessible to all communities

- [ ] U.S. maternal health among top 10 developed nations

- Medicare annual spending: $900 billion
- Cost of preventable maternal morbidity: $6-8 billion/year

### State Investment

States will need to invest in:
- Quality collaborative participation
- Medicaid rate increases
- Workforce development
- Data systems
- Community programs

Estimate: $500 million - $1 billion additional annual state spending needed

### Private Sector Role

- Hospital quality improvement investment
- Payer value-based payment programs
- Employer maternal health benefits
- Philanthropy for innovation and advocacy

## Risks and Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Political change disrupts progress | Build bipartisan support; focus on state-level wins |
| Funding inadequacy | Demonstrate ROI; incremental investment |
| Workforce insufficient | Long lead time investments; interim strategies |
| Opposition blocks key policies | Coalition building; evidence communication |
| Implementation gaps | Technical assistance; accountability mechanisms |
| Equity focus diluted | Center affected communities in leadership |

## Accountability Framework

**Annual Reporting:**
- National maternal health report card
- State-by-state comparisons
- Hospital-level outcomes (public)
- Progress toward targets

**Independent Oversight:**
- National maternal health commission
- Community advisory boards
- Annual public hearings
- Progress audits
