# Medicaid: Stakeholders

## Overview

Medicaid's federal-state structure creates a complex stakeholder landscape spanning government at all levels, healthcare providers, managed care organizations, and the 85+ million Americans who depend on the program. Understanding these interests is essential for navigating Medicaid policy.

---

## Primary Stakeholders: Beneficiaries

### Children and Families

| Group | Size | Key Interests |
|-------|------|---------------|
| Children on Medicaid/CHIP | 40 million | Comprehensive coverage, EPSDT benefits |
| Parents on Medicaid | 10 million | Family coverage, continuity |
| Pregnant women | 2 million | Prenatal care, delivery coverage |
| Foster children | 500,000 | Mandatory coverage, mental health |

**Concerns**:

- Coverage stability during income fluctuations
- Provider access, especially specialists
- Administrative barriers to enrollment/renewal
- Benefit adequacy for developmental needs

### Adults

| Group | Size | Key Interests |
|-------|------|---------------|
| Expansion adults | 20 million | Continued expansion, benefit stability |
| Traditional adults | 10 million | Eligibility protection, benefits |
| Coverage gap population | 2.2 million | Access to any coverage |

**Concerns**:

- Work requirement threats
- Renewal complexity
- Provider acceptance
- Mental health and substance abuse coverage

### Elderly and Disabled

| Group | Size | Key Interests |
|-------|------|---------------|
| Dual eligible (Medicare + Medicaid) | 12 million | Coordinated care, LTSS |
| Disabled (Medicaid only) | 6 million | Comprehensive benefits, HCBS |
| Nursing home residents | 1.4 million | Quality of care, Medicaid payment |
| HCBS recipients | 4.5 million | Community living, service access |

**Concerns**:

- HCBS waiting lists (650,000+)
- Nursing home quality
- Care coordination complexity
- Asset/income rules for LTSS

## Healthcare Providers

### Hospitals

| Category | Number | Medicaid Dependence |
|----------|--------|---------------------|
| Safety-net hospitals | 500+ | 40%+ of patients |
| Rural hospitals | 1,800+ | Critical revenue source |
| Children's hospitals | 220 | High Medicaid share |
| Teaching hospitals | 400+ | Major payer |

**Interests**:

- Payment rates (currently 60-70% of costs for many)
- Expansion (reduces uncompensated care)
- DSH payments (Disproportionate Share Hospital)
- Medicaid managed care contract terms

**Position**: Strongly support expansion, higher payments, oppose cuts.

### Community Health Centers (FQHCs)

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Number of health centers | 1,400+ |
| Patients served | 30+ million |
| Medicaid patients | 50%+ of patient base |
| Uninsured patients | 20%+ |

**Interests**:

- Prospective payment system (PPS) rates
- Wrap-around payments in managed care
- Grant funding stability
- Scope of practice expansion

**Position**: Strong expansion advocates, concerned about managed care underpayment.

### Physicians

| Category | Participation Rate | Key Concern |
|----------|-------------------|-------------|
| Primary care | 70% accept new patients | Payment rates |
| Specialists | 60% accept new patients | Payment rates, administrative burden |
| Psychiatrists | 55% accept new patients | Payment rates, capacity |
| Dentists | 40% accept new patients | Very low payment |

**Interests**:

- Higher payment rates (currently 60-75% of Medicare)
- Reduced prior authorization burden
- Simplified managed care contracting
- Liability protection

**Position**: Mixed; support higher payments, wary of mandatory participation.

### Nursing Homes

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Number of facilities | 15,000+ |
| Medicaid share of residents | 60%+ |
| Medicaid share of revenue | 50%+ |
| For-profit share | 70% |

**Interests**:

- Payment adequacy (staffing costs rising)
- Regulatory requirements (staffing mandates)
- Occupancy rates
- Private equity concerns

**Position**: Support higher Medicaid payment, resist new mandates.

### Home and Community-Based Service Providers

| Category | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Home health agencies | Personal care, skilled nursing |
| Adult day programs | Community-based day services |
| Supported living | Residential services |
| Personal care attendants | Direct support workers |

**Interests**:

- Wage increases for direct care workers
- Rate adequacy
- Workforce recruitment/retention
- Service flexibility

**Position**: Support HCBS expansion, higher worker wages.

## Managed Care Organizations

### Major Medicaid MCOs

| Company | Medicaid Enrollment | Revenue | States |
|---------|---------------------|---------|--------|
| Centene | 16 million | $130+ billion total | 29 |
| UnitedHealth | 8 million | Part of $300+ billion | 26 |
| Molina | 5 million | $30+ billion | 19 |
| Elevance (Anthem) | 5 million | Part of $150+ billion | 21 |
| Humana | 2 million | Part of $90+ billion | 12 |

**Interests**:

- Enrollment stability and growth
- Adequate capitation rates
- Favorable risk adjustment
- Reduced quality/reporting requirements
- Multi-year contracts

**Position**: Support expansion (more enrollees), resist rate cuts and regulatory burden.

### MCO Concerns

| Issue | MCO Position |
|-------|--------------|
| Rate adequacy | Advocate for rate increases |
| Medical loss ratio requirements | Resist stricter requirements |
| Quality measures | Seek flexibility |
| Prior authorization reform | Resist restrictions |
| Unwinding | Concerned about enrollment loss |

## Government Stakeholders

### Federal Government

**Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS)**

| Role | Function |
|------|----------|
| State plan approval | Approve eligibility, benefits, payment |
| Waiver authority | Approve 1115, 1915 waivers |
| Guidance and regulations | Interpret statute |
| Enforcement | Monitor compliance, withhold funds |
| Data collection | Enrollment, spending, quality reporting |

**Congress**

| Committee | Role |
|-----------|------|
| Senate Finance | Authorizing legislation |
| House Energy & Commerce | Authorizing legislation |
| Appropriations committees | FMAP, discretionary programs |
| Budget committees | Reconciliation, spending levels |

**Interests**:

- Cost control
- Coverage (varies by party)
- State flexibility vs. national standards
- Fraud prevention
- Program integrity

### State Governments

**Governors**

| Interest | Manifestation |
|----------|---------------|
| Budget | Medicaid is largest/second-largest budget item |
| Flexibility | Seek waivers, resist mandates |
| Coverage decisions | Expansion, eligibility |
| Delivery system | Managed care, payment reform |

**State Legislatures**

| Interest | Manifestation |
|----------|---------------|
| Appropriations | State match funding |
| Eligibility | Set income/asset limits within federal parameters |
| Benefits | Optional services |
| Oversight | Agency accountability |

**State Medicaid Directors**

| Role | Function |
|------|----------|
| Program administration | Day-to-day management |
| Waiver development | Design and implement waivers |
| Managed care oversight | Contract with MCOs |
| Policy development | Advise governor/legislature |

### Local Governments

| Role | Function |
|------|----------|
| County welfare offices | Eligibility determination (some states) |
| Public hospitals | Safety-net care |
| Local health departments | Public health, some services |
| Behavioral health authorities | Mental health/substance abuse |

## Power Analysis

### Who Has the Most Influence

| Actor | Power Source | Influence Level |
|-------|--------------|-----------------|
| CMS | Regulatory authority, FMAP | Very high |
| Congress | Statute, appropriations | Very high |
| State governors | Program administration | High |
| MCOs | Market share, lobbying | High |
| Hospital associations | Lobbying, jobs | High |
| State Medicaid directors | Technical expertise | Medium-high |
| Consumer advocates | Grassroots, media | Medium |
| Beneficiaries | Votes | Medium (often low turnout) |

### Who Has the Least Influence

| Actor | Barrier | Influence Level |
|-------|---------|-----------------|
| Coverage gap population | No coverage, low income | Very low |
| HCBS waiting list population | Scattered, diverse needs | Low |
| Direct care workers | Low wages, fragmented | Low |
| Rural Medicaid enrollees | Geographic isolation | Low |
