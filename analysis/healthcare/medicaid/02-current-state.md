# Medicaid: Current State

## Overview

Medicaid is the largest health insurance program in the United States, covering over 85 million Americansâ€”more than Medicare, employer coverage, or any single private insurer. As a joint federal-state program, Medicaid exhibits extreme variation across 56 jurisdictions, creating a patchwork where access, benefits, and quality depend heavily on where one lives.

## Coverage Gap

### Who Is in the Gap

The coverage gap consists of adults in non-expansion states who:

- Earn too much for traditional Medicaid (often $0 income limit)
- Earn too little for ACA marketplace subsidies (below 100% FPL)
- Have no other affordable coverage option

| Characteristic | Coverage Gap Population |
|----------------|------------------------|
| Total | 2.2 million |
| Working | 60% |
| Parents | 40% |
| White | 40% |
| Black | 27% |
| Hispanic | 26% |
| Median income | 50% FPL (~$7,500/year for individual) |

### Geographic Concentration

| Texas | 771,000 | 35% |
| Florida | 445,000 | 22% |
| Georgia | 248,000 | 20% |

## Post-COVID Unwinding

|--------|-------|
| Start date | March 2020 |
| End date | March 2023 |
| Peak enrollment | 94 million |

### Unwinding Impact (2023-2024)

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Total disenrolled | 15+ million |
| Procedural terminations | 70%+ |
| Children disenrolled | 5+ million |
| States with highest rates | Texas, Florida, Georgia |
| Re-enrollment rate | Varies widely |

### Procedural vs. Ineligibility Terminations

| Termination Type | Share | Description |
|------------------|-------|-------------|
| Procedural | 70% | Didn't return paperwork, couldn't verify |
| Eligibility | 30% | Actually exceeded income limits |

**Concern**: Most people losing coverage are eligible but failed to complete administrative requirements.
