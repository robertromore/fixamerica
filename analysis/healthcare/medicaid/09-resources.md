# Medicaid: Resources

## Overview

This resource guide connects researchers, advocates, policymakers, and citizens with the data, organizations, and tools needed to understand Medicaid and advocate for program improvement.

## Academic Research

### Foundational Studies

- Finkelstein, Amy, et al. "The Oregon Health Insurance Experiment: Evidence from the First Year." *Quarterly Journal of Economics* 127 (2012): 1057-1106.
- Baicker, Katherine, et al. "The Oregon Experiment—Effects of Medicaid on Clinical Outcomes." *New England Journal of Medicine* 368 (2013): 1713-1722.
- Miller, Sarah, et al. "Medicaid and Mortality: New Evidence from Linked Survey and Administrative Data." *Quarterly Journal of Economics* 136 (2021): 1783-1829.
- Goodman-Bacon, Andrew. "Public Insurance and Mortality: Evidence from Medicaid Implementation." *Journal of Political Economy* 126 (2018): 216-262.

### Expansion Research

- Sommers, Benjamin D., et al. "Changes in Utilization and Health Among Low-Income Adults After Medicaid Expansion." *JAMA Internal Medicine* 176 (2016): 1541-1548.
- Miller, Sarah, and Laura Wherry. "Health and Access to Care during the First 2 Years of the ACA Medicaid Expansions." *New England Journal of Medicine* 376 (2017): 947-956.
- Borgschulte, Mark, and Jacob Vogler. "Did the ACA Medicaid Expansion Save Lives?" *Journal of Health Economics* 72 (2020): 102333.

### Work Requirements Research

- Sommers, Benjamin D., et al. "Medicaid Work Requirements—Results from the First Year in Arkansas." *New England Journal of Medicine* 381 (2019): 1073-1082.
- Gangopadhyaya, Anuj, et al. "Medicaid Work Requirements in Arkansas Led to Coverage Losses." *Health Affairs* 39 (2020): 30-37.

### Federalism and Politics

- Thompson, Frank, and Michael Gusmano. "The Administrative Presidency and Fractured Federalism." *Publius* 44 (2014): 426-450.
- Michener, Jamila. "Policy Feedback in a Racialized Polity." *Policy Studies Journal* 47 (2019): 423-450.
- Grogan, Colleen, and Sunggeun Park. "The Racial Divide in State Medicaid Expansions." *Journal of Health Politics, Policy and Law* 42 (2017): 539-572.
