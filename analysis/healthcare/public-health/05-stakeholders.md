# Public Health: Stakeholders

## Who Is Affected

### Primary Stakeholders

| Group | Impact | Size |
|-------|--------|------|
| General public | Beneficiaries of public health protection | 330 million |
| Vulnerable populations | Disproportionate disease burden | Varies by condition |
| Children | Vaccination, developmental programs | 73 million |
| Elderly | Prevention, pandemic vulnerability | 54 million |
| Low-income populations | Greater exposure, less access to prevention | 38 million |
| Communities of color | Health disparities, environmental burdens | 130 million |

### Secondary Stakeholders

**Public Health Workforce**

- Federal employees (CDC, NIH, FDA): ~75,000
- State health department staff: ~100,000
- Local health department staff: ~125,000
- Related public health workers: Varies

**Healthcare System**

- Hospitals: Handle public health failures (unvaccinated illness, outbreaks)
- Physicians: Implement prevention (vaccines, screening)
- Laboratories: Disease testing and surveillance
- Insurers: Cover (or don't cover) preventive services

**Research Community**

- Academic public health schools
- NIH-funded researchers
- Private research institutions
- Pharmaceutical companies (vaccines, treatments)

**Private Sector**

- Employers: Workplace health, insurance costs
- Food industry: Regulation target and partner
- Pharmaceutical industry: Vaccines, treatments
- Technology companies: Data systems, communication

## Who Has Power

### Decision Makers

**Federal**:

- Congress: Appropriations, authorization, oversight
- President: Appointments, emergency declarations
- HHS Secretary: Department leadership
- CDC Director: Agency leadership, guidance
- Surgeon General: Public communication ("nation's doctor")
- FDA Commissioner: Drug, device, food safety

**State**:

- Governors: State health policy, emergencies
- State legislators: Funding, authority
- State health officers: Technical leadership
- Attorneys general: Enforcement

**Local**:

- Mayors and county executives: Local policy
- Local health officers: Community public health
- Boards of health: Governance

### Influencers

| Actor | Influence | Position |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| APHA (American Public Health Association) | Professional voice | Pro-investment |

| Academic public health schools | Research, training | Pro-investment |
| Healthcare industry | Healthcare perspective | Mixed |

## Stakeholder Interests

### Public Health Professional Perspectives

| Concern | Priority |
|---------|----------|
| Sustainable funding | Critical |
| Workforce development | Critical |
| Data modernization | High |
| Political independence | High |
| Public trust | High |
| Authority to act | Important |

### Healthcare System Perspectives

| Concern | Position |
|---------|----------|
| Prevention reduces demand | Theoretically support; practically ambivalent |
| Outbreak response | Want public health capacity |
| Reporting requirements | Seek efficiency |
| Immunization | Support (revenue from administration) |

### Political Perspectives

| Group | Position |
|-------|----------|
| Public health advocates | Substantial investment needed |
| Fiscal conservatives | Skeptical of spending increases |
| Libertarians | Oppose public health authority |
| Public health researchers | Evidence-based investment |
| Healthcare industry | Support some investment (especially pandemic prep) |

### Positions on Key Reforms

| Stakeholder | CDC Funding Increase | Workforce Investment | Data Modernization | Public Health Authority |
|-------------|---------------------|---------------------|-------------------|------------------------|
| Public health professionals | Strongly support | Strongly support | Strongly support | Support |
| Healthcare industry | Support | Support | Support | Cautious |
| Business community | Cautious support | Support | Support | Mixed |
| Fiscal conservatives | Skeptical | Skeptical | May support | Oppose expansion |
| Libertarians | Oppose | Oppose | Neutral | Strongly oppose |
