# Public Health: Implementation Roadmap

## Strategic Approach

Public health reform requires capitalizing on post-pandemic awareness while building sustainable systems that persist beyond crisis. The roadmap sequences immediate stabilization with long-term institutional rebuilding.

## Phase 1: Stabilization (Years 1-2)

### Workforce Retention and Recruitment

**Actions**:

- $1 billion emergency workforce fund
- Retention bonuses for existing staff
- Accelerated hiring for critical positions
- Harassment protection and support
- Competitive salary adjustments

**Milestones**:

- Workforce decline halted by Year 1
- 15,000 new public health workers hired by Year 2
- Vacancy rates reduced by 50%

### CDC Funding Restoration

**Actions**:

- Increase CDC budget by 50% ($4.5 billion increase)
- Protect Prevention and Public Health Fund
- End reliance on emergency supplementals
- Multi-year appropriations where possible

**Milestones**:

- FY 2026 appropriation includes increase
- Prevention Fund protected from raids
- Core programs fully funded

### Data System Emergency Improvements

**Actions**:

- Electronic lab reporting universal requirement
- Real-time case reporting pilot expansion
- Demographic data completeness standards
- CDC technical assistance deployment

**Milestones**:

- 90% electronic lab reporting by Year 2
- Real-time reporting in 25 states
- Demographic completeness improved 30%

## Phase 2: Building Capacity (Years 3-5)

### State and Local Investment

**Actions**:

- $4.5 billion annual grants to state/local health departments
- Foundational capability standards
- Performance accountability
- Technical assistance for under-resourced jurisdictions
- Equity requirements

**Milestones**:

- All jurisdictions meeting foundational standards by Year 5
- Per capita spending increased 50%
- Disparities in capacity reduced

### Workforce Pipeline

**Actions**:

- Public Health Service Corps expansion (5x current size)
- Training program modernization grants
- Mid-career training programs
- Diverse recruitment initiatives
- Leadership development programs

**Milestones**:

- 10,000 loan repayment participants annually
- Training programs updated nationwide
- Leadership pipeline established
- Diversity increased 30%

### Data Modernization Completion

**Actions**:

- National data exchange standards implemented
- Real-time surveillance in all jurisdictions
- Syndromic surveillance integration
- Analytics capability at state level
- Interoperability with healthcare systems

**Milestones**:

- Real-time reporting nationwide by Year 4
- All states with analytics capacity
- Healthcare data integration operational

## Phase 3: Sustainable Systems (Years 5-10)

### Pandemic Preparedness Maturation

**Actions**:

- Strategic National Stockpile fully rebuilt
- Domestic manufacturing capacity established
- Global surveillance integration
- Regular exercises and updates
- Surge capacity maintained

**Milestones**:

- 90-day stockpile for major pandemic
- Domestic vaccine manufacturing for 100 million doses/month
- Annual preparedness exercises
- International partnerships operational

### Sustainable Funding Mechanism

**Actions**:

- Public health trust fund established
- Dedicated revenue stream
- Multi-year funding authority
- Automatic stabilizers for emergencies

**Milestones**:

- Trust fund legislation enacted
- Funding insulated from annual appropriations
- Emergency funding triggers established

### Legal Framework Modernization

**Actions**:

- Model state public health law adoption
- Federal coordination authority clarified
- Scientific integrity protections
- Accountability mechanisms

**Milestones**:

- Model law adopted in 30+ states
- Federal authority updated
- Scientific integrity rules in place

## Risk Mitigation

### Political Risks

**Concerns**:

- Post-pandemic complacency
- Budget competition
- Political interference
- State resistance

**Mitigation**:

- Build bipartisan support (national security framing)
- Demonstrate cost-effectiveness
- Scientific integrity protections
- Federal-state partnership approach

### Implementation Risks

**Concerns**:

- Workforce recruitment difficulty
- State capacity variation
- Technology challenges
- Coordination failures

**Mitigation**:

- Competitive compensation
- Technical assistance for under-resourced
- Phased technology implementation
- Clear coordination frameworks

### Sustainability Risks

**Concerns**:

- Funding diverted after crisis fades
- Preparedness erodes without crisis
- Political will dissipates

**Mitigation**:

- Trust fund structure
- Mandatory vs. discretionary funding
- Regular exercises and public reporting
- Accountability for readiness
