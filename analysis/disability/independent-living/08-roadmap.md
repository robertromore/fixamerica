# Independent Living: Implementation Roadmap

## Strategic Approach

### Theory of Change

Independent living reform requires building on the momentum generated by COVID-19's exposure of institutional care failures and ARPA's demonstration that HCBS investment works. The strategy leads with administrative actions that do not require new legislation, followed by legislative campaigns for structural Medicaid reform. State-level advocacy and litigation create pressure from below while federal action establishes the framework from above. The workforce crisis serves as both a barrier and an opportunity: solving the workforce problem is a prerequisite for HCBS expansion and provides a popular, sympathetic narrative for public engagement.

### Strategic Pillars

| Pillar | Purpose | Key Actions |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| **Administrative foundation** | Use existing CMS and DOJ authority | HCBS quality rule, Olmstead enforcement, FMAP incentives |
| **Workforce stabilization** | Make DSP a viable career | Wage floor, career pathways, labor protections |
| **Legislative structural reform** | Eliminate institutional bias | HCBS Access Act, Medicaid rebalancing |
| **Housing and infrastructure** | Build community capacity | Section 811 expansion, visitability, housing integration |
| **State innovation** | Demonstrate models | Leading state partnerships, pilot programs |

### Political Windows

| Window | Opportunity |
|--------|-------------|
| **ARPA sustainability** | States facing HCBS funding cliffs create urgency for federal action |
| **CMS rulemaking** | HCBS access and quality rule finalization |
| **Olmstead anniversary (2024)** | 25th anniversary creates narrative opportunity |
| **Budget reconciliation** | 51-vote pathway for Medicaid reform |
| **State legislative sessions** | DSP wage increases achievable state by state |
| **Nursing home crisis coverage** | Media attention sustains public support for alternatives |

## Phase 1: Administrative Action and Foundation (2026-2028)

### Objective

Maximize existing federal authority to strengthen HCBS, stabilize the workforce, and enforce Olmstead, while building the coalition and evidence base for legislative reform.

### CMS Administrative Actions

| Action | Authority | Timeline |
|--------|----------|----------|
| Finalize HCBS access and quality rule | Medicaid regulatory authority | Q1 2026 |
| 80% pass-through requirement for HCBS payments | CMS rulemaking | Q2 2026 |
| Enhanced FMAP for Community First Choice expansion | Existing ACA authority | Q3 2026 |
| Streamlined HCBS waiver renewal process | Administrative simplification | Q4 2026 |
| Permanent Money Follows the Person program | Administrative extension | Q1 2027 |
| HCBS Settings Rule enforcement | Existing rule compliance | Ongoing |

### DOJ Enforcement Actions

| Action | Authority | Timeline |
|--------|----------|----------|
| Launch Olmstead Enforcement Initiative | ADA enforcement authority | Q1 2026 |
| Open investigations in 10 states without Olmstead plans | ADA Title II | Q2 2026 |
| Negotiate new consent decrees in 5 states | Settlement authority | 2026-2027 |
| Publish Olmstead compliance guidance | DOJ guidance authority | Q3 2026 |

### Workforce Stabilization

| Initiative | Mechanism | Timeline |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| CMS guidance on DSP rate methodology | Technical assistance letter | Q2 2026 |
| DOL DSP apprenticeship program | Apprenticeship authority | Q3 2026 |
| State DSP wage increase campaigns | Advocacy + legislative | 2026-2028 |
| ARPA sustainability bridge funding request | OMB budget process | FY 2027 |

### Coalition Building

| Coalition | Members | Strategy |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| HCBS Access Alliance | Disability rights + AARP + labor + HCBS providers | Joint legislative campaign |
| Workforce Coalition | SEIU + AFSCME + PHI + disability rights | DSP wage campaign |
| Housing Partnership | HUD + CMS + housing advocates + disability rights | Integrated housing pilots |
| State Innovation Network | 10 leading states + CMS | Model program development |

### Milestones

- HCBS access and quality rule finalized: Q1 2026
- 80% pass-through requirement effective: Q4 2026
- 10 new Olmstead investigations opened: Q4 2026

## Phase 2: Legislative Reform (2028-2031)

### Objective

Enact the structural Medicaid reforms needed to make HCBS a mandatory benefit, establish a DSP wage floor, and expand accessible housing.

### Federal Legislation

| Bill | Purpose | Coalition Strategy |
|------|---------|-------------------|
| HCBS Access Act | Make HCBS mandatory Medicaid benefit | Disability + aging + labor + fiscal moderates |
| Direct Support Professional Workforce Act | Federal DSP wage floor and career pathways | Labor + disability + centrist |
| Affordable Accessible Housing Act | Section 811 expansion + visitability | Housing + disability + aging |
| Olmstead Enforcement Act | Strengthen DOJ enforcement tools | Disability rights + civil rights |
| CIL Empowerment Act | Triple CIL funding | Disability community + bipartisan |

### Budget Strategy

| Funding Source | Amount | Mechanism |
|---------------|--------|-----------|
| HCBS mandatory benefit | $25-30 billion/year net | Enhanced FMAP declining over 5 years |
| DSP wage floor | $15-20 billion/year | Enhanced FMAP for wage-related increases |
| Section 811 expansion | $2 billion/year | Direct appropriation |
| CIL funding | $300 million/year | Title VII reauthorization |
| Olmstead enforcement | $30 million/year | DOJ appropriation |

### State-Level Campaigns

| Target | Number of States | Strategy |
|--------|-----------------|----------|
| DSP minimum wage legislation | 20 states | State legislative campaigns |
| Olmstead plan requirements | 15 states without plans | Litigation + legislative |
| HCBS waiver expansion | 10 lagging states | CMS encouragement + advocacy |
| Self-direction expansion | 5 states without full self-direction | CMS guidance + state advocacy |

### Milestones

- HCBS Access Act introduced: Q1 2029
- DSP Workforce Act passes Senate committee: Q3 2029
- Section 811 expansion appropriated: FY 2029
- 20 states achieve DSP wages above $20/hour: Q4 2030
- HCBS Access Act enacted: Q4 2030
- HCBS waitlist reduced by 40%: Q4 2031
- CIL funding reaches $300 million: FY 2031

## Phase 3: System Transformation (2031-2034)

### Objective

Implement mandatory HCBS benefit, scale workforce development, transform housing infrastructure, and shift the LTSS system from institutional default to community default.

### HCBS Implementation

| Action | Timeline |
|--------|----------|
| States submit HCBS expansion plans | Within 12 months of enactment |
| Phase 1 enrollment expansion (50% of waitlist) | Years 1-2 |
| Phase 2 enrollment expansion (remaining waitlist) | Years 2-3 |
| Full mandatory HCBS implementation | Year 5 |
| Enhanced FMAP phase-down to standard match | Years 3-5 |

### Workforce Scale-Up

| Target | Metric | Timeline |
|--------|--------|----------|
| DSP workforce growth | Add 500,000 new DSPs | 3 years |
| National credentialing | 50% of DSPs credentialed | 3 years |
| Career pathway adoption | All states with 3-tier career ladder | 3 years |
| Turnover reduction | From 43.5% to below 25% | 3 years |
| Vacancy reduction | From 13.8% to below 5% | 3 years |

### Housing Expansion

| Target | Current | Goal | Timeline |
|--------|---------|------|----------|
| Section 811 units | 34,000 | 100,000 | 3 years |
| Integrated housing pilots | 5 | 30 states | 3 years |
| Visitability in new construction | Not required | All federal-funded | 2 years |
| Housing-HCBS coordination | Rare | All states | 3 years |

### Milestones

- HCBS waitlist eliminated in 25 states: Q4 2032
- 1 million new DSPs recruited and trained: Q4 2033
- DSP turnover below 25% nationally: Q4 2033
- 100,000 Section 811 units available: FY 2034
- All states have Olmstead compliance plans: Q4 2033
- HCBS share of LTSS spending exceeds 75%: FY 2034

## Phase 4: Full Implementation (2034-2037)

### Objective

Achieve full independent living capacity nationwide, with HCBS as the default service model, a stable and professional direct support workforce, and adequate accessible housing.

### Targets

| Metric | Target | Baseline (2024) |
|--------|--------|-----------------|
| HCBS waitlist | Zero | 820,000+ |
| HCBS share of LTSS spending | 80%+ | 60% |
| DSP median wage | $25+/hour (inflation-adjusted) | $15.50 |
| DSP annual turnover | Below 20% | 43.5% |
| Section 811 units | 200,000 | 34,000 |
| States with comprehensive Olmstead plans | 50 | 23 |
| Self-direction participation | 30% of HCBS | 19% |
| CIL funding | $300 million+ | $109 million |
| Nursing home residents (appropriate placement only) | Below 800,000 | 1.3 million |

### Sustaining Systems

| System | Function |
|--------|----------|
| CMS HCBS quality monitoring | Continuous federal oversight of access, quality, outcomes |
| DSP credentialing maintenance | National standard with state implementation |
| Olmstead compliance monitoring | DOJ ongoing enforcement with annual reporting |
| Housing production pipeline | Steady-state Section 811 and LIHTC production |
| CIL network sustainability | Permanent federal funding with state match |
| Self-direction infrastructure | Mature fiscal intermediary and support systems |
| Technology integration | Ongoing adoption of assistive and remote support technology |

## Risk Assessment

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|-----------|
| HCBS Access Act fails to pass | Medium | Critical | Incremental expansion through CMS waivers and budget reconciliation |
| Institutional industry blocks rebalancing | Medium | High | Olmstead litigation; public narrative of nursing home failures |
| Workforce shortages persist despite wage increases | Medium | High | Immigration reform for care workers; technology supplements |
| State implementation varies widely | High | Medium | CMS technical assistance; conditional FMAP |
| Political administration changes | Medium | High | Prioritize legislation over executive action; build state-level programs |
| Funding sustainability after enhanced FMAP expires | Medium | High | Demonstrate cost neutrality; permanent enhanced FMAP |
| Housing production insufficient | Medium | Medium | Alternative models (shared housing, host families, accessory dwelling units) |
