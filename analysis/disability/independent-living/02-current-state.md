# Independent Living: Current State

## Present Conditions

The independent living landscape in the United States is defined by a fundamental contradiction: the legal right to community integration exists, the evidence overwhelmingly supports community-based care, and the preference of people with disabilities is clear -- yet the systems necessary to realize independent living remain chronically underfunded, fragmented, and institutionally biased. The post-COVID period has intensified both the crisis and the awareness of it, as nursing home failures during the pandemic exposed the consequences of institutional dependency while the American Rescue Plan Act (ARPA) HCBS funding demonstrated what investment could accomplish.

### Post-ARPA Sustainability Concerns

- Temporary wage increases funded by ARPA are expiring in most states (2024-2025)
- DSPs who received temporary bonuses face effective pay cuts as enhanced funding ends
- State budgets may not sustain expanded services without continued federal support
- Waitlists, which declined temporarily, are expected to grow again as enhanced funding expires
