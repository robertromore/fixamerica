# Housing Accessibility for People with Disabilities: Roadmap

## Overview

This roadmap outlines a phased strategy for transforming housing accessibility for people with disabilities, from immediate program expansion and enforcement improvements to long-term structural changes in building codes and Medicaid. Each phase builds on the prior phase, creating momentum, evidence, and institutional capacity for subsequent reforms.

## Phase 1: Immediate Relief and Foundation (Years 1-3)

### Objective

Expand existing programs, strengthen enforcement of current law, and lay groundwork for structural reforms through executive action, appropriations, and targeted legislation.

### Actions

| Action | Lead Actor | Timeline | Dependencies | Success Metric |
|--------|-----------|----------|--------------|----------------|
| Increase Section 811 funding to $500 million | Congress (appropriations) | Year 1 | Budget request | Appropriation enacted; 15,000 new units over 3 years |
| Expand Mainstream Vouchers by 30,000 | Congress (appropriations) | Year 1-2 | Budget request | Vouchers allocated to PHAs |
| Create federal home modification grant program | Congress (authorization + appropriation) | Year 1-2 | New legislation | $500 million authorized; first grants in Year 2 |
| Executive order on accessible federal housing | President | Year 1 | None | All new federally funded housing exceeds minimum accessibility |
| Establish HUD FHA design enforcement unit | HUD | Year 1-2 | Appropriations increase of $30 million | Unit operational; first proactive compliance reviews |
| Issue CMS guidance on Medicaid housing-related services | CMS | Year 1 | None; builds on existing guidance authority | Updated guidance issued; state Medicaid plan amendments |
| CDBG/HOME conditions on group home zoning | HUD rulemaking | Year 2-3 | Proposed rule, public comment | Final rule effective |
| National accessible housing registry (design phase) | HUD | Year 2-3 | Appropriation of $20 million | Registry design completed; pilot in 5 states |

### Quick Wins

- HUD guidance letter to public housing authorities on accessible unit inventory and reporting (90 days)
- CMS letter to state Medicaid directors on HCBS housing transition services (90 days)
- DOJ *Olmstead* enforcement guidance update (6 months)
- HUD technical assistance for PHAs on accessible unit marketing and allocation (Year 1)

### Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Probability | Mitigation Strategy |
|------|------------|-------------------|
| Appropriations shortfall | High | Prioritize budget-neutral executive actions; build bipartisan coalition through aging-in-place framing |
| Industry litigation against zoning conditions | Medium | Extensive administrative record; rely on FHA authority |
| State resistance to Medicaid housing guidance | Medium | Provide technical assistance and enhanced FMAP incentives |
| Administration change reverses executive order | Medium | Pursue parallel legislative action; embed in HUD regulations |

## Phase 2: Legislative Campaigns (Years 3-6)

### Objective

Enact federal legislation addressing the most significant housing accessibility gaps, building on program expansions and enforcement improvements from Phase 1.

### Actions

| Action | Lead Actor | Timeline | Dependencies | Success Metric |
|--------|-----------|----------|--------------|----------------|
| Disability Housing Accessibility Act (comprehensive legislation) | Congress | Year 3-4 | Phase 1 demonstrates program feasibility | Bill enacted |
| LIHTC accessibility enhancement (Section 42 amendment) | Congress | Year 3-4 | Bipartisan LIHTC reauthorization effort | Section 42 amended; QAP guidance issued |
| FHA design pre-construction review mandate | Congress | Year 4-5 | Phase 1 enforcement data demonstrates need | Legislation enacted; HUD standards published |
| National visitability standard (IRC/IBC amendment campaign) | ICC/Congress | Year 4-6 | Coalition building with AARP and aging organizations | Model code adopted; federal incentives for state adoption |
| Medicaid HCBS as mandatory benefit | Congress | Year 5-6 | Fiscal data from Phase 1 showing institutional cost savings | Legislation enacted; phase-in begins |
| Home modification grant program expansion to $1 billion/year | Congress | Year 5-6 | Phase 1 program demonstrates effectiveness | Appropriations increased |

### Coalition Strategy

- **Housing program expansion**: Coalition with NLIHC, CBPP, affordable housing developers, and local housing authorities who benefit from increased federal funding
- **LIHTC accessibility**: Partner with state HFA directors who administer LIHTC and can advocate for workable requirements; engage developers who have voluntarily exceeded accessibility minimums
- **Visitability**: Critical to build AARP coalition (aging in place) and universal design professionals. Frame as "future-proof housing" for aging boomers. Engage homebuilders through cost-sharing and transition period
- **Medicaid HCBS**: Coalition with *Olmstead* advocates, nursing home reform organizations, and state budget hawks who recognize that community care costs less than institutional care
- **Home modification**: Broad coalition with AARP, home improvement industry, occupational therapists, and aging organizations

### Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Probability | Mitigation Strategy |
|------|------------|-------------------|
| NAHB blocks visitability legislation | High | Build AARP coalition; pursue state-level wins to demonstrate feasibility; engage supportive builders |
| LIHTC developers resist accessibility increase | Medium | Phase in requirements; provide enhanced credits for accessible units; demonstrate minimal cost impact |
| Medicaid HCBS cost opposition | High | Document institutional cost comparisons; propose phased implementation; seek CBO scoring showing long-term savings |
| FHA pre-construction review opposition | Medium | Frame as helping builders comply rather than punishing them; offer technical assistance |

## Phase 3: Structural Transformation (Years 6-10)

### Objective

Achieve fundamental changes that make accessible, affordable housing the norm rather than the exception, including universal visitability, adequate housing program funding, and elimination of the Medicaid institutional bias.

### Actions

| Action | Lead Actor | Timeline | Dependencies | Success Metric |
|--------|-----------|----------|--------------|----------------|
| National visitability code adoption (majority of states) | ICC/states | Year 6-8 | Phase 2 model code adoption; state advocacy campaigns | 30+ states adopt visitability building code |
| Eliminate Section 8 disability waiting lists | Congress/HUD | Year 7-9 | Progressive funding increases from Phases 1-2 | Average wait time under 1 year |
| Full HCBS mandatory implementation | CMS/states | Year 7-10 | Phase 2 legislation enacted; state plan amendments | HCBS waiting lists eliminated; institutional populations reduced 30% |
| Universal accessible housing registry | HUD | Year 7-8 | Phase 1-2 registry development and piloting | National registry operational; MLS integration complete |
| Accessible housing performance standards in all federal programs | HUD/Treasury/FHFA | Year 8-10 | Data infrastructure from registry; program reforms | All federal housing programs meet enhanced accessibility standards |

### Long-Term Targets (Year 10)

| Metric | Current Baseline | Year 5 Target | Year 10 Target |
|--------|-----------------|---------------|----------------|
| Housing units with visitability features | 3.8% | 8% | 20% |
| Post-1991 multifamily FHA compliance | <10% | 40% | 80% |
| Section 811 units | 35,000 | 60,000 | 120,000 |
| Mainstream Vouchers | 62,000 | 100,000 | 200,000 |
| Section 8 disability wait time (average) | 2-5 years | 1-3 years | <1 year |
| SSI rent-to-income ratio (national average) | 121% | 100% | 80% |
| HCBS waiting list size | 650,000 | 300,000 | 0 |
| Home modifications funded annually | ~20,000 (all programs) | 50,000 | 100,000 |
| Accessible LIHTC units (% of new production) | 5% | 10% | 15% |

## Success Metrics Summary

### Short-Term (Years 1-3)

- Section 811 funding doubled to $500 million/year
- 30,000 new Mainstream Vouchers allocated
- Federal home modification grant program operational; 30,000+ modifications funded
- HUD FHA design enforcement unit operational; 200+ compliance reviews conducted
- CDBG/HOME zoning conditions finalized
- Accessible housing registry piloted in 5 states

### Medium-Term (Years 3-6)

- Comprehensive disability housing legislation enacted
- LIHTC accessibility requirement increased to 15% of units
- FHA pre-construction review mandated
- Visitability model code adopted by ICC
- Medicaid HCBS mandatory benefit legislation enacted
- Home modification program expanded to $1 billion/year

### Long-Term (Years 6-10)

- 30+ states adopt visitability building codes
- Section 8 disability waiting lists under 1 year
- HCBS waiting lists eliminated
- 20% of housing stock has visitability features
- National accessible housing registry fully operational
- People with disabilities have materially expanded housing choices
