# Caregiver Support: What Citizens Can Do

## Overview

Reforming the caregiver support system requires action at every level, from individual community engagement to federal policy advocacy. The actions below are organized by type and effort level, recognizing that caregivers themselves often have limited time and energy for advocacy.

---

## Individual Actions

### Learn and Share

- **Educate yourself** about caregiver issues by reading reports from AARP, PHI National, and the National Alliance for Caregiving
- **Share caregiver stories** (with consent) to make the invisible labor of caregiving visible in your community
- **Talk to family and friends** about caregiving as a shared social responsibility, not a private burden
- **Follow** disability rights and caregiver advocacy organizations on social media to stay informed
- **Attend** local forums, town halls, or webinars on caregiving and disability policy

### Support Caregivers in Your Community

- **Offer specific help** to caregivers you know (meals, transportation, companionship for care recipients) rather than general offers
- **Provide respite** by spending time with a friend or neighbor's care recipient so the caregiver can take a break
- **Recognize direct care workers** in your life as professionals; treat them with respect and advocate for their fair treatment
- **Support businesses** that provide caregiver-friendly policies and services
- **Join or start** a caregiver support group in your community or faith organization

## Advocacy Actions

### Contact Elected Officials

- **Call your members of Congress** to support the Better Care Better Jobs Act and HCBS funding increases
- **Write to your state legislators** about Medicaid HCBS expansion, waiting list reduction, and direct care worker wages
- **Meet with legislative staff** during district office hours to discuss caregiver support in personal terms
- **Submit testimony** during state Medicaid budget hearings advocating for HCBS investment
- **Thank legislators** who vote for caregiver support measures to reinforce positive action

### Key Federal Priorities to Advocate For

| Priority | Bill/Action | Who to Contact |
|----------|-------------|----------------|
| HCBS expansion | Better Care Better Jobs Act | U.S. Senators and Representatives |
| Family caregiver tax credit | Caregiver tax credit proposals | U.S. Senators and Representatives |
| Direct care worker wages | CMS HCBS access rule | CMS Administrator; U.S. Senators |
| NFCSP funding increase | HHS Appropriations | U.S. Representatives (Appropriations) |
| Self-direction expansion | CMS guidance | CMS Administrator |

### Key State Priorities to Advocate For

| Priority | Action | Who to Contact |
|----------|--------|----------------|
| HCBS waiting list reduction | State Medicaid budget | State legislators; Governor |
| Direct care worker wages | State minimum wage for HCBS | State legislators |
| Family caregiver programs | State caregiver support legislation | State legislators |
| Self-direction options | State Medicaid waiver amendments | State Medicaid director |
| LTSS insurance | State LTSS trust fund proposals | State legislators; Governor |

## Organize and Mobilize

### Join Organizations

- **AARP** - Join and participate in caregiver advocacy campaigns: <https://www.aarp.org/caregiving/>
- **National Alliance for Caregiving** - Support research and policy advocacy: <https://www.caregiving.org/>
- **Caring Across Generations** - Join the cross-generational care campaign: <https://caringacross.org/>
- **ADAPT** - Participate in community integration advocacy: <https://adapt.org/>
- **National Council on Independent Living** - Support disability rights advocacy: <https://ncil.org/>
- **National Domestic Workers Alliance** - Support direct care worker organizing: <https://www.domesticworkers.org/>

### Build Local Coalitions

- **Connect** disability organizations, aging organizations, labor unions, and faith communities in your area around shared caregiving priorities
- **Partner** with your local Center for Independent Living to advocate for HCBS expansion
- **Work with** local Area Agency on Aging to expand caregiver services
- **Engage** employer groups to support workplace caregiver flexibility policies
- **Collaborate** with healthcare providers who see the impact of caregiver burnout and service gaps

### Amplify the Issue

- **Write letters to the editor** or op-eds in local newspapers about the caregiving crisis
- **Testify** at city council, county commission, or state legislative hearings
- **Organize** community forums on caregiving featuring people with disabilities, family caregivers, and direct care workers
- **Use social media** to share policy information and personal caregiving stories (hashtags: #CaregiverSupport, #CareEconomy, #HCBSNow)
- **Contact local media** to cover caregiver workforce shortages and their impact on your community

## Civic Participation

### Vote and Engage in Elections

- **Vote for candidates** who prioritize caregiver support, HCBS expansion, and disability rights
- **Ask candidates** during town halls and debates about their positions on caregiving policy
- **Attend** candidate forums organized by disability and caregiver organizations
- **Volunteer** for campaigns that prioritize care policy
- **Register to vote** and help caregivers and people with disabilities access the ballot (many face accessibility barriers)

### Participate in Policy Processes

- **Comment on proposed rules** when CMS, DOL, or state agencies propose regulations affecting caregivers and HCBS (public comment periods are a powerful tool)
- **Serve on advisory boards** including state Medicaid advisory committees, caregiver advisory councils, and disability planning councils
- **Apply for appointments** to RAISE Family Caregivers Advisory Council and similar bodies
- **Attend** CMS listening sessions on HCBS access and quality
- **Participate** in state needs assessments and HCBS waiver renewal processes

## If You Are a Caregiver

### Protect Yourself

- **Learn your rights** under the FMLA, ADA (if you have a disability), and state caregiver protection laws
- **Document your caregiving** hours and expenses for potential tax benefits and future Social Security claims
- **Access respite services** through your local Area Agency on Aging or Center for Independent Living
- **Connect with support groups** for emotional support and practical advice
- **Take care of your health**: caregiver burnout has serious health consequences

### If You Are a Paid Caregiver

- **Know your rights** under the Fair Labor Standards Act (minimum wage, overtime)
- **Contact your state labor agency** if you are not being paid minimum wage or overtime
- **Consider unionization** through SEIU, AFSCME, or other unions organizing home care workers
- **Pursue training and credentials** to advance your career and increase your earning potential
- **Report** unsafe working conditions to OSHA or your state occupational safety agency
