# Caregiver Support: Implementation Roadmap

## Overview

This roadmap sequences caregiver support reforms across three phases, building from immediately achievable actions through medium-term structural reforms to long-term transformative changes. The sequencing reflects political feasibility, administrative capacity, and the principle that early wins build momentum and coalition strength for larger reforms.

---

## Phase 1: Immediate Reforms (Years 1-2)

### 1.1 Permanent HCBS FMAP Enhancement

**Action**: Enact permanent 10 percentage point FMAP increase for Medicaid HCBS spending

**Prerequisites**: Congressional legislation (budget reconciliation eligible)

**Key Steps**

1. Reintroduce Better Care Better Jobs Act provisions as standalone legislation
2. Build coalition support from governors (bipartisan HCBS support exists among governors)
3. Attach to must-pass legislation if standalone path fails
4. CMS issues implementation guidance within 90 days of enactment
5. States submit spending plans within 180 days

**Success Metrics**

- Legislation enacted within 18 months
- All 50 states submit enhanced HCBS spending plans within 1 year
- HCBS spending share increases by 3-5 percentage points within 2 years

### 1.2 Family Caregiver Tax Credit

**Action**: Create refundable tax credit of up to $5,000 for qualifying family caregivers

**Prerequisites**: Congressional legislation; IRS implementation

**Key Steps**

1. Draft legislation defining qualifying caregiver, care recipient, and minimum caregiving hours
2. Build bipartisan support (tax credits have stronger Republican support than direct spending)
3. IRS develops form and instructions for claiming credit
4. Coordinate with state tax agencies for state-level piggyback credits

**Success Metrics**

- Legislation enacted within 12 months
- 3-5 million family caregivers claim credit in first year
- Average benefit: $3,500 per qualifying caregiver

### 1.3 NFCSP Funding Increase

**Action**: Double NFCSP funding from $200 million to $400 million through appropriations

**Prerequisites**: Congressional appropriation

**Key Steps**

1. Include increase in HHS appropriations bill
2. Direct ACL to prioritize respite services and underserved populations
3. Expand eligibility to all family caregivers regardless of care recipient age
4. Establish rural access set-aside (15% of new funds)

**Success Metrics**

- Funding increase enacted within 12 months
- Caregivers served increases from 700,000 to 1.2 million
- Average respite hours per caregiver increases by 50%

### 1.4 Executive Actions

**Action**: Presidential executive order directing federal agencies to strengthen caregiver support

**Prerequisites**: Presidential action; no legislation required

**Key Steps**

1. Direct CMS to issue HCBS access rule requiring states to ensure adequate provider networks
2. Direct DOL to strengthen enforcement of FLSA protections for home care workers
3. Direct OPM to expand federal employee caregiver leave policies
4. Direct VA to expand caregiver support program eligibility
5. Establish interagency caregiver policy coordination council

**Success Metrics**

- Executive order signed within 6 months
- CMS HCBS access rule proposed within 12 months
- Federal employee caregiver leave expansion implemented within 6 months

## Phase 2: Structural Reforms (Years 3-5)

### 2.1 Medicaid HCBS Entitlement

**Action**: Amend Social Security Act to make HCBS an entitlement on par with institutional care

**Prerequisites**: Phase 1 FMAP enhancement; congressional legislation

**Key Steps**

1. Draft legislation converting HCBS from optional to mandatory Medicaid service
2. Phase in entitlement over 3 years to allow state capacity building
3. Establish federal minimum service package requirements
4. Create federal HCBS quality standards with CMS enforcement authority
5. Phase down institutional FMAP for states not meeting rebalancing targets

**Success Metrics**

- Legislation enacted by Year 3
- HCBS waiting lists reduced by 50% by Year 4
- HCBS waiting lists eliminated by Year 6
- HCBS spending reaches 65% of total LTSS by Year 5

### 2.2 Direct Care Workforce Compensation Standards

**Action**: Establish federal minimum compensation standards for Medicaid-funded direct care workers

**Prerequisites**: Phase 1 FMAP enhancement; data on ARPA wage impact

**Key Steps**

1. CMS issues rule requiring Medicaid HCBS rates sufficient for 150% of local minimum wage
2. Require pass-through of 80% of HCBS payments to worker compensation
3. Phase in over 3 years with enhanced FMAP to cover costs
4. Establish worker benefit requirements (health insurance, paid leave) for full-time workers
5. DOL and CMS coordinate enforcement

**Success Metrics**

- Rule finalized by Year 3
- Median direct care wage reaches $20/hour by Year 5
- Turnover rate declines to under 30% by Year 5
- Workers receiving public benefits decreases from 43% to under 20%

### 2.3 Self-Direction Expansion

**Action**: Require all states to offer self-direction in every HCBS waiver program

**Prerequisites**: Fiscal intermediary infrastructure development

**Key Steps**

1. CMS issues guidance requiring self-direction as an option in all 1915(c) waivers
2. Fund fiscal intermediary capacity building in underserved states ($200 million over 3 years)
3. Establish federal self-direction quality and safety standards
4. Allow family member payment (including spouses) in all self-direction programs
5. Fund Centers for Independent Living to provide peer support for self-directing consumers

**Success Metrics**

- All states offer self-direction by Year 4
- Self-direction participation increases from 1.3 million to 2 million
- Consumer satisfaction scores for self-direction exceed agency-directed services

### 2.4 Workforce Pipeline Development

**Action**: Launch national direct care workforce development initiative

**Prerequisites**: Federal funding; state and educational institution partnerships

**Key Steps**

1. Establish Direct Care Workforce Fund ($1 billion annually)
2. Create national portable credential for direct care workers
3. Fund registered apprenticeships in all 50 states
4. Establish student loan forgiveness for direct care workers (3-year service commitment)
5. Partner with community colleges for training programs
6. Address immigration barriers for foreign-born care workers

**Success Metrics**

- 100,000 new credentialed workers per year by Year 5
- Apprenticeship programs operating in all 50 states by Year 4
- Workforce shortage reduced by 30% by Year 5

## Phase 3: Transformative Reforms (Years 6-10)

### 3.1 National LTSS Insurance Program

**Action**: Establish social insurance for long-term services and supports

**Prerequisites**: Phases 1 and 2 reforms demonstrating system capacity; public support building

**Key Steps**

1. Study commission established in Year 3 to design program (building on Washington WA Cares experience)
2. Legislation introduced by Year 6 with 0.5-1.0% payroll contribution
3. Benefits phased in over 3 years
4. Cash benefits can be used for HCBS, family caregiver compensation, or institutional care
5. Coordinate with Medicaid to supplement rather than replace existing coverage
6. Built-in actuarial review and sustainability mechanisms

**Success Metrics**

- Study commission report by Year 5
- Legislation enacted by Year 7
- First benefits paid by Year 9
- Universal LTSS coverage established by Year 10

### 3.2 Comprehensive Family Caregiver Protection

**Action**: Enact comprehensive family caregiver economic protection legislation

**Prerequisites**: Demonstrated effectiveness of Phase 1 tax credit; political momentum

**Key Steps**

1. Establish Social Security caregiver credits for periods of reduced employment
2. Expand FMLA to cover ongoing caregiving (not just initial onset) with partial wage replacement
3. Prohibit employment discrimination based on caregiver status
4. Create portable caregiver benefits (retirement savings, healthcare) independent of employment
5. Fund national caregiver health and wellness program

**Success Metrics**

- Legislation enacted by Year 7
- Lifetime earnings loss for caregivers reduced by 50%
- Caregiver workforce exit rate reduced from 10% to under 5%
- Caregiver health outcomes improve measurably

### 3.3 Complete Medicaid LTSS Rebalancing

**Action**: Achieve 75% HCBS share of total Medicaid LTSS spending nationwide

**Prerequisites**: HCBS entitlement; workforce capacity; LTSS insurance supplementation

**Key Steps**

1. Implement escalating FMAP reductions for institutional spending above 25% threshold
2. Fund remaining institutional transitions through expanded Money Follows the Person
3. Convert remaining small institutions to community-based settings
4. Maintain some institutional capacity for individuals who genuinely require 24/7 nursing care
5. Continuous quality monitoring and outcome measurement

**Success Metrics**

- 75% HCBS share by Year 10
- Institutional census reduced to medically necessary placements only
- Zero *Olmstead* violations due to waiting lists or service inadequacy

## Risk Assessment

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|------------|
| Federal funding cuts | Moderate | Critical | Build bipartisan support; demonstrate cost-effectiveness |
| State implementation failure | Moderate | High | Enhanced CMS oversight; technical assistance; FMAP incentives |
| Workforce shortage persists despite investment | Moderate | High | Immigration reform; technology; competitive wages |
| Nursing home industry blocks rebalancing | High | High | Gradual phase-in; transition assistance for affected facilities |
| Political opposition to payroll tax | High | High | Start with state-level models; build evidence base |
| Administrative complexity delays implementation | Moderate | Moderate | Phased rollout; federal technical assistance |
