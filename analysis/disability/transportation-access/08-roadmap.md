# Transportation Access: Roadmap

## Phase 1: Quick Wins and Regulatory Action (Years 1-2)

### Objective

Achieve immediate improvements through executive action, regulatory decisions, and targeted legislation that do not require comprehensive reauthorization or major new appropriations. Focus on standards adoption, paratransit technology pilots, and ride-hailing accountability.

### Actions

| Action | Type | Lead Actor | Timeline | Est. Cost |
|--------|------|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Adopt PROWAG as enforceable federal standards | Regulatory (DOJ/DOT rulemaking) | DOJ, DOT | Months 1-12 | Minimal (federal level) |
| Finalize NHTSA autonomous vehicle accessibility standards | Regulatory | NHTSA | Months 6-18 | Minimal (rulemaking) |
| Launch 20 paratransit technology modernization pilots | Administrative (FTA grants) | FTA | Months 3-18 | $100M in competitive grants |
| Issue federal ride-hailing accessibility guidance | Administrative | DOT, DOJ | Months 3-12 | Minimal |
| Expand ASAP program funding through supplemental appropriation | Legislative | Congress | Year 1 | $500M supplemental |
| Issue CMS guidance permitting NEMT-transit coordination | Administrative | CMS | Months 3-9 | Budget-neutral |
| Require disability representation on MPO advisory boards | Administrative (FTA guidance) | FTA | Months 6-12 | Minimal |
| Conduct national transit accessibility audit | Administrative | FTA, U.S. Access Board | Months 6-24 | $25M |

### Milestones

- Month 6: PROWAG rulemaking initiated; CMS NEMT guidance issued; paratransit pilot grants awarded
- Month 12: PROWAG adopted; ride-hailing guidance issued; national accessibility audit underway
- Month 18: NHTSA AV standards finalized; 20 paratransit pilots operational; ASAP supplemental funding awarded
- Month 24: National accessibility audit complete with baseline data for all transit systems

## Phase 2: Legislative Reform and Infrastructure Investment (Years 2-5)

### Objective

Enact comprehensive legislation through the next surface transportation reauthorization that establishes dedicated accessibility funding, mandates ride-hailing accessibility, creates rural accessible transportation programs, and integrates NEMT with public transit.

### Actions

| Action | Type | Lead Actor | Timeline | Est. Cost |
|--------|------|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Establish National Accessible Transit Infrastructure Fund | Legislative (reauthorization) | Congress | Year 2-3 | $5B/year |
| Enact federal ride-hailing WAV mandate | Legislative | Congress | Year 2 | Industry-funded via surcharges |
| Create Rural Accessible Transportation Program | Legislative (reauthorization) | Congress | Year 2-3 | $1.5B/year |
| Mandate NEMT-transit coordination nationwide | Legislative + Regulatory | Congress, CMS | Year 2-3 | Budget-neutral to positive |
| Establish Pedestrian Infrastructure Accessibility Grant Program | Legislative (reauthorization) | Congress | Year 2-3 | $2B/year |
| Require accessibility transition plans from all transit agencies | Legislative | Congress, FTA | Year 3 | Planning costs: $50-100M |
| Scale paratransit technology modernization to all major agencies | Administrative | FTA | Years 3-5 | $500M total |
| Expand NEMT to essential non-medical trips | Legislative + Regulatory | Congress, CMS | Years 3-5 | $2-3B/year |

### Milestones

- Year 2: Ride-hailing WAV mandate enacted; WAV service required in top 50 markets
- Year 3: Surface transportation reauthorization enacted with accessibility funding provisions; rural program established; pedestrian grant program created
- Year 4: Transit agencies submit accessibility transition plans; NEMT-transit integration in 25+ states; ride-hailing WAV in all markets
- Year 5: $25B in accessibility capital projects underway; paratransit modernization complete in major agencies; rural accessible transportation in 40+ states

## Phase 3: System Transformation (Years 5-10)

### Objective

Complete the transformation from a partially accessible, fragmented transportation system to a universally accessible, integrated mobility network that serves people of all abilities in urban, suburban, and rural areas.

### Actions

| Action | Type | Lead Actor | Timeline | Est. Cost |
|--------|------|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Complete priority rail station accessibility projects | Capital (federal + local) | Transit agencies, FTA | Years 5-8 | Continued $5B/year |
| Deploy accessible autonomous vehicles in commercial service | Industry + Regulatory | AV companies, NHTSA | Years 5-8 | Industry-funded |
| Achieve on-demand paratransit nationwide | Administrative + Technology | Transit agencies, FTA | Years 5-7 | Operational savings offset costs |
| Integrate all mobility services (transit, paratransit, ride-hailing, NEMT) | Technology + Policy | Transit agencies, technology providers | Years 5-10 | $500M-1B for integration platforms |
| Complete pedestrian infrastructure accessibility in all federally-funded areas | Capital (federal + state + local) | State/local governments, FHWA | Years 5-10 | Continued $2B/year federal |
| Achieve accessible rural transportation in all counties | Administrative + Capital | State agencies, FTA | Years 7-10 | Continued $1.5B/year |
| Establish permanent accessibility maintenance standards | Regulatory | FTA, FHWA, DOJ | Year 8 | Minimal (regulatory) |
| Conduct 10-year comprehensive accessibility assessment | Administrative | FTA, U.S. Access Board | Year 10 | $50M |

### Milestones

- Year 6: 50% of legacy rail stations fully accessible; on-demand paratransit in 100+ agencies
- Year 7: Accessible AVs in commercial service in 25+ markets; rural accessible transportation in all states
- Year 8: 75% of rail stations accessible; integrated mobility platforms in 50+ metro areas
- Year 10: 90%+ of transit systems fully accessible; on-demand paratransit nationwide; comprehensive accessibility assessment complete

## Risk Mitigation

### Political Risks

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation Strategy |
|------|------------|--------|---------------------|
| Reauthorization delayed or reduced | Medium | High | Pursue administrative actions independently; use continuing resolution authorities |
| Highway lobby blocks transit accessibility funding | Medium | High | Frame as infrastructure, not transit; build rural-urban coalition |
| Ride-hailing industry lobbying defeats WAV mandate | Medium | Medium | Pursue state-by-state mandates in parallel; use litigation as complement |
| AV industry delays accessibility standards | Medium | Medium | NHTSA mandate pre-empts industry timeline; consumer advocacy |

### Implementation Risks

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation Strategy |
|------|------------|--------|---------------------|
| Transit agencies lack capacity for capital projects | Medium | Medium | FTA technical assistance; phased deployment; construction management support |
| Paratransit technology fails to scale | Low | Medium | Multiple technology vendors; proven models (Boston, Dallas, Seattle) |
| WAV vehicle supply insufficient | Medium | Medium | Manufacturing incentives; federal fleet procurement; vehicle conversion subsidies |
| Rural transportation models not sustainable | Medium | Medium | Flexible program design; multiple delivery models; integration with NEMT |

### Equity Risks

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation Strategy |
|------|------------|--------|---------------------|
| Technology solutions exclude non-smartphone users | Medium | High | Maintain phone booking; in-person options at transit offices |
| Urban investments proceed while rural needs deferred | Medium | High | Dedicated rural program with separate funding; rural-first pilot grants |
| Accessibility improvements benefit some disability types more than others | Medium | Medium | Universal design standards; advisory boards representing all disability types |
