# Transportation Access: Actions

## For Individuals

### Learn First

**Understand the Problem**

- [ ] Read [Current State](02-current-state.md) to understand transportation access barriers for people with disabilities
- [ ] Read [Root Causes](04-root-causes.md) to understand why accessible transportation remains inadequate
- [ ] Learn about the ADA's transportation provisions (Title II, Subtitle B) and paratransit requirements
- [ ] Review the Bureau of Transportation Statistics data on travel patterns of people with disabilities
- [ ] Understand your local transit agency's accessibility features, paratransit eligibility process, and service area

**Know Your Rights**

- [ ] Research your ADA right to accessible public transit and complementary paratransit
- [ ] Learn the paratransit eligibility process for your transit agency (49 C.F.R. Section 37.121-37.155)
- [ ] Understand the ADA prohibition on paratransit trip denials due to capacity (49 C.F.R. Section 37.131(f))
- [ ] Know that ride-hailing services must not discriminate based on disability under Title III of the ADA
- [ ] Contact your state Protection and Advocacy agency if you experience transit accessibility violations
- [ ] Learn the complaint process: file with FTA or DOJ if your transit agency violates ADA requirements

### Take Action

**Contact Representatives**

- [ ] Call your members of Congress to support increased funding for the All Stations Accessibility Program (ASAP)
- [ ] Email your state legislators about pedestrian infrastructure accessibility (curb ramps, sidewalks, accessible signals)
- [ ] Write to your city council about local transit accessibility gaps and sidewalk maintenance failures
- [ ] Attend transit agency board meetings and raise accessibility concerns during public comment
- [ ] Contact your governor about state-level ride-hailing accessibility requirements

**Engage with Transit Planning**

- [ ] Attend your metropolitan planning organization (MPO) meetings and demand disability representation
- [ ] Participate in transit agency public hearings on service changes, capital plans, and accessibility projects
- [ ] Submit public comments on NHTSA's autonomous vehicle accessibility rulemaking
- [ ] Respond to FTA notices of proposed rulemaking related to transit accessibility
- [ ] Participate in CMS public comment periods on NEMT policy changes

**Join the Movement**

- [ ] Sign up with ADAPT, AAPD, or your local disability rights organization focused on transportation
- [ ] Join your transit agency's accessibility advisory committee (most agencies are required to have one)
- [ ] Connect with the National Aging and Disability Transportation Center (NADTC) for resources
- [ ] Follow transit accessibility policy news through NCD and NDRN
- [ ] Join or form a local coalition of disability, aging, and transit equity organizations

**Share Your Story**

- [ ] Document your transportation access experiences (paratransit delays, trip denials, inaccessible stations, ride-hailing refusals)
- [ ] File formal ADA complaints when you experience transportation discrimination
- [ ] Share your story with advocacy organizations for policy campaigns
- [ ] Testify at state and local legislative hearings on transit accessibility
- [ ] Write letters to the editor about transportation barriers and their impact on independence
- [ ] Use social media to document and publicize specific accessibility failures

### Vote and Support

**Direct Support**

- [ ] Donate to disability rights organizations working on transportation policy (ADAPT, DRA, NFB, United Spinal)
- [ ] Volunteer with organizations advocating for transit accessibility reform
- [ ] Support candidates who endorse accessible transportation investment
- [ ] Vote YES on state and local ballot measures for transit funding (which includes accessibility improvements)

**Ongoing Engagement**

- [ ] Vote in every election, including state and local elections where transit funding decisions are made
- [ ] Track transit accessibility legislation in your state and in Congress
- [ ] Stay informed about FTA ASAP grants and other accessibility funding opportunities in your area
- [ ] Participate in the paratransit rider advisory committees at your local transit agency
- [ ] Monitor ride-hailing company compliance with local accessibility requirements

## For Communities

### Local Transportation Access Campaigns

**Assess the Landscape**

1. Map your community's accessible transit coverage: Which areas have accessible fixed-route service? Where are the gaps?
2. Document pedestrian infrastructure barriers: missing sidewalks, non-compliant curb ramps, inaccessible pedestrian signals
3. Survey community members with disabilities about transportation needs, barriers, and unmet demand
4. Evaluate paratransit service quality: on-time performance, trip denial rates, wait times, service area adequacy
5. Assess ride-hailing accessibility: Is wheelchair-accessible vehicle (WAV) service available? What are wait times?

**Build a Coalition**

1. Recruit disability organizations, aging services agencies, veterans' groups, and transit rider organizations
2. Engage healthcare providers and hospitals that see patients miss appointments due to transportation barriers
3. Connect with employers who cannot hire or retain workers with disabilities due to transportation gaps
4. Partner with pedestrian and bicycle advocacy groups (who share sidewalk and crossing infrastructure concerns)
5. Include people with diverse disability types as leaders and spokespeople (mobility, vision, hearing, cognitive)
6. Engage environmental and transit justice organizations that share goals of transit investment

**Community Transportation Initiatives**

- Organize "accessibility audits" of transit stations, bus stops, and pedestrian routes
- Create community scorecards for local transit agency accessibility performance
- Establish peer support programs connecting experienced paratransit riders with new users
- Advocate for snow and ice removal ordinances that include accessible routes and transit stops
- Organize "ride-along" events for elected officials and transit board members to experience paratransit service
- Document and publicize the economic cost of transportation inaccessibility in your community

## For Organizations

### Transit Agencies

**Improve Accessibility Operations**

- [ ] Conduct comprehensive accessibility audits of all stations, stops, vehicles, and information systems
- [ ] Establish real-time tracking of wheelchair lift and ramp failures with mandatory repair timelines
- [ ] Train all operators on disability awareness, ramp deployment, wheelchair securement, and service animal policies
- [ ] Implement real-time transit information in accessible formats (screen reader compatible apps, audio announcements, visual displays)
- [ ] Develop paratransit technology modernization plans (same-day booking, real-time tracking, reduced wait times)

**Plan for Accessibility**

- [ ] Include people with disabilities in all service planning, capital planning, and technology procurement processes
- [ ] Develop accessibility transition plans with specific timelines and funding commitments
- [ ] Apply for FTA ASAP grants and Section 5310 funding for accessibility capital projects
- [ ] Pilot technology-enabled paratransit modernization (partner with ride-hailing or microtransit platforms)
- [ ] Coordinate with housing and employment agencies to align transit service with disability community needs

### Employers

**Address Transportation Barriers for Employees with Disabilities**

- [ ] Survey employees and job applicants with disabilities about transportation barriers to employment
- [ ] Provide transit subsidies or transportation assistance as a reasonable accommodation under the ADA
- [ ] Offer flexible scheduling to accommodate paratransit reservation requirements and pickup windows
- [ ] Partner with transit agencies on employer shuttle or vanpool programs that include accessible vehicles
- [ ] Advocate with local transit agencies for service to employment centers

### Healthcare Providers

**Address Transportation as a Social Determinant of Health**

- [ ] Screen patients for transportation barriers to medical care
- [ ] Connect patients with Medicaid NEMT, paratransit, and community transportation resources
- [ ] Document missed appointments due to transportation for advocacy and policy purposes
- [ ] Advocate within healthcare systems for transportation assistance programs
- [ ] Support integration of NEMT with public transit to improve efficiency and expand access

### Technology Companies

**Build Accessible Transportation Technology**

- [ ] Design ride-hailing and mobility apps with full accessibility (screen reader compatibility, simple interfaces, multiple input methods)
- [ ] Develop wheelchair-accessible vehicle options and integrate them into ride-hailing platforms
- [ ] Include people with disabilities in user testing and design research for transportation technology
- [ ] Design autonomous vehicles with accessibility from inception (wheelchair access, audio interfaces, remote assistance)
- [ ] Share accessibility data and best practices across the transportation technology industry

## For Advocates and Policymakers

### Federal Level

**Legislative Priorities (119th-120th Congress)**

1. Expand and permanently authorize the All Stations Accessibility Program (ASAP) at $1 billion per year
2. Enact federal ride-hailing WAV mandate requiring equivalent service for wheelchair users
3. Establish Rural Accessible Transportation Program with dedicated formula funding
4. Adopt PROWAG as enforceable federal standards for pedestrian infrastructure
5. Finalize NHTSA autonomous vehicle accessibility standards
6. Require NEMT-transit coordination through Medicaid reform

**Regulatory Priorities**

1. DOJ/DOT: Adopt PROWAG as binding accessibility standards for public rights-of-way
2. NHTSA: Finalize autonomous vehicle accessibility rule with strong wheelchair access requirements
3. FTA: Issue guidance on paratransit technology modernization and same-day booking requirements
4. CMS: Issue guidance permitting Medicaid NEMT-transit coordination and expanding trip purposes
5. FTA: Require disability representation on all MPO advisory boards receiving federal funds

### State Level

**Model Legislation Priorities**

1. State ride-hailing WAV mandates (using New York City model)
2. Pedestrian infrastructure accessibility requirements and funding
3. State transit accessibility reporting and accountability standards
4. Snow and ice removal requirements for accessible routes and transit stops
5. State autonomous vehicle accessibility standards (for states regulating AV deployment)

**Administrative Actions**

1. State DOT accessibility audits and transition plans
2. State Medicaid NEMT coordination with public transit
3. State procurement policies requiring accessible vehicles in transit fleets
4. Integration of transportation access into state disability services planning

### Building Effective Transportation Access Advocacy

**Messaging Principles**

| Do | Do Not |
|----|--------|
| Frame accessible transportation as a civil right and economic investment | Frame as charity or special accommodation |
| Quantify the cost of inaccessibility ($30+ billion annually in lost productivity and healthcare) | Lead with the cost of accessibility improvements alone |
| Use personal stories alongside data (homebound individuals, missed medical appointments, lost jobs) | Rely solely on statistics |
| Emphasize universal benefit (aging population, parents, travelers, temporary injuries) | Present as benefiting only people with formal disabilities |
| Connect to employment, healthcare, and community participation outcomes | Present transportation as isolated infrastructure issue |
| Frame as completing the ADA's unfinished promise | Frame as new or additional mandates |

**Coalition Building**

- Disability organizations provide moral authority, personal stories, and legal expertise
- AARP and aging organizations provide political influence (56 million Americans over 65)
- Transit agencies can be allies when advocacy supports increased funding
- Healthcare organizations can document the health cost of transportation inaccessibility
- Employers can demonstrate the economic cost of inaccessible transportation
- Environmental groups share goals of transit investment and reduced car dependency
- Veterans' organizations provide bipartisan credibility for accessible transportation
