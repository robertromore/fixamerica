## Legal Resources

### Key Statutes

| Law | Citation | Transportation Relevance |
|-----|----------|--------------------------|
| Americans with Disabilities Act, Title II | 42 U.S.C. Section 12131 et seq. | Public transit accessibility; paratransit mandate |
| Americans with Disabilities Act, Title III | 42 U.S.C. Section 12181 et seq. | Private transportation accessibility |
| ADA Amendments Act of 2008 | P.L. 110-325 | Broadened disability definitions affecting transit eligibility |
| Rehabilitation Act, Section 504 | 29 U.S.C. Section 794 | Accessible transit in federally funded programs |
| Federal Transit Act | 49 U.S.C. Section 5301 et seq. | Transit funding including accessibility programs |
| Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (IIJA) | P.L. 117-58 (2021) | ASAP program; increased transit funding |
| Medicaid NEMT | 42 U.S.C. Section 1396a(a)(4) | Non-emergency medical transportation for Medicaid beneficiaries |

### Key Court Cases

| Case | Citation | Transportation Significance |
|------|----------|----------------------------|
| *APTA v. Lewis* | 655 F.2d 1272 (D.C. Cir. 1981) | Struck down DOT transit accessibility regulations (pre-ADA) |
| *Olmstead v. L.C.* | 527 U.S. 581 (1999) | Community integration right; transportation as prerequisite |
| *Anderson v. Rochester-Genesee Reg'l Transp. Auth.* | 337 F.3d 201 (2d Cir. 2003) | ADA paratransit eligibility standards |
| *Disability Rights Advocates v. Uber Technologies* | No. 3:21-cv-00521 (N.D. Cal.) | Ride-hailing wheelchair accessibility |
| *NFB v. Uber Technologies* | No. 3:14-cv-04086 (N.D. Cal. 2014) | Service animal refusal by ride-hailing drivers |

|------------|----------|-------|
