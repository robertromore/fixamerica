# Education and IDEA: Roadmap

## Phase 1: Immediate Actions (2025-2026)

### Build the Coalition

**Core Coalition Partners**

- Parent organizations (COPAA, The Arc, NCLD, Autism Society)
- Disability rights organizations (NDRN, DREDF, TASH)
- Education organizations (CEC, NASDSE)
- Teachers' unions (NEA, AFT -- on funding and workforce issues)
- Civil rights organizations (NAACP, MALDEF -- on disproportionality)

**Key Activities**

- Establish IDEA Full Funding Coalition with unified legislative priorities
- Commission updated cost-benefit analysis of IDEA full funding
- Develop shared messaging: "Congress Made a Promise. It's Time to Keep It."
- Build relationships with key committee staff in both parties

### Support Active Legislative Campaigns

**119th Congress (2025-2026) Priorities**

| Priority | Bill | Action Needed |
|----------|------|---------------|
| IDEA full funding | IDEA Full Funding Act | Push for committee hearings; build bipartisan co-sponsor list |
| Teacher pipeline | Special Education Teacher Pipeline Act | Bipartisan support exists; push for floor vote |
| Discipline reform | Keep Kids in School Act | Build evidence base; address Republican concerns |
| Transition services | Workforce Innovation Opportunities | Strengthen IDEA-WIOA coordination in reauthorization |

### OSEP Regulatory Engagement

- Submit comments on proposed discipline rules
- Advocate for strengthened disproportionality monitoring
- Push for AT guidance integration into IEP process requirements
- Support results-driven accountability implementation

### State-Level Action

**Target States for Special Education Funding Reform**

| State | Issue | Strategy |
|-------|-------|----------|
| Texas | Low state special education funding; historical enrollment cap effects | Advocacy for increased state funding; monitor enrollment recovery |
| California | High cost; teacher shortage | Support special education teacher recruitment incentives |
| Florida | Limited state funding supplement | Push for state IDEA match increase |
| New York | Strong laws but implementation gaps | Monitor implementation of existing requirements |
| Ohio | Moderate funding; disproportionality concerns | Coalition with civil rights organizations |

## Phase 2: Short-Term Goals (2026-2028)

### Federal Legislative Victories

**Primary Targets**

- Begin IDEA funding phase-in (Year 1: $2-3 billion increase)
- Enact Special Education Teacher Pipeline Act
- Advance IDEA reauthorization through committee
- Pass dispute resolution reform as standalone or IDEA amendment

**Regulatory Actions**

- OSEP finalizes strengthened discipline rules
- OSEP issues guidance on inclusive education best practices
- Department of Education increases OCR disability complaint processing capacity

### State-Level Expansion

**Workforce**

- 15 states establish special education teacher scholarship programs
- 10 states increase special education teacher pay differentials
- National caseload standards adopted by 20 states

**Inclusion**

- 10 states adopt presumption of inclusion in state special education regulations
- Fund inclusive education demonstration projects in 50 school districts
- Develop and disseminate inclusive education implementation toolkits

**Dispute Resolution**

- 20 states establish or expand parent advocacy programs
- IEP facilitation available in 30 states
- State complaint resolution timelines reduced in 15 states

## Phase 3: Medium-Term Goals (2028-2032)

### IDEA Reauthorization

**Target**: Comprehensive IDEA reauthorization enacted by 2030

**Key Elements**

- Mandatory spending category for IDEA Part B (removes from annual appropriations)
- Full funding phased in to 40% by FY 2035
- Strengthened LRE provisions with rebuttable presumption of inclusion
- Disproportionality reforms with enforcement teeth
- Transition services expansion (begin at age 14)
- AT requirements strengthened
- Dispute resolution reform (federally funded parent advocacy)
- Evidence-based practice requirements
- Updated disability categories
- Mental health integration requirements

### Outcome Targets

| Metric | Current | 2032 Target |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| Federal cost share | 15% | 25% (on path to 40%) |
| Students in general education 80%+ | 66% | 75% |
| IEP graduation rate | 72% | 80% |
| Post-secondary enrollment (within 2 years) | 33% | 45% |
| Competitive employment (within 2 years) | 43% | 55% |
| Special education teacher shortage | 39,000 vacancies | 15,000 vacancies |
| Disproportionality (risk ratio, Black/White) | 1.2 | 1.0-1.1 |

### Systemic Changes

- Results-driven accountability fully implemented in all states
- Parent advocacy programs available in all 50 states
- Inclusive education infrastructure established in majority of districts
- AT assessment and provision integrated into standard IEP process
- Transition services connected to VR and adult services in all states

## Phase 4: Long-Term Goals (2032-2040)

### Full IDEA Implementation

- Federal funding at 40% of excess cost (approximately $37-40 billion Part B)
- Special education teacher workforce adequate (less than 5,000 vacancies nationally)
- Inclusion rate: 80%+ of students with disabilities in general education 80%+ of day
- Graduation rate: 85%+ for students with IEPs
- Post-secondary enrollment: 55%+
- Competitive employment: 65%+
- Racial disproportionality eliminated in identification, placement, and discipline
- Every family has access to free independent educational advocacy

### Education System Transformation

- Universal Design for Learning implemented in all schools
- General education teachers prepared to teach diverse learners
- Special education and general education fully integrated (not parallel systems)
- AT seamlessly integrated into educational technology
- Post-school outcome tracking informs IEP quality

## Risk Assessment

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|------------|
| Funding increases diverted by recession | Medium | High | Mandatory spending category protects from annual cuts |
| IDEA reauthorization delayed beyond 2030 | High | Medium | Pursue priority reforms as standalone bills |
| State resistance to inclusion mandates | Medium | Medium | Pair mandates with technical assistance and funding |
| Political polarization blocks bipartisan action | Medium | High | Maintain bipartisan champion model; state-level action |
| Teacher shortage worsens despite investment | Medium | High | Multiple strategies: pay, working conditions, pipeline, retention |
