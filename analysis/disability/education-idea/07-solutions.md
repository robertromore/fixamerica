# Education and IDEA: Solutions

## Guiding Principles

### Criteria for Evaluating IDEA Reforms

| Criterion | Definition | Why It Matters |
|-----------|------------|----------------|
| **Adequacy** | Sufficient resources for every child to receive FAPE | Underfunding is the foundational problem |
| **Equity** | Elimination of racial and socioeconomic disparities | IDEA's civil rights purpose requires equal treatment |
| **Inclusion** | Maximum appropriate general education participation | LRE is both a legal requirement and best practice |
| **Outcomes** | Focus on educational results, not just procedural compliance | Children deserve genuine learning, not just paperwork |
| **Family empowerment** | Meaningful participation regardless of income or language | Procedural rights require practical access |
| **Workforce quality** | Well-prepared, well-supported, well-compensated educators | Teachers are the primary determinant of service quality |

## Policy Proposals

### Tier 1: IDEA Full Funding

**Problem**: The federal government covers approximately 15% of excess special education costs instead of the promised 40%, leaving states and districts to absorb a $23+ billion annual gap.

**Proposal: IDEA Full Funding Act**

- Phase in 40% federal cost share over 10 fiscal years
- Increase IDEA Part B appropriations by approximately $2.3 billion per year for 10 years
- Tie funding to mandatory spending category (remove from annual appropriations)
- Maintain current IDEA formula with adjustments for poverty concentration
- Include accountability provisions linking funding to outcome improvements

**Estimated Cost**: $2.3 billion per year additional, reaching $23 billion additional by Year 10 (total Part B: approximately $37.6 billion at full funding)

**Evidence Base**: States that spend more on special education produce better outcomes. A 2023 National Council on Disability analysis found that adequate funding is the single most important predictor of special education quality across states.

### Tier 2: Workforce Development

**Problem**: 44 states report critical special education teacher shortages; 16% annual attrition drains the workforce; caseloads exceed recommended levels.

**Proposal: Special Education Workforce Investment Act**

- Create federal scholarship program: full tuition for special education teacher candidates who commit to 5 years of service in high-need schools
- Establish loan forgiveness: $10,000/year for 5 years for special education teachers in high-need areas
- Fund 50 additional university-based special education teacher preparation programs
- Require all teacher preparation programs to include meaningful special education coursework (12+ credits)
- Establish national recommended caseload standards (8-15 students depending on disability category)
- Fund paraprofessional training and career ladder programs
- Increase Medicare/Medicaid school-based services reimbursement rates for speech, OT, and PT

**Estimated Cost**: $2-3 billion per year

### Tier 3: Inclusion and Equity Reform

#### Strengthen LRE Requirements

**Problem**: Despite decades of law and research, 34% of students with disabilities spend less than 80% of the day in general education, and placement decisions are often driven by administrative convenience rather than individual need.

**Proposal**

- Establish rebuttable presumption of general education placement in IDEA regulations
- Require districts to document specific evidence that general education with supplementary aids and services cannot provide FAPE before moving to more restrictive placement
- Fund inclusive education infrastructure: co-teaching training, universal design for learning (UDL) implementation, accessible curriculum materials
- Establish OSEP technical assistance center for inclusive education
- Set national inclusion target: 80% of students with disabilities in general education 80%+ of day by 2035

#### Address Racial Disproportionality

**Problem**: Black and Native American students are significantly overrepresented in special education identification and restrictive placements; Latino students are underidentified in some categories.

**Proposal**

- Require states identified with significant disproportionality to redirect 15% of IDEA funds to address root causes (current requirement; strengthen enforcement)
- Fund culturally responsive evaluation training for all assessment personnel
- Require evaluation teams to include professionals with cultural competence in the student's cultural and linguistic background
- Establish OSEP monitoring for disproportionate discipline of students with IEPs
- Mandate disaggregated outcome reporting by race, ethnicity, language, and income

### Tier 4: Dispute Resolution Reform

**Problem**: Due process costs families $10,000-$50,000+, creating a two-tier system where only affluent families can challenge inadequate services.

**Proposal**

- Establish federally funded parent advocacy program: free independent educational advocate for any family requesting one
- Expand IEP facilitation: trained neutral facilitators available for any IEP meeting
- Create state-level special education ombudsman offices
- Simplify state complaint process and reduce response timelines from 60 to 30 days
- Establish mandatory early dispute resolution: 15-day resolution session with trained mediator before due process
- Provide attorney fee shifting for prevailing parents at all levels (strengthen existing provision)
- Fund legal aid organizations to provide special education representation

**Estimated Cost**: $500 million per year for parent advocacy and legal assistance programs

### Tier 5: Transition and Post-School Outcomes

**Problem**: Students with IEPs graduate at 72% (vs. 87% for all students) and enroll in post-secondary education at 33% (vs. 66%). Post-school employment rates are inadequate.

**Proposal**

- Begin transition planning at age 14 (or upon entering 9th grade) in all states
- Require transition IEPs to include specific post-secondary education or employment goals with actionable steps
- Create IDEA-WIOA coordination requirements: vocational rehabilitation must participate in transition IEPs
- Establish paid work experience requirement: every student with an IEP participates in competitive integrated employment or work-based learning before graduation
- Create post-school outcome tracking system: follow students for 5 years after exit to measure employment, education, and independent living outcomes
- Fund competitive integrated employment programs in partnership with local businesses

### Tier 6: IDEA Modernization

**Problem**: IDEA has not been reauthorized since 2004. The law does not address current educational technologies, evidence-based practices, or the post-COVID landscape.

**Proposal: IDEA Reauthorization**

- Update disability categories to reflect current diagnostic understanding (e.g., autism spectrum, ADHD, specific learning disabilities)
- Integrate evidence-based practices requirements (e.g., structured literacy for dyslexia, applied behavior analysis for autism)
- Add explicit requirements for assistive technology consideration in every IEP
- Address virtual and hybrid learning for students with disabilities
- Update discipline provisions to strengthen protections and address disproportionality
- Add mental health services integration requirements
- Modernize data collection and reporting requirements

## Implementation Considerations

### Political Feasibility

| Reform | Feasibility | Path |
|--------|------------|------|
| IDEA full funding (phased) | Medium | Bipartisan co-sponsors exist; CBO cost is obstacle |
| Workforce development | High | Near-universal support; modest cost |
| Inclusion strengthening | Medium | Research support; implementation resistance |
| Dispute resolution reform | Medium-High | Broad agreement on need; funding question |
| Transition improvement | Medium-High | Employment framing attracts business support |
| IDEA reauthorization | Low-Medium | Comprehensive bills are harder to pass; timing uncertain |

### Cost-Benefit Summary

| Reform | Estimated Annual Cost | Estimated Annual Benefit | ROI |
|--------|----------------------|--------------------------|-----|
| IDEA full funding (Year 10) | $23 billion additional | $40-60 billion (improved employment, reduced adult services) | 1.7-2.6x |
| Workforce development | $2-3 billion | $5-10 billion (improved outcomes, reduced attrition costs) | 2-4x |
| Dispute resolution reform | $500 million | $2-3 billion (reduced litigation, improved compliance) | 4-6x |
| Transition improvement | $1 billion | $5-10 billion (increased employment, reduced SSI/SSDI dependency) | 5-10x |

*Estimates based on extrapolation from NLTS2 employment outcome data, SSI/SSDI cost data, and special education cost research.*
