# Psychiatric Disabilities: Resources

## Overview

This document compiles key resources for understanding psychiatric disability policy, law, treatment systems, and advocacy. Resources are organized by type and relevance to support researchers, policymakers, advocates, and practitioners working to improve outcomes for people with psychiatric disabilities.

## Federal Law and Regulation

### Statutes

- **Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990** (42 U.S.C. 12101 et seq.) - Prohibits discrimination against people with disabilities, including psychiatric disabilities, in employment (Title I), government services (Title II), and public accommodations (Title III).
- **ADA Amendments Act of 2008** (P.L. 110-325) - Broadened ADA coverage to restore protection for episodic conditions and eliminated consideration of mitigating measures (medication) in disability determination.
- **Rehabilitation Act of 1973, Section 504** (29 U.S.C. 794) - Prohibits disability discrimination in federally funded programs.
- **Fair Housing Act** (42 U.S.C. 3601 et seq.) - Prohibits housing discrimination based on disability, including psychiatric disability; requires reasonable accommodation.
- **Mental Health Parity and Addiction Equity Act of 2008** (P.L. 110-343, 29 U.S.C. 1185a) - Requires insurance coverage for mental health conditions to be comparable to coverage for physical conditions.
- **Affordable Care Act** (P.L. 111-148, 2010) - Established mental health as an essential health benefit; expanded Medicaid coverage.
- **Community Mental Health Act of 1963** (P.L. 88-164) - Authorized federal funding for community mental health centers (largely unfulfilled).
- **Omnibus Budget Reconciliation Act of 1981** - Converted federal CMHC funding into block grants, reducing total federal mental health funding by 25%.
- **Excellence in Mental Health and Addiction Treatment Act of 2014** (P.L. 113-93, Section 223) - Established Certified Community Behavioral Health Clinic (CCBHC) demonstration program.
- **National Suicide Hotline Designation Act of 2020** (P.L. 116-172) - Designated 988 as the national suicide prevention and mental health crisis number.

### Regulations

- **EEOC Enforcement Guidance on the ADA and Psychiatric Disabilities** (1997, updated 2008) - Guidance on ADA Title I application to psychiatric conditions in employment.
- **HUD Guidance on Reasonable Accommodations for Persons with Disabilities** (2004, updated 2020) - Fair housing guidance relevant to psychiatric disability accommodation.
- **MHPAEA Final Rule** (2024) - Updated parity regulations strengthening enforcement of non-quantitative treatment limitations.
- **Medicaid IMD Exclusion** (42 C.F.R. 435.1010) - Prohibits Medicaid payment for care in institutions for mental diseases with more than 16 beds.

## Key Court Decisions

- *Olmstead v. L.C.*, 527 U.S. 581 (1999) - Unjustified institutionalization of people with disabilities violates ADA Title II; states must provide community-based services when appropriate.
- *O'Connor v. Donaldson*, 422 U.S. 563 (1975) - Cannot confine a non-dangerous mentally ill person who is capable of surviving in the community.
- *Addington v. Texas*, 441 U.S. 418 (1979) - Civil commitment requires "clear and convincing evidence" standard.
- *Youngberg v. Romeo*, 457 U.S. 307 (1982) - Institutionalized persons have a right to minimally adequate training and conditions.
- *Wyatt v. Stickney*, 325 F. Supp. 781 (M.D. Ala. 1971) - Right to treatment in the least restrictive environment.
- *Buck v. Bell*, 274 U.S. 200 (1927) - Upheld forced sterilization of institutionalized persons (never formally overruled; widely condemned).
- *Sutton v. United Air Lines*, 527 U.S. 471 (1999) - Required consideration of mitigating measures in disability determination (overruled by ADAAA 2008).
- *Toyota Motor Manufacturing v. Williams*, 534 U.S. 184 (2002) - Narrowed "substantially limits" standard (overruled by ADAAA 2008).
- *Heller v. Doe*, 509 U.S. 312 (1993) - Upheld lower standard of proof for commitment of intellectually disabled persons.
- *Kansas v. Hendricks*, 521 U.S. 346 (1997) - Upheld civil commitment of sexually violent predators after criminal sentence completion.

## Government Reports and Data Sources

- SAMHSA. *National Survey on Drug Use and Health (NSDUH)*. Annual. Primary source for mental illness prevalence, treatment access, and demographic data.
- SAMHSA. *Behavioral Health Barometer*. Annual state-level data on mental health and substance use.
- SAMHSA. *National Mental Health Services Survey (N-MHSS)*. Facility-level data on mental health treatment.
- NIMH. *Mental Illness Statistics*. Annual compilation of prevalence and incidence data.
- BLS. *Current Population Survey: Persons with a Disability*. Annual. Employment data for people with disabilities.
- EEOC. *Charge Statistics*. Annual. ADA charge data by impairment type.
- HUD. *Annual Homeless Assessment Report to Congress*. Primary data on homelessness.
- HUD. *Fair Housing and Equal Opportunity Data*. Fair housing complaint data.
- SSA. *Annual Statistical Report on the Social Security Disability Insurance Program*. SSDI/SSI data.
- BJS. *Survey of Inmates in State and Federal Correctional Facilities*. Incarceration data.
- Treatment Advocacy Center. *Serious Mental Illness Prevalence in Jails and Prisons*. Criminal justice data.
- HRSA. *Designated Health Professional Shortage Areas Statistics*. Workforce shortage data.
- NRI (National Association of State Mental Health Program Directors Research Institute). *State Mental Health Agency Revenue and Expenditure Study*. State funding data.
- National Council on Disability. *From Institutions to Community Living: Olmstead Progress*. Annual Olmstead implementation assessment.

## International Frameworks and Comparisons

- **Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD)** - UN treaty affirming rights of people with disabilities, including psychiatric disabilities. Article 12 (legal capacity) and Article 14 (liberty and security) are particularly relevant. The U.S. has signed but not ratified.
- **UK Mental Health Act 1983 (amended 2007)** - Provides framework for involuntary treatment with significant rights protections.
- **Time to Change (UK)** - National anti-stigma campaign (2007-2021); model for evidence-based anti-stigma programming.
- **Beyond Blue (Australia)** - National mental health organization combining treatment access with anti-stigma work.
- **Opening Minds (Canada)** - Mental Health Commission of Canada's anti-stigma initiative.
- **Italian Psychiatric Reform (Law 180/1978)** - Abolished psychiatric hospitals; model for community-based care (with significant implementation challenges).
