# Communication Access: Roadmap

## Overview

This roadmap outlines a phased implementation strategy for improving communication access for deaf, hard of hearing, deafblind, and speech-disabled Americans. The three phases move from executive actions and regulatory reforms achievable without new legislation, through targeted legislative campaigns, to long-term structural transformation. Each phase builds on prior accomplishments, creating momentum and institutional infrastructure for subsequent reforms.

## Phase 1: Foundation Building (Years 1-3)

### Objective

Establish regulatory foundations for communication access reform, launch enforcement improvements, strengthen existing programs, and achieve targeted wins using existing federal authority.

### Actions

| Action | Lead Actor | Timeline | Dependencies | Success Metric |
|--------|-----------|----------|--------------|----------------|
| Issue HHS guidance on healthcare communication access standards | HHS OCR | Year 1 | None; builds on existing ADA authority | Guidance published; technical assistance provided to 5,000 facilities |
| CMS revises SGD coverage policy to allow tablet-based AAC | CMS | Year 1 | None; administrative action within existing authority | Policy revision effective; approval timelines reduced to 60 days |
| FCC accelerates NG911 deployment timeline | FCC | Year 1 | Existing NG911 authority | Revised deployment schedule; federal funding prioritized |
| FCC initiates TRS modernization rulemaking | FCC | Year 1-2 | None; existing authority under CVAA | NPRM published; stakeholder input gathered |
| Issue executive order on communication access in federal programs | President | Year 1 | None | Executive order signed; agency implementation plans within 180 days |
| Increase DOJ ADA communication access enforcement funding | Congress (appropriations) | Year 1-2 | Budget request from DOJ | $30 million increase in disability rights enforcement |
| FCC issues captioning quality and ASR standards NPRM | FCC | Year 2 | TRS modernization context | Proposed rule published |
| Fund interpreter training program expansion grants | HRSA/HHS | Year 2-3 | Appropriations | 25 new or expanded ITP programs funded |
| Establish PSAP text-to-911 mandate (100% coverage) | FCC | Year 2-3 | NG911 infrastructure | 100% of PSAPs accept text-to-911 |
| Launch healthcare communication access audit pilot | HHS OCR | Year 3 | Enforcement funding increase | 300 healthcare facilities audited in first year |

### Quick Wins

- CMS guidance letter to state Medicaid directors on AAC device coverage consistency (achievable within 90 days)
- FCC enforcement action on broadcast captioning quality violations (achievable within 6 months using existing authority)
- DOJ technical assistance update on ADA effective communication requirements including VRI limitations (achievable within 6 months)
- Federal agency communication access self-assessment requirement via executive order (achievable within 1 year)

### Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Probability | Mitigation Strategy |
|------|------------|-------------------|
| Healthcare industry challenges HHS guidance | Medium | Build extensive administrative record; rely on well-established ADA and Section 504 authority |
| NG911 deployment delays due to PSAP funding gaps | Medium-High | Prioritize federal infrastructure funding; identify state cost-sharing mechanisms |
| Administration change reverses executive order | Medium | Pursue parallel legislative action; embed requirements in CMS and FCC regulations |
| Interpreter training expansion limited by faculty shortages | High | Fund train-the-trainer programs; support deaf community mentorship models |

## Phase 2: Legislative Campaigns (Years 3-6)

### Objective

Enact targeted federal legislation establishing the National Communication Access Fund, expanding the interpreter workforce, codifying healthcare communication standards, and modernizing telecommunications relay services.

### Actions

| Action | Lead Actor | Timeline | Dependencies | Success Metric |
|--------|-----------|----------|--------------|----------------|
| National Communication Access Fund Act | Congress | Year 3-4 | Phase 1 regulatory foundation demonstrates feasibility | Legislation enacted; fund operational by Year 5 |
| Interpreter Workforce Development Act (scholarships, ITP expansion, specialization training) | Congress | Year 3-4 | Phase 1 training expansion pilots | Legislation enacted; 5,000 scholarships per year funded |
| Healthcare Communication Access Act (codify standards) | Congress | Year 4-5 | Phase 1 HHS guidance and audit results | Legislation enacted; binding standards within 2 years |
| TRS Modernization Act (digital communication relay) | Congress | Year 4-5 | Phase 1 FCC rulemaking | Legislation enacted; video conferencing relay pilot |
| AAC Access and Rights Act (device funding reform, accommodation standards) | Congress | Year 4-5 | Phase 1 CMS policy revision | Legislation enacted; 30-day AAC approval mandate |
| Captioning Quality and Coverage Expansion Act | Congress | Year 5-6 | Phase 1 FCC rulemaking and industry data | Legislation enacted; online video captioning phased in |
| NG911 Completion and Accessibility Act | Congress | Year 5-6 | Phase 1 deployment progress | Legislation enacted; full NG911 funded and mandated |

### Coalition Strategy

- **Healthcare communication standards**: Coalition with patient safety organizations (Joint Commission, Leapfrog Group), hospital quality groups, and malpractice insurers who recognize communication failure as a liability risk
- **National Communication Access Fund**: Coalition with aging organizations (AARP) who represent 48 million Americans with hearing loss; frame as benefiting all Americans who will experience hearing loss
- **Interpreter workforce**: Coalition with higher education associations, labor organizations, and rural health advocates who need interpreter services in their communities
- **TRS modernization**: Coalition with technology companies' accessibility teams, employers who need accessible workplace communication, and telehealth advocates
- **AAC access**: Coalition with ALS Association, United Cerebral Palsy, National Aphasia Association, and autism organizations whose constituencies include AAC users
- **Emergency communication**: Coalition with first responder organizations, emergency management agencies, and public safety advocates

### Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Probability | Mitigation Strategy |
|------|------------|-------------------|
| Legislative gridlock prevents major bills | High | Pursue provisions as riders on must-pass legislation; pursue state-level action simultaneously |
| Healthcare industry lobbying defeats communication access standards | Medium-High | Build patient safety coalition as counterweight; use Phase 1 audit data to demonstrate need |
| TRS Fund expansion faces budget opposition | Medium | Present as revenue-neutral through updated contribution mechanism; demonstrate economic returns |
| Technology industry resists binding platform standards | Medium | Leverage government procurement requirements; highlight competitive advantage of accessible products |

## Phase 3: Structural Transformation (Years 6-10)

### Objective

Achieve systemic changes that make communication access the default in American public life rather than an accommodation to be requested, building on legislative and regulatory foundations from Phases 1 and 2.

### Actions

| Action | Lead Actor | Timeline | Dependencies | Success Metric |
|--------|-----------|----------|--------------|----------------|
| Universal communication access standard for all new government facilities | GSA/Access Board | Year 6-7 | Healthcare standards codified; fund operational | Standard adopted; new construction 100% communication-accessible |
| Communication platform accessibility certification program | FCC | Year 6-8 | Technology platform standards enacted | All major platforms certified; market incentives for accessibility |
| Comprehensive interpreter credential reciprocity (interstate compact) | States/NCSL | Year 7-9 | Interpreter workforce expansion from Phase 2 | 40+ states participate; interpreter portability achieved |
| Communication access quality measures in healthcare value-based payment | CMS | Year 7-9 | Healthcare communication standards; data infrastructure | Communication access measures in Medicare/Medicaid payment models |
| AI communication technology accessibility standards | FCC/NIST | Year 8-10 | Technology platform standards; AI governance framework | AI captioning, translation, and communication tools meet accessibility benchmarks |
| Disability communication research initiative | NIDCD/NIH | Year 8-10 | Data infrastructure; workforce capacity | Research funding doubled; communication access outcomes studied |

### Long-Term Targets (Year 10)

| Metric | Current Baseline | Year 5 Target | Year 10 Target |
|--------|-----------------|---------------|----------------|
| Interpreter requests filled (national average) | 50-70% | 75% | 90%+ |
| Deaf patients denied interpreter in healthcare | 63% report denial | 35% | 10% |
| PSAPs with full NG911 (text, video, RTT) | 70% (text only) | 90% (text); 50% (video) | 100% (all modalities) |
| Broadcast captioning accuracy | 95-98% | 98% | 99%+ |
| AAC device approval timeline | 3-6 months | 60 days | 30 days |
| VRS calls with video conferencing integration | 0% | 10% (pilot) | 50%+ |
| States with interpreter licensure | 31 | 40 | 50 (all states plus DC) |
| Healthcare facilities with communication access plans | <20% | 50% | 90%+ |

## Success Metrics Summary

### Short-Term (Years 1-3)

- HHS healthcare communication access guidance published and technical assistance provided
- CMS AAC device policy reformed; tablet-based AAC permitted under Medicare
- 100% of PSAPs accept text-to-911
- 25 new or expanded interpreter training programs funded
- 300+ healthcare facilities audited for communication access compliance
- Executive order on federal program communication access signed and implemented
- FCC captioning quality and TRS modernization rulemakings initiated

### Medium-Term (Years 3-6)

- National Communication Access Fund enacted and operational
- 5,000 interpreter training scholarships per year
- Binding healthcare communication access standards enacted
- TRS modernization legislation enacted; video conferencing relay pilot launched
- AAC Access and Rights Act enacted; 30-day approval mandate
- Online video captioning requirements phased in
- NG911 completion legislation enacted and funded

### Long-Term (Years 6-10)

- 90%+ interpreter request fulfillment rate nationally
- Communication access denial in healthcare reduced by 85%
- Full NG911 deployment with all communication modalities
- Interstate interpreter credential reciprocity achieved
- Communication access integrated into healthcare quality payment models
- AI communication technology meets accessibility standards
