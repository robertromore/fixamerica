# Healthcare Access for People with Disabilities: Roadmap

## Overview

This roadmap outlines a phased implementation strategy for improving healthcare access for people with disabilities. The three phases move from executive actions and regulatory reforms achievable without new legislation, through targeted legislative campaigns, to long-term structural transformation. Each phase builds on the prior one, creating momentum and institutional infrastructure for subsequent reforms.

## Phase 1: Foundation Building (Years 1-3)

### Objective

Establish regulatory foundations, launch enforcement improvements, and achieve targeted legislative wins on issues with existing bipartisan support.

### Actions

| Action | Lead Actor | Timeline | Dependencies | Success Metric |
|--------|-----------|----------|--------------|----------------|
| Adopt MDE standards as mandatory via Section 1557 rulemaking | HHS OCR | Year 1 | None; builds on 2024 Section 1557 final rule | Final rule published in Federal Register |
| Issue executive order on disability health equity | President | Year 1 | None | Executive order signed; agency plans submitted within 180 days |
| Increase HHS OCR enforcement funding | Congress (appropriations) | Year 1-2 | Budget request from HHS | $50 million increase in OCR disability enforcement |
| Finalize disability data collection standards | HHS Office of the Assistant Secretary for Planning and Evaluation | Year 1-2 | Coordination with OMB on survey instruments | Standards published; implementation guidance issued |
| Launch voluntary compliance program with tax credits | IRS/HHS | Year 2 | Legislation expanding Section 44 tax credit | 5,000 practices enrolled in first year |
| Prior authorization reform for DME | CMS (Medicaid/Medicare rulemaking) | Year 2-3 | Proposed rule, public comment | Average DME approval time reduced by 50% |
| Fund 100 additional PM&R residency positions | HRSA/Congress | Year 2-3 | GME appropriations | Positions funded and matched |
| Establish disability healthcare audit program | HHS OCR | Year 3 | Enforcement funding increase | 500 facilities audited in first year |

### Quick Wins

- CMS guidance letter to state Medicaid directors on managed care network adequacy for disability specialists (achievable within 90 days)
- HHS Office for Civil Rights technical assistance for healthcare facilities on accessible medical equipment (achievable within 6 months)
- CDC expansion of BRFSS disability health indicators (achievable within 1 year through existing survey infrastructure)

### Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Probability | Mitigation Strategy |
|------|------------|-------------------|
| Industry litigation against mandatory MDE standards | High | Build extensive administrative record; rely on existing ADA/Section 504 authority |
| Appropriations shortfall for enforcement | Medium | Prioritize budget-neutral regulatory actions; seek bipartisan support through aging/veteran coalitions |
| State resistance to data collection requirements | Medium | Provide federal technical assistance and funding; make reporting a condition of Medicaid participation |
| Administration change reverses executive order | Medium | Pursue parallel legislative action; embed requirements in CMS regulations harder to reverse |

## Phase 2: Legislative Campaigns (Years 3-6)

### Objective

Enact targeted federal legislation addressing the most significant healthcare access barriers, building on regulatory foundations from Phase 1 and evidence generated by data collection improvements.

### Actions

| Action | Lead Actor | Timeline | Dependencies | Success Metric |
|--------|-----------|----------|--------------|----------------|
| Disability Healthcare Accessibility Act (mandatory MDE standards codified in statute) | Congress | Year 3-4 | Phase 1 rulemaking demonstrates feasibility | Bill enacted |
| Medicare dental, vision, and hearing benefit expansion | Congress | Year 3-4 | Builds on bipartisan support | Benefit available to Medicare beneficiaries |
| Medical Education Disability Competency Act (LCME/ACGME accreditation requirements) | Congress | Year 4-5 | AAMC curriculum objectives provide framework | Accreditation standards adopted |
| Comprehensive prior authorization reform (federal legislation) | Congress | Year 4-5 | Phase 1 CMS rulemaking demonstrates impact | Legislation enacted; prior authorization timelines mandated |
| Mental Health Parity Enforcement Act (dedicated enforcement funding and staffing) | Congress | Year 5-6 | GAO reports on enforcement gaps | Enforcement staff tripled; compliance examinations quadrupled |
| Disability Health Workforce Act (residency expansion, loan forgiveness, CHW programs) | Congress | Year 5-6 | Phase 1 residency pilots demonstrate need | 300+ new residency positions; loan forgiveness program operational |
| Medicaid HCBS expansion (permanent enhanced FMAP for community health services) | Congress | Year 5-6 | Phase 1 data showing HCBS cost-effectiveness | Enhanced FMAP enacted; waiting lists reduced by 50% |

### Coalition Strategy

- **Accessible equipment**: Coalition with aging organizations (AARP), obesity health groups, and maternal health advocates who benefit from accessible medical equipment
- **Medicare dental/vision/hearing**: Broad bipartisan appeal; coalition with dental and optometric professional associations
- **Medical education reform**: Partner with medical students (who increasingly support disability curriculum) and disability studies academic programs
- **Prior authorization reform**: Coalition with AMA, specialty medical societies, and patient advocacy organizations across disease categories
- **Mental health**: Coalition with NAMI, APA, veteran organizations, and law enforcement agencies seeking alternatives to mental health crisis response

### Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Probability | Mitigation Strategy |
|------|------------|-------------------|
| Legislative gridlock prevents major bills | High | Pursue provisions as riders on must-pass legislation (appropriations, NDAA); pursue state-level action as alternative |
| Insurance industry lobbying defeats prior authorization reform | Medium-High | Build AMA/provider coalition as counterweight; use state reform successes as evidence |
| Medicare expansion costs trigger opposition | Medium | Present CBO scoring showing long-term savings; propose phased implementation |
| Medical education lobby resists accreditation mandates | Medium | Frame as competency enhancement, not burden; highlight consumer demand for disability-competent providers |

## Phase 3: Structural Transformation (Years 6-10)

### Objective

Achieve systemic changes that make healthcare accessibility the default rather than the exception, building on legislative and regulatory foundations from Phases 1 and 2.

### Actions

| Action | Lead Actor | Timeline | Dependencies | Success Metric |
|--------|-----------|----------|--------------|----------------|
| Universal accessibility standard for all new healthcare construction | HHS/HUD rulemaking | Year 6-7 | MDE standards codified in statute | Standard adopted; new construction 100% accessible |
| Disability health equity performance measures in value-based payment | CMS | Year 6-8 | Disability data infrastructure from Phase 1; quality measures developed | Disability-disaggregated quality measures in Medicare/Medicaid payment |
| Community health worker network for disability healthcare navigation | HRSA/states | Year 7-9 | CHW training programs from Phase 2 | CHW programs in all states; measurable access improvements |
| Accessible telehealth certification program | FCC/HHS | Year 7-8 | Telehealth accessibility standards from Phase 1 rulemaking | All federally funded telehealth platforms certified |
| Comprehensive disability health research agenda | NIH/NIDILRR | Year 8-10 | Data infrastructure; workforce capacity | Research funding doubled; disability health included in NIH strategic plan |
| Disability-inclusive healthcare quality accreditation | Joint Commission/NCQA | Year 8-10 | Industry buy-in through voluntary compliance programs | Accreditation standards adopted; compliance tied to Medicare participation |

### Long-Term Targets (Year 10)

| Metric | Current Baseline | Year 5 Target | Year 10 Target |
|--------|-----------------|---------------|----------------|
| Primary care offices with accessible exam tables | 8.4% | 35% | 75% |
| Medical schools with required disability curriculum | <20% | 50% | 100% |
| Unmet healthcare needs (disability vs. general gap) | 2.5x | 1.8x | 1.2x |
| Mammography screening rate for women with mobility disabilities | 53% (vs. 76% general) | 65% | 73% |
| Average DME approval time | 45-90 days | 15-30 days | 7-14 days |
| Mental health treatment gap (disability) | 15.7% cannot access | 10% | 5% |
| Disability-disaggregated quality measures in CMS programs | 0 | 5 | 15 |
| Disability health competency exam pass rate (new physicians) | N/A | Baseline established | 90%+ |

## Success Metrics Summary

### Short-Term (Years 1-3)

- Mandatory MDE standards adopted through rulemaking
- 10% increase in accessible exam table installations documented
- Disability data collection standardized across HHS programs
- 500+ healthcare facility audits completed
- DME prior authorization processing time reduced by 50%

### Medium-Term (Years 3-6)

- Federal legislation enacted for MDE standards, prior authorization reform, and Medicare dental benefit
- LCME adopts disability competency accreditation standard
- 300+ new residency positions in disability specialties
- Preventive screening disparity reduced by 30%
- Medicaid HCBS waiting lists reduced by 50%

### Long-Term (Years 6-10)

- Healthcare access gap between disabled and non-disabled populations reduced by 50%
- Universal accessibility standard for new healthcare construction
- Disability health equity integrated into value-based payment models
- Critical mass of disability-competent providers in workforce
