# Institutionalization: Roadmap

## Overview

This roadmap outlines a phased implementation strategy for ending unnecessary institutionalization in the United States. It recognizes that systemic change requires simultaneous action on multiple fronts -- legal, legislative, administrative, and cultural -- and that the pace of reform must be fast enough to end ongoing civil rights violations while careful enough to build genuine community capacity.

## Phase 1: Immediate Actions (Years 1-2)

### Executive Branch Actions

| Action | Agency | Authority | Timeline |
|--------|--------|-----------|----------|
| Issue executive order on Olmstead compliance | White House | Executive authority | Month 1 |
| Establish DOJ Olmstead Enforcement Task Force | DOJ | Existing authority | Month 3 |
| Issue CMS guidance requiring community-first assessments for all Medicaid LTSS | CMS | Medicaid authority | Month 6 |
| Direct HUD to prioritize disability housing | HUD | Existing authority | Month 6 |
| Launch DSP recruitment campaign | ACL/CMS | Existing authority | Month 6 |
| Increase Olmstead enforcement investigations | DOJ | Existing authority | Ongoing |

### Legislative Quick Wins

| Bill | Mechanism | Likelihood | Impact |
|------|-----------|------------|--------|
| HCBS Access Act | Enhanced FMAP for HCBS; waiting list reduction grants | Moderate | High |
| Money Follows the Person reauthorization | Permanent authorization with increased funding | High (bipartisan) | Moderate |
| DSP wage floor pilot | Medicaid waiver authority for DSP minimum wage pilots | Moderate | Moderate |
| Olmstead Enforcement Act | Dedicated DOJ enforcement funding | Low-Moderate | High |

### Administrative Rule Changes

- CMS rulemaking: Require states to submit waiting list reduction plans as condition of Medicaid participation
- CMS rulemaking: Strengthen HCBS settings rule enforcement (2014 rule compliance deadline was March 2023; many states remain out of compliance)
- HUD rulemaking: Visitability standards for all federally funded new construction
- DOL rulemaking: Include DSPs in overtime and minimum wage protections where exemptions apply

### Coalition Building

- Convene national Olmstead implementation summit with disability organizations, HCBS providers, state officials, and family advocates
- Launch public education campaign on community integration success stories
- Build bipartisan congressional caucus on community living
- Engage state legislatures through National Conference of State Legislatures partnerships

## Phase 2: Structural Reform (Years 2-5)

### Major Legislation

**HCBS Entitlement Act**

- Make HCBS a mandatory Medicaid benefit with a phased implementation timeline
- Establish federal service floor: personal care, supported employment, residential support, respite, crisis services, transportation
- Provide 90% FMAP for new HCBS spending for the first 5 years
- Require states to eliminate waiting lists within 5 years
- Estimated cost: $150-250 billion over 10 years (offset partially by reduced institutional spending)

**Community Integration Workforce Act**

- Establish federal DSP minimum wage at 150% of state/federal minimum wage
- Create DSP career ladder with federal certification standards
- Fund training programs through ACL grant program ($500 million/year)
- Require Medicaid rate pass-through of 80% of rate increases to worker wages
- Create DSP visa category for international workers

**Accessible Housing Expansion Act**

- Triple Section 811 funding ($700 million/year)
- Create HCBS Housing Trust Fund ($2 billion/year)
- Federal visitability mandate for all new federally funded construction
- Preempt restrictive local zoning for disability housing
- Expand Housing Choice Vouchers with disability-specific allocation

### Olmstead Enforcement

| Target | Action | Timeline |
|--------|--------|----------|
| 10 new state investigations per year | DOJ enforcement | Year 2-5 |
| Private right of action with damages | Legislative | Year 3 |
| Olmstead compliance as Medicaid condition | CMS rulemaking | Year 3 |
| Independent Olmstead Commission | Legislative | Year 4 |

### State Implementation Support

- Federal technical assistance teams deployed to states
- Peer learning networks connecting high-performing and developing states
- Innovation grants for community infrastructure development
- Data systems: National HCBS quality reporting system launched

## Phase 3: Community Infrastructure Build-Out (Years 5-8)

### Milestones

| Milestone | Target | Metric |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| HCBS waiting lists eliminated | Year 7 | Zero waiting list in all states |
| DSP vacancy rate below 5% | Year 7 | National Core Indicators data |
| Accessible housing units created | 500,000 new units | HUD data |
| Nursing home population (under 65) reduced by 50% | Year 8 | CMS data |
| ICF/IID population reduced by 75% | Year 8 | RISP data |
| Olmstead compliance in all states | Year 8 | DOJ/Commission assessment |

### Institutional Transition Plans

- States develop facility-by-facility transition timelines
- Worker transition programs operational (retraining, redeployment)
- Community crisis services fully operational in all states
- Quality assurance systems mature and data-driven

### Guardianship Reform

- Federal supported decision-making legislation enacted
- All states implement periodic guardianship review
- Supported decision-making programs operational in all states
- Guardianship rates declining measurably

## Phase 4: Full Community Integration (Years 8-12)

### End-State Vision

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Institutional population (unnecessary) | Near zero |
| HCBS availability | Universal entitlement, no waiting lists |
| DSP workforce | Stable, adequately compensated, career-track |
| Accessible housing | Supply meets demand |
| Quality metrics | HCBS outcomes exceed institutional baselines |
| Olmstead compliance | All states in compliance |

### Remaining Institutional Capacity

A small number of institutional beds will remain necessary for:

- Short-term psychiatric stabilization (community-based alternatives preferred)
- Forensic populations (individuals court-ordered to secure settings)
- Individuals who genuinely, freely, and with full knowledge of alternatives choose institutional settings

All other institutional populations should be served in community settings.

### Ongoing Maintenance

- Annual Olmstead compliance monitoring
- Continuous HCBS quality improvement
- Workforce investment adjusted for labor market conditions
- Housing pipeline sustained
- Guardianship system monitored for institutional bias

## Risk Factors and Contingencies

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|------------|------------|
| Congressional failure to pass HCBS entitlement | High | Pursue administrative reforms; state-by-state advocacy; litigation |
| State resistance to implementation | High | Enhanced FMAP incentives; DOJ enforcement; conditions on Medicaid participation |
| Workforce crisis worsens | Moderate | Emergency wage supplements; international recruitment; technology assistance |
| Housing market constraints | High | Alternative housing models (shared housing, host family); rapid rehousing |
| Quality failures in community settings | Moderate | Robust quality assurance; ombudsman programs; incident reporting |
| Political backlash after transition incident | Moderate | Proactive communication; data on comparative safety; rapid response protocols |
| Nursing home industry lobbying blocks reform | High | Bipartisan fiscal arguments; COVID-19 narrative; family advocacy |
