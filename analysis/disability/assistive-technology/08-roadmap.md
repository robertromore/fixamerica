# Assistive Technology: Roadmap

## Phase 1: Immediate Actions (2025-2026)

### Build the Coalition

**Core Coalition Partners**

- Disability rights organizations (DREDF, NDRN, RESNA, ATIA)
- AARP (aging population AT needs)
- Consumer rights groups (PIRG, iFixit)
- Veterans' organizations (Paralyzed Veterans of America, DAV)
- Bipartisan congressional champions

**Key Activities**

- Establish AT Access Coalition with unified legislative priorities
- Develop shared messaging platform linking AT to independence, employment, and cost savings
- Commission economic analysis of AT access reforms (ROI data)
- Build relationships with key committee staff on both sides of the aisle

### Support Active Legislative Campaigns

**119th Congress (2025-2026) Priorities**

| Priority | Bill | Action Needed |
|----------|------|---------------|
| AT Act Reauthorization | AT Act Reauthorization Bill | Advocate for $100 million authorization; bipartisan cosponsors |
| Wheelchair right-to-repair | Wheelchair Right-to-Repair Act | Build on state momentum; committee hearings |
| Medicare hearing aids | Medicare Hearing Aid Coverage Act | CBO score reduction strategies; bipartisan support |
| OTC AT expansion | New legislation needed | Develop draft legislation; FDA engagement |

### Advance State-Level Reforms

**Target States for Right-to-Repair (2025-2026)**

## Phase 2: Short-Term Goals (2026-2028)

### Federal Legislative Victories

**Primary Targets**

- Enact AT Act reauthorization at $75-100 million (minimum: maintain current funding with inflation adjustment)
- Pass federal wheelchair right-to-repair legislation
- Advance Medicare hearing aid coverage through committee (full passage may require Phase 3)
- Introduce AT price transparency legislation

**Regulatory Actions**

- Push CMS to expand Medicare AAC coverage criteria
- Advocate for FDA to create OTC categories for additional AT devices
- Support NIST development of AT interoperability standards

### Expand State Activity

**Right-to-Repair**

- Target 10 additional states for AT right-to-repair legislation
- Use Colorado, California, and Minnesota experience to build case studies
- Develop model state AT right-to-repair bill for NCSL dissemination

**Insurance Mandates**

- Advance hearing aid coverage mandates in remaining 25 states
- Introduce prosthetic parity legislation in states without it
- Push for Medicaid AT floor in states with minimal coverage

### Build AT Workforce Pipeline

- Secure federal funding for AT scholarship program
- Establish 5 new AT graduate training programs
- Launch telehealth AT assessment pilot programs in 10 states
- Develop AT professional certification pathways for emerging technology

## Phase 3: Medium-Term Goals (2028-2032)

### Medicare AT Coverage Expansion

- Enact Medicare hearing aid coverage
- Expand Medicare DME definition to include functional AT needs
- Add home modification benefit to Medicare
- Reform competitive bidding program to prioritize quality and access

### Market Transformation

- Achieve federal AT right-to-repair law
- Establish AT price transparency requirements
- FDA OTC categories operational for 5+ AT device types
- AT interoperability standards adopted by major manufacturers

### Workforce and Infrastructure

- AT workforce shortage reduced by 30%
- Telehealth AT assessment available in all 50 states
- National AT device exchange and reuse system operational
- 20 new AT graduate training programs established

### Research Acceleration

- NIDILRR AT research budget increased to $100 million
- NSF AT innovation program operational
- AT commercialization pipeline established
- Accessibility requirements integrated into major federal research programs

---

## Phase 4: Long-Term Goals (2032-2040)

### Comprehensive AT Access System

- Universal AT coverage through Medicare, Medicaid, and private insurance
- Federal Medicaid AT floor fully implemented
- AT assessment, training, maintenance, and replacement covered as standard benefits
- AT abandonment rate reduced from 30-50% to under 15%

### Competitive AT Market

- AT pricing reflects competitive market dynamics (markups under 100% for most categories)
- Full right-to-repair for all AT devices
- OTC AT options available for most non-complex device categories
- Interoperability standards universally adopted

### Technology Integration

- Universal design standard in all federally funded technology development
- AT seamlessly integrated with smart home, IoT, and communication platforms
- AI-powered AT widely available and affordable
- Emerging technologies (autonomous vehicles, smart cities) accessible by design
