# Assistive Technology: Actions

## For Individuals

### Learn First

**Understand the Problem**

- [ ] Read [Current State](02-current-state.md) to understand AT access barriers
- [ ] Read [Root Causes](04-root-causes.md) to understand why the system fails
- [ ] Learn about AT policy through NCD and RESNA publications (see [Resources](09-resources.md))
- [ ] Understand your own insurance coverage for AT (Medicare, Medicaid, or private)

**Know Your Rights**

- [ ] Research AT rights under the ADA, Section 504, and your state disability law
- [ ] Contact your state AT program for free device demonstrations and loan services
- [ ] Learn about the appeals process for insurance AT denials
- [ ] Connect with your state Protection and Advocacy agency for AT legal assistance

### Take Action

**Contact Representatives**

- [ ] Email your state legislators about AT right-to-repair legislation
- [ ] Call your members of Congress to support AT Act reauthorization
- [ ] Write to CMS during public comment periods on DME coverage decisions
- [ ] Attend town halls and raise AT access as a constituent concern

**Join the Movement**

- [ ] Sign up with RESNA, ATIA, or a disability rights organization focused on AT
- [ ] Join your state AT program's advisory council or consumer board
- [ ] Follow AT policy news through NDRN and NCD
- [ ] Connect with local disability advocacy groups

**Share Your Story**

- [ ] Document your AT access experiences (coverage denials, device failures, repair delays)
- [ ] Share your story with advocacy organizations for policy campaigns
- [ ] Testify at state legislative hearings on AT issues
- [ ] Write letters to the editor about AT access barriers
- [ ] Use social media to raise awareness about AT policy gaps

### Vote and Support

**Direct Support**

- [ ] Donate to disability rights organizations working on AT policy
- [ ] Volunteer with organizations advocating for AT reform
- [ ] Support candidates who endorse AT access and disability rights
- [ ] Vote YES on state ballot measures related to right-to-repair or disability services

**Ongoing Engagement**

- [ ] Vote in every election, including state and local
- [ ] Track AT-related legislation in your state and in Congress
- [ ] Stay informed about AT policy developments
- [ ] Participate in public comment periods for AT-related regulations

## For Communities

### Local AT Access Campaigns

**Assess the Landscape**

1. Identify AT access barriers in your community (transportation to AT services, availability of repair services, insurance coverage gaps)
2. Contact your state AT program to understand available services
3. Map local AT service providers, repair shops, and advocacy organizations
4. Survey community members with disabilities about unmet AT needs

**Build a Coalition**

1. Recruit disability organizations, aging services, veterans' groups, and consumer rights organizations
2. Engage local healthcare providers who prescribe AT
3. Connect with local businesses that employ people with disabilities
4. Partner with academic institutions that have AT or rehabilitation programs
5. Include people who use AT as leaders and spokespeople

**Community AT Programs**

- Organize community AT device exchange and reuse events
- Establish local AT repair workshops (partner with makerspaces and community colleges)
- Create peer support programs connecting experienced AT users with new users
- Advocate for accessible community technology (libraries, recreation centers, public spaces)

## For Organizations

### Employers

**Improve AT Access in Your Workplace**

- [ ] Contact the Job Accommodation Network (JAN) for free AT consultation (1-800-526-7234)
- [ ] Develop AT accommodation policy with input from employees with disabilities
- [ ] Budget for AT accommodations (median cost: $500 per accommodation)
- [ ] Train HR and IT staff on common AT solutions
- [ ] Include AT needs in job interview accessibility procedures

**Advocate for AT Policy**

- [ ] Join employer coalitions supporting AT access reform
- [ ] Share accommodation success stories to counter employer fear of AT costs
- [ ] Support AT coverage in employee health insurance plans
- [ ] Advocate for tax incentives for workplace AT investment

### Healthcare Providers

**Improve AT Service Delivery**

- [ ] Develop AT screening protocols for patients with disabilities
- [ ] Build referral relationships with certified AT professionals (ATPs, rehabilitation engineers)
- [ ] Advocate within your organization for AT-related training for clinical staff
- [ ] Learn the AT coverage and appeals processes for major insurers

**Advocate for Coverage Reform**

- [ ] Support professional association positions on AT coverage expansion
- [ ] Document coverage denials and their clinical impact for advocacy use
- [ ] Participate in CMS rulemaking on DME coverage decisions
- [ ] Train residents and students on AT assessment and prescription

### Technology Companies

**Build Accessible Products**

- [ ] Adopt universal design principles in product development
- [ ] Include people with disabilities in user testing and design research
- [ ] Publish accessibility conformance reports (VPATs) for all products
- [ ] Support open AT interoperability standards
- [ ] Ensure products work with common AT (screen readers, switch access, eye tracking)

**Support the AT Ecosystem**

- [ ] Partner with AT manufacturers on interoperability
- [ ] Sponsor AT innovation grants and hackathons
- [ ] Share accessibility expertise with smaller companies
- [ ] Advocate for accessibility requirements in technology standards

## For Advocates and Policymakers

### Federal Level

**Legislative Priorities (119th Congress)**

1. AT Act reauthorization with increased funding ($100 million target)
2. Federal wheelchair right-to-repair legislation
3. Medicare hearing aid coverage
4. AT price transparency requirements
5. AT workforce development funding

**Regulatory Priorities**

1. CMS: Expand Medicare AAC and DME coverage criteria
2. FDA: Create OTC categories for additional AT devices
3. FCC: Strengthen communications accessibility enforcement
4. NIST: Develop AT interoperability standards

### State Level

**Model Legislation Priorities**

1. AT right-to-repair laws (using Colorado model)
2. Hearing aid insurance coverage mandates (adults and children)
3. Prosthetic parity laws
4. Medicaid AT coverage expansion
5. State AT program supplemental funding

**Administrative Actions**

1. Medicaid waiver amendments to expand AT coverage
2. State procurement policies requiring accessible technology
3. State employee health plan AT coverage improvements
4. State licensing requirements for AT professionals

### Building Effective AT Advocacy

**Messaging Principles**

| Do | Do Not |
|----|--------|
| Frame AT as investment with measurable ROI | Frame AT as charity or welfare |
| Lead with independence and employment outcomes | Lead with cost figures |
| Use personal stories alongside data | Rely solely on statistics |
| Emphasize bipartisan appeal (veterans, aging, fiscal responsibility) | Frame as partisan issue |
| Connect AT to mainstream technology rights (right-to-repair) | Present AT as niche concern |

**Coalition Building**

- Disability organizations provide moral authority and personal stories
- AARP provides political influence for aging-related AT (hearing aids, vision aids)
- Consumer rights groups (PIRG, iFixit) bring right-to-repair infrastructure
- Veterans' organizations provide bipartisan credibility
- Employers provide economic impact data
- Healthcare providers provide clinical evidence
