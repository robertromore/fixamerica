# Developmental Disabilities: Actions

## Overview

This guide provides concrete actions that individuals, families, organizations, and communities can take to advance developmental disability reform. Actions range from immediate personal steps to sustained advocacy campaigns.

---

## Individual Actions

### Learn and Understand

1. **Educate yourself about developmental disabilities**: Read first-person accounts by people with IDD, not just clinical or professional perspectives
2. **Understand the self-advocacy movement**: Learn about People First, SABE, and ASAN to understand what people with IDD are advocating for in their own words
3. **Learn about your state's DD system**: Contact your state DD agency to understand available services, waiting lists, and eligibility
4. **Understand Medicaid HCBS**: Learn how your state's Medicaid waiver system works for people with developmental disabilities
5. **Follow disability policy developments**: Subscribe to newsletters from The Arc, NDRN, ASAN, and your state DD Council

### Support and Include

1. **Employ people with disabilities**: If you are in a hiring position, consider supported employment partnerships
2. **Patronize inclusive businesses**: Support businesses that employ people with IDD
3. **Include people with disabilities in community life**: Invite participation in recreational, civic, religious, and social activities
4. **Be an ally against ableism**: Speak up when you witness discrimination, exclusion, or demeaning language
5. **Volunteer**: Contribute time to disability organizations, Special Olympics, Best Buddies, or similar programs

### Vote and Advocate

1. **Contact your federal legislators**: Call or write to support the HCBS Access Act, the Transformation to Competitive Integrated Employment Act, and increased DD Act funding
2. **Contact your state legislators**: Advocate for DSP wage increases, waiting list reduction, supported decision-making legislation, and state subminimum wage bans
3. **Attend public hearings**: Testify at state legislative and Medicaid hearings about DD service needs
4. **Vote based on disability policy positions**: Research candidates' positions on Medicaid, disability services, and civil rights

## Family Actions

### Navigate the System

1. **Apply for services early**: If your family member may need adult DD services, apply for Medicaid HCBS waivers as soon as eligible (waiting lists can be years long)
2. **Begin transition planning early**: Start planning for the school-to-adult transition by age 14, even if IDEA does not require it until 16
3. **Explore self-directed services**: Ask your state DD agency about budget authority and employer authority options for self-directed services
4. **Investigate supported decision-making**: Before seeking guardianship, explore supported decision-making agreements, powers of attorney, and other less restrictive alternatives
5. **Open an ABLE account**: Explore ABLE accounts (26 U.S.C. Section 529A) for tax-advantaged savings that do not affect SSI/Medicaid eligibility
6. **Plan for the future**: Develop a Letter of Intent and consider special needs trusts, ABLE accounts, and future care planning

### Advocate

1. **Join The Arc or your state's family advocacy organization**: Participate in advocacy days, action alerts, and policy committees
2. **Share your family's story**: Legislators respond to personal stories; testify about your experiences with waiting lists, service gaps, and system failures
3. **Connect with other families**: Join support groups, parent networks, and online communities
4. **Advocate for your family member's rights**: File complaints with Protection and Advocacy (P&A) agencies when rights are violated
5. **Support self-advocacy**: Help your family member develop self-advocacy skills and participate in self-advocacy organizations

### Support the Workforce

1. **Treat DSPs as professionals**: Recognize the skill and dedication required for direct support work
2. **Advocate for DSP wages**: Support state and federal efforts to increase DSP compensation
3. **Provide backup**: Offer respite and backup support to DSPs to prevent burnout
4. **Participate in quality monitoring**: Engage with NCI surveys and quality review processes

## Community Actions

### Promote Inclusion

1. **Welcome group homes**: Support community residential options in your neighborhood; oppose NIMBYism
2. **Inclusive recreation**: Work with local parks, recreation departments, and community organizations to include people with IDD
3. **Faith community inclusion**: Advocate for inclusive religious education, worship, and fellowship programs
4. **Accessible community events**: Ensure community events are accessible and welcoming
5. **First responder training**: Support training for police, fire, and EMS on interacting with people with developmental disabilities

### Employment

1. **Business partnerships**: Connect with supported employment providers to create job opportunities
2. **Customized employment**: Work with employers to identify unmet business needs that can be matched with job seekers with IDD
3. **Internship and apprenticeship programs**: Create pathways for youth with IDD to gain work experience
4. **Mentor programs**: Pair employees with IDD with workplace mentors

## Organizational Actions

### Service Providers

1. **Advocate for rate increases**: Organize with other providers to document the impact of inadequate Medicaid rates on services and workforce
2. **Invest in DSP development**: Pursue NADSP credentialing; create career ladders and training opportunities
3. **Transform service models**: Transition from congregate settings to individualized community supports
4. **Embrace self-direction**: Support individuals and families who choose to self-direct their services
5. **Use technology**: Implement remote supports, assistive technology, and electronic health records to improve efficiency and quality
6. **Collect and report data**: Participate in NCI; use data for continuous quality improvement

### Employers

1. **Partner with VR agencies**: Contact your state Vocational Rehabilitation agency to learn about supported employment and customized employment services
2. **Use tax credits**: Take advantage of the Work Opportunity Tax Credit (WOTC) and the Disabled Access Credit (Section 44) when hiring employees with disabilities
3. **Create inclusive workplaces**: Implement universal design principles, provide accommodations, and foster inclusive culture
4. **Retain employees with disabilities**: Provide ongoing support, mentoring, and career development

## Policy Advocacy Actions

### Federal Level

| Action | Target | Method |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Support HCBS Access Act | Congress | Letters, calls, advocacy days |
| Support Transformation to CIE Act | Congress | Coalition letters, testimony |
| Oppose Medicaid block grants/caps | Congress | Public comment, coalition advocacy |
| Support DD Act reauthorization and increased funding | Congress | Appropriations testimony |
| Support DSP Wage Act | Congress | Coalition letters, media |
| Support IDEA Full Funding Act | Congress | Bipartisan advocacy |

### State Level

| Action | Target | Method |
|--------|--------|--------|
| DSP wage increases | State legislature | Budget testimony, media campaigns |
| Waiting list reduction | State legislature/governor | Data-driven advocacy, personal stories |
| Supported decision-making legislation | State legislature | Coalition advocacy, model legislation |
| State subminimum wage ban | State legislature | Self-advocate testimony, state campaigns |
| Employment First enforcement | State DD agency | Policy advocacy, rulemaking comment |
| Medicaid rate reform | State Medicaid agency | Rate study participation, public comment |

### Local Level

| Action | Target | Method |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Inclusive zoning | City/county council | Fair Housing Act education, advocacy |
| Accessible public facilities | Local government | ADA compliance monitoring |
| Inclusive recreation | Parks and recreation departments | Collaboration, inclusion planning |
| School inclusion | School boards | IDEA advocacy, school board testimony |
