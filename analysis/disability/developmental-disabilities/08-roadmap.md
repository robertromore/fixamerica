# Developmental Disabilities: Roadmap

## Overview

This roadmap sequences developmental disability reforms across three phases, recognizing that some changes require legislative action while others can proceed through executive authority, administrative reform, or state-level innovation. The sequencing prioritizes building workforce and infrastructure capacity before expanding service entitlements, and establishing demonstration models before mandating national changes.

| Action | Timeline | Target |

### System Maintenance

| Action | Purpose | Ongoing |
|--------|---------|---------|
| NCI benchmarking | Quality improvement | Annual |
| DSP wage indexing | Maintain competitive wages | Annual adjustment |
| Rate adequacy reviews | Prevent erosion of rates | Biennial |
| Waiting list monitoring | Prevent recurrence | Continuous |
| *Olmstead* compliance reviews | Federal enforcement | Continuous |

### Long-Term Goals

- Competitive integrated employment rate exceeds 40% for adults with IDD
- All community residential settings meet HCBS Settings Rule criteria
- Supported decision-making is the default alternative to guardianship in all states
- DSPs earn at least $22/hour (2026 dollars) with full benefits
- Technology-enabled supports reduce need for 24/7 in-person staffing where appropriate
- Full community inclusion: recreation, civic participation, social relationships are normative

## Sequencing Rationale

### Why Workforce Comes Before Expansion

Expanding service entitlements without first building workforce capacity would create legal rights without practical access. The DSP shortage is already the binding constraint; adding demand without addressing supply would worsen quality and access.

### Why Phase-Out Before Mandate

Phasing out subminimum wages and building competitive employment infrastructure creates the service models needed before HCBS mandatory coverage drives more people into the employment service system.

### Why Quality Oversight Runs Parallel

Quality and safety infrastructure must be built simultaneously with service expansion to prevent the historical pattern where rapid expansion produces quality failures that generate backlash against community services.

## Risk Assessment

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|------------|
| Legislative failure (HCBS Act) | Medium-High | Very High | Pursue administrative alternatives; state-level action |
| Workforce shortage persists despite investment | Medium | High | Technology supplements; immigration reform for care workers |
| State implementation variation | High | Medium | Strong CMS oversight; enhanced FMAP tied to compliance |
| Political backlash against spending | Medium | High | Demonstrate cost-effectiveness; bipartisan messaging |
| Provider resistance to transformation | Medium | Medium | Transition support; gradual timelines |
| Quality failures during transition | Medium | High | Robust oversight; incident tracking; independent ombudsman |
