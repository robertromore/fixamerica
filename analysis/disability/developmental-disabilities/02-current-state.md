# Developmental Disabilities: Current State

## Prevalence and Demographics

### Population Estimates

| Category | Estimate | Source |
|----------|----------|--------|
| Children ages 3-17 with any developmental disability | 17.8% (approximately 1 in 6) | CDC, MMWR Surveillance Summary (2024) |
| Autism spectrum disorder prevalence (age 8) | 1 in 36 (2.8%) | CDC, ADDM Network (2023) |
| Intellectual disability prevalence | 1-3% of population | AAIDD (2023) |
| Total Americans with IDD (all ages) | 7.4 million estimated | ACL (2024) |
| Adults with IDD receiving state DD agency services | 1.47 million | RISP, University of Minnesota (2024) |
| Adults with IDD not receiving formal services | Estimated 3-4 million | NCI/NASDDDS estimates (2024) |

### Prevalence Trends

Reported prevalence of developmental disabilities has increased significantly over the past two decades, driven primarily by increased autism diagnoses and improved identification of conditions that previously went undiagnosed.

| Condition | 2000 Prevalence | 2024 Prevalence | Change |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|--------|
| Autism spectrum disorder | 1 in 150 (age 8) | 1 in 36 (age 8) | +317% |
| ADHD (ages 3-17) | 7.8% | 11.4% | +46% |
| Intellectual disability | 1.2% | 1.1% | Stable |
| Cerebral palsy | 3.1 per 1,000 | 2.9 per 1,000 | Slight decline |
| Down syndrome (births) | 1 in 800 | 1 in 700 | +14% |

*Sources: CDC ADDM Network (2023); National Survey of Children's Health (2023); CDC MMWR (2024).*

### Demographic Disparities

| Factor | Finding | Source |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Race and autism diagnosis | Black and Hispanic children diagnosed 1-2 years later than White children on average | CDC ADDM (2023) |
| Income and services | Children from low-income families 30% less likely to receive early intervention | ACL (2024) |
| Geography | Rural areas have 40% fewer specialized providers per capita | HRSA Area Health Resources Files (2024) |
| Gender | Males diagnosed with autism at 4:1 ratio compared to females; gap narrowing with improved identification | CDC ADDM (2023) |
| Co-occurring conditions | 40-60% of people with IDD have co-occurring mental health conditions | NADD (2024) |

## Service System Overview

### Medicaid: The Primary Funding Source

Medicaid funds approximately 75% of all long-term services and supports (LTSS) for people with developmental disabilities in the United States.

**Medicaid IDD Spending (FY 2023)**

| Category | Spending | Share |
|----------|----------|-------|
| HCBS waivers (Section 1915(c)) | $79.2 billion | 67.7% |
| ICF/IDD (institutional) | $12.3 billion | 10.5% |
| Managed LTSS | $16.8 billion | 14.4% |
| State Plan HCBS (Section 1915(i) and (k)) | $8.7 billion | 7.4% |
| **Total Medicaid IDD LTSS** | **$117 billion** | **100%** |

*Source: MACPAC, MACStats (2024); Truven/IBM Watson Health Medicaid expenditure data.*

**Spending Trends**

- HCBS spending has increased from 20% of IDD Medicaid spending in 1995 to nearly 68% in 2023
- ICF/IDD spending has declined from 80% to approximately 10.5% over the same period
- Total IDD Medicaid spending has grown at approximately 5-7% annually
- COVID-era American Rescue Plan Act (ARPA) provided $12.7 billion in enhanced HCBS FMAP (FY 2021-2025)

### HCBS Waiver Waiting Lists

The most significant access barrier in the developmental disabilities system is the persistent waiting list for Medicaid HCBS waivers.

| Metric | Value | Source |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Total individuals on waiting lists | 802,000+ | KFF (2024) |
| States with waiting lists | 43 | KFF (2024) |
| Average wait time | 39 months (median); up to 10+ years in some states | RISP (2024) |
| Annual deaths on waiting lists | Not systematically tracked; estimated thousands | NCD (2023) |

**Longest Waiting Lists by State (2024)**

| State | Individuals Waiting | Average Wait Time |
|-------|--------------------|--------------------|

**States Without Waiting Lists (2024)**

*Sources: Kaiser Family Foundation, HCBS Waiver Waiting Lists (2024); RISP (2024).*

## Workforce Crisis

### Direct Support Professional (DSP) Data

Direct support professionals are the frontline workforce that makes community living possible for people with developmental disabilities. The workforce is in a deepening crisis.

**Workforce Statistics (2024)**

| Metric | Value | Source |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Total DSPs (estimated) | 1.2-1.4 million | PHI (2024) |
| Median hourly wage | $15.50 | BLS Occupational Employment and Wage Statistics (2024) |
| Median annual income | $28,780 | BLS (2024) |
| Annual turnover rate | 43-54% | NCI Staff Stability Survey (2024) |
| Vacancy rate | 13-17% | ANCOR Workforce Survey (2024) |
| DSPs relying on public assistance | 45% | PHI (2024) |
| DSPs without employer health insurance | 38% | PHI (2024) |

**Wage Comparisons**

| Occupation | Median Hourly Wage | Difference from DSP |
|------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Direct support professional | $15.50 | Baseline |
| Fast food worker | $14.35 | -$1.15 |
| Retail salesperson | $15.76 | +$0.26 |
| Certified nursing assistant | $17.25 | +$1.75 |
| Home health aide | $15.70 | +$0.20 |
| Janitor/cleaner | $16.18 | +$0.68 |

*Source: BLS Occupational Employment and Wage Statistics, May 2024.*

**Impact of Workforce Shortages**

| Impact | Prevalence | Source |
|--------|------------|--------|

| Individuals unable to access services | 46% of providers turned away new referrals | ANCOR (2024) |

## Transition and Aging

### The Transition Cliff

Young adults with developmental disabilities face a dramatic loss of services when they age out of the IDEA entitlement at age 21-22.

| System | Child/Youth | Adult |
|--------|------------|-------|
| Education | IDEA entitlement (ages 3-21) | No entitlement; adult education limited |
| Healthcare | Medicaid (often automatic) | Must qualify independently; gaps common |
| Service coordination | IEP process with school | Case management if available; often waiting lists |
| Day activities | School-based | Day programs, employment; often unavailable |
| Funding | Federal education mandate | State Medicaid waivers (not entitlement) |

**Transition Outcomes**

| Metric | Value | Source |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Youth with IDD disconnected from services 2 years post-school | 35% | NLTS2012 (2023) |
| Youth with IDD employed 1 year after school exit | 26% | NLTS2012 (2023) |
| Youth with IDD living independently 2 years post-school | 17% | NLTS2012 (2023) |
| Average wait time for adult DD services after school exit | 3.5 years | RISP (2024) |

## Key Takeaways

1. **The waiting list crisis is the defining failure**: Over 800,000 people waiting years for community-based services that the Supreme Court ruled they are entitled to under *Olmstead*
2. **The workforce crisis threatens the entire system**: DSPs earn poverty-level wages for essential work, driving turnover and service reductions that directly harm people with disabilities
3. **Employment outcomes remain unacceptable**: Only 19% of adults with IDD have competitive integrated employment, and subminimum wage persists despite growing opposition
4. **Deinstitutionalization is incomplete**: While the population in large institutions has declined dramatically, 17 states still operate multiple state institutions
5. **The transition cliff is devastating**: Young adults with IDD lose educational entitlements and face multi-year waits for adult services
6. **Demographic pressures are growing**: Rising autism prevalence, aging population, and aging caregivers will increase demand for services
7. **COVID-19 exposed systemic fragility**: The pandemic revealed how vulnerable community-based service systems are to workforce disruptions and public health emergencies
8. **Federal investment is insufficient**: Core DD Act programs have been flat-funded for decades while need has grown substantially
