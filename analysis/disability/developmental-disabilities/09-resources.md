## Foundational Legal Texts

### Federal Statutes

- Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, 42 U.S.C. Sections 12101-12213
- ADA Amendments Act of 2008, P.L. 110-325
- Developmental Disabilities Assistance and Bill of Rights Act of 2000, 42 U.S.C. Sections 15001-15115
- Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA), 20 U.S.C. Sections 1400-1482
- Social Security Act, Title XIX (Medicaid), 42 U.S.C. Sections 1396-1396w-5
- Fair Labor Standards Act, Section 14(c), 29 U.S.C. Section 214(c)
- Rehabilitation Act of 1973, 29 U.S.C. Sections 701-797
- Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA), P.L. 113-128 (2014)
- ABLE Act of 2014, 26 U.S.C. Section 529A

### Key Court Decisions

| Case | Citation | Holding |
|------|----------|---------|
| *Olmstead v. L.C.* | 527 U.S. 581 (1999) | Unnecessary institutionalization is ADA discrimination |
| *Youngberg v. Romeo* | 457 U.S. 307 (1982) | Constitutional right to minimally adequate conditions in institutions |
| *Halderman v. Pennhurst* | 451 U.S. 1 (1981) | DD Act does not create enforceable rights (federalism limits) |
| *City of Cleburne v. Cleburne Living Center* | 473 U.S. 432 (1985) | Disability-based zoning exclusion violates Equal Protection |
| *Buck v. Bell* | 274 U.S. 200 (1927) | Upheld forced sterilization (never explicitly overruled) |

### Key Regulations

- HCBS Settings Rule, 42 C.F.R. Section 441.301(c)(4)-(5) (2014; compliance deadline extended)
- ICF/IDD Conditions of Participation, 42 C.F.R. Part 483, Subpart I
- Section 14(c) regulations, 29 C.F.R. Part 525
- IDEA regulations, 34 C.F.R. Parts 300 and 303

## Essential Reading

### Books

- Braddock, David, and Susan Parish. "An Institutional History of Disability." In *Handbook of Disability Studies*, edited by Gary Albrecht, Katherine Seelman, and Michael Bury, 11-68. Thousand Oaks: Sage, 2001.
- Carey, Allison C. *On the Margins of Citizenship: Intellectual Disability and Civil Rights in Twentieth-Century America*. Philadelphia: Temple University Press, 2009.
- Trent, James W. *Inventing the Feeble Mind: A History of Intellectual Disability in the United States*. Oxford: Oxford University Press, 2017.
- Wehmeyer, Michael L. *The Story of Intellectual Disability: An Evolution of Meaning, Understanding, and Public Perception*. Baltimore: Brookes, 2013.
- Wolfensberger, Wolf. *The Principle of Normalization in Human Services*. Toronto: NIMR, 1972.
- Taylor, Steven J. *Acts of Conscience: World War II, Mental Institutions, and Religious Objectors*. Syracuse: Syracuse University Press, 2009.
