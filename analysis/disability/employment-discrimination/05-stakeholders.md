# Employment Discrimination: Stakeholders

## Primary Stakeholders

### Workers and Job Seekers with Disabilities

| Group | Population | Employment Challenges | EEOC Charge Activity |
|-------|-----------|----------------------|---------------------|
| People with physical disabilities | 13.7 million | Workplace accessibility, physical job requirements | High |
| People with sensory disabilities | 11.5 million (hearing), 7.6 million (vision) | Communication access, technology barriers | Moderate |
| People with cognitive/intellectual disabilities | 16.4 million | Hiring bias, supported employment, subminimum wage | Low (underreported) |
| People with psychiatric disabilities | 14 million | Stigma, accommodation resistance, disclosure dilemma | High |
| People with chronic health conditions | 25+ million | Attendance policies, leave issues, medical monitoring | Very high |
| Neurodiverse individuals (autism, ADHD, dyslexia) | Estimated 15-20% of population | Interview bias, personality testing, workplace culture | Growing |
| Veterans with disabilities | 4.7 million | Transition, PTSD stigma, TBI accommodation | Moderate |

*Source: CDC Disability and Health Data System (2023); BLS (2024).*

### Employers

| Type | Number | ADA Obligations | Compliance Level |
|------|--------|----------------|-----------------|
| Small employers (15-49 employees) | ~700,000 | Full ADA Title I obligations | Often unaware of requirements |
| Medium employers (50-499 employees) | ~200,000 | Full ADA + FMLA obligations | Variable compliance |
| Large employers (500+ employees) | ~20,000 | Full ADA + OFCCP (if contractor) | Generally higher compliance; formal programs |

| Federal contractors | ~250,000 | Section 503: 7% utilization goal | 5-6% average disability rate |

*Source: Census Bureau County Business Patterns (2023); OPM (2024); OFCCP (2023).*

## Secondary Stakeholders

### Families and Caregivers

- 53 million Americans serve as unpaid caregivers (AARP/NAC, 2020)
- Family members often navigate employment systems on behalf of disabled relatives
- Parents of adults with intellectual disabilities are key stakeholders in subminimum wage debate
- Families bear economic costs of employment exclusion (lost household income, caregiving time)

## Decision Makers

### Federal Decision Makers

| Decision Maker | Role | Current Posture |
|---------------|------|----------------|
| EEOC Chair and Commissioners | Set enforcement priorities, issue guidance | FY 2024-2028 plan prioritizes disability |
| OFCCP Director | Enforce Section 503 contractor requirements | Active but under-resourced |
| DOL Secretary | Oversee Section 14(c), WIOA, workforce programs | Supports competitive employment transition |
| SSA Commissioner | Administer SSDI/SSI benefit rules | Institutional resistance to benefit reform |
| Rehabilitation Services Administration | Administer VR program | Employment-first emphasis |

## Coalition Mapping

### Pro-Enforcement / Employment Reform Coalition

**Core members:** AAPD, NDRN, DREDF, NOD, Disability:IN, ASAN, The Arc, NDSS, APSE

**Aligned interests:**

- Strengthen EEOC enforcement
- Eliminate subminimum wage
- Regulate AI hiring tools
- Reform benefit system work disincentives
- Expand competitive integrated employment

**Strengths:**

- Broad cross-disability representation
- Growing corporate partnerships (through Disability:IN, NOD)
- Bipartisan tradition of disability employment legislation
- Data and research support from JAN, Kessler, Cornell

**Weaknesses:**

- Internal divisions on subminimum wage (some family groups support preserving option)
- Limited political contributions and lobbying capacity
- Competing with numerous other policy priorities for attention
- Difficulty organizing a geographically dispersed constituency

### Employer / Status Quo Coalition

**Core members:** U.S. Chamber of Commerce, NFIB, SHRM (moderate), Equal Employment Advisory Council

**Aligned interests:**

- Oppose expanded ADA mandates
- Resist AI hiring regulation
- Maintain employer flexibility in accommodation decisions
- Support voluntary compliance over enforcement expansion
- Oppose subminimum wage elimination (some members)

**Strengths:**

- Significant financial and lobbying resources
- Strong congressional relationships
- Ability to frame issues as "business burden"
- Support from defense-side employment law bar

**Weaknesses:**

- Opposing disability employment is politically unpopular
- Growing corporate disability inclusion commitments create internal tension
- Labor market tightness increases employer interest in disability employment
