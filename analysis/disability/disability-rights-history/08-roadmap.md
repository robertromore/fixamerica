# Disability Rights History: Roadmap

## Overview

This roadmap outlines a phased implementation strategy for elevating disability rights history to its rightful place in American public consciousness, education, and cultural institutions. The three phases move from urgent preservation and achievable early wins, through legislative campaigns and institutional development, to long-term structural transformation. Each phase builds on the prior one, creating momentum and institutional infrastructure for subsequent reforms.

## Phase 1: Preservation and Foundation (Years 1-3)

### Objective

Preserve irreplaceable oral histories, achieve early legislative and administrative wins, and build the institutional foundation for larger initiatives.

### Actions

| Action | Lead Actor | Timeline | Dependencies | Success Metric |
|--------|-----------|----------|--------------|----------------|
| Launch Emergency Oral History Initiative | Library of Congress + NEH | Year 1 | NEH appropriation | 200 interviews completed in Year 1; 500 by Year 3 |
| Nominate 504 sit-in site as National Historic Landmark | NPS | Year 1 | None (nomination already in progress) | Landmark designation approved |
| Presidential proclamation elevating Disability History Month | President | Year 1 | None | Proclamation with institutional engagement directives |
| Publish model disability history K-12 curriculum standards | NCD + Department of Education | Year 1-2 | NCD report; DOEd coordination | Standards published; distributed to all state education boards |
| Enact state disability history education requirements in 5 early-adopter states | State legislatures | Years 1-3 | Model legislation and advocacy | 5 states enact requirements |
| Digitize existing disability rights archives at 10 major repositories | Library of Congress + university partners | Years 1-3 | NEH/NIDILRR funding | 10 collections digitized and online |
| Launch Smithsonian disability rights history exhibition | Smithsonian NMAH | Year 2-3 | Congressional authorization or Smithsonian initiative | Exhibition opens; 500,000+ visitors in first year |
| Establish interagency Disability History Month coordination | White House + federal agencies | Year 2 | Executive order | Federal agencies develop annual programming |

### Quick Wins

- Presidential executive order directing federal agencies to recognize Disability History Month with substantive programming (achievable within 90 days)
- NPS expedited review of 504 sit-in site National Register nomination (achievable within 6 months)
- NEH special initiative on disability history research and public programs (achievable within 1 year through existing grant mechanisms)
- Congressional resolution recognizing the 504 sit-in anniversary (achievable within 6 months)

### Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Probability | Mitigation Strategy |
|------|------------|-------------------|
| Movement veterans die before interviews completed | High | Prioritize oldest participants; deploy multiple interview teams simultaneously |
| State legislatures resist curriculum mandates | Medium | Begin with voluntary adoption of model standards; build teacher advocacy |
| Appropriations shortfall for oral history initiative | Medium | Seek private foundation funding as supplement; partner with existing oral history programs |
| Administration change reverses executive order | Medium | Pursue parallel legislative action; embed requirements in agency authorization bills |

## Phase 2: Legislative Campaigns and Institutional Development (Years 3-6)

### Objective

Enact federal disability history education legislation, authorize a national museum initiative, and build the academic and cultural infrastructure for sustained engagement with disability history.

### Actions

| Action | Lead Actor | Timeline | Dependencies | Success Metric |
|--------|-----------|----------|--------------|----------------|
| Disability Rights History Education Act (federal incentive legislation) | Congress | Year 3-4 | Model curriculum from Phase 1; state adoption as evidence | Bill enacted |
| National Disability Rights Museum authorization | Congress | Year 4-5 | Smithsonian exhibition demonstrates public interest | Museum study commission authorized |
| CRPD ratification campaign | Senate | Year 3-5 | Bipartisan coalition building with veteran and disability organizations | Senate committee hearing; floor vote |
| Endow 10 disability history academic positions | Universities + private donors + NEH | Years 3-6 | NEH matching grant program | 10 endowed chairs or centers established |
| ADA enforcement funding increase | Congress (appropriations) | Year 4-5 | Phase 1 data on enforcement gaps | $100 million increase across DOJ, HHS OCR, and EEOC |
| National disability rights memorial authorization | Congress | Year 5-6 | Phase 1 historic site designations build momentum | Memorial commission authorized; design competition launched |
| Expand disability history curriculum adoption to 20 states | State legislatures | Years 3-6 | Phase 1 early-adopter success and model legislation | 20 states with disability history requirements |

### Coalition Strategy

- **Education legislation**: Coalition with National Education Association, social studies professional organizations, civil rights museums, and parent advocacy groups
- **Museum authorization**: Coalition with Smithsonian supporters, disability organizations, veterans groups, and Congressional disability caucus members
- **CRPD ratification**: Coalition with veteran organizations (including Disabled American Veterans), bipartisan foreign policy organizations, and international disability groups
- **Enforcement funding**: Coalition with civil rights organizations, legal aid groups, and Congressional appropriators with disability connections

### Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Probability | Mitigation Strategy |
|------|------------|-------------------|
| Legislative gridlock prevents education bill | Medium-High | Attach provisions to education authorization bills; pursue through Department of Education administrative guidance |
| Museum authorization delayed by budget politics | Medium | Develop private fundraising capacity; pursue temporary exhibition and digital museum as alternatives |
| CRPD fails Senate vote again | High | Continue building support; seek Senate leadership commitment to floor vote |
| Academic institutions slow to create disability history positions | Medium | NEH matching grants incentivize institutional investment; highlight growing student demand |

## Phase 3: Structural Transformation (Years 6-10)

### Objective

Achieve permanent institutional recognition of disability rights history through a national museum, comprehensive education integration, robust archival infrastructure, and international engagement.

### Actions

| Action | Lead Actor | Timeline | Dependencies | Success Metric |
|--------|-----------|----------|--------------|----------------|
| National Disability Rights Museum design and construction begins | Museum commission | Years 6-10 | Congressional authorization and funding | Site selected; design approved; construction underway |
| Digital Disability Rights Museum fully operational | Smithsonian + Library of Congress | Year 6-7 | Phase 2 digitization; platform development | Interactive platform with 10,000+ artifacts and oral histories |
| Disability history in majority of state K-12 standards | State education boards | Years 6-10 | Phase 2 federal incentives and state advocacy | 35+ states with disability history requirements |
| National disability rights memorial dedicated | Memorial commission | Year 8-10 | Phase 2 authorization and fundraising | Memorial opens on National Mall or prominent DC location |
| Disability history integrated into mainstream academic history | Universities | Years 6-10 | Phase 2 endowed positions and scholarship | Disability history recognized as a standard subfield by AHA |
| CRPD ratified (if not achieved in Phase 2) | Senate | Years 6-8 | Continued advocacy; favorable Senate composition | Ratification achieved |
| Comprehensive national historic site network | NPS | Years 6-10 | Phase 1-2 designations | 15+ disability rights sites designated as National Historic Landmarks or Sites |

### Long-Term Targets (Year 10)

| Metric | Current Baseline | Year 5 Target | Year 10 Target |
|--------|-----------------|---------------|----------------|
| States with disability history K-12 requirements | 2 (NJ, IL) | 15 | 35+ |
| Oral history interviews preserved and digitized | ~200 (scattered) | 1,000 | 2,500+ |
| Disability rights National Historic Landmarks | 0 | 3 | 15+ |
| Universities with disability history faculty | ~10 | 25 | 50+ |
| Public awareness of disability rights movement (survey) | <15% can name a key event | 35% | 60%+ |
| National disability history museum | None | Smithsonian exhibition open | Permanent museum under construction or open |
| CRPD ratification | Not ratified | Senate vote held | Ratified |
| Disability History Month public awareness | <5% of Americans aware | 20% | 40%+ |

## Success Metrics Summary

### Short-Term (Years 1-3)

- 500+ oral history interviews completed and preserved
- 5 states enact disability history education requirements
- 504 sit-in site designated as National Historic Landmark
- Smithsonian disability rights exhibition opens
- 10 major archive collections digitized and accessible online
- Model K-12 curriculum standards published

### Medium-Term (Years 3-6)

- Federal disability history education incentive legislation enacted
- National museum authorized by Congress
- CRPD ratification vote held in Senate
- 20 states with disability history requirements
- 10 endowed disability history academic positions established
- National memorial authorized

### Long-Term (Years 6-10)

- National disability rights museum under construction or open
- Disability history taught in majority of U.S. schools
- Public awareness of disability rights movement comparable to other civil rights movements
- Comprehensive digital archive freely accessible
- National memorial dedicated
- Disability history recognized as standard academic subfield
