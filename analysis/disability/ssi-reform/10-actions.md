# SSI Reform: Actions

## For Individuals

### Learn First

**Understand the Problem**

- [ ] Read [Current State](02-current-state.md) to understand SSI's structural failures
- [ ] Read [Root Causes](04-root-causes.md) to understand why these failures persist
- [ ] Learn the difference between SSI and SSDI (most people conflate them)
- [ ] Review the marriage penalty, asset limits, and ISM rules (see [Solutions](07-solutions.md))

**Know Your State**

- [ ] Find out if your state provides SSI supplementation and at what level
- [ ] Research your state's Medicaid rules for SSI recipients (Section 1619(b) protections)
- [ ] Identify your state's ABLE program and its features
- [ ] Learn who represents you on the House Ways and Means Committee and Senate Finance Committee

### Take Action

**Contact Representatives**

- [ ] Email your senators and representative about the SSI Savings Penalty Elimination Act
- [ ] Call your representative's office and ask their position on SSI asset limit reform
- [ ] Attend town halls and raise SSI issues -- many legislators are unaware of the $2,000 asset limit
- [ ] Write letters to the editor about SSI reform, especially around the marriage penalty
- [ ] Request a meeting with your representative's disability policy staffer

**Share Stories**

- [ ] If you or someone you know receives SSI, share experiences with Congressional offices (with permission)
- [ ] Write op-eds for local newspapers about how SSI's rules affect real people
- [ ] Provide testimony to state or federal legislative committees (disability rights organizations can connect you)
- [ ] Document specific examples of how the marriage penalty, asset limits, or ISM rules cause harm

**Join the Movement**

- [ ] Join The Arc, NAMI, or another disability rights organization active on SSI
- [ ] Follow CBPP, Justice in Aging, and NOSSCR for policy updates
- [ ] Subscribe to SSI reform newsletters and action alerts
- [ ] Participate in SSI-related advocacy days and Hill visits

**Spread the Word**

- [ ] Share SSI reform information on social media
- [ ] Explain the $2,000 asset limit to people who are unaware (most Americans are)
- [ ] Discuss the marriage penalty with faith communities and family values organizations
- [ ] Bring SSI issues to existing advocacy groups you belong to (labor, faith, civic)

## For Organizations

### Disability Rights Organizations

- [ ] Make SSI reform a legislative priority alongside ADA enforcement and SSDI reform
- [ ] Develop and distribute easy-to-understand fact sheets on SSI's failures
- [ ] Coordinate with anti-poverty organizations for broader coalition
- [ ] Provide Congressional testimony and policy expertise
- [ ] Train beneficiaries as self-advocates who can share their stories
- [ ] Monitor and publicize state supplementation levels

### Faith Communities

- [ ] Highlight the marriage penalty as a family values issue
- [ ] Organize letter-writing campaigns to legislators
- [ ] Provide meeting space for SSI reform advocacy groups
- [ ] Connect SSI issues to broader social justice and dignity-of-life teachings
- [ ] Support SSI recipients in congregations with practical assistance and advocacy

### Legal Aid Organizations

- [ ] Represent SSI applicants and beneficiaries in administrative proceedings
- [ ] Challenge unlawful overpayment recovery practices
- [ ] Document patterns of SSA errors for systemic advocacy
- [ ] Train pro bono attorneys on SSI law and representation
- [ ] File administrative complaints and, where appropriate, federal litigation on systemic issues

### Labor Unions

- [ ] Support SSA employee funding through AFGE partnerships
- [ ] Include SSI reform in broader economic justice platforms
- [ ] Advocate for SSI work incentive improvements that support union membership among workers with disabilities
- [ ] Engage members who have family members on SSI

### Employers and Business Organizations

- [ ] Support SSI work incentive reforms that make it easier for people with disabilities to enter the workforce
- [ ] Partner with supported employment providers
- [ ] Advocate for SSA administrative funding (faster processing benefits employers seeking to hire)
- [ ] Provide testimony on how the current system's work disincentives reduce the available labor pool

## For Policymakers

### Federal Legislators

- [ ] Co-sponsor the SSI Savings Penalty Elimination Act (bipartisan, low cost, high impact)
- [ ] Hold hearings on SSI adequacy in Ways and Means and Finance Committees
- [ ] Request updated CBO and GAO analyses of SSI reform proposals
- [ ] Include SSI asset limit reform in budget reconciliation packages
- [ ] Support SSA administrative funding in annual appropriations
- [ ] Commission a National Academy of Sciences study on SSI modernization

### State Legislators

- [ ] Increase state SSI supplementation or establish a supplement if your state lacks one
- [ ] Expand ABLE account programs and promote enrollment
- [ ] Review state Medicaid rules to ensure Section 1619(b) protections are implemented effectively
- [ ] Pass resolutions calling on Congress to reform SSI

### Executive Branch Officials

- [ ] SSA Commissioner: Implement ISM simplification within existing administrative authority
- [ ] SSA Commissioner: Reduce overpayment recovery rates and expand waiver criteria
- [ ] OMB Director: Prioritize SSA administrative funding in budget proposals
- [ ] Domestic Policy Council: Develop comprehensive SSI reform legislative package
