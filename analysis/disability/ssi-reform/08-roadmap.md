# SSI Reform: Roadmap

## Phase 1: Immediate Actions (2025-2026)

### Build Awareness and Coalition

**Education**

- Understand SSI's current structure and its failures (see [Current State](02-current-state.md))
- Learn the difference between SSI and SSDI (many people conflate them)
- Research existing reform proposals: SSI Savings Penalty Elimination Act, SSI Restoration Act
- Familiarize yourself with international comparisons (see [Solutions](07-solutions.md))

**Coalition Building**

- Connect with disability rights organizations active on SSI (The Arc, NAMI, CBPP, Justice in Aging)
- Engage faith communities on the marriage penalty (family values frame)
- Reach out to fiscal conservative organizations open to asset limit reform (Niskanen Center)
- Build relationships with SSA employee unions (AFGE)

### Support Active Legislative Campaigns

**Current Opportunities**

- SSI Savings Penalty Elimination Act: Bipartisan bill to raise asset limits; lowest-hanging fruit
- SSI Restoration Act: Comprehensive reform bill; raise awareness even if passage unlikely immediately
- SSA administrative funding: Annual appropriations process
- ABLE Act expansion: Build on bipartisan success of age-46 expansion

**How to Help**

- Contact senators and representatives about SSI Savings Penalty Elimination Act
- Share beneficiary stories with media and Congressional offices
- Support organizations lobbying for SSA appropriations increases
- Attend town halls and raise SSI issues with members of Congress

## Phase 2: Legislative Foundation (2026-2028)

### Targeted Bipartisan Reforms

**Asset Limit Modernization** (Highest probability of passage)

- Advance SSI Savings Penalty Elimination Act through committee markup
- Build bipartisan co-sponsor list (currently has Republican support)
- Include automatic inflation indexing to prevent future freezes
- Attach to must-pass legislation if freestanding bill stalls

**Marriage Penalty Fix**

- Introduce standalone marriage penalty elimination bill
- Frame as family values issue to attract conservative support
- Cost is modest ($2-3 billion/year); easier to offset than comprehensive reform
- Build coalition with religious organizations, disability groups, and family policy advocates

**ISM Simplification**

- Support SSA's administrative simplification of food ISM rules
- Push for legislative elimination of shelter ISM or replacement with flat deduction
- Engage SSA employees as advocates for simplification (reduces their workload)

### Administrative Improvements

**SSA Funding**

- Campaign for mandatory administrative funding in next budget agreement
- Document connection between SSA understaffing and SSI problems
- Build bipartisan "investment saves money" narrative using GAO data

**Overpayment Reform**

- Push for administrative changes to overpayment recovery (5% withholding cap, expanded waivers)
- Support IT modernization funding for real-time wage verification
- Advocate for $100 minimum overpayment threshold

## Phase 3: Comprehensive Reform (2028-2032)

### SSI Modernization Act

**Core Components**

- Raise benefits to FPL (phased in over 3-5 years)
- Codify asset limit at $10,000/$20,000 with indexing (if not already passed)
- Eliminate marriage penalty
- Simplify income-counting rules comprehensively
- Reform deeming rules
- Mandate adequate SSA administrative funding

**Legislative Strategy**

- Introduce comprehensive bill early in new Congress
- Seek inclusion in budget reconciliation if possible (avoids filibuster)
- Build on Phase 2 successes to demonstrate bipartisan feasibility
- Use demonstration project results to support evidence-based provisions

### Work Incentive Redesign

- Increase earned income exclusion to $400/month
- Create a benefit offset similar to SSDI's $1-for-$2 model
- Extend Medicaid protection period for working SSI recipients
- Invest in supported employment infrastructure
- Pilot a "benefits counselor" program to help recipients navigate work transitions

## Phase 4: Long-Term System Transformation (2032+)

### Structural Modernization

- Evaluate whether SSI and SSDI should be integrated into a unified disability benefit
- Consider replacing means-tested SSI with a universal disability supplement (basic income for people with disabilities)
- Implement full real-time benefit calculation and adjustment
- Harmonize SSI with SNAP, Medicaid, and housing assistance to reduce cliff effects and benefit interaction penalties

### Technology and Administration

- Complete SSA IT modernization
- Implement fully digital application and reporting processes
- Develop AI-assisted eligibility determination with human oversight
- Create beneficiary self-service portals for income reporting, account management, and benefits counseling

### Policy Evaluation

- Commission 10-year evaluation of SSI reform outcomes
- Compare U.S. disability benefit system with international models
- Adjust benefit levels, exclusions, and program parameters based on evidence

## Risk Assessment

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|------------|
| Budget constraints prevent benefit increases | High | High | Pursue asset limit and marriage penalty fixes first; build fiscal case |
| SSA cannot implement reforms with current systems | Medium | High | Include mandatory IT funding in any reform legislation |
| State supplement reductions offset federal increases | Medium | Medium | Include maintenance of effort requirements |
| Political backlash against "welfare expansion" | Medium | Medium | Frame reforms around savings, marriage, and work -- not welfare |
| Economic recession shifts priorities | Medium | Medium | Position SSI reform as economic stimulus (benefits spent locally) |
| Administrative capacity insufficient for transition | Medium | High | Phase in reforms; hire and train staff before implementation |
