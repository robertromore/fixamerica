# Intersectionality: Resources

## Overview

This document compiles key resources for understanding the intersection of disability with race, gender, sexual orientation, immigration status, and poverty. Resources are organized by type and relevance to support researchers, policymakers, advocates, and practitioners working to advance intersectional disability equity.

## Federal Law and Regulation

### Disability Statutes

- **Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990** (42 U.S.C. 12101 et seq.) - Prohibits disability discrimination in employment (Title I), government services (Title II), and public accommodations (Title III). Does not explicitly address intersectional discrimination.
- **Rehabilitation Act of 1973, Section 504** (29 U.S.C. 794) - Prohibits disability discrimination in federally funded programs. Applied in *Olmstead v. L.C.* to mandate community integration.
- **Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA)** (20 U.S.C. 1400 et seq.) - Requires free appropriate public education for children with disabilities. Includes disproportionality monitoring by race.
- **Social Security Act, Title XVI (SSI)** (42 U.S.C. 1381 et seq.) - Supplemental Security Income for people with disabilities. Excludes non-citizens during first 5 years; excludes undocumented immigrants entirely.
- **Affordable Care Act** (P.L. 111-148, 2010) - Expanded Medicaid coverage and prohibited disability discrimination in healthcare. Section 1557 prohibits discrimination on multiple bases.

### Civil Rights Statutes

- **Civil Rights Act of 1964, Title VII** (42 U.S.C. 2000e et seq.) - Prohibits employment discrimination based on race, color, sex, religion, and national origin. Does not cover disability.
- **Fair Housing Act** (42 U.S.C. 3601 et seq.) - Prohibits housing discrimination based on race, color, sex, religion, national origin, familial status, and disability as separate protected classes.
- **Violence Against Women Act** (34 U.S.C. 12291 et seq.) - Addresses gender-based violence. Limited disability-specific provisions.
- **Personal Responsibility and Work Opportunity Reconciliation Act of 1996** (P.L. 104-193) - Restricted immigrant access to SSI and other federal benefits.

## Key Court Decisions

### Intersectional Discrimination

- *DeGraffenreid v. General Motors*, 413 F. Supp. 142 (E.D. Mo. 1976) - Rejected intersectional claim by Black women, establishing single-axis framework that persists in many jurisdictions.
- *Lam v. University of Hawaii*, 40 F.3d 1551 (9th Cir. 1994) - Recognized that discrimination based on the combination of race and sex is distinct from either alone.
- *Frappied v. Affinity Gaming*, 966 F.3d 1038 (9th Cir. 2020) - Applied intersectional analysis to compound discrimination claim involving race, sex, and age.
- *Jeffers v. Thompson*, 264 F. Supp. 2d 314 (D. Md. 2003) - Recognized intersectional disability and race discrimination claim.

### Disability and Race

- *Olmstead v. L.C.*, 527 U.S. 581 (1999) - Required community integration for people with disabilities. The plaintiffs, Lois Curtis and Elaine Wilson, were Black women with intellectual disabilities and mental illness -- their race shaped their institutional experience though the legal analysis focused on disability.
- *Larry P. v. Riles*, 793 F.2d 969 (9th Cir. 1984) - Found racially biased IQ testing in special education placement disproportionately labeling Black children.
- *Buck v. Bell*, 274 U.S. 200 (1927) - Upheld forced sterilization of people with disabilities. Eugenics programs disproportionately targeted women of color with disabilities.

### Disability and Gender

- *Bragdon v. Abbott*, 524 U.S. 624 (1998) - HIV status as disability under ADA. The case involved a woman denied dental care, intersecting disability and healthcare access.
- *Alexander v. Choate*, 469 U.S. 287 (1985) - Established disparate impact analysis under Section 504, applicable to intersectional disparities.

## International Frameworks

- **Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD)** - UN treaty recognizing disability rights regardless of identity. U.S. has signed but not ratified.
- **UK Equality Act 2010, Section 14** - Provides for "combined discrimination: dual characteristics" claims (race + disability, etc.). Not yet fully in force.
- **EU Framework Directive on Equal Treatment** - Prohibits discrimination on multiple grounds including disability, race, sex, and sexual orientation.
- **Canadian Human Rights Act** - Prohibits discrimination on multiple grounds; Canadian courts have recognized intersectional claims.
- **Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination Against Women (CEDAW)** - General Recommendation No. 18 addresses women with disabilities.
- **Inter-American Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination Against Persons with Disabilities** - OAS treaty with intersectional provisions.
