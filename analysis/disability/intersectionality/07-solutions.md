# Intersectionality: Solutions

## Overview

Addressing the failure of disability policy to account for intersectional identities requires structural reform across legal frameworks, data systems, service delivery, organizational governance, and funding mechanisms. No single intervention will close the disparities documented in the preceding files. The compounding nature of intersectional disadvantage means that reforms must operate simultaneously across multiple systems -- legal, institutional, cultural, and economic -- to break the reinforcing cycles that perpetuate unequal outcomes. The proposals below are organized from highest-impact structural reforms to targeted interventions, with each building on the others to create a self-reinforcing system of intersectional equity.

## Legal and Regulatory Solutions

### 1. Recognize Intersectional Discrimination in Federal Law

**Problem addressed**: American civil rights law treats protected classes as separate axes, forcing plaintiffs to choose a single basis for discrimination claims and preventing courts from recognizing compounded disadvantage.

**Proposal**: Amend the ADA, Title VII, and other federal civil rights statutes to explicitly recognize intersectional discrimination as a cognizable legal theory, allowing claims based on the interaction of two or more protected characteristics.

**Key elements**:

- Add a provision to each major civil rights statute stating that discrimination based on the combination of two or more protected characteristics is a distinct form of discrimination
- Instruct courts that intersectional claims need not prove discrimination on each axis independently
- Authorize the EEOC to develop guidance on investigating and adjudicating intersectional claims
- Establish intersectional discrimination as an aggravating factor in damages calculations
- Create a unified federal anti-discrimination framework that covers disability, race, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, national origin, age, and immigration status

**Evidence**: The Ninth Circuit's recognition of intersectional claims in *Lam v. University of Hawaii* (1994) and *Frappied v. Affinity Gaming* (2020) demonstrates judicial willingness. The European Union's Framework Directive and the UK Equality Act 2010 (Section 14) provide international models for codifying intersectional protections.

**Estimated impact**: Would transform the legal landscape for the approximately 16 million people of color with disabilities, 33 million women with disabilities, and millions of LGBTQ+ and immigrant people with disabilities who currently face compounded discrimination without adequate legal remedy.

### 2. Strengthen EEOC Intersectional Enforcement

**Problem addressed**: Less than 5% of EEOC charges allege multiple protected classes, and the agency lacks formal guidance on investigating intersectional discrimination.

**Proposal**: Direct the EEOC to issue formal guidance on intersectional discrimination, train investigators in intersectional analysis, and track intersectional charge outcomes.

**Key elements**:

- Issue formal interpretive guidance recognizing intersectional discrimination under Title VII and the ADA
- Train all EEOC investigators and attorneys on intersectional analysis
- Require intake forms and charge processing systems to capture multiple protected class allegations
- Publish annual data on intersectional charges, resolutions, and outcomes
- Designate intersectional discrimination specialists in each EEOC district office
- Conduct proactive investigations of industries with documented intersectional disparities

**Evidence**: The EEOC began developing intersectionality guidance in 2025. The agency already has enforcement authority over both Title VII and the ADA; guidance clarifying intersectional application requires no new legislation.

### 3. Reform ADA Enforcement for Equity

**Problem addressed**: ADA enforcement mechanisms work best for white, middle-class, English-speaking individuals with physical disabilities and fail those at the intersections of multiple marginalized identities.

**Proposal**: Require ADA enforcement entities (DOJ, EEOC, federal agencies) to incorporate equity analysis into enforcement priorities and outcomes tracking.

**Key elements**:

- Require annual equity reports from DOJ Civil Rights Division, EEOC, and HHS Office for Civil Rights documenting enforcement outcomes disaggregated by race, gender, language, and other demographics
- Prioritize enforcement actions addressing systemic intersectional discrimination
- Fund culturally and linguistically accessible complaint processes
- Establish community-based intake partnerships with organizations serving people of color, immigrants, and LGBTQ+ individuals with disabilities
- Create an ADA Equity Office within DOJ to coordinate intersectional enforcement across federal agencies

## Data and Research Solutions

### 4. Mandate Intersectional Disability Data Collection

**Problem addressed**: Most federal disability data systems do not routinely collect or publish intersectional crossbreaks, making disparities invisible and preventing evidence-based policy.

**Proposal**: Require all federal agencies that collect disability data to simultaneously collect and publish data disaggregated by race, ethnicity, gender, age, language, and (where feasible) sexual orientation and gender identity.

**Key elements**:

- Amend federal data collection statutes to require intersectional disaggregation of disability data
- Mandate that CMS, DOE, DOJ, BLS, SSA, and ACL publish disability data with race, ethnicity, gender, and language crossbreaks annually
- Add sexual orientation and gender identity questions to disability surveys (with appropriate privacy protections)
- Fund expansion of sample sizes to enable meaningful intersectional analysis
- Require intersectional disparities analysis in all federally funded disability research
- Establish minimum standards for state-level intersectional disability data collection as a condition of federal funding

**Evidence**: The Office of Management and Budget's 2024 revisions to statistical race and ethnicity categories demonstrate that data collection reform is feasible. CMS's equity plan (2022) already requires some intersectional data but implementation is incomplete.

**Estimated impact**: Would transform the evidence base for disability policy, enabling targeted interventions for the populations facing the greatest disparities.

### 5. Fund Intersectional Disability Research

**Problem addressed**: Academic disability research overwhelmingly centers white, middle-class experiences, and intersectional disability research is disproportionately unfunded.

**Proposal**: Create dedicated federal research funding streams for intersectional disability research and require intersectional analysis in all federally funded disability studies.

**Key elements**:

- Establish a $50 million annual NIH research program on intersectional disability health disparities
- Require all NIDILRR-funded research to include intersectional analysis as a standard component
- Fund researcher development programs for scholars of color with disabilities
- Create community-based participatory research grants that center people at the intersections
- Require diversity in research participant recruitment across all federal disability studies
- Publish annual reports on intersectional disability research funding and output

## Service Delivery Solutions

### 6. Redesign Disability Services for Cultural Responsiveness

**Problem addressed**: Disability service systems were designed around white, English-speaking, middle-class norms and fail to serve people from diverse cultural, linguistic, and identity backgrounds.

**Proposal**: Require federally funded disability services to meet cultural responsiveness standards and provide linguistically accessible services.

**Key elements**:

- Develop and implement National Standards for Culturally and Linguistically Appropriate Disability Services (modeled on HHS CLAS Standards)
- Require disability service providers receiving federal funding to demonstrate cultural responsiveness through staff training, language access plans, and outcome tracking by demographics
- Fund translation and interpretation services for disability programs in languages beyond English and ASL, including international sign languages
- Require person-centered planning processes that accommodate diverse cultural frameworks for disability, autonomy, and family involvement
- Establish community health worker and peer navigator programs staffed by people from the communities served
- Incentivize hiring direct support professionals who share racial, linguistic, and cultural backgrounds with service recipients

**Evidence**: HHS CLAS Standards have improved health equity in healthcare settings. The Medicaid managed care quality framework already includes some cultural competency requirements. Community health worker programs have demonstrated effectiveness in reducing health disparities (CDC Community Preventive Services Task Force, 2024).

### 7. Address Intersectional Violence Against People with Disabilities

**Problem addressed**: Women with disabilities experience violence at twice the rate of women without disabilities, and women of color with disabilities face compounded barriers to safety.

**Proposal**: Create integrated violence prevention and response systems that address the intersection of disability, gender, race, and other identities.

**Key elements**:

- Require all domestic violence shelters receiving federal funding to meet physical and programmatic accessibility standards
- Fund specialized advocacy programs for women of color with disabilities, LGBTQ+ people with disabilities, and immigrant women with disabilities experiencing violence
- Train law enforcement on disability-specific dynamics of domestic violence, sexual assault, and abuse
- Amend the Violence Against Women Act to include explicit provisions for women with disabilities, including accessible protective orders and court processes
- Fund research on violence against people at the intersections of disability and other marginalized identities
- Require accessible and culturally appropriate sexual assault forensic examination services

### 8. Eliminate Immigration-Based Disability Service Exclusions

**Problem addressed**: Immigration status determines whether a person with a disability can access basic services, with undocumented immigrants excluded from SSI, Medicaid, and most disability programs entirely.

**Proposal**: Expand eligibility for disability services regardless of immigration status and reduce barriers created by immigration enforcement fear.

**Key elements**:

- Eliminate the 5-year SSI waiting period for lawful permanent residents
- Extend Medicaid eligibility for disability services to all residents regardless of immigration status (phased implementation)
- Issue DHS guidance clarifying that seeking disability services does not trigger public charge determinations
- Establish firewalls between disability service agencies and immigration enforcement
- Fund language access programs in disability services beyond English and Spanish
- Create specialized disability service navigators for immigrant communities

## Organizational and Movement Solutions

### 9. Transform Disability Organization Governance for Equity

**Problem addressed**: The disability rights movement has been disproportionately led by white, male, physically disabled individuals, shaping organizational priorities and leaving intersectional concerns as secondary issues.

**Proposal**: Use federal funding requirements and foundation partnership to drive diversity in disability organization leadership and governance.

**Key elements**:

- Require organizations receiving ACL, NIDILRR, or other federal disability grants to report demographic data on board, leadership, and staff
- Establish equity benchmarks for federally funded disability organizations, with progressive requirements over 5 years
- Create a federal grant program specifically for disability organizations led by people of color, women, LGBTQ+ individuals, and immigrants with disabilities
- Fund leadership development programs for people at the intersections of disability and other marginalized identities
- Require advisory bodies for federal disability programs (e.g., National Council on Disability, President's Committee for People with Intellectual Disabilities) to reflect the demographic diversity of the disability community
- Fund cross-movement convenings bringing together disability, racial justice, gender equity, LGBTQ+, and immigrant rights organizations

### 10. Build Cross-Movement Coalitions

**Problem addressed**: The disability rights movement, civil rights movement, feminist movement, LGBTQ+ movement, and immigrant rights movement have operated in parallel, with limited structural alliance despite shared constituencies.

**Proposal**: Create infrastructure and incentives for sustained cross-movement coalition building.

**Key elements**:

- Fund joint policy research and advocacy projects that bridge disability and racial justice, gender equity, LGBTQ+ rights, and immigrant rights
- Create a federal interagency working group on intersectional disability policy, including representatives from DOJ, CMS, DOE, HHS, DOL, and DHS
- Establish model memoranda of understanding between disability service agencies and civil rights, domestic violence, LGBTQ+, and immigrant service agencies
- Fund cross-training programs between disability service providers and providers serving other marginalized communities
- Support joint advocacy campaigns (e.g., Medicaid expansion, police reform, housing access) that unite disability and racial justice constituencies

## Economic Solutions

### 11. Reform Benefit Programs to Address Compounding Disadvantage

**Problem addressed**: Benefit programs (SSI, SSDI, Medicaid) treat disability as a single axis and do not account for the additional costs and barriers faced by people at the intersections.

**Proposal**: Reform disability benefit programs to recognize and address intersectional disadvantage.

**Key elements**:

- Eliminate SSI asset limits, which disproportionately trap people of color with disabilities in poverty due to the racial wealth gap
- Adjust Medicaid funding formulas to account for intersectional disparities, directing additional resources to states and communities with documented compounding disadvantage
- Create supplemental support for disability benefit recipients who face language barriers, lack of culturally appropriate services, or geographic isolation
- Expand ABLE account eligibility and outreach to communities of color and immigrant communities
- Reform SSDI work incentive programs to account for intersectional employment barriers (e.g., racial discrimination + disability discrimination)

### 12. Target Economic Development to Intersectional Communities

**Problem addressed**: Economic development programs for people with disabilities do not target the communities facing the most severe compounding disadvantage.

**Proposal**: Create targeted economic development programs for people at the intersections of disability and other forms of marginalization.

**Key elements**:

- Establish set-aside contracting programs for businesses owned by people of color with disabilities, women with disabilities, and veterans with disabilities
- Fund entrepreneurship programs specifically designed for people at the intersections
- Create supported employment programs with cultural and linguistic competency in communities of color
- Expand customized employment and self-employment programs in underserved communities
- Fund community development financial institutions (CDFIs) focused on disability and racial equity
