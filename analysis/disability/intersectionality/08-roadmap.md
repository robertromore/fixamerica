# Intersectionality: Implementation Roadmap

## Strategic Approach

### Theory of Change

Intersectional disability reform requires building the evidence base first (data), then changing the rules (law and regulation), then transforming institutions (service delivery and organizations), and finally sustaining progress through accountability systems. Each phase creates the conditions for the next. Without intersectional data, policymakers cannot design targeted interventions. Without legal recognition of intersectional discrimination, enforcement cannot address compounded disadvantage. Without institutional change, legal reforms remain paper protections. Without sustained accountability, progress reverses.

### Strategic Pillars

| Pillar | Purpose | Key Actions |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| **Data infrastructure** | Make disparities visible and measurable | Intersectional data mandates, research funding, reporting requirements |
| **Legal framework** | Provide enforceable rights against compounded discrimination | Intersectional discrimination recognition, EEOC guidance, ADA equity reform |
| **Service transformation** | Ensure disability services reach all communities | Cultural responsiveness standards, language access, violence prevention |
| **Organizational equity** | Center people at the intersections in leadership and decision-making | Governance reform, cross-movement coalitions, leadership development |
| **Economic inclusion** | Break the compounding cycle of disability and poverty by race | Benefit reform, targeted economic development, asset limit elimination |

### Political Windows

| Window | Opportunity |
|--------|-------------|
| **EEOC intersectionality guidance development** | Agency already developing guidance; advocacy can shape content (2025-2026) |
| **CMS equity plan implementation** | Active framework for integrating racial equity into Medicaid/Medicare (ongoing) |
| **VAWA reauthorization** | Opportunity to include disability-specific violence provisions |
| **ADA anniversary years** | 2025 (35th), 2030 (40th) generate political attention for ADA reform |
| **Census/ACS data updates** | New data with enhanced race/ethnicity categories available starting 2026 |
| **Anti-DEI backlash response** | Framing intersectional reform as evidence-based rather than ideological can preempt opposition |

## Phase 1: Data Foundation and Executive Action (2026-2028)

### Objective

Use existing executive authority to establish intersectional data infrastructure, issue enforcement guidance, and begin institutional reforms that do not require new legislation.

### Executive Actions

| Action | Authority | Timeline | Lead Agency |
|--------|----------|----------|-------------|
| Issue EEOC intersectional discrimination guidance | EEOC enforcement authority | Q2 2026 | EEOC |
| Direct federal agencies to publish disability data with intersectional crossbreaks | OMB statistical policy authority | Q3 2026 | OMB |
| Issue executive order on intersectional disability equity | Presidential authority | Q1 2026 | White House |
| Require CMS equity metrics in Medicaid HCBS waiver programs | CMS administrative authority | Q4 2026 | CMS |
| Issue DHS guidance on disability services and public charge | DHS regulatory authority | Q2 2027 | DHS |

### Data Infrastructure

| Initiative | Implementation |
|-----------|---------------|
| OMB directive on intersectional disability data | Require all federal disability surveys to include race, ethnicity, gender, language crossbreaks |
| CMS intersectional quality metrics | Add race-by-disability outcome measures to Medicaid managed care quality framework |
| DOE disproportionality enforcement | Strengthen enforcement of IDEA racial disproportionality requirements |
| EEOC charge data reform | Publish annual data on intersectional discrimination charges and outcomes |
| Pilot SOGI data collection in disability surveys | ACL-funded pilot in 5 states adding sexual orientation/gender identity to disability needs assessments |

### Enforcement Initiatives

| Action | Implementation |
|--------|---------------|
| EEOC intersectional investigation training | Train all investigators in intersectional analysis; 500 staff in first year |
| DOJ ADA equity audits | Pilot intersectional compliance reviews in 3 sectors: healthcare, education, employment |
| Community intake partnerships | Fund 20 community-based organizations to serve as ADA complaint intake points for communities of color, immigrants, and LGBTQ+ individuals |
| Language access enforcement | HHS OCR enforcement of language access requirements in disability services |

### Organizational Reform

| Initiative | Implementation |
|-----------|---------------|
| ACL grantee demographic reporting | Require all ACL-funded organizations to report board, leadership, and staff demographics |
| Leadership development program | Fund 100 leadership development fellowships for people of color, women, LGBTQ+ individuals, and immigrants with disabilities |
| Cross-movement convenings | Host 4 regional convenings bringing together disability, racial justice, gender equity, LGBTQ+, and immigrant rights organizations |

### Milestones

- EEOC intersectional discrimination guidance issued: Q2 2026
- OMB intersectional data directive issued: Q3 2026
- First federal intersectional disability data report published: Q4 2027
- 20 community-based ADA complaint intake partnerships operational: Q2 2028
- CMS intersectional quality metrics in place for HCBS waivers: Q4 2027
- 100 intersectional leadership fellows in first cohort: Q4 2028

## Phase 2: Legislative Reform and Institutional Change (2028-2031)

### Objective

Secure legislative changes that codify intersectional protections, mandate data collection, reform benefit programs, and fund institutional transformation.

### Federal Legislation

| Bill | Purpose | Coalition |
|------|---------|-----------|
| Intersectional Discrimination Recognition Act | Amend civil rights statutes to recognize intersectional claims | Disability rights + civil rights + women's organizations + LGBTQ+ organizations |
| Intersectional Disability Data Act | Mandate intersectional data collection across federal agencies | Bipartisan data modernization coalition |
| Culturally Responsive Disability Services Act | Fund and require cultural responsiveness in federally funded disability services | Disability rights + racial justice + immigrant rights |
| SSI Equity Act | Eliminate asset limits and immigration-based exclusions | Disability rights + anti-poverty + immigrant rights |
| Intersectional Violence Prevention Act | Strengthen protections for women with disabilities experiencing violence | Women's organizations + disability rights + LGBTQ+ organizations |

### Service System Transformation

| Initiative | Scale |
|-----------|-------|
| National Standards for Culturally Responsive Disability Services | Developed and implemented by CMS and ACL |
| Language access expansion in disability programs | Translation and interpretation in 15+ languages in all federally funded disability services |
| Community health worker disability programs | 1,000 community health workers in underserved disability communities |
| Cross-training between disability and civil rights service providers | 5,000 providers trained annually |

### Research and Workforce Development

| Initiative | Scale |
|-----------|-------|
| NIH intersectional disability health disparities program | $50 million annually for 5 years |
| NIDILRR intersectional research requirement | All funded projects include intersectional analysis |
| Scholar development for researchers of color with disabilities | 200 doctoral fellowships over 5 years |
| Community-based participatory research grants | 50 grants to organizations led by people at the intersections |

### Milestones

- Intersectional Discrimination Recognition Act introduced: Q1 2029
- Intersectional Disability Data Act enacted: Q4 2029
- National Culturally Responsive Disability Service Standards published: Q2 2030
- SSI asset limits eliminated: Q4 2030
- NIH intersectional disability program fully funded: Q1 2029
- First intersectional disability data report with full federal coverage: Q4 2030

## Phase 3: Systemic Integration (2031-2034)

### Objective

Embed intersectional analysis as standard practice across all disability policy, service delivery, research, and advocacy -- creating self-sustaining systems that do not depend solely on advocacy or enforcement.

### Legal and Enforcement Integration

| Initiative | Mechanism |
|-----------|-----------|
| Intersectional discrimination case law development | DOJ and private litigants build body of precedent under new statutory framework |
| Federal court training on intersectionality | Federal Judicial Center curriculum on intersectional discrimination analysis |
| State intersectional anti-discrimination laws | Model legislation adopted in 25+ states |
| International intersectionality coordination | Alignment with EU, UK, and Canadian intersectional anti-discrimination frameworks |

### Service System Maturation

| Initiative | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| Cultural responsiveness certification for disability providers | All federally funded providers certified |
| Language access in disability services | Full compliance with language access requirements across all programs |
| Integrated violence prevention and disability services | Co-located services available in all states |
| Immigration firewall implementation | Complete separation of disability service agencies from immigration enforcement in all states |

### Data and Research Maturation

| Milestone | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| Routine intersectional disability data publication | All federal agencies publish annually |
| SOGI data in disability surveys | Standard in all major federal disability surveys |
| Intersectional disability research output | 500+ peer-reviewed publications annually |
| Community-based research infrastructure | Research partnerships in 100+ communities |

### Organizational Transformation

| Milestone | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| Disability organization leadership diversity | Major national organizations reflect demographic diversity of disability community |
| Cross-movement coalition infrastructure | Sustained alliances between disability, racial justice, gender equity, LGBTQ+, and immigrant rights organizations |
| Intersectional policy platforms | All major disability organizations adopt explicit intersectional policy platforms |

### Milestones

- Intersectional Discrimination Recognition Act enacted: Q2 2031
- 25 states adopt intersectional anti-discrimination laws: Q4 2032
- All federally funded disability providers meet cultural responsiveness standards: Q4 2033
- Federal intersectional disability data infrastructure fully operational: Q2 2032
- Cross-movement coalition operating in all 50 states: Q4 2034

## Phase 4: Full Implementation and Accountability (2034-2036)

### Objective

Achieve measurable reductions in intersectional disability disparities, with self-sustaining accountability systems maintaining progress.

### Targets

| Metric | Target | Baseline (2024) |
|--------|--------|-----------------|
| Employment rate gap (Black PWD vs. white PWD) | <3 percentage points | 6 percentage points |
| Poverty rate gap (Black PWD vs. white PWD) | <5 percentage points | 11 percentage points |
| Healthcare access gap (Hispanic PWD vs. white PWD) | <5 percentage points on uninsured rate | 14 percentage points |
| Incarceration rate ratio (Black PWD vs. all PWD) | <1.5x | 2x |
| Violence rate gap (women with disabilities vs. women without) | <1.3x | 1.6x |
| Intersectional discrimination cases (EEOC) | 20%+ of all charges | <5% |
| Disability organizations with diverse leadership | 80%+ reflect community demographics | Estimated <30% |
| LGBTQ+ disability data availability | Standard in all federal surveys | Absent from most |

### Sustaining Systems

| System | Function |
|--------|----------|
| Intersectional data infrastructure | Annual publication of comprehensive intersectional disability data by all federal agencies |
| Legal accountability | Intersectional discrimination claims routinely litigated and adjudicated under federal and state law |
| Institutional accountability | Federally funded disability organizations required to demonstrate diversity in governance and outcomes |
| Research pipeline | Sustained federal funding for intersectional disability research; diverse researcher workforce |
| Cross-movement alliances | Standing coalitions maintaining political pressure for equity |

## Risk Assessment

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|-----------|
| Anti-DEI legislation blocks intersectional data collection | Medium | High | Frame as evidence-based disparities reduction, not DEI; build bipartisan support through fiscal efficiency arguments |
| Congressional legislation fails to pass | High | High | Prioritize executive action and agency guidance; build state-level alternatives |
| Organizational resistance to governance reform | Medium | Medium | Tie reforms to funding conditions; support internal advocates |
| Immigration enforcement politics derails service access reform | High | High | Advance through administrative guidance and state-level policy; build humanitarian case |
| Intersectional analysis dismissed as "identity politics" | Medium | Medium | Emphasize empirical evidence of compounded disadvantage; use universalist framing |
| Insufficient cross-movement buy-in | Medium | Medium | Fund convenings and joint projects; demonstrate shared policy interests |
| Research funding cuts | Medium | Medium | Build industry and foundation partnerships; embed intersectional requirements in existing research programs |
