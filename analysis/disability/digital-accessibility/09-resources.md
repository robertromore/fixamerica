# Digital Accessibility: Resources

## Overview

This document compiles key resources for understanding digital accessibility policy, law, technology, and advocacy. Resources are organized by type and relevance to support researchers, policymakers, advocates, and practitioners working to improve digital access for people with disabilities.

## Federal Law and Regulation

### Statutes

- **Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990** (42 U.S.C. 12101 et seq.) - Prohibits discrimination against people with disabilities in public accommodations (Title III) and government services (Title II). Foundational authority for digital accessibility requirements.
- **Rehabilitation Act of 1973, Section 508** (29 U.S.C. 794d) - Requires federal agencies to make information and communications technology accessible. Amended 1998 to include ICT standards.
- **Rehabilitation Act of 1973, Section 504** (29 U.S.C. 794) - Prohibits disability discrimination in federally funded programs, including digital services.
- **Twenty-First Century Communications and Video Accessibility Act** (P.L. 111-260, 2010) - Requires advanced communications services and video programming to be accessible.
- **Telecommunications Act of 1996, Section 255** (47 U.S.C. 255) - Requires telecommunications equipment and services to be accessible.

### Regulations

- **ADA Title II Web Accessibility Rule** (28 C.F.R. Part 35, 2024) - Requires state and local government web content and mobile apps to conform to WCAG 2.1 Level AA.
- **Section 508 Standards (ICT Final Rule)** (36 C.F.R. Part 1194, 2017) - Technical standards for federal ICT, incorporating WCAG 2.0 Level AA.
- **FCC Rules on Advanced Communications** (47 C.F.R. Part 14) - Accessibility requirements for VoIP, video conferencing, and messaging services.

## Key Court Decisions

- *National Federation of the Blind v. Target Corp.*, 452 F. Supp. 2d 946 (N.D. Cal. 2006) - Websites connected to physical stores are ADA Title III public accommodations.
- *Robles v. Domino's Pizza LLC*, 913 F.3d 898 (9th Cir. 2019) - ADA applies to websites and apps of public accommodations; DOJ regulations not a prerequisite.
- *Gil v. Winn-Dixie Stores Inc.*, 993 F.3d 1266 (11th Cir. 2021) - Website must have nexus to physical location; created circuit split.
- *Andrews v. Blick Art Materials LLC*, 268 F. Supp. 3d 381 (E.D.N.Y. 2017) - Standalone websites are places of public accommodation under ADA.
- *Langer v. Kohl's Department Stores*, No. 21-cv-01608 (C.D. Cal. 2022) - Mootness doctrine applied when defendant remediated during litigation.
- *Gomez v. General Nutrition Corp.*, 323 F. Supp. 3d 1368 (S.D. Fla. 2018) - ADA standing requires intent to return to or be deterred from website.
- *Olmstead v. L.C.*, 527 U.S. 581 (1999) - Foundational ADA case on integration mandate; applied to digital contexts.
