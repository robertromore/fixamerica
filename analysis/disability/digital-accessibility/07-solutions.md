# Digital Accessibility: Solutions

## Overview

Addressing digital inaccessibility requires a multi-layered approach spanning federal regulation, enforcement reform, education pipeline changes, industry standards, procurement leverage, and technology development. No single intervention will solve the problem; the persistent 95%+ noncompliance rate reflects systemic failures that require systemic solutions. The proposals below are organized from highest-impact structural reforms to targeted interventions, with each building on the others to create a self-reinforcing accessibility ecosystem.

## Regulatory Solutions

### 1. Finalize ADA Title III Web Accessibility Regulation

**Problem addressed**: No explicit federal regulation requires private-sector websites and applications to meet specific accessibility standards.

**Proposal**: DOJ should issue a final rule under ADA Title III establishing WCAG 2.1 Level AA (or the most current stable version) as the mandatory accessibility standard for websites and mobile applications of businesses that qualify as places of public accommodation.

**Key elements**:
- Apply to all businesses covered by ADA Title III with a website or mobile application
- Phase compliance timelines by business size (large businesses: 18 months; small businesses: 36 months)
- Establish safe harbor for organizations demonstrating good-faith compliance efforts, including conformance testing and remediation plans
- Require regular updates to reference the most current stable WCAG version
- Include accessibility statements and feedback mechanisms as required components

**Evidence**: The 2024 ADA Title II rule provides a template. The European Accessibility Act demonstrates private-sector regulation is workable. Every DOJ digital accessibility settlement since 2015 has referenced WCAG 2.1 AA or higher.

**Estimated impact**: Would provide clear compliance targets for all private-sector web entities, reducing litigation ambiguity and driving investment in accessibility.

### 2. Update Section 508 Standards to WCAG 2.2

**Problem addressed**: Current Section 508 standards reference WCAG 2.0 (2008), which is outdated and does not address mobile, cognitive, or authentication accessibility.

**Proposal**: The Access Board should update Section 508 technical standards to reference WCAG 2.2 Level AA and establish a regular 3-year update cycle.

**Key elements**:
- Adopt WCAG 2.2 Level AA as the technical standard for all federal ICT
- Require conformance testing for new WCAG 2.2 criteria (focus appearance, dragging movements, consistent help, redundant entry, accessible authentication)
- Establish a predictable update process tied to W3C standard releases
- Include emerging technology provisions for AI, IoT, and XR

**Evidence**: The current 7-year lag between WCAG 2.0 publication and Section 508 refresh was excessive. A 3-year cycle would keep federal standards current while providing adequate transition time.

### 3. Establish Digital Accessibility Regulatory Authority

**Problem addressed**: No single federal agency has comprehensive authority and resources for digital accessibility regulation and enforcement.

**Proposal**: Create a Digital Accessibility Division within DOJ's Civil Rights Division, or establish an independent Office of Digital Accessibility, with dedicated staff, budget, and enforcement authority.

**Key elements**:
- Dedicated team of 50+ attorneys, technologists, and investigators focused on digital accessibility
- Authority to conduct proactive compliance reviews (not just complaint-driven investigation)
- Technical testing capabilities for web, mobile, software, and emerging technologies
- Budget of $50 million annually (comparable to other specialized civil rights enforcement units)
- Public reporting of compliance data and enforcement actions

## Enforcement Solutions

### 4. Strengthen Private Right of Action

**Problem addressed**: Court decisions on standing, mootness, and damages have weakened private enforcement of digital accessibility rights.

**Proposal**: Amend the ADA to clarify that digital accessibility violations create standing for any person with a disability who encounters barriers, with statutory damages of $1,000-$10,000 per violation.

**Key elements**:
- Eliminate the nexus-to-physical-location requirement created by *Gil v. Winn-Dixie*
- Establish statutory damages for digital accessibility violations (currently unavailable under ADA Title III)
- Prevent mootness dismissals when defendants fix issues after suit is filed without ongoing monitoring
- Protect attorney fees for prevailing plaintiffs
- Create rebuttable presumption that WCAG conformance satisfies ADA requirements

### 5. Proactive Federal Enforcement

**Problem addressed**: Complaint-driven enforcement misses systemic violations and places the burden on disabled individuals to identify and report barriers.

**Proposal**: Federal agencies should conduct proactive compliance audits of government websites, federally funded entities, and high-impact private-sector services.

**Key elements**:
- Annual automated scanning of all federal government websites (approximately 12,000)
- Quarterly audits of the top 500 most-visited commercial websites
- Targeted reviews of healthcare, education, financial services, and government contractors
- Public accessibility scorecards with compliance timelines for noncompliant entities
- Escalating penalties for repeated noncompliance: warning, corrective action plan, financial penalties

### 6. Federal Procurement Enforcement

**Problem addressed**: 40% of federal IT procurements do not include adequate Section 508 requirements (GAO, 2023).

**Proposal**: Strengthen procurement enforcement through mandatory accessibility evaluation in all federal technology acquisitions.

**Key elements**:
- Require independently verified Accessibility Conformance Reports (ACRs) replacing self-reported VPATs
- Include accessibility as a scored evaluation factor in all IT acquisitions above $25,000
- Establish a federal Approved Accessibility Products List for commonly procured technology
- Require accessibility performance clauses in federal contracts with remediation requirements
- Train all federal contracting officers on Section 508 evaluation

**Estimated impact**: Federal IT spending exceeds $100 billion annually (FY 2024, OMB). Using this purchasing power to demand accessibility would transform the enterprise software market.

## Education Solutions

### 7. Integrate Accessibility into CS and Design Education

**Problem addressed**: Fewer than 3% of CS programs require accessibility coursework.

**Proposal**: Integrate accessibility as a required competency in computer science and design degree programs through accreditation standards and federal funding incentives.

**Key elements**:
- ABET accreditation criteria should include accessibility as a required outcome for CS and software engineering programs
- NSF and Department of Education grants should require accessibility components in funded curricula
- Create a national accessibility curriculum framework with course modules, assignments, and projects
- Fund faculty development programs in accessibility education
- Require accessibility portfolio items for CS degree completion

**Evidence**: Security education followed a similar path -- from optional specialization to required competency -- driven by industry demand and accreditation changes. Accessibility should follow the same trajectory.

### 8. Professional Development and Certification

**Problem addressed**: The accessibility workforce is approximately 6,500 certified professionals against demand exceeding 100,000 positions.

**Proposal**: Expand accessibility professional development through employer incentives, certification programs, and apprenticeships.

**Key elements**:
- Employer tax credits for accessibility training and IAAP certification (modeled on Section 44 small business credits)
- DOL apprenticeship programs for accessibility specialists
- GSA-hosted accessibility bootcamps for federal employees and contractors
- Continuing education requirements for accessibility professionals comparable to cybersecurity certifications
- Scholarship programs for disabled individuals pursuing accessibility careers

## Technology Solutions

### 9. Accessible-by-Default Platforms and Tools

**Problem addressed**: Content management systems, frameworks, and development tools often produce inaccessible output by default.

**Proposal**: Incentivize and eventually require that platforms producing web content include accessible defaults and accessibility checking tools.

**Key elements**:
- FTC guidance that marketing a website builder or CMS as producing "professional" or "compliant" websites when output is inaccessible constitutes deceptive marketing
- GSA partnership with major CMS providers to develop accessible default themes and templates
- Accessibility linting integration into major code editors and CI/CD pipelines
- Open-source accessibility testing tool development funded by NSF and NIST
- AI-assisted accessibility remediation tools with human review requirements

### 10. Improve Automated Testing

**Problem addressed**: Automated testing catches only 30-40% of WCAG issues, giving organizations false confidence in their compliance.

**Proposal**: Invest in next-generation automated testing that combines AI, heuristic analysis, and assistive technology simulation to increase detection coverage.

**Key elements**:
- NIST research program on automated accessibility evaluation ($10 million annually)
- Standardized accessibility testing benchmarks and tool certification
- Required combination of automated and manual testing for compliance claims
- Public accessibility testing infrastructure (free tools for small businesses and nonprofits)
- Standardized accessibility conformance reporting format

### 11. Accessible Emerging Technology Standards

**Problem addressed**: AI, XR, IoT, and other emerging technologies lack accessibility standards, creating new barriers.

**Proposal**: Fund accelerated standards development for emerging technology accessibility.

**Key elements**:
- W3C WAI funding increase for WCAG 3.0 and emerging technology guidelines ($5 million annually)
- NIST accessibility standards for AI systems, including bias testing for disability discrimination
- Access Board guidelines for IoT device accessibility
- FCC accessibility requirements for connected devices
- Mandatory accessibility evaluation in federal AI procurement and deployment

## Economic Solutions

### 12. Financial Incentives for Accessibility

**Problem addressed**: Businesses, especially small businesses, cite cost as a barrier to accessibility investment.

**Proposal**: Expand and create targeted financial incentives for digital accessibility.

**Key elements**:
- Increase Section 44 small business tax credit from $5,000 to $10,000 maximum annual expenditure for accessibility
- Create a new business tax deduction for accessibility auditing and remediation costs
- SBA-funded accessibility grants for small businesses ($1,000-$5,000 for initial assessment and basic remediation)
- State-level accessibility incentive programs modeled on energy efficiency programs
- Insurance premium reductions for businesses demonstrating accessibility compliance
