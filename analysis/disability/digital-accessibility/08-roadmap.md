# Digital Accessibility: Implementation Roadmap

## Strategic Approach

### Theory of Change

Digital accessibility reform requires a multi-front strategy that leverages executive action for quick wins, procurement power for market influence, education reform for long-term workforce change, and legislative action for permanent standards. The 2024 ADA Title II web accessibility rule establishes regulatory precedent; the task now is to extend that framework to the private sector, strengthen enforcement, and build the workforce necessary for sustained compliance.

### Strategic Pillars

| Pillar | Purpose | Key Actions |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| **Regulatory foundation** | Establish clear, enforceable standards | Title III rulemaking, Section 508 update |
| **Procurement leverage** | Use federal buying power to transform market | VPAT reform, accessibility scoring |
| **Education pipeline** | Build long-term workforce capacity | Curriculum reform, certification expansion |
| **Enforcement capacity** | Make noncompliance costly | Proactive audits, penalty escalation |
| **Industry partnership** | Engage willing companies as allies | Accessible defaults, platform partnerships |

### Political Windows

| Window | Opportunity |
|--------|-------------|
| **Title II rule implementation** | Compliance deadlines (2026-2027) create awareness and infrastructure |
| **European Accessibility Act** | EU compliance by June 2025 creates global pressure and proves feasibility |
| **Section 508 reauthorization discussions** | Opportunity to update standards and strengthen enforcement |
| **Federal budget cycle** | Annual appropriations for enforcement and NIST/NSF research |
| **ABET accreditation review** | CS program accreditation criteria update cycles |

## Phase 1: Foundation and Executive Action (2026-2028)

### Objective

Leverage existing authority to establish enforcement infrastructure, update standards, and begin education reform without new legislation.

### Executive Actions

| Action | Authority | Timeline | Lead Agency |
|--------|----------|----------|-------------|
| Initiate ADA Title III web accessibility rulemaking | ADA enforcement authority | Q1 2026 | DOJ |
| Update Section 508 to reference WCAG 2.2 | Rehabilitation Act | Q2 2026 | Access Board |
| Issue executive order on federal digital accessibility | Presidential authority | Q1 2026 | White House |
| Reform VPAT process to require independent verification | Procurement authority | Q3 2026 | GSA |
| Launch federal accessibility scorecard program | OMB oversight authority | Q4 2026 | OMB/GSA |

### Procurement Reforms

| Reform | Implementation |
|--------|---------------|
| Require accessibility as scored evaluation factor | OMB memorandum to CFO Act agencies |
| Establish accessibility performance clauses | FAR clause development |
| Launch Approved Accessibility Products List | GSA pilot with top 100 enterprise products |
| Train contracting officers on 508 evaluation | GSA Academy curriculum addition |

### Education Initiatives

| Initiative | Implementation |
|-----------|---------------|
| NSF accessibility curriculum grants | $10 million in grant funding for 20 pilot programs |
| ABET engagement on accessibility criteria | Formal petition from disability rights and industry coalition |
| GSA accessibility bootcamp program | Quarterly training for federal employees (500/year) |
| IAAP certification scholarship program | 500 scholarships annually ($1,500 each) |

### Enforcement Actions

| Action | Implementation |
|--------|---------------|
| Automated scanning of all federal websites | Deploy GSA scanning infrastructure |
| Quarterly compliance reports by agency | OMB reporting requirement |
| DOJ proactive investigations of top 50 most-visited commercial websites | Civil Rights Division initiative |
| Public accessibility scorecard launch | Data dashboard with agency scores |

### Milestones

- ADA Title II web accessibility rule compliance deadline (large entities): April 2026
- ADA Title III NPRM published: Q4 2026
- Section 508 updated to WCAG 2.2: Q2 2027
- Federal website compliance reaches 60% (from 30%): Q4 2027
- VPAT reform finalized: Q1 2028
- First 20 CS programs add required accessibility coursework: Q4 2028

## Phase 2: Legislative and Standards Reform (2028-2030)

### Objective

Secure legislative changes that establish permanent digital accessibility requirements, strengthen enforcement tools, and fund education and workforce development.

### Federal Legislation

| Bill | Purpose | Coalition |
|------|---------|-----------|
| Digital Accessibility Act | Codify WCAG compliance requirement for Title III entities | Disability rights + large tech + centrist business |
| Section 508 Modernization Act | Mandate regular standards updates, increase enforcement authority | Bipartisan federal modernization coalition |
| Accessible Technology Education Act | Fund accessibility in CS education, create scholarship programs | Education + tech + disability coalition |
| ADA Digital Rights Restoration Act | Establish statutory damages, clarify standing for digital claims | Disability rights + plaintiff's bar |

### Standards Development

| Standard | Action | Timeline |
|----------|--------|----------|
| WCAG 3.0 | Support W3C completion with $5 million federal funding | Target 2029 finalization |
| AI accessibility | NIST guidelines for accessible AI systems | 2028-2029 |
| IoT accessibility | Access Board guidelines for connected devices | 2028-2030 |
| XR accessibility | W3C XR accessibility guidelines | 2029-2030 |

### Workforce Development

| Initiative | Scale |
|-----------|-------|
| Accessibility apprenticeship program (DOL) | 1,000 apprenticeships annually |
| Community college accessibility certificate programs | 50 programs nationwide |
| Federal accessibility internship program | 200 positions annually |
| Industry accessibility mentorship network | 500 mentor-mentee pairs |

### Milestones

- ADA Title III web accessibility rule finalized: Q2 2028
- Digital Accessibility Act introduced in Congress: Q1 2029
- 50 CS programs require accessibility coursework: Q4 2029
- IAAP-certified professionals reach 20,000: Q4 2029
- NIST AI accessibility guidelines published: Q2 2029
- Federal website compliance reaches 80%: Q4 2030

## Phase 3: Systemic Integration (2030-2033)

### Objective

Embed accessibility as a standard practice in technology development, procurement, and education, creating self-sustaining systems that do not depend solely on enforcement.

### Industry Transformation

| Initiative | Mechanism |
|-----------|-----------|
| Accessibility quality certification for software | Industry-led certification with federal recognition |
| Accessible CMS/framework certification | FTC-backed certification for platforms meeting accessible-default standards |
| Corporate accessibility reporting | SEC disclosure requirements for large public companies |
| Supply chain accessibility requirements | Federal contractor flow-down requirements |

### Education Integration

| Milestone | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| ABET accreditation includes accessibility | All accredited CS programs |
| Accessibility in K-12 coding education | State standards frameworks |
| Design school accessibility requirements | NASAD accreditation criteria |
| Continuing education requirements | State licensing boards |

### Enforcement Maturation

| Development | Status |
|------------|--------|
| Digital Accessibility Division fully staffed | 50+ attorneys and technologists |
| Proactive compliance review program | 500+ reviews annually |
| State-level digital accessibility enforcement | 25+ states with active programs |
| International enforcement cooperation | EU-US accessibility data sharing |

### Milestones

- Digital Accessibility Act enacted: Q1 2031
- 100 CS programs require accessibility: Q4 2031
- Private-sector website compliance reaches 50%: Q4 2032
- WCAG 3.0 adopted as federal standard: Q2 2032
- Accessibility professional workforce reaches 50,000: Q4 2033

## Phase 4: Full Implementation (2033-2036)

### Objective

Achieve digital accessibility as the default condition for American digital infrastructure, with compliance rates exceeding 80% and self-sustaining systems maintaining progress.

### Targets

| Metric | Target | Baseline (2024) |
|--------|--------|-----------------|
| Top 1 million homepages with WCAG compliance | 80%+ | 4.1% |
| Federal website compliance | 98%+ | 30% |
| CS programs with required accessibility | 90%+ | <3% |
| IAAP-certified professionals | 100,000+ | 6,500 |
| Fortune 500 with dedicated accessibility teams | 80%+ | 28% |
| Small business website accessibility | 60%+ | <10% |

### Sustaining Systems

| System | Function |
|--------|----------|
| Regular standards update cycle | 3-year Access Board review cycle |
| Continuous federal monitoring | Automated scanning with manual review |
| Industry self-regulation backstop | Certification programs with enforcement consequences |
| Education pipeline maintenance | Accreditation requirements sustain curriculum |
| International harmonization | Ongoing alignment with EU, UK, Canada standards |

## Risk Assessment

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|-----------|
| Title III rulemaking blocked by industry lobbying | Medium | Critical | Build bipartisan support; leverage EU precedent |
| Congressional legislation fails to pass | Medium | High | Focus on executive action and procurement leverage |
| Workforce development lags demand | High | Medium | Multiple pathways: bootcamps, certificates, degrees |
| Standards development too slow for emerging tech | High | Medium | Interim guidance documents; performance-based standards |
| Political administration change derails executive actions | Medium | High | Prioritize legislative codification; build state-level programs |
| Technology evolution outpaces compliance | Medium | Medium | Flexible, outcome-based standards; regular update cycles |
