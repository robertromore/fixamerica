## Key Reports and Publications

### Foundational Documents

- Budapest Open Access Initiative. "Read the Budapest Open Access Initiative." February 14, 2002. <https://www.budapestopenaccessinitiative.org/read/>
- Suber, Peter. *Open Access.* Cambridge, MA: MIT Press, 2012.
- Willinsky, John. *The Access Principle: The Case for Open Access to Research and Scholarship.* Cambridge, MA: MIT Press, 2006.

### Policy Analysis

- National Academies of Sciences, Engineering, and Medicine. *Open Science by Design: Realizing a Vision for 21st Century Research.* Washington, DC: National Academies Press, 2018.
- Tennant, Jonathan P., et al. "The Academic, Economic and Societal Impacts of Open Access: An Evidence-Based Review." *F1000Research* 5 (2016): 632.
- Piwowar, Heather, et al. "The State of OA: A Large-Scale Analysis of the Prevalence and Impact of Open Access Articles." *PeerJ* 6 (2018): e4375.

### Data and Reproducibility

- Wilkinson, Mark D., et al. "The FAIR Guiding Principles for Scientific Data Management and Stewardship." *Scientific Data* 3 (2016): 160018.
- Open Science Collaboration. "Estimating the Reproducibility of Psychological Science." *Science* 349, no. 6251 (2015): aac4716.
- Ioannidis, John P.A. "Why Most Published Research Findings Are False." *PLoS Medicine* 2, no. 8 (2005): e124.

### Publishing Economics

- Buranyi, Stephen. "Is the Staggeringly Profitable Business of Scientific Publishing Bad for Science?" *The Guardian*, June 27, 2017.
- Lariviere, Vincent, Stefanie Haustein, and Philippe Mongeon. "The Oligopoly of Academic Publishers in the Digital Era." *PLoS ONE* 10, no. 6 (2015): e0127502.
- Schimmer, Ralf, Kai Karin Geschuhn, and Andreas Vogler. "Disrupting the Subscription Journals' Business Model for the Necessary Large-Scale Transformation to Open Access." Max Planck Digital Library, 2015.

### Recent Analyses

- Brainard, Jeffrey. "White House Requires Immediate Free Access to All U.S.-Funded Research Papers by 2025." *Science*, August 25, 2022.
- Else, Holly. "Open-Access Plan S to Allow High-Cost Publishing Routes." *Nature* 624 (2023): 16-17.

## Legal Resources

- Consolidated Appropriations Act of 2008, Division G, Title II, Section 218 (NIH Public Access Policy statutory basis)
- 17 U.S.C. sections 101-122 (Copyright Act provisions relevant to scholarly works)
- *Authors Guild v. Google*, 804 F.3d 202 (2d Cir. 2015) (fair use and text mining)
- Creative Commons licenses, particularly CC-BY 4.0: <https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/4.0/>
