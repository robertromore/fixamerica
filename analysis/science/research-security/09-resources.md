# Research Security: Resources

## Overview

This document provides a curated collection of resources for understanding research security policy, including government documents, academic research, advocacy organization publications, news coverage, and legal materials. Resources are organized by type and include brief descriptions of relevance.

## Government Documents and Policy

### Presidential Directives

- **NSDD-189** (1985): *National Policy on the Transfer of Scientific, Technical, and Engineering Information*. Establishes the fundamental research exclusion -- the principle that basic research results should remain unrestricted unless classified. Reaffirmed by subsequent administrations.
- **NSPM-33** (2021): *National Security Presidential Memorandum on United States Government-Supported Research and Development National Security Policy*. Establishes standardized disclosure requirements and research security framework. Implementation guidance published by OSTP in January 2022 and updated February 2023.
- **Presidential Proclamation 10043** (2020): Suspends entry of certain Chinese students and researchers associated with military-civil fusion entities.

### OSTP Guidance

- OSTP. *Guidance for Implementing National Security Presidential Memorandum 33 (NSPM-33) on National Security Strategy for United States Government-Supported Research and Development*. January 2022. Updated February 2023.
- OSTP. *Research Security Programs at Covered Institutions: Guidance for Agencies*. August 2024.

## Legal Resources

### Key Court Cases

- *United States v. Xi* (E.D. Pa. 2015): Charges against Temple University physicist Xiaoxing Xi dropped after the government's own expert acknowledged the device was misidentified.
- *United States v. Chen* (D. Mass. 2021-2022): Charges against MIT Professor Gang Chen dropped when DOJ acknowledged the disclosure forms did not require the information alleged as omitted.
- *United States v. Hu* (E.D. Tenn. 2020): University of Tennessee professor Hu Anming acquitted on all counts; jury found insufficient evidence of intent to defraud.
- *United States v. Lieber* (D. Mass. 2021): Harvard professor Charles Lieber convicted of false statements and tax offenses related to concealing Wuhan University of Technology payments.
- *United States v. Lee* (D.N.M. 1999-2000): Los Alamos scientist Wen Ho Lee -- 58 of 59 charges dropped; judge apologized for government's treatment of defendant.
