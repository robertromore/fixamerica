# Diversity in STEM: Resources

## Government Reports and Data

- **National Science Foundation, National Center for Science and Engineering Statistics (NCSES). "Diversity and STEM: Women, Minorities, and Persons with Disabilities in Science and Engineering."** Published biennially. The most comprehensive federal data source on STEM demographics, covering education, employment, and career outcomes by gender, race/ethnicity, disability status, and field. Available at: <https://ncses.nsf.gov/pubs/nsf23315/>
- **National Science Foundation. "Survey of Earned Doctorates."** Published annually. Reports demographic data on all doctoral degree recipients from U.S. institutions, including field of study, funding sources, and post-graduation plans. Available at: <https://ncses.nsf.gov/surveys/earned-doctorates/>
- **National Science Foundation. "Higher Education Research and Development (HERD) Survey."** Published annually. Reports R&D expenditures at U.S. institutions of higher education by source of funding, field, and institution. Available at: <https://ncses.nsf.gov/surveys/higher-education-research-development/>
- **National Institutes of Health. "Data Book."** Published annually. Reports data on NIH funding, applications, awards, and workforce demographics. Available at: <https://report.nih.gov/nihdatabook/>
- **National Academies of Sciences, Engineering, and Medicine. *Sexual Harassment of Women in Academic Science, Engineering, and Medicine.* Washington, DC: The National Academies Press, 2018.** Landmark report documenting the prevalence, causes, and consequences of sexual harassment in academic STEM. Available at: <https://nap.nationalacademies.org/catalog/24994/>
- **National Academies of Sciences, Engineering, and Medicine. *Beyond Bias and Barriers: Fulfilling the Potential of Women in Academic Science and Engineering.* Washington, DC: The National Academies Press, 2007.** Comprehensive analysis of structural barriers to women's advancement in academic STEM.
- **Congressional Research Service. "Broadening Participation in STEM: Overview of Federal Programs."** Updated periodically. Summary of federal programs aimed at increasing diversity in STEM, including authorization, appropriations, and program descriptions.
- **Government Accountability Office. "Women in STEM Research: Better Data and Information Sharing Could Improve Oversight of Federal Grant-Making." GAO-16-14, 2015.** Analysis of gender disparities in federal research funding and recommendations for improved data collection.

## Peer-Reviewed Research

### Funding Disparities

- Ginther, Donna K., Walter T. Schaffer, Joshua Schnell, Beth Masimore, Faye Liu, Laurel L. Haak, and Raynard Kington. "Race, Ethnicity, and NIH Research Awards." *Science* 333, no. 6045 (2011): 1015-1019. Landmark study documenting the racial gap in NIH R01 funding.
- Hoppe, Travis A., Aviva Litovitz, Kristine A. Willis, Rebecca A. Meseroll, Matthew J. Perkins, B. Ian Hutchins, Alison F. Davis, Michael S. Lauer, Hannah A. Valantine, James M. Anderson, and George M. Santangelo. "Topic Choice Contributes to the Lower Rate of NIH Awards to African-American/Black Scientists." *Science Advances* 5, no. 10 (2019): eaaw7238. Follow-up study showing that topic choice partly mediates the racial funding gap.

### Hiring and Evaluation Bias

- Moss-Racusin, Corinne A., John F. Dovidio, Victoria L. Brescoll, Mark J. Graham, and Jo Handelsman. "Science Faculty's Subtle Gender Biases Favor Male Students." *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences* 109, no. 41 (2012): 16474-16479. Randomized experiment demonstrating gender bias in faculty evaluation of student applicants.
- Eaton, Asia A., Jessica F. Saunders, Ryan K. Jacobson, and Keon West. "How Gender and Race Stereotypes Impact the Advancement of Scholars in STEM." *Sex Roles* 82 (2020): 127-141. Review of research on how gender and race stereotypes affect hiring, promotion, and evaluation in STEM.
- Milkman, Katherine L., Modupe Akinola, and Dolly Chugh. "What Happens Before? A Field Experiment Exploring How Pay and Representation Differentially Shape Bias on the Pathway Into Organizations." *Journal of Applied Psychology* 100, no. 6 (2015): 1678-1712. Field experiment showing that faculty are less responsive to emails from women and minority prospective students.

### Harassment and Climate

- Clancy, Kathryn B. H., Robin G. Nelson, Julienne N. Rutherford, and Katie Hinde. "Survey of Academic Field Experiences (SAFE): Trainees Report Harassment and Assault." *PLOS ONE* 9, no. 7 (2014): e102172. Study documenting high rates of harassment during scientific fieldwork.
- Cech, Erin A., and Tom J. Waidzunas. "Systemic Inequalities for LGBTQ Professionals in STEM." *Science Advances* 7, no. 3 (2021): eabe0933. Survey documenting exclusion, harassment, and career effects for LGBTQ+ STEM professionals.
- Johnson, P. A., S. E. Widnall, and F. F. Benya, eds. *Sexual Harassment of Women in Academic Science, Engineering, and Medicine.* Washington, DC: National Academies Press, 2018.

### Pipeline and Attrition

- Miller, Casey W., and Keivan G. Stassun. "A Test That Fails." *Nature* 510, no. 7504 (2014): 303-304. Analysis showing that GRE scores do not predict doctoral completion or publication success.
- Langin, Katie. "A Wave of Graduate Programs Drops the GRE Application Requirement." *Science*, May 29, 2019.
- Estrada, Mica, Myra Burnett, Andrew G. Campbell, Patricia B. Campbell, Wilfred F. Denetclaw, Carlos G. Gutierrez, Sylvia Hurtado, et al. "Improving Underrepresented Minority Student Persistence in STEM." *CBE -- Life Sciences Education* 15, no. 3 (2016): es5. Review of evidence-based practices for improving persistence.

### Diversity and Scientific Innovation

- Freeman, Richard B., and Wei Huang. "Collaborating with People Like Me: Ethnic Coauthorship within the United States." *Journal of Labor Economics* 33, no. S1 (2015): S289-S318. Study showing that ethnically diverse research teams produce higher-impact publications.
- AlShebli, Bedoor K., Talal Rahwan, and Wei Lee Woon. "The Preeminence of Ethnic Diversity in Scientific Collaboration." *Nature Communications* 9 (2018): 5163. Analysis demonstrating that ethnically diverse research teams produce more cited and more novel work.
- Hong, Lu, and Scott E. Page. "Groups of Diverse Problem Solvers Can Outperform Groups of High-Ability Problem Solvers." *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences* 101, no. 46 (2004): 16385-16389.

### Stereotype Threat and Belonging

- Steele, Claude M., and Joshua Aronson. "Stereotype Threat and the Intellectual Test Performance of African Americans." *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology* 69, no. 5 (1995): 797-811. Foundational study on stereotype threat.
- Walton, Gregory M., and Geoffrey L. Cohen. "A Brief Social-Belonging Intervention Improves Academic and Health Outcomes of Minority Students." *Science* 331, no. 6023 (2011): 1447-1451.
