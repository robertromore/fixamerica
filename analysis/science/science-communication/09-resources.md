# Science Communication: Resources

## Overview

This document provides a curated collection of resources for further research on science communication, including academic literature, reports, organizations, data sources, and media. Resources are organized by category and annotated with relevance to the analysis in this section.

## Key Books

### Foundational Texts

- Oreskes, Naomi, and Erik M. Conway. *Merchants of Doubt: How a Handful of Scientists Obscured the Truth on Issues from Tobacco Smoke to Global Warming*. New York: Bloomsbury Press, 2010.
    - Essential reading on the history and strategy of organized science denial; documents how industry-funded campaigns created public doubt about settled science.

- Kahan, Dan M. "Fixing the Communications Failure." *Nature* 463 (2010): 296-297.
    - Landmark article demonstrating that the deficit model of science communication is empirically wrong; introduces the cultural cognition framework.

- Scheufele, Dietram A., and Nicole M. Krause. "Science Audiences, Misinformation, and Fake News." *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences* 116, no. 16 (2019): 7662-7669.
    - Comprehensive analysis of how misinformation operates in science contexts and why traditional corrections fail.

- Jamieson, Kathleen Hall, and Joseph N. Cappella. *Echo Chamber: Rush Limbaugh and the Conservative Media Establishment*. Oxford: Oxford University Press, 2008.
    - Examines how partisan media ecosystems distort scientific information and create alternative information environments.

- Nisbet, Matthew C. "Communicating Climate Change: Why Frames Matter for Public Engagement." *Environment: Science and Policy for Sustainable Development* 51, no. 2 (2009): 12-23.
    - Influential work on values-based framing in science communication; demonstrates that connecting science to audience values improves reception.

### Recent and Emerging Works

- van der Linden, Sander. *Foolproof: Why Misinformation Infects Our Minds and How to Build Immunity*. New York: W.W. Norton, 2023.
    - Presents the inoculation/prebunking approach to misinformation resistance, with emphasis on science communication.

- Oreskes, Naomi. *Why Trust Science?* Princeton: Princeton University Press, 2019.
    - Philosophical examination of why scientific consensus deserves public trust, addressing both legitimate skepticism and manufactured doubt.

- Brossard, Dominique, and Dietram A. Scheufele, eds. *The Oxford Handbook of the Science of Science Communication*. Oxford: Oxford University Press, 2024.
    - Comprehensive academic reference covering all dimensions of science communication research.

- Caulfield, Timothy. *Relax: A Guide to Everyday Health Decisions with More Facts and Less Worry*. Toronto: Allen Lane, 2020.
    - Accessible demonstration of evidence-based health communication in an environment of health anxiety and misinformation.

## Key Reports and Policy Documents

### Government Reports

- National Academies of Sciences, Engineering, and Medicine. *Communicating Science Effectively: A Research Agenda*. Washington, DC: National Academies Press, 2017.
    - Comprehensive review of the science of science communication; identifies research priorities.
    - <https://doi.org/10.17226/23674>

- National Academies of Sciences, Engineering, and Medicine. *Understanding and Addressing Misinformation about Science*. Washington, DC: National Academies Press, 2024.
    - Current assessment of the misinformation landscape and evidence-based countermeasures.

- National Science Board. *Science and Engineering Indicators 2024*. Alexandria, VA: National Science Foundation, 2024.
    - Chapter 7 covers public attitudes toward science and technology, scientific literacy, and media consumption.
    - <https://ncses.nsf.gov/indicators>

- Government Accountability Office. *Science and Technology: Agencies Need to Strengthen Policies for Managing Public Communications*. GAO-23-105540. Washington, DC: GAO, 2023.
    - Analysis of federal agency science communication policies and gaps.

### Foundation and Nonprofit Reports

- Pew Research Center. "Americans' Trust in Scientists, Other Groups Declines." Washington, DC: Pew Research Center, 2024.
    - Most current comprehensive survey of public trust in science; tracks trends and demographic variation.
    - <https://www.pewresearch.org/science/>

- Wellcome Trust. *Wellcome Global Monitor 2024*. London: Wellcome Trust, 2024.
    - International comparative data on public attitudes toward science across 140+ countries.
    - <https://wellcome.org/reports/wellcome-global-monitor>

- Reuters Institute for the Study of Journalism. *Digital News Report 2025*. Oxford: Reuters Institute, 2025.
    - Annual report on global news consumption patterns, including science and health news.
    - <https://reutersinstitute.politics.ox.ac.uk/digital-news-report/>

- Center for Countering Digital Hate. *The Disinformation Dozen*. Washington, DC: CCDH, 2021.
    - Identifies the 12 individuals responsible for producing the majority of anti-vaccine misinformation on social media.
    - <https://counterhate.com/research/>

- Knight Foundation and Gallup. *American Views 2024: Trust, Media and Democracy*. Miami: Knight Foundation, 2024.
    - Survey data on media trust including science and health information channels.

## Relevant Legislation

### Existing Law

- 42 U.S.C. Chapter 79---Science and Technology Policy, Organization and Priorities (establishes OSTP)
- 42 U.S.C. Section 1862 (NSF broader impacts requirement)
- 5 U.S.C. Section 2302(b)(8)---Whistleblower protections (general, applicable to scientists)
- OSTP Memorandum on Scientific Integrity (2021)

### Proposed Legislation

- Scientific Integrity Act (introduced in 117th, 118th Congress)---protections for government scientists
- PRESS Act (Protect Reporters from Exploitative State Spying)---shield law for journalists
- Local Journalism Sustainability Act---tax credits for journalism employment

### Relevant Court Cases

- *Garcetti v. Ceballos*, 547 U.S. 410 (2006)---limited First Amendment protection for government employee speech pursuant to official duties
- *Packingham v. North Carolina*, 582 U.S. 98 (2017)---social media as protected forum for speech
- *NetChoice v. Paxton*, 598 U.S. ___ (2024)---platform content moderation and First Amendment
