# STEM Workforce: Resources

## Key Reports and Studies

### Government Reports

- **NIH Biomedical Research Workforce Working Group Report (2012)**. Shirley Tilghman, chair. Landmark analysis of the unsustainability of the biomedical workforce pyramid. Recommended reducing PhD enrollment and improving postdoc conditions. <https://acd.od.nih.gov/documents/reports/Biomedical_research_wgreport.pdf>

- **National Academies. *The Postdoctoral Experience Revisited* (2014)**. Comprehensive review of postdoctoral training, compensation, and career outcomes. Recommends five-year time limits, salary increases, and employee classification. <https://nap.nationalacademies.org/catalog/18982/the-postdoctoral-experience-revisited>

- **National Academies. *Graduate STEM Education for the 21st Century* (2018)**. Recommends restructuring doctoral education to prepare students for diverse careers. <https://nap.nationalacademies.org/catalog/25038/graduate-stem-education-for-the-21st-century>

- **NSF Survey of Earned Doctorates (annual)**. The primary data source on PhD production, demographics, and initial career plans. <https://ncses.nsf.gov/surveys/earned-doctorates/>

- **NSF Survey of Doctorate Recipients (biennial)**. Longitudinal survey tracking career outcomes of PhD holders. <https://ncses.nsf.gov/surveys/doctorate-recipients/>

- **NSF Science and Engineering Indicators (biennial)**. Comprehensive data on the U.S. science and engineering enterprise, including workforce statistics. <https://ncses.nsf.gov/indicators>

- **Congressional Research Service. *The H-1B Visa Program* (updated regularly)**. Nonpartisan overview of H-1B policy, statistics, and reform proposals. <https://crsreports.congress.gov>

### Academic Research

- Stephan, Paula. *How Economics Shapes Science*. Harvard University Press, 2012. Essential analysis of the economic forces driving the academic labor market.

- Teitelbaum, Michael. *Falling Behind? Boom, Bust, and the Global Race for Scientific Talent*. Princeton University Press, 2014. Documents the recurring cycle of shortage claims and overproduction in STEM.

- Cyranoski, David, et al. "The PhD Factory." *Nature* 472 (2011): 276-279. Influential overview of PhD overproduction globally.

- Sauermann, Henry and Michael Roach. "Why Pursue the Postdoc Path?" *Science* 352, no. 6286 (2016): 663-664. Analyzes postdoc career expectations versus outcomes.

- Ginther, Donna K., et al. "Race, Ethnicity, and NIH Research Awards." *Science* 333, no. 6045 (2011): 1015-1019. Documents racial disparities in NIH grant success rates.

- Garrison, Heather H., et al. "Closing the Gap: STEM Education and Career Pathways." *Federation of American Societies for Experimental Biology (FASEB) Journal* (2022). Workforce pipeline analysis.

### Industry and Advocacy Reports

- **National Postdoctoral Association. *Institutional Policy Report* (2024)**. Survey of postdoc policies, salaries, and benefits across U.S. institutions. <https://www.nationalpostdoc.org>

- **AAUP. *Annual Report on the Economic Status of the Profession* (annual)**. Data on faculty salaries, tenure status, and contingent employment. <https://www.aaup.org/our-work/research>

- **Coalition for Next Generation Life Sciences. Career Outcomes Data**. Voluntary career outcome reporting by participating institutions. <https://nglscoalition.org>

- **FWD.us. STEM Immigration Reports**. Policy analysis on STEM visa and green card reform. <https://www.fwd.us>
