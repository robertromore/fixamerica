# Research Ethics: Resources

## Overview

This document provides key references and further reading for understanding research ethics policy in the United States. Resources are organized by category and include primary legal sources, government reports, academic literature, and advocacy materials.

---

## Primary Legal Sources

### Federal Statutes

- **National Research Act of 1974** (P.L. 93-348): Established the National Commission and mandated IRBs. <https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/STATUTE-88/pdf/STATUTE-88-Pg342.pdf>
- **Animal Welfare Act** (7 U.S.C. 2131-2159): Federal regulation of animal research. <https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/USCODE-2023-title7/pdf/USCODE-2023-title7-chap54.pdf>
- **Health Research Extension Act of 1985** (P.L. 99-158): Mandated IACUCs and PHS Policy compliance. <https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/STATUTE-99/pdf/STATUTE-99-Pg820.pdf>
- **National AI Initiative Act of 2020** (P.L. 116-283, Division E): Established AI coordination mechanisms. <https://www.congress.gov/bill/116th-congress/house-bill/6216>
- **21st Century Cures Act** (P.L. 114-255): Included provisions for clinical trial modernization and informed consent. <https://www.congress.gov/bill/114th-congress/house-bill/34>

### Federal Regulations

- **Common Rule** (45 C.F.R. Part 46): Human subjects research protections. <https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-45/subtitle-A/subchapter-A/part-46>
- **FDA Human Subject Protections** (21 C.F.R. Parts 50, 56): FDA regulations for clinical trials. <https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-21/chapter-I/subchapter-A/part-50>
- **Animal Welfare Act Regulations** (9 C.F.R. Parts 1-4): USDA implementing regulations. <https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-9/chapter-I/subchapter-A>

### Federal Policy Documents

- **The Belmont Report** (1979): Ethical principles for human subjects research. <https://www.hhs.gov/ohrp/regulations-and-policy/belmont-report/index.html>
- **PHS Policy on Humane Care and Use of Laboratory Animals** (2015 edition): <https://olaw.nih.gov/policies-laws/phs-policy.htm>
- **United States Government Policy for Oversight of Dual Use Research of Concern** (2024 update): <https://www.whitehouse.gov/ostp/news-updates/>
- **NIST AI Risk Management Framework** (AI 100-1, 2023): <https://www.nist.gov/artificial-intelligence/risk-management-framework>
- **OMB Memorandum M-24-10** (Advancing Governance, Innovation, and Risk Management for Agency Use of Artificial Intelligence): <https://www.whitehouse.gov/omb/briefing-room/>

## Key Court Cases

- *Grimes v. Kennedy Krieger Institute*, 782 A.2d 807 (Md. 2001): Maryland Court of Appeals ruled that parents cannot consent to enroll children in nontherapeutic research that poses risk of harm
- *Moore v. Regents of the University of California*, 51 Cal. 3d 120 (1990): California Supreme Court addressed patient rights over biological materials used in research
- *Havasupai Tribe v. Arizona Board of Regents* (2010, settled): Landmark case regarding tribal consent and secondary use of biological specimens collected for one purpose and used for another without consent
- *Washington University v. Catalona*, 490 F.3d 667 (8th Cir. 2007): Research participants' rights over donated biological materials
- *Suthers v. Amgen*, 441 F. Supp. 2d 478 (S.D.N.Y. 2006): Standing of research participants to challenge ethical violations

## Recommended Reading Order

For readers new to research ethics policy:

1. The Belmont Report (foundational principles)
2. Reverby, *Examining Tuskegee* (historical context)
3. Klitzman, *The Ethics Police?* (IRB system analysis)
4. Beauchamp and Childress, *Principles of Biomedical Ethics* (ethical framework)
5. Casadevall and Imperiale, "Risks and Benefits of Gain-of-Function Experiments" (DURC overview)
6. Metcalf and Crawford, "Where Are Human Subjects in Big Data Research?" (digital research ethics)
