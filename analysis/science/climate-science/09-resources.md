# Climate Science: Resources

## Key Reports and Assessments

### Federal Government Reports

- **Fifth National Climate Assessment (NCA5)**, U.S. Global Change Research Program, November 2023. <https://nca2023.globalchange.gov/>
    - The most comprehensive assessment of climate change impacts, risks, and adaptation options across the United States
- **IPCC Sixth Assessment Report (AR6)**, Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change, 2021-2023. <https://www.ipcc.ch/assessment-report/ar6/>
    - The authoritative international assessment of climate science, impacts, and mitigation options
- **State of the Climate Report**, NOAA National Centers for Environmental Information, annual. <https://www.ncei.noaa.gov/monitoring>
    - Annual summary of global and U.S. climate conditions, trends, and extremes
- **NASA Global Climate Change: Vital Signs of the Planet**. <https://climate.nasa.gov/>
    - Real-time climate data, visualizations, and explanations of key climate indicators
- **U.S. Global Change Research Program Annual Reports**. <https://www.globalchange.gov/>
    - Documentation of federally funded climate research activities and findings

### Congressional and Oversight Reports

- Congressional Research Service. "Federal Climate Change Funding." R47655. 2024.
- Government Accountability Office. "Climate Change: Information on Potential Economic Effects Could Help Guide Federal Efforts to Reduce Fiscal Exposure." GAO-17-720. 2017.
- Government Accountability Office. "Polar Weather Satellites: NOAA Is Working to Ensure Continued Data Collection." GAO-16-773. 2016.
- National Academies of Sciences, Engineering, and Medicine. *A Strategy for the Future of the U.S. Global Change Research Program*. 2017.
- Inspector General Reports on Scientific Integrity at EPA, NOAA, and NASA (various years).

### Scientific Integrity Reports

- Union of Concerned Scientists. *Sidelining Science Since Day One*. January 2018.
- Union of Concerned Scientists. *The State of Science in the Trump Era*. 2019.
- Government Accountability Project. Reports on federal whistleblower cases involving climate scientists (various years).
- National Academy of Sciences. *Fostering Integrity in Research*. 2017.

## Legal References

### Key Statutes

- Global Change Research Act of 1990, 15 U.S.C. 2921-2961 (mandates National Climate Assessment and USGCRP coordination)
- National Climate Program Act, 15 U.S.C. 2901-2908 (establishes national climate program)
- National Aeronautics and Space Act of 1958, as amended, 51 U.S.C. 20102 (NASA's mission including Earth science)
- Whistleblower Protection Enhancement Act of 2012, 5 U.S.C. 2302
- Information Quality Act (Section 515 of P.L. 106-554)

### Key Court Cases

- *Massachusetts v. EPA*, 549 U.S. 497 (2007) -- Supreme Court held that greenhouse gases are air pollutants under the Clean Air Act
- *West Virginia v. EPA*, 597 U.S. 697 (2022) -- Supreme Court limited EPA's ability to regulate power plant emissions under the Clean Air Act's "major questions doctrine"
