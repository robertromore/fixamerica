# Emerging Technology Research: Roadmap

## Overview

This roadmap sequences reforms across three phases, prioritizing actions that build political momentum, address the most urgent gaps, and create institutional foundations for longer-term change. Each phase includes legislative, executive, and institutional actions.

## Phase 1: Foundation (Years 1-2)

### Priority 1.1: Establish National Emerging Technology Coordination Office

**Actions:**

- Draft and introduce legislation creating NETCO with bipartisan sponsors
- Secure Commerce Committee and Armed Services Committee jurisdiction agreements
- President nominates Director; Senate confirmation process
- Initial staff recruitment from OSTP, NSC, and agency details
- Commission first National Emerging Technology Strategy assessment

**Milestones:**

| Milestone | Target Date | Success Metric |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| Legislation introduced | Month 3 | Bipartisan co-sponsors from both chambers |
| Hearings completed | Month 6 | Testimony from agencies, academia, industry |
| Legislation enacted | Month 12 | Signed into law |
| Director confirmed | Month 18 | Senate confirmation vote |
| First strategy published | Month 24 | Interagency-coordinated document |

### Priority 1.2: Close Most Urgent CHIPS Act Funding Gaps

**Actions:**

- Introduce supplemental appropriations for NSF TIP Directorate ($3.3B)
- Fund NIST quantum and semiconductor research programs ($900M)
- Appropriate Regional Technology Hub first-round full funding ($1.9B)
- Attach to annual appropriations or defense authorization bill

**Milestones:**

| Milestone | Target Date | Success Metric |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| Appropriations request submitted | Month 2 | OMB submission |
| Committee markup | Month 6 | Favorable committee vote |
| Floor vote | Month 10 | Passage in both chambers |
| Funds disbursed | Month 14 | Agency obligation of funds |

### Priority 1.3: Executive Actions on Research Governance

Actions requiring no legislation:

- **OSTP directive** on dual-use risk assessment procedures for federally funded emerging technology research
- **OMB guidance** requiring agencies to include emerging technology coordination in budget submissions
- **Commerce Department** acceleration of CHIPS R&D program implementation
- **NSF** expansion of NAIRR pilot scope and partnerships
- **NIH/HHS** strengthening of gain-of-function oversight and IBC inspection protocols
- **NIST** publication of post-quantum cryptography migration guidance for federal agencies

**Milestones:**

| Milestone | Target Date | Success Metric |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| OSTP dual-use directive | Month 3 | Published and disseminated |
| OMB coordination guidance | Month 6 | Incorporated in budget process |
| NAIRR expansion | Month 8 | 100+ institutions participating |
| PQC migration timeline | Month 12 | Federal agency deadlines set |

### Priority 1.4: Begin Workforce Pipeline Investments

**Actions:**

- Launch semiconductor workforce training programs at community colleges near new fab sites
- Expand NSF REU program by 25% in first year
- Initiate interagency review of federal STEM compensation scales
- Begin processing of visa reform legislation

**Milestones:**

| Milestone | Target Date | Success Metric |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|

| REU expansion | Month 9 | 25% increase in positions funded |

## Risk Management

### Political Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Administration change reverses executive actions | High | High | Embed reforms in statute, not EOs |
| Appropriations sequestration | Medium | High | Multi-year advance appropriations |
| Industry lobbying derails regulation | High | Medium | Build bipartisan coalitions, cite competitiveness |
| China crisis accelerates security-first approach | Medium | Medium | Pre-position safety governance as competitiveness tool |

### Implementation Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| NETCO becomes bureaucratic bottleneck | Medium | High | Sunset provision, lean staffing mandate |
| NAIRR compute allocation becomes politicized | Low | Medium | Independent governance board, merit-based allocation |
| Dual-use oversight creates compliance burden | Medium | Medium | Tiered approach, small-entity exemptions |
| Workforce programs fail to attract participants | Low | Medium | Competitive compensation, loan forgiveness |

### Technology Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| AI capability advances outpace governance | High | High | Adaptive mechanisms, regular review cycles |
| Quantum computing timeline differs from projections | Medium | Low | Technology-neutral framing where possible |
| Biosafety incident during reform process | Low | Very High | Accelerate Phase 2 dual-use oversight |
| Semiconductor supply chain disruption (Taiwan) | Medium | Very High | Accelerate domestic manufacturing timeline |

## Success Metrics

### Short-Term (Years 1-2)

- NETCO established and operational
- CHIPS Act R&D funding gap reduced by 50%
- NAIRR participation tripled from pilot levels
- Executive actions on dual-use oversight implemented

### Medium-Term (Years 2-4)

- Comprehensive dual-use oversight legislation enacted
- Full NAIRR authorization and initial deployment
- Technology transfer processing times reduced by 75%
- International cooperation agreements with 5+ allies

### Long-Term (Years 4-7)

- CHIPS Act fully appropriated
- Semiconductor workforce gap reduced by 50%
- Dual-use oversight demonstrably effective without chilling research
- US maintains or improves competitive position in all critical technology domains
