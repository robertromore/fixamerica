# Science Advice: Resources

## Overview

This document provides key references, further reading, and data sources for understanding the science advice landscape in the United States. Resources are organized by type and topic area.

## Primary Legal Sources

### Statutes

- **National Science and Technology Policy, Organization, and Priorities Act of 1976** (P.L. 94-282; 42 U.S.C. ch. 79) -- Establishes OSTP and defines its mission
- **Technology Assessment Act of 1972** (P.L. 92-484; 2 U.S.C. ch. 34) -- Created OTA; still technically law though unfunded since 1995
- **Federal Advisory Committee Act** (FACA; 5 U.S.C. App. 2) -- Governs formation and operation of federal advisory committees
- **Foundations for Evidence-Based Policymaking Act of 2018** (P.L. 115-435) -- Requires evaluation officers, chief data officers, learning agendas, and open data
- **America COMPETES Act** (P.L. 110-69, 2007; reauthorized 2010) -- Authorizes science and technology programs across agencies
- **CHIPS and Science Act** (P.L. 117-167, 2022) -- Major science authorization including provisions affecting OSTP and interagency coordination

### Executive Orders and Presidential Memoranda

- **Executive Order 13845** (2018) -- Established the National Council for the American Worker (Trump administration approach to workforce)
- **Presidential Memorandum on Scientific Integrity** (March 9, 2009) -- Obama administration directive on evidence in policymaking
- **Executive Order 14007** (2021) -- Biden administration executive order on scientific integrity and evidence-based policymaking
- **OSTP Scientific Integrity Framework** (January 2023) -- Government-wide framework for scientific integrity policies

### Key Court Cases

- *Daubert v. Merrell Dow Pharmaceuticals*, 509 U.S. 579 (1993) -- Established standards for scientific evidence in federal courts; influenced regulatory science standards
- *Motor Vehicle Manufacturers Association v. State Farm*, 463 U.S. 29 (1983) -- Requires agencies to explain departures from prior evidence-based policies

## Books

### Essential Reading

- Pielke, Roger A. Jr. *The Honest Broker: Making Sense of Science in Policy and Politics*. Cambridge University Press, 2007. -- Foundational framework for understanding science advisory roles
- Jasanoff, Sheila. *The Fifth Branch: Science Advisers as Policymakers*. Harvard University Press, 1990. -- Classic analysis of advisory committee politics
- Golden, William T., ed. *Science and Technology Advice to the President, Congress, and Judiciary*. 2nd ed. Transaction Publishers, 1995. -- Comprehensive collection of perspectives from former advisors
- Kevles, Daniel J. *The Physicists: The History of a Scientific Community in Modern America*. Harvard University Press, 1995. -- History of science-government relations from World War I through the Cold War

- Smith, Bruce L.R. *The Advisers: Scientists in the Policy Process*. Brookings Institution Press, 1992.

## Organizations

### Government

- **OSTP** -- Office of Science and Technology Policy
- **NSTC** -- National Science and Technology Council (interagency coordination body chaired by the president)
- **PCAST** -- President's Council of Advisors on Science and Technology
- **CRS** -- Congressional Research Service
- **GAO STAI** -- Government Accountability Office Science, Technology Assessment, and Innovation team
- **OMB Evidence Team** -- Office of Management and Budget team overseeing Evidence Act implementation

### Independent/Quasi-Governmental

- **National Academies of Sciences, Engineering, and Medicine** -- Congressionally chartered advisory body
- **STPI** -- Science and Technology Policy Institute (IDA FFRDC supporting OSTP)
- **JASON** -- Independent scientific advisory group, primarily serving DOD

### Advocacy and Research

- **Union of Concerned Scientists** -- Science advocacy; scientific integrity monitoring
- **AAAS** -- American Association for the Advancement of Science; operates the Science and Technology Policy Fellowship program
- **Federation of American Scientists** -- Science policy advocacy; maintains OTA archive
- **Research!America** -- Advocacy for research investment
- **Bipartisan Policy Center** -- Governance reform including science advisory capacity
- **Day One Project** -- Science policy ideas for incoming administrations
- **INGSA** -- International Network for Government Science Advice; comparative perspective
