# Science Policy: Resources

## Overview

This document compiles key resources for understanding American science policy, including foundational reports, data sources, organizations, and further reading. Resources are organized by type for easy reference.

---

## Foundational Reports and Analyses

### Government and Quasi-Government Reports

| Report | Author | Year | Significance |
|--------|--------|------|--------------|
| *Science: The Endless Frontier* | Vannevar Bush | 1945 | Foundational document of U.S. science policy |
| *Rising Above the Gathering Storm* | National Academies | 2005 | Catalyzed the America COMPETES Act |
| *Rising Above the Gathering Storm, Revisited* | National Academies | 2010 | Found competitiveness situation had worsened |
| *Restoring the Foundation* | AAAS | 2014 | Comprehensive assessment of the research enterprise |
| *The Perils of Complacency* | Information Technology and Innovation Foundation | 2020 | U.S.-China competition in advanced technology |
| *Scientific Integrity Framework* | OSTP | 2023 | Government-wide scientific integrity standards |
| *Nelson Memo on Public Access* | OSTP | 2022 | Zero-embargo open access for federally funded research |
| *NSF Science & Engineering Indicators* | NSF/NCSES | Biennial | Comprehensive data on the U.S. science and engineering enterprise |

### AAAS R&D Budget Analyses

The American Association for the Advancement of Science (AAAS) produces the most comprehensive annual analysis of federal R&D spending, available at <https://www.aaas.org/programs/r-d-budget-and-policy>.

Key annual publications:

- *AAAS Report: Research & Development FY [Year]*
- *Historical Trends in Federal R&D*
- *Agency-by-agency budget analysis*
- *Intersociety Working Group R&D Data*

## Key Books

### Foundational Texts

- Bush, Vannevar. *Science: The Endless Frontier*. Washington, DC: U.S. Government Printing Office, 1945.
- Stokes, Donald E. *Pasteur's Quadrant: Basic Science and Technological Innovation*. Washington, DC: Brookings Institution Press, 1997.
- Kevles, Daniel J. *The Physicists: The History of a Scientific Community in Modern America*. Cambridge, MA: Harvard University Press, 1995.

### Contemporary Analysis

- Mervis, Jeffrey. *The Science of Science Policy: A Handbook*. Stanford, CA: Stanford University Press, 2018.
- Stephan, Paula. *How Economics Shapes Science*. Cambridge, MA: Harvard University Press, 2012.
- Teitelbaum, Michael S. *Falling Behind? Boom, Bust, and the Global Race for Scientific Talent*. Princeton, NJ: Princeton University Press, 2014.
- Oreskes, Naomi, and Erik M. Conway. *Merchants of Doubt: How a Handful of Scientists Obscured the Truth on Issues from Tobacco Smoke to Global Warming*. New York: Bloomsbury Press, 2010.
- Pielke, Roger A. *The Honest Broker: Making Sense of Science in Policy and Politics*. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press, 2007.
- Sarewitz, Daniel. *Frontiers of Illusion: Science, Technology, and the Politics of Progress*. Philadelphia: Temple University Press, 1996.

### Research Integrity and Open Science

- Ritchie, Stuart. *Science Fictions: How Fraud, Bias, Negligence, and Hype Undermine the Search for Truth*. New York: Metropolitan Books, 2020.
- Harris, Richard. *Rigor Mortis: How Sloppy Science Creates Worthless Cures, Crushes Hope, and Wastes Billions*. New York: Basic Books, 2017.
- Suber, Peter. *Open Access*. Cambridge, MA: MIT Press, 2012.

### Science and Politics

- Mooney, Chris. *The Republican War on Science*. New York: Basic Books, 2005.
- Pielke, Roger A. *The Honest Broker: Making Sense of Science in Policy and Politics*. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press, 2007.
- Jasanoff, Sheila. *The Fifth Branch: Science Advisers as Policymakers*. Cambridge, MA: Harvard University Press, 1990.
