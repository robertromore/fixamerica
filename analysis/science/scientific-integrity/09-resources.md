## Independent Monitoring and Advocacy Organizations

### Retraction Watch

- Website: <https://retractionwatch.com/>
- Retraction Watch Database: <https://retractiondatabase.org/>
- Founded by Ivan Oransky and Adam Marcus (2010)
- The most comprehensive independent source for tracking retractions, integrity investigations, and misconduct news

### Committee on Publication Ethics (COPE)

- Website: <https://publicationethics.org/>
- COPE provides guidelines to journals on handling research and publication misconduct
- Issues flowcharts for handling specific integrity situations
- Membership includes over 12,000 journals

### PubPeer

- Website: <https://pubpeer.com/>
- Platform for anonymous post-publication review and discussion
- Key venue for raising concerns about published research
- Has been instrumental in identifying problematic papers that led to formal investigations

### Center for Open Science

- Website: <https://www.cos.io/>
- Promotes open science practices including preregistration, data sharing, and reproducibility
- Operates the Open Science Framework (OSF)
- Developed the TOP Guidelines (Transparency and Openness Promotion)

### For Better Science

- Website: <https://forbetterscience.com/>
- Investigative journalism on research misconduct by Leonid Schneider
- Covers integrity cases internationally
- Provides detailed analysis of institutional responses to misconduct

### Research Integrity Alliance

- Coalition of organizations and individuals advocating for research integrity reform
- Coordinates advocacy for stronger federal oversight and whistleblower protections

## Legal Resources

### Federal Statutes and Regulations

- 42 C.F.R. Part 93: Public Health Service Policies on Research Misconduct
- 42 U.S.C. Section 289b: Misconduct provisions of the Public Health Service Act
- 31 U.S.C. Sections 3729-3733: False Claims Act (applicable to research grant fraud)
- 18 U.S.C. Section 1001: False statements to federal agencies
- 5 U.S.C. Section 2302: Whistleblower protections for federal employees

### Key Court Cases

- *United States ex rel. Berman-McSweeney v. Tremont Research Institute*, No. 4:09-cv-2423 (N.D. Ohio) -- False Claims Act in research context
- *United States v. Poehlman*, No. 2:05-cr-38 (D. Vt. 2005) -- First criminal conviction of a researcher for misconduct
- *Imanishi-Kari v. United States*, HHS DAB No. 1582 (1996) -- Due process in ORI proceedings
- *Gino v. Simonsohn et al.* (D. Mass. 2023) -- Defamation claims against misconduct accusers

### Model Legislation

- Whistleblower Protection Enhancement Act of 2012 (5 U.S.C. Section 2302 amendments) -- Model for research-specific protections
- State anti-SLAPP statutes (California Code of Civil Procedure Section 425.16; Texas Civil Practice and Remedies Code Section 27) -- Models for anti-SLAPP provisions
- European Union Directive 2019/1937 on Whistleblower Protection -- International model
