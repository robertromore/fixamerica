# Conservation: Opposition

## Overview

Opposition to conservation comes from industries seeking resource access, ideological opponents of federal land management, and some local interests concerned about economic impacts. Understanding opposition arguments and tactics enables more effective advocacy.

| Industry | Opposition Focus |
|----------|------------------|

## Opposition Arguments Examined

### Economic Arguments

| Claim | Counter-Evidence |
|-------|------------------|
| **Job losses** | Recreation creates more jobs |
| **Economic harm** | Conservation brings benefits |
| **Resource needs** | Alternatives exist |
| **Local impact** | Tourism economy |

### Management Arguments

| Claim | Counter |
|-------|---------|
| **State better** | Less funding, capacity |
| **Private efficient** | Public goods not served |
| **Local knowledge** | Federal science resources |
| **Multiple use** | Already law |

### ESA Arguments

| Claim | Counter |
|-------|---------|
| **Low recovery rate** | Underfunding, delay |
| **Process problems** | Political interference |
