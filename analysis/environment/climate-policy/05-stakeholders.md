# Climate Policy: Stakeholders

## Overview

Climate policy involves diverse stakeholders with different interests, resources, and levels of influence. Understanding these stakeholders is essential for effective advocacy and coalition building.

## Government Stakeholders

### Federal Executive

| Agency | Role | Position |
|--------|------|----------|
| **White House** | Policy coordination | Strong climate action |
| **EPA** | Environmental regulation | Aggressive rulemaking |
| **DOE** | Energy policy, research | Clean energy push |
| **DOI** | Public lands | Balancing development/conservation |
| **USDA** | Agriculture, forestry | Climate-smart programs |
| **DOT** | Transportation | EV, transit focus |
| **Treasury** | Tax policy, climate finance | IRA implementation |
| **State** | International diplomacy | Climate engagement |

### Congress

| Group | Position |
|-------|----------|
| **Democratic leadership** | Strong climate support |
| **Progressive Democrats** | Push for more action |
| **Moderate Democrats** | Balance economy/climate |
| **Republican leadership** | Generally opposed |
| **Some moderate Republicans** | Selective support |

### State Governments

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Climate leaders** | California, New York, Washington |
| **Climate laggards** | Texas, West Virginia |
| **Swing states** | Pennsylvania, Michigan |

## Industry Stakeholders

### Fossil Fuel Industry

| Actor | Position | Influence |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| **ExxonMobil** | Gradual transition | Major lobbying |
| **Chevron** | Similar | Political donations |
| **Coal companies** | Opposition | Declining influence |
| **Natural gas** | Bridge fuel narrative | Significant influence |
| **API** | Industry coordination | Major lobbying |

**Interests:**

- Protect existing assets
- Slow transition timeline
- Favorable regulatory treatment
- Access to public lands
- Export markets

### Clean Energy Industry

| Actor | Position | Influence |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| **Solar companies** | Strong climate action | Growing |
| **Wind developers** | Strong climate action | Significant |
| **EV manufacturers** | Accelerate transition | Major (Tesla) |
| **Battery companies** | Expand incentives | Growing |
| **ACORE, SEIA, ACP** | Industry associations | Significant |

**Interests:**

- Extend tax credits
- Streamline permitting
- Grid investment
- Manufacturing support
- Workforce development

## Coalition Dynamics

### Pro-Climate Coalition

| Member | Contribution |
|--------|--------------|
| **Environmental groups** | Advocacy, expertise |
| **Clean energy industry** | Economic argument |
| **Youth** | Moral urgency |
| **Public health** | Health framing |
| **Some businesses** | Corporate support |
| **Cities** | Implementation |

**Strengths:** Broad support, growing economic power
**Weaknesses:** Internal tensions (pace, approach)

### Anti-Climate Coalition

| Member | Contribution |
|--------|--------------|
| **Fossil fuel industry** | Resources, lobbying |
| **Some manufacturers** | Competitiveness concerns |
| **Fossil fuel workers** | Transition fears |
| **Conservative groups** | Ideological opposition |

**Strengths:** Concentrated resources, political access
**Weaknesses:** Shrinking public support, economic headwinds
