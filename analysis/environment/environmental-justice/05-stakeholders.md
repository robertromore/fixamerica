# Environmental Justice: Stakeholders

## Overview

Environmental justice involves diverse stakeholders with varying interests, resources, and influence. Affected communities seek protection and meaningful participation, while industry often resists additional requirements. Government agencies face competing pressures, and various organizations work across the spectrum. Understanding stakeholder dynamics is essential for building effective coalitions and addressing opposition.

## Affected Communities

| Community | Primary Concerns |
|-----------|------------------|
| **Low-income neighborhoods** | Multiple pollution sources, limited resources |
| **Black communities** | Air pollution, lead, toxic facilities |

| **Native American communities** | Extraction, contamination, sacred sites |
| **Asian American/Pacific Islander** | Regional industrial impacts, language access |
| **Immigrant communities** | Fear of reporting, workplace exposure |
| **Rural poor** | Extraction industries, agricultural chemicals |

## Industry Stakeholders

### Facility Operators

| Sector | Position |
|--------|----------|
| **Petrochemical** | Resist additional requirements |
| **Manufacturing** | Concerned about costs, permitting |
| **Waste management** | Oppose siting restrictions |
| **Energy production** | Variable depending on fuel |
| **Mining** | Resist liability expansion |

### Industry Associations

| Association | Position |
|-------------|----------|
| **American Chemistry Council** | Opposes cumulative impact rules |
| **U.S. Chamber of Commerce** | General regulatory opposition |
| **American Petroleum Institute** | Defends refinery operations |
| **National Mining Association** | Resists additional requirements |
| **Waste industry groups** | Oppose facility restrictions |

### Industry Arguments

| Argument | Claim |
|----------|-------|
| **Jobs** | Facilities provide employment |
| **Economics** | Additional costs unaffordable |
| **Science** | Cumulative impacts uncertain |
| **Precedent** | Slippery slope concerns |
| **Competitiveness** | Will lose to less regulated areas |
