# Environmental Justice: Overview

## Executive Summary

Environmental justice addresses the disproportionate environmental burdens borne by low-income communities and communities of color. These populations face higher exposure to pollution, toxic facilities, and climate impacts while receiving fewer environmental protections and benefits. Despite decades of executive orders and advocacy, environmental racism persists across virtually every metricâ€”from air quality to hazardous waste siting to flood vulnerability. Achieving environmental justice requires fundamental changes to permitting, enforcement, investment, and decision-making processes.

## Scope

This analysis covers:

- **Distributional justice**: Unequal environmental burdens
- **Procedural justice**: Meaningful participation in decisions
- **Recognition justice**: Acknowledging affected communities
- **Restorative justice**: Remedying past harms
- **Climate justice**: Equitable climate impacts and solutions
- **Cumulative impacts**: Multiple, overlapping burdens
- **Federal, state, and local dimensions**

## Path Forward

Achieving environmental justice requires:

1. **Cumulative impact requirements** in permitting
2. **Enforcement equity** with measurable goals
3. **Community participation** in all decisions
4. **Targeted investment** in overburdened areas
5. **Data collection** on disparities
6. **Legal tools** for affected communities
7. **Climate justice** integration
8. **Remediation** of historical harms
