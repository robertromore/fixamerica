# Clean Energy: Stakeholders

## Overview

The clean energy transition involves diverse stakeholders with different interests, resources, and influence. Understanding these dynamics is essential for building coalitions and navigating opposition.

## Government Stakeholders

### Federal Agencies

| Agency | Role | Position |
|--------|------|----------|
| **DOE** | Research, loans, policy | Strong clean energy push |
| **EPA** | Environmental regulation | Emissions rules |
| **FERC** | Grid, markets | Transmission, markets |
| **Treasury** | Tax credits | IRA implementation |
| **Interior** | Public lands | Offshore, federal lands |
| **Agriculture** | Rural energy | Rural programs |

| Group | Position |

## Coalition Dynamics

### Pro-Clean Energy Coalition

| Member | Contribution |
|--------|--------------|
| **Clean energy industry** | Economic argument, lobbying |
| **Environmental groups** | Public pressure, expertise |
| **Tech companies** | Buyer power |
| **Labor (some)** | Jobs narrative |
| **Farmers** | Rural support |
| **Cities** | Demand, implementation |

**Strengths:** Economic momentum, public support, technology
**Weaknesses:** Geographic concentration, internal tensions

| Member | Concern |
|--------|---------|
