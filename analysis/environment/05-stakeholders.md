# Environment: Stakeholders

## Who Is Harmed

### Environmental Justice Communities

Communities bearing disproportionate environmental burdens:

| Community Type | Environmental Burden | Examples |
|----------------|---------------------|----------|
| **Fenceline communities** | Adjacent to polluting facilities | Cancer Alley (LA), Richmond (CA) |
| **Indigenous communities** | Extraction, contamination, sacred sites | Navajo Nation (uranium), Standing Rock |
| **Black communities** | Industrial siting, lead, air pollution | Flint, South Side Chicago |
| **Latino communities** | Pesticides, industrial pollution | Central Valley farmworkers |
| **Low-income areas** | Multiple pollution sources | South Bronx, East L.A. |

**Health Disparities**

| Disparity | Scale |
|-----------|-------|
| Asthma rates | 40% higher in minority communities |
| Proximity to hazardous waste | 1.5x more likely for Black communities |
| Pesticide exposure | Farmworkers face 39x higher exposure |
| Lead poisoning | 5x higher in Black children |

### Future Generations

| Harm | Mechanism |
|------|-----------|
| **Climate debt** | Current emissions impose future costs |
| **Stranded assets** | Fossil fuel infrastructure worthless |
| **Ecosystem loss** | Biodiversity not recoverable |
| **Resource depletion** | Water, soil, minerals exhausted |
| **Adaptation costs** | Trillions in infrastructure changes |

### Children

Children are especially vulnerable:

| Impact | Evidence |
|--------|----------|
| **Developmental harm** | Lead, pesticides, endocrine disruptors |
| **Respiratory illness** | Air pollution, asthma |
| **Climate anxiety** | Mental health impacts |
| **Heat vulnerability** | School, playground exposure |
| **Future impacts** | Will live longest with consequences |

### Wildlife and Ecosystems

| Impact | Scale |

### Global South

| U.S. Impact | Harm |
