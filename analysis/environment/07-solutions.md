# Environment: Solutions

## Guiding Principles

### Core Values

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Science-based** | Policy follows scientific evidence |
| **Precautionary** | Act on risks before certain harm |
| **Polluter pays** | Costs borne by those who cause harm |
| **Environmental justice** | Fair distribution of burdens and benefits |
| **Intergenerational equity** | Protect future generations |
| **Public trust** | Government protects common resources |
| **Transparency** | Open information about environmental conditions |

### Criteria for Evaluation

| Criterion | Question |
|-----------|----------|
| **Effectiveness** | Will it achieve environmental goals? |
| **Equity** | Who bears costs and benefits? |
| **Efficiency** | Does it minimize costs for given benefit? |
| **Enforceability** | Can it be implemented and monitored? |
| **Durability** | Will it survive political changes? |
| **Scalability** | Can it work at necessary scale? |
| **Co-benefits** | What additional gains (jobs, health)? |

## Policy Proposals: Climate

### Carbon Pricing

**Option 1: Carbon Tax**

| Element | Design |
|---------|--------|
| **Starting price** | $50/ton COâ‚‚, rising $10/year |
| **Coverage** | All fossil fuels at point of production/import |
| **Revenue use** | Dividend to households, transition support |
| **Border adjustment** | Tax on carbon-intensive imports |
| **Exemptions** | None (transparent pricing) |

**Option 2: Cap-and-Trade**

| Element | Design |
|---------|--------|
| **Cap trajectory** | Decline to net-zero by 2050 |
| **Allocation** | Auction permits; no free allocation |
| **Trading** | Market-based permit trading |
| **Price floor/ceiling** | Stability mechanisms |
| **Revenue use** | Transition investment, dividends |

**Comparison**

| Factor | Carbon Tax | Cap-and-Trade |
|--------|------------|---------------|
| Price certainty | Yes | No |
| Emissions certainty | No | Yes |
| Administrative simplicity | Higher | Lower |
| Political feasibility | Lower (tax) | Higher (cap) |
| U.S. precedent | None federal | RGGI, California |

## Alternative Approaches

### Market-Based

| Approach | Assessment |
|----------|------------|
| **Carbon tax only** | Necessary but not sufficient |
| **Cap-and-trade only** | Needs complementary policies |
| **Voluntary corporate action** | Insufficient without mandates |
| **Innovation investment only** | Takes too long without deployment policy |

**Verdict**: Market mechanisms valuable but must be part of comprehensive approach with standards, investment, and equity provisions.

### Command-and-Control

| Approach | Assessment |
|----------|------------|
| **Technology mandates** | Effective but may not be cost-optimal |
| **Emission standards** | Clear, enforceable |
| **Bans (e.g., coal phase-out)** | Provides certainty |
| **Performance standards** | Flexibility within requirements |

**Verdict**: Standards provide certainty and enforce minimums; combine with market flexibility.

### Public Investment

| Approach | Assessment |
|----------|------------|
| **Green New Deal** | Comprehensive but politically challenging |
| **Infrastructure investment** | Broad support; insufficient alone |
| **Federal green bank** | Leverage private capital |
| **Public ownership** | Some clean energy could be public |

**Verdict**: Significant public investment necessary to drive transition at scale.

### Litigation-Led

| Approach | Assessment |
|----------|------------|
| **State AG suits** | Promising, slow, uncertain |
| **Constitutional claims** | Important but not sufficient |
| **Private nuisance** | Accountability mechanism |
| **Securities enforcement** | Disclosure and accountability |

**Verdict**: Litigation important for accountability; cannot substitute for policy.

### Recommended Comprehensive Approach

Combine all mechanisms:

1. **Carbon pricing** to correct market failure
2. **Standards** to ensure minimums
3. **Public investment** to accelerate transition
4. **Environmental justice** at center of all policies
5. **Litigation** for accountability
6. **Political reform** to enable action

## Trade-offs

### Economic Transition

| Trade-off | Approach |
|-----------|----------|
| **Fossil fuel job losses** | Just transition programs, place-based investment |
| **Stranded assets** | Managed decline, compensation debates |
| **Consumer costs** | Rebates, efficiency investments, protect low-income |
| **Industrial competitiveness** | Border adjustments, domestic manufacturing support |

### Speed vs. Justice

| Tension | Resolution |
|---------|------------|
| **Fastest path may not be fairest** | Build EJ into policy design |
| **Community input takes time** | Front-load engagement, don't skip |
| **Resources to overburdened communities** | Justice40, targeted investment |

### Local vs. Federal

| Tension | Resolution |
|---------|------------|
| **Federal standards vs. local autonomy** | Floor not ceiling; allow stronger state/local action |
| **Permitting speed vs. environmental review** | Categorical exclusions for proven clean tech |
| **Rural vs. urban priorities** | Place-based policy, resource equity |

### Technology Choices

| Choice | Consideration |
|--------|---------------|
| **Nuclear** | Zero-carbon but waste, cost, proliferation concerns |
| **Natural gas bridge** | Lower than coal but still emissions, locks in infrastructure |
| **Carbon capture** | May extend fossil fuel use; unproven at scale |
| **Biofuels** | Land use, food competition concerns |

### Scale vs. Participation

| Tension | Resolution |
|---------|------------|
| **Large-scale projects vs. local control** | Community benefit agreements |
| **Utility-scale vs. distributed** | Portfolio approach |
| **Efficiency vs. democratic input** | Meaningful participation, not just notification |
