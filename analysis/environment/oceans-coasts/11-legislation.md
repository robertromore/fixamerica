# Oceans and Coasts: Legislation and Legal Framework

## Overview

Protecting oceans and coasts requires comprehensive legislation addressing marine conservation, sustainable fisheries, pollution reduction, and coastal resilience. This document provides draft legislation for key reforms at federal and state levels.

## Federal Legislation

### Ocean Conservation and Climate Act

**Purpose**: Establish comprehensive ocean protection and climate adaptation.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Ocean Conservation and
Climate Act".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds that—
    (1) ocean health is declining due to climate change,
    pollution, and overexploitation;
    (2) coastal communities face increasing threats;
    (3) marine conservation is essential;
    (4) climate adaptation is urgent; and
    (5) comprehensive action is necessary.

SEC. 3. 30X30 OCEAN CONSERVATION.

(a) GOAL.—It is the goal of the United States to
conserve at least 30 percent of ocean waters under
U.S. jurisdiction by 2030.

(b) CONSERVATION PLAN.—The Secretary shall develop
a national ocean conservation plan to achieve this goal.

(c) PRIORITY AREAS.—The plan shall identify priority
areas based on—
    (1) biodiversity value;
    (2) ecosystem services;
    (3) climate resilience;
    (4) connectivity; and
    (5) stakeholder input.

(d) PROTECTION LEVELS.—Protected areas shall include
strong protections restricting extractive activities.

SEC. 4. NATIONAL MARINE SANCTUARY EXPANSION.

(a) NEW DESIGNATIONS.—The Secretary shall consider
designation of additional national marine sanctuaries.

(b) EXPEDITED PROCESS.—The Secretary shall establish
an expedited designation process.

(c) MANAGEMENT.—Sanctuary management shall address
climate change impacts.

SEC. 5. COASTAL RESILIENCE.

(a) PROGRAM.—There is established a National Coastal
Resilience Program.

(b) FUNDING.—$2 billion annually is authorized for
coastal resilience grants.

(c) PRIORITIES.—Funding shall prioritize—
    (1) nature-based solutions;
    (2) vulnerable communities;
    (3) critical infrastructure;
    (4) climate adaptation; and
    (5) environmental justice communities.

SEC. 6. BLUE CARBON.

(a) PROGRAM.—The Secretary shall establish a blue
carbon program to protect and restore coastal ecosystems.

(b) INVENTORY.—The Secretary shall inventory coastal
blue carbon resources.

(c) PROTECTION.—Blue carbon ecosystems shall be
prioritized for protection.

(d) RESTORATION.—$500 million annually is authorized
for coastal ecosystem restoration.

SEC. 7. OCEAN ACIDIFICATION.

(a) RESEARCH.—Ocean acidification research shall be
expanded.

(b) MONITORING.—A national ocean acidification
monitoring network shall be established.

(c) ADAPTATION.—Strategies for affected fisheries
and ecosystems shall be developed.

SEC. 8. CLIMATE-READY FISHERIES.

(a) REQUIREMENT.—Fishery management plans shall
incorporate climate change.

(b) ADAPTIVE MANAGEMENT.—Plans shall include adaptive
management for shifting stocks.

(c) RESEARCH.—Research on climate impacts on fisheries
shall be expanded.

SEC. 9. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act takes effect upon enactment.
```

### Marine Plastic Pollution Prevention Act

**Purpose**: Reduce marine plastic pollution.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Marine Plastic Pollution
Prevention Act".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds that—
    (1) plastic pollution threatens marine ecosystems;
    (2) millions of tons enter oceans annually;
    (3) source reduction is essential;
    (4) international action is needed; and
    (5) the U.S. should lead.

SEC. 3. SOURCE REDUCTION.

(a) PLASTIC REDUCTION TARGETS.—The Administrator
shall establish targets for reducing plastic pollution.

(b) SINGLE-USE RESTRICTIONS.—The Administrator shall
identify single-use plastics for restriction.

(c) PRODUCER RESPONSIBILITY.—Extended producer
responsibility for plastic packaging shall be
established.

SEC. 4. MARINE DEBRIS PROGRAM.

(a) ENHANCEMENT.—The NOAA Marine Debris Program shall
be enhanced.

(b) FUNDING.—$100 million annually is authorized.

(c) PRIORITIES.—The program shall prioritize—
    (1) removal of existing debris;
    (2) prevention at source;
    (3) research; and
    (4) international cooperation.

SEC. 5. INTERNATIONAL ACTION.

(a) TREATY.—The United States shall support a strong
international treaty on plastic pollution.

(b) TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE.—The United States shall
provide technical assistance to other nations.

SEC. 6. RESEARCH.

(a) MICROPLASTICS.—Research on microplastic impacts
shall be expanded.

(b) ALTERNATIVES.—Research on plastic alternatives
shall be supported.

SEC. 7. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act takes effect upon enactment.
```

### Magnuson-Stevens Act Reauthorization

**Purpose**: Update fisheries management for sustainability and climate.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Magnuson-Stevens
Fishery Conservation and Management Reauthorization
Act of [Year]".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds that—
    (1) sustainable fisheries require continued
    science-based management;
    (2) climate change affects fish stocks;
    (3) ecosystem-based approaches are needed; and
    (4) fishing communities need support.

SEC. 3. CLIMATE ADAPTATION.

(a) REQUIREMENT.—Fishery management plans shall
address climate change impacts.

(b) SHIFTING STOCKS.—Management shall accommodate
shifting species distributions.

(c) ALLOCATION.—Allocation mechanisms shall adapt
to climate impacts.

SEC. 4. ECOSYSTEM-BASED MANAGEMENT.

(a) REQUIREMENT.—Management shall consider ecosystem
interactions.

(b) FORAGE FISH.—Forage fish shall be managed for
ecosystem function.

(c) HABITAT.—Essential fish habitat protections
shall be strengthened.

SEC. 5. REBUILDING.

(a) TIMELINE.—Overfished stocks shall be rebuilt as
quickly as possible.

(b) FLEXIBILITY.—Limited flexibility for ecological
or economic factors.

(c) ACCOUNTABILITY.—Annual catch limits shall remain
mandatory.

SEC. 6. MONITORING.

(a) ELECTRONIC MONITORING.—Electronic monitoring
shall be expanded.

(b) OBSERVER COVERAGE.—Observer programs shall be
adequately funded.

(c) DATA.—Fishery data systems shall be improved.

SEC. 7. RECREATIONAL FISHERIES.

(a) DATA.—Recreational catch data shall be improved.

(b) MANAGEMENT.—Recreational fisheries management
shall be science-based.

SEC. 8. FISHING COMMUNITIES.

(a) TRANSITION ASSISTANCE.—Assistance shall be
available for communities affected by fishery changes.

(b) CLIMATE ADAPTATION.—Support for fishing community
climate adaptation.

SEC. 9. AUTHORIZATION.

[Funding levels for NOAA Fisheries]

SEC. 10. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act takes effect upon enactment.
```

## State Model Legislation

### Model Coastal Resilience Act

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "[State] Coastal Resilience
Act".

SECTION 2. FINDINGS.

The Legislature finds that sea level rise and coastal
hazards threaten [State]'s coast and comprehensive
resilience planning is essential.

SECTION 3. COASTAL RESILIENCE PLAN.

(a) The [Agency] shall develop a comprehensive
coastal resilience plan.

(b) The plan shall address—
    (1) sea level rise projections;
    (2) coastal flooding;
    (3) erosion;
    (4) storm surge;
    (5) nature-based solutions; and
    (6) vulnerable communities.

SECTION 4. SEA LEVEL RISE PROJECTIONS.

(a) The [Agency] shall adopt official sea level rise
projections.

(b) Projections shall be updated every 5 years.

(c) State agencies shall use projections in planning.

SECTION 5. NATURE-BASED SOLUTIONS.

(a) Nature-based solutions shall be prioritized where
effective.

(b) Living shorelines shall be preferred over hardened
structures where feasible.

SECTION 6. VULNERABLE COMMUNITIES.

(a) Environmental justice communities shall be
prioritized for resilience investments.

(b) Assistance shall be provided for adaptation planning.

SECTION 7. FUNDING.

[Funding mechanism for coastal resilience]

SECTION 8. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act takes effect on [date].
```

## Constitutional Considerations

### Federal Authority

| Basis | Application |
|-------|-------------|
| **Commerce Clause** | Interstate, foreign fisheries |
| **Admiralty** | Maritime jurisdiction |
| **Treaty Power** | International agreements |
| **Property Clause** | Federal waters |

### State Authority

| Basis | Application |
|-------|-------------|
| **Police power** | Coastal regulation |
| **Public trust** | Tidelands, waters |
| **State waters** | 0-3 miles |

### International

| Framework | Scope |
|-----------|-------|
| **UNCLOS** | Law of the sea (U.S. not party) |
| **Fisheries treaties** | Shared stocks |
| **Pollution treaties** | MARPOL, plastics |

## Loopholes, Shortcomings, and Rectification

### Potential Loopholes

| Loophole | Description | Severity |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| **Exemptions** | Certain activities excluded | Medium |
| **Enforcement** | Limited offshore capacity | High |
| **International** | Flag state problems | High |
| **Definitions** | What counts as "protected" | Medium |
| **Climate adaptation** | Insufficient flexibility | Medium |

### Shortcomings

| Issue | Impact | Root Cause |
|-------|--------|------------|
| **UNCLOS** | U.S. not party | Senate opposition |
| **Funding** | Inadequate investment | Political will |
| **Enforcement** | Limited capacity | Resources |
| **International** | Global action needed | Coordination |
| **Climate** | Outpacing response | Systemic |

### Rectification Procedures

1. **Close exemptions** for conservation areas
2. **Strengthen enforcement** with adequate resources
3. **International engagement** including UNCLOS
4. **Clear protection standards** for MPAs
5. **Climate adaptation** built into all management
6. **Adequate funding** for ocean programs
7. **Monitoring** expanded and sustained
8. **Ecosystem-based** approach required
9. **Community involvement** in all decisions
10. **Environmental justice** requirements

### General Implementation Concerns

| Concern | Mitigation |
|---------|------------|
| **Political reversal** | Durable protection |
| **Climate acceleration** | Adaptive management |
| **Stakeholder opposition** | Inclusive process |
| **Funding adequacy** | Dedicated sources |
