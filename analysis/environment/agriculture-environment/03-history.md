# Agriculture and Environment: History

## Overview

The relationship between American agriculture and the environment has evolved through distinct phases: early exploitation leading to the Dust Bowl, the conservation response of the 1930s, post-war industrialization with growing environmental impacts, the environmental awakening of the 1970s, conservation program development in the 1980s-1990s, and contemporary focus on climate and sustainability.

|--------|-------------|

| **Homestead Act (1862)** | Accelerated settlement |

| **Irrigation development** | Western water projects |

### Environmental Consequences

| Impact | Location |
|--------|----------|

| **Wetland drainage** | Midwest, others |
