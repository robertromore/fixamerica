# Agriculture and Environment: Overview

## Executive Summary

American agriculture is both a significant contributor to environmental degradation and a potential solution for climate change and ecological restoration. Industrial farming depletes soil, pollutes waterways, generates greenhouse gases, and threatens biodiversity, while consuming vast quantities of water and driving habitat loss. Yet agriculture can also sequester carbon, protect watersheds, maintain wildlife habitat, and build ecosystem resilience. Transitioning to sustainable agriculture requires reforming farm policy, supporting conservation practices, reducing chemical inputs, and integrating environmental outcomes into the food system.

## Scope

This analysis covers:

- **Agricultural pollution**: Nutrient runoff, pesticides, waste
- **Soil health**: Degradation, conservation, carbon
- **Water impacts**: Irrigation, contamination, depletion
- **Greenhouse gas emissions**: Livestock, fertilizers, land use
- **Biodiversity**: Habitat loss, pollinator decline
- **Land use**: Conservation, conversion, management
- **Sustainable practices**: Regenerative, organic, conservation
- **Farm policy**: Environmental implications

## Path Forward

Sustainable agriculture transition requires:

1. **Conservation program expansion** with environmental outcomes
2. **Soil health** practices at scale
3. **Nutrient management** reducing pollution
4. **Integrated pest management** reducing chemicals
5. **Livestock reform** addressing emissions and waste
6. **Water efficiency** in irrigation
7. **Market transformation** valuing sustainability
8. **Research investment** in sustainable systems
