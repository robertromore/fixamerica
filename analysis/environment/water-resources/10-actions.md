# Water Resources: Actions for Citizens

## Overview

Citizens can advance water protection through personal conservation, community engagement, policy advocacy, and environmental stewardship. Whether focused on local water quality, infrastructure investment, or broader water policy, individual and collective action can drive meaningful change.

## Personal Actions

### Conservation at Home

| Action | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Fix leaks** | Save water, reduce bills |
| **Efficient fixtures** | WaterSense products |
| **Landscape wisely** | Native, drought-tolerant plants |
| **Reduce outdoor use** | Smart irrigation |
| **Full loads only** | Dishwasher, laundry |

### Water Quality Protection

| Action | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Proper disposal** | Don't dump chemicals |
| **Reduce pesticides** | Protect runoff |
| **Pick up pet waste** | Reduce bacteria |
| **Maintain septic** | Prevent contamination |
| **Dispose of meds properly** | Pharmacy take-back |

### Know Your Water

| Action | Why |
|--------|-----|
| **Read water reports** | Know your quality |
| **Test if concerned** | Private wells especially |
| **Understand bills** | Know where money goes |
| **Report problems** | Odor, color, taste |
