# Water Resources: Legislation and Legal Framework

## Overview

Comprehensive water reform requires legislation addressing infrastructure investment, water quality, allocation, affordability, and governance. This document provides draft legislation for key reforms at federal and state levels.

## Federal Legislation

### Water Infrastructure Modernization Act

**Purpose**: Establish dedicated funding for water infrastructure and ensure universal safe water access.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Water Infrastructure
Modernization Act".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds that—
    (1) America's water infrastructure is deteriorating;
    (2) millions lack access to safe drinking water;
    (3) lead contamination threatens public health;
    (4) investment gaps exceed $600 billion;
    (5) comprehensive action is necessary.

SEC. 3. DRINKING WATER INFRASTRUCTURE PROGRAM.

(a) AUTHORIZATION.—There is authorized to be appropriated
$10 billion annually for drinking water infrastructure.

(b) STATE REVOLVING FUND ENHANCEMENT.—
    (1) The capitalization grants shall be increased.
    (2) Disadvantaged community subsidization shall be
    increased to 40 percent.
    (3) Principal forgiveness shall be expanded.

(c) LEAD SERVICE LINE REPLACEMENT.—
    (1) All lead service lines shall be replaced within
    10 years.
    (2) Utilities shall complete inventories within 3 years.
    (3) Federal funding shall cover full replacement costs
    in disadvantaged communities.
    (4) Both public and private portions shall be replaced.

SEC. 4. SMALL SYSTEM ASSISTANCE.

(a) TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE.—$500 million annually for
technical assistance to small systems.

(b) CAPACITY DEVELOPMENT.—States shall develop programs
for small system capacity building.

(c) REGIONALIZATION.—Incentives shall be provided for
regional cooperation.

SEC. 5. WATER QUALITY STANDARDS.

(a) EMERGING CONTAMINANTS.—The Administrator shall
establish standards for emerging contaminants within
3 years.

(b) CUMULATIVE IMPACTS.—Standards shall consider
cumulative exposure to multiple contaminants.

(c) MONITORING.—Monitoring shall be expanded in
underserved communities.

SEC. 6. AFFORDABILITY.

(a) LOW-INCOME WATER ASSISTANCE.—There is established
a permanent Low-Income Household Water Assistance Program
with $2 billion annual authorization.

(b) RATE DESIGN.—Utilities receiving federal funds shall
implement affordability programs.

(c) SHUTOFF PROTECTIONS.—Procedures shall protect
vulnerable households from disconnection.

SEC. 7. TRIBAL WATER.

(a) AUTHORIZATION.—$5 billion is authorized for tribal
water infrastructure.

(b) SETTLEMENTS.—Funds are authorized for tribal water
rights settlements.

(c) ACCESS.—All tribal communities shall have access
to safe drinking water within 10 years.

SEC. 8. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act takes effect upon enactment.
```

### Sustainable Water Supply Act

**Purpose**: Reform water allocation to ensure long-term sustainability.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Sustainable Water Supply Act".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds that—
    (1) water scarcity threatens many regions;
    (2) aquifers are being depleted unsustainably;
    (3) climate change is altering water availability;
    (4) existing allocation systems are inadequate; and
    (5) federal leadership is needed.

SEC. 3. GROUNDWATER SUSTAINABILITY.

(a) NATIONAL GROUNDWATER ASSESSMENT.—The Secretary
shall assess groundwater conditions nationwide.

(b) STATE SUSTAINABILITY PLANS.—States receiving
federal water funds shall develop groundwater
sustainability plans.

(c) MONITORING.—A national groundwater monitoring
network shall be established.

(d) RESEARCH.—Research shall be expanded on aquifer
recharge and sustainability.

SEC. 4. INTERSTATE WATER COMPACT REFORM.

(a) CLIMATE ADAPTATION.—Interstate compacts shall
incorporate climate adaptation provisions.

(b) ENVIRONMENTAL FLOWS.—Compacts shall protect
minimum environmental flows.

(c) TRIBAL RIGHTS.—Compacts shall recognize tribal
reserved rights.

(d) FLEXIBILITY.—Compacts shall include mechanisms
for responding to changing conditions.

SEC. 5. WATER RECYCLING AND REUSE.

(a) NATIONAL PROGRAM.—A national water recycling
program is established.

(b) FUNDING.—$500 million annually is authorized
for water recycling projects.

(c) STANDARDS.—The Administrator shall develop
national water reuse standards.

SEC. 6. CONSERVATION.

(a) EFFICIENCY STANDARDS.—The Secretary shall update
water efficiency standards for fixtures and appliances.

(b) AGRICULTURAL EFFICIENCY.—Programs shall support
agricultural water efficiency.

(c) LANDSCAPE TRANSFORMATION.—Programs shall support
water-efficient landscaping.

SEC. 7. DROUGHT RESPONSE.

(a) PLANNING.—States shall develop drought response
plans as condition of federal funding.

(b) EMERGENCY ASSISTANCE.—Emergency drought assistance
is authorized.

(c) RESILIENCE.—Infrastructure investments shall
consider drought resilience.

SEC. 8. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act takes effect upon enactment.
```

### Clean Water Restoration Act

**Purpose**: Strengthen Clean Water Act protections and address nonpoint source pollution.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Clean Water Restoration Act".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds that—
    (1) half of assessed waters remain impaired;
    (2) jurisdictional uncertainty undermines protection;
    (3) nonpoint source pollution is inadequately addressed;
    (4) stronger protections are needed.

SEC. 3. JURISDICTIONAL CLARIFICATION.

(a) WATERS OF THE UNITED STATES.—The term "waters of
the United States" shall include all waters with a
significant nexus to traditionally navigable waters.

(b) WETLANDS.—Wetlands adjacent to protected waters
shall be protected.

SEC. 4. NONPOINT SOURCE POLLUTION.

(a) PROGRAM ENHANCEMENT.—The nonpoint source program
shall be strengthened with enforceable requirements
for states failing to meet water quality standards.

(b) AGRICULTURAL POLLUTION.—
    (1) Best management practices shall be required in
    impaired watersheds.
    (2) Nutrient management shall be required for
    concentrated animal feeding operations.
    (3) Buffer requirements shall be established.

(c) STORMWATER.—Stormwater programs shall be expanded
with green infrastructure requirements.

SEC. 5. ENFORCEMENT.

(a) CITIZEN SUITS.—Citizen suit provisions shall be
strengthened.

(b) PENALTIES.—Civil penalties shall be increased.

(c) STATE PROGRAM OVERSIGHT.—EPA oversight of state
programs shall be enhanced.

SEC. 6. FUNDING.

(a) CLEAN WATER SRF.—$10 billion annually is authorized.

(b) NONPOINT SOURCE.—$500 million annually for
nonpoint source programs.

(c) WATERSHED RESTORATION.—$1 billion annually for
watershed restoration.

SEC. 7. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act takes effect upon enactment.
```

## State Model Legislation

### Model Groundwater Sustainability Act

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "[State] Groundwater
Sustainability Act".

SECTION 2. FINDINGS.

The Legislature finds that groundwater depletion
threatens the state's water future and sustainable
management is essential.

SECTION 3. GROUNDWATER MANAGEMENT AREAS.

(a) The [Agency] shall designate groundwater management
areas where overdraft occurs.

(b) Local groundwater agencies shall develop sustainability
plans for designated areas.

SECTION 4. SUSTAINABILITY REQUIREMENTS.

(a) Sustainability plans shall achieve sustainable yield
within 20 years.

(b) Plans shall include—
    (1) basin assessment;
    (2) sustainability goals;
    (3) monitoring program;
    (4) management actions; and
    (5) implementation schedule.

SECTION 5. PERMITTING.

(a) New groundwater withdrawals in critical areas shall
require permits.

(b) Existing withdrawals shall be registered.

SECTION 6. ENFORCEMENT.

The [Agency] shall enforce sustainability requirements
where local agencies fail to act.

SECTION 7. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act takes effect on [date].
```

### Model Water Affordability Act

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "[State] Water Affordability Act".

SECTION 2. FINDINGS.

The Legislature finds that all residents deserve access
to affordable water service.

SECTION 3. CUSTOMER ASSISTANCE PROGRAMS.

(a) Each water utility shall establish a customer
assistance program for low-income households.

(b) Programs shall include—
    (1) bill discounts;
    (2) flexible payment plans;
    (3) arrearage management; and
    (4) efficiency assistance.

SECTION 4. SHUTOFF PROTECTIONS.

(a) No utility shall disconnect service without—
    (1) 30 days written notice;
    (2) offer of payment plan; and
    (3) information on assistance programs.

(b) Disconnection shall be prohibited for—
    (1) households with medical necessity;
    (2) extreme weather conditions; and
    (3) pending assistance applications.

SECTION 5. FUNDING.

The [State Water Assistance Fund] shall support utility
customer assistance programs.

SECTION 6. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act takes effect on [date].
```

## Loopholes, Shortcomings, and Rectification

### Potential Loopholes

| Loophole | Description | Severity |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| **Exemptions** | Agricultural, small sources | High |
| **Jurisdictional gaps** | WOTUS uncertainty | High |
| **Enforcement discretion** | Inconsistent application | Medium |
| **State variation** | Implementation differences | High |
| **Grandfathering** | Existing uses protected | Medium |

### Shortcomings

| Issue | Impact | Root Cause |
|-------|--------|------------|
| **Funding gaps** | Insufficient investment | Political will |
| **Nonpoint sources** | Major pollution source uncontrolled | Agricultural power |
| **Groundwater** | State-by-state patchwork | Federalism |
| **Climate adaptation** | Inadequate preparation | Short-term focus |
| **Affordability** | Millions burdened | Rate structure |

### Rectification Procedures

1. **Clarify WOTUS** through legislation
2. **Strengthen nonpoint source** with enforceable requirements
3. **Federal groundwater baseline** while respecting state role
4. **Climate requirements** in all water planning
5. **Affordability mandate** for federal funding recipients
6. **Enforcement resources** tied to need
7. **Small system support** for compliance
8. **Tribal priority** in funding allocation
9. **Environmental flows** protection requirements
10. **Performance metrics** with accountability

### General Implementation Concerns

| Concern | Mitigation |
|---------|------------|
| **State resistance** | Funding incentives |
| **Affordability** | Assistance programs |
| **Political reversal** | Bipartisan support |
| **Climate uncertainty** | Adaptive management |
| **Funding adequacy** | Dedicated sources |
