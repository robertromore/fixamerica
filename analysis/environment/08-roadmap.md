# Environment: Roadmap

## Implementation Strategy Overview

Environmental progress requires action across multiple fronts simultaneously: federal policy, state innovation, regulatory enforcement, corporate accountability, and grassroots mobilization. Given the climate timeline, speed is essential, but durable change requires building political support and addressing equity. This roadmap balances urgency with strategic sequencing.

## Phase 2: Foundation Building (Years 1-2)

### Federal Legislative Priorities

**Priority 1: Wetland Restoration Act**

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Purpose** | Restore Clean Water Act protection after *Sackett* |
| **Scope** | Protect all waters "affecting" navigable waters |
| **Mechanism** | Congressional override of *Sackett* |
| **Feasibility** | Moderate (clear environmental harm from decision) |

**Priority 2: Environmental Justice for All Act**

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Purpose** | Codify EJ requirements |
| **Components** | Cumulative impacts, community participation, civil rights |
| **Mechanism** | Amend environmental statutes |
| **Feasibility** | Moderate (builds on existing EO) |

**Priority 3: End Fossil Fuel Subsidies Act**

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Purpose** | Remove tax preferences for fossil fuels |
| **Components** | Repeal percentage depletion, intangible drilling costs, etc. |
| **Revenue** | Redirect to clean energy, transition |
| **Feasibility** | Low-moderate (significant opposition) |

**Path to Passage**

1. Build bipartisan climate caucus
2. Attach to must-pass legislation where possible
3. Use reconciliation for budget-related provisions
4. Filibuster reform if necessary for major legislation

### Key Sequencing

| Dependency | Why It Matters |
|------------|----------------|
| Executive action before legislation | Demonstrates feasibility, builds support |
| State action before federal | Provides models, builds pressure |
| Coalition building before major legislation | Need political support |
| Political reform before comprehensive climate law | Filibuster blocks climate legislation |
| Just transition with decarbonization | Political durability, equity |

## Risks and Contingencies

### Political Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|------------|
| **Administration change** | Varies | High | Build state-level durability; codify progress |
| **Supreme Court** | High | High | Legislative solutions; state action |
| **Filibuster blocks legislation** | High | High | Reconciliation; filibuster reform |
| **Industry opposition** | Certain | Medium | Build countervailing power |
| **Public backlash to costs** | Medium | Medium | Carbon dividends; protect low-income |

### Economic Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|------------|
| **Recession reduces investment** | Medium | Medium | Counter-cyclical green investment |
| **Supply chain constraints** | High | Medium | Domestic manufacturing; diversification |
| **Energy price spikes** | Medium | High | Efficiency investment; managed transition |
| **Stranded asset crisis** | Low | High | Managed decline; financial stability |

### Technical Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|------------|
| **Grid reliability** | Medium | High | Storage, transmission investment |
| **Battery supply** | Medium | Medium | Diversification, recycling |
| **Carbon capture fails** | High | Medium | Don't rely on it; direct reduction |
| **Technology cost increases** | Low | Medium | Industrial policy |

### Climate Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|------------|
| **Climate impacts accelerate** | High | High | Adaptation investment; faster mitigation |
| **Tipping points crossed** | Medium | Extreme | Precautionary action; adaptation |
| **International failure** | Medium | High | U.S. leadership; border adjustment |

### Contingency Plans

**If Administration Changes**

1. Accelerate state-level implementation
2. Maximize regulatory finalization before transition
3. Litigation to defend progress
4. Build bipartisan state coalitions
5. Prepare for next federal opportunity

**If Supreme Court Continues Limiting EPA**

1. Pursue legislative solutions
2. Shift to state-level authority
3. Litigation strategy adaptation
4. Private enforcement mechanisms
5. Market-driven transition

**If Comprehensive Legislation Fails**

1. Maximize executive authority
2. State-level comprehensive action
3. Litigation accountability
4. Private sector pressure
5. Build for next Congress

**If Climate Impacts Accelerate**

1. Emergency adaptation investment
2. Managed retreat programs
3. Disaster resilience
4. Accelerate mitigation
5. International climate migration planning

## Success Scenario

### What Victory Looks Like

**By 2030**

- U.S. emissions 50% below 2005
- 80%+ clean electricity
- EV majority of new car sales
- Environmental justice codified
- Climate adaptation underway
- Leading international climate action

**By 2050**

- Net-zero emissions economy
- 100% clean electricity
- Transportation electrified
- Buildings decarbonized
- Industry transformed
- Ecosystems recovering
- Environmental justice achieved
- Climate-resilient infrastructure

### What Failure Looks Like

- Continued emissions growth
- Climate impacts accelerating
- Environmental justice communities still burdened
- Ecosystems collapsing
- Water shortages, heat deaths
- Climate migration, instability
- Intergenerational injustice

The stakes could not be higher. The roadmap is clear. The question is whether political will and sustained action can match the urgency of the challenge.
