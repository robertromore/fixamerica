# Fiscal Policy: Legislation and Legal Framework

## Overview

Fiscal policy reform requires legislation addressing both process dysfunction and substantive fiscal imbalances. This document provides draft federal legislation for budget process reform and automatic stabilizers, state model legislation for fiscal management, and comprehensive analysis of potential loopholes and implementation challenges.

## Constitutional Considerations

| Provision | Effect on Fiscal Policy |
|-----------|------------------------|
| Article I, Section 7 | Revenue bills originate in House |

## Federal Legislation

### Fiscal Responsibility Act (Enhanced)

**Purpose**: Prevent government shutdowns, reform debt ceiling, and establish fiscal sustainability framework.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SEC. 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Fiscal Responsibility Act".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds the following:
(1) Government shutdowns disrupt public services, harm federal
    employees, and damage the economy.
(2) Debt ceiling crises threaten the full faith and credit of the
    United States and create unnecessary economic uncertainty.
(3) The federal budget process has failed to produce timely
    appropriations for decades.
(4) Long-term fiscal sustainability requires a framework for
    managing debt relative to economic output.
(5) Process reform is essential to enable substantive fiscal
    policy improvement.

SEC. 3. AUTOMATIC CONTINUING APPROPRIATIONS.

(a) IN GENERAL.—If any regular appropriation bill for a fiscal year
    does not become law before the beginning of such fiscal year or
    a joint resolution making continuing appropriations is not in
    effect, there are appropriated such sums as may be necessary to
    continue any project or activity for which funds were provided
    in the preceding fiscal year.

(b) LEVEL OF FUNDING.—Appropriations and funds made available under
    subsection (a) shall be at a rate of operations not exceeding—
    (1) the rate provided in the applicable appropriation Act for
        the preceding fiscal year;
    (2) in the absence of such Act, the rate provided in the
        applicable appropriation Act for the fiscal year preceding
        the preceding fiscal year, adjusted for inflation; or
    (3) the rate provided in the most recently enacted joint
        resolution making continuing appropriations.

(c) DURATION.—Appropriations and funds made available under this
    section shall continue until—
    (1) enactment of an applicable regular appropriation bill or
        joint resolution making continuing appropriations; or
    (2) the end of the fiscal year.

(d) TERMS AND CONDITIONS.—Appropriations made under this section
    shall be subject to the terms and conditions of the most recently
    enacted appropriation Act for such project or activity.

(e) PROHIBITION ON NEW ACTIVITIES.—No appropriation or funds made
    available under this section shall be used to initiate or resume
    any project or activity for which funds were not available during
    the preceding fiscal year.

SEC. 4. DEBT CEILING REFORM.

(a) AUTOMATIC INCREASE.—Section 3101(b) of title 31, United States
    Code, is amended by adding at the end:

    "Notwithstanding the preceding sentence, when the Secretary
    determines that the debt subject to limit is within $100,000,000,000
    of the limit under this section, the Secretary shall notify Congress.
    If Congress does not enact legislation reducing the debt or
    increasing the limit within 30 days of such notification, the
    limit shall be automatically increased by the amount necessary
    to meet the obligations of the United States for the next fiscal
    year, as estimated by the Congressional Budget Office."

(b) CONGRESSIONAL OVERRIDE.—The automatic increase under subsection
    (a) may be overridden by a joint resolution of Congress enacted
    within 15 days of the automatic increase.

(c) EXPEDITED PROCEDURES.—A joint resolution under subsection (b)
    shall be subject to the following expedited procedures:
    (1) The resolution shall be referred to the appropriate
        committees, which shall report it without amendment within
        5 days.
    (2) The resolution is privileged and may be called up at any
        time by the Majority Leader.
    (3) Debate shall be limited to 10 hours, equally divided.
    (4) No amendments shall be in order.
    (5) A vote on final passage shall occur immediately upon
        conclusion of debate.

SEC. 5. DEBT-TO-GDP TARGET.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—It is the policy of the United States that the
    ratio of debt held by the public to gross domestic product should
    not exceed 100 percent.

(b) MONITORING.—The Congressional Budget Office shall include in
    each Budget and Economic Outlook a projection of the debt-to-GDP
    ratio and an assessment of compliance with the target.

(c) CORRECTIVE PROCEDURES.—
    (1) If CBO projects the debt-to-GDP ratio will exceed 100 percent
        within 10 years, the budget committees shall report legislation
        within 180 days designed to reduce the projected ratio.
    (2) Such legislation shall be subject to the expedited procedures
        of section 4(c).

(d) EMERGENCY EXCEPTION.—The target under subsection (a) may be
    exceeded during a period of—
    (1) war declared by Congress;
    (2) national emergency declared by the President; or
    (3) economic recession as determined by NBER.

SEC. 6. PAY-AS-YOU-GO ENHANCEMENT.

(a) EXTENSION.—The Statutory Pay-As-You-Go Act of 2010 is amended
    to extend the scoring window from 10 years to 20 years.

(b) SUPERMAJORITY WAIVER.—The requirement under section 4(b) of
    the Statutory Pay-As-You-Go Act of 2010 that legislation not
    increase the deficit may only be waived by a three-fifths vote
    of each House.

(c) EMERGENCY DEFINITION.—For purposes of emergency designations
    under the Statutory Pay-As-You-Go Act, an emergency exists only
    when—
    (1) the President has declared a national emergency; or
    (2) the economy is in recession as determined by NBER.

SEC. 7. BIENNIAL BUDGETING PILOT.

(a) AUTHORIZATION.—The Chairman of the Committee on the Budget of
    each House may designate up to 4 appropriations bills to be
    enacted on a biennial basis.

(b) EVALUATION.—The Government Accountability Office shall evaluate
    the effectiveness of biennial budgeting and report to Congress
    within 5 years.

SEC. 8. FISCAL TRANSPARENCY.

(a) TAX EXPENDITURE BUDGET.—The President's budget shall include
    a comprehensive tax expenditure budget presenting tax expenditures
    in parallel format to direct spending.

(b) GENERATIONAL ACCOUNTING.—The Congressional Budget Office shall
    prepare annual generational accounts showing the present value
    of lifetime net tax burdens for different age cohorts.

(c) FULL ACCRUAL ACCOUNTING.—Within 5 years, federal financial
    statements shall include full accrual accounting for all
    obligations, including pension and healthcare commitments.

SEC. 9. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act shall take effect on the date of enactment, except that:
(a) Section 3 shall apply to fiscal years beginning after the date
    of enactment.
(b) Section 5 shall apply to budgets submitted after the date of
    enactment.
```

**Explanation**:

- **Section 3**: Automatic CR prevents shutdowns while preserving congressional authority
- **Section 4**: Automatic debt ceiling increase with congressional override option
- **Section 5**: Establishes debt-to-GDP target with corrective procedures
- **Section 6**: Strengthens PAYGO with longer window and supermajority waiver
- **Section 7**: Pilots biennial budgeting for selected bills
- **Section 8**: Improves fiscal transparency

**Potential Challenges**:

| Challenge | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| Loss of leverage | Shutdown threat is destructive leverage |
| Constitutional concerns | Congress can set automatic appropriations |
| Enforcement | Procedural rules create barriers |
| Flexibility | Emergency exceptions provided |

**Refinements**:

- Could include specific penalty for missing deadline
- Could add balanced budget amendment pathway
- Could strengthen enforcement mechanisms

### Automatic Stabilizer Enhancement Act

**Purpose**: Strengthen automatic fiscal stabilizers to improve counter-cyclical response and reduce need for discretionary action.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SEC. 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Automatic Stabilizer Enhancement Act".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds the following:
(1) Automatic stabilizers reduce the severity and duration of
    economic recessions.
(2) The United States has weaker automatic stabilizers than other
    developed nations.
(3) Discretionary fiscal responses are often delayed and politically
    contentious.
(4) Enhanced automatic stabilizers would provide faster, more
    predictable economic support during downturns.
(5) Automatic mechanisms remove political uncertainty and improve
    economic planning.

SEC. 3. ENHANCED UNEMPLOYMENT INSURANCE.

(a) EXTENDED BENEFITS TRIGGER.—Section 203 of the Federal-State
    Extended Unemployment Compensation Act of 1970 is amended to
    provide that extended benefits shall be available in any State
    when—
    (1) the State insured unemployment rate equals or exceeds
        5.5 percent; or
    (2) the total unemployment rate exceeds 6 percent.

(b) DURATION EXTENSION.—During any period when extended benefits
    are available under subsection (a), the maximum duration of
    regular compensation plus extended benefits shall be—
    (1) 39 weeks if the State insured unemployment rate is between
        5.5 and 7 percent;
    (2) 52 weeks if the State insured unemployment rate is between
        7 and 9 percent;
    (3) 65 weeks if the State insured unemployment rate exceeds
        9 percent.

(c) FEDERAL FUNDING.—During any period when extended benefits are
    available under this section, the Federal Government shall pay
    100 percent of the cost of such benefits.

(d) BENEFIT AMOUNT.—The weekly benefit amount during periods of
    extended benefits shall not be less than 50 percent of the
    individual's prior average weekly wage, up to a State maximum.

(e) COVERAGE EXPANSION.—The Secretary of Labor shall promulgate
    regulations expanding unemployment insurance coverage to include—
    (1) workers who are self-employed;
    (2) workers in the gig economy;
    (3) part-time workers seeking full-time employment.

(f) TRUST FUND SOLVENCY.—The Secretary shall ensure that the
    Federal Unemployment Account maintains a balance sufficient
    to fund anticipated extended benefits during economic downturns.

SEC. 4. SUPPLEMENTAL NUTRITION ASSISTANCE PROGRAM ENHANCEMENT.

(a) AUTOMATIC BENEFIT INCREASE.—Section 8 of the Food and Nutrition
    Act of 2008 is amended to provide that during any month when the
    national unemployment rate exceeds 6 percent, the maximum
    allotment under section 8 shall be increased by 15 percent.

(b) PHASE-OUT.—The benefit increase under subsection (a) shall be
    phased out over 6 months once the unemployment rate falls below
    6 percent for 3 consecutive months.

(c) WORK REQUIREMENTS.—Section 6(o) time limits on able-bodied
    adults without dependents shall be suspended during any period
    when the national unemployment rate exceeds 6.5 percent.

SEC. 5. MEDICAID ENHANCED FEDERAL MATCHING.

(a) AUTOMATIC FMAP INCREASE.—During any quarter when the national
    unemployment rate exceeds 6.5 percent, the Federal medical
    assistance percentage for each State shall be increased by—
    (1) 6.2 percentage points if the national unemployment rate
        is between 6.5 and 8 percent;
    (2) 9.3 percentage points if the national unemployment rate
        is between 8 and 9.5 percent;
    (3) 12.4 percentage points if the national unemployment rate
        exceeds 9.5 percent.

(b) DURATION.—The increased matching under subsection (a) shall
    continue until the national unemployment rate falls below
    6 percent for 2 consecutive quarters.

(c) MAINTENANCE OF EFFORT.—As a condition of the increased matching,
    States shall not reduce Medicaid eligibility, benefits, or
    provider payments during the period of increased matching.

SEC. 6. INFRASTRUCTURE COUNTER-CYCLICAL FUND.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—There is established in the Treasury a fund to
    be known as the "Counter-Cyclical Infrastructure Fund".

(b) AUTHORIZATION.—There are authorized to be appropriated to the
    Fund $50,000,000,000 to remain available until expended.

(c) ACTIVATION.—The Secretary of Transportation shall obligate
    funds from the Fund when—
    (1) the national unemployment rate exceeds 7 percent; and
    (2) the Director of the Congressional Budget Office certifies
        that GDP growth has been below 1 percent for 2 consecutive
        quarters.

(d) ELIGIBLE PROJECTS.—Funds shall be used for—
    (1) repair and maintenance of existing infrastructure;
    (2) projects that can begin construction within 90 days;
    (3) projects with completed environmental reviews.

(e) STATE AND LOCAL MATCHING.—Federal funds may cover up to
    100 percent of project costs during activation periods.

(f) DEACTIVATION.—Acceleration of projects under this section
    shall cease when the unemployment rate falls below 6 percent.

SEC. 7. PAYROLL TAX RELIEF.

(a) AUTOMATIC REDUCTION.—During any quarter when the national
    unemployment rate exceeds 7.5 percent, the rate of tax imposed
    by section 3101(a) of the Internal Revenue Code (employee share
    of OASDI) shall be reduced by 2 percentage points.

(b) TRUST FUND TRANSFER.—Any reduction in revenue to the Federal
    Old-Age and Survivors Insurance Trust Fund resulting from
    subsection (a) shall be offset by transfers from general revenues.

(c) PHASE-OUT.—The rate reduction under subsection (a) shall be
    phased out over 2 quarters once the unemployment rate falls
    below 7 percent for 2 consecutive months.

SEC. 8. STATE FISCAL STABILIZATION.

(a) AUTOMATIC GRANTS.—During any fiscal year when real GDP declines
    or the national unemployment rate exceeds 7 percent, the Secretary
    of the Treasury shall make stabilization payments to States.

(b) AMOUNT.—The amount of stabilization payments shall be based on—
    (1) population;
    (2) decline in State revenue; and
    (3) State unemployment rate relative to national average.

(c) USE OF FUNDS.—Stabilization payments may be used for—
    (1) education;
    (2) public safety;
    (3) infrastructure;
    (4) Medicaid; and
    (5) other essential services.

(d) AUTHORIZATION.—There are authorized to be appropriated such
    sums as may be necessary to carry out this section.

SEC. 9. COORDINATION AND MONITORING.

(a) COUNCIL OF ECONOMIC ADVISERS.—The Council shall report quarterly
    on the activation status of automatic stabilizers and their
    economic effects.

(b) CONGRESSIONAL NOTIFICATION.—The Secretary of the Treasury shall
    notify Congress within 24 hours of any automatic stabilizer
    activation.

(c) GAO REVIEW.—The Comptroller General shall review the operation
    of automatic stabilizers after each recession and recommend
    improvements.

SEC. 10. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act shall take effect 180 days after enactment.
```

**Explanation**:

- **Section 3**: Enhanced UI with automatic triggers, extended duration, and federal funding
- **Section 4**: SNAP benefits automatically increase during downturns
- **Section 5**: Medicaid matching increases automatically with unemployment
- **Section 6**: Pre-funded infrastructure acceleration
- **Section 7**: Payroll tax relief with trust fund protection
- **Section 8**: State fiscal stabilization grants

**Potential Challenges**:

| Challenge | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| Cost concerns | Counter-cyclical; saves money long-term |
| Work incentive effects | Evidence shows minimal |
| State dependence | Maintenance of effort required |
| Trigger calibration | Based on historical patterns |

## State Model Legislation

### State Rainy Day Fund Enhancement Act (Model)

**Purpose**: Require states to maintain adequate reserves for economic downturns and establish contribution rules.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "[State] Fiscal Resilience Act".

SECTION 2. FINDINGS.

The Legislature finds that:
(a) State revenues are volatile and decline significantly during
    economic recessions.
(b) Balanced budget requirements force pro-cyclical fiscal policy
    that worsens economic downturns.
(c) Adequate reserves can stabilize state services and employment
    during recessions.
(d) Many states have insufficient reserves to withstand moderate
    economic downturns.

SECTION 3. BUDGET STABILIZATION FUND.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—There is established a fund to be known as the
    "Budget Stabilization Fund".

(b) TARGET BALANCE.—The Fund shall maintain a target balance equal
    to 15 percent of the prior fiscal year's general fund expenditures.

(c) DEPOSITS.—
    (1) In any fiscal year when actual general fund revenues exceed
        the official revenue estimate by more than 2 percent, the
        excess shall be deposited in the Fund until the target
        balance is reached.
    (2) In any fiscal year when the Fund balance is below the
        target, an amount equal to 1 percent of general fund
        expenditures shall be appropriated to the Fund.

SECTION 4. WITHDRAWALS.

(a) AUTHORIZATION.—Withdrawals from the Fund may be authorized by
    the Legislature when—
    (1) actual revenues fall below the official estimate by 3 percent
        or more;
    (2) the State unemployment rate exceeds 7 percent; or
    (3) the Governor declares a fiscal emergency.

(b) LIMITATIONS.—
    (1) No more than one-third of the Fund balance may be withdrawn
        in any single fiscal year.
    (2) Withdrawals shall be replenished within 5 fiscal years.

(c) USE OF FUNDS.—Withdrawals may be used only for—
    (1) maintaining essential services;
    (2) avoiding layoffs of state employees; and
    (3) continuing capital projects.

SECTION 5. INVESTMENT.

The State Treasurer shall invest Fund assets in safe, liquid
instruments appropriate for potential near-term withdrawal.

SECTION 6. REPORTING.

The State Treasurer shall report annually on the Fund balance,
investment returns, and projected adequacy.

SECTION 7. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act takes effect on [date].
```

### State Pension Funding Discipline Act (Model)

**Purpose**: Ensure sustainable pension funding and prevent underfunding that threatens fiscal stability.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "[State] Pension Funding Discipline Act".

SECTION 2. FINDINGS.

The Legislature finds that:
(a) Many state and local pension systems are significantly underfunded.
(b) Underfunding creates long-term fiscal stress and threatens
    retirement security.
(c) Accounting gimmicks and unrealistic assumptions have masked
    the true extent of pension liabilities.
(d) Sustainable funding requires discipline and transparency.

SECTION 3. ACTUARIAL STANDARDS.

(a) DISCOUNT RATE.—The discount rate used to calculate pension
    liabilities shall not exceed the yield on 20-year Treasury
    securities plus 2 percentage points.

(b) MORTALITY TABLES.—Mortality assumptions shall be based on the
    most recent tables published by the Society of Actuaries.

(c) ACTUARIAL CERTIFICATION.—An independent actuary shall certify
    annual valuations.

SECTION 4. FUNDING REQUIREMENTS.

(a) ANNUAL CONTRIBUTION.—Each pension plan sponsor shall contribute
    annually at least the actuarially determined contribution.

(b) AMORTIZATION.—Unfunded liabilities shall be amortized over not
    more than 25 years using a level dollar method.

(c) ENFORCEMENT.—If a plan sponsor fails to make required contributions
    for 2 consecutive years, the State Treasurer may intercept State
    funds otherwise payable to the sponsor.

SECTION 5. BENEFIT RESTRICTIONS.

(a) NEW BENEFITS.—No pension benefit enhancement may be enacted
    unless fully funded over 15 years.

(b) COST-OF-LIVING.—Automatic cost-of-living adjustments may be
    suspended if funded ratio falls below 80 percent.

SECTION 6. TRANSPARENCY.

(a) ANNUAL REPORTING.—Each pension plan shall publish annually:
    (1) funded ratio using standardized assumptions;
    (2) employer contribution as percentage of payroll;
    (3) comparison to peer plans; and
    (4) 30-year projection of funding status.

(b) PUBLIC ACCESS.—Reports shall be published on a public website.

SECTION 7. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act takes effect for fiscal years beginning after [date].
```

### Counter-Cyclical State Budget Act (Model)

**Purpose**: Allow states to run deficits during recessions with offsetting surpluses during expansions.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "[State] Counter-Cyclical Budget Act".

SECTION 2. FINDINGS.

The Legislature finds that:
(a) Traditional balanced budget requirements force pro-cyclical
    fiscal policy.
(b) Cutting spending during recessions worsens economic downturns.
(c) A cyclically-balanced budget provides fiscal stability while
    maintaining discipline.

SECTION 3. BALANCED BUDGET OVER THE CYCLE.

(a) POLICY.—It is the policy of this State that the budget shall
    be balanced over the economic cycle, rather than annually.

(b) CYCLE DEFINITION.—The economic cycle shall be measured from
    the last month of one recession to the last month of the
    next recession, as determined by the National Bureau of
    Economic Research.

SECTION 4. DEFICIT AUTHORIZATION.

(a) RECESSION PERIOD.—During any period when the State unemployment
    rate exceeds 7 percent or real State GDP declines, the Legislature
    may authorize expenditures exceeding revenues by up to 5 percent
    of the prior year's general fund budget.

(b) FUNDING.—Deficits authorized under this section shall be funded
    from the Budget Stabilization Fund or through borrowing with a
    5-year repayment schedule.

SECTION 5. SURPLUS REQUIREMENT.

(a) EXPANSION PERIOD.—During any period when the State unemployment
    rate is below 5 percent for 12 consecutive months, the Legislature
    shall appropriate surpluses to:
    (1) repay any borrowing under Section 4;
    (2) replenish the Budget Stabilization Fund; and
    (3) reduce long-term liabilities.

(b) SURPLUS TARGET.—During expansion periods, the State shall
    target surpluses equal to at least 2 percent of general fund
    revenues.

SECTION 6. MONITORING.

The State Economist shall report quarterly on the State's position
in the economic cycle and fiscal balance over the cycle.

SECTION 7. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act takes effect on [date].
```

## Regulatory Framework

### Treasury Guidance on Automatic Stabilizers

**Draft Treasury Guidance**:

```text
TREASURY GUIDANCE 20XX-XX

SECTION 1. PURPOSE.

This guidance provides procedures for implementation of automatic
stabilizers enacted under the Automatic Stabilizer Enhancement Act.

SECTION 2. UNEMPLOYMENT RATE DETERMINATION.

(a) The Bureau of Labor Statistics monthly employment situation
    release shall be the official source for unemployment rates.

(b) Automatic stabilizer triggers shall be evaluated within 5
    business days of each BLS release.

(c) State-specific triggers shall use State unemployment rates
    from the BLS.

SECTION 3. NOTIFICATION PROCEDURES.

(a) When an automatic stabilizer trigger is met, the Secretary
    shall notify:
    (1) Congress within 24 hours;
    (2) Relevant executive agencies within 48 hours; and
    (3) The public through press release within 72 hours.

(b) Each notification shall include:
    (1) The specific stabilizer activated;
    (2) The trigger condition met;
    (3) The estimated fiscal impact; and
    (4) The expected duration.

SECTION 4. INTERAGENCY COORDINATION.

The Secretary shall convene an interagency task force including:
(a) Department of Labor;
(b) Department of Health and Human Services;
(c) Department of Transportation;
(d) Council of Economic Advisers; and
(e) Office of Management and Budget.

SECTION 5. PHASE-OUT PROCEDURES.

(a) When economic conditions improve beyond trigger thresholds,
    stabilizers shall be phased out over 2 quarters.

(b) Phase-out schedules shall be announced 30 days in advance.
```

## Loopholes, Shortcomings, and Rectification

### Fiscal Responsibility Act

**Potential Loopholes**:

| Loophole | Description | Severity |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| Emergency designation abuse | Declare emergencies to bypass PAYGO | High |
| Timing games | Shift costs outside scoring window | High |
| Baseline manipulation | Inflate baseline to reduce apparent cost | Medium |
| Automatic CR exploitation | Use CR funding as leverage for other demands | Medium |
| Debt ceiling override gaming | Repeated overrides normalize process | Medium |
| Off-budget entities | Create off-budget agencies to avoid limits | High |
| Reconciliation abuse | Use budget process for non-fiscal legislation | Medium |
| Trust fund accounting | Obscure true fiscal picture | Medium |

**Shortcomings**:

| Issue | Impact | Root Cause |
|-------|--------|------------|
| No enforcement teeth | Rules waived routinely | Political will |
| 100% debt target too high | Doesn't reduce debt | Political compromise |
| Supermajority waiver insufficient | Still achievable | Partisan coordination |
| No revenue side | Only spending focus | Political asymmetry |
| Biennial pilot too limited | Doesn't test fully | Institutional resistance |
| Transparency provisions weak | Limited accountability | Complexity |

**Rectification Procedures**:

1. **Emergency designation abuse**: Require supermajority vote and independent certification of emergency status; limit duration of emergency to 1 year without renewal; require specific link between emergency and spending.

2. **Timing games**: Extend scoring window to 30 years for major legislation; require accrual accounting for all commitments; include long-term CBO score with every bill.

3. **Baseline manipulation**: Standardize baseline methodology in statute; require independent verification; prohibit policy changes in baseline.

4. **Off-budget gaming**: Require all federal spending to be on-budget; eliminate off-budget designation except Social Security trust funds; include government-sponsored enterprises in fiscal calculations.

5. **Reconciliation abuse**: Define "budget-related" narrowly in statute; strengthen Byrd Rule; require independent parliamentarian ruling with supermajority override.

6. **Enforcement mechanisms**: Create automatic sequestration if PAYGO scorecard shows deficit; apply to both spending and tax committees; no exemptions without supermajority.

### Automatic Stabilizer Enhancement Act

**Potential Loopholes**:

| Loophole | Description | Severity |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| Trigger manipulation | States inflate unemployment stats | Low |
| Benefit gaming | Individuals exploit extended duration | Medium |
| State maintenance of effort evasion | Technical compliance only | High |
| Infrastructure project selection | Political rather than economic criteria | Medium |
| Payroll tax diversion | Trust fund transfers delayed | Medium |
| Recession definition gaming | NBER timing subject to delay | Low |
| Coverage expansion limits | Self-employment definition narrow | Medium |
| State matching manipulation | Reduce other spending to offset | High |

**Shortcomings**:

| Issue | Impact | Root Cause |
|-------|--------|------------|
| Trigger thresholds may be wrong | Under- or over-response | Economic uncertainty |
| UI coverage expansion incomplete | Many workers still excluded | Administrative complexity |
| Infrastructure fund pre-funding uncertain | May not be appropriated | Budget politics |
| State coordination weak | Uneven implementation | Federalism |
| No automatic revenue response | Only spending side | Political resistance |
| Stabilizers may be insufficient | Major recessions need more | Scale limitations |

**Rectification Procedures**:

1. **Trigger calibration**: Mandate GAO review after each recession with recommendations for threshold adjustment; include multiple indicators beyond unemployment rate; allow CBO to recommend adjustments.

2. **Coverage gaps**: Require Labor Department to expand coverage regulations every 5 years; establish minimum coverage standards tied to labor force participation; create new category for gig economy workers.

3. **State compliance**: Condition all enhanced federal funds on verified maintenance of effort; require independent audit; establish clawback for non-compliance.

4. **Infrastructure selection**: Create pre-approved project list based on objective criteria; require merit-based selection process; limit discretion in project choice.

5. **Funding adequacy**: Establish formula linking automatic stabilizers to recession severity; authorize unlimited appropriations during severe downturns; create Federal Reserve backstop for extreme scenarios.

6. **Federal-state coordination**: Create formal intergovernmental coordination mechanism; require state stabilization plans as condition of federal funds; establish minimum state rainy day fund requirements.

### State Model Legislation

**Potential Loopholes**:

| Loophole | Description | Severity |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| Revenue estimate manipulation | Inflate estimates to avoid deposits | High |
| Withdrawal criteria gaming | Declare emergencies liberally | High |
| Pension assumption gaming | Use unrealistic assumptions | Very High |
| Funded ratio calculation | Different methods yield different results | High |
| Counter-cyclical deficit abuse | Run deficits during expansion | Medium |
| Transparency evasion | Technical compliance, not meaningful disclosure | Medium |

**Shortcomings**:

| Issue | Impact | Root Cause |
|-------|--------|------------|
| 15% rainy day target may be insufficient | Large recessions exhaust funds | Calibration |
| Pension enforcement weak | No federal backstop | Federalism |
| Counter-cyclical model requires discipline | Surpluses difficult politically | Political economy |

**Rectification Procedures**:

1. **Revenue estimate gaming**: Require independent revenue forecasting board; compare estimates to actuals with accountability reporting; mandate automatic deposits based on formula rather than estimates.

2. **Emergency abuse**: Define emergency narrowly; require supermajority for declaration; limit duration and require payback schedule.

3. **Pension assumptions**: Establish uniform national standards through federal condition on tax exemption; require disclosure using standardized assumptions; create federal certification process.

4. **Funded ratio manipulation**: Require multiple calculation methods with all results disclosed; establish minimum standards for federal tax exemption; create independent certification.

5. **Existing underfunding**: Require amortization plans within 5 years of enactment; condition federal aid on compliance; create workout process for severely underfunded systems.

### General Implementation Concerns

**Cross-Cutting Issues**:

| Issue | Affects | Mitigation |
|-------|---------|------------|
| Political will erosion | All reforms | Automatic mechanisms, supermajority requirements |
| Economic forecast uncertainty | Triggers, targets | Multiple indicators, adjustment procedures |
| Federal-state coordination | Stabilizers, state legislation | Clear federal minimum standards |
| Gaming and manipulation | All provisions | Independent verification, transparency |
| Complexity | All reforms | Simplification, public education |
| Partisan capture | Enforcement | Bipartisan structures, independent administration |

**Monitoring Requirements**:

| Metric | Frequency | Responsible Entity |
|--------|-----------|---------------------|
| PAYGO scorecard | Continuous | CBO/OMB |
| Debt-to-GDP ratio | Quarterly | Treasury, CBO |
| Automatic stabilizer activation | Monthly | Treasury |
| State rainy day fund adequacy | Annual | GAO |
| Pension funded ratios | Annual | Labor, independent actuary |
| Budget process compliance | Annual | GAO |

**Amendment Procedures**:

1. **Threshold adjustment**: GAO review after each recession with binding recommendations unless Congress overrides by supermajority within 90 days.

2. **Trigger calibration**: Automatic recalibration every 10 years based on economic research; requires CBO certification of methodology.

3. **Enforcement mechanism updates**: GAO biennial assessment with recommendations; Congress must respond to recommendations within 1 year.

4. **Emergency procedure refinement**: After each emergency use, mandatory review and reporting on effectiveness; automatic sunset of emergency authorities after 2 years.

5. **State coordination updates**: Federal-state working group meets annually to identify implementation issues; recommendations for legislative adjustment.
