# Fiscal Policy: What You Can Do

## Overview

Individual citizens have limited direct influence over federal fiscal policy, but collective action, informed engagement, and sustained advocacy can shift the political landscape. This document outlines concrete actions for individuals, organizations, and communities to promote fiscal policy reform.

## Individual Actions

### Become Informed

**Understand the Basics**:

| Action | Resource |
|--------|----------|
| Learn budget basics | CBO Primer, CRFB guides |
| Track current fiscal status | Treasury Fiscal Data |
| Understand automatic stabilizers | Economic policy primers |
| Know entitlement projections | Trustees Reports |

**Follow Developments**:

| Source | Content | Frequency |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| CBO reports | Budget projections | Periodic |
| Treasury statements | Current fiscal status | Monthly |
| Budget negotiations news | Political developments | Ongoing |
| Think tank analysis | Policy options | Ongoing |

**Evaluate Claims Critically**:

| Claim | Question to Ask |
|-------|-----------------|
| "Tax cuts pay for themselves" | What does CBO say? |
| "Deficits don't matter" | What are long-term projections? |
| "Spending cut X solves problem" | What's the magnitude vs. the deficit? |
| "Program Y is bankrupting us" | What share of budget is it? |

### Engage Elected Officials

**Contact Congress**:

| Method | Effectiveness | Tips |
|--------|---------------|------|
| Phone calls | High for volume | Be specific, brief |
| Personal letters | Medium | Handwritten most effective |
| Email | Low but trackable | Reference specific legislation |
| Town halls | High for visibility | Ask pointed questions |
| District office visits | Very high | Request meetings |

**Key Messages**:

| Issue | Message |
|-------|---------|

| Debt ceiling | "Eliminate debt ceiling crisis potential" |
| Stabilizers | "Support automatic stabilizer legislation" |

**When to Contact**:

| Event | Timing |
|-------|--------|
| Appropriations season | September-October |
| Debt ceiling debate | As ceiling approaches |

**Actions**:

### Sample Communications

**Phone Script (Budget Process)**:

"Hello, I'm [name], a constituent from [city]. I'm calling to urge [Representative/Senator] to support legislation requiring automatic continuing resolutions to end government shutdown threats. Shutdowns harm federal workers, contractors, and public services. Can I count on their support?"

**Letter Template (Fiscal Sustainability)**:

"Dear [Representative/Senator],

As your constituent, I am concerned about long-term fiscal sustainability. I support bipartisan efforts to address entitlement program solvency and establish responsible fiscal frameworks. I urge you to work across party lines on these issues that affect my generation and those to come.

Sincerely,
[Name, Address]"
