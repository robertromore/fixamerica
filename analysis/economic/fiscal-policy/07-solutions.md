# Fiscal Policy: Solutions

## Overview

Reforming American fiscal policy requires addressing both process dysfunction and substantive fiscal imbalances. Solutions range from technical fixes to the budget process to fundamental changes in how we think about fiscal sustainability. This document outlines comprehensive reforms at multiple levels of ambition.

## Principles for Reform

### Goals of Fiscal Policy Reform

| Goal | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Functionality | Government funded predictably |
| Stabilization | Counter-cyclical capacity |
| Sustainability | Long-term balance achievable |
| Transparency | Public understands choices |
| Accountability | Politicians responsible for decisions |
| Investment | Adequate public goods provision |

### Design Principles

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| Automatic where possible | Reduce need for political action |
| Bipartisan structure | Survive political transitions |
| Enforcement mechanisms | Rules must have teeth |
| Flexibility | Accommodate genuine emergencies |
| Simplicity | Public can understand |

## Budget Process Reforms

### End Government Shutdowns

**Automatic Continuing Resolution**:

| Element | Design |
|---------|--------|
| Trigger | If appropriations not passed by deadline |
| Funding level | Prior year, inflation-adjusted |
| Duration | Until appropriations pass |
| Limits | Cannot exceed current services |

**Benefits**:

- Eliminates shutdown threat
- Removes hostage-taking opportunity
- Maintains government operations
- Creates positive incentive to negotiate

**Alternative: Full-Year Automatic Appropriation**:

- If deadline passes, full-year CR at prior level
- Agencies have certainty
- Congress loses leverage but gains functionality

### Debt Ceiling Reform

**Option 1: Abolish Entirely**:

| Argument For | Argument Against |
|--------------|------------------|
| Serves no fiscal purpose | Symbolic commitment |
| Creates crises | Provides negotiating moment |
| Threatens default | Forces fiscal debate |
| No other country uses it | Historical practice |

**Option 2: Automatic Increase**:

| Mechanism | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| Gephardt Rule | Deemed increased when budget passes |
| Tied to appropriations | Ceiling matches spending authorized |
| Presidential authority | President can increase with congressional override |

**Option 3: Suspension Norm**:

- Routinely suspend rather than increase
- Removes leverage while maintaining form
- Already done repeatedly in recent years

### Biennial Budgeting

**Design**:

| Year 1 | Year 2 |
|--------|--------|
| Full budget process | Oversight focus |
| Appropriations | Supplementals only |
| Authorization | Reauthorization |
| Major legislation | Technical corrections |

**Benefits**:

- More time for deliberation
- Agencies can plan better
- Reduces crisis frequency
- Allows oversight focus

**Concerns**:

- Reduces congressional control
- Economic conditions change
- Forecasting more difficult

### Budget Resolution Reform

**Make It Binding**:

| Current | Reformed |
|---------|----------|
| Non-binding targets | Enforceable caps |
| Often not passed | Required by deadline |
| No penalty | Automatic consequences |

**Automatic Resolution**:

- If not passed by April 15, prior year resolution continues
- CBO determines current services baseline
- Reconciliation still available

### Reconciliation Reform

**Restore Original Purpose**:

| Original Intent | Current Practice | Reform |
|-----------------|------------------|--------|
| Deficit reduction | Partisan legislation | Limit to deficit-related |
| Expedited process | Avoid filibuster | Maintain expedition |
| Mandatory/revenue | Major policy changes | Restrict scope |

**Byrd Rule Enhancement**:

- Stricter definition of budget-related
- Close loopholes that enable policy changes
- Independent enforcement

## Automatic Stabilizers

### Enhanced Unemployment Insurance

**Current Problems**:

| Issue | Current | Reform |
|-------|---------|--------|
| Duration | 26 weeks fixed | Automatic extension in recession |
| Replacement rate | ~40% | 50-60% with phase-down |
| Coverage | 28% of unemployed | Expand eligibility |
| Trust fund | State-level, often depleted | Federal backstop |

**Design Features**:

- Trigger: Unemployment rate crosses threshold
- Extended benefits: 13-26 additional weeks automatically
- Federal funding: 100% during recession
- Work-sharing: Encourage partial layoffs over full

### Automatic Infrastructure Investment

**Counter-Cyclical Acceleration**:

| Condition | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| Unemployment rises above threshold | Accelerate shovel-ready projects |
| State revenues fall | Federal match increases |
| Credit markets tighten | Federal direct investment |

**Implementation**:

- Pre-approved project list maintained
- Environmental reviews completed in advance
- Federal-state partnerships structured
- Automatic triggers specified

### Automatic Revenue Adjustments

**Counter-Cyclical Tax Policy**:

| Condition | Automatic Response |
|-----------|-------------------|
| GDP falls below trend | Payroll tax holiday |
| Unemployment spikes | Enhanced EITC |
| Deflation risk | Automatic stimulus |

**Design Considerations**:

- Clear triggers prevent discretion
- Phase-out as conditions improve
- Revenue-neutral over cycle
- Minimal political interference

### SNAP and Safety Net Enhancement

**Automatic Benefit Increases**:

| Program | Automatic Feature |
|---------|-------------------|
| SNAP | Benefits increase with unemployment |
| Medicaid | Automatic federal match increase |
| Housing | Emergency voucher expansion |
| TANF | Federal funding supplement |

## Fiscal Rules and Sustainability

### Debt-to-GDP Target

**Design Options**:

| Target | Enforcement |
|--------|-------------|
| Stabilize at 100% | Requires primary balance |
| Reduce to 80% over 20 years | Gradual adjustment |
| 60% target (EU-style) | More aggressive |

**Enforcement Mechanisms**:

- Automatic spending/revenue adjustments
- Sequestration trigger
- Presidential rescission authority
- Congressional supermajority for exceptions

### Swiss-Style Debt Brake

**Mechanism**:

```text
Expenditure ceiling = Structural revenue Ã— Cyclical adjustment factor
```

**Features**:

| Element | Design |
|---------|--------|
| Structural balance | Required over cycle |
| Cyclical flexibility | Deficits OK in recession |
| Compensation account | Deviations must be corrected |
| Exception procedure | Supermajority for emergencies |

**Results in Switzerland**:

- Debt/GDP reduced from 50% to 25%
- Counter-cyclical flexibility maintained
- Bipartisan support achieved

### PAYGO Restoration

**Strengthen PAYGO**:

| Current Problems | Fix |
|------------------|-----|
| Waived routinely | Require supermajority |
| Excludes discretionary | Expand coverage |
| Short time horizon | 20-year window |
| Gaming through timing | Stricter scoring |

**Statutory vs. Constitutional**:

| Statutory | Constitutional |
|-----------|----------------|
| Easier to pass | More durable |
| Easier to waive | More rigid |
| Adjustable | Amendment required |

## Entitlement Reform

### Social Security Sustainability

**Revenue Options**:

| Option | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| Raise cap to $250K | Significant revenue |
| Eliminate cap entirely | More revenue, distributive questions |
| Apply to investment income | Broadens base |
| Increase payroll rate modestly | Direct approach |

**Benefit Options**:

| Option | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| Increase retirement age | Reflects longevity |
| Change COLA formula | Reduce benefit growth |
| Means-test benefits | Target to needy |
| Modify benefit formula | Progressive adjustment |

**Balanced Approach**:

- 75-year solvency target
- Revenue and benefit changes
- Protect low-income beneficiaries
- Phase in gradually

### Medicare Cost Control

**Structural Reforms**:

| Reform | Mechanism |
|--------|-----------|
| Global budgeting | Total spending cap |
| Reference pricing | Payment based on lowest-cost option |
| Site-neutral payment | Same price regardless of location |
| Drug negotiation | Government negotiates prices |

**Beneficiary Cost-Sharing**:

| Reform | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| Income-related premiums | Higher-income pay more |
| Medigap restrictions | Limit supplemental coverage |
| Unified deductible | Replace current complexity |

**Delivery System Reform**:

- Accountable Care Organizations
- Bundled payments
- Value-based care
- Reduce fee-for-service incentives

### Medicaid Sustainability

**State-Federal Partnership**:

| Option | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Maintain current system | Open-ended federal match |
| Per-capita cap | Federal funding per beneficiary |
| Block grant | Fixed federal amount |
| Federal takeover | Reduce state variation |

**Cost Control**:

- Managed care requirements
- Site-neutral payment
- Drug rebate strengthening
- Administrative simplification

## Revenue Reforms

### Base Broadening

**Tax Expenditure Reform**:

| Expenditure | Reform Option |
|-------------|---------------|
| Mortgage interest | Cap at $500K, convert to credit |
| SALT deduction | Maintain cap or eliminate |
| Charitable | Floor before deduction, cap |
| Health exclusion | Cap value |
| Retirement | Lower limits |

**Revenue Potential**:

- Tax expenditures cost $1.5 trillion annually
- Even modest reforms yield significant revenue
- Base broadening enables lower rates

### Capital Income Taxation

**Reforms**:

| Reform | Revenue |
|--------|---------|
| Tax capital gains as income | $200+ billion/decade |
| Tax gains at death | $40+ billion/year |
| Mark-to-market | Significant but complex |
| Carried interest as income | $15+ billion/decade |

## Implementation Framework

### Sequencing

**Phase 1: Process Reform (Immediate)**:

1. Automatic CR to prevent shutdowns
2. Debt ceiling reform
3. Budget timeline adjustments
4. Enhanced transparency

**Phase 2: Automatic Stabilizers (Near-Term)**:

1. Enhanced unemployment insurance
2. Automatic SNAP adjustments
3. Counter-cyclical infrastructure
4. State fiscal stabilization

**Phase 3: Fiscal Rules (Medium-Term)**:

1. Debt target framework
2. PAYGO strengthening
3. Enforcement mechanisms
4. Emergency procedures

1. Entitlement sustainability
