# Consumer Debt: Actions

## Overview

This document outlines concrete actions that individuals, organizations, and communities can take to address the consumer debt crisis. From personal financial empowerment to collective political action, meaningful change requires engagement at multiple levels.

## Individual Actions

### Know Your Rights

**Credit Reporting Rights**

| Right | Law | Action |
|-------|-----|--------|
| Free annual credit report | FCRA | Use AnnualCreditReport.com weekly |
| Dispute inaccurate information | FCRA | File written disputes with bureaus |
| Fraud alerts | FCRA | Place alerts if identity theft suspected |
| Credit freeze | State/federal law | Freeze credit to prevent new accounts |
| Opt-out of prescreened offers | FCRA | OptOutPrescreen.com |

**Debt Collection Rights**

| Right | Law | Action |
|-------|-----|--------|
| Validation notice | FDCPA | Request within 30 days |
| Cease communication | FDCPA | Send written request |
| Sue for violations | FDCPA | Consult attorney if harassed |
| Time-barred debt defense | State law | Know your state's statute of limitations |
| No workplace contact | FDCPA | Inform collector you cannot receive calls at work |

**Student Loan Rights**

| Right | Program | Action |
|-------|---------|--------|
| Income-driven repayment | Federal loans | Apply at studentaid.gov |
| PSLF eligibility | Federal loans | Use PSLF Help Tool |
| Deferment/forbearance | Federal loans | Request when needed |
| Borrower defense | If defrauded | File claim with Dept. of Education |
| Disability discharge | TPD | Apply through Nelnet |

### Financial Empowerment

**Debt Management Strategies**

| Strategy | Best For | How It Works |
|----------|----------|--------------|
| Avalanche method | Minimizing interest | Pay highest rate first |
| Snowball method | Motivation/momentum | Pay smallest balance first |
| Consolidation | Multiple debts | Single payment, potentially lower rate |
| Balance transfer | Credit card debt | 0% promotional rate |
| Refinancing | Private loans | Lower rate if credit improved |

**Building Financial Resilience**

| Action | Target | Method |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Emergency fund | $1,000 minimum | Automatic savings transfers |
| Track spending | Know where money goes | Budgeting apps |
| Reduce high-interest debt | Credit cards first | Extra payments |
| Increase income | Side gig, raises | Skill development |
| Protect credit score | Monitor regularly | Free monitoring services |

### Protecting Yourself

**Before Borrowing**

- [ ] Compare multiple lenders
- [ ] Calculate total cost of borrowing
- [ ] Read all terms and conditions
- [ ] Understand repayment requirements
- [ ] Consider if borrowing is necessary
- [ ] Check lender reputation (CFPB complaints)

**If You're in Debt**

- [ ] Know all your debts (pull credit reports)
- [ ] Prioritize high-interest debt
- [ ] Explore assistance programs
- [ ] Consider nonprofit credit counseling
- [ ] Know bankruptcy options
- [ ] Document all communications with collectors

**Avoiding Predatory Products**

| Red Flag | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| No credit check required | Very high rates likely |
| Extremely easy approval | Predatory terms |
| High pressure sales | Not in your interest |
| Upfront fees | Potential scam |
| Unclear terms | Hidden costs |
| Payday/title loan | 400%+ APR |

## Advocacy Actions

### Contacting Elected Officials

**Federal Level**

| Official | Key Issues | Contact Method |
|----------|------------|----------------|
| U.S. Senators | Student loan forgiveness, interest rate caps | Phone, email, visit |
| U.S. Representatives | CFPB funding, bankruptcy reform | Phone, email, town halls |
| President/Executive Branch | Executive actions on student loans | White House contact form |

**State Level**

| Official | Key Issues | Contact Method |
|----------|------------|----------------|
| Governor | State consumer protection | Phone, email |
| State legislators | Interest rate caps, payday lending | Phone, testimony |
| Attorney General | Enforcement priorities | Written comment |

**Effective Contact Tips**

1. Be specific about the issue and your position
2. Share personal story if relevant
3. Make a clear ask (support specific bill, take action)
4. Follow up
5. Thank officials who take supportive action

**Sample Script (Student Loans)**

> "I'm calling to urge [Representative Name] to support student loan debt relief. I'm one of 45 million Americans burdened by student debt. [Personal detail]. I ask that [specific ask: support bill X, urge administration action, etc.]. This issue affects [number] constituents in your district. Thank you for your consideration."

## Key Takeaways

1. **Know your rights**: Laws protect consumers, but you must know and assert them
2. **Take action on multiple fronts**: Personal, community, and political action all matter
3. **Connect with others**: Collective action more powerful than individual
4. **Share your story**: Personal stories drive policy change
5. **Stay informed**: Track policy developments
6. **Support organizations**: Advocacy groups need resources and volunteers
7. **Vote and engage**: Electoral engagement essential for systemic change
