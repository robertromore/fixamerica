## Medical Debt

### A Uniquely American Crisis

Medical debt distinguishes the U.S. from all peer nations and represents an intersection of healthcare and financial policy failure.

**Scale of the Problem**

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Americans with medical debt | 100 million |
| Total medical debt | $220 billion |
| Medical debt in collections | $88 billion |
| Average medical debt balance | $2,000 |
| Median (those with debt) | $1,000 |

**Who Carries Medical Debt**

| Characteristic | Share with Medical Debt |
|----------------|------------------------|
| Uninsured | 47% |
| Medicaid | 28% |
| Employer insurance | 18% |
| Medicare | 12% |
| Income under $40,000 | 31% |
| Income over $100,000 | 12% |
| Black Americans | 28% |
| White Americans | 17% |

### Medical Bankruptcy

| Statistic | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Bankruptcies with medical component | 62% |
| Annual medical bankruptcies | ~500,000 |
| Insured filers with medical debt | 78% |
| Average medical debt in bankruptcy | $12,000 |

### Common Causes of Medical Debt

1. **Surprise billing** - Out-of-network charges
2. **High deductibles** - Average $1,700 individual
3. **Uncovered services** - Denials and exclusions
4. **Chronic illness** - Ongoing treatment costs
5. **Emergency care** - Unplanned expenses
6. **Cancer treatment** - Average out-of-pocket $12,000/year

### Recent Developments

**No Surprises Act (2022)**

- Bans surprise bills for emergency care
- Limits out-of-network charges at in-network facilities
- Establishes arbitration process
- Early evidence shows reduced surprise bills

**Credit Reporting Changes**

- Three bureaus removing medical debt under $500 (2023)
- Paid medical debt no longer reported (2022)
- Unpaid medical debt 1-year waiting period before reporting

## Payday and Small-Dollar Lending

### Predatory Lending Landscape

**Payday Loan Statistics**

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Annual borrowers | 12 million |
| Average loan amount | $375 |
| Average fees per loan | $55 |
| Effective APR | 400% |
| Average borrower loans/year | 8 |
| Total fees paid | $9 billion |

**The Debt Trap Cycle**

```text
Loan → 2-Week Term → Can't Repay → Rollover → New Fees → Repeat

Average payday borrower:
- Takes 8 loans per year
- Pays $520 in fees on $375 borrowed
- Is in debt 200+ days per year
```

### State Regulation

| Regulatory Approach | States | Impact |
|--------------------|--------|--------|
| APR caps (36% or lower) | 18 + DC | Eliminates most payday lending |
| Rate caps (higher than 36%) | 8 | Some restrictions |
| Fee/term limits | 14 | Moderate restrictions |
| Minimal regulation | 10 | Full predatory lending |

### Alternatives Underdeveloped

- Credit union payday alternatives: Limited availability
- Bank small-dollar loans: Few offerings
- Postal banking: Not available
- Employer paycheck advances: Growing but limited

### Student Loan Discharge

- **Undue hardship** standard: extremely difficult to meet
- Less than **0.1%** of student loan borrowers attempt discharge
- Of those attempting, **40%** receive some relief
- Brunner test requires proof of "certainty of hopelessness"

## Key Takeaways

1. **Debt burden is substantial**: $17+ trillion total, $104,000 per household
2. **Student debt unique crisis**: $1.77 trillion, 45 million borrowers
3. **Medical debt is policy failure**: 100 million affected, 62% of bankruptcies
4. **Credit cards at record**: $1.14 trillion at 27% average APR
5. **Predatory lending persists**: 12 million payday borrowers paying 400% APR
6. **Racial disparities severe**: Black borrowers face higher rates, defaults, collections
7. **System access limited**: Bankruptcy difficult, credit reporting flawed
8. **Recent reforms mixed**: Some progress, ongoing litigation
