# Consumer Debt: Solutions

## Guiding Principles

### Criteria for Evaluating Reforms

| Criterion | Definition | Why It Matters |
|-----------|------------|----------------|
| **Relieves current burden** | Addresses existing debt | People suffering now need help |
| **Prevents future debt** | Addresses root causes | Sustainable solution |
| **Protects vulnerable** | Prioritizes those most harmed | Equity focus |
| **Economically sound** | Benefits outweigh costs | Political viability |
| **Administratively feasible** | Can be implemented | Practical impact |
| **Politically durable** | Can withstand opposition | Long-term change |

### The Framework

Comprehensive debt reform requires three simultaneous approaches:

1. **Relief**: Address existing debt burden
2. **Prevention**: Stop predatory lending and address root causes
3. **Alternatives**: Create better options for credit needs

## Student Loan Solutions

### Tier 1: Debt Cancellation

**Broad Federal Loan Forgiveness**

*Key Provisions*

- Cancel $50,000 per borrower (Warren/Schumer proposal)
- Income-targeted ($125,000 individual / $250,000 household)
- All federal loan types included
- Automatic; no application required

*Evidence*

- $1.77 trillion in federal loans outstanding
- $50K cap covers 84% of borrowers entirely
- Economic stimulus: $173 billion in first year
- Disproportionately benefits Black borrowers

*Advantages*

- Immediate relief for 45 million
- Administrative simplicity (already have data)
- Addresses racial wealth gap
- Economic multiplier effects

*Challenges*

- Cost ($500-1,000 billion depending on design)
- Supreme Court limited executive authority
- "Unfairness" political argument
- Congressional action likely needed

**Targeted Forgiveness Programs**

| Program | Eligibility | Relief |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| Public Service Loan Forgiveness | 10 years public service | Full forgiveness |
| Teacher Loan Forgiveness | 5 years teaching | $5,000-$17,500 |
| Income-Driven Forgiveness | 20-25 years of payments | Remaining balance |
| Borrower Defense | Defrauded by school | Full forgiveness |
| Total/Permanent Disability | Unable to work | Full forgiveness |
| Closed School Discharge | School closed | Full forgiveness |

### Tier 2: Structural Reform

**Free Public College**

*How It Works*

- Federal-state partnership
- Federal government provides funding
- States agree to eliminate tuition at public institutions
- Maintenance of effort requirements

*Evidence*

- Many states had near-free public college until 1980s
- Other countries (Germany, Norway, etc.) provide free university
- Tennessee, New York have pilot programs
- Cost: $80-100 billion annually

*Advantages*

- Eliminates future student debt (public schools)
- Makes education decision about merit, not money
- Reduces inequality
- Long-term investment in human capital

*Challenges*

- Federal-state coordination
- Private school concerns
- Funding sustainability
- Political opposition

**Student Loan Interest Rate Reform**

| Reform | Current | Proposed |
|--------|---------|----------|
| Interest rate | 5-8% federal | 0% or cost of borrowing |
| Capitalization | Interest adds to principal | No capitalization |
| While in school | Interest accrues | No interest accrual |
| IBR interest | Forgiven after years | Never accrues in IBR |

**Income-Driven Repayment Improvement**

*SAVE Plan Features (2023)*

- Payments 5% discretionary income (undergrad)
- 225% poverty line protected
- No interest capitalization
- 10-year forgiveness for balances under $12K

*Further Improvements Needed*

- Automatic enrollment
- Simpler application
- Better servicer accountability
- Tax treatment of forgiveness

### Tier 3: Accountability

**For-Profit College Reform**

| Reform | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| Gainful employment rule | Programs must lead to jobs |
| 90/10 rule strengthening | Limit federal aid dependence |
| Cohort default rate consequences | Hold schools accountable |
| Recruitment restrictions | End predatory marketing |
| Borrower defense processing | Help defrauded students |

**Servicer Accountability**

- Performance-based contracts
- Strong oversight and penalties
- Borrower satisfaction metrics
- Error rate consequences

## Medical Debt Solutions

### Tier 1: Debt Relief

**Medical Debt Forgiveness Programs**

*State/Local Programs*

| Jurisdiction | Approach | Amount |
|--------------|----------|--------|
| Cook County, IL | Buy and forgive | $1 billion |
| Toledo, OH | ARP funds | $240 million |
| New Orleans | City funds | $130 million |
| Arizona | State funds | Proposed |

*Federal Approach*

- Create federal fund to purchase and forgive medical debt
- $10 billion would clear most medical debt in collections
- Purchase at pennies on dollar through RIP Medical Debt model
- Target underserved communities

**Credit Reporting Reform**

*Recent Changes*

- Medical debt under $500 excluded (2023)
- Paid medical debt removed
- One-year delay before reporting unpaid

*Further Reform Needed*

- Remove all medical debt from credit reports
- Medical debt unique; reflects system failure
- Not predictive of creditworthiness

### Tier 2: Prevention

**Universal Healthcare**

The only complete solution to medical debt:

| System | Medical Debt | Bankruptcy |
|--------|--------------|------------|
| Single-payer | Near zero | Near zero |
| Universal multi-payer | Very low | Very low |
| Current U.S. system | $220 billion | 500,000/year |

**Incremental Reforms**

| Reform | Mechanism | Impact |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Public option | Competition | Lower premiums |
| Medicare buy-in (50+) | Coverage expansion | Protect near-elderly |
| Medicaid expansion | Full ACA implementation | Cover 2+ million |
| Subsidy increases | ACA enhancement | Reduce out-of-pocket |

**Hospital Accountability**

*Financial Assistance Requirements*

- Strengthen nonprofit hospital charity care obligations
- Publicize availability (currently hidden)
- Standardize application processes
- Enforce in exchange for tax exemption

*Price Transparency*

- Enforce hospital price transparency rules
- Require good-faith estimates
- Penalize noncompliance

### Tier 3: Collection Reform

**Limit Collection Powers**

| Current Practice | Reform |
|------------------|--------|
| Wage garnishment | Prohibit for medical debt |
| Credit reporting | Remove medical debt |
| Lawsuit filing | Require verification |
| Interest accrual | Cap at 0% for medical |
| Collection timeline | Limit to 3 years |

**Hospital Collection Practices**

- Screen for financial assistance before collection
- Prohibit collection while assistance pending
- End "extraordinary collection actions" on eligible patients
- Require binding payment plans

## Credit Card and Interest Rate Solutions

### Interest Rate Caps

**Federal Usury Law**

*Key Provisions*

- 36% APR cap on all consumer credit (military standard)
- Includes all fees in APR calculation
- Preempts state law only upward (states can go lower)
- Applies to all lenders, including fintech

*Evidence*

- Military Lending Act shows 36% cap viable
- States with caps don't show credit deserts
- CFPB found credit remains available
- Industry profits would remain substantial

*Advantages*

- Eliminates worst predatory lending
- Simple, clear standard
- Already applies to military
- Precedent in most other countries

*Challenges*

- Industry opposition
- Claims of credit rationing
- Preemption of higher state caps
- Online lending enforcement

### Fee Reform

**CFPB Junk Fee Proposal**

| Fee | Current | Proposed |
|-----|---------|----------|
| Late fees | Up to $41 | $8 max |
| Over-limit fees | Common | Prohibited unless opted in |
| Annual fees | Varies | Disclosed prominently |
| Cash advance fees | 3-5% | Clearer disclosure |

### Transparency Improvements

- Standardized credit card agreement
- Payoff calculator on every statement
- Total cost of credit disclosure
- Comparison shopping tools

## Payday Lending Solutions

### Option 1: Interest Rate Cap

**36% APR Cap**

- Effectively eliminates payday lending model
- $15 per $100 fee maximum (vs. current $15 per 2 weeks)
- States with caps show model works
- 18 states + DC already have effective caps

### Option 2: Structural Reform

**Ability-to-Repay Requirement**

- Lenders must verify income and expenses
- Cannot lend if repayment would cause hardship
- CFPB rule (weakened under Trump) provides template
- Prevents debt trap cycle

**Loan Limits**

| Feature | Reform |
|---------|--------|
| Rollover limit | 1 maximum |
| Cooling-off period | 30 days between loans |
| Database | Track lending across lenders |
| Payment structure | Must reduce principal |

### Option 3: Better Alternatives

**Postal Banking**

*Key Features*

- Small-dollar loans through post offices
- Basic banking services
- Already exists in other countries
- Serves 6,000+ locations including rural areas

*Services Could Include*

| Service | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Small loans | Up to $1,000, 28% APR max |
| Check cashing | At cost |
| Bill payment | At cost |
| Savings accounts | Basic, insured |
| Debit cards | No overdraft fees |

**Credit Union Alternatives**

- Payday Alternative Loans (PALs)
- Up to $2,000
- 28% APR maximum
- 1-6 month terms
- Requires credit union membership

**Emergency Savings Programs**

- Automatic enrollment in workplace savings
- Prize-linked savings accounts
- Match programs for emergency funds
- Reduces need for emergency credit

## Bankruptcy Reform

### Student Loan Discharge

**Reform Options**

| Option | Mechanism |
|--------|-----------|
| Restore full discharge | Treat like other unsecured debt |
| 10-year waiting period | Discharge after attempting repayment |
| Hardship test reform | Brunner test elimination |
| Partial discharge | Income-based reduction |

**Fresh Start Through Bankruptcy Act**

- Bipartisan bill
- Would restore discharge after 10 years
- Or after demonstrated hardship earlier
- Addresses "undue hardship" problem

### Broader Access Improvements

**Fee Waivers**

- Expand income-based fee waivers
- Reduce filing complexity
- Increase pro bono representation

**Means Test Reform**

- Simplify Chapter 7 means test
- Recognize medical and student debt
- Regional cost of living adjustments

**Homestead Exemptions**

- Federal minimum exemption
- Prevent state shopping
- Protect primary residence

## Credit Reporting Reform

### Accuracy Improvement

**Reform Measures**

| Issue | Reform |
|-------|--------|
| Error rates high | Free weekly credit reports permanent |
| Disputes difficult | Burden of proof on bureaus |
| Reinsertion | Prohibit without new verification |
| Data mixing | Require better matching algorithms |
| Consumer notification | Alert when report accessed |

### Limiting Negative Information

**Time Limits**

| Information Type | Current | Proposed |
|------------------|---------|----------|
| Bankruptcies | 7-10 years | 5 years |
| Collections | 7 years | 3 years |
| Late payments | 7 years | 3-5 years |
| Medical debt | 1 year (new) | Eliminate entirely |

### Alternative Models

**Public Credit Registry**

- Government-operated alternative
- Focus on accuracy over profit
- Consumer-controlled access
- Transparent algorithms

**Non-Debt-Based Credit Scoring**

- Rent payment history
- Utility payment history
- Income verification
- Cash flow analysis

## Debt Collection Reform

### CFPB Regulation F Improvements

**Additional Reforms Needed**

| Area | Reform |
|------|--------|
| Contact frequency | Limit to 3/week total (not per debt) |
| Time of contact | Daytime hours only |
| Workplace contact | Prohibit entirely |
| Electronic contact | Require opt-in |
| Debt validation | Within 5 days, detailed |
| Statute of limitations | Prohibit collection on time-barred debt |

### Litigation Reform

**Protect Debtors in Court**

- Require debt documentation
- Prohibit default judgments without service proof
- Mandate legal representation in garnishment hearings
- Cap garnishment at 10% of income
- Exempt public benefits entirely

### Zombie Debt Prohibition

- Time-barred debt cannot be collected
- Cannot re-age debts
- Cannot sue on discharged debts
- Penalties for violations

## Comprehensive Solutions

### The Debt Jubilee Model

*Concept*

- Periodic debt forgiveness programs
- Biblical precedent (every 50 years)
- Modern adaptation: targeted relief

*Implementation*

- Means-tested relief
- Focus on oldest debts
- Priority for medical, student, emergency debt
- Paired with prevention measures

### Universal Basic Services

*Preventing Debt Through Public Provision*

| Service | Mechanism | Debt Prevented |
|---------|-----------|----------------|
| Healthcare | Single-payer | Medical debt |
| Education | Free public | Student debt |
| Childcare | Public provision | Credit card |
| Housing | Public investment | Housing burden |
| Transportation | Public transit | Auto loan |

### Financial Education (Limited Effectiveness)

*What Works*

- Just-in-time education (at point of decision)
- Counseling for existing debtors
- Simple, actionable information

*What Doesn't Work*

- High school financial literacy courses (no lasting effect)
- Complex planning requirements
- Victim-blaming approaches

## International Models

### Germany

- Free university education
- Universal healthcare
- Strong consumer protection
- Household debt/GDP: 54%

### Australia

- Income-contingent student loans
- Loans forgiven after death/long periods
- Universal healthcare
- Household debt/GDP: 120% (housing-driven)

### United Kingdom

- Income-contingent loans forgiven after 30 years
- National Health Service
- Strong debt advice sector
- FCA regulation of consumer credit

### Japan

- Low household debt culture
- Universal healthcare
- Limited consumer credit reliance
- Household debt/GDP: 65%

## Key Takeaways

1. **Relief and prevention needed**: Address existing debt AND root causes
2. **Universal programs most effective**: Healthcare, education solve debt at source
3. **Interest rate caps work**: 36% cap eliminates worst predatory lending
4. **Bankruptcy should include student loans**: Restore discharge availability
5. **Medical debt is healthcare policy failure**: Only universal coverage fully addresses
6. **Credit reporting needs overhaul**: Consumer-focused rather than lender-focused
7. **Alternatives to predatory lending**: Postal banking, credit unions, emergency savings
8. **Debt collection must be reformed**: Current practices are abusive
