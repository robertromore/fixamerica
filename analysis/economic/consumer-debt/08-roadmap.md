# Consumer Debt: Implementation Roadmap

## Overview

Addressing America's consumer debt crisis requires a phased approach that provides immediate relief while building toward structural reform. This roadmap outlines a realistic sequence of reforms over 10+ years, accounting for political constraints, administrative capacity, and the need for durable change.

## Phase 2: Foundation Building (Years 2-4)

### Legislative Agenda

**Consumer Debt Protection Act**

*Components*

- Federal 36% APR cap
- Comprehensive usury law
- Closes bank partnership loopholes
- Creates enforcement mechanisms

*Strategy*

1. Build coalition (consumer groups, faith communities, civil rights)
2. State-level wins build momentum
3. Media campaign on predatory lending harms
4. Push during Democratic trifecta window

**Student Debt Relief Act**

*Components*

- Statutory authority for broad forgiveness
- Cap at $50,000 per borrower
- Income targeting
- Future borrower protections

*Strategy*

1. Build public support (62%+ favorable)
2. Counter "unfairness" narrative
3. Economic stimulus framing
4. Address Supreme Court concerns

**Medical Debt Relief Act**

*Components*

- Federal fund for debt purchase/forgiveness
- Hospital accountability requirements
- Credit reporting prohibition
- Collection practice limits

*Strategy*

1. Attach to healthcare legislation
2. Emphasize COVID impact
3. Hospital accountability framing
4. State pilot program evidence

### Messaging Framework

**For General Public**

- "The system is rigged against working families"
- "Debt shouldn't be a life sentence"
- "Other countries don't have this problem"
- "Relief AND reform, not one or the other"

**For Skeptics**

- "This is about fixing a broken system"
- "Your taxes already subsidize predatory lenders"
- "Debt traps hurt the economy"

**For Policymakers**

- Economic stimulus effects
- Racial equity implications

### Overcoming Opposition

**Industry Counter-Strategies**

| Industry Tactic | Counter-Strategy |
|-----------------|------------------|
| "Credit will disappear" | Evidence from rate cap states |
| "Personal responsibility" | System designed to trap |
| "Unfair to those who paid" | Progress doesn't require past suffering |
| "Moral hazard" | Current system has moral hazard |
| Campaign contributions | Small-donor mobilization |

**Building Durable Change**

- Constitutional amendments where possible
- Broad-based programs (harder to cut)
- Administrative capacity building
- Public engagement and education
- Documentation of success

## Key Takeaways

1. **Executive action first**: Use existing authority immediately
2. **State labs**: Build momentum through state-level wins
3. **Sequence matters**: Relief enables structural reform politically
4. **Build coalitions**: Broad support essential for durability
5. **Anticipate opposition**: Prepare counter-narratives
6. **Measure progress**: Data-driven adjustment
7. **Play long game**: Structural change takes decade+
