# Corporate Governance Reform: Roadmap

## Overview

Transforming American corporate governance from shareholder primacy to stakeholder capitalism requires a multi-decade strategy combining incremental wins with movement building for fundamental reform. This roadmap outlines a realistic path forward.

## Strategic Framework

### Theory of Change

| Level | Strategy |
|-------|----------|
| Ideas | Shift discourse from shareholder primacy to stakeholder capitalism |
| Institutions | Build organizations and coalitions for reform |
| Incentives | Change what corporations are rewarded for |
| Laws | Enact binding requirements |
| Enforcement | Ensure accountability |

### Sequencing Logic

| Phase | Focus | Why First |
|-------|-------|-----------|
| Disclosure | Transparency reforms | Builds case for further reform |
| Incentives | Compensation, buyback reform | Changes corporate behavior |
| Structure | Board composition, worker voice | Creates new power centers |
| Purpose | Stakeholder duties, federal chartering | Transforms fundamental purpose |

## Phase 4: Fundamental Reform (Years 10-20)

### Federal Chartering

**Building Support**

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| Academic consensus | Research and advocacy |
| Political alignment | Coalition building |
| Crisis opportunity | Be ready with proposals |
| Legislative push | Introduction and advocacy |

**Implementation Design**

| Element | Preparation |
|---------|-------------|
| Charter requirements | Draft model federal charter |
| Agency structure | Design enforcement body |
| Transition rules | Grandfather existing corporations |

### Stakeholder Duties

**Constitutional Considerations**

| Issue | Strategy |
|-------|----------|
| Property rights | Frame as regulation, not taking |
| Contract freedom | Emphasize chartering as privilege |
| Commerce clause | Federal power clear |

**Legislative Path**

| Approach | Timing |
|----------|--------|
| Permissive stakeholder duties | Earlier |
| Mandatory stakeholder duties | Later |
| Full federal corporate law | Long-term |
