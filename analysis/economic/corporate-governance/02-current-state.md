# Corporate Governance Reform: Current State

## The Problem Today

### Shareholder Primacy in Practice

The dominant ideology governing American corporations holds that the sole purpose of business is to maximize shareholder value. This doctrine, though not legally mandated, has become entrenched through:

**Legal Framework**

| Jurisdiction | Rule |
|--------------|------|
| Delaware (68% of Fortune 500) | Directors owe fiduciary duty to shareholders |
| Most states | Business judgment rule protects shareholder-focused decisions |
| Federal securities law | Quarterly reporting encourages short-term focus |
| Court precedent | Revlon doctrine requires shareholder value maximization in sales |

**Corporate Behavior**

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| S&P 500 buybacks (2010-2019) | $5.3 trillion |
| S&P 500 dividends (2010-2019) | $3.8 trillion |
| Buybacks + dividends as % of profits | 91% |
| Capital expenditure growth (decade avg) | 1.5% annually |

## Key Statistics

### Corporate Purpose

**Business Roundtable Statement (2019)**

- 181 CEOs signed statement endorsing stakeholder capitalism
- Follow-up studies found no change in actual behavior
- Stock buybacks increased after statement
- Worker layoffs continued at same pace

**Stakeholder Treatment**

| Stakeholder | Treatment |
|-------------|-----------|
| Shareholders | Record returns, dividends, buybacks |
| Executives | Compensation at all-time highs |
| Workers | Wage stagnation, benefit cuts |
| Communities | Plant closures, tax avoidance |
| Environment | Limited voluntary action |
