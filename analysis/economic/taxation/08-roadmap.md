# Taxation: Roadmap

## Overview

Achieving meaningful tax reform requires a strategic, phased approach that builds power over time while exploiting political opportunities. This roadmap outlines a path from current conditions to a fairer, more adequate tax system over 10-15 years.

## Strategic Context

### Current Political Landscape

| Factor | Status |
|--------|--------|
| 2025 TCJA expirations | Major legislative window |
| IRS funding | Partially restored but vulnerable |
| International coordination | OECD minimum tax advancing |
| Public opinion | Supports taxing wealthy, enforcement |
| Political division | Major reform difficult |

### Windows of Opportunity

| Opportunity | Timing | Potential |
|-------------|--------|-----------|
| TCJA expiration | 2025 | High (must-pass legislation) |
| Budget reconciliation | Any unified government | Medium |
| International agreement | Ongoing | OECD minimum tax |
| State action | Continuous | Laboratory for reform |
| Crisis response | Unpredictable | Revenue needs during crisis |

## Phase 1: Foundation (Years 1-3)

### Objective

Protect and expand enforcement capacity; enact achievable reforms; build infrastructure for larger change.

### Priority Actions

#### 1.1 Protect IRS Funding

| Action | Timeline | Actor |
|--------|----------|-------|
| Defend Inflation Reduction Act funding | Immediate | Congress |
| Hire and train enforcement staff | Years 1-3 | IRS |
| Technology modernization | Years 1-5 | IRS |
| Focus on high-income enforcement | Immediate | IRS |

**Metrics**:

- Audit rates for high-income taxpayers
- Revenue collected per enforcement dollar
- Technology implementation milestones

#### 1.2 Close Carried Interest Loophole

| Action | Timeline | Actor |
|--------|----------|-------|
| Build bipartisan coalition | Year 1 | Advocacy groups |
| Regulatory approach if legislation fails | Year 1-2 | Treasury |
| Standalone legislation | Year 1-2 | Congress |

**Talking Points**:

- "Billionaire hedge fund managers pay lower rates than nurses"
- Bipartisan rhetorical support (Trump, Obama)
- Narrow, targeted reform
- $15 billion in revenue

#### 1.3 Enhanced Reporting Requirements

| Cryptocurrency reporting | Year 1-2 | Treasury |

#### 1.4 State-Level Action

## Phase 2: TCJA Opportunity (Years 3-5)

### Objective

Use 2025 TCJA expirations to reshape individual tax code toward greater progressivity.

### The 2025 Moment

**Provisions Expiring**:

| Provision | Effect of Expiration |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Individual rate cuts | Rates return to 39.6% top rate |
| $10,000 SALT cap | Cap expires |
| 20% pass-through deduction | Deduction expires |
| Doubled standard deduction | Returns to lower amount |
| Increased child tax credit | Reduces |

**Strategic Opportunity**: Must-pass legislation creates leverage for broader reform.

### Priority Actions

#### 2.1 Individual Rate Reform

| Action | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Allow high-income cuts to expire | Top rate returns to 39.6% |
| Add millionaire surtax | 5% additional on income over $1M |
| Make middle-class cuts permanent | Extend 12%, 22% brackets |
| Expand EITC and CTC | Refundable credits for workers |

**Distributional Goal**: Top 1% pays more; bottom 80% pays same or less.

#### 2.2 Capital Gains Reform

| Action | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Increase capital gains rates | 28% for high earners |
| Require realization at death | End stepped-up basis benefit |
| Limit deferral mechanisms | Opportunity zones, like-kind exchanges |

#### 2.3 Pass-Through Reform

| Action | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| End Section 199A deduction | Expires anyway |
| Partnership anti-abuse rules | Prevent labor income conversion |
| Self-employment tax expansion | Cover more income types |

#### 2.4 SALT Deduction Resolution

| Option | Who Supports |
|--------|--------------|
| Eliminate cap | High-tax state Democrats |
| Convert to credit | Progressives |
| Maintain cap | Deficit hawks, Republicans |
| Partial increase | Compromise position |

**Recommended**: Convert to flat credit (same dollar value regardless of bracket).

### Phase 2 Deliverables

| Deliverable | Metric |
|-------------|--------|
| Progressive rate structure | Top rate 39.6%+ with surtax |
| Capital gains reformed | Higher rates, death realization |
| Pass-through equalized | 199A expired, anti-abuse rules |
| Middle class protected | Effective rate stable or lower |

## Phase 3: Corporate Reform (Years 4-7)

### Objective

Raise corporate revenue and close international loopholes through domestic and international action.

### Priority Actions

#### 3.1 Domestic Corporate Reform

| Action | Timeline | Revenue |
|--------|----------|---------|
| Increase corporate rate to 28% | Year 4-5 | $700B/10 years |
| Strengthen book minimum tax | Year 4-5 | Significant |
| Limit stock buyback benefits | Year 4-6 | Moderate |
| Close depreciation loopholes | Year 5-7 | Moderate |

#### 3.2 International Tax Reform

| Action | Timeline | Mechanism |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| OECD Pillar Two implementation | Year 4-5 | Legislative |
| GILTI reform | Year 4-6 | Legislative |
| Country-by-country minimum | Year 5-7 | Legislative |
| Formulary apportionment study | Year 5-7 | Treasury |

**International Coordination**:

- Work with OECD partners
- Prevent race to bottom
- Address tax havens cooperatively

#### 3.3 Industry-Specific Reform

| Industry | Reform | Timeline |
|----------|--------|----------|
| Oil and gas | End fossil fuel preferences | Year 5-6 |
| Real estate | Reform 1031, depreciation | Year 5-7 |
| Pharmaceuticals | Domestic production requirements | Year 5-7 |
| Financial services | Carried interest, derivatives | Years 1-3 (earlier) |

### Phase 3 Deliverables

| Deliverable | Metric |
|-------------|--------|
| Corporate rate increased | 28% statutory rate |
| International loopholes closed | GILTI strengthened |
| Industry subsidies reformed | Key preferences eliminated |
| Revenue increased | $100B+ annually |

### Objective

Implement more fundamental reforms including wealth taxation and comprehensive enforcement.

### Priority Actions

#### 4.1 Wealth Tax or Alternative

|------|----------|
| Constitutional analysis | Year 7 |
| Legislation drafted | Year 7-8 |
| Enactment (if constitutional) | Year 8-9 |
| Implementation | Year 9-11 |

**Path B: Billionaire Minimum Tax**

| Step | Timeline |
|------|----------|
| Legislation drafted | Year 7 |
| Build support | Year 7-8 |
| Enactment | Year 8-9 |
| Implementation | Year 9-10 |

**Path C: Constitutional Amendment** (if needed)

| Step | Timeline |
|------|----------|
| State resolution campaign | Years 5-10 |
| Congressional proposal | Year 10+ |
| Ratification | Year 12-15 |

#### 4.2 Estate Tax Transformation

| Action | Timeline |
|--------|----------|
| Lower exemption to $3.5M | Year 7-8 |
| Increase rates to 45-55% | Year 7-8 |
| Close GRAT, valuation loopholes | Year 7-9 |
| Dynasty trust reform | Year 8-10 |

#### 4.3 Mark-to-Market Taxation

| Step | Timeline |
|------|----------|
| Study and design | Year 7-8 |
| Phased implementation | Year 9-11 |
| Public market assets first | Year 9 |
| Private assets later | Year 10-11 |

#### 4.4 IRS Transformation

| Action | Timeline |
|--------|----------|
| Full technology modernization | Year 7-10 |
| Direct filing nationwide | Year 8-10 |
| Real-time information matching | Year 8-12 |
| Automatic enforcement for simple cases | Year 10-12 |

### Phase 4 Deliverables

| Deliverable | Metric |
|-------------|--------|
| Wealth taxed annually | Wealth tax or minimum tax enacted |
| Estate tax strengthened | $3.5M exemption, 45%+ rate |

## Risk Management

### Key Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Political reversal | Entrench reforms, build public support |
| Constitutional challenge | Careful drafting, backup plans |
| Capital flight | Exit taxes, international coordination |
| Avoidance innovation | Anti-abuse rules, continuous updating |
| Economic downturn | Countercyclical design |

### Contingency Plans

| Scenario | Response |
|----------|----------|
| Court strikes down wealth tax | Pivot to minimum tax or amendment |
| Republican unified government | Defend IRS, state action |
| Economic crisis | Revenue needs create opportunity |
| International cooperation fails | Unilateral measures |
