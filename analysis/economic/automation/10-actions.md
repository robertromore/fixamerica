## For Communities

### Local Reform Campaigns

**Assess the Landscape**

1. Research local industries facing automation
2. Identify workers at risk
3. Map existing workforce programs
4. Assess political leadership receptiveness

**Build a Coalition**

1. Recruit diverse leadership
2. Engage labor organizations
3. Connect with faith communities
4. Partner with community organizations
5. Include affected workers' voices

**Choose Your Reform**

| Reform | Best For |
|--------|----------|
| **Workforce investment** | Communities with training infrastructure |
| **Automation disclosure** | Cities with large automated employers |
| **UBI pilots** | Cities with supportive leadership |
| **Targeted transition programs** | Regions facing specific industry disruption |

## For Advocates

### Running a Local Campaign

**Phase 1: Preparation**

1. Research automation trends in your area
2. Identify affected workers and industries
3. Build core team and leadership
4. Develop budget and fundraising plan
5. Craft messaging strategy

**Phase 2: Coalition Building**

1. Recruit diverse visible leaders
2. Engage labor organizations
3. Build faith community support
4. Connect with affected workers
5. Document coalition publicly

**Phase 3: Campaign**

1. If ballot initiative: gather signatures
2. If council adoption: lobby members
3. Public education campaign
4. Earned media strategy
5. Mobilize public support

**Phase 4: Implementation**

1. Work with officials on rollout
2. Worker education about new programs
3. Employer engagement
4. Defend against challenges
5. Document success

### Advocacy Best Practices

**Messaging Principles**

- Lead with worker stories
- Focus on dignity and fairness
- Connect automation to broader inequality
- Counter "inevitable" framing
- Present positive vision

**Coalition Building**

- Labor/community/faith alliances
- Include tech workers concerned about ethics
- Engage affected communities directly
- Give partners ownership and visibility
- Maintain coalition through implementation

**Working with Media**

- Build relationships with local reporters
- Provide clear, quotable soundbites
- Offer data and research
- Respond quickly to industry claims
- Submit regular op-eds

### State-Level Advocacy

**Legislative Strategy**

1. Identify legislative champions
2. Draft or support specific legislation
3. Build constituent pressure
4. Testify at hearings
5. Track and publicize votes

**Key State Targets**

| Policy | Pathway |
|--------|---------|
| Automation disclosure | Legislation |
| Workforce investment | Budget process |
| UBI pilots | Legislation + appropriation |
| Unemployment modernization | Legislation |
