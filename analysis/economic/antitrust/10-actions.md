# Antitrust: Actions

## Individual Actions

### Stay Informed

**Follow Antitrust News**

| Source | Type | Access |
|--------|------|--------|
| Matt Stoller's "BIG" newsletter | Analysis | Substack (free) |
| Capitol Forum | News service | Subscription |
| ProMarket | Academic blog | Free |
| Antitrust case filings | Primary sources | PACER, agency websites |

**Understand Your Industries**

- Research concentration in sectors you work in or buy from
- Track mergers in industries that affect you
- Understand how market power affects your daily life

### Consumer Actions

**Diversify Where Possible**

| Monopoly | Alternative |
|----------|-------------|
| Google Search | DuckDuckGo, Brave Search, Bing |
| Chrome browser | Firefox, Brave, Safari |
| Amazon shopping | Direct from manufacturers, local retail |
| Meta social media | Mastodon, alternative platforms |
| Big bank | Credit unions, community banks |

**Vote with Your Dollars**

- Support local and independent businesses
- Use credit unions instead of big banks
- Buy from farmers markets when possible
- Choose independent pharmacies, restaurants, retailers

**Data Protection**

- Limit data shared with dominant platforms
- Use privacy-focused alternatives
- Understand terms of service
- Exercise data rights where available

### Worker Actions

**Know Your Rights**

| Issue | Action |
|-------|--------|
| Non-compete agreements | Know if enforceable in your state |
| No-poach agreements | Report violations to FTC |
| Wage information | Understand labor market |
| Unionization | Exercise collective bargaining rights |

**Job Market Concentration**

- Research employer concentration in your field
- Consider geographic mobility
- Build skills that work across employers
- Support union organizing

### Small Business Actions

**Document Concentration Harms**

- Track platform fees and changes
- Document unfair treatment by dominant firms
- Record supplier concentration problems
- Keep records for potential investigations

**Participate in Proceedings**

- Comment on FTC and DOJ investigations
- Submit information to congressional investigations
- Work with trade associations on antitrust issues

## Civic Engagement

### Contact Elected Officials

**Key Messages**

| Audience | Message |
|----------|---------|
| Senators | Support antitrust legislation |
| Representatives | Fund enforcement agencies |
| State legislators | State-level competition laws |
| State AG | Investigate local concentration |

**How to Be Effective**

1. Be specific about antitrust concerns in your area
2. Share personal stories of monopoly harm
3. Name specific legislation to support
4. Follow up with office staff

### Engage with Agencies

**FTC Public Comment**

- Comment on proposed rules
- Provide information about anticompetitive conduct
- Support enforcement actions
- Attend public workshops

**Submit Complaints**

| Agency | Complaint Type | How to Submit |
|--------|----------------|---------------|
| FTC | Unfair competition | ftc.gov/complaint |
| DOJ Antitrust | Criminal violations | justice.gov/atr |
| State AG | State-level violations | State AG website |

### Vote

**Federal Elections**

- Research candidates' antitrust positions
- Support candidates who favor enforcement
- Prioritize antitrust in policy questionnaires

**State Elections**

- Attorney General races matter most
- State legislators can pass competition laws
- Governors appoint state regulators

**Organizations to Support**

**Ways to Support**

- Attend events

## Professional Actions

### Lawyers

**Pro Bono Work**

- Represent small businesses in antitrust matters
- Support class action litigation
- Assist reform organizations

**Professional Organizations**

- Engage with ABA Antitrust Section
- Present on reform issues
- Write for legal publications

### Economists

**Research**

- Study concentration in understudied markets
- Provide empirical support for reform
- Critique Chicago School assumptions

**Expert Testimony**

- Support enforcement actions
- Testify in legislative hearings
- Assist reform organizations

### Journalists

**Coverage Priorities**

- Local concentration stories
- Merger impacts on communities
- Worker experiences with monopsony
- Political economy of antitrust

**Investigative Projects**

- Industry-specific concentration
- Killer acquisitions
- Platform abuse
- Labor market suppression

### Academics

**Research Agenda**

- Historical antitrust analysis
- Empirical concentration studies
- Labor market monopsony
- Platform economics

**Public Engagement**

- Op-eds and policy briefs
- Congressional testimony
- Media commentary
- Student education

### Tech Workers

**Internal Advocacy**

- Raise concerns about anticompetitive practices
- Document problematic policies
- Support ethical tech initiatives

**Whistleblowing**

- Report antitrust violations to agencies
- Work with journalists on investigations
- Know legal protections
