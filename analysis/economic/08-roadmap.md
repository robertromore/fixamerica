# Economic Reform: Roadmap

## Overview

Economic reform requires a phased approach that:

1. Builds power through early wins
2. Sequences reforms based on dependencies
3. Coordinates political and economic strategies
4. Maintains flexibility to respond to opportunities
5. Sustains momentum over decades

The roadmap below presents an ambitious but achievable path, recognizing that progress will be uneven and setbacks will occur.

## Dependencies

### What Must Come First

```text
Political Reform
       ↓
Reduce Money in Politics → More Responsive Congress
       ↓
Tax Reform Possible → Revenue for Investment
       ↓
Public Investment → Economic Growth for All
       ↓
Worker Power → Sustained Broad Prosperity
```

### Reform Dependencies

| This Reform | Requires | Enables |
|-------------|----------|---------|
| Campaign finance reform | Political organizing | All other reforms easier |
| Tax enforcement | IRS funding | Revenue for programs |
| Antitrust enforcement | Agency funding, judicial appointments | Competitive markets |
| Labor law reform | Congressional majorities | Worker power |
| Industrial policy | Revenue, institutional capacity | Manufacturing revival |
| Wealth tax | Constitutional clarity, state experiments | Major redistribution |

### Sequencing Logic

1. **Executive actions first**: Don't require legislation
2. **State-level experiments**: Build evidence, momentum
3. **Popular reforms early**: Minimum wage, tax enforcement
4. **Build capacity**: Fund agencies, develop expertise
5. **Structural reforms later**: Require more power, political capital

## Risks and Contingencies

### Political Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|------------|------------|
| Election losses | Moderate | Insulate executive actions; state focus |
| Judicial invalidation | Moderate | Careful drafting; court reform |
| Backlash mobilization | Moderate | Broad coalitions; popular framing |
| Coalition fracture | Low-Moderate | Shared victories; mutual support |

### Economic Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|------------|------------|
| Recession | Unknown | Automatic stabilizers; countercyclical policy |
| Capital flight | Low | Transition support; enforcement |
| Inflation | Variable | Careful policy design; supply investment |
| Industry disruption | Moderate | Transition assistance; retraining |

### Implementation Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|------------|------------|
| Agency capture | Moderate | Appointment quality; oversight |
| Enforcement failure | Moderate | Funding; whistleblower protections |
| Workarounds/loopholes | High | Adaptive regulation; closure provisions |
| Capacity constraints | Moderate | Investment in government capacity |

### Contingency Plans

**If Federal Legislation Blocked**

- Intensify state-level action
- Use executive authority maximally
- Build for future legislative windows
- Maintain organizing pressure

**If Courts Invalidate Reforms**

- Design reforms to survive judicial review
- Use alternative legal strategies
- Build case for judicial reform
- State court opportunities

**If Economic Crisis Occurs**

- Have policies ready for crisis moment
- Frame crisis as result of status quo
- Push bold action when possible
- Protect vulnerable populations

**If Opposition Mobilizes Effectively**

- Strengthen grassroots capacity
- Expand coalition
- Adapt messaging
- Long-term perspective
