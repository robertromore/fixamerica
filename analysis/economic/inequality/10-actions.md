## For Communities

### Local Reform Campaigns

**Assess the Landscape**

1. Research local minimum wage laws
2. Check if local government can act
3. Identify existing reform supporters
4. Assess political leadership receptiveness

**Build a Coalition**

1. Recruit diverse leadership
2. Engage labor organizations
3. Connect with faith communities
4. Partner with small businesses
5. Build visible coalition

**Choose Your Reform**

| Reform | Best For |
|--------|----------|
| **Local minimum wage** | Cities with authority |
| **Living wage for contractors** | Any city with contracts |
| **Fair scheduling** | Cities with retail/service economy |
| **Paid sick leave** | Cities without state preemption |

## For Advocates

### Running a Local Campaign

**Phase 1: Preparation**

1. Research legal requirements
2. Study successful campaigns elsewhere
3. Build core team and leadership
4. Develop budget and fundraising plan
5. Craft messaging strategy

**Phase 2: Coalition Building**

1. Recruit diverse visible leaders
2. Engage labor organizations
3. Build faith community support
4. Connect with business allies
5. Document coalition publicly

**Phase 3: Campaign**

1. If ballot initiative: gather signatures
2. If council adoption: lobby members
3. Public education campaign
4. Earned media strategy
5. GOTV for election (if ballot)

**Phase 4: Implementation**

1. Work with officials on rollout
2. Worker education about new rights
3. Employer compliance outreach
4. Defend against challenges
5. Document success

### Advocacy Best Practices

**Messaging Principles**

- Lead with shared values (fairness, hard work)
- Use specific local examples
- Tell worker stories
- Focus on solutions, not just problems
- Counter opposition talking points directly

**Coalition Building**

- Labor/community/faith alliances
- Diverse leadership essential
- Give partners ownership and visibility
- Maintain coalition through implementation
- Celebrate shared wins

**Working with Media**

- Build relationships with local reporters
- Provide clear, quotable soundbites
- Offer data and research
- Respond quickly to opposition claims
- Submit regular op-eds

### State-Level Advocacy

**Legislative Strategy**

1. Identify legislative champions
2. Draft or support legislation
3. Build constituent pressure
4. Testify at hearings
5. Track and publicize votes

**Ballot Initiative Strategy**

1. Research signature requirements
2. Draft initiative language carefully
3. Build signature-gathering operation
4. Run campaign to pass
5. Defend against legal challenges

## State-Specific Action Guides

### States with Ballot Initiatives

**Priority Actions**

1. Connect with state-specific organizations
2. Volunteer for active campaigns
3. Prepare for signature gathering
4. GOTV for ballot measures

### States without Ballot Initiatives

**Strategy**: Legislative path or local adoption

**Actions**

1. Build legislative champions
2. Focus on city-level reforms
3. Create demonstration effects
4. Build pressure for state legislation

### States with Preemption

**Where state law blocks local action**

**Actions**

1. Challenge preemption laws
2. Work at state level
3. Use non-preempted tools (community agreements)
4. Document harm from preemption
