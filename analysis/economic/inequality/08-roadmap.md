# Economic Inequality: Roadmap

## Phase 1: Immediate Actions (Now)

### Learn and Connect

**Education**

- Understand how inequality has grown
- Learn key statistics and trends
- Study successful reforms elsewhere
- Know the key organizations and resources

**Connect**

- Join labor or economic justice organization
- Find local coalition work
- Connect across racial and geographic lines
- Subscribe to reform newsletters

### Support Active Campaigns

**Current Opportunities**

- Minimum wage campaigns (state/local)
- Union organizing drives
- Progressive candidate campaigns
- Fair taxation initiatives

**How to Help**

- Volunteer for campaigns
- Donate to organizations
- Contact representatives
- Share information

### Advocate Now

**Contact Representatives**

- Call about PRO Act, minimum wage, taxes
- Attend town halls
- Submit letters to editor
- Testify at hearings

## Phase 2: Short-Term Goals (1-2 Years)

### State and Local Wins

**Target: Minimum Wage Increases**

| Priority | Opportunity |
|----------|-------------|
| States below $15/hour | Ballot initiatives or legislation |
| Cities | Local minimum wage campaigns |
| Cost of living adjustments | Index to inflation |

**Target: Progressive State Taxes**

- Millionaire taxes
- Capital gains taxation
- Estate/inheritance taxes
- Closing corporate loopholes

**Target: Worker Power**

- Public employee bargaining
- Domestic worker bills of rights
- Gig worker protections
- Card check at state level

### Federal Groundwork

**Tax Enforcement**

- Support IRS funding increase
- Document wealthy tax evasion
- Advocate for closing loopholes

**Labor Rights**

- Push PRO Act (or components)
- Executive actions on worker classification
- Federal contractor requirements

### Build Infrastructure

**Organizational Capacity**

- Grow labor movement
- Build economic justice coalitions
- Develop policy expertise
- Train next generation

**Research and Evidence**

- Document what works
- Track inequality trends
- Counter opposition research
- Develop new proposals

## Phase 3: Medium-Term Goals (3-5 Years)

### Critical Mass of State Reforms

**Target: 20+ States with $15+ Minimum Wage**

- Continued ballot initiatives
- Legislative adoption
- Indexing to inflation
- Elimination of subminimum wages

**Target: Significant Union Growth**

- 15%+ private sector unionization
- Strategic sector organizing
- Public support rebuilding
- Labor law reform in states

### Federal Legislation Progress

**PRO Act Passage**

- Build Senate support
- Document current law failures
- Public pressure campaign
- Coalition building

**Tax Reform**

- Close loopholes as stepping stone
- Build case for rate increases
- Document who pays what
- Counter anti-tax messaging

**Expanded EITC/Child Tax Credit**

- Make expanded CTC permanent
- Expand EITC to childless workers
- Improve administration
- Monthly payment option

### Political Reform Connection

**Campaign Finance**

- Reduce money's influence
- See [Campaign Finance: Roadmap](../../political/campaign-finance/08-roadmap.md)

**Electoral Reform**

- More representative democracy
- See [Electoral Reform: Roadmap](../../political/electoral-reform/08-roadmap.md)

## Phase 4: Long-Term Transformation (5+ Years)

### Comprehensive Tax Reform

**Progressive System**

- Top rates of 50-70%
- Capital gains as ordinary income
- Robust estate tax
- Wealth tax (if constitutional)
- Corporate tax reform

### Strong Labor Movement

**Sectoral Bargaining**

- Industry-wide standards
- Wage boards
- Worker representation
- Portable benefits

### Universal Services

**Programs Built**

- Universal pre-K
- Affordable childcare
- Debt-free college
- Universal healthcare

### Structural Change

**New Economy**

- Antitrust enforcement
- Corporate governance reform
- Stakeholder capitalism
- Democratic economy elements

## Risks and Contingencies

### Risk: Economic Downturn

**Threat**: Recession weakens support for redistribution

**Mitigation**

- Frame reforms as economic stimulus
- Emphasize automatic stabilizers
- Build on crisis response programs
- Counter austerity messaging

**Contingency**: Use crisis to expand safety net (New Deal model)

### Risk: Backlash Politics

**Threat**: Racial/cultural backlash undermines coalition

**Mitigation**

- Maintain cross-racial solidarity
- Universal framing over targeted
- Address legitimate concerns
- Avoid culture war traps

**Contingency**: Focus on economic common ground; state/local progress

### Risk: Capital Flight

**Threat**: Wealthy/corporations move assets abroad

**Mitigation**

- International cooperation (minimum taxes)
- Exit taxes
- Enforcement investment
- Most research shows fears overstated

**Contingency**: Adjust policies based on evidence; don't let threats prevent action

### Risk: Legal Challenges

**Threat**: Courts strike down reforms

**Mitigation**

- Design reforms for constitutional durability
- Multiple pathways to goals
- Build litigation capacity
- Long-term court composition change

**Contingency**: Redesign reforms; pursue constitutional amendments if needed

### Risk: Capture and Erosion

**Threat**: Reforms weakened in implementation or over time

**Mitigation**

- Build durable institutions
- Ongoing monitoring and adjustment
- Maintain organizing capacity
- Index to inflation/growth

**Contingency**: Defend and repair programs; learn from failures

## Success Metrics

### Leading Indicators

- Union election petitions
- Minimum wage campaigns launched
- Tax reform bills introduced
- Public opinion shifts
- Organizing activity

### Lagging Indicators

- Gini coefficient
- Top 1% share
- Median wage growth
- Poverty rate
- Mobility metrics
- Wealth gap (racial, overall)

### Ultimate Success

- Income and wealth gaps narrowing
- Middle class expanding
- Poverty declining
- Mobility increasing
- Racial gaps closing
- Working families thriving
