# Economic Reform: Actions

## For Individuals

### As a Citizen

**Stay Informed**

| Action | Resources |
|--------|-----------|
| Follow economic news critically | Beyond business media; include EPI, CBPP |
| Understand your own economic situation | Track wages vs. productivity in your field |
| Learn about policy debates | Tax reform, antitrust, labor policy |
| Recognize economic narratives | "Job creators," "trickle-down," "free markets" |

**Vote and Engage Politically**

| Action | How |
|--------|-----|
| Vote in all elections | Primary and general; state and local matter |
| Research candidates on economic issues | Check voting records, donor sources |
| Contact elected officials | Calls, letters, town halls on specific issues |
| Support reform candidates | Donations, volunteering |
| Testify at hearings | State and local policy opportunities |

**Advocate Publicly**

| Action | Method |
|--------|--------|
| Write letters to editors | Local papers, online publications |
| Use social media strategically | Share credible information |
| Talk to friends and family | Personal conversations matter |
| Participate in public comment | Regulatory proceedings, local government |

### As a Consumer

**Ethical Consumption**

| Action | Consideration |
|--------|---------------|
| Support worker-friendly businesses | Unionized, living wages, good conditions |
| Shop local and small | Reduce monopoly power |
| Consider labor practices | Where and how products made |
| Use ethical banking | Credit unions, community banks, CDFIs |
| Avoid predatory finance | Payday loans, high-fee products |

**Limitations of Consumer Action**

- Individual choices cannot substitute for policy change
- Not everyone has resources for ethical consumption
- Systemic problems require systemic solutions
- Consumer campaigns complement but don't replace advocacy

### As a Worker

**Know Your Rights**

| Right | Details |
|-------|---------|
| Minimum wage | Federal $7.25; state/local often higher |
| Overtime | Over 40 hours for most workers |
| Organizing | Protected right to form/join unions |
| Safety | OSHA protections |
| Anti-discrimination | Title VII, other statutes |
| Wage payment | All wages owed must be paid |

**Organize Your Workplace**

| Step | Resources |
|------|-----------|
| Talk to coworkers | Build relationships, understand concerns |
| Contact a union | SEIU, CWA, UFCW, or industry-specific |
| Learn about organizing | Labor Notes, union training |
| Know your protections | Unfair labor practices, retaliation protection |
| Consider worker centers | For non-traditional organizing |

**Report Violations**

| Violation | Report To |
|-----------|-----------|
| Wage theft | State labor department, DOL |
| Safety hazards | OSHA |
| Discrimination | EEOC, state civil rights |
| Retaliation | NLRB, relevant agency |
| Tax fraud by employer | IRS |

### As an Investor

**Shareholder Activism**

| Action | Method |
|--------|--------|
| Vote proxies | Support worker-friendly resolutions |
| File resolutions | If you own enough shares |
| Engage companies | Letters to management, board |
| Support ESG | Environmental, social, governance factors |

**Investment Choices**

| Approach | Options |
|----------|---------|
| Avoid egregious companies | Research labor, environmental practices |
| Invest in worker-owned | Employee-owned funds |
| Use community banks | Keep capital local |
| Consider impact investing | Align investments with values |

### As a Taxpayer

**Tax Compliance**

| Action | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| Pay your fair share | Contribute to public goods |
| Report tax fraud | IRS whistleblower program |
| Advocate for enforcement | Support IRS funding |

**Understand the System**

| Topic | Learn About |
|-------|-------------|
| Effective tax rates | How they differ by income source |
| Tax expenditures | "Spending" through tax code |
| Who pays what | Distribution of tax burden |
| Loopholes | How wealthy avoid taxes |
