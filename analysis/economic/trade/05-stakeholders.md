# Trade Policy: Stakeholders

## Who Has Power

### Executive Branch

**U.S. Trade Representative (USTR)**:

| Role | Power | Current Status |
|------|-------|----------------|
| Lead negotiator | Sets agreement terms | Emphasizes worker-centered trade |
| TPA authority | Implements fast track | Limited new agreements |
| Enforcement | Section 301, WTO cases | Active on China |
| Advisory committees | Convenes industry input | Remains corporate-heavy |

**Commerce Department**:

| Role | Power | Current Status |
|------|-------|----------------|
| Trade remedies | Anti-dumping, countervailing duties | Active enforcement |
| Export controls | Technology restrictions | Expanding on China |
| Section 232 | National security tariffs | Steel/aluminum |
| Industrial policy | CHIPS implementation | Major role |

**Treasury Department**:

| Role | Power | Current Status |
|------|-------|----------------|
| Currency policy | Manipulation designation | Reluctant to act |
| Sanctions | Economic leverage | Active on Russia, limited China |
| CFIUS | Investment screening | Expanded role |
| IMF/World Bank | International influence | Status quo |

**State Department**:

| Role | Power | Current Status |
|------|-------|----------------|
| Diplomatic relations | Trade-offs with other issues | Balancing act |
| Economic statecraft | Trade as foreign policy tool | Increasing |
| Alliance management | Friend-shoring | Priority |

### Legislative Branch

**Senate Finance Committee**:

| Power | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Jurisdiction over trade | Primary oversight |
| TPA renewal | Must authorize |
| Treaty advice/consent | If trade treaties used |
| Confirmation | Trade officials |

**House Ways and Means Committee**:

| Power | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Revenue aspects | Tariff authority |
| TPA participation | Joint role |
| Oversight | USTR, Commerce |

**Key Members**:

| Member | Role | Position |
|--------|------|----------|
| Sen. Ron Wyden (D-OR) | Finance Chair | Worker-focused, skeptical |
| Sen. Mike Crapo (R-ID) | Finance Ranking | Traditional free trade |
| Rep. Jason Smith (R-MO) | Ways & Means Chair | Populist-leaning |
| Rep. Richard Neal (D-MA) | Ways & Means Ranking | Labor-focused |

### International Bodies

**World Trade Organization**:

| Role | Power | Status |
|------|-------|--------|
| Dispute settlement | Binding rulings | Paralyzed (no Appellate Body) |
| Rule-making | Negotiations | Stalled |
| Monitoring | Trade reviews | Limited impact |

**International Monetary Fund**:

| Role | Power | Status |
|------|-------|--------|
| Currency surveillance | Can name manipulators | Diplomatic |
| Conditionality | Loan terms | Not relevant to major economies |
| Technical assistance | Limited | |

## Who Benefits

### Multinational Corporations

**Winners from Current System**:

| Sector | Benefits |
|--------|----------|
| Tech companies | Global supply chains, China market access |
| Retailers | Cheap imports, global sourcing |
| Finance | Capital mobility, global operations |
| Agriculture | Export markets (mostly) |
| Pharmaceuticals | IP protection, extended patents |

**Specific Corporate Interests**:

| Company Type | Trade Interest | Lobbying Focus |
|--------------|----------------|----------------|
| Apple, Nike | China manufacturing | Maintain access |
| Walmart, Target | Low-cost imports | Oppose tariffs |
| Boeing, Caterpillar | Exports | Market access |
| Pfizer, Merck | IP protection | Extend patents |
| Goldman, JPMorgan | Financial services | Market opening |

### Importers and Retailers

| Stakeholder | Interest | Political Power |
|-------------|----------|-----------------|
| Retail industry | Low tariffs | Major lobby |
| Import brokers | Volume | Industry association |
| E-commerce | Duty-free thresholds | Growing lobby |
| Consumers | Lower prices | Diffuse, weak |

### Agricultural Exporters

| Corn | Significant | Farm lobby |
| Pork | Growing | Industry groups |
| Beef | Some exports | Commodity groups |
| Wheat | Significant | Farm lobby |

### Shareholders and Executives

| Benefit | Mechanism |
|---------|-----------|
| Higher profits | Lower labor costs offshore |
| Stock appreciation | Globalization arbitrage |
| Executive compensation | Tied to stock |
| Dividend growth | Increased margins |

## Who Loses

### Manufacturing Workers

**Most Affected Groups**:

| Sector | Job Losses (2000-2020) | Recovery? |
|--------|------------------------|-----------|
| Textiles/apparel | 700,000+ | No |
| Furniture | 200,000+ | Partial |
| Electronics | 400,000+ | No |
| Auto parts | 300,000+ | Some |
| Steel | 150,000+ | Some |
| Machinery | 300,000+ | Some |

**Worker Demographics**:

| Characteristic | Impact |
|----------------|--------|
| Less than BA degree | Most affected |
| Older workers | Hardest to re-employ |
| Male | Disproportionate |
| Rural/small town | Concentrated |
| Union members | Jobs most targeted |

### Manufacturing Communities

**Community Impacts**:

| Effect | Communities |
|--------|-------------|
| Tax base erosion | Thousands of towns |
| School funding decline | Manufacturing regions |
| Property value collapse | Rust Belt, textile South |
| Social fabric fraying | "Deaths of despair" |
| Population loss | Out-migration |

**Case Studies**:

| Community | Industry Lost | Current Status |
|-----------|---------------|----------------|
| Youngstown, OH | Steel | Struggling |
| Detroit, MI | Auto | Partial recovery |
| Kannapolis, NC | Textiles | Reinventing |
| Gary, IN | Steel | Declining |
| Danville, VA | Textiles, tobacco | Very difficult |
