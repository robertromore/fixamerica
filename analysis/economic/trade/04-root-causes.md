# Trade Policy: Root Causes

## Why Policy Has Failed Workers

American trade policy has consistently benefited corporations and consumers while imposing concentrated costs on workers and communities. This is not accident but designâ€”rooted in structural features of policymaking, economic ideology, and political economy.

## Systemic Factors

### Corporate Capture of Trade Policy

**The Revolving Door**:

| Role | Trade Policy Influence |
|------|----------------------|
| U.S. Trade Representative | Often from business/law firms |
| Trade advisory committees | Dominated by corporate reps |
| Negotiators | Move to lobbying after government |
| Think tanks | Corporate-funded research |

**Trade Advisory Committee Composition (Pre-Reform)**:

| Committee Type | Corporate Reps | Labor Reps | Public Interest |
|----------------|----------------|------------|-----------------|
| Industry SACs | 90%+ | <5% | <5% |
| ACTPN | 85%+ | <10% | <5% |
| ATAC | 95%+ | <5% | <5% |

**Result**: Trade agreements drafted to maximize corporate preferences.

### Economic Ideology

**The Free Trade Consensus**:

| Belief | Reality |
|--------|---------|
| Trade always net beneficial | True in aggregate, devastating for losers |
| Workers will adjust | Adjustment far harder than assumed |
| Comparative advantage works | Assumes factors immobile; they're not |
| Markets allocate efficiently | Ignores market failures, power imbalances |
| Rising tide lifts all boats | Tide concentrated gains at top |

**Professional Failure**:

- Economists dismissed distributional concerns
- Models assumed perfect adjustment
- Empirical evidence ignored until too late
- "China Shock" forced reassessment
- Admission: "We got it wrong"

### Political Economy

**Concentrated Benefits, Diffuse Costs**:

| Interest | Organization | Political Power |
|----------|--------------|-----------------|
| Importing corporations | Highly organized | Massive lobbying |
| Export industries | Well organized | Significant lobbying |
| Consumers | Diffuse | Almost none |
| Displaced workers | Geographically dispersed | Weak voice |
| Communities | Local | No national lobby |

**Why Losers Lose**:

- Job losses happen over years (less visible)
- Workers scattered across industries/regions
- No "displaced worker" lobby
- Union decline weakened labor voice
- Media focused on consumer savings

### Asymmetric Information

| Information Advantage | Holder | Effect |
|----------------------|--------|--------|
| Agreement details | Negotiators, corporations | Public can't evaluate |
| Economic modeling | Think tanks | Biased assumptions hidden |
| Impact studies | Agencies, academics | Often after-the-fact |
| Adjustment costs | Not tracked | Ignored in decisions |

## Structural Problems

### Trade Promotion Authority (Fast Track)

**How It Works**:

- President negotiates
- Congress can only vote yes/no
- No amendments allowed
- Limited debate time

**Problems**:

| Issue | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| Executive dominance | Congress marginalized |
| Corporate access | Business shapes negotiations |
| Labor exclusion | Worker interests ignored |
| Transparency | Negotiations secret |
| Urgency | No time for analysis |

### WTO Dispute Resolution

**Structural Weaknesses**:

| Problem | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| Slow process | 2-5 years for resolution |
| Weak remedies | Only future compliance |
| No damages | Past harm uncompensated |
| Appellate Body paralysis | Appeals blocked since 2019 |
| Enforcement gaps | Winners can't enforce |

### Currency Manipulation Tolerance

**Why Currency Manipulation Persisted**:

| Factor | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| Treasury reluctance | Fear of retaliation, market disruption |
| Corporate interests | Cheap imports benefited importers |
| Macroeconomic trade-offs | Low interest rates, cheap goods |
| Diplomatic concerns | Don't anger trading partners |
| Legal complexity | Hard to define "manipulation" |

**Effects of Overvalued Dollar**:

- U.S. exports more expensive
- Imports cheaper
- Manufacturing disadvantage
- Persistent trade deficits
- Job losses

### Intellectual Property Overreach

**IP Provisions in Trade Agreements**:

| Provision | Corporate Benefit | Public Cost |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------|
| Extended patents | Pharma profits | Higher drug prices |
| ISDS for IP | Corporate enforcement | Sovereignty concerns |
| Criminal penalties | Strong protection | Chilling effects |
| Data exclusivity | Generic delays | Access problems |

**Who Shapes IP Policy**:

- Pharmaceutical industry
- Entertainment industry
- Technology industry
- NOT consumers, generics, public health

## Political Failures

### Bipartisan Abandonment of Workers

**Democratic Failures**:

- Clinton: NAFTA, China PNTR
- Obama: TPP pursuit
- Assumed gains would trickle down
- Relied on TAA (inadequate)
- Lost working-class voters

**Republican Failures**:

- Pushed free trade aggressively
- Opposed worker protections
- Blocked TAA funding
- Prioritized corporate interests
- Then pivoted to populism (Trump)

### Adjustment Assistance Failure

**Trade Adjustment Assistance Problems**:

| Issue | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| Underfunding | ~$800M annually vs. billions needed |
| Narrow eligibility | Many displaced workers don't qualify |
| Training failures | Programs often ineffective |
| Geographic limits | Workers can't relocate |
| Duration limits | Benefits end too soon |
| Healthcare gaps | Insurance support inadequate |

**What Would Have Been Needed**:

- Universal adjustment assistance
- Wage insurance for career
- Robust healthcare (not tied to employer)
- Community investment
- Place-based policies
- Decades of support

### Enforcement Neglect

**Why Trade Agreements Go Unenforced**:

| Provision | Enforcement Reality |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Labor standards | Almost never enforced |
| Environmental standards | Rarely enforced |
| IP protection | Selectively enforced (for corporations) |
| Currency manipulation | Treasury won't act |
| Subsidies | WTO slow/ineffective |
| Market access | Slow progress |

**Enforcement Resources**:

| Agency | Trade Enforcement Budget | Adequate? |
|--------|-------------------------|-----------|
| USTR | ~$70M | No |
| ITC | Limited | No |
| Commerce (trade remedies) | Limited | No |
| CBP (enforcement) | Customs focus | Trade secondary |

## Ideological Blindness

### Comparative Advantage Assumptions

**What Theory Assumes**:

| Assumption | Reality |
|------------|---------|
| Factors can't move across borders | Capital highly mobile |
| Full employment | Displaced workers often never re-employed |
| Adjustment is quick | Takes decades, if ever |
| Countries have different endowments | China had labor AND targeted industries |
| No economies of scale | Scale matters enormously |
| No industrial policy | China has aggressive industrial policy |

### National Security Neglect

**Ignored Until Too Late**:

| Risk | Recognition |
|------|-------------|
| Pharmaceutical dependence | COVID-19 revealed |
| PPE dependence | COVID-19 revealed |
| Semiconductor dependence | 2021 shortage revealed |
| Rare earth dependence | Clean energy transition revealed |
| Defense industrial base | Gradual awareness |

**Why Security Ignored**:

- Trade economists don't think about security
- Security establishment didn't track supply chains
- Corporate interests in China investment
- "End of history" complacency
- Cheap imports popular

### China Miscalculation

**The Grand Miscalculation**:

| Assumption | Reality |
|------------|---------|
| Trade would liberalize China | Strengthened CCP legitimacy |
| WTO would constrain China | China gamed rules |
| China would follow market principles | State capitalism intensified |
| Technology transfer was acceptable | IP theft massive |
| Engagement would work | Competition intensified |

## Contributing Factors

### Media Coverage

**Trade Coverage Failures**:

| Bias | Effect |
|------|--------|
| Consumer focus | "Lower prices" framing |
| Abstract economics | Job losses not personalized |
| Corporate sources | Business perspective dominated |
| Complexity | Trade deals not explained |
| Short-term focus | Long-term effects ignored |

### Academic Economics

**Professional Failures**:

| Issue | Problem |
|-------|---------|
| Model assumptions | Unrealistic adjustment |
| Distributional neglect | Focused on aggregate gains |
| Political economy blindness | Ignored power dynamics |
| Data limitations | Didn't track actual impacts |
| Ideology | Free trade as article of faith |

**Belated Reassessment**:

- Autor, Dorn, Hanson (2013): China shock far worse than predicted
- Rodrik: Trade agreements overreached
- Summers: "Responsible nationalism" needed
- Blanchard: Globalization went too far

### Union Decline

**Labor's Weakened Voice**:

| Year | Union Density | Trade Policy Influence |
|------|---------------|----------------------|
| 1970 | 27% | Significant voice |
| 1990 | 16% | Declining influence |
| 2010 | 12% | Marginalized |
| 2023 | 10% | Minimal |

**Chicken and Egg**:

- Trade policy weakened unions
- Weaker unions couldn't influence trade policy
- Downward spiral

## Feedback Loops

### Race to the Bottom

| Mechanism | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| Low-wage competition | U.S. wages stagnate |
| Tax competition | Corporate taxes cut |
| Regulatory competition | Standards weakened |
| Union avoidance | Bargaining power declines |

### Political Alienation

| Effect | Consequence |
|--------|-------------|
| Working-class abandonment | Lost faith in both parties |
| Geographic concentration | Swing state impacts |
| Cultural resentment | Elite vs. forgotten |
| Populist opening | Trump, Sanders |
| Policy instability | Lurching between approaches |
