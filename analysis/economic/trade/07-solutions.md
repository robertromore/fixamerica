# Trade Policy: Solutions

## Comprehensive Trade Reform

Fixing American trade policy requires action across multiple dimensions: worker protection, supply chain security, enforcement, industrial policy, and international standards.

## Worker Protection and Adjustment

### Universal Trade Adjustment 2.0

**Redesigned Trade Adjustment Assistance**:

| Component | Current | Proposed |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| Eligibility | Narrowly trade-related | All displaced workers |
| Duration | 2 years max | 3-5 years with career support |
| Wage insurance | Limited | 70% wage replacement for 2 years |
| Healthcare | COBRA subsidy | Full coverage during transition |
| Training | Often ineffective | Sector partnerships, apprenticeships |
| Relocation | Limited | Substantial support |
| Community investment | None | Place-based economic development |

**Wage Insurance Program**:

| Feature | Specification |
|---------|---------------|
| Replacement rate | 50-70% of wage difference |
| Duration | 2-3 years |
| Eligibility | Workers 50+ or long-tenured |
| Phase-out | Gradual to incentivize re-employment |
| Cost | ~$5-10B annually |

**Community Investment Fund**:

| Component | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|
| Infrastructure grants | Rebuild affected regions |
| Business development | Attract new employers |
| Education investment | Workforce preparation |
| Tax incentives | Target trade-affected areas |
| Healthcare facilities | Replace lost employer coverage |

### Sectoral Employment Programs

| Sector | Program Design |
|--------|---------------|
| Manufacturing | Apprenticeships, community college partnerships |
| Clean energy | Transition programs for displaced workers |
| Healthcare | Fast-track credentialing |
| Tech/IT | Coding bootcamps with job guarantees |
| Infrastructure | Direct public employment |

## Supply Chain Security

### Critical Sector Reshoring

**Priority Sectors**:

| Sector | Vulnerability | Approach |
|--------|--------------|----------|
| Semiconductors | Critical | CHIPS Act + expansion |
| Pharmaceuticals | Critical | Domestic production incentives |
| Medical devices | Critical | FDA + incentives |
| Rare earths | Critical | Mining + processing investment |
| EV batteries | High | IRA provisions + more |
| Steel/aluminum | High | Maintain tariffs + modernization |
| PPE/medical supplies | High | Strategic reserve + capacity |

**Tools for Reshoring**:

| Tool | Application |
|------|-------------|
| Production tax credits | Semiconductor, batteries, pharma |
| Investment tax credits | Manufacturing equipment |
| Procurement preferences | Buy American strengthening |
| Tariffs | Strategic protection |
| Export controls | Prevent technology transfer |
| CFIUS expansion | Screen strategic investments |

### Friend-Shoring and Diversification

**Ally Manufacturing Networks**:

| Region | Sectors | Approach |
|--------|---------|----------|
| European Union | Pharma, aerospace, machinery | Regulatory alignment |
| Japan | Semiconductors, auto | Trade agreement |
| Korea | Semiconductors, batteries | Trade agreement |
| Taiwan | Semiconductors | Security + trade |
| India | Pharma, IT services | Development partnership |
| Mexico | Auto, manufacturing | USMCA enforcement |

**Diversification Requirements**:

| Requirement | Mechanism |
|-------------|-----------|
| No single-source dependencies | Procurement rules |
| Minimum ally content | Origin requirements |
| Strategic reserve maintenance | Government stockpiles |
| Supply chain transparency | Disclosure mandates |

### Strategic Reserves and Stockpiling

## Trade Enforcement

### Strengthened Section 301

**Enhanced Authority**:

| Current | Proposed |
|---------|----------|
| Case-by-case investigation | Systematic monitoring |
| Tariffs main tool | Multiple remedies available |
| Limited scope | Broad unfair practices |
| Political constraints | Streamlined action |

### Currency Manipulation Response

**Countervailing Currency Intervention**:

| Component | Mechanism |
|-----------|-----------|
| Automatic determination | Objective criteria |
| Countervailing duties | Offset currency advantage |
| Treasury designation | Required action |
| IMF coordination | Multilateral pressure |

**Criteria for Manipulation**:

| Factor | Threshold |
|--------|-----------|
| Bilateral trade surplus | $20B+ with U.S. |
| Current account surplus | 3%+ of GDP |
| Persistent intervention | Net purchases 2%+ of GDP |

### Intellectual Property Enforcement

| Tool | Application |
|------|-------------|
| Section 301 tariffs | IP theft retaliation |
| Export controls | Prevent technology transfer |
| Entity list | Restrict Chinese tech firms |
| Visa restrictions | Researchers at problematic institutions |
| Investment screening | Technology-acquiring investments |

### Labor and Environmental Enforcement

**Rapid Response Mechanism (USMCA Model)**:

| Feature | Function |
|---------|----------|
| Facility-specific enforcement | Not just country-wide |
| Fast timeline | 90-day investigation |
| Remedies | Tariffs on specific facility |
| Independent panels | Binding decisions |

**Expand to All Agreements**:

- Retrofit existing agreements
- Require in new agreements
- Unilateral action where needed
