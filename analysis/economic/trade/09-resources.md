# Trade Policy: Resources

## Essential Reading

### Books

**Historical Context**:

| Title | Author | Why Read |
|-------|--------|----------|
| *Clashing over Commerce* | Douglas Irwin | Definitive U.S. trade history |
| *The Globalization Paradox* | Dani Rodrik | Trade-offs of globalization |
| *Straight Talk on Trade* | Dani Rodrik | Critique of free trade orthodoxy |
| *Free Trade Under Fire* | Douglas Irwin | Defense of trade (for balance) |
| *The Wealth and Poverty of Nations* | David Landes | Long-term economic development |

**China and Globalization**:

| Title | Author | Why Read |
|-------|--------|----------|

| *The Hundred-Year Marathon* | Michael Pillsbury | China strategy perspective |
| *Destined for War* | Graham Allison | U.S.-China competition |
| *China Inc.* | Ted Fishman | Rise of Chinese manufacturing |

**Worker Impact**:

| Title | Author | Why Read |
|-------|--------|----------|
| *Factory Man* | Beth Macy | Human story of manufacturing decline |
| *Janesville* | Amy Goldstein | Community impact of plant closure |
| *Glass House* | Brian Alexander | Lancaster, Ohio and deindustrialization |
| *Beaten Down, Worked Up* | Steven Greenhouse | Labor movement and trade |

**Policy and Reform**:

| Title | Author | Why Read |
|-------|--------|----------|

| *Has Globalization Gone Too Far?* | Dani Rodrik | Early warning (1997) |
| *The Travels of a T-Shirt* | Pietra Rivoli | Global supply chains explained |
