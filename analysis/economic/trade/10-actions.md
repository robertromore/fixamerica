# Trade Policy: Actions

## What Citizens Can Do

### Individual Actions

#### Learn and Engage

| Action | Why It Matters | How To Do It |
|--------|---------------|--------------|
| Understand trade policy basics | Informed advocacy | Read resources in this guide |
| Follow trade legislation | Know what's at stake | congress.gov, news sources |
| Recognize trade impacts locally | Personal connection | Research local employers |
| Understand global supply chains | See the big picture | Company disclosures, reports |

#### Consumer Choices

| Action | Impact | Considerations |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| Buy American when possible | Supports domestic jobs | Often more expensive |
| Check country of origin | Awareness | Labels required for most goods |
| Support local manufacturing | Community benefit | May require research |
| Consider worker conditions | Ethical consumption | Certifications, research |

**Important Caveat**: Individual consumer choices cannot substitute for policy change. Systemic problems require systemic solutions.

### Advocacy Campaigns

#### Federal Legislation

**Priority Bills to Support**:

| Bill | Purpose | Action |
|------|---------|--------|
| Trade Enforcement and Worker Protection Act | Comprehensive reform | Contact Congress |
| Currency manipulation legislation | Address unfair practices | Support sponsors |
| Industrial policy bills | Strategic investment | Bipartisan support |
| TAA expansion | Worker protection | Urgent need |

**How to Contact Congress**:

1. **Phone**: Most effective for immediate response
   - Find numbers: house.gov, senate.gov
   - Call district offices for availability
   - Be concise: name, address, issue, position

2. **Written Communication**:
   - Letters more impactful than email
   - Personal stories most effective
   - Reference specific bills by number

3. **In-Person Meetings**:
   - Request meeting with staff
   - Bring local examples
   - Follow up in writing

#### Counter Misinformation

| Myth | Fact | Source |
|------|------|--------|
| "Trade deficits don't matter" | Persistent deficits cost jobs, capacity | Research |
| "Tariffs always hurt consumers" | Trade-offs exist; jobs matter too | Economic analysis |
| "Manufacturing jobs are gone forever" | Policy choices matter; reshoring possible | Current trends |
| "Free trade benefits everyone" | Concentrated costs on workers, communities | China shock research |
