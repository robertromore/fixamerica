# Public Investment: Actions

## What You Can Do

Public investment happens—or fails to happen—because of political decisions. Citizens can influence these decisions at federal, state, and local levels. This guide provides concrete actions organized by time commitment and impact.

## Individual Actions

### Stay Informed (Low Time, Foundation)

**Track Infrastructure in Your Area**

| Action | How |
|--------|-----|
| Know your infrastructure grade | Search your state at infrastructurereportcard.org |
| Monitor local projects | Subscribe to city/county infrastructure updates |
| Track federal spending | Visit build.gov for IIJA projects |
| Follow R&D policy | AAAS R&D budget updates |

**Understand the Issues**

| Resource | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| ASCE Infrastructure Report Card | Overall assessment |
| Congressional Research Service reports | Policy details |
| Local journalism | Community-specific issues |
| Think tank analyses | Policy options |

### Contact Elected Officials (Medium Time, Direct Impact)

**When to Contact**

| Trigger | Action |
|---------|--------|
| Budget deliberations | Support investment funding |
| Infrastructure bills | Support passage |
| Grant applications | Support local applications |
| Project opposition | Counter-mobilize |

**How to Contact Effectively**

| Method | Best For |
|--------|----------|
| Phone calls | Immediate issues, counted as constituent contact |
| Written letters | Detailed arguments, permanent record |
| Town halls | Direct interaction, public visibility |
| Social media | Building momentum, visibility |

**Sample Messages**

**Supporting Infrastructure Investment**:
> "I'm calling to urge [Representative/Senator] to support robust funding for [specific program]. Our community depends on [specific infrastructure] and current conditions are [problem]. Investment in [project type] would [benefit]."

**Supporting R&D Funding**:
> "As a constituent, I'm asking [Representative/Senator] to support increased funding for [NSF/NIH/DOE]. Federal research investment created [examples like internet, GPS] and continued investment is essential for American competitiveness."

### Vote and Engage Politically (Medium Time, Systemic Impact)

**Candidate Evaluation**

| Question | Why It Matters |
|----------|---------------|
| Position on infrastructure spending | Direct policy impact |
| Position on research funding | Future competitiveness |
| Tax policy positions | Investment financing |
| Record on past votes | Actions vs. rhetoric |

**Ballot Measures**

Many states and localities vote directly on infrastructure:

| Measure Type | Typical Content |
|--------------|-----------------|
| Bond measures | Capital project funding |
| Tax measures | Revenue for infrastructure |
| Transit measures | Public transportation |
| School bonds | Education facilities |

**Research and vote on all ballot measures affecting infrastructure.**

### Public Comment and Testimony (Medium Time, Direct Impact)

**Opportunities**

| Process | How to Participate |
|---------|-------------------|
| Transportation planning | MPO public meetings |
| Environmental review | Public comment periods |
| Budget hearings | Testify on priorities |
| Regulatory proceedings | Submit comments |

**Effective Testimony**

| Element | Approach |
|---------|----------|
| Personal stake | Explain how issue affects you |
| Specific request | Clear ask, not general complaint |
| Evidence | Data supporting your position |
| Solutions | Not just problems |
| Brief | Respect time limits |

## Getting Started Checklist

### This Week

- [ ] Look up your state's infrastructure grade
- [ ] Find one local infrastructure issue affecting you
- [ ] Identify your U.S. Representative and Senators

### This Month

- [ ] Contact one elected official about infrastructure
- [ ] Attend one public meeting (city council, MPO, etc.)
- [ ] Join one organization focused on infrastructure

### This Year

- [ ] Vote in all elections, researching candidate infrastructure positions
- [ ] Research and vote on infrastructure-related ballot measures
- [ ] Engage in one sustained campaign for investment in your community
- [ ] Build relationships with local officials
