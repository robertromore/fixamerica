# Public Investment: Resources

## Essential Reading

### Overview and Policy

**The Entrepreneurial State** by Mariana Mazzucato (2013)
Argues that government investment, not just private enterprise, drives innovation. Documents public investment behind technologies from the internet to smartphones.

**Concrete Economics** by Stephen S. Cohen and J. Bradford DeLong (2016)
Historical analysis of American economic development showing the central role of government policy and investment in shaping the economy.

**The Fifth Risk** by Michael Lewis (2018)
Examines the critical but invisible work of federal agencies, including infrastructure and research programs, and the risks of neglecting government capacity.

**Jump-Starting America** by Jonathan Gruber and Simon Johnson (2019)
Proposal for massive increase in R&D spending distributed across the country to spread innovation and opportunity.

### Infrastructure

**The Road Taken** by Henry Petroski (2016)
Engineering perspective on American infrastructure, from the Interstate system to present challenges.

**Move: The Forces Uprooting Us and Shaping Humanity's Destiny** by Parag Khanna (2021)
Global perspective on infrastructure and human geography, including how infrastructure shapes migration and opportunity.

**Infrastructure: A Field Guide to the Industrial Landscape** by Brian Hayes (2014)
Visual tour of American infrastructure systems explaining how they work.

### Research and Development

**The Linear Model of Innovation** by Beno√Æt Godin (2006)
Academic analysis of the relationship between basic research and technological innovation.

**Science: The Endless Frontier** by Vannevar Bush (1945)
The foundational document of postwar American science policy, still relevant today.

**The New Geography of Innovation** by various (2019)
Analysis of where innovation happens and policies to spread it more broadly.

### Industrial Policy

**How Asia Works** by Joe Studwell (2013)
Comparative analysis of economic development in Asia, including the role of industrial policy.

**American Compass essays on industrial policy**
Contemporary conservative arguments for active industrial policy.

**Trade Wars Are Class Wars** by Matthew C. Klein and Michael Pettis (2020)
Analysis of global trade imbalances and their implications for American industry.

## Academic Research

### Infrastructure Economics

Aschauer, David Alan. "Is Public Expenditure Productive?" *Journal of Monetary Economics* 23, no. 2 (1989): 177-200.
- Seminal paper finding high returns to public infrastructure investment

Congressional Budget Office. "Public Spending on Transportation and Water Infrastructure, 1956 to 2017." October 2018.
- Comprehensive data on trends in public infrastructure spending

Bivens, Josh. "The Short- and Long-Term Impact of Infrastructure Investments on Employment and Economic Activity in the U.S. Economy." Economic Policy Institute, 2014.
- Analysis of employment effects of infrastructure spending

Leduc, Sylvain, and Daniel Wilson. "Roads to Prosperity or Bridges to Nowhere? Theory and Evidence on the Impact of Public Infrastructure Investment." *NBER Macroeconomics Annual* 27, no. 1 (2012): 89-142.
- Evidence on infrastructure investment returns

### Research and Innovation

Mowery, David C., and Nathan Rosenberg. *Technology and the Pursuit of Economic Growth*. Cambridge University Press, 1989.
- Historical analysis of technology policy and economic growth

Gruber, Jonathan, and Simon Johnson. "Jump-Starting America: How Breakthrough Science Can Revive Economic Growth and the American Dream." *Working Paper*, MIT, 2019.
- Case for expanded and distributed R&D investment

Bloom, Nicholas, et al. "Are Ideas Getting Harder to Find?" *American Economic Review* 110, no. 4 (2020): 1104-44.
- Analysis of research productivity and implications for R&D investment

### Public-Private Partnerships

Engel, Eduardo, Ronald Fischer, and Alexander Galetovic. *The Economics of Public-Private Partnerships: A Basic Guide*. Cambridge University Press, 2014.
- Economic framework for evaluating PPPs

Hodge, Graeme A., and Carsten Greve. "Public-Private Partnerships: An International Performance Review." *Public Administration Review* 67, no. 3 (2007): 545-558.
- Comparative analysis of PPP outcomes
