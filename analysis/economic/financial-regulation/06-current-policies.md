# Current Financial Regulation Policies

## Overview

The current U.S. financial regulatory framework is a patchwork of pre-crisis rules, post-2008 Dodd-Frank reforms, subsequent rollbacks, and ongoing gaps. This chapter maps the current landscape, including what exists, what has been weakened, and what remains unaddressed.

## Major Regulatory Agencies

### Federal Reserve

**Jurisdiction:**

- Bank holding companies and their subsidiaries
- Systemically important financial institutions (SIFIs)
- Payment systems
- Monetary policy

**Key Tools:**

- Stress testing (CCAR, DFAST)
- Enhanced prudential standards
- Capital and liquidity requirements
- Supervision and examination

**Current State:**

- Chair Jerome Powell has maintained most post-crisis framework
- Some stress test modifications reducing rigor
- Enhanced scrutiny of crypto activities
- Interest rate policy overshadows regulatory function

### Office of the Comptroller of the Currency (OCC)

**Jurisdiction:**

- National banks
- Federal savings associations
- Federal branches of foreign banks

**Key Authorities:**

- Charter approval and revocation
- Examination and supervision
- Preemption of state consumer laws

**Current State:**

- Has considered special purpose national bank charters for fintech
- Preemption authority remains controversial
- Mixed record on consumer protection

### Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC)

**Jurisdiction:**

- Securities markets
- Investment advisers
- Broker-dealers
- Public company disclosure

**Key Authorities:**

- Registration and disclosure requirements
- Enforcement actions
- Rulemaking for securities markets
- Investment adviser regulation

**Current State (under Chair Gary Gensler):**

- Aggressive enforcement stance
- Cryptocurrency crackdown
- Climate disclosure rules
- Enhanced market structure rules
- Private equity fee disclosure requirements

### Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC)

**Jurisdiction:**

- Derivatives markets
- Commodity futures
- Swaps (post-Dodd-Frank)

**Key Authorities:**

- Clearing requirements
- Margin requirements
- Position limits
- Swap dealer registration

**Current State:**

- Underfunded relative to mandate expansion
- Cryptocurrency jurisdiction disputed with SEC
- Enforcement-focused approach

### Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB)

**Jurisdiction:**

- Consumer financial products and services
- Mortgage lending
- Credit cards
- Payday and auto lending
- Credit reporting

**Key Authorities:**

- Rulemaking for consumer protection
- Examination of large banks and nonbanks
- Enforcement actions
- Consumer complaint database

**Current State (under Director Rohit Chopra):**

- Aggressive enforcement restored
- New rules on junk fees, overdrafts, credit cards
- Open banking/data portability rules
- Constitutional challenge to funding structure (resolved in CFPB's favor)

### Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation (FDIC)

**Jurisdiction:**

- Deposit insurance
- State-chartered banks (shared with states)
- Bank resolution

**Key Authorities:**

- Deposit insurance assessments
- Orderly liquidation authority
- Bank supervision

**Current State:**

- 2023 bank failures (SVB, Signature, First Republic) tested resolution framework
- Questions about implicit guarantees for uninsured deposits
- Merger review tightening

### Financial Stability Oversight Council (FSOC)

**Jurisdiction:**

- Systemic risk identification
- SIFI designation
- Regulatory coordination

**Key Authorities:**

- Designate nonbank SIFIs for enhanced supervision
- Recommend heightened standards
- Monitor emerging risks

**Current State:**

- Revived after dormancy under Trump administration
- Reconsidering activities-based approach vs. entity designation
- Increased focus on climate risk, digital assets

## Post-Dodd-Frank Framework

### What Was Enacted

**Structural Changes:**

- Creation of CFPB and FSOC
- Orderly Liquidation Authority
- Swaps pushed to clearinghouses
- Volcker Rule (proprietary trading restrictions)
- Enhanced capital and liquidity requirements

**Consumer Protection:**

- Qualified Mortgage (QM) rules
- Ability-to-Repay requirements
- Mortgage servicing standards
- Remittance transfer protections

**Systemic Risk:**

- Stress testing requirements
- Living wills for large institutions
- Single-counterparty credit limits
- Enhanced risk management

### What Was Rolled Back

**Economic Growth Act of 2018:**

| Original Dodd-Frank Requirement | Change |
|--------------------------------|--------|
| Enhanced supervision threshold: $50 billion | Raised to $250 billion |
| Stress testing for $10-50 billion banks | Eliminated |
| Company-run stress tests for $10-50 billion | Eliminated |
| Risk committee requirements | Narrowed applicability |

**Regulatory Actions:**

| Rule | Change |
|------|--------|
| Volcker Rule | Simplified compliance, expanded exemptions |
| Stress testing | Reduced frequency, less severe scenarios |
| Leverage ratio | Supplementary leverage ratio exemptions |
| Resolution planning | Reduced frequency for smaller firms |
| SIFI designations | All nonbank designations removed |

### What Was Never Addressed

| Gap | Status |
|-----|--------|
| Too Big to Fail | Banks larger than 2008 |
| Shadow banking | Most nonbank lenders unregulated |
| Private equity oversight | Minimal disclosure requirements |
| Executive compensation | Say-on-pay is non-binding |
| Credit rating agencies | Same firms, same model |
| Stock buybacks | Restrictions repealed |
| Glass-Steagall restoration | Never seriously considered |

## Current Regulatory Hotspots

### Cryptocurrency and Digital Assets

**Current Framework:**

- SEC: Securities laws apply to most tokens
- CFTC: Bitcoin and Ethereum as commodities
- FinCEN: Anti-money laundering requirements
- OCC: Guidance on bank crypto activities
- Fed: Master account access restrictions
- States: Patchwork of money transmitter laws

**Key Developments:**

- Major SEC enforcement actions against exchanges
- Proposed legislation stalled in Congress
- Bank regulators cautious about crypto exposure
- Stablecoin oversight remains uncertain

### Climate Risk

**Current Framework:**

- SEC: Climate disclosure rule finalized (litigation pending)
- Fed: Climate scenario analysis pilot
- OCC: Principles for climate risk management
- FSOC: Reports identifying climate as systemic risk

**Status:**

- No mandatory climate stress tests
- Disclosure rules facing legal challenges
- Political opposition to "ESG" regulation

### Private Credit

**Current Framework:**

- Minimal SEC oversight of private funds
- No capital or leverage requirements
- Limited disclosure to investors
- No systemic risk monitoring

**Concerns:**

- Private credit has grown to $1.5+ trillion
- Interconnection with regulated banking system
- Opacity prevents risk assessment
- Potential for rapid contagion

### Bank Mergers

**Current Framework:**

- Regulatory approval required (Fed, OCC, FDIC, or state)
- Community Reinvestment Act consideration
- Competition analysis
- Financial stability consideration (Dodd-Frank)

**Recent Developments:**

- Regulators signaling more rigorous review
- Updated merger guidelines proposed
- Some large mergers delayed or blocked
- Focus on community impact

### Nonbank Mortgage Lending

**Current Framework:**

- State licensing and supervision
- CFPB authority over consumer protection
- No prudential (safety and soundness) oversight
- No federal capital requirements

**Concerns:**

- Nonbanks originate 70%+ of mortgages
- Concentrated among few large servicers
- Liquidity vulnerabilities in stress
- Limited resolution framework

## International Comparison

| Issue | U.S. Approach | European Approach | UK Approach |
|-------|--------------|-------------------|-------------|
| Bank structure | No separation requirement | Ring-fencing in some countries | Ring-fencing required |
| Capital requirements | Basel III implementation | Basel III implementation | Basel III implementation |
| Consumer protection | CFPB (agency) | Varies by country | FCA conduct regulation |
| Derivatives | Central clearing, swap execution | EMIR clearing requirements | UK EMIR |
| Crypto | Enforcement-led, no comprehensive law | MiCA comprehensive framework | Evolving framework |
| Climate risk | Contested disclosure rules | Mandatory disclosures, taxonomy | Climate stress tests |

## Gaps in Current Framework

### Structural Vulnerabilities

1. **Fragmented Regulation**: Multiple agencies with overlapping/conflicting mandates
2. **Regulatory Arbitrage**: Activities shift to less regulated entities
3. **Shadow Banking Growth**: Nonbank lending largely unregulated
4. **Too Big to Fail Persists**: Largest banks even larger
5. **Enforcement Resource Gap**: Regulators outmatched by industry

### Emerging Risks

1. **Private Credit Opacity**: Rapid growth with minimal oversight
2. **Crypto Volatility**: Unclear regulatory framework
3. **Climate Transition**: Physical and transition risks not addressed
4. **Leveraged Lending**: CLO market approaching pre-crisis levels
5. **AI in Finance**: Algorithmic trading and lending raise new issues

### Political Constraints

1. **Industry Lobbying**: Continuous pressure to weaken rules
2. **Judicial Hostility**: Courts limiting agency authority
3. **Congressional Gridlock**: No significant reform legislation
4. **Revolving Door**: Personnel movement undermines independence

## Conclusion

The current regulatory framework is stronger than the pre-2008 regime but weaker than it was before 2017 rollbacks. Significant gaps remain, particularly in nonbank oversight, private credit, and structural reform.
