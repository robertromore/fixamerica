# Economic Mobility: Actions

## Overview

Increasing economic mobility requires action at every level--from individual choices to systemic advocacy. This document provides concrete actions for different audiences, from concerned citizens to policymakers.

## Individual Actions

### Educate Yourself

| Action | Resources |
|--------|-----------|
| Explore the Opportunity Atlas | opportunityatlas.org - find mobility data for your area |
| Read foundational research | Chetty papers, Rothstein's *The Color of Law* |
| Understand local context | Research school funding, housing policy in your area |
| Follow mobility researchers | @Aboringdystopia, @SaezEcon, @RichardVReeves |
| Subscribe to relevant newsletters | CBPP, Urban Institute, Brookings |

### Personal Finance for Mobility

| Action | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| Build emergency savings | Prevent downward mobility from crises |
| Invest for children | 529 plans, savings accounts |
| Homeownership (where appropriate) | Wealth building |
| Avoid predatory products | High-interest debt destroys mobility |
| Use EITC, CTC | Claim all eligible credits |

### Effective Messaging

| Do | Don't |
|----|----|
| Lead with opportunity | Lead with redistribution |
| Focus on children | Blame parents |
| Use concrete examples | Use jargon |
| Cite research | Make unsubstantiated claims |

## Specific Campaign Ideas

### Local Pre-K Campaign

1. Research current state of early childhood in your area
2. Build coalition: parents, educators, businesses, pediatricians
3. Develop funding proposal (local option tax, reallocation)
4. Engage school board, city council
5. Generate media attention
6. Pursue ballot initiative if necessary

### Zoning Reform Campaign

1. Map exclusionary zoning in your area
2. Build YIMBY coalition
3. Engage with planning commission
4. Develop specific code changes
5. Counter NIMBY opposition
6. Build support among renters, young people

### Criminal Justice Reform Campaign

1. Research local incarceration data
2. Build coalition: civil liberties, faith, victims, business
3. Target prosecutorial elections
4. Advocate for sentencing reform
5. Support reentry programs
6. Expand Ban the Box
