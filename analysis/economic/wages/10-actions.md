# Wages: Actions

## Individual Actions

### If You're a Low-Wage Worker

**Know Your Rights**

| Right | Protection |
|-------|------------|
| Minimum wage | Employers must pay at least federal/state minimum |
| Overtime | Time-and-a-half after 40 hours (if non-exempt) |
| Tips | Employers cannot take your tips |
| Pay stubs | Right to itemized wage statements |
| Retaliation | Illegal to fire for wage complaints |

**Document Everything**

| Item | Why |
|------|-----|
| Hours worked | Write down start/end times daily |
| Pay received | Keep all pay stubs |
| Breaks taken | Document if denied breaks |
| Tips earned | Track daily tips |
| Communications | Save texts, emails about pay |

**Report Violations**

| Violation | Where to Report |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Federal minimum wage | DOL Wage and Hour: 1-866-487-9243 |
| State wage violations | State labor department |
| Tip theft | DOL or state agency |
| Retaliation | DOL, EEOC, state agency |
| Multiple violations | Worker center, legal aid |

**Find Legal Help**

| Resource | Contact |
|----------|---------|
| Legal Aid | lawhelp.org |
| Worker Centers | Find local center through NDLON |
| Private attorneys | Many take wage cases on contingency |
| Law school clinics | Free legal help from students |

### If You're a Middle-Income Worker

**Check Your Classification**

| Question | If Yes, You May Be Misclassified |
|----------|----------------------------------|
| Paid salary under $35,568? | Should get overtime |
| Called "manager" but don't supervise? | May be non-exempt |
| Work more than 40 hours without overtime? | Review exempt status |
| Classified as contractor but controlled by employer? | May be employee |

**Advocate in Your Workplace**

| Action | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Talk with coworkers about pay | Information sharing legal and protected |
| Document unpaid work | Build case for back pay |
| Report concerns to HR | Create paper trail |
| Contact union organizers | Explore collective action |

## Collective Actions

### Workplace Organizing

**Form or Join a Union**

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| Find interested coworkers | Build organizing committee |
| Contact union organizers | AFL-CIO, SEIU, relevant union |
| Sign authorization cards | 30% needed for election (50% for card check) |
| Win election | NLRB-supervised vote |
| Negotiate contract | Collective bargaining |

**Non-Union Collective Action**

| Action | Protection |
|--------|------------|
| Discuss wages with coworkers | Protected by NLRA |
| Petition employer together | Concerted activity protected |
| Walk out over wages | May be protected strike |
| File group complaint | Class/collective action |

## Political Actions

### Voting

**Research Candidates**

| Issue | Questions to Ask |
|-------|------------------|
| Minimum wage | Support $15+ federal minimum? |
| Tipped minimum | Support One Fair Wage? |
| Overtime | Support expanding coverage? |
| Worker classification | Support ABC test? |
| Enforcement | Support DOL funding? |

**Key Races**

| Level | Impact |
|-------|--------|
| President | DOL appointments, executive orders |
| Senate | Confirm DOL, filibuster votes |
| House | Pass wage legislation |
| Governor | State enforcement, vetoes |
| State legislature | State minimum wage |
| Local | City minimum wage, preemption |

### Direct Engagement

**Contact Representatives**

| Method | Effectiveness |
|--------|---------------|
| Phone calls | High impact, track volume |
| Town halls | Public, visible |
| Office visits | Personal, memorable |
| Letters/emails | Creates record |
| Social media | Public pressure |

**Sample Message: Raise the Wage Act**

> "I urge you to support the Raise the Wage Act to increase the federal minimum wage to $17 and eliminate the tipped minimum wage. The current $7.25 minimum hasn't increased since 2009 and has lost over 30% of its value. Workers deserve a living wage."

**Sample Message: Overtime**

> "I urge you to support the Department of Labor's proposed overtime rule to raise the salary threshold to $58,656. Millions of workers are working 50+ hours a week without overtime because the threshold hasn't kept pace with inflation."

## Professional Actions

### If You're an Employer

**Pay Fair Wages**

| Action | Benefit |
|--------|---------|
| Pay living wages | Lower turnover, higher productivity |
| Eliminate tipped wages | Pay full minimum + tips |
| Transparent pay ranges | Attract talent, reduce discrimination |
| Regular raises | Keep pace with cost of living |

**Support Wage Policy**

| Action | How |
|--------|-----|
| Join Business for a Fair Minimum Wage | Public advocacy |
| Testify for increases | Legislative hearings |
| Op-eds and media | Counter industry opposition |
| Model practices | Show it can work |
