## Wage Theft

### Scale of the Problem

| Metric | Estimate |
|--------|----------|
| Annual wage theft (low estimate) | $15 billion |
| Annual wage theft (high estimate) | $50 billion |
| Workers affected annually | ~2.4 million |
| Average loss per affected worker | $3,300 |

**Common Forms of Wage Theft**

| Type | Description | Prevalence |
|------|-------------|------------|
| Minimum wage violations | Paying below legal minimum | 17% of low-wage workers |
| Overtime violations | Not paying 1.5x for hours over 40 | 19% of eligible workers |
| Off-the-clock work | Unpaid prep, cleanup, breaks | 25% of hourly workers |
| Tip theft | Employer taking tips | 12% of tipped workers |
| Misclassification | Treating employees as contractors | 10-30% of workers |
| Illegal deductions | Uniform costs, breakage, shortages | 5-10% of workers |

## Overtime and Exempt Status

### Current Overtime Rules

**Fair Labor Standards Act Requirements**

| Requirement | Current Rule |
|-------------|--------------|
| Overtime trigger | 40 hours/week |
| Overtime rate | 1.5x regular pay |
| Salary threshold for exemption | $35,568/year ($684/week) |
| Duties test | Executive, administrative, professional |
