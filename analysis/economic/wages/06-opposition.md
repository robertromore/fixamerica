# Wages: Opposition

## Who Opposes Wage Increases

### Industry Groups

**National Restaurant Association**

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Nickname | "The Other NRA" |
| Primary target | Tipped minimum wage |
| Annual lobbying | $6.6 million |
| Strategy | State preemption, oppose ballot measures |
| Key victory | Tipped minimum frozen since 1991 |

**U.S. Chamber of Commerce**

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Members | 3 million businesses |
| Annual lobbying | $81 million (largest in US) |
| Position | Oppose all minimum wage increases |
| Argument | Job losses, small business harm |
| Strategy | Federal lobbying, state-level preemption |

**International Franchise Association**

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Focus | Joint employer liability |
| Position | Franchisors shouldn't be liable for franchisee wages |
| Strategy | Legislation, DOL rulemaking |
| Key victory | 2020 DOL joint employer rule |

**National Retail Federation**

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Members | Major retailers, trade groups |
| Position | Oppose minimum wage mandates |
| Argument | Retail margins too thin |
| Contradiction | Many members (Walmart, Target) raised wages voluntarily |

### Gig Economy Companies

**Uber, Lyft, DoorDash, Instacart Coalition**

| Campaign | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| California Prop 22 (2020) | $200 million campaign |
| Result | Exempted gig workers from AB5 |
| Current strategy | Replicate in other states |
| Argument | "Flexibility" for workers |
| Counter | Workers prefer employee status with flexibility |

## Opposition Arguments

### Economic Arguments

**Job Loss Claims**

| Claim | Response |
|-------|----------|
| "Minimum wage increases cause unemployment" | Meta-analysis of 138 studies finds minimal effect |
| "Small businesses can't afford it" | Most minimum wage workers at large employers |
| "Teen unemployment will rise" | 89% of affected workers are adults |
| "Automation will accelerate" | Automation driven by technology, not wages |

**Research Reality**

| Study | Finding |
|-------|---------|
| Card & Krueger (1994) | No job loss from NJ minimum wage increase |
| Dube, Lester, Reich (2010) | No negative employment effects |
| CBO (2021) | $15 wage: 1.4M lifted from poverty; 0.9M jobs lost (contested) |
| Cengiz et al. (2019) | No job losses for workers below new minimum |

**Inflation Claims**

| Claim | Response |
|-------|----------|
| "Higher wages cause higher prices" | Estimates: 0.4% price increase per 10% wage increase |
| "Spiral of wage-price inflation" | Minimum wage too small a share of economy |
| "Hurts the people it's meant to help" | Wage gains exceed price increases for low-wage workers |

### Small Business Arguments

**Claims vs. Reality**

| Claim | Reality |
|-------|---------|
| "Mom and pop shops will close" | Most minimum wage workers at large firms |
| "Small businesses operate on thin margins" | Pass-through of costs modest |
| "Will force cuts to employee hours" | Studies show minimal hour reductions |
| "Small businesses oppose increases" | 67% of small business owners support $15 minimum |

**Who Actually Employs Minimum Wage Workers**

| Employer Size | Share of Minimum Wage Workers |
|---------------|------------------------------|
| 500+ employees | 67% |
| 100-499 employees | 15% |
| Under 100 employees | 18% |

### Market-Based Arguments

**Let the Market Decide**

| Argument | Counter |
|----------|---------|
| "Wages reflect productivity" | Productivity up 64%, wages up 14% since 1979 |
| "Workers can negotiate" | Power imbalance, monopsony |
| "Competition will raise wages" | Evidence shows employers don't compete |
| "Low wages reflect low skills" | Essential workers proved value in pandemic |

**Preferred Alternative: EITC**

| Argument | Counter |
|----------|---------|
| "Expand EITC instead" | Both work together; EITC subsidizes employers |
| "Taxpayer-funded, not employer-funded" | Employers should pay living wages |
| "Better targeted to poor" | Minimum wage reaches more workers |
| "No job losses" | Already have EITC; minimum wage still needed |

### Tipped Minimum Wage Arguments

**Industry Claims**

| Claim | Reality |
|-------|---------|
| "Servers prefer tips" | Surveys show preference for stable wages + tips |
| "Total earnings exceed minimum" | 12.8% poverty rate among tipped workers |
| "One Fair Wage would eliminate tips" | States with full minimum wage have same tip rates |
| "Restaurants would close" | States with $12+ tipped minimum thriving |

### Gig Economy Arguments

**Flexibility Narrative**

| Claim | Reality |
|-------|---------|
| "Workers want flexibility" | Workers want flexibility AND protections |
| "Not meant to be full-time" | 56% rely on gig work as primary income |
| "They're entrepreneurs" | No control over pricing, terms |
| "Benefits would raise costs" | Companies highly profitable |

## Opposition Tactics

### Legislative Strategies

**Federal Level**

| Tactic | Implementation |
|--------|----------------|
| Filibuster | Blocks minimum wage increases |
| Budget reconciliation rules | Parliamentarian blocked $15 in 2021 |
| Committee blocking | Prevent votes on increases |
| Delay | "Study it more" |

**State Level**

| Tactic | States Affected |
|--------|-----------------|
| Preemption laws | 26 states block local minimum wages |
| Tipped minimum exceptions | Most states |
| Agricultural worker exemptions | Many states |
| Small business exemptions | Various states |

## Weaknesses in Opposition

### Factual Problems

**Predictions That Failed**

| Prediction | Reality |
|------------|---------|
| "Seattle $15 will destroy jobs" | Employment grew, economy thrived |
| "California will lose restaurants" | Restaurant employment increased |
| "Fast food will automate" | Still labor-intensive |
| "Workers will be fired" | Hours and employment stable |

### Political Vulnerabilities

**Public Opinion Gap**

| Position | Public Support |
|----------|---------------|
| $15 minimum wage | 62% support |
| Equal pay | 85% support |
| Overtime expansion | 70% support |
| Hold employers accountable for wage theft | 80%+ support |

**Ballot Initiative Record**

| Year | State | Result |
|------|-------|--------|
| 2014 | Alaska, Arkansas, Nebraska, South Dakota | Passed (Red states) |
| 2016 | Arizona, Colorado, Maine, Washington | Passed |
| 2020 | Florida | Passed (60%+, Trump won state) |
| 2024 | Multiple states | Expected to pass |

### Internal Contradictions

**Corporate Actions vs. Lobbying**

| Company | Action | Lobbying |
|---------|--------|----------|
| Amazon | $15 minimum (2018) | Supports $15 federal (after reaching it) |
| Walmart | Multiple raises | Still opposes some increases |
| Target | $24/hour starting (2024) | Trade groups still oppose |
| Costco | $17+ starting | Contradicts industry claims |
