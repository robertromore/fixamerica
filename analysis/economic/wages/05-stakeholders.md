# Wages: Stakeholders

## Workers Affected

### Low-Wage Workers

**Who They Are**

| Category | Number |
|----------|--------|
| Workers earning $15/hour or less | 32 million |
| Workers at or below federal minimum | 1.1 million |
| Tipped workers | 5.5 million |
| Home health aides | 2.6 million |
| Retail workers | 15.6 million |
| Food service workers | 11.5 million |

**Demographics**

| Characteristic | Percentage of Low-Wage Workers |
|----------------|-------------------------------|
| Women | 58% |
| Workers over 25 | 89% |
| Workers with high school or less | 45% |
| Black workers | 15% (overrepresented) |
| Hispanic workers | 23% (overrepresented) |
| Parents | 28% |

**Stakes**

| Concern | Impact |
|---------|--------|
| Poverty | 2.7 million working poor |
| Food insecurity | 1 in 7 low-wage workers |
| Healthcare access | 30% uninsured |
| Housing instability | 40% cost-burdened |
| Reliance on public assistance | $153 billion in public costs for low-wage workers |

### Middle-Class Workers

**Who They Are**

| Category | Number |
|----------|--------|
| Workers earning $15-30/hour | 55 million |
| Non-supervisory workers | 80% of workforce |
| Workers without college degrees | 100 million |

**Stakes**

| Concern | Impact |
|---------|--------|
| Wage stagnation | 50 years of flat real wages |
| Lost overtime protections | 50 million formerly eligible, now exempt |
| Benefit erosion | Pensions replaced by 401(k)s |
| Downward mobility | Children earning less than parents |

### Tipped Workers

**Who They Are**

| Category | Number |
|----------|--------|
| Restaurant servers | 2.5 million |
| Bartenders | 650,000 |
| Other tipped workers | 2.4 million |
| Total tipped workforce | 5.5 million |

**Demographics**

| Characteristic | Rate |
|----------------|------|
| Women | 66% |
| Single mothers | 13% |
| Poverty rate | 12.8% (2x national average) |
| Reliance on tips | 58% of income |

**Stakes**

| Concern | Impact |
|---------|--------|
| Income volatility | Tips vary day to day |
| Sexual harassment | Highest rates of any industry |
| Poverty | $2.13 base makes tips essential |
| Employer tip theft | 12% experience it |

### Gig Workers

**Who They Are**

| Category | Number |
|----------|--------|
| Ride-share drivers | 1.5 million |
| Delivery drivers | 2.5 million |
| Other app-based workers | 1 million |
| All independent contractors | 16 million |

**Stakes**

| Concern | Impact |
|---------|--------|
| No minimum wage protection | Many earn below minimum |
| No overtime | Work 50+ hours without premium |
| No benefits | 100% responsible for healthcare |
| No unemployment insurance | COVID showed vulnerability |
| No workers' compensation | Injuries not covered |

### Misclassified Workers

**Who They Are**

| Industry | Estimated Misclassification Rate |
|----------|--------------------------------|
| Construction | 10-30% |
| Trucking | 20-40% |
| Janitorial | 15-25% |
| Home healthcare | 10-20% |

**Stakes**

| Concern | Impact |
|---------|--------|
| Lost wages | $3.4 billion annually |
| No legal protections | Minimum wage, overtime don't apply |
| Tax liability shifted | Must pay self-employment tax |
| Benefits | None provided |

## Power Holders

### Business Interests

**National Restaurant Association**

| Role | Position |
|------|----------|
| Members | 500,000+ restaurants |
| Lobbying spending | $6.6 million (2023) |
| Primary goal | Maintain $2.13 tipped minimum |
| Strategy | State preemption, oppose ballot measures |

**U.S. Chamber of Commerce**

| Role | Position |
|------|----------|
| Members | 3 million businesses |
| Lobbying spending | $81 million (2023) |
| Position on minimum wage | Oppose increases |
| Position on overtime | Oppose threshold increases |

**International Franchise Association**

| Role | Position |
|------|----------|
| Members | 1,400 franchise brands |
| Primary goal | Maintain joint employer protection |
| Strategy | Oppose AB5-style laws |
| Lobbying | $3.2 million (2023) |

**Gig Economy Companies**

| Company | Lobbying Spending (2023) |
|---------|-------------------------|
| Uber | $3.8 million |
| Lyft | $2.1 million |
| DoorDash | $1.5 million |
| Instacart | $1.1 million |
| Strategy | Prop 22-style carve-outs |

**Service Employees International Union (SEIU)**

| Role | Power |
|------|-------|
| Members | 2 million |
| Fight for $15 campaign | Lead organizer |
| Political spending | $200+ million (2020 cycle) |
| Focus | Low-wage workers, healthcare |

**AFL-CIO**

| Role | Power |
|------|-------|
| Members | 12.5 million (through affiliates) |
| Lobbying | $9.5 million (2023) |
| Position | $17+ minimum wage, overtime expansion |
| Political action | Largest labor federation |

**United Food and Commercial Workers (UFCW)**

| Role | Power |
|------|-------|
| Members | 1.3 million |
| Industries | Retail, food processing |
| Focus | Retail worker wages |
| Recent wins | Kroger, Safeway contracts |

**UNITE HERE**

| Role | Power |
|------|-------|
| Members | 300,000 |
| Industries | Hotels, food service |
| Focus | Tipped workers, One Fair Wage |
| Strategy | City-by-city organizing |

**Economic Policy Institute**

| Role | Contribution |
|------|--------------|
| Type | Research and policy |
| Key work | Minimum wage research, wage tracker |
| Influence | Cited by policymakers |
| Position | Strong minimum wage increases |

**National Employment Law Project (NELP)**

| Role | Contribution |
|------|--------------|

| Focus | Worker misclassification, wage theft |
| Strategy | Litigation, model legislation |
| Position | ABC test, strong enforcement |

**One Fair Wage**

| Role | Contribution |
|------|--------------|
| Type | Advocacy organization |
| Focus | Ending tipped minimum wage |
| Strategy | Ballot initiatives, direct action |
| Wins | Seven states eliminated tipped minimum |

**Fight for $15**

| Role | Contribution |
|------|--------------|
| Type | Worker movement |
| Focus | $15 minimum wage, union rights |
| Strategy | Strikes, political pressure |
| Wins | State laws, corporate policies (Amazon, Target) |

### Government Actors

**Department of Labor**

| Role | Power |
|------|-------|
| Wage and Hour Division | Enforces FLSA |
| Authority | Minimum wage, overtime, child labor |
| Limitation | Underfunded, 760 investigators |
| Regulatory power | Overtime threshold, classification |

**Congressional Committees**

| Committee | Role |
|-----------|------|
| Senate HELP Committee | Labor legislation |
| House Education and Labor | FLSA amendments |
| Key barrier | Senate filibuster |

**State Labor Departments**

| Role | Power |
|------|-------|
| Enforcement | State wage laws |
| Variation | Some states aggressive, others weak |
| Examples | California DLSE strong; Texas minimal |

## Influence Dynamics

### Business vs. Labor

**Resource Imbalance**

| Metric | Business | Labor |
|--------|----------|-------|
| Lobbying spending | $3.8 billion/year | $50 million/year |
| Campaign contributions | $2.9 billion (2020) | $241 million (2020) |
| Think tanks | Multiple well-funded | Limited resources |
| Media access | Extensive | Limited |

### Federal vs. State Action

**Current Dynamics**

| Level | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Federal | Gridlock since 2009 |
| States | 30+ above federal minimum |
| Cities | 40+ with local minimums |
| Preemption | 26 states block local increases |

### Public Opinion vs. Policy

**Opinion-Policy Gap**

| Issue | Public Support | Policy Status |
|-------|---------------|---------------|
| $15 minimum wage | 62% | Not enacted federally |
| Equal pay enforcement | 85% | Weak enforcement |
| Overtime expansion | 70% | Limited increase |
| Paid family leave | 84% | No federal mandate |
