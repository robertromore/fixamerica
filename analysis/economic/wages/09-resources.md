## Key Studies and Reports

### Minimum Wage Research

**Meta-Analyses**

| Study | Citation | Finding |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Dube (2019) | "Minimum Wages and the Distribution of Family Incomes" | Optimal minimum is ~59% of median |
| Cengiz et al. (2019) | "The Effect of Minimum Wages on Low-Wage Jobs" | No job losses, wages rise |
| Neumark & Shirley (2022) | "Myth or Measurement: The New Economics of the Minimum Wage" | Mixed results |

**Landmark Studies**

| Study | Citation | Finding |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Card & Krueger (1994) | "Minimum Wages and Employment: A Case Study" | No job loss in NJ |
| Dube, Lester, Reich (2010) | "Minimum Wage Effects Across State Borders" | No negative employment effects |
| CBO (2021) | "Effects of a $15 Minimum Wage" | 900K job loss estimate (contested) |

**City Studies**

| Study | Citation | Finding |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Jardim et al. (2017) | "Minimum Wage Increases, Wages, and Low-Wage Employment" | Seattle hours reduced |
| Reich et al. (2017) | "Seattle's Minimum Wage Experience" | Positive effects |
| Allegretto et al. (2018) | "The New Wave of Local Minimum Wage Policies" | No employment effects |
