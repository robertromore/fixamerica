# Wages: History

## Origins

### Pre-Industrial America

**Early Wage Labor**

| Era | Characteristics |
|-----|-----------------|
| Colonial period | Master-servant relationships; indentured servitude |
| Early Republic | Artisan wages; piece-rate work |
| Antebellum | Factory system emerges; wage labor expands |
| Post-Civil War | Free labor ideology; industrial wage system |

**Early Labor Organizing**

| Event | Significance |
|-------|--------------|
| Philadelphia Cordwainers Strike (1806) | First recorded strike for wages |
| Workingmen's Parties (1820s-30s) | 10-hour day movement |
| Lowell Mill Strikes (1834, 1836) | Women workers protest wage cuts |
| Commonwealth v. Hunt (1842) | Unions declared legal |

### Gilded Age (1870s-1900)

**Wage Conditions**

| Metric | Conditions |
|--------|------------|
| Average work week | 60-70 hours |
| Average daily wage | $1.00-$2.00 |
| Child labor | 1.75 million children employed |
| Workplace deaths | 35,000 annually |

**Major Labor Actions**

| Event | Year | Issue |
|-------|------|-------|
| Great Railroad Strike | 1877 | 10% wage cut |
| Haymarket Affair | 1886 | 8-hour day |
| Homestead Strike | 1892 | Wage cuts |
| Pullman Strike | 1894 | Wage cuts |

## Key Turning Points

### Progressive Era (1900-1920)

**First Minimum Wage Laws**

| Development | Year | Details |
|-------------|------|---------|
| Massachusetts | 1912 | First state minimum wage (women only) |
| Additional states | 1913-1920 | 14 states adopt similar laws |
| Federal rejection | 1918 | Congress declines national minimum |
| *Adkins v. Children's Hospital* | 1923 | Supreme Court strikes down DC minimum wage |

**Labor Standards Progress**

| Reform | Year | Impact |
|--------|------|--------|
| Triangle Fire | 1911 | Spurs workplace safety laws |
| Department of Labor | 1913 | Federal labor agency created |
| Adamson Act | 1916 | 8-hour day for railroad workers |
| Child labor laws | 1916 | First federal restrictions (struck down 1918) |

### The New Deal (1933-1945)

**Fair Labor Standards Act (1938)**

| Provision | Initial Standard |
|-----------|------------------|
| Minimum wage | $0.25/hour (1938) |
| Maximum workweek | 44 hours (reduced to 40 by 1940) |
| Overtime pay | Time-and-a-half after maximum |
| Child labor ban | Under 16 prohibited in most industries |

**FLSA Coverage**

| Year | Workers Covered |
|------|-----------------|
| 1938 | 20% (11 million) |
| 1950 | 54% |
| 1966 | 75% |
| Today | 84% |

**Labor Movement Growth**

| Year | Union Membership |
|------|-----------------|
| 1933 | 2.9 million (5.8%) |
| 1938 | 8.0 million (14.6%) |
| 1945 | 14.3 million (35.4%) |

### Post-War Golden Age (1945-1973)

**Wage and Productivity Alignment**

| Period | Productivity Growth | Wage Growth |
|--------|---------------------|-------------|
| 1948-1973 | 96.7% | 91.3% |
| Pattern | Wages tracked productivity closely |

**Minimum Wage Increases**

| Year | Rate | 2024 Dollars |
|------|------|--------------|
| 1950 | $0.75 | $9.56 |
| 1956 | $1.00 | $11.28 |
| 1963 | $1.25 | $12.54 |
| 1968 | $1.60 | $14.09 (peak purchasing power) |

**Key Developments**

| Event | Year | Impact |
|-------|------|--------|
| Treaty of Detroit | 1950 | UAW-GM contract sets pattern bargaining |
| AFL-CIO merger | 1955 | Labor movement consolidation |
| Equal Pay Act | 1963 | Gender-based wage discrimination banned |
| Civil Rights Act | 1964 | Race/sex discrimination in employment banned |

## Timeline

### 1970s: The Great Divergence Begins

**Productivity-Pay Decoupling**

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1971 | Nixon wage-price controls |
| 1973 | OPEC oil embargo; stagflation begins |
| 1974 | Minimum wage raised to $2.00 |
| 1975 | Real wages begin long decline |
| 1978 | California Proposition 13; tax revolt |

**Structural Changes**

| Factor | Impact on Wages |
|--------|-----------------|
| Deindustrialization | High-wage manufacturing jobs disappear |
| Oil shocks | Inflation erodes real wages |
| Automation | Reduces labor bargaining power |
| Globalization begins | Low-wage competition emerges |

### 1980s: The Reagan Revolution

**Policy Changes**

| Policy | Effect on Wages |
|--------|-----------------|
| PATCO firing (1981) | Signal against unions |
| Tax cuts (1981) | Shifted burden from capital to labor |
| NLRB appointments | Anti-union enforcement |
| Minimum wage freeze | $3.35 from 1981-1990 (9 years) |

**Minimum Wage Purchasing Power**

| Year | Rate | 2024 Dollars |
|------|------|--------------|
| 1981 | $3.35 | $11.27 |
| 1985 | $3.35 | $9.54 |
| 1989 | $3.35 | $8.27 |
| Value lost | - | 27% |

**Labor Movement Decline**

| Year | Union Membership Rate |
|------|----------------------|
| 1980 | 23.0% |
| 1985 | 18.0% |
| 1990 | 16.1% |

### 1990s: The New Economy

**Minimum Wage Resumes Increases**

| Year | Rate | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| 1990 | $3.80 | First increase in 9 years |
| 1991 | $4.25 | Further increase |
| 1996 | $4.75 | Clinton era |
| 1997 | $5.15 | Remained until 2007 |

**Tipped Minimum Wage Frozen**

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1991 | Tipped minimum set at $2.13 |
| 1996 | Decoupled from regular minimum |
| 1991-present | No federal increase (33+ years) |

**Welfare Reform Impact**

| Effect | Impact on Wages |
|--------|-----------------|
| Work requirements | Increased low-wage labor supply |
| Time limits | Pressure to accept any job |
| EITC expansion | Subsidized low-wage employers |

### 2000s: Stagnation and Crisis

**Minimum Wage Decade**

| Year | Rate | Real Value (2024) |
|------|------|-------------------|
| 2000 | $5.15 | $9.17 |
| 2007 | $5.85 | $8.64 (first increase in 10 years) |
| 2008 | $6.55 | $9.33 |
| 2009 | $7.25 | $10.37 |

**Financial Crisis Effects**

| Impact | Effect on Wages |
|--------|-----------------|
| Unemployment spike | 10% (2009) |
| Labor market slack | Suppressed wage growth |
| Foreclosures | Workers desperate for any job |
| Recovery | Slow, "jobless recovery" |

### 2010s: Fight for $15 and State Action

**State and Local Movement**

| Year | Development |
|------|-------------|

| 2014 | Seattle passes $15 minimum wage |
| 2015 | New York, California announce $15 path |
| 2016 | 20+ states raise minimum wages |

**Federal Inaction**

| Period | Federal Minimum |
|--------|-----------------|
| 2009-2024 | $7.25 (15+ years, longest period without increase) |
| Previous longest | 1997-2007 (10 years at $5.15) |

**Overtime Rule Battles**

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2004 | Bush administration raises threshold to $23,660 |
| 2016 | Obama rule ($47,476 threshold) blocked by courts |
| 2019 | Trump administration sets $35,568 threshold |

**COVID-19 Impact**

| Labor shortages (2021-22) | Drove wage increases |
| "Great Resignation" | Workers demanded better pay |
| Inflation spike | Eroded real wage gains |

**Recent Developments**

| Year | Development |
|------|-------------|

| 2022 | 26 states above federal minimum |
| 2023 | California raises to $16 |
| 2024 | 30+ states above federal minimum |

## Lessons from the Past

### What Has Worked

**Successful Policies**

| Policy | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| FLSA (1938) | Established wage floor, overtime, child labor ban |
| Minimum wage increases (1938-1968) | Kept pace with productivity |
| Strong union presence | Lifted all wages through pattern bargaining |
| Equal Pay Act / Civil Rights Act | Reduced discrimination |

### What Has Failed

**Failed Approaches**

| Approach | Problem |
|----------|---------|
| Relying on market alone | Wages stagnated for decades |
| Infrequent minimum wage increases | Lost purchasing power to inflation |
| Weak enforcement | Wage theft epidemic |
| Narrow FLSA exemptions | Gamed by employers |

### Recurring Patterns

**Historical Constants**

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Employer resistance to increases | Every minimum wage debate |
| Predicted job losses don't materialize | Study after study shows minimal effects |
| States lead when federal government fails | Current state/local movement |
| Strong labor = rising wages | 1940s-1970s vs. post-1980 |
| Erosion without indexing | Purchasing power declines between increases |
