# Wages: Solutions

## Minimum Wage Reform

### Raise the Federal Minimum Wage

**Proposal: $17 Minimum Wage with Indexing**

| Element | Specification |
|---------|---------------|
| Target rate | $17/hour |
| Phase-in | 5 years |
| Indexing | Automatic adjustment to median wage |
| Regional variation | None (floor, not ceiling) |
| Subminimum wages | Eliminate |

**Impact Estimates**

| Metric | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| Workers with higher wages | 33 million |
| Workers lifted from poverty | 1.3 million |
| Wage increases | $144 billion annually |
| Ripple effects | Wages rise above new minimum too |

**Evidence Base**

| Study | Finding |
|-------|---------|
| Dube (2019) | Optimal minimum is 59% of median |
| CBO (2021) | $15 minimum: 900K jobs lost vs. 1.4M out of poverty |
| Cengiz et al. (2019) | No job losses in decades of increases |
| Seattle study | Employment grew after $15 implementation |

### Eliminate the Tipped Minimum Wage

**One Fair Wage**

| Element | Specification |
|---------|---------------|
| Current tipped minimum | $2.13/hour |
| Proposal | Full minimum wage for all workers |
| Tip credit | Eliminate |
| States already doing this | CA, WA, OR, NV, MT, MN, AK |

**Benefits**

| Benefit | Evidence |
|---------|----------|
| Reduced poverty | 12.8% poverty rate would fall |
| Reduced sexual harassment | 5x higher in tipped states |
| Stable income | Less volatility for workers |
| Enforcement simplification | Easier to verify compliance |

**Implementation**

| Phase | Timeline |
|-------|----------|
| Year 1 | Raise to $6.00/hour |
| Year 2 | Raise to $9.00/hour |
| Year 3 | Raise to $12.00/hour |
| Year 4 | Equal to regular minimum |

### Automatic Indexing

**Indexing Options**

| Method | Advantages | Disadvantages |
|--------|------------|---------------|
| Consumer Price Index (CPI) | Maintains purchasing power | Doesn't account for productivity |
| Median wage | Maintains relative position | More volatile |
| Productivity | Reconnects pay and output | Politically complex |
| Greater of CPI or median | Prevents erosion | Higher increases |

**State Models**

| State | Indexing Method |
|-------|-----------------|
| Washington | CPI annually |
| California | Paused if economy contracts |
| Colorado | CPI and wage data |
| Arizona | CPI annually |

## Overtime Reform

### Expand Overtime Eligibility

**Raise Salary Threshold**

| Current | Proposed |
|---------|----------|
| $35,568/year | $75,000/year |
| 15% of salaried workers | 55% of salaried workers |

**Automatic Updating**

| Approach | Mechanism |
|----------|-----------|
| Percentile-based | 40th percentile of full-time salaried workers |
| Annual adjustment | Updates automatically |
| Prevents erosion | No congressional action needed |

**Strengthen Duties Test**

| Current Problem | Solution |
|-----------------|----------|
| Vague "executive" duties | Require actual supervisory authority |
| "Administrative" loophole | Narrow to genuine business decisions |
| "Professional" expansion | Limit to traditional learned professions |
| Primary duty | Must be majority of time |

### Address Comp Time Concerns

**Worker-Protective Comp Time**

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| Worker choice | Must be voluntary |
| Rate | 1.5 hours for each overtime hour |
| Cash-out right | Can request cash at any time |
| Expiration | Must be paid out within 30 days of request |
| No coercion | Protected from retaliation |

## Worker Classification Reform

### ABC Test for All Workers

**Standard**

| Prong | Requirement |
|-------|-------------|
| A - Autonomy | Free from control and direction |
| B - Business | Outside usual course of hiring entity's business |
| C - Custom | Customarily engaged in independent business |
| All three | Must satisfy all to be independent contractor |

**Implementation**

| Level | Action |
|-------|--------|
| Federal | Apply to FLSA, tax code, NLRA |
| State | Model legislation for state laws |
| Enforcement | Presumption of employee status |
| Penalties | Back wages, benefits, taxes |

## Wage Theft Enforcement

### Strengthen Enforcement

**Increase Enforcement Capacity**

| Current | Proposed |
|---------|----------|
| 760 investigators | 2,000 investigators |
| 175,000 workers per investigator | 70,000 workers per investigator |
| 175-year inspection cycle | 50-year inspection cycle |
| $230 million recovered | $1 billion+ recovered |

**Enhanced Penalties**

| Violation | Current | Proposed |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| Minimum wage violation | Back wages + liquidated damages | 3x back wages + penalties |
| Repeat violation | Same | 4x back wages + criminal referral |
| Willful violation | Back wages | Criminal prosecution |
| Retaliation | Reinstatement | Treble damages |

### Private Enforcement

**Strengthen Private Right of Action**

| Element | Specification |
|---------|---------------|
| Class actions | Preserve and protect |
| Attorney's fees | Mandatory for prevailing plaintiffs |
| Statute of limitations | 6 years (up from 2-3) |
| Liquidated damages | Automatic, not discretionary |

**Wage Lien Laws**

| Mechanism | Benefit |
|-----------|---------|
| Automatic lien | Workers can secure claim against employer assets |
| Priority | Wage claims first in bankruptcy |
| Personal liability | Officers personally liable |
| Bonding | Require wage bonds in high-violation industries |

### Prevention Mechanisms

**Wage Bond Requirements**

| Element | Specification |
|---------|---------------|
| High-violation industries | Construction, agriculture, janitorial |
| Bond amount | Equal to quarterly payroll |
| Trigger | Serious violation or multiple violations |
| Release | After compliance period |

**Payroll Transparency**

| Requirement | Benefit |
|-------------|---------|
| Itemized pay stubs | Workers can verify pay |
| Work hour records | Required retention and access |
| Regular pay schedules | Predictable payment |
| Electronic records | Department access |

## CEO Pay and Pay Equity

### CEO-Worker Pay Ratio Reforms

**Tax Incentives/Penalties**

| Ratio | Tax Adjustment |
|-------|----------------|
| Below 50:1 | 2% rate reduction |
| 100:1 to 200:1 | 2% rate increase |
| 200:1 to 300:1 | 4% rate increase |
| Above 300:1 | 6% rate increase |

**Government Contracting**

| Ratio | Consequence |
|-------|-------------|
| Above 100:1 | Ineligible for federal contracts |
| Above 50:1 | Points deducted in contract evaluation |
| Below 25:1 | Preference in contract award |

**Salary Range Disclosure**

| Requirement | Benefit |
|-------------|---------|
| Job postings include salary range | Information equality |
| Range limits | Within 20% spread |
| Internal posting | Current employees informed |
| Enforcement | Fines for non-compliance |

**Pay Data Reporting**

| Requirement | Entity |
|-------------|--------|
| EEO-1 Component 2 | Restore compensation data collection |
| Public disclosure | Summary data publicly available |
| Audit requirements | Companies must analyze pay gaps |
| Corrective action | Address identified disparities |

### Equal Pay Enforcement

**Paycheck Fairness Act**

| Provision | Effect |
|-----------|--------|

| Strengthen "bona fide factor" defense | Must be job-related and business necessity |
| Protect pay discussions | Workers can share salary information |
| Enhanced penalties | Compensatory and punitive damages |

## International Models

| Feature | Application |
