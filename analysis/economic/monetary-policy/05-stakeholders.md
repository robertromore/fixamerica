# Monetary Policy: Stakeholders

## Who Is Affected

### Primary Stakeholders

**Workers and Job Seekers**

| Interest | Impact of monetary policy |
|----------|--------------------------|
| Employment | Rate hikes cause job losses |
| Wages | Tight labor markets raise wages |
| Job security | Recessions threaten employment |
| Bargaining power | Unemployment weakens leverage |

| Group | Vulnerability to Fed policy |
|-------|---------------------------|
| Low-wage workers | First fired in downturns |
| Young workers | Recessions scar careers |
| Minority workers | Higher unemployment sensitivity |
| Gig workers | No unemployment insurance |

**Savers and Retirees**

| Interest | Impact |
|----------|--------|
| Savings returns | Low rates hurt |
| Fixed income | Inflation erodes purchasing power |
| Pension values | Rate changes affect funding |
| Social Security | COLA adjustments lag inflation |

| Group | Specific concern |
|-------|-----------------|
| Retirees on fixed income | Low rates devastating |
| Future retirees | Asset inflation helps |
| Pension beneficiaries | Funding status matters |
| Small savers | Negative real returns |

**Homeowners and Homebuyers**

| Interest | Impact |
|----------|--------|
| Mortgage rates | Directly affected by Fed |
| Home values | Low rates inflate prices |
| Affordability | Trade-off: rates vs. prices |
| Refinancing | Rate changes create windows |

| Group | Situation |
|-------|-----------|
| Current homeowners | Benefit from appreciation |
| First-time buyers | Priced out by both |
| Renters | No wealth building |
| Real estate investors | Leverage amplifies gains |

### Financial Sector Stakeholders

**Banks**

| Interest | Impact |
|----------|--------|
| Net interest margin | Spread between rates |
| Loan demand | Rates affect borrowing |
| Asset values | Bond portfolios sensitive |
| Regulation | Fed is regulator too |

| Bank type | Policy sensitivity |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Large banks | Systemic importance |
| Regional banks | Rate risk exposure |
| Community banks | Lending margins squeezed |
| Investment banks | Trading affected |

**Asset Managers**

| Interest | Impact |
|----------|--------|
| Asset prices | QE inflates |
| Fees | Based on assets under management |
| Performance | Benchmark returns |
| Risk appetite | Fed policy shapes |

**Insurance Companies**

| Interest | Impact |
|----------|--------|
| Investment returns | Low rates hurt |
| Liabilities | Duration matching |
| Product pricing | Annuity rates |
| Solvency | Asset-liability match |

### Business Stakeholders

**Large Corporations**

| Interest | Impact |
|----------|--------|

| Investment decisions | Rate-sensitive |
| Currency effects | Dollar strength |

**Small Businesses**

| Interest | Impact |
|----------|--------|

| Consumer demand | Affected by policy |
| Survival | Recessions existential |

**Real Estate Developers**

| Interest | Impact |
|----------|--------|
| Financing costs | Major factor |
| Property values | Directly affected |
| Construction demand | Rate-sensitive |
| Commercial viability | Cap rates matter |

## Stakeholder Influence

### Who Has Power

| Stakeholder | Influence mechanism | Level |
|-------------|---------------------|-------|
| Large banks | Board positions, lobbying | Very high |
| Wall Street | Personnel pipeline | High |
| Academic economists | Ideas, legitimacy | High |
| Congress | Oversight, legislation | Medium |
| Business groups | Lobbying | Medium |
| Financial media | Narrative framing | Medium |
| Workers | Votes (indirect) | Low |
| Small savers | None | Very low |

### Who Should Have More Voice

| Stakeholder | Rationale |
|-------------|-----------|
| Workers | Half of dual mandate |
| Consumers | Directly affected |
| Small businesses | Credit access |
| Community groups | Local conditions |
| Future generations | Long-term consequences |

## Stakeholder Positions on Reform

### Governance Reform

| Stakeholder | Position |
|-------------|----------|
| Big banks | Oppose changes |
| Progressives | Support democratization |
| Conservatives | Mixed (some want less Fed power) |
| Academic mainstream | Generally supportive of status quo |
| Labor | Support worker representation |

### Mandate Expansion

| Stakeholder | Position on expanding mandate |
|-------------|-------------------------------|
| Climate activists | Support climate mandate |
| Financial industry | Oppose |
| Full employment advocates | Support employment priority |
| Inflation hawks | Oppose expansion |
| Racial justice groups | Support equity considerations |

### Transparency

| Stakeholder | Position |
|-------------|----------|
| Financial industry | Prefer less |
| Public interest groups | Want more |
| Academic researchers | Want more data |
| Congress | Varies by party |
| Media | Generally support transparency |
