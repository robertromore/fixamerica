# Immigration: Legislation and Legal Framework

## Overview

Immigration reform requires comprehensive legislation addressing legal immigration backlogs, border processing, asylum, the unauthorized population, enforcement, and courts. This document provides draft legislation for key reforms and analysis of existing legal frameworks.

## Comprehensive Immigration Reform Act

**Purpose**: Establish a functional immigration system with legal pathways, earned legalization, smart enforcement, and due process.

### Title I: Legal Immigration Reform

```text
SECTION 101. SHORT TITLE.

This title may be cited as the "Legal Immigration
Modernization Act".

SEC. 102. ELIMINATION OF PER-COUNTRY LIMITS.

(a) FAMILY-SPONSORED.—Section 202(a)(2) of the
Immigration and Nationality Act is amended by
striking the 7 percent limitation for family-
sponsored immigrants.

(b) EMPLOYMENT-BASED.—Section 202(a)(2) is
amended by striking the 7 percent limitation
for employment-based immigrants.

SEC. 103. VISA RECAPTURE.

(a) RECAPTURE.—Immigrant visas that were
authorized but unused in fiscal years 1992
through the fiscal year preceding enactment
are recaptured and made available.

(b) AVAILABILITY.—Recaptured visas shall be
available in addition to annual limitations.

SEC. 104. EMPLOYMENT-BASED REFORM.

(a) NUMERICAL LIMITATION.—Section 201(d) is
amended by increasing the annual employment-
based limitation to 290,000.

(b) STEM EXEMPTION.—Aliens with advanced
degrees in STEM fields from U.S. institutions
shall be exempt from numerical limitations.

(c) ENTREPRENEUR VISA.—A new category for
entrepreneurs creating jobs in the United
States is established.

SEC. 105. FAMILY-BASED REFORM.

(a) IMMEDIATE RELATIVES.—Spouses and minor
children of lawful permanent residents shall
be treated as immediate relatives.

(b) BACKLOG CLEARANCE.—Additional visas are
authorized to clear existing backlogs within
5 years.

SEC. 106. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This title takes effect on the date of enactment.
```

### Title II: DREAM Act

```text
SECTION 201. SHORT TITLE.

This title may be cited as the "Development,
Relief, and Education for Alien Minors Act"
or the "DREAM Act".

SEC. 202. CONDITIONAL PERMANENT RESIDENT STATUS.

(a) ELIGIBILITY.—The Secretary shall grant
conditional permanent resident status to an
alien who—
    (1) was younger than 18 years of age on
    the date the alien initially entered the
    United States;
    (2) has been continuously present in the
    United States for not less than 4 years;
    (3) has been a person of good moral
    character;
    (4) is not inadmissible or deportable on
    certain criminal or security grounds;
    (5) has been admitted to an institution
    of higher education, has earned a high
    school diploma, or is enrolled in
    secondary school.

(b) DURATION.—Conditional status shall be
valid for 10 years.

SEC. 203. REMOVAL OF CONDITIONS.

(a) ELIGIBILITY.—The Secretary shall remove
conditions if the alien—
    (1) has maintained continuous presence;
    (2) has acquired a degree from an
    institution of higher education or
    completed at least 2 years toward a
    bachelor's degree;
    (3) has served in the Armed Forces for
    at least 2 years; or
    (4) has been employed for at least 3
    years and demonstrates economic
    self-sufficiency.

(b) ADJUSTMENT.—Upon removal of conditions,
the alien shall be granted lawful permanent
resident status.

SEC. 204. NATURALIZATION.

Aliens granted lawful permanent resident
status under this title may apply for
naturalization under standard procedures.
```

### Title III: Legalization

```text
SECTION 301. SHORT TITLE.

This title may be cited as the "Earned
Legalization Act".

SEC. 302. REGISTRATION.

(a) REQUIREMENT.—Aliens present in the United
States without lawful status shall register
during a 1-year registration period.

(b) ELIGIBILITY.—An alien is eligible who—
    (1) has been continuously present for
    5 years preceding enactment;
    (2) was not convicted of a felony or
    3 or more misdemeanors;
    (3) is not inadmissible on security grounds;
    (4) pays required fees and penalties.

(c) PROVISIONAL STATUS.—Registered aliens
shall receive provisional legal status with
work authorization.

SEC. 303. ADJUSTMENT TO LPR STATUS.

(a) ELIGIBILITY.—A registered alien may
adjust to lawful permanent resident status
after—
    (1) 6 years in provisional status;
    (2) payment of all taxes owed;
    (3) demonstration of English proficiency
    and civics knowledge;
    (4) continuous employment or enrollment
    in education;
    (5) payment of additional fees.

(b) TIMING.—Adjustment shall occur after
all aliens in the existing backlog have
been processed.

SEC. 304. INELIGIBILITY.

(a) CRIMINAL GROUNDS.—Aliens convicted of
felonies, aggravated felonies, or 3 or more
misdemeanors are ineligible.

(b) SECURITY GROUNDS.—Aliens inadmissible
on terrorism or security grounds are ineligible.

SEC. 305. FEES.

(a) APPLICATION FEE.—A fee of $500 for
registration.

(b) PENALTY.—A penalty of $1,000 for
unlawful presence.

(c) ADJUSTMENT FEE.—Standard USCIS fees
for adjustment.
```

### Title IV: Border Security and Processing

```text
SECTION 401. SHORT TITLE.

This title may be cited as the "Border
Security and Processing Act".

SEC. 402. BORDER PROCESSING CAPACITY.

(a) PERSONNEL.—The Secretary shall hire
sufficient personnel to process all arrivals
in a timely manner.

(b) FACILITIES.—The Secretary shall construct
or acquire facilities for humane processing.

(c) TECHNOLOGY.—The Secretary shall deploy
technology for identification and screening.

SEC. 403. PORTS OF ENTRY.

(a) MODERNIZATION.—Land ports of entry shall
be modernized to handle current volumes.

(b) STAFFING.—Adequate CBP officers shall
be assigned to each port.

SEC. 404. LEGAL PATHWAYS.

(a) APPOINTMENT SYSTEM.—A robust appointment
system shall be maintained for asylum seekers.

(b) REGIONAL PROCESSING.—The Secretary shall
establish regional processing centers in
partnership with other nations.

(c) PAROLE PROGRAMS.—Legal pathways shall
be available as alternatives to irregular
migration.

SEC. 405. INTEROPERABILITY.

Systems shall allow information sharing
between DHS components and with international
partners.
```

### Title V: Asylum Reform

```text
SECTION 501. SHORT TITLE.

This title may be cited as the "Asylum
Reform Act".

SEC. 502. TIMELY ADJUDICATION.

(a) TIMELINE.—Initial asylum adjudications
shall be completed within 6 months of
application.

(b) ASYLUM OFFICERS.—Sufficient asylum
officers shall be hired to meet timelines.

SEC. 503. ACCESS TO COUNSEL.

(a) RIGHT TO COUNSEL.—Asylum seekers shall
have access to legal representation.

(b) LEGAL ORIENTATION.—The Secretary shall
ensure access to legal orientation programs.

(c) APPOINTED COUNSEL.—For unaccompanied
minors and those with mental disabilities,
counsel shall be appointed.

SEC. 504. WORK AUTHORIZATION.

Asylum applicants shall receive work
authorization within 30 days of filing
a complete application.

SEC. 505. CREDIBLE FEAR.

(a) STANDARD.—The credible fear standard
shall be applied consistent with statutory
requirements.

(b) REVIEW.—Immigration judges shall review
negative determinations de novo.

SEC. 506. PROTECTION.

Nothing in this title shall be construed
to diminish protections under the 1951
Refugee Convention or 1967 Protocol.
```

### Title VI: Immigration Courts

```text
SECTION 601. SHORT TITLE.

This title may be cited as the "Immigration
Court Reform Act".

SEC. 602. ARTICLE I COURT.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—There is established an
Article I Immigration Court of the United
States, independent of the Department of
Justice.

(b) JURISDICTION.—The Court shall have
jurisdiction over removal proceedings,
applications for relief, and related matters.

SEC. 603. JUDGES.

(a) APPOINTMENT.—Immigration judges shall
be appointed by the President with advice
and consent of the Senate for 15-year terms.

(b) QUALIFICATIONS.—Judges shall have at
least 7 years of immigration law experience.

(c) REMOVAL.—Judges may be removed only for
cause by the Chief Judge.

(d) NUMBER.—The Court shall have sufficient
judges to adjudicate cases within 1 year.

SEC. 604. DUE PROCESS.

(a) RIGHTS.—Respondents shall have the right
to—
    (1) notice of charges;
    (2) reasonable time to prepare;
    (3) present evidence;
    (4) cross-examine witnesses;
    (5) appeal adverse decisions.

(b) BOND.—Bond determinations shall consider
flight risk and danger, with presumption
of release.

SEC. 605. APPEALS.

(a) BOARD.—The Board of Immigration Appeals
shall be established within the Court.

(b) REVIEW.—Appeals shall be decided within
180 days.
```

### Title VII: Employment Verification

```text
SECTION 701. SHORT TITLE.

This title may be cited as the "Employment
Verification Act".

SEC. 702. MANDATORY E-VERIFY.

(a) REQUIREMENT.—All employers shall verify
work authorization through E-Verify.

(b) PHASE-IN.—
    (1) Federal contractors—Year 1
    (2) Employers with 500+ employees—Year 2
    (3) Employers with 50+ employees—Year 3
    (4) All employers—Year 4

SEC. 703. IMPROVEMENTS.

(a) ACCURACY.—E-Verify shall be improved
to reduce error rates below 0.1%.

(b) PHOTO MATCHING.—Photo tool shall be
expanded.

(c) BIOMETRICS.—Biometric verification
shall be phased in.

SEC. 704. EMPLOYER PROTECTIONS.

(a) SAFE HARBOR.—Employers who use E-Verify
in good faith shall not be liable for
unauthorized employment.

(b) DISCRIMINATION.—Employers shall not
discriminate based on national origin or
citizenship status.

SEC. 705. WORKER PROTECTIONS.

(a) CONTEST PERIOD.—Workers shall have
10 business days to contest tentative
non-confirmations.

(b) REMEDIES.—Workers harmed by errors
shall have access to administrative remedies.

SEC. 706. PENALTIES.

(a) KNOWING VIOLATIONS.—Civil penalties
for knowing employment of unauthorized
workers.

(b) PATTERN VIOLATIONS.—Enhanced penalties
for pattern or practice violations.

(c) CRIMINAL.—Criminal penalties for
repeated willful violations.
```

## State Model Legislation

### Model State DREAM Act

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "[State]
DREAM Act".

SECTION 2. IN-STATE TUITION.

An individual shall be eligible for in-state
tuition at public institutions of higher
education if the individual—
    (1) attended a [State] high school for
    3 or more years;
    (2) graduated from a [State] high school
    or received a GED in [State];
    (3) registers or is registered at an
    accredited institution of higher education;
    (4) files an affidavit stating intent to
    legalize status when eligible.

SECTION 3. STATE FINANCIAL AID.

Eligible students may apply for state
financial aid programs on the same basis
as other state residents.

SECTION 4. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act takes effect on [date].
```

## Constitutional Considerations

### Federal Authority

| Basis | Application |
|-------|-------------|
| **Naturalization Clause** | Plenary power over immigration |
| **Commerce Clause** | Regulation of labor, international commerce |
| **Foreign Affairs** | Inherent sovereign power |
| **Spending Power** | Conditions on state funding |

### Constraints

| Provision | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Due Process** | Applies to all persons |
| **Equal Protection** | Limited in immigration context |
| **First Amendment** | Association, religion protections |
| **Fourth Amendment** | Search and seizure limits |

### Federal Preemption

| Issue | Framework |
|-------|-----------|
| **State enforcement** | *Arizona v. United States* limits |
| **Benefits** | Federal floor, state discretion |
| **Employment** | Federal E-Verify, state mandates |
| **Sanctuary** | Anti-commandeering |

## Loopholes, Shortcomings, and Rectification

### Potential Loopholes

| Loophole | Description | Severity |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| **Backlog surge** | Rush to file before cutoffs | Medium |
| **Fraud in legalization** | False claims of presence | High |
| **E-Verify gaming** | Identity fraud | Medium |
| **Employer subcontracting** | Liability avoidance | Medium |
| **Asylum abuse** | Non-meritorious claims | Medium |

### Shortcomings

| Issue | Impact | Root Cause |
|-------|--------|------------|
| **Future flow** | May not match labor needs | Political constraints |
| **Implementation capacity** | Overwhelmed systems | Resource limits |
| **Enforcement balance** | Political pressure | Polarization |
| **International factors** | Push factors continue | Beyond US control |

### Rectification Procedures

1. **Robust fraud detection** in legalization
2. **Biometric verification** for identity
3. **Employer accountability** through E-Verify
4. **Adequate resources** for implementation
5. **Regular adjustment** of visa numbers
6. **Regional cooperation** on migration
7. **Standing commission** for oversight
8. **Sunset and review** provisions
9. **Data collection** for evaluation
10. **Anti-discrimination** enforcement
