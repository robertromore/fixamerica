# Legal Immigration: Legislation and Legal Framework

## Overview

Legal immigration reform requires legislation addressing per-country caps, numerical limits, processing efficiency, and pathway modernization. This document provides draft legislation for key reforms and analysis of existing legal frameworks.

## Per-Country Cap Elimination

### Fairness for High-Skilled Immigrants Act

**Purpose**: Eliminate per-country caps for employment-based green cards to end discrimination based on country of birth.

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Fairness for High-Skilled
Immigrants Act".

SEC. 2. ELIMINATION OF PER-COUNTRY NUMERICAL
LIMITATION FOR EMPLOYMENT-BASED IMMIGRANTS.

(a) IN GENERAL.—Section 202(a)(2) of the Immigration
and Nationality Act (8 U.S.C. 1152(a)(2)) is amended—
    (1) by striking "7 percent" and inserting "15 percent"
    for fiscal years 1 through 3 after enactment; and
    (2) by striking subparagraph (A) entirely for fiscal
    year 4 and thereafter.

(b) TRANSITION RULES.—
    (1) RESERVED VISAS.—For the first 3 fiscal years
    after enactment, not more than 85 percent of
    employment-based visas shall be allocated to
    nationals of any single country.
    (2) ELIMINATION.—Beginning in fiscal year 4, the
    per-country limit is eliminated entirely for
    employment-based categories.

SEC. 3. FAMILY-SPONSORED PER-COUNTRY LEVELS.

Section 202(a)(2) is amended by striking "7 percent"
and inserting "15 percent" for family-sponsored
immigrants.

SEC. 4. TRANSITION PERIOD PROTECTIONS.

(a) NO DISPLACEMENT.—During the transition period,
applicants from countries other than India and China
shall receive not less than the number of visas they
would have received under prior law.

(b) ADDITIONAL VISAS.—Visas unused by other
countries during the transition period shall be
made available to applicants from backlogged
countries.

SEC. 5. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act takes effect on the first day of the first
fiscal year beginning after the date of enactment.
```

## Visa Recapture

### Visa Recapture Act

**Purpose**: Recover immigrant visas that were authorized but went unused in prior years.

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Visa Recapture Act".

SEC. 2. RECAPTURE OF UNUSED IMMIGRANT VISAS.

(a) FAMILY-SPONSORED.—Notwithstanding section
201(c) of the Immigration and Nationality Act,
the number of family-sponsored immigrants for
fiscal years after enactment shall be increased
by the cumulative number of visas authorized
but unused in fiscal years 1992 through the
fiscal year preceding enactment.

(b) EMPLOYMENT-BASED.—Notwithstanding section
201(d), the number of employment-based
immigrants shall be similarly increased by
unused visas from fiscal years 1992 through
the preceding fiscal year.

(c) AVAILABILITY.—Recaptured visas shall be
made available—
    (1) in addition to otherwise available visas;
    (2) until exhausted; and
    (3) in accordance with existing preference
    categories.

SEC. 3. PREVENTION OF FUTURE LOSS.

Section 201 is amended by adding:

"(g) UNUSED VISAS.—Any immigrant visa number
authorized for a fiscal year but not used before
the end of that fiscal year shall remain available
for use in subsequent fiscal years."

SEC. 4. ESTIMATED RECOVERY.

Based on historical data, approximately 220,000
family-sponsored and 180,000 employment-based
visas are available for recapture.
```

## Employment-Based Increases

### STEM Immigration Act

**Purpose**: Exempt STEM advanced degree holders from numerical limitations and increase employment-based caps.

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "STEM Immigration Act".

SEC. 2. STEM EXEMPTION.

(a) IN GENERAL.—Section 201(b)(1) of the Immigration
and Nationality Act is amended by adding:

"(G) Aliens who have earned a master's degree or
higher in a field of science, technology,
engineering, or mathematics from an institution
of higher education accredited by a nationally
recognized accrediting agency in the United States."

(b) FIELDS.—The Secretary of Homeland Security,
in consultation with the Secretary of Labor and
the Director of the National Science Foundation,
shall define qualifying STEM fields by regulation.

SEC. 3. EMPLOYMENT-BASED INCREASE.

Section 201(d)(1) of the Immigration and Nationality
Act is amended by striking "140,000" and inserting
"290,000".

SEC. 4. CATEGORY ALLOCATION.

The increased employment-based visas shall be
allocated:
    (a) EB-1: 80,000 (28%)
    (b) EB-2: 100,000 (34%)
    (c) EB-3: 80,000 (28%)
    (d) EB-4: 15,000 (5%)
    (e) EB-5: 15,000 (5%)

SEC. 5. LABOR MARKET ADJUSTMENT.

The Secretary shall, every 3 years, assess whether
the employment-based allocation matches labor
market needs and submit recommendations to Congress.
```

## Family-Based Reform

### Immediate Relatives Expansion Act

**Purpose**: Treat spouses and minor children of lawful permanent residents as immediate relatives.

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Immediate Relatives
Expansion Act".

SEC. 2. SPOUSES AND CHILDREN OF LPRS AS
IMMEDIATE RELATIVES.

Section 201(b)(2)(A)(i) of the Immigration and
Nationality Act is amended by striking "citizens
of the United States" and inserting "citizens of
the United States or lawful permanent residents".

SEC. 3. CONFORMING AMENDMENTS.

(a) Section 203(a)(2)(A) (F2A category) is
repealed.

(b) The preference allocation percentages in
section 203(a) are adjusted proportionally.

SEC. 4. TRANSITION.

(a) PENDING PETITIONS.—F2A petitions pending on
the date of enactment shall be processed as
immediate relative petitions.

(b) PRIORITY DATES.—Priority dates shall be
maintained for processing order.

SEC. 5. BACKLOG CLEARANCE.

(a) SUPPLEMENTAL VISAS.—In addition to visas
otherwise available, 50,000 additional visas
per year shall be made available for 5 years
to clear the existing F2A backlog.

(b) SUNSET.—The supplemental visas terminate
after the backlog is cleared or 5 years,
whichever is earlier.
```

## Processing Reform

### USCIS Accountability Act

**Purpose**: Establish processing time standards, improve transparency, and ensure adequate resources.

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "USCIS Accountability Act".

SEC. 2. PROCESSING TIME STANDARDS.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—Not later than 180 days after
enactment, the Secretary shall establish processing
time standards for each application and petition type.

(b) INITIAL STANDARDS.—
    (1) Form I-130: 180 days
    (2) Form I-140: 120 days
    (3) Form I-485: 180 days
    (4) Form N-400: 120 days
    (5) Form I-765: 60 days

(c) ACCOUNTABILITY.—If a case exceeds the applicable
processing standard, the applicant may—
    (1) request expedited adjudication; and
    (2) receive a 50% refund of the filing fee if
    the case exceeds 150% of the standard.

SEC. 3. TRANSPARENCY.

(a) MONTHLY REPORTS.—USCIS shall publish monthly
reports including:
    (1) processing times by form type and location;
    (2) pending case counts;
    (3) approval and denial rates;
    (4) RFE issuance rates.

(b) INDIVIDUAL TRACKING.—Applicants shall have
online access to real-time case status.

SEC. 4. FUNDING.

(a) APPROPRIATIONS.—There is authorized to be
appropriated $500 million annually to supplement
fee revenue for backlog reduction.

(b) PREMIUM PROCESSING.—Premium processing shall
be expanded to additional form types, with
proceeds dedicated to capacity building.

SEC. 5. TECHNOLOGY MODERNIZATION.

(a) ELECTRONIC FILING.—Within 2 years, all forms
shall be available for electronic filing.

(b) DIGITAL PROCESSING.—Within 3 years, USCIS
shall implement end-to-end digital processing.

(c) INTEROPERABILITY.—Systems shall be interoperable
with DOS, DOL, and CBP systems.
```

## Standing Commission

### Immigration Levels Commission Act

**Purpose**: Establish a standing commission to recommend immigration levels based on economic and demographic factors.

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Immigration Levels
Commission Act".

SEC. 2. ESTABLISHMENT.

There is established the United States Immigration
Levels Commission as an independent agency.

SEC. 3. MEMBERSHIP.

(a) COMPOSITION.—The Commission shall consist of
9 members:
    (1) 3 appointed by the President;
    (2) 2 appointed by the Senate Majority Leader;
    (3) 2 appointed by the Senate Minority Leader;
    (4) 1 appointed by the Speaker of the House;
    (5) 1 appointed by the House Minority Leader.

(b) QUALIFICATIONS.—Members shall have expertise
in economics, demography, labor markets, or
immigration policy.

(c) TERMS.—Members serve 6-year staggered terms.

SEC. 4. DUTIES.

(a) ANNUAL REPORT.—The Commission shall submit
an annual report to Congress including:
    (1) assessment of current immigration levels;
    (2) analysis of labor market needs;
    (3) demographic projections;
    (4) recommendations for visa number adjustments.

(b) FACTORS.—The Commission shall consider:
    (1) unemployment rates by sector and region;
    (2) job vacancy data;
    (3) wage trends;
    (4) population growth and aging;
    (5) international competitiveness;
    (6) backlog levels.

SEC. 5. CONGRESSIONAL CONSIDERATION.

(a) EXPEDITED PROCEDURES.—Commission recommendations
shall receive expedited consideration under modified
rules.

(b) UP-OR-DOWN VOTE.—Congress shall vote on
recommendations within 90 days.

(c) DEFAULT.—If Congress does not act,
recommendations take effect automatically.
```

## State Model Legislation

### Model State In-State Tuition Act

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "[State] In-State
Tuition Equity Act".

SECTION 2. ELIGIBILITY.

A student shall be eligible for in-state tuition
at public institutions of higher education if
the student—
    (1) attended a [State] high school for 3 or
    more years;
    (2) graduated from a [State] high school or
    received an equivalent credential in [State];
    (3) registers or is enrolled at a public
    institution of higher education in [State];
    (4) if not a citizen or lawful permanent
    resident, files an affidavit stating intent
    to apply for legal status as soon as eligible.

SECTION 3. STATE AID ELIGIBILITY.

Eligible students may apply for state financial
aid programs on the same basis as other residents.

SECTION 4. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act takes effect on [date] and applies to
students enrolling for the academic year
beginning [date] and thereafter.
```

## Constitutional Considerations

### Federal Authority

| Basis | Application |
|-------|-------------|
| **Naturalization Clause** | Art. I, § 8, cl. 4 - plenary power |
| **Commerce Clause** | Regulation of labor |
| **Foreign Affairs** | Inherent sovereign power |
| **Spending Power** | Conditions on funding |

### Constraints

| Provision | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Due Process** | Applies to all persons |
| **Equal Protection** | Limited in immigration context |
| **Plenary Power Doctrine** | Broad deference to Congress |
| **Consular Nonreviewability** | Limited judicial review |

### Key Precedents

| Case | Holding |
|------|---------|
| **Fiallo v. Bell** | Deference to Congress on categories |
| **Kleindienst v. Mandel** | Facially legitimate reason standard |
| **Kerry v. Din** | Limits on visa denial review |
| **Sessions v. Dimaya** | Void for vagueness applies |

## Loopholes, Shortcomings, and Rectification

### Potential Loopholes

| Loophole | Description | Severity |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| **Transition period gaming** | Rush applications during transition | Medium |
| **STEM field manipulation** | Inflate STEM credentials | Medium |
| **Labor certification fraud** | Fake job offers | High |
| **Document fraud** | False evidence | High |
| **Sponsor abuse** | Employer exploitation | Medium |

### Shortcomings

| Issue | Impact | Root Cause |
|-------|--------|------------|
| **Implementation capacity** | Overwhelmed USCIS | Resource constraints |
| **Enforcement gaps** | Employer compliance | Limited DOL resources |
| **Future flow** | May not meet needs | Political constraints |
| **Integration** | No support services | Not included |

### Rectification Procedures

1. **Fraud detection systems** for document verification
2. **Biometric verification** for identity confirmation
3. **Employer audits** for labor certification compliance
4. **STEM field verification** through credential review
5. **Whistleblower protections** for reporting abuse
6. **Monitoring requirements** for sponsored workers
7. **Regular review** of visa numbers vs. demand
8. **Data collection** for program evaluation
9. **Anti-discrimination enforcement** for worker protection
10. **Appeal mechanisms** for wrongful denials

### Implementation Concerns

| Concern | Mitigation |
|---------|------------|
| **USCIS capacity** | Phased implementation |
| **Transition disruption** | Grandfathering rules |
| **Political reversal** | Statutory requirements |
| **Litigation** | Careful legal drafting |
