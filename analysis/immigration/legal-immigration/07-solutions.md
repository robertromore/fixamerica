# Legal Immigration: Solutions

## Overview

Fixing legal immigration requires clearing accumulated backlogs, modernizing visa categories, eliminating per-country caps that create extreme disparities, improving processing efficiency, and creating mechanisms for ongoing adjustment. Solutions must address both immediate humanitarian needs and long-term system design.

## Clear Existing Backlogs

### Visa Recapture

| Solution | Description | Impact |
|----------|-------------|--------|
| **Recapture unused visas** | Count visas not issued 1992-present | 200,000+ recovered |
| **No future loss** | Unused visas roll over | Prevent future waste |
| **Expedited processing** | Fast-track recaptured visas | Immediate backlog relief |

### Temporary Surge Capacity

| Approach | Implementation | Duration |
|----------|----------------|----------|
| **Double caps temporarily** | 5-year surge period | Until backlog cleared |
| **Exempt immediate relatives** | Remove from 480,000 cap | Permanent change |
| **Supplemental appropriation** | Fund processing capacity | 3-5 years |

### Treat LPR Spouses as Immediate Relatives

| Current | Proposed | Impact |
|---------|----------|--------|
| **F2A category** | Immediate relative | 600,000+ wait eliminated |
| **Numerical limit** | Unlimited | Family reunification |
| **Multi-year waits** | Months | Humanitarian benefit |

## Eliminate Per-Country Caps

### Full Elimination

| Approach | Implementation | Effect |
|----------|----------------|--------|
| **Remove 7% limit** | First-come-first-served | End discrimination |
| **Apply to all categories** | Family and employment | System-wide reform |
| **Transition period** | Phase in over 5 years | Prevent sudden shifts |

### Partial Reforms

| Option | Description | Trade-off |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| **Raise cap to 15%** | Double current limit | Partial relief |
| **Employment only** | Keep family caps | Easier passage |
| **Carve-outs** | Specific exemptions | Targeted relief |
