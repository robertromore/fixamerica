# Legal Immigration: Stakeholders

## Overview

Legal immigration stakeholders include prospective immigrants waiting in backlogs, US families seeking reunification, employers needing workers, government agencies administering the system, legal professionals navigating complexity, and advocacy organizations on all sides. Their competing interests shape the political dynamics that have prevented reform.

## Prospective Immigrants

### Employment-Based Applicants

| Subgroup | Size | Primary Concern | Position |
|----------|------|-----------------|----------|
| **Indian professionals** | 600,000+ in backlog | 90-year EB wait | Eliminate per-country caps |
| **Chinese professionals** | 100,000+ in backlog | Multi-year waits | Increase numbers |
| **Other nationals** | 100,000+ | Processing delays | Faster adjudication |
| **H-1B workers** | 500,000+ | Status uncertainty | Clear path to green card |

### Family-Based Petitioners

| Subgroup | Size | Primary Concern | Position |
|----------|------|-----------------|----------|
| **Spouses of LPRs** | 600,000+ waiting | Multi-year separation | Immediate relative treatment |
| **Siblings of citizens** | 2+ million waiting | Decade+ waits | Maintain category |
| **Mexican applicants** | 1.2+ million waiting | 20+ year waits | Eliminate per-country caps |
| **Filipino applicants** | 400,000+ waiting | 25+ year waits | Clear backlogs |

### Diversity Visa Applicants

| Characteristic | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Size** | 11+ million annual applicants |
| **Success rate** | ~0.5% selected |
| **Geographic distribution** | Africa 50%+, Europe 25%+ |
| **Motivation** | Only path for many countries |
