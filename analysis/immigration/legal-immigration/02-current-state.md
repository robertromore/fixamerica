# Legal Immigration: Current State

## Overview

The legal immigration system processes approximately one million green cards annually, but massive backlogs have accumulated due to demand far exceeding numerical limits. Processing times have increased dramatically, fee increases have created accessibility barriers, and per-country caps create severe inequities. The system is overwhelmed and underfunded despite being fee-based.

## System Challenges

### Structural Issues

| Issue | Impact |
|-------|--------|
| **Statutory caps unchanged since 1990** | Cannot meet modern demand |
| **Per-country limits** | Extreme disparities |
| **Category rigidity** | Doesn't match needs |
| **No visa recapture** | Lost visas not recovered |

### Operational Issues

| Issue | Impact |
|-------|--------|
| **Fee-funded model** | Revenue volatility |
| **Paper-based systems** | Inefficiency |
| **Inconsistent adjudication** | Unpredictable outcomes |
| **Insufficient staffing** | Backlogs grow |
