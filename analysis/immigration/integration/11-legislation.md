# Immigrant Integration: Legislation and Legal Framework

## Overview

Integration legislation addresses federal coordination, naturalization reform, English learning access, credential recognition, and language access. This document provides draft legislation for key reforms and analysis of existing legal frameworks.

## New Americans Act

**Purpose**: Establish federal integration infrastructure and coordination.

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "New Americans Act".

SEC. 2. OFFICE OF NEW AMERICANS.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—There is established within the
Executive Office of the President the Office of New
Americans.

(b) DIRECTOR.—The Office shall be headed by a
Director appointed by the President with advice
and consent of the Senate.

(c) FUNCTIONS.—The Office shall:
    (1) coordinate Federal integration policies;
    (2) advise the President on integration matters;
    (3) administer grants to State and local
    governments;
    (4) collect and disseminate data on integration
    outcomes;
    (5) identify and share best practices.

SEC. 3. CITIZENSHIP AND INTEGRATION GRANT PROGRAM.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—The Director shall establish a
grant program to support State and local
integration efforts.

(b) ELIGIBLE ACTIVITIES.—Grants may be used for:
    (1) citizenship preparation services;
    (2) English language instruction;
    (3) civic integration programs;
    (4) workforce integration services;
    (5) welcoming community initiatives.

(c) AUTHORIZATION.—There is authorized to be
appropriated $200,000,000 annually for this program.

SEC. 4. INTERAGENCY COUNCIL ON IMMIGRANT INTEGRATION.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—There is established the
Interagency Council on Immigrant Integration.

(b) MEMBERSHIP.—The Council shall include:
    (1) the Director of the Office of New Americans
    (chair);
    (2) the Secretary of Homeland Security;
    (3) the Secretary of Education;
    (4) the Secretary of Labor;
    (5) the Secretary of Health and Human Services;
    (6) such other officials as the President
    designates.

(c) FUNCTIONS.—The Council shall:
    (1) coordinate integration activities across
    agencies;
    (2) develop the National Integration Strategy;
    (3) report annually to Congress.

SEC. 5. NATIONAL INTEGRATION STRATEGY.

(a) DEVELOPMENT.—Within 1 year of enactment, the
Council shall develop a National Integration
Strategy.

(b) CONTENTS.—The Strategy shall include:
    (1) integration goals and metrics;
    (2) agency responsibilities;
    (3) resource requirements;
    (4) timeline for implementation.

(c) UPDATE.—The Strategy shall be updated every
3 years.

SEC. 6. DATA AND RESEARCH.

(a) DATA COLLECTION.—The Director shall establish
a system for collecting integration outcome data.

(b) RESEARCH AGENDA.—The Director shall develop
a research agenda on integration.

(c) ANNUAL REPORT.—The Director shall report
annually to Congress on integration outcomes.
```

## Citizenship Accessibility Act

**Purpose**: Reduce barriers to naturalization.

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Citizenship
Accessibility Act".

SEC. 2. FEE REDUCTION AND WAIVERS.

(a) FEE REDUCTION.—The naturalization application
fee shall not exceed $400.

(b) FEE WAIVER.—The Secretary shall waive fees
for applicants with household income below 200
percent of the federal poverty level.

(c) INSTALLMENT PAYMENTS.—The Secretary shall
allow applicants to pay fees in installments
over 6 months.

SEC. 3. PROCESSING TIME.

(a) STANDARD.—The Secretary shall process
naturalization applications within 6 months
of filing.

(b) RESOURCES.—The Secretary shall hire
sufficient adjudicators to meet this standard.

(c) REPORTING.—The Secretary shall report
quarterly on processing times.

SEC. 4. TEST ACCESSIBILITY.

(a) PLAIN LANGUAGE.—The citizenship test shall
be written in plain, accessible language.

(b) STUDY MATERIALS.—The Secretary shall provide
free study materials in multiple languages.

(c) ACCOMMODATIONS.—The Secretary shall provide
reasonable accommodations for:
    (1) individuals with disabilities;
    (2) individuals over age 65;
    (3) individuals with limited formal education.

(d) ALTERNATIVE DEMONSTRATION.—The Secretary
shall allow applicants to demonstrate English
and civics knowledge through alternative means
including:
    (1) completion of approved courses;
    (2) portfolio assessment;
    (3) interview demonstration.

SEC. 5. OUTREACH AND EDUCATION.

(a) NATIONAL CAMPAIGN.—The Secretary shall
conduct a national outreach campaign to promote
naturalization.

(b) COMMUNITY PARTNERSHIPS.—The Secretary shall
partner with community organizations to provide
citizenship preparation services.

(c) EMPLOYER ENGAGEMENT.—The Secretary shall
develop employer engagement programs to support
employee naturalization.

SEC. 6. AUTHORIZATION.

There is authorized to be appropriated $50,000,000
annually for implementation of this Act.
```

## English Learning Expansion Act

**Purpose**: Expand access to English instruction.

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "English Learning
Expansion Act".

SEC. 2. INCREASED FUNDING.

(a) AUTHORIZATION.—Section 211 of the Adult
Education and Family Literacy Act is amended
to authorize $1,500,000,000 annually.

(b) INTEGRATION SET-ASIDE.—Not less than 25
percent of funds shall be used for integrated
English literacy and civics education.

SEC. 3. CAPACITY EXPANSION.

(a) WAITLIST ELIMINATION.—States shall develop
plans to eliminate waitlists for English
instruction within 3 years.

(b) INNOVATIVE DELIVERY.—The Secretary shall
support innovative delivery models including:
    (1) workplace-based instruction;
    (2) technology-enhanced learning;
    (3) family literacy programs;
    (4) community-based delivery.

SEC. 4. QUALITY STANDARDS.

(a) TEACHER QUALIFICATIONS.—The Secretary shall
establish minimum qualifications for adult
English instructors.

(b) CURRICULUM STANDARDS.—The Secretary shall
support development of evidence-based curricula.

(c) OUTCOME MEASURES.—The Secretary shall establish
outcome measures beyond test scores including:
    (1) employment outcomes;
    (2) educational advancement;
    (3) civic participation.

SEC. 5. ACCESS IMPROVEMENTS.

(a) SCHEDULE FLEXIBILITY.—Programs shall offer
instruction at times accessible to working adults.

(b) CHILDCARE.—The Secretary shall support
childcare provision to enable participation.

(c) DISTANCE LEARNING.—The Secretary shall
support development of quality distance learning
options.

SEC. 6. RESEARCH AND EVALUATION.

The Secretary shall conduct research on effective
English instruction for adult immigrants and
evaluate program outcomes.
```

## Professional Credential Recognition Act

**Purpose**: Facilitate recognition of foreign credentials.

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Professional
Credential Recognition Act".

SEC. 2. FEDERAL STANDARDS.

(a) STUDY.—The Secretary of Labor, in consultation
with professional licensing bodies, shall study
foreign credential recognition and identify
barriers.

(b) MODEL STANDARDS.—Based on the study, the
Secretary shall develop model standards for
expedited credential recognition.

SEC. 3. STATE INCENTIVES.

(a) GRANTS.—The Secretary shall provide grants
to States that adopt expedited credential
recognition processes.

(b) CRITERIA.—Expedited processes shall:
    (1) evaluate credentials within 90 days;
    (2) provide clear pathways to licensure;
    (3) allow supervised practice during evaluation;
    (4) recognize substantially equivalent training.

SEC. 4. BRIDGE PROGRAMS.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—The Secretary shall support
bridge programs that help internationally trained
professionals meet licensing requirements.

(b) ELIGIBLE PROGRAMS.—Programs may include:
    (1) supplemental training;
    (2) clinical experience;
    (3) examination preparation;
    (4) language development.

SEC. 5. EVALUATION ASSISTANCE.

(a) PROGRAM.—The Secretary shall establish a
program to assist immigrants with credential
evaluation costs.

(b) ELIGIBILITY.—Assistance shall be available
to individuals with household income below 300
percent of federal poverty level.

SEC. 6. INTERSTATE COMPACTS.

The Secretary shall facilitate development of
interstate compacts for credential recognition
in priority professions.

SEC. 7. AUTHORIZATION.

There is authorized to be appropriated $100,000,000
annually for this Act.
```

## Language Access Act

**Purpose**: Strengthen language access requirements.

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Language Access Act".

SEC. 2. FEDERAL AGENCY REQUIREMENTS.

(a) IN GENERAL.—Each Federal agency shall provide
meaningful access to programs and services for
individuals with limited English proficiency.

(b) REQUIREMENTS.—Agencies shall:
    (1) translate vital documents into languages
    spoken by substantial populations;
    (2) provide oral interpretation services;
    (3) train staff on language access;
    (4) develop and publish language access plans.

(c) ENFORCEMENT.—Individuals may file complaints
with the Department of Justice, which shall have
enforcement authority.

SEC. 3. FEDERALLY FUNDED PROGRAMS.

(a) CONDITION.—Receipt of Federal financial
assistance shall be conditioned on compliance
with language access requirements.

(b) GUIDANCE.—The Attorney General shall issue
guidance on compliance.

SEC. 4. STANDARDS.

(a) INTERPRETER STANDARDS.—The Attorney General
shall establish minimum standards for interpreters
in Federal programs.

(b) TRANSLATION STANDARDS.—The Attorney General
shall establish standards for translated materials.

SEC. 5. RESOURCES.

(a) CENTRALIZED SERVICES.—GSA shall provide
centralized interpretation and translation services
for Federal agencies.

(b) TECHNOLOGY.—The Attorney General shall support
development of technology-assisted language access.

SEC. 6. REPORT.

The Attorney General shall report annually to
Congress on Federal language access compliance.
```

## State Model Legislation

### Model State Integration Act

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "[State] New
Americans Act".

SECTION 2. OFFICE OF NEW AMERICANS.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—There is established within
the [Governor's Office/Department] the Office
of New Americans.

(b) DUTIES.—The Office shall:
    (1) coordinate state immigrant integration
    policies;
    (2) advise the Governor on integration;
    (3) administer integration grants;
    (4) collect integration data;
    (5) provide technical assistance to localities.

SECTION 3. INTERAGENCY COUNCIL.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—There is established the
Interagency Council on Immigrant Integration.

(b) MEMBERSHIP.—Members shall include relevant
agency heads and community representatives.

SECTION 4. STATE INTEGRATION PLAN.

The Office shall develop a State Integration Plan
with goals, strategies, and metrics.

SECTION 5. AUTHORIZATION.

There is appropriated $[amount] for the Office.
```

### Model State Tuition Equity Act

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "[State] Tuition
Equity Act".

SECTION 2. IN-STATE TUITION.

(a) ELIGIBILITY.—A student shall be eligible
for in-state tuition at public institutions
if the student:
    (1) attended a [State] high school for not
    less than 3 years; and
    (2) graduated from a [State] high school or
    obtained a GED in [State].

(b) IMMIGRATION STATUS.—Immigration status shall
not affect eligibility.

SECTION 3. STATE FINANCIAL AID.

Students eligible under this Act shall also be
eligible for state financial aid programs.

SECTION 4. NON-DISCRIMINATION.

Public institutions shall not inquire about
immigration status for tuition classification.
```

### Model Professional Licensing Reform Act

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "[State] Professional
Licensing Reform Act".

SECTION 2. EXPEDITED REVIEW.

Licensing boards shall evaluate credentials of
internationally trained applicants within 90 days.

SECTION 3. SUPERVISED PRACTICE.

Boards shall allow supervised practice while
applications are pending.

SECTION 4. PATHWAY CLARITY.

Boards shall publish clear pathways to licensure
for internationally trained applicants.

SECTION 5. BRIDGE PROGRAMS.

The [Agency] shall support bridge programs for
priority professions with high immigrant
participation.
```

## Loopholes, Shortcomings, and Rectification

### Potential Loopholes

| Loophole | Description | Severity |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| **Funding vulnerability** | Appropriations dependent | High |
| **Coordination gaps** | Agency resistance | Medium |
| **State non-compliance** | Voluntary participation | High |
| **Quality variance** | Service inconsistency | Medium |
| **Eligibility gaming** | Benefit access | Low |

### Shortcomings

| Issue | Impact | Root Cause |
|-------|--------|------------|
| **Unauthorized excluded** | Partial integration | Political compromise |
| **Resource limitations** | Unmet demand | Funding |
| **Implementation variation** | Geographic disparities | Federalism |
| **Monitoring gaps** | Outcome uncertainty | Data systems |
| **Political vulnerability** | Policy instability | Administrative authority |

### Rectification Procedures

1. **Dedicated funding streams** to protect appropriations
2. **Statutory mandates** for agency coordination
3. **Strong incentives** for state participation
4. **Quality standards** with enforcement
5. **Comprehensive eligibility** for all immigrants
6. **Adequate resources** to meet demand
7. **Geographic equity** requirements
8. **Robust data systems** for monitoring
9. **Statutory codification** to reduce vulnerability
10. **Regular evaluation** and adjustment

### Implementation Concerns

| Concern | Mitigation |
|---------|------------|
| **Coordination challenges** | Clear authority, resources |
| **State capacity** | Technical assistance, funding |
| **Provider capacity** | Phase-in, workforce development |
| **Data systems** | Investment in infrastructure |
| **Political sustainability** | Bipartisan coalition |
