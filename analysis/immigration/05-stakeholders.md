# Immigration: Stakeholders

## Overview

Immigration policy affects a vast array of stakeholders with diverse, often conflicting interests. Understanding these stakeholders—their motivations, resources, and positions—is essential for navigating reform. Key divisions exist between employers and workers, restrictionists and expansionists, enforcement agencies and advocacy groups, and federal and state/local governments.

## Immigrant Communities

### Unauthorized Immigrants

| Interest | Priority |
|----------|----------|
| **Legal status** | Protection from deportation |
| **Work authorization** | Employment eligibility |
| **Family unity** | Keep families together |
| **Driver's licenses** | Daily life access |
| **Education access** | Children's opportunities |

**Characteristics:**

- 11 million people
- Median 15+ years in US
- 5 million US citizen children
- Limited political voice (can't vote)
- Fear of visibility
