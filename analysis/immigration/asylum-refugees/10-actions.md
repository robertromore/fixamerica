# Asylum and Refugees: Actions for Citizens

## Overview

Citizens can support asylum seekers and refugees through direct service, advocacy, community sponsorship, counter-misinformation, and electoral engagement. Whether welcoming new neighbors or pushing for policy reform, individual and community action makes a meaningful difference.

| Distinction | Importance |
|-------------|------------|
| **Refugee vs. asylum seeker** | Different processes |

| **Protection vs. relief** | Legal categories |

## Counter Misinformation

### Common Myths

| Myth | Reality |
|------|---------|
| **"Economic migrants"** | Many face persecution |
| **"Skip the line"** | No line for most |
| **"Asylum fraud"** | Difficult to qualify |
| **"Security threat"** | Most vetted arrivals |
| **"Taking resources"** | Contribute economically |

### Effective Responses

| Approach | Method |
|----------|--------|
| **Personal stories** | Real refugee experiences |
| **Facts** | Data from reliable sources |
| **Law** | What protection requires |
| **History** | American refugee tradition |
| **Values** | Compassion, fairness |
