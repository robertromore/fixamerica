# Asylum and Refugees: History

## Overview

US refugee and asylum policy evolved from Cold War-era programs favoring those fleeing communism, through the Refugee Act of 1980 establishing a universal framework, to ongoing debates about who deserves protection and how to manage claims. Each era reflected both humanitarian ideals and political calculations.

## Early Refugee Policy (1948-1980)

### Post-WWII Displacement

| Year | Development |
|------|-------------|
| **1948** | Displaced Persons Act (400,000 Europeans) |
| **1950** | UN Refugee Convention (US signed 1968 Protocol) |
| **1953** | Refugee Relief Act (200,000+) |
| **1956** | Hungarian refugee parole (38,000) |
| **1959** | Cuban refugee program begins |

### Cold War Framework

| Characteristic | Implementation |
|----------------|----------------|
| **Anti-communist focus** | Fleeing communism prioritized |
| **Parole authority** | Ad hoc admissions by President |
| **No asylum statute** | Only overseas processing |
| **Ideological screening** | Pro-US orientation |

### Cuban Refugee Program

| Period | Admissions |

### Vietnamese Refugees

| Period | Admissions |

## Refugee Act of 1980

### Key Provisions

| Provision | Change |
|-----------|--------|
| **Universal definition** | UN Convention standard |
| **Asylum codified** | First statutory right |
| **Separate admissions** | From preference system |
| **Annual ceiling** | Presidential determination |
| **ORR created** | Resettlement services |

### Definition of Refugee

```text
"Any person who is outside any country of such
person's nationality... and who is unable or
unwilling to return to, and is unable or
unwilling to avail himself or herself of the
protection of, that country because of
persecution or a well-founded fear of
persecution on account of race, religion,
nationality, membership in a particular
social group, or political opinion."
```

### Implementation Challenges

| Challenge | Outcome |
|-----------|---------|
| **Mariel boatlift (1980)** | 125,000 Cubans, overwhelmed system |
| **Haitian arrivals** | Different treatment than Cubans |
| **Central Americans** | Denied despite violence |
| **Asylum backlogs** | Began almost immediately |

## Central American Asylum Crisis (1980s)

### Salvadoran and Guatemalan Claims

| Country | Applicants | Grant Rate |
|---------|------------|------------|
| **El Salvador** | 500,000+ | 2-3% |
| **Guatemala** | 150,000+ | 1-2% |
| **Nicaragua** | 50,000+ | 25%+ |

### Sanctuary Movement

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Religious congregations** | Provided shelter |
| **Underground railroad** | Transported refugees |
| **Civil disobedience** | Defied federal policy |
| **Prosecution** | Some leaders convicted |

### Legal Challenges

| Case | Outcome |
|------|---------|
| **ABC Settlement (1990)** | Class relief for Salvadorans, Guatemalans |
| **NACARA (1997)** | Adjustment for Central Americans |
| **TPS** | Created partially in response |

## 1996 Reforms and Aftermath

### IIRIRA Changes

| Change | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **One-year deadline** | Must file within year |
| **Expedited removal** | Credible fear screening |
| **Bars to asylum** | Expanded grounds |
| **Firm resettlement** | Bar to asylum |

### Impact of Changes

| Effect | Outcome |
|--------|---------|
| **One-year bar** | Many genuine refugees denied |
| **Expedited removal** | Faster processing, due process concerns |
| **Deterrence** | Limited effect |
| **Backlogs** | Continued growing |

## Historical Lessons

### What Has Worked

| Approach | Outcome |
|----------|---------|
| **Adequate resources** | Timely decisions |
| **Legal representation** | Fair outcomes |
| **Regional processing** | Reduced border pressure |
| **Integration support** | Successful resettlement |

### What Has Failed

| Approach | Outcome |
|----------|---------|
| **Deterrence** | Limited effect |
| **Restrictions** | Litigation, ineffective |
| **Underfunding** | Backlogs grow |
| **Inconsistency** | Unfair outcomes |
