# Asylum and Refugees: Stakeholders

## Overview

Asylum and refugee policy involves diverse stakeholders including those seeking protection, government agencies administering the system, legal service providers, resettlement organizations, advocacy groups, and political actors. Their competing interests shape the ongoing policy debates.

## Asylum Seekers and Refugees

### Asylum Seekers

| Subgroup | Primary Interest | Position |
|----------|------------------|----------|
| **At border** | Access to process | Fair hearing |
| **Affirmative applicants** | Timely decision | Reduce backlog |
| **In proceedings** | Legal representation | Due process |
| **Detained** | Release, fair bond | Alternatives to detention |
| **With families** | Keep families together | Family unity |

### Refugees Abroad

| Group | Primary Interest |
|-------|------------------|
| **UNHCR-referred** | Resettlement |
| **Priority populations** | Expedited processing |
| **Long-term displaced** | Durable solution |
| **Persecuted minorities** | Protection |

### Specific Populations

| Population | Unique Needs |
|------------|--------------|

## Power Analysis

### High Influence, High Interest

| Stakeholder | Source of Power |
|-------------|-----------------|
| **DHS/DOJ** | Implementation authority |
| **Congress** | Funding, legislation |
| **Courts** | Policy constraints |
| **Media** | Narrative framing |

### High Interest, Lower Influence

| Stakeholder | Limitation |
|-------------|------------|
| **Asylum seekers** | No political voice |
| **Refugees abroad** | Cannot advocate |
| **Legal services** | Insufficient capacity |
| **Resettlement orgs** | Government dependent |

| Actor | Veto Power |

| **Senate filibuster** | Block legislation |
| **President** | Ceiling determination |
| **Countries of origin** | Return cooperation |
