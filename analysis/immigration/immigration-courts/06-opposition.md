# Immigration Courts: Opposition to Reform

## Overview of Opposition

Opposition to immigration court reform comes from multiple directions: those who benefit from current executive control, those concerned about costs, those focused on enforcement efficiency, and those resistant to any change that might slow removals.

## Opposition to Court Independence

### Executive Branch Concerns

| Concern | Argument |
|---------|----------|
| **Policy control** | AG needs adjudication authority |
| **Coordination** | Immigration requires unified policy |
| **Flexibility** | Executive can respond to conditions |
| **Accountability** | President accountable for immigration |

### Political Concerns

| Concern | Argument |
|---------|----------|
| **Lose control** | Can't direct outcomes |
| **Judicial activism** | Independent judges may be lenient |
| **Precedent** | Hard to change direction |
| **Appointments** | Less political influence |

### Institutional Resistance

| Source | Concern |
|--------|---------|
| **DOJ leadership** | Loss of authority |
| **EOIR management** | Status quo preference |
| **Administration** | Policy implementation |

## Opposition to Resource Expansion

### Fiscal Concerns

## Counter-Arguments to Reform

### Independence Counter

| Counter | Response |
|---------|----------|
| **DOJ coordination** | Courts function independently in other areas |
| **Policy consistency** | Legislation, not AG review |
| **Accountability** | Judicial accountability exists |

### Resource Counter

| Counter | Response |
|---------|----------|
| **Magnet effect** | Evidence limited |
| **Cost-benefit** | Detention costs more |
| **Due process** | Constitutional obligation |

### Counsel Counter

| Counter | Response |
|---------|----------|
| **Efficiency** | Represented cases more efficient |
| **Civil vs. criminal** | Consequences similar |
| **International norms** | Other countries provide |

### Timing Arguments

## Good Faith vs. Bad Faith

### Good Faith Opposition

| Position | Characteristic |
|----------|----------------|
| **Cost concerns** | Legitimate fiscal worry |
| **Efficiency focus** | Genuine concern |
| **Coordination needs** | Real administrative issue |
| **Gradual reform** | Incremental approach |

### Bad Faith Opposition

| Position | Characteristic |
|----------|----------------|
| **Enforcement above all** | Due process secondary |
| **Policy control** | Maintain political influence |
| **Delay tactics** | Prevent any reform |
| **Moving goalposts** | Never support anything |
