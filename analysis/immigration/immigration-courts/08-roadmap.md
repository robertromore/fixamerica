# Immigration Courts: Implementation Roadmap

## Overview

Implementing immigration court reform requires both immediate administrative improvements and long-term structural change. This roadmap outlines phases from administrative action through Article I court creation, with contingencies for different political scenarios.

## Scenario Planning

### Scenario A: Reform-Supportive Government

| Phase | Focus | Timeline |
|-------|-------|----------|
| **Immediate** | Administrative improvements | Months 1-12 |
| **Near-term** | Resource expansion | Years 1-3 |
| **Medium-term** | Article I legislation | Years 2-4 |
| **Long-term** | Independent court operation | Years 4-10 |

### Scenario B: Divided Government

| Phase | Focus | Timeline |
|-------|-------|----------|
| **Immediate** | Administrative action | Months 1-12 |
| **Ongoing** | Resource appropriations | Annual |
| **Opportunistic** | Bipartisan reforms | As possible |
| **Long-term** | Build support | Continuous |

### Scenario C: Hostile Government

| Phase | Focus | Timeline |
|-------|-------|----------|
| **Defensive** | Protect due process | Immediate |
| **Litigation** | Challenge overreach | Ongoing |
| **State action** | Legal services funding | Continuous |
| **Preparation** | Position for future | Long-term |
