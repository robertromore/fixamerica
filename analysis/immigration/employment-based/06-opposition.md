# Employment-Based Immigration: Opposition

## Overview of Opposition

Opposition to employment-based immigration expansion comes from labor concerns, restrictionist ideology, and skepticism about employer practices. These objections span wage effects, job displacement, program integrity, and broader immigration policy debates.

## Labor Market Opposition

### Wage Depression Arguments

| Argument | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Cheaper labor** | H-1B workers paid less |
| **Supply increase** | More workers, lower wages |
| **Replacement** | Hire H-1B over US worker |
| **Prevailing wage** | Below market rates |

### Evidence Assessment

| Claim | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| **Overall wages** | Mixed, small effects |
| **Specific occupations** | Some suppression |
| **Entry level** | More impact |
| **Senior level** | Less impact |

### Job Displacement Arguments

| Argument | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Direct replacement** | US workers fired, H-1B hired |
| **Opportunity denial** | Jobs not offered to US |
| **Career ladder** | Entry blocked |
| **Age discrimination** | Older workers replaced |

### Specific Concerns

| Concern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| **Disney case** | US workers trained replacements |
| **Southern California Edison** | Layoffs, H-1B hires |
| **IT sector** | Outsourcing model |
