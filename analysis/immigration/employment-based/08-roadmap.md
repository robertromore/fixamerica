# Employment-Based Immigration: Implementation Roadmap

## Overview

Implementing employment-based immigration reform requires both administrative action and legislative change. This roadmap outlines phases from immediate regulatory improvements through comprehensive legislative reform.

## Scenario Planning

### Scenario A: Reform-Supportive Government

| Phase | Focus | Timeline |
|-------|-------|----------|
| **Immediate** | Administrative improvements | Months 1-12 |
| **Near-term** | Targeted legislation | Years 1-2 |
| **Medium-term** | Comprehensive reform | Years 2-4 |
| **Long-term** | System modernization | Years 4-10 |

### Scenario B: Divided Government

| Phase | Focus | Timeline |
|-------|-------|----------|
| **Immediate** | Administrative action | Months 1-12 |
| **Ongoing** | Bipartisan negotiation | Years 1-4 |
| **Opportunistic** | Piecemeal reforms | As possible |

### Scenario C: Restrictive Government

| Phase | Focus | Timeline |
|-------|-------|----------|
| **Defensive** | Protect current rights | Immediate |
| **Litigation** | Challenge restrictions | Ongoing |
| **Preparation** | Build support | Long-term |

### Political Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|

| **Litigation** | Careful legal drafting |
