# Family-Based Immigration: Legislation and Legal Framework

## Overview

Family-based immigration legislation addresses backlogs, per-country caps, V visa revival, CSPA improvements, and mixed-status family relief. This document provides draft legislation for key reforms and analysis of existing legal frameworks.

## Family Visa Backlog Relief Act

**Purpose**: Increase family preference visa numbers and clear backlogs.

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Family Visa Backlog
Relief Act".

SEC. 2. INCREASED ALLOCATION.

(a) IN GENERAL.—Section 201(c) of the Immigration
and Nationality Act is amended by striking
"226,000" and inserting "480,000" for fiscal
years 1 through 10 after enactment, and "350,000"
thereafter.

(b) CATEGORY ALLOCATION.—The increased visas
shall be distributed proportionally among
preference categories.

SEC. 3. RECAPTURE.

(a) IN GENERAL.—All family preference immigrant
visas that were authorized but not issued from
fiscal year 1992 through the fiscal year preceding
enactment shall be available for issuance.

(b) AVAILABILITY.—Recaptured visas shall be
available in addition to the annual allocations
under section 201(c).

(c) ALLOCATION.—Recaptured visas shall be
distributed to clear backlogs in order of
priority date.

SEC. 4. DEPENDENT EXEMPTION.

(a) IN GENERAL.—Spouses and minor children of
family preference immigrants shall not be counted
against the numerical limitations.

(b) CALCULATION.—The numerical limitations shall
apply only to principal beneficiaries.

SEC. 5. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act shall take effect on the first day of
the first fiscal year beginning after enactment.
```

## Family Per-Country Cap Reform Act

**Purpose**: Eliminate or reform per-country caps for family-based immigration.

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Family Fairness Act".

SEC. 2. ELIMINATION OF PER-COUNTRY CAPS.

(a) IN GENERAL.—Section 202(a)(2) of the Immigration
and Nationality Act is amended by striking "7
percent" and inserting "15 percent" for the first
5 fiscal years after enactment, and eliminating
the limitation entirely thereafter.

(b) TRANSITION PROTECTION.—During the transition
period:
    (1) Not less than 10 percent of family preference
    visas shall be reserved for countries other than
    the two countries with the largest backlogs;
    (2) No beneficiary with a priority date before
    enactment shall lose their place in line.

SEC. 3. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act shall take effect on the first day of
the first fiscal year beginning after enactment.
```

## V Visa Revival Act

**Purpose**: Restore and expand the V visa program.

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Family Reunification
Act".

SEC. 2. V NONIMMIGRANT STATUS.

(a) IN GENERAL.—Section 101(a)(15)(V) of the
Immigration and Nationality Act is amended to
read as follows:
    "(V) subject to section 214(q), an alien who
    is the beneficiary of an approved petition
    under section 203(a) and who is waiting for
    an immigrant visa to become available."

(b) ELIGIBILITY.—An alien is eligible for V status
if:
    (1) the alien is the beneficiary of an approved
    I-130 petition;
    (2) the alien has been waiting for an immigrant
    visa for 2 or more years; and
    (3) the alien is admissible.

SEC. 3. DURATION.

V status shall be valid until the alien's
immigrant visa becomes available and the alien
adjusts status or departs to obtain an immigrant
visa abroad.

SEC. 4. WORK AUTHORIZATION.

An alien in V status shall be authorized for
employment in the United States.

SEC. 5. TRAVEL.

An alien in V status may travel abroad and be
readmitted in V status.

SEC. 6. ADJUSTMENT OF STATUS.

An alien in V status who entered legally may
adjust status under section 245 when an
immigrant visa becomes available.
```

## Child Status Protection Improvement Act

**Purpose**: Strengthen protections against children aging out.

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Child Protection
Improvement Act".

SEC. 2. AGE CALCULATION.

(a) AMENDMENT.—Section 203(h) of the Immigration
and Nationality Act is amended to provide that
for purposes of determining eligibility as a
child:
    (1) The age of the child shall be the age on
    the date the petition was filed;
    (2) If the petitioner naturalizes, the child's
    age shall be frozen at the date of naturalization.

(b) SEEK TO ACQUIRE.—The requirement to seek to
acquire status within 1 year is eliminated.

SEC. 3. CATEGORY PRESERVATION.

(a) IN GENERAL.—A child who was a minor on the
date of petition filing shall retain child status
regardless of subsequent aging or marriage.

(b) DERIVATIVE.—Derivative children of employment-
based immigrants shall be protected from aging
out.

SEC. 4. RETROACTIVE APPLICATION.

This Act shall apply to all pending petitions
and any petition denied solely because the child
aged out.
```

## Mixed-Status Family Relief Act

**Purpose**: Provide relief for mixed-status families.

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Family Unity Act".

SEC. 2. PROSPECTIVE APPLICATION OF BARS.

(a) IN GENERAL.—Section 212(a)(9)(B) and (C) of
the Immigration and Nationality Act shall apply
only to unlawful presence accruing after the
date of enactment.

(b) WAIVER.—Unlawful presence accruing before
enactment may be waived upon payment of a
penalty fee of $1,000.

SEC. 3. ADJUSTMENT ELIGIBILITY.

(a) SECTION 245(i) REVIVAL.—Section 245(i) is
amended to extend to aliens who:
    (1) are beneficiaries of a petition filed
    before [date of enactment]; or
    (2) were present in the United States on
    [date of enactment].

(b) PENALTY.—Aliens adjusting under this section
shall pay a penalty of $1,000 in addition to
regular fees.

SEC. 4. PAROLE IN PLACE.

(a) AUTHORIZATION.—The Secretary may grant parole
in place to the spouse, parent, or child of a
United States citizen or lawful permanent
resident for urgent humanitarian reasons or
significant public benefit.

(b) ADJUSTMENT.—An alien granted parole in place
may adjust status under section 245 if otherwise
eligible.
```

## State Model Legislation

### Model State Family Support Act

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "[State] Family
Support Act".

SECTION 2. STATE BENEFITS.

Immigrants in approved family petition status
awaiting visa availability shall be eligible
for state-funded benefits on the same basis
as lawful permanent residents.

SECTION 3. DRIVER'S LICENSE.

Immigrants in V visa status or with approved
I-130 petitions shall be eligible for driver's
licenses.

SECTION 4. EDUCATION.

Children of immigrants waiting for visa
availability shall be eligible for in-state
tuition at public institutions.
```

## Loopholes, Shortcomings, and Rectification

### Potential Loopholes

| Loophole | Description | Severity |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| **Sham marriages** | Immigration fraud | High |
| **Document fraud** | Relationship proof | Medium |
| **Transition gaming** | Exploit cutoffs | Medium |

### Shortcomings

| Issue | Impact | Root Cause |
|-------|--------|------------|
| **Years to clear** | Backlog persistence | Scale |
| **Transition bumps** | Some worse off | Cap elimination |
| **Processing delays** | Additional wait | Capacity |
| **V visa abuse** | Potential overstays | Program design |

### Rectification Procedures

1. **DNA testing** for relationship verification
2. **Marriage fraud** enhanced investigation
3. **Transition protection** for affected countries
4. **Processing resources** adequate staffing
5. **V visa monitoring** compliance requirements
6. **CSPA automation** age calculation
7. **Waiver standards** clear guidance
8. **Adjustment access** broader eligibility
9. **Consular capacity** sufficient staffing
10. **Regular review** of numerical adequacy

### Implementation Concerns

| Concern | Mitigation |
|---------|------------|
| **USCIS capacity** | Phased implementation |
| **Consular backlog** | DOS resources |
| **Transition fairness** | Protected categories |
| **Fraud prevention** | Enhanced verification |
