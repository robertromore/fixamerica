# Family-Based Immigration: Solutions

## Overview

Family-based immigration reform requires backlog relief, per-country cap reform, protection for mixed-status families, and process improvements. Solutions must balance family reunification with practical processing capacity and political feasibility.

## Backlog Relief

### Increase Numerical Limits

| Approach | Numbers |
|----------|---------|
| **Double preference** | 452,000 annually |
| **50% increase** | 339,000 annually |
| **Demand-based** | Adjust to backlog |

### Visa Recapture

| Source | Estimated |
|--------|-----------|
| **Unused family** | 200,000+ historic |
| **Employment spillover** | When available |
| **One-time clearance** | Backlog focus |

### Category-Specific Relief

| Category | Priority |
|----------|----------|
| **F2A** | Spouse/children of LPRs |
| **F1/F2B** | Adult children |
| **F4** | Siblings (longest wait) |

## Per-Country Cap Reform

### Elimination

| Approach | Implementation |
|----------|----------------|
| **Full elimination** | First-come-first-served |
| **Phased** | Gradual increase |
| **Hybrid** | Partial protection for small countries |

### Transition Protection

| Protection | Mechanism |
|------------|-----------|
| **Floor** | Minimum for all countries |

| **Timeline** | Gradual transition |

|--------|----------------|
