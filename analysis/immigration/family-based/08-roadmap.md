# Family-Based Immigration: Implementation Roadmap

## Overview

Implementing family-based immigration reform requires administrative action, targeted legislation, and comprehensive reform. This roadmap outlines phases for different political scenarios.

## Scenario Planning

### Scenario A: Reform-Supportive Government

| Phase | Focus | Timeline |
|-------|-------|----------|
| **Immediate** | Administrative improvements | Months 1-12 |
| **Near-term** | Targeted legislation | Years 1-2 |
| **Medium-term** | Comprehensive reform | Years 2-5 |
| **Long-term** | Backlog elimination | Years 5-15 |

### Scenario B: Divided Government

| Phase | Focus | Timeline |
|-------|-------|----------|
| **Immediate** | Administrative action | Months 1-12 |
| **Ongoing** | Bipartisan opportunities | Years 1-4 |
| **Opportunistic** | Piecemeal reforms | As possible |

### Scenario C: Hostile Government

| Phase | Focus | Timeline |
|-------|-------|----------|
| **Defensive** | Protect current categories | Immediate |
| **Litigation** | Challenge restrictions | Ongoing |
| **Preparation** | Build support | Long-term |
