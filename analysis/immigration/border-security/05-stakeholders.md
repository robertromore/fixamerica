# Border Security: Stakeholders

## Overview

Border security stakeholders include federal agencies managing operations, border communities affected by migration and enforcement, migrants seeking entry, business interests in trade and labor, advocacy organizations, political actors, and international partners. Their competing interests shape the ongoing policy debates.

## Federal Agencies

### Customs and Border Protection (CBP)

| Division | Role | Interest |
|----------|------|----------|
| **Border Patrol** | Between ports enforcement | Resources, safety |
| **Office of Field Operations** | Ports of entry | Staffing, infrastructure |
| **Air and Marine** | Aviation, maritime | Equipment, coverage |
| **Leadership** | Policy implementation | Authority, resources |

## Power Analysis

### High Influence, High Interest

| Stakeholder | Source of Power |
|-------------|-----------------|
| **CBP/DHS** | Implementation authority |
| **Congress** | Funding, legislation |
| **Border state governors** | Political voice, state actions |
| **Restrictionist groups** | Grassroots mobilization |

### High Interest, Lower Influence

| Stakeholder | Limitation |
|-------------|------------|

| **Humanitarian groups** | Limited political power |
| **Trade industry** | Competing priorities |

| Actor | Veto Power |
