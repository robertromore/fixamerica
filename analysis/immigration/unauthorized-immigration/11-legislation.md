# Unauthorized Immigration: Legislation and Legal Framework

## Overview

Legislation addressing the unauthorized population encompasses legalization programs, DACA statutory protection, agricultural worker provisions, TPS conversion, and enforcement reforms. This document provides draft legislation for key reforms and analysis of existing legal frameworks.

## DREAM Act

**Purpose**: Provide a path to permanent status and citizenship for individuals who arrived as children.

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Dream Act of [Year]".

SEC. 2. CONDITIONAL PERMANENT RESIDENT STATUS.

(a) ELIGIBILITY.—The Secretary may adjust the status
of an alien to that of conditional permanent resident
if the alien:
    (1) was under 18 years of age on the date of
    initial entry;
    (2) has been continuously present in the United
    States for not less than 4 years;
    (3) has graduated from high school, obtained a
    GED, or is enrolled in secondary school;
    (4) is not inadmissible under section 212(a)(2)
    for criminal grounds, with exceptions for minor
    offenses; and
    (5) is not a threat to national security.

(b) APPLICATION PERIOD.—Applications may be filed
during a 5-year period beginning 180 days after
enactment.

(c) CONDITIONAL PERIOD.—Conditional status shall
be valid for 10 years.

SEC. 3. REMOVAL OF CONDITIONS.

(a) REQUIREMENTS.—The Secretary shall remove
conditions if the alien:
    (1) has maintained continuous presence;
    (2) has completed at least 2 years of:
        (A) postsecondary education; or
        (B) military service; or
        (C) employment; and
    (3) has not become inadmissible.

(b) ADJUSTMENT.—Upon removal of conditions, the
alien shall be granted lawful permanent resident
status.

SEC. 4. NATURALIZATION.

Aliens granted permanent residence under this Act
may apply for naturalization after 5 years of
permanent residence.

SEC. 5. INAPPLICABILITY OF BARS.

(a) UNLAWFUL PRESENCE.—Sections 212(a)(9)(B) and
212(a)(9)(C) shall not apply to applicants under
this Act.

(b) ENTRY WITHOUT INSPECTION.—Section 212(a)(6)(A)
shall not apply to applicants under this Act.

SEC. 6. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS.

There is authorized to be appropriated such sums
as necessary to carry out this Act.
```

## Comprehensive Legalization Act

**Purpose**: Provide earned legalization for the long-term unauthorized population.

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Comprehensive
Legalization Act".

SEC. 2. REGISTERED PROVISIONAL IMMIGRANT STATUS.

(a) ELIGIBILITY.—An alien is eligible for registered
provisional immigrant status if the alien:
    (1) has been continuously present in the United
    States since [date];
    (2) has not been convicted of:
        (A) an aggravated felony;
        (B) 3 or more misdemeanors; or
        (C) unlawful voting;
    (3) has paid all Federal taxes owed or entered
    a payment plan;
    (4) is admissible under section 212(a), with
    waivers available; and
    (5) pays applicable fees.

(b) DURATION.—Registered provisional immigrant
status shall be valid for 6 years and renewable.

(c) BENEFITS.—Aliens in registered provisional
status shall be:
    (1) authorized for employment;
    (2) permitted to travel;
    (3) eligible for Social Security number.

(d) LIMITATIONS.—Aliens in registered provisional
status shall not be:
    (1) eligible for Federal means-tested benefits;
    (2) eligible for premium tax credits under ACA.

SEC. 3. ADJUSTMENT TO LAWFUL PERMANENT RESIDENCE.

(a) ELIGIBILITY.—The Secretary may adjust the status
of an alien in registered provisional status to
lawful permanent resident if the alien:
    (1) has maintained registered provisional status
    for not less than 10 years;
    (2) has paid all Federal taxes;
    (3) demonstrates knowledge of English and civics;
    (4) has maintained continuous employment or
    enrollment in education; and
    (5) pays applicable fees.

(b) BACK OF LINE.—No alien may adjust status under
this section until all family-sponsored and
employment-based visa backlogs existing on date
of enactment are cleared.

SEC. 4. FEES.

(a) APPLICATION FEE.—The Secretary shall charge
a fee of $500 for initial applications.

(b) PENALTY.—Applicants shall pay a $1,000 penalty.

(c) FEE WAIVERS.—The Secretary may waive fees for
applicants demonstrating financial hardship.

SEC. 5. EXCLUSIONS.

This Act shall not apply to aliens who:
    (1) entered the United States after [date];
    (2) are subject to a final order of removal; or
    (3) have been removed and reentered illegally
    after [date].
```

## Agricultural Worker Legalization

**Purpose**: Provide legal status for agricultural workers.

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Farm Workforce
Modernization Act".

SEC. 2. CERTIFIED AGRICULTURAL WORKER STATUS.

(a) ELIGIBILITY.—An alien is eligible for certified
agricultural worker status if the alien:
    (1) performed agricultural labor for not less
    than 1,035 hours or 180 work days during the
    24-month period preceding application;
    (2) is not inadmissible on criminal grounds;
    (3) pays applicable fees.

(b) STATUS DURATION.—Certified agricultural worker
status shall be valid for 5.5 years.

(c) EMPLOYMENT AUTHORIZATION.—Aliens in certified
status are authorized for employment in any
occupation.

SEC. 3. ADJUSTMENT TO PERMANENT RESIDENCE.

(a) ELIGIBILITY.—The Secretary shall adjust status
to lawful permanent resident if the alien:
    (1) has performed agricultural labor for:
        (A) 10 years prior to application, not less
        than 575 hours or 100 work days during each
        of 4 years; or
        (B) 10 years prior plus 4 years after
        application, with required work; and
    (2) pays applicable fees.

(b) SPOUSES AND CHILDREN.—The spouse and children
of a certified agricultural worker may be included.

SEC. 4. H-2A REFORM.

(a) 3-YEAR VISAS.—H-2A visas shall be valid for
3 years.

(b) PORTABILITY.—H-2A workers may change employers.

(c) YEAR-ROUND WORK.—H-2A shall be available for
year-round agricultural work.
```

## DACA Statutory Protection

**Purpose**: Codify DACA program in statute.

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "DACA Codification Act".

SEC. 2. STATUTORY PROTECTION FOR DACA RECIPIENTS.

(a) DEFERRED ACTION.—Any alien who has been granted
deferred action under the program known as DACA
shall be deemed to have lawful immigration status.

(b) EXTENSION.—Deferred action status shall be
renewed automatically unless:
    (1) the alien becomes inadmissible on criminal
    grounds;
    (2) the alien departs the United States without
    advance parole; or
    (3) the alien requests cancellation.

(c) NEW APPLICATIONS.—The Secretary shall accept
new applications from aliens who meet eligibility
criteria.

SEC. 3. WORK AUTHORIZATION.

Aliens with deferred action status under this Act
shall be authorized for employment.

SEC. 4. ADJUSTMENT OF STATUS.

(a) PAROLE.—The Secretary may grant advance parole
to DACA recipients for any lawful purpose.

(b) ADJUSTMENT.—DACA recipients who have been
paroled may adjust status under section 245 if
otherwise eligible.

SEC. 5. PATH TO CITIZENSHIP.

DACA recipients may apply for lawful permanent
residence under the DREAM Act or other applicable
provisions.
```

## TPS Conversion Act

**Purpose**: Provide a path to permanent status for long-term TPS holders.

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "TPS Adjustment Act".

SEC. 2. ADJUSTMENT TO PERMANENT RESIDENCE.

(a) ELIGIBILITY.—The Secretary shall adjust the
status of an alien to lawful permanent resident
if the alien:
    (1) has held TPS status for not less than
    5 years continuously;
    (2) was physically present in the United States
    on [date];
    (3) is admissible as an immigrant; and
    (4) pays applicable fees.

(b) WAIVER OF BARS.—Sections 212(a)(9)(B) and
212(a)(9)(C) shall not apply to adjustment under
this section.

(c) REGISTRY DATE.—For purposes of section 249,
the registry date shall be updated to [date]
for aliens adjusting under this section.

SEC. 3. DEPENDENTS.

The spouse and unmarried children under 21 of
an alien adjusting under this section may also
adjust if:
    (1) the dependent has held derivative TPS
    status; or
    (2) the dependent entered the US before [date]
    and has been continuously present.
```

## State Model Legislation

### Model State Driver's License Access Act

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "[State] Driver's
License Access Act".

SECTION 2. ELIGIBILITY.

Any resident of [State] who can prove identity
and state residency may apply for a driver's
license or identification card, regardless of
immigration status.

SECTION 3. DOCUMENTATION.

(a) IDENTITY.—Identity may be proven by:
    (1) valid unexpired foreign passport;
    (2) valid consular identification;
    (3) other reliable identity documents.

(b) RESIDENCY.—Residency may be proven by:
    (1) utility bills;
    (2) bank statements;
    (3) lease agreements;
    (4) other documentation showing state address.

SECTION 4. MARKING.

Licenses issued under this section shall:
    (1) comply with state security features;
    (2) indicate "Not valid for federal purposes"
    if not REAL ID compliant;
    (3) not indicate immigration status.

SECTION 5. CONFIDENTIALITY.

(a) PROHIBITION.—The Department of Motor Vehicles
shall not:
    (1) disclose information to federal immigration
    authorities;
    (2) condition license issuance on immigration
    status inquiry.

(b) EXCEPTION.—This prohibition shall not apply
to valid judicial warrants.
```

### Model State Tuition Equity Act

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "[State] Tuition
Equity Act".

SECTION 2. IN-STATE TUITION ELIGIBILITY.

(a) ELIGIBILITY.—A student shall be eligible for
in-state tuition at public institutions of higher
education if the student:
    (1) attended a [State] high school for 3 or
    more years;
    (2) graduated from a [State] high school or
    received a GED in [State]; and
    (3) signs an affidavit stating intent to legalize
    immigration status when eligible.

(b) NO IMMIGRATION STATUS REQUIREMENT.—Immigration
status shall not affect eligibility under this
section.

SECTION 3. STATE FINANCIAL AID.

Students eligible for in-state tuition under this
Act may also be eligible for state financial aid
programs.

SECTION 4. NO DISCRIMINATION.

Public institutions shall not:
    (1) inquire about immigration status for purposes
    of tuition classification;
    (2) deny admission based on immigration status;
    (3) report students to federal authorities.
```

## Loopholes, Shortcomings, and Rectification

### Potential Loopholes

| Loophole | Description | Severity |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| **Document fraud** | Fabricated presence evidence | High |
| **Date gaming** | False entry date claims | Medium |
| **Criminal bars** | Inconsistent application | Medium |
| **Fee structure** | Barriers for low-income | Medium |
| **Processing delays** | Effective denial | High |

### Shortcomings

| Issue | Impact | Root Cause |
|-------|--------|------------|
| **Backlog wait** | Delays permanent status | Back of line provision |
| **Exclusions** | Many left out | Political compromise |
| **No new line** | Future unauthorized | Inadequate future flow |
| **State variation** | Unequal access | Federal-state divide |
| **Employer gaps** | Continued exploitation | Limited labor provisions |

### Rectification Procedures

1. **Biometric verification** for identity and presence
2. **Clear evidentiary standards** with flexibility
3. **Adequate processing resources** to prevent delays
4. **Fee waivers** for hardship cases
5. **Meaningful future flow** to prevent new unauthorized
6. **Worker protections** during transition
7. **State coordination** for document access
8. **Anti-fraud measures** with due process
9. **Clear criminal bar definitions** consistently applied
10. **Regular progress reporting** and adjustments

### Implementation Concerns

| Concern | Mitigation |
|---------|------------|
| **USCIS capacity** | Major resource investment |
| **Fraud prevention** | Verification systems |
| **Enrollment** | Robust outreach |
| **Political vulnerability** | Statutory protection |
| **Employer compliance** | E-Verify coordination |
