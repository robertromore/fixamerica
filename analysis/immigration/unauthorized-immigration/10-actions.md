# Unauthorized Immigration: Actions for Citizens

## Overview

Citizens can engage with unauthorized immigration issues through advocacy, community support, electoral participation, and direct action. Whether supporting comprehensive reform or protecting vulnerable neighbors, informed engagement shapes policy outcomes.

## Understanding the Issues

### Learn the Basics

| Topic | Why It Matters |
|-------|----------------|
| **Population demographics** | Who unauthorized immigrants are |
| **DACA/TPS** | Specific protected populations |
| **Economic contributions** | Tax and labor participation |
| **Legal options** | What paths exist (or don't) |
| **Reform proposals** | What changes are proposed |

### Key Distinctions

| Distinction | Importance |
|-------------|------------|
| **EWI vs. overstay** | Different entry circumstances |
| **DACA vs. unauthorized** | Legal status differences |
| **Legalization vs. amnesty** | Framing and requirements |
| **Path to citizenship vs. status** | Different outcomes |

## Counter Misinformation

### Common Myths

| Myth | Reality |
|------|---------|
| **"Criminals"** | Lower crime rates than native-born |
| **"Don't pay taxes"** | $48B+ annually |
| **"Take benefits"** | Mostly ineligible |
| **"Take jobs"** | Complement workforce |
| **"Easy to come legally"** | No line for most |

### Effective Responses

| Approach | Method |
|----------|--------|
| **Data** | Actual statistics |
| **Stories** | Real people affected |
| **Economic** | Contribution evidence |
| **Questions** | Encourage reflection |
