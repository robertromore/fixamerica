# Trauma-Informed Care: Resources

This section provides a curated list of key research, reports, data sources, and organizations on trauma-informed care, adverse childhood experiences, and evidence-based trauma treatment. Resources are organized by type to support policymakers, advocates, practitioners, and researchers seeking to advance trauma-informed system transformation.

---

## Academic Research

### Peer-Reviewed Studies

- Felitti, V.J., Anda, R.F., Nordenberg, D., et al. "Relationship of Childhood Abuse and Household Dysfunction to Many of the Leading Causes of Death in Adults: The Adverse Childhood Experiences (ACE) Study." *American Journal of Preventive Medicine* 14, no. 4 (1998): 245-258. <https://doi.org/10.1016/S0749-3797(98)00017-8> - The landmark study establishing the dose-response relationship between ACEs and adult health outcomes.

- Hughes, K., Bellis, M.A., Hardcastle, K.A., et al. "The Effect of Multiple Adverse Childhood Experiences on Health: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis." *The Lancet Public Health* 2, no. 8 (2017): e356-e366. <https://doi.org/10.1016/S2468-2667(17)30118-4> - Comprehensive meta-analysis confirming ACE-health outcome relationships across 37 studies.

- Karlin, B.E., Ruzek, J.I., Chard, K.M., et al. "Dissemination of Evidence-Based Psychological Treatments for Posttraumatic Stress Disorder in the Veterans Health Administration." *Journal of Traumatic Stress* 23, no. 6 (2010): 663-673. <https://doi.org/10.1002/jts.20588> - Documents the VA's national rollout of CPT and PE to 10,000+ clinicians.

- Morland, L.A., Mackintosh, M.-A., Glassman, L.H., et al. "Home-Based Delivery of Variable Length Prolonged Exposure Therapy: A Comparison of Clinical Outcomes." *Journal of Clinical Psychology* 76, no. 1 (2020): 46-57. - Demonstrates comparable efficacy of telehealth-delivered trauma treatment versus in-person.

- Kubiak, S.P., Covington, S.S., & Hillier, C. "Trauma-Informed Corrections." In *Social Work in Juvenile and Criminal Justice*, edited by D.W. Springer & A.R. Roberts, 2017. - Evidence review of trauma-informed programming in correctional settings showing 15-25% recidivism reductions.

- Maynard, B.R., Farina, A., Dell, N.A., & Kelly, M.S. "Effects of Trauma-Informed Approaches in Schools: A Systematic Review." *Campbell Systematic Reviews* 15, no. 1-2 (2019): e1018. <https://doi.org/10.1002/cl2.1018> - Systematic review documenting 30-50% reductions in suspensions in TIC-adopting schools.

- Dorado, J.S., Martinez, M., McArthur, L.E., & Leibovitz, T. "Healthy Environments and Response to Trauma in Schools (HEARTS): A Whole-School, Multi-Level, Prevention and Intervention Program for Creating Trauma-Informed, Safe and Supportive Schools." *School Mental Health* 8 (2016): 163-176. <https://doi.org/10.1007/s12310-016-9177-0> - San Francisco HEARTS program outcomes.

- Conn, A.-M., Szilagyi, M.A., Jee, S.H., et al. "Parental Perspectives of Screening for Adverse Childhood Experiences in Pediatric Primary Care." *Families, Systems, & Health* 36, no. 1 (2018): 62-72. - Montefiore study demonstrating 90% parental acceptability of ACE screening in pediatrics.

- Cloitre, M., Shevlin, M., Brewin, C.R., et al. "The International Trauma Questionnaire: Development of a Self-Report Measure of ICD-11 PTSD and Complex PTSD." *Acta Psychiatrica Scandinavica* 138, no. 6 (2018): 536-546. - Validation of the complex PTSD diagnosis recognized in ICD-11.

- Watson, A.C., Compton, M.T., & Draine, J.N. "The Crisis Intervention Team (CIT) Model: An Evidence-Based Policing Practice?" *Policing: An International Journal* 40, no. 1 (2017): 106-120. - Evidence that CIT-trained officers are 28% less likely to use force.

### Working Papers and White Papers

- Shonkoff, J.P. & Garner, A.S. "The Lifelong Effects of Early Childhood Adversity and Toxic Stress." *Pediatrics* 129, no. 1 (2012): e232-e246. - AAP technical report establishing the toxic stress framework.

- Salloum, A., Kondrat, D.C., Johnco, C., & Olson, K.R. "The Role of Self-Care on Compassion Satisfaction, Burnout, and Secondary Trauma among Child Welfare Workers." *Children and Youth Services Review* 49 (2015): 54-61. - Evidence on secondary traumatic stress prevention in the child welfare workforce.

- Brown, D.W., Anda, R.F., Tiemeier, H., et al. "Adverse Childhood Experiences and the Risk of Premature Mortality." *American Journal of Preventive Medicine* 37, no. 5 (2009): 389-396. - Demonstrates 19.6-year reduction in life expectancy for individuals with 6+ ACEs.

## Government Reports and Data

### Federal Agencies

- Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. "Preventing Adverse Childhood Experiences: Leveraging the Best Available Evidence." 2019, updated 2022. <https://www.cdc.gov/violenceprevention/aces/fastfact.html> - CDC's comprehensive ACE prevention framework and data.

- Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. "Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System (BRFSS): ACE Module." Updated annually. <https://www.cdc.gov/brfss/> - Population-level ACE prevalence data adopted by 48 states.

- Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration. "SAMHSA's Concept of Trauma and Guidance for a Trauma-Informed Approach." HHS Publication No. (SMA) 14-4884. 2014. <https://store.samhsa.gov/product/SAMHSA-s-Concept-of-Trauma-and-Guidance-for-a-Trauma-Informed-Approach/SMA14-4884> - The foundational federal TIC framework defining six guiding principles.

- Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration. "National Survey on Drug Use and Health (NSDUH)." Annual. <https://www.samhsa.gov/data/data-we-collect/nsduh-national-survey-drug-use-and-health> - Annual data on mental health treatment access, including PTSD treatment rates.

- Department of Veterans Affairs / Department of Defense. "VA/DoD Clinical Practice Guideline for the Management of Posttraumatic Stress Disorder and Acute Stress Disorder." Version 4.0. 2023. <https://www.healthquality.va.gov/guidelines/MH/ptsd/> - Gold standard clinical guideline rating CPT, PE, and EMDR as Level A treatments.

- Health Resources and Services Administration. "Health Workforce Shortage Areas." Updated quarterly. <https://data.hrsa.gov/topics/health-workforce/shortage-areas> - Data on mental health professional shortage areas by county.

### Congressional Reports

- Congressional Research Service. "Federal Programs and Responses to Adverse Childhood Experiences (ACEs)." Report R47215 (2022). - Overview of federal ACE-related programs and funding streams.

- Government Accountability Office. "Behavioral Health: Research on Health Care Costs of Untreated Conditions Is Limited." GAO-19-274 (2019). <https://www.gao.gov/products/gao-19-274> - GAO analysis of the economic costs of untreated behavioral health conditions.

- Congressional Budget Office. "Federal Spending on Children's Programs." Annual analysis. - Data on federal investment in children's health, education, and welfare programs.

### Agency Data Portals

- SAMHSA. National Child Traumatic Stress Network (NCTSN) Resource Library. <https://www.nctsn.org/resources> - Largest repository of child trauma treatment resources, training materials, and implementation guides.

- CDC. ACE Data Portal. <https://www.cdc.gov/violenceprevention/aces/ace-brfss.html> - State-by-state ACE prevalence data from the BRFSS module.

- HHS Administration for Children and Families. Adoption and Foster Care Analysis and Reporting System (AFCARS). <https://www.acf.hhs.gov/cb/data-research/adoption-fostercare> - National foster care data including entries, exits, placement stability, and outcomes.

## Books

- van der Kolk, B. *The Body Keeps the Score: Brain, Mind, and Body in the Healing of Trauma*. Penguin Books, 2014. - Widely read synthesis of trauma neuroscience and treatment; foundational text for clinicians and policymakers.

- Harris, N.B. *The Deepest Well: Healing the Long-Term Effects of Childhood Adversity*. Houghton Mifflin Harcourt, 2018. - Written by California's first Surgeon General; documents the science and policy implications of ACEs.

- Perry, B.D. & Winfrey, O. *What Happened to You? Conversations on Trauma, Resilience, and Healing*. Flatiron Books, 2021. - Accessible overview of trauma science and healing, reaching a broad public audience.

- Bloom, S.L. & Farragher, B. *Restoring Sanctuary: A New Operating System for Trauma-Informed Systems of Care*. Oxford University Press, 2013. - Comprehensive model for organizational trauma-informed transformation.

- Anda, R.F. & Felitti, V.J. "The Adverse Childhood Experiences Study." In *The Impact of Early Life Trauma on Health and Disease*, edited by R.A. Lanius et al. Cambridge University Press, 2010. - Original ACE researchers' comprehensive account of the study and its implications.

- Bath, H. & Seita, J. *The Three Pillars of Transforming Care: Trauma and Resilience in the Other 23 Hours*. UW Faculty of Education Publishing, 2018. - Practical guide for residential and community-based trauma-informed care implementation.

- Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration. *Trauma-Informed Care in Behavioral Health Services: Treatment Improvement Protocol (TIP) Series 57*. HHS, 2014. - Comprehensive clinical guide for implementing TIC in behavioral health settings.

## Legal Resources

### Case Law

- *Youngberg v. Romeo*, 457 U.S. 307 (1982). - Established constitutional right to minimally adequate care and safe conditions for individuals in state custody; foundational for trauma-informed institutional care.

- *Deshaney v. Winnebago County*, 489 U.S. 189 (1989). - Established limits on state obligation to protect children from parental harm; relevant to child welfare trauma policy.

- *Brown v. Plata*, 563 U.S. 493 (2011). - Required California to reduce prison overcrowding due to inadequate mental health care; relevant to incarceration and trauma.

- *Endrew F. v. Douglas County School District*, 580 U.S. 386 (2017). - Heightened standard for adequacy of educational services under IDEA; relevant to trauma-affected students' educational rights.

### Statutory References

- 42 U.S.C. 290bb-36b (Garrett Lee Smith Memorial Act) - Authorizes youth suicide prevention and mental health programs.
- 42 U.S.C. 5101 et seq. (Child Abuse Prevention and Treatment Act, CAPTA) - Federal framework for child maltreatment prevention and response.
- Public Law 115-271 (SUPPORT for Patients and Communities Act, 2018) - Expanded behavioral health services and trauma screening provisions.
- Public Law 115-123 (Family First Prevention Services Act, 2018) - Expanded Title IV-E funding for evidence-based prevention services, including trauma treatment for at-risk families.
- 42 U.S.C. 290bb-32 (National Child Traumatic Stress Initiative) - Authorizes NCTSN funding for child trauma treatment and training.
- 42 U.S.C. 300x-1 et seq. (Community Mental Health Services Block Grant) - Authorizes SAMHSA block grants to states for mental health services.
