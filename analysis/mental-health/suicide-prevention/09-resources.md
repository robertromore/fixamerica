# Suicide Prevention: Resources

This section provides a curated list of key research, reports, data sources, and organizations for those seeking deeper engagement with suicide prevention policy. Resources are organized by type and cover the full spectrum from academic research to advocacy organizations, government data portals, and practical crisis intervention tools.

## Books

- Case, A., & Deaton, A. *Deaths of Despair and the Future of Capitalism*. Princeton University Press, 2020. - Analysis of rising mortality from suicide, drugs, and alcohol among non-college-educated Americans
- Joiner, T. *Why People Die by Suicide*. Harvard University Press, 2005. - The interpersonal-psychological theory of suicide (thwarted belonging, perceived burdensomeness, acquired capability)
- Shneidman, E.S. *The Suicidal Mind*. Oxford University Press, 1996. - Foundational text defining "psychache" (unbearable psychological pain) as the driver of suicide
- Hempel, S. *Lethal Means: Why Common Sense Gun Safety Saves Lives*. Prometheus Books, 2022. - Evidence-based analysis of means restriction as suicide prevention
- Linehan, M.M. *Building a Life Worth Living: A Memoir*. Random House, 2020. - Developer of dialectical behavior therapy describes her own suicidal history and the evolution of DBT
- Durkheim, E. *Suicide: A Study in Sociology*. 1897; reprint, Free Press, 1997. - The foundational sociological analysis of suicide as shaped by social integration and regulation
- Stanley, B., & Brown, G.K. "Safety Planning Intervention: A Brief Intervention to Mitigate Suicide Risk." *Cognitive and Behavioral Practice* 19.2 (2012): 256-264. - The widely adopted safety planning approach used in Zero Suicide and VA programs

## Legal Resources

### Case Law

- *District of Columbia v. Heller*, 554 U.S. 570 (2008) - Established individual right to firearm possession; stated right is "not unlimited"
- *New York State Rifle & Pistol Association v. Bruen*, 597 U.S. 1 (2022) - Applied historical test to Second Amendment claims; implications for ERPO and waiting period litigation
- *Redington v. State* (Indiana, 2020) - Upheld Indiana's ERPO ("Jake Laird Law") against due process challenge
- *Hope v. State* (Florida, 2023) - Florida appellate court upheld state ERPO law

### Statutory References

- 42 U.S.C. 290bb-36 (Garrett Lee Smith Memorial Act) - Youth and campus suicide prevention grants
- 38 U.S.C. 1720F (Joshua Omvig Veterans Suicide Prevention Act) - VA suicide prevention program mandate
- 47 U.S.C. 251(e)(4) (National Suicide Hotline Designation Act of 2020) - 988 designation
- Pub. L. 117-159 (Bipartisan Safer Communities Act, 2022) - Crisis services funding, community mental health, ERPO implementation support
- 29 U.S.C. 1185a (Mental Health Parity and Addiction Equity Act) - Insurance parity requirements
