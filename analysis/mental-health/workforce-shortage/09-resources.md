# Mental Health Workforce Shortage: Resources

This section provides a curated list of key research, government reports, data sources, and advocacy organizations relevant to the mental health workforce shortage in the United States.

---

## Academic Research

### Peer-Reviewed Studies

- Hilty, D.M., Ferrer, D.C., Parish, M.B., Johnston, B., Callahan, E.J., & Yellowlees, P.M. "The Effectiveness of Telemental Health: A 2013 Review." *Telemedicine and e-Health* 19, no. 6 (2013): 444-454. <https://doi.org/10.1089/tmj.2013.0075> - Comprehensive review establishing telemental health outcome equivalence with in-person care.
- Davidson, L., Bellamy, C., Guy, K., & Miller, R. "Peer Support Among Persons with Severe Mental Illnesses: A Review of Evidence and Experience." *World Psychiatry* 11, no. 2 (2012): 123-128. <https://doi.org/10.1016/j.wpsyc.2012.05.009> - Foundational evidence for peer specialist effectiveness in reducing hospitalization.
- Chinman, M., George, P., Dougherty, R.H., Daniels, A.S., Ghose, S.S., Swift, A., & Delphin-Rittmon, M.E. "Peer Support Services for Individuals with Serious Mental Illnesses: Assessing the Evidence." *Psychiatric Services* 65, no. 4 (2014): 429-441. <https://doi.org/10.1176/appi.ps.201300244> - Systematic review supporting peer support as evidence-based practice.
- Shanafelt, T.D., Hasan, O., Dyrbye, L.N., Sinsky, C., Satele, D., Sloan, J., & West, C.P. "Changes in Burnout and Satisfaction with Work-Life Balance in Physicians and the General U.S. Working Population Between 2011 and 2014." *Mayo Clinic Proceedings* 90, no. 12 (2015): 1600-1613. <https://doi.org/10.1016/j.mayocp.2015.08.023> - Key study on physician burnout trends and contributing factors.
- West, C.P., Dyrbye, L.N., Erwin, P.J., & Shanafelt, T.D. "Interventions to Prevent and Reduce Physician Burnout: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis." *The Lancet* 388, no. 10057 (2016): 2272-2281. <https://doi.org/10.1016/S0140-6736(16)31279-X> - Meta-analysis demonstrating that organizational interventions reduce burnout by 30-50%.
- Cabral, R.R. & Smith, T.B. "Racial/Ethnic Matching of Clients and Therapists in Mental Health Services: A Meta-Analytic Review of Preferences, Perceptions, and Outcomes." *Journal of Counseling Psychology* 58, no. 4 (2011): 537-554. <https://doi.org/10.1037/a0025266> - Evidence that racial concordance improves treatment engagement and outcomes.
- Alegria, M., Chatterji, P., Wells, K., Cao, Z., Chen, C.N., Takeuchi, D., Jackson, J., & Meng, X.L. "Disparity in Depression Treatment Among Racial and Ethnic Minority Populations in the United States." *Psychiatric Services* 59, no. 11 (2013): 1264-1272. - Documents disparities in treatment access by race/ethnicity.
- Bashshur, R.L., Shannon, G.W., Bashshur, N., & Yellowlees, P.M. "The Empirical Evidence for Telemedicine Interventions in Mental Disorders." *Telemedicine and e-Health* 22, no. 2 (2016): 87-113. <https://doi.org/10.1089/tmj.2015.0206> - Evidence base for telepsychiatry effectiveness across diagnoses.

### Working Papers and White Papers

- Annapolis Coalition on the Behavioral Health Workforce. "An Action Plan for Behavioral Health Workforce Development." Updated 2023. <https://annapoliscoalition.org/action-plan/> - Foundational framework for workforce strategy.
- Lancet Commission on Global Mental Health and Sustainable Development. "Global Costs of Mental Illness." 2024. - Documents the $280 billion annual U.S. economic cost of untreated mental illness.
- RAND Corporation. "Nurse Practitioner and Physician Assistant Practice: An Analysis of State Legislation." Working Paper. 2021. <https://www.rand.org/pubs/research_reports/RRA1080-2.html> - Analysis of NP practice outcomes across state regulatory environments.

## Government Reports and Data

### Federal Agencies

- Health Resources and Services Administration. "Health Professional Shortage Areas (HPSAs)." Updated quarterly. <https://data.hrsa.gov/topics/health-workforce/shortage-areas> - Primary source for HPSA designation data, including 6,559 mental health HPSAs and 8,220 practitioner shortfall.
- Health Resources and Services Administration. "National Health Service Corps." 2024. <https://nhsc.hrsa.gov/> - Program data on NHSC behavioral health placements (~6,800), retention rates, and loan repayment awards.
- Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration. "National Survey on Drug Use and Health (NSDUH)." Annual. <https://www.samhsa.gov/data/data-we-collect/nsduh-national-survey-drug-use-and-health> - Primary source for national prevalence of mental illness (57.8 million adults) and treatment access.
- Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration. "Certified Community Behavioral Health Clinics: 2024 Report." 2024. <https://www.samhsa.gov/certified-community-behavioral-health-clinics> - Data on CCBHC operations (~540 clinics), staffing outcomes, and access improvements.
- Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services. "Medicare Physician Fee Schedule." Updated annually. <https://www.cms.gov/medicare/physician-fee-schedule/search> - Official reimbursement rates for behavioral health services (90837 at $131.65, 2024).
- Bureau of Labor Statistics. "Occupational Outlook Handbook: Mental Health Counselors and Marriage and Family Therapists." Updated 2023. <https://www.bls.gov/ooh/community-and-social-service/mental-health-counselors-and-marriage-and-family-therapists.htm> - Salary data, employment projections, and workforce demographics.
- Bureau of Labor Statistics. "Occupational Outlook Handbook: Substance Abuse, Behavioral Disorder, and Mental Health Counselors." Updated 2023. <https://www.bls.gov/ooh/community-and-social-service/substance-abuse-behavioral-disorder-and-mental-health-counselors.htm>

### Congressional Reports

- Congressional Research Service. "Mental Health Care Professional Shortage Areas (HPSAs)." 2023. - Overview of HPSA designation methodology and policy options.
- Congressional Research Service. "The Mental Health Workforce: A Primer." 2023. - Comprehensive introduction to workforce supply, demand, and federal programs.
- Government Accountability Office. "Behavioral Health: Available Workforce Information and Federal Actions to Help Recruit and Retain Providers." GAO-23-105250 (2023). <https://www.gao.gov/products/gao-23-105250> - GAO analysis of workforce data gaps and federal program effectiveness.
- Medicare Payment Advisory Commission (MedPAC). "Report to the Congress: Medicare Payment Policy." March 2024. <https://www.medpac.gov/> - Analysis of Medicare behavioral health reimbursement relative to other specialties.
- Medicaid and CHIP Payment and Access Commission (MACPAC). "Behavioral Health in the Medicaid Program." 2023. <https://www.macpac.gov/> - Data on Medicaid behavioral health reimbursement (~72% of Medicare) and provider participation (35.7% of psychiatrists accepting new Medicaid patients).

### Agency Data Portals

- HRSA. Health Workforce Data. <https://data.hrsa.gov/topics/health-workforce> - Comprehensive data on health workforce supply, distribution, and shortage areas.
- SAMHSA. Behavioral Health Services Locator. <https://findtreatment.gov/> - Facility-level data on behavioral health providers and services.
- CMS. Provider Enrollment Data. <https://data.cms.gov/provider-characteristics/medicare-provider-enrollment> - Data on provider participation in Medicare, including opt-out rates.

## Books

- Grob, G.N. & Goldman, H.H. *The Dilemma of Federal Mental Health Policy: Radical Reform or Incremental Change?* Rutgers University Press, 2006. - Historical analysis of federal mental health policy cycles relevant to understanding workforce policy development.
- Frank, R.G. & Glied, S.A. *Better But Not Well: Mental Health Policy in the United States Since 1950*. Johns Hopkins University Press, 2006. - Foundational text on the evolution of U.S. mental health policy, including workforce dimensions.
- Hoge, M.A., Morris, J.A., Daniels, A.S., Stuart, G.W., Huey, L.Y., & Adams, N. *An Action Plan on Behavioral Health Workforce Development*. Annapolis Coalition on the Behavioral Health Workforce, 2007. - The landmark workforce development framework that continues to inform federal strategy.
- Insel, T. *Healing: Our Path from Mental Illness to Mental Health*. Penguin Press, 2022. - Former NIMH director's assessment of the mental health system's failures, including workforce inadequacy.
- Mechanic, D. & Olfson, M. *Mental Health and Social Policy: Beyond Managed Care*. Pearson, 2016. - Analysis of how financing structures shape workforce supply and distribution.

## Think Tank Analysis

### Research Organizations

- Brookings Institution. "Addressing the Mental Health Workforce Shortage." 2023. <https://www.brookings.edu/> - Policy recommendations for federal workforce investment with cost-benefit analysis.
- Bipartisan Policy Center. "Tackling America's Mental Health and Addiction Crisis Through Primary Care Integration." 2023. <https://bipartisanpolicy.org/> - Analysis of Collaborative Care Model workforce requirements and scaling barriers.
- Commonwealth Fund. "The U.S. Mental Health System: Spending, Workforce, and Access." 2023. <https://www.commonwealthfund.org/> - Comparative analysis of U.S. mental health workforce supply versus peer nations.
- Kaiser Family Foundation. "Mental Health and Substance Use State Fact Sheets." 2023. <https://www.kff.org/statedata/mental-health-and-substance-use-state-fact-sheets/> - State-level data on workforce supply, Medicaid coverage, and access metrics.
- Milbank Memorial Fund. "Reforming State Scope of Practice Laws to Expand Access to Mental Health Services." 2023. <https://www.milbank.org/> - Analysis of scope-of-practice reform as a workforce expansion strategy.

### Policy Briefs

- National Council for Mental Wellbeing. "CCBHC Impact Report." 2023. <https://www.thenationalcouncil.org/> - Data on CCBHC outcomes: 15-25% salary increases, 18-22% turnover, expanded access.
- National Academy for State Health Policy. "States Expand the Behavioral Health Workforce." 2023. <https://www.nashp.org/> - State-level innovation in workforce expansion, licensing reform, and telehealth.
- Milliman Research. "Addiction and Mental Health vs. Physical Health: Widening Disparities in Network Use and Provider Reimbursement." 2023. <https://www.milliman.com/> - Data on out-of-network utilization (5.4x for psychiatry, 5.8x for psychotherapy vs. medical care).

## Legal Resources

### Case Law

- *Wit v. United Behavioral Health*, No. 14-cv-02346 (N.D. Cal. 2019). - Landmark case finding insurer violated parity by applying overly restrictive coverage criteria for mental health services.
- *New York State Psychiatric Association v. UnitedHealth Group*, 798 F.3d 125 (2d Cir. 2015). - Challenged insurer reimbursement practices for behavioral health services.

### Statutory References

- 42 U.S.C. Section 254b-254e - National Health Service Corps authorizing statute, including behavioral health placements.
- 42 U.S.C. Section 1395x(s)(2) - Medicare covered services definitions relevant to behavioral health provider types.
- Pub. L. 110-343, Section 512 - Mental Health Parity and Addiction Equity Act of 2008.
- Pub. L. 116-260, Division CC, Section 126 - Consolidated Appropriations Act of 2021, authorizing 1,000 new GME slots.
- Pub. L. 117-169 (Inflation Reduction Act) - Medicare telehealth provisions relevant to behavioral health.
- 42 U.S.C. Section 290bb-34 - SAMHSA grant authority for behavioral health workforce programs.
