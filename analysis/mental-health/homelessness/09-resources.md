## Books

- Padgett, D., Henwood, B., & Tsemberis, S. *Housing First: Ending Homelessness, Transforming Systems, and Changing Lives*. Oxford University Press, 2016. - The definitive account of the Housing First model by its developers, including research evidence and implementation guidance.
- Desmond, M. *Evicted: Poverty and Profit in the American City*. Crown, 2016. - Pulitzer Prize-winning investigation of eviction as a driver of homelessness, with implications for mental health and housing stability.
- Torrey, E. F. *American Psychosis: How the Federal Government Destroyed the Mental Health Treatment System*. Oxford University Press, 2014. - Critical history of deinstitutionalization and the failure to build community alternatives.
- Hoch, C., & Slayton, R. A. *New Homeless and Old: Community and the Skid Row Hotel*. Temple University Press, 1989. - Documents the loss of SRO housing and its impact on homelessness.
- Deutsch, A. *The Shame of the States*. Harcourt, Brace, 1948. - The foundational expose of conditions in state psychiatric hospitals that catalyzed deinstitutionalization.
- Rosenheck, R., Kasprow, W., Frisman, L., & Liu-Mares, W. *Cost-Effectiveness of Supported Housing for Homeless Persons with Mental Illness*. Department of Veterans Affairs, 2003. - Early VA research demonstrating cost-effectiveness of supportive housing for veterans.
- Lurie, I., & Riccucci, N. M. *At the Front Lines of the Welfare System: A Perspective on the Decline in Welfare Caseloads*. Rockefeller Institute Press, 2003. - Context on safety net implementation affecting mentally ill populations.

## Legal Resources

### Case Law

- *City of Grants Pass v. Johnson*, 603 U.S. ___ (2024) - Held that the Eighth Amendment does not prohibit enforcement of anti-camping ordinances against homeless individuals, even when no shelter is available. Overturned Ninth Circuit's *Martin v. Boise* framework.
- *Martin v. City of Boise*, 920 F.3d 584 (9th Cir. 2019) - Held that the Eighth Amendment prohibits criminalizing sleeping outdoors when insufficient shelter exists. Effectively overruled by *Grants Pass* (2024).
- *O'Connor v. Donaldson*, 422 U.S. 563 (1975) - Held that a state cannot constitutionally confine a non-dangerous individual who is capable of surviving safely in freedom.
- *Addington v. Texas*, 441 U.S. 418 (1979) - Established clear and convincing evidence as the standard of proof for involuntary civil commitment.
- *Wyatt v. Stickney*, 325 F. Supp. 781 (M.D. Ala. 1971) - Established the constitutional right to adequate treatment for people involuntarily committed to state psychiatric hospitals.
- *Olmstead v. L.C.*, 527 U.S. 581 (1999) - Held that unjustified institutional isolation of people with disabilities is discrimination under the ADA; requires states to provide community-based services when appropriate.
- *Pottinger v. City of Miami*, 810 F. Supp. 1551 (S.D. Fla. 1992) - Early case finding unconstitutional the arrest of homeless individuals for performing life-sustaining activities in public.

### Statutory References

- 42 U.S.C. ch. 119 (McKinney-Vento Homeless Assistance Act) - Primary federal homelessness legislation, including CoC, ESG, and PATH authorization.
- 42 U.S.C. Section 290cc-21 et seq. (PATH Act) - Authorization for Projects for Assistance in Transition from Homelessness targeting people with SMI.
- HEARTH Act, P.L. 111-22 (2009) - Reauthorization and modernization of McKinney-Vento, establishing prevention focus and performance measurement.
- 21st Century Cures Act, P.L. 114-255 (2016) - Expanded mental health parity enforcement, crisis services, and CCBHC funding.
- Bipartisan Safer Communities Act, P.L. 117-159 (2022) - Funded 988 crisis infrastructure and community mental health services.
- California CARE Act, Cal. Welf. & Inst. Code Section 5970 et seq. (2022) - Created court-ordered community treatment plans for individuals with severe mental illness.
- California AB 2162 (2018) - Established by-right zoning for supportive housing in California.
