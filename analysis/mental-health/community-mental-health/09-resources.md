## Legal Resources

### Case Law

- *Olmstead v. L.C.*, 527 U.S. 581 (1999). - Established that unjustified institutional isolation of individuals with disabilities, including mental illness, violates the ADA; foundational case for community mental health expansion
- *Wyatt v. Stickney*, 325 F. Supp. 781 (M.D. Ala. 1971). - Established constitutional right to minimally adequate treatment in state psychiatric hospitals; early impetus for community alternatives
- *O'Connor v. Donaldson*, 422 U.S. 563 (1975). - Held that a state cannot constitutionally confine a non-dangerous individual with mental illness who is capable of surviving in the community; reinforced community treatment principle
- *Addington v. Texas*, 441 U.S. 418 (1979). - Established "clear and convincing evidence" standard for civil commitment, balancing state interest in treatment with individual liberty

### Statutory References

- 42 U.S.C. Section 300x et seq. - Community Mental Health Services Block Grant statutory framework
- 42 U.S.C. Section 1396a(a)(29) - Medicaid CCBHC State Plan Amendment authority (added by P.L. 117-159)
- Section 223, Protecting Access to Medicare Act of 2014 (P.L. 113-93) - Original CCBHC demonstration authorization
- P.L. 117-159 (Bipartisan Safer Communities Act of 2022) - CCBHC permanent Medicaid option and $250 million expansion grants
- 42 U.S.C. Section 1396d(a)(B) - Medicaid Institution for Mental Diseases (IMD) exclusion
- 29 U.S.C. Section 1185a - Mental Health Parity and Addiction Equity Act (MHPAEA) statutory framework
- P.L. 88-164 (Mental Retardation and Community Mental Health Centers Construction Act of 1963) - Original CMHC authorization
- 42 CFR Part 2 - Confidentiality of substance use disorder patient records (federal regulation governing SUD data sharing)
