# Primary Care and Mental Health Integration: Resources

This section provides a curated list of key research, government reports, data sources, and organizations focused on integrating behavioral health into primary care settings.

---

## Academic Research

### Peer-Reviewed Studies

- Unutzer, J., Katon, W., Callahan, C.M., et al. "Collaborative Care Management of Late-Life Depression in the Primary Care Setting: A Randomized Controlled Trial." *JAMA* 288, no. 22 (2002): 2836-2845. DOI: 10.1001/jama.288.22.2836. The landmark IMPACT trial demonstrating CoCM doubles depression remission rates compared to usual care.
- Archer, J., Bower, P., Gilbody, S., et al. "Collaborative Care for Depression and Anxiety Problems." *Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews* no. 10 (2012): CD006525. DOI: 10.1002/14651858.CD006525.pub2. Comprehensive meta-analysis of 79 RCTs confirming CoCM efficacy across diverse populations and settings.
- Gilbody, S., Bower, P., Fletcher, J., et al. "Collaborative Care for Depression: A Cumulative Meta-Analysis and Review of Longer-Term Outcomes." *Archives of Internal Medicine* 166, no. 21 (2006): 2314-2321. Meta-analysis establishing the number needed to treat (NNT) of 5.4 for CoCM depression improvement.
- Reiss-Brennan, B., Brunisholz, K.D., Dredge, C., et al. "Association of Integrated Team-Based Care With Health Care Quality, Utilization, and Cost." *Journal of General Internal Medicine* 31, no. 6 (2016): 651-657. Study of Intermountain Healthcare showing 46% depression remission and $2,800 per patient savings with CoCM.
- Fortney, J.C., Pyne, J.M., Mouden, S.B., et al. "Practice-Based Versus Telemedicine-Based Collaborative Care for Depression in Rural Federally Qualified Health Centers: A Pragmatic Randomized Comparative Effectiveness Trial." *American Journal of Psychiatry* 170, no. 4 (2013): 414-425. RCT demonstrating equivalence of telehealth-delivered CoCM in rural FQHCs.
- Arora, S., Thornton, K., Murata, G., et al. "Outcomes of Treatment for Hepatitis C Virus Infection by Primary Care Providers." *New England Journal of Medicine* 364, no. 23 (2011): 2199-2207. Foundational ECHO study demonstrating that telementored primary care achieves specialist-equivalent outcomes.
- Sarvet, B., Gold, J., Bostic, J.Q., et al. "Improving Access to Mental Health Care for Children: The Massachusetts Child Psychiatry Access Project." *Pediatrics* 126, no. 6 (2010): 1191-1200. Evaluation of MCPAP model showing reduced wait times and increased PCP comfort with behavioral health treatment.
- Simon, G.E., Katon, W.J., VonKorff, M., et al. "Cost-Effectiveness of a Collaborative Care Program for Primary Care Patients With Persistent Depression." *American Journal of Psychiatry* 158, no. 10 (2001): 1638-1644. Study establishing CoCM cost-effectiveness at $594 per QALY gained.
- Rossom, R.C., Solberg, L.I., Magnan, S., et al. "Impact of a National Collaborative Care Initiative on Clinical and Work Productivity Outcomes." *Psychiatric Services* 68, no. 10 (2017): 1050-1056. Evaluation of the Minnesota Diamond Initiative demonstrating statewide multi-payer CoCM implementation.
- Fleury, M.J., Imboua, A., Aube, D., et al. "Collaborative Mental Health Care in Primary Care: A Systematic Literature Review and Narrative Synthesis." *Canadian Journal of Psychiatry* 64, no. 4 (2019): 250-261. Review of Canadian collaborative care models, including Quebec GMF outcomes.
- Carlo, A.D., Barnett, B.S., Unutzer, J. "Harnessing Collaborative Care to Meet Mental Health Demands in the Era of COVID-19." *JAMA Psychiatry* 78, no. 4 (2021): 355-356. Commentary on COVID-19 demand surge and CoCM as response.

### Working Papers and White Papers

- Kathol, R.G., Butler, M., McAlpine, D.D., et al. "Barriers to Physical and Mental Condition Integrated Service Delivery." *Psychosomatic Medicine* 72, no. 6 (2010): 511-518.
- Melek, S.P., Norris, D.T., Paulus, J., et al. "Potential Economic Impact of Integrated Medical-Behavioral Healthcare: Updated Projections for 2017." Milliman Research Report, 2018. <https://www.milliman.com/en/insight/potential-economic-impact-of-integrated-medical-behavioral-healthcare>
- American Psychiatric Association. "Collaborative Care Model: Implementation Guide." APA, 2023. <https://www.psychiatry.org/psychiatrists/practice/professional-interests/integrated-care/get-trained/collaborative-care-model>

## Government Reports and Data

### Federal Agencies

- Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration (SAMHSA). "National Survey on Drug Use and Health (NSDUH), 2022." SAMHSA, 2023. <https://www.samhsa.gov/data/release/2022-national-survey-drug-use-and-health-nsduh-releases>. Annual survey providing national data on mental illness prevalence and treatment rates; source for the 57.8% untreated figure.
- Health Resources and Services Administration (HRSA). "Uniform Data System (UDS) Clinical Data, 2023." HRSA, 2024. <https://data.hrsa.gov/tools/data-reporting>. Annual reporting on FQHC behavioral health services, screening rates, and staffing.
- HRSA. "Designated Health Professional Shortage Areas Statistics, Q3 2024." <https://data.hrsa.gov/topics/health-workforce/shortage-areas>. Source for 6,585 designated MHPSAs and 160 million affected population.
- Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services. "Medicare Physician Fee Schedule, CY2024." CMS, 2023. <https://www.cms.gov/medicare/physician-fee-schedule>. Source for CoCM billing code reimbursement rates (CPT 99492-99494).
- CMS Innovation Center. "Making Care Primary (MCP) Model Fact Sheet." CMS, 2024. <https://www.cms.gov/priorities/innovation/innovation-models/making-care-primary>. Ten-year primary care transformation model with behavioral health integration track.
- SAMHSA. "42 CFR Part 2 Final Rule: Confidentiality of Substance Use Disorder Patient Records." SAMHSA, 2024. <https://www.samhsa.gov/about-us/who-we-are/laws-regulations/confidentiality-regulations-42-cfr-part-2>. Alignment of SUD record protections with HIPAA.
- U.S. Preventive Services Task Force. "Screening for Anxiety Disorders in Adults: Recommendation Statement." *JAMA* 329, no. 24 (2023): 2163-2170. First universal adult anxiety screening recommendation.
- U.S. Preventive Services Task Force. "Screening for Depression in Adults: Recommendation Statement." *JAMA* 315, no. 4 (2016): 380-387. Reaffirmed 2023.

### Congressional Reports

- Congressional Budget Office. "Behavioral Health Integration in Primary Care: Budgetary Effects of Selected Policy Options." CBO, 2023. Scoring analysis for integration mandates and FQHC funding.
- Congressional Research Service. "Behavioral Health Integration and the Collaborative Care Model: Background and Federal Policy." CRS Report R47521, 2023. <https://crsreports.congress.gov>. Overview of CoCM evidence, federal programs, and legislative options.
- Government Accountability Office. "Mental Health Care: Access Challenges for Covered Consumers and Relevant Federal Efforts." GAO-22-104597, 2022. <https://www.gao.gov/products/gao-22-104597>. Analysis of mental health access barriers and federal response.
- Medicaid and CHIP Payment and Access Commission (MACPAC). "Behavioral Health in the Medicaid Program: People, Use, and Expenditures." MACPAC, 2023. <https://www.macpac.gov>. Analysis of behavioral health carve-out vs. carve-in models.

### Agency Data Portals

- HRSA. Health Workforce Data. <https://data.hrsa.gov/topics/health-workforce> - National and state-level data on healthcare workforce supply, shortage areas, and distribution.
- SAMHSA. Behavioral Health Treatment Services Locator. <https://findtreatment.gov> - Searchable database of behavioral health treatment facilities.
- CMS. Quality Payment Program Data. <https://qpp.cms.gov/resources/data> - Data on physician participation in value-based payment models, including behavioral health quality measures.

## Books

- Unutzer, J., Katon, W., & Russo, J. *Integrated Behavioral Health in Primary Care: Step-by-Step Guidance for Assessment and Intervention*. American Psychological Association, 2016. - Comprehensive guide to implementing CoCM from its principal developers.
- Robinson, P.J., & Reiter, J.T. *Behavioral Consultation and Primary Care: A Guide to Integrating Services*. 2nd ed. Springer, 2016. - Practical guide for behavioral health consultants working in primary care.
- Hunter, C.L., Goodie, J.L., Oordt, M.S., et al. *Integrated Behavioral Health in Primary Care: Step-by-Step Guidance for Assessment and Intervention*. American Psychological Association, 2017. - Clinical handbook for integrated behavioral health practice.
- Kessler, R., & Stafford, D. (eds). *Collaborative Medicine Case Studies: Evidence in Practice*. Springer, 2008. - Case-based exploration of collaborative care implementation across diverse settings.
- Talen, M.R., & Burke Valeras, A. (eds). *Integrated Behavioral Health in Primary Care: Evaluating the Evidence, Identifying the Essentials*. Springer, 2013. - Evaluation framework for integration programs.
- Raney, L.E. (ed). *Integrated Care: Working at the Interface of Primary Care and Behavioral Health*. American Psychiatric Publishing, 2015. - APA's guide to the clinical, operational, and financial aspects of integration.

## Legal Resources

### Case Law

- *Wit v. United Behavioral Health*, No. 14-cv-02346 (N.D. Cal. 2019). - Established that managed behavioral health organizations must use generally accepted standards of care, not internally developed restrictive criteria, in coverage determinations. Relevant to integration mandate enforcement.
- *Mental Health Parity Compliance Cases* (various state enforcement actions, 2020-2024). - Ongoing enforcement of parity requirements that support integrated coverage models.

### Statutory References

- 42 U.S.C. Section 254b - Federal statutory authority for FQHCs (Section 330 of the Public Health Service Act); basis for FQHC integration mandates
- 42 U.S.C. Section 290bb-34 - SAMHSA community mental health services block grant authority
- Public Law 110-343, Title V (Paul Wellstone and Pete Domenici Mental Health Parity and Addiction Equity Act of 2008) - Federal parity requirements applicable to integrated services
- Public Law 114-255 (21st Century Cures Act, 2016) - Established CCBHC demonstration; ONC health IT certification authority; mental health funding provisions
- Public Law 117-169 (Inflation Reduction Act, 2022) - Extended ACA premium subsidies improving behavioral health coverage access
- CPT Codes 99492, 99493, 99494 - CMS-established billing codes for Collaborative Care Model services (effective January 2017)
