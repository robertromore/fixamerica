## Legal Resources

### Case Law

- *Hays v. Leavitt*, 583 F.3d 1093 (8th Cir. 2009) - Upheld Medicare's authority to set conditions on telehealth coverage, including geographic restrictions.
- *Gonzales v. Oregon*, 546 U.S. 243 (2006) - Limited DEA authority to override state medical practice standards, relevant to federal-state tension in telehealth prescribing regulation.
- *FTC v. BetterHelp*, No. 2023 (FTC Administrative Action, 2023) - $7.8 million settlement establishing FTC authority over mental health platform data practices.
- *FTC v. GoodRx*, No. 2023 (FTC Administrative Action, 2023) - $1.5 million settlement for sharing user health data with advertisers, extending FTC health data privacy enforcement.
- *United States v. Smith* (Done Health indictment), No. 2024 (C.D. Cal. 2024) - Federal indictment of telehealth company founder on fraud and drug distribution charges related to telehealth prescribing of controlled substances.

### Statutory References

- 42 U.S.C. 1395m(m) (Social Security Act Section 1834(m)) - Governs Medicare telehealth coverage, including geographic and originating-site restrictions.
- 21 U.S.C. 829(e) (Ryan Haight Online Pharmacy Consumer Protection Act) - Requires in-person examination before telehealth prescribing of controlled substances.
- 21st Century Cures Act, Pub. L. 114-255, Section 3060 - Exempts certain clinical decision support software from FDA medical device regulation.
- Consolidated Appropriations Act of 2023, Pub. L. 117-328, Division FF, Title I - Made buprenorphine telehealth prescribing permanent; eliminated X-waiver requirement.
- Bipartisan Infrastructure Law, Pub. L. 117-58, Title IV - Authorized $65 billion for broadband infrastructure including the BEAD Program.
- Bipartisan Safer Communities Act, Pub. L. 117-159 (2022) - Provided $1 billion for school-based mental health services, including telehealth.
- VA MISSION Act, Pub. L. 115-182, Section 151 (2018) - Authorized veterans to receive VA telehealth services regardless of state of residence.
- CONNECT for Health Act (introduced, not enacted) - Proposed permanent removal of Medicare telehealth geographic and originating-site restrictions.

### Regulatory References

- DEA Temporary Rule, 88 FR 12875 (2023) - Temporary extension of Ryan Haight Act waiver for telehealth prescribing.
- DEA Proposed Rule, 89 FR (2024) - Proposed permanent framework for telehealth prescribing of controlled substances.
- CMS Final Rule, 87 FR 70002 (2022) - CY 2023 Physician Fee Schedule, including temporary telehealth provisions.
- FDA Draft Guidance, "Clinical Decision Support Software" (2023) - Draft guidance clarifying regulatory boundaries for digital health tools.
