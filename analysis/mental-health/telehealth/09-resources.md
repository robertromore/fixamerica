## Legal Resources

### Case Law

- *Hays v. Leavitt*, 583 F.3d 1093 (8th Cir. 2009) - Upheld Medicare's authority to set conditions on telehealth coverage, including geographic restrictions.
- *Gonzales v. Oregon*, 546 U.S. 243 (2006) - Limited DEA authority to override state medical practice standards, relevant to federal-state tension in telehealth prescribing regulation.
- *FTC v. BetterHelp*, No. 2023 (FTC Administrative Action, 2023) - $7.8 million settlement establishing FTC authority over mental health platform data practices.

### Statutory References

- 42 U.S.C. 1395m(m) (Social Security Act Section 1834(m)) - Governs Medicare telehealth coverage, including geographic and originating-site restrictions.
- 21 U.S.C. 829(e) (Ryan Haight Online Pharmacy Consumer Protection Act) - Requires in-person examination before telehealth prescribing of controlled substances.
- 21st Century Cures Act, Pub. L. 114-255, Section 3060 - Exempts certain clinical decision support software from FDA medical device regulation.
- Consolidated Appropriations Act of 2023, Pub. L. 117-328, Division FF, Title I - Made buprenorphine telehealth prescribing permanent; eliminated X-waiver requirement.
- Bipartisan Infrastructure Law, Pub. L. 117-58, Title IV - Authorized $65 billion for broadband infrastructure including the BEAD Program.
