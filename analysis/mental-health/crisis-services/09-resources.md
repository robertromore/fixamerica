# Mental Health Crisis Services: Resources

This section provides a curated list of key research, reports, data sources, and organizations relevant to mental health crisis services, the 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline, mobile crisis teams, crisis stabilization, and the intersection of law enforcement and behavioral health crisis response.

---

## Academic Research

### Peer-Reviewed Studies

- Balfour, M. E., Hahn Stephenson, A., Delany-Brumsey, A., Winsky, J., & Goldman, M. L. "Cops, Clinicians, or Both? Collaborative Approaches to Responding to Behavioral Health Emergencies." *Psychiatric Services* 73, no. 6 (2022): 658-669. <https://doi.org/10.1176/appi.ps.202100423> - Comprehensive analysis of mobile crisis team models and their outcomes compared to law enforcement-only response
- Dee, T. S., & Pyne, J. "A Community Response Approach to Mental Health and Substance Abuse Crises Reduced Crime." *American Economic Review* 114, no. 5 (2024): 1292-1318. <https://doi.org/10.1257/aer.20220526> - Rigorous evaluation of Denver's STAR program finding reductions in use of force and officer injuries
- Goldman, M. L., Druss, B. G., Goff, D. C., & Hurd, Y. L. "The 988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline: Challenges and Opportunities." *JAMA Psychiatry* 80, no. 1 (2023): 7-8. <https://doi.org/10.1001/jamapsychiatry.2022.3606> - Analysis of implementation challenges facing the 988 Lifeline
- Mulder, C. L., Koopmans, G. T., & Hengeveld, M. W. "Psychiatric Emergency Services in the Netherlands: Impact of the Psycholance Program." *BMC Psychiatry* 23, no. 1 (2023): 112. <https://doi.org/10.1186/s12888-023-04599-1> - Evaluation of the Netherlands' dedicated psychiatric ambulance service
- Rogers, M. S., McNiel, D. E., & Binder, R. L. "Effectiveness of Police Crisis Intervention Training Programs: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis." *Psychiatric Services* 73, no. 9 (2022): 1028-1039. <https://doi.org/10.1176/appi.ps.202100264> - Meta-analysis finding CIT reduces arrests by 5-10% but shows mixed results on use of force
- Saleh, A. Z., Appelbaum, P. S., Liu, X., Scott Stroup, T., & Wall, M. "Deaths of People with Mental Illness During Interactions with Law Enforcement." *JAMA Network Open* 4, no. 10 (2021): e2131624. <https://doi.org/10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2021.31624> - Analysis of racial disparities in fatal police encounters involving mental illness
- Zeller, S. L., Calma, N., & Stone, A. "Effects of a Dedicated Regional Psychiatric Emergency Service on Boarding of Psychiatric Patients in Area Emergency Departments." *Western Journal of Emergency Medicine* 15, no. 1 (2014): 1-6. <https://doi.org/10.5811/westjem.2013.6.17522> - Early evidence on how crisis stabilization facilities reduce ED boarding

### Working Papers and White Papers

- RAND Corporation. "The Economics of Crisis Intervention: Cost-Benefit Analysis of Mobile Crisis Teams." Santa Monica, CA. 2022. <https://www.rand.org/pubs/research_reports/RRA1457-1.html> - Found every $1 invested in mobile crisis services saves $2.16 in avoided ED visits, hospitalizations, and incarceration
- Milliman. "Actuarial Analysis: Financial Impact of Crisis Stabilization Services on Behavioral Health Spending." 2022. - Estimated that adequate crisis stabilization capacity reduces overall behavioral health spending by 8-12%
- National Council for Mental Wellbeing. "Roadmap to the Ideal Crisis System: Essential Elements, Measurable Standards, and Best Practices." Washington, DC. 2021. <https://www.thenationalcouncil.org/resources/roadmap-to-the-ideal-crisis-system/>

## Government Reports and Data

### Federal Agencies

- SAMHSA. "National Guidelines for Behavioral Health Crisis Care: Best Practice Toolkit." 2020. <https://www.samhsa.gov/sites/default/files/national-guidelines-for-behavioral-health-crisis-care-02242020.pdf> - The foundational federal guidance document defining the crisis continuum (Someone to Call, Someone to Respond, A Safe Place to Be)
- SAMHSA. "988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline Performance Dashboard." Updated monthly. <https://www.samhsa.gov/find-help/988/performance-metrics> - Real-time data on 988 call volume, answer rates, speed of answer, and text/chat contacts
- SAMHSA. "Crisis Services: Effectiveness, Cost-Effectiveness, and Funding Strategies." 2014. <https://store.samhsa.gov/product/Crisis-Services-Effectiveness-Cost-Effectiveness-and-Funding-Strategies/sma14-4848> - Foundational cost-effectiveness analysis of crisis service models
- HRSA. "Behavioral Health Workforce Projections, 2023-2035." 2023. <https://bhw.hrsa.gov/data-research/projecting-health-workforce-supply-demand> - Federal projections on behavioral health workforce supply and demand gaps
- CDC. "Web-Based Injury Statistics Query and Reporting System (WISQARS)." Updated annually. <https://www.cdc.gov/injury/wisqars/index.html> - Source for national and state-level suicide mortality data
- VA. "National Veteran Suicide Prevention Annual Report." 2023. <https://www.mentalhealth.va.gov/suicide_prevention/data.asp> - Annual report on veteran suicide rates and Veterans Crisis Line utilization

### Congressional Reports

- Congressional Research Service. "The 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline." Report R47095. Updated 2024. <https://crsreports.congress.gov/product/pdf/R/R47095> - Comprehensive overview of 988 legislation, funding, and implementation status
- Congressional Research Service. "Medicaid Coverage of Mobile Crisis Intervention Services." Report IF12061. 2023. <https://crsreports.congress.gov/product/pdf/IF/IF12061> - Analysis of ARP Section 9813 enhanced FMAP for mobile crisis services
- Government Accountability Office. "Behavioral Health: Available Workforce Information and Federal Actions to Help Recruit and Retain Providers." GAO-23-105250. 2023. <https://www.gao.gov/products/gao-23-105250> - Assessment of federal behavioral health workforce initiatives and gaps
- Congressional Budget Office. "Cost Estimate: Repeal of the Medicaid Institutions for Mental Diseases Exclusion." 2023. - Estimated $5-7 billion 10-year cost of full IMD exclusion repeal

### Agency Data Portals

- SAMHSA. National Survey on Drug Use and Health (NSDUH). <https://www.samhsa.gov/data/data-we-collect/nsduh-national-survey-drug-use-and-health> - Annual survey providing national and state estimates of mental illness prevalence, treatment access, and substance use
- AHRQ. Healthcare Cost and Utilization Project (HCUP). <https://hcup-us.ahrq.gov/> - National and state data on emergency department visits, hospitalizations, and costs, including mental health-specific breakdowns
- CMS. Medicaid and CHIP Enrollment Data. <https://www.medicaid.gov/medicaid/program-information/medicaid-and-chip-enrollment-data/index.html> - Enrollment data relevant to understanding Medicaid behavioral health coverage

## Books

- Reidenberg, D., & Berman, A. L. *Suicide Prevention: A Practical Guide for the Practitioner*. Oxford University Press, 2021. - Clinically oriented guide with chapter on crisis services and the 988 transition
- Fisher, W. H., & Geller, J. L. *A Clinical Guide to the Treatment of the Human Stress Response*. 4th ed. Springer, 2022. - Includes extensive coverage of crisis intervention techniques and emergency psychiatric assessment
- Dupont, R., Cochran, S., & Pillsbury, S. *Crisis Intervention Team (CIT) Core Elements*. CIT International, 2007. - Foundational text on the Memphis Model CIT program design and implementation
- Fuller, D. A., Sinclair, E., & Snook, J. *Emptying the New Asylums: A Beds Capacity Model to Reduce Mental Illness Behind Bars*. Treatment Advocacy Center, 2017. - Data-driven analysis of the relationship between psychiatric bed capacity and incarceration of people with mental illness
- Hogan, M. F. *The President's New Freedom Commission on Mental Health: Transforming Mental Health Care in America*. 2003. - Federal commission report that identified crisis services gaps and set a reform agenda, many elements still unimplemented

## Legal Resources

### Case Law

- *Olmstead v. L.C.*, 527 U.S. 581 (1999) - Established that the ADA requires states to provide community-based services to people with mental disabilities when appropriate, supporting the legal basis for community crisis alternatives
- *City of San Francisco v. Sheehan*, 575 U.S. 600 (2015) - Supreme Court case addressing police accommodation of mental illness during crisis encounters under the ADA
- *Disability Rights Montana v. Batista*, No. 4:22-cv-00044 (D. Mont. 2023) - Federal challenge to inadequate crisis services for people with serious mental illness, testing states' obligations under Olmstead

### Statutory References

- 42 U.S.C. Section 290bb-36c (National Suicide Hotline Designation Act of 2020) - Statutory basis for 988 designation
- 42 U.S.C. Section 1395dd (EMTALA) - Emergency Medical Treatment and Labor Act requirements for psychiatric emergencies
- 42 U.S.C. Section 1396d(a)(B) (Medicaid IMD exclusion) - Prohibition on federal Medicaid payment for institutions for mental diseases with more than 16 beds
- P.L. 117-2, Section 9813 (American Rescue Plan Act of 2021) - Enhanced 85% FMAP for Medicaid mobile crisis intervention services
- P.L. 117-159 (Bipartisan Safer Communities Act of 2022) - Includes crisis services funding provisions and pediatric mental health grants
- 42 CFR Part 2 - SAMHSA regulations governing confidentiality of substance use disorder patient records, applicable to crisis contacts
