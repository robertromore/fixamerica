## Books

- Chamberlin, J. *On Our Own: Patient-Controlled Alternatives to the Mental Health System*. McGraw-Hill, 1978. - Foundational text of the psychiatric survivor movement and peer-controlled services
- Clay, S., Schell, B., Corrigan, P. W., & Ralph, R. O. (Eds.). *On Our Own, Together: Peer Programs for People with Mental Illness*. Vanderbilt University Press, 2005. - Comprehensive overview of peer support program models and their development
- Copeland, M. E. *Wellness Recovery Action Plan (WRAP)*. Peach Press, 2002. - The original text defining the most widely used peer-led self-management tool in mental health
- Davidson, L., Rakfeldt, J., & Strauss, J. *The Roots of the Recovery Movement in Psychiatry: Lessons Learned*. Wiley-Blackwell, 2010. - Historical and theoretical foundations of recovery-oriented peer services
- Deegan, P. E. "Recovery as a Self-Directed Process of Healing and Transformation." *Occupational Therapy in Mental Health* 17, no. 3-4 (2002): 5-21. - Influential articulation of recovery as lived experience rather than clinical outcome
- Mead, S. *Peer Support: A Theoretical Perspective*. Intentional Peer Support, 2003. - Theoretical framework for intentional peer support distinguishing it from clinical and paraprofessional roles
- White, W. L. *Slaying the Dragon: The History of Addiction Treatment and Recovery in America*. 2nd ed. Chestnut Health Systems, 2014. - Comprehensive history including the development of peer recovery support in addiction treatment

## Legal Resources

### Case Law

- *Olmstead v. L.C.*, 527 U.S. 581 (1999). - Established the right to community-based services for people with disabilities, supporting the policy basis for community peer services as an alternative to institutionalization
- *Hargrave v. Vermont*, 340 F.3d 27 (2d Cir. 2003). - Affirmed the validity of psychiatric advance directives, relevant to peer-led crisis planning and WRAP programs

### Statutory References

- 42 U.S.C. 1396a (Medicaid State Plan requirements) - Basis for peer support Medicaid billing authorization
- 42 U.S.C. 290bb-36 (Suicide prevention programs) - Relevant to peer roles in 988 crisis response
- 42 U.S.C. 300x-1 (Community Mental Health Services Block Grant) - SAMHSA block grant funding that supports peer services
- P.L. 115-271 (SUPPORT for Patients and Communities Act, 2018) - Authorized Medicare coverage of peer support for opioid treatment
- P.L. 117-159 (Bipartisan Safer Communities Act, 2022) - $250 million for community mental health services including peer support
- P.L. 117-98 (988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline Act, 2022) - Designated 988 as national crisis line; peers included in follow-up workforce
- 38 U.S.C. 7402 - Establishes peer specialist positions within VA medical centers
