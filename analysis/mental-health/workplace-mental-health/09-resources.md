# Workplace Mental Health: Resources

This section provides a curated list of key research, reports, legal resources, and organizations focused on workplace mental health policy, occupational stress, psychosocial hazard regulation, and employee assistance programs.

---

## Academic Research

### Peer-Reviewed Studies

- Karasek, Robert A. "Job Demands, Job Decision Latitude, and Mental Strain: Implications for Job Redesign." *Administrative Science Quarterly* 24, no. 2 (1979): 285-308. - Foundational study establishing the Job Demand-Control model of workplace stress.
- Siegrist, Johannes. "Adverse Health Effects of High-Effort/Low-Reward Conditions." *Journal of Occupational Health Psychology* 1, no. 1 (1996): 27-41. - Established the Effort-Reward Imbalance model, a major framework for understanding occupational stress.
- Theorell, Tores, et al. "A Systematic Review Including Meta-Analysis of Work Environment and Depressive Symptoms." *BMC Public Health* 15, no. 738 (2015). <https://doi.org/10.1186/s12889-015-1954-4> - Meta-analysis confirming workplace psychosocial factors as causal contributors to depression.
- Fleming, William. "Employee Well-being Outcomes from Individual-Level Mental Health Interventions: Cross-Sectional Evidence from the United Kingdom." *Industrial Relations Journal* 54, no. 2 (2023): 162-182. <https://doi.org/10.1111/irj.12418> - Landmark Oxford study finding no measurable benefit from individual-level wellness interventions across 46,336 workers.
- Kivimaki, Mika, et al. "Long Working Hours and Risk of Coronary Heart Disease and Stroke: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis of Published and Unpublished Data." *The Lancet* 386, no. 10005 (2015): 1739-1746. - Demonstrated increased cardiovascular and mental health risk from long working hours.
- Tsutsumi, Akizumi, et al. "Effect of the National Stress Check Program on Mental Health Among Workers in Japan." *Journal of Occupational Health* 62, no. 1 (2020): e12157. - Evaluation of Japan's mandatory workplace stress assessment program.
- Harvey, Samuel B., et al. "Can Work Make You Mentally Ill? A Systematic Meta-Review of Work-Related Risk Factors for Common Mental Health Problems." *Occupational and Environmental Medicine* 74, no. 4 (2017): 301-310. - Comprehensive review identifying specific workplace conditions that cause mental illness.
- Hassard, Juliet, et al. "The Cost of Work-Related Stress to Society: A Systematic Review." *Journal of Occupational Health Psychology* 23, no. 1 (2018): 1-17. - Systematic review of economic costs of workplace stress across multiple countries.
- Petrie, Katherine, et al. "Prevalence of Workplace Distress in the Australian Healthcare Sector: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis." *Australian and New Zealand Journal of Psychiatry* 55, no. 12 (2021): 1168-1182. - Evidence on healthcare worker mental health preceding Australia's psychosocial hazard reforms.

### Working Papers and White Papers

- Chisholm, Dan, et al. "Scaling-up Treatment of Depression and Anxiety: A Global Return on Investment Analysis." *The Lancet Psychiatry* 3, no. 5 (2016): 415-424. - WHO-supported analysis estimating 4:1 ROI for workplace mental health interventions.
- Deloitte. "Mental Health and Employers: Refreshing the Case for Investment." Deloitte UK, 2020. <https://www2.deloitte.com/uk/en/pages/consulting/articles/mental-health-and-employers-refreshing-the-case-for-investment.html> - Business case analysis showing 5:1 average ROI for employer mental health investments.
- McKinsey Health Institute. "Addressing Employee Burnout: Are You Solving the Right Problem?" McKinsey & Company, 2022. <https://www.mckinsey.com/mhi/our-insights/addressing-employee-burnout-are-you-solving-the-right-problem> - Analysis showing toxic workplace behavior as the leading predictor of burnout, not individual factors.

## Government Reports and Data

### Federal Agencies

- National Institute of Mental Health (NIMH). "Mental Illness." Statistics page, updated 2023. <https://www.nimh.nih.gov/health/statistics/mental-illness> - Prevalence data for mental health conditions among U.S. adults.
- Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration (SAMHSA). "National Survey on Drug Use and Health (NSDUH) 2022." 2023. <https://www.samhsa.gov/data/release/2022-national-survey-drug-use-and-health-nsduh-releases> - Comprehensive survey data on mental health and substance use among working-age adults.
- Bureau of Labor Statistics. "National Compensation Survey: Employee Benefits in the United States." March 2023. <https://www.bls.gov/ncs/ebs/> - EAP access rates by employer size, industry, and worker characteristics.
- Department of Labor. "Family and Medical Leave in 2018: 25 Years After the FMLA." 2023. <https://www.dol.gov/agencies/oasp/evaluation/completedstudies/fmla2018> - Survey data on FMLA utilization, including mental health leave.
- U.S. Surgeon General. "Framework for Workplace Mental Health and Well-Being." HHS, October 2022. <https://www.hhs.gov/surgeongeneral/priorities/workplace-well-being/index.html> - Five essentials framework for voluntary employer action.
- Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. "Vital Signs: Health Worker-Perceived Working Conditions and Symptoms of Poor Mental Health." MMWR, October 2023. - Burnout prevalence data for healthcare workers pre- and post-pandemic.
- NIOSH. "Total Worker Health." Program page. <https://www.cdc.gov/niosh/twh/default.html> - NIOSH initiative integrating occupational safety with worker well-being.

### Congressional Reports

- Congressional Research Service. "The Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA): Statutory Language and Recent Issues." R45127 (2023). - Overview of ADA provisions relevant to mental health accommodations.
- Congressional Research Service. "The Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA): An Overview." R44835 (2023). - Analysis of FMLA coverage gaps, including mental health.
- Government Accountability Office. "Workplace Safety and Health: Additional Efforts Needed to Help Protect Health Care Workers from Workplace Violence." GAO-16-11 (2016). - GAO report on workplace violence prevention relevant to psychosocial hazard discussion.
- Government Accountability Office. "Mental Health: HHS Could Play a Greater Role in Helping States Address Workforce Challenges." GAO-23-105597 (2023). - Analysis of mental health workforce shortages affecting workplace programs.

### Agency Data Portals

- EEOC. Charge Statistics. <https://www.eeoc.gov/data/charge-statistics> - Annual data on discrimination charges including disability (mental health) claims.
- OSHA. Inspection Data. <https://www.osha.gov/data/inspections> - Workplace inspection data (limited psychosocial hazard data currently).
- BLS. Survey of Occupational Injuries and Illnesses (SOII). <https://www.bls.gov/iif/> - Annual workplace injury and illness data including some mental health categories.

## Books

- Maslach, Christina, and Michael P. Leiter. *The Burnout Challenge: Managing People's Relationships with Their Jobs*. Harvard University Press, 2022. - Leading burnout researchers on organizational causes and solutions.
- Edmondson, Amy C. *The Fearless Organization: Creating Psychological Safety in the Workplace for Learning, Innovation, and Growth*. Wiley, 2018. - Foundational work on psychological safety as an organizational attribute.
- Leka, Stavroula, and Aditya Jain. *Health Impact of Psychosocial Hazards at Work: An Overview*. WHO, 2010. - WHO synthesis of evidence on psychosocial hazards and health outcomes.
- Pfeffer, Jeffrey. *Dying for a Paycheck: How Modern Management Harms Employee Health and Company Performance and What We Can Do About It*. Harper Business, 2018. - Stanford professor's analysis of management practices that damage worker health.
- Murthy, Vivek H. *Together: The Healing Power of Human Connection in a Sometimes Lonely World*. Harper Wave, 2020. - U.S. Surgeon General's work on loneliness and connection, foundational to the 2022 workplace framework.
- Kelloway, E. Kevin, and Christopher Cooper. *Occupational Health and Safety for Small and Medium Sized Enterprises*. Edward Elgar, 2011. - Practical guidance on implementing occupational health programs in smaller organizations.

## Legal Resources

### Case Law

- *Chevron U.S.A. v. Natural Resources Defense Council*, 467 U.S. 837 (1984). - Established deference to agency interpretation of statutes; relevant to OSHA authority over psychosocial hazards. Note: partially overturned by *Loper Bright Enterprises v. Raimondo* (2024).
- *Loper Bright Enterprises v. Raimondo*, 603 U.S. ___ (2024). - Overturned Chevron deference; increases legal risk for OSHA psychosocial hazard standard.
- *Toyota Motor Manufacturing v. Williams*, 534 U.S. 184 (2002). - ADA disability definition case subsequently overturned by ADAAA (2008); established narrow interpretation later expanded.
- *Sutton v. United Air Lines*, 527 U.S. 471 (1999). - Pre-ADAAA case limiting ADA disability definition; overturned by ADAAA's broader approach to mental health conditions.
- *West Virginia v. EPA*, 597 U.S. 697 (2022). - Major questions doctrine case; potential basis for challenging OSHA psychosocial hazard standard as exceeding statutory authority.
- *Bragdon v. Abbott*, 524 U.S. 624 (1998). - Established broad interpretation of ADA disability for conditions affecting major life activities, applicable to mental health.

### Statutory References

- 29 U.S.C. 654 (OSH Act, General Duty Clause) - Employer obligation to provide workplace free of recognized hazards; potential authority for psychosocial hazard enforcement.
- 42 U.S.C. 12101 et seq. (Americans with Disabilities Act) - Prohibition on disability discrimination; reasonable accommodation requirements for mental health conditions.
- 29 U.S.C. 2601 et seq. (Family and Medical Leave Act) - Up to 12 weeks unpaid leave for serious health conditions including mental health.
- 29 U.S.C. 1001 et seq. (ERISA) - Governs employer-sponsored benefit plans including EAPs; potential vehicle for EAP modernization standards.
- 42 U.S.C. 18022(b) (Affordable Care Act, Essential Health Benefits) - Mental health as essential health benefit in marketplace plans.
- Pub. L. 110-343, Div. C, Title V (Mental Health Parity and Addiction Equity Act) - Insurance coverage parity requirements for mental health and substance use disorder treatment.
