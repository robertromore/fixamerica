# Inpatient Psychiatric Care: Resources

This section provides a curated list of key research, government reports, datasets, and organizations focused on psychiatric inpatient care, bed capacity, the IMD exclusion, emergency department boarding, and related policy issues.

## Books

- Torrey, E. F. *American Psychosis: How the Federal Government Destroyed the Mental Illness Treatment System*. Oxford University Press, 2014. - Documents the history of deinstitutionalization and the failure to build community alternatives. Essential reading on the IMD exclusion's origins and impact.
- Rosenberg, L. *Our Necessary Shadow: The Nature and Meaning of Psychiatry*. Simon & Schuster, 2014. - Examination of psychiatric care systems from a clinical perspective.
- Earley, P. *Crazy: A Father's Search Through America's Mental Health Madness*. Berkley Books, 2006. - Account of navigating the inpatient psychiatric system as a family member. Widely cited in congressional testimony.
- Whitaker, R. *Mad in America: Bad Science, Bad Medicine, and the Enduring Mistreatment of the Mentally Ill*. Basic Books, 2002. - Critical history of psychiatric treatment in the United States, including institutional abuses and pharmaceutical industry influence.
- Grob, G. N. *From Asylum to Community: Mental Health Policy in Modern America*. Princeton University Press, 1991. - Definitive academic history of deinstitutionalization.
- Grob, G. N., & Goldman, H. H. *The Dilemma of Federal Mental Health Policy: Radical Reform or Incremental Change?*. Rutgers University Press, 2006. - Analysis of federal mental health policymaking, including the tension between comprehensive reform and incrementalism.
- Powers, R. *No One Cares About Crazy People: The Chaos and Heartbreak of Mental Health in America*. Hachette Books, 2017. - Personal and policy account of the mental health system's failures, combining family narrative with historical analysis.
- Deutsch, A. *The Shame of the States*. Harcourt, Brace, 1948. - The landmark expose of state psychiatric hospital conditions that catalyzed mid-20th century reform. Out of print but available in many academic libraries; historically essential.
- Goffman, E. *Asylums: Essays on the Social Situation of Mental Patients and Other Inmates*. Anchor Books, 1961. - Foundational sociological analysis of institutional life, influential in both academic and advocacy circles. Introduced the concept of the "total institution."
- Sisti, D. A. *Psychiatry in Crisis: At the Crossroads of Social Medicine, Managed Care, and Neuroscience*. Johns Hopkins University Press, 2020. - Analysis of the contemporary inpatient care crisis from a bioethics perspective.
- Jaffe, D. J. *Insane Consequences: How the Mental Health Industry Fails the Mentally Ill*. Prometheus Books, 2017. - Advocacy-oriented analysis of the mental health system's failures, with specific focus on the need for inpatient capacity and treatment mandates.

## Legal Resources

### Case Law

- *O'Connor v. Donaldson*, 422 U.S. 563 (1975). - Established that a state cannot constitutionally confine a non-dangerous individual who is capable of surviving safely in the community, setting the foundation for modern civil commitment standards.
- *Olmstead v. L.C.*, 527 U.S. 581 (1999). - Held that unjustified institutionalization of persons with mental disabilities is a form of discrimination under the ADA; established the right to community-based treatment when appropriate.
- *Addington v. Texas*, 441 U.S. 418 (1979). - Established the "clear and convincing evidence" standard for civil commitment proceedings.
- *Zinermon v. Burch*, 494 U.S. 113 (1990). - Addressed procedural due process concerns in voluntary psychiatric admissions when patients may lack capacity to consent.
- *Youngberg v. Romeo*, 457 U.S. 307 (1982). - Established the right of involuntarily committed persons to reasonably safe conditions of confinement, freedom from unreasonable bodily restraints, and minimally adequate training.
- *Buck v. Bell*, 274 U.S. 200 (1927). - Upheld compulsory sterilization of institutionalized psychiatric patients; never formally overruled; represents the darkest chapter of institutional psychiatric care.
- *Wyatt v. Stickney*, 325 F. Supp. 781 (M.D. Ala. 1971). - Established the right to treatment for involuntarily committed psychiatric patients and set minimum standards for institutional conditions, staffing, and individualized treatment plans.
- *Jackson v. Indiana*, 406 U.S. 715 (1972). - Held that an incompetent criminal defendant cannot be held in a psychiatric institution indefinitely solely on the basis of incompetence to stand trial.
- *Lessard v. Schmidt*, 349 F. Supp. 1078 (E.D. Wis. 1972). - Landmark federal district court decision requiring procedural due process protections in civil commitment proceedings, including notice, right to counsel, and the beyond-a-reasonable-doubt standard (later modified by *Addington*).

### Statutory References

- 42 U.S.C. 1396d(a)(B) - The Medicaid IMD exclusion. Defines "institution for mental diseases" and excludes coverage for adults ages 21-64 in facilities with more than 16 beds.
- 42 U.S.C. 1396d(i) - Definition of "institution for mental diseases" as a hospital, nursing facility, or other institution of more than 16 beds primarily engaged in providing diagnosis, treatment, or care of persons with mental diseases.
- 29 U.S.C. 1185a - Mental Health Parity and Addiction Equity Act (MHPAEA), requiring parity between mental health/substance use benefits and medical/surgical benefits.
- 42 U.S.C. 290bb-36 - Emergency Mental Health Centers grant program (Section 520E-3 of the Public Health Service Act).
- Public Law 114-255, Title XIII - The 21st Century Cures Act (2016), including mental health provisions such as the Assistant Secretary for Mental Health and Substance Use.
- Public Law 117-328, Division FF - The Bipartisan Safer Communities Act (2022) and associated mental health funding provisions.
- 42 U.S.C. 1395d(b)(3) - Medicare 190-day lifetime limit on inpatient psychiatric hospital care.
- 42 U.S.C. 10801 et seq. - Protection and Advocacy for Individuals with Mental Illness Act (PAIMI), authorizing state P&A systems to investigate conditions in psychiatric facilities.
