# Inpatient Psychiatric Care: Resources

This section provides a curated list of key research, government reports, datasets, and organizations focused on psychiatric inpatient care, bed capacity, the IMD exclusion, emergency department boarding, and related policy issues.

## Government Reports and Data

### Federal Agencies

- Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration (SAMHSA). "National Mental Health Services Survey (N-MHSS)." Annual. <https://www.samhsa.gov/data/data-we-collect/n-mhss-national-mental-health-services-survey> - Comprehensive data on mental health facilities, beds, and services by state.
- Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS). "Section 1115 SMI/SED Demonstration Waivers." Updated regularly. <https://www.medicaid.gov/medicaid/section-1115-demonstrations/1115-substance-use-disorder-demonstrations/section-1115-demonstrations-substance-use-disorders-serious-mental-illness-and-serious-emotional-disturbance/index.html> - Status and evaluation reports for state IMD waivers.
- Agency for Healthcare Research and Quality (AHRQ). "Healthcare Cost and Utilization Project (HCUP) Statistical Briefs: Mental Health." Various dates. <https://hcup-us.ahrq.gov/reports/statbriefs/sb_mh.jsp> - Data on psychiatric inpatient utilization, length of stay, and costs.
- Bureau of Justice Statistics. "Mental Health Problems of Prison and Jail Inmates." Updated 2023. <https://bjs.ojp.gov/library/publications/mental-health-problems-prison-and-jail-inmates> - Data on mental illness prevalence among incarcerated populations.
- Department of Veterans Affairs. "VA Mental Health Care Fact Sheet." Updated annually. <https://www.mentalhealth.va.gov/> - VA psychiatric bed capacity and utilization data.

### Congressional Reports

- Congressional Research Service. "The Medicaid IMD Exclusion and Mental Health Care." Report R46646. 2023. <https://crsreports.congress.gov> - Comprehensive analysis of the IMD exclusion, its history, and reform options.
- Congressional Research Service. "Mental Health Parity and Addiction Equity Act (MHPAEA): A Guide to Its Implementation." Report R45154. 2023. <https://crsreports.congress.gov> - Analysis of parity law compliance and enforcement.
- Government Accountability Office. "Behavioral Health: Patient Access, Provider Claims, and the Effect of the COVID-19 Pandemic." GAO-21-437. 2021. <https://www.gao.gov/products/gao-21-437>
- Government Accountability Office. "Mental Health: HHS Could Play a Larger Role in Helping States Address Housing Shortages for People with Mental Illness." GAO-19-202. 2019. <https://www.gao.gov/products/gao-19-202>

### Agency Data Portals

- CMS Hospital Compare. Psychiatric Hospital Quality Measures. <https://www.medicare.gov/care-compare> - Public data on psychiatric hospital quality metrics including readmission rates, follow-up after hospitalization, and patient experience.
- SAMHSA Behavioral Health Treatment Services Locator. <https://findtreatment.samhsa.gov/> - Facility-level data on mental health treatment providers.
- HRSA Health Workforce Data. <https://data.hrsa.gov/topics/health-workforce> - Data on psychiatric workforce supply, distribution, and shortage areas.

## Legal Resources

### Case Law

- *O'Connor v. Donaldson*, 422 U.S. 563 (1975). - Established that a state cannot constitutionally confine a non-dangerous individual who is capable of surviving safely in the community, setting the foundation for modern civil commitment standards.
- *Olmstead v. L.C.*, 527 U.S. 581 (1999). - Held that unjustified institutionalization of persons with mental disabilities is a form of discrimination under the ADA; established the right to community-based treatment when appropriate.
- *Addington v. Texas*, 441 U.S. 418 (1979). - Established the "clear and convincing evidence" standard for civil commitment proceedings.
- *Zinermon v. Burch*, 494 U.S. 113 (1990). - Addressed procedural due process concerns in voluntary psychiatric admissions when patients may lack capacity to consent.
- *Youngberg v. Romeo*, 457 U.S. 307 (1982). - Established the right of involuntarily committed persons to reasonably safe conditions of confinement, freedom from unreasonable bodily restraints, and minimally adequate training.

### Statutory References

- 42 U.S.C. 1396d(a)(B) - The Medicaid IMD exclusion. Defines "institution for mental diseases" and excludes coverage for adults ages 21-64 in facilities with more than 16 beds.
- 42 U.S.C. 1396d(i) - Definition of "institution for mental diseases" as a hospital, nursing facility, or other institution of more than 16 beds primarily engaged in providing diagnosis, treatment, or care of persons with mental diseases.
- 29 U.S.C. 1185a - Mental Health Parity and Addiction Equity Act (MHPAEA), requiring parity between mental health/substance use benefits and medical/surgical benefits.
- 42 U.S.C. 290bb-36 - Emergency Mental Health Centers grant program (Section 520E-3 of the Public Health Service Act).
- Public Law 114-255, Title XIII - The 21st Century Cures Act (2016), including mental health provisions such as the Assistant Secretary for Mental Health and Substance Use.
- Public Law 117-328, Division FF - The Bipartisan Safer Communities Act (2022) and associated mental health funding provisions.
