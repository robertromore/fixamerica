# Mental Health Insurance Parity: Resources

This section provides a curated list of key research, government reports, legal references, and advocacy resources on mental health insurance parity law, enforcement, and reform.

---

## Academic Research

### Peer-Reviewed Studies

- Barry, C.L., & Huskamp, H.A. "Moving Beyond Parity -- Mental Health and Addiction Care Under the ACA." *New England Journal of Medicine* 365, no. 11 (2011): 973-975. <https://doi.org/10.1056/NEJMp1108649>
- Bishop, T.F., Press, M.J., Keyhani, S., & Pincus, H.A. "Acceptance of Insurance by Psychiatrists and the Implications for Access to Mental Health Care." *JAMA Psychiatry* 71, no. 2 (2014): 176-181. <https://doi.org/10.1001/jamapsychiatry.2013.2862>
- Bishop, T.F., et al. "Psychiatrist Acceptance of New Patients by Insurance Type." *JAMA* 330, no. 12 (2023): 1168-1170. - Updated analysis finding 38% psychiatrist acceptance rate for commercially insured patients.
- Busch, S.H., & Barry, C.L. "Mental Health Disorders in Childhood: Assessing the Burden on Families." *Health Affairs* 26, no. 4 (2007): 1088-1095.
- Ettner, S.L., Frank, R.G., & Kessler, R.C. "The Impact of Psychiatric Disorders on Labor Market Outcomes." *Industrial and Labor Relations Review* 51, no. 1 (1997): 64-81.
- Goldman, H.H., Frank, R.G., Burnam, M.A., et al. "Behavioral Health Insurance Parity for Federal Employees." *New England Journal of Medicine* 354, no. 13 (2006): 1378-1386. - Early evidence that parity increases utilization without disproportionate cost increases.
- Huskamp, H.A., et al. "The Effect of Parity on Mental Health and Substance Abuse Benefits in Employer-Sponsored Plans." *Journal of Health Economics* 27, no. 5 (2008): 1332-1340.
- Mark, T.L., et al. "Changes in US Spending on Mental Health and Substance Use Disorders, 1986-2014, and Returns on Investment." *Health Affairs* 35, no. 6 (2016): 1058-1066.
- Mark, T.L., et al. "Increasing Medicaid Payment Rates for Psychiatrists and Impact on Access." *Psychiatric Services* 69, no. 10 (2018): 1097-1100. - Found that a 10% reimbursement rate increase was associated with a 4.5% increase in psychiatrist Medicaid participation.
- McGinty, E.E., et al. "Improving the Use of Evidence-Based Mental Health Practices." *Health Affairs* 34, no. 3 (2015): 456-462.

### Working Papers and White Papers

- Melek, S., Norris, D., & Paulus, J. "Addiction and Mental Health vs. Physical Health: Widening Disparities in Network Use and Provider Reimbursement." Milliman Research Report. 2019. <https://www.milliman.com/en/insight/addiction-and-mental-health-vs-physical-health-widening-disparities>
- Melek, S., Norris, D., Paulus, J., Matthews, K., & Weaver, A. "Milliman Parity Benchmarking Report." Milliman Research Report. 2023. - Updated analysis of denial rate, out-of-network, and reimbursement disparities.
- Melek, S., et al. "Mental Health and Substance Use Disorder Parity: A Comparison of Commercial Insurance Reimbursement Rates." Milliman. 2024. - Documented MH/SUD reimbursement at 77-83% of Medicare rates vs. 105-130% for medical/surgical.
- The Kennedy Forum. "Don't Deny Me: NQTL Parity Compliance Guide." 2023. <https://www.thekennedyforum.org/dont-deny-me>
- The Kennedy Forum. "State Parity Enforcement: Best Practices and Recommendations." 2023.
- National Safety Council. "Mental Health Cost Calculator: The Cost of Depression in the Workplace." 2023. <https://www.nsc.org/mental-health-employer> - Estimated $47.6 billion annual cost of depression to employers.

## Legal Resources

### Case Law

- *Wit v. United Behavioral Health*, 2020 WL 5422137 (N.D. Cal. 2020) - Landmark ruling that UBH used internal guidelines more restrictive than generally accepted standards of care to deny MH/SUD claims, violating ERISA fiduciary duties. Affirmed in part by Ninth Circuit (2023).
- *New York State Psychiatric Association v. UnitedHealth Group*, 798 F.3d 125 (2d Cir. 2015) - Established that out-of-network reimbursement rates for MH/SUD providers could constitute a parity violation.
- *Harlick v. Blue Shield of California*, 686 F.3d 699 (9th Cir. 2012) - Held that MHPAEA applies to state-mandated benefits, expanding the scope of parity protections.
- *Joseph F. v. Sinclair Services Co.*, 158 F. Supp. 3d 1239 (D. Utah 2016) - Found that a plan's more restrictive concurrent review process for MH/SUD inpatient care violated MHPAEA.
- *David P. v. United Healthcare Insurance Co.*, 2019 WL 1429520 (D. Utah 2019) - Applied the NQTL parity standard to prior authorization denial of residential MH treatment.
- *Vorpahl v. Harvard Pilgrim Health Care*, 2020 WL 7388620 (D. Mass. 2020) - Found that applying more stringent medical necessity criteria to MH/SUD residential treatment violated MHPAEA.

### Statutory References

- 29 U.S.C. section 1185a - Mental Health Parity and Addiction Equity Act (MHPAEA), as codified in ERISA
- 42 U.S.C. section 300gg-26 - MHPAEA requirements for public health service act plans
- 26 U.S.C. section 9812 - MHPAEA requirements under the Internal Revenue Code
- 42 U.S.C. section 18022(b)(1)(E) - ACA essential health benefits requirement for MH/SUD coverage
- 42 U.S.C. section 1812(b)(3) - Medicare 190-day lifetime limit on psychiatric hospital stays
- Pub. L. 116-260, section 203 - Consolidated Appropriations Act of 2021, NQTL comparative analysis requirement
- 89 Fed. Reg. 77586 (Sept. 9, 2024) - 2024 MHPAEA Final Rule
