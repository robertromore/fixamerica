# Older Adult Mental Health: Resources

This section provides a curated list of key research, reports, data sources, and organizations relevant to older adult mental health policy in the United States. Resources are organized by type and annotated with relevance notes to guide researchers, advocates, and policymakers.

---

## Academic Research

### Peer-Reviewed Studies

- Unutzer, J., Katon, W., Callahan, C.M., et al. "Collaborative Care Management of Late-Life Depression in the Primary Care Setting: A Randomized Controlled Trial." *JAMA* 288, no. 22 (2002): 2836-2845. - Landmark IMPACT trial demonstrating that collaborative care doubles depression treatment effectiveness in older adults. Enrolled 1,801 patients across 18 clinics. Foundation of CoCM billing codes.
- Unutzer, J., Katon, W.J., Fan, M.Y., et al. "Long-Term Cost Effects of Collaborative Care for Late-Life Depression." *American Journal of Managed Care* 14, no. 2 (2008): 95-100. - IMPACT cost-effectiveness analysis showing $3,363 per patient savings over four years through reduced medical utilization. Critical evidence for fiscal arguments supporting collaborative care investment.
- Bruce, M.L., Ten Have, T.R., Reynolds, C.F., et al. "Reducing Suicidal Ideation and Depressive Symptoms in Depressed Older Primary Care Patients: A Randomized Controlled Trial." *JAMA* 291, no. 9 (2004): 1081-1091. - PROSPECT study showing collaborative care reduces suicidal ideation by 2.2x in older primary care patients. Key evidence for suicide prevention in primary care settings.
- Holt-Lunstad, J., Smith, T.B., Layton, J.B. "Social Relationships and Mortality Risk: A Meta-Analytic Review." *PLoS Medicine* 7, no. 7 (2010): e1000316. - Established that social isolation carries mortality risk comparable to smoking 15 cigarettes per day. Based on meta-analysis of 148 studies with 308,849 participants. Foundational citation for social isolation policy.
- Ciechanowski, P., Wagner, E., Schmaling, K., et al. "Community-Integrated Home-Based Depression Treatment in Older Adults: A Randomized Controlled Trial." *JAMA* 291, no. 13 (2004): 1569-1577. - PEARLS program demonstrating effective home-based depression treatment for isolated older adults using problem-solving therapy and behavioral activation.
- Belle, S.H., Burgio, L., Burns, R., et al. "Enhancing the Quality of Life of Dementia Caregivers from Different Ethnic or Racial Groups: A Randomized Controlled Trial." *Annals of Internal Medicine* 145, no. 10 (2006): 727-738. - Senior REACH trial showing structured caregiver interventions reduce depression by 30%. Multiethnic sample demonstrates cultural adaptability.
- Teri, L., Huda, P., Gibbons, L., Young, H., van Leynseele, J. "STAR: A Dementia-Specific Training Program for Staff in Assisted Living Residences." *The Gerontologist* 45, no. 5 (2005): 686-693. - Demonstrated 30-40% reduction in behavioral symptoms of dementia through staff training without medication. Basis for non-pharmacological behavioral management approaches.
- Hubley, S., Lynch, S.B., Schneck, C., Thomas, M., Shore, J. "Review of Key Telepsychiatry Outcomes." *World Journal of Psychiatry* 6, no. 2 (2016): 269-282. - Evidence that telepsychiatry produces outcomes equivalent to in-person care for depression and anxiety. Key citation for telehealth permanence advocacy.
- Wolitzky-Taylor, K.B., Castriotta, N., Lenze, E.J., Stanley, M.A., Craske, M.G. "Anxiety Disorders in Older Adults: A Comprehensive Review." *Depression and Anxiety* 27, no. 2 (2010): 190-211. - Comprehensive review of anxiety disorder prevalence and treatment in older adults. Documents underdetection and treatment gaps.
- Burnette, C., Ramchand, R., Ayer, L. "Gatekeeper Training for Suicide Prevention: A Theoretical Model and Review of the Empirical Literature." *RAND Health Quarterly* 5, no. 1 (2015). - Review of gatekeeper training effectiveness for suicide prevention, including older adult populations. Supports the QPR model and community-based prevention.
- Szanton, S.L., Xue, Q.L., Leff, B., et al. "Effect of a Biobehavioral Environmental Approach on Disability Among Low-Income Older Adults: A Randomized Clinical Trial." *JAMA Internal Medicine* 179, no. 2 (2019): 204-211. - CAPABLE trial showing $10,000 per participant Medicare savings through home-based environmental and behavioral intervention. Demonstrates functional improvement reduces depression.
- Gupta, A., Howell, S.T., Yannelis, C., Gupta, A. "Does Private Equity Investment in Healthcare Benefit Patients? Evidence from Nursing Homes." *NBER Working Paper* 28474 (2021). - Documents higher mortality, lower staffing, and more quality deficiencies in PE-owned nursing homes. Critical evidence for ownership-structure reform arguments.
- Ashraf, S., Zayas, C.E., Enguidanos, S. "Nursing Home Staff Turnover and Its Association with Quality." *Health Affairs* 42, no. 4 (2023): 578-586. - Documents 52% average annual CNA turnover and its negative effects on care quality and resident outcomes.
- Luoma, J.B., Martin, C.E., Pearson, J.L. "Contact with Mental Health and Primary Care Providers Before Suicide: A Review of the Evidence." *American Journal of Psychiatry* 159, no. 6 (2002): 909-916. - Established that only 20% of older adults who die by suicide had mental health contact in the prior 30 days. Key evidence for primary care-based suicide prevention.
- Shear, M.K., Reynolds, C.F., Simon, N.M., et al. "Prolonged Grief Disorder and the DSM-5-TR." *World Psychiatry* 21, no. 1 (2022): 3-4. - Discussion of prolonged grief disorder's addition to DSM-5-TR and its implications for older adult bereavement care.

### Working Papers and White Papers

- Bartels, S.J., Naslund, J.A. "The Underside of the Silver Tsunami: Older Adults and Mental Health Care." *New England Journal of Medicine* 368, no. 6 (2013): 493-496. - Influential commentary on the gap between older adult mental health needs and available services.
- Jeste, D.V., Blazer, D.G., First, M. "Aging-Related Diagnostic Variations: Need for Diagnostic Criteria Appropriate for Elderly Psychiatric Patients." *Biological Psychiatry* 58, no. 4 (2005): 265-271. - Discussion of diagnostic challenges specific to older adult mental health, including atypical depression presentation.
- Kessler, R.C., Berglund, P., Demler, O., et al. "Lifetime Prevalence and Age-of-Onset Distributions of DSM-IV Disorders in the National Comorbidity Survey Replication." *Archives of General Psychiatry* 62, no. 6 (2005): 593-602. - Foundational epidemiological data on mental health conditions across the lifespan.
- Cacioppo, J.T., Hawkley, L.C., Norman, G.J., Berntson, G.G. "Social Isolation." *Annals of the New York Academy of Sciences* 1231 (2011): 17-22. - Explains biological mechanisms linking social isolation to health outcomes including HPA axis activation and inflammatory cascades.
- Meeks, T.W., Vahia, I.V., Lavretsky, H., Kulkarni, G., Jeste, D.V. "A Tune in A Minor Can Be B Major: A Review of Epidemiology, Illness Course, and Public Health Implications of Subthreshold Depression in Older Adults." *Journal of Affective Disorders* 129, nos. 1-3 (2011): 126-142. - Comprehensive review of subsyndromal depression in older adults, documenting its prevalence and functional impact.

## Government Reports and Data

### Federal Agencies

- Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration (SAMHSA). "2023 National Survey on Drug Use and Health." 2024. <https://www.samhsa.gov/data/nsduh> - Annual survey data on mental health conditions and substance use among adults aged 65+. Primary data source for prevalence estimates.
- Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC). "Web-based Injury Statistics Query and Reporting System (WISQARS)." Updated 2024. <https://www.cdc.gov/injury/wisqars/> - Suicide data by age, sex, race, and method, including older adult breakdowns. Essential for tracking suicide rate trends.
- Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS). "Medicare Mental Health Services Utilization Data." Annual. <https://data.cms.gov/> - Data on Medicare mental health claims, provider participation, and spending. Key for tracking service access and utilization trends.
- Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS). "Nursing Home Compare Data." Updated quarterly. <https://data.cms.gov/provider-data/> - Facility-level data on antipsychotic use, staffing, and quality measures. Essential for monitoring nursing home mental health care quality.
- Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS). "National Partnership to Improve Dementia Care Data." Updated quarterly. <https://www.cms.gov/medicare/health-safety-standards/quality-safety-oversight-general-information/nursing-home-quality-initiatives> - Tracks antipsychotic reduction progress at national, state, and facility levels.
- Administration for Community Living (ACL). "Older Americans Act Program Performance Reports." Annual. <https://acl.gov/programs/performance> - Data on OAA program participation, including senior center attendance and nutrition program reach.
- Health Resources and Services Administration (HRSA). "Health Professional Shortage Areas." Updated regularly. <https://data.hrsa.gov/topics/health-workforce/shortage-areas> - Geographic data on mental health professional shortage areas affecting older adults.
- National Institute of Mental Health (NIMH). "Mental Health Information: Older Adults." <https://www.nimh.nih.gov/health/topics/older-adults-and-mental-health> - Research summaries and statistics on mental health in aging.
- U.S. Surgeon General. "Our Epidemic of Loneliness and Isolation: The U.S. Surgeon General's Advisory on the Healing Effects of Social Connection and Community." 2023. <https://www.hhs.gov/surgeongeneral/priorities/connection/index.html> - Landmark advisory declaring loneliness and isolation a public health epidemic with specific recommendations for older adults.
- HHS Office of Inspector General. "Some Medicare Advantage Organization Denials of Prior Authorization Requests Raise Concerns About Beneficiary Access to Medically Necessary Care." OEI-09-18-00260. 2022. - Documents 13% inappropriate denial rate for MA prior authorizations, including behavioral health services.

### Congressional Reports

- Congressional Research Service. "Mental Health and Medicare: Coverage and Policy Issues." Report R47259. 2023. - Analysis of Medicare mental health benefit structure, gaps, and legislative options. Useful for understanding the specific statutory provisions that create barriers.
- Congressional Research Service. "The Older Americans Act: Overview and Funding." Report R43414. Updated 2024. - Comprehensive overview of OAA programs, funding history, and reauthorization issues. Includes per-capita funding trends.
- Government Accountability Office. "Behavioral Health: Available Workforce Information and Federal Actions to Help Recruit and Retain Providers." GAO-23-105250. 2023. - Analysis of behavioral health workforce shortages, including geriatric specialties. Documents geographic maldistribution.
- Government Accountability Office. "Nursing Homes: CMS Should Do More to Ensure That Residents' Mental Health Needs Are Met." GAO-22-104053. 2022. - Investigation of nursing home mental health care quality and federal oversight gaps. Includes recommendations for CMS action.
- Government Accountability Office. "PASARR: Screening and Assessment of Nursing Home Applicants with Mental Illness." GAO-22-106001. 2022. - Examination of PASARR implementation failures and inappropriate nursing home placement of individuals with serious mental illness.
- Medicare Payment Advisory Commission (MedPAC). "Report to Congress: Medicare Payment Policy." Annual, most recent March 2024. - Includes recommendations on psychiatric hospital 190-day limit, Medicare Advantage network adequacy, and reimbursement policy. MedPAC's technical analysis carries significant weight with Congress.

### Agency Data Portals

- CMS. Medicare Provider Utilization and Payment Data. <https://data.cms.gov/> - Provider-level Medicare claims data, including mental health service utilization and reimbursement.
- CDC. National Center for Health Statistics (NCHS). <https://www.cdc.gov/nchs/> - National health survey data including mental health condition prevalence by age group.
- AHRQ. Medical Expenditure Panel Survey (MEPS). <https://meps.ahrq.gov/> - Data on healthcare utilization, expenditures, and insurance coverage, including psychotropic medication use and polypharmacy rates.
- Census Bureau. American Community Survey. <https://www.census.gov/programs-surveys/acs> - Demographic data on older adult population, living arrangements, disability status, and geographic distribution.

## Books

- Blazer, D.G. *The Age of Melancholy: Major Depression and Its Social Origins*. Routledge, 2005. - Analysis of social and environmental factors contributing to late-life depression. Accessible overview for non-clinical audiences.
- Grob, G.N. *From Asylum to Community: Mental Health Policy in Modern America*. Princeton University Press, 1991. - Historical account of deinstitutionalization and its impact on older adults. Essential background for understanding why the current system fails.
- National Academies of Sciences, Engineering, and Medicine. *Social Isolation and Loneliness in Older Adults: Opportunities for the Health Care System*. National Academies Press, 2020. - Landmark consensus report documenting health consequences of social isolation and policy responses. Most frequently cited source in this analysis.
- Bartels, S.J., Pratt, S.I. *Integrated Care for Older Adults: A Practical Guide*. Springer, 2016. - Practical guide to implementing integrated mental health and primary care for older populations. Useful for practitioners implementing CoCM models.
- Institute of Medicine. *The Mental Health and Substance Use Workforce for Older Adults: In Whose Hands?*. National Academies Press, 2012. - Comprehensive analysis of the geriatric mental health workforce crisis and policy recommendations. Foundational document for workforce reform proposals.
- Holt-Lunstad, J., ed. *Social Connections and Health*. Oxford University Press, 2022. - Collection of research on social relationships, loneliness, and health outcomes across the lifespan. Includes biological mechanism evidence.
- Conwell, Y., Van Orden, K., Caine, E.D. *Suicide in Older Adults*. Psychiatric Clinics of North America series, 2011. - Clinical and epidemiological overview of older adult suicide with prevention strategies. Standard reference for clinicians and researchers.
- Unutzer, J., Park, M. "Older Adults with Severe, Treatment-Resistant Depression." *JAMA* 308, no. 9 (2012): 909-918. - Clinical guide to managing treatment-resistant depression in older adults, addressing when and how to escalate care.
- Cohen, G.D. *The Creative Age: Awakening Human Potential in the Second Half of Life*. Harper, 2000. - Pioneering work challenging ageist assumptions about cognitive and emotional capacity in later life. Important for reframing cultural attitudes.
- Murthy, V. *Together: The Healing Power of Human Connection in a Sometimes Lonely World*. Harper Wave, 2020. - Surgeon General's exploration of loneliness as a public health crisis. Provides narrative context for policy advocacy.

## Legal Resources

### Case Law

- *Olmstead v. L.C.*, 527 U.S. 581 (1999). - Established that unjustified institutional segregation of persons with disabilities constitutes discrimination under the ADA; foundational for community-based mental health services for older adults. States' obligations under Olmstead include providing community-based alternatives to institutional mental health care.
- *Medicare and Medicaid Patient and Program Protection Act cases*. - Framework for enforcement of nursing home quality standards, including mental health care requirements.
- *Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services v. [various nursing home facilities]*. - Enforcement actions related to OBRA 1987 requirements for psychotropic medication use, PASARR compliance, and resident rights.

### Statutory References

- 42 U.S.C. 1395d - Medicare inpatient psychiatric benefit, including the 190-day lifetime limit for freestanding psychiatric hospitals. The specific provision targeted for repeal by the Medicare Geriatric Mental Health Parity and Access Act.
- 42 U.S.C. 1396r (Nursing Home Reform Act, OBRA 1987) - Requirements for nursing home care quality, including pre-admission screening (PASARR), psychotropic medication restrictions, and resident rights. The statutory foundation for CMS nursing home quality oversight.
- 42 U.S.C. 3001 et seq. (Older Americans Act) - Federal framework for community-based aging services, including senior centers, nutrition programs, and supportive services. Up for reauthorization with proposed Social Connection title.
- Pub. L. 110-343, Div. C, Title V (Mental Health Parity and Addiction Equity Act, 2008) - Established mental health parity requirements for insurance; indirect impact on Medicare through policy norm-setting.
- Pub. L. 110-275 (Medicare Improvements for Patients and Providers Act, MIPPA, 2008) - Phased in Medicare mental health payment parity (reducing outpatient coinsurance from 50% to 20% by 2014).
- Pub. L. 117-159 (Bipartisan Safer Communities Act, 2022) - Authorized Medicare coverage for marriage and family therapists and licensed mental health counselors, effective January 2024.
- Pub. L. 116-260 (Consolidated Appropriations Act, 2021) - Included extensions of telehealth flexibilities and mental health provisions during the COVID-19 emergency.
