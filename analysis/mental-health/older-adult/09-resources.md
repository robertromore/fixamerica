# Older Adult Mental Health: Resources

This section provides a curated list of key research, reports, data sources, and organizations relevant to older adult mental health policy in the United States.

---

## Academic Research

### Peer-Reviewed Studies

- Unutzer, J., Katon, W., Callahan, C.M., et al. "Collaborative Care Management of Late-Life Depression in the Primary Care Setting: A Randomized Controlled Trial." *JAMA* 288, no. 22 (2002): 2836-2845. - Landmark IMPACT trial demonstrating that collaborative care doubles depression treatment effectiveness in older adults.
- Bruce, M.L., Ten Have, T.R., Reynolds, C.F., et al. "Reducing Suicidal Ideation and Depressive Symptoms in Depressed Older Primary Care Patients: A Randomized Controlled Trial." *JAMA* 291, no. 9 (2004): 1081-1091. - PROSPECT study showing collaborative care reduces suicidal ideation by 2.2x in older primary care patients.
- Holt-Lunstad, J., Smith, T.B., Layton, J.B. "Social Relationships and Mortality Risk: A Meta-Analytic Review." *PLoS Medicine* 7, no. 7 (2010): e1000316. - Established that social isolation carries mortality risk comparable to smoking 15 cigarettes per day.
- Ciechanowski, P., Wagner, E., Schmaling, K., et al. "Community-Integrated Home-Based Depression Treatment in Older Adults: A Randomized Controlled Trial." *JAMA* 291, no. 13 (2004): 1569-1577. - PEARLS program demonstrating effective home-based depression treatment for isolated older adults.
- Belle, S.H., Burgio, L., Burns, R., et al. "Enhancing the Quality of Life of Dementia Caregivers from Different Ethnic or Racial Groups: A Randomized Controlled Trial." *Annals of Internal Medicine* 145, no. 10 (2006): 727-738. - Senior REACH trial showing structured caregiver interventions reduce depression by 30%.
- Teri, L., Huda, P., Gibbons, L., Young, H., van Leynseele, J. "STAR: A Dementia-Specific Training Program for Staff in Assisted Living Residences." *The Gerontologist* 45, no. 5 (2005): 686-693. - Demonstrated 30-40% reduction in behavioral symptoms of dementia through staff training without medication.
- Hubley, S., Lynch, S.B., Schneck, C., Thomas, M., Shore, J. "Review of Key Telepsychiatry Outcomes." *World Journal of Psychiatry* 6, no. 2 (2016): 269-282. - Evidence that telepsychiatry produces outcomes equivalent to in-person care for depression and anxiety.
- Wolitzky-Taylor, K.B., Castriotta, N., Lenze, E.J., Stanley, M.A., Craske, M.G. "Anxiety Disorders in Older Adults: A Comprehensive Review." *Depression and Anxiety* 27, no. 2 (2010): 190-211. - Comprehensive review of anxiety disorder prevalence and treatment in older adults.
- Burnette, C., Ramchand, R., Ayer, L. "Gatekeeper Training for Suicide Prevention: A Theoretical Model and Review of the Empirical Literature." *RAND Health Quarterly* 5, no. 1 (2015). - Review of gatekeeper training effectiveness for suicide prevention, including older adult populations.

### Working Papers and White Papers

- Bartels, S.J., Naslund, J.A. "The Underside of the Silver Tsunami: Older Adults and Mental Health Care." *New England Journal of Medicine* 368, no. 6 (2013): 493-496. - Influential commentary on the gap between older adult mental health needs and available services.
- Jeste, D.V., Blazer, D.G., First, M. "Aging-Related Diagnostic Variations: Need for Diagnostic Criteria Appropriate for Elderly Psychiatric Patients." *Biological Psychiatry* 58, no. 4 (2005): 265-271. - Discussion of diagnostic challenges specific to older adult mental health.
- Kessler, R.C., Berglund, P., Demler, O., et al. "Lifetime Prevalence and Age-of-Onset Distributions of DSM-IV Disorders in the National Comorbidity Survey Replication." *Archives of General Psychiatry* 62, no. 6 (2005): 593-602. - Foundational epidemiological data on mental health conditions across the lifespan.

## Government Reports and Data

### Federal Agencies

- Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration (SAMHSA). "2023 National Survey on Drug Use and Health." 2024. <https://www.samhsa.gov/data/nsduh> - Annual survey data on mental health conditions and substance use among adults aged 65+.
- Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC). "Web-based Injury Statistics Query and Reporting System (WISQARS)." Updated 2024. <https://www.cdc.gov/injury/wisqars/> - Suicide data by age, sex, race, and method, including older adult breakdowns.
- Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS). "Medicare Mental Health Services Utilization Data." Annual. <https://data.cms.gov/> - Data on Medicare mental health claims, provider participation, and spending.
- Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS). "Nursing Home Compare Data." Updated quarterly. <https://data.cms.gov/provider-data/> - Facility-level data on antipsychotic use, staffing, and quality measures.
- Administration for Community Living (ACL). "Older Americans Act Program Performance Reports." Annual. <https://acl.gov/programs/performance> - Data on OAA program participation, including senior center attendance and nutrition program reach.
- Health Resources and Services Administration (HRSA). "Health Professional Shortage Areas." Updated regularly. <https://data.hrsa.gov/topics/health-workforce/shortage-areas> - Geographic data on mental health professional shortage areas affecting older adults.
- National Institute of Mental Health (NIMH). "Mental Health Information: Older Adults." <https://www.nimh.nih.gov/health/topics/older-adults-and-mental-health> - Research summaries and statistics on mental health in aging.

### Congressional Reports

- Congressional Research Service. "Mental Health and Medicare: Coverage and Policy Issues." Report R47259. 2023. - Analysis of Medicare mental health benefit structure, gaps, and legislative options.
- Congressional Research Service. "The Older Americans Act: Overview and Funding." Report R43414. Updated 2024. - Comprehensive overview of OAA programs, funding history, and reauthorization issues.
- Government Accountability Office. "Behavioral Health: Available Workforce Information and Federal Actions to Help Recruit and Retain Providers." GAO-23-105250. 2023. - Analysis of behavioral health workforce shortages, including geriatric specialties.
- Government Accountability Office. "Nursing Homes: CMS Should Do More to Ensure That Residents' Mental Health Needs Are Met." GAO-22-104053. 2022. - Investigation of nursing home mental health care quality and federal oversight gaps.
- Medicare Payment Advisory Commission (MedPAC). "Report to Congress: Medicare Payment Policy." Annual, most recent March 2024. - Includes recommendations on psychiatric hospital 190-day limit, Medicare Advantage network adequacy, and reimbursement policy.

### Agency Data Portals

- CMS. Medicare Provider Utilization and Payment Data. <https://data.cms.gov/> - Provider-level Medicare claims data, including mental health service utilization and reimbursement.
- CDC. National Center for Health Statistics (NCHS). <https://www.cdc.gov/nchs/> - National health survey data including mental health condition prevalence by age group.
- AHRQ. Medical Expenditure Panel Survey (MEPS). <https://meps.ahrq.gov/> - Data on healthcare utilization, expenditures, and insurance coverage, including psychotropic medication use.

## Books

- Blazer, D.G. *The Age of Melancholy: Major Depression and Its Social Origins*. Routledge, 2005. - Analysis of social and environmental factors contributing to late-life depression.
- Grob, G.N. *From Asylum to Community: Mental Health Policy in Modern America*. Princeton University Press, 1991. - Historical account of deinstitutionalization and its impact on older adults.
- National Academies of Sciences, Engineering, and Medicine. *Social Isolation and Loneliness in Older Adults: Opportunities for the Health Care System*. National Academies Press, 2020. - Landmark consensus report documenting health consequences of social isolation and policy responses.
- Bartels, S.J., Pratt, S.I. *Integrated Care for Older Adults: A Practical Guide*. Springer, 2016. - Practical guide to implementing integrated mental health and primary care for older populations.
- Institute of Medicine. *The Mental Health and Substance Use Workforce for Older Adults: In Whose Hands?*. National Academies Press, 2012. - Comprehensive analysis of the geriatric mental health workforce crisis and policy recommendations.
- Holt-Lunstad, J., ed. *Social Connections and Health*. Oxford University Press, 2022. - Collection of research on social relationships, loneliness, and health outcomes across the lifespan.
- Conwell, Y., Van Orden, K., Caine, E.D. *Suicide in Older Adults*. Psychiatric Clinics of North America series, 2011. - Clinical and epidemiological overview of older adult suicide with prevention strategies.

## Legal Resources

### Case Law

- *Olmstead v. L.C.*, 527 U.S. 581 (1999). - Established that unjustified institutional segregation of persons with disabilities constitutes discrimination under the ADA; foundational for community-based mental health services for older adults.
- *Medicare and Medicaid Patient and Program Protection Act cases*. - Framework for enforcement of nursing home quality standards, including mental health care requirements.

### Statutory References

- 42 U.S.C. 1395d - Medicare inpatient psychiatric benefit, including the 190-day lifetime limit for freestanding psychiatric hospitals.
- 42 U.S.C. 1396r (Nursing Home Reform Act, OBRA 1987) - Requirements for nursing home care quality, including pre-admission screening (PASARR), psychotropic medication restrictions, and resident rights.
- 42 U.S.C. 3001 et seq. (Older Americans Act) - Federal framework for community-based aging services, including senior centers, nutrition programs, and supportive services.
- Pub. L. 110-343, Div. C, Title V (Mental Health Parity and Addiction Equity Act, 2008) - Established mental health parity requirements for insurance; indirect impact on Medicare through policy norm-setting.
- Pub. L. 110-275 (Medicare Improvements for Patients and Providers Act, MIPPA, 2008) - Phased in Medicare mental health payment parity (reducing outpatient coinsurance from 50% to 20% by 2014).
- Pub. L. 117-159 (Bipartisan Safer Communities Act, 2022) - Authorized Medicare coverage for marriage and family therapists and licensed mental health counselors, effective January 2024.
