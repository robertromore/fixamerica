## Books

- Haidt, J. *The Anxious Generation: How the Great Rewiring of Childhood Is Causing an Epidemic of Mental Illness*. Penguin Press, 2024. - Comprehensive argument about smartphones and social media as drivers of the youth mental health crisis
- Shonkoff, J.P. & Phillips, D.A. (Eds.) *From Neurons to Neighborhoods: The Science of Early Childhood Development*. National Academies Press, 2000. - Foundational text on early brain development and the impact of adversity

## Legal Resources

### Case Law

- *Parham v. J.R.*, 442 U.S. 584 (1979) - Established procedural due process requirements for commitment of minors to mental institutions; balanced parental authority with children's liberty interests
- *Rosie D. v. Romney*, 410 F. Supp. 2d 18 (D. Mass. 2006) - Class action enforcing EPSDT mental health services for Medicaid-enrolled children in Massachusetts; established precedent for judicial enforcement of children's mental health entitlements
- *Ginsberg v. New York*, 390 U.S. 629 (1968) - Upheld state authority to restrict minors' access to material that would be protected for adults; foundational precedent for age-based digital regulation
- *NetChoice v. Paxton*, 598 U.S. ___ (2024) - Supreme Court addressed state authority to regulate social media platforms; significant for the constitutionality of youth online safety legislation
- *Katie A. v. Bonta*, No. CV 02-05662 (C.D. Cal. 2011) - Class action requiring California to provide intensive mental health services to foster children under EPSDT

### Statutory References

- 42 U.S.C. 1396d(r) (EPSDT benefit) - Medicaid entitlement to comprehensive pediatric health services including mental health
- Public Law 117-159 (Bipartisan Safer Communities Act, 2022) - $1.7 billion for youth mental health programs
- Public Law 110-343, Sec. 512 (Mental Health Parity and Addiction Equity Act, 2008) - Requires insurance coverage parity between mental health and physical health
- 20 U.S.C. 7101-7122 (ESSA Title IV-A, Student Support and Academic Enrichment Grants) - Federal funding authority for school-based mental health services
- 42 U.S.C. 290bb-40 (Children's Mental Health Services Program) - SAMHSA authority for children's system of care grants
