# Criminal Justice and Mental Health: Resources

This section provides a curated list of key research, reports, data sources, and organizations focused on the intersection of mental illness and the criminal justice system in the United States.

---

## Academic Research

### Peer-Reviewed Studies

- Binswanger, I. A., Stern, M. F., Deyo, R. A., et al. "Release from Prison -- A High Risk of Death for Former Inmates." *New England Journal of Medicine* 356, no. 2 (2007): 157-165. <https://doi.org/10.1056/NEJMsa064115> - Landmark study finding 12.7x mortality risk in first two weeks post-release.

- Compton, M. T., Bakeman, R., Broussard, B., et al. "The Police-Based Crisis Intervention Team (CIT) Model: II. Effects on Level of Force and Resolution, Referral, and Arrest." *Psychiatric Services* 65, no. 4 (2014): 523-529. <https://doi.org/10.1176/appi.ps.201300108> - Foundational study on CIT outcomes, later updated in Compton et al. (2022).

- Compton, M. T., Broussard, B., Munetz, M. R., et al. "Crisis Intervention Team (CIT) Training: Evolving Evidence and Implications." *Psychiatric Services* 73, no. 9 (2022): 1034-1042. - Updated meta-analysis of CIT outcomes showing 28-36% injury reduction.

- McNiel, D. E. & Binder, R. L. "Effectiveness of a Mental Health Court in Reducing Criminal Recidivism and Violence." *American Journal of Psychiatry* 164, no. 9 (2007): 1395-1403. Updated meta-analysis (2022). - Key evidence for 20% average recidivism reduction.

- Pirelli, G., Zapf, P. A., & Gottdiener, W. H. "Competency to Stand Trial Research: Guidelines and Future Directions." *Journal of Forensic Psychology Practice* 22, no. 1 (2022): 37-73. - Comprehensive meta-analysis of competency restoration outcomes showing 70-80% success rates.

- Redlich, A. D., Steadman, H. J., Callahan, L., Robbins, P. C., Vesselinov, R., & Ozdogru, A. "Patterns of Practice in Mental Health Courts: A National Survey." *Law and Human Behavior* 46, no. 4 (2022): 306-320. - Research on racial disparities in mental health court referrals.

- Steadman, H. J., Osher, F. C., Robbins, P. C., Case, B., & Samuels, S. "Prevalence of Serious Mental Illness Among Jail Inmates." *Psychiatric Services* 60, no. 6 (2009): 761-765. - Important prevalence study.

- Teplin, L. A. "The Prevalence of Severe Mental Disorder Among Male Urban Jail Detainees: Comparison with the Epidemiologic Catchment Area Program." *American Journal of Public Health* 80, no. 6 (1990): 663-669. - First rigorous epidemiological study of mental illness in jails.

- Watson, A. C., Compton, M. T., & Pope, L. G. "Crisis Intervention Teams: A Systematic Review of the Evidence Base." *Schizophrenia Bulletin* 47, no. 5 (2021): 1327-1340. - Systematic review finding 50-60% arrest rate reduction for CIT mental health calls.

### Working Papers and White Papers

- Gowensmith, W. N., Frost, L. E., Speelman, D. W., & Therson, D. E. "Lookin' for Beds in All the Wrong Places: Outpatient Competency Restoration as a Promising Approach to a National Crisis." Working Paper, University of Denver, 2023. - Documents the 300% increase in competency evaluations since 2000.

- RAND Corporation. "Mental Health Courts: A Cost-Benefit Analysis." Research Brief RB-10256-DOJ, 2022. - Key finding: $2.21 return for every $1 invested.

- RAND Corporation. "Evaluation of the Los Angeles County Office of Diversion and Reentry." Research Report, 2021. - Finding $7.20 return for every $1 invested in comprehensive diversion.

## Government Reports and Data

### Federal Agencies

- Bureau of Justice Statistics. "Indicators of Mental Health Problems Reported by Prisoners and Jail Inmates, 2011-12." NCJ 250612, 2017; updated 2023. <https://bjs.ojp.gov/> - Primary source for incarceration mental health prevalence data (44% jail, 37% state prison).

- Bureau of Justice Statistics. "Use of Restrictive Housing in U.S. Prisons and Jails, 2011-12." 2021. <https://bjs.ojp.gov/> - Source for 29% SMI rate in restrictive housing and solitary confinement data.

- Government Accountability Office. "Mental Health: HHS Could Play a Larger Role in Helping Criminal Justice and Mental Health Systems Coordinate." GAO-20-554, September 2020. <https://www.gao.gov/products/gao-20-554>

- Health Resources and Services Administration. "Behavioral Health Workforce Projections, 2020-2035." 2023. <https://bhw.hrsa.gov/> - Source for psychiatrist shortfall estimate of 14,280.

- Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration. "National Survey on Drug Use and Health (NSDUH): Mental Health Findings, 2022." 2023. <https://www.samhsa.gov/data/nsduh> - National data on mental health treatment rates (46.2% of adults with any mental illness received treatment).

- Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration. "988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline Performance Metrics." 2024. <https://www.samhsa.gov/find-help/988> - Operational data: 5.6 million contacts in first 18 months, 85% answer rate.

### Congressional Reports

- Congressional Research Service. "Mental Health and the Criminal Justice System." Report R44859, updated 2023. <https://crsreports.congress.gov/> - Comprehensive overview of federal programs and policy options.

- Congressional Budget Office. "Preliminary Cost Estimate: Medicaid Reentry Waiver Implementation." 2024. - Estimated $3.5 billion over 10 years.

### Agency Data Portals

- Bureau of Justice Statistics. National Prisoner Statistics Program. <https://bjs.ojp.gov/data-collection/national-prisoner-statistics-nps-program> - Annual data on prison populations, including mental health indicators.

- SAMHSA. Treatment Episode Data Set (TEDS). <https://www.samhsa.gov/data/data-we-collect/teds-treatment-episode-data-set> - Data on admissions to substance use and mental health treatment facilities.

- SAMHSA. 988 Dashboard. <https://www.samhsa.gov/find-help/988> - Real-time 988 Lifeline performance data.

## Books

- Earley, P. *Crazy: A Father's Search Through America's Mental Health Madness*. G.P. Putnam's Sons, 2006. - First-person account of navigating the mental health-criminal justice system; widely cited narrative.

- Erickson, P. E. & Erickson, S. K. *Crime, Punishment, and Mental Illness: Law and the Behavioral Sciences in Conflict*. Rutgers University Press, 2008. - Academic treatment of legal and clinical tensions.

- Ford, M. *The International Handbook of Suicide Prevention*. 2nd ed. Wiley, 2016. - Includes chapters on suicide prevention in correctional settings.

- Lamb, H. R. & Weinberger, L. E. *Deinstitutionalization: Promise and Problems*. Jossey-Bass, 2001. - Authoritative account of the deinstitutionalization process and its consequences.

- Roth, A. *Insane: America's Criminal Treatment of Mental Illness*. Basic Books, 2018. - Comprehensive narrative history of the criminalization of mental illness.

- Slate, R. N., Buffington-Vollum, J., & Johnson, W. W. *The Criminalization of Mental Illness: Crisis and Opportunity for the Justice System*. 3rd ed. Carolina Academic Press, 2019. - Standard academic textbook on the topic.

- Torrey, E. F. *American Psychosis: How the Federal Government Destroyed the Mental Health Treatment System*. Oxford University Press, 2013. - Critical history of federal mental health policy failures.

- Winerip, M. & Schwirtz, M. *The Wards Island Story: Inside America's Largest Mental Health Jail*. (Reporting series). *New York Times*, 2014-2015. - Pulitzer-nominated investigative journalism on Rikers Island mental health conditions.

## Legal Resources

### Case Law

- *Estelle v. Gamble*, 429 U.S. 97 (1976) - Established that deliberate indifference to serious medical needs violates the Eighth Amendment.

- *Bowring v. Godwin*, 551 F.2d 44 (4th Cir. 1977) - Extended right to psychiatric care equal to right to medical care for prisoners.

- *Ruiz v. Estelle*, 503 F. Supp. 1265 (S.D. Tex. 1980) - Defined six components of minimally adequate mental health care in prisons.

- *Olmstead v. L.C.*, 527 U.S. 581 (1999) - Established right to community-based services for people with disabilities under ADA.

- *Brown v. Plata*, 563 U.S. 493 (2011) - Held that California prison overcrowding violated the Eighth Amendment, with mental health conditions as a central focus.

- *Trueblood v. DSHS*, No. 14-cv-01178 (W.D. Wash. 2015-ongoing) - Leading case establishing timely competency evaluation and restoration as a constitutional right.

- *O'Connor v. Donaldson*, 422 U.S. 563 (1975) - Held that non-dangerous mentally ill persons cannot be confined against their will.

- *Addington v. Texas*, 441 U.S. 418 (1979) - Required clear and convincing evidence for civil commitment.

### Statutory References

- 42 U.S.C. Section 300x et seq. - Community Mental Health Services Block Grant.
- 42 U.S.C. Section 1396d(a) - Medicaid coverage of mental health services; IMD exclusion at Section 1396d(i).
