# Civil Commitment and Involuntary Treatment: Resources

This section provides a curated list of key research, reports, legal materials, and advocacy resources on civil commitment, involuntary treatment, and assisted outpatient treatment in the United States.

---

## Academic Research

### Peer-Reviewed Studies

- Swartz, M.S., et al. "New York State Assisted Outpatient Treatment Program Evaluation." *Duke University School of Medicine / NYS Office of Mental Health*, 2009. - Landmark evaluation of Kendra's Law showing 77% reduction in hospitalization, 83% reduction in arrests, 87% reduction in incarceration.
- Kisely, S.R., Campbell, L.A., & O'Reilly, R. "Compulsory Community and Involuntary Outpatient Treatment for People with Severe Mental Disorders." *Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews* 3 (2017): CD004408. - Meta-analysis finding no significant reduction in hospitalization from compulsory community treatment absent adequate services.
- Burns, T., et al. "Community Treatment Orders for Patients with Psychosis (OCTET): A Randomised Controlled Trial." *Lancet* 381.9878 (2013): 1627-1633. - UK randomized trial of community treatment orders finding no significant effect on readmission.
- Snowden, L.R., Hastings, J.F., & Alvidrez, J. "Overrepresentation of Black Americans in Psychiatric Inpatient Care." *Psychiatric Services* 60.6 (2009): 779-785. - Documented racial disparities in involuntary psychiatric admission.
- Monahan, J., et al. "Rethinking Risk Assessment: The MacArthur Study of Mental Disorder and Violence." *Oxford University Press*, 2001. - Foundational study on violence risk assessment accuracy, finding ~70-75% accuracy with best clinical tools.
- Compton, M.T., et al. "The Police-Based Crisis Intervention Team (CIT) Model." *Psychiatric Services* 70.1 (2019): 44-54. - Found CIT training reduced arrests of people with mental illness by 18% but did not reduce use of force.
- Tsemberis, S., Gulcur, L., & Nakae, M. "Housing First, Consumer Choice, and Harm Reduction for Homeless Individuals with a Dual Diagnosis." *American Journal of Public Health* 94.4 (2004): 651-656. - Demonstrated 80% reduction in homelessness with Housing First model.
- Bond, G.R., et al. "Assertive Community Treatment for People with Severe Mental Illness: Critical Ingredients and Impact on Patients." *Disease Management and Health Outcomes* 9.3 (2001): 141-159. - Evidence for ACT reducing hospitalization by 20-40%.
- Honegger, L.N. "Does the Evidence Support the Case for Mental Health Courts?" *Law and Human Behavior* 39.5 (2015): 478-488. - Evidence review of mental health court effectiveness.
- Katsakou, C., & Priebe, S. "Outcomes of Involuntary Hospital Admission: A Review." *Acta Psychiatrica Scandinavica* 114.4 (2006): 232-241. - Review of patient experiences and outcomes of involuntary treatment.
- Wynn, R. "Coercion in Psychiatric Care: Clinical, Legal, and Ethical Controversies." *International Journal of Law and Psychiatry* 78 (2021): 101726. - Analysis of Norway's capacity-based commitment reform.

### Working Papers and Reports

- RAND Corporation. "Assisted Outpatient Treatment: Evaluating the Evidence." 2020. - Comprehensive evidence review concluding AOT is effective when coupled with intensive services.
- Medicaid and CHIP Payment and Access Commission (MACPAC). "Behavioral Health in the Medicaid Program -- People, Use, and Expenditures." 2023. - Analysis of Medicaid spending on behavioral health, including IMD exclusion impact.
- Amador, X. "I Am Not Sick, I Don't Need Help!" Vida Press, 2000; updated 2012. - Foundational work on anosognosia in schizophrenia; estimates 40-50% prevalence.

- Stefan, S. *Rational Suicide, Irrational Laws: Examining Current Approaches to Suicide in Policy and Law*. Oxford University Press, 2016. - Analysis of legal frameworks governing self-harm and suicide risk in civil commitment.
- Bloom, J.D., & Buckley, M.C. *Oregon's Psychiatric Security Review Board*. Springer, 2020. - Case study of Oregon's forensic mental health oversight system.
- Deutsch, A. *The Shame of the States*. Harcourt, Brace, 1948. - Foundational investigative work documenting conditions in state mental hospitals.
- Goffman, E. *Asylums: Essays on the Social Situation of Mental Patients and Other Inmates*. Anchor Books, 1961. - Influential sociological analysis of total institutions.
- Saks, E.R. *The Center Cannot Hold: My Journey Through Madness*. Hyperion, 2007. - First-person account of living with schizophrenia while navigating the commitment system, by a law professor.

## Legal Resources

### Case Law

- *O'Connor v. Donaldson*, 422 U.S. 563 (1975) - State cannot confine nondangerous individual capable of surviving safely in freedom.
- *Addington v. Texas*, 441 U.S. 418 (1979) - Minimum standard of proof for civil commitment is "clear and convincing evidence."
- *Youngberg v. Romeo*, 457 U.S. 307 (1982) - Involuntarily committed persons have liberty interests in safety, freedom from restraint, and minimally adequate training.
- *Olmstead v. L.C.*, 527 U.S. 581 (1999) - Unjustified institutionalization is discrimination under the ADA; requires community-based treatment when appropriate.
- *Washington v. Harper*, 494 U.S. 210 (1990) - State may administer medication to a prisoner against their will if they are dangerous and the treatment is medically appropriate.
- *Sell v. United States*, 539 U.S. 166 (2003) - Forced medication for competence restoration requires case-by-case judicial review.
- *Jackson v. Indiana*, 406 U.S. 715 (1972) - Indefinite commitment solely for competency restoration violates due process.
- *Vitek v. Jones*, 445 U.S. 480 (1980) - Transfer of a prisoner to a mental hospital requires due process protections including notice and a hearing.
- *Lessard v. Schmidt*, 349 F. Supp. 1078 (E.D. Wis. 1972) - First federal court ruling requiring criminal-procedure-level due process for civil commitment.

### Statutory References

- 42 U.S.C. 1396d(a)(B) (Medicaid IMD Exclusion) - Prohibits federal Medicaid reimbursement for care in institutions for mental diseases with more than 16 beds for patients aged 21-64.
- 42 U.S.C. 10801 et seq. (Protection and Advocacy for Individuals with Mental Illness Act) - Establishes the P&A system in every state.
- 42 U.S.C. 12101 et seq. (Americans with Disabilities Act) - Basis for *Olmstead* community integration mandate.
- N.Y. Mental Hygiene Law 9.60 (Kendra's Law) - New York's assisted outpatient treatment statute; national model for AOT.
- 42 U.S.C. 300x et seq. (Community Mental Health Services Block Grant) - Authorizes federal block grants to states for community mental health services.
- Pub. L. 113-93, Section 223 (2014) - Authorized the Certified Community Behavioral Health Clinic (CCBHC) demonstration.
