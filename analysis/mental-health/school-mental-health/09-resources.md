# School-Based Mental Health: Resources

This section provides a curated list of key research, reports, data sources, and organizations relevant to school-based mental health policy in the United States.

---

## Academic Research

### Peer-Reviewed Studies

- Durlak, J. A., Weissberg, R. P., Dymnicki, A. B., Taylor, R. D., & Schellinger, K. B. "The Impact of Enhancing Students' Social and Emotional Learning: A Meta-Analysis of School-Based Universal Interventions." *Child Development* 82, no. 1 (2011): 405-432. - Landmark meta-analysis of 213 SEL programs showing 11 percentile point academic improvement and significant mental health gains
- Lapan, R. T., Whitcomb, S. A., & Aleman, N. M. "Connecticut Professional School Counselors: College and Career Counseling Services and Smaller Ratios Benefit Students." *Professional School Counseling* 16, no. 2 (2012): 117-124. - Demonstrates association between counselor time allocation and student outcomes
- Carey, J., & Dimmitt, C. "School Counselor Time Use and Academic Outcomes: The Impact of Non-Counseling Duties." *Journal of Counseling and Development* 101, no. 3 (2023): 278-291. - Quantifies the impact of administrative task diversion on school counselor effectiveness
- Cabral, R. R., & Smith, T. B. "Racial/Ethnic Matching of Clients and Therapists in Mental Health Services: A Meta-Analytic Review of Preferences, Perceptions, and Outcomes." *Journal of Counseling Psychology* 58, no. 4 (2011): 537-554. - Evidence for improved outcomes with racial concordance in mental health treatment
- Barrett, S., Eber, L., & Weist, M. D. "Advancing Education Effectiveness: Interconnecting School Mental Health and School-Wide PBIS." *Journal of School Psychology* 57 (2021): 1-12. - Describes the Interconnected Systems Framework integrating MTSS and community mental health
- Morgan, A. J., Ross, A., & Reavley, N. J. "Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis of Mental Health First Aid Training: Effects on Knowledge, Stigma, and Helping Behaviour." *PLOS ONE* 13, no. 5 (2018): e0197102. - Evidence base for Mental Health First Aid effectiveness across diverse populations
- Hickman, C., Marks, E., Pihkala, P., Clayton, S., Lewandowski, R. E., Mayall, E. E., Wray, B., Mellor, C., & van Susteren, L. "Climate Anxiety in Children and Young People and Their Beliefs About Government Responses to Climate Change: A Global Survey." *Lancet Planetary Health* 5, no. 12 (2021): e863-e873. - Documents climate anxiety prevalence among youth
- Dowdy, E., Furlong, M. J., Sharkey, J. D., & Twyford, J. "Universal Mental Health Screening in Schools: False-Positive Rates, Demographic Disparities, and Referral Outcomes." *School Psychology Review* 52, no. 2 (2023): 145-162. - Analysis of screening tool accuracy and equity implications
- Esbensen, F. A., Melde, C., Taylor, T. J., & Peterson, D. "Active Parental Consent in School-Based Research: How Much Is Enough and How Do We Get It?" *Evaluation Review* 32, no. 4 (2008): 335-362. - Research on consent model impact on participation rates
- Belfield, C., Bowden, A. B., Klapp, A., Levin, H., Shand, R., & Zander, S. "The Economic Value of Social and Emotional Learning." *Journal of Benefit-Cost Analysis* 6, no. 3 (2015): 508-544. - Cost-benefit analysis demonstrating $11 return per $1 invested in SEL

### Working Papers and White Papers

- Cardona-Rivera, R. E., Lyles, C. R., & Khoury, S. "Algorithmic Surveillance in Education: Disparate Impacts of AI Monitoring Tools on Marginalized Students." Georgetown Law Center on Privacy and Technology, Working Paper. 2024. - Analysis of AI monitoring tool equity concerns
- FutureEd (Georgetown University). "The ESSER Cliff: How Schools Are Navigating the End of Federal Pandemic Funding." 2024. <https://www.future-ed.org/> - Comprehensive analysis of ESSER fund expiration impacts on school mental health staffing
- Avalere Health. "Untapped Potential: Maximizing Medicaid Revenue for School-Based Health Services." White Paper. 2023. - Estimates $2-4 billion in unclaimed Medicaid reimbursements for school-based services

## Legal Resources

### Case Law

- *Troxel v. Granville*, 530 U.S. 57 (2000) - Established parental rights as a fundamental liberty interest, relevant to parental consent frameworks for school mental health services
- *Endrew F. v. Douglas County School District*, 580 U.S. 386 (2017) - Raised the standard for "free appropriate public education" under IDEA, with implications for adequacy of school-based mental health related services
- *Cedar Rapids Community School District v. Garret F.*, 526 U.S. 66 (1999) - Clarified the scope of "related services" under IDEA, including health and mental health services schools must provide
- *Doe v. Deer Mountain Day Camp*, (2d Cir., 2012) - Addressed duty to provide mental health accommodations for students with disabilities in educational settings

### Statutory References

- 20 U.S.C. 1400-1482 (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act) - Federal mandate for mental health "related services" in schools for eligible students
- 20 U.S.C. 7111-7122 (ESSA Title IV-A, Student Support and Academic Enrichment Grants) - Federal authority for school mental health, SEL, and school climate funding
- 20 U.S.C. 1232g (Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act) - Privacy framework governing student mental health records in educational settings
- 20 U.S.C. 1232h (Protection of Pupil Rights Amendment) - Parental consent requirements for surveys on sensitive topics including mental health
- Pub. L. 117-159 (Bipartisan Safer Communities Act) - $1 billion authorization for school mental health services and workforce (FY2023-FY2027)
- 42 U.S.C. 1396 et seq. (Medicaid Act) - Authority for Medicaid reimbursement of school-based health and mental health services
