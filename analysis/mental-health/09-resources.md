# Mental Health Policy: Resources

This section provides a curated list of key research, reports, data sources, and organizations relevant to U.S. mental health policy. Sources are organized by type to facilitate research across the domains covered in this analysis: treatment access, workforce, crisis services, youth mental health, serious mental illness, insurance parity, and funding reform.

---

## Academic Research

### Peer-Reviewed Studies

- Unutzer, J., et al. "Collaborative Care Management of Late-Life Depression in the Primary Care Setting: A Randomized Controlled Trial." *JAMA* 288, no. 22 (2002): 2836-2845. DOI: 10.1001/jama.288.22.2836. -- Foundational IMPACT trial demonstrating the effectiveness of the Collaborative Care Model for depression treatment in primary care.

- Archer, J., et al. "Collaborative Care for Depression and Anxiety Problems." *Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews* no. 10 (2012): CD006525. DOI: 10.1002/14651858.CD006525.pub2. -- Systematic review of 79 randomized controlled trials confirming the effectiveness of collaborative care for depression and anxiety.

- Bond, G.R., et al. "Assertive Community Treatment for People with Severe Mental Illness: Critical Ingredients and Impact on Patients." *Disease Management and Health Outcomes* 9, no. 3 (2001): 141-159. -- Comprehensive review of ACT effectiveness in reducing hospitalization and incarceration.

- Chinman, M., et al. "Peer Support Services for Individuals with Serious Mental Illnesses: Assessing the Evidence." *Psychiatric Services* 65, no. 4 (2014): 429-441. DOI: 10.1176/appi.ps.201300244. -- Systematic review of peer support specialist effectiveness.

- Culhane, D.P., Metraux, S., & Hadley, T. "Public Service Reductions Associated with Placement of Homeless Persons with Severe Mental Illness in Supportive Housing." *Housing Policy Debate* 13, no. 1 (2002): 107-163. -- Landmark study documenting cost offsets from permanent supportive housing.

- McNiel, D.E. & Binder, R.L. "Effectiveness of a Mental Health Court in Reducing Criminal Recidivism and Violence." *American Journal of Psychiatry* 164, no. 9 (2007): 1395-1403. -- Evidence on mental health court outcomes.

- Twenge, J.M., et al. "Worldwide Increases in Adolescent Loneliness." *Journal of Adolescence* 93 (2021): 257-269. DOI: 10.1016/j.adolescence.2021.06.006. -- Cross-national evidence on adolescent mental health trends.

- Richardson, L.P., et al. "Evaluation of the Patient Health Questionnaire-9 Item for Detecting Major Depression Among Adolescents." *Pediatrics* 126, no. 6 (2010): 1117-1123. -- Evidence supporting universal screening for adolescent depression.

- Busch, S.H. & Barry, C.L. "Mental Health Disorders in Childhood: Assessing the Burden on Families." *Health Affairs* 26, no. 4 (2007): 1088-1095. -- Analysis of economic burden of childhood mental illness on families.

- Snowden, L.R., Hastings, J.F., & Alvidrez, J. "Overrepresentation of Black Americans in Psychiatric Inpatient Care." *Psychiatric Services* 60, no. 6 (2009): 779-785. -- Research on racial disparities in psychiatric commitment.

- Hilty, D.M., et al. "A Scoping Review of Telemental Health: Definitions, Competencies, Evidence, and Training." *World Journal of Psychiatry* 13, no. 8 (2023): 541-572. -- Comprehensive review of telemental health effectiveness.

- Clark, D.M. "Realizing the Mass Public Benefit of Evidence-Based Psychological Therapies: The IAPT Program." *Annual Review of Clinical Psychology* 14 (2018): 159-183. DOI: 10.1146/annurev-clinpsy-050817-084833. -- Analysis of the UK's IAPT program design and outcomes.

- Mundinger, M.O., et al. "Primary Care Outcomes in Patients Treated by Nurse Practitioners or Physicians." *JAMA* 283, no. 1 (2000): 59-68. -- Evidence on comparable outcomes between nurse practitioner and physician care.

- Stanik-Hutt, J., et al. "The Quality and Effectiveness of Care Provided by Nurse Practitioners." *Journal for Nurse Practitioners* 9, no. 8 (2013): 492-500. -- Systematic review of nurse practitioner care quality.

### Working Papers and White Papers

- Milliman, Inc. "Addiction and Mental Health vs. Physical Health: Widening Disparities in Network Use and Provider Reimbursement." November 2023. <https://www.milliman.com/en/insight/addiction-mental-health-vs-physical-health-widening-disparities> -- Annual analysis of parity disparities in network use and reimbursement.

- RAND Corporation. "Mental Health Care for Service Members and Veterans: Improving Systems and Services." 2023. <https://www.rand.org/topics/military-and-veteran-health.html> -- Research on veteran mental health system performance.

- National Academies of Sciences, Engineering, and Medicine. *Permanent Supportive Housing: Evaluating the Evidence for Improving Health Outcomes Among People Experiencing Chronic Homelessness*. Washington, DC: National Academies Press, 2018. DOI: 10.17226/25133. -- Comprehensive evidence review on permanent supportive housing.

## Government Reports and Data

### Federal Agencies

- Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration (SAMHSA). "2022 National Survey on Drug Use and Health (NSDUH)." 2023. <https://www.samhsa.gov/data/release/2022-national-survey-drug-use-and-health-nsduh-releases> -- Primary federal data source on mental illness prevalence and treatment rates.

- SAMHSA. "National Mental Health Services Survey (N-MHSS)." Annual. <https://www.samhsa.gov/data/data-we-collect/n-mhss-national-mental-health-services-survey> -- Survey of all known mental health treatment facilities in the U.S.

- SAMHSA. "988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline Performance Dashboard." 2024. <https://www.samhsa.gov/find-help/988/performance-metrics> -- Real-time data on 988 Lifeline contacts, answer rates, and response times.

- SAMHSA. "National Expenditures for Mental Health Services and Substance Abuse Treatment." 2023. <https://store.samhsa.gov/product/national-expenditures-mental-health-services-substance-abuse-treatment> -- Federal analysis of total U.S. mental health spending.

- SAMHSA. "Certified Community Behavioral Health Clinic Demonstration Program: Report to Congress." 2023. -- Evaluation of CCBHC program outcomes.

- Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC). "Youth Risk Behavior Surveillance System (YRBS)." Biennial. <https://www.cdc.gov/yrbs/index.html> -- National data on youth mental health, substance use, and risk behaviors.

- CDC. "Web-based Injury Statistics Query and Reporting System (WISQARS)." 2024. <https://www.cdc.gov/injury/wisqars/index.html> -- Fatal and nonfatal injury data including suicide statistics.

- Health Resources and Services Administration (HRSA). "Designated Health Professional Shortage Areas Statistics." Updated quarterly. <https://data.hrsa.gov/topics/health-workforce/shortage-areas> -- Data on Mental Health Professional Shortage Areas.

- Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS). "Behavioral Health Services in Medicaid." 2023. <https://www.medicaid.gov/medicaid/benefits/behavioral-health-services/index.html> -- Data on Medicaid mental health coverage and spending.

- Department of Labor, Employee Benefits Security Administration. "Report to Congress on the Mental Health Parity and Addiction Equity Act." Annual. <https://www.dol.gov/agencies/ebsa/laws-and-regulations/laws/mental-health-and-substance-use-disorder-parity> -- Federal parity enforcement activity and compliance reports.

### Congressional Reports

- Congressional Research Service. "Mental Health Parity: Federal and State Action." CRS Report R45147. Updated 2023. <https://crsreports.congress.gov/> -- Overview of federal and state parity legislation and enforcement.

- Congressional Research Service. "The 988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline." CRS Report R47200. Updated 2024. -- Analysis of 988 implementation, funding, and challenges.

- Congressional Research Service. "Mental Health and Substance Use Disorder Workforce: Key Issues." CRS Report R47834. 2023. -- Analysis of mental health workforce shortages and policy options.

- Government Accountability Office. "Mental Health: HHS Could Improve Efforts to Support Providers and Enhance Oversight of Federal Programs." GAO-23-105597. 2023. <https://www.gao.gov/products/gao-23-105597> -- GAO assessment of federal mental health program oversight.

- Government Accountability Office. "Mental Health Parity: Federal Guidance and Enforcement Have Not Ensured Issuers' Compliance." GAO-20-150. 2019. <https://www.gao.gov/products/gao-20-150> -- Landmark GAO report on parity enforcement failures.

### Agency Data Portals

- SAMHSA. Behavioral Health Data Portal. <https://www.samhsa.gov/data/> -- Central repository for federal behavioral health data including NSDUH, N-MHSS, and TEDS.
- HRSA. Area Health Resources Files. <https://data.hrsa.gov/topics/health-workforce/ahrf> -- County-level data on health workforce and facilities.
- CDC WONDER. <https://wonder.cdc.gov/> -- Access to public health data including mortality statistics.
- HUD. Annual Homeless Assessment Report (AHAR) Data. <https://www.huduser.gov/portal/datasets/ahar.html> -- Data on homelessness including mental health status.

## Books

- Torrey, E. Fuller. *American Psychosis: How the Federal Government Destroyed the Mental Illness Treatment System*. Oxford University Press, 2014. -- Comprehensive history of deinstitutionalization and its consequences, from the perspective of a leading advocate for people with serious mental illness.

- Rosenberg, L. & DeMartino, R. *Healing: Our Path from Mental Illness to Mental Health*. Harper, 2022. -- Overview of the mental health system's failures and a case for reform, by former SAMHSA administrator and National Council for Mental Wellbeing CEO.

- Insel, T. *Healing: Our Path from Mental Illness to Mental Health*. Penguin Press, 2022. -- Former NIMH director's analysis of why the mental health system fails and what a recovery-oriented system would look like.

- Haidt, J. *The Anxious Generation: How the Great Rewiring of Childhood Is Causing an Epidemic of Mental Illness*. Penguin Press, 2024. -- Analysis of social media's role in the youth mental health crisis.

- Whitaker, R. *Anatomy of an Epidemic: Magic Bullets, Psychiatric Drugs, and the Astonishing Rise of Mental Illness in America*. Broadway Books, 2010. -- Critical examination of psychopharmacology and its long-term effects on mental illness outcomes.

- Earley, P. *Crazy: A Father's Search Through America's Mental Health Madness*. Berkley Books, 2006. -- First-person account of navigating the mental health system for a family member with serious mental illness.

- Frank, R.G. & Glied, S.A. *Better But Not Well: Mental Health Policy in the United States Since 1950*. Johns Hopkins University Press, 2006. -- Academic analysis of mental health policy evolution from deinstitutionalization through the parity movement.

- Roth, A. *Insane: America's Criminal Treatment of Mental Illness*. Basic Books, 2018. -- Examination of the criminalization of mental illness and the failures of the community mental health system.

## Legal Resources

### Case Law

- *O'Connor v. Donaldson*, 422 U.S. 563 (1975). -- Established that a state cannot constitutionally confine a non-dangerous individual with mental illness who is capable of surviving safely in freedom.

- *Olmstead v. L.C.*, 527 U.S. 581 (1999). -- Landmark ADA decision requiring states to provide community-based services to persons with mental disabilities when appropriate, establishing the integration mandate.

- *Wit v. United Behavioral Health*, No. 14-cv-02346 (N.D. Cal. 2019, 2020). -- Major parity case finding UBH systematically applied restrictive medical necessity criteria to behavioral health claims in violation of plan terms. Subsequently upheld in part on appeal.

- *New York State Conference of Blue Cross & Blue Shield Plans v. Travelers Insurance Co.*, 514 U.S. 645 (1995). -- ERISA preemption case relevant to state parity enforcement authority.

- *Heller v. Doe*, 509 U.S. 312 (1993). -- Supreme Court case on due process standards in civil commitment proceedings.

### Statutory References

- 42 U.S.C. 18031(j) -- Affordable Care Act mental health essential health benefit requirement.
- 29 U.S.C. 1185a -- Mental Health Parity and Addiction Equity Act (MHPAEA) as codified.
- 42 U.S.C. 290bb-36 -- 988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline authorization.
- 42 U.S.C. 1396a -- Medicaid state plan requirements, including the IMD exclusion at 42 U.S.C. 1396d(a)(B).
- 42 U.S.C. 290bb-31 et seq. -- SAMHSA Community Mental Health Services Block Grant authorization.
- Pub. L. 114-255 -- 21st Century Cures Act (2016), including mental health provisions.
- Pub. L. 117-159 -- Bipartisan Safer Communities Act (2022), including community mental health and 988 provisions.
