# Public Media: Legislation and Legal Framework

## Overview

Public media legislation addresses funding levels, governance structure, editorial independence, and the system's role in addressing information needs. This document provides draft legal text for strengthening public media at the federal and state levels.

## Federal Legislation

### Public Media Sustainability Act

**Purpose**: Increase and stabilize federal funding for public media.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Public Media Sustainability Act of 2025".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds that—
    (1) public media provides trusted information to over 100 million
    Americans weekly;
    (2) the United States invests far less in public media per capita
    than peer democracies;
    (3) the collapse of local commercial news has increased the need
    for public media journalism;
    (4) educational programming benefits children and communities;
    (5) public media serves rural and underserved communities often
    not served by commercial media; and
    (6) stable, adequate funding is essential to public media's
    mission.

SEC. 3. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS.

(a) CORPORATION FOR PUBLIC BROADCASTING.—Section 396(k)(1)(A) of
the Communications Act of 1934 (47 U.S.C. 396(k)(1)(A)) is amended—
    (1) by striking the existing authorization amounts; and
    (2) by inserting the following:
        "(A) $1,000,000,000 for fiscal year 2026;
        "(B) $1,100,000,000 for fiscal year 2027;
        "(C) $1,200,000,000 for fiscal year 2028;
        "(D) $1,300,000,000 for fiscal year 2029;
        "(E) $1,400,000,000 for fiscal year 2030; and
        "(F) for each fiscal year thereafter, the amount authorized
        for the preceding fiscal year, adjusted for inflation."

(b) ADVANCE APPROPRIATION.—Amounts appropriated under this section
shall be available for obligation during the second fiscal year
following the fiscal year for which the appropriation is made.

SEC. 4. LOCAL JOURNALISM INITIATIVE.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—Within the amounts authorized under section 3,
the Corporation for Public Broadcasting shall establish a Local
Journalism Initiative.

(b) PURPOSES.—The Initiative shall—
    (1) provide grants to public broadcasting stations for local
    news and journalism activities;
    (2) support regional journalism collaborations among stations;
    (3) fund journalist positions at stations in underserved
    communities;
    (4) support training for local journalists; and
    (5) encourage partnerships with other local news organizations.

(c) ALLOCATION.—Not less than $150,000,000 annually shall be
allocated to the Local Journalism Initiative.

(d) PRIORITY.—In awarding grants, the Corporation shall give
priority to—
    (1) stations serving communities without other local news
    sources;
    (2) regional collaborations among multiple stations;
    (3) stations serving diverse communities; and
    (4) innovative journalism models.

SEC. 5. DIGITAL TRANSFORMATION.

(a) DIGITAL INVESTMENT.—Within the amounts authorized, the
Corporation shall allocate not less than $100,000,000 annually
for digital infrastructure and innovation, including—
    (1) streaming and digital distribution platforms;
    (2) station digital capacity building;
    (3) digital journalism tools and training; and
    (4) audience research and development.

SEC. 6. REPORTING.

(a) ANNUAL REPORT.—The Corporation shall report annually to Congress
on—
    (1) the distribution of funds;
    (2) local journalism activities supported;
    (3) digital investments made;
    (4) audience reach and engagement; and
    (5) progress toward public interest goals.

SEC. 7. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act shall take effect on October 1, 2025.
```

**Explanation**:

- Doubles CPB funding over five years
- Provides automatic inflation adjustment
- Creates dedicated local journalism initiative
- Invests in digital transformation
- Maintains existing governance structure

### Public Media Dedicated Funding Act

**Purpose**: Establish a dedicated funding stream to insulate public media from annual political fights.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Public Media Dedicated Funding Act
of 2025".

SEC. 2. PUBLIC MEDIA TRUST FUND.

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—There is established in the Treasury a trust fund
to be known as the "Public Media Trust Fund" (referred to as the
"Fund").

(b) TRANSFERS.—
    (1) INITIAL CAPITALIZATION.—There shall be transferred to the
    Fund $10,000,000,000 from the general fund of the Treasury.
    (2) ONGOING TRANSFERS.—Beginning in fiscal year 2027, there
    shall be transferred to the Fund annually—
        (A) 5 percent of proceeds from spectrum auctions conducted
        by the Federal Communications Commission; and
        (B) such other amounts as Congress may appropriate.

(c) INVESTMENT.—The Secretary of the Treasury shall invest amounts
in the Fund in interest-bearing obligations of the United States.

(d) AVAILABILITY.—Amounts in the Fund shall be available, without
fiscal year limitation, for transfer to the Corporation for Public
Broadcasting for the purposes authorized under the Communications
Act of 1934.

(e) TRANSFER AMOUNTS.—
    (1) The Corporation shall receive annually from the Fund an
    amount equal to the greater of—
        (A) the interest earned on Fund investments; or
        (B) $500,000,000.
    (2) Amounts in excess of paragraph (1) shall remain in the
    Fund to grow the endowment.

SEC. 3. INDEPENDENCE.

(a) The Fund shall operate independently of the annual appropriations
process.

(b) Nothing in this Act shall be construed to—
    (1) authorize government control over programming decisions;
    (2) affect the editorial independence of public media stations;
    or
    (3) supersede the existing prohibitions on government
    interference in content.

SEC. 4. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act shall take effect upon enactment.
```

### CPB Governance Reform Act

**Purpose**: Strengthen the independence and effectiveness of CPB governance.

**Draft Text**:

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "CPB Governance Reform Act of 2025".

SEC. 2. BOARD COMPOSITION.

(a) QUALIFICATIONS.—Section 396(c)(1) of the Communications Act of
1934 is amended to require that—
    (1) at least 3 members have significant experience in public
    broadcasting, journalism, or media;
    (2) at least 2 members have significant experience in education;
    (3) at least 2 members have experience in business, finance,
    or management; and
    (4) members shall collectively reflect the geographic, cultural,
    and demographic diversity of the United States.

(b) INDEPENDENCE.—No person shall serve as a member of the Board
who—
    (1) is a registered lobbyist;
    (2) serves as an officer or employee of any political party; or
    (3) has served as a political appointee in the executive branch
    within the preceding 3 years.

SEC. 3. TERM LIMITS.

(a) Members shall serve for a single term of 6 years.

(b) Members may not serve more than one full term.

SEC. 4. TRANSPARENCY.

(a) MEETINGS.—Board meetings shall be open to the public except
when discussing personnel matters, legal strategy, or proprietary
business information.

(b) FINANCIAL DISCLOSURE.—Board members shall file annual financial
disclosure statements.

(c) REPORTING.—The Corporation shall publish—
    (1) all grant decisions and amounts;
    (2) Board meeting minutes (with appropriate redactions); and
    (3) annual audited financial statements.

SEC. 5. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act shall take effect upon enactment and apply to appointments
made after such date.
```

## State Model Legislation

### Model State Public Media Support Act

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "[State] Public Media Support Act".

SECTION 2. FINDINGS.

The Legislature finds that—
    (1) public broadcasting stations serve communities throughout
    [State];
    (2) public media provides trusted news and educational
    programming;
    (3) the collapse of local commercial news has increased demand
    for public media journalism; and
    (4) state support can strengthen public media's community
    service.

SECTION 3. STATE FUNDING.

(a) There is appropriated annually from the general fund to the
[State Arts Council/Education Department] $[X] for grants to public
broadcasting stations licensed to serve [State].

(b) Grants shall be distributed based on—
    (1) population served;
    (2) local programming produced; and
    (3) community service provided.

SECTION 4. LOCAL JOURNALISM PRIORITY.

Not less than 25 percent of funds appropriated under this Act shall
support local news and journalism activities.

SECTION 5. MATCHING REQUIREMENT.

Grants under this Act shall require a match of at least $1 for
every $1 of state funds from non-state sources.

SECTION 6. REPORTING.

Recipient stations shall report annually on—
    (1) use of state funds;
    (2) local programming produced; and
    (3) community service activities.

SECTION 7. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act takes effect July 1, [year].
```

### Model Tax Incentive for Public Media Act

```text
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "[State] Public Media Tax Credit Act".

SECTION 2. TAX CREDIT.

(a) A taxpayer may claim a credit against [state income tax] equal
to 50 percent of contributions made to qualified public broadcasting
stations, up to a maximum credit of $500 per taxpayer.

(b) For corporate taxpayers, the maximum credit shall be $5,000.

SECTION 3. QUALIFIED STATION.

A "qualified public broadcasting station" means a station—
    (1) licensed by the Federal Communications Commission as a
    noncommercial educational station;
    (2) affiliated with the Public Broadcasting Service or National
    Public Radio; and
    (3) serving [State].

SECTION 4. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act applies to contributions made on or after January 1, [year].
```

## Constitutional Considerations

### First Amendment

| Issue | Analysis |
|-------|----------|
| **Government funding of speech** | Permissible if content-neutral |
| **Editorial independence** | Constitutionally required |
| **Viewpoint neutrality** | Government cannot mandate content |
| **Public forum** | Stations not public forums |

### Key Safeguards

| Safeguard | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|
| **CPB insulation** | Separate from executive |
| **Decentralization** | No government programming |
| **Editorial independence mandate** | Statutory protection |
| **No prior restraint** | Cannot review before broadcast |

## Loopholes, Shortcomings, and Rectification

### Potential Loopholes

| Loophole | Description | Severity |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| **Appropriations vulnerability** | Annual fights continue | High |
| **Board politicization** | Partisan appointments | Medium |
| **Vague mandates** | Mission drift possible | Medium |
| **Formula gaming** | Distribution manipulation | Low |

### Shortcomings

| Issue | Impact | Root Cause |
|-------|--------|------------|
| **Still depends on Congress** | Political vulnerability | No dedicated stream |
| **Station fragmentation** | Inefficiency | Historical structure |
| **Governance weaknesses** | Political interference | Appointment process |
| **Limited enforcement** | Mission drift | Weak accountability |

### Rectification Procedures

1. **Dedicated funding stream** through trust fund or spectrum auction allocation
2. **Independent appointments** with professional qualifications
3. **Clear performance metrics** with public reporting
4. **Mission accountability** through regular reviews
5. **Station collaboration incentives** in funding formula
6. **Editorial independence protections** with enforcement mechanisms
