# Digital Advertising: History

## Early Digital Era (1994-2004)

### First Banner Ad (1994)

| Development | Significance |
|-------------|--------------|
| **AT&T banner on HotWired** | First web ad |
| **Cost-per-impression model** | Borrowed from traditional |
| **Simple tracking** | Click-through rates |
| **Direct sales** | Publisher sold directly |

### Dot-Com Era

| Development | Period |
|-------------|--------|
| **Banner ad growth** | Late 1990s |
| **Ad networks emerge** | DoubleClick (1996) |
| **Targeting begins** | Basic demographic |
| **Dot-com bust** | 2000-2001 |

### Google AdWords (2000)

| Innovation | Impact |
|------------|--------|
| **Search advertising** | Intent-based targeting |
| **Self-service** | Democratized advertising |
| **Auction model** | Market-based pricing |
| **Pay-per-click** | Performance model |

## Lessons from History

### What Enabled Platform Power

| Factor | Mechanism |
|--------|-----------|
| **Network effects** | More users = more valuable |
| **Data accumulation** | Better targeting |
| **Vertical integration** | Own the stack |
| **Acquisitions** | Eliminated competition |
| **First-mover** | Established position |

### What Harmed Publishers

| Factor | Mechanism |
|--------|-----------|
| **Commoditization** | Content less valuable |
| **Intermediation** | Platforms between publisher and advertiser |
| **Fee extraction** | Multiple layers |
| **Audience capture** | Platforms own relationship |
| **Data asymmetry** | Platforms have more |
