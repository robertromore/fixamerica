# Local Journalism: Current State

## The News Desert Crisis

### Geographic Distribution

As of 2024, the local news crisis has created vast information gaps across America:

| Category | Count | % of U.S. Counties |
|----------|-------|-------------------|
| **No local newspaper** | 204 | 6.5% |
| **One newspaper only** | 1,562 | 49.7% |
| **Ghost newspapers** (minimal staff) | 1,000+ | ~32% |
| **Robust local coverage** | <400 | <13% |

### Regional Patterns

| Region | Status |
|--------|--------|
| **Rural America** | Most severely affected; vast news deserts |
| **Small-town America** | One-paper towns with declining coverage |
| **Suburban areas** | Mixed; some startup activity |
| **Urban cores** | Better served but still declining |
| **Appalachia** | Severe news deserts across region |
| **Great Plains** | Sparse coverage, vast distances |
| **Deep South** | High concentration of news deserts |

### State-Level Variations

| State | Papers Lost (2005-2024) | Current Status |
|-------|------------------------|----------------|
| **Texas** | 200+ | Large rural news deserts |
| **California** | 150+ | Urban areas better served |
| **Ohio** | 80+ | Severe suburban/rural decline |
| **Pennsylvania** | 100+ | Major metro papers struggling |
| **Florida** | 90+ | Mixed; some nonprofit emergence |
| **New York** | 100+ | NYC strong; upstate devastated |

## The Newspaper Industry

### Employment Collapse

| Year | Newspaper Employees | Change from Peak |
|------|--------------------|--------------------|
| 2006 | 74,400 | Peak employment |
| 2010 | 46,700 | -37% |
| 2015 | 41,400 | -44% |
| 2020 | 30,800 | -59% |
| 2023 | ~24,000 | -68% |

### Circulation Decline

| Metric | 2005 | 2023 | Change |
|--------|------|------|--------|
| Daily print circulation | 53.3M | 20.9M | -61% |
| Sunday print circulation | 57.8M | 23.5M | -59% |
| Digital subscriptions | Minimal | ~15M | Growing |
| Total reach | Dominant | Minority | Transformed |

### Revenue Collapse

| Revenue Type | 2006 | 2023 | Change |
|--------------|------|------|--------|
| Print advertising | $46.6B | ~$9B | -81% |
| Digital advertising | $2.7B | ~$3.5B | +30% |
| Circulation revenue | $10.2B | ~$11B | +8% |
| Total revenue | ~$60B | ~$24B | -60% |

## Types of Local News Outlets

### Daily Newspapers

**Major Metro Dailies**

| Status | Examples | Characteristics |
|--------|----------|-----------------|
| **Stable nonprofit** | Philadelphia Inquirer, Salt Lake Tribune | Mission-focused, diversified |
| **Investment-backed** | Los Angeles Times (Biotech owner) | Uncertain future |
| **Hedge fund owned** | Denver Post, Chicago Tribune (Alden) | Stripped for profit |
| **Family owned** | Few remaining | Often best journalism |

**Small Daily Papers**

| Ownership | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| **Chain-owned** | Staff cuts, shared content, reduced local coverage |
| **Hedge fund** | Aggressive cost-cutting, often merged or closed |
| **Independent** | Struggling but often maintaining quality |
| **Nonprofit conversion** | Emerging model (Salt Lake Tribune) |

## Coverage Quality

### What's Being Covered

| Coverage Area | Status |
|---------------|--------|
| **Breaking news** | Still covered (if there's anyone to cover it) |
| **Crime/courts** | Reduced; often wire/press release based |
| **Local government** | Severely reduced in most markets |
| **K-12 education** | Minimal in most markets |
| **Local business** | Declining |
| **Community events** | Often crowdsourced |
| **Investigations** | Rare; resource-intensive |

### What's Disappeared

| Coverage Type | Impact |
|---------------|--------|
| **Statehouse bureaus** | Down 50%+ since 2000 |
| **Full-time city hall reporters** | Rare outside largest cities |
| **County government coverage** | Nearly extinct in rural areas |
| **School board meetings** | Rarely attended by reporters |
| **Local court proceedings** | Minimal coverage |
| **Zoning/planning meetings** | Usually uncovered |

## The Workforce Crisis

### Who Remains in Local Journalism

| Challenge | Impact |
|-----------|--------|
| **Experience drain** | Veterans took buyouts; institutional knowledge lost |
| **One-person newsrooms** | Can't cover full community |
| **Generalist demands** | Same person reports, photographs, edits, posts |
| **Low pay** | Starting salaries often $30-40K |
| **No job security** | Frequent layoffs, closures |
| **Burnout** | High turnover |

### Demographics

| Issue | Status |
|-------|--------|
| **Diversity** | Newsrooms whiter than communities served |
| **Age** | Younger reporters (experienced left); older holdouts |
| **Geography** | Urban areas attract talent; rural areas struggle |
| **Education** | J-school graduates avoiding local news |

## Current Debates

### Key Policy Questions

| Question | Positions |
|----------|-----------|
| **Should government fund journalism?** | Yes: public good / No: independence concerns |
| **Should platforms pay for news?** | Yes: they profit from it / No: market dynamics |
| **Tax incentives for subscribers?** | Yes: encourage support / No: inefficient |
| **Nonprofit conversion pathway?** | Yes: sustainability / No: complexity |

### Industry Debates

| Issue | Debate |
|-------|--------|
| **Paywall vs. free** | Revenue vs. reach tradeoffs |
| **Print vs. digital-only** | Some readers want print; expensive |
| **Consolidation** | Efficiency vs. local focus |
| **Collaboration** | Sharing resources vs. competition |

## Warning Signs

### Communities at Risk

| Indicator | Risk |
|-----------|------|
| **Single remaining paper** | One closure away from news desert |
| **Hedge fund acquisition** | Likely cuts coming |
| **Aging owner, no succession** | Closure risk |
| **Declining ad revenue** | Sustainability crisis |
| **Staff under 5** | Cannot adequately cover community |

### National Trends

| Trend | Implication |
|-------|-------------|
| **Continued ad migration to digital** | More papers will fail |
| **AI content generation** | May reduce human reporting |
| **Subscription fatigue** | Limits digital subscription model |
| **Philanthropy limits** | Not enough to replace market failure |
