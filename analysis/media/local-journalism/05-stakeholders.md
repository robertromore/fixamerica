# Local Journalism: Stakeholders

## Who Is Harmed

### Communities Without Local News

**News Desert Residents**

| Population | Impact |
|------------|--------|
| **Rural Americans** | ~60 million in counties with weak/no coverage |
| **Small-town residents** | Limited information about local government |
| **Low-income communities** | Less likely to have surviving local news |
| **Communities of color** | Often underserved by mainstream local media |

**Specific Harms**

| Harm | Evidence |
|------|----------|
| **Higher government costs** | Municipal borrowing costs 5-11 basis points higher |
| **More corruption** | Fewer FBI corruption convictions (less detection) |
| **Lower civic engagement** | 8-10% lower voter turnout |
| **Less electoral competition** | Fewer candidates for local office |
| **Worse public health** | Health crises go unreported |

### Local News Workers

**Displaced Journalists**

| Impact | Scale |
|--------|-------|
| **Job losses** | 70%+ of newspaper jobs since 2006 |
| **Career disruption** | Mid-career journalists with no alternatives |
| **Skills mismatch** | Print skills less valued in digital |
| **Geographic limitations** | Jobs concentrated in major metros |
| **Wage decline** | Remaining jobs pay less |

**Current Newsroom Workers**

| Challenge | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Overwork** | Fewer staff covering same territory |
| **Low pay** | Starting salaries $30-40K common |
| **Job insecurity** | Constant layoff threat |
| **Burnout** | High turnover, stress |
| **Limited advancement** | Fewer senior positions |

### Local Governments

| Official Type | Impact of News Loss |
|---------------|---------------------|
| **Mayors/managers** | Less public accountability (mixed impact) |
| **School boards** | Decisions made without public scrutiny |
| **County officials** | Rural areas especially unmonitored |
| **City councils** | Meetings rarely covered |

**Note**: Some officials benefit from less scrutiny; others genuinely want coverage to inform constituents.

### Local Businesses

| Impact | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Advertising loss** | No effective local advertising vehicle |
| **Information gap** | Less coverage of local economy |
| **Corruption risk** | Business environment less transparent |
| **Community weakening** | Weaker community hurts local business |

### Democratic Institutions

| Institution | Impact |
|-------------|--------|
| **Elections** | Less informed voting, nationalized races |
| **Courts** | Reduced transparency, less coverage |
| **Schools** | Board decisions unscrutinized |
| **Police** | Less accountability journalism |

## Who Benefits from the Status Quo

### Digital Platforms

**Google**

| Benefit | Mechanism |
|---------|-----------|
| **Free content** | News improves Google search without payment |
| **Advertising revenue** | Captures ad spend that funded journalism |
| **Data extraction** | News consumption provides user data |
| **No liability** | Section 230 shields from responsibility |

**Facebook/Meta**

| Benefit | Mechanism |
|---------|-----------|
| **Engagement** | News drives engagement without cost |
| **Ad targeting** | News reading provides behavioral data |
| **No content costs** | Users share news for free |
| **Traffic leverage** | Publishers depend on Facebook for audience |

### Hedge Fund Owners

**Alden Global Capital and Similar**

| Benefit | How |
|---------|-----|
| **Asset extraction** | Sell real estate, equipment |
| **Cash flow** | Extract profits without reinvestment |
| **Debt engineering** | Financial maneuvering |
| **Tax benefits** | Depreciation, interest deductions |

### Those Who Avoid Scrutiny

| Actor | Benefit from News Loss |
|-------|----------------------|
| **Corrupt officials** | Less investigation, exposure |
| **Corporate bad actors** | Local pollution, labor violations unreported |
| **Developers** | Zoning deals less scrutinized |
| **Police departments** | Misconduct less likely to be reported |
| **School administrators** | Decisions made without coverage |

## Stakeholder Strategies

### For News Advocates

| Strategy | Target |
|----------|--------|
| **Make it bipartisan** | Emphasize local, not "media" |
| **Economic framing** | Jobs, local business, government costs |
| **Community focus** | Local, not national politics |
| **Success stories** | Highlight working models |

### For Policymakers

| Approach | Rationale |
|----------|-----------|
| **Start state-level** | Less partisan; laboratories |
| **Tax incentives first** | Less controversial than direct funding |
| **Platform accountability** | Popular across spectrum |
| **Civic infrastructure frame** | Like roads, schools |

### For Platforms

| Pressure Point | Mechanism |
|----------------|-----------|
| **Regulatory threat** | Creates incentive to cooperate |
| **Public pressure** | Brand damage |
| **Antitrust action** | Existential threat |
| **International precedent** | Australia, Canada models |
