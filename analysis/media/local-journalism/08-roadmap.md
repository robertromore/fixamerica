# Local Journalism: Roadmap

## Strategic Framework

### Vision

By 2035, every American community has access to sustainable local news coverage that enables civic participation, government accountability, and community connection.

### Key Milestones

| Year | Goal |
|------|------|
| **2025** | Stop the bleeding; stabilize existing outlets |
| **2027** | State-level programs in majority of states |
| **2029** | Federal support infrastructure in place |
| **2032** | News desert elimination in progress |
| **2035** | Sustainable local news ecosystem nationwide |

## Implementation Strategies

### Political Strategy

| Approach | Rationale |
|----------|-----------|
| **Bipartisan framing** | Local news, not "media" |
| **Rural emphasis** | News deserts are rural |
| **Economic arguments** | Government costs, local business |
| **State-first** | Build momentum for federal |

### Coalition Building

| Partner | Role |
|---------|------|
| **Foundations** | Funding, advocacy |
| **Industry associations** | Unified voice |
| **Good government groups** | Transparency message |
| **Labor unions** | Worker perspective |
| **Local officials** | Constituent voice |

### Messaging Strategy

| Audience | Message |
|----------|---------|
| **Conservatives** | Local accountability, not federal media |
| **Progressives** | Community information access |
| **Moderates** | Civic infrastructure investment |
| **Business** | Advertising, economic information |

## Risk Assessment

### Political Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| **Partisan polarization** | Strict bipartisan framing |
| **Media distrust** | Local, not national focus |
| **Spending opposition** | Tax credits vs. direct spending |
| **First Amendment concerns** | Structural, not content support |

### Implementation Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| **Platform withdrawal** | Mandatory mechanisms as backup |
| **Hedge fund resistance** | Regulatory pressure |
| **Insufficient funding** | Build case with state successes |
| **Quality concerns** | Standards and training |

### Market Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| **Continued advertising decline** | Diversified revenue mandates |
| **AI disruption** | Training, human value emphasis |
| **Subscriber fatigue** | Community ownership models |
| **Platform changes** | Platform-independent distribution |

## Dependencies and Sequencing

### Critical Dependencies

| Initiative | Depends On |
|------------|------------|
| **Federal legislation** | Bipartisan coalition, political will |
| **Platform payments** | Legislation or voluntary deals |
| **State programs** | Political leadership, funding |
| **Nonprofit growth** | Foundation investment, tax policy |

### Sequencing Logic

```
State Experiments (2025-2026)
         ↓
    Demonstrate Success
         ↓
Federal Legislation (2026-2028)
         ↓
    Scaled Investment
         ↓
Industry Restructuring (2028-2032)
         ↓
    Sustainable Ecosystem (2032+)
```

## Contingency Plans

### If Federal Action Stalls

| Contingency | Action |
|-------------|--------|
| **State-led approach** | Interstate compacts, coordinated state action |
| **Foundation coalition** | Major coordinated philanthropic effort |
| **Platform deals** | Negotiated voluntary arrangements |
| **Market-based solutions** | Accelerate alternative models |

### If Platform Resistance Succeeds

| Contingency | Action |
|-------------|--------|
| **Public investment emphasis** | Direct funding over platform payments |
| **Antitrust enforcement** | Break up platforms |
| **International coordination** | Pressure through trade relationships |
| **Alternative distribution** | Reduce platform dependency |
