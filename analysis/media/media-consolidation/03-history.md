# Media Consolidation: History

## Early Media Ownership (Pre-1980)

### The Era of Diverse Ownership

| Era | Characteristic |
|-----|----------------|
| **1900-1930** | Family-owned newspapers; local radio |
| **1930-1950** | Network radio emerges; newspaper chains begin |
| **1950-1970** | TV networks established; local ownership norm |
| **1970-1980** | Ownership limits maintained; diversity valued |

### Original FCC Ownership Rules

| Rule | Year | Purpose |
|------|------|---------|
| **Local ownership preference** | 1940s | Community ties |
| **3 station limit** | 1941 | Prevent concentration |
| **Newspaper-broadcast ban** | 1975 | Cross-media diversity |
| **Duopoly prohibition** | 1940s | Market competition |

### Rationale for Limits

The FCC justified ownership limits based on:

1. **Marketplace of ideas**: Democracy requires diverse voices
2. **Local accountability**: Local owners serve local communities
3. **Competition**: Multiple owners drive quality
4. **Public interest**: Airwaves are public trust

## The Telecommunications Act of 1996

### The Landmark Deregulation

| Provision | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| **National radio cap eliminated** | Enabled iHeart, Clear Channel |
| **Local radio limits loosened** | 8 stations per market |
| **TV ownership cap raised** | To 35% of households |
| **Cross-ownership relaxed** | More combinations allowed |

### Immediate Consequences

**Radio Consolidation**

| Year | Metric |
|------|--------|
| 1996 | ~10,000 radio station owners |
| 2000 | ~3,800 owners |
| 2005 | Continued decline |
| Today | Handful of major owners |

**Clear Channel (now iHeart)**

| Year | Stations |
|------|----------|
| 1995 | 43 stations |
| 2000 | 1,200+ stations |
| Peak | 1,200+ stations |

### Bipartisan Support

The 1996 Act passed with bipartisan support based on:

- Promise of "convergence" benefits
- Industry lobbying
- Belief that competition would emerge
- Deregulation ideology across parties

## Lessons from History

### What Enabled Consolidation

| Factor | Mechanism |
|--------|-----------|
| **Deregulation ideology** | Both parties accepted it |
| **Industry lobbying** | Well-funded advocacy |
| **Promise of efficiency** | Never delivered |
| **Weak enforcement** | Limits not enforced |
| **Financial engineering** | Debt-fueled acquisitions |

### Consequences Over Time

| Decade | Primary Impact |
|--------|----------------|
| **1980s** | Chain ownership replaces family ownership |
| **1990s** | Radio consolidated; TV ownership expands |
| **2000s** | Mega-mergers; newspaper chains weaken |
| **2010s** | Hedge funds extract; local news collapses |
| **2020s** | Crisis response; nonprofit models emerge |

### Patterns to Note

1. **Deregulation preceded crisis** - Limits relaxed, then problems emerged
2. **Financial engineering destroys journalism** - Debt and extraction incompatible with quality
3. **Local always suffers** - Consolidation cuts local first
4. **Irreversibility** - Once lost, local ownership rarely returns
5. **Courts have limited impact** - Ultimately defer to FCC
