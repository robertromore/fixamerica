## Lessons from History

### Patterns

| Pattern | Evidence |
|---------|----------|
| **Network effects create monopolies** | Each platform dominant in category |
| **Platforms cycle through publishers** | Court, then abandon |
| **Crises drive moderation** | External pressure changes policy |
| **Regulation lags technology** | Years of catch-up |
| **International action leads** | EU ahead of U.S. |

### What History Suggests

1. **Platform power is structural** - Won't self-correct
2. **Voluntary action is temporary** - Responds to pressure
3. **News is expendable to platforms** - Not core business
4. **Regulation is necessary** - Markets won't solve
5. **Speed matters** - Harms accumulate during delay
