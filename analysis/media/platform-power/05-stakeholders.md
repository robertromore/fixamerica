# Platform Power: Stakeholders

## Who Is Harmed

### News Publishers

| Harm | Mechanism |
|------|-----------|
| **Revenue extraction** | Platforms capture advertising |
| **Traffic dependency** | Rely on platform referrals |
| **Algorithm vulnerability** | Visibility controlled by platforms |
| **Content use** | Headlines, snippets used without payment |
| **Competition** | Platforms compete for attention |

### Journalists

| Harm | Mechanism |
|------|-----------|
| **Job loss** | Revenue decline cuts newsrooms |
| **Harassment** | Platform-enabled abuse |
| **Manipulation** | Algorithm gaming |
| **Devaluation** | Content treated as commodity |

### Citizens/Users

| Harm | Mechanism |
|------|-----------|
| **Misinformation exposure** | Algorithms amplify false content |
| **Privacy violation** | Extensive data collection |
| **Filter bubbles** | Limited information exposure |
| **Manipulation** | Behavioral targeting |
| **Addiction patterns** | Engagement optimization |

### Democracy

| Harm | Mechanism |
|------|-----------|
| **Information inequality** | Uneven access to quality news |
| **Polarization** | Algorithm-driven division |
| **Foreign interference** | Platforms as manipulation vectors |
| **Concentrated speech power** | Private companies control discourse |

### Small Businesses

| Harm | Mechanism |
|------|-----------|
| **Advertising dependency** | Must advertise on platforms |
| **Algorithm changes** | Business model disruption |
| **Data asymmetry** | Platforms know more about their customers |
| **Platform fees** | Payment for visibility |

### Content Creators

| Harm | Mechanism |
|------|-----------|
| **Revenue precarity** | Algorithm changes = income loss |
| **Platform dependency** | No alternative distribution |
| **Rule uncertainty** | Arbitrary enforcement |
| **Demonetization** | Sudden income cut |

## Who Benefits

### Platform Companies

| Company | Benefits |
|---------|----------|
| **Google** | $238B revenue; search monopoly |
| **Meta** | $117B revenue; social monopoly |
| **Amazon** | Growing ad business; data |
| **Apple** | App store fees; services revenue |

### Platform Shareholders

| Benefit | Scale |
|---------|-------|
| **Stock appreciation** | Trillions in market cap |
| **Dividends/buybacks** | Tens of billions |
| **Monopoly returns** | Above-market profits |

### Platform Executives

| Benefit | Example |
|---------|---------|
| **Compensation** | CEO pay in hundreds of millions |
| **Power** | Control over global information |
| **Influence** | Political access |

### Advertisers (Partially)

| Benefit | Caveat |
|---------|--------|
| **Targeting precision** | But dependent on platforms |
| **Measurability** | But platforms control measurement |
| **Reach** | But at platform's price |

### Disinformation Actors

| Actor | Benefit |
|-------|---------|
| **Foreign adversaries** | Manipulation infrastructure |
| **Domestic bad actors** | Reach without accountability |
| **Scammers** | Access to victims |

## Stakeholder Dynamics

### Power Analysis

| Stakeholder | Power Level | Direction |
|-------------|-------------|-----------|
| **Platforms** | Very high | Status quo |
| **Federal government** | High (unused) | Varies |
| **EU** | High (active) | Regulation |
| **Publishers** | Low | Change |
| **Users** | Very low | Change |
| **Civil society** | Low | Change |

### Coalition Opportunities

| Coalition | Members | Focus |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| **Antitrust coalition** | Publishers, small business, advocates | Competition |
| **News coalition** | Publishers, journalists, local news | Platform payment |
| **Privacy coalition** | Advocates, users | Data protection |
| **Bipartisan tech reform** | Left and right | Different motives; some overlap |

### Challenges

| Challenge | Description |
|-----------|-------------|

| **User ambivalence** | Consumers like free services |
| **Advertiser dependency** | Businesses rely on platforms |
| **Complexity** | Issues hard to communicate |
