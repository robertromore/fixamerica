## Section 230 Status

### Current Law

Section 230 of the Communications Decency Act provides:

| Protection | Scope |
|------------|-------|
| **Publisher immunity** | Platforms not liable for user content |
| **Good Samaritan protection** | Can moderate without becoming publishers |
| **Broad interpretation** | Courts have interpreted expansively |

### Recent Developments

| Development | Impact |
|-------------|--------|
| **Supreme Court cases (2023)** | Declined to narrow Section 230 significantly |
| **State laws** | Texas, Florida laws (challenged) attempt reform |
| **Political pressure** | Both parties criticize but for different reasons |

## International Comparisons

### Other Democracies' Approaches

| Country/Region | Approach |
|----------------|----------|
| **European Union** | Digital Services Act, Digital Markets Act |
| **Australia** | News Media Bargaining Code (platform payments) |
| **Canada** | Online News Act (Meta removed news) |
| **UK** | Online Safety Bill |
| **Brazil** | Platform regulation debates |

### U.S. vs. EU

| Dimension | U.S. | EU |
|-----------|------|-----|
| **Content liability** | Section 230 immunity | Conditional immunity (DSA) |
| **Transparency** | Voluntary | Mandatory requirements |
| **Algorithm disclosure** | None | Required for large platforms |
| **Researcher access** | Limited | Mandated |
| **Enforcement** | Minimal | Active, significant fines |
