# Press Freedom: History

## Constitutional Foundation

### First Amendment (1791)

| Text | Interpretation |
|------|----------------|
| "Congress shall make no law... abridging the freedom... of the press" | Foundational protection |

### Early Understanding

| Period | Characterization |
|--------|------------------|
| **Founding era** | Protection against prior restraint primary concern |
| **Sedition Act (1798)** | First major test; expired, widely condemned |
| **19th century** | Limited federal role; states varied |
| **Pre-WWI** | Relatively robust freedom |

## Major Eras

### World War I Era (1917-1920)

| Development | Impact |
|-------------|--------|
| **Espionage Act (1917)** | Criminalized some speech/publication |
| **Sedition Act (1918)** | Broader restrictions (repealed 1920) |
| **Early First Amendment cases** | *Schenck*, *Abrams* - "clear and present danger" |
| **Press prosecutions** | Socialist and antiwar publications targeted |

### Between the Wars (1920-1941)

| Development | Impact |
|-------------|--------|
| **Near v. Minnesota (1931)** | Prior restraint unconstitutional |
| **Grosjean v. American Press (1936)** | Tax on press struck down |
| **Press professionalization** | Journalism education, ethics codes |
| **Radio emergence** | New medium, different regulation |

### World War II Era (1941-1945)

| Development | Impact |
|-------------|--------|
| **Office of Censorship** | Voluntary code for war |
| **Generally cooperative** | Press self-regulated for war effort |
| **Limited prosecutions** | Unlike WWI |

### Cold War Era (1945-1970)

| Development | Impact |
|-------------|--------|
| **Smith Act prosecutions** | Communist press targeted |
| **McCarthyism** | Chilling effect on journalism |
| **New York Times v. Sullivan (1964)** | "Actual malice" standard |
| **Red Lion Broadcasting (1969)** | Fairness Doctrine upheld |

### Pentagon Papers Era (1971)

| Development | Impact |
|-------------|--------|
| **New York Times v. United States** | Prior restraint rejected |
| **Nixon administration hostility** | Enemies list, threats |
| **Investigative journalism golden age** | Watergate, etc. |

### Post-Watergate (1972-1980)

| Development | Impact |
|-------------|--------|
| **Branzburg v. Hayes (1972)** | No constitutional reporter's privilege |
| **Watergate coverage** | Press as accountability force |
| **Shield law movement** | States begin passing protections |
| **FOIA strengthened (1974)** | Post-Watergate reforms |

### Reagan Era (1981-1992)

| Development | Impact |
|-------------|--------|
| **Fairness Doctrine eliminated (1987)** | Deregulation of broadcast |
| **Grenada/Panama pool restrictions** | Military-media tensions |
| **Leak prosecutions** | Some increase |
| **Corporate consolidation begins** | Economic pressure |

### Digital Era Begins (1993-2001)

| Development | Impact |
|-------------|--------|
| **Internet emergence** | New distribution, new questions |
| **24-hour news cycle** | Speed pressure |
| **Consolidation accelerates** | Fewer owners |
| **Clinton administration** | Generally positive press relations |

### Post-9/11 Era (2001-2008)

| Development | Impact |
|-------------|--------|
| **PATRIOT Act** | Expanded surveillance |
| **Embedded journalism** | Iraq War coverage model |
| **Judith Miller case** | Jailed for protecting source |
| **NSA surveillance revealed** | Warrantless wiretapping |
| **Leak concerns grow** | National security frame |

### Obama Era (2009-2016)

| Development | Impact |
|-------------|--------|
| **Record Espionage Act prosecutions** | 8 cases vs. 3 total before |
| **AP phone records seizure** | DOJ scandal |
| **James Risen case** | Fought subpoena for years |
| **Snowden revelations** | Mass surveillance exposed |
| **DOJ guidelines tightened** | After AP scandal |
| **Reporter's privilege push** | Failed in Congress |

### Trump Era (2017-2020)

| Development | Impact |
|-------------|--------|
| **"Enemy of the people" rhetoric** | Delegitimization |
| **Continued leak prosecutions** | Reality Winner, etc. |
| **CNN lawsuit** | Credential revocation challenged |
| **2020 protest violence** | Record journalist arrests |
| **Press under attack** | Physical threats increase |

### Biden Era (2021-Present)

| Development | Impact |
|-------------|--------|
| **DOJ guidelines strengthened** | Limits on subpoenas |
| **Surveillance revelations** | Apple/Google data demands exposed |
| **Rhetoric normalized** | Return to standard relations |
| **Ongoing prosecutions** | Assange case continues |
| **Federal shield law** | Still no progress |

## Landmark Cases

### Source Protection

| Case | Year | Holding |
|------|------|---------|
| **Branzburg v. Hayes** | 1972 | No First Amendment reporter's privilege |
| **Zurcher v. Stanford Daily** | 1978 | Newsrooms can be searched |
| **Cohen v. Cowles Media** | 1991 | Breach of confidentiality can be sued |
| **Miller/Cooper cases** | 2005 | No privilege against grand jury |

### Prior Restraint

| Case | Year | Holding |
|------|------|---------|
| **Near v. Minnesota** | 1931 | Prior restraint presumptively unconstitutional |
| **New York Times v. United States** | 1971 | Pentagon Papers can publish |
| **Nebraska Press v. Stuart** | 1976 | Gag orders rarely appropriate |
| **Snepp v. United States** | 1980 | CIA agreements enforceable |

### Defamation

| Case | Year | Holding |
|------|------|---------|
| **New York Times v. Sullivan** | 1964 | Actual malice standard for public officials |
| **Gertz v. Robert Welch** | 1974 | Different standard for private figures |
| **Hustler v. Falwell** | 1988 | Parody/satire protected |
| **Masson v. New Yorker** | 1991 | Fabricated quotes can be actionable |

### Access

| Case | Year | Holding |
|------|------|---------|
| **Richmond Newspapers v. Virginia** | 1980 | Right of access to trials |
| **Globe Newspaper v. Superior Court** | 1982 | Limits on courtroom closure |
| **Press-Enterprise Co. v. Superior Court** | 1986 | Access to jury selection |

## Shield Law History

### State Development

| Era | Development |
|-----|-------------|
| **1896** | Maryland passes first shield law |
| **1930s-1960s** | Slow growth |
| **1970s** | Post-Branzburg expansion |
| **1980s-2000s** | Most states adopt |
| **Present** | 40 states + DC have some form |

### Federal Attempts

| Year | Attempt | Result |
|------|---------|--------|
| **1970s** | Various bills | No passage |
| **2005-2009** | Free Flow of Information Act | Passed House, stalled Senate |
| **2013** | Schumer-Graham bill | No action |
| **2023** | PRESS Act | Passed House, Senate pending |

## FOIA History

### Timeline

| Year | Development |
|------|-------------|
| **1966** | FOIA enacted |
| **1974** | Strengthened post-Watergate |
| **1996** | Electronic FOIA amendments |
| **2007** | OPEN Government Act |
| **2016** | FOIA Improvement Act |

### Pattern

| Era | Characterization |
|-----|------------------|
| **Post-passage** | Agencies resistant |
| **Post-Watergate** | Strengthened |
| **Reagan era** | Executive Order limits |
| **Post-9/11** | National security exemptions expand |
| **Obama promise** | "Transparency" but mixed record |
| **Trump era** | Delays, denials increase |
| **Biden era** | Some improvement |

## Lessons from History

### What Protects Press Freedom

| Factor | Historical Evidence |
|--------|---------------------|
| **Constitutional text** | Foundation but not sufficient |
| **Judicial interpretation** | Courts often protective |
| **Public support** | Essential for political protection |
| **Press solidarity** | Industry advocacy matters |
| **Crisis moments** | Pentagon Papers, Watergate |

### What Threatens Press Freedom

| Factor | Historical Evidence |
|--------|---------------------|
| **National security claims** | WWI, Cold War, post-9/11 |
| **Political hostility** | Nixon, Trump |
| **Economic pressure** | Consolidation, decline |
| **Technology changes** | New challenges with each era |
| **Public distrust** | Enables political attacks |
