# Press Freedom: Stakeholders

## Who Is Affected

### Journalists

| Category | How Affected |
|----------|--------------|
| **National reporters** | Surveillance, source prosecution, access |
| **Local reporters** | Less legal protection, SLAPP suits |
| **Investigative journalists** | Most vulnerable to prosecution |
| **Freelancers** | Least institutional protection |
| **Foreign correspondents** | International risks |
| **Photojournalists** | Physical danger at protests |

### News Organizations

| Type | Stakes |
|------|--------|
| **Major newspapers** | Legal costs, source protection |
| **Broadcast networks** | License concerns, access |
| **Local outlets** | Cannot afford legal defense |
| **Digital natives** | Platform vulnerability |
| **Wire services** | Must protect global network |

### Sources and Whistleblowers

| Category | Stakes |
|----------|--------|
| **Government employees** | Job, freedom, safety |
| **Corporate whistleblowers** | Employment, legal risk |
| **Intelligence sources** | Extreme personal risk |
| **Confidential informants** | Identity protection critical |

### The Public

| Interest | Connection to Press Freedom |
|----------|----------------------------|
| **Right to know** | Depends on journalist ability to gather news |
| **Government accountability** | Requires watchdog journalism |
| **Public health** | Needs accurate information |
| **Consumer protection** | Investigative journalism role |

## Who Has Power

### Government Actors

| Actor | Power | Interests |
|-------|-------|-----------|
| **President** | Sets tone, DOJ direction, classification | Control information, avoid embarrassment |
| **Attorney General** | Prosecution decisions, guidelines | Law enforcement, precedent |
| **Intelligence agencies** | Surveillance, classification | Protect sources and methods |
| **Congress** | Legislation, oversight | Varies by member; often hostile |
| **Courts** | Privilege recognition, orders | Resolve disputes; defer on security |

### Executive Branch Specific

| Agency | Role in Press Freedom |
|--------|----------------------|
| **DOJ** | Prosecution, subpoena policy |
| **FBI** | Leak investigations, surveillance |
| **NSA** | Surveillance capabilities |
| **CIA** | Classification, foreign operations |
| **DHS/CISA** | Credential decisions |

### Platform Companies

| Company | Power | Role |
|---------|-------|------|
| **Google** | Search visibility, data | Can reveal sources to government |
| **Apple** | Device security, data | Same |
| **Meta** | Distribution, data | Same |
| **Twitter/X** | Distribution, political discourse | Hosting, amplification |

### Industry and Advocacy

| Actor | Power | Interests |
|-------|-------|-----------|
| **Media companies** | Lobbying, legal capacity | Protect business, some press freedom |
| **Press freedom organizations** | Advocacy, legal defense | Maximize protection |
| **Journalism associations** | Standards, advocacy | Professional interests |
| **First Amendment lawyers** | Litigation, expertise | Legal protection expansion |

## Stakeholder Positions

### On Federal Shield Law

| Stakeholder | Position | Rationale |
|-------------|----------|-----------|
| **News organizations** | Strong support | Source protection essential |
| **Press freedom groups** | Strong support | Core protection |
| **DOJ** | Opposed (traditionally) | Want subpoena power |
| **Intelligence community** | Opposed | National security concerns |
| **Civil liberties groups** | Generally support | Free press value |
| **Some conservatives** | Mixed | Free press vs. leak concerns |

### On Espionage Act Reform

| Stakeholder | Position | Rationale |
|-------------|----------|-----------|
| **Press freedom advocates** | Support reform | Overbroad application |
| **National security hawks** | Oppose reform | Deterrence needed |
| **Civil liberties groups** | Support reform | First Amendment concerns |
| **Intelligence community** | Oppose reform | Protect classified info |
| **News organizations** | Support reform | Chilling effect |

### On FOIA Strengthening

| Stakeholder | Position | Rationale |
|-------------|----------|-----------|
| **Journalists** | Strong support | Need access |
| **Transparency advocates** | Strong support | Core mission |
| **Federal agencies** | Resistance | Burden, discretion |
| **Privacy advocates** | Some concerns | Personal information |
| **Business groups** | Mixed | Transparency vs. proprietary info |

### On Surveillance Reform

| Stakeholder | Position | Rationale |
|-------------|----------|-----------|
| **Press freedom groups** | Support limits | Source protection |
| **Civil liberties groups** | Support limits | Privacy rights |
| **Intelligence community** | Oppose limits | Collection authority |
| **Law enforcement** | Oppose limits | Investigation tools |
| **Tech companies** | Mixed | User privacy vs. government relations |

## Power Analysis

### Most Powerful Stakeholders

| Rank | Stakeholder | Why |
|------|-------------|-----|
| 1 | **Executive branch** | Prosecution power, surveillance |
| 2 | **Federal courts** | Privilege recognition |
| 3 | **Congress** | Legislation |
| 4 | **Major news organizations** | Litigation capacity, lobbying |
| 5 | **Platform companies** | Data, distribution |

### Least Powerful Stakeholders

| Stakeholder | Why Weak |
|-------------|----------|
| **Freelance journalists** | No institutional backing |
| **Local news outlets** | No legal resources |
| **Individual sources** | Isolated, vulnerable |
| **General public** | Diffuse interest |

## Coalition Analysis

### Press Freedom Coalition

| Member | Contribution |
|--------|--------------|
| **News organizations** | Resources, lobbying |
| **Press freedom groups** | Advocacy, expertise |
| **Civil liberties groups** | Legal, political support |
| **Academic experts** | Research, credibility |
| **Some bipartisan legislators** | Political access |

### Coalition Challenges

| Challenge | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Definition debates** | Who counts as journalist |
| **National security split** | Some accept exceptions |
| **Partisan dynamics** | Press seen as partisan |
| **Public support weak** | Low trust in media |

### Opposition Coalition

| Member | Interest |
|--------|----------|
| **DOJ/prosecutors** | Investigation tools |
| **Intelligence agencies** | Classification, surveillance |
| **Some politicians** | Avoid accountability |
| **SLAPP plaintiffs** | Silence critics |

## Engagement Strategies

### For Journalists and News Organizations

| Strategy | Approach |
|----------|----------|
| **Legal preparedness** | Know rights, have counsel |
| **Source protection** | Encryption, secure communication |
| **Solidarity** | Support others under attack |
| **Public education** | Explain why press freedom matters |

### For Advocates

| Strategy | Approach |
|----------|----------|
| **Bipartisan framing** | Press freedom not partisan |
| **Case documentation** | Track and publicize incidents |
| **Legal support** | Fund defense, bring cases |
| **Legislative advocacy** | Shield law, FOIA reform |

### For Sources

| Strategy | Approach |
|----------|----------|
| **Secure communication** | Signal, SecureDrop |
| **Legal counsel** | Know rights before talking |
| **Documentation** | Record evidence |
| **Understand risks** | Shield laws don't always protect |
