# Current State: Overview

## Purpose

This section describes present conditions in Israel, the West Bank, and Gaza as of early 2026, providing the factual foundation for solution analysis.

> **Note:** Conditions, particularly in Gaza, are evolving rapidly. This snapshot reflects conditions in January 2026 based on available reporting. Readers should verify current conditions against recent sources.

## Executive Summary

### The Territories

**Israel** (9.8 million people) is a parliamentary democracy with an advanced economy, currently governed by its most right-wing coalition in history. Palestinian citizens (~21% of population) hold citizenship but face documented disparities. The country is experiencing profound internal tensions over judicial reform, the secular-religious divide, and war conduct.

**The West Bank** (3.2 million Palestinians) remains under Israeli military occupation, divided into Areas A, B, and C under the Oslo framework. Over 700,000 Israeli settlers now live in the West Bank and East Jerusalem. The Palestinian Authority governs population centers with severely limited powers and faces a deep legitimacy crisis.

**Gaza** (pre-war: 2.2 million) has been devastated by Israeli military operations following the October 7, 2023 Hamas attack. Tens of thousands killed, 60-70% of housing destroyed, most infrastructure non-functional. Humanitarian catastrophe with famine conditions, healthcare collapse, and mass displacement. No agreed post-war governance plan.

**East Jerusalem** (~370,000 Palestinians, ~570,000 Jews) remains a flashpoint. Palestinians have "permanent resident" status but not citizenship. Settlement expansion continues. The Temple Mount/Haram al-Sharif "status quo" is under pressure.

### The Political Landscape

**Israeli politics** has shifted dramatically rightward. The Netanyahu government depends on far-right coalition partners who oppose any Palestinian state. The "peace camp" has collapsed as a political force. October 7 created a political crisis with deep divisions over hostages vs. military objectives, and over accountability for the intelligence failure.

**Palestinian politics** remains paralyzed by the Fatah-Hamas split. The PA is widely seen as corrupt and illegitimate. Hamas's governmental structures have been destroyed but it continues guerrilla resistance. No elections since 2006. No legitimate succession plan for the aging Abbas.

**Regionally**, the Abraham Accords represent a shift toward normalization with Israel, though October 7 has complicated this—Saudi normalization now appears contingent on a Palestinian state path. Iran's "axis of resistance" (Hezbollah, Houthis, Iraqi militias) opened multiple fronts in solidarity with Gaza.

**Internationally**, the conflict has produced ICC arrest warrants for Israeli and Hamas leaders, ICJ rulings against the occupation, and accelerating recognition of Palestine (~145 countries). The gap between international legal consensus and on-the-ground reality continues to widen.

### Current Conflict (October 2023-Present)

**Military operations** continue in Gaza with cycles of withdrawal and re-entry. Hamas is degraded but not eliminated. West Bank operations have intensified. The Lebanon border remains tense with ~60,000 Israelis displaced from the north.

**Ceasefire negotiations** have produced only brief pauses. Key sticking points: permanent ceasefire, Israeli withdrawal, hostage/prisoner exchanges, Hamas's future role, reconstruction control. Neither side willing to accept the other's minimum demands.

**Displacement** is massive: ~1.5-1.8 million Gazans remain displaced, many multiple times. ~60,000 Israelis from northern communities. October 7 survivors from Gaza envelope communities largely remain displaced.

### Economic Impact

**Israel's economy** has contracted: reservist mobilization, tourism collapse, credit downgrades, budget strain from war costs. Tech sector resilient but affected.

**Palestinian economy**: West Bank devastated by work permit suspensions and movement restrictions. Gaza economy has effectively ceased to exist—90%+ unemployment, infrastructure destroyed. Reconstruction estimated at $50-80+ billion over decades.
