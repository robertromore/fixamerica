# Confederation Model

## Overview

Confederation frameworks propose two sovereign states (Israel and Palestine) that share certain institutions and allow freedom of movement and residence across the entire territory.

## Core Concept

*To be developed*

- Two distinct states with full sovereignty
- Shared institutions for specific functions
- Open borders with freedom of movement
- Residence rights across both states
- Citizenship tied to one state

## Key Elements

### Sovereignty

*To be developed*

- Each state controls own territory
- Separate citizenship
- Distinct national identities preserved
- International recognition for both

### Shared Institutions

*To be developed*

- Joint economic council

### Freedom of Movement

*To be developed*

### Settlements Under Confederation

*To be developed*

## Arguments For

*To be developed*

- Addresses settlement problem

- Flexible and innovative
- Builds on EU model

## Arguments Against

*To be developed*

- Security coordination challenges
- May be worst of both worlds
