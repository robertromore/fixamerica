*RCV runoff required (no first-round majority). Third-party votes (5.7%) redistributed. Final: 51.9% after redistribution.*

## ⚠️ OUTCOME DIFFERS

Under the Electoral College, the Electoral College winner **lost** the first-round popular vote.

Under Regional Federalism's system (NPV with RCV if no majority), **D** would have won instead of **R**.

## Swing State Analysis

**Closest state:** MI (margin: 0.22%)

**Votes to flip outcome:** ~27,500

**Decisive states (closest to tipping point):** MI, PA

### Key Insight

Under the Electoral College, the election outcome depends on a small number of swing states. In 2016, approximately **27,500 votes** in the decisive states could have changed the outcome.

Under Regional Federalism, every vote counts equally regardless of state, eliminating the swing state phenomenon.
